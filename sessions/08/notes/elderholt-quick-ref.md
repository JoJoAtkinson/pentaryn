# Elderholt — DM Quick Reference

Fast-access notes for running a session in Elderholt. Everything links back to the source files.

---

## What Is Elderholt?

- Hidden coven polity / witch enclave in a dark pine forest
- ~500 souls total. ~100 active witches. **12 Elders**.
- Alignment: True Neutral (protective, terrifying)
- Capital: **Elderstead** — disguised as a failing frontier outpost
- The Witch-Tree (Silniel) dominates the town square
- Beneath the town: **the Axiom** — buried runic chamber where the Voice is loudest

## The Voice — Core Magic Rules

- Not a deity — a nameless, impersonal force. **Never give it a name.**
- Witches bargain with it; every answer has a cost
- Speaks differently to each witch (dead lover's voice, chorus of children, your own voice arguing back)
- Safest to access in **ancient ruins** and the **Axiom** beneath Elderstead
- Young children can hear it passively without cost — but the moment an adult *asks* a child to channel it, the child pays the toll

## Two Witch Paths

| Ward-Witch | Deep-Witch |
|---|---|
| Protection, sight, endurance | Deepest secrets, transformation |
| Living runes toe-to-scalp | Body warped by bargains |
| Violet glowing eyes | Ember sigils, shifting shadows |
| Cautious, stays within wards | Reaches past wards, pays in flesh |

Both paths are valid. Same class/spells can end up either way depending on choices.

## How Outsiders Are Handled

- No standing treaties. Favors are personal, case-by-case.
- Envoys petition the whole coven; each witch decides alone.
- One witch might answer; the rest might simply watch.
- Only existential threats bring the coven to act as one (handful of times per century).
- Players arriving as outsiders will be received in the central hall **under careful wards**.

> Source: [elderholt.md](../../../world/factions/elderholt/elderholt.md)

## Things Outsiders Get Wrong

1. The Witch-Tree is Elderholt's patron goddess (she's a sister who paid the highest price)
2. The witches worship the tree (they honor her, never worship)
3. Elderholt is weak because it's small (if they acted as one, they could crush any faction)
4. The witches are unified (every decision is individual)

## The Unthreaded (Non-Witches)

- Men and women who don't hear the Voice still live in Elderholt
- Farm, fish, keep bees, tend ovens
- Respect the coven, some fear it, a few resent it
- **Do not ask for magic** — witches do not ask for understanding
- Festivals: **Emberwake** (winter), **Tidebound Fair** (summer)
- Food: root stews, black pine bread with honey, smoked fish
- Artistic traditions: wood carving (ward-like patterns) and quilting (story-quilts passed through families)

## Critical Lore to Remember

- **Only girls** are tithed to the Voice. Boys' anger gives the Voice "a sharper edge."
- Marwen is an exception — born male, reborn female through fire at age 6. Ishvar opposed her initiation. The Axiom accepted her.
- **Using children as shields** for bargains is the blackest sin. Punishable by death or Voice-judgment.
- The witches **sealed the Underdark** — Kharbora killed matron mothers, Taramet forged the door. Key is lost.
- **Broken/lost witches** exist in the wild — corrupted, monstrous, can sense their own kind. Marrith and Raven handle them.

## Adventure Hooks (From Source Material)

- A Threadling's dreams echo a presence from the tree's roots
- Someone breaks a ward-ring from the inside — no one remembers doing it
- An envoy arrives with a petition; only one witch answers, and her answer contradicts the others
- A broken-loss witch is spotted in the wild — send the crow
- Naelith speaks a prophecy that may or may not be for *now*
