# Elder Dynamics & Relationships

Who gets along, who doesn't, and how they interact in council. Quick RP reference.

---

## Power Blocs / Tensions

### Ishvar vs. Brannith (The Core Tension)

- **Ishvar** speaks first in council — frames everything as doctrine and "greater good"
- **Brannith** speaks last — enforces the doctrine exactly as written, exposing every flaw
- Ishvar can't manipulate Brannith because Brannith complies *too literally*
- This Ishvar/Brannith dynamic is the coven's self-correcting mechanism

### Marrith: The Final Line

- Feared because she proved she will execute *anyone*, including her own daughter
- When Marrith arrives, witches stop arguing
- She doesn't hunt monsters — she stops witches before they become them

### Kharbora vs. Vezrugar (Opposites)

- **Kharbora:** sacrificed body, kept her mind and emotions fully intact
- **Vezrugar:** sacrificed emotions, kept her body (thin and lantern-lit, but physical)
- Both are warnings — different prices, same lesson

## Who to Use When

| Situation | Best Elder |
|---|---|
| PCs need help at a terrible price | **Arlen** (she'll take the toll herself) |
| PCs expect a normal court/negotiation | **Ishvar** (gives them a contract-trap) |
| PCs need uncomfortable truth | **Brannith** (plain, specific, difficult to misunderstand) |
| PCs face a hard moral choice about sacrifice | **Kharbora** (won't choose for them) |
| PCs need to know what they're feeling | **Vezrugar** (reads emotions, offers no comfort) |
| PCs encounter prophecy or time-sensitive info | **Naelith** (may be early, late, or impossible to act on) |
| PCs face a corrupted witch or ritual gone wrong | **Marrith** (measured annihilation) |
| PCs encounter undead or existential threat | **Raven** (deadpan, overwhelming necromancy) |
| PCs seek weather aid or dreams | **Silniel** (silent, through feeling and dream) |
| PCs need a crafted item or tool | **Taramet** (she'll make it, can't keep it) |
| PCs need knowledge of other cultures | **The Wayward Witch** (if you can find her) |
| PCs interact with witch-children in Elderstead | **Calyrix** (she's the walls, mirrors, and doorframes) |

---

## Council Seating Order (Implied)

1. **Ishvar** frames the question (doctrine, precedent)
2. Other elders weigh in
3. **Brannith** speaks last (shows what the doctrine *actually* looks like when enforced)

Silniel doesn't attend (she's a tree). Raven avoids gatherings. The Wayward Witch is rarely present. Calyrix assembles as a consensus-self but speaks in the plural.
