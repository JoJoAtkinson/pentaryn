# Elderholt Roleplay Cheat Sheet

Quick voice lines, atmosphere notes, and RP anchors for running Elderholt at the table.

---

## The Feel of Elderstead

- Rain-blackened shingles patched with tar
- Leaning porches, a weary market square
- Resin-sour pine forest, always damp
- The enormous Witch-Tree dominates the skyline — birds, lanterns, ribbons, bones, charms
- Beneath: a deliberate lattice of chambers, sealed wards, soft humming from below
- Lanterns dim when Kharbora walks past
- Walls whisper corrections (Calyrix)

---

## Read-Aloud: Arriving at Elderstead

> The forest breaks without warning. One moment you are pushing through wet pine boughs and the next you are standing at the edge of a settlement that looks like it has been losing a slow war with the weather for decades. Shingles curl from rooftops like peeling bark. Tar patches gleam dully in what little light reaches through the canopy.
>
> At the center of town, an oak so large it seems impossible rises from the cobbles. Lanterns and bone charms sway from its branches. Ribbons dark with age flutter between carved offerings. Birds watch you from every bough. The air smells of resin, cold stone, and something older — something that hums just below hearing.
>
> A woman in a plain shawl waits at the entrance to a low, wood-framed hall. She does not smile. She does not need to.
>
> "The Elders will hear you," she says. "Under wards."

## Quick Voice Lines by Elder

Use these as openers or reactions to get into character fast.

**Arlen** (warm, broken):
> "I'll carry it. I've carried worse."

**Brannith** (cackle, too many teeth):
> "Of course you can, little debt. The question is whether you should."

**Calyrix** (whisper from a wall):
> "We remember when you said that. You don't."

**Ishvar** (gentle, precise):
> "That is a generous offer. Let me show you what it will cost."

**Kharbora** (snarl from shadow):
> "Don't look at me for permission. It's your flesh on the table."

**Marrith** (flat, final):
> "I warned once. I will not warn again."

**Naelith** (calm, dislocated):
> "You already asked me this. Or you will. Either way, the answer doesn't help."

**Raven** (deadpan):
> "I love visitors."

**Taramet** (distracted, fidgeting):
> "Hold still. I'm making something for you. You'll know what when it's done."

**Vezrugar** (steady, reads your emotions):
> "You are afraid. Not of me — of what I'll say. You're right to be."

## Rules to Remember at the Table

1. **Witches don't pray.** They bargain. The Voice is a force, not a god.
2. **No one gives the Voice a name.** Names are doors and chains.
3. **Children hear the Voice passively — never ask them to channel it.** That's the blackest sin.
4. **Each witch decides alone** whether to help outsiders. No binding treaties.
5. **Rank = toll paid**, not badges or uniforms.
6. **Elderholt doesn't rule territory.** They claim no land beyond the forest.
7. **"Leave the witches alone"** is the world's simplest survival rule.

## Sensory Anchors

| Sense | Anchor |
|---|---|
| **Sight** | The Witch-Tree; ward-runes glowing faintly on skin; shadows that move wrong |
| **Sound** | Charms clattering in wind; whispered corrections from walls; a hum from below |
| **Smell** | Resin-sour pine; cold stone; old ink; ash |
| **Touch** | The cold radiating from Kharbora; steady warmth from Arlen; the root-sitting chill |
| **Taste** | Root stew, black pine bread, honey, smoked fish |

## Common Unthreaded Details (Background Color)

- Wood carvings on doorframes, walking sticks, cutlery — ward-like patterns that aren't wards
- Story-quilts hanging in homes, each frame telling a life tale
- **Emberwake** festival (winter) — fire, community, witches invited as guests not leaders
- **Tidebound Fair** (summer) — outsiders might mistake it for a witches' ceremony
