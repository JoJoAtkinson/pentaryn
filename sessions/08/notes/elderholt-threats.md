# Elderholt Threats & Encounters

Threats the party might face in or around Elderholt, plus the coven's known dangers.

---

## Broken-Loss Witches

Deep-witches who went too far, lost themselves, and became monsters.

**Key rules:**
- They are alone — no coven, no tradition, no support
- They can **sense their own kind** (like smoke finding a crack)
- Some left for the right reasons, some for the wrong ones
- They may not even look like witches anymore — monsters through and through
- **Cannot be reasoned with**
- Marrith and Raven are the ones who handle them

**If the party encounters one:**
- **Marrith's instructions to Marwen:** Run. If you can't run, talk — promise you can heal it (there is no cure). Buy time for allies. Send the crow.
- They are extreme threats — do not let PCs face one unprepared

### Known: Mara "Centipede" (Ardenholt)

- Human head on enormous centipede body
- Lurks in ancient ruins where the Voice comes through cleanly
- Splintered mind — lucid for a line, then lost
- Won't initiate violence; if pressed, incapacitates and escapes
- The coven doesn't know she exists (lost to time)
- **Threat level:** Psychological horror, control magic, prophecy-at-a-cost

## The Underdark (Sealed)

- Elderholt's witches acted as one to purge the Underdark
- **Kharbora** went down with other elders and killed several drow matron mothers (she remembers her part in detail, not the wider story)
- **Taramet** forged a massive door to seal a corridor to the deep unknown
- The key is **lost** — Taramet doesn't seem worried
- This is a dormant threat: what's behind that door?

## Internal Threats

### Ishvar's Slow Corruption

- She bends the spirit of the coven while staying within the letter
- Accusing her outright would tear the coven apart
- The Brannith check is the only thing keeping her in line
- If Brannith were ever removed or destroyed, Ishvar would be unchecked

### Calyrix's Decay

- She's growing more abstract, responses slower, less emotionally nuanced
- When a part of her crumbles (physical anchor destroyed), she rebalances but loses something permanently
- She has not been whole in centuries
- If enough anchors are destroyed, what happens?

### Naelith's Prophecy

- Spoke Marwen's name in a dark prophecy: "great ruin and destruction"
- Deliberately keeps distance from Marwen
- Refuses to elaborate
- Is this prophecy about Marwen *causing* ruin, or ruin coming *for* her?

---

## The Voice Itself

- Not a threat in the traditional sense — it's a force, like a wildfire
- It does not hate, love, or judge
- It takes its due without malice
- **It is not omniscient** — Raven proved it can be outmaneuvered through secrecy
- But it needs only a **name** to choose a different toll
- It's loudest beneath Elderstead in the Axiom
