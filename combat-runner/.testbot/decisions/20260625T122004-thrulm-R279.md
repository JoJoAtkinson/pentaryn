### Feel analysis

**FEEL-VS-R279-01 (LOG ONLY): Marwen is auto-downed by Void Scream on any beholder-first initiative.**
Marwen's 32 HP max vs Void Scream 33 avg psychic on a failed save: she cannot survive a failed save.
With WIS +2 vs DC 16, she fails 65% of the time. On beholder-first initiative (which happens roughly
when beholder rolls 11+ on d20+3 and Marwen rolls below 14 on d20+3 — very common), Marwen goes down
before her first turn. She has no counterplay: Counterspell does not work on creature abilities, and
Misty Step (bonus action, not main action) would need to be preemptively used on a prior turn.
This may be design intent ("meant to lose" — wizard always falls first), but at a minimum, the
encounter should telegraph this threat so the DM can keep Marwen at range.

**FEEL-VS-R279-02 (LOG ONLY): FRIGHTENED creates total melee lockout for Compass Edge.**
After Void Scream, Bazgar and Sabriel cannot advance toward the beholder (FRIGHTENED: cannot
willingly move closer to the source of fear while in line of sight). The beholder hovers 40-60 ft
up near the altar — out of melee range — and can simply hold position. Both melee PCs become
defensive: they can Dodge, make ranged attacks with disadvantage, or try to break line of sight
(which the encounter terrain may not support). Bazgar as a Battlemaster has Precision Attack
(removes one roll from disadvantage) but this eats superiority dice rapidly. The beholder's
optimal response is to stay airborne and use Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA) each round,
averaging 22 force + 13 bludgeoning per round while Sabriel and Bazgar are locked in place.

**FEEL-VS-R279-03 (LOG ONLY): Shrine-proximity disadvantage is invisible without DM track.**
The within-10-ft condition fires only at the moment of Void Scream. If the DM isn't actively
measuring, the disadvantage is silently missed. No DB field or runner prompt surfaces this.
Low priority since it only affects one condition.

**Legendary action economy — Void Scream context:**
- Pattern confirmed: Drain Divinity (3 LA, all-in) dominates against divine casters. Even after
  Void Scream downs Sabriel, the beholder would have used Drain Divinity if she were still up.
- After Void Scream as main action: Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA) is optimal follow-up.
  Spend all 3 LA efficiently. No degenerate "always Move" or "always Tentacle" pattern here —
  the mix is tactically appropriate.
- Move (1 LA): only useful if party breaks out of 30-ft radius. In this scenario, never needed.

**Beholder threatens Marwen in ≤2 rounds — scenario notes confirm ✓** (she's down Round 1).
