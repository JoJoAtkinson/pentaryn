## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `antireality-timing`

**Slice description (from rotation.yml):** Bazgar attacks beholder — +7 to hit vs AC 17. Beholder uses Antireality (reaction, once/round) to impose disadvantage on the first attack roll each round. Simulate 3 rounds, 2 attacks per round (Extra Attack). Record flip rate and economy.

### Setup

- Bazgar attack bonus: +7 (assumed STR +4 at Fighter 5, PB +3)
- Beholder AC: 17 base; 17 in-chamber (Void-Feeding affects attack rolls and damage, NOT AC)
- To hit: needs roll ≥ 10. P(hit, single roll) = 11/20 = **55%**
- With Antireality disadvantage: P(both dice ≥ 10) = (11/20)² = 121/400 = **30.25%**
- Effective reduction per Antireality use: **-25 percentage points** on targeted attack roll

### 3-Round Simulation

Antireality fires on Attack 1 each round (beholder uses reaction immediately when Bazgar declares the first attack). Attack 2 is a normal roll (reaction spent).

| Rnd | Atk | Mode | Dice | Result | Without-dis would hit? | Flipped? |
|-----|-----|------|------|--------|------------------------|----------|
| 1 | 1 | dis | 12, 16 → keep 12; +7=19 | **HIT** vs 17 | Yes (16+7=23) | No — hit anyway |
| 1 | 2 | norm | 8; +7=15 | MISS vs 17 | n/a | — |
| 2 | 1 | dis | 6, 14 → keep 6; +7=13 | MISS vs 17 | Yes (14+7=21 HIT) | **YES — flipped** |
| 2 | 2 | norm | 12; +7=19 | **HIT** vs 17 | — | — |

**Results:**
- Total hits: 2/6 (33%) — close to expected 2.56 over 3 rounds

- Hits blocked by Antireality: 2 of what would have been 4 hits = **50% reduction** in this simulation (expected long-run: ~35% reduction of what would otherwise hit)

### BUG-F7-02: Quantified impact of "+2 AC" vs intended "disadvantage"

The DB `effect` field for `antireality` reads "+2 AC" (BUG-F7-02, open 128+ fires). A DM running the encounter live sees the runner surface "+2 AC" — which they would apply as a transient AC boost to the beholder.

Effect of "+2 AC" interpretation:
- Bazgar needs roll ≥ 12 (not 10) to hit AC 19
- P(hit) = 9/20 = **45%** — a straight roll reduction, not disadvantage
- This is a significantly WEAKER protection than the intended disadvantage: 45% vs 30.25%

The intended behavior (disadvantage → 30%) is meaningfully stronger than the "+2 AC" misread (45%). The bug makes Antireality **less** protective than designed by ~15 percentage points per use. Over 3 rounds, the "+2 AC" version allows ~0.45 more hits than intended (expected 1.35 hits on disadvantaged rolls under +2 AC, vs 0.9 under disadvantage).

**At the table, the wrong interpretation favors the party**, which inverts the design intent. BUG-F7-02 should be prioritized.

### Pre-roll timing (confirmed active per .md note)

The stat block's FIX-F33-A note says the runner fires the Antireality prompt **post-hit** (after the attack resolves on the damage event). At the table:
1. Attacker declares attack.
2. Attack die rolled.
3. If hit: damage event fires.
4. **Runner prompt for Antireality appears** — but the attack already landed.

The DM must remember to declare Antireality before step 2. The runner provides no pre-roll trigger. This is an ongoing runner-architecture gap: the stat block fix is in the .md, the DB `effect` still says "+2 AC", and the runner cannot surface a pre-roll reaction prompt. These are three separate bugs with only the .md partially fixed.

### Antireality stacking with existing disadvantage

PHB rule: advantage and disadvantage do not stack (straight roll results when both apply). If Bazgar is FRIGHTENED by Void Scream (a real threat — DC 16 Wis save, Bazgar assume Wis +1 = 70% fail rate), he already attacks the beholder at disadvantage (beholder in line of sight). In this state:

- Beholder uses Antireality → adds a second source of disadvantage → **straight roll** (double disadvantage = single disadvantage = advantage/disadvantage cancel if they were equal count)

Wait — correct RAW: two sources of disadvantage do NOT equal advantage. Double disadvantage still equals disadvantage (PHB: "if a combination of factors gives you both advantage and disadvantage, you have neither, regardless of how many sources of each"). Multiple sources of disadvantage: "no matter how many sources of disadvantage you have, if you don't also have advantage, you roll two dice and take the lower result." So double disadvantage = still disadvantage.

Correction to rotation.yml note: "if Bazgar already has disadvantage from another source, Antireality is wasted" is **incorrect**. Antireality stacked with existing disadvantage still results in disadvantage (same two-dice-take-lower). Antireality is not wasted when stacking with other disadvantage sources — it correctly adds its second source. The reaction timing note in the .md (FIX-F33-A) is the only functional concern.

However, if Bazgar has ADVANTAGE (e.g., from a flanking ally or Sabriel's Guided Strike equivalent), and the beholder adds Antireality's disadvantage → straight roll (cancel each other). In that case Antireality converts a 55% advantage-boosted hit (~72%) back to a straight 55% roll — a meaningful save.

**NEW-R223-01 (MEDIUM — rule clarification needed):** The rotation.yml note "if Bazgar already has disadvantage from another source, Antireality is wasted" is mechanically incorrect (disadvantage stacking is still disadvantage). The note should read: "Antireality is wasted only if the attacker has advantage — the two cancel to a straight roll." Update rotation.yml. This is a minor mis-spec in the test harness, not in the NPC stat block.
