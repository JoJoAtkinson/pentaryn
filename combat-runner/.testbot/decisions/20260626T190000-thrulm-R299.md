### Feel Observations (thrulm-disintegration-recharge)

**1. Disintegration Ray threat model vs Bazgar (49 HP):**

Bazgar is the sturdiest party member (49 HP, AC 18). At full HP, DR one-shots on ~50% of
damage rolls when it hits. At the typical mid-combat Bazgar HP (~30 HP after two rounds with
the R298-observed damage output), DR nearly guarantees one-shot on any hit. Once the fight
reaches round 3 and Bazgar is below 45 HP, DR becomes "hit and win" — ending the encounter
with Bazgar ash and no possible recovery at level 5.

This is **per design** (FEEL-003 from prior fires). Joe has seen this repeatedly across
R296–R299. No change recommended — just confirming the pattern holds.

**2. DR recharge rate at encounter scale:**

33% recharge per turn. Against a ~3-round encounter, expected DR uses: 1 (initial) + ~0.7
additional = ~1.7 total. Against a 5-round encounter: ~2.3 total. In practice, the beholder
will threaten one disintegration "kill window" per 2-3 rounds. Against a party that is meant
to lose in 3-4 rounds total, this means DR fires roughly once — possibly twice if lucky. The
intended drama of "will it disintegrate someone?" is appropriately tense, not guaranteed.

**3. Drain Divinity — fourth consecutive missed opportunity (FEEL-DRAIN-ALTITUDE-R299-01):**

In the `thrulm-disintegration-recharge` prelude, Sabriel is already at 0 HP dying. Drain
Divinity has no target. This is the fourth consecutive Phase B simulation where Drain Divinity
did not fire — R296 (void-scream-aoe: Sabriel at range), R297 (rager-berserk-gate: beholder
altitude), R298 (tentacle-grapple: beholder altitude), R299 (Sabriel already down).

**Pattern report for Joe:** In 4 of 4 recent simulations, Drain Divinity (3 LA, 30 ft range)
did not fire. Two reasons by turn: (a) beholder at hover altitude out of 30 ft range, or
(b) Sabriel dropped before the beholder could descend and use it. Both are self-caused — the
beholder's natural play drops Sabriel before Drain Divinity can operate. The ability may be
mechanically unreachable against a well-played Sabriel who stays at range, or it may work
only in the first round before combat stabilizes. Recommend Joe decide: extend range to 60 ft
OR redesign as a "beholder successfully grapples a divine caster" trigger rather than a
range-limited LA.

**COND-BLINDED-R299-01 (NEW)**
*Severity: medium/mechanical. Log only — needs Joe's decision.*

The beholder's "Alien Perception" trait states explicitly: "it cannot be blinded." The
condition immunity block in the stat block does NOT include `blinded` (lists: charmed,
exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned, prone, restrained). The runner has no
mechanism to reject `@blinded` because it matches by condition-immunity list only.

Evidence: Scenario `thrulm-beholder-hp-track` steps 6–9 apply `@blinded 2`, advance 2 rounds,
then assert the condition is gone — the scenario treats blinded as a valid temporary condition
on the beholder. The scenario will PASS (condition ticks down correctly) even though the
beholder cannot actually be blinded.

**Joe's decision needed:** Add `blinded` to the condition immunity block (fast, consistent
with trait text), OR reword "Alien Perception" to say "disadvantage on attacks cannot be
imposed by lack of vision" (removing the blanket claim, since the trait is really about the
beholder not relying on eyes). Either is safe. The current mismatch is a silent mechanical bug:
a DM who reads only the immunity list could apply Blinding Smite/Color Spray/etc. to the
beholder and have it stick.

so the beholder can drain AND attack on the same turn without spending LA.
