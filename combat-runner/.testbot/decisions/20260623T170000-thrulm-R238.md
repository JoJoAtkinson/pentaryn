### New Phase A Finding — CS-DRIFT-DC-03

**Rager Taunt DC 12:** The Taunt bonus action forces a DC 12 Charisma save. Standard formula using CHA (9, −1): 8 + PB(+2) + CHA(−1) = **9**. DC 12 is not derivable from CHA. It IS derivable from STR (8 + 2 + 2 = 12) or CON (same), but neither is the standard ability for a Taunt (Charisma-flavored mechanic). Contrast with CS-DRIFT-DC-02 (Shardcaller DC 13 +1 beyond max formula) — the Rager DC at least matches *some* stat, but it requires picking STR or CON as the basis without documentation.

Severity: LOW. Design intent might be STR-intimidation DC, which is thematically defensible ("the rager's raw physical threat is what forces the save") but should be noted. At level 5, DC 12 saves are trivially made by Bazgar (CHA probably +0, needs 12+ on d20 = 45% fail rate) — the mechanic is moderately impactful but not broken.

**No source-file change. Log only (design call).**

### Legendary Action Economy After Disintegration

After the ray fires, the beholder has 3 LA to spend at the end of Bazgar's or Sabriel's turn. With Bazgar disintegrated (dead), only Sabriel remains.

- **Drain Divinity (3 LA):** Sabriel (divine martial L5, CHA +2) vs DC 16. Needs 14+ on d20. P(fail) ≈ 65%. EV: 0.65 × (2 × highest_slot_level) THP. If Sabriel still has 2nd-level slots: 0.65 × 4 = 2.6 THP. Poor value.
- **Void Ray + Tentacle (2+1 LA):** Avg 22 force (DC 16 DEX) + avg 14 bludgeoning. Total 36 avg, split across two separate actions. Very good.
- **Dominant pattern:** With only Sabriel alive and a 1v1 state, the beholder should switch from Drain Divinity to Void Ray + Tentacle for efficient kill — exactly the kind of rotation analysis this scenario unlocks.

**Legendary economy observation:** In the 1-vs-1 endgame after Bazgar's disintegration, Drain Divinity loses almost all value. The beholder's optimal play shifts entirely to Void Ray + Tentacle. This rotation shift is **not documented in tactics** — the .md says "Against Clerics/Paladins: Focuses on Drain Divinity" but doesn't condition this on party size. This is worth flagging (existing FEEL-R228-01 touches this but not the 1v1 rotation specifically).

### FEEL-003 / FEEL-R225-01 Confirmed

The scenario demonstrates: with average damage (45 force), any PC who has taken more than 4 HP of prior damage is permanently killed by Disintegration Ray. At party level 5, "permanently killed" means:

- No Revivify (requires 300gp diamond + target not dead for more than 1 minute — Revivify is a 3rd-level spell, potentially available to Sabriel as divine martial, but it can't restore the disintegrated condition per the wording)
- No Raise Dead (5th-level spell, not available at L5)
- True Resurrection (9th-level) or Wish (9th-level) are the only options — both impossible at party level

This is a permanent, irreversible character death outcome on a fight that the *design intent* describes as "the party is meant to lose." The design goal is a harrowing loss the party retreats from, not a campaign-ending permanent death.

The distinction matters: **meant to lose ≠ meant to die permanently.** Compass Edge losing this encounter should create narrative momentum (they retreat, regroup, seek help, return stronger). Permanently disintegrating Bazgar or Marwen collapses the campaign, not escalates it.

**Recommendation (for Joe):** Add a `near-death escape clause` for Disintegration Ray specific to this encounter:

> *"If this damage would reduce a creature to 0 HP, the creature instead drops to 1 HP and is **stunned** until the start of its next turn (the god's bound energy intercedes, refusing to let another soul be unmade in this hollow). A creature saved this way cannot be saved again by this clause for 24 hours."*

This preserves the terror of the ray while removing the permanent-death outcome. The "god's bound energy" framing fits the lore. Alternatively, simply remove the permanent-death clause from this specific encounter's beholder (keep it in the .md as flavor but rule it inactive unless PCs are higher level).
