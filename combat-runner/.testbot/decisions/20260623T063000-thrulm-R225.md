### Round 2 — Beholder's turn

**Disintegration Ray (recharge 5, available) vs Bazgar (lowest HP, melee threat):**

- To hit: +7 (base +6 + Void-Feeding) vs AC 18. Needs 11+ on d20 → P(hit) = 50%
- Damage: 10d8 force, average 45
- Bazgar at 12 HP: any hit with 10d8 kills and disintegrates (min roll 10 > 12 HP? actually min is 10d8 min = 10; 10 < 12, so there's a ~2% chance Bazgar survives on the lowest possible roll). Effectively: P(disintegrated | hit) ≈ 98%

**P(Bazgar disintegrated this turn) ≈ 50% × 98% = 49%**

If ray hits: Bazgar is **permanently dead**. No resurrection below True Resurrection / Wish (neither available at L5). Campaign impact: character gone.

If ray misses (50%): Beholder falls back to multiattack (2× Tentacle + Maw) vs Bazgar.

Bazgar dies (or is KO'd) regardless. The question is whether the death is permanent.

**Beholder bonus action: Shrine-Drift** — repositions toward Sabriel.

**Legendary Actions (3 LA) vs Sabriel:**

- Tentacle (1 LA): +7 vs AC 19, needs 12+ → P(hit) = 45% → expected 6.3 damage. Sabriel: ~9 HP

### Key findings from simulation

#### 1. Permanent death rate ~49% for Bazgar per ray use at mid-fight HP

The ray is 10d8 avg force — against any PC at 12 HP or less, it's near-certain permanent death on a hit. At 50% hit chance, this is close to a coin flip per use. For a "designed to lose" encounter, this is expected — but the DM MUST be aware and the briefing note needs to be surfaced by the runner.

**DB suppression makes this worse:** The runner currently outputs "Disintegration Ray: roll 10d8 force" without any permanent-death reminder. A DM relying on the runner display could fail to call out the disintegration condition until after the session, at which point a player believes their character is merely KO'd. This is a DM-experience bug, not a balance bug.

#### 2. Recharge-5 creates encounter pacing spikes

The ray fires ~once every 3 rounds. In a 3-5 round L5 encounter, it fires 1-2 times. Round where it fires: encounter-defining moment. Round where it doesn't: multiattack deals ~25 avg — still threatening but survivable for Bazgar (49 HP → 3 rounds of sustained multiattack needed, but beholder front-loads damage via LA synergy).

#### 3. Sabriel's sanctified weapon is the party's only real answer

Against the beholder's 17 AC (nonmagic weapon resistance reduces non-sanctified hits to half), Sabriel's sanctified weapon hits at 75% (1d8+4 base + 1d8 extra = ~13.5 avg per hit × 2 attacks × 0.75 = ~20 avg/round). This is the party's best output. Without Sabriel or without the sanctified weapon, the encounter is significantly more one-sided.

Implication: Sabriel staying healthy is critical. The beholder's optimal play (Void Ray via LA at Sabriel after Bazgar's turn, Tentacle via LA after Sabriel's turn) chips her to ~15-20 HP before the beholder even acts. By round 3, Sabriel is in lethal range without any defense options.

#### 4. Drain Divinity irrelevant in disintegration-threat context

With Marwen already down and Bazgar in melee, the beholder would need to spend 3 LA to drain Sabriel's spell slots while forgoing the Void Ray+Tentacle combo (~25 avg damage across two targets). Drain Divinity vs Sabriel's 3rd-level slot = 6 THP. That's a terrible trade. Confirmed: Drain Divinity doesn't fire in any simulation run so far (R224 round-1-opener, R225 disintegration-threat). It's designed as an anti-cleric-dominant mechanic but fires zero times in practice.

#### 5. Lair action monotony in round 3+

Once the party is reduced to 1 standing PC, every lair action applies to the same target. Unstable Ground then multiattack with advantage is mechanically correct but cinematically repetitive. No lair action serves as a memorable moment when used against a solo PC. The **Manifest Thralls** lair action (THP to charmed derro + reaction attack) is useless if no thralls are present — and thralls will typically be killed by the party before reaching this stage.

### Log-Only Items (New This Fire)

#### BUG-R225-01 (HIGH — disintegration_ray missing permanent-death rider)

**Finding:** The `disintegration_ray` DB row has no `effect` field or `rider_on_kill` field. The stat block specifies: "If this damage reduces the target to 0 hit points, the target is disintegrated — cannot be restored to life except by true resurrection or wish." This is a campaign-critical condition that the runner does not surface in its output.

A DM using only the runner display will roll 10d8, see "Bazgar falls to 0 HP," and may not realize the character is permanently dead unless they've read the stat block recently.

**Fix path:** Add via `combat_action_upsert` an `effect` field:
> "DEATH CONDITION: If this damage reduces target to 0 HP, they are **disintegrated** (turned to ash). Cannot be restored to life except by *true resurrection* or *wish*. This is permanent — clarify with the player immediately."

Cannot auto-fix (requires MCP `combat_action_upsert` call). Log for interactive session.

#### BUG-R225-02 (MEDIUM — Tentacle Lash grapple encoded as STR save, not contested check)

**Finding:** The `multiattack` and `tentacle_legendary` DB rows encode the grapple condition as:
```json
"apply_condition_on_hit": {"condition": "grappled", "save_dc": 16, "save_ability": "str"}
```
This generates a "STR save DC 16" prompt — incorrect. Grapple escape is a **contested Athletics/Acrobatics check** (PHB "Grappled" condition), not a saving throw. R220 fixed the .md text for the Maw (correcting "saving throw" to "ability check"), but the DB row was not updated to match.

The stat block .md correctly says "grappled (escape DC 16)" which implies an ability check, not a save. DC 16 ability check to escape is the correct shorthand (contested vs the beholder's Athletics +? — actually beholder uses STR for contested grapple, and with STR 8 / -1, contested Athletics is d20-1 vs defender's Athletics or Acrobatics... this is its own inconsistency; beholder may grapple via STR which is poor, or by Tentacle which might use a bespoke mechanic).

**Impact:** Runner outputs incorrect rule prompt. Low-to-medium impact — DMs generally know grapple rules but a runner-reliant DM may apply an incorrect STR save instead of a contested check.

**Fix path:** Via `combat_action_upsert`, either:
- Remove `save_ability: "str"` and add an `effect` note clarifying "escape requires action + Athletics or Acrobatics check vs DC 16 (using the beholder's contested roll result or DC 16 fixed)," OR
- Change `save_ability` to `"Ath/Acr"` if the runner supports non-standard save labels

Cannot auto-fix without knowing runner's `apply_condition_on_hit` schema for contest mechanics. Log for interactive session.

#### FEEL-R225-01 (CRITICAL-DESIGN — permanent PC death at L5)

**Finding:** Disintegration Ray has ~49% chance of permanently killing Bazgar per use when he's at mid-fight HP (~20). Sabriel faces ~45% per use if she's sole standing PC at round 3 HP (~9). At level 5, the party has zero access to True Resurrection or Wish. Any disintegrated PC is **gone from the campaign**.

R224's FEEL-003 flagged this. This simulation quantifies it: not a tail risk, it's a near-coin-flip per use.

**Joe's decision required:** Three options, each with design implications:

| Option | Effect | Implication |
|--------|--------|------------|
| A: Keep as-is | 49% permanent death per ray use | Encounter is campaign-milestone TPK; player is permanently losing a character |
| B: Soft disintegrate | Permanent death only if target is already dead (0 HP + failed 3 death saves), not just reduced to 0 | Party can still be "destroyed" but not erased; revival options remain |
| C: Level-appropriate substitute | "Reduced to 0 HP by this ray cannot be stabilized normally; requires Medicine DC 20 or magical healing > 0 to stabilize" | Preserves lethality feel, removes campaign-ending condition; thematically weaker |

None of these can be auto-fixed. Joe's call.

#### FEEL-R225-02 (LOW — lair action monotony in 1-PC endgame)

**Finding:** When party is reduced to 1 standing PC (round 3+), all lair actions target the same combatant. Mechanically correct, cinematically flat. Manifest Thralls (THP to charmed derro) is useless without thralls. The encounter's "feel" peak occurs in rounds 1-2; by round 3 it's cleanup.

**Recommendation (log only):** Consider adding a "narrative escape trigger" — if the party has no divine PC standing and the beholder has been damaged to below 50 HP, a "the beholder pauses — it senses you're no longer a threat to the shrine" moment creates a retreat opening. This doesn't change combat mechanics but adds a survivable exit for a "designed to lose" encounter.
