### New Phase A finding this run

**DB-SCHEMA-01 — Legendary action cost not encoded in DB spec (log only)**

The beholder has three legendary actions at varying costs:
- `tentacle_legendary` — costs 1 LA
- `void_ray_legendary` — costs 2 LA
- `drain_divinity` — costs 3 LA

None of the three DB rows carry a `legendary_cost` field. The combat runner has no way to decrement the LA pool when these actions are used from the DB alone — it relies entirely on the LLM to infer cost from the narration preview or the DM's manual tracking. In the `thrulm-beholder-alpha-strike` scenario, when the DM types "drain", the runner will execute the narration and roll but will NOT surface a "3 LA spent" prompt. The DM must remember cost externally.

This is a schema gap, not an authoring error: the `combat_action_upsert` schema apparently has no `legendary_cost` field. Fix path: schema extension (human decision); workaround: add cost to `narration_preview` for visibility (e.g., "*(Costs 3 Legendary Actions)* — Sacred power tears itself free...").

### Drain Divinity analysis (Q1 + FEEL-10)

**Q1 — Does drain_divinity surface cleanly as a legendary-cost utility?**

From DB: `drain_divinity` type `"utility"`, verbs match. The action will trigger and narrate. However, **the DB row has no `legendary_cost` field** — the runner cannot decrement the LA pool automatically. DM must manually track that Drain Divinity consumed all 3 LA. This is **DB-SCHEMA-01** (new, flagged above).

The narration preview truncates: "Sacred power tears itself free — the beholder drinks the space where faith on..." — the cost information is absent from the preview. Recommend appending `*(Costs 3 Legendary Actions)*` to the `narration_preview` as an interim workaround until schema supports `legendary_cost`.

**FEEL-10 — Drain Divinity is sub-optimal vs Compass Edge at level 5 (nuance to known concern)**

The briefing's known-state flags "Drain Divinity: degenerate every round vs divine casters — consider cooldown or 2-LA cost." That concern is valid for high-level divine casters (5th-level slots → 10 THP per drain). But against Compass Edge at level 5:

- Sabriel (divine martial 5) highest spell slot: **2nd-level → 4 THP gained**.
- Drain Divinity costs **3 LA** to gain 4 THP and drain one 2nd-level slot.
- Alternative: 3 LA spent as Tentacle (1) + Void Ray (2) = avg 36 damage and two grapple opportunities.
- Trade-off: Drain Divinity at this party level is **strictly weaker** than Tentacle + Void Ray in both damage output and resource attrition.

At level 5, the "degenerate vs divine casters" concern resolves itself — the beholder's optimal play is to ignore Drain Divinity and maximise damage/grapple pressure. Drain Divinity only becomes degenerate at higher PC levels (9+ where divine casters have 5th-level slots). The "consider cooldown or 2-LA cost" redesign from the briefing is a valid future fix, but not urgent for this party.

**Corollary:** In the alpha-strike scenario, the beholder's optimal R1 play is:
- End-of-Marwen's-turn: Tentacle (1 LA) vs Marwen
- R1 action: Multiattack (Tentacle×2 + Maw) — downs Marwen
- End-of-Bazgar's-turn: Void Ray (2 LA) vs Bazgar
- R2 end-of-turn: Drain Divinity (3 LA) vs Sabriel as clean-up

This is exactly what unfolded in the simulation above. The scenario tests cleanly.

### Phase B feel assessment

**FEEL-11 — Alpha strike is "lethal, not unfair" but offers zero counterplay**

The scenario delivers on "party is meant to lose." Every death is mechanically clean. But the encounter has **no window** where any party decision changes the outcome:

- Marwen goes down before getting a second turn.
- Bazgar's Battlemaster maneuvers are mostly dead against beholder's immunity suite (prone, frightened immunities).
- Sabriel's resource drain via Drain Divinity is sub-optimal at L5 but still accelerates the loss.
- LR never fires.

This is FEEL-06 restated but with the alpha-strike scenario confirming the pattern. The design intent ("meant to lose") is achieved, but through complete mechanical determinism, not tactical overwhelm. Flagging again for Joe — the most actionable fix from R124 suggestion (c): one round of beholder delay (it perceives them but doesn't act on init count 1) would give Marwen one additional turn and let at least one party decision land.
