---

### State assumptions

- Beholder: full HP (110), Disintegration Ray available, Void-Feeding active (+1 to attacks in chamber, total +7 to hit)
- Marwen: 32 HP, AC 15, Wis save +1 (assumed), DEX save +2
- Party not yet acted (beholder opens)

---

### Attack probability

**Disintegration Ray vs Marwen (AC 15):**  
+7 to hit vs AC 15 — needs 8+ on d20 = **65% hit chance**.

**On hit: 10d8 force damage**  
- Minimum: 10. Maximum: 80. Mean: 45. Std dev ≈ √(10 × 5.25) ≈ 7.25.
- Marwen HP: 32. P(10d8 ≤ 32) ≈ P(Z ≤ (32−45)/7.25) ≈ P(Z ≤ −1.79) ≈ **3.6%** → she survives the hit.
- P(10d8 ≥ 32) ≈ **96.4%** → she reaches 0 HP.

**Disintegration special rule:** "If this damage reduces the target to 0 HP, the target is disintegrated (turned to ash). Cannot be restored to life except by true resurrection or wish."  
This is NOT the standard instant-death excess-damage rule. Any hit that reduces Marwen to 0 HP = **permanent death**. The 32-HP survivor threshold only matters for the ~3.6% of hits that roll ≤31 total (and still bring her to 0 HP, they just don't permanently kill her — she gets death saves).

**Effective probabilities:**
| Outcome | Probability |
|---------|-------------|
| Miss — Marwen unharmed, beholder's action wasted | 35% |
| Hit, 10d8 ≤ 31 — Marwen reaches 0 HP, death saves (revivify works) | ~2.3% (3.6% × 65%) |
| Hit, 10d8 ≥ 32 — Marwen permanently dead, no revivify | **~62.7%** (96.4% × 65%) |

---

### The problem: variance on a permanent consequence

With no countermeasure available (the ray resolves before Marwen acts on her first turn), there is a **~62.7% per-use chance that Marwen dies permanently before she ever takes an action in this encounter.** This is not a calibration issue — it's an intentional threat ceiling for a CR 13 boss vs a CR 5 party. But the permanent death clause amplifies the variance from "character is knocked out" to "character is removed from the campaign."

Specific table-feel concern: on a round-1 disintegration before Marwen acts, the Wizard player has contributed nothing to the fight and their character is gone. This differs from death-by-multiattack, where Marwen at least gets to cast a spell first (she has higher initiative than most party members due to DEX +2 = typically init 12-14). The ray denies agency and is permanent.

This is consistent with the encounter intent (meant to lose), but needs a DM callout: **"Use Ray as a closer, not an opener."** Once Marwen has acted and depleted a spell slot, her removal is dramatically satisfying (she contributed). Pre-turn removal is the bad case.

---

### Miss case: feel analysis

On a miss (35% — needs 7 or less on d20):
- Beholder's entire main action is wasted.
- Marwen survives to act. She likely casts Slow (55% hit per R144 analysis) or Fireball.
- From the party's perspective: the beholder's terrifying opener whiffed. The encounter suddenly feels winnable.
- On a hit (65%): the party's strongest utility caster is dead and the encounter is over.

This **35% miss rate creates a binary experience**: either the party is immediately devastated or briefly buoyed. Neither case makes for a satisfying 2-4-round arc. Multiattack's partial-damage structure avoids this problem — every attack contributes something, and a "miss" on one tentacle doesn't nullify the action.

**Confirm FEEL-R144-01** (Multiattack first, Ray only when softened): this scenario analysis supports the previous run's finding that Ray should be held until Marwen has already been weakened (i.e., after multiattack round 1 brings her near death, Ray on round 2 is the killing blow rather than the opening shot).

---

### Recharge cadence check

After turn 1 (assuming Ray fires), recharge 5-6 = 33% chance per round.  
Expected rounds between uses: 3. In a 2-3-round encounter, Ray fires:
- Round 1: available (by scenario assumption)
- Round 2: 33% chance
- Round 3: 33% chance

P(at least one Ray in rounds 2-3) = 1 − (1−0.33)² ≈ **55%**. So the beholder likely gets one additional ray. If Marwen is already at 0 HP, it retargets Bazgar (AC 18; +7 vs 18 = 50% hit) or Sabriel (AC 19; +7 vs 19 = 45% hit).

Against AC 18: 50% hit × 96.4% permanent death ≈ **48% chance of permanent death per round-2+ use** vs Bazgar (assumed HP 49; 10d8 min 10, max 80 — Bazgar survives hits of ≤49: P(10d8 ≤ 49) ≈ P(Z ≤ (49−45)/7.25) ≈ P(Z ≤ 0.55) ≈ 71%. So only ~29% of hits kill Bazgar permanently). P(permanent death): 50% × 29% ≈ **14.5%**.

Vs Marwen is the near-guaranteed-kill case; vs the melee characters (higher HP) it's much less spikey. The ray's permanent-death implication is specifically Marwen's problem, not a general encounter issue.
