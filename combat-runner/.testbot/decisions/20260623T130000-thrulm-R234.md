### New Phase A Finding

**VERB-UX-01 (LOW — NEW):** The verb `"tentacle"` appears in beholder-thrulm's `multiattack` action verbs `["attack", "multiattack", "hit", "tentacle", "maw", "grab"]`. The tentacle_legendary LA uses verbs `["legendary tentacle", "lash", "reach"]`. These verbs do not actually overlap — there is no routing conflict. However, a DM naturally typing "tentacle" intending to fire a single LA tentacle attack (costs 1 LA) will trigger the full 3-attack multiattack instead. The correct verb "lash" is non-obvious. Neither the cheat sheet nor the beholder .md documents this distinction.

*Log-only. Suggest adding a parenthetical "(use 'lash' for LA tentacle; 'tentacle' fires full multiattack)" to the beholder's Legendary Actions section or cheat sheet.*

### Turn 1 — Sabriel

### Reaction — Oath-Breaking Retaliation (BUG-R156-01 gate)

Shrine-Touched can see Sabriel. Reaction fires.

Shrine-Axe attack roll: +4 to hit vs AC 19. Needs 15+ on d20 → P(hit normal) = 30%.

**Unstable Form timing:** The trait says advantage "until the end of its next turn." The reaction fires during Sabriel's turn — before the Shrine-Touched has taken its own next turn. Per 5e timing rules, "until the end of its next turn" encompasses everything between now and the end of that future turn, including reactions taken on other creatures' turns. Unstable Form advantage **applies** to this reaction attack.

P(hit with advantage) = 1 − (0.70²) = **51%.** Notable — Unstable Form effectively converts a weak-hit reaction (30%) into a coin-flip (51%).

With advantage: rolls (d20=6, d20=14) → take 14 → 18 vs AC 19 → **MISS** (just short).

DB output: **NARRATES WITHOUT DICE** (BUG-R156-01 confirmed). Runner says "Pain becomes prophecy — the shrine screams through its vessel" but produces no attack roll or damage output. DM must manually roll: +4 to hit, on hit → 5 (1d8+1) slashing + 4 (1d8) necrotic. With Unstable Form, roll twice and take higher.

Self-damage: Shrine-Touched takes 2 (1d4) psychic. 20 → **18 HP.** *(Also not automated — DM applies manually.)*

**2. (BUG-R218-01 CONFIRMED — secondary regression gate)**

Ancient Resonance outputs 2d10 necrotic but suppresses the secondary 1d4 psychic damage on a failed save. The difference is small (avg 2 hp) but the psychic damage also has flavor significance (creatures that fail "brush against something beyond comprehension"). Test FAILS on this condition as well.

---

**3. (FEEL-R234-01 — NEW, LOW) Unstable Form advantage applies to Oath-Breaking Retaliation; undocumented**

Per 5e timing: "until the end of its next turn" encompasses the shrine-touched's own future turn and any reactions it takes before that turn arrives. Unstable Form advantage on the reaction attack roll is valid. This converts the reaction from a 30% hit chance to a 51% hit chance against AC 19 — a meaningful bump that is completely silent in the runner since neither Unstable Form nor the reaction attack roll is automated.

DM tracking requirement: when the shrine-touched takes 10+ damage in a turn and uses Oath-Breaking Retaliation as a reaction, the DM must (1) remember Unstable Form fired, (2) apply advantage to the manual reaction roll. Two manual steps, neither prompted by the runner.

---

**4. (CONFIRMED — fire/radiant counterplay is functional)**

Vulnerability to fire/radiant creates a meaningful knowledge-skill check: players who read the lore (or succeed on an Arcana check) and target correctly delete a Shrine-Touched in 1-2 turns. Players using physical damage face a creature that heals itself (Shrine-Bound psychic resistance absorbs minor self-damage) and fights harder when wounded. The design intent is intact. No flag needed.

---

**5. (OBSERVATION — Shrine-Touched multiattack has no automatic Unstable Form tracking)**

The multiattack fires twice and the runner shows two Shrine-Axe attacks. But whether Unstable Form is currently active (advantage on those rolls) requires DM awareness. This is a runner automation gap that touches every Shrine-Touched turn after it takes 10+ damage. Flagged as an automation gap (existing under FEEL-R234-01, not a new separate item — same root cause as the reaction timing issue above).
