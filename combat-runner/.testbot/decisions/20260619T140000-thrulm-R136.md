### Disintegration Ray + Healing Word interaction note

The two-round sequence is deterministic given average dice:
- Round 1: Multiattack kills Marwen (0 HP). Healing Word brings Marwen to 4 HP.
- Round 2: Disintegration Ray fires (33% chance per turn after round 1) — permanently kills Marwen.

Expected turns for Disintegration Ray to recharge after round 1: geometric with P=1/3 → expected 3 turns. But against a 4-HP target with +7 to hit vs AC 15, every recharge of the Ray translates to an immediate death. The beholder is effectively in a race to recharge before the party can get Marwen above survivable threshold.

To survive permanent death, the party needs Marwen at HP > (Marwen's own max HP + current HP) when the Ray fires — i.e., above 32 HP — which requires a 3rd or 4th level Cure Wounds (Sabriel burning two level-3 slots in one action). Not possible with a bonus-action heal; requires burning a full action.

### Feel Assessment

**FEEL-NEW-01: The round-1 opener reveals Marwen has no safe window at all.**

With beholder initiative +3 vs Marwen +2, beholder wins 55% of the time — and on the turns it doesn't win, it's often tied or one number behind (Marwen gets one spell off). Even when Marwen acts first, the beholder's legendary actions after Marwen's own turn can deliver Void Ray (22 force) before Bazgar acts. Marwen's action economy in round 1 is: cast something, then survive the beholder's Void Ray after her turn (DC 16 DEX, likely fail at +2 DEX = 50%). If she fails: 22 force → 32-22 = 10 HP, then multiattack next beholder turn would finish her.

This is by design (party is meant to lose) but the specific pathology is: **Marwen doesn't get to contribute meaningfully.** She has 1-2 turns of existence before she is either grapple-Maw'd (round 1 if beholder wins init) or Disintegration Ray'd (round 2 guaranteed if she was healed). The Healing Word spent by Sabriel in round 1 is purely buying Marwen one more turn of existence — it doesn't change the outcome, it delays it.

**FEEL-NEW-02: "Multiattack or save for Disintegration Ray" is a false dilemma.**

The scenario asks the DM to consider this as a decision point. In practice: Disintegration Ray recharges on a 5–6, so the beholder has it available at the start of its first turn only 33% of the time. When available, the choice is trivially "use the Ray on the wizard." When not available (67% of the time), there is no decision — the beholder multiattacks. A genuine decision only arises if BOTH the Ray is available AND the beholder has a reason to hold it (which it doesn't, given Marwen's 32 HP is well within Ray range). The DM has no real choice to make at this juncture.

**FEEL-NEW-03: LA economy in round 1 leaves Sabriel untouched.**

After spending 1 LA (Tentacle MISS after Marwen's turn) + 2 LA (Void Ray on Bazgar after his turn) = 3 LA total, the beholder has no resources for Sabriel this round. Sabriel gets a free round at full HP. A beholder with 3 LA that spends all of them on Tentacle (1) + Void Ray (2) after every other creature's turn creates a predictable LA pattern. If the DM consistently uses the same pattern, experienced players will recognize the beholder's LAs are exhausted after the first 2 PCs and Sabriel is safe to act freely on round 1.

**LA distribution note:** After Sabriel's turn (IC 6) in round 1, beholder has 0 LA. Sabriel knows she is unthreatened by legendary actions on her turn. This predictability slightly undercuts the "overwhelming" feel the encounter is supposed to project. A randomized LA spend (sometimes spending both on Tentacle × 2 instead of Tentacle + Void Ray) would feel less telegraphed.

**Bazgar assessment:** With Precision Attack available (superiority die), Bazgar directly counters one Antireality flip per round. This is mechanically satisfying and makes his Battlemaster subclass relevant vs the beholder. The resource trade (superiority die vs reaction) is fair and interesting.
