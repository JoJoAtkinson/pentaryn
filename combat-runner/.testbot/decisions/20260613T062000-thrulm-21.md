### Mechanical assessment

**Rime Reflex is correctly authored** — the DB trigger matches the intended mechanic (melee damage within 5 ft). The action is present, typed as `reaction`, with correct trigger fields.

**Potential failure mode — melee source metadata (FEEL-F21-01):**

The scenario uses raw `-15` HP deduction rather than a full `<who> <damage> <type>` command (e.g. `"1 15 bludgeon"`). The trigger match `"melee damage within 5 ft"` requires the runner to know the damage source is melee. If the runner's event system only sees a `damage` event with no source classification, `rime_reflex` may silently fail to prompt.

Comparable scenarios use similarly raw damage inputs:
- `thrulm-beholder-antireality-reaction`: `-15` → expects reaction prompt. Antireality trigger is `event: action_executed, match: "attacked by a visible attacker"` — this may derive source from the *action event* (not the damage event), making it more robust than rime_reflex's `event: damage` trigger.
- `thrulm-shrine-touched-unstable-form`: `-12` → triggers threshold-based trait (damage count, not source type) — no melee/ranged distinction required.

Rime Reflex is the most demanding of these: it requires the runner to distinguish melee vs. ranged damage from an unqualified `-N` input. If the runner uses a "last attacking entity was adjacent" heuristic, this works in the scenario context (prelude establishes barbarian is adjacent). If the runner requires explicit source typing, `reaction_prompts_min: 1` fails silently.

**Severity:** Low (mountin-pass encounter, out of thrulm cron scope). But the pattern (melee-source dependency in raw damage input) also applies to `oath_breaking_retaliation` (shrine-touched, `event: damage, match: "melee damage from visible attacker within 5 ft"`). If the runner doesn't handle this, OBR also never fires during raw `-N` playtest inputs.
