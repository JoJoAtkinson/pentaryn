## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `thrulm-lair-prone-melee-synergy`

### Scenario setup

R1 init roll. Assumptions:
- Beholder DEX +3 → avg roll 13.5, acting in the 10–18 range
- Bazgar DEX −1 → avg roll 9.5, acting in the 5–15 range
- Lair action fires at init 20 (before any PC or beholder acts)

### Round 1 walkthrough

**Init 20 (lair): Unstable Ground → Bazgar**

Bazgar DEX save: DC 16, mod −1. Needs 17 on die. P(fail) = 80%. Assuming fail: Bazgar is **prone**.

**Beholder turn (say init 16, before Bazgar at init 8):**

Shrine-Drift (BA) not used here — assume R1 descent already happened on a prior turn (or beholder is already
at melee altitude). Beholder executes Multiattack (2× Tentacle Lash + Maw).

Prone → melee attackers within reach have advantage (PHB p. 292). Tentacle Lash reach = 10 ft → qualifies.

- **TL1 vs prone Bazgar (AC 18):** +7 to hit with advantage. Need 11+ on die. P(hit single) = 50%. P(hit w/ adv) = 75%.
  - Hit → 13 (3d6+3) bludgeoning + grapple (escape DC 16). Grapple active.
- **TL2 vs prone Bazgar (same target for Maw setup):** 75% hit chance.
  - Hit → 13 (3d6+3) bludgeoning + second grapple (beholder has 4 tentacles).
- **Maw vs grappled Bazgar (AC 18):** +7 to hit with advantage. P(hit) = 75%.
  - Auto-crit rider fires (target is grappled): 8d8+3 avg **39** piercing (instead of 4d8+3 avg 21).

**Damage math (optimal: both TL hit, Maw crits):**
- TL1: 13 + TL2: 13 + Maw (crit): 39 = **65 bludgeoning/piercing**
- Bazgar at 49 HP → **downed in one turn before acting** (with 16 HP overkill).

**P(this sequence) given Bazgar is prone and beholder acts first:**
- P(both TL hit) = 0.75 × 0.75 = 0.5625 (56%)
- P(Maw hits) given at least one grapple active: P(at least one TL hit) × P(Maw hits) ≈ 0.94 × 0.75 = 70%
  - But only the auto-crit path downs Bazgar in one hit: needs both TL (for grapple) + Maw = 56% × 75% ≈ 42%
  - Non-crit Maw path (only one TL hits): 13 + 21 avg = 34 dmg, Bazgar survives at 15 HP

**P(R1 pre-turn knockout) overall:**
- P(prone from UG) × P(beholder acts before Bazgar) × P(full autocrit sequence)
- ≈ 0.80 × 0.60 × 0.42 ≈ **~20% per encounter**

### Feel assessment

The ~20% one-round-before-turn knockout is within "lethal encounter" design intent (CR 13 vs level 5 party
is designed to be unwinnable). However the *source* of the kill combines a lair action (no counterplay,
init 20) with a melee-advantage bonus that is invisible to the runner (BUG-A52-01: runner doesn't prompt
"attacks vs prone have advantage"). The DM must independently recall PHB p. 292 to apply it.

**Is this "unfair rather than lethal"?** Borderline. The party loses this encounter by design; a R1 knockout
of the Fighter before he acts is within the expected lethality band. The lack of counterplay (can't prevent
lair action; low Dex saves on Bazgar make prone nearly automatic) feels more unfair than typical initiative
bad luck. But this is probably acceptable as part of the "sealed hollow horror" tone.

### NEW FINDING: Post-Multiattack Shrine-Drift for DD descent (FEEL-R98-01)

Scenario also revealed an undocumented tactical sequence: if the beholder needs to descend for Drain
Divinity range (DD has 30 ft range; beholder hovers 40–60 ft up — BUG-A12-06), it can:

1. **Action:** Multiattack (TL grapple Bazgar, Maw auto-crit) — damage dealt while grapple is active
2. **BA:** Shrine-Drift 30 ft descent — grapple on Bazgar ends (Shrine-Drift is not normal movement;
   per FIX-FC48-B, does not drag grappled creatures; if beholder moves >10 ft from Bazgar, grapple ends)
3. **LAs:** Hold all 3 for DD post-Sabriel (beholder is now within 30 ft)

The grapple ending via Shrine-Drift happens AFTER damage is dealt from the Multiattack action — damage
already resolved, grapple release changes position but doesn't undo HP loss. If the Maw auto-crit downed
Bazgar, this is a net gain (beholder repositions AND downs the fighter). If Bazgar survived, losing the
grapple costs the beholder its primary damage-amplification tool on subsequent turns.

This interaction is not documented in the beholder's tactics section or in any FIX note. The existing notes
address Shrine-Drift vs grapple (FIX-FC48-B) and DD vs hover range (BUG-A12-06) separately but don't
combine them into a single R1 sequence.

**Recommendation (log only, don't auto-fix):** Add a tactics note covering this sequence under the
"Against Melee" section: "Post-Multiattack Shrine-Drift descent: if DD is the R1 LA target, execute
Multiattack first (grapple + Maw auto-crit), then Shrine-Drift (BA) to descend within 30 ft for DD.
The grapple ends when Shrine-Drift moves the beholder >10 ft from the grappled target, but damage
from the Multiattack action was already resolved. Trade-off: damage amplification via grapple is
lost for subsequent turns; DD window opens."
