**Title:** "Antireality reaction — disadvantage flip rate and economy"

### Setup

| Combatant | HP | AC | Init mod | To-hit vs beholder |
|-----------|----|----|----------|--------------------|
| Bazgar (Fighter/BM 5) | 49 | 18 | +0 (Str-heavy) | +7 vs AC 17 |
| Beholder | 110 | 17 | +3 | — |

**AR mechanics (correct per stat block):** When beholder is targeted by an attack it can see, it may use its reaction to impose DISADVANTAGE on that attack roll — before the roll resolves. Once per round.

**AR mechanics (wrong per DB, BUG-F7-02):** "+2 AC against the triggering attack. Declare BEFORE the attack roll resolves."

### Hit-rate math

| Attack condition | Threshold | Formula | Hit rate |
|----------------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Bazgar normal | need 10+ | 11/20 | **55%** |
| Bazgar w/ AR (correct) | lower of 2d20 ≥ 10 | (11/20)² | **30%** |
| Bazgar w/ AR (wrong DB: +2 AC → AC 19) | need 12+ | 9/20 | **45%** |

**DB bug impact:** The "+2 AC" mechanic gives Bazgar a 45% hit rate against the reaction instead of the correct 30% — a 15 percentage-point error in Bazgar's favor. At ~8.5 avg damage per hit, this is ~1.28 extra expected damage per AR-covered attack. Over 3 rounds: ~3.8 HP under-protection against the correct mechanic.

### AR outcome probability breakdown (per attack with AR active)

| Outcome | Condition | P |
|---------|-----------|---|
| AR **flips** hit → miss | first die ≥ 10, second < 10 | (11/20)(9/20) ≈ **24.75%** |
| AR **fails** (lower roll still hits) | both dice ≥ 10 | (11/20)² ≈ **30.25%** |
| AR **wasted** (would miss anyway) | higher die < 10 | (9/20)² ≈ **20.25%** |
| AR **irrelevant** (Bazgar not attacking beholder this turn) | — | — |

*(Remaining ~24.75% = higher ≥ 10 but lower < 10 — same as "flip" probability; the total sums to 100%.)*

### Concrete simulation: 3 rounds, 6 attacks (AR on Attack 1 each round)

**Round 1:**
- **Attack 1 (AR active):** d20 = 16, 7 → take lower → 7+7=14 vs AC 17 → **MISS**. Without AR: 16+7=23 → HIT. **AR flipped this attack.** Beholder: 110 HP (unchanged).
- **Attack 2 (normal):** d20 = 13 → 13+7=20 ≥ 17 → **HIT**. Damage: 1d8+4 = 8 (avg). Beholder: 110→102.

**Round 2:**

**Round 3:**

### Battlemaster Precision Attack interaction

Bazgar has 4 superiority dice (d8) as a Battlemaster 5. Precision Attack (add d8 to attack roll, declared after roll before DM confirms hit/miss) can partially counter AR:

- **Without Precision:** AR on Attack 1 → ~30% hit rate
- **With Precision (avg d8 = 4.5 added):** effective attack bonus becomes +11.5 avg vs AC 17 → need (17-11.5)=5.5+ on d20. Both rolls of disadvantage need to be ≥ 6, so P(lower ≥ 6) = (15/20)² = 56.25% → roughly cancels AR entirely on Precision turns.

**Optimal Bazgar play:** reserve Precision Attack specifically for AR turns (Attack 1). On AR turns with Precision, hit rate climbs from 30% back to ~56%, making AR nearly ineffective. On Attack 2 (no AR), use Trip Attack, Menacing Attack, or other maneuvers for on-hit riders.

**Table impact:** A Battlemaster 5 who plays optimally against AR will roughly halve AR's protection value. DM should expect this; the AR window is narrowest when Bazgar is managing his superiority dice conservatively (e.g., late in a fight after using some maneuvers).

---

### Taunt/disadvantage stacking interaction

If a Rager has Taunted Bazgar (DC 12 Cha save — ~60% failure rate for Bazgar with Str as dump stat) and Bazgar elects to attack the beholder despite the Taunt, Bazgar already has disadvantage. AR then has no additional effect (5e RAW: disadvantage doesn't stack). Beholder would spend its reaction for nothing.

**The beholder must identify active disadvantage sources before declaring AR:**
- Check: is Bazgar Taunted by a living Rager? → Do NOT use AR on that attack.
- Check: does Bazgar have Disadvantage from another source (prone, restrained, frightened)? → Do NOT use AR.
- Only use AR when the attacker's roll is at a straight +7 (no pre-existing disadvantage).

Current beholder tactics section has no mention of this. It only says "once per round" and gives the DM timing guidance (FIX-F33-A). The stacking interaction is a meaningful decision point, especially in R1 when Rager Taunt is often active.

---

### AR target priority (which PC's Attack 1 to cover)

If multiple PCs attack the beholder in the same round, the beholder holds AR for the highest-damage first attack. Against the Compass Edge party:

| PC | Attack bonus | Avg damage per hit | AR priority |
|----|-------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Bazgar | +7 | ~8.5 (1d8+4 if GWM off) | Medium |
| Sabriel (divine martial) | ~+7 | ~21+ with 3rd-level smite (2d8+5 radiant+physical) | **HIGHEST** |
| Marwen | ~+5 (spell attack) | ~14 (2d10 avg Fire Bolt) | Low-Med |

Sabriel's smite-augmented attacks bypass the beholder's nonmagic B/P/S resistance AND deal radiant damage (which the beholder has no resist to). AR on Sabriel's smite attack is worth ~21+ expected damage prevented per flip; AR on Bazgar's plain swing is worth ~8.5. **Spend AR on Sabriel's first attack each round whenever possible.**

Current tactics say nothing about AR target priority. The DM running this encounter will instinctively use AR on the first attack that targets the beholder, which is often Bazgar (higher init) rather than Sabriel (lower init). This is suboptimal play.

**FEEL-F66-01 (NEW — Log only): Precision Attack partially counters AR — optimal Bazgar play halves its effectiveness.**

AR's 30% hit rate is strong on paper, but a Battlemaster 5 who manages Precision wisely can push it back to ~56%. This is the beholder's most sophisticated interaction with Bazgar and isn't mentioned anywhere in the stat block or tactics notes. The DM should know: "If Bazgar is spending d8s early, AR becomes more impactful later. If Bazgar is saving Precision, AR is nearly negated on his Attack 1 each round."

**Recommend:** Add a parenthetical to the Antireality entry in beholder.md: *(Battlemaster note: a Precision Attack against the disadvantaged roll substantially counters AR — expect Bazgar to spend a superiority die on Attack 1 when AR is declared. AR is most effective in the late fight once Bazgar's dice are depleted.)*

**FEEL-F66-02 (NEW — Log only): Taunt-stacking gap — beholder should hold AR when attacker already has disadvantage.**

No current guidance exists for the "attacker already has disadvantage" case. In mixed formations (Rager + Beholder), Rager Taunt is highly likely to be active on Bazgar in R1-R2. The beholder holding its reaction in this case is strictly correct, but a DM without this guidance will reflexively spend AR on any incoming attack. Over a 3-round fight with Rager alive: ~2 AR activations will be wasted on Taunted attackers at the table without this rule.

**Recommend:** Add to Antireality in beholder.md: *(Taunt check: if a Rager-Taunt is currently active on this attacker AND the attacker is targeting the beholder — they already have disadvantage. Do NOT use Antireality; hold the reaction for an un-Taunted attacker. Antireality on a disadvantaged attacker is a wasted reaction.)*
