## Phase B — Scenario: matron-slots adapted to thrulm (drain-divinity slot tracking)

*scenarios.yml index 2 (matron-slots) adapted: slot-decrement mechanic → Drain Divinity vs Sabriel.*
*Thrulm-rotation slice 4 (drain-divinity-vs-sabriel) aligns with this test theme.*

### Setup (per rotation.yml slice definition)
Party: Bazgar Fighter 5 (HP 49, AC 18), Marwen Wizard 5 (HP 32, AC 15),
Sabriel divine-martial 5 (HP 44, AC 19).
Beholder in shrine chamber (Void-Feeding active, +1 attack/damage all rolls).
Sabriel assumed: CHA save +2 total (CHA mod + PB without proficiency, or CHA +2 proficent — rotation
specifies "+2 Cha" as save modifier). Paladin-class slots: 4×1st, 3×2nd, 2×3rd.

### Simulated sequence: 3 rounds of Drain Divinity

Drain Divinity: costs 3 LA (full budget), range 30 ft, DC 16 CHA save.
P(fail) = P(d20+2 < 16) = 13/20 = 65%.

**Round 1 — end of Bazgar's turn, beholder descends to 30 ft of Sabriel:**
- Beholder spends all 3 LA on Drain Divinity.
- Sabriel CHA save: d20+2 vs DC 16. Expected fail (65%).
- On fail: Sabriel loses highest slot (3rd-level); beholder gains 6 THP (2 × 3).
- Remaining slots: 4×1st, 3×2nd, 1×3rd.
- Beholder LA spent: 3 of 3. No Tentacle, no Void Ray this round.

**Round 2 — beholder refreshes 3 LA at start of its turn, descends again:**
- Drain Divinity vs Sabriel again (still within 30 ft).
- Expected fail (65%). Highest slot now 3rd-level (1 remaining).
- On fail: loses last 3rd-level slot; beholder gains 6 THP (replaces R1 THP — same value).
- Remaining slots: 4×1st, 3×2nd, 0×3rd.
- LA spent: 3 of 3 again. Second consecutive round of zero offensive LA output.

**Round 3 — Sabriel moves away; beholder must choose:**
- Sabriel's highest slot is now 2nd-level. DD within 30 ft requires beholder stay in melee range.
- If beholder uses DD: Sabriel loses 2nd-level slot, beholder gains 4 THP (2 × 2). 
  This is diminishing returns — 4 THP < 6 THP, loses 1 second-level slot vs 1 third-level.
- Bazgar (AC 18) gets melee attacks on the descended beholder each round: +7 vs AC 17 (base;
  Antireality can flip the first attack to disadvantage). Bazgar multiattack: 2 attacks.
  Attack 1: Antireality → disadvantage → P(hit) ≈ 30%. Attack 2: normal → P(hit) ≈ 55%.
  Expected damage from Bazgar R1: 0.30 × ~12 + 0.55 × ~12 = 3.6 + 6.6 = ~10.2 EV per round.
  Over 3 rounds of DD: ~30 EV damage from Bazgar alone (Sabriel can also attack at melee range).

### Opportunity cost analysis

**DD (3 LA) vs Tentacle (1 LA) + Void Ray (2 LA):**

Tentacle vs Marwen (lowest HP, AC 15): +7 to hit, P(hit) = 13/20 = 65%.
- Hit: 3d6+4 (in chamber) avg = 14.5 → EV = 0.65 × 14.5 = **9.4 + grapple**
Void Ray vs Bazgar (AC 18; DEX +1 → DC 16 DEX save, fail on roll ≤14 = 14/20 = 70%):
- 4d10+1 (chamber) avg = 23. Half = 11.5.
- EV = 0.70 × 23 + 0.30 × 11.5 = 16.1 + 3.45 = **19.6**
- Total Tentacle+VR EV: ~29 damage expected per round.

DD output per round: 6 THP gain + 65% chance to strip one spell slot.
Sabriel loses ~1.3 spell slot levels per round from DD (65% × 2 rounds = ~1.3 slots in 2 rounds).

**Slot drain value for this party:**
- Bazgar (Fighter): no spell slots. DD irrelevant against him.
- Marwen (Wizard): DD range is 30 ft, Marwen stays at distance (60+ ft range caster). 
  Beholder cannot both descend to 30 ft of Sabriel AND be within 30 ft of Marwen simultaneously.
  DD can only hit Sabriel in practice.
- Sabriel's 3rd-level slots represent: Divine Smite (2d8+level bonus on hit) or cure wounds /
  Bless. Each lost slot ≈ 1 missed opportunity worth ~14 EV (Divine Smite) or ~healing action.
- However: Bless concentration (advantage on attack rolls + d4 to all saves) is the highest-value
  spell at this level. If Bless is up, stripping a 3rd-level slot saves ~9 avg damage per Bazgar
  attack (advantage adds ~4, d4 saves saves ~2.5 per save). But Bless concentrates on its own —
  DD only removes the slot, not the active concentration.

### Feel findings

**FEEL-004: Drain Divinity optimal window is pre-Bless round 1 only**
DD makes mathematical sense exactly once: before Sabriel casts Bless on round 1. If Sabriel goes
before the beholder (lower initiative than beholder's +3), she may Bless before DD can fire.
DD at LA end-of-turn timing means it fires AFTER Sabriel's action on each round. If Sabriel acted
and Blessed on round 1, DD is already "late" — it strips a slot she didn't need this round.
The beholder should DD only if Sabriel has not yet Blessed; otherwise Tentacle+VR dominates.

This creates a one-round decision point with no in-game signal for the DM. The tactics text
says "Focuses on Drain Divinity to remove their healing and buffs" — this is too broad and
will mislead DMs into over-using DD.

**Recommendation (log only — tactics section edit, not auto-fix):**
Add to beholder tactics section: "Use Drain Divinity proactively in round 1 before Bless lands.
After round 2, prefer Tentacle+Void Ray (29 EV/round vs ~10 EV THP + 0.65 slots/round from DD).
DD is dead weight once divine slots are 1st-level only."

**FEEL-005: Descent required for Drain Divinity exposes beholder to melee**
The beholder's default position is 40-60 ft hover (out of melee, above the party).
DD's 30 ft range forces descent. Bazgar (Fighter 5, 2 attacks, likely GWF or PAM) deals ~20-25
EV/round at melee range. Over 3 rounds of DD: ~60-75 EV from Bazgar alone.
Beholder has 110 HP — giving up 60-75 HP across 3 rounds to drain ~3 spell slots (worth ~40 EV
in Divine Smites) is a losing trade unless Sabriel's Bless would otherwise win the fight.

The cheat sheet already notes "30 ft range — beholder must descend" but doesn't quantify the
melee-exposure cost. Consider adding a note: "Descending within 30 ft of Bazgar accepts ~20 EV/round
in melee damage — only justify if Sabriel has ≥2 3rd-level slots remaining."

### Summary: slot-tracking mechanic (adapted matron-slots test)

**DB slot tracking for Drain Divinity:** The DB row uses a `utility` type with an `effect` field
describing slot loss. The runner does NOT automatically decrement Sabriel's slot count (no slot-
tracking integration from DB into PC tabs). The DM must manually track Sabriel's slots and
announce them when targeted by DD. This is expected behavior (PC stat tracking is manual),
but warrants a DM reminder note in the cheat sheet alongside the DD entry.
