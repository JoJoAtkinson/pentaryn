### Recharge cycle observation (core of this scenario)

At **start of STD-2's R2 turn**, checklist item 1 fires: "If Ancient Resonance is USED, roll d6 — recovers on 5–6." AR was NOT fired (stagger hold kept it available). So **no recharge roll needed** — AR is still available.

This is the critical finding: **the scenario `thrulm-shrine-touched-resonance-cycle` expects AR to fire (type "resonance") and then tests the recharge roll across a round. In a natural combat slice, AR may never fire in R1 due to the 2-target range constraint.** The scenario is mechanically sound as a GUI regression test (direct verb dispatch → recharge roll fires correctly) but the natural encounter path may never exercise the recharge cycle at all if the party controls range.

**Simulated recharge (treating the scenario as if AR fired R1):**

Post-fire state: AR on cooldown. R2 start-of-turn: roll 1d6. Results:
- 1–4 (P=67%): no recharge. STD-2 must Multiattack.
- 5–6 (P=33%): AR recharged. STD-2 can fire AR if 2+ targets in cone.

In this slice, STD-1 is dead. R2 STD-2 alone: the stagger hold is lifted (only one STD alive). If AR recharged (33%), STD-2 moves and tries to get 2+ targets in cone. With party spread (Bazgar at front, Marwen/Sabriel behind), 2 targets in cone requires the party to cluster. At level 5, experienced players will spread to deny cone clustering. **AR recharge in R2 adds pressure only ~11% of the time** (33% recharge × ~33% chance of 2+ targets in cone with spread party).

### New Findings

#### FEEL-F25-01 (new): Stagger + single-kill-R1 = zero AR in short encounters

**Severity:** Low (design awareness)

When one STD dies before the second acts in R1 (common with high-nova parties — Sabriel's crit-smite combo at level 5 can one-round an STD at 45 HP), the second STD was "holding" AR by stagger and never fires it. Then in R2 the second STD has AR available but: (a) stagger no longer applies (only one STD), and (b) AR recharge is not needed since it was held (not used). So the second STD DOES have AR in R2.

However if the first STD never fired AR (range constraint prevented 2+ cone targets in R1), the total AR output in a 2-STD encounter is:
- STD-1: 0 (died before firing, only 1 target in range)
- STD-2 R2: AR available, but 33% recharge irrelevant (wasn't used). STD-2 fires AR if 2+ targets in cone.

**Net effect:** Parties that kill one STD fast and spread at range can reduce total AR fires to 0–1 per encounter. The ability feels vestigial when this happens. The stagger + range-gate creates a compounding constraint that's not obvious from the stat block.

**Suggested fix:** Allow STD to use AR as a bonus action at close range (5 ft), replacing the once-per-round stagger on the bonus-action use only. This would let a grappled or adjacent STD fire AR without action cost, maintaining melee pressure while ensuring the ability actually fires in most encounters. However, this is a significant redesign — log only.

#### FEEL-F25-02 (new): OBR self-damage barely contributes to Unstable Form threshold

**Severity:** Low (design note)

Unstable Form activates when the STD "took 10+ damage in a single turn last round." OBR self-damage (1d4 psychic = avg 2.5) is added to the total when checking the threshold. In this slice, Bazgar's hit (7) + OBR self (2) = 9 — one below the 10 threshold. The STD would have triggered Unstable Form if Bazgar had dealt 8+ instead of 7.

The OBR self-damage contribution creates near-miss threshold triggers frequently at level 5 fighter attack damage (avg 10–12 per hit). At average fighter damage, the STD almost always crosses the 10 threshold after taking a hit (10+ damage in one turn fires UF). But when the hit is slightly below average (7 in this slice), the self-damage doesn't bridge the gap.

This is a minor feel inconsistency: the player deals 7 damage, the ability punishes itself for 2, and the net outcome (9 total) narrowly misses the UF threshold. At the table, this distinction is invisible unless the DM is tracking carefully. It's correctly designed, just slightly unintuitive that OBR self-damage counts toward the threshold. **No fix needed — just flag for table awareness.**
