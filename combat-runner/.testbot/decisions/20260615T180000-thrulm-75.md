**FEEL-F75-01 (NEW — Log only): Taunt is dead weight against Wizard save-based spell lineup.**

In this scenario, the Rager taunted Marwen (DC 12 CHA, she failed). The Taunt imposed disadvantage on attack rolls vs non-Rager targets. However:
- Marwen's optimal R1 action was Fireball (DC 15 DEX save) — NOT an attack roll.
- Taunt has **zero effect** on Fireball, Web, Slow, Hold Person, Hypnotic Pattern, or any other save-based spell.
- The only Marwen spells affected by Taunt would be Scorching Ray or (pre-Wizard-5) Fire Bolt — both rarely optimal vs the beholder's resistance array.

The stat block correctly documents "Includes ranged spell attack rolls (e.g., Fire Bolt, Eldritch Blast)" — this is accurate RAW, but a Level 5 Wizard has better options than Fire Bolt vs an encounter of this threat level. Taunt disadvantage on attack rolls is meaningfully impactful vs Bazgar (who only makes attack rolls) but **functionally useless** vs Marwen's core spell menu.

**Root cause:** Taunt DC 12 is already borderline (BUG-A56-01: formula gives DC 9, not DC 12). Even if it lands, the effect is narrow (attack rolls only). Against a back-line Wizard who casts Fireball, Taunt consumes a bonus action for zero net DM-side benefit.

**Recommendation (design call for Joe):** Three options:
1. **Accept as-is.** Taunt is intentionally narrow (attack rolls). It's effective vs Bazgar and meaningless vs Marwen — that's thematic (the rager intimidates warriors, not wizards). Marwen isn't deterred because she's not aiming at the rager.
2. **Widen Taunt to include save DC.** Non-standard (no 5e precedent); increases complexity; makes Taunt meaningful vs spellcasters. Cost: power spike vs every PC type.
3. **Change Taunt target priority.** The rager should Taunt Bazgar (the melee fighter with attack rolls) rather than the wizard. Tactics note says "prefer back-line caster" — but against Marwen specifically, this is backwards. Update tactics note: "Taunt the PC whose damage is delivered via attack rolls (Fighter, Paladin weapon strikes); skip Taunt on a back-line save-based caster — the disadvantage won't affect their primary damage options."

**FEEL-F75-02 (NEW — Log only): Incoming Damage Aggro creates exploitable aggro-dump vs Taunt.**

The Rager's Taunt marks Marwen (intent: prevent her from freely attacking the Shardcaller). But the Rager also has Incoming Damage Aggro: if Bazgar hits the Rager, the Rager's first attack on its next turn is made at advantage against Bazgar. 

**Tension:** Taunt → Rager focuses on Marwen as priority Taunt target. But if Bazgar hits the Rager before its turn, IDA overrides: Rager's first attack fires at Bazgar (advantage) instead of Marwen. Bazgar can intentionally redirect Rager aggro to himself by hitting it once — pulling the Rager away from Marwen.

This creates a meaningful tactical choice for Bazgar (spend an attack to protect Marwen) that isn't currently documented in the stat block or encounter notes. The interaction is working as designed, but the counterplay isn't explicit. Consider adding a note to IDA: "IDA aggro-pull: if a PC hits the Rager to redirect it away from a protected ally, the Rager's advantage is consumed on that attacker's first attack next turn. This is a valid tactical tradeoff for the PCs."

- R2: DR fires on main action (recharge available from R1). DD fires again post-Sabriel (3 LA). 

The R2 DD firing is noteworthy: beholder spent 3 LA on DD in BOTH R1 and R2. In R2, Sabriel had only 1 L3 slot left (already stripped one in R1). R2 DD strips her last L3 slot. Sabriel is now on L2 slots only for Divine Smite — damage drops from 3d8 extra (L3 smite) to 2d8 extra (L2 smite). Effective DD attrition working as intended.

- R2: Taunt Sabriel (Sabriel SUCCEEDED save — Taunt wasted). Multiattack: 1 hit for 7 damage to Sabriel.
