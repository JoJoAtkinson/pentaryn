**DRIFT-R324-04: Maw .md "disadvantage on the saving throw" — wrong mechanic, unfixed since R220**

The stat block Maw text reads: "If the target is a creature grappled by the beholder, the
target has disadvantage on the saving throw." Maw is a melee attack roll; it has no
associated saving throw. This is dead/erroneous text.

Commit dbdb4f6 (R220) is described as fixing this ("Maw 'disadvantage on saving throw' →
'disadvantage on ability checks to escape the grapple'"), but that commit only touched
actions.jsonl — the DB narration was corrected; the `.md` was not. The `.md` still has
the original wrong text.

In D&D 5e, escaping a grapple requires a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics)
check against the grappler's Athletics check. Disadvantage on those checks is the correct
mechanic; "disadvantage on the saving throw" is not a 5e concept for grapple escape.

Action needed: Update `.md` Maw text to "If the target is a creature grappled by the
beholder, it has disadvantage on ability checks made to escape the grapple."
Not auto-fixable (mechanic text change, not damage average or tag). Mirrors the DB fix
already applied in R220.

## Phase B — Generative Playtest: drain-divinity-vs-sabriel

**Rotation index 4 of 8. Run counter: 20.**

### Setup

- Sabriel (divine martial 5, AC 19, HP 44, CHA +2)
- Spell slots: 2× 3rd-level, 3× 2nd-level, 4× 1st-level
- Beholder: HP 110, 3 legendary actions/round, in-chamber (+1 to all attacks/damage)

### Simulation — 3 rounds of Drain Divinity (3 LA each, entire budget)

After each of Sabriel's turns, beholder burns all 3 LA on Drain Divinity (target within
30 ft — beholder must have descended from default hover altitude).

| Round | Sabriel save (d20+2 vs 16) | Result | Beholder THP |
|-------|---------------------------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | 8+2 = 10 FAIL | Loses 3rd-level slot | 6 THP |
| 2 | 15+2 = 17 PASS | No effect | 6 THP (unchanged) |
| 3 | 4+2 = 6 FAIL | Loses 3rd-level slot | 6 THP (new grant = old grant; no change) |

**After 3 rounds:** Sabriel has lost both 3rd-level slots. Beholder holds 6 THP (not 16 —
THP don't stack; each new grant replaces previous only if higher).

**Wasted LA budget (round 2):** Beholder spent all 3 LA with zero effect. In round 2, the
alternative (Tentacle 1 LA + Void Ray 2 LA) would have averaged ~29 damage total.

### THP Analysis — CALC-R324-01 (corrected in rotation.yml this fire)

The rotation slice previously stated "beholder gains at most 16 temp HP (6+6+4)." This is
incorrect. In 5e, temporary hit points do not accumulate — you keep the higher of current vs
new grant. Three successful drains (3rd/3rd/2nd levels) yield:

- Drain 1 (3rd): gain 6 THP → holds 6
- Drain 2 (3rd): gain 6 THP → still 6 (equal, no change)
- Drain 3 (2nd): gain 4 THP → still 6 (lower, no change)

Maximum THP held at any moment: **6** (not 16). Expected THP per drain use ≈ 0.65 × 6 = 3.9.
**Fixed in rotation.yml this fire.**

### Feel Analysis

**FEEL-OPEN-01 confirmed (existing open issue):** Drain Divinity is economically dominated
by Tentacle + Void Ray at the same 3-LA cost (~29 expected damage vs ~4 expected THP). The
only case where Drain Divinity is rational is if Sabriel's remaining spells would produce
more than 29 "damage equivalent" — e.g., a Spirit Guardians concentration that would deal
30+ damage over multiple rounds. But at that point, it's often better to deal 29 damage
now and end the fight than strip a future spell. The 35% "wasted turn" risk makes this even
harder to justify.

**New observation — hover altitude constraint:** Drain Divinity requires the beholder to
descend from 40-60 ft to within 30 ft of Sabriel. Once descended, Bazgar (AC 18) and Sabriel
(AC 19) can melee attack the beholder. The cost of descending to use Drain Divinity is
exposing the beholder to two melee attackers who together average ~20-30 damage/round.
This cost is not captured in the simple LA-economy comparison. Effectively: Drain Divinity
(3 LA) costs 3.9 expected THP AND exposes the beholder to ~25 expected damage/round from
melee. It is economically *very* irrational when the beholder can stay at altitude and use
Void Ray for free reach.

**Recommendation for Joe:** Either (a) lower Drain Divinity LA cost to 2 (allowing
Tentacle+Drain in one round) so it's competitive, or (b) add a psychic damage rider
on successful drain (e.g., Sabriel takes 2d6 psychic damage) to give the drain an
immediate threat floor alongside the resource strip.
