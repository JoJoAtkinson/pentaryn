### New angle this fire: LA sequencing vs round context

Fire #82 already established the budget math (1+2=3 ✓) and ran the damage simulation. This fire examines a gap in the scenario's prelude: **it does not specify whether this is R1 or R2+, and does not note the initiative constraint from FIX-FC40-A.**

**The problem:** If a DM uses this scenario as a reference for R1 play, and fires Tentacle LA after Bazgar's turn (1 LA) + Void Ray LA after Marwen's turn (2 LA), they have spent all 3 LA BEFORE Sabriel acts. This violates the R1-DD-hold protocol (FIX-FC40-A): "hold all 3 LA until Sabriel completes her turn." The DM has now burned the full budget on Tentacle+VR and cannot use Drain Divinity after Sabriel — the most valuable R1 LA spend.

**When is Tentacle+VR the correct rotation?**
1. **R2+** — after R1 DD already fired (or Sabriel is down). Resume reactive LAs per FIX-FC40-A.
2. **R1 after Sabriel has already acted** — if Sabriel acts before the turn in question (high initiative Sabriel), the DD window has closed and Tentacle+VR is correct.
3. **When Sabriel uses LoH** — DD cannot target LoH (not a spell slot); redirect to Void Ray (per FEEL-F43-01 / `thrulm-dd-loh-redirect-void-ray`).

The scenario prelude "End of PC turn" is ambiguous — it covers all three cases above and also covers the WRONG case (Bazgar's or Marwen's turn in R1 while Sabriel still has slots). A DM reading this scenario cold might apply the Tentacle+VR rotation at the wrong moment.

**FEEL-F83-01 (NEW — Log only): Scenario prelude missing round-context constraint.**

`thrulm-beholder-legendary-rotation` prelude says "End of PC turn" without specifying R1 vs R2+. If a DM applies Tentacle+VR in R1 before Sabriel acts, they burn the full 3 LA budget and lose the DD window. The scenario is correct as a general LA-spend test, but lacks the constraint: "Use in R2+ or after Sabriel's turn in R1 — do NOT use in R1 before Sabriel acts if DD is viable."

**Recommendation:** Add a sentence to the prelude: "Note: in Round 1, hold all 3 LA until after Sabriel's turn (FIX-FC40-A); this rotation applies in Round 2+ or when Sabriel is already down/out of slots."

This is a scenario-description improvement, not a bug in the stat blocks. Safe to apply to `scenarios.yml` in a future fire.

---

**FEEL-F83-02 (NEW — Log only): Tentacle-grapple → Maw-autocrit counterplay (sever tentacle) undocumented.**

From the simulation: beholder fires Tentacle LA on Marwen (grappled). On the beholder's NEXT TURN, Maw auto-crit fires vs grappled Marwen — avg 8d8+3 ≈ 39 piercing, well above Marwen's remaining HP. The window for counterplay is the turns between the Tentacle LA and the beholder's next turn.

**Counterplay exists but is undocumented:** Each tentacle is targetable (AC 15, 15 HP per the stat block). Bazgar with two +7 attacks can deal avg ~20+ damage to a tentacle in one action, killing it and releasing the grapple before Maw fires. This is a meaningful tactical choice (redirect Bazgar from beholder to tentacle to save Marwen) that isn't mentioned in the beholder's tactics section or the combat cheat sheet.

**Recommendation:** Add to beholder tactics (or cheat sheet): "Tentacles (AC 15, 15 HP each) can be targeted and severed — a grappled PC is freed if its holding tentacle drops to 0 HP. Fighter with two attacks per action can sever a tentacle in one round (avg ~20 dmg vs 15 HP). This is the primary counterplay to the Maw auto-crit chain."

Not a bug; design is working correctly. DM note to add if Joe wants the encounter to feel fair rather than merely lethal.

---

**BUG-A12-06 re-observed in simulation:** The R2 DD sequence assumed the beholder pre-positioned on its R2 turn. In the scenario as written (LA rotation test), the beholder is already acting — there's no explicit turn where it descends. In a real table run, the DM may not know to pre-position before the LA window. BUG-A12-06 (44 fires open) remains the single most actionable undocumented rule.
