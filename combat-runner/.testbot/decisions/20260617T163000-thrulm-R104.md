### DR vs Multiattack decision analysis

**Option A: Disintegration Ray (recharge 5-6, starts combat available) at Marwen**

- Attack roll: +7 vs AC 15 = 65% hit chance (Void-Feeding active)
- On hit: 10d8+1, avg 46 force. Marwen HP 32. Nearly any hit disintegrates her.
  - Min damage: 11 → Marwen at 21 HP (survives, ~5% of rolls)
  - Avg damage: 46 → Marwen dead, disintegrated
  - P(disintegration) ≈ 65% × 95% ≈ **62%** this turn
- If Marwen is disintegrated R1 before acting: the player gets to make zero decisions. No spell, no move, nothing. Beholder then has no DR until recharge (5-6 per turn, ~33%/round).
- **Feel verdict:** 62% chance the wizard player is eliminated without a single action. This is "unfair rather than lethal" — the encounter is supposed to be lethal, but lethal in a way that lets players participate.

### BUG-A104-02: `thrulm-lair-prone-melee-synergy` scenario — incorrect PHB ruling in prelude

The scenario prelude (scenarios.yml, `thrulm-lair-prone-melee-synergy`) states:

> "RAW: melee attacks vs prone targets have advantage. TL (reach 10 ft) and Maw (reach 5 ft) are melee — both fire at advantage vs prone AC 18. +7 with advantage = 75% hit rate."

This is incorrect. PHB 292: **A melee attack against a prone creature has advantage only if the attacker is within 5 feet of it.** If the attacker is more than 5 ft away, the attack has **disadvantage**.

- Maw (reach 5 ft): if beholder descends to within 5 ft of prone Bazgar → advantage ✓
- TL (reach 10 ft): if beholder is 6–10 ft from prone Bazgar → attacker is MORE than 5 ft from prone target → **disadvantage, not advantage**

The beholder typically hovers; to get within 5 ft of a ground-level prone PC, it must descend to near-floor altitude (within 5 ft vertical), putting itself in dangerous melee range. The 75% hit rate claim in the prelude is wrong:
- TL with disadvantage vs AC 18: P(hit) = 1 - P(both rolls ≥10) = ... no: with disadvantage, P(hit) = P(lower of two rolls ≥ needed). +7 vs AC 18 needs 11+. P(single roll ≥11) = 50%. P(disadvantage hit) = P(both rolls ≥11) = 50% × 50% = 25%, not 75%.

Actual correct hit rates for TL vs prone Bazgar AC 18:
- Attacker within 5 ft (advantage): 1 - (0.50)² = **75%** hit
- Attacker 6-10 ft (disadvantage): (0.50)² = **25%** hit
- Attacker within 5 ft (straight, no prone): 55% hit

**The 75% rate in the prelude only applies if the beholder has specifically descended to within 5 ft of the prone PC.** The scenario should clarify this as a precondition.

**Log only** — this is a scenario prelude text error, not an auto-fixable source file.
