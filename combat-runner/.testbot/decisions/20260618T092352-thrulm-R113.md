### Round 1: DR opener

**Beholder main action — Disintegration Ray vs Marwen:**

+7 vs AC 15 → hits on 8+. P(hit) = **65%**.  
On hit: 10d8 force. Mean = 45, σ ≈ 7.25. P(10d8 ≥ 32) ≈ 96.3%.  
**P(Marwen killed by DR) ≈ 0.65 × 0.963 = 62.6%** (confirmed R97, R105, R112).

On hit ≥ 32 damage: Marwen is **disintegrated** — turned to ash.  
5e perma-death: cannot be restored by Revivify (requires creature to have died within the last
minute, not be disintegrated), Gentle Repose, or Raise Dead. **Only True Resurrection (9th-level)
or Wish can restore her.** Neither is accessible to a level-5 party.

**Lair action (init 20, after beholder):**  
Party entering room — Bazgar in range. Unstable Ground on Bazgar: DC 16 DEX vs Bazgar DEX −1.  
P(save) = 20%. P(prone) = 80%. Roll: 6 + (−1) = 5 → FAIL → **Bazgar prone**.  
No damage; positional control only. Bazgar speed halved (15 ft), melee attacks from nearby gain advantage.

**Beholder LA budget (3 total, refreshes at start of its R2 turn):**

With Marwen dead and DR spent (no multiattack this turn), the beholder's LA this round:
- Option A (DD focused): Wait for Sabriel's turn (init 12) → Drain Divinity (3 LA). DC 16 CHA.
  Sabriel CHA +2 → needs 14+. P(fail) = 65%. → Strips L3 slot, beholder gains 6 THP.
- Option B (damage focused): Tentacle (1 LA) after Sabriel → +7 vs AC 19, P=45%, EV ≈ 6.5 HP;
  Void Ray (2 LA) after Bazgar → DC 16 DEX vs Bazgar DEX −1, P(fail)=80%, avg 22 force → EV=17.6.
  Total EV ≈ 24.1 HP.

**Analysis:** With Marwen dead (no more divine healing from her), Sabriel is sole healer. Stripping
her L3 slot (DD) removes her highest-damage Smite AND her 3rd-level healing option. This is
strategically superior to 24.1 expected damage — the slot loss compounds across all remaining rounds.
DD is the dominant choice. LA spent: **DD on Sabriel (3 LA)**. Roll: 8+2=10 < 16 → FAIL.  
Sabriel loses L3 slot. Beholder: 110 + 6 THP = 110 HP + 6 THP.

### Void Ray LA sequence and type bug (BUG-R109-DB-02 extension)

In Option B (damage-focused LA), beholder would use Void Ray (2 LA, type "area" in DB) targeting
Bazgar. The DB entry `void_ray_legendary` has `"type": "area"`. The combat-runner GUI uses the type
field to select a targeting template: `area` triggers an AOE targeting prompt (radius/cone/line),
not a single-target selector. This means:
1. DM types "void ray" → GUI shows AOE template (e.g. "radius from beholder" or "click area")
2. DM intends single-target: Bazgar only
3. Mismatch produces either DM frustration or incorrect multi-target application

This is BUG-R109-DB-02 (void_ray type "area"). Confirming it corrupts the LA sequence when Option B
is chosen. Fix: change DB type to a single-target save type (`single_attack` with save note, or
create a new `targeted_save` type). Requires interactive MCP session. **Carry-forward.**

## FEEL-R113-01 — DR perma-death creates unrecoverable campaign loss at level 5

**Finding:** Disintegration Ray's perma-death rider — "reduced to 0 HP by this attack cannot be
restored to life except by true resurrection or wish" — operates at a tier completely inaccessible to
a level-5 party. The implications compound beyond the encounter:

1. **No Revivify:** Level-3 spell unavailable (disintegration explicitly excluded from its conditions).
2. **No Raise Dead:** Level-5 spell (available to Cleric/Druid level 9), not to Sabriel at level 5.
3. **No True Resurrection:** Level-9 spell. Not in reach until level 17+.
4. **Campaign impact:** Marwen is gone for the rest of the campaign unless the DM introduces a Wish or
   True Resurrection as a narrative gift. A "near win" where the beholder is eventually brought down
   but Marwen was disintegrated R1 still leaves the party short one PC permanently.

**The DR perma-death is the most punishing outcome not because of the fight itself, but because of
what it takes from the campaign.** A standard death (reducible to 0 HP) → Revivify window; DR death
→ campaign-long loss. The 62.6% kill probability means this happens in nearly 2 out of 3 encounters
where the beholder wins initiative and opens with DR.

**Recommendation:** Add DM table-prep note to encounter overview (`_overview.md` or beholder tactics):
> "The beholder will hold Disintegration Ray after it observes a spellcaster; it fires in R2 or later
> rather than R1. This gives Marwen one meaningful turn before the perma-death risk materializes.
> Alternatively: remove the perma-death rider on DR entirely for this encounter (the beholder's
> original stat block doesn't require it)."

**Not a mechanics change — log only. Needs Joe's decision before adding to overview.**

## FEEL-R113-02 — DD degenerate LA pattern: 9th consecutive confirmation

After Sabriel's turn in every round where she has at least one spell slot, Drain Divinity (3 LA) is the
strictly dominant LA choice. Move (1 LA) and Tentacle (1 LA) + Void Ray (2 LA) = 3 LA produce
~24 EV damage but don't permanently impair the party. DD strips a slot permanently.

**This is the 9th consecutive run confirming DD dominates every round Sabriel is standing.**

The briefing flagged this since fire #6. Still unresolved. The issue worsens in the DR-opener scenario:
Sabriel is now the ONLY PC with spell slots (Marwen is dead), making each DD use proportionally more
impactful. With Sabriel's L3 gone by end of R1 and L2 gone by end of R2, she has only L1 slots left
for R3. Her combat effectiveness is halved by R3 from slot stripping alone.

**Recommendation carries forward:** 2-LA cost for DD, OR once-per-long-rest cap, OR explicit
cooldown between uses. The current 3-LA-per-round-unlimited pattern means the beholder has
mathematically no reason to use Move or Tentacle LA when any divine caster is standing.
