### NEW — FEEL-F44-01: STD stagger exemption conflicts with UF priority rule

**Finding:** When a Shrine-Touched Derro is simultaneously (a) below 20 HP AND (b) has UF active AND (c) has AR recharged AND (d) ≤2 enemies are in cone, two documented tactical rules directly conflict:

1. **Stagger exemption:** "a shrine-touched below 20 HP is NOT subject to the stagger hold — it fires AR recklessly if AR is available and 2+ targets are in cone."
2. **UF priority:** "if UF is active, PREFER Multiattack over Ancient Resonance when only 2 enemies are in cone."

The stagger exemption says "fire AR." The UF priority says "use Multiattack." No explicit precedence is defined.

**The combined case is most likely to occur when:**
- Marwen fires Fireball or another AoE (deals ≥10 damage to one STD → triggers UF for that STD's next turn) AND
- The hit STD survives but is now below 20 HP (common: Fireball avg 28 to a Fireball target → 45−28 = 17 HP, exactly the scenario seeded).

In other words, this scenario is the canonical double-trigger case. It will occur in a substantial fraction of encounters where Marwen uses Fireball in R1.

**Recommended resolution:** Add explicit precedence note to STD tactics: *"Below 20 HP + UF active + ≤2 targets in cone: UF priority takes precedence — use Multiattack with advantage even though stagger hold is lifted. The 'reckless AR' language applies when the stagger hold would otherwise block AR; it does not override the UF tactical priority. Fire AR recklessly only if 3+ targets are in cone (when AR's AoE advantage outweighs UF attack advantage)."*

### NEW — FEEL-F44-02: UF priority rule (prefer Multiattack at ≤2 targets) is EV-negative vs AR

**Finding:** The UF priority rule states "PREFER Multiattack over Ancient Resonance when only 2 enemies are in cone — UF grants advantage on attack rolls, which is wasted on the save-based AR." The rule's rationale is sound (UF advantage is literally unused on a save-based ability), but the expected damage comparison shows AR still outperforms Multiattack:

**EV comparison vs standard Compass Edge party (DEX saves +1 to +0, ACs 18–19):**

| Scenario | Expected Damage |
|----------|----------------|
| AR vs Bazgar (DC 14 DEX, +1 save) | 11.1 avg |
| AR vs Sabriel (DC 14 DEX, +0 save) | 10.7 avg |
| **AR total (2 targets)** | **21.8 avg** |
| Multiattack w/ UF advantage on Bazgar (AC 18, +5 att) | 0.64 × 12.5 = 8.0 avg |
| Multiattack w/ UF advantage on Sabriel (AC 19, +5 att) | 0.578 × 12.5 = 7.2 avg |
| **Multiattack total (2 targets)** | **15.2 avg** |

**AR beats Multiattack by ~6.6 expected damage** against this party configuration. The UF priority rule optimizes for tactical feel (advantage produces visible crits and is "spent usefully") but is approximately 30% less effective in expected output.

**When the rule IS correct:** AR involves necrotic damage, which the party may resist (none of the Compass Edge PCs resist necrotic, so this doesn't apply here). In a party where PCs have high DEX saves (+4 or higher) and low ACs (≤15), Multiattack with UF advantage would outperform AR. Against the Compass Edge specifically, the rule is backward.

**Recommendation:** Revise UF priority to account for party save modifiers: *"If UF active, compare expected damage: AR = (fail_rate × 13.5 + success_rate × 5.5) per target; Multiattack = advantage_hit_rate × 12.5 per attack. Use whichever is higher for the current targets. As a rough rule: AR wins if the average PC DEX save total is +2 or lower vs DC 14; Multiattack wins if average PC DEX save total is +3 or higher. Against Compass Edge (avg DEX save ≈ +0), always prefer AR over Multiattack."*

This is a feel issue (no stat block damage is wrong); log only.
