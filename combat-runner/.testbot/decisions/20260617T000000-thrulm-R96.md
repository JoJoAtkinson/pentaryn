## Phase B — Scenario: `round-1-opener` (rotation.yml idx = 96 % 8 = 0)

**Slice definition:**
> Beholder wins initiative. Decide: multiattack now or save Disintegration Ray for Marwen
> (lowest HP, AC 15, 32 HP)? Simulate full multiattack turn including tentacle grapple
> rider. Track first-round damage output.

### Setup

- Bazgar: HP 49, AC 18. Fighter (BM) — +7 melee, DEX -1. 2 attacks per action.
- Marwen: HP 32, AC 15. Wizard — DEX +2.
- Sabriel: HP 44, AC 19. Divine martial — DEX +0.
- Beholder: HP 110, AC 17. Void-Feeding active (+1 atk/dmg). Effective TL/Maw bonus: +7 to hit.

### Initiative (representative rolls)

- Beholder DEX +3: 14 + 3 = 17 (wins initiative; acts first)
- Marwen DEX +2: 12 + 2 = 14
- Bazgar DEX −1: 8 − 1 = 7
- Sabriel DEX +0: 6 + 0 = 6

### Init 20 Lair Action

Unstable Ground → Bazgar (optimal per FIX-FC39-B: prefer melee fighters).
DC 16 DEX save; Bazgar DEX −1 → needs 17+ to succeed → 80% fail rate.
**Result: fail. Bazgar prone.**

### Beholder Turn — Path A: Multiattack (current protocol, FIX-EV7-32-A)

**TL1 vs prone Bazgar (+7, advantage per PHB p.292 melee-vs-prone):**
- Needs 11+ on either die → hit rate = 1 − (10/20)² = **75%**.
- Sim roll: 15, 8 → 15 → **HIT.** Damage: 3d6+4 (VF +1) avg 14.5 → 14. Bazgar grappled.
- Bazgar: 49 − 14 = 35 HP.

**TL2 vs prone Bazgar (+7, advantage):**
- Same 75% hit rate.
- Sim roll: 18, 5 → 18 → **HIT.** Avg 14. Bazgar double-grappled.
- Bazgar: 35 − 14 = 21 HP.

**Maw vs grappled Bazgar (AUTO-CRIT — target is grappled, per stat block):**
- Normal Maw: +7 vs AC 18. Grapple does not change attack roll.
- Bazgar also still prone: melee within 5 ft has advantage. Even at advantage, Maw still auto-crits IF it hits (the grappled-crit clause fires on a hit, not a guaranteed hit).
- +7 vs AC 18, advantage: needs 11+ on either die → 75% hit.
- Sim roll: 17, 9 → 17 → **HIT.** AUTO-CRIT: 8d8+4 avg 40. (Normal Maw: 4d8+4 avg 22.)
- Bazgar: 21 − 40 = −19 → **0 HP. DOWNED.**

**Path A R1 total damage to Bazgar: ~68 HP** (14 + 14 + 40). Bazgar (49 HP) downed in one beholder turn.

Expected value across runs:
- P(TL1 hit) = 0.75; P(TL2 hit) = 0.75.
- P(at least one TL hits → grapple) = 1 − (0.25)² = **93.75%** grapple rate.
- P(grapple established + Maw hits) ≈ 0.9375 × 0.75 = **~70% auto-crit Maw rate**.
- When auto-crit fires: 14 + 14 + 40 = 68 expected dmg → guaranteed Bazgar down.
- Without auto-crit (only 1 TL hit + Maw normal): 14 + 22 = 36 → Bazgar at 13 HP.
- Overall P(Bazgar downed in one beholder turn) ≈ **~73%** in this prone-grapple sequence.
  *(Margin cases: both TLs hit = 93.75% then Maw auto-crit = 75%; both TLs miss = 6.25% → Maw normal, no down.)*

**Beholder holds all 3 LA** (R1-LA-hold protocol for DD post-Sabriel).

### Path B: Disintegration Ray → Marwen R1 (alternative)

DR starts combat available (recharge 5–6 → begins charged per 5e convention).

**DR vs Marwen (+7 to hit, Void-Feeding; range 120 ft):**
- Needs 8+ to hit → **65% hit rate** (AC 15, +7 bonus).
- On HIT: 10d8 avg 45 force damage. Marwen has 32 HP. **Marwen at 0 HP, DISINTEGRATED.**
  - No body. LoH, Revivify, Healing Word cannot restore Marwen. Permanent elimination.
- On MISS (35%): Beholder wasted its action. No multiattack, no grapple, no Maw.

**Expected value comparison:**

| R1 Path | Expected Damage | Outcome |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| Multiattack → Bazgar (prone) | ~51 HP avg (TL1 + TL2 + Maw) | Bazgar downed ~73%; Bazgar recoverable via LoH |
| DR → Marwen | 0.65 × 45 = **29 HP avg** | 65% Marwen permanently eliminated; 35% wasted action |

Multiattack deals nearly double the expected damage and is more reliable. However, **permanent disintegration of Marwen on a hit is not recoverable** — in encounters where attrition matters over multiple encounters, DR→Marwen is strictly dominant. Within a single session (this encounter is designed as a TPK-likely), the distinction is: Bazgar can be revived by Sabriel's LoH (12+ HP pool), Marwen cannot be revived once disintegrated.

### Phase B Findings

**FEEL-R96-01 (NEW): R1 action decision tree missing DR→Marwen option.**

The tactics note (FIX-EV7-32-A) prescribes "R1 = Multiattack (grapple); R2 = Void Scream"
without discussing the DR→Marwen R1 alternative. The math shows:

- Multiattack→Bazgar: 51 avg HP, ~73% downed. Bazgar is RECOVERABLE (LoH brings him to 1 HP).
- DR→Marwen: 29 avg HP, 65% chance of PERMANENT elimination of the primary caster.

Against this specific party (Marwen is 32 HP, AC 15, primary AoE threat), DR→Marwen R1 when the beholder wins initiative is the highest-impact play and one a smart DM will reach for. The current tactics note does not address this choice.

**Table-feel concern:** DR→Marwen on initiative 17 (before Marwen at 14 acts) is a "one-round kill before the player got a turn" scenario per the briefing's unfair-vs-lethal criterion. The encounter is intended as lethal, but eliminating a PC before their FIRST initiative is a different qualitative feel than grinding them down over rounds. No note in the tactics section warns of this.

**Recommendation for tactics section:** Add a DR target-selection note:
"If beholder wins initiative and DR is available, compare: Multiattack→Bazgar (reliable ~51 avg, recoverable) vs DR→Marwen (lower avg 29, but 65% permanent elimination before she acts). Against this party, multiattack is prescribed for rounds where Sabriel's slot pool is intact (DD window more valuable). Reserve DR→Marwen for R2+ when DD has already fired and Marwen remains the last offensive threat. Note: using DR on Marwen before her first turn is technically optimal but eliminates a player before they have acted — consider table preference."

**FEEL-R96-02 (NEW): Prone+grapple Maw auto-crit quantified — Bazgar has ≤27% survival chance R1.**

Full R1 probability chain when beholder wins initiative (beholder before Bazgar in all scenarios
where beholder rolls ≥8):
1. UG at init 20 → Bazgar prone: **80% probability** (DEX -1, DC 16).
2. Given prone Bazgar: TL1 hits at 75%, TL2 hits at 75%.
3. P(≥1 TL hits → grapple) = 93.75%.
4. P(grapple + Maw hits at advantage vs AC 18) ≈ 93.75% × 75% = **70%**.
5. When auto-crit Maw fires: 14+14+40 = 68 HP >> Bazgar's 49 HP. **Guaranteed down** on this path.
6. When grapple established but Maw misses: 14+14 = 28 HP → Bazgar at 21 HP, grappled, still fighting.
7. P(Bazgar survives R1 at ≥1 HP) ≈ **~27%** (paths: both TLs miss ~6.25%, or 1 TL + Maw miss ~16%, or no prone ~20%).

This quantifies FEEL-F77-01 (19+ fires): "Bazgar zero R1 agency." With beholder winning
initiative (likely; +3 DEX vs Bazgar's -1 DEX), Bazgar has approximately **27% chance** of
taking his first turn. In the 73% case, he is downed before his initiative count.

**This is not a bug — the beholder is CR 13 and meant to dominate. It is a feel note.**
The appropriate table framing is: Bazgar's role in this encounter is "absorb the beholder's
opening salvo so the party can regroup." Consider narrating the lair action + multiattack
sequence before asking for Bazgar's initiative roll — that way the "downed before you act"
result lands as set-up rather than frustration.
