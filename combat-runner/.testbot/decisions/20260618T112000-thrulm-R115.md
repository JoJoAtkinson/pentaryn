## Phase B — Generative Playtest Slice

**Scenario:** `void-scream-clustering` (rotation.yml index 3; 115 % 8 = 3)  
**Core question:** Beholder waits until Bazgar and Sabriel are both within 30 ft of the shrine (round 2).

---

### Setup

**Context:** Round 2 open. Beholder fired Multiattack in round 1 (Bazgar at ~22 HP, grappled;
Marwen at ~25 HP after a Tentacle LA). Sabriel moved in; Bazgar is adjacent to the shrine.
Disintegration Ray (Recharge 5–6): assume recharge roll missed round 1 (beholder held it in reserve
to force this scenario). Void Scream (Recharge 6): rolls 6 on round 2 opener. Beholder uses its
action: Void Scream, 30-ft radius centered on self (beholder hovering 40 ft above altar).

All three PCs are within 30 ft of the shrine. Bazgar and Sabriel are within 10 ft of the altar base
(front-line fighters having charged in).

**WIS save assumptions per rotation.yml:**
- Bazgar: WIS +1, no WIS save proficiency (Fighter). Save: d20+1.
- Marwen: WIS +2, WIS save proficiency (Wizard). Save: d20+5.
- Sabriel: WIS +3, WIS save proficiency (divine martial). Save: d20+6.

---

### Save results — normal range (30 ft, no proximity penalty)

DC 16 WIS. Full damage 33 (6d10) psychic on fail, half (16) on success.

| PC | Save mod | P(success) | P(fail) | EV damage | PC max HP | EV HP remaining |
|----|----------|-----------|---------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Bazgar | +1 | 30% | 70% | 0.70×33 + 0.30×16 = **27.9 HP** | 22 (post-R1) | ~−5.9 HP |
| Marwen | +5 | 50% | 50% | 0.50×33 + 0.50×16 = **24.5 HP** | 25 (post-R1) | ~0.5 HP |
| Sabriel | +6 | 55% | 45% | 0.45×33 + 0.55×16 = **23.65 HP** | 40 (est. post-R1) | ~16.4 HP |

**Takeaway:** Expected EV alone puts Bazgar unconscious (~70% chance outright) and Marwen at the
edge of 0 HP (~50% chance unconscious). Sabriel survives at low HP. This is one action, before any
LA or bonus action from the beholder, and before the derro escorts act.

---

### Save results — proximity penalty (within 10 ft of shrine)

Bazgar and Sabriel are within 10 ft of the altar. They roll WIS saves with **disadvantage**.

With disadvantage: P(success) = P(lower die ≥ DC − mod) = P(both dice ≥ DC − mod).

| PC | Save mod | DC − mod | P(success w/disadv) | P(fail w/disadv) | EV damage |
|----|----------|---------|---------------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Bazgar | +1 | 15 | (6/20)² = **9%** | 91% | 0.91×33 + 0.09×16 = **31.5 HP** |
| Sabriel | +6 | 10 | (11/20)² = **30%** | 70% | 0.70×33 + 0.30×16 = **27.9 HP** |

Bazgar at 22 HP (post-R1) + 31.5 EV → **unconscious with ~91% probability**.  
Sabriel at 40 HP (post-R1) + 27.9 EV → ~12 HP remaining (expected; 70% chance of full 33 damage = ~7 HP left).

**Both front-liners have a strong chance of being down or near-death from a single Void Scream.**

### Shrine proximity disadvantage — clarity assessment

The beholder .md states: *"Creatures within 10 feet of the shrine have disadvantage on this save."*

**Tracking requirements at the table:**
1. DM must know the shrine's physical location in the room (no map in encounter folder).
2. "Within 10 ft" requires a live distance check per PC — differs from the 30-ft radius which is
   beholder-centered (wherever it is hovering).
3. Shrine size is unspecified: is "within 10 ft" measured from the altar edge, center, or aura?
4. Front-line fighters (Bazgar, Sabriel) are very likely within 10 ft if they engage melee derro
   near the shrine, but this is positional inference, not stated in the encounter.

**Feel note (FEEL-R115-01):** The proximity disadvantage has significant mechanical impact (flips
Bazgar from 30% save-chance to 9%) but provides no at-table guidance for when it applies. A DM
running cold from this stat block may forget the rule or apply it incorrectly. Add a one-line
note to either the _overview.md floor plan or the beholder stat block: e.g., *"The shrine's
altar platform extends 10 ft from center; creatures atop it save with disadvantage."*

### Recharge-6 cadence — FEEL-R115-02

Void Scream fires on recharge 6 only: P(recharge per round) = 1/6 ≈ 17%.  
Expected uses in a 4-round encounter: 4/6 ≈ **0.67 uses** (roughly 0 or 1).

Given a single use inflicts ~76.5 EV damage across the full party (normal range) or ~87 HP (with
proximity) — effectively winning the encounter in one action — the recharge-6 cadence produces
a **lottery dynamic**:

- ~50% of encounters: Void Scream never fires; beholder relies on Multiattack + LA.
- ~50% of encounters: fires once; encounter shifts dramatically toward TPK.

This is the highest single-action variance ability in the encounter, and it's entirely gated on a
d6 roll. Compare to Disintegration Ray (recharge 5–6, ~33% per round): its 45 avg is directed at one
PC and requires an attack roll. Void Scream is an AoE save with no attack roll and hits everyone.

**Feel note:** A recharge 5–6 for Void Scream would raise expected uses to 1.33 in 4 rounds
(40% to fire in any given round), reducing the lottery variance while still keeping it a rare event.
At the current recharge-6, the ability either defines the encounter or is completely irrelevant.
Not auto-fixable — design call.

### Three-ability naming cluster — FEEL-R115-03

This run involves simultaneous tracking of:
- **Void Ray** (Legendary Action, 2 LA, DEX save, 4d10 force, single target)
- **Void Scream** (Action, Recharge 6, WIS save, 6d10 psychic, 30-ft radius)
- **Void Eruption** (Lair Action, DEX save, 2d10 force, within 20 ft of shrine)
- **Disintegration Ray** (Action, Recharge 5–6, attack roll, 10d8 force, single target)

All four use "void" or "ray" in their names. Under round-timer pressure, DMs will confuse:
- Void Ray vs Void Scream (both say "Void [word]")
- Void Eruption vs Void Scream (both are AoE; Eruption lair, Scream action)
- Disintegration Ray vs Void Ray (both end in "Ray"; different mechanics)

This is a separate instance of the R114 FEEL-R114-01 naming problem (which proposed renaming
Disintegration Ray → Disintegration Beam). Extending that recommendation: rename Void Eruption
to "Shrine Eruption" (grounds it to the lair mechanic), keeping Void Ray / Void Scream as the two
ability-activation pair. Four names → still requires prep, but at least the pairs are mechanically
distinct in name-prefix now. **Not auto-fixable — design call. Needs Joe + MCP for DB rename.**
