### Feel Observations (round-1-opener)

**1. Marwen instant-death in round 1 — zero-counterplay window (FEEL-MARWEN-R300-01, NEW)**

Disintegration Ray (45 avg) vs Marwen (32 HP): the shot kills on any hit. At +9 attack bonus vs AC 15, the beholder hits on 6+, so 75% hit rate. There is one counterplay: Marwen can Counterspell. Disintegration Ray is a "Ranged Spell Attack" (stat block language) — Counterspell can interrupt it if Marwen is within 60 ft.

Counterspell mechanics at level 5: Marwen casts as a 5th-level spell slot automatically (she has no Counterspell), OR as a 3rd-level reaction (needs ability check DC = 10 + spell level if level > 3; the beholder's DR is effectively a 6th-level equivalent, so DC ~16). Expected outcome: even a successful Counterspell burns one of Marwen's three 3rd-level slots on defense, and the beholder's multiattack on Round 2 will likely still kill her.

This is per design (FEEL-003) but worth noting: against a level-5 party, Marwen's player experience is "I walked in, made one reaction roll, and died permanently." The "Meant to lose" framing is mechanical but the player experience of permanent death with no meaningful turn is harsh. Joe may want a narrative beat — beholder TARGETS Marwen last (gives her one full turn to act), or uses Void Scream first (survivable) before the DR. Does not affect the eventual outcome.

**2. Drain Divinity — fifth consecutive missed opportunity (extends FEEL-DRAIN-ALTITUDE-R299-01)**

Beholder opened with DR on Marwen instead of descending to use Drain Divinity on Sabriel. Reason: rational play — Marwen (32 HP) is a free kill from range, Drain Divinity requires 30 ft range and 3 LA. By the time Sabriel was within 30 ft (she moved to the beholder in Round 1), the beholder had already spent Antireality and hadn't descended. The beholder used multiattack in Round 2 instead, killing Sabriel faster than Drain Divinity could have drained her.

Pattern now confirmed across 5 consecutive simulations: Drain Divinity is architecturally unreachable against this party at this action economy. Full pattern summary for Joe in Phase C logged section below.

**3. Rager Taunt creates a meaningful combat fork (positive observation)**

Rager Taunted Bazgar on Round 1 (save failed). This forced Bazgar to either focus the Rager (burn Action Surge) or attack others at disadvantage. Bazgar chose to burn Action Surge, which killed the Rager but left him without that resource for the beholder. The Rager absorbed 2 rounds of Bazgar's optimal action economy. This is **functional encounter design** — the Rager serves as an action-sink that buys the beholder extra safety. Against a single Fighter, this works well.

**4. Thrall derro — zero combat contribution in 2 rounds (FEEL-THRALL-UTILITY-R300-01, NEW)**

Thrall A and B combined 4 attack rolls: 4 misses. Attack bonuses (+1 Hand Axe, +3 Dagger) vs AC 18-19 hit on 16+/15+ respectively — 25% and 30% hit rates. Against this party's AC, the thralls are effectively impossible threats. They contribute by:
- Absorbing attacks (Bazgar might spend attacks killing them)
- Serving as Compel Thrall / Manifest Thralls targets (the beholder's resource)
- Blocking movement

The problem is that in practice, Bazgar correctly ignored them both rounds and attacked the Rager. The thralls never became meaningful. If the DM follows "party is meant to lose" and doesn't meta-optimize, this is fine — thralls add narrative texture. But if running by-the-rules, experienced players will ignore thralls entirely.

**5. Antireality timing — functioned correctly**

Antireality fired on Bazgar's first attack (pre-roll, per BUG-F7-02 fix at R224). Bazgar's first attack missed due to disadvantage. Subsequent attacks hit uncontested (once per round cap). No regression observed.

**FEEL-MARWEN-R300-01 (NEW): Marwen instant-killed in round 1 with no meaningful turn**
*Severity: low/feel. Log only — design call.*

In "round-1-opener" simulation, Marwen (32 HP) was killed by Disintegration Ray in round 1 before she could act. She went 0 HP → disintegrated (permanent death) without taking a turn. Her only counterplay was a Counterspell reaction (uses a 3rd-level slot, DC ~16 ability check since DR is a high-level effect), which even if successful only delays her death by one round.

- Have beholder use Void Scream first (survives, gives Marwen a turn), then DR on Round 2
- Have beholder multiattack first (tentacle-grapple Marwen), THEN DR (requires grapple setup — more dramatic)
- Lower DR priority against Marwen: only use DR if no one is grappled yet

Does not affect the outcome (party still loses), only the shape of the defeat.

**FEEL-DRAIN-ALTITUDE-R300-01 (EXTENDS R299-01): Fifth consecutive playtest with Drain Divinity unused**
*Severity: medium/systemic. Log only — pattern now conclusive.*

Summary across 5 simulations (R296–R300):
- R296 (void-scream-aoe): Sabriel at range, beholder at altitude
- R297 (rager-berserk-gate): beholder altitude, LA not available
- R298 (tentacle-grapple): beholder altitude, used Tentacle LA instead
- R299 (disintegration-recharge): Sabriel already at 0 HP, no target
- R300 (round-1-opener): rational play chose DR → Marwen kill over descending for Drain Divinity

Root cause: the action economy math never favors Drain Divinity.
- 3 LA = entire LA budget. Spending it means zero Move, Tentacle, or Void Ray.
- 30 ft range requires beholder to descend from hover altitude, eating move action or Shrine-Drift.
- Expected THP gain: 2× spell level. Sabriel (paladin 5) highest slot = 3rd → 6 THP. Against beholder's 110 HP pool, this is cosmetic.
- Alternative spend: 2× Void Ray (2 LA each) = two targets hit for 22 force avg. This threatens 2 PCs rather than draining one.

**Recommendation for Joe (same as R299, escalated by 5th confirmation):** Drain Divinity currently dead weight. Structural fixes ordered by impact:
1. Extend range to 60 ft (keeps 3 LA cost, removes altitude barrier)
2. Lower LA cost to 2 (makes it competitive with Void Ray)
3. Redesign as multiattack rider: "If the beholder grapples a divine caster, it may drain their slot as part of the same attack" (makes grapple → drain a coherent combo)

**FEEL-THRALL-UTILITY-R300-01 (NEW): Thrall derro never land hits against this party**
*Severity: low/feel. Log only.*

In round-1-opener, 2 thralls made 4 total attack rolls (Rounds 1-2). 0 hits. Attack bonuses (+1 Hand Axe, +3 Dagger) vs AC 18-19 = 15-25% hit rate. Against the Compass Edge specifically, thralls are statistically expected to hit roughly 1 time per 4-5 attacks.

In a 3-5 round encounter, each thrall makes ~3-4 attacks = ~0.5 expected hits per thrall. Their damage (2-3 HP) is also too low to matter even on a hit.

Role in encounter is purely positional (block movement, grant flanking, serve as Compel Thrall targets) — not damage. DM notes should reflect this: thralls are best deployed as movement barriers that force PCs to spend actions clearing, not as attack threats. Currently the encounter sheet doesn't call this out explicitly.
