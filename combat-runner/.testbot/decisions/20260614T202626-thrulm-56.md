### Simulated combat segment — DD hover/range mechanics

**Setup:**
- Beholder: HP 110, AC 17, default hover ~40 ft above altar floor, Void-Feeding active (+1 attacks/damage)
- Bazgar (id: 1): HP 49, AC 18, STR fighter — on the floor, melee range of altar
- Marwen (id: 2): HP 32, AC 15, Wizard — 40 ft back from altar
- Sabriel (id: 3): HP 44, AC 19, divine martial — 10–15 ft from altar

**Key geometry:**
At default hover 40 ft, the straight-line distance from beholder (40 ft altitude) to Sabriel (0 ft, 10 ft horizontally from below) is √(40² + 10²) ≈ **41 ft** — outside DD's 30-ft range.
At descent to 20 ft, distance ≈ √(20² + 10²) ≈ **22 ft** — within DD range ✓
At descent to 25 ft, distance ≈ √(25² + 10²) ≈ **27 ft** — within DD range ✓

**Conclusion: beholder must descend to ≤25 ft altitude before firing DD.**

---

**R1 beholder turn sequence (confirming range protocol):**

*Using R1-LA-reserve protocol (FIX-FC40-A): hold all 3 LA for DD post-Sabriel.*

1. **Movement:** Beholder at 40 ft altitude. On its own turn, beholder uses 30 ft fly speed to descend: 40 → 10 ft above the floor. *(This consumes all standard movement — beholder cannot also close horizontal distance this turn unless it was already above Sabriel.)*
2. **Main action:** Multiattack (now in TL reach at 10 ft altitude, ~10 ft above Bazgar). Both TL hits → Bazgar grappled. Maw auto-crit if both land.
3. **Bonus action:** Shrine-Drift available — but descending via fly-movement is already done (BA not needed for this descent).
4. **LAs:** Hold all 3 for DD post-Sabriel.

*After Marwen's turn:* hold LAs. *After Sabriel's turn (init 12):*
DD fires at Sabriel. Beholder is now at 10 ft above the floor, within DD range of Sabriel. ✓

**BUT:** The beholder descended to 10 ft altitude. Bazgar is grappled. At 10 ft altitude, beholder is within melee reach of Bazgar's greatsword (reach 5 ft — barely). Sabriel can OA if beholder later repositions via SD without Disengaging first.

### FEEL-F56-01 (NEW): Grapple vs DD-descent trade-off is undocumented

After R1 multiattack (beholder at 10 ft, Bazgar grappled at 5 ft), the beholder needs to reposition in R2 to continue threatening Sabriel and Marwen. Two paths:

**Path A — Standard fly movement (drags grapple at half speed):**
Beholder can move up to 15 ft while dragging Bazgar (grapple rules: move at half speed). This lets the beholder haul Bazgar toward Sabriel, positioning for a R2 Maw auto-crit into Sabriel (if TL also hits her) while maintaining the grapple on Bazgar. **Advantage:** preserves grapple AND maintains DD range. **Disadvantage:** slow repositioning, can only cover 15 ft per turn.

**Path B — Shrine-Drift BA reposition (fast, no-OA):**
Per FIX-FC48-B: SD does NOT drag grappled creatures. If beholder uses SD to reposition 30 ft toward Marwen, it exits TL reach of Bazgar (reach 10 ft) → **grapple on Bazgar ends.** Bazgar stands up free (movement cost only — no action spent). Maw auto-crit opportunity lost.

**Neither path is documented in the tactics section.** The R2 decision tree (maintain grapple via slow fly drag vs release grapple for fast SD reposition) is a meaningful tactical choice with no authorial guidance. In the context of the DD descent protocol:
- If beholder descends R1 and wants to reposition R2 for VS coverage (requires SD-close), it must release the R1 grapple → loses the Maw auto-crit pressure
- If beholder drags Bazgar (half speed), it cannot cover 30 ft → cannot optimally position for VS next round

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to beholder tactics: "After descending for DD range: R2 choice between (a) dragging grappled PC at half speed (15 ft, preserves Maw crit pressure) and (b) Shrine-Drift reposition (30 ft, releases grapple). If VS is recharged and the party is clustered, use SD (accept grapple loss) to position for VS next round. If VS is not recharged and DR is available, maintain grapple via drag to set up R2 Maw auto-crit."
