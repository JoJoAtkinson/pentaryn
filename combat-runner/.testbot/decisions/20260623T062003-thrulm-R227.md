**Expected (average) outcome:** Marwen unconscious, Sabriel unconscious, Bazgar alive at 2 HP.

**On fail, FRIGHTENED also applies** (DC 16 WIS save ends, 1 minute) — **if BUG-R218-02 is fixed.** Currently it is NOT fixed; runner does not apply FRIGHTENED to Bazgar.

**Without FRIGHTENED:** Bazgar at 2 HP can attack normally. Action Surge available. 3–4 attacks vs beholder AC 17 at +4 (+7 Void-Feeding vs +4 raw? actually Bazgar's Greataxe: STR 16 = +3, PB +3 = +6 to hit). Against AC 17, needs 11+, 50% hit. **With FRIGHTENED (correct behavior):** Bazgar has disadvantage on attacks vs beholder in LOS. P(hit with disadvantage at +6 vs AC 17) = P(both d20s miss or one hits) — with disadvantage, P(hit) drops from 50% to ~25%. 4 attacks: 1 expected hit × 9.5 = 9.5 damage. Beholder: 95 → 85.5. Bazgar is also unable to willingly approach beholder.

**Impact delta: 19.0 vs 9.5 damage — FRIGHTENED halves Bazgar's expected output.** This is substantial given Bazgar is the sole remaining threat after Void Scream. BUG-R218-02 is not cosmetic; it fundamentally alters the endgame after Void Scream fires.

### Feel Assessment

**Verdict: Void Scream + Void Eruption same-round chain is the fight-ender, not a highlight.** When the beholder's lair action and Void Scream fire in the same round (which requires Void Scream to recharge — 1/6 per turn, so ~once every 6 rounds), the fight effectively ends. There's no PC-side countermeasure at level 5 (no Counterspell opportunity since Void Scream isn't a spell; no movement to break AoE since both effects already resolved).

**Is this "unfair vs lethal"?** Borderline. The Void Eruption lair action is predictable (every round, init 20). Void Scream is unpredictable (recharge 6). The combo is rare enough (~16% any given round) that parties may escape it most runs. But when it fires, there is NO counterplay — which satisfies the "lethal" framing for a designed-to-lose encounter. **Design intent confirmed as functional**: this IS the fight-ender the encounter is supposed to have.

**Clustering as the party's mistake:** The premise required all 3 PCs within 30 ft of the beholder. A smart party (using Sharpshooter or cantrips at range) can avoid this. Marwen should NEVER be within 30 ft of the beholder given her HP. This scenario tests the worst-case party position — appropriate for a regression gate.

**BUG-R218-02 (FRIGHTENED absent) impact confirmed HIGH:** FRIGHTENED on Bazgar after Void Scream is the difference between a 19-damage counter vs a 9.5-damage counter. In the endgame where Bazgar is the last PC, this determines whether the beholder descends below 75 HP (triggering retreat behavior per tactics section) or stays safe. FRIGHTENED is a critical game-state condition, not flavor text.

**Unconscious PC + Void Scream (FIX-FC48-A ruling confirmed):** Marwen entering round 3 at 0 HP (unconscious) cannot hear Void Scream — correctly excluded from its effects. This ruling prevents cascading permanent-death risk from repeated Void Screams targeting a downed PC.

### Legendary Action Economy Observation

Beholder **did not need Drain Divinity.** Void Eruption (free lair action) + Void Scream (main action) + single Tentacle LA ended the fight. 3 LA budget was sufficient for one Tentacle. Drain Divinity (3 LA) would have consumed the entire budget and left the beholder without Tentacle or Void Ray options, for a slot-strip that's strategically irrelevant once PCs are already dying. **FEEL-R224-03 / BUG-R226-02 confirmed again: Drain Divinity fires zero times in expected play.**

#### FEEL-R227-01 (HIGH — Void Eruption lair action + Void Scream same-turn chain ends encounters with no counterplay window)

**Finding:** In any round where Void Scream recharges (1-in-6, ~16%/round): the sequence is:
1. **Init 20 lair action:** Void Eruption → 11 avg force damage to all within 20 ft of shrine
2. **Beholder main action:** Void Scream → 33 avg psychic damage to all within 30 ft

**Combined: 44 avg damage** across both effects, hitting all shrine-adjacent PCs in a single round. Sabriel (44 HP at full) or Marwen (32 HP at full) can be reduced to 0 HP from this sequence starting at full health.

**PC counterplay options at level 5:** None that interrupt the chain. Counterspell doesn't apply (Void Scream is not a spell). Legendary Resistance is beholder-side. The only defense is spatial — stay >30 ft from beholder AND >20 ft from shrine simultaneously, which requires the party to forego any melee/focused attack options against the beholder.

**Design question for Joe:** Is a 16%/round instant-fight-ender with zero interrupt opportunity acceptable for the Compass Edge? Options:
- **A) Keep:** Rare enough (1/6), feels like a "big moment" when it fires. Compass Edge is meant to lose.
- **B) Lair action exclusion:** Beholder cannot use Void Eruption in the same round it uses Void Scream on its main action (flavor: the shrine's energy can't manifest twice in one round).
- **C) Range split:** Void Scream AoE is 20 ft radius (not 30), creating separation between Void Eruption targets and Void Scream targets.
