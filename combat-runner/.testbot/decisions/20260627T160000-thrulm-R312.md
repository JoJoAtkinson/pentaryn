## Phase B — Simulation: `drain-divinity-vs-sabriel`

> Beholder spends all 3 legendary actions (once per round) on Drain Divinity targeting
> Sabriel over 3 consecutive rounds. Sabriel is a divine martial 5 with assumed slots:
> 2×3rd, 3×2nd, 4×1st. DC 16 CHA save; Sabriel assumed CHA +2.

### Mechanical Setup

**DD costs:** 3 LA = the beholder's entire legendary action budget for the round.
**DD range:** 30 ft — beholder must descend from default 40-60 ft hover (already flagged FEEL-R310-01).
**Main action unaffected:** beholder still uses Multiattack on its turn.

**Save probability per use:**
DC 16, Sabriel CHA +2: needs 14+ on d20 to succeed → P(success) = 35%, P(fail) = 65%.

### Round-by-Round Simulation

**Round 1:** Beholder multiattacks (main action), then after Sabriel's turn uses Drain Divinity.

Expected multiattack damage vs Marwen (AC 15, +9 in chamber):
- Tentacle 1: 75% hit × 14 avg = 10.5 (+ grapple rider)
- Tentacle 2: 75% hit × 14 avg = 10.5 (+ grapple rider)
- Maw: 75% hit × 22 avg = 16.5
- **Multiattack expected: 37.5 hp** (Marwen at 32 HP likely unconscious after round 1)

After Sabriel's turn: Drain Divinity (3 LA):
- P(fail) = 65% → Sabriel loses her highest slot (3rd level) → beholder gains 6 THP
- P(success) = 35% → nothing

Expected: 0.65 × 6 = **3.9 THP gained**, 0.65 chance of stripping a 3rd-level slot.

**Round 2:** Assume R1 DD failed (worst case for Sabriel). She has 1×3rd, 3×2nd, 4×1st.

Beholder multiattacks (retargets Bazgar or Sabriel — Marwen likely unconscious or dead).
After Sabriel's turn: Drain Divinity (3 LA):
- P(fail) = 65% → Sabriel loses her last 3rd-level slot → beholder gains 6 THP
- P(success) = 35% → nothing

Expected: 0.65 × 6 = **3.9 THP gained**.

**Round 3:** If R1+R2 both failed, Sabriel has 3×2nd, 4×1st remaining.

After Sabriel's turn: Drain Divinity (3 LA):
- P(fail) = 65% → Sabriel loses highest remaining slot (2nd level) → beholder gains 4 THP
- P(success) = 35% → nothing

Expected: 0.65 × 4 = **2.6 THP gained**.

### Total Expected Outcome Over 3 Rounds of DD

| Scenario | P | Slots drained from Sabriel | THP gained by beholder |
|----------|---|---------------------------|------------------------|
| All 3 fail | 0.65³ = 27.4% | 3rd, 3rd, 2nd | 16 THP |
| Fail, fail, succeed | 14.8% | 3rd, 3rd | 12 THP |
| Fail, succeed, fail | 14.8% | 3rd, 2nd | 10 THP |
| Succeed, fail, fail | 14.8% | 3rd, 3rd | 12 THP |
| 1 fail, 2 successes | 7.9% each (×3) | 1 slot | 4-6 THP |
| All succeed | 0.35³ = 4.3% | 0 | 0 THP |

**Expected THP over 3 rounds:** 3.9 + 3.9 + 2.6 = **10.4 THP** (all-fail path; true expectation is ~10.1 THP accounting for path variance).

**Expected slots drained (all-fail path):** 3rd + 3rd + 2nd = removes all divine smite potential and two cure wounds equivalents.

### Opportunity Cost: DD vs LA Alternatives

The beholder forgoes LA flexibility each round it uses DD. Per round:

| LA option | Expected benefit |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Tentacle (1) + Void Ray (2) | ~10.5 damage (Tentacle on Sabriel, AC 19: 55% hit × 14 = 7.7) + 19.4 (Void Ray vs Bazgar DEX+1) = **~27 expected damage** |
| Void Ray (2) + Move (1) | 19.4 damage + repositioning value |
| Drain Divinity (3) | 0 damage + 3.9 expected THP + 65% chance to strip one slot |

Forgone damage per DD use: **~27 expected damage** — equivalent to roughly 22% of the party's combined HP over 3 rounds.

### Key Findings

**FEEL-R312-01 (new): DD target selection — Marwen is mechanically superior to Sabriel**

The tactics block says beholder "focuses on Drain Divinity to remove healing and buffs" against clerics/paladins, implying Sabriel. However, raw mechanics favor targeting Marwen (wizard):
- Marwen likely has CHA -1 to 0 → P(fail) 80-85% vs 65% for Sabriel
- Marwen's 3rd-level spells (Fireball, Counterspell) represent higher offensive/control threat than Sabriel's healing
- THP gain: 0.82 × 6 = 4.9 vs 3.9 per DD use

If the beholder uses DD on Marwen in round 1, it strips a 3rd-level slot before Marwen can Fireball, with higher success probability. This is not in the tactics block. The current text creates a mechanical suboptimality when the DM follows the "target cleric/paladin" instruction.

Recommend adding to tactics block: "*DD target priority:* when Marwen has not yet cast her high-level spell (or has failed to go first), Marwen (lower CHA, higher offensive threat) may be the better DD target in round 1."

**FEEL-R312-02 (new): DD value collapses against a party already losing**

Over 3 rounds, DD produces:
- ~10 THP (negligible vs 110 HP beholder — less than 10% HP in an encounter the beholder wins)
- Conditional slot strip from Sabriel (65% per round — not reliable enough to count on)

Meanwhile, forgoing Tentacle+VoidRay LA costs **~81 expected damage** over 3 rounds. If Marwen is already unconscious by round 1 (see FEEL-R308-01, R309-01), and Bazgar/Sabriel are taking multiattack hits, the party does not last long enough for cumulative slot drain to matter.

DD is mechanically meaningful only in a prolonged fight (6+ rounds) where the party can sustain itself through Sabriel's healing. In a "party meant to lose" design, that extended fight isn't the expected arc. DD is thematically correct but strategically suboptimal in the designed context.

This confirms and extends open issue #21. No new bug logged — adds specific numbers to the existing concern.

**AUTOMATION-GAP-R312-01 (new): DD requires 3 manual DM steps the runner won't automate**

Drain Divinity DB entry is type "utility" — runner will produce narration but won't:
1. Prompt a DC 16 CHA saving throw prompt (unlike `void_scream` which has `save.dc: 16`)
2. Identify and decrement which slot Sabriel lost (requires knowing her current slot state)
3. Add THP to the beholder's HP tracker

Compare: `void_scream` surfaces the DC and ability automatically. `drain_divinity` leaves all 3 resolution steps to the DM. For an ability the beholder is expected to use repeatedly against a divine caster, this is noticeable friction. At minimum, recommend adding a `save` block to the DB entry (dc: 16, ability: Cha, on_save: "no effect") so the runner prompts the CHA save. Slot tracking and THP remain manual — those are session-state dependent.
