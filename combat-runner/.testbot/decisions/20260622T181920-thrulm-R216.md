### Legendary Action Economy — Alpha-Strike Analysis

The three LA options evaluated against this party:

| LA Option | Cost | Round 1 Use | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Move | 1 | Drift to optimal position | Dominated by other options; only valid when repositioning is needed |
| Tentacle (LA) | 1 | Grapple Bazgar end of his turn | High value — adds grapple pressure without spending main action |
| Void Ray | 2 | 22 force vs Sabriel (reduced her to 11 HP) | Reliable; single-target but guaranteed to land unless saved |
| Drain Divinity | 3 | Strips Sabriel's level 3 slot | Spends ENTIRE budget; prevents any other LA this round |

**Drain Divinity analysis:** In the alpha-strike, the beholder chose Void Ray (2 LA) + spare (1 LA for Tentacle) over Drain Divinity (3 LA). The reason: with Sabriel at 11 HP after Void Ray, the beholder risks killing her before Drain Divinity can be useful. Against a fresh-entry Sabriel at 44 HP, Drain Divinity every round is dominant (5+ rounds of slot drain before she dies). Against a Sabriel already near death, Drain Divinity wastes the 3-LA budget when Void Ray + Tentacle finishes her faster.

**Confirmed existing concern:** The "always dominant vs divine casters" characterization only holds when the party survives long enough for multiple drain cycles. In this alpha-strike scenario (round 1–2 TPK), the beholder pivots away from Drain Divinity naturally. The design concern is specifically when the party is more durable than this and the beholder gets 4+ drain cycles — Sabriel loses all slots before she can act meaningfully.

**Identified gap:** The "always dominant" Drain Divinity problem is primarily a problem at the 5-8 round mark, not in round 1–2 fights. At this party level (level 5, 3 PCs), most fights end in round 2. Drain Divinity's degeneracy is a **mid-duration problem that the current party level rarely reaches**.

#### BUG-R216-01 (MEDIUM — orphaned mechanic)

**Maw "saving throw" clause references a non-existent save.**

**Location:** `world/factions/dulgarum-oathholds/locations/thrulm/npcs/beholder-thrulm.md` — Maw action.

**Text:** "If the target is a creature grappled by the beholder, the target has disadvantage on the saving throw."

- (a) Disadvantage on the grapple-escape ability check → uses wrong term (check ≠ save), and grapple escape advantage/disadvantage is already handled by the Grappled condition.

**Recommended fix (one of):**
- If (a) was intended: remove the clause (grapple-escape disadvantage isn't standard) or change to "disadvantage on checks to escape the grapple."
- If a broader effect was intended: replace with "while grappled by the beholder, the target has disadvantage on saving throws against the beholder's abilities" and move to a Trait.
- If it's a remnant: remove the clause entirely.
