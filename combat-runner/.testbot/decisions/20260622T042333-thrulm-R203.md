### Shrine proximity mechanics — table clarity check

Per beholder stat block: "Creatures within 10 feet of the shrine have disadvantage on this save." 

At the table, this requires tracking TWO reference points simultaneously:
1. Creature within 30 ft of beholder (VS radius, beholder-centered)
2. Creature within 10 ft of shrine (disadvantage trigger, shrine-centered)

If beholder hovers at 40−60 ft altitude above the shrine, the two zones are concentric at horizontal distance, but separate at altitude. A PC standing at the shrine base is ~40−60 ft from the hovering beholder (inside VS range, disadvantage zone) while a PC at the altar edge might be outside the 10 ft shrine zone but still inside the 30 ft beholder zone.

The dual measurement requires the DM to mark or track shrine position separately from beholder position mid-combat. This compounds with Antireality's 5-step manual override (FEEL-R196-01), producing high total DM overhead on the beholder's turn.

### FINDING-R203-01 (NEW — MEDIUM): VS avg 33 exceeds Marwen max HP 32 by 1 — arithmetic one-shot bracket established

**Root cause:** 6d10 average damage (33) is 1 HP above Marwen's maximum (32). The statistical consequence: P(6d10 ≥ 32) ≈ 58%, meaning on any VS fail, Marwen is more likely to be dropped to 0 than to survive. At full HP.

**Quantified probabilities:**
- P(VS one-shots Marwen, full HP, straight save): **~35%** (60% fail × 58% damage ≥ 32)
- P(VS one-shots Marwen, full HP, shrine proximity): **~49%** (84% fail × 58% damage ≥ 32)
- At Marwen HP 20 (one tentacle hit prior): P(6d10 ≥ 20) ≈ 97% → P(KO) ≈ **58%** (straight) / **81%** (shrine proximity)

**Comparison to DR:** Disintegration Ray one-shot P was documented as 35% (FEEL-R202-01) under recharge-confirmed conditions. VS at shrine proximity produces a *comparable kill probability for Marwen on any VS use at 2/3+ HP* — without the recharge assumption, since the kill requires only the area hitting all three targets (any of whom may be at reduced HP by round 2).

**Key distinction from DR:** DR is perm-death. VS at 0 HP is standard unconscious. Feel difference is large; mechanical lethality is similar.

**Recommendation for Joe:** Extend FEEL-R202-01's "pre-session consent" note to also cover VS: "If VS is used while PCs are clustered mid-combat (likely round 2), Marwen has a 35–49% chance of being dropped to 0 HP per VS fire. At any reduced HP this becomes 50–80%. This is an *expected* encounter outcome — confirm with players that repeated unconsciousness is part of the design, or restrict the beholder from targeting clustered low-HP PCs with VS."

### FINDING-R203-02 (NEW — LOW): Frightened from VS is near-vestigial for grappled melee fighters

**Root cause:** VS imposes Frightened on failed save (1 minute, DC 16 WIS end-of-turn ends). Frightened's two components:
1. Disadvantage on attack rolls while beholder is in LoS.
2. Target cannot willingly move *closer* to the beholder.

For Bazgar (Fighter, likely grappled by tentacle during the same or prior round):
- Component 1 applies: disadvantage on his attacks. This is real (Bazgar WIS +1, breaks on 15+ = 30%/turn, expected ~3 rounds frightened).
- Component 2: if Bazgar is grappled, he can't move at all — frightened's movement restriction is redundant.

For Sabriel (divine martial, melee engagement): same analysis. The "cannot approach" clause is vestigial the moment either fighter is grappled or otherwise immobilized.

**Net feel impact:** VS frightened is functionally "disadvantage on attack rolls for 1−3 rounds" for melee fighters. The flavor text and mechanical complexity of frightened (end-of-turn save, LoS check) is not proportionate to its functional effect when the beholder's grapple is in play simultaneously. Existing flagging in FEEL-R193-02 (grapple chain offers no Marwen counterplay) partially addresses this; the melee-fighter frightened redundancy is a separate nuance.
