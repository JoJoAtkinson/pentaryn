### Beholder turn (initiative 19): multiattack vs DR decision

**DR availability at encounter start:** 5e standard is rechargeable abilities are available at the start of combat. DR starts fresh. The beholder has both options.

#### Option A: Disintegration Ray on Marwen (32 HP, AC 15)

- DR: Ranged Spell Attack +7 (in-chamber, Void-Feeding active) vs AC 15.
- Needs 8+, **65% hit rate.**
- On hit: 10d8 = avg 45 force. 45 > 32 HP → **Marwen at 0 HP.**
- Per stat block: target reduced to 0 HP is disintegrated (ash). Cannot be restored except by *true resurrection* or *wish*.
- On miss (35%): DR expended, recharge needed. Multiattack next turn.

Expected damage to Marwen: 0.65 × 45 = **29.25 avg** — but the meaningful figure is P(instant death) = **65%.**

#### Option B: Multiattack on Marwen

Beholder targets both tentacles + maw at Marwen.

- Tentacle Lash ×2: +7 vs AC 15 → 65% hit each, 13 bludg + grapple (escape DC 16).
- Maw: +7 vs AC 15 → 65% hit, 21 piercing.

P(all 3 hit) = 0.65³ = **0.274**; damage = 13+13+21 = 47 → one-shot (47 > 32).
P(exactly tentacle1 + maw hit) = 0.65 × 0.35 × 0.65 = **0.148**; damage = 13+21 = 34 → one-shot.
P(exactly tentacle2 + maw hit) = 0.35 × 0.65 × 0.65 = **0.148**; damage = 34 → one-shot.
P(only maw hits) = 0.35² × 0.65 = **0.080**; damage = 21 → not one-shot.
P(only tentacle1 or 2 hits, no maw) = remaining probability; damage = 13 → not one-shot.

**P(Marwen at 0 HP from multiattack) ≈ 0.274 + 0.148 + 0.148 = 0.570 (57%).**

Expected damage: 0.65×13 + 0.65×13 + 0.65×21 = 8.45 + 8.45 + 13.65 = **30.55 avg.**

#### Decision comparison

| Option | P(Marwen dead R1) | Avg damage | Side effects |
|--------|-------------------|------------|--------------|
| DR | **65%** | 29.25 | Permanent death on 0 HP; DR expended |
| Multiattack | 57% | 30.55 | Grapple setup; DR still available |

DR strictly better for P(kill) and has the disintegrate outcome (permanent death). Multiattack gives slightly higher average damage and preserves DR for a future turn.

**Optimal beholder strategy:** Use DR if available (65% permanent kill is superior to 57% ordinary kill). Save multiattack for turns when DR is on recharge. The rotation.yml premise "decide: multiattack now or save DR for later" is slightly misleading — the correct answer is almost always "use DR now."

### FEEL-R161-01 (NEW) — DR on R1 = 65% permanent PC death before Marwen's first turn; no practical counterplay

Marwen at initiative 11 acts 4th in the order. If beholder fires DR on its R1 turn (initiative 19):
- Marwen has not yet had a turn.
- No PC reaction meaningfully counters a +7 ranged attack roll (no Counterspell since DR is not a spell — it's a ranged spell attack, but the triggering action is not "cast a spell").
- Shield (reaction, +5 AC to AC 20 for one attack) helps: +7 vs AC 20 → needs 13+, 40% hit. Still 40% permanent death.
- If Shield is unavailable or not prepared, beholder hits at 65%.

**Outcome distribution, R1 DR on Marwen:**
- 65%: Marwen disintegrated before her first turn. Player cannot act in this combat.
- 35%: DR misses. Marwen survives, acts at initiative 11.

This is by design (CR 13 vs L5 = intentional loss), but the **feel issue is the mode of loss, not the probability.** A 65% chance of permanent, irreversible death before a player gets their first action is a specific type of unfairness the briefing flags: "one-round kills before the player got a turn." The disintegrate outcome means standard L5 resurrection magic (Raise Dead, Revivify) cannot restore the character.

**Recommendation (LOG):** Add to beholder encounter notes (or _overview.md): "DM WARNING: Disintegration Ray available R1. If beholder wins initiative and targets Marwen, 65% chance of permanent PC death before Marwen's first action. Have a pre-session conversation with players about permanent death risk in this encounter." This is a DM communication issue, not a mechanical bug.

### FEEL-R161-02 (NEW) — Grapple + DR is not a synergistic combo; beholder tactics text implies it is

The beholder's multiattack grapples the target. The stat block's Maw says "If the target is a creature grappled by the beholder, the target has disadvantage on the saving throw." This has been flagged as STAT-R153-01 (Maw has no saving throw; the clause is orphaned).

But the deeper implication: **the grapple does not benefit Disintegration Ray either.** DR is a ranged attack roll — it is not a saving throw. A grappled target is not more vulnerable to DR (grappled only imposes speed = 0; it does not impose disadvantage on saves/attacks against it for DR purposes).

**The beholder has no grapple → DR combo.** A DM who grapples Marwen on R1 (via multiattack) and then expects to "set up" DR for advantage is mistaken — the grapple provides no mechanical benefit to DR's accuracy. The correct reading: grapple keeps the target in reach for Maw follow-up attacks (if Maw ever gets a saving throw, which it doesn't). Currently the grapple rider on Tentacle Lash provides almost no tactical value beyond immobilizing the target.

**Recommendation (LOG):** Add a tactics note: "Grapple (Tentacle Lash) does NOT improve Disintegration Ray accuracy — DR is an attack roll, not a save. Grapple primarily denies movement. Combine Multiattack (tentacles + maw) and DR on separate turns for maximum output, not as a setup combo."

This is a tactics clarity issue, not a stat block error. Ties back to STAT-R153-01 (orphaned save clause on Maw).

### FEEL-R161-03 (NEW) — Drain Divinity "has spell slots" clause: arcane vs divine ambiguity

The Drain Divinity legendary action targets "one creature within 30 feet that has spell slots, divine favor, or clerical powers."

With the Compass Edge party:
- **Sabriel** (divine martial): clearly qualifies ("divine favor" or "clerical powers").
- **Marwen** (Wizard 5): Has arcane spell slots. Per RAW ("has spell slots"), she qualifies.
- **Bazgar** (Fighter-BM 5): No spell slots (Battlemaster maneuvers are not spell slots). Does NOT qualify.

**The beholder's tactics section says:** "Focuses on Drain Divinity to remove their healing and buffs" under "Against Clerics/Paladins" — implying divine-only intent. But the mechanic is broader.

**At the table, can the beholder drain Marwen's arcane slots?**

If yes: after Marwen is killed by DR (65% R1), she can't be drained (dead). But if she survives R1, the beholder could drain her arcane slots with the same legendary action economy as targeting Sabriel.

If no (divine-only intent): the tactics section's language is the binding interpretation. Arcane wizard slots are excluded.

**Impact:** If Marwen survives R1 and the beholder uses Drain Divinity against her, the DM needs a clear ruling. Draining Marwen's highest slot (3rd level) gives the beholder 6 THP — same return as draining Sabriel's. The strategic question becomes: drain Marwen (prevents Fireball) or drain Sabriel (prevents healing/smite)?

**Recommendation (LOG):** Joe must clarify intent. Two options:
- **A (RAW-broad):** Any creature with spell slots qualifies. Add "(including arcane)" to the stat block text. Note in tactics: "prioritize divine slots (healing removed) over arcane (damage reduced)."
- **B (Design-narrow):** Drain Divinity targets divine power only — rewrite to "divine favor, clerical powers, or paladin/cleric spell slots." Marwen is excluded. This matches the flavor ("hungers for the absence of divinity").
