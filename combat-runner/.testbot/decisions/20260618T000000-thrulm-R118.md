### New bug found: Rager Taunt DC 12 doesn't derive from stated stats

**BUG-R118-TAUNT-DC-01 (new):**  
`derro-rager.md` — Taunt bonus action lists DC 12, but the derivable formula gives DC 9.

- CHA 9 → modifier **−1**
- Proficiency Bonus (CR 2 → PB **+2**)
- Formula: 8 + PB + CHA = 8 + 2 + (−1) = **9**, not 12
- The discrepancy is +3 — suggests this may be a legacy value set when the rager had higher CHA or a different PB

**Not auto-fixable.** DC is a design call: correcting to DC 9 makes Taunt trivially ignorable (need only 10+ on d20 for most PCs); DC 12 may be intentional for playability. Log only — needs Joe to decide canonical DC or adjust CHA.

**Key finding from simulation:**

Beholder acts at initiative 17 — before both Sabriel and Bazgar. It multiattacks Sabriel (+7 in chamber):
- Tentacle Lash 1 (13+7=20): HIT → 13 bludgeoning + grapple (Sabriel STR save DC 16: 12+3=15 FAIL → **grappled**)
- Tentacle Lash 2 (8+7=15): MISS
- Maw vs grappled Sabriel (16+7=23): HIT → 23 piercing

Sabriel takes **36 damage R1 before her turn**, dropping to 8 HP and grappled. At end of Thrall 2's turn, beholder spends all 3 LA on Drain Divinity (Sabriel CHA save DC 16: 8+3=11 FAIL → loses 2nd-level slot).

This confirms the **R1 initiative lethality pattern** noted in prior runs: Sabriel cannot survive two rounds of multiattack + Drain Divinity LA if beholder beats her on initiative and no interruption is possible.

### Thrall action economy analysis

**Thrall 2 turn (init 13):**

- Action: `hand_axe_weak` vs Marwen (AC 15): d20+1 vs 15 → needs 14+. Hit rate: 35%.
  - Expected hit: 0.35 × 2.5 avg = **0.875 avg damage per swing**
  - Even with `dagger_desperate` (+3): d20+3 vs 15 → needs 12+. Hit rate: 45%. Avg damage: 0.45 × 4 = **1.8 avg/swing**
- Bonus action: `compelled_movement` → thrall moves 30 ft toward Marwen

**Against AC 18 (Bazgar) or AC 19 (Sabriel):**
- `hand_axe_weak` (+1 vs AC 18): needs 17+ → 20% hit rate → **0.5 avg damage/swing**
- `hand_axe_weak` (+1 vs AC 19): needs 18+ → 15% hit rate → **0.375 avg damage/swing**
- `dagger_desperate` (+3 vs AC 18): needs 15+ → 30% hit rate → **1.2 avg damage/swing**

**Verdict:** Thralls are non-threatening vs the armor-wearing PCs (Bazgar AC 18, Sabriel AC 19). Their only material damage output is against Marwen (AC 15). Their primary mechanical role is:
1. **Compelled Movement** (BA) — positioning token for beholder-directed harassment
2. **Threatening Marwen** — 1.8 avg damage per swing is meaningful against a 32 HP wizard over 3+ thralls across multiple rounds
3. **Blocking attacks of opportunity** / absorbing area-of-effect damage

This is consistent with design intent ("pure action economy drains, not damage threats vs armor"), but is worth making explicit in the encounter notes so the DM doesn't over-prioritize thrall attacks.

### New feel notes

**FEEL-R118-01 (new):** Thrall action economy is poorly legible from the stat block alone. The `.md` lists three actions (Multiattack/1 attack, Hand Axe Weak, Dagger Desperate) but it's not obvious that the thrall's **bonus action `compelled_movement`** is the mechanically significant contribution, not the attacks. Adding a one-line DM note in the tactics section ("Thralls' primary role is Compelled Movement [BA]; their attacks are cosmetic vs AC 18–19 — focus them on Marwen") would save DM cognitive load at the table.

**FEEL-R118-02 (new):** The beholder's multiattack-before-Sabriel scenario (confirmed this run, R1 grapple + 36 damage pre-initiative) is a near-instakill setup that provides no counterplay window for the players if beholder wins initiative. This is consistent with "party is meant to lose," but the Maw doing 23 piercing to a grappled target on round 1 may feel cheap rather than threatening. Consider making the Maw advantage-on-attack-roll conditional explicit (the existing text says "disadvantage on saving throw" but there is no save — see BUG-R111-MAW-01), or replacing it with a swallow/restrain mechanic that keeps Sabriel in danger for multiple rounds instead of eliminating her in one.
