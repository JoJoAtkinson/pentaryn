### Phase B simulation — turn by turn

**Init 20 (lair action):**
Party just entered chamber, ~60 ft from altar. No PC within 20 ft of shrine → Void Eruption
misses everyone. Beholder chooses Unstable Ground (DC 16 DEX) on Bazgar.
- Bazgar DEX save: +0 → needs 16+ → P(fail) = 75%.
- On fail: prone. Bazgar spends 15 ft of 30 ft speed standing. With 15 ft remaining movement,
  he likely cannot reach the beholder (still 40–60 ft up AND 40–60 ft horizontal). Prone costs
  him positioning options, not combat actions this round.

**NEW FINDING: FEEL-008 — Beholder has no action-economy ranged attack at altitude without Disintegration Ray**

The beholder hovers 40–60 ft above the altar. Its action-economy options from altitude:

| Action | Range | Available round 1? | Notes |
|--------|-------|--------------------|-------|
| Disintegration Ray | 120 ft | 33% (recharge 5–6) | Ranged spell attack |
| Void Scream | 30-ft radius | 17% (recharge 6) | Area from beholder position |
| Multiattack | 10 ft reach | Requires descent first | Must close distance |

P(at least one ranged non-multiattack action available round 1): P(DR or VS) = 1 − (4/6)(5/6) = 1 − 0.556 = **44%**

In **56% of round-1 starts**, the beholder cannot act at altitude without descending. Its only
useful action is Multiattack, which requires a 40–60 ft descent. Descent mechanics:

- Shrine-Drift (BA): move 30 ft (descend 30 ft → now 10–30 ft above floor)
- Move (fly 30 ft): descend to 0–10 ft → enters melee range

This works, but the DM needs to know: Multiattack REQUIRES the beholder to abandon its
altitude position. The cheat sheet says "Hover above altar (40–60 ft up)" as the beholder's
default position but does NOT note that this position precludes multiattack. A DM running
this cold could forget to descend, effectively skipping the beholder's action.

**Recommendation (cheat sheet note, not auto-fixable):** Add to "When Beholder Acts":
"Multiattack requires descent to ≤10 ft. Use Shrine-Drift (BA) + fly (move) to descend
~60 ft in one turn. The beholder must choose between altitude safety (LA + Antireality
from above) and melee threat (descend to grapple + drain melee). This IS a decision point."

### Multiattack vs Marwen — expected damage (beholder descends)

In chamber, Void-Feeding always active: +1 to all attack rolls and damage.

**Tentacle Lash (×2) vs Marwen (AC 15):**
- Attack: +7 to hit vs AC 15 → needs 8+ → P(hit) = 65%
- Damage per hit: 3d6+4 (base 3d6+3 + Void-Feeding +1) → avg 14.5
- EV per Tentacle: 0.65 × 14.5 = **9.4**
- Grapple on hit: escape DC 16; Marwen Acrobatics DEX+2 → needs 14+ → P(escape) = 35%;
  P(stays grappled) = 65%

**Maw vs Marwen (AC 15):**
- Attack: +7 to hit → P(hit) = 65%
- Damage per hit: 4d8+4 (base 4d8+3 + Void-Feeding +1) → avg 22
- EV: 0.65 × 22 = **14.3**

**Total multiattack EV vs Marwen: 9.4 + 9.4 + 14.3 = 33.1**

Marwen HP is 32. Expected damage 33.1 > HP. P(all 3 attacks hit) = 0.65³ = **27%** → fully
lethal on average when all connect.

**Hit distribution:**
| Hits | P | Expected damage | Marwen result |
|------|---|-----------------|---------------|
| 3/3 | 27.4% | ~33 | Unconscious + grappled × 2 |
| 2/3 | 44.3% | ~19–24 | 8–13 HP remaining |
| 1/3 | 24.4% | ~9–14 | 18–23 HP remaining |
| 0/3 | 4.3% | 0 | Unharmed |

In the 27% all-hit case, Marwen is unconscious before she takes her first action if beholder
wins initiative (beholder +3, Marwen +2: P(beholder > Marwen roll) = ~55%). This occurs
with probability ≈ 0.27 × 0.55 = **15%** on round 1 — enough to be a real table concern.

### Targeting decision — one-note dominance confirmed

The beholder's optimal target is always Marwen (lowest HP, easiest to hit at AC 15). This
is rational given its intelligence (INT +3) but creates a predictable, one-note opener:
1. Descend toward Marwen
2. Multiattack (focus fire)
3. Repeat via Tentacle LA until she's down, then pivot to Sabriel for Drain Divinity

There's no decision point that makes the beholder vary targets based on what the party does.
The encounter design would benefit from a tactical note: "If Bazgar interposes himself between
the beholder and Marwen, the beholder evaluates whether grappling him (large HP tank, blocks
Marwen access) is preferable to ranged options."

### Legendary action economy — post-turn spend

If Marwen acts before the beholder's NEXT turn (likely, since Marwen init ~12 ≥ beholder ~13
on some rolls): beholder spends Tentacle (1 LA) after Marwen's turn → second grapple attempt.
After Bazgar/Sabriel turns: Void Ray (2 LA, DEX save DC 16) → Bazgar DEX +0, P(fail) = 75%;
EV = 0.75 × 23 + 0.25 × 11.5 = **20.1** (4d10+1 in chamber). Total LA EV round 1: ~29.5.
