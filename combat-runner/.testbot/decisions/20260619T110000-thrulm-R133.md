### Feel assessment

**FEEL-14 (NEW) — Thrall as slot-drain trap via positioning pressure**

The thrall at 0.5 DPR is not a damage threat, but the beholder's Compel Thrall BA converts the thrall into a *positioning tax* on the wizard. Two outcomes:

1. **Marwen ignores it:** She eats the 0.5 DPR and has full action economy. The thrall's contribution is near zero.
2. **Marwen reacts:** She burns a 2nd-level Misty Step slot to escape. Over 3 rounds: 3 slot levels burned on positioning rather than offense.

Which outcome fires depends on the DM playing Marwen intelligently. If the DM reasons correctly that 0.5 DPR is not worth a spell slot, outcome (1) applies. If the DM plays Marwen as a reactive spellcaster (as most players would), outcome (2) applies.

This is a hidden ability-rotation issue: the thrall is more valuable as a *slot-drain proxy* than as a direct attacker, but only when the target overreacts. Because the encounter is meant to be a loss, the "overreaction" outcome is probably fine — but the effect is indirect and DM-dependent, not a mechanical guarantee. DMs who recognize the math may discount the thrall entirely, making it dead weight.

**Recommend logging, no fix.** If Joe wants reliable pressure at 0.5 DPR, consider having the thrall impose a *condition* on a hit (shaken/rattled) rather than just damage, so the positioning tax becomes unavoidable.

**Compel Thrall + Compelled Movement double-move (FEEL-11 confirmed active in this scenario)**

The beholder used Compel Thrall (BA, count 15) to force the thrall to move toward Marwen. Then on the thrall's own turn (count 9), the thrall used Compelled Movement (BA) to move toward Marwen again. Both are distinct actions. If the Compel Thrall effect occurs mid-round (off the beholder's BA), the thrall effectively gets:
- Its own 30 ft movement (action phase)
- 30 ft from Compel Thrall (triggered by beholder BA during beholder's turn)
- 30 ft from Compelled Movement (thrall's own BA on its turn)

= 90 ft of directed movement per round. This isn't a bug — both are legit bonus actions from separate sources — but the cheat sheet doesn't flag this. A thrall starting 90+ ft from the wizard closes to adjacent in one round. FEEL-11 carry-forward with this scenario confirming it fires in practice.

**DB-OPEN-05 carry-forward confirmed: "multiattack" verb absent from thrall DB**

Scenario used "hand axe" (matches via "axe" verb) and "compelled" (exact match). But the stat block action header is "Multiattack. The derro makes one melee attack." If a DM types "multiattack" at the thrall tab, no DB row matches. LLM fallback occurs.

This is a minor UX issue (the action name misleads DMs to type the header), not a mechanical bug — the underlying attack resolves correctly when typed as "axe" or "attack." However, "multiattack" is the most natural input from reading the stat block.

**Legendary action rotation this scenario: Drain Divinity remains dominant**

Scenario confirms: with one thrall on the table and Sabriel in range of Drain Divinity, the beholder's entire 3 LA budget goes to Drain Divinity every round. The thrall adds zero legendary action leverage (it acts independently). The LA economy is unchanged from the solo-beholder scenario. Drain Divinity = all 3 LAs = confirmed dominant vs divine caster in every run.
