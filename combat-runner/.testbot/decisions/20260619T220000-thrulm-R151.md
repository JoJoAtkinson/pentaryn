## Phase B — Generative Playtest Slice

**Scenario:** `retreat-threshold` (rotation.yml index 6)  
**Title:** "Retreat at 30 HP — tactical viability"

**Questions under test:**
1. Does the beholder have a meaningful retreat path given 30 ft hover and room dimensions?
2. Does the retreat feel dramatic or anticlimactic?
3. Does Shrine-Drift function as an escape tool?

---

### Setup

- Beholder at **28 HP** (below 30 HP retreat threshold)
- Party resource state (mid-late fight assumption): Bazgar ~30 HP; Sabriel ~28 HP (smites spent); Marwen alive with 2 spell slots remaining (L2 + L3 or L1 + L5)
- 2 thrall derro alive: T1 at 15 HP, T2 at 22 HP (summoned earlier)
- Beholder Legendary Resistance: 1/3 remaining (2 spent blocking prior saves)
- Initiative order assumed: Beholder 20 → Sabriel 15 → Bazgar 13 → Marwen 10 → Thralls 8

---

### Retreat mechanics

**Beholder's movement budget per round:**

| Source | Distance | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Own movement (fly 30) | 30 ft | Provokes OA if leaving adjacent |
| Shrine-Drift (BA) | 30 ft | Phase through obstacles; 5 (1d10) if ends in object |
| Move LA × 3 (1 LA each) | 90 ft | After each of 3 PCs' turns |
| **Total** | **150 ft** | — |

A typical large vault (40–60 ft across) is crossed in the **first turn's own movement alone**. With Shrine-Drift and 3 Move LAs, the beholder covers 150 ft before any PC can act more than once.

### Party counterplay analysis

**Bazgar (Fighter-BM 5):** No fly speed, no reliable ranged weapon at range > 30 ft. Could throw a handaxe or use a longbow if equipped. Fighter has no ranged attack listed in roster. Effectively cannot pursue a hovering beholder moving 150 ft/round away.

**Marwen (Wizard 5):** Best anti-retreat options:
- **Hold Monster (L5):** Targets beholder, WIS save DC 14 (estimated: 8 + PB+3 + INT+3). Beholder WIS +2: needs 12+ to save = 55% resist. If 1 LR remaining, beholder may burn it. If LR spent: 45% chance Hold Monster sticks. Duration 1 min (concentration). This is the **only viable pursuit stopper** available.
- **Slow (L3, DC 14):** Halves beholder's speed (15 ft fly) and reduces movement. Beholder WIS save again; same 55% base. If it sticks: Move LAs still work (LA move = up to fly speed, halved to 15 ft). Total retreat distance drops to ~75 ft/round. Still fast but forces a 2nd round to fully exit.
- **Counterspell:** Only relevant if beholder uses a spell-like ability during retreat (Void Scream = possible distraction/area denial). Marwen can counter it to prevent area denial.

**Sabriel (divine martial 5):** No fly speed. Javelin (30 ft range) or a ranged smite option. Can follow through doorways if the shaft entrance is accessible.

**Thralls (blocking pursuit):** The 2 remaining thralls position to block the corridor toward the lower shaft. 22 HP + 15 HP. Against Bazgar (2 attacks, +5, ~10 avg damage): 1.5 rounds to clear both. Against Marwen: 2 rounds (cantrips/L1 spells). The thralls buy 1–2 rounds, which is exactly long enough for the beholder to complete its 150 ft exit.

---

### Feel analysis

**FEEL-R151-01 (NEW): Retreat escape is automatic — party has no meaningful counterplay without Hold Monster.**

The beholder covers 150 ft in its first retreat round (own movement + Shrine-Drift BA + 3× Move LA). Unless Marwen uses Hold Monster (L5) on the round the beholder declares retreat, the encounter ends with the beholder simply disappearing. The party watches it phase through stone and fly away at a speed no level-5 PC can match.

The thrall "blockade" (2 minions at 37 combined HP) delays pursuit by exactly 1–2 rounds — which is all the beholder needs. The blockade does not create drama; it creates time-filler while the main threat exits.

**What makes this feel anticlimactic:** The near-death beholder is at its most narratively interesting moment (the party nearly killed a CR 13 entity), but the combat resolution is completely passive for the party. They kill two weak minions and then the boss is gone. There is no decision point.

---

**FEEL-R151-02 (NEW): Shrine-Drift as escape is under-documented — DMs will miss the phase-through clause.**

Shrine-Drift (bonus action): "The beholder moves up to 30 feet. It can move through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain; it takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside a creature or object."

During a retreat, the beholder can phase through the altar stonework, pillars, and partial wall-collapses described in the environmental factors section. This is dramatically appropriate (an eldritch creature seeping through stone) but the DM must remember:
1. It costs Shrine-Drift (BA, main turn only — not available via LA).
2. It takes 1d10 self-damage if it ends inside an object (so the DM must end movement clear of solid stone).
3. The 5 ft forced-difficult-terrain through solid objects means 30 ft of phasing covers only 15 ft of real distance if the path is entirely through stone. In practice, the path likely alternates open air and stone features, so ~25 ft effective.

None of this is in the cheat sheet or tactics section. A DM who forgets Shrine-Drift can phase-through will route the beholder around obstacles, reducing escape speed and possibly creating a corridor-combat scenario that IS interesting.

**Recommendation:** Add to beholder retreat tactics: "Shrine-Drift (BA) phases through obstacles — use to exit through the altar dais or collapsed shrine walls directly. Ends-in-object costs 1d10 force damage; route movement to end in open air."

---

**FEEL-R151-03 (CONFIRM FEEL-R142-02): Move LA ×3 = 90 ft of free distance; counterplay requires Hold Monster or Slow.**

This confirms prior flag. Additional specifics:
- Against a 1-LR beholder: Hold Monster (L5, DC 14) has ~45% chance to stick (beholder burns last LR or fails). If it sticks, party has the beholder held for 10 rounds — full party focus fire at a restrained, paralyzed target could finish it.
- Against a 0-LR beholder: Hold Monster is ~45% chance to stick on the spell, guaranteed to stick (no LR to burn). This creates a second climactic "almost got away" moment.
- Against a 2-LR beholder: Both LRs are available; Marwen needs to fire Hold Monster twice (burning L5 + L5 or L5 + another concentration slot). At level 5, Marwen has only one L5 slot. Practical conclusion: if the beholder has 2 LR remaining at retreat, it escapes.

**Design tension:** The retreat is "balanced" only if the party has already spent the beholder's LR down to 0-1. If LR management was poor (party didn't force saves), the beholder's retreat is guaranteed. This is probably intentional (LR management as macro-decision) but needs a note in the tactics/encounter design section.

---

**FEEL-R151-04 (NEW): Retreat to "lower shaft" lacks mechanical grounding in the stat block or cheat sheet.**

Beholder tactics: "If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower shaft." The stat block also says the lower shaft is 60 ft beyond the main chamber, and "something down there scares even this creature."

The retreat destination is pure narration — there is no mechanical representation of what happens when the beholder reaches the lower shaft. It simply disappears from the encounter. This is fine as written, but creates a dangling hook:
- Does the encounter end at that point?
- Can the party follow (and face whatever is in the lower shaft)?
- Is the lower shaft entrance blocked/sealed until the beholder opens it?

None of this is in the cheat sheet, stat block, or encounter notes. The retreat is designed to create mystery but operationally it just means the beholder exits the combat. A DM improvising this moment will either (a) have the party follow into unknown danger, or (b) call the encounter over, leaving the party uncertain about their "win condition."

**Recommendation:** Add a 1-paragraph note to the beholder stat block or the encounter _overview.md: "When the beholder retreats, the lower shaft entrance (describe location) is passable but leads to [description]. The party may pursue; if they do, [telegraph consequence]." This anchors the retreat moment without requiring a full second encounter.

### Legendary Action economy (observed during retreat round)

All 3 LA spent on Move (1+1+1 = 3). No damage output during retreat turn (beholder action skipped attack for movement; LA budget consumed by movement).

Expected damage output on retreat turn: **0** (entirely defensive turn).

If beholder had chosen: Disintegration Ray (action) + Move LA ×3 = potential one-shot of Marwen during retreat (45 avg force, +6 to hit vs AC 15). This creates a critical DM decision: "escape cleanly vs guarantee kill a caster on the way out." Neither option is prompted by the cheat sheet.

**FEEL-R151-05 (NEW):** The retreat-turn action economy is undocumented. DM chooses between: (a) pure escape (action = nothing, all LA = Move), (b) parting shot (action = Disintegration Ray on Marwen, all LA = Move), or (c) area denial (action = Void Scream if recharged, forces WIS saves on clustered party, then all LA = Move). None of these is in the tactics section's retreat guidance. "If below 20 HP, it attempts to crush/disintegrate the party while fleeing" covers option (b) but not (c), and the 20 HP threshold is inconsistent with the 30 HP retreat trigger.
