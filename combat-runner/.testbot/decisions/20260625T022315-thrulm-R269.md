## Phase B — Playtest slice: `disintegration-threat`

*(thrulm-rotation.json index 1 — Disintegration Ray frequency, disintegration outcomes, and permanent-death feel in a 3-round fight)*

### Setup

- **Beholder:** HP 110, AC 17, init +3. Disintegration Ray AVAILABLE (first use). Void-Feeding active.
- **Compass Edge:**
  - Bazgar: Fighter 5 (Battlemaster), HP 49, AC 18, init +2
  - Marwen: Wizard 5, HP 32, AC 15, init +3
  - Sabriel: Paladin 5, HP 44, AC 19, init +2

### Initiative (representative — beholder-first scenario)

| d20 | Actor | Mod | Final |
|-----|-------|-----|-------|
| 14 | Beholder | +3 | **17** |
| 11 | Bazgar | +2 | **13** |
| 9 | Sabriel | +2 | **11** |
| 8 | Marwen | +3 | **11** (after Sabriel) |
| — | Lair action | — | **20** (fixed) |

Order: Lair (20) → Beholder (17) → Bazgar (13) → Sabriel (11) → Marwen (11)

### Round 1 — Disintegration Ray on Marwen

**Lair action (init 20):** Unstable Ground on Bazgar (DC 16 DEX, 65% prone). Imposes disadvantage on R1 engagement but has no direct effect on this slice's focus.

**Beholder turn (init 17):** Uses Disintegration Ray on Marwen as its main action (before Marwen acts at init 11).

- Attack: +6+1(VF) = +7 vs AC 15. Needs 8+. P(hit) = **65%.**
- If hit: 10d8 force (avg 45). Marwen HP = 32.
  - P(Marwen ≤ 0 HP | hit) = P(10d8 ≥ 32). 10d8 range is 10–80, avg 45, std dev ≈ 7.25.
    P(X ≥ 32) ≈ P(Z ≥ (32–45)/7.25) = P(Z ≥ –1.79) ≈ **96%.**
  - P(Marwen disintegrated | hit) ≈ P(10d8 ≥ 32) = 96% (disintegration triggers on reaching 0 HP).
- **P(Marwen disintegrated this turn) = 0.65 × 0.96 ≈ 62%.**

Marwen acts at init 11 (after beholder). If disintegrated (62% case): Marwen never acts. If alive (38% case): Marwen can cast (Fireball or Misty Step), then faces further attacks.

**Round 2 — Recharge and second use**

Beholder starts round 2 by rolling recharge for DR: P(recharge) = 2/6 = **33%.**

If recharged and Marwen alive: DR again on Marwen at the same odds → cumulative kill probability by end of round 2 ≈ 1 – (1 – 0.62) × (1 – 0.62 × 0.33) ≈ **79%.**

If Marwen is dead by round 2, beholder redirects DR to Bazgar (49 HP, AC 18). P(hit) = P(d20+7 ≥ 18) = 50%. P(disintegration | hit) = P(10d8 ≥ 49) ≈ P(Z ≥ (49–45)/7.25) ≈ P(Z ≥ 0.55) ≈ 29%. So: P(Bazgar disintegrated in one shot) ≈ 0.50 × 0.29 ≈ **14%.**

### Key findings

**FEEL-DR-R269-01 (NEW): Marwen faces a 62% chance of permanent death before her first turn.**

If the beholder opens with DR on round 1 (rational: Marwen is the highest-value spell target at AC 15 / 32 HP), Marwen's player has a 62% chance of taking zero actions before their character is permanently lost. The encounter is designed for the party to lose, but a 62% odds of immediate character permadeath (with no possible recovery for a level-5 party — True Resurrection is 9th level) sits in a different category from "lethal but memorable." The player cannot influence this outcome.

**Interaction with existing FEEL-003:** FEEL-003 notes the permanent-death concern; this fire quantifies it. At 62% in round 1 alone, the outcome is more likely than not on a typical run. Even if the DM uses the *Hard* scaling (beholder + 2 thrall derro) rather than the *Deadly* scaling, the beholder still acts first when it wins initiative.

*Joe's decision options:*
1. **Soft kill:** Remove the disintegration condition from stat block (drop to 0 HP, standard dying). Preserves the fight's lethality without permanent loss.
2. **Tactical delay:** Add a tactics note that the beholder spends round 1 observing/taunting (uses Multiattack or Drain Divinity instead) and reserves DR for round 2+. Gives Marwen at least one turn.
3. **DR without perma-death:** Reduce the DR disintegration trigger to "if reduced to negative HP equal to max HP" (i.e., instant death by massive damage, as per standard 5e rules), removing the "cannot be restored" special case. This keeps the fear of DR without the permanent-death outcome.

**FEEL-DR-R269-02 (NEW): Two DR uses in a 3-round fight at ~37% probability.**

If DR fires in round 1 and recharges in round 2 (0.65 hit × 0.33 recharge = ~21%), there's roughly 21% chance of a second use. If the first shot missed (35%) and recharges for round 2 (33%), another 12% chance. Combined: roughly 21–33% chance of two DR uses in the encounter. With Marwen as the first target and Bazgar or Sabriel as the second, two DR uses could kill two PCs permanently, ending the encounter in round 2 with complete party wipe. This is within the design intent (deadly) but the "perma-death" framing warrants re-examination.

**Recharge 5–6 (33%) is fast for an ability this lethal.** If DR is kept with the permanent-death rider, consider Recharge 6 only (16%), giving roughly one confirmed use per fight (used round 1) with a ~14% chance of a round-2 reuse. This maintains the threat without the ~33% double-permanent-death probability.

**Interaction with CS-OMIT-R259-01:** The cheat sheet SAVE DCs table lists DR as "+7 to hit" with no disintegration note. A DM running from the cheat sheet might not realize DR permanently kills a 0-HP target. The omission is now higher-priority given the 62% round-1 kill rate. Joe should add the note.

### Round 3 outlook (if DR used twice or party survives R2)

By round 3, if Marwen is disintegrated and Bazgar is bloodied (~15 HP from prior analysis), the encounter ends via:
- Bazgar → drops from LA Tentacle or remaining multiattack
- Sabriel → drops from multiattack; has lost 1–2 spell slots from Drain Divinity

The beholder is at ~55–70 HP entering round 3 (took ~40–55 damage from party counters in R1–R2). If the party somehow survives round 3 (unlikely), beholder retreats at <30 HP per tactics.
