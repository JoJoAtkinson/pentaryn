**UNFAIR-NOT-LETHAL FLAG (R121-A):**  
Bazgar goes down before initiative count 14 (his first turn) in Round 1. Sequence: lair action (prone, init 20) → beholder advantage multiattack (init 17) → Bazgar at −1 HP, never acts. This is NOT "lethal but fair" — it is **pre-turn elimination of the party's melee anchor**. The beholder winning initiative by a modest margin (17 vs 14) combined with the lair action creates a guaranteed down-before-turn window for Bazgar roughly whenever:
- Beholder wins initiative over Bazgar (P≈50% with matching mods)  
- Lair action Unstable Ground lands (P≈70%)

Joint probability ≈ 35% of Round 1s. In 35% of encounters, Bazgar never swings. This is structural — not a one-in-a-million bad luck outcome. See FEEL-R121-01 below.

### Phase B Conclusions

**(1) Which legendary action is dominant?**  
Against this 3-PC party, the optimal per-round LA sequence is:
- **Void Ray (2 LA)** at end of weakest PC's turn (targets Marwen, DC 16 Dex, ~22 avg force damage)  
- **Tentacle (1 LA)** at end of any remaining PC's turn (targets whoever is in range)

Drain Divinity (3 LA, entire budget) is dominant specifically when Sabriel is the last PC standing — it strips all her healing/smite resources before she can recover the party. But it competes with Void Ray + Tentacle for overall damage throughput. In the R121 simulation, Void Ray + Tentacle won: Marwen downed at init 11 (before Thralls even went).

No single LA option is "always dominant" — but Void Ray is the default and almost always optimal against Marwen. Drain Divinity becomes dominant in longer fights when Sabriel is the last Divine PC.

**(2) Unfair-not-lethal moments?**  
Yes — Bazgar pre-turn KO (FEEL-R121-01). Structural, not an outlier. See above.

**(3) LA economy functioning?**  
Yes. 3 LA refreshed correctly at beholder's turn. Full 3 spent per round (2+1). No budget errors detected. Void Ray correctly labeled as single-target DEX save (not attack roll), which is unambiguous during play.
