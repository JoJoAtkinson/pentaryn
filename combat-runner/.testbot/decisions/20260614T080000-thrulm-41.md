### New feel issues identified

**FEEL-F41-01 (NEW) — STD fire/radiant vulnerability is dead in standard encounter positioning:**

The cheat sheet and stat block document a fire/radiant vulnerability suppressed "within 60 ft of altar." In the Thrulm vault (a sealed hollow), the encounter entry zone is well within 60 ft of the altar for the standard setup — the STD starts in the altar zone and is always within range of the suppression.

More critically: when the STD drops below 20 HP (when fire/radiant vulnerability would be most exploitable by a retreating, weakened enemy), **Driven Escape** (bonus action, no opportunity attacks) forces the STD to move TOWARD the altar, not away. The STD always moves DEEPER into the suppression zone when low on HP, which is exactly when the vulnerability would be otherwise valuable.

Result: Sabriel's Divine Smite (radiant) deals normal (not doubled) damage for the entire fight against any STD that has not been forcibly pushed >60 ft from the altar — a condition Driven Escape actively prevents. The vulnerability is a DM-facing reference for enemy design but **provides zero PC-facing tactical signal in practice**: the cheat sheet tells players to "use fire/radiant against Shrine-Touched," but this never produces a better outcome than non-elemental damage within the altar zone.

Recommendation: either reduce altar zone radius to 30 ft (so melee-engaged STDs at the entry threshold are exploitable), or make vulnerability activation dependent on STD being in Shrine-Bound panic (>300 ft, which never happens in the vault). Low-impact unless Joe wants the vulnerability mechanic to matter in play.

**FEEL-F41-02 (NEW) — OBR self-damage feeds UF threshold invisibly; OBR vs smite-heavy melee feels punishing to the STD:**

When Sabriel lands a Divine Smite hit on the STD, OBR fires: the STD counter-swings (35% hit rate vs AC 19) and takes 1d4 psychic self-damage regardless. If OBR misses (65% probability), Sabriel used a smite slot and the STD gained nothing except self-punishment. Against a consistently high-AC paladin, OBR will miss more than it hits — from the players' perspective it reads as the enemy "retaliating itself to death," which can feel absurd rather than threatening.

The self-damage does feed the UF threshold (1d4 avg 2.5 adds to the "damage taken last round" check), but this is invisible to players without DM narration. The DM must explicitly narrate that the counter-swing's self-damage is strengthening the creature for next round's attacks.

Secondary issue: OBR fires once per round (reaction) on the FIRST hit that triggers it. Sabriel's second attack does not re-trigger OBR. The STD's reaction economy is thus dominated by a single roll that usually misses.

No stat-block fix recommended (OBR mechanic is intentional). DM guidance addition: note explicitly in tactics that "OBR miss still counts toward UF threshold via self-damage — narrate the counter-swing's failure as something cracking inside the creature, not as wasted effort."

**FEEL-F41-03 (NEW) — Scenario `thrulm-shrine-touched-unstable-form` does not test UF state:**

As noted above, the scenario is missing a `click_round` before `shrine-axe`. The UF advantage is never evaluated by the runner in the current scenario sequence. The test verifies no-crash dispatch only; UF regression coverage remains a gap. See scenarios.yml amendment note below.
