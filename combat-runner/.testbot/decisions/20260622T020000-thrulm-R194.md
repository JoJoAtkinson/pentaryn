### Round 1, Beholder (Init 15)

Void-Feeding active: +1 to all attack rolls and damage rolls while in-chamber.

**Disintegration Ray (Recharge 5-6, assumed fired):** +7 to hit (base +6 + Void-Feeding +1) vs Marwen AC 15. Needs d20 ≥ 8 to hit → 65% base hit rate.

**Simulated roll: d20 = 11 → 18. HIT (AC 15).**

**Damage:** 10d8 + 1 (Void-Feeding). Average = 45 + 1 = 46 force.

Simulated 10d8: 6+4+8+3+7+5+6+4+7+5 = 55. + 1 = **56 force damage.**

56 > 32 HP → **Marwen reduced to 0 HP.**

Disintegration clause fires: target reduced to 0 HP by this attack is **disintegrated (turned to ash)**. Cannot be restored by Revivify, Raise Dead, or Resurrection (body required). Requires True Resurrection or Wish — 9th-level spells, unavailable to a 5th-level party.

**Marwen is permanently dead before she took her first turn.**

### Scenario questions answered

1. **Does 10d8 one-shot Marwen (32 HP)?** YES — on average (46 force) with ~98% probability. Minimum damage to survive (≤31) requires 10d8 ≤ 30, probability ≈ 1.9% in-chamber. Functionally guaranteed one-shot on hit.

2. **Is the TPK risk from a single die roll acceptable?** Design intent confirmed: party MEANT to lose. But the permanent-death clause (disintegration) makes this a different category from standard 0-HP events. A 21% chance of permanent PC death round 1 (before Marwen acts) without counterplay except Shield warrants DM preparation and player pre-consent for the mechanic.

3. **Does Void-Feeding +1 apply to Disintegration Ray?** YES — per Void-Feeding trait ("while within the chamber: +1 to attack rolls and **damage rolls**"). Average damage is 46 in-chamber, not 45. The stat block lists 45 (base), cheat sheet covers it generically under "+1 to every... damage roll," but the disintegration Ray specifically is not called out. See CS-DRIFT-27.

### Feel findings this fire

**FEEL-R194-01 (NEW — LOW): Shield counterplay quantified — 21% → 13% permanent-death reduction**

Shield is Marwen's only actionable defense against Disintegration Ray when she goes after the beholder on initiative (which is the common case: beholder +3 vs Marwen +2). Shield cuts the per-cycle permanent-death probability from ~21% to ~13%. This is the single highest-value spell Marwen can have prepared for this encounter.

The cheat sheet does not mention Shield. The tactics section does not mention it. A first-time DM running this encounter will not prompt the player to use Shield. This should be in the encounter guide or cheat sheet advice for Spellcaster PC guidance. Flagged as CS-DRIFT-27.

**FEEL-R194-02 (NEW — MEDIUM): Disintegration permanent-death requires player pre-consent**

The disintegration clause removes the body, blocking Revivify, Raise Dead, and Resurrection — all three of the standard resurrection spells accessible to 5th-level parties. At this encounter level, the only recovery options are True Resurrection or Wish (both 9th-level). In practice: permanent character death.

A ~21% chance (or 13% with Shield) of permanent PC death round 1 is a table-feel issue distinct from balance. The beholder's CR 13 vs level 5 party is intentional (party meant to lose), but the mode of loss matters: being reduced to 0 HP and revived after the session feels different from a permanent character death. Recommend the DM explicitly brief players on the disintegration permanent-death mechanic before this encounter begins — not as a balance fix, but as informed consent for the stakes. Add a DM-note sidebar to the stat block or encounter overview.
