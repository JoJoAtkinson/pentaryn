### Key observation: Frightened + no-ranged-option trap (FEEL-R260-01)

Bazgar is a Battlemaster Fighter — melee primary. In the party roster as listed, he has no ranged weapon. Frightened condition means:
- Cannot willingly move toward beholder (which is 40-60 ft up and hovering)
- Disadvantage on attack rolls while beholder is in line of sight
- Combined with prone (from lair action): standing costs half movement, then he still can't advance

Bazgar's options after Round 1 Void Scream:
1. Stand up (half movement) + attack with disadvantage via Thrown Handaxe or Javelin (if he has one) → marginal
2. Stand up + Disengage + move away (waste of turn, no damage)
3. Second Wind as bonus action + Dodge → stall

None of these are satisfying fighter plays. Frightened on a melee-primary fighter with no ranged option is a "you are removed from combat" result, not a "you fight at reduced effectiveness" result.

**This is the most actionable feel issue this scenario surfaces.** The party converges (their only viable strategy), gets punished by AoE + CC, and the melee PC has no counterplay for 1-minute frightened. In a "meant to lose" encounter this is acceptable if intentional — but if Joe wants players to feel engaged even in a losing fight, Bazgar needs either a ranged weapon in the roster or Void Scream should not apply frightened to creatures who fail by 5 or less (graduated failure).
