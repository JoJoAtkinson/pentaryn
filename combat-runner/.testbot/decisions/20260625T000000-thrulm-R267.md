## Phase B — Playtest slice: `antireality-timing`

*(thrulm-rotation.json index 7 — Antireality reaction: disadvantage flip rate and economy; [BUG-F7-02 CLOSED R224])*

### Setup

- **Bazgar:** Fighter 5 (Battlemaster), HP 49, AC 18. Attack bonus +7 (assumed STR +4 + PB +3). 2 attacks per action (Extra Attack), superiority dice (d8 × 4/short rest).
- **Beholder:** HP 110, AC 17, Antireality available (reaction, once per round). Void-Feeding active in chamber.
- **Focus:** Track Antireality over Bazgar's Action across 3 rounds (6 total attacks). Antireality fires on the first attack each round (reaction resets at start of beholder's own turn).

### Hit probability baseline

| Condition | P(hit each attack) |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Bazgar normal (+7 vs AC 17, needs 10+) | 55% |
| Antireality in effect (disadvantage) | P(both rolls ≥ 10) = 0.55² ≈ 30% |
| Hit-chance reduction from Antireality | –25 percentage points per affected attack |

### Round-by-round simulation (3 rounds, 2 attacks each)

| Round | Attack | Antireality? | P(hit) | Expected hits this attack |
|-------|--------|-------------|--------|--------------------------|
| 1 | 1st (Antireality) | YES | 30% | 0.30 |
| 1 | 2nd (reaction spent) | no | 55% | 0.55 |
| 2 | 1st (Antireality) | YES | 30% | 0.30 |
| 2 | 2nd | no | 55% | 0.55 |
| 3 | 1st (Antireality) | YES | 30% | 0.30 |
| 3 | 2nd | no | 55% | 0.55 |
| **Total (3 rounds)** | | | | **2.55** |

Without Antireality: 6 × 0.55 = **3.30** expected hits.  
With Antireality (fires 3×): **2.55** expected hits.  
**Net reduction: 0.75 expected hits per 3 rounds (23% fewer hits on Bazgar's action).**

### "Flipped" hits: attacks specifically changed from hit to miss by Antireality

A "flip" occurs when: first roll ≥ 10 (would hit without disadvantage) AND second roll < 10 (the disadvantage roll that decides the outcome).

P(flip per Antireality use) = P(first ≥ 10) × P(second < 10) = 0.55 × 0.45 = 0.2475

Expected flips over 3 rounds = 3 × 0.2475 ≈ **0.74 attacks flipped from hit to miss**

At Bazgar's estimated 14–15 avg damage per hit (longsword, Dueling style, STR +4):  
Damage prevented by Antireality ≈ 0.74 × ~14.5 ≈ **~10.7 HP over 3 rounds** (out of beholder's 110 HP = ~10% total HP protected).

### Antireality economy: is it the right use of the reaction?

The beholder's only other reaction-eligible ability is nothing explicit in the stat block beyond Antireality. So Antireality is always the "correct" reaction; there is no competing reaction to weigh against it.

However, the scenario spec asks to examine WHICH attacker to target. The spec defaults to Bazgar, but Sabriel (divine martial 5) may be a better Antireality target:

| Attacker | Attack bonus | P(hit vs AC 17) | Per-hit damage (est.) | EV per-hit |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|----------------------|------------|
| Bazgar | +7 | 55% | ~14.5 (sword+Dueling) | 8.0 |
| Sabriel | +6–7 | 50–55% | ~14.5 + 1d8 (sanctified) | ~13.5 |

With a sanctified weapon, each of Sabriel's hits deals +1d8 = +4.5 avg more than Bazgar's. Antireality on Sabriel's first attack prevents ~0.25 × 18 ≈ **4.5 damage** vs ~0.25 × 14.5 ≈ **3.6 damage** for Bazgar. The beholder benefits more by firing Antireality against Sabriel. Current tactics section gives no guidance on this prioritization.

**FEEL-ANTI-R267-01 (NEW):** Antireality prevents ~10.7 avg HP over 3 rounds from a 2-attack Fighter, or ~23% reduction in Bazgar's action hits. This is mechanically sound and non-trivial (>10% of beholder HP). However, it scales with attacker damage rather than attack count — Sabriel (sanctified weapon, +1d8/hit) is a ~25% better Antireality target than Bazgar. Tactics section should name sanctified-weapon users as the Antireality priority.

### Wasted-Antireality scenario: FRIGHTENED interaction

Void Scream (recharge 6 — 16.7% chance per beholder turn) → DC 16 WIS save.  
Bazgar WIS: assume +1 (typical Fighter). P(fail) = P(d20+1 < 16) = P(d20 < 15) = 14/20 = **70%**.

If Bazgar fails: FRIGHTENED for up to 1 minute (DC 16 WIS end-of-turn save). FRIGHTENED condition imposes disadvantage on attack rolls while the beholder is in LoS.

P(Bazgar becomes frightened any given round): ~16.7% (recharge) × 70% (save fail) ≈ **11.7% per beholder turn**.

While frightened: Bazgar already has disadvantage on attacks. Antireality cannot impose additional disadvantage (5e RAW: multiple sources of disadvantage don't stack). **Antireality is completely wasted** when the beholder fires it against an already-frightened Bazgar.

Expected wasted Antireality uses per 3 rounds (given frightened can persist): modest but real. The beholder can detect this if it tracks conditions — DM should skip Antireality on Bazgar if frightened is active and redirect to Sabriel or Marwen.

**FEEL-ANTI-R267-02 (NEW):** FRIGHTENED-plus-Antireality double-disadvantage is mechanically impossible (5e stacking rules) and a wasted reaction. Tactics section and start-of-turn checklist should note: "Do not use Antireality against a target that already has disadvantage (e.g., frightened from Void Scream). Redirect to a non-frightened attacker." Especially relevant if Void Scream hits the whole party: all three PCs frightened → Antireality is wasted on any of them for the duration.

### Action Surge interaction

Bazgar (Fighter 5) has Action Surge (once/short rest). On an Action Surge turn: 4 attacks total (2 from Action, 2 from Surge).

Antireality affects attack 1 only (reaction resets only at start of beholder's own turn, not between PC's actions). Attacks 2, 3, and 4 proceed normally.

Antireality coverage on Action Surge round: 1/4 attacks affected (25%) vs normal 1/2 (50%).

Expected hits (Action Surge, with Antireality): 0.30 + 0.55 + 0.55 + 0.55 = 1.95
Expected hits (Action Surge, without Antireality): 4 × 0.55 = 2.20
Net reduction: 0.25 hits on an Action Surge round = ~3.6 damage prevented.

**FEEL-ANTI-R267-03 (NEW):** Antireality's economy is halved on Action Surge turns (1 of 4 attacks affected vs 1 of 2). If Bazgar saves Action Surge for a round when Antireality has already fired (i.e., surges after the 2nd attack), Antireality is irrelevant to the Surge attacks entirely. DM note: announce Antireality at attack announcement; Bazgar then knows the reaction is spent and may time Action Surge to minimize its impact.

### Battlemaster Precision Attack counterplay

Bazgar's Battlemaster superiority dice allow Precision Attack: add a d8 (avg 4.5) to an attack roll before or after seeing the initial d20, but before the DM determines the hit. Precision Attack can offset disadvantage by raising a low-rolled die.

Concrete interaction: Bazgar rolls disadvantage (two d20s, takes lower). If lower result is 7 (base 7+7=14, miss vs AC 17), Precision Attack adds d8 (avg 4.5) → effective roll 14+4.5=18.5, becomes a hit. This is a legal and tactically sound counterplay.

The Antireality–Precision interaction creates a minor action-economy game: the beholder fires Antireality to impose disadvantage; Bazgar may then spend a superiority die to partially negate it. Net effect: ~2.25 effective damage gain for Bazgar per Precision use on an Antireality round (saves ~1 superiority die worth of damage). This counterplay is healthy and not a balance concern — it gives Battlemaster players a meaningful micro-decision.
