**FEEL-F77-01 (NEW — Log only): SC's R1 chip damage (7) on Marwen is irrelevant to DR disintegration threshold at L5.**

SC Shard-Throw landed 7 piercing on Marwen before the beholder acted. In R2, DR hits: Marwen at 19 HP takes 45 → DISINTEGRATED regardless. The SC's R1 chip damage did not affect the DR outcome. At L5, Marwen's HP (32) is always below DR avg (45); the SC cannot "set up" a disintegration that wouldn't have occurred otherwise.

At L6+ (Marwen ~48 HP): SC chip damage (7+6 = 13 across 2 rounds) brings Marwen to 35 HP before DR. DR avg 45 > 35 → still disintegrated. Even at L6 the setup doesn't matter. DR is so high-damage (45 avg) that SC chip barely changes the threshold. The SC's real value is attrition over multiple rounds, not death-setup for the beholder.

Log-only. No design change needed.

---

**FEEL-F77-02 (NEW — Log only): SC high initiative (17) is a legitimate risk for the party — SC fires before the beholder in some runs.**

In this run, SC init 17 > beholder init 14. The SC got two Shard-Throw attacks on Marwen BEFORE the beholder acted. This means the party faces chip damage from the SC while the beholder hasn't yet committed to its action. Against Marwen (AC 15), the SC front-runs the beholder's decision on DR targeting: if SC chips Marwen to <45 HP in R1, the beholder can "confirm" DR is lethal in R2 without wasting the recharge on a target who might survive.

This is a mild feel concern: the SC is not intended to be the focus of this scenario, but high SC initiative creates a "softening" dynamic that the beholder can exploit. The SC's preemptive chip is the effective damage, not the beholder setting up — the beholder then fires the coup de grâce. The synergy is correctly tuned and does not need adjustment.

---

**FEEL-F77-03 (NEW — Log only): Lair action wasted in R3 when all targets are downed.**

At R3, all PCs are at 0 HP (Bazgar dead, Sabriel grappled at 0 HP death-saving, Marwen ash). The Lair action at init 20 has no valid targets for Unstable Ground (can't waste on downed) or Manifest Thralls (no thralls in this mini-encounter). Void Eruption could apply to Sabriel if within 20 ft. The lair action produces nothing meaningful.

This is the existing known issue (FEEL-F72 range): "Manifest Thralls is effectively useless vs AC 18+ party" — but more broadly, when the encounter has already reached TPK-stage, all three lair options become moot. The lair action is dead weight in the closing rounds.

Not a new issue categorically, but this simulation is the clearest example: **lair action fires twice in R3+, does nothing, and the fight is already decided.** If lair actions are meant to sustain pressure, consider whether the lair should have a "damage sink" option usable against 0-HP targets (e.g., a coup-de-grâce acceleration rather than Manifest Thralls THP). Joe's design call.
