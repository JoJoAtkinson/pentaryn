### The Counterspell Question

**Is Disintegration Ray (DR) counterspellable?**

The stat block entry reads: "*Ranged Spell Attack:* +6 to hit, range 120 ft." The "spell attack" keyword makes DR a spell-like ability. Under RAW 5e, Counterspell targets "a creature you can see within 60 feet of you that is casting a spell" and can interrupt any spell being cast.

- **If DR is a spell:** Marwen counters at 3rd level → automatic success (monster abilities have no "spell level"; RAW defaults to automatic success for counterspell against levelless abilities). Cost: one 3rd-level slot.
- **If DR is not a spell (ability only):** Counterspell cannot target it. Marwen has no defense. DR at avg 45 force vs AC 15: hit on d20+6 ≥ 15 → needs 9+ → 60% hit rate. On a hit: Marwen (32 HP) takes 45 avg force damage → reduced to 0 HP → **disintegrated** (no resurrection except Wish/True Resurrection).

**There is no ruling in the stat block or cheat sheet.**

### Dice-Math Playtest: Round 2 — DR fires, Marwen must decide

*(Round 1 summary: Beholder attacked Sabriel with multiattack, HP 44 → 15. Marwen cast Fireball, beholder made DEX +6 save vs DC 15 → success, half = 14 fire, beholder HP 110 → 96. Sabriel and Bazgar have dealt another ~18 damage total. Beholder at 78 HP entering round 2.)*

**Round 2, Beholder's turn:** Recharge roll for DR: d6 = 5. **DR is live.** Beholder targets Marwen (lowest HP, no cover).

- DR attack: +6 to hit vs Marwen AC 15. Need 9+. d20 = 14. 14+6 = 20 → **hits.**
- Damage: 10d8 avg 45 force. Marwen at 28 HP (took 4 from thrall last round). 28 - 45 = -17 → **0 HP**. With no HP buffer, this is a disintegration check.
- If disintegrated: Marwen is gone. No diamond dust resurrection (Revivify requires body, Raise Dead requires body; True Resurrection or Wish only).

**Marwen's only defense: Counterspell reaction.**

If Marwen burns a 3rd-level slot: automatic success (per "levelless ability" interpretation) → DR cancelled, Marwen survives.

**Expected outcome if DR is NOT counterspellable:** 60% chance of instant character death each round DR is live. DR recharges on 5-6 each beholder turn (~2/6 = 33% per turn). Expected turns until DR fires: ~3. By round 3, there's a 33% chance DR is live on any given turn → 60% hit rate × 33% recharge = ~20% chance of Marwen disintegration per beholder turn. Compounding over 4 beholder turns: ~57% cumulative character death probability.

**Result: Whether Marwen can counterspell DR changes the encounter from "extremely likely character death" to "hard but survivable."**

### Feel Analysis

**1. Unfair vs lethal?**

Disintegration with no in-stat-block counterspell ruling is the closest thing to "unfair rather than lethal" in this encounter. A PC is permanently dead (can't be raised by the party's available resources) before anyone realizes the mechanic needed a DM ruling. Even if the party loses the encounter by design, the mechanism of loss matters: unconsciousness is recoverable, disintegration is not. The absence of a ruling is a **DM guidance gap** that can turn a memorable TPK into a frustrating character retirement.

**2. Counterspell watch trigger mechanics:**

Applied to the original `aelric-counterspell-watch` scenario: the runner surfaces a reaction prompt when any spell_cast event fires, regardless of source (NPC or PC). In thrulm, this means:
- If Marwen triggers counterspell as a reaction to DR, the runner should surface the `⟳ counterspell?` prompt at the moment the beholder announces DR.
- The timing window is between the beholder "casting" DR and the attack roll resolving. The runner currently fires reaction prompts on the `damage` event (post-hit), which is **too late** for counterspell (which must be declared when "a creature begins casting a spell," not after the hit lands).

**BUG-R215-01 (see Phase C):** Counterspell window is pre-roll; runner's damage-event trigger is post-hit. This is the same timing problem as Antireality (BUG-F7-02) but for PC reactions.

**3. Recharge rotation:**

DR fired on turn 2 (d6=5) in this simulation. Against a party that survives round 1, the DR coming online in round 2 is the dominant threat. Prior simulations (R205-R214) showed the beholder rarely gets to use DR because the party usually dies from multiattack before DR recharges. This simulation (solo beholder, no minions) showed DR can fire when minions aren't soaking PC damage output. **Conclusion:** DR is only a rotation gap in mixed encounters (minions dilute the party's attention and the beholder's target priority shifts to Drain Divinity). In a solo-beholder scenario, DR is the most impactful ability in the stat block.

#### BUG-R215-01 (HIGH — timing / DM guidance gap)

**Disintegration Ray counterspellability and timing: no ruling in source docs.**

**Problem:** The stat block says "Ranged Spell Attack" for Disintegration Ray. Whether this makes DR counterspellable is a classic 5e ruling ambiguity (monster abilities vs. "casting a spell"). The stat block provides no guidance. For Marwen specifically:

- If DR is counterspellable: Marwen trades a 3rd-level slot to survive; clear exchange, good decision-point for the player.
- If DR is not counterspellable: Marwen has a ~57% cumulative character death probability over 4 beholder turns (permanent — no party-level resurrection). The DM learning this after the fact feels unfair even in an intentionally lethal encounter.

**Secondary issue:** Even if DR is counterspellable, the combat runner fires reaction prompts on the `damage` event (post-hit), which is too late for counterspell (must be declared when the creature "begins casting a spell," before the attack roll). The same timing gap exists for Antireality (BUG-F7-02). The pattern is systematic: the runner's event model triggers reactions after damage resolves, missing the pre-roll window.

**Recommended fix (stat block, 1 line):** Add to Disintegration Ray description:
> *This ability is a spell attack; it can be interrupted by Counterspell (automatic success, no spell level check required).*

**Log only — design call needed for counterspellability ruling and runner timing model.** Do not auto-fix.
