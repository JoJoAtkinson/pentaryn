### New flags found this fire — STAT-R154-01 and STAT-R154-02

#### STAT-R154-01 — Shrine-Touched Proficiency Bonus / CR mismatch (NEW)

`shrine-touched-derro.md` lists:
- **Challenge:** CR 3 (700 XP) — *not explicitly stated in the stat block; inferred from cheat sheet "Shrine-Touched (CR 3)"*
- **Proficiency Bonus:** +3 (explicitly listed in stat block)

Per 5e rules, CR 3 → PB +2, not PB +3 (PB +3 applies to CR 5–8). The listed PB +3 makes the shrine-touched mechanically equivalent to a CR 5+ creature in terms of attack/save accuracy.

**Downstream effects:**
- Shrine-Axe attack: +4 = STR+1 + PB+3. With PB+2 it should be +3. A +1 difference.
- Dex save / Con save: +5 = mod+2 + PB+3. With PB+2 they should be +4.
- Skills (Perception +1, Stealth +5): Stealth = DEX+2 + PB+3 = +5. With PB+2 would be +4.
- Ancient Resonance DC: not stat-block-derived (stated DC 14 = 8 + PB + mod requires mod+3, but best ability is DEX/CON +2; DC 14 with PB+3 = 8+3+CON2 = 13 ≠ 14). DC is effectively a flat declaration regardless.

**Disposition:** LOG ONLY. Same class of bug as BUG-PB-MISMATCH on beholder. Joe decides: lower PB to +2 (and adjust saves/attacks) OR raise CR label to 5.

#### STAT-R154-02 — Rager Taunt DC 12 formula inconsistency (NEW)

`derro-rager.md` Bonus Action: Taunt DC 12 CHA save.

Rager stats: CHA -1, PB +2 (CR 2). Standard Taunt DC formula: 8 + PB + CHA = 8+2-1 = **9**. But DC is written as **12**.

For DC 12 to be correct under standard formula: 8 + PB + ability = 12 → ability mod must be +2. Only STR (+2) or CON (+2) match. The most likely error: **CHA modifier was accidentally set as CON or STR** when authoring. DC 12 with CON +2 (8+2+2=12) or STR +2 (8+2+2=12) makes arithmetic sense.

**Impact:** DC 12 vs party CHA saves. Bazgar CHA +0: needs nat 12+ (55% fail). At correct DC 9: needs nat 9+ (70% fail). The gap is meaningful — at DC 12, Taunt is notably less reliable on high-CHA characters like Sabriel. At DC 9, it's nearly auto-fail for Bazgar.

**Disposition:** LOG ONLY. Joe decides: keep DC 12 as a design choice (the rager's madness-strength drives the taunt, using STR/CON), or correct to DC 9 (formula-correct for CHA-based ability).

### Key finding — FEEL-R154-01: FRIGHTENED condition duration lifecycle is never tested in practice (NEW)

**Summary:** The Void Scream FRIGHTENED condition has a 1-minute (10-round) duration with DC 16 WIS end-of-turn save. In this encounter at Compass Edge L5, the condition never completes a full lifecycle:

1. **Targets that fail VS at high HP are incapacitated immediately** (Marwen 32 HP → 0 in one action; Bazgar 49-33=16 HP + LA = 0). Their FRIGHTENED lasts exactly 0 turns.
2. **Targets that survive (Sabriel)** are at near-death HP and typically eliminated by round 2. Even if they make the WIS save eventually (round 3+), they don't survive long enough for the save to matter.
3. **The WIS save DC of 16** means the average non-WIS-focused PC (WIS +0 to +1) has a ~25-30% chance to clear the condition on any given turn. Expected duration before a save: ~3-4 rounds. Encounter duration: ~2-3 rounds. The condition's removal mechanic **statistically cannot resolve** before the fight ends.

**Why this matters:** The FRIGHTENED condition imposes (a) disadvantage on attack rolls vs the beholder and (b) no willful approach toward the beholder. For a PC who is incapacitated or dead, these restrictions are meaningless. The FRIGHTENED rider on Void Scream adds flavor (terrifying cosmic horror) but has no practical mechanical impact on the fight.

**Design note (log, not fix):** If Joe wants FRIGHTENED to actually affect party tactics, options include:
1. Decouple FRIGHTENED from Void Scream (make it a separate lair action or a beholder reaction, affecting one PC without the massive HP damage)
2. Reduce VS damage (e.g., 3d8 psychic = avg 13) so targets survive to experience the condition
3. Reduce DC to 13 (party can occasionally shake it mid-fight and feel a moment of relief)

### Finding — FEEL-R154-02: Shrine-Drift "drag into range" tactic not in cheat sheet or tactics section (NEW)

The beholder's LA Tentacle Lash grapples a target. Grappled targets can be dragged with beholder movement (half speed). Shrine-Drift lets the beholder move through objects/creatures. Combined:

**Tactic not documented:** After grappling Bazgar via LA Tentacle (end of Sabriel's turn), the beholder can use Shrine-Drift (bonus action) to phase toward the shrine, dragging Bazgar to within 10 feet of it. Then on the next turn, Void Scream hits Bazgar while he's in the "within 10 ft of shrine" zone → **Bazgar has disadvantage on the DC 16 WIS save.**

Neither the tactics section nor the cheat sheet mentions this combo. DMs may miss it. Bazgar going from ~25% fail-save to ~37.75% fail-save (disadvantage on DC 16 WIS+0: both rolls must miss, so ~(0.75)² = 56% fail with disadvantage... actually probability of FAILING both = chance of failing the best roll = 1 - (1-0.25)² = 1 - 0.75² = 1 - 0.5625 = 0.4375, so 44% fail rate with disadvantage vs 75% without — wait, I'm getting confused. Let me redo:

- No disadvantage: Need to roll 16+ on d20+0 → chance to PASS = 5/20 = 25%. Chance to FAIL = 75%.
- With disadvantage: Chance to PASS = (5/20)² = 6.25%. Chance to FAIL = 93.75%.

So disadvantage on this WIS save takes Bazgar from 75% likely-FRIGHTENED to 94% likely-FRIGHTENED. Negligible practical impact (he was already overwhelmingly failing). But the drag-tactic documents a real combo that DMs should know about.

**Disposition:** LOG ONLY. Add one line to beholder tactics: "If grappling a target, Shrine-Drift toward the shrine to trigger the 10-ft disadvantage zone on next Void Scream."
