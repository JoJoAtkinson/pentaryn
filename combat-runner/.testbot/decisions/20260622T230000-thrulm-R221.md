## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `lair-action-round`

**Slice:** `lair-action-round` (rotation.yml slice 5 of 8)

**Context (from rotation.yml):** Round 2, initiative count 20. Two thrall derro (HP 22, AC 14, DEX +1) are present and within 60 ft of the beholder. Bazgar is within 20 ft of the shrine. Beholder chooses one of three lair actions.

**Party state entering round 2:**
- Bazgar: ~42 HP (took ~7 damage round 1 from a thrall hand axe glancing hit)
- Sabriel: ~35 HP (closed to melee vs beholder, took ~9 from legendary tentacle)
- Marwen: 32 HP (hanging back ~45 ft)
- Beholder: ~100 HP (Sabriel landed one sanctified longsword hit, ~14 damage with +1d8)

**Thrall positions:** Both thralls within 60 ft of beholder; one is also within 20 ft of shrine (flanking Sabriel's approach path).

---

### Option A: Unstable Ground

**Target:** Bazgar (DEX save +1 vs DC 16 → need 15+; 30% save rate → 70% prone chance)

**Expected damage value:**
- Prone Bazgar: no direct damage. Beholder gets advantage on Tentacle Lash vs prone target.
- Base hit rate Tentacle vs Bazgar: +7 vs AC 18, need 11+ = 55% hit.
- With advantage (prone): P(at least one die ≥ 11) = 1 - (0.45²) = 1 - 0.2025 = 79.75%.
- Expected hit-rate improvement: +24.75%. Per legendary tentacle: 0.7 × 0.2475 × 14.5 avg damage = **+2.5 avg damage**.
- Bazgar can stand up (half move, 15 ft) on his turn, so prone only lasts until his initiative unless grappled.
- Combining with Maw (advantage vs grappled Bazgar): if beholder grappled Bazgar in round 1, adding prone multiplies pressure significantly. But in this scenario Bazgar wasn't grappled.

**Expected value: ~2.5 avg damage via improved legendary tentacle accuracy. Low-to-moderate.**

---

### Option B: Manifest Thralls

**Effect:** Up to 3 charmed derro within 60 ft gain CHA mod (= +1) THP, then immediately use their reaction to move or attack.

**THP value:** 1 THP per thrall. With first hit dealing ≥ 2 damage (guaranteed), THP is gone immediately. **Negligible.**

**Reaction attack value (2 thralls):**
- Hand Axe (Weak), +1 to hit vs AC 18 (Bazgar): need 17+, 20% hit, avg 2 (1d4) damage → **0.4 avg per thrall, 0.8 total.**
- If against Marwen (AC 15): need 14+, 35% hit, avg 2 damage → **0.7 avg per thrall, 1.4 total.**

**Note — rules conflict:** The thrall stat block explicitly states "Reactions: None. The derro cannot react independently." Manifest Thralls grants a reaction via the beholder's lair action, effectively overriding this. The word "independently" in the stat block implies beholder-directed reactions are allowed. DMs seeing "Reactions: None" on the thrall card may be confused. This is **FEEL-R221-01** (minor, log below).

**Expected value: ~1 avg damage total. Worst of the three options.**

---

### Option C: Void Eruption

**Effect:** All creatures within 20 ft of the shrine make DC 16 DEX save; 11 (2d10) force on fail, half on success.

**Targets in range:** Bazgar (within 20 ft per scenario context), Sabriel (closing to melee near altar, likely within 20 ft), and one thrall (also near shrine).

**Per target calculation (DEX +1, vs DC 16 → 30% save rate; 70% fail):**
- Expected damage per target: (0.70 × 11) + (0.30 × 5.5) = 7.7 + 1.65 = **9.35 avg**

**Party damage:** Bazgar (9.35) + Sabriel (9.35) = **18.7 avg damage**.

**Friendly-fire cost:** One thrall within 20 ft: 9.35 avg damage to own minion.
- Beholder's INT +3 and tactical awareness: beholder can recognize the trade (18.7 to party vs 9.35 to thrall). A thrall at 22 HP takes 9.35 → ~13 HP remaining. Not killed, but degraded. Still a net positive trade.

**Expected value: 18.7 avg damage to the party (before friendly-fire offset). Clear best option.**

---

### Verdict

| Option | Expected Damage to Party | Side Effects | Ranking |
|--------|--------------------------|--------------|---------|
| Void Eruption | ~18.7 avg (if 2 PCs in range) | ~9.35 friendly fire if 1 thrall in range | **1st** |
| Unstable Ground | ~2.5 (via advantage bonus) | Prone ends next turn unless grappled | 2nd |
| Manifest Thralls | ~1.0 (weak reactions) | 1 THP trivialized immediately | 3rd |

**Optimal: Void Eruption.** Every round where Bazgar and Sabriel are within 20 ft of the shrine (inevitable in melee — it's where the beholder hovers), Void Eruption deals roughly the same burst as a full legendary action. As a lair action, it's free — doesn't consume the beholder's 3 LA.

**Non-optimal: Manifest Thralls.** The 1-THP/weak-reaction combo is so underpowered that it's essentially a skipped turn. The beholder would never rationally choose it while Void Eruption is available.

### Feel Notes from Phase B

#### FEEL-R221-01 (MINOR — rules clarity)
**Manifest Thralls vs thrall "Reactions: None" conflict**
- Thrall stat block: "Reactions: None. The derro cannot react independently."
- Manifest Thralls lair action: "They can immediately use their reaction to move or make a weapon attack."
- The word "independently" makes this consistent (beholder-directed ≠ independent), but DMs reading both at the table will hesitate.
- **Needed fix:** Add parenthetical to thrall stat block: "Reactions: None (beholder lair actions may grant directed reactions; see Manifest Thralls)."
- **Not auto-fixable** (design decision clarification). Log only.

#### FEEL-R221-02 (MEDIUM — lair action monotony)
**Void Eruption is always dominant once melee closes; DM effectively auto-pilots lair actions from round 2 onward.**
- After round 1 (party closes), the lair action decision is trivially Void Eruption every time.
- At round 3-4, both Bazgar and Sabriel are reliably within 20 ft of the shrine. Void Eruption becomes a ~18.7 avg free damage burst every round.
- Unstable Ground only matters if the party is spread out (round 1 approach). Manifest Thralls never matters.
- **Result:** The lair action "decision tree" in the cheat sheet is aspirational; in practice it collapses to Void Eruption immediately and stays there.
- **Design consideration:** Manifest Thralls needs a redesign (already open) to create genuine lair-action competition. A redesign where Manifest Thralls grants the thralls a full move + attack (without +1 THP dead weight) would make it viable when thralls are in good position.

#### FEEL-R221-03 (MEDIUM — tactical lose-lose for party)
**Void Eruption specifically punishes the melee approach that's necessary to fight the beholder.**
- The beholder hovers near the altar. To attack it with sanctified weapons (Sabriel's best damage option), she must be within melee range — within 20 ft of the shrine.
- Every round she maintains this position, she takes avg 9.35 force damage from lair actions alone, before legendary actions or multiattack.
- Hanging back: beholder uses Drain Divinity (drains Sabriel's spell slots/divine abilities) and Disintegration Ray (targets Marwen).
- Closing in: Void Eruption averages 9.35/round.
- **This is "lethal not unfair"** — it matches the encounter's intent (party is meant to lose). The dual-threat structure (punished for closing AND for hanging back) creates genuine tension. However, DMs need to actively telegraph both threats early so players understand the lose-lose and feel the design intent rather than feeling cheated.
- **No fix needed. Logging as positive feel note: encounter design is working as intended.** The Void Eruption penalty for altar-proximity is the right lever.

#### FEEL-R221-04 (POSITIVE — emergent design)
**Friendly fire on thralls via Void Eruption creates a memorable table moment.**
- Beholder uses Void Eruption to damage party, incidentally hits its own thrall (9.35 avg damage to it).
- The beholder, as described, is not a mindless creature — it has INT +3 and telepathic control over thralls. It can calculate the trade and sacrifice a thrall for burst damage.
- At the table: players who realize the beholder just hurt its own minion to damage them will have a visceral "oh no" reaction to the creature's ruthlessness. This is exactly the kind of moment a CR 13 aberration boss should produce.
- **No fix needed. This is good encounter design; worth noting to DM in pre-session prep.**

#### FEEL-R221-05 (LOW — confirmation of known issue)
**Manifest Thralls lair action: 1 THP is effectively useless (confirming known issue)**
- Against a level 5 party where every hit deals ≥ 5 damage, 1 THP vanishes on the first hit. The "reaction to move or attack" rider is the actual value, but the reaction attack (hand_axe_weak, +1 to hit, 1d4 damage) is too weak to influence the encounter.
- Known issue confirmed for 9th+ fire.
