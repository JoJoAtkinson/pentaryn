### R1 Turn 1 — Beholder (init 17): Multiattack vs prone Bazgar

Per FIX-VS-R1/R2 protocol: R1 = Multiattack (not Void Scream).

**KEY INTERACTION — FEEL-F40-01:** Prone is a melee/ranged-asymmetric condition in 5e RAW:
- Melee attacks vs prone target: **advantage**
- Ranged attacks vs prone target: **disadvantage**

Tentacle Lash (melee, reach 10 ft) and Maw (melee, reach 5 ft) are both melee attacks. Unstable Ground's prone setup therefore **grants the beholder advantage on all three multiattack rolls** against Bazgar. This is additive with Void-Feeding (+1 to attack rolls in the chamber).

**Tentacle Lash vs prone Bazgar:**
- Attack: advantage + (+6 base +1 VF) = +7, advantage vs AC 18
- P(hit) without advantage: d20+7 ≥ 18 → d20 ≥ 11 → **50%**
- P(hit) with advantage: 1 - P(both miss) = 1 - (10/20)² = **75%**
- Gain from prone: +25 percentage points

TL1 result: 75% × 13 avg = **9.75 avg bludgeoning** + grapple (escape DC 16) on hit.
TL2 result: 75% × 13 avg = **9.75 avg bludgeoning** + second grapple slot on hit.

**Grapple + Prone compound state:**
If TL1 hits → Bazgar is grappled (speed 0) AND prone. With speed 0, Bazgar **cannot stand from prone** (standing requires spending half movement; speed 0 means no movement available). Bazgar is now locked: prone and unable to stand until the grapple is broken.

**Maw vs grappled prone Bazgar:**
- Grapple → Maw auto-crit rider: if Maw hits a grappled target, it is a critical hit (8d8+3, avg ~39 piercing)
- Prone → melee attack advantage: +7 with advantage vs AC 18 = **75% hit rate**
- P(Maw lands) = 75%. P(Maw crits when it lands) = 100% (grappled rider, not a natural 20 check).

Expected Maw damage: 0.75 × 39 = **29.25 avg piercing**

**Expected total R1 multiattack damage to Bazgar (prone setup):**
- TL1: 9.75
- TL2: 9.75 (regardless of TL1 outcome — separate target tracking for grapple)
- Maw: 29.25 (assuming TL1 or TL2 hit, P ≈ 94%)

**Total expected: ~48.75 avg** vs Bazgar HP 49.

Without UG-prone (TL at 50%, Maw at 50%):
- TL1: 0.50 × 13 = 6.5
- TL2: 0.50 × 13 = 6.5
- Maw: 0.50 × 39 × P(at least one TL hit) = 0.50 × 39 × 0.75 = 14.6

**Expected without prone: ~27.6 avg** — Bazgar survives R1 multiattack ~85% of the time.
**Expected with prone: ~48.75 avg** — Bazgar at 0 HP in R1 with ~80% probability.

**The lair action turns a survivable R1 multiattack into a near-certain R1 knockout.**

### Prone-advantage interaction not documented in UG tactics note

The UG tactics note (beholder-thrulm.md, Unstable Ground section) says:
> "Prone on a melee fighter: costs them movement to stand up, halves their speed, imposes disadvantage on attack rolls — sustained melee disruption."

This correctly describes the cost to the PC. It does NOT mention that prone also grants the beholder advantage on its OWN melee attacks. The DM may not realize the lair action effectively buffs the beholder's multiattack by +25pp per attack roll. This is the primary cause of the R1 near-certainty calculation above.

**FEEL-F40-01 (NEW):** Unstable Ground prone grants the beholder advantage on all melee attacks against the prone target. With Tentacle Lash (reach 10 ft) and Maw (reach 5 ft), this raises TL hit probability from 50% → 75% and Maw hit probability from 50% → 75%. Combined with the Maw auto-crit rider (grappled target), the UG→prone setup transforms an expected ~27 avg R1 damage into ~49 avg — enough to down Bazgar (HP 49) with ~80% probability. The UG tactics note should be updated to mention: "Note: prone also grants the beholder advantage on melee attacks (TL, Maw) — the lair action doubles as a multiattack accuracy boost, not just mobility disruption."
