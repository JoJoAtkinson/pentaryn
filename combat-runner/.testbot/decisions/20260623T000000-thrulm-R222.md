## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `retreat-threshold`

**Scenario context (from rotation.yml):** Beholder drops to 28 HP (below the 30-HP retreat threshold). Per tactics, it retreats toward the lower shaft and uses thralls to block pursuit. Assess mechanical viability of the retreat, dramatic feel, and whether existing actions support the escape.

---

### Party state entering the retreat turn (estimated round 4)

Estimated party state (83 HP of damage dealt to beholder to reach 28 HP):

| PC | Start HP | Estimated damage taken | Est. HP remaining |
|----|----------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Bazgar | 49 | ~24 (Void Eruption rounds 2–3 avg 9.35/ea + 1 Tentacle hit) | ~25 |
| Sabriel | 44 | ~33 (close melee + Void Eruption every round she's within 20 ft of shrine) | ~11 |
| Marwen | 32 | ~29 (Void Ray + 1 Disintegration near-miss or hit + Shield negation) | ~3 or unconscious |

**Note:** Marwen's survival to round 4 requires at least one Shield spell reaction or the beholder missing its Disintegration Ray (+7 vs AC 15 = 65% hit, avg 45 damage → ~29 expected). She is likely unconscious or severely depleted by round 4.

---

### Retreat turn mechanics

**Beholder's available actions at 28 HP:**

| Resource | Available | Used for |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| Movement (free) | 30 ft fly | Toward shaft entrance |
| Bonus Action | One of: Shrine-Drift OR Compel Thrall | Shrine-Drift (maximises distance) |
| Action | Full multiattack, or ranged attack, or Compel Thrall | One final strike before fleeing |
| Legendary Resistance (if any left) | 0–1 remaining | Hold for pursuit phase |

**Shrine-Drift as retreat tool:**
- Bonus action: move up to 30 ft, can pass through creatures and objects (as difficult terrain)
- Combined with 30 ft free movement: **60 ft total in one turn**
- Self-damage only if it ends its turn inside a creature/object — trivially avoided by ending in open corridor

**Opportunity attack trigger:**
Sabriel is likely adjacent (in melee combat). Shrine-Drift moves the beholder away from her threatened space. She gets an OA:
- +6 to hit vs AC 17: 50% hit. If sanctified weapon: avg 12 damage. Expected: 6 avg.
- Beholder at 28 HP takes 6 → 22 HP. **Still above the 20-HP "fight while fleeing" threshold**, but one more hit away from it.
- Bazgar can also OA if adjacent: +6 vs AC 17, avg ~10, expected 5. Beholder could reach ~17 HP from OAs alone.

**Both OAs connecting (P(at least one hits) = 1 - 0.5×0.5 = 75%): expected OA damage ~5.5. Beholder at 22–23 HP — within one average hit of the 20 HP threshold.**

**Conclusion on mechanics:** Shrine-Drift is a fully functional escape tool. The beholder can exit 60 ft in one turn. At ground speed 30 ft, the party cannot close the gap by running (they'd need to Dash, sacrificing their attack action). The beholder is realistically uncatchable unless the party has fly speed or teleportation.

---

### Phase transition at 20 HP

Per tactics: "If below 20 HP, it attempts to crush/disintegrate the party while fleeing."

OA damage puts the beholder potentially at 17–22 HP on the same retreat turn. **There is a real probability (roughly 40–50%) that OA damage during Shrine-Drift pushes the beholder into its 20-HP aggressive-panic mode on the very turn it tries to flee.**

This creates a dramatic inflection:
1. Beholder flees at 28 HP → Shrine-Drift through Sabriel → OA lands (50%) → 20 HP crossed → **beholder immediately switches to aggressive while fleeing mid-movement.** It now fires Disintegration Ray (if recharged) at Marwen on its action before retreating.
2. OA misses → beholder exits to 60 ft away, beyond melee range, enters pure retreat.

The two-phase retreat (flee → fight-while-fleeing) has genuine dramatic potential IF the thresholds are clarified. However:

**STAT-R166-01 (confirmed HIGH priority this fire):** The stat block reads:
- "If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower shaft…"
- "If below 20 HP, it attempts to crush/disintegrate the party while fleeing."

Both sentences are presented as independent conditions. A DM running this for the first time reads them as: once below 20 HP, BOTH conditions trigger simultaneously (flee AND fight). The intent appears to be sequential phases, but the text doesn't say "then, once below 20 HP." This is ambiguous at the table.

**Fix needed (human decision, not auto-fix):** Clarify as sequential: "If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats… Once below 20 HP during the retreat, it switches to fighting while fleeing — unleashing its strongest attacks opportunistically as it continues toward the shaft."

### Thrall blockers — viability assessment

Beholder uses Compel Thrall (BA) in a later round to position thrall derro to block the corridor:

**Thrall blocking effectiveness:**
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Thrall HP | 22 |
| AC | 14 |
| Attack (vs Bazgar AC 18) | +1 to hit = 20% hit, 1d4 = 2 avg → 0.4 avg damage |
| Bazgar attacks vs thrall (AC 14) | +6, 70% hit, ~10 avg = 7 avg per attack |
| Rounds to kill one thrall | ≤1 round (Bazgar deals 14 avg in one round, thrall has 22 HP — 2-hit kill or 1 crit) |

**Assessment:** A single thrall as blocker delays pursuit by 1 round maximum. Two thralls in a narrow corridor: 2 rounds. This buys the beholder approximately 60–120 ft of separation during the retreat — enough to reach the lower shaft entrance.

**Feel issue:** The thrall blockers FUNCTION as a delay mechanism, but they're so fragile that the delay is trivially overcome. The scene at the table: "Beholder flees, thralls shuffle in front of you. Bazgar cuts through in 2 swings. Beholder is gone." That's anticlimactic. The existing Manifest Thralls lair action (THP + reaction attack) doesn't add meaningful threat here either.

**Design consideration (FEEL-R222-01):** If the beholder escapes this way routinely, players will feel cheated — the boss just ran away. Consider: the thralls should have at least one high-pressure trait during a block scenario (e.g., the beholder grants them temporary immunity or they detonate psychically when killed). Alternatively, the retreat into the lower shaft should reveal something that makes the escape feel like a Pyrrhic victory for the beholder (it ran toward the thing it fears).

---

### Lower shaft emergent tension

The stat block notes: "Something moves down there — not the beholder, but something worse." The beholder retreats toward this place.

At the table, smart players who notice the beholder's fear of the shaft can weaponize this:
- Force the beholder deeper into the shaft (push it toward the thing it fears)
- Block the shaft entrance to trap the beholder in the main chamber

This emergent dynamic (players exploiting the boss's fear to control its retreat path) is excellent encounter design. It rewards players who read the environment and creates genuine tactical decisions beyond "kill the beholder."

**No fix needed.** This is good design — worth noting to the DM in pre-session prep.

### Legendary action economy during retreat

Even while retreating, the beholder retains 3 LA per round (refreshed at start of its turn). On the retreat turn and subsequent turns:

| LA spend | Target | Expected damage |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| Tentacle (1 LA) after Sabriel's pursuit turn | Sabriel (AC 19, +7 hit, 45% hit) | 0.45 × 14.5 = 6.5 avg |
| Void Ray (2 LA) after Bazgar's pursuit turn | Bazgar (AC 18, DC 16 DEX +1, 70% fail) | 0.70×23 + 0.30×11.5 = 19.5 avg |
| Total expected LA damage while retreating | — | **~26 avg/round** |

The beholder deals 26 avg damage per round in LA alone while retreating at full speed. At Sabriel's 11 HP, a single Void Ray during pursuit (targeting her instead of Bazgar: DC 16 vs DEX +1, 19.5 avg) likely knocks her unconscious. **The "retreat" is not a combat pause — the party takes near-full damage pressure while chasing.**

This inverts the expected dynamic (fleeing boss = easier fight) and creates sustained lethality. The encounter correctly doesn't end at the retreat threshold; it accelerates.

#### FEEL-R222-01 (MEDIUM — retreat feel / thrall blocker weakness)
**Thrall blockers trivially cleared in ≤1 round per thrall by Bazgar.** The retreat scene risks being anticlimactic: beholder Shrine-Drifts away while Bazgar cuts through 1-2 thralls in one round. The separation is real but the resistance is negligible. **Not auto-fixable** — design decision. Log only.

#### FEEL-R222-02 (HIGH — STAT-R166-01 confirmed high priority)
**The 30-HP flee threshold and 20-HP fight-while-fleeing threshold are presented as independent conditions, not sequential phases.** In play, OA damage from Shrine-Drift movement can trigger the 20-HP threshold on the same turn the beholder first tries to flee (~40-50% probability), causing both behaviors to activate simultaneously. The text needs a sequential clarifier: the 20-HP phase should be explicitly described as a sub-phase within the retreat, not a separate trigger. **STAT-R166-01 has been open since fire #33. This fire confirms the ambiguity causes genuine confusion at the table and should be prioritised for human decision.**

#### FEEL-R222-03 (POSITIVE — LA economy during retreat)
**The beholder deals ~26 avg LA damage per round while retreating at full speed.** The "retreat" doesn't reduce party pressure — it sustains or increases it. Sabriel at ~11 HP is likely knocked unconscious by a Void Ray LA during the first pursuit round. This is *lethal not unfair*: the party who pushed the beholder to 28 HP has already achieved a major milestone, but they pay for the pursuit. Good design — worth telling the DM this explicitly in pre-session notes so they don't pull punches during the retreat.

#### FEEL-R222-04 (POSITIVE — lower shaft emergent tension)
**The beholder retreating toward the thing it fears creates a player-exploitable lever.** If the party realizes the beholder avoids the lower shaft willingly, they can try to herd it — blocking the escape route forces a trapped beholder to fight to the death (more desperate, more lethal) vs. cornering it against the shaft entrance (forces it into the feared territory). This emergent tactical decision rewards players who engage with the lore. Worth flagging in the encounter notes / DM prep.
