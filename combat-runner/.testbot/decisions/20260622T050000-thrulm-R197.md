```json
{

  "verbs": ["drain", "drain divinity", "sap", "steal slot", "devour faith"],
```

**FINDING-R197-B (NEW): Round 1 altitude locks Drain Divinity completely.**

Beholder hovers 40-60 ft above the altar floor. Drain Divinity requires target within 30 ft.

Geometry: Sabriel on the floor, horizontally ~5 ft from directly below the beholder = distance ≈ √(5² + 50²) ≈ 50 ft. NOT within 30 ft. For DD to reach Sabriel, the beholder must descend to ≤30 ft altitude, which means: if Sabriel is directly below, beholder must be at ≤30 ft altitude. At 30 ft altitude, Bazgar (on the ground 30 ft away horizontally after standing and moving) is at distance √(30² + 30²) ≈ 42 ft — no melee reach. But Bazgar could still close to 5 ft horizontal = √(5² + 30²) ≈ 30.4 ft, just barely out of Tentacle reach (10 ft). However, at 25 ft altitude with Sabriel directly below: distance = 25 ft — within DD range.

**Verdict:** In Round 1, the beholder cannot use Drain Divinity without descending below 30 ft. At 25-30 ft altitude, Bazgar's melee range (Tentacle Lash reach 10 ft, but Bazgar has no reach weapon) means Bazgar would still need to be at 10 ft horizontal to attack. Realistically, the beholder can hover at 25 ft and safely use DD on Sabriel if Bazgar is on the far side of the chamber. This reinforces FEEL-R193-01 but escalates it: DD requires active altitude management that the tactics section does not address.

**FINDING-R197-C (NEW): Pack Tactics Voice + Void Ray LA creates a hidden synergy spike.**

Shardcaller's Pack Tactics Voice (passive: hit ally → target disadvantage on next save) triggers without any action cost. If the Shardcaller hits in the same initiative order window, the beholder's LA Void Ray benefits from the disadvantaged save — boosting expected damage from 18.7 to 21.0 and failure rate from 70% to 91%. This combo is unmentioned in the cheat sheet or tactics sections. It's not a bug (it's RAW), but DMs running the beholder intelligently should sequence: Shardcaller attacks first, THEN beholder uses Void Ray LA.

This also means the Shardcaller's early initiative (+2) is doubly valuable: it acts before most PCs AND sets up beholder LAs for that round's PC turns. The Shardcaller's positioning (40-60 ft back, attacks before Bazgar acts) is architecturally designed around this synergy but the synergy isn't documented anywhere.

### Feel findings this fire

**FEEL-R197-01 (NEW — HIGH): Bazgar is architecturally bypassed in Round 1.**

Bazgar's role in Round 1:
- Proned by lair action (70% chance)
- Stands, burns half movement (15 ft)
- Beholder is at 40-60 ft altitude — completely out of reach
- Bazgar's only combat targets in Round 1 are floor-level derro

Meanwhile, the beholder focuses Disintegration Ray on Marwen (P≈65% permanent death Round 1) and LA Void Ray on Sabriel. Bazgar watches his party die from the floor while killing a Shrine-Touched.

This isn't "unfair" in the balance sense (the party is meant to lose), but it is **unsatisfying design**. The encounter's tankiest, most durable PC (Bazgar, AC 18) is completely bypassed architecturally in the round that matters most. The party's deaths in Round 1 happen without any meaningful intervention from the fighter.

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to tactics: "Round 1: The beholder hovers above Bazgar's reach intentionally — the fighter cannot engage until Round 2 at earliest. Design note: this creates a 'helpless tank' feel that may need a brief lair-action option to pull Bazgar into the air OR a thrall to occupy the fighter early." Alternative: give Bazgar a thrown weapon or javelin as a party item.

**FEEL-R197-02 (NEW — MEDIUM): Init-20 lair action Unstable Ground has 70% success rate vs Bazgar specifically.**

Bazgar DEX +1: DC 16 DEX save needs 15+ → 30% success → 70% proned. This is the highest prone rate possible for this DC against a party member (Sabriel DEX +1 = same 70%; Marwen DEX +2 = 65%). The lair action reliably disadvantages the tankiest party member before they act, then the Rager charges in (initiative +0, acts later) with advantage on attacks.

The "lair action into Rager advantage" combo is potent but undocumented. Cheat sheet doesn't note this sequence.

**FEEL-R197-03 (NEW — MEDIUM): Drain Divinity is structurally inaccessible in Round 1 (extends FEEL-R193-01).**

Prior fires noted "DD requires 30 ft range vs 40-60 ft hover." This fire's Round 1 opener sim confirms: DD never fires in Round 1 because the beholder has better targets (Marwen via DR, Sabriel via LA Void Ray) and the altitude geometry makes DD inaccessible without tactical descent. DD is a 3-LA spend with conditional fire — it requires the beholder to (1) descend to ≤30 ft altitude, (2) have Sabriel within 30 ft, (3) choose to spend all 3 LAs instead of Void Ray+Move. All three conditions must align simultaneously. In practice, DD fires only if the DM consciously engineers a setup. **Recommendation:** Increase DD range to 60 ft, or reduce cost to 2 LA. This would make it viable at combat altitude without requiring descent.

**FEEL-R197-04 (NEW — LOW): Shardcaller Pack Tactics Voice + beholder Void Ray LA undocumented synergy.**

Shardcaller's Pack Tactics Voice (passive) gives a hit → target disadvantage on next save. If Shardcaller hits before a beholder LA Void Ray fires (Shardcaller init 14, beholder LA fires at end of Shardcaller's turn), the LA Void Ray benefits from the disadvantaged DEX save — boosting failure rate from 70% to 91% and expected damage from 18.7 to 21.0 force. This is a meaningful synergy that the DM should actively use but is not mentioned in the cheat sheet or tactics.
