### Round 1 — Lair action fires first

**Lair action (init 20):**

- Context: PCs have just entered the chamber. All PCs are within 60 ft of the beholder's position (beholder hovers near altar). Bazgar is front-line, ~20–30 ft from altar.
- Best lair option: **Unstable Ground** on Bazgar (the most threatening melee attacker). DC 16 Dex. Bazgar DEX −1 → d20−1 vs 16, needs 17+ to save → **85% fail**.
  - Bazgar fails. **PRONE.** −15 ft of speed to stand, disadvantage on attacks until he stands.
- Alternative: Void Eruption (DC 16 Dex, 2d10 force in 20-ft shrine radius) — party is entering and unlikely to be within 20 ft of altar yet. Skip.
- Alternative: Manifest Thralls — 1 THP on charmed derro vs AC 18+: effectively useless (carry-forward DD-MT). Skip.

**Beholder (init 19) — first actual turn:**

- PCs at entrance, ~40–60 ft from altar. Tentacle reach = 10 ft. No melee targets in range.
- FEEL-F16-01 applies: Multiattack is physically impossible. Only viable damaging action is **Disintegration Ray** (120-ft range).
- Target decision: Marwen (AC 15, 32 HP) vs Sabriel (AC 19, 44 HP, sanctified weapon = higher priority for elimination).
  - DR vs Marwen: +7 vs AC 15 (Void-Feeding +1 at shrine). Hits on 8+. P(hit) = **65%**.
  - DR vs Sabriel: +7 vs AC 19. Hits on 12+. P(hit) = **45%**.
  - Tactics: "Prioritizes disintegration of spellcasters." Marwen is the correct primary target.
- **Disintegration Ray vs Marwen:** Roll 14 → 14+7 = **21. HIT (21 ≥ 15).**

### Damage resolution: 10d8 force

- Average: 45. Minimum: 10. Maximum: 80.
- Marwen HP: 32. 

**Does any hit one-shot Marwen?**

- 10d8 minimum = 10 < 32. So a low roll survives.
- P(10d8 ≥ 32) = ? Using normal approximation: mean = 45, σ = √52.5 ≈ 7.25. P(X ≥ 32) = P(Z ≥ (32−45)/7.25) = P(Z ≥ −1.79) ≈ **96.3%**.
- P(10d8 < 32) ≈ **3.7%** — Marwen survives the hit (HP remaining: 1–22).

**For this simulation:** Roll 10d8 → average result 45. **45 > 32. Marwen at −13 HP. DISINTEGRATED.**

- Marwen: body turns to ash. No Revivify (needs body alive within 1 min). No Raise Dead (needs intact body). No Healing Word. Permanent character death.
- Resources to reverse: True Resurrection (9th-level spell, inaccessible at level 5), Wish (same). **No practical recovery available to a level-5 party.**

### NEW FINDING — FEEL-F17-01: DR minimum (10) creates a non-zero Marwen-survives window (~2.4% per R1)

The rotation slice frames this as a yes/no question: "If hit: ~45 damage. Does it one-shot?" The answer is "almost always but not always." A low-end roll (10d8 = 10–31) is a ~3.7% chance on any hit, producing a scenario where:

- DR hits Marwen (costs beholder its action + a recharge)
- Marwen survives at 1–22 HP (low rolls: e.g. 10d8 = 26 → Marwen at 6 HP)
- **No disintegration** — Marwen is not at 0 HP, so the disintegration rider does not trigger
- Marwen is injured but alive, with full spell slots and actions

This is a rare but table-meaningful outcome: the "signature kill" attempt fails dramatically (the void-creature's opening gambit lands but the wizard survives). At the table, P(survives | hit) ≈ 4% is effectively "nat-2 territory" but over multiple fires, this will happen roughly once every 14 times the beholder wins initiative.

**DM note needed:** When DR hits but rolls low, Marwen is *not* disintegrated. The disintegration rider requires that the damage "reduces the target to 0 hit points." A hit that leaves Marwen at 1 HP is a harrowing near-miss — DM should narrate this distinctly from a full disintegration.

### NEW FINDING — FEEL-F17-02: Permanent character death from pre-action single die roll — confirm quantification

Carrying FEEL-F9-01 forward with new precision:

**P(Marwen permanently disintegrated before acting, when beholder wins initiative) ≈ 62.6%.**

This is the encounter's sharpest player-experience issue. The DR is:
1. Fired before Marwen has taken any action
2. Irrecoverable for a level-5 party
3. Nearly certain to one-shot once it hits

Against the encounter's design intent ("party is meant to lose"), this is technically fine — but "losing" as "fighting valiantly and being overwhelmed" is very different from "Marwen walks into the room, never acts, and is ash."

**Recommendation for tactics text (log only):**

> *"DM note: When the beholder wins initiative and fires DR in R1, Marwen's player will be permanently eliminated from the game before taking a single turn. If running this encounter as a 'memorable loss with full engagement' (rather than a pure lethality test), consider using Void Scream or Multiattack R1 and holding DR for R2–R3 after all players have had a turn. The lethality ceiling is the same; the player experience is not."*

### NEW FINDING — FEEL-F17-03: DR recharge R2–R3 creates undocumented action-choice conflict

After DR fires in R1 (Marwen disintegrated), the beholder descends to melee range in R2. DR may recharge in R2 (30% chance). When it does, the beholder faces a new decision not covered by the tactics note:

| Option | Expected damage | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Fire DR vs Sabriel (R2, adjacent) | +7 vs AC 19 = 45% hit × ~45 = ~20 exp damage | Sabriel 44 HP → unlikely one-shot (P(10d8≥44) ≈ P(Z≥−0.14) ≈ 56%; P(disintegrate) = 45% × 56% ≈ 25%) |
| Multiattack vs Bazgar (grapple + maw) | ~2 TL + Maw = expected ~37 damage | Bazgar likely downed (49 HP → 12 expected remaining if survived R1 UG) |

**In R2 with Bazgar grappled:** Multiattack with Maw auto-crit (~37 damage, vs Bazgar's ~49 HP if unharmed or ~34 if took R1 lair UG damage) is dominant — it downs the fighter. DR would kill Sabriel only 25% of the time.

**In R2 with Bazgar already down:** DR vs Sabriel (25% disintegrate) vs Multiattack vs Sabriel (no grapple auto-crit, lower damage). DR slightly better.

**Finding:** The tactics note says "Against Casters: Prioritizes disintegration" but the note doesn't clarify whether this applies when the beholder is already in melee range with a grapple target available. In practice, Multiattack + Maw-crit is superior in most R2 scenarios. DR should be held for a turn where a squishy PC is not in melee — or when Sabriel is isolated.
