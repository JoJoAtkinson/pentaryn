### Key observations

**1. Shrine-Touched dies in Round 1.** Against a focused level-5 party (avg damage ~22–30/round combined), CR 3 HP 45 is not enough to survive. This is expected for a minion-tier creature vs level-5. The encounter design likely intends multiple shrine-touched + beholder support to make them viable. Solo is a test scenario only.

**2. Oath-Breaking Retaliation fires once (not twice) in R1 in this initiative order.** Shrine-Touched acts before Sabriel and Bazgar, so its reaction doesn't reset again before the end of the round. Only one reaction fires (vs Sabriel, with Unstable Form advantage). The scenario spec's `expect_watch` for `oath_breaking_retaliation` will fire correctly — trigger is well-matched to the `-12` input.

**3. Unstable Form advantage is meaningful and working correctly.** The +advantage from 22 damage in Sabriel's attack boosted the retaliation hit chance vs AC 19 from 30% (needs 15+) to 51% (1−0.7²). Converted a near-miss into a hit (rolled 15 of pair 8/15). Without Unstable Form, that attack misses. The mechanical loop is: party hits hard → shrine-touched retaliates with advantage → higher expected damage to party. This is a satisfying non-linear escalation that makes the creature feel more dangerous when hurt.

**4. Ancient Resonance did not fire** (rolled 3 on recharge). This confirms the dead-weight finding — with a 17% recharge chance and only 1 turn to fire, it statistically misses most encounters. See FEEL-R119-02 below.

**5. BUG-R119-REACT-01 is functionally significant.** `reaction_kind: "buff"` means when the trigger fires in the GUI, it likely surfaces as a buff/defense chip rather than an attack roll chip. The DM may see an AC or THP prompt instead of a to-hit roll, masking the attack. Moderate priority — the reaction fires correctly mechanically (DM can override) but breaks the intended UX flow.

**6. BUG-R119-RESONANCE-RIDER impact.** In the rare case Ancient Resonance fires, the GUI will only output 2d10 necrotic — the 1d4 psychic rider is lost. At DC 14 DEX, average damage shortfall vs a failed save: 2 (1d4 avg 2.5). Small but not zero.
