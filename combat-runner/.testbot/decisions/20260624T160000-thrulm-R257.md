### Phase B findings

**FEEL-R257-01: Grapple-snowball confirmed.**

On the beholder's first action, if both Tentacle Lashes hit Marwen, she is grappled + likely at 0
HP. An unconscious, grappled target at 0 HP is trivially finished on the beholder's second turn:
three auto-crits (attacks against unconscious within 5 ft are auto-crits) deal 2 failed death
saves each. Before Sabriel acts on beholder's second turn, Marwen has accumulated 6 failed death
saves — dead. The only interrupt is Sabriel spending Round 1 healing instead of attacking.

**The grapple-snowball feel:** Mechanically sound, but the player experience of Marwen is "hit,
go to 0, grappled, auto-critted to death" with one turn of window for rescue. This feels
*extremely* lethal rather than *meaningfully* lethal — there's nothing Marwen can do.

**FEEL-R257-02: No party action before beholder's full turn.**

With beholder winning initiative at 21 and lair action at 20, the party has zero actions before
~87 damage expected lands. Party has no interrupt option at level 5 (no Counterspell vs lair
actions, no Shield vs beholder multiattack, no Legendary Resistance equivalent). The opening round
is entirely the beholder's — this is probably correct for the "meant to lose" intent but worth
confirming Joe is comfortable with a situation where the party acts with Marwen already at 0 HP.

**FEEL-R257-03: Drain Divinity never fires in Round 1.**

Drain Divinity (30 ft range) requires beholder to close within 30 ft of a target. From 40-60 ft
hover altitude, it cannot use Drain Divinity without descending. In Round 1, the multiattack is
always better. Drain Divinity only fires later (Round 2+ when beholder descends). This is
mechanically correct but the "degenerate Drain Divinity" concern from prior fires applies from
Round 2 onwards, not Round 1.
