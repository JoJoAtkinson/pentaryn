### New findings

**FEEL-F54-01 (NEW): CT is tactically dominated by SD in every in-combat scenario**

The CT vs SD decision tree consistently resolves to SD. Across the simulated R2 slice:

| Bonus action | Immediate contribution | 2-turn setup value | Optimal? |
|---|---|---|---|
| CT | ~2.4 expected damage via thrall (BUG-A31-08 inflates to ~4.8 by erroneously using Dagger stats) | Thrall adjacent to target for continued weak attacks | No |
| SD | 0 immediate | ~43 avg psychic damage setup for VS next turn | Yes |

CT's contribution is below the threshold of noise (2.4 expected damage < Marwen's HP by 13×). SD enables VS which is the beholder's single highest-AoE output ability. SD is the dominant choice unless:
1. The beholder has no VS to set up (VS recharged this round and already fired)
2. All thralls are dead (CT is literally useless then)
3. The beholder wants to maintain a grapple it would break by moving

In all three edge cases, CT is still suboptimal (no VS → SD for DR range optimization; no thralls → CT does nothing).

**Root cause: CT relies on thralls, thralls are too weak.** A directed thrall move generates ~2 HP/round of expected pressure against AC 15+. The lair action Manifest Thralls (init 20) already gives thralls a free reaction attack each round without costing the beholder a bonus action. CT is thus strictly worse than the lair action even when it fires.

**Recommendation (log only — design call):** Two options for Joe:
1. Replace CT with a combat-relevant bonus action (e.g., "Void Pulse: one creature within 30 ft makes a DC 14 CON save or is stunned until start of beholder's next turn — 1/rest"). This would create a meaningful bonus action alternative to SD.
2. Remove CT entirely and add "the beholder directs thralls telepathically as a free action" to the Beholder-Thralled trait in Thrall Derro's stat block. This is more consistent with the lore ("obeys wordlessly") and removes the dead-weight mechanic.

**FEEL-F54-02 (NEW): CT/SD Ba conflict scenario reveals Shrine-Drift has no OA-avoidance clause**

The scenario prelude notes: *"Shrine-Drift does NOT have a no-OA clause (unlike Driven Escape / Tactical Retreat). DM must Disengage (action) first to avoid OAs on a retreat."*

In the R2 simulation, when the beholder uses SD to close toward Marwen (moving from altar toward entrance), it passes through Sabriel's space. SD rules say: "can move through creatures and objects as difficult terrain; takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside." The beholder can pass THROUGH Sabriel without ending inside her — no force damage. However, the beholder does NOT avoid opportunity attacks via SD (no "doesn't provoke OAs" clause).

Sabriel has a reaction available → she can strike as the beholder moves past her via SD. This is correct RAW, but the tactics section does not mention that SD provokes OAs when it exits a creature's melee range. A beholder using SD to reposition while Sabriel is adjacent will provoke Sabriel's OA.

**Tactical correction note needed in .md:** When using SD while adjacent to a melee combatant, the beholder should either (a) Disengage as its action (not available on a multiattack turn), (b) accept the OA, or (c) use standard fly movement to reposition (also provokes OA unless it disengages first). The only OA-safe repositioning is the Move legendary action (which also provokes OA — the Move LA just repositions at the cost of 1 LA, not as a Disengage). The only truly OA-safe beholder repositioning is using standard flight to leave without SD and being outside melee range at the start of the move.

This is a tactics clarity gap; no stats change needed. Log only.
