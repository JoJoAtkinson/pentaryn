## Phase B — Simulation: `retreat-threshold`

> Scenario: Beholder drops to 28 HP (below 30 HP retreat threshold). Per tactics: retreats
> toward lower shaft, using thralls to block. Does the retreat feel dramatic or anticlimactic?
> Does Shrine-Drift work as an escape tool?

### Context: Reaching 28 HP

By the time the beholder reaches 28 HP, assuming typical play:

Round 1 — Beholder multiattack vs Marwen (AC 15, HP 32): +9 to-hit, hits on 6+ (80%).
- Tentacle 1: E[dmg] = 0.80 × 13 = 10.4 (+ grapple DC 16)
- Tentacle 2: E[dmg] = 0.80 × 13 = 10.4 (+ grapple DC 16)
- Maw: E[dmg] = 0.80 × 21 = 16.8
- Expected round 1 vs Marwen: **37.6 HP** — Marwen is almost certainly at 0 HP after round 1. P(all 3 miss) = 0.8%.

Round 2–3 — Beholder focuses Bazgar/Sabriel and uses Void Ray LA. Party deals damage:
- Bazgar: 2 attacks at +7 vs AC 17, hits on 10+ (55%). Avg damage per hit ~9 (longsword + STR). 2 attacks: E[dmg] = 2 × 0.55 × 9 = 9.9/round.
- Sabriel: Similar. Plus potential Divine Smite (2d8 added = +9 avg). E[dmg] ~12–18/round with smites.
- Total party damage at round 3: ~60–80 HP if all resources engaged.

**The beholder (110 HP) reaches 28 HP after roughly 82 HP of party damage — plausible in round 3–4.**

**State at retreat trigger:**
- Marwen: likely dead (disintegrated) or incapacitated.
- Thrall derro (22 HP, AC 14): dead. Bazgar's two attacks at +7 hit on 8+ (65%), avg 9 each → E[dmg] = 0.65 × 9 × 2 = 11.7/round. Any thrall that started combat dies in round 2 at latest.
- Sabriel: likely used 2–3 spell slots for smites; 20–35 HP remaining.
- Bazgar: took some tentacle lash hits (E[dmg] ~10–13/round) but still up; likely 20–35 HP remaining.

### Retreat Mechanics Analysis

**Beholder's options on the retreat turn (28 HP, fresh LA budget of 3):**

1. **Bonus Action: Shrine-Drift** (30 ft, passes through creatures as difficult terrain).
   - "Through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain" = each 5 ft of creature-occupied space costs 10 ft of movement. A Medium creature in the path costs 10 ft to pass through. Two PCs in a line cost 20 ft. With 30 ft, the beholder clears ~15 ft of occupied space + 10 ft beyond = 25 ft net displacement. Passes through both melee combatants.
   - **Critical mechanic: the beholder physically passes THROUGH the party.** Stone ripples visually. Highly dramatic.
   - The "takes 5(1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside a creature" clause only applies if it STOPS inside — it can pass through freely.

2. **Main Action: Fly 30 ft** (using base fly speed in the same turn as Shrine-Drift).
   - Total movement on beholder's turn: up to **60 ft** from starting position, phasing through all obstacles.

3. **Legendary Actions — Move (1 LA)** at end of each enemy turn:
   - 3 LAs × LA Move (30 ft each) = **+90 ft** between turns if all 3 spent on movement.
   - Combined: 60 ft (own turn) + 90 ft (LA Moves) = **150 ft maximum retreat distance in one round.**
   - Even conservative (0 LAs spent on Move, just own-turn movement): 60 ft is sufficient to reach the lower shaft entrance from anywhere in the main vault.

**Verdict: MECHANICALLY SOUND.** The beholder has reliable escape tools in Shrine-Drift + fly speed. The retreat is tactically viable even with no LAs available.

### Feel Analysis

**FEEL-R314-01 — Thrall blocker strategy is stale at 28 HP**

Tactics text: "retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit."

By the time the beholder reaches 28 HP (round 3–4), all thrall derro (22 HP, AC 14) are typically dead. Thralls take ~12 expected damage per round from Bazgar alone — they die in 2 rounds. No fresh thralls can be created (Clay-Shaping takes 1 minute). Manifest Thralls lair action grants only 1 THP + a reaction move — doesn't create new thralls.

**The blocking strategy is unreliable without at least 1 surviving thrall.** At this HP threshold, the beholder must retreat without a screen.

Recommend: update tactics line to "retreats toward lower shaft; if any thralls survive, Compel Thrall directs them to block pursuit" — acknowledges the conditional nature.

**FEEL-R314-02 — Retreat trigger ambiguity with THP from Drain Divinity**

Drain Divinity generates THP equal to twice the drained spell level. Against Sabriel with 3rd-level slots, Drain Divinity grants 6 THP per use. If used twice in round 2–3, the beholder could be at 28 current HP + 12 THP. The retreat trigger is stated as "below 30 HP" without clarifying current HP vs. effective HP.

A beholder at 28 current HP + 12 THP has 40 effective HP, feels healthy, and should NOT retreat. But the trigger reads ambiguously. At the table: the DM might trigger the retreat when current HP hits 28, even if THP is active — incorrect behavior.

Recommend: "retreats when **current HP** (not counting temporary hit points) drops below 30."

**FEEL-R314-03 — Lower shaft contradiction (new)**

`beholder-thrulm.md` weaknesses section states: "The deeper shaft: It cannot go deeper into the lower chamber willingly; something down there scares even this creature."

`beholder-thrulm.md` tactics section states: "If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower shaft."

These directly contradict. The beholder is simultaneously described as unwilling to enter the lower shaft AND retreating toward it under pressure.

The `_overview.md` "Potential Endings" section also lists: "Drive the beholder deeper: It retreats to the lower shaft; the problem is delayed, not solved" — suggesting the retreat is intentional, not just a typo.

Options (Joe's call):
1. Change weaknesses text: "It avoids the lower shaft under normal circumstances, but desperation overrides fear." Keeps the retreat tactic.
2. Change tactics text: "retreats toward the shaft entrance without descending; blocks the entrance with thralls." Creates a different dramatic beat (beholder cornered at the shaft, unwilling to go further).
3. Keep the contradiction and use it as narrative tension: the beholder retreating toward something it fears is itself a signal to the party that the lower shaft contains something worse.

**Option 3 may be the most interesting at the table, but DMs need an explicit note that the contradiction is intentional.** No auto-fix; Joe's call.

**FEEL-R314-04 — Shrine-Drift as escape: dramatically strong, DM timing note needed**

Shrine-Drift allowing the beholder to phase through the party is thematically perfect and mechanically sound. The stone ripple visual cue (from the Antireality description) makes it identifiable. However, the first time a DM uses Shrine-Drift to escape through both melee fighters simultaneously, it may feel unexpected to players.

The checklist doesn't mention Shrine-Drift as an escape option (it's listed only under "Bonus Actions" in the stat block). The cheat sheet doesn't flag it under the beholder's retreat triggers.

Recommend: Add to the cheat sheet's "When Beholder Acts" section: "RETREAT (below 30 HP): BA Shrine-Drift + fly 30 ft = 60 ft escape in one turn, phasing through all combatants."

**Shrine-Drift mechanics edge case:** If the beholder is grappled (a PC with Athletics/Acrobatics could grapple it if within melee range), the grapple reduces fly speed to 0. Shrine-Drift is movement, so a grappled beholder cannot use it either. The beholder must first break the grapple (action: Athletics/Acrobatics vs DC 16, or Antireality on the original grapple attack, or Legendary Resistance). This creates a legitimate PC counter to the retreat. Nothing in the cheat sheet notes this as an option. Recommend: "Grapple counters Shrine-Drift escape — PCs with Athletics/Acrobatics can pin the beholder pre-retreat."
