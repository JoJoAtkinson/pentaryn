## Phase B — Generative Playtest Slice

**Slice:** `disintegration-threat` (rotation.yml index 1, counter 137 % 8 = 1)  
**Premise:** Assume Disintegration Ray available on turn 1. Beholder targets Marwen (32 HP, AC 15). Evaluate threat ceiling and whether the permanent-death mechanic is acceptable at this party level.

---

### Clarification: Ray is always available on round 1

Per 5e recharge rules: a "Recharge 5–6" ability starts the encounter **recharged** (available). The beholder enters combat with Disintegration Ray loaded. The "assume recharge" framing in the scenario description is technically always true for round 1 — this is not a 33% scenario, it is a 100% scenario for the opening turn.

After use, the Ray needs 5–6 on a d6 (33% per beholder turn) to recharge.

---

### Simulation: Round 1 Disintegration Ray vs Marwen

**Attack roll:** +7 (in-chamber) vs Marwen AC 15. Needs d20 ≥ 8. P(hit) = 13/20 = **65%**.

**Damage:** 10d8 force. Mean = 45, σ ≈ 7.25 (variance = 10 × 63/12 = 52.5).

| Threshold | Probability |
|-----------|-------------|
| P(10d8 ≥ 32) = P(kill from full HP) | ≈ 96.3% |
| P(10d8 < 10) = P(minimum roll or near it) | ≈ 0% (minimum is exactly 10) |
| P(10d8 ≥ 64) = P(instant death at full HP, excess ≥ max HP 32) | ≈ 0.4% |

*(Using normal approximation: P(X < 32) ≈ P(z < (32-45)/7.25) = P(z < -1.79) ≈ 3.7%)*

**P(permanent death, round 1):**  
P(beholder wins initiative, ~55%) × P(Ray hits) × P(Ray kills):  
≈ 0.55 × 0.65 × 0.963 ≈ **34.4%** of encounters begin with Marwen's permanent death before she acts.

If beholder wins initiative (guaranteed in this slice's scenario): P ≈ 0.65 × 0.963 ≈ **63%**.

---

### Simulation: Rounds 2+ after Healing Word

After R136 established the pattern: beholder multiattacks (kills Marwen), Sabriel heals with Healing Word (Marwen at ~4 HP). Ray must recharge (33% per beholder turn). If Ray recharges:

- Marwen at 4 HP: P(10d8 ≥ 4) = 100% (minimum roll is 10). P(kill) = P(hit) = **65%**.
- Expected turns for recharge: geometric mean 3 turns.
- P(Ray fires within 3 beholder turns) = 1 − (2/3)^3 ≈ 70%.

So even after a Healing Word, there is a ≈70% chance the Ray fires again within 3 rounds, and when it fires on a 4-HP Marwen, it is a guaranteed kill. Healing Word is probabilistically futile against a rechargeable Disintegration Ray.

---

### Feel Assessment

**FEEL-R137-01 (NEW): Ray always-available round 1 + permanent death on any kill = mandatory player-elimination at level 5.**

The combination of four design choices creates a play-experience problem:

1. **Ray starts recharged** (always available on beholder's first turn)
2. **High hit probability** (65% vs Marwen AC 15 with +7 in-chamber)
3. **Average damage almost always exceeds Marwen's max HP** (45 avg vs 32 max — 96% kill probability on hit)
4. **Permanent death on any kill** (not just instant-kill-tier; standard 5e instant death requires excess ≥ max HP, but Disintegration Ray applies permanent death to any 0-HP result)

The result: roughly **1 in 3 encounters** begin with Marwen's player being permanently removed before their first action. This is the "one-round kill before the player got a turn" criterion from Phase B step 3 — and at level 5, "True Resurrection or Wish" as the only revival option means the character is gone from the campaign.

**Distinction: lethal vs unfair.** The party is designed to lose this encounter — that is fine and expected. The beholder killing Marwen at the end of a brutal fight is appropriate. The beholder killing Marwen before Marwen acts, permanently, with no recovery path is a different category of outcome: the player sat down, rolled initiative, and then watched their character be removed from the game before they touched a die.

**Comparison with standard 5e beholder:** The official beholder (MM) has eye rays (Disintegration Ray is one of 10 rays, chosen randomly), not as a rechargeable multiuse ability. Accessing Disintegration Ray is probabilistic and shares economy with 9 other effects. This homebrew beholder concentrates that power into a guaranteed-available opening-turn option, which dramatically inflates the round-1 threat ceiling.

**Options for Joe to consider (log only — not implementing):**
- (a) Ray starts uncharged; beholder must roll 5–6 to gain access (33% per turn). Round 1: 0% ray availability, party gets at least one full round before facing perma-death risk.
- (b) Permanent disintegration only applies when excess damage ≥ target's max HP (standard instant-death threshold). This preserves the Ray's lethality while allowing death saves for hits that don't one-shot.
- (c) Reduce Ray's to-hit to +4 (CR-appropriate PB+3 approach, matching the rest of the beholder's profile). P(hit) drops from 65% to 50% — meaningful reduction in round-1 perma-death probability.
- (d) None — current design is intentional and the encounter is meant to be unwinnable; permanent death is an accepted outcome.

**FEEL-R137-02 (NEW): Healing Word is mechanically futile against Disintegration Ray pattern.**

If the beholder kills Marwen with a multiattack in round 1 and Sabriel uses Healing Word to revive her (burning a spell slot), the beholder's Ray recharges within ~3 turns (70% probability) and then kills Marwen again permanently. Sabriel's Healing Word slot is spent on a one-turn reprieve, not a recovery. This is a specific feel problem: healing feels meaningless because it can't bring Marwen above the Ray's one-shot threshold (would require ~14d4+2 = avg 36 HP from a BA Healing Word, which is not possible at level 5). Suggest noting this in the encounter overview so the DM can telegraph it: "Healing Marwen back to consciousness will not keep her alive against the Ray; consider instead spending the slot on offense."
