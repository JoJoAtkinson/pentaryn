### Additional findings

**FEEL-NEW-04 (NEW): LA Tentacle targeting is not always "set up for DR."**  
The rotation.yml describes Tentacle LA after Marwen as grappling her before the beholder DR fires. But in this run's initiative order (beholder acts AFTER the PCs), there is NO pre-DR grapple available via LA — the beholder's DR and LA are both on the same turn/order. The Tentacle grapple only helps for the beholder's NEXT main action (Maw). For the DR turn specifically, grappling Marwen beforehand provides zero mechanical benefit (DR has no "grappled" rider). If the tactical intent is to set up Maw (not DR), the Tentacle LA should target a PC who will SURVIVE to be Maw'd next round — which is Bazgar (more HP), not Marwen (likely dead to DR).

**Implication:** The canonical LA sequence "Tentacle after Marwen → Void Ray after Bazgar" may be suboptimal when DR is available. Better sequence when DR is charged: Tentacle after Bazgar (grapple him for next Maw) + Void Ray after Marwen (pressure her, force Sabriel to burn LoH). This preserves DR for the main action against whichever target is most vulnerable.

**FEEL-NEW-05 (NEW): Drain Divinity is NOT "degenerate every round."**  
With 3 PCs, the beholder's LA trigger points per round = 3 (after each PC's turn). Using Drain Divinity (3 LA, all-in after one trigger) is MUTUALLY EXCLUSIVE with any other LA use that round. The beholder either:
- Uses DD (3 LA total, one trigger, all budget spent): strips 1 slot from Sabriel, gains 4–6 THP
- Uses T(1)+VR(2) (two triggers, same total 3 LA): deals ~27 expected damage to party members

With 2 PCs (after Marwen dies): same mutual exclusivity. To use DD after Sabriel's turn, beholder must hold all 3 LA through Bazgar's turn (no LA response to Bazgar).

**Correction to open item "Drain Divinity: degenerate every round vs divine casters":** This flag overstates the problem. DD is at most usable once per round, and using it means forgoing all other LA damage (~27 expected DPR). The trade-off is real and non-trivial, not degenerate. The concern about a 2-LA cost or cooldown may be unnecessary. DD vs T+VR is a legitimate tactical decision, not an autopilot.

**FEEL-NEW-06 (NEW): High variance in round-1 outcomes creates bimodal table experience.**  
P(Tentacle LA hits Marwen, AC 15) = 65%. P(DR hits Marwen, AC 15) = 65%.  
P(BOTH hit) = 42%: Marwen takes 14 bludg + grappled, then 45 force = dead, possibly permanent.  
P(at least one misses) = 58%: Marwen survives round 1 and contributes next round.  
P(BOTH miss) = 12%: Marwen is completely untouched and can act freely.

This run was the P=12% "both miss" case. The bimodal outcome (Marwen dies R1 vs survives R1+) creates dramatically different table feels: "we just died before contributing" vs "we have a real fight here." Neither is wrong for this encounter's intent (party meant to lose), but the binary nature of Marwen's survival/death may be worth noting as a design property. No fix suggested — this is natural variance.
