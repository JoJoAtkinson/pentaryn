### Feel Analysis

**1. DWD offensive contribution is near-zero vs AC 18+ party.**
At +2 to hit with advantage vs AC 18, each DWD has 44% hit rate for 3 damage. Two DWDs deal ~5 avg damage/round to Bazgar over a 2-round fight (~10 avg total). For CR 1/8 creatures, this is correct design — they function as HP gates and TD formation pieces, not threat sources. The formation feels purposeful without being dangerous to Bazgar. *(Confirmed, no fix needed.)*

**2. Shardcaller back-line positioning creates a range problem for its own attacks.**
SC at 50 ft from party = Shard-Throw at long range (disadvantage, >30 ft). R1 multiattack on Marwen fires at 25% hit rate vs the expected 50%. The 40-ft gap between SC's optimal "40-60 ft back" position and the party entrance means SC's own R1 pressure is minimal — until the party advances into the chamber. **New feel note: SC back-line position sacrifices its own R1 attack efficiency to preserve its safety. This is a design trade-off, not a bug, but it may be invisible to a DM who doesn't know the range rules apply.** Consider adding a range-distance note to the SC tactics section: "If party enters at ≥30 ft from SC position, R1 shard-throws are at disadvantage (long range) — move closer on R2 if melee threat hasn't closed."

**3. SC CW vs TD: the "highest-init alive ally" rule is systematically wrong in mixed tank-wall formations.**
SC's CW targeting protocol ("highest-initiative alive ally") will always select DWD-A (init 16) over the Rager (init 14). But DWD-A has Tactical Drilling (advantage already active when both DWDs flank same target) — making CW wasted (advantage doesn't stack). The Rager is the correct CW target (no inherent advantage source; its Multiattack benefits directly from CW advantage), but the Rager is often at init ≤14 and at 40 ft from SC (outside CW 30-ft range until Rager charges in).

**Conclusion:** In tank-wall formations, SC's CW protocol has a structural failure mode: the highest-init ally (DWD) has advantage blocked by TD, while the correct CW target (Rager) may be out of range when SC acts in R1. CW goes unused effectively. This will occur in any Rager+DWD encounter where both DWDs are alive.

**4. Marwen's Fireball is the formation-breaker.**
Marwen's 3rd-level Fireball one-shots the SC (33 HP vs 28 avg damage) with 60% probability on R1. If SC is the only back-line threat, Fireball is oversized (using a limited resource to kill a CR 2 creature). The formation design should either: (a) provide SC with cover to survive R1, or (b) accept that SC is trading its HP against Marwen's Fireball slot (a roughly equal resource exchange at L5). Either framing is viable. Worth flagging for Joe: is the Fireball-vs-SC trade intentional? If yes, the cheat sheet should note that SC dies on a R1 Fireball; if not, add cover mechanics to the chamber map.

**5. Tank-wall lasts 1-2 rounds, as expected for CR 2/CR 1/8 derro vs L5 party.**
The encounter is a speed bump before the main beholder fight, consuming ~1 Fireball slot and ~1-2 healing word charges (if needed). This is appropriate. No balance issue.
