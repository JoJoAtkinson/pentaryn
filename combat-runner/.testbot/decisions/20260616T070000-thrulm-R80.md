### FEEL-F80-01 (NEW — LOG ONLY): DD is structurally non-fireable R1 from standard hover; Void Ray is the de facto dominant LA strategy

**Finding:** The R1-LA-hold protocol (FIX-FC40-A) assumes DD fires immediately post-Sabriel (3 LA). This is **only true if the beholder is already within 30 ft of Sabriel** at the moment her turn ends. In every simulated final-confrontation where the beholder starts at standard hover altitude (40-60 ft above altar, 70+ ft from the party threshold), DD is out of range when Sabriel ends her R1 turn.

**The math:** To close range AND fire DD in a single LA window requires Move (1 LA) + DD (3 LA) = **4 LA total — impossible with a 3 LA budget.** Two Move LAs + DD = 5 LA — also impossible. The beholder cannot reach DD range AND fire DD in one LA window from hover.

**Consequence:** In R1, the beholder's actual dominant LA strategy is:
1. Move (1 LA) — descend 30 ft, still out of full DD range
2. Void Ray (2 LA) — target Marwen (AC 15, 65% fail, 22 damage, likely drops her)

This wastes the entire DD advantage. DD **first becomes fireable in R2** after the beholder descends on its own turn (R1 init 14) via fly movement + Shrine-Drift BA.

**Revised protocol implied by this finding:** The R1-LA-hold protocol should state: *if the beholder is at hover altitude when Sabriel ends her turn, hold ALL 3 LA (do not spend Move+VR), take the beholder's own turn normally (multiattack + Shrine-Drift descent), then post-Sabriel in R2 fire DD (3 LA) with the full budget.* The R1 sacrifice is accepting one round without LA pressure in exchange for DD executing properly in R2.

**Alternative:** Add a "pre-descent" note to beholder tactics: if the beholder wins initiative and acts before Sabriel in R1, spend fly movement to descend on its OWN turn (before holding LAs), so that DD range is achievable when the LA window opens. This only works if beholder init > Sabriel init — the opposite of the most common ordering (beholder tends to roll mid, Sabriel can roll high).

**Feels: One-note LA pattern.** In 5/5 simulated rounds of final-confrontation, beholder LA = Move+VR or pure VR (Void Ray). DD never fires R1 from hover. The "dominant LA" is always Void Ray, exactly as flagged in the briefing. This is an ability-rotation gap: DD (the thematically correct counter to Sabriel) practically never fires on schedule.

**Status: LOG ONLY.** Design call: (a) reduce DD cost to 2 LA so Move+DD = 3 LA (fits R1 budget), or (b) extend DD range to 60 ft so descent isn't required, or (c) add a "beholder descends first turn" rule in tactics text. All three require Joe's decision.
