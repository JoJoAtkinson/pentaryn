### Phase B Conclusions

**(1) Which LA is always dominant in multi-derro context?**  
In final-confrontation, optimal LA sequence:
- Post-Sabriel: Drain Divinity (3 LA) — strips smite economy decisively; Sabriel failed both saves this sim (60% fail rate with CHA +3, DC 16)
- Remaining 0 LA after DD. On turns where DD is on cooldown (it's a 3-LA full-spend): uses Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA) on weaker targets.

Drain Divinity remains dominant specifically targeting Sabriel. Shardcaller + Shrine-Touched handle Marwen suppression independently, freeing beholder to focus DD on Sabriel. Multi-derro context actually IMPROVES DD's dominance by offloading Marwen damage to minions.

**(2) Unfair-not-lethal moments?**  
- Marwen Disintegrated R2: **Dramatic and fair** — Marwen acted, spent a spell slot, Fireball fired. Getting disintegrated after a turn is "lethal and fair."
- Bazgar pre-turn KO via Shrine-Touched (R2, advantage from Unstable Form): **FEEL-R122-01** — Shrine-Touched's Unstable Form advantage on attacks after 10+ damage makes it significantly more dangerous in R2 after absorbing a Fireball hit. With advantage vs AC 18: hit rate jumps from 35% → 58%. Two advantage attacks + Call Weakness from Shardcaller = near-guaranteed down. The Unstable Form + Shardcaller Call Weakness chain creates a "the harder you hit it, the more it hurts you" feedback loop that can feel punishing.

**(3) LA economy functioning?**  
Yes. DD (3 LA) consumed post-Sabriel turn. Void Ray (2 LA) + Void Ray not reachable in same round with DD. Clean.

**(4) Shrine-Touched Oath-Breaking Retaliation + Unstable Form combo?**  
Not triggered in this sim (no party member attacked shrine-touched in R2 due to Bazgar being down after Sabriel's LoH). The combo would have fired if Bazgar attacked shrine-touched, but Bazgar was near-dead and focused on survival.  
**FEEL-R122-02** — The Oath-Breaking Retaliation self-damage (1d4 psychic) is meaningless due to shrine-touched's psychic resistance. Average self-damage = 1.25 × 0.5 (resistance) = 0.6 HP/reaction. A free-fire reaction with effectively zero cost. The self-damage was presumably meant to discourage spam use, but it doesn't function as a limiter.
