## Phase B — Generative Playtest Slice

**Scenario:** `retreat-threshold` — Beholder drops to 28 HP (below 30 HP tactical threshold). Simulate retreat toward lower shaft; evaluate feel and mechanical viability.

### Setup

- Beholder at 28 HP (reached by Bazgar landing two hits in Round 4, ~12 + 10 damage)
- Thrall derro: 2 remaining, HP 22 each, AC 14, both within 60 ft of beholder
- Party HP: Bazgar ~35/49, Sabriel ~42/44, Marwen ~14/32 (took Void Ray previously)
- Beholder has 1 Legendary Resistance remaining; LA budget refreshes at start of its turn

### Initiative order (fresh roll, Round 5)

| Creature | Roll | Mod | Init |
|----------|------|-----|------|
| **Init 20** | — | — | 20 (lair action) |
| Beholder | 14 | +3 | **17** |
| Sabriel | 9 | +4 | **13** |
| Bazgar | 6 | +1 | **7** |
| Marwen | 5 | +2 | **7** (DEX 15 > Bazgar DEX 10; Marwen first at tie) |
| Thrall 1 | 4 | +1 | **5** |
| Thrall 2 | 1 | +1 | **2** |

*Beholder acts before Sabriel this round — retreat turn comes before another round of PC attacks.*

### Init 20 — Lair Action

Beholder chooses **Manifest Thralls** (both thralls in range):
- Each thrall gains 1 THP (CHA +1) and a reaction to move up to 30 ft
- Thrall 1 uses reaction-move to reach shaft entrance (blocking position)
- Thrall 2 uses reaction-move to flank the shaft passage (second blocker)

*Rationale: Unstable Ground would prone Bazgar (useful), but Manifest Thralls positions two blockers in advance of the retreat, without costing the beholder's turn. The 1 THP is irrelevant; the positioning matters.*

### Beholder turn (init 17, 28 HP)

**Bonus action:** Shrine-Drift — moves 30 ft toward the lower shaft, passing through stone obstacles. Enters the rock face at the shaft entrance.

**Movement (fly 30 ft):** Continues 30 ft deeper in the same direction (now inside rock passage or in the open shaft beyond).

**Action:** Dash — additional 30 ft of movement.

**Total retreat this turn: 90 ft.** Beholder is now 60–90 ft from the party, in or through the shaft entrance.

**Key finding — Shrine-Drift end-inside-stone:** If the beholder ends its turn inside solid rock (e.g., 5 ft into the shaft wall), it takes **5 (1d10) force damage** (→ 23 HP) but becomes **completely untargetable** until its next turn. No attack, spell, or effect with a line-of-sight or line-of-effect requirement can reach it. At 23 HP with multiple dangerous PCs in range, this is a mechanically rational choice — 5 self-damage in exchange for a guaranteed survival turn. No PC ability at level 5 can pierce solid stone targeting.

**Compel Thrall conflict confirmed:** Shrine-Drift (BA) consumes the bonus action. The beholder **cannot** also use Compel Thrall (BA) to direct thralls on this turn. The lair-action Manifest Thralls at init 20 is the ONLY way to use thralls for blocking on the retreat turn. The tactics text says "using thralls to block pursuit" — this is only achievable via Manifest Thralls (lair action) + their reaction, NOT via Compel Thrall on the beholder's own turn. **Tactics section should clarify this sequence.**

### PC turns during retreat

**Sabriel (init 13):** Moves 30 ft toward shaft entrance (now adjacent to Thrall 1 as blocker). If she Divinities through or past the thrall: 1 opportunity attack from Thrall 1 (+1 vs AC 19, 15% hit, 2 avg damage — negligible). Beholder is 60–90 ft away inside or through rock. If inside rock: untargetable. If in open shaft passage: she's out of weapon range but could throw a javelin (+7 vs AC 17, 55% hit, avg 8) or use a ranged divine ability if available.

**Bazgar (init 7):** Has Action Surge remaining. Dash + Action Surge Dash = 120 ft total movement theoretically, but shaft entrance is blocked by Thrall 1 and Thrall 2. Needs to cut through thralls (or Disengage as bonus action — Fighter doesn't have Cunning Action). If he attacks Thrall 1 (AC 14, 22 HP): likely kills it in one attack (Bazgar avg 12 + potential maneuver = ~18–24 damage). Now shaft entrance is clear. But beholder is 60+ ft in and either inside stone or around a bend.

**Marwen (init 7):** 14 HP remaining. Best option: **Misty Step** (BA, 30 ft teleport) past the thrall blocker into the shaft passage, then **Scorching Ray** or **Fireball** targeting the beholder if it's in the open shaft. If beholder is inside stone: Misty Step is wasted this turn. If beholder is in open passage: Scorching Ray at +7 vs AC 17 (55% hit), three rays, avg ~18 damage if 2 rays hit. Marwen at 14 HP targeting a 23 HP beholder is putting herself in range of Void Ray.

### Legendary actions during retreat

Beholder refreshed to 3 LA at the start of its turn (Round 5). Spent none during its own turn.

**After Sabriel's turn (init 13):** Beholder is 60–90 ft away (or inside stone). If in open shaft:
- Tentacle Lash LA (1): Reach 10 ft — NOT IN RANGE
- Void Ray LA (2): Range 120 ft — **USABLE**. Target Sabriel (highest remaining HP, 42 HP, divine martial — beholder's preferred Drain Divinity target it can no longer reach). DC 16 DEX vs Sabriel (assume DEX +3, needs 13+ → 60% fail). Expected damage: 0.60 × 23 + 0.40 × 11.5 = 18.4 avg. Sabriel 42 HP → ~24 HP. Not lethal but meaningful pressure.
- Beholder uses **Void Ray** on Sabriel (2 LA spent, 1 LA remaining).

**After Bazgar's turn (init 7):** Beholder 1 LA remaining.
- Tentacle: not in range
- Void Ray: costs 2 LA, only 1 remaining → unavailable
- **Move** (1 LA): beholder flies 30 ft deeper into shaft (now 90–120 ft away, likely around a bend — line of sight broken). 0 LA remaining.

**After Marwen's turn (init 7):** 0 LA. Nothing.

**Result:** Beholder escapes the chamber entirely after the Thrall turns. It fired one Void Ray at Sabriel (18.4 avg) while retreating — "kill shot on the way out" doesn't materialize against Sabriel (still alive), but softens her. Marwen at 14 HP is the greater risk if the beholder had fired at her (expected damage 0.65 × 23 + 0.35 × 11.5 = 19 avg → Marwen unconscious).

**Optimal retreat choice identified:** Beholder should target Marwen with the Void Ray LA (64% fail rate at DEX +2, expected ~19 avg — likely drops Marwen to 0 HP and potentially triggers Disintegration Ray if recharged). Targeting Sabriel is sub-optimal unless the beholder is specifically trying to stop healing.

### Retreat feel assessment

**Mechanically sound:** The retreat sequence is clean and internally consistent. No rule violations. lair-action Manifest Thralls at init 20 → beholder's own turn: Shrine-Drift + Fly + Dash = 90 ft + optional "end in stone" invulnerability → Void Ray (2 LA) on Marwen during PC turns → escape complete. No auto-fix needed.

**Narratively anticlimactic:** The beholder at 28 HP "disappears into the mountain." The party reduced a CR 13 creature to 25% HP with a level 5 party — that's a narrative win — but the escape leaves no final confrontation. The beholder slides into stone and is just... gone. There's no "final lunge," no last desperate attack that makes the escape feel earned. Shrine-Drift makes the exit too clean.

**Potential design recommendations (log only — Joe decides):**
1. Add a descriptive note to the Tactics section on what "retreat into the lower shaft" looks like: "The void-shape presses against the rock face, grooves aligning with the stone grain until they merge. The air pressure drops. The rock where it disappeared still vibrates for a minute, then goes still." Gives the DM a scripted moment so the retreat feels dramatic rather than procedural.
2. Consider a "retreat reaction" option: the beholder can use its reaction (if Antireality hasn't fired this round) to trigger Shrine-Drift immediately when HP drops below threshold, even on a PC's turn. This turns the retreat into a visible dramatic beat (triggered by the killing blow that should have ended it) rather than a boring "beholder escapes on its next turn."
3. The "it cannot go deeper into the lower chamber willingly" note in the stat block directly contradicts the retreat tactic. At 28 HP, does survival instinct override fear? The DM has no guidance. **Recommend adding a one-line note to Tactics:** "At or below 30 HP, survival overrides the fear of the lower shaft — the creature retreats downward despite what waits there."

### MECHANICAL-R182-01: Disintegration Ray trigger clarification

"If this damage **reduces** the target to 0 hit points, the target is disintegrated." RAW interpretation: the target must be at positive HP before the hit. A target already unconscious (0 HP, making death saves) cannot be "reduced to 0" — they're already there. Hitting an unconscious PC with Disintegration Ray deals damage but **does not trigger disintegration** (the "reduced to 0" condition doesn't fire).

**Impact:** If Marwen is at 0 HP (unconscious) and the beholder fires Disintegration Ray at her, it deals 45 avg force damage to the unconscious PC (applying the instant-death rule if she's at 0 HP: 45 ≥ 32 max HP → instant death), but NOT the disintegration effect (no True Resurrection needed — just dead). DM should target conscious PCs with Disintegration Ray to trigger the disintegration rider; targeting unconscious PCs wastes the "turned to ash" threat.

**Status: LOG ONLY — RAW ruling, not a bug. Suggest adding DM note to DR stat block entry.**

### MECHANICAL-R182-02: Beholder inside stone — LA and action eligibility

While inside solid rock (Shrine-Drift "end in object" condition), the beholder:
- Has no line of sight to any creature (vision blocked by stone)
- Has no line of effect for spells/attacks requiring targeting
- Cannot use Void Ray, Tentacle, Drain Divinity, or Disintegration Ray (all require a target it "can see" or targeting a point)
- CAN use Shrine-Drift again on its next turn to re-emerge
- Takes 5 (1d10) force damage at start of any turn spent inside an object (per bonus action description — "if it ends its turn inside a creature or object")

Wait: re-reading — "takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its **turn** inside a creature or object." This is the END of turn condition; if the beholder ends its turn inside stone, it takes damage. It doesn't take damage on the START of its next turn. So the beholder can:
1. End Turn 5 inside stone (takes 5 avg damage, now 23 HP; untargetable)  
2. Start Turn 6 inside stone (no damage)
3. Use movement on Turn 6 to emerge from stone before using any actions

This clarification means the beholder gets a complete "invulnerability turn" between retreat and re-emergence — one full round where no PC action affects it. **This should be noted in the DM-facing Shrine-Drift description.**

**Status: LOG ONLY — rules clarification, not a bug.**
