## Phase B — Simulation: `disintegration-threat`

> Assume recharge rolls 5-6 on turn 1. Beholder targets Marwen (32 HP, AC 15). Roll 10d8 force +9
> vs AC 15. If hit: average ~46 damage. Does it one-shot a 32-HP Wizard? If yes, is the TPK risk
> from a single die roll acceptable for this encounter's intent?

### Attack Roll

**In-chamber bonus (Void-Feeding always active):** DB to_hit_bonus = 8; +1 Void-Feeding = **+9 total**

- Target: Marwen, AC 15
- Needs: 15 − 9 = **6+ on d20**
- P(hit) = 15/20 = **75%**

### Damage

- DB: 10d8 force, damage_modifier = 0; +1 Void-Feeding applied at table = **10d8+1 force**
- Average: (10 × 4.5) + 1 = **46 force**
- Range: 11 (min) to 81 (max)

### Does it one-shot Marwen (32 HP)?

P(KO | hit) = P(10d8+1 ≥ 32) = P(10d8 ≥ 31)

Using normal approximation: 10d8 ~ N(μ = 45, σ ≈ 7.25)

P(10d8 < 31) ≈ P(z < (30.5 − 45) / 7.25) = P(z < −2.00) ≈ **2.3%**

**P(KO | hit) ≈ 97.7%**

### Combined probability: Marwen permanently dead in round 1

P(hit) × P(KO | hit) = 0.75 × 0.977 ≈ **73.3%**

On a KO: Disintegration Ray rider fires → Marwen is ash. Cannot be restored except by True
Resurrection (7th level spell, 500 gp diamond required, requires cleric 13+) or Wish. The Compass
Edge at level 5 has no access to either spell.

### Recharge Rate and Expected DR Uses Per Combat

Recharge 5–6 = 33% per turn. Expected turns between recharges: ~3.

In a fight the party "loses" in 4–6 rounds:
- Expected DR activations: **1–2 uses total** (activation on turn 1 per scenario + 33% to recharge
  each subsequent turn)
- R2 recharge probability from R1 use: 33%. P(DR fires R1 + R3) ≈ 33%.
- Two DR uses in 4 rounds = P(fires R1, recharges R2, fires R3) = 0.33 ≈ 33%.

**In ~33% of fights, DR fires at least twice.** Second use targets the next weakest alive target
(Sabriel or Bazgar). P(second permanent death) = 20–34%.

### Key Findings

**FEEL-R309-01: 73% chance of permanent PC death on the beholder's FIRST action**

At the Compass Edge's level (5), the party cannot recover Marwen if she is disintegrated. This is
not a "dramatic loss" — it is a campaign disruption. The design note "party is meant to lose" implies
a loss they can come back from: retreat, surrender, near-death escape. Disintegration makes a
60–73% chance of that not being possible.

The feel problem is not "DR is too strong" — it is that the permanent death rider creates a
campaign-level consequence from a routine encounter-level ability. This diverges from the encounter
intent.

**Recommendation for Joe (design decision):** Consider one of:
- Remove the permanent death rider for Compass Edge tier, replacing it with "cannot regain HP until
  they complete a long rest" (a dramatic but not permanent consequence)
- Make the beholder's Tactics note explicit: "Do not use Disintegration Ray until the target is
  below 20 HP; use it to finish, not to open"
- Change DR to recharge 6 (16.7%) to reduce the frequency of the permanent-death risk

**FEEL-R309-02: Permanent death extends to ALL party members, not just the squishiest**

The threat ceiling analysis shows Sabriel (33.5%) and Bazgar (20.4%) are also at meaningful risk
of permanent disintegration on any DR hit. In a 4-6 round fight, there is a non-trivial chance the
beholder disintegrates 2 party members before they can retreat.

This creates a fight where the "dramatic loss" scenario (retreat after taking heavy losses) is
frequently preempted by a permanent loss. The beholder's design is internally consistent (it is
CR 13 and it should be terrifying) but the permanent death rider extends the lethality past the
design intent of an "outmatched but survivable" encounter.

**FEEL-R309-03: Recharge 5-6 for permanent death ability creates campaign variance, not just
encounter variance**

DR at recharge 5-6 fires ~1.8 times in a 5-round fight. Each activation is a ~20–73% chance of a
permanent PC death. The variance of campaign outcomes from a single thrulm encounter is enormous:

- Best case: DR never hits → party loses (death saves, retreat) but can continue
- Median case: DR hits once → Marwen permanently gone → campaign impact significant
- Worst case: DR hits twice → Marwen + Sabriel both gone → effectively campaign-ending for this group

For a fight the party is *intended to run from after realizing they can't win*, this variance
profile seems unintended. Reference: FEEL-003 (flagged in scenarios.yml) — Joe flagged permanent
death on a "meant to lose" fight as a concern; this simulation puts numbers on the probability.

**Dominant strategy note:** This simulation confirms R308's FEEL-R308-01 — beholder's tactically
dominant play is to *not* open with DR. Multiattack (R308) is strictly better on R1; DR is best
used as a finisher at R2-3 when a target is already low. If the beholder AI follows "wait for
target below 10 HP," it reduces the permanent death probability substantially while preserving the
threatening nature of the ability.
