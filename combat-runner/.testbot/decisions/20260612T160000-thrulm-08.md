## Phase B findings — antireality-timing

### FEEL-F8-01: Antireality +2 AC (DB) is economically weak at party level

With the DB's current "+2 AC declared post-roll" mechanic, Antireality only flips a hit when the attack total lands exactly at 17 or 18 (AC + 1 to AC + 2). Against a +7 attacker (Bazgar) vs AC 17:

- P(attack is a marginal hit at 17–18 total) = P(d20 = 10 or 11) = **10%** per attack
- With 2 attacks/round: P(at least one marginal hit) = 1 − (0.9)² = **19%** per round
- Beholder only burns Antireality for marginal hits (optimal play); all hits at 19+ go through unimpeded

Against Sabriel (+5 to hit vs AC 17): same 10% marginal window. Against Marwen (spell attacks +5): same.

**Conclusion:** The DB's +2 AC mechanic has ~10% effectiveness per attack at this party level. Most hits skip right through it. The reaction feels invisible to players and futile to the DM.

---

### FEEL-F8-02: Antireality disadvantage (.md) overwhelmed by Action Surge

The .md's "disadvantage on one attack roll" mechanic is significantly better — ~25% flip rate per covered attack (55% hit → 30% with disadvantage). But:

- Antireality is once-per-round (one reaction)
- Bazgar (Battlemaster 5) can Action Surge for 4 attacks in round 1
- Antireality covers exactly 1 of those 4 attacks; the other 3 hit at full probability

Simulation — Round 1 Bazgar Action Surge (4 attacks, all vs beholder):
- Attack 1 (with disadvantage): 30% hit → expected 0.30 × avg damage
- Attacks 2–4 (no disadvantage): 55% hit each → expected 0.55 × avg damage × 3
- Total expected: ~2.0 hits avg (vs 2.2 without Antireality)
- Antireality saves 0.2 expected hits ≈ ~2 expected damage per round

Against a party average of 2 attacks per round (Bazgar 2, Sabriel 2), Antireality reduces expected hits from ~1.1/round to ~0.85/round per attacker. This is perceptible but mild.

**Conclusion:** Even with the corrected .md mechanic, Antireality is a flavour reaction rather than a meaningful defensive tool against volume attacks. The beholder's primary defence vs physical attackers is already flight + grapple control; Antireality as-written is a minor supplement.

**Carry:** If Joe wants Antireality to feel impactful, consider "impose disadvantage on ALL attack rolls by one attacker until the start of that attacker's next turn" — costs same reaction, covers the full Action Surge.

### INFO-F8-01: Antireality pre-roll vs post-roll timing (part of BUG-F7-02)

The DB says Antireality is "declared AFTER seeing the attack roll." This was the old +2 AC mechanic's special feature (optimal-play window: wait for the roll, decide if AC boost flips it).

The current .md mechanic is "impose disadvantage on that attack roll" — which is inherently PRE-ROLL (disadvantage must apply before dice are rolled). The DB trigger text preserves the old post-roll declaration, which is now mechanically wrong.

**Consequence at table:** If the DM reads the DB output and declares Antireality after the roll, they can't apply disadvantage (roll already happened). The DM must either re-roll with disadvantage (wrong timing) or default to the broken +2 AC behavior. Fire #7 flagged this; confirming it's a live DM confusion risk.

### FEEL-F8-03: Drain Divinity dominance is round-dependent

Fire #7's beholder-r1-la-hold simulation confirmed DD is correct R1 play (strips L3 smite slot, excellent value). Phase B this fire examined whether DD is "always dominant":

| Round | Sabriel's slot pool | DD value | VR value | Verdict |
|-------|---------------------|----------|----------|---------|
| R1 | Full (L3+L2+L1) | High (strips L3 = prevents 18-avg smite) | 13 avg direct damage | **DD wins** |
| R2 | L2+L1 remaining (used L3 R1) | Medium (strips L2 = prevents 9-avg smite) | 13 avg direct | VR close; context-dependent |
| R3 | L1 only (or depleted) | Low (strips L1 = prevents 4-avg; or 0 if no slots) | 13 avg direct | **VR wins** |
| R3 (Sabriel KO'd) | N/A | 0 (target down) | 13 avg direct | **VR wins strictly** |

The documented R1-hold protocol is correct. The tactics section covers "switch to Void Ray or Tentacle after L2+ slots are exhausted" — but this caveat is buried 6 paragraphs deep in the DD tactics. A cold DM may default to DD every round, wasting 3 LA (all LA in a round) for 0 effective value.

**Carry (log only):** Add a one-liner to the Legendary Actions section flagging "if Sabriel has no L2+ slots remaining, skip DD; use Void Ray instead."
