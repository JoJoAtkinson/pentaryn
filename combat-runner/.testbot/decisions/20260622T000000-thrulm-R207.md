### Key findings from this Phase B

**FEEL-R207-01 (High — radiant vulnerability is fight-ender against any Paladin).**
Sabriel's first Divine Smite hit (17 base → 32 doubled) drops shrine-touched from 45 to 13 HP in a single attack — before the shrine-touched has even had a turn. A second hit (any radiant) eliminates it. The shrine-touched has 0 rounds of effective combat against a divine martial with radiant damage.

Consequence: the shrine-touched's most interesting mechanics (Oath-Breaking Retaliation reaction, Unstable Form, Ancient Resonance) barely fire before the creature dies. In this run:
- Oath-Breaking Retaliation fired once (reaction to Sabriel's first hit) — HIT Sabriel for 8.
- Unstable Form advantage activated — contributed to two reaction/multiattack swings, one landed.
- Ancient Resonance: never got a recharge attempt at 45 HP; never fired.
- Combat lasted ~3 PC attacks total (Sabriel ×2, Bazgar ×1).

**Counterpoint:** the encounter is designed for the party to struggle against the BEHOLDER, not the shrine-touched. Shrine-touched is a minion. If she uses smite to one-shot it, she burns spell slots — which the beholder then harvests via Drain Divinity. This is actually coherent design: the beholder incentivizes burning resources early.

**FEEL-R207-02 (Medium — Oath-Breaking Retaliation timing creates double-punish vs melee).**
When shrine-touched took 32 radiant damage, Unstable Form activated at the same instant as the reaction (both trigger on "takes damage"). The reaction attack had advantage (Unstable Form already active). This is internally consistent but creates an unusual interaction: the blow that nearly kills the shrine-touched simultaneously gives it a powerful reaction. The feel is "you hit it so hard it hit back even harder." At level 5 vs a CR 3 creature, this is fine — the shrine-touched still dies quickly. But against a melee-only party (no radiant), this loop could feel more punishing.

**FEEL-R207-03 (Low — Ancient Resonance never fired in simulation; recharge rate OK).**
Probability of recharge 5-6 on d6 = 33%. In a 1-2 round combat vs Compass Edge, expected AR uses ≈ 0.33. Ancient Resonance is effectively a "you get lucky once in three shrine-touched fights" reward. This is fine for a CR 3 minion. No balance flag.

**DB-BUG-R156-01 CONFIRMED ACTIVE:** Both Oath-Breaking Retaliation triggers in this run produced no dice output. DM must remember to roll 1d8+1 slashing + 1d8 necrotic manually on each reaction fire. This is a live table hazard — DMs running this encounter will frequently omit the necrotic damage type entirely.

**AUTOMATION GAP CONFIRMED:** Unstable Form advantage had to be tracked manually (see A207-08). When combined with the fact that Oath-Breaking Retaliation fires at the same moment as Unstable Form activation, the DM must make two simultaneous judgments: (1) Unstable Form just activated, (2) reaction is available, (3) reaction has advantage. The combat runner surfaces none of this automatically.
