## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `round-1-opener`

**Slice description (rotation.md slice 0):** Beholder acts first (initiative 20+). Simulate: lair action → bonus action Shrine-Drift → multiattack (2× Tentacle Lash + Maw) → legendary actions vs all three PCs. Track HP. Look for action-economy dominance and grapple-snowball risk.

### Party assumptions (Level 5 Compass Edge)

- Bazgar: STR 16 (+3), DEX 11 (+0 or +1), AC 18, HP 49. Athletics check: d20+6.
- Marwen: DEX +2, WIS +2, HP 32, AC 15. 
- Sabriel: DEX +1, WIS +3 (divine martial), HP 44, AC 19. Carries sanctified weapon (+1d8 bonus/hit vs beholder).

### Initiative order

- Beholder DEX +3 → avg 13.5 initiative. Lair action fires on init count 20 (always before beholder's own initiative).
- Party: Bazgar +1 avg 11.5, Marwen +2 avg 12.5, Sabriel +1 avg 11.5.
- **Effective order this round:** Init 20 (lair action) → Beholder's own turn → [party, in various orders after].

The party receives the full lair+turn combo before any PC can act unless a PC rolls ≥ 14 (to beat the beholder's own initiative).

### Round 1 — Init 20: Lair Action

**Unstable Ground** vs Bazgar (most likely to be in range, leading the march):
- DC 16 DEX save. Bazgar DEX +1: needs 15+, P(fail) = 70%.
- On fail: Bazgar goes prone. Standing costs half movement (15 ft of his 30 ft speed).

**Void Eruption** is less likely (party just arrived, may not be within 20 ft of shrine yet).

Assuming **Unstable Ground hits** (70% likely): Bazgar is prone at the start of the encounter.

### Round 1 — Beholder's Turn

**Bonus action: Shrine-Drift.** Repositions to within 10 ft of the altar (Void-Feeding already active) and within reach of Bazgar. All attack bonuses: +7 (base +6, Void-Feeding +1), damage +1.

**Action: Multiattack** — 2× Tentacle Lash + 1× Maw, all vs Bazgar.

Bazgar is prone. Beholder descends to within 5 ft (adjacent) for advantage on melee attacks.

**Attack rolls (advantage, +7 vs AC 18, hits on 11+, P(hit with adv) = 1−(10/20)² = 75%):**

| Attack | Hit chance | Avg damage (on hit) | Effect |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|--------|
| Tentacle Lash 1 | 75% | 14 (3d6+4 in-chamber) | Grappled (escape DC 16) |
| Tentacle Lash 2 | 75% | 14 | Grappled (second tentacle) |
| Maw | 75% | 22 (4d8+4 in-chamber) | Disadvantage on escape checks if grappled |

**Expected damage to Bazgar this turn:** 0.75×14 + 0.75×14 + 0.75×22 = 10.5 + 10.5 + 16.5 = **37.5 avg**

Bazgar after beholder's action: 49 − 37.5 = **11.5 HP expected remaining**.

**One-round KO probability:**

P(Bazgar at 0 HP before his first turn) = P(total damage ≥ 49):
- Both TL + Maw hit (0.75³ = 42.2%): 50 avg damage → Bazgar at −1 HP. **KO'd.**
- 1 TL + Maw hit (2×0.75×0.25×0.75 = 28.1%): 36 avg → 13 HP. Survives.
- Both TL, no Maw (0.75²×0.25 = 14.1%): 28 avg → 21 HP. Survives.
- Outliers covered by math above.

**P(Bazgar KO'd before acting) ≈ 42%.** This is on top of the 70% chance he was already prone from the lair action.

### Legendary Actions (after each PC turn)

After Marwen's turn: **Void Ray** (2 LA, DC 16 DEX save, 23 avg force).
- Marwen DEX +2: needs 14+ to succeed → P(fail) = 65%.
- Expected damage: 0.65×23 + 0.35×11.5 = **~19 avg**.
- Marwen: 32 − 19 = **13 HP after one Void Ray**.

After Sabriel's turn: **Tentacle Lash** (1 LA, +7 vs AC 19, 14 avg).
- Needs 12+: P(hit) = 45%. Expected: 6.3 avg damage + possible grapple.

**Total LA damage this round:** ~25 avg split across Marwen and Sabriel.

### Round 2 projection (Bazgar survived at ~13 HP, grappled)

Bazgar options:
1. Escape grapple (Action): d20+6 vs DC 16. Maw disadvantage on escape check → P(success) ≈ 45%.
2. Attack beholder: two attacks, +7 vs AC 17, 55% hit, ~9 avg per hit. Expected: ~9.9 damage to beholder.

If Bazgar escapes (45%): prone is no longer relevant (he stands using half move). Still in beholder's reach next turn.
If Bazgar attacks instead (55%): deals ~10 expected damage. Beholder at ~100 HP. Beholder hits back: second multiattack with Maw advantage (grappled = still adjacent) → expected ~37 damage again → Bazgar KO'd end of round 2 (nearly certain).

**Round 2 beholder lair action: Manifest Thralls** (if thralls present) or **Void Eruption** (if party has converged near shrine). Void Eruption: all within 20 ft of shrine, DC 16 DEX save, 11 avg. If Sabriel closed to melee, she's within range: expected ~7 damage. Low value vs a single Tentacle reposition.

### Key findings from simulation

**1. Pre-turn KO rate is too high.**
42% chance Bazgar is eliminated before his first action. Combined with the 70% chance of being prone from the lair action, the "lair→prone→advantage multiattack" sequence is the dominant threat, not the beholder's per-action power alone. The synergy is what creates the unfairness.

**2. Grapple-snowball confirmed in round-1 context.**
If Bazgar survives round 1 at 13 HP, he is almost certainly grappled (P≥75% given the above). His round 2 choice is: escape (45%, wastes turn) or attack (deals ~10 expected damage while taking ~37). The outcome is the same — dead by round 2 — but fighting back does more cumulative damage to the beholder.

**3. Drain Divinity is strictly worse than Void Ray + Tentacle every round.**
Drain Divinity (3 LA) vs Sabriel: removes one 3rd-level slot → beholder gains 6 THP. Alternative: Void Ray (2 LA, ~19 avg to Marwen) + Tentacle (1 LA, 6.3 avg damage + grapple attempt). The LA combo deals ~25 expected damage and extends the grapple. Drain Divinity would need to fire vs Sabriel (highest slot) AND net much more than 6 THP to compete. It does not.

**Drain Divinity has zero opportunity to fire optimally in this simulation.** The beholder is within 30 ft only during the multiattack turn (descends to adjacent Bazgar). Using all 3 LA on Drain Divinity (vs Sabriel, 30 ft away) would require descending AND targeting Sabriel instead of Marwen. The positioning math means Drain Divinity and the multiattack can't both fire at their optimal targets in the same round.

**4. Party best response: Spread out to 25+ ft apart on entry.**
If PCs spread: Void Eruption hits only 1 PC (if anyone is near the shrine); Unstable Ground hits only 1 PC; Void Scream (30 ft radius) can still catch 2 PCs. Spreading doesn't eliminate the threat but forces single-target attacks, giving the other PCs free turns.

**5. Lair action / multiattack synergy flags a design tension.**
The beholder is "meant to be lethal" — a CR 13 vs L5 party. But there's a meaningful difference between "lethal" and "acts twice before anyone else." The lair-action-then-own-turn sequence means the beholder gets a free prone-application before the multiattack. This is intended by the lair action design (init count 20), but in the round-1 opener it creates a 42% chance of a "no turn" KO. For a "meant to lose" encounter, this is on the edge of acceptable — flagging for Joe's design judgment.

### Log-Only Items (New This Fire)

#### FEEL-R224-01 (HIGH — round-1 pre-turn KO rate)
**Finding:** Lair action (Unstable Ground → prone) + beholder's own multiattack with advantage creates a 42% chance Bazgar is KO'd before his first turn. The 70% lair-action hit rate × 75% multiattack hit rate per attack = overwhelming pressure in the opening round. "Unfair rather than lethal" criteria: PC gets no turn before death.
**Recommendation (log only):** Consider staggering the lair action to init count 15 (loses to the beholder but fires after at least one PC acts), OR limiting Unstable Ground to once per combat rather than every init-20. Neither changes the "designed to lose" feel — it just ensures each PC acts at least once before dying.

#### FEEL-R224-02 (MEDIUM — prone+advantage synergy with multiattack)
**Finding:** The beholder's prone-from-lair + advantage-on-melee combo is not documented in the cheat sheet's tactical section. The round-by-round checklist says to use Unstable Ground, but doesn't note that this enables advantage on the follow-up multiattack. DMs may not make the connection; if they don't descend to 5-ft adjacent after prone application, they lose the advantage benefit and the opener is significantly weaker (~55% hit rate instead of 75%).
**Recommendation:** Add a note to the cheat sheet or tactics section: "If Unstable Ground goes prone target, descend to within 5 ft before multiattack for advantage on all three attacks this turn."

#### FEEL-R224-03 (HIGH — Drain Divinity fires zero times in expected play)
**Finding:** Round-1 opener confirms that Drain Divinity (3 LA, 30 ft range, vs spellcasters only) never competes with Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA). The beholder must descend to ≤30 ft to use Drain Divinity, which exposes it to Sabriel's sanctified melee attacks. The expected value of 6 THP (from a 3rd-level slot) vs ~25 avg damage from the LA combo makes Drain Divinity strictly dominated every round unless:
- (a) Sabriel is the only remaining conscious PC, AND
- (b) the beholder has 3 full LA from the previous round.
This essentially never happens. The briefing already flags this as a known issue (fire #6), but this simulation is the first to quantify it as *strictly dominated* (not just usually suboptimal).
**Joe's decision needed:** Lower to 2 LA cost (competes with Void Ray), or widen range to 60 ft (matches other LAs and removes the "must descend" penalty), or accept that this is a flavour-only option that never fires in practice.
