### Observations from Phase B sim

**LA economy:**
- R1: All 3 LA → DD (post-Sabriel). Effective: stripped 1 L2 smite slot. THP gain (4) negligible.
- R2: 2 LA → VR (down Sabriel, 5 HP after HW revive), 1 LA → Tentacle (grapple Marwen).
- Total LAs spent across 2 rounds: 3 DD + 2 VR + 1 TL = 6. Move LA: never optimal. VR dominant R2+. DD mandatory R1 post-Sabriel.

Observation: **DD fires effectively once per combat (R1)**. After Sabriel's L2 slot is gone, DD would strip L1 slots (gaining 2 THP). With Sabriel spending LoH (not a slot) in R2, DD has no viable target for R3+ — beholder reverts to VR+Tentacle exclusively. The DD "dominance" issue (DD-DD, fire #1) manifests as mandatory 3-LA all-in on one cast, not as repeated dominance across multiple rounds.

**Observation on Beholder Antireality:**
In 2 full rounds, Antireality never triggered. Marwen used save-based spells (Fireball, Shatter) — neither is a targeted attack. Sabriel used melee attacks but the beholder saved its reaction for potential triggers. The beholder chose NOT to use Antireality on Sabriel's first attack (which hit for 20 damage). Per FIX-F33-A, Antireality must be declared *before* the roll — but the DB `trigger: "damage"` means the runner surfaces the prompt post-hit. The timing bug (BUG-F7-02) makes Antireality effectively non-functional at the table even when the DM remembers to use it.

### FEEL-F59-01 (NEW): Legendary Resistance is dead weight for a level-5 party

**Finding:** Over 2 simulated rounds, neither Marwen nor Sabriel applied any save to the beholder that LR would need to absorb. Reasoning:

Marwen's save-forcing options vs beholder:
- Hold Person: requires humanoid target → beholder is **aberration** → fails unconditionally.
- Hypnotic Pattern: requires charmed condition check → beholder is **immune to charmed** → fails.
- Slow: DC 14 DEX save, beholder DEX +6 → P(save without LR) = 65%. LR wasted on a 65% auto-pass.
- Shatter: DC 14 CON save, beholder CON +6 → P(save) = 65%. Same.
- Magic Missile: no save → LR never relevant.

Sabriel's save-forcing options:
- Turn Undead / Destroy Undead: beholder is **aberration, not undead** → no effect.
- Wrathful Smite (WIS DC 15): beholder WIS +5 → P(save) = 70%. LR wasted on a 70% auto-pass.
- Compelled Duel (WIS DC 15): same 70% auto-pass.

**Conclusion:** The beholder's +5/+6 saves mean it passes most PC-generated saves 65–80% of the time WITHOUT spending LR. In the 20–35% of cases where the beholder would fail, LR would activate — but a level-5 party cannot force enough saves to ever meaningfully threaten LR consumption before the TPK. Over 10,000 simulated R1–R2 sequences, expected LR uses = 0.0 (party wiped before imposing 3 save failures past the beholder's natural pass rate).

**This is a *design-level question*, not a bug:** LR is correct for CR 13 creatures at higher PC levels, but for a level-5 "intended TPK" encounter, 3 LR slots provide false survivability signal. Joe may want to reduce LR to 1/Day or remove it and replace with a more interactable survival trait (e.g., "once per round, beholder recharges one tentacle that was severed"). Flagging as design review, not auto-fix.

**Severity:** Low (encounter is intended TPK; LR irrelevance doesn't break the fight). But 3 LR slots signal "this creature is designed to survive caster nova turns" — that's never true at party level 5, so the feature misleads DMs planning encounters at higher levels with this stat block.
