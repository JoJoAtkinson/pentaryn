## Phase A — New Findings This Fire

### NEW-R94-01 — Antireality trigger.event = "damage" (sub-bug of BUG-F7-02)

**Raw DB entry (confirmed via `grep` on actions.jsonl):**
```json
{
  "npc": "beholder-thrulm",
  "action": "antireality",
  "type": "reaction",
  "reaction_kind": "buff",
  "verbs": [],
  "trigger": {"scope": "self", "event": "damage", "match": "targeted by an attack the beholder can see"},
  "narration": "Space ripples. The blow lands on a place the beholder almost was.",
  "effect": "+2 AC against the triggering attack. Declare BEFORE the attack roll resolves — if this changes the roll from a hit to a miss, the attack misses. (Once per round.)"
}
```

The `trigger.event` is `"damage"` — meaning the runner surfaces the Antireality prompt **after
the attack resolves on a hit** (damage event). But the stat block and the `effect` field itself
both say to "declare BEFORE the attack roll resolves." The trigger timing contradicts the intended
mechanic: if the reaction fires on the damage event (post-roll), imposing disadvantage
retroactively is meaningless — the die has already been cast.

**Three bugs in this row (BUG-F7-02 cluster):**
1. `effect` says "+2 AC" — should be "impose disadvantage on the triggering attack roll"
2. `trigger.event` = "damage" — should fire on `"attack_declared"` or equivalent pre-roll hook
3. `verbs` = `[]` — no manual verb fallback; DM cannot call antireality explicitly (BUG-R83-01)

All three require `combat_action_upsert`. **Log only — needs Joe + interactive MCP session.**

## Phase B — Scenario Audit: `retreat-threshold` (rotation.yml idx = 94 % 8 = 6)

**Slice definition:**
> Beholder drops to 28 HP (below threshold). Per tactics: retreats toward lower shaft, uses thralls
> to block. Does it have a meaningful path given hover 30 ft speed and the encounter room
> dimensions? Does the retreat feel dramatic or anticlimactic? Check if any existing action
> supports the retreat mechanically (Shrine-Drift is the bonus action move; verify it works as an
> escape tool).

### Setup

- Beholder: 28 HP (just crossed 30 HP retreat threshold after party output ~82 HP damage)
- 2 Thrall Derro alive: HP 22 each, AC 14
- Party state (representative): Bazgar 49 HP (unstruck this round), Marwen 15 HP (bloodied from
  prior DR), Sabriel 33 HP (VS partial hit R2)
- Beholder is at 25 ft above altar floor after the R2 Shrine-Drift descent
- Lower shaft entrance: ~70 ft across the chamber floor from the altar

### Round — Beholder Retreats

**Init 20 — Lair action:**
Context: Bazgar is the closest melee threat (at the altar base, 25 ft from shaft entrance). Void
Eruption is only relevant if a PC is within 20 ft of shrine — Bazgar is. DC 16 DEX, 11 (2d10)
force. Bazgar DEX save +0 vs DC 16 → 75% fail → expected 8 avg (11 × 75% = 8.25) force damage.
Bazgar: 49 − 8 = 41 HP (expected). Lair action delivers damage without consuming beholder action ✓.

Alternatively: Manifest Thralls — 2 thralls get 1 THP, reaction attacks vs Bazgar (AC 18) at +1.
Hit rate: 1d20+1 vs 18 = 20% each. 2 thralls = 40% expected to land at least one attack.
Average damage if one hits: 2 (1d4). Essentially useless; confirms known FEEL-R83-01/Manifest
Thralls dead-weight note.

**Optimal lair choice this round:** Void Eruption (Bazgar in range), not Manifest Thralls.

**Beholder turn — retreat mechanics:**

Movement: Shrine-Drift (BA, 30 ft toward shaft entrance — phases through altar structure, no OA
since Shrine-Drift is a special ability move, not standard movement). Arrives 30 ft closer to shaft
(now at 40 ft distance from shaft). Remaining standard fly speed: 30 ft.

**CRITICAL SEQUENCE PROBLEM:** If beholder uses Shrine-Drift (BA) to move 30 ft, it has
expended its BA. Its standard movement (30 ft fly) gets it 30 ft more. Total: 60 ft. The shaft
entrance is ~70 ft away → **beholder is 10 ft short of reaching the shaft this turn**.

But here is the OA exposure question: the beholder's *standard movement* (not Shrine-Drift) passes
through Bazgar's melee reach (5 ft). Does Bazgar get an OA?

- Shrine-Drift: the beholder moves through Bazgar's space as difficult terrain (passes through
  creatures). This does not provoke OAs — it is a special bonus-action repositioning.
- Standard fly movement (30 ft): if this movement passes within 5 ft of Bazgar (melee reach) and
  the beholder then leaves that reach, Bazgar gets an OA per RAW.

**Bazgar OA on standard retreat movement:**
- Bazgar: +7 to hit vs AC 17 → needs 10+ → 55% hit
- Expected damage: 1d12+5 (BM Fighter w/ +5 STR, Greatsword — assumed) = avg 11.5
  - Or if magic weapon: 1d12+5+1 = 12.5
- At 28 HP: single OA hit → avg 12 → 16 HP remaining → crosses 20 HP desperate threshold

**The two-threshold collapse:** The gap between "retreat" (30 HP) and "desperate" (20 HP) is only
10 HP. A single Bazgar OA deals expected 11–12 damage — which crosses the desperate threshold with
~55% probability in a single retaliatory swing. In practice, the beholder enters the "desperate"
phase mid-retreat on the same turn it first attempts to flee. The two-phase retreat described in
the tactics section collapses into a single-turn event.

**Desperate phase conflict:** At 20 HP, tactics say "attempts to crush/disintegrate the party
while fleeing." But:
- Shrine-Drift (BA) is already used for this turn's movement
- Standard movement is partially used to approach shaft
- If beholder also uses its action (Disintegration Ray or Void Scream), it cannot move further

It cannot simultaneously: (a) fire DR, (b) use Shrine-Drift, AND (c) move full speed toward shaft.
Action + BA are both consumed by the attack and the Shrine-Drift respectively. The beholder is
stuck: attack OR flee, not both.

**No Disengage action in DB:** Confirmed — `python scripts/combat_actions_db.py list --npc beholder-thrulm` shows no disengage/dash verb. To avoid OAs during retreat, the beholder would need to Disengage (action) but then cannot fire DR or VS on the retreat turn. The combat-runner has no "beholder disengage" verb to dispatch.

**Shrine-Drift through solid matter:** If the shaft entrance requires passing through a solid wall
or pillar, Shrine-Drift can phase through it — but the beholder takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it
ends its turn inside an object. At 16–28 HP, a bad roll (10 force) could trigger death before
reaching the shaft. FEEL-R87-02: "Shrine-Drift wall-phasing undocumented" — this scenario confirms
the risk is real and the stat block note ("takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends turn inside a
creature or object") is the relevant mechanic.

**Thrall blocking:** With 2 thralls alive, the beholder could use Compel Thrall (BA) to move them
into Bazgar's path. BUT: Shrine-Drift was used as the BA this turn — CT is unavailable. The
thralls act independently on their own initiative, and they will likely not move to block unless
the beholder previously directed them (their initiative was earlier in the round).

### Phase B Findings

**FEEL-R94-01 (NEW):** The retreat-threshold scenario confirms FEEL-R87-01 with quantified data.
At 28 HP beholder, the OA from Bazgar expected at 55% hit rate deals ~12 damage (avg), which:
(a) crosses the 20 HP desperate threshold with ~55% probability in a single swing, and
(b) if Sabriel adds Sacred Flame or smite range attacks, the threshold can be crossed before the
beholder reaches the shaft entrance.

**The 30–20 HP window is too narrow for a meaningful retreat phase in practice.** The beholder
goes from "I should retreat" to "I'm in desperate mode" within a single turn in the majority of
playthroughs. Recommend: raise the retreat threshold to 40 HP so the beholder has 20 HP of buffer
between "I start retreating" and "desperate behavior." This gives 2-3 turns of visible retreat
before it becomes a desperate last stand. [Design call — flag for Joe.]

**FEEL-R94-02 (NEW):** The tactic "uses thralls to block pursuit" requires the beholder to use
Compel Thrall (BA) to redirect thralls. But in the retreat scenario, Shrine-Drift (BA) is the
primary escape tool — using both on the same turn is illegal. The beholder must choose: escape
via Shrine-Drift OR direct thralls as blockers. This is a genuine no-win tradeoff that isn't
acknowledged in the tactics section. If thrall-blocking is intended as the retreat's counterplay,
Shrine-Drift should not be the same BA slot — or the tactics note must acknowledge the choice.
[Design call — flag for Joe.]

**FEEL-R94-03 (NEW):** No Disengage verb exists in the beholder's DB. Retreating via standard
movement provokes OA from Bazgar (55% hit, ~12 avg damage). The beholder has no ability to safely
disengage; Shrine-Drift phases through space but doesn't suppress OAs on the standard movement
portion. **Recommendation:** add `disengage` as a beholder action (spending its full action) with
verb "escape / flee / disengage" — or add a note to the tactics section that the beholder accepts
1-2 OAs during retreat (budget for it in retreat HP calculations). Without this, the retreat
sequence has a hidden mechanic that consistently punishes the "correct" tactical choice.

(index 36) covers the Shrine-Drift verb dispatch. The verb fires. The retreat narrative is
table-side. ✓
