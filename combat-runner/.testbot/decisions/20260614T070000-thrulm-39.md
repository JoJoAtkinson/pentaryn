DR is effectively a 62–65% instakill on Marwen in any R2 context. On a miss (35%), Marwen survives with no damage — DR deals no "chip" on a miss. The binary outcome (instakill or miss entirely) is the defining feel characteristic of DR vs Marwen.

### Feel analysis: Disintegration + "no body" rider interaction

When DR reduces Marwen to 0 HP, the disintegration rider triggers: "the target is turned to ash... cannot be restored to life except by true resurrection or wish."

At party level 5, neither True Resurrection (9th level) nor Wish (9th level) is accessible. A disintegrated Marwen is **permanently dead** for the rest of the campaign unless Joe explicitly makes those spells available through story means.

**Critical implication:** DR vs Marwen at level 5 is not "down a PC for this encounter" — it is potentially "lose a PC for the campaign." This far exceeds the normal consequence of a PC being downed in a lethal-intent encounter. The "meant to lose" design intent covers TPKs (all PCs downed, revived by captor/story), but permanent character death via disintegration is a qualitatively different outcome.

In practice, Joe likely runs this as a dramatic near-death moment (ash = down, revivify available post-rescue). But the .md currently states the rider applies unconditionally. If Joe intends the disintegration rider to be permanent, Marwen's player should know this before the encounter (informed consent). If he intends it to be reversible via story (ash can be collected, revivify applied offscreen), the stat block should note the table ruling.

**FEEL-F39-01 (NEW):** DR disintegration rider at level 5 = potential permanent PC death with no mechanical counterplay (no save, no death saves, no revivify). This crosses from "lethal encounter" to "permanent character removal" territory. Recommend Joe add a table ruling in the stat block: either "disintegration is permanent (player informed)" or "disintegration = down, no body available for in-combat recovery but revivify still works post-fight (ash collected)." The current text is ambiguous about whether the permanence is in-world mechanical or DM-waivable.

### Interaction of DR slot tracking with recharge die

The `assert_slot {remaining: 0}` check verifies that firing DR sets the slot to 0 immediately. On the beholder's next turn start, the runner should prompt a recharge roll (d6 ≥ 5 to restore, per `recharge: 5` in DB). If the recharge check returns 5 or 6, DR becomes available again (remaining: 1).

This state transition (0 → 1 on 5-6, or stays 0 on 1-4) is what gives DR its 33% per-turn recharge probability. In a 4-round fight, expected DR uses from R2 onward = 1 (R2 baseline) + 3×0.33 = 2 expected total. Against a party of 3, 2 DR uses = potentially 2 disintegrated PCs if both hit and damage thresholds are met. Combined with VS in one of those rounds, the beholder has enough coverage to effectively end the encounter by R3–R4.

No mechanical bug in slot tracking expected. The scenario exists to catch regressions where the runner fails to decrement the recharge slot on fire.
