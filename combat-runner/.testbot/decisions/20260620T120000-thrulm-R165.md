### Option A: Unstable Ground (target: Bazgar within 20 ft)

- DC 16 DEX save. Bazgar DEX +1: needs 15+ → P(save) = 30% → P(fail, prone) = **70%**
- On fail: Bazgar falls prone → costs 15 ft (half speed) to stand; all melee attacks against him have advantage; his own attacks while prone have disadvantage
- Beholder LA (tentacle_legendary) does NOT benefit from prone — tentacle range 10 ft, beholder hovers 40+ ft above. No immediate attacker-advantage for the beholder.
- Benefit realized if: (a) thralls move adjacent and attack with advantage, or (b) Bazgar doesn't stand (stays prone, attacks at disadvantage all turn)
- Thrall A attacks prone Bazgar with advantage: +1 to hit vs AC 18 → base P(hit) = 20%; with advantage P(at least one ≥17) = 1−(0.80)² = **36%**. Expected: 0.36×2.5 = **0.9 damage**
- **Primary effect:** Bazgar wastes 15 ft of movement standing OR takes disadvantage on his next attacks. Costs the fighter one round of full effectiveness.
- **Expected damage gain this round from prone:** ~0.9 per thrall (both attack) = 1.8 hp, plus subjective loss of Bazgar's attack round

### Option B: Manifest Thralls (thrall derro within 60 ft)

- Both thralls gain THP = beholder's CHA modifier (+1) = **1 THP each**
- Each may immediately use their reaction to move or make a weapon attack
- Best use: both attack Bazgar (nearest, lowest AC at 18)
  - Hand Axe Weak: +1 to hit vs AC 18 → needs 17+ on d20 → P(hit) = **20%**
  - Expected damage per thrall: 0.20 × 2.5 = **0.5**
  - Two thralls combined: **1.0 expected damage total**
- 1 THP offers no meaningful protection vs AC 18 party damage output (one Greataxe hit from Bazgar = avg 8 damage; thrall has 22 HP; 1 THP saves ≤1 of that before the thrall drops)
- **Verdict:** Near-zero mechanical impact. 1 THP = irrelevant. Two reactions = ~1.0 expected damage. This lair action achieves approximately nothing against the Compass Edge.

### Option C: Void Eruption (20 ft of shrine)

Stat block: "Each creature within 20 feet of the shrine must make a DC 16 Dexterity saving throw, taking 11 (2d10) force damage **on a failed save**."

**Critical note — no half on success.** The stat block does not include "or half as much on a successful one." This is all-or-nothing: fail = 11 avg force, success = 0. This is unusual for 5e area effects (Fireball, Thunderwave, etc. all halve on success). Intentional or oversight — see FEEL-R165-01.

**Sub-scenario A — Only Bazgar within 20 ft of shrine (thralls repositioned outside):**
- Bazgar DEX +1 vs DC 16: P(fail) = **70%** → Expected: 0.70 × 11 = **7.7 avg force**

**Sub-scenario B — Bazgar + both thralls within 20 ft of shrine:**
- Bazgar: 7.7 expected (same as above)
- Thrall A DEX +1 vs DC 16: P(fail) = 70% → Expected: 7.7 force (friendly fire — damages beholder's own thrall)
- Thrall B: same
- **Total expected:** 7.7 + 7.7 + 7.7 = 23.1 force, but 15.4 of it is friendly fire against thralls at 22 HP. Statistically kills both thralls in 2 Void Eruption uses.
- **Verdict:** Only viable when beholder has repositioned thralls clear of the 20-ft radius BEFORE firing Void Eruption.

**Sub-scenario C — Bazgar and Sabriel both within 20 ft (DM draws party toward shrine):**
- Bazgar: 7.7 expected
- Sabriel DEX +2 vs DC 16: P(fail) = 65% → Expected: 0.65 × 11 = **7.15**
- Combined: **~14.85 avg force**, no half — maximum lair damage output possible
- This scenario requires both melee PCs to close on the shrine simultaneously, which is the correct positioning outcome if the party engages derro near the altar

Manifest Thralls never optimal.

### Lair action frequency and coverage concern

With 3 options available each round, the decision tree is:
1. Is anyone near shrine AND no friendly thralls in zone? → Void Eruption
2. Are there PC melee threats near the beholder? → Unstable Ground
3. Otherwise? → Manifest Thralls (dead option; use to generate 1 THP buffer on thralls, which is still negligible)

**In practice, there is no round where Manifest Thralls is the right call.** This means the beholder has effectively 2 lair actions, not 3. The presence of a dead option in the rotation is a table-feel drag — the DM occasionally has to use it when the other two don't apply, and nothing meaningful happens.

**DB note:** All 3 lair actions (unstable_ground, manifest_thralls, void_eruption) are absent from actions.jsonl (DB-OPEN-06, open 60+ fires). If the DM types "eruption" or "ground" in the GUI, the runner will not find these as verbs — the DM must narrate all lair actions out-of-band. With 3–5 activations per encounter, this is significant friction.

---

### Void Eruption no-half: positional counterplay analysis

Without half on success, Void Eruption is unusual because:
1. **It punishes melee PCs specifically** — ranged casters won't be within 20 ft of shrine; the fighter and divine martial will be. This creates class-based pressure: melee PCs take Void Eruption, spellcasters take Disintegration Ray. Every PC is targeted by a different beholder system.
2. **No cover counterplay** — half-damage on success creates cover-finding / high-DEX save opportunities. All-or-nothing means the DC 16 save is the only variable.
3. **Expected damage variance:** For Bazgar (70% fail): 30% chance of 0 damage, 70% chance of 11+ damage. Over 3-5 lair uses: expected total 3×7.7=23.1 to 5×7.7=38.5 force damage. Not lethal on its own, but meaningful chip damage across a fight.

**Recommendation:** Keep all-or-nothing if intentional (the shrine's force is binary — you feel it or you don't). Add a stat block note: "This effect deals no damage on a successful save." to make intent explicit and prevent DM from adding half by habit.

### ROTATION-R165-01: thrulm-rotation.json slices vs rotation.yml

`thrulm-rotation.json` contains a `slices` array: `["threshold-patrol","shrine-wedge","tank-wall","beholder-escorts-limited","final-confrontation","solo-rager-rush","shardcaller-team","empty-void"]`. These names don't match any slice IDs in `rotation.yml` (`round-1-opener`, `disintegration-threat`, etc.). Only `next_index` is used from the JSON (per observed cron behavior — R163–R164 both used rotation.yml at position next_index). The `slices` list in the JSON appears to be a legacy/vestigial draft that was replaced by the detailed `rotation.yml` file.

**Risk:** A future cron implementation that reads the JSON's `slices` array as canonical would pick `"solo-rager-rush"` for index 5, not `"lair-action-round"`. This would silently produce wrong scenario labels with no error.

**Fix:** Either (a) update the JSON's `slices` to match rotation.yml IDs, or (b) remove the `slices` list from the JSON (keep only `next_index`). Safe edit; no stat block impact.
