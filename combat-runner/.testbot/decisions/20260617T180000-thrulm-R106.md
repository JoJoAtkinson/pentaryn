### Void Ray vs Disintegration Ray naming analysis

The slice asks: is the naming unambiguous for the DM at the table?

**Finding: BUG-R106-01 — "force ray" verb collision between DR and VR (NEW)**

From `actions.jsonl`:
- `disintegration_ray` verbs: `["disintegrate","disintegration","ray","force ray","beam"]`
- `void_ray` verbs: `["void ray","void","force ray","ray (legendary)"]`

**"force ray" appears in BOTH verb lists.** If the DM types "force ray" into the runner, the matching logic will return two candidate actions. Depending on order-of-file or runner precedence, this could dispatch either DR or VR — nondeterministic from the DM's perspective.

Additionally: "ray" appears in DR's verbs but not VR's. A DM who types "ray" gets only DR (correct if they meant DR, a bug if they meant VR). A DM who types "force ray" intending VR (a force-damage LA) may fire DR (the main-action recharge) instead.

The VR DB entry contains a save.notes field that partially documents the area-type confusion: "Despite the area-format output, treat this as a targeted attack on one creature." This is a workaround for the known `void_ray type="area"` bug (BUG-F7-02/A31-07 context). But it doesn't resolve the "force ray" verb collision.

**Disambiguation table (current state):**

| Verb | Dispatches to |
|------|--------------|
| "disintegrate" | DR only ✓ |
| "disintegration" | DR only ✓ |
| "ray" | DR only (ambiguous intent) |
| "force ray" | **BOTH — collision BUG-R106-01** |
| "beam" | DR only ✓ |
| "void ray" | VR only ✓ |
| "void" | VR only ✓ |
| "ray (legendary)" | VR only ✓ |

**Recommendation:** Remove "force ray" from DR's verb list. DR has sufficient unique verbs (disintegrate/disintegration/beam). VR retains "force ray" as a valid descriptor (VR is force damage, single-target). This is a DB fix requiring `combat_action_upsert` + validate — cannot auto-apply without MCP session. Log only.

### LA sequence vs DD-hold protocol conflict

**FEEL-R106-01 (NEW): rotation.yml `legendary-action-loop` slice contradicts FIX-FC40-A**

The slice prescribes: Tentacle (1 LA) after Marwen, VR (2 LA) after Bazgar = 3 LA fully spent before Sabriel acts.

But FIX-FC40-A (R1-LA-reserve protocol) requires: **hold ALL 3 LA through Bazgar's and Marwen's turns → spend 3 LA on DD after Sabriel ends her turn.**

These are mutually exclusive:
- **Option A (slice prescription):** Tentacle+VR = 38 avg HP (two targets), 0 LA for DD. Sabriel uses all her smite slots freely because DD never fires.
- **Option B (FIX-FC40-A):** 0 LA until Sabriel's turn ends → 3 LA → DD → CHA save DC 16 vs +5. If DD fires, Sabriel loses her L3 slot; beholder gains 6 temp HP. Bazgar and Marwen take 0 LA damage this round.

Expected value comparison (with Sabriel having L3 smite slot available):
- Option A: 26 avg HP total (LA phase) + Sabriel uses L3 smite freely (avg 14d6=49 extra radiant, but she'll likely spend only one smite)
- Option B: 0 HP (LA phase) + 50% DD succeeds → Sabriel loses L3 smite → she avoids spending it; beholder gains 6 THP

Sabriel's L3 smite = 4d8 avg = 18 additional damage per attack. If DD strips it (50% chance), beholder saves ~18 damage over the fight. Option B expected "damage saved" = 50% × 18 = 9 expected damage prevented. Option A deals 26 expected HP and gets Marwen grappled. **Option A wins on raw damage; Option B wins on strategic attrition of Sabriel's highest-value resource.**

The slice is teaching a valid rotation but one that is explicitly *wrong* per FIX-FC40-A when Sabriel has L2+ slots. The slice should add:

> **Caveat (applies only when Sabriel has no remaining L2+ slots or is downed):** If Sabriel has L2+ spell slots, the FIX-FC40-A DD-hold protocol (save all 3 LA for after Sabriel's turn, spend on Drain Divinity) supersedes this sequence. The Tentacle+VR split described here is the fall-back rotation for Round 2+ when DD opportunity has passed or Sabriel's slots are exhausted.

**Log only** — rotation.yml is a testbot guidance file; no player-facing impact. But the conflict could cause future runs to incorrectly evaluate LA economy by comparing "Tentacle+VR output" to "DD value" without noting they're mutually exclusive.

### Void Scream double-duty analysis (bonus observation)

In this initiative order (beholder init 21, beholder acts before lair), the prescribed protocol is:
- R1: Beholder main action = Multiattack. Hold all LA for DD-post-Sabriel (or spend on Tentacle+VR if DD path is blocked).
- VS (recharge 6) is held for R2.

If VS fires on R2 (init 21, beholder's turn), all 3 PCs are likely still in range (30 ft radius from beholder). VS avg 33 psychic, DC 16 Wis save:
- Bazgar Wis: likely +1 → 65% fail → avg 33 damage → Bazgar at 25 HP − 22 (half on save 65% fail rate): **expected VS damage to Bazgar ≈ 0.65×33 + 0.35×16.5 = 21.45 + 5.78 = 27.2**. Bazgar at 25 HP − 27 = **downed**. High probability.
- Marwen Wis: likely +1-2 → similar fail rate. Marwen at 18 HP (after LA Tentacle). Marwen − 22 (avg at moderate fail) → downed likely.
- Sabriel Wis: +3 (divine martial) → 65% fail → avg 27 expected → Sabriel at 44 HP − 27 = 17 HP, barely survives.

**VS on R2 following Tentacle LA grapple of Marwen**: Marwen is grappled (can't move) but can still save on VS (no grapple disadvantage on saves). However, Marwen at 18 HP vs VS expected 27 HP damage → Marwen likely downed R2 regardless. The grapple from the LA R1 didn't add much; VS on R2 would have downed her without the prior grapple.

**FEEL-R106-02 (NEW): Tentacle LA R1 into VS R2 combo on Marwen is redundant.** If VS fires R2 and one-shots Marwen anyway, the Tentacle (1 LA, costs the first of 3 LAs) that grappled Marwen R1 is wasted — it restricted her movement for one round and dealt 14 HP to a PC who would have been downed by VS the next turn regardless. The 1 LA would have been better spent on Tentacle vs Bazgar (who survives R2 VS at 25 HP, and Tentacle on Bazgar → Maw auto-crit on beholder's R2 turn could preemptively down Bazgar before VS even fires).

This isn't a rules bug — it's a feel issue: the prescribed Tentacle-Marwen LA is suboptimal if VS is about to fire on R2, and the optimal Tentacle target in that case is Bazgar. The slice description doesn't account for "VS planned next main turn" when prescribing the Tentacle target.
