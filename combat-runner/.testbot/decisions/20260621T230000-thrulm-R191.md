### Feel analysis

**Finding 1 — Three compounding bugs in one scenario:**

This scenario (`thrulm-shrine-touched-retaliation`) simultaneously hits:
1. BUG-R156-01 (OBR fires prompt, no dice rolled)
2. BUG-R159-01 (Ancient Resonance missing 1d4 psychic on failed save)
3. Unstable Form automation gap (DM must manually track and apply advantage)

All three gaps compound against the shrine-touched's core design fantasy ("dangerous when hurt, punishes attackers, escalating doom"). In a live runner session, the DM gets a narration prompt for OBR but must manually resolve the attack; sees resonance damage but must mentally add the psychic rider; and must remember Unstable Form advantage on top of that. The creature's mechanics are more complete on paper than they are in the runner.

**This scenario is the canonical regression test for BUG-R156-01 and BUG-R159-01.** Until those DB rows are updated, this scenario will surface only partial mechanics.

---

**Finding 2 — FEEL-R191-01 (NEW): Shrine-Touched effective HP vs Sabriel is ~22, not 45**

The shrine-touched has fire and radiant **vulnerability** (damage ×2). Sabriel is a level 5 divine martial with access to Divine Smite.

Level 5 divine martial Smite (2nd-level slot): 1d8+5 (weapon, STR+3+PB+2) + 2d8 (smite radiant) = 4.5+5+9 = 18.5 avg raw → **×2 vulnerability = 37 avg damage.**

This one-shots the shrine-touched (45 HP) approximately **P(kill in one Smite) ≈ 70%** (need ≥23 raw damage: P(1d8+5+2d8 ≥ 23) ≈ 70%).

Even without a Smite, Sabriel's regular radiant-damage option (Guiding Bolt or a blessed weapon): 4d6 radiant = 14 avg raw × 2 = 28 avg. Shrine-Touched dies in two regular radiant hits at most.

**Consequence:** The shrine-touched's entire design — Unstable Form triggers at ≥10 damage, OBR punishes attackers, Ancient Resonance escalates — requires the creature to survive 2+ turns under pressure. Against a party with a divine martial, it rarely survives to Round 2 from the shrine-touched's perspective. The "fights harder when hurt" theme never lands at this party composition.

**Rotation gap confirmed:** Expected Ancient Resonance uses before shrine-touched death vs Sabriel: ≤1 (first use is nearly the last). Recharge 5-6 (55% per round) is irrelevant if the creature dies in 1–2 turns.

**Recommend:** Increase Shrine-Touched HP to 60–65 (from 45) to allow 2 rounds of meaningful "bloodied" combat. This keeps it fragile to fire/radiant while giving Unstable Form and OBR time to register.

**Finding 3 — FEEL-R191-02 (NEW): Unstable Form advantage is most impactful for Shrine-Axe, but scenario fires resonance instead**

The scenario script fires Ancient Resonance (step 4) immediately after the OBR reaction (step 3). On the shrine-touched's regular turn (not captured in this scenario), it would use its Shrine-Axe multiattack with Unstable Form advantage. But:

- Ancient Resonance is a DEX save, not an attack roll — Unstable Form's advantage does not apply.
- The scenario tests OBR and resonance but NOT the Unstable Form advantage on attacks.

**Automation gap note:** To test Unstable Form advantage, the scenario would need to fire the shrine-touched's Shrine-Axe multiattack AFTER the OBR reaction. As written, this scenario does not expose the Unstable Form automation gap (it would pass without it). A follow-up scenario should add: `{kind: type, tab: shrine-touched-derro, text: "attack"}` to verify the multiattack output acknowledges advantage.

**Finding 4 — FEEL-R191-03 (NEW): OBR self-damage rate-limited to 1/round creates slower self-destruction than intended**

Oath-Breaking Retaliation reads: "Regardless of whether the attack hits, the derro takes 2 (1d4) psychic damage as the shrine's power consumes it from within."

Since OBR is a reaction, it can only trigger once per round (one reaction per creature). Even if 3 party members attack the shrine-touched in one round, OBR fires only once, and the self-damage is 1d4 (avg 2.5) per round maximum.

Over the expected 2–3 round encounter lifespan (before fire/radiant kills it): total self-damage ≈ 5–7.5 HP. This is ~10-17% of 45 HP — noticeable but not the "consuming from within" arc the text implies.

**Feel note:** The self-destruction theme reads more impactfully if OBR could trigger multiple times (e.g., once per attacker who deals damage, or free reaction once per turn rather than once per round). At current rate-limiting, the self-damage is incidental flavor, not a mechanical trajectory. Design question for Joe: is the once-per-round cap intentional, or should the self-damage accumulate per hit (at 1 per trigger, each attacker driving the shrine-touched faster toward its own death)?

### Legendary action economy note (broader encounter context)

The shrine-touched fights independently of the beholder's LA economy. In a round where the shrine-touched acts at init ~11 and Sabriel at ~11 (tied or adjacent):

- If Sabriel acts first (higher DEX tiebreak): hits for 12, OBR fires, shrine-touched retaliates, then shrine-touched takes resonance action — all before the beholder's LA cycle at the end of Sabriel's turn.
- This means the shrine-touched's counterplay (OBR + Resonance) can trigger BEFORE the beholder has used its legendary actions on Sabriel's turn end.

Beholder LA after Sabriel's turn: Void Ray (2 LA, DC 16 DEX, 22 avg force). If Sabriel just took OBR retaliation and resonance damage, the Void Ray arriving at end-of-Sabriel's-turn creates a compressing sequence. Sabriel's expected damage in one round:
- OBR (if hit, 30% vs AC 19): 9 avg × 0.30 = 2.7 expected
- Resonance (60% fail): 13 avg × 0.60 = 7.8 expected  
- Void Ray (60% fail, assuming DEX+1): 22 × 0.60 + 11 × 0.40 = 17.6 expected
- Total: ~28 avg damage on Sabriel in one combat round (44 HP → 16 HP)

Sabriel is functionally at one-hit range for Round 2. This is working-as-designed (party meant to lose) but the shrine-touched + beholder LA sequencing can feel unresponsive to counterplay.
