### New Findings

#### FEEL-F28-01: CW is a single-use R1 buff on the Rager; range-gated at R2+

**Severity:** Low-Medium — affects every encounter with this party composition.

**Finding:** The Shardcaller's Call Weakness (CW, 3/day) is designed to buff allies' attack rolls. However, the 30-ft range creates a structural dead zone: in R1, the Rager and SC are both at mid-field (~25 ft apart), so CW fires on the Rager. After the R1 charge, the Rager closes ~40–50 ft to reach melee, pushing the SC→Rager gap to ~45–55 ft — outside CW range. **CW can only fire on the Rager in R1 when SC's initiative is higher than the Rager's.** 

With SC init +2 and Rager init +0, SC beats Rager in initiative roughly 55% of the time (accounting for ties). In the other ~45% of encounters, either the Rager acts first (charges before CW can fire) or ties resolve ambiguously. In practice:

- R1, SC > Rager in initiative: CW fires on Rager before charge. 1 charge spent, 2 wasted.
- R1, SC ≤ Rager in initiative: Rager charges first; SC→Rager gap already 45+ ft. CW stranded from turn 1. All 3 charges wasted.

The Thrall Derro (range issue: within 30 ft only if near SC, but directed to charge toward PCs) and the Beholder (+7 to-hit already, CW adds minimal incremental value vs AC 18 at 45%) are poor secondary targets.

**Result:** 2 of 3 CW charges are reliably stranded every combat.

**Suggested resolution options (log only):**
- Reduce CW to 1/day (removes bookkeeping on 2 unused charges; same functional output)
- Extend CW range to 60 ft (buffs Rager in R2 when Rager has charged to melee)
- Allow CW to target the beholder (CW +6→+7 tentacle lash is mechanically minor but adds variety)

#### FEEL-F28-02: SC bonus-action conflict is sharpened when party wins initiative

**Severity:** Low — edge case in ~30–40% of combats.

**Finding:** In combats where Marwen (init +2) ties the SC (init +2) and rolls higher, or any PC closes to within 15 ft before SC's turn, Tactical Retreat fires as the SC's bonus action — blocking CW from firing at all.

SC's bonus-action priority (from checklist): TR fires over CW when a melee threat is within 15 ft. In a combat where the party advances aggressively in R1 (e.g., Bazgar charges the back line), SC must TR on its turn, losing the only window to CW the Rager before it charges.

This is a known design tradeoff (TR for survival vs CW for offense), but the practical frequency is higher than the tactic text suggests — the "TR over CW" path triggers in roughly 1/3 of combats, making CW on the Rager even less reliable than FEEL-F28-01 suggests.

**No fix suggested** — the mechanic is correct; the feel issue is that CW's value depends on a narrow initiative band (SC acts before Rager AND no PC closes within 15 ft). Documentation note only.
