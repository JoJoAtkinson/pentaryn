### New observations this fire

#### 1. Verb collision: "ray" → disintegration_ray, not void_ray_legendary

`disintegration_ray` verbs include `"ray"`. `void_ray_legendary` verbs include `"void ray"`, `"force ray"`, `"legendary ray"` — but not bare `"ray"`.

**Risk:** A DM at the table who types "ray" (common shorthand for Void Ray LA) will trigger Disintegration Ray (a recharge-5 main action) instead of void_ray_legendary (a 2-LA cost legendary action). The consequences differ significantly:
- Disintegration Ray: consumes recharge, outputs 10d8 force (attack roll), surfaces permanent-death condition
- Void Ray LA: costs 2 LA, outputs 4d10 force (DEX save DC 16)

Neither is the other. This is a **new verb-collision bug** not logged in R225.

**Fix path (log only):** Remove `"ray"` from `disintegration_ray` verbs, or add disambiguation prompt. Via `combat_action_upsert`. → **BUG-R226-01**

#### 2. Drain Divinity as LA is geometrically impossible at default hover height

Drain Divinity (3 LA) requires the target to be **within 30 ft**. The beholder's default combat hover position is 40-60 ft above the shrine altar. LA are used at the END of OTHER creatures' turns, not during the beholder's own turn when it could use Shrine-Drift (BA) to descend.

**Therefore:** If the beholder did not explicitly descend (via Shrine-Drift bonus action) during its own main turn, it will be out of Drain Divinity LA range for the entire round. The beholder can only use Drain Divinity as an LA if:
1. It descended on its main turn (spending its BA), AND
2. It chose to remain within 30 ft of a divine PC

This makes Drain Divinity a proactive decision at the START of the beholder's main turn (use BA to descend) rather than a reactive LA option. Yet the tactics text and cheat sheet treat it as an anytime LA option. This is a **tactics representation gap** — DMs running from the cheat sheet will attempt Drain Divinity as an LA and be confused when the target is 45 ft below.

**Implication for FEEL-R224-03 (Drain Divinity fires zero times in expected play):** The zero-fire rate isn't just about LA economy opportunity cost — it's that the LA is geometrically unavailable unless the beholder specifically set it up on its main turn by descending. Log as **BUG-R226-02 (MEDIUM — Drain Divinity LA requires positional setup not documented in tactics/CS)**.

#### 3. All-3-PCs grappled risk by end of round 1

Beholder multiattack = 2 Tentacle Lashes + 1 Maw. Tentacle LA = 1 more lash. In round 1:
- If both main-turn Tentacle Lashes hit different PCs → 2 grappled (Marwen + Bazgar, likely)
- If Tentacle LA after Marwen's turn targets Sabriel and hits → 3 grappled simultaneously

P(all three lashes hit different PCs given P(hit_Marwen)=0.65, P(hit_Bazgar)=0.50, P(hit_Sabriel)=0.45):
= 0.65 × 0.50 × 0.45 × (prob beholder targets all three) ≈ **15% per round**

If this occurs: all 3 PCs have speed 0. Beholder can drag them with Compel Thrall (BA), Maw vs any grappled PC, and systematic elimination. This is a near-TPK setup in round 1.

**Feel note:** 15% chance is not negligible over 3-5 rounds. It will happen ~once every 2-3 full encounters. The "designed to lose" framing holds, but a round-1 all-grappled scenario has ZERO counterplay — no PC can move, every attack on the beholder is at normal (not advantage) roll, escape requires spending the entire action. **BUG-R226-03 (FEEL — triple-grapple in round 1 possible with no counterplay window).**

#### 4. Void Ray damage in-chamber vs DB value

`void_ray_legendary` DB: `"damage": {"dice": "4d10", "type": "force"}` — no `damage_modifier` field. Void-Feeding adds +1 to damage rolls. Runner will output 4d10 without the +1. The cheat sheet's "22 (4d10) force" doesn't mention the in-chamber +1 either.

**Consequence:** In the sim above, Void Ray does 23 on fail (4d10+1), but runner will report 22 max. This is a consistent Void-Feeding omission across all beholder attack DB rows (noted in cheat sheet: "runner shows +6; correct in-chamber value is +7"). Same applies to damage. Not a new bug, but this fire confirms it applies to Void Ray specifically. Carry-forward monitoring note.

### Log-Only Items (New This Fire)

#### BUG-R226-01 (MEDIUM — verb collision "ray" → disintegration_ray vs intended void_ray)

**Finding:** Typing "ray" at the combat runner triggers `disintegration_ray` (recharge-5 main action, 10d8 attack roll) instead of `void_ray_legendary` (2-LA, 4d10 DEX save). A DM using "ray" as shorthand for the Void Ray LA gets the wrong ability — wrong action economy, wrong dice, wrong mechanic type.

**Fix path:** Via `combat_action_upsert`, remove `"ray"` from `disintegration_ray.verbs`. Replace with something unambiguous ("disintegrate", "DR", "death ray"). Or add `"void ray"` disambiguation check to the runner. Cannot auto-fix.

#### BUG-R226-02 (MEDIUM — Drain Divinity LA geometrically unavailable at default hover height)

**Finding:** Drain Divinity range is 30 ft. Default beholder hover is 40-60 ft above altar. Legendary actions execute at end of OTHER creatures' turns — the beholder cannot use Shrine-Drift (its movement BA) during LA. Therefore Drain Divinity as an LA is only available if the beholder descended on its OWN main turn (spending BA on Shrine-Drift to close within 30 ft).

**Impact:** Tactics section and cheat sheet treat Drain Divinity as an available LA option each round. In practice it requires explicit setup the previous turn, and descending puts the beholder in melee range (Tentacle Lash territory, Antireality exposed). DMs reading the CS at the table will attempt the LA and discover the range problem mid-combat.

**Fix path (log only):** Add to cheat sheet tactics section: "Drain Divinity requires beholder to descend to within 30 ft on its own turn (Shrine-Drift BA) — plan one turn ahead." Also add to the Drain Divinity stat block text as a positioning note.

#### BUG-R226-03 (FEEL — triple-grapple round-1 scenario has zero counterplay)

**Finding:** Beholder can target all 3 PCs with Tentacle Lash in round 1 (2 from multiattack + 1 LA). If all three land (~15% per round), all PCs have speed 0 and must spend their full action escaping grapple (DC 16 contested — hard for Marwen). This removes all tactical options for a full round and accelerates the "designed to lose" outcome into a no-agency TPK.

**Joe's decision required:** Is a 15% round-1 all-grappled outcome acceptable? Options:
| Option | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| A: Keep | 15% coinflip into zero-agency TPK setup; acceptable for boss encounter |
| B: Rule beholder targets no more than 2 PCs with tentacles per round | Cap grapple to create some positioning counterplay |
| C: LA Tentacle costs 2 actions instead of 1 | Slows grapple-stacking; reduces Tentacle+Void Ray LA combo to 3 actions exactly (still works) |
