## Phase B — Playtest slice: `drain-divinity-vs-sabriel`

*(thrulm-rotation.json index 4 — Drain Divinity legendary action economy test)*

### Setup

- **Beholder:** HP 110, AC 17, 3 LA/round. Void-Feeding active. Hovering 40-50 ft above altar.
- **Drain Divinity (Costs 3 LA):** Targets one creature within 30 ft that has spell slots/divine powers. DC 16 CHA save. On fail: lose highest remaining spell slot; beholder gains THP = twice that slot's level. Range constraint: beholder must descend to ≤30 ft to use.
- **Sabriel (divine martial 5):** HP 44, AC 19. Assumed Paladin 5 slot table: 2×3rd, 3×2nd, 4×1st. CHA assumed +2. CHA save bonus: +2 (no CHA save proficiency unless Paladin). Wait — Paladins DO get CHA and WIS save proficiencies at level 1. So Sabriel's CHA save = CHA +2 + PB +3 = +5. Reread rotation.yml: "assume +2 Cha for Sabriel." This appears to be the total modifier assumed in the rotation, not just the CHA score modifier.
  - Using rotation assumption of CHA save +2: P(fail) = P(d20+2 < 16) = P(d20 < 14) = 0.65.
  - If Paladin with prof (+5): P(fail) = P(d20+5 < 16) = P(d20 < 11) = 0.50.
  - Both cases simulated below.

### Three-round Drain Divinity simulation

**Assumption A (rotation spec: +2 total modifier — 65% fail rate):**

| Round | Slot drained (on fail) | Beholder THP gained | P(fail) | E[THP] |
|-------|------------------------|---------------------|---------|--------|
| 1 | 3rd-level (one of 2) | 6 | 65% | 3.9 |
| 2 | 3rd-level (last one) | 6 | 65% | 3.9 |

**P(all 3 saves failed):** 0.125 (12.5%).  
**E[slots lost over 3 rounds]:** 1.5 slots.

### Key questions from rotation spec

**Q1: Is this mechanically sound?**
Yes. The slot-drain chain is internally consistent. Draining 3rd-level slots removes Sabriel's highest-impact actions (Divine Smite at 3rd level = 4d8 radiant, avg 18). The DC 16 vs +2 CHA (65% drain rate) is aggressive; if Sabriel has Paladin CHA-save proficiency (+5), the drain rate is 50% — more balanced. The cheat sheet, however, uses "+2" without proficiency, which may understate Sabriel's actual save bonus. **Flag for Joe: clarify whether Sabriel has CHA save proficiency. Paladin 5 normally does; if so, the rotation spec's +2 is incorrect.**

**Q2: Does spending all 3 LA on one effect feel correct?**
Mechanically valid. Thematically strong (hunger feeds on faith's absence). But it creates a deterministic one-note pattern — see FEEL-DD-R264-01 below.

**Q3: THP gain — relevant to combat?**
No. THP = 6/6/4 from three rounds, non-additive. At beholder HP 110, the THP represents a minor buffer absorbed by any single hit. Bazgar's multiattack (Fighter 5, action surge available) outputs ~20-30 damage per action. The THP never meaningfully changes the beholder's survival. The THP mechanic reads as flavor; it should either be buffed to be relevant or explicitly acknowledged as flavor-only in the cheat sheet to prevent DM confusion.

**Descent requirement analysis (FEEL-DD-R264-02):**
To use Drain Divinity, beholder must be within 30 ft of Sabriel on the ground. Beholder's normal hover altitude: 40-60 ft. Required descent: 10-30 ft. Available via:
1. Shrine-Drift (bonus action, 30 ft move) — drops beholder from 40 ft to 10 ft if fully committed; but the beholder then risks ending turn low (Sabriel melee range on her turn if she isn't frightened).
2. Legendary Move (1 LA) — costs 1 LA from the 3 available; net: 2 LA remaining after Move, still enough for nothing (DD costs 3). So Move + DD in same "round" = not possible without starting the round close.

**Practical constraint**: the beholder must position within 30 ft BEFORE Drain Divinity fires (end of Sabriel's turn = after Sabriel's move). If the beholder used Shrine-Drift as its bonus action on its own turn to descend, and Sabriel hasn't closed to melee, the window opens. But if Sabriel uses her turn to RETREAT (frightened PCs can't approach, but already-frightened Sabriel may move away), the range can exceed 30 ft and DD fails to fire.

This creates a **genuine decision point**: a frightened Sabriel moving away each turn may effectively negate Drain Divinity. The beholder's only response is Legendary Move (1 LA) to re-close. This interplay isn't currently documented.
