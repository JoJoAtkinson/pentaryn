**Source (rotation.yml, index 6):** *Beholder drops to 28 HP (below threshold). Per tactics:
retreats toward lower shaft, uses thralls to block. Does it have a meaningful path given hover

---

### Setup

**Context:** Beholder has taken meaningful damage and is at 28 HP (below the 30 HP retreat
threshold stated in tactics). The party has taken casualties or damage consistent with 2-3
rounds of combat against a CR 13 beholder. For this simulation:

- Bazgar: 21 HP (took Tentacle + Void Ray)
- Marwen: 0 HP (stabilized, making death saves — unconscious since round 1)
- Sabriel: 30 HP (took Void Eruption lair action)
- Beholder: 28 HP (Sabriel landed radiant smite + Bazgar landed 2 attacks using Precision Attack)
- Active thralls: 2 (HP 22 and 14 respectively — one took splash damage)
- No derro ragers remaining (Bazgar eliminated them)
- Shardcaller: still active at ~25 HP, 40 ft back

---

### Retreat Mechanic Analysis

**Does Shrine-Drift work as an escape tool?**

Shrine-Drift (bonus action): "The beholder moves up to 30 feet. It can move through other
creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain; it takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it
ends its turn inside a creature or object."

- Range: 30 ft per use (bonus action, 1/turn — no per-rest limit stated)
- Phasing: can pass through walls, pillars, collapsed shrine remains
- Self-damage: 5 avg if ending inside object (usually avoidable)
- Movement action: 30 ft fly available on top of Shrine-Drift → **60 ft total per turn**

**Retreat path from 28 HP threshold:**

Encounter description (from _overview.md if present, otherwise inferred from cheat sheet geometry):
Beholder hovers 40-60 ft above shrine altar in a large vault. Lower shaft entrance is beyond
the main chamber (~60+ ft from altar).

At 28 HP, beholder retreat sequence:
- Turn N: Shrine-Drift (bonus action, 30 ft toward shaft entrance, passing through stone if needed)
            + Move action (30 ft fly toward shaft)
            = 60 ft progress toward shaft entrance
- Thralls move to block party (Compel Thrall BA if beholder isn't using it for Shrine-Drift... wait)

**CONFLICT IDENTIFIED — BUG-R333-01:**

Shrine-Drift is the beholder's **bonus action**. Compel Thrall is ALSO the beholder's **bonus action**
(1/turn, same slot). Per 5e: a creature can take only ONE bonus action per turn. So the beholder
cannot BOTH Shrine-Drift AND Compel Thrall in the same turn.

Retreat scenario:
- If beholder uses Shrine-Drift (BA) to escape → cannot Compel Thrall that turn → thralls act
  independently (no redirect) but may still attack or use their own initiative
- If beholder uses Compel Thrall (BA) to redirect thralls as a rear guard → cannot Shrine-Drift
  → must use normal 30 ft fly speed only → retreat is slower (30 ft/turn vs 60 ft/turn)

This conflict is not documented anywhere in the stat block, start-of-turn checklist, tactics
section, or cheat sheet. The tactics section says "uses thralls to block pursuit" but doesn't
note that this costs the Shrine-Drift bonus action. At the table, a DM choosing the "dramatic
retreat with thrall screen" will implicitly choose to not Shrine-Drift — losing half their
escape distance per turn.

**Severity:** Medium. Affects late-fight decisions in the one scenario where retreat matters.
Auto-fix not appropriate (design call). Log for Joe.

---

### Retreat Path Viability

**With Shrine-Drift (60 ft/turn, no thrall direction):**

If shaft entrance is 90-120 ft away (typical large vault size), beholder can reach it in 2 turns
without thrall blocking. Party must close 90-120 ft in 2 turns or the beholder escapes.

- Bazgar move: 30 ft/turn (or 60 with Dash). Can use Dash to cover 60 ft in one turn.
  If beholder starts 90 ft away, Bazgar needs 2 turns to reach it at Dash pace — but beholder
  will be AT the shaft by then.
- Sabriel (30 ft speed, divine martial): similar. May not be able to stop the beholder.
- Marwen unconscious — no help.

**With Compel Thrall (30 ft/turn + thrall screen):**

Thrall intercepts Bazgar at 30 ft: Bazgar must eliminate thrall (~22 HP, AC 14, +1 to hit = 2
attacks likely kills it) or go around (costs 5 ft disengage or OA). This costs Bazgar ~1 turn.
Meanwhile beholder retreats 30 ft at fly speed. 90-ft gap: beholder needs 3 turns.

**Result:** Both retreat paths are viable mechanically, but they represent different design choices
that are NOT documented as an either/or decision point for the DM.

---

### Feel Assessment

**FEEL-R333-01: Retreat scene exists in tactics text but has no mechanical hook at the table**

The tactics section says "if reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower
shaft, using thralls to block pursuit." This is flavor text, not a mechanical protocol. At the
table:
- DM must remember to trigger the retreat (no checklist item prompts it)
- Thrall-blocking vs Shrine-Drift conflict (above) goes undocumented
- Lower shaft is referenced ("something worse down there") but has no stat block or encounter note
- The retreat could be completely bypassed if party is fast enough — is there a minimum distance
  the beholder MUST cover before the shaft closes? No text covers this.

**FEEL-R333-02: Retreat at 28 HP may be too late for a 3-PC level-5 party**

At 28 HP, the beholder can still deal roughly:
- Multiattack: ~21 (14+14+22×70% hit Maw on grappled) = brutal burst
- Void Ray (2 LA): ~18.7 expected force
- Tentacle LA (1 LA): ~10.5 expected

If the party is at Bazgar 21 HP + Sabriel 30 HP (total 51 combined HP), the beholder's one
final multiattack round could drop one or both of them. The retreat is almost certainly a "fake"
— the beholder doesn't have a true escape route if the party chooses to ignore their own lives
and chase. But if they DON'T chase, the encounter just ends with the beholder at 28 HP, which
is anticlimactic.

**Recommendation:** Add a start-of-turn checklist item: "If beholder HP ≤ 30 AND the lower shaft
is accessible: begin retreat protocol. Priority this turn: Move toward shaft (bonus action
Shrine-Drift OR Compel Thrall, not both). If the shaft is sealed/blocked: abandon retreat,
fight to the death." This makes the retreat mechanically explicit.

**FEEL-R333-03: "Something worse" down the lower shaft is a narrative promise without delivery**

Both the stat block and the tactics reference "something in the lower shaft that scares even this
creature." This is a strong atmospheric hook that could function as a de-escalation tool (party
can threaten to open the shaft to force the beholder into a deal). But there are no mechanics,
no encounter note, no hints about what the threat is. At the table, a curious player who asks
"what does the beholder fear in that shaft?" currently has no answer — the DM must improvise.

This is a worldbuilding gap rather than a mechanical bug. Log only.

---

### LA Economy During Retreat Round

When retreating, beholder has 3 LA available. Best spend:
- Move (1 LA) — 30 ft positioning
- Tentacle (1 LA) on Bazgar pursuing — avg 14 bludg + grapple
- Void Ray (2 LA) on Sabriel pursuing — avg 23 force (with Void-Feeding +1)
= 4 LA total needed. But only 3 LA available. Must choose Move+Tentacle OR Tentacle+VoidRay.

**Dominant choice during retreat: Tentacle+VoidRay (damage > repositioning).**
The beholder uses Shrine-Drift (BA) to cover distance instead of LA Move, freeing the LA budget
for maximum damage output. This is the single most efficient retreat sequence but requires
knowing to use BA for movement and LA for attacks — not explicit in tactics.
