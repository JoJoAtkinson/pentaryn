### Simulation A — Disintegration Ray hits Marwen (round 1, pre-charged)

**Attack: +7 vs Marwen AC 15 → needs 8+ → 65% hit.**

Roll: d20+7 = 15+7 = 22 vs AC 15. **HIT.**

Damage: 10d8 force. Representative roll: 4+7+2+8+3+6+1+7+5+8 = **51 force.**
(Average: 45; Void-Feeding +1 = 46. Either way: 46+ >> Marwen max HP 32.)

Marwen: 32 HP → 0 HP. Reduced to 0 HP. **DISINTEGRATED.** Turned to ash.

At level 5: No True Resurrection, no Wish. Marwen's player has lost their character on round 1,
before acting (initiative order: Beholder 18 > Marwen 14).

**Time-to-fire:** DR is pre-charged at combat start. Beholder acts on init 18 before Marwen
(init 14). Marwen has not rolled a die in this combat session.

### Simulation B — Disintegration Ray misses (35% case), then Drain Divinity vs LA split

**Attack: d20+7 = 6+7 = 13 vs AC 15. MISS.** (Rolled a 6; happens 35% of the time.)

DR is spent. Recharge required (d6 = 5 or 6, 33% per round).

**Beholder has 3 LA available (refreshed at start of beholder's turn).**

#### Option 1: Drain Divinity (3 LA) on Marwen after her turn

After Marwen acts (init 14), beholder uses Drain Divinity:
- Target: Marwen (Wizard 5 — has spell slots, qualifies)
- DC 16 CHA save: Marwen CHA +0 → needs 16+ (25% save). Expected: **75% fail.**
- On fail: Marwen loses highest slot (3rd level, if not already spent). Beholder gains 6 THP.
- Beholder HP-equivalent gain: 6 THP (marginal)
- Marwen loses: one 3rd-level slot (Fireball, Counterspell, Hypnotic Pattern, etc.)
- Remaining LA: **0** (all 3 spent on Drain Divinity)

**Expected value of Drain Divinity on Marwen:**
- 0 immediate damage
- 0.75 × (1 slot lost) = prevention of ~0.75 × avg spell damage (~25 for Fireball) ≈ **18.75 EV** over future rounds
- 6 THP buffer for beholder

#### Option 2: Tentacle LA (1 LA) + Void Ray LA (2 LA) split

After Marwen's turn: **Tentacle (1 LA)** → +7 vs Marwen AC 15 → 65% hit → 13 avg damage + grapple.
After Bazgar's turn: **Void Ray (2 LA)** → DC 16 DEX save vs Bazgar DEX +1 → 70% fail → 23 avg force.

**Expected value of split:**
- Tentacle: 0.65 × 13 = **8.45 avg damage**
- Void Ray: 0.70 × 23 = **16.1 avg damage**
- Total: **24.55 avg damage to party**
- Grapple: if Tentacle hits, Marwen is grappled (65% chance). Sets up Maw autocrit on
  unconscious target next turn (if beholder doesn't use DR on Marwen when recharged).

**Comparison:**

| LA Option | Immediate Damage | Future Value | LA Cost |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|---------|
| Drain Divinity (Marwen) | 0 | ~18.75 EV (slot prevention) + 6 THP | 3 LA (all) |
| Tentacle + Void Ray | 24.55 avg | grapple control | 3 LA (1+2) |

**Finding:** Against Marwen specifically, Drain Divinity is **inferior in immediate output** (0 vs 24.55 damage) and roughly comparable in future-value terms (18.75 EV prevention vs grapple + forced healing expenditure). However, the beholder loses **all LA flexibility** with Drain Divinity, while the split delivers 24+ guaranteed damage and leaves the grapple in play.

#### Option 3: Drain Divinity (3 LA) on Sabriel after her turn (divine caster focus)

After Sabriel acts (init 8), beholder uses Drain Divinity:
- Target: Sabriel (divine martial — has divine spell slots)
- DC 16 CHA save: Sabriel CHA +2 → needs 14+ (35% save). Expected: **65% fail.**
- On fail: Sabriel loses highest slot (3rd level paladin slot). Beholder gains 6 THP.
- Sabriel loses: one 3rd-level Divine Smite (= 4d8 radiant = avg 18 radiant damage prevented)

**Expected value of Drain Divinity on Sabriel:**

- 6 THP

vs the split (Tentacle on Sabriel + Void Ray on Bazgar):
- Tentacle: 0.45 × 13 = 5.85 avg damage (AC 19 is harder to hit: needs 10+, 55% hit)
- Void Ray: 0.70 × 23 = 16.1 avg damage (same as above, targeting Bazgar)
- Total: **21.95 avg damage**

**Against Sabriel, Drain Divinity is even more inferior:** 11.7 EV prevention vs 21.95 immediate damage.

### NEW FEEL-R185-01: Drain Divinity is quantifiably suboptimal in rounds 1–2

**Summary:** Across all Phase B simulations, Drain Divinity (3 LA) is strictly dominated by the
Tentacle (1 LA) + Void Ray (2 LA) split in the first two rounds of combat:

| Scenario | Drain Divinity EV | Split EV | Deficit |
|----------|------------------|----------|---------|
| vs Marwen (wizard) | ~18.75 (future only) | 24.55 (immediate) + grapple | –5.8 + grapple |
| vs Sabriel (divine) | ~11.7 (future only) | 21.95 (immediate) + grapple | –10.25 + grapple |

Drain Divinity becomes competitively valuable only in **mid-to-late combat (rounds 3+)** when:
- The beholder has already applied grapple control via Multiattack
- Sabriel is running low on slots (her remaining smites are her primary output)
- The beholder's HP is high enough that the 6 THP buffer matters

**Design recommendation (LOG ONLY — Joe decides):**

The tactics section currently reads: "Against Clerics/Paladins: Focuses on Drain Divinity to remove
their healing and buffs. It hates sanctified weapons and holy water."

This guidance leads DMs to open with Drain Divinity every round, spending all 3 LA for less expected
value than the split. A two-line addition would fix this:

> *"Drain Divinity is most effective mid-to-late combat (round 3+) after the beholder has
> established grapple control. In rounds 1–2, the Tentacle (1 LA) + Void Ray (2 LA) split deals
> ~24 avg damage vs Drain Divinity's 0 — use the split first, then drain slots once Sabriel's
> smites become the primary damage threat."*

### NEW FEEL-R185-02: Disintegration Ray vs Multiattack — EV comparison when pre-charged

When DR is pre-charged at round 1:

| Action Choice | Expected Damage | Variance | Secondary Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Disintegration Ray (vs Marwen) | 0.65 × 45 = **29.25 avg** | High (35% = 0 damage) | 65% permanent kill |
| Multiattack (TL×2 + Maw vs party) | ~23.85 avg (three independent rolls) | Low | Grapple control |

DR has higher single-action EV (29.25 vs 23.85) but carries 35% catastrophic miss (action entirely
wasted). Multiattack is more reliable: the three independent attack rolls almost guarantee some
damage output. Moreover, Multiattack generates **grapple control** which multiplies future damage
(unconscious grappled target = Maw autocrit next round).

The tactics section says "Prioritizes disintegration of spellcasters" without noting this trade-off.
A DM following this literally will open with DR every time it's charged, even when Multiattack
would yield better floor damage and tactical positioning.

**Design recommendation (LOG ONLY):**

> *"Use Disintegration Ray on a fresh charge (recharge roll at the start of the beholder's turn)
> only when Marwen is not already grappled or below 15 HP. If she's already set up for a Maw
> autocrit, the Multiattack delivers equal or higher expected value with grapple control preserved."*

### Probability distribution: how long before Marwen is permanently dead?

With DR pre-charged at round 1 (65% hit → instant death):
- P(dead round 1) = 0.65
- P(alive after round 1) = 0.35 → DR not fired or missed

For subsequent rounds (assuming DR must recharge after firing):
- P(recharge per round) = 0.33; then P(hit | charged) = 0.65
- P(fires and hits a given round | no prior kill) ≈ 0.33 × 0.65 = 0.215

Cumulative P(Marwen permanently dead within N rounds):
- Round 1: 65%
- Round 2: 65% + 0.35 × 21.5% = **72.5%**
- Round 3: 72.5% + 0.275 × 21.5% ≈ **78.4%**
- Round 4: ~82%
- Round 5: ~85%

Even if Marwen survives round 1 (35% chance), the beholder has ~73–85% probability of
permanently killing her within the first 3–5 rounds. The party cannot meaningfully intervene
(no Counterspell-as-reaction exists for attack rolls; no teleportation at level 5 without Misty
Step which costs Marwen her own action; no cover that negates the beholder's truesight).

**This is the correct design intent (party is meant to lose), but the permanent death outcome —
no raise dead at level 5 — should be a DM-informed decision before the encounter starts.**

### Antireality on Disintegration Ray

If the beholder uses DR as a ranged spell attack and a PC uses Sharpshooter or a reaction to
impose disadvantage back... actually no PC at level 5 in this party has such a reaction.

But: can Antireality apply to DR? Antireality fires "when the beholder is targeted by an attack
it can see." DR is an action the beholder TAKES, not an attack against it. Antireality cannot
protect DR from anything; it's a reaction the beholder uses when being attacked, not when it
attacks. No interaction here.
