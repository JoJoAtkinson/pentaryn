### Phase B — Manual Simulation: Maw Auto-Crit Kill Vector (Round 1)

**Encounter slice context (R1, full party):**
- Party: Bazgar (HP 49, AC 18), Marwen (HP 32, AC 15), Sabriel (HP 44, AC 19)
- Beholder: HP 110, AC 17, Void-Feeding active (+1 attack/damage in shrine chamber)

**Initiative (representative roll):**

| Init | Actor | HP |
|------|-------|-----|
| 20 | Lair action | — |
| 15 | Beholder | 110 |
| 13 | Bazgar | 49 |
| 12 | Marwen | 32 |
| 10 | Sabriel | 44 |

**Init 20 — Lair Action:**

Choice: Unstable Ground on Bazgar (melee fighter, DC 16 DEX, Bazgar DEX −1 → needs 17+ to succeed → ~80% fail rate).
- Expected: fail → Bazgar prone.

**Beholder's turn (init 15):**

Beholder uses Shrine-Drift (BA) to descend from 40-ft hover to ≤5 ft above Bazgar. This consumes the bonus action (cannot use Compel Thrall this turn).

Multiattack at ≤5 ft from prone Bazgar: both TL (10-ft reach) and Maw (5-ft reach) qualify for melee-vs-prone advantage (PHB p.292: attacker within 5 ft of prone target). **All three attacks have advantage.**

```
TL-1 with advantage vs prone Bazgar (AC 18):
  Base hit needed: 11+. Hit rate without advantage: 55%.
  With advantage: P(hit) = 1 − (1−0.55)² = 1 − 0.2025 = 79.75% ≈ 80%.
  Expected: hit. Damage: 13 (3d6+3) bludg. Bazgar grappled.

TL-2 with advantage vs prone grappled Bazgar (AC 18):
  Same odds: 80%. Expected: hit. Damage: 13. Second tentacle latches.

Maw with advantage vs prone grappled Bazgar (AC 18):
  Hit rate: 80%. If hit AND target is grappled → auto-crit: 8d8+3 avg 39 (not 4d8+3 avg 21).
  Expected: hit. Damage: 39 (auto-crit).

Total R1 main-action damage on Bazgar (expected path):
  13 + 13 + 39 = 65.  Bazgar (49 HP) → 0 HP, ~16 overflow.
```

**Probability of Bazgar downed by multiattack (prone path):**
- P(at least 1 TL hit, enabling grapple + auto-crit Maw): 1 − P(both miss) = 1 − 0.20² = 96%.
- P(Maw hits with advantage): 80%.
- P(Bazgar downed) = 96% × 80% = **~77%** (any 1-TL + Maw hit is sufficient because 13+39=52 > 49).
- Combined with UG prone probability: 0.80 × 0.77 = **~62% chance Bazgar is downed before his initiative (13) ever arrives.**

**Compare: without prone (no UG descent, standard multiattack at non-advantage):**
- TL hit rate: 55% each. P(at least 1 TL hit) = 80%. Maw hit rate: 55% (no advantage).
- P(Bazgar downed from multiattack): P(at least-1-TL + Maw hit, 52+ dmg): 80% × 55% = **44%**.

UG + prone advantage raises the R1 kill probability by +33 percentage points (44% → 77%).

**Rounds 2–3 (62% path — Bazgar downed R1):**

Remaining party: Marwen (32 HP) and Sabriel (44 HP). Beholder has 3 LA to spend (held for post-Sabriel DD per FIX-FC40-A).

- Bazgar's turn: unconscious, rolls death saves.
- Marwen's turn: casts something (Fireball if Sabriel is nearby? Or Scorching Ray at beholder). Beholder resists nonmagic B/P/S but NOT fire. Fireball 8d6 avg 28, no resistance: beholder 110 → 82 HP.
- Sabriel's turn: Lay on Hands (action, not a spell — 25 HP pool) to revive Bazgar at 25 HP. **DD watch fires post-Sabriel turn.** LoH is not a spell slot → DD cannot target it. Beholder redirects: **Void Ray (2 LA, DC 16 DEX, 22 force avg)** at Bazgar (now at 25 HP, DEX −1 → 80% fail). Expected: 22 damage → Bazgar at 3 HP. 1 LA remaining: Tentacle at Marwen post-Sabriel turn? No — LA fires only after another creature's turn, and Sabriel just ended hers. 1 LA can fire at end of the *next* PC's turn (Bazgar's turn if he recovers).

*Sabriel's LoH + VR redirect: Bazgar returned to 25 HP, then immediately struck for 22 avg damage, net 3 HP remaining. He hasn't taken a turn yet this round. Sabriel spent her action on LoH and cannot smite R1. DD still fires after Sabriel's R2 turn (if any slots remain).*

- Beholder R2 turn (3 LA refreshed): Multiattack targeting Marwen (lowest HP, AC 15). TL: +7 vs AC 15 = 65% hit. Maw: +7 vs AC 15 = 65% hit. If grappled: 8d8+3 auto-crit avg 39. P(grapple + crit Maw): 65% × 65% = 42%. Expected damage on Marwen ≈ 0.65×13 + 0.42×39 = 8.5 + 16.4 = 24.9 avg. Marwen (32 HP) → ~7 HP. Vulnerable to DR this turn if recharged (33% chance).

**Round 3 outlook:** Bazgar at 3 HP, Marwen at ~7 HP, Sabriel at 44 HP (no actions spent except LoH). Beholder at 82 HP, 3 LA available, DR possibly recharged. Party is in crisis by end of round 2.

### Phase B — Simulation Observations

**1. Auto-crit Maw target ambiguity: not a problem when beholder descends.** When both TL hit Bazgar and the beholder is at ≤5 ft, Maw clearly targets grappled Bazgar. No third-PC confusion if the DM follows FIX-FC39-A ("Maw MUST then target the grappled PC"). Scenario assertion satisfied. ✓

**2. The real ambiguity: what if the beholder doesn't descend?** At 10-ft hover: TL hits, Bazgar grappled, but Maw (5-ft reach) cannot reach a target 10 ft away. The multiattack can't fire the Maw component. DM options: (a) skip Maw; (b) retarget Maw at another creature within 5 ft (none present at 10-ft hover). Neither is documented. This is the new FEEL-R90-01 gap below.

**3. UG + prone + descent creates ~77% R1 kill vector.** This is higher than prior estimates because NEW-R85-01 (which said TL never benefits from prone advantage) was incorrect. At ≤5 ft, TL has advantage too. The 77% path (any TL hit + Maw crit) occurs 77% of the time the beholder descends; descent is available whenever Shrine-Drift (BA) is free (i.e., not spent on Compel Thrall). In R1, SD is free. So the UG+descent+multiattack sequence is available ~80% of fights (UG fail rate), with ~77% kill probability on the sequence → net 62% Bazgar-downed-R1 rate.

**4. Legendary action analysis.** The simulation confirms FEEL-R83-02 (one-note LA strategy): post-R1, all reactive LAs cycle to Tentacle + VR. Move LA is never optimal. DD depletes to useless by R2 (Sabriel's smite slots stripped). After that, the LA decision tree reduces to: Tentacle (1 LA) after the lower-HP PC's turn, VR (2 LA) after the higher-threat PC's turn. No other LA combination produces more damage.
