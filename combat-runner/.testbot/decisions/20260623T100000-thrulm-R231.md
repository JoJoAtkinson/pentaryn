### Key Observations

**1. (FEEL-RETREAT-01 / HIGH-NEW) The retreat is the most lethal phase, not a de-escalation.**

At 27 HP, the beholder's turn still consists of full Multiattack (two Tentacle hits = two KOs in this simulation) plus bonus-action escape through stone. There is no "hurt animal" feel — it's an apex predator exiting at full lethality. The 30 HP threshold reads as "retreat begins" but mechanically it's "alpha strike + disappear." The party has no action-economy moment to stop it or race it. This contradicts the design intention of a tactical retreat with counterplay.

**Design flag:** Consider reducing the beholder to single attack on a retreat turn (if at <30 HP, cannot use Multiattack — uses only one Tentacle Lash), or triggering a Void Scream auto-recharge on the retreat turn as a different kind of "dramatic exit."

---

**2. (FEEL-RETREAT-02 / MEDIUM — CONFIRMED THIS FIRE) Bonus action conflict: "thralls block pursuit" tactic is mechanically impossible.**

The tactics section says: "retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit."

To execute this intent, the beholder would need:
- Bonus action: **Compel Thrall** (moves thrall 30 ft to block corridor)
- Bonus action: **Shrine-Drift** (beholder phases through stone to escape)

These are both bonus actions. A creature gets one bonus action per turn. The beholder cannot do both. If it uses Shrine-Drift (necessary to actually escape through stone), Thrall 1 stays put. If it uses Compel Thrall to reposition the thrall, it can't phase through the wall this turn and must use its fly speed (30 ft) to reach the shaft entrance — likely staying within melee range of the party for one more round.

**Fix path (Joe's call):** Either give Compel Thrall a free "on retreat" trigger (when beholder HP < 30, once per combat), or redesign Shrine-Drift as a full movement option (not a bonus action) so the beholder can use its BA for Compel Thrall.

---

**3. (FEEL-RETREAT-03 / LOW-NEW) Legendary Action line-of-sight through stone wall — undefined.**

After phasing through stone, the beholder has 3 LA available. Void Ray (2 LA) is "Ranged Spell Attack: +6 to hit, range 120 ft." Ranged attacks typically require line of sight.

The beholder's Alien Perception says it senses creatures within 60 ft of the shrine through void-field, but sensing ≠ attack-targeting. Without an explicit exception like "ignores line of sight," the beholder should NOT be able to use Void Ray through stone.

If this ruling holds: the beholder exits with 3 LA and can only use Move (1 LA) from the other side of the wall. Its entire LA budget is wasted on the retreat turn. This feels anticlimactic and also removes potential "finishing blow through the wall" pressure.

**Fix path (Joe's call):** Add a line to Alien Perception or the LA section: "The beholder can use Legendary Actions through stone and walls while it can sense creatures through the shrine's void-field." Or: "Legendary Actions that require a ranged attack still require line of sight."

---

**4. (FEEL-RETREAT-04 / LOW-NEW) No chase-scene drama — party is already wiped.**

The retreat scenario assumes the party has taken meaningful damage before the beholder retreats. In the simulation, the party entered the retreat round with: Bazgar at 22 HP, Marwen at 8 HP, Sabriel already at 0 HP on death saves. The beholder's exit multiattack finishes Bazgar and Marwen on the same turn it escapes. There is no round in which the party attempts to "catch the beholder" — they're all unconscious before they get a turn after the retreat begins.

This means the "retreat" encounter beat does not happen for the Compass Edge party at this health distribution. The party either dies before the beholder retreats, or (if surviving more of the fight) reaches the threshold in a state where even one multiattack finishes them. The window in which the party is alive enough to chase but the beholder is low enough to flee is very narrow (likely one specific HP range window).

**Not a bug — matches the design (party is MEANT to lose).** But if Joe ever wants to run this encounter with a stronger or better-rested party, the "retreat" beat is currently the least interesting possible outcome: the beholder exits, the party collapses, nobody chases.
