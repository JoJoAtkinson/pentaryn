### Playtest Simulation

**Setup:** Single Deep Watch Derro (27 HP, AC 15) in defensive position. Crossbow leveled at Marwen (AC 15, 32 HP). Bazgar closing (AC 18, 49 HP). Sabriel supporting.

**Initiative (average d20+mod):**
- Marwen (DEX +2): ~13 | Deep Watch (DEX +2): ~13 | Sabriel (DEX +1): ~12 | Bazgar (DEX +0): ~11
- Order: Marwen → Deep Watch (lose ties) → Sabriel → Bazgar

**Round 1:**

Marwen (wizard-level damage, e.g. Scorching Ray ×3 at +7, 2d6 each):
- Hit rate vs AC 15: 65% per ray. Expected: ~3 × 7 × 0.65 ≈ **14 damage**.
- Deep Watch HP: 27 − 14 = **13 HP**.

Deep Watch turn — verb: "crossbow":
- +4 to hit vs Marwen AC 15. Needs 11+ (50% hit).
- Hit: 6 (1d8+2) piercing. Miss on average (roll 10.5 + 4 = 14.5 < 15).
- Expected: ~3 damage/round to Marwen.
- Let's say it hits: Marwen HP 32 − 6 = **26 HP**.

Sabriel (divine martial, +6 to hit, 1d8+3 weapon + 2nd-level smite 3d8):
- Hit rate vs AC 15: 60%. Expected ~9 weapon + ~13 smite = 22 avg on hit.
- Deep Watch HP: 13 − 22 = **−9 HP → 0 HP. Dead.**

**Deep Watch dies end of Round 1. Never gets to use multiattack.**

**Verb: "multi" (tested in scenario after "crossbow"):**
- Routes to `multiattack` action: two hand_axe attacks at +2, 1d6+0 slashing.
- vs Bazgar AC 18: needs 16+ on d20 with +2 → **35% hit rate** per attack.
- vs Marwen AC 15: needs 13+ → **40% hit rate** per attack.
- With Tactical Drilling (ally within 5 ft, no ally present in this scenario): N/A.
- Expected damage (Marwen, both hitting): 2 × (3.5 × 0.40) = **2.8 avg per full multiattack action**.

### Key Findings

**Check 1 — Verb routing:** `crossbow` → correct single_attack action (+4, 1d8+2). ✓  
**Check 2 — Multiattack verb routing:** `multi` → correct multiattack (2× hand axe +2, 1d6). ✓  
**Check 3 — Tactical Drilling in narration:** DB `multiattack` narration: *"Two quick axe strikes — disciplined, coordinated, economical."* No mention of Tactical Drilling advantage. **DM must track ally-within-5-ft manually.** → AUTOMATION-GAP-R324-01 (new this fire, see Phase C).

### Feel Observations

1. **Solo DW dies round 1** before multiattack can fire in realistic combat. The scenario is needed for verb-routing validation but doesn't represent actual table feel.

2. **Tactical Drilling is the defining feature of the DW type** — but it only matters when ≥2 DW are adjacent. In the thrulm encounter, Deep Watch are typically outnumbered or separated by mid-combat. If the DW spawns count is small, the trait is dead weight. **Recommend: run at least 2 DW in formation for the encounter to function as designed.**

3. **Crossbow at +4 vs a level-5 party:** hits Bazgar on 70+ (35%), Marwen on 55+ (45%), Sabriel on 65+ (25%). Sub-3 expected damage per turn per DW. Even 4 DW massed crossbow fire = ~12 avg damage/round to Marwen — enough to threaten over 2-3 rounds only. They're harassment, not threat. This is correct for the encounter design (party should be focused on the beholder).

4. **Cunning Action (Disengage) with no retreat target:** In the chamber layout, the DW can disengage from melee to gain position. But with 27 HP vs a level-5 party, surviving to use Cunning Action twice is uncommon. In practice, DW may not disengage at all — they die before their second turn. Consider whether Cunning Action matters enough to keep in DB (it does — used for flavor/LLM narration even if not mechanically impactful).

### Beholder Legendary Action Economy (carry from R321)

No beholder in this scenario. Carrying FEEL-OPEN-01 status: Drain Divinity remains the degenerate choice whenever Sabriel is within 30 ft (which requires beholder to descend from hover). If the beholder hovers above 30 ft, Drain Divinity never fires — it only activates when the beholder descends for Void Scream range too. This creates a natural soft gating in positioning: beholder at 40+ ft hover cannot use Drain Divinity, Void Scream, or Maw, reducing it to Tentacle Lash legendary, Void Ray legendary, or Disintegration Ray (if recharged). Carry R321 analysis.
