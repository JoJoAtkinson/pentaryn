### Round 1

**Init 14 — Marwen:** Hits shrine-touched with Scorching Ray or Fire Bolt (shrine-touched is vulnerable to fire). Fire Bolt: +5 vs AC 16, 2d10 fire. Hits on 11+ → ~50% hit. On hit: 2d10 avg 11 × 2 (vulnerability) = avg 22 fire damage. But let's use the scenario's `-12` damage input specifically (DM narrates a sword hit from Bazgar for 12 damage — this is the scenario trigger, not a specific spell).

The scenario applies 12 damage directly:  
**Shrine-Touched: 45 → 33 HP**

**Simultaneous triggers on damage event:**

**Trigger A — Unstable Form:** 12 damage ≥ 10 threshold → shrine-touched gains advantage on attack rolls until end of its next turn.

**Trigger B — Oath-Breaking Retaliation (reaction):** When taking damage from a visible source, make one Shrine-Axe attack against the attacker; take 1d4 psychic self-damage.

**FEEL-07 analysis — trigger ordering, this run:**

OBR is a reaction *to* the damage event. Unstable Form is a passive trait that triggers *on* the same damage event. Both triggers share the same initiating event. Under standard 5e rules, when a creature controls the order of simultaneous triggers, it orders them favorably.

**Ruling A (recommended):** Apply Unstable Form first (as a passive state change), then OBR attack with advantage.  
**Ruling B (conservative):** OBR fires before Unstable Form takes effect; OBR at normal accuracy (no advantage).

Ruling A is mechanically more coherent: Unstable Form represents the shrine flooding power through the vessel in response to damage — the same power surge that enables the retaliatory strike. The trigger resolution should be simultaneous. **Recommend Joe confirm Ruling A as the official table ruling for this encounter.**

**OBR attack (with Ruling A, advantage):**
- +4 to hit with advantage vs Bazgar AC 18
- Flat hit chance: 35% (needs 14+). With advantage: ~57% (1 - (13/20)²)
- On hit: 1d8+1 slashing (avg 5.5) + 1d8 necrotic (avg 4.5) = avg 10 damage to Bazgar
- Self-damage: 1d4 psychic avg 2 → shrine-touched: 33 - 2 = **31 HP**

Expected output: Bazgar takes ~5-6 damage (avg, 57% chance of full 10).

**What the watch system should do:** On applying `-12` to shrine-touched, the combat runner's reaction engine should detect event `damage` on scope `self` and surface `oath_breaking_retaliation` as a suggested reaction. This is the primary test of the scenario's `expect_watch` step.

### FEEL-07 note for this scenario specifically

After a single 12-damage hit, the shrine-touched is:
- At 31 HP (69% health)
- Has advantage on ALL attack rolls until end of its next turn (Unstable Form)
- Has already punished the attacker with OBR
- About to use Ancient Resonance to area-blast anyone in a 15-ft cone

This flip-from-damaged-to-dangerous is a strong feel moment — the shrine-touched rewards the party for "pulling the trigger" by becoming immediately more lethal. The counterplay is clear (fire/radiant from range to avoid OBR), and the threat escalation is thematic.

The automation gap (Unstable Form not in DB) is the main concern: a distracted DM might forget to apply advantage, and the shrine-touched would feel like a normal melee NPC rather than the escalating threat it's designed to be.

### Ancient Resonance + Compass Edge threat assessment

Two-round slice: if shrine-touched fires Ancient Resonance Round 1 (hits Bazgar 60% chance, ~13 damage avg) and uses Shrine-Axe multiattack with Unstable Form Round 2 (two +4 adv. attacks, ~57% each, avg ~9 per hit):
- R1: Ancient Resonance → ~8 avg expected damage to Bazgar (13 × 60%)
- R2: Multiattack with adv. → ~10 avg expected damage (two attacks × 57% × ~9)
- Total: ~18 avg damage in 2 rounds against Bazgar (49 HP)

This is meaningful damage but not a solo-kill threat. Against Marwen (32 HP), if the cone hits her: she's more endangered (possibly down in 2 hits if unlucky). The shrine-touched's fire/radiant vulnerability means a single Fireball from Marwen (8d6 avg 28 × 2 = 56 damage, DC 15 DEX +5 save; shrine-touched fails ~55%) ends it in one spell. The lethality is asymmetric in both directions — fire kills it fast, and it punishes melee with OBR.
