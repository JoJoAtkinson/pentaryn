### Feel analysis — VS clustering scenario

**Finding 1: VS + shrine proximity creates two simultaneous spatial measurements.**

DM must track:
1. Who is within 30 ft of the BEHOLDER (moving, hovering, 3D measurement) → determines VS targets
2. Of those, who is within 10 ft of the SHRINE (fixed, but how is the shrine defined — the altar stone? the pedestal center? the raised dais?) → determines disadvantage

Without a prepared battle map marking both positions explicitly, DMs will either:
- Ignore the shrine proximity clause (forgetting it mid-combat)
- Misapply it (using beholder distance instead of shrine distance)
- Or delay combat to measure carefully (breaks pacing)

The shrine proximity rule had zero effect in rounds 1–2 in R185/R186 because the DM (bot) didn't apply it. This fire explicitly simulated it and it was decisive: Bazgar's 30% → 9% success rate swing is the difference between "Bazgar has a fighting chance" and "Bazgar fails 91% of the time."

**FEEL-R187-01 (NEW):** Shrine proximity disadvantage for VS save is mechanically significant (30% → 9% success for Bazgar) but requires prep that the current materials don't support clearly. Recommend either:
- Simplify to "within 30 ft of the shrine have disadvantage" (same radius as VS, one measurement), OR
- Add explicit DM prep note to beholder stat block and cheat sheet: "Mark the shrine on the battle map before combat. Bazgar/Rager will be within 10 ft during rounds 2–3. Disadvantage applies to them."

**Finding 2: VS instant-death hazard on dying PCs — CS-DRIFT-16 actively dangerous.**

FC-48-A ruling is in the beholder stat block (lines 70–71) but NOT in the cheat sheet. In this simulation, VS downed both Bazgar and Marwen. If VS fires again next round while they're at 0 HP:
- Marwen: 0 HP, max HP 32. VS avg 33 psychic > max HP 32 → **INSTANT DEATH** if DM applies VS to an unconscious Marwen.
- Bazgar: 0 HP, max HP 49. VS avg 33 < max HP 49 → death saves × 3 failures, but not instant death.

CS-DRIFT-16 is a table-safety hazard specifically for Marwen. A DM without the FC-48-A ruling will kill Marwen permanently without the disintegration mechanic. This permanently removes the PC through a ruling oversight, not design intent.

**FEEL-R187-02 (NEW — HIGH):** CS-DRIFT-16 (FC-48-A absent from cheat sheet) creates an active instant-death risk for Marwen in round 3+ when both VS fires and Marwen is at 0 HP. The ruling IS in the stat block but is 2 sub-bullets below the main VS text and easy to miss under combat pressure. Recommend adding to cheat sheet "When Beholder Acts" section: "VS: unconscious PCs cannot hear it (FC-48-A, PHB 294) — exclude them from targeting."

**Finding 3: VS fires in round 2 = effective TPK before meaningful party agency.**

Round count analysis:
- Round 1: Party acts once each (Bazgar attacks, Sabriel attacks, Marwen casts). Beholder acts + LA.
- Round 2: Beholder fires VS (init 19 = first actor). Bazgar and Marwen are down. Sabriel frightened.
- Party has had 3 total turns (1 each) across 2 rounds before effectively losing the encounter.

The design says the party is "meant to lose." However, "losing" via VS in round 2 before the party has exhausted options is qualitatively different from "losing" over 4–5 rounds after meaningful choices. VS round 2 bypasses:
- Bazgar's Action Surge (never used)
- Sabriel's best spell slots (never spent)
- Any attempt at tactical positioning or retreat

**FEEL-R187-03 (NEW — LOG ONLY):** Void Scream Recharge 6 creates high variance in encounter feel. P(fires in round 2 specifically) = 1/6 ≈ 17%. But P(fires at least once in rounds 1–4) ≈ 52%. When VS fires with 2–3 PCs in range after prior HP attrition, it's effectively a TPK trigger. When it doesn't fire, the encounter grinds to 4–5 rounds of action-economy loss. The variance creates two very different encounter experiences with the same stat block.

Consider: Recharge 5–6 (33% per turn) smooths this. P(fires at least once in rounds 1–4) ≈ 80%, making VS more reliably part of the encounter rather than a coin-flip event. Joe decides.

**Finding 4: Frightened removes Sabriel's primary output for 2–3 turns.**

With divine martial 5's primary damage being melee weapon + Divine Smite, frightened condition (cannot approach, disadvantage on attacks while beholder in LOS) effectively removes ~70% of Sabriel's damage output until she shakes it. Average turns to shake frightened at WIS +3: d20+3 vs DC 16, 40% per turn → 2.5 turns average. In a 3-4 round encounter, frightened covers most of Sabriel's remaining combat time.
