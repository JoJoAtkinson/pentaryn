## Phase B — Manual playtest: void-scream-clustering

*Rotation.yml slice 3. Tests: Void Scream area damage, save timing, shrine-proximity disadvantage clarity, and whether the 3-PC clustering scenario is achievable in practice.*

---

### Encounter setup

- Beholder HP 110, AC 17, Void-Feeding active (+1 atk/dmg inside chamber → +7 total attack)
- Bazgar HP 49, AC 18, WIS +1, DEX −1 — Orc Fighter (Battlemaster) 5
- Marwen HP 32, AC 15, WIS +1 (assumed) — Wizard 5
- Sabriel HP 44, AC 19, WIS +3 (assumed) — divine martial 5
- R2 context: R1 multiattack + DD (post-Sabriel protocol) already resolved

---

### Critical pre-condition gap: R1 one-shot rate vs slice assumption

The slice assumes Bazgar is alive in R2 ("both within 30 ft of the shrine"). The R1 multiattack protocol works against this:

**R1 beholder action (initiative advantage, Bazgar prone from UG lair action):**

| Attack | To-hit bonus | vs AC | Hit rate | Damage on hit |
|--------|-------------|-------|----------|---------------|
| Tentacle 1 vs Bazgar (prone → advantage) | +7 | 18 | ~70% | 14 bludgeon + grapple |
| Tentacle 2 vs Bazgar (prone, advantage) | +7 | 18 | ~70% | 14 bludgeon |
| Maw vs grappled Bazgar (auto-crit) | +7 | 18 | auto | 8d8+3 avg 39 pierce |

Total R1 damage if both tentacles hit: 14 + 14 + 39 = **67 damage** → Bazgar 49 HP → 0 HP (unconscious).

Even with only one tentacle hit (grapple established), maw auto-crit fires: 14 + 39 = **53 damage** → Bazgar at 0 HP.

P(at least 1 tentacle hits with advantage, prone) ≈ 1 − 0.30² = **91%**.

**Result: Bazgar is unconscious entering R2 in ~91% of runs.** The 3-PC VS cluster scenario is a theoretical ceiling, not a normal-play outcome.

### Void Scream simulation — realistic R2 configuration

Assumed R2 state: Bazgar unconscious (VS excluded per ruling FIX-FC48-A), Sabriel ~30 HP (took 14 from R1 tentacle), Marwen ~32 HP (untouched).

**VS fires at Sabriel + Marwen (Bazgar excluded, unconscious).**

**Sabriel** WIS +3 vs DC 16 (no shrine proximity disadvantage assumed — 20 ft from altar):

| Outcome | Probability | HP after | Condition |
|---------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Fail (d20+3 < 16 → d20 < 13) | 60% | 30 − 33 = **0 HP** | FRIGHTENED (moot, unconscious) |
| Pass | 40% | 30 − 16 = **14 HP** | — |

**Marwen** WIS +1 vs DC 16 (no proximity disadvantage):

| Outcome | Probability | HP after | Condition |
|---------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Fail (d20+1 < 16 → d20 < 15) | 70% | 32 − 33 = **0 HP** | FRIGHTENED (moot, unconscious) |
| Pass | 30% | 32 − 16 = **16 HP** | — |

**Expected R2 end-state:**
- Bazgar: 0 HP (from R1)
- Sabriel: 60% × 0 HP + 40% × 14 HP = expected ~5.6 HP
- Marwen: 70% × 0 HP + 30% × 16 HP = expected ~4.8 HP

**P(all 3 PCs unconscious after R2) = P(Sabriel fails) × P(Marwen fails) = 0.60 × 0.70 = 42%.** TPK by end of R2 in ~42% of runs (before the beholder even gets its R3 turn).

**P(exactly 1 PC standing) = 0.60×0.30 + 0.40×0.70 = 0.18 + 0.28 = 46%.** That standing PC (Marwen or Sabriel) is facing a fresh beholder with 3 LA and multiattack. Expected survival: 1 additional round.

### Shrine-proximity disadvantage simulation

If Bazgar had survived R1 and advanced to within 10 ft of the altar (melee engaged at the altar base):

**Bazgar** WIS +1 vs DC 16 **with disadvantage**:

P(success with disadv) = P(d20+1 ≥ 16)² = 0.30² = **9%**

Vs normal position (no disadvantage): **30%**

The swing from 30% → 9% is a 21 pp difference. On VS's 33 avg psychic damage, this materialises as roughly 7 expected additional damage per PC in the altar zone. Against a 49-HP fighter, that's significant.

---

### VS high-variance note

6d10 has high variance (SD ≈ 9.3; range 6–60). Against Marwen at full HP (32):

| Roll range | Probability | Result |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| ≤ 31 | ~47% | Marwen survives (1–31 HP) on a failed save |
| 32–60 | ~53% | Marwen at 0 HP on a failed save |

Combined with 70% fail rate: P(Marwen 0 HP from VS) ≈ 0.70 × 0.53 = **37%**. This is lower than the "always-one-shots Marwen" intuition from using average damage (33 ≥ 32). The dice distribution matters: VS does NOT always one-shot Marwen, even on a failed save.

---

### Legendary action economy check (post-VS R2)

After VS in R2, the beholder has its 3 LA available. With Bazgar unconscious and both Sabriel/Marwen likely down:

- Tentacle LA (1): valid only against standing PCs — likely no valid targets
- Void Ray LA (2): same
- Move LA (1): repositioning if needed

In the ~42% TPK scenario, the beholder has 3 unused LA at end of R2. **All 3 LA options are dead weight after a TPK — this confirms VS R2 is over-achieving relative to the party's ability to survive.**

In the ~46% single-survivor scenario, the beholder spends 1 LA (Tentacle or Move) at most. The Drain Divinity window closed in R1; DD is irrelevant in R2+ with the divine caster likely unconscious.

**Conclusion: LA economy in R2+ is almost entirely unused because the fight ends or the valid targets disappear before LAs can be spent.** The 3-LA/round budget matters only in R1 (DD window) and theoretically in R3+ if the fight extends.

## Feel issues this fire

### FEEL-F11-01: VS 3-PC cluster scenario is a theoretical ceiling, not normal play

The rotation slice is labeled "void-scream-clustering" and tests all 3 PCs being caught. In practice this happens in <9% of runs (requires both tentacles to miss AND Bazgar to survive R1: P(both tentacles miss with advantage) ≈ 9%). The more realistic VS scenario is 2-PC (Sabriel + Marwen), which is still fight-ending.

**Implication for the slice:** The slice is still valid as a "what if VS hits a full party" ceiling test, but should add a note that the 3-PC configuration requires very lucky R1 defense. Consider adding a branch for the more probable 2-PC scenario to get representative numbers.

### FEEL-F11-02: VS shrine-proximity disadvantage not in cheat sheet — cold DM miss

Confirmed from Phase A. The cheat sheet SAVE DCs table entry for VS does not include the shrine-proximity (10 ft) disadvantage modifier. A DM running cold from the cheat sheet will never apply it. This degrades the beholder's VS efficiency in the critical R2 round for any PC near the altar.

The rule is unambiguous in the stat block: "Creatures within 10 feet of the shrine have disadvantage on this save." It belongs on the cheat sheet.

**Suggested cheat sheet patch (Joe to approve):**
Change the VS row in the SAVE DCs table from:
> `WIS (psychic damage; on FAILED save: also FRIGHTENED 1 min, DC 16 Wis save ends)`

To:
> `WIS (psychic; FAILED = also FRIGHTENED 1 min; **within 10 ft of shrine: disadvantage**)`

### FEEL-F11-03: Shrine-proximity zone (10 ft from altar stone) nearly impossible to reach while also in beholder melee range

The beholder hovers "40–60 ft up" above the altar. A PC in melee range of the beholder (within 10 ft of the beholder) is 30–50 ft above the altar stone — well outside the 10-ft shrine-proximity zone. Conversely, a PC at the altar stone base (within 10 ft) is 30–50 ft below the beholder's hover position and cannot melee the beholder without climbing or jumping.

**Result:** Shrine-proximity disadvantage and beholder melee range are nearly mutually exclusive terrain zones in a theater-of-mind fight. The disadvantage rule has the most bite against PCs who:
1. Approach the altar to examine/interact with the shrine, OR
2. Are knocked prone (by Unstable Ground) near the altar and don't get up

For a party that fights the beholder at mid-chamber (30+ ft from altar), the shrine-proximity modifier never activates. The cheat sheet gap (FEEL-F11-02) compounds this: DMs won't even know the rule exists, making the altar-zone sub-mechanic effectively invisible at most tables.
