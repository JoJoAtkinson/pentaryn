## Phase B — Playtest Slice: disintegration-threat

**Setup:** Beholder wins initiative (DEX +3). It has Disintegration Ray available (assume
recharge fires round 1). Target: Marwen (Wizard 5, 32 HP, AC 15). Beholder is in the
shrine chamber — Void-Feeding always active: +1 to all attack rolls and damage rolls.

**Attack resolution:**
- Roll: d20 + 7 vs AC 15 → needs 8+ to hit → 65% hit rate
- On hit: 10d8 + 1 force damage
  - Average: 46 damage
  - Minimum (all 1s): 11 damage
  - P(damage ≥ 32 | hit) ≈ 97% (well above 2σ from average)

**Outcome probability:**
- P(Marwen permanently killed T1, before she acts) ≈ 65% × 97% ≈ **63%**

**FEEL-R177-01 — PERMANENT DEATH, ZERO COUNTERPLAY, ROUND 1 (log only)**
The Disintegration Ray "cannot be restored except by true resurrection or wish" clause
is effectively permanent character death at level 5. A 63% chance of permanently killing
the party's most vulnerable member before she takes a single action is the "unfair rather
than lethal" category the briefing asks about. The distinction from "lethal" is zero
player agency: Marwen cannot shield, duck, counterspell, or act. The beholder goes first
and she either survives (35%) or is ash (65%).

Possible mitigations for Joe to consider (design call, not auto-fixable):
1. Limit "disintegrated" to creatures reduced to negative max HP (≥64 damage vs Marwen) —
   reduces instant-disintegration rate from 63% to ~14%.
2. Give party a "Round 0" scout action (they hear the beholder before it hears them) so
   Marwen can pre-cast Mirror Image or Blur.
3. Require the beholder to grapple a target before it can target them with the ray
   (thematic: it needs to "sense" via the void-touch before disintegrating).

Threat ceiling takeaway: Even if the ray misses, the beholder's multiattack averages ~51
damage across rounds 1–2 against Marwen without the ray (two tentacle hits + maw). She
cannot survive past round 2 in any scenario without active healing or escape. This is
consistent with the "party is meant to lose" design, but the PERMANENT death clause is
what makes T1 feel wrong — a dying Marwen at 0 HP with death saves still gives the party
counterplay. Disintegration removes that.

## Phase B — Open DB Bugs Confirmed Still Present

**BUG-F7-02 (open since fire #7): Antireality effect field says "+2 AC retroactive"**
DB `antireality` row `effect` field reads:
"REACTION (when hit by an attack the beholder can see): gain +2 AC against that attack,
declared after seeing the roll. If the retroactive AC meets or beats the roll, the attack misses."

Stat block says: "impose disadvantage on that attack roll (once per round)" — declared
BEFORE the attack roll. Retroactive AC boost vs. pre-roll disadvantage is mechanically
significant. Requires `combat_action_upsert` via MCP to correct. Cannot fix unattended.

**BUG-OPEN: void_ray_legendary type='area' (confirmed at R177)**
The `void_ray_legendary` DB row has `"type":"area"` with `"area":"1 target within 120 ft"`.
Single-target forced-save attacks should be `utility` or `single_attack` (no area selector).
The `area` type causes the runner to render a target-area UI widget instead of a single-target
selector. Requires `combat_action_upsert` via MCP to fix type field.
