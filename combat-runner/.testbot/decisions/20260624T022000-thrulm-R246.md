### Key Findings

**1. (CONFIRMED, FEEL-R228-01) Drain Divinity never fires in this sequence — dominated by Tentacle+Void Ray.**

Drain Divinity (3 LA, within 30 ft, DC 16 CHA, loses highest slot → 2× slot-level THP):
- Requires beholder within 30 ft of a divine caster. Beholder default hover: 40-60 ft above altar.
- To get within 30 ft of Sabriel (who is on the ground): Shrine-Drift BA (costs the only bonus action this turn).
- Three LA → Drain Divinity once. If Sabriel fails (65% — CHA +2, needs 14+): loses one 3rd-level slot (highest she has at level 5) → beholder gains 6 THP. Expected THP: 0.65×6 = 3.9.
- Full 3 LA budget spent. No Void Ray. No Tentacle. 3.9 expected THP gain vs 28.8 expected damage from Tentacle+Void Ray.
- Even in a world where Drain Divinity gets a 3rd+3rd+2nd (three uses, Sabriel full slots): 0.65×6 + 0.65×6 + 0.65×4 = 3.9+3.9+2.6 = 10.4 expected THP + slot denial. Still not competitive with 28.8 damage.
- **Drain Divinity is only worth using if:** (a) beholder below ~30 HP and needs THP survivability AND (b) beholder is already within 30 ft AND (c) the slot-denial win-condition matters (Sabriel is the party's only healer, currently at full slots, and the fight will last 5+ more rounds). In a 3-4 round fight at this party level, none of these conditions hold.
- FEEL-R228-01 confirmed at age 18 fires. **Needs Joe's decision.**

**2. (CONFIRMED, BUG-VOIDRAY-TYPE) Void Ray legendary type "area" in DB.**

Simulating the DM typing "void ray" → routes to void_ray_legendary (verbs: ["void ray", "void blast", "force ray", "legendary ray"]) ✓ correct resolution. However, type "area" means the runner's narration/logic will treat it as an area ability. Single-target save abilities targeting one creature within 120 ft should be `single_attack` with a save spec (or a dedicated type). Carry-forward.

**3. (CONFIRMED, BUG-R226-01) Verb "ray" alone routes to disintegration_ray.**

If a DM types "ray" or "beam" during the LA phase intending Void Ray, they get disintegration_ray (action) instead. Verb collision confirmed. The cheat sheet labels are clear ("Void Ray" vs "Disintegration Ray"), but a DM running at speed might type "ray." At-table clarification needed, or verb "ray" should be removed from disintegration_ray verbs. Carry-forward — needs MCP fix.

**4. (NEW, CS-DRIFT-R246-02, LOW) Optimal LA sequence depends on initiative order, but rotation.yml prescribes a fixed PC order (Marwen → Bazgar → Sabriel) that doesn't match realistic initiative.**

With realistic DEX-sorted initiative (Bazgar 15, Sabriel 13, Marwen 11), the beholder's LA opportunities fire in that order. The rotation.yml slice description prescribes "Tentacle after Marwen, Void Ray after Bazgar, 0 after Sabriel" — implying Marwen acts first. At a real table, if Bazgar acts first, the beholder faces a different decision: spend 2 LA on Void Ray after Bazgar (leaving 1 for Tentacle on Sabriel/Marwen later) OR spend 1 LA on Tentacle after Bazgar (leaving 2 for Void Ray after Sabriel/Marwen). The 2+1 split vs 1+2 split doesn't change the total expected damage (~28.8 either way) since the abilities are used in both sequences — the order just changes which PC gets each. The rotation's prescribed sequence is a simplification, not an error. Log only — no correction needed, but the rotation.yml example could add an initiative-order note.

**5. (NAMING AUDIT, PASS) Void Ray vs Disintegration Ray table-disambiguation confirmed.**

Cheat sheet SAVE DCs table correctly labels:
- Disintegration Ray: "+7 to hit (attack roll)" — unambiguous as an attack roll, not a save
- Void Ray: "DC 16 DEX save — force damage"

No DM reading the cheat sheet in-game would confuse these. The naming clarity is sufficient at the table even if DB verb routing has a bug. ✓

**6. (NEW, FEEL-R246-01, LOW) Grapple-lockdown loop creates "sticky Marwen" dynamic over multiple rounds.**

Tentacle LA on Marwen: 65% hit → grapple. Marwen escape: Acrobatics (likely prof + DEX +3 → d20+5 vs DC 16 → 55% success) OR Athletics (DEX/STR → much lower). If Marwen uses her action to cast (doesn't attempt escape), she remains grappled. Next beholder multiattack: two Tentacle attacks AND one Maw (with disadvantage on escape checks while grappled). The Maw rider "disadvantage on ability checks to escape" compounds the lockdown — once grappled, escape gets harder. Over 3 rounds with Maw hits, Marwen can be taken from 32 HP to 0 HP purely through grapple-Maw combo without any other ability. **This is good encounter design tension** (beholder exploits low-AC/low-HP target), not a feel problem. Documented for DM awareness: protecting Marwen requires either breaking grapple (requires another PC's action to assist — Help action gives advantage, and Marwen may succeed) or Marwen burning her action to escape rather than casting. At-table decision point.
