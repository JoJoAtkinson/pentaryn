### Feel analysis

**FEEL-F26-01 (21-fire carry) — confirmed again:**

Taunt on Marwen delayed zero damage. SC took 13 thunder on the same round the Rager used its bonus action to Taunt. The Rager's optimal play was Taunt Bazgar (all attack rolls → 55% fail CHA save → ~20% damage reduction on 2 Extra Attacks × 11 avg = ~4.4 avg damage saved per round). Instead it taunted the wizard, saving 0 damage.

Quantified R1 opportunity cost of Taunt-Marwen vs Taunt-Bazgar:

| Taunt target | Effect | R1 damage prevented |
|---|---|---|
| Marwen | Disadvantage on attack-roll spells (Fire Bolt, SR) | 0 (Shatter not affected) |
| Bazgar | Disadvantage on all Extra Attacks vs non-Rager | ~4 avg (2 attacks × 60%→40% hit × 11) |

Delta: 4 damage/round in favor of Taunt-Bazgar. Over 2 rounds before the Rager dies: 8 damage difference. Not combat-deciding, but non-zero.

**FEEL-F46-01 (NEW) — Rager tactics note self-contradiction:**

The Rager stat block Taunt tactics line reads: *"Target priority: prefer the PC NOT already engaging you in melee — the back-line caster or healer is highest value (forces them to redirect attacks onto the Rager or suffer disadvantage vs. the Shardcaller)."*

This contradicts the Taunt mechanic's own clarification note *(MQ-TW36-C): "Includes ranged spell attack rolls (e.g., Fire Bolt, Eldritch Blast) — Taunt disadvantage applies to any **attack roll** targeting a non-rager."*

Against Compass Edge: Marwen's primary offense is save-based spells (Shatter, Fireball, Thunderwave) — Taunt applies to none of them. The tactics note's claim that "back-line caster = highest value" is incorrect for this party. Correct priority for Compass Edge: **Taunt Bazgar** (Fighter/Battlemaster — 100% attack rolls, Extra Attack × 2, Maneuver riders all ride attack rolls). Sabriel is second (smite riders on attack rolls). Marwen is last (save spells dominate).

**Recommended tactics-note addition:** *"Against save-spell-dominant casters (Wizard, Druid, Cleric), Taunt is near-useless — their primary offense bypasses attack rolls entirely. Against Compass Edge: Taunt Bazgar (Fighter) or Sabriel (Paladin smite), not Marwen. Exception: if Marwen is using Fire Bolt or Scorching Ray as her main cantrip/action, Taunt is worth spending."*

This is a documentation update (tactics section), not an auto-fixable stat-block error. Flagging for Joe's next pass.

**Shard-Barrage R1 on clustered party (FEEL-F46-02 NEW — minor):**

The Shardcaller opened with Shard-Barrage on 3 targets. DC 13 DEX, 3d6 ≈ 10 avg. Total distributed across the party: ~25 expected damage (assuming 2 of 3 fail). This is the highest-impact SC opening play. The Barrage hit Marwen (who then cast at 22 HP instead of 32) — this means if the beholder fires VS on R2 (avg 33 damage), Marwen is downed on a fail AND on a pass (22 - 16 = 6 HP on pass; 22 - 33 = -11 → 0 on fail). 

Combined Barrage + VS pressure confirms FEEL-F38-02 (any prior damage to Marwen makes VS R2 always fatal). The SC Barrage effectively seals Marwen's fate before the beholder's R2 turn even if she makes the VS save. DM should be aware: the SC Barrage is not just a nuisance hit on R1 — against a 32 HP Marwen, it converts VS from "fatal on fail" to "fatal regardless."
