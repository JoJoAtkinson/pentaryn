- Lay on Hands: 25 HP pool (1 use = any amount ≤25 HP)

### Round 1 — Beholder main action and LA economy

**Beholder action: Disintegration Ray (available at encounter start) on Marwen.**
- In-chamber: +7 to hit (base +6 + Void-Feeding +1) vs AC 15 → needs 8+ → P(hit) = **65%**
- On hit: 45 (10d8) force. Marwen max HP = 32. **Hit = instant 0 HP → disintegration threshold check.**
- On miss (35%): no damage; ray wasted.
- Expected outcome: Marwen has a 65% chance of dropping to 0 HP (disintegrated) before she acts.

**LA allocation — three strategic options:**

| Strategy | After Marwen | After Bazgar | After Sabriel | Net LA spend |
|----------|-------------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| A: Pure Drain Divinity | 0 LA | 0 LA | Drain Divinity (3 LA) | 3 |
| B: Pressure mix | Tentacle (1 LA) | Void Ray (2 LA) | 0 LA | 3 |
| C: Damage-then-drain | Void Ray (2 LA) | — | Drain Divinity (1 LA?) | IMPOSSIBLE |

**Strategy C is impossible.** Void Ray + Drain Divinity = 2+3 = 5 LA (budget is 3). Tentacle + Drain Divinity = 1+3 = 4 LA (also impossible). **Drain Divinity is mutually exclusive with any other LA use in the same round.** This is the binary economy.

**Strategy A (Pure Drain Divinity):**
After Sabriel's turn: Drain Divinity (3 LA) → DC 16 CHA save.
- Sabriel: d20 + 5, needs 11+ → P(save) = 50%.
- **On fail:** Sabriel loses 3rd-level slot. Beholder gains 6 THP. Sabriel loses a Divine Smite (2d8+) or 3rd-level prepared spell.
- **On pass:** Wasted. Marwen and Bazgar both got free turns of attacks (no LA harassment).
- Marwen free turn value: if alive, Fireball (DC 15 DEX, 8d6 = 28 avg) hits beholder + any derro in cone. Beholder DEX save +6; vs DC 15 needs 9+ = P(save) = 60%. Half on success = 14. Expected: 0.4×28 + 0.6×14 = 11.2 + 8.4 = **19.6 avg damage to beholder per Fireball** (if it hits the beholder in the cone).

**Strategy B (Pressure mix):**
- Tentacle after Marwen: +7 vs AC 15 → 65% hit → 14 bludgeoning avg + grapple (escape DC 16; Marwen STR ~−1, virtually impossible to escape). **Expected 9 damage, Marwen grappled.**
- Void Ray after Bazgar: DC 16 DEX save. Bazgar DEX +1, needs 15+ → P(save) = 30%. **70% fail → 23 (4d10+1) force damage.** Expected: 0.70×23 + 0.30×11 = 16.1 + 3.3 = **19.4 avg force damage to Bazgar.** Bazgar at 49 HP (no prone penalty to save; already stood up on his turn) → 49−19 = 30 HP.
- No LA after Sabriel. Sabriel acts freely.

### Round 1 outcome comparison

**Strategy A vs B — what the beholder gains:**

| Strategy | Damage output via LA | Slot drain |
|----------|---------------------|-----------|
| A: Pure Drain Divinity | 0 LA damage | 50%: strip 3rd slot (6 THP); 50%: nothing |
| B: Pressure mix | ~28 avg LA damage (9+19) to Marwen+Bazgar | 0 slot drain |

At party HP totals: Marwen 32, Bazgar 49, Sabriel 44 = 125 total.

- Strategy A advantage: strips Sabriel's highest smite slot with 50% probability; if Sabriel is the last PC standing (rounds 3+), Drain Divinity becomes the dominant source of chip damage.
- Strategy B advantage: consistent damage pressure; ~22% of Bazgar's HP + Marwen possibly grappled. Over 3 rounds: ~84 avg LA damage, which exceeds Bazgar's HP.

**Optimal strategy finding (key Phase B result):**

The beholder should use Strategy B (Tentacle + Void Ray) while multiple PCs are active, then switch to Strategy A (Drain Divinity) when only Sabriel remains. The crossover point is when Marwen and Bazgar are unconscious — at that point, the beholder has no efficient Void Ray targets (Bazgar at 0 HP excluded; Marwen at 0 HP excluded) and Sabriel is the sole target.

**This transition is NOT described in the tactics text.** The current tactics text says "Against Clerics/Paladins: Focuses on Drain Divinity" without regard to party count. A DM following this literally will use Strategy A every round, wasting LA budget while Marwen and Bazgar are still active and attacking.

### THP accumulation — round 2+ edge case

Scenario: Strategy A succeeds in Round 1 (Sabriel fails her save). Beholder gains 6 THP.

Round 2: Bazgar hits for Greataxe (8 avg, +4 to hit vs AC 17, hits on 13+ = 40% → expected 3.2 dmg/round). Sabriel hits for Shrine-Axe analog / weapon (+4 to hit = 40% → ~8 avg = 3.2 dmg/round). Two PCs = ~6.4 avg damage per round to beholder.

After round 2: beholder has absorbed ~6.4 damage → THP at 6 − 6.4 = 0 THP (eroded in ~1 round).

Round 2 Drain Divinity succeeds on Sabriel again: beholder gains 4 THP (2nd-level slot). 4 THP > 0 remaining → new THP = 4. This is fine.

Round 3 Drain Divinity succeeds: beholder gains 4 THP (another 2nd-level slot). But if round 3 beholder took only 2 damage this round, THP remaining = 2. New THP = 4 > 2 → replaces. Fine.

**The anti-synergy only triggers if the beholder takes LESS than the new THP amount of damage between drains.** Against Compass Edge (2 melee PCs with ~40% hit rate and ~8 dmg per hit = ~6.4 dmg/round), the THP buffer clears fast enough that the replacement rule rarely fires. This is LOW practical impact but HIGH conceptual confusion — DMs may not remember the replacement rule during a tense combat.

**In practice:** FEEL-R164-01 (THP don't stack) is real but low impact for this party. FEEL-R164-02 (THP formula when divine-ability drain: if Sabriel uses a Channel Divinity use, she doesn't have a "spell slot" to lose — what level counts for THP?) is a more urgent edge case.

### Target selection — Drain Divinity on Marwen (wizard)?

The beholder's Drain Divinity stat block reads: "one creature within 30 feet that has **spell slots**, divine favor, or clerical powers."

Marwen (Wizard 5) has 3rd-level spell slots. **She qualifies.**

Marwen's CHA save vs DC 16: estimated d20 + (−1 to 0) → P(save) ≈ 20–25%. The beholder succeeds **75–80% of the time.**

Stripping Marwen's 3rd-level slot = removing Fireball (8d6 = 28 avg, Sabriel doesn't have this) or Counterspell. If Marwen is the beholder's primary kill target (via DR), draining her Fireball BEFORE she uses it is extremely valuable.

**Verdict:** Draining Marwen is mechanically superior to draining Sabriel if the beholder wants to neutralize offensive spell output:
- Marwen: 75–80% success, removes offensive slot (Fireball/Counterspell)
- Sabriel: 50% success, removes healing/smite slot

The tactics text only calls out Clerics/Paladins as Drain Divinity targets. This is a **tactics text gap.**

### Drain Divinity range vs beholder altitude

Drain Divinity range: **30 feet.**
Beholder hover altitude (per tactics and cheat sheet): **40–60 feet.**

A PC at floor level, directly beneath a beholder hovering at 40 ft = 40 ft distance from beholder. **40 ft > 30 ft range → Drain Divinity cannot be used.**

For Drain Divinity to reach a floor-level PC:
- Beholder must descend to ≤30 ft altitude, OR
- PC must be elevated on terrain ≥10 ft high

At 30 ft altitude, the beholder's Tentacle Lash (reach 10 ft) still cannot reach floor-level PCs (tentacle must reach DOWN 30 ft, but tentacle reach is 10 ft horizontal + 10 ft vertical at best). This altitude geometry is unaddressed in tactics text.

**Practical implication:** The beholder needs to hover at ~25–30 ft altitude to use Drain Divinity. At that altitude, Bazgar (5e melee fighter with 5 ft reach, can jump using bonus action Athletics) still can't engage — but the beholder is within Shrine-Drift range of descending into melee if not careful. This is a **good design tension** (descend for drain = vulnerability) but completely undocumented.
