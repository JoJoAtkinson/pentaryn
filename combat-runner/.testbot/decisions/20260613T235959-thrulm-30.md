### Feel Assessment

#### Tactical Drilling creates the only interesting DWD moment

With TD, two adjacent DWDs hit Bazgar at 28% per attack (+2 with advantage vs AC 18). Without TD (after DWD-1 dies), DWD-2 hits Bazgar on a 16+ straight roll = 15% per attack. TD roughly doubles hit rate. The pair is slightly threatening — but 28% × 2 attacks × 2 DWDs = avg ~3 hits per round × 3.5 damage = ~10.5 damage/round combined. Against 49 HP Bazgar, 5 rounds to down him. With party retaliation, both DWDs die in ~1.5 rounds. DWDs are speed bumps that reduce 3-5 HP per PC before dying.

#### FEEL-F30-01 (new): DWD Cunning Action Disengage condition too restrictive

**Finding:** The tactics note authorizes Disengage only "if the DW is below 10 HP and needs to fall back to the gate without provoking OAs." In the crossbow-pivot scenario, Disengage is also correct at ANY HP when the DWD wants to switch to ranged attack from melee. A DWD at 14 HP (above the 10-HP gate) trying to shoot Marwen should Disengage before repositioning, but current tactics language doesn't authorize it.

**Consequence:** A DM reading the note literally will NOT Disengage at 14 HP, move toward Marwen, and take the Bazgar OA — which at +7 vs AC 15 (55% hit, ~11 avg damage) likely kills the DWD at 14 HP before the crossbow fires. The intended crossbow play is systematically blocked by an OA the tactics don't flag.

**Suggested update (log only — do not edit source):**
> *Cunning Action (Disengage): Use to avoid OAs when retreating (below 10 HP) OR when pivoting to ranged attack (crossbow) from melee at any HP. At any HP, moving out of melee to fire the crossbow without Disengage is suicidal vs a Fighter (OA +7 vs AC 15 = 55% hit, avg 11 dmg). Disengage is the correct BA whenever the DWD plans a crossbow shot on the same turn it leaves melee range.*

#### FEEL-F30-02 (new): Multiattack → crossbow downgrade not flagged in tactics

**Finding:** Multiattack gives 2 Hand Axe attacks; Crossbow gives only 1 ranged attack. Switching to crossbow in R2 HALVES attack count. Even though the crossbow has better to-hit (+4 vs +2, due to DEX over STR), the net output drops:

- 2× Hand Axe (straight roll, TD lost after DWD-1 dies): 2 × 15% × 3.5 = 1.05 expected damage/round
- 1× Crossbow (+4 vs AC 15): 50% × 6.5 = 3.25 expected damage/round

Crossbow is 3× better per-action when TD is already lost. But the tactics section doesn't note the "after ally dies: switch to crossbow" pattern explicitly. The "focus-fire with allies" note implies staying in melee formation; "Cunning Action to Disengage" implies falling back. Neither explicitly says "if solo, crossbow outperforms single melee axe."

**Severity:** Low — the emergent play is correct, just undocumented.

#### Crossbow vs prone target (carry-forward check)

If the lair action had proned Bazgar before DWDs act in R1, the crossbow switch would be wrong: prone targets have disadvantage on all attacks against them (ranged included). Per FIX-EV7-34-A in beholder-thrulm.md: "do NOT use DR or Void Ray against a prone target." Same logic applies to DWD crossbow vs a prone Bazgar. This is a niche case for this formation, but the DWD tactics don't mention prone-target avoidance for the crossbow. Carry-forward as FEEL-F30-03.

**Severity:** Low — correctable by Joe with a single line edit.

**Location:** `COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md`, "ROUND-BY-ROUND CHECKLIST" section, item 5.
