### New findings from this simulation

**FEEL-012: Tentacle LA grapple on Marwen has disproportionate impact at 3-PC table.**

With 3 PCs and the grappled condition (speed 0) on Marwen, she loses:
- Ability to retreat from the beholder if she needs range
- Any movement-dependent spell positioning
- The ability to flee if the party calls a retreat (escape requires beating DC 16 Athletics/Acrobatics)

Marwen's best escape stat: DEX 14 (+2) for Acrobatics → 35% escape per action. Expected escape time:
~2.9 actions. At a 3-PC table with no monk, rogue with Uncanny Dodge, or spell that breaks grapple
(Misty Step works — she can teleport while grappled in 5e, since it doesn't require speed), Marwen
effectively burns a spell slot to escape OR a full action.

Not a bug. But at a 3-PC table, the grapple tax on the wizard is real. **Log only** — the beholder
correctly prioritizes control. Worth noting for DM: Misty Step (Marwen likely has it as a 2nd slot)
defeats the grapple for 1 slot. The beholder should re-grapple on the following round.

---

**FEEL-013: Bazgar DEX +1 assumption is unverified in combat-roster.yml.**

The slice and TOOL-R162-01 corrected the fail rate assumption to 70% (Bazgar DEX +1). However,
`world/party/the-compass-edge/combat-roster.yml` does NOT list individual PC ability scores — only
name, id, max_hp, and ac. The DEX +1 assumption comes from prior fire analysis, not a verified stat.

If Bazgar has DEX +0 (common for STR-focused fighters): fail rate = 75% (d20 ≥ 15 → 25% save).
Expected Void Ray: 0.75 × 23 + 0.25 × 11 = 17.25 + 2.75 = 20.0 (vs 19.4 in the slice).
Difference: +0.6 expected damage — negligible. The 70%/75% distinction doesn't materially change
the encounter outcome.

**No fix needed.** Noting that the combat-roster.yml schema intentionally omits ability scores
(they're tracked at the table, not in the file). The slice's DEX assumption is reasonable.

---

**FEEL-014: Optimal LA sequence depends on PC-turn order — not always Tentacle-Marwen-first.**

The prescribed sequence (Tentacle after Marwen → Void Ray after Bazgar) requires Marwen to act
BEFORE Bazgar. If initiative is: Bazgar → Marwen → Sabriel, then after Bazgar's turn the beholder
would optimally spend 2 LA (Void Ray) on Bazgar, and after Marwen's turn spend 1 LA (Tentacle) on
Marwen. Same total spend, different order.

The prescribed sequence reflects the case where Marwen acts first. At table, DM should select target
for each LA at the moment it fires — the "prescribed sequence" in the slice is one representative
ordering. The 3-LA budget math (1+2=3) holds regardless of order.

**No fix needed.** The slice is a representative example, not a rigid rule.
