### SABRIEL-SAVE-R304-01 — Rotation scenario assumes no save proficiency for Sabriel (design flag)

The drain-divinity-vs-sabriel rotation slice specifies "assume +2 Cha for Sabriel." A
Paladin-archetype divine martial at level 5 typically receives CHA saving throw proficiency
(PHB Paladin class feature). With PB +3 at level 5: effective CHA save = +2+3 = +5 vs DC 16
(needs 11+, 50% success) rather than the scenario's implied 35% success rate. This changes
the expected slot drain by ~26%:
- Without proficiency: 65% fail, EV = 0.65 slots/round
- With proficiency: 50% fail, EV = 0.50 slots/round

The combat roster (`world/party/the-compass-edge/combat-roster.yml`) carries no save bonuses.
Simulation assumptions cannot be verified without PC sheets. Log as prep dependency.

## Phase B — Generative Playtest Slice: `drain-divinity-vs-sabriel`

**Setup:** Beholder has 3 LA available. Sabriel is the target (divine martial level 5).
Assumed spell slots: two 3rd-level, three 2nd-level, four 1st-level (per rotation spec).
CHA save: +2 (no proficiency assumed, per rotation spec — see SABRIEL-SAVE-R304-01).
DC 16 → needs 14+ → **35% success, 65% fail**.

Drain Divinity requires beholder within 30 ft. Default hover is 40-60 ft above altar.
Beholder must descend to use Drain Divinity — forfeiting altitude, exposing itself to Bazgar
(melee, +7 to hit vs AC 17; ~55% hit rate when grounded; +9 attack with Void-Feeding active)
and to Sabriel's sanctified weapons (+1d8/hit).

---

### Simulation: 3 consecutive rounds of Drain Divinity (all 3 LA → one use per round)

**Round 1:**
Beholder descends. DC 16 CHA save (Sabriel, +2): 65% fail.
- **Fail (65%):** Sabriel loses one 3rd-level slot. Beholder gains 6 THP.
  Sabriel slots remaining: 1×3rd, 3×2nd, 4×1st.
- **Success (35%):** No effect. Beholder spent all 3 LA and dealt 0 damage.

**Round 2 (assuming round 1 failed):**
Beholder must stay within 30 ft (or re-descend). DC 16 CHA save: 65% fail.
- **Fail (65% conditional):** Sabriel loses last 3rd-level slot. Beholder gains 6 THP.
  If round 1 THP still intact (6), new 6 THP does not stack — beholder keeps 6.
  If round 1 THP was fully consumed by damage, beholder gains fresh 6 THP.
  Sabriel slots remaining: 0×3rd, 3×2nd, 4×1st.
- **Success (35%):** No effect. Two LA rounds wasted.

**Round 3 (both prior rounds failed):**
DC 16 CHA save: 65% fail. Highest remaining slot is now 2nd level.
- **Fail (65% conditional):** Sabriel loses one 2nd-level slot. Beholder gains 4 THP.
  4 THP < 6 (current THP if intact) → no benefit.
  Sabriel slots remaining: 0×3rd, 2×2nd, 4×1st.
- **Success (35%):** No effect.

**Combined probability (all 3 uses fail):** 0.65³ ≈ 27.4%
**At least 1 success:** 72.6% (meaning at least one of the 3 uses had zero effect)

---

### Expected value comparison: Drain Divinity (3 LA all-in) vs Tentacle + Void Ray (1+2 LA)

**Drain Divinity (Costs 3 LA, one target within 30 ft):**
- Per round, assuming Sabriel burns highest slots with Divine Smite:
  - EV of 3rd-level slot drained = 0.65 × avg smite damage prevented
  - 3rd-level smite: 4d8 extra radiant = avg 18 forgone
  - EV per round: 0.65 × 18 = **~11.7 expected damage prevented**
- Beholder THP gain: 0.65 × 6 = avg 3.9 THP — marginal at this damage scale
- Range constraint: beholder must descend, exposing itself to melee (Bazgar 50% hit rate,
  Sabriel sanctified +1d8/hit)

**Tentacle Lash (1 LA) + Void Ray (2 LA) — standard LA spending:**
- Tentacle Lash: +9 in chamber vs AC 19 (Sabriel) → needs 10+ → 55% hit.
  Expected damage: 0.55 × (3d6+4 avg 14.5) = **~8.0 avg** + grapple rider if relevant.
  Or target Marwen (AC 15): needs 6+ → 75% hit → 0.75 × 14.5 = **~10.9 avg**.
- Void Ray: DC 16 DEX save vs Bazgar (+1 DEX assumed) → needs 15+ → 70% fail.
  Expected damage: 0.70 × 23 + 0.30 × 11.5 = **~19.5 avg** (4d10+1 in chamber).
- Combined expected LA damage: ~10.9 + 19.5 = **~30.4 avg per round** (targeting Marwen + Bazgar)
  vs Drain Divinity's **~11.7 expected damage prevented**.

**Conclusion: Tentacle + Void Ray is ~2.6× more efficient than Drain Divinity against Compass Edge.**

### Feel observations

**FEEL-R304-01 — Drain Divinity 3-LA is tactically sub-optimal vs Compass Edge (log only)**

The beholder's tactics text says it "focuses on Drain Divinity to remove healing and buffs"
against clerics/paladins. Against Compass Edge specifically, this is a self-punishing
strategy: Drain Divinity prevents ~11.7 expected damage per round (conditional on Sabriel
burning smite every round) vs ~30.4 avg damage from Tentacle + Void Ray. The tactics text
will lead a by-the-book DM to sub-optimal play, slowing down a fight that's meant to feel
inevitably lethal.

Recommendation: Add a caveat to the beholder's tactics section: "Use Drain Divinity only
when Sabriel is visibly burning slots (actively casting Smite or healing); otherwise prefer
Tentacle+Void Ray for raw LA efficiency." This is a DM-prep note, not a stat change.

**FEEL-R304-02 — THP gain from Drain Divinity is functionally irrelevant (log only)**

6 THP from a 3rd-level drain vs Bazgar's DPR (~9.5 avg per round, 2 attacks × 50% hit ×
avg 9.5/hit) means the THP lasts less than one of Bazgar's turns. It's tactile flavor with
no mechanical weight. Suggest either removing the THP mechanic or scaling it meaningfully
(e.g., gain HP equal to the slot level × 5, or grant one legendary resistance instead).

**FEEL-R304-03 — 30 ft range forces beholder to descend — this is GOOD design, keep it**

The positional cost of Drain Divinity is the most interesting thing about it: the beholder
must leave its high hover to reach Sabriel on the chamber floor. This creates a genuine
decision point (sacrifice altitude safety for the ability) and gives the party an
exploitation window (melee PC can now reach beholder). This should be highlighted more
prominently in the stat block tactics section — currently it's in the cheat sheet but
not in the beholder's md tactics text.

**FEEL-R304-04 — "Degenerate" concern from briefing is overstated for this party (log only)**

The known-state briefing flags Drain Divinity as "degenerate every round vs. divine casters."
Simulation shows this is only true when the divine caster is burning 3rd-level slots every
round (heavy smite / Cure Wounds cadence). Against Sabriel at level 5, who may only have
2 × 3rd-level slots total, the beholder can at most drain both over 2 rounds (at 27% full
probability) — then Drain Divinity becomes a 2nd-level drain (even lower EV). The "always
dominant" concern would be more valid against a Cleric with short rest slots or a higher-level
divine caster. At this encounter's party level, it's a trap for the beholder, not a party-crusher.

Recommendation: De-escalate the known-state note from "degenerate" to "sub-optimal trap vs
Compass Edge; revisit if party level increases or Sabriel has heavy-heal cadence."
