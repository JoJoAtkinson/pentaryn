## Phase B — Grapple Lock Simulation

**Setup:** Beholder opens R1 by targeting Bazgar (Fighter 5, HP 49, AC 18, STR 18 → Athletics +7). Beholder makes 2 Tentacle Lash attacks, then Maw if grappled. Tracks escape attempts over 3 rounds.

---

### Grapple Acquisition (R1 Multiattack)

Tentacle +6 vs AC 18: needs 12+, **45% hit rate**.

| Event | Probability | Bazgar HP remaining |
|-------|------------|---------------------|
| Both tentacles miss | 30.25% | 49 HP (ungrappled, beholder can't use auto-crit Maw) |
| Exactly one tentacle hits | 49.5% | ~36 HP (grappled; Maw follows with advantage potential) |
| Both tentacles hit | 20.25% | ~23 HP (grappled; two grapple-maintaining tentacles) |

After grapple acquisition, Maw (+6 vs AC 18, **45% hit**, auto-crit if grappled → 8d8+3 ≈ 39 avg):

- If at least one Tentacle hit (69.75% chance of grapple): Maw auto-crit on hit.
- Maw expected value vs grappled Bazgar: 39 × 0.45 = **17.6 avg**.
- Combined R1 expected damage on Bazgar: ~13.5 (1 tentacle, 45% weighted) + 17.6 (Maw) ≈ **31 avg**.

At 49 HP, Bazgar is at ~18 HP after R1 expected damage. Worst case (both tentacles + Maw): ~13.5 + 13.5 + 39 = **66 damage** — dead outright if all three hit (20.25% × 45% ≈ 9% of outcomes).

---

### Escape Cycle Analysis (R2–R3)

Escape: Bazgar uses full action, rolls Athletics +7 vs DC 16 → needs 9+, **60% success**.

Re-grapple after escape: Beholder uses 1 LA (Tentacle, costs 1 LA) → +6 vs AC 18, **45% re-grapple**.

| Round | Bazgar spends action on | Expected DPR on beholder | DPR forgone |
|-------|------------------------|--------------------------|-------------|
| Escape attempt | Athletics check (no damage) | 0 | ~9–10 DPR |
| Free (attacking) | 2 Fighter attacks | ~9.4 DPR | — |

**Action economy toll:** Every escape turn costs Bazgar his full action, spending 1:1 against the beholder's 1-LA re-grapple (which costs the beholder 1 of 3 legendary actions). The beholder retains 2 LA for Void Ray (2 LA) or additional Tentacle (1 LA each).

---

### 3-Round Outcome Distribution (probability Bazgar is grappled at start of each round)

| Round start | P(Bazgar grappled) | P(auto-crit Maw threatens this round) |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------------------------------|
| R1 (before beholder acts) | 0% | — |
| R2 | ~70% (if beholder landed at least 1 tentacle R1) | 70% × 45% = 31.5% |
| R3 | ~50% (escape cycle ongoing) | ~50% × 45% = 22.5% |

---

### Key Findings

**FEEL-G1 (High):** Auto-crit Maw creates a "forced escape tax" that feels repetitive. The loop: beholder grapples → Bazgar escapes (full action) → beholder re-grapples (1 LA) → repeat. This generates 2–3 rounds of Bazgar dealing 0 DPR. A party with only 3 PCs cannot afford a fighter spending multiple turns on escape attempts.

**FEEL-G2 (Medium):** The beholder should prefer targeting DIFFERENT creatures with Tentacle re-grapples instead of re-grappling Bazgar. "Re-grapple the same target who just escaped" is mechanically optimal but narratively flat. At-table suggestion: after a successful escape, redirect the freed tentacle at Marwen or Sabriel to spread grapple pressure.

**FEEL-G3 (Medium):** Escape DC 16 vs Bazgar Athletics +7 is 60% — fair. But the re-grapple rate (45%) means Bazgar has only a 60% × 55% = 33% chance of being free AND staying free after any given turn-pair. The net "free" probability plateau is ~33% per 2-turn window. Over a 4-round encounter, Bazgar spends roughly half his turns on escape. This is a significant DPR reduction.

**BUG-G1 (logged as BUG-A32-07):** Maw auto-crit not encoded in DB. The 39 avg critical damage vs 21 avg base damage difference means the runner systematically under-reports Maw damage when a grappled target is struck. DM must track manually.
