## Phase B — Scenario: `drain-divinity-vs-sabriel` (thrulm-rotation index 4)

*Slice definition: Simulate a round where the beholder spends all 3 legendary actions on Drain
Divinity vs Sabriel (divine martial 5). Compare temp HP gained vs damage forgone from
1 Tentacle + 1 Void Ray alternative. Flag if the trade is clearly suboptimal.*

---

### Setup

- Sabriel: divine martial 5 (Paladin / oath-class). HP 44, AC 19, heavy armor + shield.
  Assumed CHA save +6 (CHA 16, +3 mod, +3 PB — standard Paladin CHA save proficiency).
  3rd-level spell slots: 2 × 3rd (highest available at level 5 Paladin).
  Divine Smite from 3rd-level slot: 4d8 = avg 18 extra damage per hit.
- Beholder HP 110, in-chamber (Void-Feeding +1 atk/dmg always active). 3 LA available.
- Beholder range from Sabriel: **variable** — see positioning analysis below.
- Drain Divinity: 3 LA, 30 ft range, DC 16 CHA save. On fail: target loses highest slot; beholder gains 2×slot_level THP.

### Save probability

| PC | CHA save mod | P(fail DC 16) |
|----|-------------|----------------|
| Sabriel (Paladin 5, CHA +3, PB +3) | +6 | 45% (needs 10+ = 55% succeed) |

If Sabriel has CHA 18 (+4): save mod +7, P(fail) = 35%.  
Worst-case for party: CHA 14 (+2): save mod +5, P(fail) = 55%.  
All estimates assume no Legendary Resistance (LR burned here is 0).

### Option A — Drain Divinity (3 LA)

Expected outcomes when beholder spends 3 LA on Drain Divinity vs Sabriel:
- P(success strip) = 45% (base CHA +6 assumption).  
  → On strip: Sabriel loses 3rd-level slot. Beholder gains 6 THP.  
  → Value of slot stripped: 3rd-level Divine Smite = avg 18 bonus damage per hit. If Sabriel
    hits ~50% of the time (vs beholder AC 17 at +8 or +9 attack), slot is worth ~9 expected
    damage forgone. A 3rd-level cure spell is worth 14-21 HP expected value — call it 15.
    Use 15 as the slot-denial EV.
- Expected THP from strip: 0.45 × 6 = **2.7 expected THP**
- Expected slot-denial EV: 0.45 × 15 = **6.75 expected damage-equivalent forgone**
- Option A total EV ≈ **9.5 expected-equivalent** (slot denial + THP)
- Damage dealt to Sabriel this action: **0**

### Option B — Tentacle Lash LA (1 LA) + Void Ray LA (2 LA) = 3 LA total

**Tentacle Lash (1 LA):** +7 to hit in-chamber vs AC 19. Needs 12+ on d20 = 45% hit.
- Expected damage: 0.45 × 14 (3d6+4 in-chamber) = **6.3 expected damage**
- Hit also grapples (escape DC 16). Expected grapple value: constrains Sabriel's movement,
  gives Maw advantage on next multiattack. Treat as free rider.

**Void Ray (2 LA):** DC 16 DEX save. Sabriel in heavy armor: DEX likely 10-12 (+0 to +1).
- Assume DEX save +1 (no proficiency). Needs 15+ to succeed = 30% success, 70% fail.
- Expected: 0.70 × 22 + 0.30 × 11 = 15.4 + 3.3 = **18.7 expected damage**

Option B total: 6.3 + 18.7 = **25.0 expected damage** to Sabriel.

### Trade comparison

| Option | LA cost | Damage to party | Other effect |
|--------|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| A: Drain Divinity | 3 | 0 | 6.75 EV slot denial + 2.7 THP (45% success) |
| B: Tentacle + Void Ray | 3 | 25.0 expected | Grapple rider on Sabriel |

**Option B delivers 2.6× more expected value than Option A. The trade is clearly suboptimal.**

For Drain Divinity to be break-even, the denied slot would need to be worth ≥ 25/0.45 ≈ **55 expected
damage-equivalent** — which no 3rd-level Paladin slot achieves. At level 5, there is no slot level
where Drain Divinity out-values 3 LA of direct damage.

### Positioning dependency (critical finding)

Drain Divinity has **30 ft range**. The beholder's default hover is 40-60 ft from the floor (per
tactics section). To use Drain Divinity:

1. Beholder must descend to within 30 ft of Sabriel (vertically).
2. Using a Legendary Move (1 LA) to descend costs 1 LA from the 3 LA budget.
3. After Move: 2 LA remaining — **not enough for Drain Divinity (3 LA cost)**.

Consequence: Drain Divinity is only usable if the beholder is **already within 30 ft at the time
it wants to fire**. This requires either:
- Positioning during the beholder's own turn (using its movement + Shrine-Drift bonus action to
  descend before the first PC acts), which exposes it to full melee range from all PCs, OR
- A round spent at low altitude (first round only, before PCs close), burning Shrine-Drift BA.

In the standard "beholder hovering at altitude" default position, Drain Divinity is functionally
unavailable without sacrificing the Move LA. But spending 1 Move + 2 Void Ray = same 3 LA and
deals more damage (18.7 EV vs 9.5 EV from Drain Divinity, even if already in range).

**The 30 ft range makes Drain Divinity an either-never-or-sacrifice-play.** The "degenerate every
round vs divine casters" concern from the known state may be overstated — in practice the beholder
cannot reach Sabriel at 30 ft without entering her threat range (divine martial with potentially
sanctified weapons).

### Legendary action economy summary for this slice

| Comparison | Drain Divinity spend | Tentacle + Void Ray spend |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| EV output | ~9.5 equivalent | ~25.0 damage |
| Positioning cost | Must be ≤30 ft | Any range (120 ft) |
| Beholder exposure | Within Sabriel's melee range | Safe at altitude |
| Sabriel response on beholder's turn | Can melee-attack beholder if adjacent | Cannot (beholder at altitude) |

### New findings from this slice

**FEEL-021:** Drain Divinity's 30 ft range forces the beholder to choose between (a) using the
ability and exposing itself to melee from Sabriel / Bazgar or (b) staying at altitude and never
using it. The existing known-state note says "consider cooldown or 2-LA cost" but the range
constraint is the bigger barrier. Recommend: **extend Drain Divinity to 60 ft** (matching Compel
Thrall), which lets the beholder use it without descending. At 60 ft, it becomes viable as a 3-LA
spend when Sabriel is the round's best target. **Log only — design call.**

**FEEL-022:** Drain Divinity THP economy is negligible. 6 THP (from a 3rd-level strip) is absorbed
in <1 average incoming hit from the party (Bazgar at +7 attack vs AC 17 deals 10-18 damage per
hit). If THP were replaced with HP regeneration (e.g., "beholder regains HP equal to twice the
slot level" — same numbers but real HP), the ability would have meaningful durability value. As THP,
it evaporates before the next attack. Combined with the 45% fail rate, the beholder heals an
expected 2.7 THP per 3 LA spent — far below the 25 expected damage forgone. **Log only.**
