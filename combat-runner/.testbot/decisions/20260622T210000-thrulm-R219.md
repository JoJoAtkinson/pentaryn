#### FEEL-R219-02 (MINOR — automation gap)

**Unstable Form advantage applies to Oath-Breaking Retaliation attacks but is never surfaced by runner.**

**Location:** `shrine-touched-derro.md`, Unstable Form trait + `oath_breaking_retaliation` reaction.

**Issue:** Oath-Breaking Retaliation triggers on any damage ≥1. Unstable Form activates on any hit that deals ≥10 damage in a single turn. When a ≥10-damage hit triggers both simultaneously, the retaliation attack should be rolled with advantage. The runner surfaces neither trait automatically.

**Quantified impact:** Against Sabriel (AC 19), Shrine-Axe retaliation without advantage needs 15+ (30% hit). With Unstable Form advantage: need both d20s to fail (1-(0.3²)) → wait, need the HIGHER of two dice to be 15+. P(at least one ≥ 15) = 1 - P(both < 15) = 1 - (14/20)² = 1 - 0.49 = **51% hit.** That's a 21-point probability gain from a forgotten trait.

In the Phase B simulation: the initial -28 hit triggered both Oath-Breaking Retaliation AND Unstable Form. The retaliation rolled 20 vs AC 19 with advantage — a hit that would likely have been a miss without it (raw roll was 15, barely meeting AC).

**Design note:** This is FEEL-R218-01's inverse. FEEL-R218-01 noted Unstable Form advantage is wasted on Ancient Resonance turns (save, not attack). FEEL-R219-02 notes the advantage DOES matter on Oath-Breaking Retaliation turns — when a hit dealing ≥10 damage triggers the reaction, the advantage-enhanced retaliation is a meaningful threat multiplier. It's just invisible to the runner.

**Needed fix:** No DB fix possible without restructuring Unstable Form as a trigger event. Log for human review: if Unstable Form is automated in a future DB update, it should apply to `oath_breaking_retaliation` attack rolls.
