## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `lair-action-round`

*Scenario from rotation.yml (index 5):* "2 thrall derro present (HP 22, AC 14 each). Bazgar within 20 ft of shrine. Both derro within 60 ft of beholder. Simulate optimal and non-optimal lair action choices."

### Setup State

- **Beholder:** 90 HP (mid-combat, taken some hits), Void-Feeding active, 3 LA available, Antireality available.
- **Bazgar:** HP 40, AC 18, DEX +1. Within 20 ft of the shrine. Grappled earlier this combat — now free.
- **Marwen:** HP 18, AC 15. ~35 ft from shrine.
- **Sabriel:** HP 32, AC 19. ~30 ft from shrine.
- **Thrall 1:** HP 22, AC 14. 15 ft from Bazgar. Within 60 ft of beholder.
- **Thrall 2:** HP 22, AC 14. 20 ft from Sabriel. Within 60 ft of beholder.

Initiative count 20 — lair action fires before anyone acts this round.

---

### Option A: Void Eruption (optimal)

All creatures within 20 ft of shrine must make DC 16 DEX save. Only Bazgar qualifies.

- **Bazgar DEX +1:** Needs 15+ on d20 to succeed. P(succeed) = 6/20 = **30%**. P(fail) = **70%**.
- **Damage on fail:** 11 (2d10) force. **No "half on success" in stat block** (all-or-nothing — confirmed in source text; see FEEL-R230-02 note below).
- **EV:** 0.70 × 11 + 0.30 × 0 = **7.7 avg force damage.**
- Bazgar: 40 → 32.3 expected HP.

If Marwen or Sabriel enter the 20 ft radius: EV multiplies. Against Marwen (DEX +3, needs 13+, 40% success → 60% fail): 0.60 × 11 = 6.6 additional. Full party within shrine radius = 7.7 + 6.6 + 0.70 × 11 = **21.7 avg** from a single lair action.

**Table experience:** Bazgar rolls a save. High stakes — no counterplay except staying outside the 20 ft radius. Clean and quick.

---

### Option B: Unstable Ground (middle)

One creature within 60 ft, DC 16 DEX save or fall prone. Optimal target: Bazgar.

- **P(prone):** 70% (same save).
- **Direct damage:** 0.
- **Setup value:** If Bazgar is prone, melee attacks against him have advantage (ranged have disadvantage). Bazgar must spend half movement (15 ft of 30) to stand.
  - Thrall Dagger (+3 vs AC 18 with advantage): P(hit) = 1 − (0.75)² = **44%**. Avg 4 damage. EV per thrall = 1.76.
  - 2 thralls × 0.70 (probability Bazgar is prone) × 1.76 = **2.46 avg setup damage next round.**
- Bazgar's own attack: prone doesn't affect melee attacks he makes, only movement.

**Total EV this round:** 0. **Next-round conditional EV:** ~2.46 (requires Bazgar stays prone AND thralls act next turn).

**Table experience:** Creates a meaningful decision for Bazgar: stand up (costs 15 ft movement, full attack) vs stay prone (free action, but attacks against you have advantage). More interesting from a positioning standpoint. Synergizes with the Rager if also present.

---

### Option C: Manifest Thralls (non-optimal)

2 charmed thralls within 60 ft gain 1 THP each (beholder CHA +1 = minimum 1). Each can use reaction to move or attack.

- **THP:** 1 HP per thrall. Against AC 18+ party dealing 5-12 damage per hit, 1 THP is a rounding error. **Negligible.**
- **Reaction attack (Dagger +3 vs Bazgar AC 18):** P(hit) = 30%. Avg 4 damage. EV = 1.2 per thrall.
  - 2 thralls both attack: **2.4 avg damage total.**
- **Reaction move:** Repositions thralls 30 ft (no OA). Better attack position next turn.
  - If repositioned to flank, next-turn attacks may be at advantage: EV rises to ~3.5. But still much weaker than Void Eruption.

**Total EV:** 2.4 avg (attacks) or ~0 + setup (reposition). **1 THP is effectively wasted.**

**Table experience:** The DM must choose move vs attack for each thrall separately. Both feel weak. The mechanic exists but doesn't matter.

### Key Observations

**1. Lair action + going-first creates no-counterplay opening.** Void Eruption fired before any PC acted (init 20), then the beholder went first (Init 18) and knocked Sabriel to 0 HP before she could act. Pattern from R229 repeats: the party's divine caster (most vulnerable to the beholder's strategy) is eliminated or neutralized before her first turn in a majority of initiative orderings where the beholder wins initiative.

**2. Void Eruption dominance is structural, not situational.** In this scenario with only Bazgar in range, Void Eruption still outperformed Manifest Thralls 3:1. If the party clusters (which they must to fight effectively at melee), the dominance increases to 10:1+ (3 PCs in 20 ft = ~21 EV vs 2.4). The lair action rotation has one correct answer whenever anyone is near the altar. **The DM has no meaningful lair action decision to make.**

**3. Manifest Thralls 1 THP confirmed useless at this party level.** 1 THP on a CR 1/4 thrall (HP 22, AC 14) against a party dealing 5–14 damage per hit is absorbed on the first attack roll that hits. The reaction-attack opportunity (2.4 avg total) does not compensate. Confirmed: Manifest Thralls never fires as the optimal lair action in Compass Edge encounters.

**4. Void Eruption "no half on save" notation.** The stat block reads "taking 11 (2d10) force damage on a failed save" — no "half on a successful one." This is all-or-nothing: 11 on fail, 0 on success. The cheat sheet doesn't flag this explicitly. A DM used to AoE half-damage convention may apply half on success (which would change EV from 7.7 to 9.35), making the ability moderately stronger than written. See FEEL-R230-02 below.

**5. Unstable Ground is the most interesting lair action but the least played.** It creates a genuine positional mini-game (Bazgar's half-movement decision, advantage for melee attackers) but loses on EV every round anyone is near the shrine. In an encounter designed around the shrine altar, "near the shrine" will be true every round after the party engages. Unstable Ground effectively only fires if the party plays defensively at range — which loses them the encounter against the beholder's ranged attacks anyway.

#### FEEL-R230-01 (HIGH/NEW) — Manifest Thralls lair action mathematically dominated under all Compass Edge conditions

**Finding:** Across all lair action round configurations tested this fire, Manifest Thralls generates at most 2.4 avg EV (2 thralls with dagger reaction attacks). Void Eruption generates 7.7 avg (1 PC in range) to 21.7 avg (all 3 PCs in range). Unstable Ground generates ~2.5 avg conditional setup value. Manifest Thralls is strictly dominated by both alternatives when THP matters (1 THP < any hit) and when the party's AC (18–19) makes reaction attacks ineffective at +1 to +3 to-hit.

**Root cause:** THP formula = CHA modifier (minimum 1) = **1 THP**. The beholder's CHA is +1. This was already flagged in known state. Simulation this fire confirms it contributes zero marginal value in practice. The reaction attack opportunity (EV 2.4) is real but insufficient to compete.

**Redesign options (Joe's call):**
| Option | Effect |
|---|---|
| Raise THP to 5 × charmed thralls count (2 thralls = 10 THP total) | Reduces 1 hit from party but still weak if party hits for 8+ |
| Replace THP with "thralls gain +2 AC until end of beholder's next turn" | Reduces expected hits, more relevant at table |
| Change reaction effect to "thrall makes one melee attack with ADVANTAGE instead of a reaction" | 2 thralls with advantage: EV 3.5 each = 7.0 total, competitive with Unstable Ground |
| Redesign as "one thrall immediately acts as if it is its initiative turn" | Full-turn action economy is worth ~3-4 avg damage; still weaker than Void Eruption but meaningful |

#### FEEL-R230-02 (LOW/NEW) — Void Eruption "no half on success" not flagged in cheat sheet; DM confusion risk

**Finding:** `beholder-thrulm.md` Lair Actions section: "taking 11 (2d10) force damage on a failed save." No "or half as much on a successful one." This is all-or-nothing (unlike most 5e AoE effects). The COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md SAVE DCs section lists this ability without clarifying the "no half on success" behavior.

At-table risk: DMs familiar with 5e AoE conventions (Fireball, Shatter) will instinctively give half on a success. This raises effective EV from 7.7 to 9.35 per target — about 21% stronger than written.

**Fix path:** Add "(no damage on successful save)" parenthetical to the Void Eruption entry in the cheat sheet SAVE DCs table. No stat block change needed.
