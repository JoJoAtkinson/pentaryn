### Key findings

#### FEEL-R155-01: Action Surge creates meaningful first-round agency but doesn't shift the outcome (NEW)

Bazgar's Action Surge dealt **31 damage** in a single turn, enough to put the beholder at 65/110 HP entering Round 2. Combined with Marwen's Fireball (14 more), the party dealt **45 damage in Round 1** — roughly 41% of the beholder's HP pool from burst fire.

This is the "correct feel" for an unwinnable encounter: the party gets a moment of agency, their burst tools connect, but the beholder absorbs the punishment and wins anyway. Bazgar's player spent their major cooldown resources (Action Surge, 2 superiority dice) and moved the beholder from 110 → 65 HP without altering the outcome.

**Verdict:** The encounter threateens/unfair vs. party dichotomy is working as intended. The burst round *feels* dangerous to the beholder but isn't. The round-2 helplessness is earned, not arbitrary. No design changes needed.

#### FEEL-R155-02: Battlemaster Trip Attack wasted on beholder — prone immunity undocumented (NEW)

Bazgar could have used Trip Attack (knock prone on failed STR save) but used Menacing Attack instead. If Bazgar had used Trip Attack:
- The 1d8 extra damage from the superiority die would still apply
- The prone condition would be null (beholder is immune to prone)
- Bazgar's player would likely feel cheated, especially if they didn't read the beholder's full stat block before combat

The cheat sheet's damage resistances/immunities table (at the bottom) lists condition immunities for the beholder but buries prone in a list of 8 conditions. There is no "this is why Shove Prone/Trip Attack's condition is wasted" callout anywhere.

**Disposition:** LOG (CS-DRIFT-11). Add "Prone immune — superiority die damage applies; Trip Attack / Shove Prone condition does not" to beholder entry in cheat sheet. Joe's call.

#### FEEL-R155-03: Action Surge tracking is entirely DM-manual — no runner support (NEW)

Action Surge (1/short rest) and Battlemaster superiority dice (4×d8 at level 5) are the highest-output PC resources in the encounter. The combat runner has no mechanism to:
- Track whether Bazgar's Action Surge has been used
- Track how many superiority dice remain
- Track DC for Battlemaster maneuvers (DC = 8 + STR mod + PB = 8+4+3 = 15)

This is expected for PC actions (PC stats are in the roster YAML, not the DB), but it means the DM must track these manually alongside the runner output. No bug — noting as an automation gap in the PC-tracking story.
