### Key findings from Phase B

#### FEEL-R159-01 (NEW) — Unstable Form has no DB automation path; high DM cognitive load at damage resolution

When shrine-touched takes ≥10 damage in a single turn, Unstable Form grants advantage on attacks until end of its next turn. There is NO DB mechanism to auto-apply this:
- No condition trigger
- No passive toggle
- The multiattack row has no conditional advantage flag

At the table, damage resolution fires the `oath_breaking_retaliation` reaction prompt simultaneously. The DM must:
1. Process the damage number
2. Surface the reaction prompt and decide whether to use it
3. Roll the reaction attack (manually, no DB roll)
4. Apply self-psychic damage (1d4)
5. ALSO remember to apply Unstable Form for the shrine-touched's next turn

Four simultaneous cognitive tasks at one damage event. Unstable Form is the most easily dropped.

**Disposition:** LOG. Priority medium. Add to shrine-touched .md: "DM NOTE: When Unstable Form fires, immediately place a token or note on the shrine-touched's area noting 'ADV through [next turn end]'. Do not rely on memory." No DB fix possible without structural combat-runner support for passive conditional advantages.

#### FEEL-R159-02 (NEW) — Unstable Form + Oath-Breaking Retaliation timing ambiguity (RAW resolution documented)

When shrine-touched takes ≥10 damage and the attacker is visible:
- **Unstable Form:** "gains advantage on attack rolls until the end of its next turn"
- **Oath-Breaking Retaliation:** immediate reaction attack on the attacker

Does Unstable Form advantage apply to the reaction attack?

**RAW ruling:** The reaction fires during the attacker's turn (the turn the damage was dealt). Unstable Form's window is "until the end of its NEXT turn" — that is, until the end of the shrine-touched's own upcoming turn. The reaction attack fires BEFORE the shrine-touched's own turn begins. Therefore:
- The reaction attack occurs OUTSIDE the Unstable Form window.
- Advantage does NOT apply to the Oath-Breaking Retaliation reaction attack.
- Advantage DOES apply to the shrine-touched's multiattack on its own next initiative count.

This is non-obvious at the table. A DM seeing "gained advantage from taking damage" may instinctively apply it to the immediate reaction attack as well.

**Recommended clarification in shrine-touched.md:**
> *Timing note: Unstable Form advantage applies to the shrine-touched's own turn attacks only. It does NOT apply to the Oath-Breaking Retaliation reaction (which fires on the attacker's turn).*

**Disposition:** LOG. Add timing note to shrine-touched.md Reactions section or Traits section as a DM cue. Not a stat-block error — just clarification.

#### FEEL-R159-03 — Ancient Resonance 15-ft cone catches full party in Thrulm hollow (confirmed, by design)

In this simulation, all 3 PCs were within the 15-foot cone. The Thrulm chamber's tight dimensions make this nearly guaranteed when the party is in melee with a shrine-touched near the altar. Three-target resonance output: ~32 avg damage (necrotic + psychic) on expected save rates vs Compass Edge.

At ~33% recharge rate (Recharge 5-6), this fires once per ~3 rounds. In the context of the full encounter (also facing beholder + Void Scream), the combined area-damage threat is significant:
- Beholder Void Scream: 33 avg psychic vs all within 30 ft
- Shrine-Touched Ancient Resonance: ~27 avg necrotic vs all within 15 ft
- Total area-threat potential in one round: ~60 avg HP if both fire in same round

Marwen's 32 HP cannot survive both firing at her without prior healing. However, both require specific recharge conditions, so simultaneous firing is ~10% probability per round.

**Disposition:** LOG as encounter feel note. The area-threat stacking is by design (CR 13 beholder + support cast = unwinnable). No balance change needed; add to DM notes that Ancient Resonance timing should be reserved for full-party clustering moments.
