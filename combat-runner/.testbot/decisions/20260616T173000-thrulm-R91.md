### Manual Simulation

**Setup:**
- Derro Rager (HP 52, AC 16) in melee. Derro Shardcaller (HP 33, AC 14) at 40-ft back-line.
- Marwen (Wizard 5, HP 32, AC 15) in combat.

**Rager Taunt (BA):**
- DC 12 CHA save vs Marwen. Marwen CHA ~+0 (Wizard), d20 vs DC 12, needs 12+, 45% success.
- If Marwen fails (55% chance): disadvantage on **attack rolls** vs non-Rager targets.
- Effect: Marwen's d20 attack rolls (Fire Bolt, Scorching Ray) vs Shardcaller are at disadvantage.
- Effect does NOT apply to: saving throw spells (Fireball, Shatter, Thunderwave, Hold Person).

**Marwen's action — Shatter (save-based):**
- Shatter: 3rd-level, 3d8 thunder in 10-ft radius, DC 14 CON save.
- Taunt disadvantage (attack rolls only) is irrelevant — Shatter is a CON save, not an attack roll.
- Shardcaller CON 12 (+1): DC 14 CON save, needs 13+, 40% success.
  - Expected: fail (60%). Damage: avg 13.5 → 13.
  - On success: half (6.75 → 6).
  - Expected damage: 0.60 × 13.5 + 0.40 × 6.75 = 8.1 + 2.7 = 10.8 avg.
  - Scenario uses 13 (full damage) for regression test simplicity. ✓

**Assert HP:** Shardcaller: 33 − 13 = 20. ✓

**Conclusion:** Scenario PASS. Taunt condition correctly doesn't suppress save-based spell damage. ✓

### Phase B — Manual Generative Analysis: Taunt Target Value vs. Save-Based Casters

**Key finding:** At level 5, Marwen has likely prepared Fireball (3d8+3=3rd-level slot, save-based) as
her primary AoE. Taunt's "attack-roll disadvantage" has NO effect on:
- Fireball (DEX save)
- Shatter (CON save)
- Thunderwave (CON save)
- Hold Person (WIS save)
- Hypnotic Pattern (WIS save)
- Magic Missile (no roll at all)

Taunt DOES affect:
- Fire Bolt (cantrip, attack roll)
- Scorching Ray (attack rolls)

At level 5, Marwen's highest-value actions are save-based (Fireball, Counterspell). Taunting her costs
the Rager a bonus action and achieves near-zero tactical effect unless Marwen defaults to Fire Bolt.

**By contrast, Taunting Bazgar:**
- Bazgar (Fighter 5): ALL attacks are weapon attack rolls (+7 to hit).
- Taunt on Bazgar → disadvantage on all Greataxe attacks vs non-Rager = -55% → ~30% hit rate on Rager's allies.
- This IS the correct Taunt target when the shardcaller or shrine-touched need protection.

**Tactical gap identified:** The Rager's Taunt priority note says "prefer the back-line caster" to
force redirection. This is correct when the caster uses attack-roll spells. Against a save-based wizard
(Marwen at level 5), Taunting Bazgar is strictly better: it disrupts ~45% of his attack rolls on
non-Rager targets while leaving the Rager as the path-of-least-resistance for Bazgar's melee.

**FEEL-R91-01 (NEW, LOG ONLY):** Rager Taunt priority note (tactics section) recommends "prefer the
back-line caster." Against a level 5 save-based wizard (Fireball primary), this is near-useless. The
note should add: "Taunt is most effective vs attack-roll PCs (Fighter, Barbarian, Rogue). If the
primary threat is a save-based caster (Fireball, Shatter, hold spells), prefer Taunting the melee
PC instead — the caster bypasses Taunt entirely via save-DC spells, while a taunted Fighter loses
~25 percentage points of hit rate on every weapon swing against non-Rager allies." Joe: decide whether
to add this caveat or leave Taunt priority as is.
