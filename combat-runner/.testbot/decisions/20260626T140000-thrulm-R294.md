### Manual Dice Math — What Berserk SHOULD Do

**Berserk text:** "Makes one Greataxe attack against each creature it can reach. Cannot move this turn."

**Positioning analysis for Thrulm chamber (large vault):**

| Combat state | PCs in reach | Berserk EV | Multiattack EV | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bazgar solo engaged | 1 | 0.35 × 8.5 = **3.0** | 2 × 0.35 × 8.5 = **5.9** | **−2.9 (Berserk loses)** |
| Bazgar + Sabriel (flank) | 2 | 2 × 0.35 × 8.5 = **5.9** | 2 × 0.35 × 8.5 = **5.9** | **0 (tie — no-move penalty = net loss)** |
| All 3 PCs adjacent | 3 | 3 × 0.35 × 8.5 = **8.9** | 2 × 0.35 × 8.5 = **5.9** | **+3.0** |

Notes:
- To-hit at +4 vs AC 18 (Bazgar) = 35%; vs AC 15 (Marwen) = 50%; vs AC 19 (Sabriel) = 30%.
- Mixed-AC with 3 PCs: EV = (0.35 + 0.50 + 0.30) × 8.5 ≈ **9.8** (vs Multiattack 5.9 = +3.9).
- Even in the best case (all 3 PCs adjacent, 1/3 recharge rate), Berserk only
  outperforms Multiattack by ~3–4 EV, once every 3 turns on average → **net EV advantage: +1.0–1.3/turn**.

**No-movement penalty cost:** The Rager cannot close to a new target or reposition. In a fight where
PCs maintain range discipline (Marwen stays 30+ ft back, Sabriel stays 10 ft back), only Bazgar is
ever in reach. **Berserk fires into a 1-target state every time the party plays tactically.**

**Rotation-gap conclusion:** Berserk is dominated by Multiattack in ≥65% of realistic combat states
(1–2 PCs in reach). The 33% recharge rate combined with the dominant-alternative (Multiattack, always
available) makes Berserk dead weight in standard Thrulm positioning.

### Legendary Action Economy — Beholder Focus (Supplementary, this run)

For context: while Berserk is this fire's primary focus, the rager is always used alongside
the beholder. The beholder's LA economy directly shapes whether the party has *any* actions free
to cluster near the rager (enabling the Berserk multi-target upside).

**Drain Divinity vs party positioning:**

If Beholder uses Drain Divinity (3 LA) on Sabriel, Sabriel cannot afford to
be within 30 ft of the beholder. She stays at range → moves AWAY from the rager.
Result: the party naturally spreads to avoid the beholder's drain range, making the rager's
Berserk multi-target scenario impossible.

**The beholder's LA degenerate strategy indirectly kills the rager's Berserk upside.**

Drain Divinity causes the party to spread. Spread party = only Bazgar in rager's reach. Berserk
hits 1 target = dominated by Multiattack. This is a systemic feedback loop where the beholder's
most dominant LA also removes the primary condition for the rager's recharge ability to matter.

**FEEL-RAGER-BERSERK-EVA-R294-01 (NEW): Berserk dominated by Multiattack in ≥65% of combat states.**

Berserk EV exceeds Multiattack only when 3 PCs are all simultaneously adjacent. With 3 PCs at
Level 5 in a large vault (Thrulm chamber), party positioning naturally prevents this:
- Marwen stays 30+ ft back (fragile, AC 15 — will die to Void Scream if she clusters)
- Sabriel stays 10–15 ft back (avoids Drain Divinity range on beholder, avoids melee with rager)
- Only Bazgar presents as a melee target

Even with 3 PCs adjacent, Berserk outperforms Multiattack by only ~3–4 EV, once per 3 turns →
net time-averaged advantage ≈ +1 EV/turn. The no-movement penalty further depresses this.

**Systemic feedback:** The beholder's Drain Divinity (open item #4) actively prevents the party
from clustering, making Berserk's 3-target upside structurally unreachable when the beholder is present.

**Recommendation (log for Joe):** Consider one of:
(a) Remove no-movement penalty — let the rager charge through after Berserking (more dangerous, more fun)
(b) Replace Berserk with a bonus-action Reckless Charge: move up to speed and attack all PCs the rager
    passes through (requires touch-and-go opportunity attack logic the runner doesn't support)
(c) Make Berserk the rager's **round-1 opener** only (not recharge) — it moves INTO combat swinging at
    whatever it passes, then settles into Multiattack. Removes the "dead weight recharge" feel.

**Priority: LOW** (design call; affects feel not mechanical correctness).
