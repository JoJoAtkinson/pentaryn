## Phase B — Generative Playtest Slice

**Scenario:** `drain-divinity-vs-sabriel` (rotation.yml index 4; 116 % 8 = 4)  
**Core question:** Beholder spends all 3 legendary actions on Drain Divinity targeting Sabriel (divine
martial, level 5). Simulate 3 consecutive rounds. Is the mechanic sound? Does 3-LA cost feel correct?

---

### Setup

**Sabriel's spell slot pool (level 5 full caster):** 4×1st, 3×2nd, 2×3rd  
**Sabriel's CHA save:** +2 (assumed per rotation.yml)  
**Drain Divinity:** DC 16 CHA save. On fail: loses highest-level slot. Beholder gains THP = 2× spell level lost.

**LA economy:** Drain Divinity costs 3 LA. Beholder refreshes 3 LA at start of its own turn. Therefore:
- Drain Divinity fires AT MOST once per round (uses all 3 LA at end of one PC's turn)
- "3 saves" = 3 consecutive rounds, not 3 uses in one round

**P(save fail):** DC 16, Sabriel saves at +2. Need 14+ on d20 to succeed. P(fail) = 13/20 = **65%**. P(success) = 35%.

---

### Round-by-round simulation

**Round 1 — Beholder uses Drain Divinity (3 LA) at end of first PC's turn**

Target slot: 3rd-level (highest, ×2 available).

| Outcome | Probability | Effect |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Fail | 65% | Sabriel loses one 3rd-level slot. Beholder gains 6 THP. |
| Success | 35% | No effect. |

EV THP gain = 0.65 × 6 = **3.9 THP**

---

**Round 2 — Drain Divinity again (3 LA)**

Regardless of R1 outcome, highest remaining slot is still 3rd-level (×2 if R1 succeeded, ×1 if R1 failed). Same mechanics.

| Outcome | Probability | Effect on THP |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Fail | 65% | Sabriel loses one more 3rd-level slot. Beholder gets 6 THP. |
| | | **But: if R1 also failed, beholder already has 6 THP. THP do not stack — equal award is NOT gained.** |
| Success | 35% | No effect. |

**THP stacking discovery (new — see FEEL-R116-01):**

5e PHB p.198: "If you have temporary hit points and receive more of them, you decide whether to keep the ones you have or gain the new ones. They can't be added together."

If R1 failed (65% likely), beholder has 6 THP entering R2. R2 Drain Divinity against a 3rd-level slot again gives 6 THP — equal to current, provides **0 marginal benefit**. Beholder still has 6 THP.

**True EV THP from R2:** 0.35 × (6 THP, beholder had 0 THP from R1 miss) = 0.35 × 6 = 2.1 THP EV marginal gain. Combined with R1: total EV THP = **3.9 + 2.1 = 6.0 THP over first two rounds** (because if R1 hit, R2 adds 0 on the 65% branch).

Actually more precisely:
- P(both fail) = 0.65 × 0.65 = 42%: beholder has 6 THP after R1; R2 gives 0 additional THP (equal). Net = 6 THP.
- P(R1 fail, R2 success) = 0.65 × 0.35 = 23%: beholder has 6 THP. Net = 6 THP.
- P(R1 success, R2 fail) = 0.35 × 0.65 = 23%: beholder had 0 THP after R1; R2 gives 6 THP. Net = 6 THP.
- P(both succeed) = 0.35 × 0.35 = 12%: Net = 0 THP.

**EV THP after 2 rounds = 0.88 × 6 + 0.12 × 0 = 5.3 THP** (not 7.8 as naive calculation would suggest).

---

**Round 3 — Drain Divinity again (3 LA)**

After R1+R2:
- P(both 3rd-level slots gone, 42%): Highest = 2nd-level. Drain gives 4 THP.
  - Current THP is 6 (from R1). 4 < 6 → beholder keeps 6. **Net THP gain = 0.**
- P(one 3rd-level slot remains, 46%): Highest = 3rd-level. Drain gives 6 THP.
  - If beholder has 6 THP already: 0 marginal gain. If 0 THP: +6.
- P(both 3rd-level slots still present, 12%): As above, same logic.

In all cases where the beholder's THP is already 6 (from any prior successful drain): subsequent drains yield **0 THP** regardless of slot level drained (as long as drain ≤ 3rd-level = 6 THP). THP from a 2nd-level drain (4 THP) actually decrements the beholder's THP protection.

**True total THP across 3 rounds of Drain Divinity:** Expected **~5–6 THP** total. This is 5% of the beholder's HP pool (110 HP). The THP ramp the design implies ("drain 3rd, then 2nd, then 1st" = 6+4+2 cumulative) is fully blocked by the no-stacking rule.

---

### Slot denial impact on Sabriel

| Path | P(path) | 3rd slots lost | 2nd slots lost | Key ability lost |
|------|---------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|
| All 3 fail | 27% | 2 | 1 | Revivify ×2, one 2nd-level (e.g. Aid/Shining Smite) |
| R3 success | 18% | 2 | 0 | Both Revivifys gone |
| R2 success | 15% | 1 | 0 | One Revivify gone |
| R1+R2 success | 12% | 0 | 0 | No slots lost |
| Various | ~28% | mixed | | |

**EV slots lost = 3 × 0.65 = 1.95 slots.** Expected outcome: ~2 slots drained, likely both 3rd-level.

**Losing both 3rd-level slots (42% chance by end of round 2):** Sabriel cannot Revivify, cannot Dispel Magic, cannot Blinding Smite at 3rd level. She retains 3×2nd and 4×1st. Her offensive output drops significantly; critically, the party loses its resurrection safety net.

---

### Legendary action economy comparison

In rounds where beholder uses Drain Divinity (3 LA), it forgoes all other LA options:

| Alternative use of 3 LA | Damage dealt | Other effect |
|------------------------|-------------|-------------|
| Tentacle (1) + Void Ray (2) | ~13 bludg + grapple + ~22 force avg | Grapple sets up next Maw with disadvantage |
| Tentacle × 3 | ~39 avg bludgeoning (up to 3 targets) | 3 grapple attempts |
| Move + Tentacle + Void Ray | repositioning + ~35 avg | Position advantage |
| **Drain Divinity × 1** | **0 HP damage** | **6 THP + 65% one 3rd-level slot removed** |

The opportunity cost of Drain Divinity: ~35–39 avg damage foregone. In exchange: 6 THP + 65% chance of removing Revivify. Against Compass Edge specifically, this trade is sound **only because** Revivify is the difference between TPK and a narrow party survival. If the party had no Revivify (or had already used them), the trade is strictly inferior to Tentacle+Void Ray in every round.

**Conclusion:** Drain Divinity is contextually correct vs. Sabriel but only as an OPENING move (round 1–2, before both 3rd-level slots are gone). From round 3 onward, Drain Divinity gives 0 marginal THP (2nd-level drain = 4 THP, beholder already has 6) and only a 65% slot removal on a slot tier that matters less. The ability needs an exit condition: "once Sabriel is out of 3rd-level slots, Drain Divinity is mechanically dead weight vs her."

### Phase B — New feel notes

**FEEL-R116-01 (new):** Drain Divinity THP gain is effectively **one-time**. THP from repeated drains of the same or lower slot level does not stack (PHB p.198). After the first successful 3rd-level drain (6 THP), all subsequent uses provide 0 marginal THP — the design's implied "ramp up THP as you drain" is blocked by the stacking rule. At-table the DM will likely forget this; many tables will mistakenly add THP on each drain. Recommend clarifying in the ability text: *"The beholder may gain these temporary hit points only if they exceed its current temporary hit point total."* Not auto-fixable — wording change; needs Joe.

**FEEL-R116-02 (new):** The 3-LA cost makes Drain Divinity a **round-exclusive** choice. In any round the beholder uses Drain Divinity, it deals zero HP damage (the LA pool is spent). This creates a binary feel: either the beholder is killing PCs (Tentacle+Void Ray) or it's stripping Sabriel's spells (Drain Divinity), never both. Against a 3-PC party at level 5 where every PC HP pool matters, this may produce round-1 degenerate play: the beholder should almost certainly open with Drain Divinity round 1 (before Sabriel acts), then Tentacle+Void Ray every round after. This is known-open; confirming the degenerate loop holds.

**FEEL-R116-03 (new, refinement of known open):** Drain Divinity vs. a non-Sabriel target (e.g., Marwen at Wizard 5) is even weaker: Marwen has 4×1st, 3×2nd, 2×3rd (same pool) but her slots enable damage (Fireball at 3rd) rather than team survival (Revivify). Spending 3 LA to remove one Fireball slot vs. dealing ~35-39 avg damage with Tentacle+Void Ray is likely wrong. The ability is *specifically* designed for anti-paladin/anti-cleric use. Confirm the encounter design intent and add a note to beholder tactics: "Only use Drain Divinity if Sabriel still has 3rd-level slots. Switch to Tentacle+Void Ray once her top-tier slots are gone."
