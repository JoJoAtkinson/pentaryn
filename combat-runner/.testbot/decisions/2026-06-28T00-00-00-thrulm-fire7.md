**Decision needed:** Should Arcana and Perception be raised to +8/+7 (consistent with PB+5), or should they stay at +6/+5 (and the prior save/attack corrections be reconsidered)?

### NEW: BUG-FIRE7-02 — Thrall "no saves" language contradicts Fractured Will

`thrall-derro.md` Saving Throws line: `"None; dominated mind has no saves"`  
Fractured Will trait: `"...it makes a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw."`

The "no saves" phrasing is misleading. In 5e terms, "no saving throw proficiencies" is correct; the creature can still attempt ability saves without proficiency. The current wording implies the creature *cannot make saves at all*, then immediately asks it to.

**Proposed clarification (log-only; text-level ambiguity, no mechanical change):**  
Replace `"None; dominated mind has no saves"` with `"None (no proficiency in any save)"` to match the actual 5e meaning and stop contradicting Fractured Will.  
Human review needed before applying.

## Phase B Findings

Scenario adapted from rotation #4 (action economy theme → beholder LA budget focus).  
Ran 3 rounds with dice math.

### B-1: Drain Divinity dominant — confirmed again

With Sabriel (divine) on field, Drain Divinity (3 LA = full budget) is correct play every round  
whenever she is within 30 ft. Competing uses (Move+Tentacle, Void Ray, etc.) are strictly worse  
as long as Sabriel has spell slots remaining.

In this run: beholder drained one 3rd-level and one 2nd-level slot in two rounds.  
Sabriel was reduced to 5-slot utility by Round 3. No counterplay from party observed —  
Bazgar cannot reach beholder unless it descends; descending to use Drain Divinity then triggers  
Bazgar melee (risky but worth it for the beholder).

**Still open. Joe's call on cooldown or 2-LA cost (as flagged fire #6).**

### B-2 (NEW): Beholder action starvation when both rays are on recharge

Round 2: Disintegration Ray not recharged (no 5-6), Void Scream not recharged (no 6).  
At 40-ft hover height, beholder had **zero main-action attacks**.  
Options available: Shrine-Drift (bonus action reposition), Compel Thrall (thralls were dead),  
Clay-Shaping (1-minute ritual — impossible mid-combat).

Resolution this run: beholder descended to melee, used Multiattack (Tentacle ×2 + Maw).  
This exposed it to Bazgar's opportunity attacks and Action Surge on next turn.

**No non-recharge ranged attack exists for the beholder at distance.**  
SRD beholders have eye ray attacks as their fallback. This stat block strips those.  
A basic "eye-stalk" ranged attack (no recharge, lower damage, maybe 1d8+3 force, +8 to hit,  
range 60 ft) would fill dead turns without changing the recharge economy.  
**Log-only. Design decision for Joe.**

### B-3: Thrall utility ~0 in this fight config

Both thralls dropped to a single Fireball (28 avg vs 22 HP, 55% fail rate).  
Their only measurable contribution: forced Marwen to spend her 3rd-level slot slot on Fireball  
instead of a potentially higher-value spell (Fireball is optimal here, so this is fine).  
From round 2 onward they were gone; beholder lost Compel Thrall entirely.

This doesn't warrant a fix but confirms the "Manifest Thralls 1 THP" lair action futility —  
if thralls die in round 1, there's nothing to grant THP to.

### B-4: Encounter is lethal-not-unfair

Marwen died on beholder's first turn but got one meaningful full round (nuked two enemies).  
The encounter feels like a "the party was warned this is CR 13" moment, not a gotcha.  
Rager + beholder LA combo creates genuine trapped-feeling pressure on Bazgar.  
No balance concerns beyond the already-flagged ones.
