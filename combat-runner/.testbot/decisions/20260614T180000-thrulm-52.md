**BUG-A52-01 — no prone-advantage prompt in runner output (NEW):**
Unstable Ground fires at init 20 and applies prone to Bazgar. The beholder acts immediately after (init 18). The runner outputs the UG narration and effect, but neither the UG effect text NOR the multiattack/tentacle_lash DB entries remind the DM that melee attacks vs a prone target have advantage. At-table risk: DM resolves TL1 and TL2 without advantage, missing a ~25% per-die hit-rate improvement (+18.75 pp improvement on P(≥1 TL hit)). The advantage from prone turns the grapple-establishment probability from 75% to 93.75%. This is the primary mechanical function of Unstable Ground as a synergy setup — and the runner is silent about it.

**Fix:** Add to `unstable_ground` effect text: `"(Until it stands — costs half movement — melee attackers within 5 ft roll attacks with ADVANTAGE.)"` This is PHB p.292 restated, but necessary when DM transitions immediately from lair action to beholder attack. Requires `combat_action_upsert`.

**Initiative-order DD sensitivity (confirmed):**
When Sabriel wins initiative over the beholder (19 > 18, as simulated), the R1-DD-hold protocol is moot — Sabriel has already acted before the beholder's first turn. DD can only fire post-Sabriel R2 in this init order. This is the BUG-DD-temporal-ordering-note case: "If beholder has higher initiative, accept that DD strips only leftover slots." In this fire's sim: beholder acted R2 after LoH, and DD fired R2 post-Sabriel (stripping her L3 slot after she already spent LoH as an action). Confirms that beholder being slower than Sabriel shifts DD value from "strip full slot pool" to "strip residual post-LoH slots only."

**FEEL-F52-01 (NEW — VS coverage vs grapple maintenance conflict):**
This scenario exposes a positional dilemma not previously logged. When beholder has grappled Bazgar (prone, restrained to beholder's reach), and wants to fire VS R2 to catch all three PCs:

- VS has 30-ft radius. If Marwen hangs back at 35+ ft (sound wizard play), beholder must **Shrine-Drift** (bonus action, 30 ft) toward Marwen to catch her in VS.
- But: FIX-FC48-B says Shrine-Drift does NOT drag grappled creatures. A 30-ft Shrine-Drift toward Marwen would move the beholder out of its own 10-ft TL reach → **grapple on Bazgar breaks.**
- Trade-off: fire VS with partial coverage (Bazgar + Sabriel only, Marwen excluded) OR Shrine-Drift to catch Marwen (releasing Bazgar's grapple → losing the Maw auto-crit opportunity this round + losing Void-Feeding positioning near shrine).
- The tactics note for VS does not address this tension. The current tactics just say "R2 VS" without noting the bonus-action conflict with Shrine-Drift when a grapple is maintained.
- **Recommendation:** Add to VS positioning note: "If maintaining a grapple on Bazgar (TL reach 10 ft), do NOT Shrine-Drift to catch a back-line caster beyond 30 ft — the drift breaks the grapple and loses Maw auto-crit positioning. Fire partial-coverage VS (Bazgar + Sabriel) rather than drift-to-include-Marwen. The grapple is worth more than one additional VS target at this party level (Maw crit ~40 avg > one additional VS inclusion at 65% fail × 33 avg = 21.5 expected)." *Joe edits tactics.*

**FEEL-F52-02 (NEW — DR vs disintegrated PC timing in prone chain):**
In R2 simulation: DR recharged and Marwen (10 HP) was disintegrated before Bazgar (prone, 10 HP, grappled) was finished off by TL. This creates a sequencing note: **the beholder should always fire DR before continuing Multiattack if DR is recharged and a low-HP conscious target exists.** The prone chain (Bazgar still grappled from R1) has a Maw auto-crit available, but firing DR first on the 10-HP wizard is strictly better: guaranteed kill + permanent death. Firing the Multiattack first (on Bazgar) delays DR by one resolution step and risks the wizard acting before DR fires. This isn't a bug (the beholder can sequence DR then Multiattack freely since DR is the action and Multiattack is also the action — they're alternatives), but it reinforces the "fire DR first when recharged" principle in the tactics. Already implied by tactics note, but the prone simulation makes it explicit: when TL grapple is already established from a prior round, the Maw auto-crit opportunity persists — don't rush to Multiattack R2 if DR is live.
