### Feel Analysis — Void Scream at Full-Party Fail

**VS damage vs party HP pools:**
- Bazgar (49 HP): 33 psychic = 67% HP → critical but alive.
- Marwen (32 HP): 33 psychic = 103% HP → **always downed on a failed save** (max HP 32 < avg VS damage 33). Any failed WIS save = Marwen unconscious. P(Marwen fails) ≈ 65% (WIS +2, DC 16 → needs 14+, 35% save rate).
- Sabriel (44 HP): 33 psychic = 75% HP → critical but alive.

**VS WIS save rates vs party (DC 16):**
- Bazgar (WIS likely +0): needs 16+ = 25% save → **75% fail rate**.
- Marwen (WIS +2 typical): needs 14+ = 35% save → **65% fail rate**.
- Sabriel (divine martial, WIS +2–+4): needs 12–14+ = 35–55% save rate. Using WIS +2: 65% fail.

**Expected per-PC outcomes on VS (no Bless):**
- Bazgar: P(downed) = 0 (33 < 49; even max roll 60 doesn't one-shot at full HP — wait, 6d10 max = 60 > 49 → possible. P(6d10 ≥ 49) = P(each d10 ≥ 8.16 avg...) low. Expected damage 33; downed only if unlucky roll. At full HP, P(Bazgar downed by VS) ≈ ~8%.
- Marwen: P(downed on fail) = 100% (avg damage > max HP). P(fail) ≈ 65%. P(Marwen downed) = 65%.
- Sabriel: P(downed) ≈ 3–5% on avg roll; more on high rolls. P(6d10 ≥ 44) ≈ 12%.

So if VS fires with all 3 at full HP: ~65% chance Marwen goes down, ~8% Bazgar down, ~12% Sabriel down. With prior R1 damage (Bazgar at 16–31 HP after multiattack, Sabriel at 36–44 HP), the probability of one or more PCs downed by VS rises significantly.

**FEEL-R83-01 (NEW — LOG ONLY): VS → FRIGHTENED rider is mechanically decisive; no scenario tests it**

The HP-tracking scenario (`thrulm-void-scream-aoe`) tests raw damage. But VS has a second rider on FAILED save: **FRIGHTENED for 1 minute** (DC 16 WIS save each turn ends it). The frightened condition:
- Imposes **disadvantage on all attack rolls** while the beholder is in LoS.
- Prevents the target from **willingly moving closer** to the beholder.

For Bazgar at 16 HP (frightened after VS): he **cannot close to melee with the beholder**. His entire combat role (melee Fighter) is negated. He can attack at disadvantage from range (javelins: +5 hit, 1d6+4, marginal output) or do nothing useful. Similarly Sabriel at 11 HP (frightened) cannot advance for smites.

After a full-party failed VS save: two melee PCs are locked out of their primary offense against the beholder, and Marwen is at 0 HP. The remaining party has **zero effective offense against the beholder until a DC 16 WIS save is passed at end of a PC's turn** (35–65% per turn = 1–2 rounds average). In those 1–2 rounds, the beholder acts twice more.

This is not a bug (beholder is meant to crush), but the FRIGHTENED rider after VS creates a "mechanics shut-out" that feels different from "lethal but counterplay-possible." There is no action the party can take to counter the frightened condition except succeeding the save. No item, no positioning, no spell easily breaks it at L5 (Calm Emotions is L2 but Marwen is downed).

**Recommendation (LOG ONLY):** Consider whether Frightened should have a shorter duration or a weaker version on VS. The existing clarification in the stat block ("Creatures within 10 ft of the shrine have disadvantage on this save") stacks the shrine-proximity disadvantage with the already-high DC — PCs near the altar fail even more reliably. If two PCs are melee-range (within 10 ft of altar when VS fires), they fail at advantage: P(fail at disadvantage) = (14/20)² = 49% save → 51% fail. Less severe, but still high.

**No new VS scenario to add this fire** — a test asserting `@frightened` condition on a PC after VS would require the runner to auto-apply conditions from save-based effects, which the runner does not yet support (it expects manual DM application). A `no_crash` scenario would be redundant with `thrulm-void-scream-verb`.

## Feel Assessment: `empty-void`

### FEEL-R83-02 (NEW — LOG ONLY): 3 LA windows per round against 3-PC party creates 4 effective beholder turns

In the no-derro configuration, the beholder has THREE distinct LA windows per round (end of Sabriel's turn, Marwen's turn, Bazgar's turn). Combined with its own main action, this gives the beholder **4 effective attack sequences per round**:
1. Own turn: Multiattack (2 TL + Maw) — 37.7 avg damage, potentially downs one PC.
2. After Sabriel: 3 LA → DD (strip slot) OR Tentacle+VoidRay (2+1 LA).
3. After Marwen: 1–2 LA → Tentacle or Void Ray.
4. After Bazgar: 1 LA → Tentacle.

This is the "Drain Divinity de facto dominant" problem (FEEL-F80-01) extended: in a full-round LA budget, the beholder can effectively take 2 extra full attacks on top of its action. Against a 3-PC party, the LA economy makes the beholder equivalent to ~1.5× its CR in practical offensive output. Since the party can't add more PCs to dilute the LA windows, this asymmetry is structural.

**Impact on encounter design:** The LA budget (3 LA/round, recharge each own turn) is standard 5e beholder design. The issue is that with only 3 PCs (vs. the typical 4–5), each PC's turn ends with a full or partial LA window. Against the "meant to lose" design intent, this is fine — but the ratio means the party has genuinely zero counterplay windows. Any action Sabriel or Bazgar takes is immediately answered by a LA.

**Recommendation (LOG ONLY):** The 3-PC limitation for Compass Edge means the beholder gets 3 LA windows per round — same count as PC actions. This is effectively a 1:1 action economy. For the "meant to lose" narrative, this is appropriate. But DM note: **players may feel the encounter has no fair-pressure gradient** since every action they take is immediately answered. Consider whether the "you're meant to lose but it should feel like a fight" design goal requires at least 1 round of PC agency before the LA windows kick in (i.e., explicitly note that the beholder should NOT spend R1 LAs on non-DD purposes, since wasting R1 LA on Tentacle when DD is the priority is suboptimal — FIX-FC40-A already documents this).

### FEEL-R83-03 (NEW — LOG ONLY): "Burn Antireality" tactic undocumented — creates meaningful PC agency

In the empty-void simulation, Sabriel attacked first (init 12) and burned the beholder's Antireality reaction. This meant Marwen's Fireball and Bazgar's melee attacks in the same round were NOT penalized by Antireality (it's once per round).

**The correct PC tactic:** Whoever acts first after the beholder (and has an attack that can draw Antireality) should burn it intentionally — even at the cost of a disadvantaged attack — so that subsequent PCs attack at normal odds. This is a meaningful tactical decision that creates **genuine PC agency** in an otherwise hopeless fight.

However, this tactic is **not documented anywhere** — not in the beholder's stat block, not in the cheat sheet, not in the encounter overview. DMs running this encounter may not realize Antireality can be deliberately exhausted in this way. Players certainly won't know without a DM hint.

**Recommendation (LOG ONLY):** Add a DM note to `beholder-thrulm.md` Antireality section or the cheat sheet: "Antireality refreshes each round at the start of the beholder's turn (per standard 5e reaction rules). Once spent in a round, all subsequent attacks vs the beholder are at normal odds until the beholder's next turn. Experienced players can coordinate to burn Antireality with a low-value action (thrown dagger, cantrip) before the main attacker acts — this is intended counterplay, not an exploit."

### FEEL-R83-04 (NEW — LOG ONLY): Void-Feeding "+1 to damage rolls" — does it boost save-based abilities?

**Finding:** Beholder Void-Feeding trait says "+1 to attack rolls and damage rolls" while within the chamber. The VS, UG, and VE abilities deal damage via saves, not attacks. In 5e, "damage rolls" applies to dice rolled after an attack or ability deals damage — including save-based damage (e.g., a Fireball's damage roll applies DEX save modifiers, but the die roll is still a "damage roll").

If VF applies to save-based damage: VS = 6d10+1 avg 34 (not 33), VE = 2d10+1 avg 12 (not 11). The stat block states "33 (6d10)" and "11 (2d10)" — these values do NOT include a +1. Either:
- (a) The +1 was intentionally excluded from save-based abilities (VF only boosts attack damage), OR
- (b) The stated averages are missing the +1 and should be "34 (6d10+1)" and "12 (2d10+1)."

**Impact:** VS avg going from 33 to 34 does not change Marwen's outcome (both exceed max HP 32). UG (no damage) unaffected. VE 11→12 is marginal. The damage stakes don't shift meaningfully, but the stat block inconsistency is a correctness gap that DMs may notice.

**NOT an auto-fix** (would require a design decision about VF scope; could be intentional). LOG ONLY for Joe.
