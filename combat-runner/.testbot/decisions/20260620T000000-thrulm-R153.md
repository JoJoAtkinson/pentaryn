### New bug found — STAT-R153-01 (NEW)

**Beholder Maw: orphaned "disadvantage on the saving throw" clause**

`beholder-thrulm.md`, Actions section:

> **Maw.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 21 (4d8 + 3) piercing damage. If the target is a creature grappled by the beholder, the target has disadvantage on the saving throw.

The Maw action has no saving throw — it is a straight melee attack (to-hit roll, then damage). The clause "the target has disadvantage on the saving throw" is orphaned text with no referent in the current action. Not present in any previous decisions log; appears to predate the action's current form.

**Possible original intent:** An earlier version of Maw may have included a "swallow" or similar secondary effect with a saving throw (e.g., Swallowed condition requiring CON save). That secondary effect was removed but the clause was left behind.

**Impact at table:** A DM reading this raw will be confused which saving throw applies. If they improvise a "saving throw vs being mangled" it creates a phantom mechanic. The DB row for `multiattack` (which contains Maw) has no saving throw field, so the runner is not affected — the confusion is entirely at the stat block text level.

**Disposition:** LOG ONLY (design call). Safe fix would be removing the orphaned clause entirely. But "if grappled, disadvantage on STR (Athletics) checks to escape" would also be a plausible correction (and mechanically interesting). Joe must decide.

### New Finding — FEEL-R153-01 (NEW)

**Round 1 alpha-strike eliminates Marwen before her first turn — "unfair not lethal"**

When the beholder wins initiative (DEX +3 gives initiative 17–20 on avg), Marwen (lowest AC and HP) never takes a turn before being unconscious. On the average multiattack split (65%/65%/65%), the expected damage is 32.5 vs Marwen's 32 HP — a coin-flip on whether she's exactly dead or has 1 HP remaining. On any above-average roll distribution, she's down.

**Why this matters:** The briefing defines "unfair rather than lethal" as "one-round kills before the player got a turn." This is the exact scenario. The party *is meant to lose*, but the point is to give them a harrowing fight with decisions — not to eliminate a PC from the encounter before they roll initiative.

**Frequency:** Beholder beats Marwen in initiative when it rolls ≥ Marwen's roll (both at +2/+3 base = ~50% beholder-first). In roughly half of encounters, Marwen acts before the beholder. In the other half, she doesn't.

**Aggravating factor:** Even if Marwen survives turn 1 (beholder targeting others), the beholder will have her grappled and unconscious by round 2 via the same pattern. The question is whether she got one action first.

**Options for Joe (design calls, logged not fixed):**
1. **Beholder targeting rule:** Add to tactics — "In round 1, beholder targets the divine caster (Sabriel) with Tentacle Lashes to grapple, saving Maw/DR for after grapple is established. Targeting Marwen immediately is optimal but removes her from play before she acts." This is a flavor/feel note, not a rule.
2. **Reduce multiattack to 2 attacks on round 1:** Beholder "tests" the party first. Mechanically: drop Maw from the action, fire only 2× Tentacle Lash. Expected damage drops to 21 avg — Marwen survives turn 1.
3. **Initiative reorder:** Give Marwen advantage on initiative (familiar Alertness or similar) so she reliably goes before the beholder or at least some rounds.
4. **No change needed:** The CR 13 beholder is *supposed* to instantly threaten a L5 party. Marwen dropping round 1 is intended. Document it explicitly so the DM knows to narrate "this is how outclassed they are."

### Legendary Action Economy — Round 1

**After Sabriel's turn (acts at init 16, before Bazgar/Marwen):**
- Beholder spends 1 LA: **Tentacle Lash (1 LA)** on Sabriel. +7 vs AC 19 (needs 12+, 45% hit). Avg 14 bludgeoning on hit. Grapple rider DC 16 STR: Sabriel is divine martial (likely STR +3-4), needs 13-12+ to escape = 40-45% escape. Expected: Sabriel grappled more often than not.
- LA remaining: 2.

**After Bazgar's turn (acts at init 13):**
- Beholder spends 2 LA: **Void Ray (2 LA)** on Bazgar. DC 16 DEX save. Bazgar (Fighter, likely DEX +0 to +1): needs 15-16+, ~20-25% succeed → 75-80% fail → avg 22 force damage.
- LA remaining: 0.

**After Marwen's turn (init 10):**
- Marwen is unconscious. No turn. No LA needed.

**Round 1 LA damage summary:**
- Tentacle on Sabriel: 0.45 × 14 = **6.3 avg HP** (+ grapple condition)
- Void Ray on Bazgar: 0.78 × 22 = **17.2 avg HP**
- **Total LA round 1: ~23.5 avg HP** across two targets

**Economy verdict:** 3 LA producing 23.5 avg damage (+ Sabriel grappled) on top of the multiattack that already downed Marwen. Round 1 is extremely high damage output. Party enters round 2 with:
- Bazgar: ~27 HP (down 22)
- Sabriel: ~37-38 HP (grappled, down 6-7)
- Marwen: unconscious (0 HP, death saves)
- Beholder: 110 HP

**Feel:** Round 1 output is mechanically correct but creates a severely asymmetric second round — 2 PCs vs a CR 13 monster with full resources, while the third makes death saves. This is "designed lethal" per the encounter intent.

### Drain Divinity timing in round-1 context

Drain Divinity costs all 3 LA. If the beholder opens with Drain Divinity after Sabriel's turn:
- No Tentacle LA, no Void Ray LA.
- Drain Divinity DC 16 CHA: Sabriel (divine martial, likely CHA +2-3): needs 13-14+, ~35-40% succeed, ~60-65% fail → Sabriel loses highest slot (L3 = 6 THP to beholder) or divine ability.
- Result: beholder gains 6 THP; Sabriel loses one L3 slot.

**Comparison vs Tentacle+VoidRay:**
- Drain Divinity: 6 THP (conditional on fail) + resource denial
- Tentacle + Void Ray: 6.3 + 17.2 = 23.5 avg damage (hard HP removal from 2 PCs)

Against a L5 party, Drain Divinity is almost always worse than Tentacle+Void Ray in round 1. Sabriel has 2× L3, 3× L2, 4× L1 at level 5 — losing one L3 is notable but doesn't neutralize her. The 23.5 avg direct damage is a more immediate threat multiplier.

**Known issue re-confirmed (BUG-DD-01):** Drain Divinity is dominant only if the party has a divine caster with many high-level slots (e.g., L7+ cleric). Against L5 divine martial Sabriel with 2× L3 slots, Drain Divinity's expected value is roughly one free Void Ray equivalent per 3 LA spent. This tracks with the prior "Drain Divinity is degenerate every round vs. divine casters" flag — but at L5, it's actually *weaker* than Tentacle+Void Ray, not stronger. The "degenerate" risk materializes at higher levels. No design change needed for this encounter; the concern is forward-looking if this encounter runs at L7+.
