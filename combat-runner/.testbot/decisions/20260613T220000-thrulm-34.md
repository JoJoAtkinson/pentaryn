## Phase A — New Findings

### FEEL-F34-01: Cheat sheet omits Altar Zone fire/radiant suppression (AUTO-FIXED)

`COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md` vulnerabilities table listed Shrine-Touched as vulnerable to fire/radiant without noting this vulnerability is suppressed within 60 ft of the altar. Since shrine-touched rarely leave the altar zone, a DM reading the cheat sheet would plan around Fireball doing double damage and be surprised at the table. Fixed: added "(suppressed within 60 ft of altar — pull them away first)" to the vulnerability column and updated the "What Kills What Fastest" row accordingly.

### FEEL-F34-02: Compel Thrall vs "enslaved" immunity — ambiguous (log only)

Thrall Derro condition immunities include "charmed (enslaved instead)." Compel Thrall targets "one creature charmed by [the beholder]." Thralls are not technically charmed — they are enslaved. Whether Compel Thrall can target a thrall is ambiguous. The flavor note in Compel Thrall says "usually a dominated derro," implying intent is yes, but RAW the condition tag is different. Joe must rule: does "enslaved" count as "charmed" for the purpose of Compel Thrall targeting? Suggest: yes, add a parenthetical "(or enslaved by it)" to the Compel Thrall effect text.

### FEEL-F34-03: Bazgar mechanical uselessness against beholder (log only)

From Phase B simulation: Bazgar's attacks deal half damage to the beholder (nonmagic weapon, no sanctified status). Only Sabriel's blessed dwarven blade bypasses the resistance. In a standard fight, Bazgar delivers ~4 avg damage per attack vs the beholder's 110 HP — a rounding error. Meanwhile, the beholder's R1 multiattack into Bazgar (lowest initiative at +0, always last) drops him before his first turn.

This is "lethal by design" (CR 13 vs L5), but the combination of:
1. Bazgar attacks for halved damage  
2. Bazgar always goes last (init +0)  
3. Beholder targets the front-line fighter first  

...means Bazgar has a high probability of contributing zero damage and being downed before acting in R1. This feels less "lethal" and more "pointless" for the player. Design consideration: give the encounter a second blessed weapon, or give Bazgar a combat role that isn't weapon attacks (pushing the beholder into difficult terrain, aiding Sabriel's grapple escape).

No mechanical change warranted — log for Joe's review.

### FEEL-F34-04: Tentacle target-split guidance missing (log only)

The beholder's multiattack fires two tentacle lashes before Maw. The tactics notes document that Maw should target the grappled PC, but there is no guidance on whether Tentacle 1 and Tentacle 2 should target the *same* PC or *different* PCs.

**Two-target (split):** Spreads grapple control — two PCs constrained, one takes Maw auto-crit (~52 avg: 13 tentacle + 39 Maw crit on a 45-55% hit). Higher battlefield control.

**Single-target (stack):** Both tentacles focus one PC — 26 bludgeoning + Maw auto-crit 39 = 65 avg damage to one PC. With average hit rates, this one-rounds any PC except Bazgar from full HP. Does not control a second PC.

At 3 PCs, splitting feels less "spikey" and more tactically interesting. Recommend: split tentacles to two different PCs when two or more are in range; if only one PC is in melee range, stack both onto them. This should be added to beholder tactics notes (not auto-fixed — design call).
