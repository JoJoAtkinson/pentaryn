### New Phase A findings this run

**STAT-01 — Shrine-Touched Perception bonus off by 1 (arithmetic; log only)**  
Shrine-Touched stat block explicitly states `Proficiency Bonus +3`. With WIS -1:
- Stealth: DEX +2 + PB +3 = +5 ✓ (matches listed +5)
- DEX save: DEX +2 + PB +3 = +5 ✓
- CON save: CON +2 + PB +3 = +5 ✓
- Perception: WIS -1 + PB +3 = **+2** — but stat block lists **+1**

Perception is off by 1 vs the declared PB. Not in auto-fix category (only damage averages are auto-fixable). Log for Joe to confirm PB or Perception is authoritative.

**STAT-02 — Beholder Maw has orphaned "saving throw" clause (log only)**  
`Maw` stat block says: *"If the target is a creature grappled by the beholder, the target has disadvantage on the saving throw."* Maw is a pure melee weapon attack with no saving throw. The clause references a saving throw that does not exist in this action. This is dead text — likely a remnant of an earlier design (e.g., a swallow/stun rider). Misleading for DMs; they have no saving throw to apply it to. Needs author decision: add a save effect to Maw, or delete the clause.

**CS-DRIFT-01 — Cheat sheet "Shrine-Touched vulnerability suppressed near altar" not in stat block (log only)**  
The COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md states in two places:  
- Resistance table: `fire, radiant *(suppressed within 60 ft of altar — pull them away first)*`  
- "What Kills What Fastest": `fire/radiant vulnerability suppressed within 60 ft of altar`

The Shrine-Touched stat block has:
- `Damage Vulnerabilities: fire, radiant` — unconditional, no suppression clause
- `Shrine-Bound` trait: damage from being 300+ ft away from shrine; no vulnerability suppression mechanic

The altar-zone suppression is **nowhere in the stat block**. Either:  
(a) The cheat sheet documents an intended design rule that was never added to the stat block, or  
(b) The cheat sheet fabricated this mechanic.  

If (a), the stat block needs a new trait. If (b), the cheat sheet needs correction. Joe to decide.

**CS-DRIFT-02 — Thrall Derro CR absent from stat block (log only)**  
The COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md lists the Thrall as CR 1/4. The `thrall-derro.md` stat block has no Challenge Rating line. PB +2 (explicit in stat block) is consistent with CR 0–4, so CR 1/4 is plausible but unconfirmed. Author should add a CR line to the stat block.

Or Unstable Ground again on Sabriel (grappled PC with disadvantage on the prone save could be stacked). Most likely: **Void Eruption** if party has closed on the shrine.

If Sabriel is grappled: Maw on Sabriel, +7 vs AC 19, avg 22 piercing. Sabriel at 8 HP → **0 HP**. Both Marwen and Sabriel are down.

Bazgar faces the beholder solo.

**Void Scream condition test (Recharge 6 = ~1/6 per turn):** If VS fires on Round 2 (or any round), with Bazgar the sole conscious PC: DC 16 WIS save. Fighter WIS likely +0 to +1. Needs 15–16+ on die. ~25% success rate. **High probability of Frightened.**

**Frightened impact analysis (core of condition-duration-tick test, adapted):**
- Duration: 1 minute (10 rounds). DC 16 WIS end-of-turn save to clear.
- Bazgar frightened: disadvantage on attacks while beholder in LoS; can't move closer to beholder.
- *Practical impact:* Beholder hovers at melee range (fly 30 ft) to use Tentacle Lash. Bazgar as melee fighter doesn't need to move closer — the beholder IS adjacent for its own attacks. The "can't move closer" restriction barely matters when the beholder stays in melee range. Disadvantage on attack rolls IS significant for Bazgar (reduces expected hits). But he's already likely the last PC standing.
- *Duration tracking:* At WIS +0, Bazgar clears Frightened on DC 16 with ~25% chance/turn = expected ~4 rounds to clear. Given Bazgar will die before 4 rounds at 49 HP vs beholder's sustained damage (~14+22 = 36/round via Tentacle+Maw), the condition will likely never clear naturally.

**Verdict:** Frightened from Void Scream against this party functions as a minor attack-roll tax, not a meaningful tactical variable. The condition's duration is irrelevant in practice because the fight ends before natural expiry. The DC 16 WIS save for condition removal has no counterplay for a non-WIS Fighter.

### Phase B conclusions

**FEEL-01 — Marwen one-rounded before she acts (every fight at avg initiative)**  
Beholder Init +3 avg 13.5 vs Marwen Init +2 avg 12. On average rolls, beholder acts before Marwen ~60–65% of the time. With avg multiattack damage ~36 vs her 32 HP, she's dropped before getting a turn in most rounds. Design intent is "meant to lose," but a PC being eliminated before acting = zero counterplay. Recommend: either give Marwen a reaction option (Shield spell, Silvery Barbs) or document the expected casualty pattern in the encounter notes so the table doesn't feel cheated.

**FEEL-02 — Frightened from Void Scream near-inert vs Compass Edge**  
Bazgar: melee, beholder closes to him anyway. Marwen: likely save-spell wizard, no attack rolls to penalize. Sabriel: melee divine martial, disadvantage on attacks is the one meaningful penalty. But Sabriel also tends to be the Drain Divinity target and falls early. Net result: Void Scream's FRIGHTENED rider delivers less value than its design implies vs this specific party. This doesn't need a fix but DMs should know the rider's real impact at this table.

**FEEL-03 — Drain Divinity timing degenerate (reconfirmed)**  
3-LA cost makes Drain Divinity sub-optimal compared to Tentacle (1 LA) + Void Ray (2 LA) = two separate impacts across two creature turns. Against Sabriel, Drain Divinity steals slots — but if Sabriel is grappled Round 1 and dropping Round 2, the beholder never has a clean 3-LA window focused on her before she falls. Dominant LA cycle remains: post-Bazgar Tentacle → post-Marwen Void Ray. Drain Divinity fires zero times in the avg encounter path. Already flagged in briefing as "degenerate"; this run confirms the routing.

**FEEL-04 — Manifest Thralls lair action confirmed useless in default setup**  
2 THP (CHA +1) to up to 3 thralls in a default encounter that starts with zero thralls. Even with 2–3 thralls present, 2 THP is trivially absorbed. The reaction-attack trigger redesign (flagged in briefing) would make this lair action actually useful.

**DB-OPEN-04 — Unstable Form (shrine-touched) automation gap (new this run)**  
`Unstable Form` trait: "After taking 10 or more damage in a single turn, it gains advantage on attack rolls until the end of its next turn." This is a meaningful reactive buff (essentially a triggered damage-threshold bonus), but the shrine-touched DB has no row for it. DM must manually track and apply. During simulation, when shrine-touched takes 12 damage (per thrulm-shrine-touched-retaliation scenario intent), Unstable Form fires — and the combat runner cannot surface or track this. Recommend adding a `utility` DB row with trigger on `"damage"` ≥ 10 in single turn.

**DB-OPEN-05 — Thrall-derro lacks "multiattack" verb (new this run)**  
The thrall's stat block defines a "Multiattack" action (one melee attack). The DB has `hand_axe_weak` (verbs: attack, axe, hit, swing, strike) and `dagger_desperate`. The verb "multiattack" is not registered for the thrall. A DM typing "multiattack" in the runner tab would get LLM fallback (max_llm_fallback: 0 in thrulm scenarios → potential assertion failure). Add "multiattack" to `hand_axe_weak` verbs list via `combat_action_upsert`, or author a `multiattack` row that delegates to `hand_axe_weak`.
