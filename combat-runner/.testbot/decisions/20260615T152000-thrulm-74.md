### Phase B — Analysis

**Stagger rule (key recharge mechanic):**

The stagger rule prevented double-AR in Round 1. If both STDs had fired AR simultaneously in R1:
- Bazgar: 2× 14 avg = 28 damage (49 HP → 21; survivable but tight)
- Marwen: 2× 14 avg = 28 damage (32 HP → **4 HP — at risk**)

With stagger correctly enforced, single AR + Fireball response kept the party functional. Stagger is the primary balance valve. DM discipline: the stagger hold must be assessed **at round start**, before any actions resolve — if the DM waits until STD-B's actual turn to check, there's a risk of incorrectly allowing STD-B to fire AR if STD-A's turn has already passed.

**Recharge roll in combat:**

AR failed to recharge on STD-A's Round 2 roll (33% recharge rate). This created a "weakened berserker" state: STD-A had Unstable Form (advantage on attacks), was fleeing via Driven Escape (BA), and used Multiattack. The combination reads as desperate and thematically appropriate. No mechanical issue — recharge works as designed.

**FEEL-F74-01 (NEW — Log only): OBR absorbs first attack of a multiattack, leaving subsequent attacks undefended.**

When Bazgar attacked STD-A twice in Round 1, OBR fired after Attack 1 (consuming the reaction), then Attack 2 landed without triggering OBR again (reaction already spent). For experienced players, this creates a predictable "tank the OBR with Attack 1, free second swing" pattern. The OBR is most valuable against:
- Single-attack PCs (clerics, certain paladins at L5 without Extra Attack)
- First PC to attack in a round when the STD still has its reaction
- Sequences where the PC triggers OBR before multiple allies swing in the same round

Against multiattack PCs like Bazgar (Extra Attack), OBR provides only one counter-swing per round regardless of how many times they hit. This is RAW-correct and not a bug, but worth documenting so the DM doesn't expect OBR to protect against full-multiattack combos.

**DM guidance:** When facing a multiattack PC, OBR fires on the first hit and spends the reaction for the round. Do not roll OBR for subsequent hits from the same PC. Plan STD placement to position OBR triggers on early-initiative PCs (ideally before Bazgar or Sabriel act) or on PCs with single attacks.

**FEEL-F74-02 (NEW — Log only): AR concentration-check reminder not surfaced automatically by the runner.**

The `ancient_resonance` DB `save.notes` field includes: "⚠️ Any caster who failed this save must make a Concentration check (DC = max(10, half total damage taken))." This is correct per 5e RAW. However, the combat runner does NOT auto-prompt the concentration check when AR hits a caster. In a live session:
- STD fires "resonance" verb
- Runner outputs: save result, damage
- No follow-up prompt appears: "Marwen is concentrating — Con save DC max(10, 7)"

The DM must recall this from memory or the stat block. Given the number of concurrent effects in the beholder encounter (grapple tracking, legendary actions, lair actions), the concentration check is a high-probability miss item.

**Risk:** If Marwen entered the beholder fight with Hypnotic Pattern, Web, or Slow (all concentration), a single failed AR save breaks the spell AND costs Marwen an L2/L3 slot — a double-hit the runner doesn't highlight.

**Recommend:** Add a lair-action or runner-side reminder prompt for AR. Until MCP fix, add a DM note to the shrine-touched tactics section: "Any caster who fails AR must immediately make a Con save (DC = max(10, half damage taken)) or lose any concentration spell."

**AR + Marwen risk at L5:**

Marwen at HP 32 is extremely vulnerable to AR. A failed save deals avg ~14 damage (2d10 ≈ 11 + 1d4 ≈ 2.5). Two AR hits in separate rounds bring Marwen from 32 HP to ~4 HP before Void Scream. If the beholder encounter follows this STD wave without a short rest, Marwen is nearly guaranteed to be below half HP when the beholder fires Void Scream.

This is not an imbalance flag — it's an intended "attrition gauntlet" design — but it means AR should be treated as high-priority fire even against AC 15 (Dex +2, 45% fail rate). The STD's cone attack synergizes with Marwen's inevitable back-line position (within Bazgar and Sabriel's 15-ft zone).

**LA economy / ability rotation (beholder not present in this slice):**

The two-STD wave tests STD mechanics in isolation. Key rotation findings:
- AR stagger fires correctly (1 AR per round from higher-init STD)
- Driven Escape correctly priority-queued below 20 HP
- Unstable Form (advantage) correctly triggered by Fireball damage threshold
- OBR fires once per round per STD regardless of multiattack hits

All five STD checklist items behaved as documented. Rotation gap: concentration check (FEEL-F74-02).
