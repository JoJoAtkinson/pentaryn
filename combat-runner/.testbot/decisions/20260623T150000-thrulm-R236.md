### Dice Math — What Berserk Would Do If Fixed

**Scenario:** Rager is mid-round-2, bloodied (HP ~27 after round-1 damage). Bazgar in melee range, Marwen 25 ft away (out of 5-ft reach), Sabriel at 10 ft (in reach). Two targets in reach.

**With recharge=5 (33% chance available round 2):**

Attack 1 vs Bazgar (AC 18): +4 to hit → hits on 14+, **35% hit rate**.
- Expected damage: 8 × 0.35 = **2.8**

Attack 2 vs Sabriel (AC 19): +4 to hit → hits on 15+, **30% hit rate**.
- Expected damage: 8 × 0.30 = **2.4**

Berserk total expected: **5.2 damage**, split across 2 targets.

Compare to multiattack (2 attacks, concentrated on best target = Bazgar AC 18):
- 2 × (8 × 0.35) = **5.6 damage**, all to Bazgar.

Berserk produces 5.2 spread vs multiattack 5.6 concentrated. **Multiattack is strictly better against any two targets at AC 18+.** Berserk only wins when 3+ PCs are in reach (unlikely at level 5 fight geometry) and even then only marginally.

**2. (NEW — FEEL-R236-01) Berserk is never the optimal action in typical Compass Edge geometry**

Analysis: At level 5, Compass Edge has two melee-capable PCs (Bazgar, Sabriel) and one back-line caster (Marwen). In a typical round:
- Bazgar is in the rager's 5-ft reach.
- Sabriel is adjacent or 10 ft away (divine martial, closes fast).
- Marwen is 30-40 ft away (never in 5-ft reach unless forced).

Berserk fires on 1-2 targets at best. Expected damage is equal to or slightly below multiattack. The ability is penalized (immobility) without adequate reward.

**Against 1 target:** Berserk = 1 attack (worse than multiattack 2 attacks — straightforward loss).
**Against 2 targets:** Berserk = ~5.2 total vs multiattack ~5.6 on best target. Loss by 0.4 damage, spread instead of burst.
**Against 3 targets:** Would need all 3 PCs in 5-ft reach simultaneously — geometrically unlikely given Marwen hangs back.

The only scenario where Berserk wins is a corner scenario (all PCs cornered in a 5-ft cluster). At level 5, skilled players avoid this.

Recommendation (log only — design call): Consider changing Berserk to "reach = 10 ft on this turn" (representing a spinning arc of the axe), which would pull Marwen into range if she's within 10 ft. Or change to recharge 4-6 to improve availability. Or keep as-is but accept it's a "late-round desperation" ability that fires rarely and mostly misses.

**3. (CONFIRMED — Berserk + Taunt synergy, no DB gap)**

Berserk says "cannot move" but does NOT prevent bonus actions. Rager CAN still use Taunt (bonus action) on the same turn as Berserk. This is mechanically valid but undocumented.

Simulation: Berserk (attack all in reach, no move) + Taunt (CHA DC 12 save or disadvantage vs non-Rager) on same turn.
- Against Bazgar: CON/STR saves are strong, but Taunt is CHA save. Bazgar Fighter: CHA save likely +0, +3 (PB) = +3. DC 12 → succeeds on 9+, **60% resist rate**. Not dominant.
- Taunt + Berserk means the rager attacks multiple targets, then forces one of them to focus-fire on the rager (disadvantage vs others). If the Taunt hits, any attempt by that PC to pivot to another target has disadvantage. Mechanically coherent, but the Taunt success rate at DC 12 is low.

No DB gap — Taunt is a utility verb, works as-is. Noting for tactical cheat sheet use.

---

**4. (CONFIRMED — Berserk timing vs Madness Endurance)**

Madness Endurance: "When it takes damage, it gains +1 to attack rolls until the end of its next turn."
Berserk: typically fires on the rager's turn, before it has been hit *this round* (it acts mid-initiative). Madness Endurance bonus requires prior damage. At round 1: no damage taken yet → no +1. At round 2+: depends on whether damage occurred before the rager's turn in that same round.

In the representative initiative (Sabriel 17 → Marwen 14 → Rager 10 → Bazgar 9): if Sabriel or Marwen hit the rager before init count 10, Madness Endurance +1 is active on Berserk. If not (they targeted other enemies), Berserk fires without the bonus. This is initiative-order dependent — the +1 is available about 50% of the time when Berserk fires.

No new flag; confirming prior analysis.
