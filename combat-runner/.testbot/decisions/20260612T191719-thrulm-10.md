### LA execution — Round 2

**Beholder's R2 action:** Multiattack (2× Tentacle + Maw). Grapple attempt on Bazgar.  
*(3 LA budget refreshed at start of beholder's R2 turn.)*

**After Marwen's turn (R2) — Tentacle LA (1 LA), targeting Marwen:**

Marwen just cast Misty Step or Scorching Ray (typical L5 wizard action). Beholder responds:

- Attack: +7 to hit (base +6, +1 VF) vs AC 15
- Hit threshold: d20 ≥ 8 → P(hit) = 13/20 = **65%**
- Hit: 3d6+3 = avg 14 bludgeoning. Marwen is grappled (escape DC 16 — Athletics/Acrobatics).
- Marwen STR likely −1 (Wizard), Acrobatics likely +2. Escape probability: Acrobatics d20+2 ≥ 16 → P(escape) = 7/20 = 35%.
- Expected damage: 0.65 × 14 = **9.1 per LA action**
- Narrative impact: Marwen grappled = speed 0, disadvantage on DEX checks, set up for Maw auto-crit in R3 action.

**After Bazgar's turn (R2) — Void Ray LA (2 LA), targeting Bazgar:**

Bazgar just used Action Surge (Fighter 5). Beholder spends 2 LA:

- Save: DC 16 DEX, Bazgar DEX mod −1. d20 − 1 ≥ 16 → needs 17 on die → P(fail) = 4/20 = **20%**
- Fail: 4d10 force = avg 22. Save: 11 force.
- Expected: 0.20 × 22 + 0.80 × 11 = 4.4 + 8.8 = **13.2 expected**
- Note: Void Ray vs Bazgar is weak because Bazgar's DEX is terrible (−1 mod → 80% save rate). VR works much better against Marwen (DEX +2 → 65% fail rate) or Sabriel (DEX +0 → 80% fail rate).

**Total R2 LA damage: 9.1 + 13.2 = ~22.3 expected across 3 LA.**

**The routing failures for "tentacle", "maw", "bite" are the root cause of A10-05.** A DM spending 1 LA with a short verb gets the full 3-attack multiattack narration and dice instead of the single-attack LA action. This produces two bad outcomes: (a) the DM sees 3 attack rolls when they intended 1, and (b) the "costs 1 LA" qualifier is absent from the output.

**The "move" gap** is lower severity (the legendary Move is mechanically trivial — just 30 ft fly) but means the DM gets no LA-specific output when reposition is the best play.

## Feel issues this fire

### FEEL-F10-01: Slice LA budget overflow exposes a real table problem

The rotation slice accidentally reproduced a real DM mistake: burning Move+Tentacle then reaching for Void Ray and not having the LA budget. At the table, a cold DM who front-loads Move+Tentacle leaves exactly 0 LA for Void Ray and must pass. This is a trap for DMs who don't memorize LA costs.

**Table implication:** The Void Ray (2 LA) is the highest-damage LA option available that isn't Drain Divinity. A DM who wastes both Move (1) and Tentacle (1) on two cheaply-purchased LAs before noticing the Void Ray cost will get nothing from the last third of their LA budget. Over a typical 4-round fight, this is ~one full Void Ray worth of damage lost per run (~13 avg force damage per wasted opportunity).

**Suggested DM prompt (log only):** Add a "LA Cost Quick-Reference" line to the beholder's Legendary Actions section: `Move: 1 | Tentacle: 1 | Void Ray: 2 | Drain Divinity: 3`. Currently the costs are embedded in the prose descriptions and easy to miss mid-table.

### FEEL-F10-02: "Move" LA has no DB entry — gaps in action economy

The legendary "Move" (fly up to 30 ft) has no `combat_action_upsert` entry in the DB. The DM typing "move" at the beholder's tab gets a global `dash` output (no LA context, no cost note). Minor for combat (the repositioning itself is obvious) but contributes to the DM not tracking the 1 LA spend.

**No fix needed unless Joe wants full LA parity in the DB.** The action is mechanically trivial; a note in the beholder's Legendary Actions block is sufficient.

### FEEL-F10-03: Void Ray is a poor LA against Bazgar but there's no guidance to retarget

VR vs Bazgar (DEX −1) has 80% save rate → only 20% fail → avg ~13 damage with 80% landing 11. Against Marwen (DEX +2): 65% fail → avg ~17. The tactics section doesn't note that Void Ray should be retargeted toward higher-DEX PCs when Bazgar is the only available target. A DM following the slice as written will spend 2 LA for ~13 expected damage when the same 2 LA against Marwen yields ~17.
