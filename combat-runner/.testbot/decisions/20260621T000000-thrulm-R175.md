### Scenario: `antireality-timing` — Antireality disadvantage flip rate and economy

**Setup:**
- Bazgar (Fighter Battlemaster 5, HP 49, AC 18) attacks beholder in melee, 2 attacks per Action (Extra Attack).
- Beholder AC 17. In-chamber: Void-Feeding active (+1 attack).
- Bazgar's attack bonus vs beholder: assume +7 to hit (STR +3, PB +4 at level 5, +0 from weapon or magic). [Note: rotation.yml specifies +7 vs AC 17.]
- Antireality: "When the beholder is targeted by an attack it can see, it can use its reaction to impose disadvantage on that attack roll (once per round)."
- DB BUG-F7-02 in play: runner prompts Antireality as "+2 AC post-hit" — DM must manually override to declare pre-roll.

---

### Hit rate math

**+7 to hit vs AC 17:** needs die ≥ 10. Hits on 10–20 = 11 results / 20 = **55.0%** base.

**With disadvantage (Antireality declared pre-roll):**
P(lowest die ≥ 10) = P(d1 ≥ 10) × P(d2 ≥ 10) = (11/20)² = 121/400 = **30.25%**.

Antireality reduces one attack from 55% → 30.25% (delta: **−24.75% per use**).

---

### 3-round simulation: Bazgar's Action (2 attacks/round)

Assuming beholder wins initiative (goes before Bazgar): reaction refreshes before Bazgar acts each round.

| Round | Attack | Antireality? | Hit rate |
|-------|--------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | 1 | Yes (reaction spent) | 30.25% |
| 1 | 2 | No (reaction gone) | 55.00% |
| 2 | 1 | Yes (reaction refreshed at beholder's turn) | 30.25% |
| 2 | 2 | No | 55.00% |
| 3 | 1 | Yes | 30.25% |
| 3 | 2 | No | 55.00% |

**Expected hits over 3 rounds:** 3 × (0.3025 + 0.55) = 3 × 0.8525 = **2.56 hits**

**Without any Antireality:** 6 × 0.55 = **3.30 hits**

**Hits suppressed over 3 rounds:** 3.30 − 2.56 = **0.74 hits** ≈ 1 in 4 rounds Antireality converts a hit to a miss.

**Per-round suppression: ~0.25 attacks canceled per round** — meaningful but not decisive.

### Damage impact (with beholder resistance)

Beholder has resistance to nonmagic B/P/S. Assume Bazgar uses a non-sanctified weapon.

- Typical greatsword hit: 2d6 + STR mod. Avg pre-resist: ~10–11. Post-resistance: ~5–6.
- Suppression of 0.74 hits over 3 rounds × 5.5 avg/hit ≈ **4.1 avg damage prevented by Antireality** over 3 rounds.
- Against beholder 110 HP: Antireality saves roughly 4% of the beholder's HP per 3 rounds from Bazgar alone.

If Bazgar has a **sanctified weapon** (+1d8, no resistance):
- Avg hit: ~14.5. Suppression: 0.74 × 14.5 ≈ **10.7 damage prevented** over 3 rounds — more meaningful (~10% HP buffer).

### FEEL-R175-01 (NEW — MEDIUM): Antireality is wasted on FRIGHTENED targets

**Void Scream** imposes FRIGHTENED on failed saves (DC 16 WIS, lasts up to 1 minute with repeated saves). FRIGHTENED PCs have disadvantage on all attack rolls while the beholder is in line of sight.

**Conflict:** If Bazgar is FRIGHTENED from a prior Void Scream, he already rolls with disadvantage. When the beholder uses Antireality against a FRIGHTENED Bazgar:
- Under 5e RAW: multiple sources of disadvantage do not stack (PHB p. 173). Advantage and disadvantage each apply once; having disadvantage twice = still one instance of disadvantage.
- Result: **Antireality is mechanically wasted** against a FRIGHTENED attacker — it spends the reaction but changes nothing.

**Impact on the encounter:** Void Scream fires on Recharge 6 (every ~1.67 rounds on average). If VS hits and Bazgar fails WIS (65% chance: needs 15+ with WIS +1), Bazgar is FRIGHTENED for up to 1 minute — 10 rounds of Antireality dead weight, one reaction wasted each round the beholder uses it against him.

**Optimal play:** Beholder should save Antireality for Sabriel or Marwen's attacks while Bazgar is FRIGHTENED. The stat block's Tactics section does not mention this interaction. A DM running the beholder at full tactical intelligence should mentally check "is this attacker FRIGHTENED?" before declaring Antireality. No prompt in the stat block or cheat sheet.

**Recommendation (log-only):** Add a DM note to the Antireality description: "Skip Antireality against FRIGHTENED attackers — they already roll with disadvantage. Use the reaction on the next non-frightened attacker that turn instead."

### FEEL-R175-02 (NEW — MEDIUM): BUG-F7-02 actively trains DMs to use Antireality wrong

**The core problem:** BUG-F7-02 (open 100+ fires) makes the combat runner prompt Antireality as a post-hit "+2 AC" effect — declared AFTER the roll resolves. The stat block, cheat sheet, and rotation.yml all agree the correct behavior is: declare BEFORE the roll, imposing pre-roll disadvantage.

**Beyond being wrong, the DB text is actively harmful:** Every session the runner is used without fixing BUG-F7-02, the DM sees the "+2 AC post-hit" prompt and internalizes it. This trains the DM's reflexes to think of Antireality as a retroactive AC boost rather than a pre-roll declaration. When the DM eventually reads the stat block (or a player questions the timing), the DM discovers they've been running Antireality wrong for the entire campaign arc.

**The timing difference is mechanically significant:**
- Post-hit "+2 AC": Announced after you know the attack roll. If the roll was 15 vs AC 17: no need for Antireality (miss anyway). If the roll was 19 vs AC 17: use Antireality → retroactively apply +2 → AC 19 → still hit. The DM has perfect information and wastes no reactions.
- Pre-roll disadvantage (correct): Declared before knowing the roll. The beholder must predict whether an attack will land and spend the reaction prophylactically. Sometimes the beholder uses Antireality and the attacker would have missed anyway (wasted reaction). This is the intended risk/reward.

**The DB bug eliminates the intended strategic cost of Antireality entirely.** The reaction is always-correct-to-use when post-prompting, because the DM has perfect information. With the correct pre-roll rule, Antireality on a weak attacker (low bonus) may be wasted. This matters for design: Antireality should sometimes miss its mark.

**Recommendation:** This reinforces the urgency of BUG-F7-02. It is not just a DB text error — it is a systematic distortion of the reaction's strategic character across every session. Priority should be upgraded if interactive MCP time becomes available.

### FEEL-R175-03 (NEW — LOW): Antireality negligible on Bazgar's Action Surge turn

Bazgar (Fighter Battlemaster 5) has Action Surge: once per short rest, grants one additional action → +2 attacks → 4 total attacks on that turn.

**With Antireality on first attack, 3 remaining at normal odds:**

P(Bazgar lands at least one hit in 4 attacks):
- Without Antireality: 1 − (0.45)⁴ = 1 − 0.0410 = **95.9%**
- With Antireality on attack 1: 1 − (0.6975 × 0.45³) = 1 − (0.6975 × 0.0911) = 1 − 0.0635 = **93.6%**

Antireality on an Action Surge turn suppresses P(any hit) by only **2.3%** — effectively negligible. The beholder's reaction is almost always wasted here; the three remaining normal attacks overwhelm any protection from the first-attack flip.

**Better use of Antireality on Bazgar's Action Surge turn:** Skip Antireality entirely on Bazgar, use it on Sabriel's or Marwen's simultaneous attack if one occurs in the same round. If no other attacker acts this round, the beholder may simply absorb the Action Surge and use Antireality next round.

**The stat block provides no guidance on when NOT to use Antireality.** The DM must reason through this at the table. A tactical note would help: "Antireality's per-attack benefit is highest when the target makes 1–2 attacks. Against Action Surge (4 attacks), the first-attack flip is insufficient to suppress Bazgar; consider saving the reaction for another PC's attack the same round."
