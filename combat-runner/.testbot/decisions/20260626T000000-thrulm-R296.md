### Dice Math

**Hit probability:**

| Attacker | To-hit (in chamber) | Target AC | Need | Hit% |
|----------|---------------------|-----------|------|------|
| Disintegration Ray | +7 (+6 base +1 VF) | Bazgar AC 18 | 11+ | 50% |

**Damage if hit:**

| Damage expression | Average | + Void-Feeding | One-shot Bazgar (49 HP)? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10d8 force | 45 | 46 | P(10d8+1 ≥ 49) ≈ 38% |

**Permadeath probability per use:** P(hit) × P(KO | hit) ≈ 0.50 × 0.38 ≈ **19%** per use of
Disintegration Ray.

With recharge 5–6 (1/3 chance per round), the beholder attempts the ray on average once every 3
rounds. Over a 6-round fight, it attempts ~2 rays → ~34% cumulative probability of at least one
Bazgar permadeath event. This is high for a level-5 encounter where "losing is intended" — the
party will die, but dying and *permanently ceasing to exist* are different player experiences.

### Feel Note (carry-forward, confirmed)

**FEEL-003-CONFIRMED:** Permanent death via Disintegration Ray at level 5 is a significant
stakes escalation. The cheat sheet has no callout about permanent death. The DB narration has none.
The stat block text buried at the bottom of the Actions section is easily missed at the table.

Recommendation for Joe: Consider one of:
1. Add the permadeath rider to the DB via `combat_action_upsert` (minimum fix — DM sees it)
2. Add a bold warning to the cheat sheet: "**DISINTEGRATION = PERMANENT DEATH.** No revivify,
   raise dead, resurrection. Only true resurrection or wish can save them."
3. Decide whether to soft-kill this for Compass Edge (make it an incapacitation, not permanent
   death — e.g., "disintegrated into crystalline stasis" that can be reversed once the beholder
   is killed). A "meant to lose" fight doesn't have to mean perma-death.
