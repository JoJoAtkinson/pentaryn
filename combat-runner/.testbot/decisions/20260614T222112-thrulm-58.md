### Supplementary Phase B — Shardcaller Pack Tactics Voice synergy with beholder Void Ray

*Context: Phase A confirmed the shardcaller DB. Pack Tactics Voice is a passive that has not been analyzed in any prior fire. This supplement evaluates its interaction with beholder LA economy.*

**Pack Tactics Voice (PTV):** "When an ally within 30 ft hits, the target has disadvantage on the *next* saving throw before end of its next turn."

This passive fires whenever *any* ally within 30 ft lands a hit on a target. With the shardcaller positioned 40–60 ft from enemies, PTV range is its primary constraint — at 40–60 ft, Ragers in melee are likely 45–65 ft away from the SC, outside 30 ft range. The shardcaller must move to ~30 ft of the frontline to make PTV relevant.

---

**Synergy analysis: PTV → beholder Void Ray LA (2 LA)**

Void Ray forces a DC 16 DEX save (22 avg force damage). Against the party:
- Bazgar DEX −1: P(fail) = (16 − (10 + (−1))) / 20 = 35% (no disadv). With disadvantage: rolls two d20, takes lower → P(fail) ≈ 57%.
- Marwen DEX +2: P(fail) = (16 − 12) / 20 = 20% base. With disadvantage: ≈ 36%.
- Sabriel DEX +0: P(fail) = (16 − 10) / 20 = 30% base. With disadvantage: ≈ 51%.

**PTV → VR gain table:**

| Target | Base P(fail) | Disadvantage P(fail) | Avg VR damage gain |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Bazgar | 35% → 7.7 avg | 57% → 12.5 avg | **+4.8 HP** |
| Marwen | 20% → 4.4 avg | 36% → 7.9 avg | **+3.5 HP** |
| Sabriel | 30% → 6.6 avg | 51% → 11.2 avg | **+4.6 HP** |

*(VR avg: 22 on fail, 11 on save. Expected = P(fail)×22 + P(save)×11)*

At 2 LA cost per VR, the PTV setup is worth using if the SC can land a hit *immediately before* the beholder spends its LA (i.e., SC acts before the PC, and the beholder's LA fires post-that-same-PC's-turn). In practice, this requires:
1. SC acts in initiative before the target PC, AND
2. SC hits the target PC (1d8+2 range; +4 vs AC → ~35% hit vs Bazgar AC 18), AND
3. The PC hasn't yet used their turn (PTV disadvantage is "on the next saving throw before end of PC's next turn" — if the PC takes their turn and makes a save, PTV expires).

The window for PTV → VR LA is narrow: SC hits a PC, beholder fires VR LA *after that same PC's turn ends*. If the PC makes no saves before their turn ends (e.g., they attack but don't make a save), PTV carries into the beholder's LA window. This is actually available most turns since PCs are unlikely to make a saving throw during their own turn unless they use a spell.

---

**FEEL-F58-01 (NEW): Shardcaller PTV is a passive with non-obvious LA synergy; never documented**

PTV creates meaningful expected-damage uplift (+3.5–4.8 HP per VR) but is not mentioned in the beholder tactics section or the cheat sheet. DMs will not naturally think to coordinate "SC must hit first, then beholder spends VR LA against the same target" — it reads as coincidental, not tactical.

The synergy is real (measurable) but requires:
- SC within 30 ft of the frontline (risky for the SC; usually at 40–60 ft)
- SC hitting the VR target before the beholder LA fires
- Beholder reserving VR LA rather than spending it before the SC acts

**Practical assessment:** In a full 6-NPC encounter (beholder + Rager(s) + SC + DWD + STD + Thrall), the SC hitting before the beholder LA fires is common. But SC repositioning within 30 ft to activate PTV exposes it to melee. PTV is strongest when the frontline NPCs close distance to the PCs — then the SC is naturally within 30 ft of the action. In early-encounter positioning (R1), SC is at 40–60 ft (beyond PTV range). PTV becomes relevant R2+ when PCs advance toward the altar or the frontline NPCs are killed and the SC is closing range.

**Recommendation (log only — Joe decides):** Add to beholder tactics: "If a Shardcaller is alive and within 30 ft of the current target, hold one Void Ray LA for after the SC's turn rather than spending it earlier — PTV disadvantage on the same target's next save is worth the wait (~+4 avg damage, no resource cost)."
