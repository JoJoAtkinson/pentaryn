**New Phase A finding — Manifest Thralls lair action target contradiction:**
The lair action text reads "Up to three derro **charmed by the beholder** and within 60 feet..."
In the standard encounter, NO NPCs meet this criterion:
- Thrall Derro: "enslaved" (not charmed; their condition immunity entry is "charmed (enslaved instead)")
- Deep Watch / Rager / Shardcaller / Shrine-Touched: not charmed by the beholder
- Clay-Shaping derro: would qualify, but Clay-Shaping requires 1 minute (unusable in combat)

Consequently, Manifest Thralls can never fire in a standard combat encounter — not merely because
the THP is low (1, as already logged) but because no valid targets exist unless the DM ran
Clay-Shaping as a pre-encounter setup. **Log only (design call). See FEEL-017 below.**

Separately, the same lair action grants "They can immediately use their reaction to move or make
a weapon attack." Thrall Derro's stat block says "Reactions: None." Even if the "enslaved" vs
"charmed" gap were resolved, thralls cannot use this granted reaction. **See FEEL-018 below.**

## Phase B — Scenario: `void-scream-clustering` (thrulm-rotation index 3)

*Slice definition: All 3 Compass Edge PCs clustered in the shrine chamber. Beholder fires Void
Scream (Recharge 6). Verify save math with shrine-disadvantage mechanic; assess frightened cascade
effect on L5 party positioning; check beholder LA economy on the VS turn.*

---

### Setup

- Party: Bazgar (Ftr5, HP 49, AC 18, WIS ~+0), Marwen (Wiz5, HP 32, AC 15, WIS ~+1), Sabriel
  (divine martial 5, HP 44, AC 19, WIS save ~+5 with proficiency)
- All 3 PCs within 30 ft of beholder; Bazgar and Marwen within 10 ft of shrine (disadvantage on VS save)
- Beholder HP 110, in-chamber Void-Feeding active (+1 all atk/dmg); 3 LA available
- Void Scream: DC 16 WIS, 6d10 psychic avg 33 fail / 16 save; frightened 1 min on fail (DC 16 Wis ends)
- VS uses beholder's **action** — LAs remain available for between-turn spending this round

### Save probability table

| PC | WIS save mod | Disadvantage? | P(fail) |
|----|-------------|---------------|---------|
| Bazgar (Ftr5, WIS ~+0) | +0 | Yes (within 10 ft shrine) | 1 − (5/20)² = **94%** |
| Marwen (Wiz5, WIS ~+1) | +1 | Yes | 1 − (6/20)² = **91%** |
| Sabriel (divine martial 5, WIS +5 proficiency) | +5 | No | (11/20) = **55%** |

### Expected damage

| PC | Avg fail dmg | Avg save dmg | Expected | HP after |
|----|-------------|-------------|----------|----------|
| Bazgar | 33 | 16 | (0.94×33)+(0.06×16) = 32.0 | **17.0** |
| Marwen | 33 | 16 | (0.91×33)+(0.09×16) = 31.5 | **0.5** |
| Sabriel | 33 | 16 | (0.55×33)+(0.45×16) = 25.4 | **18.6** |

**Total expected VS damage to party: ~89 HP across three PCs.**

### Marwen knockout probability

Marwen (32 HP) drops to 0 if she fails the save AND 6d10 ≥ 32. Since mean 6d10 = 33 and the
distribution is symmetric, P(6d10 ≥ 32) ≈ 52%. Combined with P(fail) = 91%:

**P(Marwen to 0 HP from a single VS) ≈ 0.91 × 0.52 ≈ 47%.**

On a save she takes ~16 half damage, leaving 16 HP — not dead, but half her pool. Marwen has no
HP safety net beyond Marwen's own spell slots (no party Healer unless Sabriel fills the role).

### Frightened cascade analysis

On a failed save, Marwen (91% fail probability) and Bazgar (94% fail probability) are likely
frightened. Frightened imposes:
- Disadvantage on attack rolls while the beholder is in line of sight
- Cannot willingly move closer to the beholder

The beholder hovers near the shrine altar. Frightened PCs cannot approach the altar area. A
frightened Sabriel (who might be the party's divine martial / healer) cannot close to heal,
cannot use any ability that requires being within range of the beholder (e.g. Lay on Hands), and
cannot approach downed allies near the shrine without moving toward the beholder.

For a 3-PC table with no class that can break Frightened at level 5 (Calm Emotions requires
Cleric 3+ and is generally available, but Sabriel might not have it; Paladin Aura of Courage
requires Paladin 10+), frightened PCs are effectively forced to fight at disadvantage or flee.

Frightened + 3-LA beholder is brutal: on turns following VS, the beholder spends:
- Tentacle LA (1) on Marwen (likely already down) or Bazgar
- Void Ray LA (2) on Sabriel (DC 16 DEX, avg 22 force)
These LAs hit disadvantaged fighters at full force with no PC counterplay.

### LA economy on VS turn

VS is the beholder's own-turn action. On the VS turn:
- Beholder action: Void Scream (6d10 psychic AoE, +frightened)
- Beholder bonus action: Shrine-Drift (free repositioning through stone) OR Compel Thrall
- Beholder has 3 LA available for the **next** creature's-turn firings

There is no competition between VS and LA actions — VS does not consume LAs. However, VS
consumes the beholder's own action, meaning no Disintegration Ray this round. The beholder
should prefer VS over DR when ≥2 PCs are clustered; VS deals ~64–89 EV to 3 targets vs DR's
45 to one. VS is clearly dominant vs clustered parties. See FEEL-019.

### Beholder action hierarchy (updated for this slice)

| Priority | Condition | Action |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | ≥2 PCs within 30 ft AND VS recharged (16.7%/round) | **Void Scream** |
| 2 | Single PC within 120 ft AND DR available | **Disintegration Ray** |
| 3 | Default | Multiattack |

Drain Divinity is **always** between-turn LA spending (3 LA) when Sabriel is within 30 ft.
It cannot occur on the beholder's own action. This hierarchy is consistent with all prior slices.

### New findings

**FEEL-015 (updated from R286 carry-forward): Void Scream near-kills Marwen with 47%
probability on single cast.** The beholder should expect to drop the wizard within the first VS
fire. If recharge triggers in round 2 (16.7% chance), Marwen may not have recovered. The party
effectively has no safe "tank the VS" option — the only counter is spreading out (preventing
clustering), which the tunnel encounter geometry does not readily support. DM note: VS fires
more decisively than the "meant to lose" framing implies; it may end Marwen before meaningful
resistance. Consider signaling it via sound/wind before it fires so PCs can react (doesn't
prevent it but gives a fear moment rather than a gotcha).

**FEEL-019: VS vs DR action-competition does not exist — they compete for turns only.**
Both are the beholder's action. When VS is recharged, VS is always superior vs ≥2 PCs. The
cheat sheet should clarify that VS fires immediately when recharged AND multiple PCs are
clustered — do not save VS "for a better moment." Waiting typically loses 1d10 avg damage per
PC not hit. **Log only.**

**FEEL-020: Shrine-disadvantage mechanic creates deterministic targeting.**
Bazgar and Marwen near the shrine fail VS with near-certainty (94%, 91%). The shrine-proximity
penalty functionally removes the save for anyone near the altar. Combined with FEEL-010 (DR
near-certainty kills Marwen) and this VS analysis, the encounter has two "Marwen dies"
mechanisms with high base rates. DM should track which mechanism fires first; the second
becomes irrelevant. **Log only.**
