## FEEL-VERB-AMBIGUITY-R277-01 — LOG ONLY

**File:** `combat-runner/actions.jsonl` (beholder-thrulm rows)

The verb `"tentacle"` appears in BOTH the `multiattack` verb list
(`["attack", "multiattack", "hit", "tentacle", "maw", "grab"]`) AND is a substring match
for `tentacle_legendary` (`["legendary tentacle", "lash", "reach"]`).

DM typing `tentacle` at the table routes to **multiattack** (full 3-attack sequence), not
the legendary single-tentacle action. This is confusing at the table — a DM wanting to spend
1 LA for a single tentacle strike types "tentacle" and gets the full action.

**Recommendation:** Remove `"tentacle"` from multiattack verbs; add `"full attack"` or
`"three attacks"` instead. Keep `"lash"` as the unique legendary identifier. Requires
`combat_action_upsert` for the multiattack row — do not auto-fix.

## Phase B — Scenario: thrulm-beholder-tentacle-grapple

**Scenario ID:** thrulm-beholder-tentacle-grapple (run-counter 37 → index 17)

Party (Compass Edge, level 5): Bazgar HP 49 AC 18, Marwen HP 32 AC 15, Sabriel HP 44 AC 19.
Beholder HP 110 AC 17. Void-Feeding active (+1 to all beholder attacks/damage in chamber).

### Initiative (representative roll)
- Beholder: d20+3 → avg 13 → init 13
- Bazgar: d20+1 → avg 11 → init 11
- Sabriel: d20+1 → avg 11 → init 11
- Marwen: d20+2 → avg 12 → init 12

Representative order: Beholder (13) → Marwen (12) → Bazgar (11) → Sabriel (11)

### Round 1

**Beholder turn (init 13)**  
Multiattack: 2× Tentacle Lash + Maw. Targets: Marwen nearest.

*Tentacle Lash #1 vs Marwen (AC 15):* +7 to hit (base +6, Void-Feeding +1). Avg 17 vs 15 → hit. Damage: 3d6+3+1 = avg 14.5 → 14 bludgeoning. Marwen HP: 32 → 18. Marwen **grappled** (escape DC 16).

*Tentacle Lash #2 vs Bazgar (AC 18):* +7 to hit. Avg 17 vs 18 → miss on typical roll. Let's give beholder a 12: 19 vs 18 → hit. Damage: 14 bludgeoning. Bazgar HP: 49 → 35. Bazgar **grappled** (escape DC 16).

*Maw vs Marwen (grappled):* +7 to hit. Avg 17 vs 15 → hit. Damage: 4d8+3+1 = avg 22. Marwen HP: 18 → **−4 → 0 HP, unconscious.** On avg rolls Marwen is down before her first turn.

**FEEL FLAG:** Marwen (32 HP, AC 15) falls in the beholder's opening action before she acts. Average multiattack (tentacle + maw = ~36 damage) exceeds her max HP. This happens ~55% of the time the beholder goes first. The encounter is designed as a loss, but a PC going unconscious without any turn is table-feel-negative.

**Marwen turn (init 12)** — skipped (unconscious). Marwen must roll death saves.

**Bazgar turn (init 11)**  
Bazgar is grappled but not restrained; can still attack. Battlemaster has access to trip/disarm/precision attack. Uses Action Surge (Fighter 5) for 2 attacks + Action Surge 2 more attacks? No: at level 5 Fighters get Extra Attack (2 attacks), not Action Surge (1 use). Let's say standard 2 attacks on the beholder.

*Beholder declares Antireality (reaction) on first attack:* Bazgar's attack roll is at disadvantage. Attack 1 rolls (d20, d20) lower = (9, 14) → 14. +6 to hit (STR +2 + PB +4 = +6 at level 5). 14+6 = 20 vs AC 17 → hit despite disadvantage. Damage: 1d10+2 (longsword/battleaxe) = avg 7. Beholder HP: 110 → 103.

*Attack 2 (no Antireality this round — reaction used):* 10+6 = 16 vs AC 17 → miss typical. Or 12+6 = 18 vs 17 → hit. Let's say hit. 7 damage. Beholder HP: 103 → 96.

Bazgar attempts to escape grapple (bonus action or action?). Grapple escape is a contested Athletics/Acrobatics check on its own action — Bazgar would spend an action. STR +4 vs beholder STR -1 + PB +3 = +2. Bazgar rolls 10+4=14 vs beholder 10+2=12 → escapes.

**Beholder Legendary Actions after Bazgar:**
With Sabriel (divine martial) up next: **Drain Divinity (3 LA)**. Sabriel within 30 ft? Beholder is hovering; if Sabriel closed to melee, she's within 30 ft. Sabriel makes DC 16 CHA save. Divine martial CHA +3: 10+3=13 vs DC 16 → fails. Sabriel loses her highest spell slot (3rd level = 3). Beholder gains 6 THP.

This confirms: Drain Divinity is the dominant LA choice every round vs Sabriel. The beholder never uses Void Ray (2 LA) or the 1-LA Tentacle when Sabriel is present with spell slots.

**Sabriel turn (init 11)**  
Sabriel is a divine martial (likely Paladin). Has lost a 3rd-level slot. Uses Divine Smite + weapon attack? No 3rd-level slots now. Uses 2nd-level smite: attack + 3d8 radiant (2d8 base + 1d8 level bonus). Hits beholder with advantage if both tentacles busy on Bazgar and Marwen. Without advantage: 12+5 = 17 vs AC 17 → hit. Damage: 1d8+4+2d8 smite = avg 4+2+9 = 15. With sanctified weapon: +1d8 = avg 15+4.5 = 19-20. Beholder HP: 96 → ~76.

**Lair Action (init count 20):**  
Before beholder's turn: Void Eruption (20 ft of shrine). If Bazgar and Sabriel are near the shrine/altar, DC 16 DEX save, 11 (2d10) force damage. Both likely near altar. Bazgar: 10+1 = 11 vs DC 16 → fail. 11 damage. Bazgar HP: 35 → 24. Sabriel: 10+1 = 11 vs DC 16 → fail. 11 damage. Sabriel HP: 44 → 33.

### Round 2

**Beholder turn:**
Disintegration Ray recharge? Roll d6: 1-4 = no. Let's say not available.
Multiattack vs Bazgar (grapple escaped) and Sabriel.

Tentacle Lash vs Bazgar (+7 vs AC 18): needs 11 → 50% hit. 14 damage. Bazgar HP: 24 → 10.  
Tentacle Lash vs Sabriel (+7 vs AC 19): needs 12 → 45% hit. 14 damage. Sabriel HP: 33 → 19.  
Maw vs whoever's grappled.

**Beholder Legendary Actions after Bazgar (Round 2):**  
Sabriel still has 2nd-level slots. Drain Divinity again (3 LA). Sabriel CHA save: 10+3=13 vs DC 16 → fails. Loses 2nd-level slot (2). Beholder gains 4 THP. Drain Divinity is still optimal.

### Analysis

**Round 1 threat assessment:**  
- Marwen down before her first action (avg-roll scenario) — encounter clock is already up for one PC.  
- Beholder HP 96 after Bazgar's two hits; full HP was 110. Party dealt ~14 damage Round 1.
- Beholder effectively heals 6 THP from Drain Divinity every LA cycle.

**Can the party kill the beholder before a TPK?**  
Beholder at ~76 HP after round 1 all party actions. Party can deal ~20-30 damage/round with Sabriel's smites + Bazgar. Beholder has ~76 HP left = 3+ rounds of sustained damage IF the party stays up. But with Marwen unconscious Round 1 and Bazgar at 24 HP after lair action, Round 2 likely drops Bazgar. Sabriel alone faces the beholder in Round 3. This is within the "meant to lose" design intent.

**Drain Divinity dominance confirmed.** Sabriel has ~2-3 spell slots at level 5 (she's already lost 3rd-level slot). Once she's out of slots, Drain Divinity still costs 3 LA to drain "one use of a divine ability" (e.g. Channel Divinity, Lay on Hands) — but this becomes much weaker. The degenerate strategy plateaus once slots are gone.

**One-note legendary economy:** Drain Divinity (3 LA) is used every single round while Sabriel has slots. Void Ray (2 LA) costs less but deals only ~22 force — less value than removing a 3rd-level slot AND getting 6 THP. This won't change until Sabriel is out of slots. Flagged as known open (fire #6).

**Antireality timing confirmed:** The reaction (impose disadvantage before the attack roll) would correctly fire on Bazgar's first attack. Bazgar still hit through disadvantage in the example — Antireality is good but not auto-block.

**Grapple → Maw combo:** Strong but fair. The grapple rider on Maw (disadvantage on escape checks) is the correct fix from R220. However: the Maw deals more damage to a grappled target than a free target only via the disadvantage on escape checks, not via any attack bonus. DM might expect Maw to get advantage vs grappled targets (common houserule); the stat block does not. This is a design call, not a bug.

### FEEL-R277-02: Marwen one-shot is likely (not just possible)

**Flag:** On avg initiative roll, beholder wins init ~60% of the time vs the party. When it
does, multiattack (2 tentacle lashes + maw) averages 14+14+22 = 50 damage across two targets —
but if both tentacles go to Marwen, she takes 14+22 = 36 before the Maw is even needed.
Actual realistic sequence: 1 tentacle to Marwen (14), maw to Marwen (22) = 36 vs 32 HP → 0.
Marwen doesn't get to act.

**Joe must decide:** Is a level-5 wizard dying before her first turn acceptable for this encounter,
given it's designed as a loss? If not, consider: (a) beholder targets Sabriel or Bazgar first
(tactics change), (b) Marwen gets a Blur/Blink buff before the encounter, or (c) beholder's
first action is Drain Divinity (3 LA on initiative 20 lair prep) rather than multiattack.

Option (c) is particularly interesting: if the beholder always opens with Drain Divinity (spending
all 3 LA to drain Sabriel before anyone acts), it sacrifices first-round multiattack for economy.
This is thematically correct (it hates divine casters) and gives Marwen her first turn.
