### Phase B — Simulation Observations

**1. AR fires twice without crash.** "resonance" verb resolves to `ancient_resonance` ✓. Both STDs fire AR in the same round when both below 20 HP. Scenario's `no_crash` assertion is satisfied. ✓

**2. Stagger-exempt covers single low-HP STD case correctly.** When only ONE STD is below 20 HP, it fires AR recklessly while the healthy partner holds. This is the intended use case.

**3. FEEL-R89-01 (new) — simultaneous low-HP stagger elimination creates paradox:** when Marwen's Fireball hits BOTH STDs equally, both land below 20 HP simultaneously, and the stagger exemption applies to BOTH. This means a Fireball that barely doesn't kill the STDs (bringing both from 45 to 17 HP) is *worse* than a Fireball that kills one and leaves one standing — because the kill eliminates one AR, while the "half-damage" result triggers 2× AR via double stagger-exempt. The stagger rule was authored for the single-low-HP case; the double-simultaneous case is an unaddressed edge condition. LOG ONLY.

**4. FEEL-R89-02 (new) — priority conflict: reckless AR vs Unstable Form:** The "below 20 HP fires AR recklessly" note doesn't address the case where Unstable Form is simultaneously active. UF tactics say: "PREFER Multiattack over AR when only 2 enemies are in cone." At below 20 HP, does reckless AR override UF preference? Below-20-HP AR-reckless and UF-Multiattack-preference give opposite signals when 2 enemies are in cone. LOG ONLY.
