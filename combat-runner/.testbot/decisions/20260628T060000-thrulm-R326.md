### Round 4 (Beholder at 38 HP — above retreat threshold)

**Init count 20 — Lair Action:**
Beholder is above retreat threshold; it's still pressing the fight. Thrall is within 20 ft of shrine (12 HP). Void Eruption would hit: Bazgar (15 ft from shrine) and Thrall (10 ft from shrine). Sabriel is 25 ft from shrine (outside range). Not ideal for Void Eruption (only hits 1 PC + own thrall). Beholder uses **Unstable Ground** on Bazgar (DC 16 DEX).
- Bazgar DEX modifier: +0. Needs 16+ on d20 to succeed. P(fail) = 75%.
- **Outcome (expected): Bazgar is prone.**

**Beholder's turn (at 38 HP):**
- Start-of-turn checklist: Void Scream recharge? d6 → fails (not 6). Disintegration Ray? d6 → fails (not 5-6). Antireality available.
- Bazgar is prone (Unstable Ground just applied) and is the nearest PC. The beholder descends to attack range (within 10 ft of Bazgar).
- **Multiattack:** 2 Tentacle Lash + 1 Maw.

| Attack | Base bonus | Void-Feeding | vs AC | Notes |
|--------|-----------|--------------|-------|-------|
| Tentacle Lash 1 | +8 | +1 = +9 | Bazgar AC 18 (but prone — no distance penalty; beholder is adjacent) | Need 9+ on d20 → 60% hit. Hit: 13+1=14 bludgeoning. **Grappled (escape DC 16).** |
| Tentacle Lash 2 | +9 | — | Bazgar AC 18 | 60% hit. Hit: 14 bludgeoning. Second grapple. |
| Maw | +9 | — | Bazgar AC 18 (grappled → disadvantage on ESCAPE check, not on the attack) | 60% hit. Hit: 21+1=22 piercing. |

> Prone gives melee attackers within 5 ft **advantage** on attack rolls. The beholder is a melee attacker.
> Tentacle Lash at advantage vs Bazgar: P(hit) = 1 − (9/20)² = 1 − 0.2025 = **79.75%**.

Corrected with advantage (Bazgar is prone, beholder is adjacent):
- TL1: 79.75% × 14 = **11.2 expected damage**
- TL2: 79.75% × 14 = **11.2 expected damage**
- Maw: 79.75% × 22 = **17.5 expected damage** (grappled Bazgar, Maw gains no additional advantage)

**Total expected: 39.9 damage to Bazgar this turn.** Bazgar at 28 HP → -11.9 HP → **0 HP (unconscious).**

Bazgar goes down. He has 49 max HP; excess damage is ~12, well below 49, so no instant death.

*Bazgar makes death save on his turn.*

**Beholder after Multiattack:** Uses Shrine-Drift (bonus action) to move 30 ft back toward the shrine altar, passing through the collapsed shrine column. Now hovering 40 ft above the altar. Out of melee reach of remaining party.

### End of Beholder's turn → Legendary Actions

Bazgar is unconscious. End of beholder's turn: 3 Legendary Actions available.

**Legendary Action choice after beholder's own turn (before Bazgar's death save):**

The beholder has 3 LA. Options:
- Move (1 LA): Already used Shrine-Drift to reposition; could gain another 30 ft for total safety.
- Tentacle (1 LA): Additional Tentacle Lash on Bazgar (unconscious → auto-crit, 2× damage dice = 2 × 3d6 + 3 = avg 24 damage; BUT this would apply to an unconscious creature, dealing death save failure ×2).
- Void Ray (2 LA): Any target within 120 ft, DC 16 DEX save.
- Drain Divinity (3 LA): One target within 30 ft, DC 16 CHA save.

**FEEL-R326-01 — Auto-crit on Tentacle LA vs unconscious Bazgar:**
If the beholder uses Tentacle legendary action on an unconscious Bazgar, the attack auto-crits (unconscious creature = attackers within 5 ft have advantage; melee hits auto-crit). This deals **2 × 3d6 + 3 = avg 24 bludgeoning damage**. Bazgar at 0 HP takes 2 failed death saves. On a second Tentacle LA (1 more LA), that's a total of 4 failed death saves = instant death.

With 3 legendary actions, the beholder could:
1. Tentacle LA on Bazgar (1 LA) → 2 death save failures
2. Tentacle LA on Bazgar again (1 LA) → 2 more death save failures → **Bazgar dies instantly**
3. Move (1 LA) to reposition further

This is mechanically legal but creates a **no-counterplay kill**: Bazgar went unconscious, and before any other creature's turn (LA fire between Beholder's turn and Bazgar's death save turn), the beholder spends 2 LA to finish him. Bazgar never gets to attempt a death save. He goes from "1d20 away from stabilizing" to dead before his own turn.

> Note: R324 previously flagged FEEL-OPEN-04 about disintegration ray permadeath. This is a new variant — using Tentacle legendary action to convert an unconscious creature's 0 HP to instant death in the window between the beholder's turn and the downed PC's death save, using only 2 LA (not even needing the 3-LA Drain Divinity or the Disintegration recharge).

**Expected optimal beholder play at this moment:**
- Tentacle LA (1 LA) on unconscious Bazgar → auto-crit → 2 death-save failures
- Tentacle LA (1 LA) again → auto-crit → 2 more failures → Bazgar dies
- Move (1 LA) to further 30 ft

This uses ALL 3 LA and kills a PC instantly, all within the LA window before Bazgar's first death save.

### **THE RETREAT THRESHOLD: What happens now**

The beholder crosses below 30 HP during Marwen's Fireball. Retreat behavior per stat block:
> "If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit. If below 20 HP, it attempts to crush/disintegrate the party while fleeing."

The beholder is at ~19-24 HP. This is below 30 but may or may not be below 20.

**End of Marwen's turn → Beholder Legendary Actions:**

3 LA available. Sabriel is 30 ft from altar (not within 30 ft of beholder hovering at 40 ft altitude — so Drain Divinity is out of range unless beholder descends). Marwen is 25 ft from shrine.

The beholder is now in retreat. It uses:
- **Drain Divinity (3 LA):** Target Sabriel (if beholder descends 10 ft to bring Sabriel within 30 ft). DC 16 CHA save vs Sabriel (+3 CHA + PB +3 prof = +6). Needs 10+ to succeed, 55% success. On fail (45%): Sabriel loses her highest remaining spell slot (let's say 4th-level). Beholder gains 2 × 4 = 8 THP.

> **FEEL-R326-02 — Drain Divinity on the retreat trigger turn:**
> The beholder descends slightly to bring Sabriel within 30 ft, uses all 3 LA on Drain Divinity, **then retreats on its own turn.** It drained Sabriel's healing resources while simultaneously beginning its escape. Sabriel's 4th-level slot was potentially her "stabilize Bazgar + continue fighting" resource. The party has no opportunity to stop this — the LA fires *between* Marwen's turn and the Thrall's turn (next in order), before the party can react.

### Round 4 end — Pursuit analysis

The beholder crossed ~90 ft in one turn (30 fly + 30 Shrine-Drift + 30 Legendary Move). It is now behind solid rock with no opportunity to be followed in one round.

Party movement in one round:
- Bazgar: unconscious, not moving
- Sabriel: 30 ft movement; can't reach the wall the beholder passed through in 1 round if it was 40 ft away
- Marwen: 30 ft movement; same constraint

The beholder outpaces the entire party by ~60 ft this turn and is now behind solid obstacles. **Escape is certain once the retreat is triggered.**

The party had exactly ONE action window (the Marwen Fireball that triggered the retreat) to finish the beholder before it vanished. With ~20 HP remaining and the party having used most resources, a killing blow in that same Fireball was unlikely (Fireball already did 18 damage; 20 remaining HP; party would need another 20 damage from Sabriel or remaining resources in the same turn, which Sabriel can't contribute at altitude).

### NEW FINDINGS — FEEL

**FEEL-R326-01 — Legendary Tentacle auto-crit kills downed PCs in pre-death-save window**

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Mechanic | After dropping a PC to 0 HP with Multiattack, the beholder's 3 LA (available at the end of its own turn) can be spent: Tentacle LA × 2 = 2 death-save auto-crit failures each = 4 death-save failures = instant PC death, all before the downed PC's first death save. 2 LA cost. |
| Why unfair vs. lethal | The PC never gets to roll a death save. The beholder can guarantee a kill on any 0 HP PC using just 2 of its 3 LA per round, with a +9 to-hit auto-crit against an unconscious target. No save, no counterplay. |
| Specifically dangerous | If the party doesn't protect the downed PC (Shield of Faith, War Caster OA, getting between the beholder and the body), any downed PC is dead within the same round they went down. For a 3-PC party at this HP level (Bazgar 0, Marwen 16, Sabriel 22), losing Bazgar this way leaves two very low-HP PCs vs a beholder still with meaningful HP. |
| Prior flag | FEEL-OPEN-04 flagged Disintegration Ray permadeath (~15%/use). This is a MORE reliable permadeath vector: guaranteed if beholder spends 2 LA. No recharge needed. |
| Recommendation | Log for Joe: is 2-LA instant kill on downed PCs intended? If yes, the encounter design should acknowledge this explicitly (one-way door once any PC is dropped). If no, consider ruling that unconscious creatures within 5 ft only grant advantage (not auto-crit) for the legendary tentacle (treating LA as non-melee-range-qualifying for auto-crit purposes), or add a grace-round rule. |
| Status | Log-only (design call) |

**FEEL-R326-02 — Drain Divinity + Retreat combo: double punishment with no counterplay**

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Mechanic | At the retreat threshold, the beholder can: (a) Descend slightly to bring Sabriel within 30 ft. (b) Spend all 3 LA on Drain Divinity (in the window between Marwen's turn and the Thrall's turn). (c) Retreat on its own turn using fly + Shrine-Drift. |
| Impact | Sabriel loses her highest spell slot (the resource she'd use to stabilize Bazgar or continue the fight) while the beholder simultaneously escapes. The party takes a resource drain AND loses the target before they can respond. |
| Why different from FEEL-OPEN-01 | FEEL-OPEN-01 flags Drain Divinity as economically irrational in normal combat (costs 3 LA for ~4 THP vs 22 dmg from Void Ray). At the retreat threshold, the beholder has NO use for the 3 LA after this round (it's fleeing). The THP don't matter; Drain Divinity becomes the *only rational choice* at retreat because it costs the party something while the beholder gains irrelevant THP. The retreat context inverts the economic analysis. |
| Recommendation | Log for Joe: the retreat-threshold is the one scenario where Drain Divinity becomes optimal (party can't benefit from 3 LA spent on damage if the beholder is fleeing anyway). This creates a guaranteed worst-of-all-worlds for the party: dropping the beholder below 30 HP simultaneously triggers its escape AND causes Drain Divinity on the most resource-heavy PC. |
| Status | Log-only (design call) |

**FEEL-R326-03 — Lore contradiction: beholder retreat to "deeper shaft" vs "cannot go deeper willingly"**

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Location 1 | Tactics section: "If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats **deeper into the lower shaft**" |
| Location 2 | Weaknesses section: "**The deeper shaft:** It cannot go deeper into the lower chamber willingly; something down there scares even this creature" |
| Contradiction | The retreat behavior sends the beholder exactly where it is stated to never go willingly. |
| Impact | If the DM plays both as written, the beholder is simultaneously fleeing to and incapable of reaching the lower shaft. At the retreat trigger, the DM must choose to either (a) ignore the tactical retreat note or (b) ignore the weakness. Either choice undermines one design intent. |
| Possible resolution | Retreat should be toward a **defensible position in the main chamber** (behind the altar, up a pillar) or toward a side passage, not the lower shaft. The lower shaft can remain as a secondary location for the <20 HP "crushing while fleeing" phase, with the contradiction resolved by noting fear > retreat-survival instinct only at very low HP. |
| Status | Log-only (design call for Joe) |

**FEEL-R326-04 — Escape velocity: party cannot catch a retreating beholder**

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Beholder movement | Fly 30 ft (action or Move) + Shrine-Drift BA 30 ft (through walls) + Legendary Move 30 ft = up to 90 ft per turn. Passes through solid rock. |
| Party movement | Bazgar 30 ft (unconscious), Marwen 30 ft, Sabriel 30 ft. No fly speed. Cannot pass through walls. |
| Gap | Beholder gains 60 ft per turn on the fastest party member. Even with Misty Step (Sabriel/Marwen), closing the gap requires a spell slot and still only teleports 30 ft. |
| Consequence | Once the retreat triggers, the encounter is functionally over. The party cannot pursue, cannot finish, and faces a beholder that may return next round or next encounter at full-ish HP. The 30-HP retreat threshold creates a hard "timer" the party must beat — but the timer is unannounced and the escape is instantaneous. |
| Note | This is consistent with the encounter's "meant to lose" design. The beholder escaping is narratively appropriate. The concern is whether the window (from 100 HP to 30 HP) feels like a genuine chase or just "one minute you're fighting, the next it's gone." Given party burst potential (~50 DPR with all resources), the beholder's HP buffer is adequate. |
| Status | Design note for Joe — not a bug, but the "chase" element is currently zero (no mechanics for pursuit). If narrative pursuit matters, add a mechanic. |
