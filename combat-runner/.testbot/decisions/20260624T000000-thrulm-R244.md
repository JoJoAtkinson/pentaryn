## Phase B — drain-divinity-vs-sabriel

**Scenario:** Beholder spends all 3 legendary actions on Drain Divinity targeting Sabriel each round for 3 consecutive rounds. Sabriel CHA +2, DC 16 → fail on 1–13 (65%), save on 14–20 (35%). L5 slots assumed: 4×1st, 3×2nd, 2×3rd.

### Simulation (all-fail scenario, 65% per save):

| Round | Sabriel slot lost | Sabriel slots remaining | Beholder THP gained |
|-------|------------------|-------------------------|---------------------|
| R1 | 3rd-level | 1×3rd, 3×2nd, 4×1st | 6 THP |
| R2 | 3rd-level | 0×3rd, 3×2nd, 4×1st | 6 THP (prior absorbed by attacks) |
| R3 | 2nd-level | 0×3rd, 2×2nd, 4×1st | 4 THP (prior absorbed by attacks) |

Expected THP over 3 rounds (accounting for per-round attrition from ~28+ avg party damage): 6+6+4 = **16 THP total** — achievable because THP are consumed by incoming damage between rounds. The rotation slice's "at most 16 temp HP (6+6+4)" is numerically correct in a live combat context.

### BUG-R244-01 — LOW — rotation.yml THP wording imprecise

**File:** `combat-runner/.testbot/rotation.yml`, slice `drain-divinity-vs-sabriel`  
**Text:** "beholder gains at most 16 temp HP (6+6+4)"  
**Issue:** The "(6+6+4)" notation implies THP stack additively, which they do not under 5e PHB p.198 — when a creature gains THP it already has, it keeps the higher value. The 16 THP total is only achievable if each pool is fully consumed by damage before the next Drain Divinity fires. In practice against this party (~28–42 avg damage/round), the 6 THP pool is very likely spent before round 2, so the calculation is functionally correct. The wording misleads future analysis; a reader running the slice analytically (zero attrition between uses) would compute max(6,6,4)=6 THP.  
**Correction note (log only, do not edit rotation.yml):** Add clarification that the 16 THP assumes THP are consumed between uses; without attrition the correct value is max(6,6,4)=6.

### FEEL-R244-01 — LOW — strategic cost of Drain Divinity (all 3 LA each round)

Using all 3 LA on Drain Divinity sacrifices expected LA damage output:
- Tentacle Lash vs Marwen (AC 15, +7 hit in chamber): 65% hit × 14.5 avg = **~9.4 expected damage** + grapple rider
- Void Ray vs Bazgar (AC 18, DC 16 DEX save, DEX +1 → 65% fail): 0.65×23 + 0.35×11.5 = **~19 expected damage**
- Total LA damage foregone: **~28 avg per round**

Exchange rate for Drain Divinity (all 3 LA): forgo 28 avg damage for 6 THP + one spell slot removed from Sabriel.

**Assessment:** Mechanically a poor trade unless the goal is specifically denying Sabriel's 3rd-level slots (Divine Smite burst, healing). Against this 3-PC party (already near TPK), Drain Divinity rounds are most valuable when:
1. Sabriel has her 3rd-level slots and is close to the beholder (within 30 ft — see FEEL-R244-02)
2. The beholder is above 60 HP (THP matter less at high HP; damage pressure matters more)
3. The DM specifically wants to punish divine resources as a thematic beat

Using Drain Divinity in rounds 1–3 pure is almost always weaker than Tentacle+VoidRay. Recommend adding a DM guidance note: reserve Drain Divinity for the moment it hurts most (e.g., after Sabriel uses a smite that turns the fight), not as an opener. This is a feel note, not a stat change. **Log only.**

### FEEL-R244-02 — INFO — Drain Divinity 30-ft range vs beholder's default hover height

**Issue:** Drain Divinity (legendary action) has 30 ft range. The beholder's default tactical position is 40–60 ft above the chamber floor (COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md line 23). Sabriel standing at ground level directly below a beholder hovering at 40 ft = 40 ft distance > 30 ft range. The beholder cannot fire Drain Divinity from its standard position.

**To use Drain Divinity:** The beholder must first move within 30 ft of Sabriel (e.g., use Shrine-Drift bonus action or Move LA to descend), then fire Drain Divinity as LA at end of a subsequent creature's turn. This exposes it to melee opportunity attacks from Bazgar.

**Impact on Phase B scenario:** The 3-round Drain Divinity scenario implicitly assumes the beholder has already descended. This positioning cost is real and should be noted at the table. Drain Divinity is NOT a free resource-drain from safety — the beholder pays with exposure.

**Cheat-sheet note:** Line 32 says "30 ft range" but the tactical implications are not spelled out. Adding "(must be within 30 ft of target — descend from hover first)" would help DMs avoid missing the constraint. **Log only** — CS edit deferred to DM review.
