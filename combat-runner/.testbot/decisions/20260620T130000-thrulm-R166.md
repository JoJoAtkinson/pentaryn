### Scenario: `retreat-threshold` — Retreat at 30 HP, tactical viability

**Premise:** Beholder drops to 28 HP. Per tactics section, it should retreat to the lower shaft, using thralls to block. Does it have a mechanically viable escape path? Does the retreat feel dramatic or anticlimactic?

---

### State entering the retreat decision

Representing a fight that has reached the 30 HP threshold. By this point the party has dealt 82 damage to the beholder (110 → 28). At level 5 with 3 PCs, that takes approximately 3-4 rounds. Likely party state at round 4:
- Bazgar: ~33 HP (took some tentacle hits, healed one minor recovery)
- Marwen: 0 HP (standard FEEL-R161-01 outcome — out by round 1-2 via Disintegration Ray)
- Sabriel: ~25 HP (took Void Rays, lost 2 spell slots to Drain Divinity)

Remaining thralls/derro: likely 0-1 (party has been eliminating minions while beholder whittles PCs). The "use thralls to block pursuit" retreat tactic may already be moot.

---

### Movement math — retreat path analysis

**Available movement per round (retreating beholder):**

| Source | Distance | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Action: Fly 30 ft | 30 ft | Standard fly speed |
| Bonus action: Shrine-Drift | 30 ft | Passes through creatures/objects; 5 (1d10) force dmg if ending turn INSIDE an object |
| LA: Move × 1 | 30 ft | 1 LA; can be used 3 times = 90 ft if all 3 LA spent on Move |
| **Total (all Move LAs)** | **150 ft/round** | Requires spending all 3 LA on Move; zero offensive LA |
| **Total (no Move LAs)** | **60 ft/round** | Shrine-Drift + Fly; preserves 3 LA for Tentacle/VoidRay |

**Path to lower shaft:** Per encounter overview: the lower shaft entrance is "60 feet beyond the main chamber." Beholder starts hover position 40-60 ft above the altar. The altar is roughly centered in the chamber. Distance from hover position to lower shaft entrance: 40-60 ft vertical descent + 30-60 ft horizontal traversal = 70-120 ft total.

**Retreat timeline:**

| Round | Movement | Cumulative progress | Remaining HP (taking OAs) |
|-------|----------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| Retreat round 1, no Move LAs | 60 ft | 60 ft toward shaft | 28 - OAs |
| Retreat round 2, no Move LAs | 60 ft | 120 ft — reaches shaft entrance | 28 - 2×OA cycles |
| Retreat round 1, all Move LAs | 150 ft | 150 ft — clears chamber entirely | 28 - OAs (all LA used for movement, no Tentacle/VoidRay) |

**Conclusion on path viability:** With Shrine-Drift + Fly speed only (no LAs), retreat takes 2 rounds. This creates a 2-round endgame pursuit opportunity for the party. With all 3 LAs spent on Move, retreat is 1 round but the beholder does zero damage.

---

### Opportunity attack exposure during retreat

Shrine-Drift allows the beholder to **pass through** PCs without stopping, but does **not** grant immunity to opportunity attacks. Any PC within 5 ft of the beholder's path can take an OA as the beholder leaves their reach.

At 28 HP, one solid OA from Bazgar ends the encounter immediately:
- Bazgar +5 to hit (est.) vs AC 17: 45% hit base. On hit: 1d12+3 avg 9.5, plus potential Battlemaster superiority die (avg +5.5) = avg ~15 damage.
- P(one OA kills at 28 HP): if hit (45%): need to deal ≥28 remaining HP. With maneuver: 0.45 × P(≥28 in one swing) ≈ unlikely but possible (max greataxe is 12+3+d8+3=21 with maneuver; can't quite 1-shot from 28 HP). But 2 OAs from Bazgar + Sabriel would likely finish it.
- The beholder can use Antireality (reaction) to impose disadvantage on one OA per round — reducing one OA's hit rate from 45% to ~22%.

**Key question:** Does Shrine-Drift allow the beholder to descend through the floor (solid stone) to reach the lower shaft below, avoiding all OAs from PCs above?

The text says "can move through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain." Stone floor = object. **Yes, the beholder can descend through solid stone floors.** If it Shrine-Drifts straight down through the floor into the lower shaft, it takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside the stone, but can emerge in the shaft below. At 28 HP, a successful floor-phase costs avg 5 damage (28 → 23 HP) but avoids all PC OAs.

**This is the optimal retreat path** if the lower shaft exists directly below the chamber floor: Shrine-Drift through the floor (5 force damage, land in shaft below) + Fly 30 ft deeper into shaft. No OA exposure. Total cost: 5 avg force damage. Reach shaft entrance in 1 turn.

---

### Feel analysis: dramatic vs. anticlimactic

**The floor-phase escape is mechanically optimal but dramatically unsatisfying:**
- The party has whittled the beholder from 110 HP to 28 HP over 3-4 hard rounds.
- The beholder uses its bonus action to phase through the floor. It's gone.
- Players have no reaction or counterplay window — no pursuit, no death blow, no narrative moment.
- The beholder reappears in the lower shaft "too scared to go there but went anyway because it was even more scared of the party."

**Compounding contradiction (STAT-R166-01):** If the beholder cannot go into the lower shaft willingly (per Weaknesses), the floor-phase escape is doubly invalid — the beholder won't go there, and even if it does, the DM has no ruling for what it does instead.

**A retreat that uses OA exposure and thrall-blocking is more dramatic:**
- Beholder retreats through the chamber (triggering 1-2 OAs from Bazgar and Sabriel).
- 1-2 surviving thralls step into the path as obstacles (Compel Thrall redirects them as a wall).
- Beholder reaches the far tunnel leading out of Thrulm (not the lower shaft — per the contradiction fix).
- If the party can deal 28+ HP before the beholder exits the chamber over 2 rounds, they get the kill. Otherwise, the beholder escapes and the lore consequence plays out.

This 2-round pursuit window gives the party meaningful agency without trivializing the threat. Much better feel.

---

### Shrine-Drift as escape tool: verdict

Shrine-Drift works as an escape tool, but too well. The "ends turn inside object" clause only fires if it STOPS inside stone, not if it passes through. A retreating beholder can use Shrine-Drift to phase through all solid obstacles with only a single 5-force-damage hazard per bonus action if it mismanages distance. At 28 HP, this is survivable.

**Potential fix:** Add "while retreating, the beholder does not phase through the chamber floor — something below pulls back at it." This is flavor-consistent with the lower-shaft fear and prevents the instant-disappear escape. Forces the beholder to retreat horizontally through the chamber (triggering OAs, creating dramatic tension, rewarding party for having chased it down).

---

### Summary table: retreat feel metrics

| Scenario | OA exposure | Turns to escape | Party agency | Feel rating |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|--------------|-------------|
| Floor-phase through stone | 0 | 1 | None | Very anticlimactic |
| No Move LAs, through chamber | 1-2 rounds | 2 | 2 rounds of OAs, pursuit | Good |
| All Move LAs (150 ft/round) | ≤1 round | 1 | 1 OA window | Marginal |
| Thralls block + chamber retreat | 1-2 rounds | 2-3 | 2-3 rounds, choices | Best |

**Optimal DM ruling for table feel:** Beholder retreats horizontally through the chamber (Shrine-Drift + Fly), not through the floor. Uses Compel Thrall (once per turn BA — but BA is already used by Shrine-Drift; cannot do both same turn!). This means beholder must choose: Shrine-Drift escape speed OR Compel Thrall thrall-wall. It cannot do both. Retreat drama is: does it sacrifice movement for a thrall wall, or sprint and hope PCs can't catch it?

**NEW BUG FOUND:** On the retreat turn, the beholder wants to use BOTH Shrine-Drift (BA for movement) AND Compel Thrall (BA for thrall redirection). Both are bonus actions. Only one bonus action per turn (5e RAW). The beholder cannot execute the retreating-while-commanding-thralls tactic as written — it must pick one.

This is a design intent issue. If the retreat tactic requires thrall-wall positioning, the beholder needs either: (a) a free action or legendary action version of Compel Thrall, or (b) the thralls must already be in position before retreat begins. Neither is currently specified.
