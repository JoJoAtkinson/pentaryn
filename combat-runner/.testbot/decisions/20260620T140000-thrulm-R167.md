### Scenario: `antireality-timing` — Antireality reaction disadvantage flip rate and economy

**Setup per rotation description:**
Bazgar (+7 to hit, 2 attacks/round, d12+3 avg 9.5 per hit, potential superiority die +d8=4.5 avg) attacks beholder (AC 17). Needs d20 ≥ 10 to hit. Base hit rate: 11/20 = **55%**.

Beholder uses Antireality (reaction, once per round, refreshes on beholder's own turn) to impose **disadvantage** on the first attack each round.

---

### Math: correct (pre-roll disadvantage) implementation

With disadvantage, hit rate on affected attack:
- P(hit w/ disadv.) = P(both rolls ≥ 10) = (11/20)² = 121/400 = **30.25%**
- Hit rate reduction per affected attack: 55% → 30.25% = **−24.75 percentage points**

Over 3 rounds (6 total attacks from Bazgar, 3 with disadvantage / 3 normal):

| Attack | Antireality? | Hit rate | Expected hits |
|--------|-------------|---------|---------------|
| R1 Atk1 | Yes | 30.25% | 0.30 |
| R1 Atk2 | No | 55% | 0.55 |
| R2 Atk1 | Yes | 30.25% | 0.30 |
| R2 Atk2 | No | 55% | 0.55 |
| R3 Atk1 | Yes | 30.25% | 0.30 |
| R3 Atk2 | No | 55% | 0.55 |
| **Total** | | | **2.56 hits** |

Without any Antireality: 6 × 55% = **3.30 expected hits**.  
Hits saved by Antireality: **0.74** over 3 rounds (~0.25/round).  
Damage mitigated (avg 9.5/hit): **~7 HP over 3 rounds**, **~2.3 HP/round**.

Framed against 110 beholder HP: Antireality buys roughly 1 extra round at current party output if applied optimally. Modest but not negligible in the context of a fight the party is meant to lose.

---

### Math: current (bugged "+2 AC post-hit") implementation

The DB entry says: "gain +2 AC against that attack, declared after seeing the roll. If the retroactive AC meets or beats the roll, the attack misses."

With +7 attacker vs AC 17, the beholder's effective AC becomes 19 when Antireality fires. A smart DM uses Antireality only when the total attack roll is 17 or 18 (d20 = 10 or 11) — the only range where +2 AC flips a hit to a miss:

- P(roll converts from hit to miss via +2 AC) = 2/20 = **10%** of all attacks (hits on rolls 17–18; rolls 19+ still hit)
- Reduction per Antireality use: 55% → 45% effective hit rate
- Hits saved per Antireality use: **0.10 per attack** (vs 0.2475 in the correct version)

The bugged implementation is **~2.5× weaker** than the intended mechanic. Over 3 rounds of Bazgar:
- Hits saved (bugged): 3 × 0.10 = 0.30 hits
- Damage mitigated (bugged, avg 9.5/hit): **~2.9 HP over 3 rounds** (~1 HP/round)

At 1 HP/round damage mitigation, Antireality barely registers against a 110-HP boss. The intended version (2.3 HP/round) is still modest, but at least meaningful across a 6-round fight.

---

### Antireality economy vs. all three PCs

Antireality refreshes at the **start of the beholder's turn** (5e RAW). The beholder has one reaction per round total — it covers one attack from any PC.

Optimal use: save for whoever has the highest expected damage per hit. With three level-5 PCs:
- Bazgar: +7 to hit (55% hit), avg 9.5–14 per hit → expected DPR ~10–13; highest raw output
- Sabriel: divine martial, likely +7 to hit (55% hit), similar damage; divine smite can spike to 4d8+STR on a hit
- Marwen: Wizard, likely +5 to hit (45% hit), lower per-hit damage but persistent concentration spells

**Optimal policy:** Use Antireality on Sabriel when she has smite resources; otherwise on Bazgar for sustained mitigation.

**Antireality wasted case (NEW — FEEL-R167-01):** If Void Scream's FRIGHTENED condition applies to Bazgar or Sabriel, they already roll attacks with disadvantage (5e RAW: disadvantage on attacks while beholder is in line of sight). Per 5e rules, disadvantage doesn't stack — if the PC already has disadvantage, Antireality adds nothing. The runner currently has no condition-tracking logic to warn the DM that a frightened attacker already rolls with disadvantage, so the DM may burn the reaction on a redundant application. Frequency: Void Scream on a 6-recharge fires ~16% of rounds; FRIGHTENED lasts up to 1 minute. In any round where VS landed on an attacker, Antireality on that attacker is wasted.

---

### Antireality DB timing — runner architecture note

The runner triggers `antireality` on the `damage` event (post-hit), per the DB trigger field. This means the combat runner CANNOT implement the correct pre-roll disadvantage mechanic natively — it only fires after the attack resolves. The cheat sheet and stat block correctly describe the intended pre-roll timing, but the DM must manually override the runner prompt timing (as noted in beholder-thrulm.md's DM timing note and cheat sheet's "When Beholder Acts" section).

BUG-F7-02 remains the highest-priority interactive fix (requires `combat_action_upsert` in an interactive MCP session with the effect field corrected to reflect pre-roll disadvantage and trigger changed to `pre-roll` or `declared` event type, if the runner supports that trigger kind).
