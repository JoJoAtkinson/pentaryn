### Feel notes

**FEEL-F253-06 (Thrall combat ineffectiveness):** Expected thrall damage output: 0.7/round
(35% × 2 avg). Against AC 15 Marwen (lowest AC party member). The thrall is mechanically
irrelevant as a damage dealer. Its real function is positional disruption — occupying
Marwen's space, threatening opportunity attacks if she moves. But since the beholder is
already grappling Marwen by round 1 (likely), the thrall's positioning role is redundant.

**FEEL-F253-07 (Compel Thrall bonus action conflict):** The beholder wants to use:
- Shrine-Drift (BA): reposition 30 ft
- Compel Thrall (BA): redirect thrall

These compete for the same bonus action slot. With legendary Move available (1 LA),
Shrine-Drift BA rarely wins the priority check. But Compel Thrall for 0.7 expected damage
redirection is also borderline negative-value (beholder spends a resource for 0.7 DPR).
The beholder's bonus action is almost always better spent on Shrine-Drift (repositioning
affects all attacks) than Compel Thrall (adds 0.7 DPR). Thralls are likely most useful
as bodies blocking retreat paths rather than as commanded attackers.

**FEEL-F253-08 (Compel Thrall vs Compelled Movement overlap):** Two mechanics do the same
thing: beholder's Compel Thrall (BA) costs beholder's bonus action to redirect the thrall;
thrall's own Compelled Movement (BA) is the thrall's bonus action, beholder chooses
direction. In practice, if the thrall hasn't used its bonus action yet, the DM should use
Compelled Movement (free to the beholder) rather than Compel Thrall (costs beholder BA).
This overlap creates confusion at the table without mechanical benefit. Suggest: reserve
Compel Thrall for "emergency redirect mid-turn" only when the thrall's own bonus action
is already spent.

**FEEL-F253-09 (Beholder LA economy in 2-round kill scenario):** If Marwen dies round 1
(beholder multiattack fully hits), the beholder still has 3 LA to spend. After Sabriel
(divine martial), it fires Drain Divinity (3 LA, costs ALL remaining LA): Sabriel DC 16
CHA save, fails → loses highest slot. Drain Divinity then consumes the full LA budget,
leaving Bazgar's turn without LA coverage. This is the known degenerate pattern — Drain
Divinity is "all-in" at the cost of any reactive LA use. With Marwen already dead,
Void Ray (2 LA) on Sabriel and Tentacle (1 LA) on Bazgar deals 22 avg force to Sabriel
+ another grapple on Bazgar, which is more flexible and arguably better value.

**Drain Divinity dominance note:** Against Sabriel (level 5 divine martial, est. 2× 3rd-level
slots + Lay on Hands), losing one 3rd-level slot is painful but the party survives it.
Drain Divinity's value scales with: number of remaining slots × slot level. At full pool
(post-rest), Drain Divinity for a 3rd-level slot = 6 THP for beholder + one less cure/smite.
Void Ray (2 LA) gives 22 avg damage. If Sabriel has 44 HP, Void Ray is more impactful per
LA cost. Drain Divinity's "best case" is early in the fight before slots are spent; its
"worst case" is when the target is out of slots (does nothing useful). This confirms the
open recommendation: lower Drain Divinity to 2 LA cost, or add a secondary trigger
(beholder gains advantage vs that creature for 1 minute in addition to THP).
