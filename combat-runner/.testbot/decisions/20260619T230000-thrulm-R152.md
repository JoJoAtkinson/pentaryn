### Probability math

**vs AC 17 with +7 attack bonus:** needs 10+ on d20.  
P(hit, no AR) = 11/20 = **55%**  
P(hit, AR-disadvantage) = (11/20)² = 121/400 ≈ **30.25%**  
P(hit, AR-retroactive +2 AC) = needs 12+, so 9/20 = **45%**

**Per attack-1 (with Antireality):**
- Correct mechanic: AR saves 55% − 30% = **25% of attack-1s from hitting**
- DB-text mechanic ("+2 AC"): AR saves 55% − 45% = **10% of attack-1s from hitting**

Bazgar averages ~8.5 HP per hit (1d8+4).

**3-round damage mitigation:**

| AR Mechanic | Expected hits saved (3 rounds) | Expected HP mitigated |
|-------------|-------------------------------|----------------------|
| Correct (disadvantage) | 3 × 0.25 = 0.75 hits | 0.75 × 8.5 ≈ **6.4 HP** |
| DB text (+2 AC post-roll) | 3 × 0.10 = 0.30 hits | 0.30 × 8.5 ≈ **2.6 HP** |

**BUG-F7-02 impact:** DB-text Antireality is ~2.5× weaker than stat-block Antireality. A DM running from the runner-prompted effect text applies a materially weaker mechanic.

### New finding: Antireality targeting priority (FEEL-R152-01)

**Setup:** Sabriel (divine martial 5) attacks in the same round as Bazgar. Sabriel carries a sanctified weapon (+1d8 extra damage per hit, per beholder weaknesses section). Bazgar does not.

Assume Sabriel's attack bonus ≈ +7 (same range as Bazgar for simplicity; divine martial 5 is STR/DEX based with PB +3). Sabriel's per-hit damage: 1d8+4 (weapon) + 1d8 (sanctified) = avg 4.5+4+4.5 = **13 HP per hit**.

**Priority choice:**
- AR on Bazgar's attack-1: saves 25% × 8.5 = **2.1 HP expected per round**
- AR on Sabriel's attack-1 (sanctified): saves 25% × 13 = **3.3 HP expected per round**

Targeting Sabriel's sanctified attack is worth **+1.2 HP/round** on average.

**Cumulative over a 5-round fight:** AR misallocated to Bazgar loses ~6 HP of additional mitigation vs optimal (Sabriel-first) allocation. Not huge but meaningful in a fight where the beholder's HP matters for retreat timing.

**FEEL-R152-01 (NEW):** Beholder tactics section says "Against Melee: grapples with tentacles and drags into difficult terrain" but gives no guidance on Antireality targeting priority. The optimal priority is to Antireality the PC dealing the highest expected damage per hit — generally the divine martial with sanctified weapon (Sabriel) over the standard fighter (Bazgar). This is not documented in tactics or cheat sheet. A DM defaulting to "fire AR on whoever swings first" will underperform by ~6 HP over the fight, which can affect retreat timing.

### New finding: Antireality and Advantage interaction (STAT-R152-01)

The rotation.yml note flags: "if Bazgar already has disadvantage from another source, Antireality is wasted (advantage/disadvantage don't stack — straight roll results instead)."

Scenario where this matters: Rager Taunt (DC 12 CHA, 55% fail) gives Bazgar disadvantage on attacks against targets OTHER than the Rager. If Bazgar attacks the beholder (not the Rager), Bazgar rolls with disadvantage. Beholder then declares Antireality. Per 5e RAW, disadvantage doesn't stack — Bazgar still rolls with disadvantage (one lower result), but Antireality adds nothing. **Antireality is wasted.**

Similarly, if Marwen uses Hold Person or Slow that already imposes movement restrictions and Bazgar has forced disadvantage from terrain (prone, restrained), Antireality on that attack is worth 0 additional mitigation.

**DM guidance gap (not already logged):** Tactics section should note "Do not declare Antireality if the attacker already has disadvantage from another source — it is wasted. Save the reaction for an attack that has advantage or rolls straight."

**STAT-R152-01 (NEW):** Antireality + prior-disadvantage = zero benefit. Beholder should not declare Antireality on a Taunted Bazgar attacking the beholder while a Rager Taunt is active. This interaction (AR wasted by preexisting disadvantage) is absent from tactics. At the table, a DM who reflexively declares AR on every Bazgar attack will sometimes spend the reaction for no benefit.
