## Phase B — Scenario: retreat-threshold (thrulm-rotation.json index 6)

*Slice: "Beholder drops to 28 HP — retreat toward lower shaft, thralls block. Dramatic or anticlimactic?"*

### Setup
Party: Bazgar Fighter 5 (HP 49, AC 18), Marwen Wizard 5 (HP 32, AC 15; assume at 15 HP after
earlier damage), Sabriel divine-martial 5 (HP 44, AC 19; 1 third-level slot remaining after
prior Drain Divinity). Beholder at 28 HP (taken 82 damage), 2 thrall derro present (HP 22, AC 14).
All within the main vault. Beholder hovering near altar, 40 ft from the Deeper Shaft entrance.

### Movement budget on retreat turn

The beholder's full movement kit in one turn:
| Source | Distance | Note |
|--------|----------|------|
| Speed (fly 30) | 30 ft | Main movement |
| Shrine-Drift (BA) | 30 ft | Passes through creatures/objects; takes 5 (1d10) force if ends INSIDE |
| Legendary Move (1 LA) | 30 ft | End of another creature's turn |
| Total (main turn) | 60 ft | Speed + Shrine-Drift |
| Total (pre-turn LA) | 90 ft | Speed + Shrine-Drift + 1 pre-turn Legendary Move |

With 90 ft of available movement and the shaft entrance ~40 ft away (from altar position), the
beholder CAN reach the shaft mouth in one turn. The vault is described as "vast" with no stated
dimensions — the shaft entrance may be further than 40 ft if the vault is large (80-120 ft
across). **If the shaft is 60+ ft from the altar, the beholder cannot clear the gap in one
main-turn burst and needs either a prior Legendary Move or a second turn to reach safety.**

**This ambiguity is a DESIGN GAP.** The _overview.md does not specify vault dimensions or the
shaft entrance's distance from the altar. A DM running this encounter blind cannot determine
whether the retreat is a one-turn escape or a multi-turn chase. Recommend adding approximate
dimensions to `_overview.md` or the cheat sheet (e.g., "Deeper Shaft entrance: 60 ft from
altar, north wall").

### FEEL-006: Retreat turn forces BA conflict — Shrine-Drift vs Compel Thrall

The tactics section says: "If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower
shaft, using thralls to block pursuit."

**The action economy during the retreat turn:**
- Speed: 30 ft movement toward shaft
- Shrine-Drift (BA): 30 ft movement through obstacles — beholder's fast escape mechanism
- Compel Thrall (1/Turn, BA): redirect a thrall to block the party's path

The beholder cannot use BOTH Shrine-Drift AND Compel Thrall in the same turn — both use the
bonus action slot (RAW: one BA per turn). The tactics text implies simultaneous use, which is
not possible.

**Consequence at the table:** The DM must choose:
1. Shrine-Drift: fast escape (60 ft in one turn), but thralls act on their own initiative
   (cannot be directed this turn). Result: beholder likely escapes, but thralls may not
   block effectively.
2. Compel Thrall: place a thrall in the party's path, but beholder moves only 30 ft (speed).
   Result: tactical blocking, but beholder stays in range of party attacks for one more round.

Neither choice is wrong — but the tactics text should clarify the priority. If escape is the
goal, Shrine-Drift dominates. If stalling is the goal, Compel Thrall dominates.

**Recommendation (log only — tactics text, not auto-fix):**
Add to beholder tactics section under "Retreat": "Action economy note: Shrine-Drift (BA) and
Compel Thrall (BA) cannot both be used in the same turn. Prioritize Shrine-Drift to escape;
thralls will move toward blocking positions on their own initiative next round (DEX +1, likely
near end of order). Spend 1 LA on Legendary Move if available to extend reach."

### FEEL-007: Retreat threshold unreachable at Hard/Deadly difficulty

The 30 HP threshold triggers after the party deals 80 damage to a 110 HP beholder.

**Expected damage output from Compass Edge per round (Hard difficulty):**
- Bazgar: 2 attacks, +7 hit vs AC 17. Attack 1 with Antireality (disadvantage): P(hit) ≈ 0.30.
  Attack 2 (normal): P(hit) ≈ 0.55. Avg hit damage ~12 (1d8+6 longsword at level 5, rough est.).
  EV: 0.30×12 + 0.55×12 = 3.6 + 6.6 = **10.2/round** (while beholder is at altitude, non-melee).
- Marwen (Wizard): likely using Fireball (8d6, avg 28) once, then ranged cantrips.
  Round 1 Fireball: beholder succeeds/fails DC save. P(fail) = P(d20+3 < DC) — unknown beholder
  DEX save modifier. With Dex +3+PB (unclear): assume Dex save +6 → P(fail) = 45% → EV of
  Fireball vs beholder = 0.45×28 + 0.55×14 = 12.6 + 7.7 = **20.3 on the round it fires**.
  Subsequent rounds: cantrips ~4-5 EV/round. Beholder also has resistance to nonmagic B/P/S
  (Marwen's cantrips are likely fire/force — no resistance).
- Sabriel: divine martial, 2 attacks assumed, +3-5 hit. Sanctified weapon (if available): +1d8
  bonus per hit vs beholder (custom). EV: 2 attacks at P(hit) ≈ 0.40 vs AC 17 → ~0.80 hits
  × (8 + 4.5 sanctified) = ~10 EV/round (assumes sanctified weapon).

**Total party EV/round (sustained, non-Fireball rounds):** ~10 + 5 + 10 = **25 EV/round**.

At 25 EV/round, the beholder reaches 30 HP from 110 HP in 3.2 rounds on average. With beholder's
Antireality (reducing Bazgar's first hit each round) and potential Legendary Resistance usage,
this is closer to 4-5 rounds.

**In a "meant to lose" encounter**, the beholder is doing ~25-35 EV/round back to the party:
- Disintegration Ray one-shots Marwen on turn 1 (if it fires: 45 force avg vs 32 HP → dead)
- Void Scream hits all three on recharge 6 (~17% per round): 33 avg psychic × 3 PCs = 99 EV
- Multiattack: 2 tentacles (13 avg) + maw (21 avg) = 47 EV if all hit (~60% likely to hit at
  least 2 of 3 attacks)

**Realistic outcome**: With Marwen eliminated round 1 (Disintegration Ray + Fireball reorder)
or severely damaged, and Sabriel's slots drained, the party's sustained DPS drops to ~15 EV/round
from Bazgar and Sabriel. At 15 EV/round, reaching 30 HP from 110 HP takes **5.3 rounds**.
In 5 rounds, the beholder deals approximately:
- Round 1: Disintegration Ray (~45 to Marwen) → Marwen likely 0 HP
- Rounds 2-5: Multiattack (47 EV) + Void Scream (fires ~once in 5 rounds: 33×3/3 ≈ 33 avg)
  + LA Void Ray (2 LA, 22 avg ×2 rounds = 44) → total ~47×4 + 33 + 44 = **265 EV in 5 rounds**

Party total HP: 49+32+44 = 125 HP. With resistance/legendary not factored, party is in TPK
territory well before round 5. **The retreat threshold is effectively unreachable at Hard/Deadly
difficulty vs a level 5 party of 3.** It only functions in Easy/Medium mode (reduced thralls,
no lair actions) or if the beholder uses suboptimal play.

**Design implication:** The retreat mechanic is decorative at the intended difficulty. The
30 HP threshold should either be lowered (e.g., 50 HP / ~50% health) to make it reachable
in realistic play, or the tactics section should note "this threshold is only relevant in
Easy/Medium difficulty; at Hard/Deadly, retreat is likely pre-empted by party TPK."

### FEEL-008: Shrine-Drift phase-through damage at low HP is risky

At 28 HP, using Shrine-Drift through a solid stone wall (to reach the shaft) risks the beholder
ending its turn inside stone (5 (1d10) force damage). With 28 HP, rolling maximum (10 damage)
from Shrine-Drift self-collision would leave the beholder at 18 HP — still alive but a single
failed Legendary Resistance away from death. The self-damage risk is thematically appropriate
but practically low-stakes (beholder is likely to escape or die regardless). Flag for DM
awareness only.

**The vault's "vast" description implies the beholder likely does NOT need to phase through
walls** to reach the shaft — it can fly through open space. Shrine-Drift's phase-through
property is an escape valve for blocked corridors, not needed in open vault terrain. The beholder's
retreat is a straight flight path in the open vault. This makes the retreat less dramatic than
the stat block implies.
