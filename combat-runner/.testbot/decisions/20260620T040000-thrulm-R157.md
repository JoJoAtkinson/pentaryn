#### BUG-R157-01 — `"ray"` verb dispatch ambiguity in thrulm-beholder-alpha-strike scenario (NEW)

The `thrulm-beholder-alpha-strike` scenario script (round 2) sends:
```
{kind: type, tab: beholder-thrulm, text: "ray"}
```

DB verb lookup:
- `disintegration_ray` verbs include `"ray"` (match: exact substring)
- `void_ray_legendary` verbs: `["void ray", "void blast", "force ray", "legendary ray"]` — none include bare `"ray"`

**Result:** `"ray"` dispatches to `disintegration_ray` (recharge 5), NOT `void_ray_legendary`. This is probably intended — the scenario wants to test DR's recharge label (scenario notes: "does disintegration_ray correctly label recharge=5?"). However, DR fired in multiattack on round 1 in this scenario — wait, no: multiattack uses Tentacle Lash × 2 + Maw. DR is a separate action. The scenario sends `"multiattack"` round 1 and `"ray"` round 2.

**Potential runner issue:** DR was not used round 1 (multiattack fires Tentacle × 2 + Maw, not DR). DR enters round 2 with no previous use. It should be available. However, DR has recharge 5, meaning the runner tracks it as a recharge ability. If the runner incorrectly initializes DR as "on cooldown" (recharge abilities start available unless stated otherwise), typing `"ray"` round 2 would hit a cooldown block instead of firing.

**Verdict:** Most likely not a bug — DR should start available, and `"ray"` → `disintegration_ray` is the correct dispatch. This tests whether the runner fires DR with the correct label and recharge note. The `scenario.targets.max_llm_fallback: 0` confirms the expectation that DR fires deterministically, without LLM. If DR somehow shows as "on cooldown" round 2 after not being used, that IS a runner bug worth flagging.

**Disposition:** LOG. Verify in a live runner session that `disintegration_ray` initializes as available (not on cooldown at start of encounter).

### Key findings

#### FEEL-R157-01 — Drain Divinity full-LA spend exposes beholder to Fighter burst: tactical tradeoff undocumented (NEW)

When Drain Divinity fired at the end of Bazgar's turn (consuming all 3 LA), the beholder had NO legendary responses during Sabriel's turn, Shardcaller's turn, Marwen's turn, or Rager's turn. On a round where Bazgar acts before DD fires:

The tradeoff in this simulation:
- DD benefit: Sabriel loses 3rd-level slot (prevents one smite = ~avg 13.5 radiant damage lost)
- Beholder THP from DD: 6 THP (soaked by Sabriel's first hit, Attack 1, 25 damage total = 6 THP gone + 19 real damage to beholder)
- Beholder loses: Tentacle (1 LA) + Void Ray (2 LA) — could have dealt ~13 + 18 = 31 HP damage to party instead

**Net analysis:** DD nets the beholder 6 THP (immediately absorbed by Sabriel's smite hit), prevents one future smite (avg ~13 dmg saved), and costs the beholder one round of offensive LA pressure. Vs. Tentacle + Void Ray (could have grappled Sabriel AND hit Bazgar for 31 total HP damage), the tradeoff is roughly break-even in HP math, with DD being better if the slot would have gone toward a level 3 smite (radiant = effective vs beholder) and worse if Void Ray targets soft HP targets.

**The DM instinct to use DD "for free" (it's just resource drain!) is misleading.** Spending all 3 LA on DD sacrifices a full round of legendary offensive pressure. This tradeoff should be documented in the tactics section:

*"Drain Divinity (3 LA): use when the divine caster has high-impact slots remaining (3rd level or higher) AND the beholder doesn't need Void Ray + Tentacle LA to threaten vulnerable targets that turn. Against a balanced party, DD is often weaker than Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA) unless specifically targeting a cleric with Heal prepared."*

**Disposition:** LOG. Add tradeoff note to beholder tactics section and cheat sheet "When Beholder Acts" checklist.

#### FEEL-R157-02 — Disintegration Ray missed due to Lair Action knockdown: unintentional counterplay (NEW, POSITIVE)

Round 2: Lair Action Unstable Ground knocked Sabriel prone. When the beholder then fired Disintegration Ray (ranged attack, >5 ft away), the prone condition imposed disadvantage on the ranged attack roll, causing a miss. The lair action inadvertently protected the target it knocked down from a follow-up DR.

This is **accidental counterplay** — the beholder's own lair action partially shielded the target from its most powerful attack. In isolation, this is just bad sequencing: the beholder should fire DR before the lair action fires (but lair actions fire on count 20, fixed). Alternatively, the beholder should target a DIFFERENT prone target for DR, or use DR only on standing targets.

**Impact:** Not significant enough to redesign — DR still hits standing targets freely. But it reveals that the beholder's lair actions and main actions can conflict. The tactics section says "uses Disintegration Ray freely" without noting that prone targets are harder to hit at range.

**Disposition:** LOG. Add DM note: "When Unstable Ground knocked a target prone, delay Disintegration Ray until they stand (next round) or target a standing PC instead."

#### FEEL-R157-03 — Pack Tactics Voice (Shardcaller) + Void Scream (Beholder): undocumented save-crushing synergy (NEW)

The Shardcaller passive **Pack Tactics Voice** reads: "When an ally of the shardcaller hits a target within 30 feet of the shardcaller, the target has disadvantage on the next saving throw it makes before the end of its next turn."

If a Rager or Deep Watch ally hits a PC in melee on their turn, and the beholder then fires **Void Scream** (DC 16 WIS) while that PC is still within 30 ft of the shardcaller, the PC saves at **disadvantage** against Void Scream.

**Math:** Bazgar (WIS +0) vs DC 16:
- Normal: 5/20 = 25% pass rate
- Disadvantage: (5/20)² ≈ 6.25% pass rate

A Rager Greataxe hit on Bazgar → Pack Tactics Voice fires → Bazgar saves vs Void Scream at 6.25% chance of success. Effectively an auto-fail whenever the Rager lands a hit.

**Conditions for this combo:**
1. An ally (Rager, Deep Watch) must hit a PC within 30 ft of the shardcaller
2. The beholder must fire Void Scream the SAME ROUND (not triggered, just timed correctly)
3. Void Scream must recharge (5-6, 33% per round)
4. The PC must remain within 30 ft of both the shardcaller and the beholder

This is not guaranteed every round but IS achievable in rounds 2-3 when the Rager has closed to melee and the shardcaller is positioned mid-chamber. The shardcaller's 40-60 ft standoff naturally covers the mid-chamber zone where the party meets the Rager.

**This combo is nowhere in the cheat sheet or tactics section.** DMs who miss it lose one of the encounter's most tactically interesting synergies.

**Disposition:** LOG. Add to both beholder tactics and shardcaller tactics: "Pack Tactics Voice + Void Scream synergy: if any ally hits a PC within 30 ft of the shardcaller, that PC saves at disadvantage on the next save — including Void Scream. Time Void Scream to fire after a Rager/Deep Watch hit for near-guaranteed FRIGHTENED application."

#### FEEL-R157-04 — Beholder retreat threshold (30 HP): confirming the party CAN threaten it (NEW, POSITIVE DATA)

Round 2 end: Beholder at 28 HP after sustained damage from Bazgar (Action Surge, 31 HP) and Sabriel (21 HP smite, 13 HP radiant). **The party almost killed the beholder in 2 rounds.** The beholder retreated.

This is notable because previous runs (R154–R156) often ended with the party wiped before the beholder reached 30 HP. This run shows: if the Fighter uses Action Surge aggressively AND Sabriel can land a smite before losing slots to DD, the party CAN push the beholder to retreat within 2 rounds.

**Data point for balance:** Average beholder HP loss this run = (110-28) = 82 HP over 2 rounds with sustained focus. Party deals ~41 HP/round at peak (Action Surge rounds) against the beholder. The beholder retreats at 30 HP, so the "kill budget" is effectively 80 HP over the encounter. With typical (non-Action Surge) rounds = ~22 HP/round from Bazgar + ~13 HP/round from Sabriel ≈ 35 HP/round, the party needs roughly 2.3 rounds of sustained focus to trigger retreat. This aligns with the 2-3 round encounter duration observed across all runs.

**Finding:** The encounter is mechanically functional — the party isn't utterly helpless, the beholder DOES retreat as intended, and the trigger threshold (30 HP) is reachable within the expected encounter window. The "feel unfair rather than lethal" threshold wasn't crossed here. The design is working as intended at the HP math level.

**Disposition:** LOG (positive). Carry-forward: FEEL-R151-01 (retreat has no PC counterplay) is still valid — the party can't chase the beholder deeper without ending the encounter. The issue is that "almost killed" and "beholder retreated safely" feel narratively incomplete.
