### Extended analysis: Berserk expected value vs Multiattack

This is the core feel question — does Berserk fire at the right moments?

**Berserk vs 3 targets (scenario best case):**
- vs Bazgar AC 18 (+5, advantage): P(hit) = 1−0.60² = **64%**. E[dmg] = 0.64 × 8.5 = 5.4
- vs Marwen AC 15 (+5, no adv): P(hit) = **55%**. E[dmg] = 0.55 × 8.5 = 4.7
- vs Sabriel AC 19 (+5, no adv): P(hit) = **35%**. E[dmg] = 0.35 × 8.5 = 3.0
- **Total Berserk E[dmg] vs 3 PCs: ~13.1**

**Berserk vs 2 targets (Bazgar + Sabriel, Marwen back-line):**
- E[dmg] = 5.4 + 3.0 = **8.4**

**Berserk vs 1 target (Bazgar only, standard R1):**
- E[dmg] = 5.4 — **strictly worse than Multiattack (2 attacks vs Bazgar: 0.64×8.5 + 0.40×8.5 = 8.8)**

**Multiattack vs 1 target (Bazgar, ME+IDA):**
- Attack 1 (advantage): P(hit) = 64%, E[dmg] = 5.4
- Attack 2 (no advantage, ME only): P(hit) = 40%, E[dmg] = 3.4
- **Total Multiattack E[dmg] vs Bazgar: 8.8**

**Decision table (Berserk vs Multiattack, with Madness Endurance active):**

| # PCs in reach | Berserk E[dmg] | Multiattack E[dmg] (vs Bazgar) | Better choice |
|----------------|---------------|-------------------------------|---------------|
| 1 (Bazgar only) | 5.4 | 8.8 | Multiattack |
| 2 (Bazgar + Sabriel) | 8.4 | 8.8 | Multiattack by slim margin |
| 3 (all PCs) | 13.1 | 8.8 | **Berserk** |
| 2 (Bazgar + Marwen) | 5.4+4.7=10.1 | 8.8 | **Berserk** |

The breakeven is 2 targets only if one of them is Marwen (AC 15). Vs Bazgar + Sabriel (both high-AC), Berserk is marginally *worse* than Multiattack.

### Feel Assessment

#### New finding: FEEL-F27-01 — Berserk almost never reaches the 2-PC threshold without proactive setup

**Severity:** Medium — affects every Rager combat with this party.

**Finding:** The encounter begins with the party at the vault entrance (~40–60 ft from the altar). Standard R1 flow:
1. Beholder opens with Multiattack from the shrine.
2. Rager charges Bazgar (movement ~30 ft). Only Bazgar is in range at end of charge (Sabriel typically stops at 20–30 ft; Marwen at 40+ ft).
3. R1 Rager has **1 PC in reach** → Berserk E[dmg] 5.4 vs Multiattack 8.8 → Multiattack is correct play. But the tactics note says "use R1 if the rager can reach 2+ enemies after its charge" — this is correctly conditional, but there is no guidance on what to do when only 1 PC closes.

In this encounter, Sabriel may advance R1 to deliver LoH or a melee attack on the beholder (not on the rager), leaving the rager surrounded by Bazgar only. Berserk then fires wastefully. The Rager's tactics section says "Use only when already within 5 ft of two or more enemies" — this is correct but may not be followed if the DM interprets the scenario prelude ("Three PCs in reach") as the standard case.

**Distribution estimate across a typical fight:**
- R1: 1 PC in reach (Bazgar alone): ~70% of R1s → Multiattack preferred
- R2: 2 PCs in reach possible (Bazgar grappled by beholder, Sabriel enters melee): ~40% of R2s
- R3+: varies; beholder Maw combo typically downs a PC by mid-R2, reducing viable Berserk targets

Practical result: Berserk fires at full value (3 targets) roughly once per 3–4 encounters. It usually fires against 1 target (wasted) or 2 targets (marginal).

**Suggested note for tactics section (log only — do not edit source):**
> *Berserk against 1 target is suboptimal — hold until 2+ PCs are in reach. With Compass Edge, Sabriel often delays into melee range on R2 when she has no LoH target; Berserk's window is R2 when both Bazgar and Sabriel are adjacent. Against Marwen-only (caster at back-line), Berserk vs 2 PCs still beats Multiattack.*

#### Recharge mechanic — correct

Berserk recharge (5–6) on click_round is mechanically sound. 33% recharge creates meaningful tension: the rager can't guarantee R2 Berserk, pushing the DM to decide whether to hold or use R1 (often incorrectly). The recharge rate is balanced for this party — 3-target Berserk on two consecutive rounds would deal ~26 damage to Bazgar in 2 turns, approaching lethal without a kill; at 33% recharge probability, this rarely chains.
