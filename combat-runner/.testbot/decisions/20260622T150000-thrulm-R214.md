### Phase B Analysis

**1. Was any outcome unfair rather than lethal?**

- Sabriel dropping to 0 HP on the beholder's main turn (round 1) before Marwen and the Rager/Thralls have acted is harsh but within design intent ("party is meant to lose"). Sabriel *did* act and deal 23 damage before going down.
- **The most unfair interaction is BUG-F7-02:** If the DM runs Antireality from the DB ("+2 AC after roll"), the beholder on average fails to block ~60% of hits that would have been negated by disadvantage. Conversely, when the beholder rolls high for the attack, the DM has no mechanic to respond at all. Antireality as a reaction is nearly non-functional under the DB text.

**2. Beholder legendary action economy (Drain Divinity vs Void Ray vs Move):**

- **Drain Divinity (3 LA):** Dominant when Sabriel is conscious and within 30 ft. Uses the *entire* LA budget — zero LAs left for other options. In round 1, Sabriel went down on the beholder's action, which meant Drain Divinity (3 LA) had no target before Sabriel's turn. The beholder fell back to Tentacle + Void Ray (1+2 LA), which was actually more damaging this round (killed Marwen's remaining HP with Void Ray).
- **Void Ray (2 LA):** High value against mid-HP targets (Marwen, 32 HP → 0 in one Void Ray hit). Best for finishing off wounded PCs.
- **Move (1 LA):** Never used this simulation — the beholder repositioned with Shrine-Drift bonus action. Move legendary is redundant given Shrine-Drift (same 30 ft, pass-through walls) except that Shrine-Drift is a bonus action (uses the bonus action slot).
- **Conclusion:** Against this party, Move LA is dead weight when Shrine-Drift is available. The beholder should always use Shrine-Drift for mobility (bonus action) and spend LAs on Tentacle + Void Ray or pure Drain Divinity. This confirms the "Move is never dominant" finding from prior fires — but the new angle is that **Move and Shrine-Drift overlap almost entirely**, making Move LA economically worthless.

**3. Recharge ability rotation:**

- Void Scream (Recharge 6) and Disintegration Ray (Recharge 5-6) didn't fire in 2 rounds. Their 1/6 or 2/6 per-turn recharge rate means they average every 3-6 rounds. For a party that goes down in round 2-3, these abilities effectively never fire. This is a **rotation gap** for Disintegration Ray — at L5 with fast party elimination, the beholder never uses its most dramatic ability. (Void Scream less relevant since the party is often too spread out at the 30-ft radius.)

#### NEW-R214-01 (MEDIUM — feel / DM guidance)

**Move legendary action is dead weight when Shrine-Drift is available.**

The beholder's Move legendary action (1 LA: fly up to 30 ft) duplicates Shrine-Drift bonus action (30 ft, pass-through). In every round where the beholder uses its bonus action on anything at all (Shrine-Drift or Compel Thrall), it has no reason to spend 1 LA on Move — the bonus action already provides the same mobility plus pass-through capability. In rounds where the beholder doesn't need to move, Move is irrelevant.

- **In practice (this simulation + R205–R213):** Move legendary was never the dominant LA choice in any fire. Observations: 100% of simulated rounds where Sabriel was conscious favored Drain Divinity (3 LA); when Sabriel was unconscious, Tentacle (1 LA) + Void Ray (2 LA) was preferred. Move never fired.
- **Design question:** Was Move intended as a fallback for rounds when the beholder can't use Drain Divinity or Void Ray? If so, the current setup is: Move (1 LA) for free repositioning, available only when not spending all LAs on DD or Void Ray. This is a fine design but means Move only ever fires in "safe" rounds (beholder is winning, needs to maintain position). In the encounter as designed (party losing round 2-3), those "safe rounds" don't exist.
- **Suggested fix (optional):** Replace or expand Move to a more distinctive option — e.g., "Move + one free Tentacle grapple attempt" (1 LA), giving it a reason to use at any point. Or leave as is; the beholder is CR 13, its LA economy is powerful enough without an optimized Move action.
