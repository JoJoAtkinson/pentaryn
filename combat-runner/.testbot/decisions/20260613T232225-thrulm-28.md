### Feel simulation: multiattack damage profile

**Setup:** Full encounter load. Beholder wins initiative (DEX +3 vs. Bazgar DEX −1: beholder ~60% chance to go first).

**Lair action (init 20, fires before beholder):** Unstable Ground on Bazgar (DC 16 Dex, +1 mod — wait, Bazgar has DEX mod −1). P(fail) = P(Bazgar rolls ≤ 16 on 1d20−1) = P(d20 ≤ 17) = 85%. Expected: Bazgar goes prone.

**Beholder turn (e.g., init 18):** Multiattack — 2 TL + 1 Maw.

- Bazgar is prone: melee attacks vs prone have **advantage** (5e RAW PHB).
- TL reach is 10 ft — still melee, advantage applies.
- TL #1 +7 (VF) vs AC 18 with advantage: P(hit) = 1 − (11/20)² = 1 − 0.3025 = **69.75%**. E[dmg] = 0.698 × 14 = **9.8**. If hit → Bazgar grappled.
- TL #2 +7 vs AC 18 with advantage: same. E[dmg] = **9.8**.
- Maw vs grappled Bazgar (P_grappled = 0.698 from TL1): auto-crit on hit. P(hit) = 0.5.
  - E[Maw | grappled] = 0.5 × (8d8+3+1 avg ≈ 40) = **20**
  - E[Maw | not grappled] = 0.5 × 22 = **11**
  - E[Maw] = 0.698 × 20 + 0.302 × 11 = 14.0 + 3.3 = **17.3**

**E[total beholder multiattack vs prone Bazgar] = 9.8 + 9.8 + 17.3 = 36.9 HP**

Without prone (beholder acts before lair action, or Bazgar saves):  
E[total] = 0.5×14 + 0.5×14 + E[Maw] = 7+7+16.5 = **30.5 HP**

Bazgar has 49 HP. Expected turns to down from beholder multiattack alone:
- With prone: 49 / 36.9 = **1.33 rounds** (typically dead in 2 rounds, sometimes R1)
- Without prone: 49 / 30.5 = **1.61 rounds**

The scenario confirms this: even a below-average multiattack (47 total, no prone bonus simulated in scenario) leaves Bazgar at 2 HP. **Bazgar's effective combat lifespan is 1–2 beholder multiattacks.**

Per FIX-EV7-32-A (R1 = Multiattack, hold all 3 LA for DD post-Sabriel):

1. Lair (init 20): Unstable Ground on Bazgar ✓
2. Beholder action: Multiattack on Bazgar (closest, highest threat) ✓

**LA economy this scenario:** The R1 hold protocol is correct but creates a window where Bazgar is at 2 HP and no LA fires to finish him. The beholder correctly holds LAs (DD window on Sabriel). This is the right call. However:

If Sabriel uses her R1 action to Lay on Hands Bazgar (Bazgar: 2 HP → 27 HP), beholder immediately fires DD post-Sabriel's turn, stripping Sabriel's highest spell slot (burning the heal investment). The LoH → DD interaction:
- LoH is not a spell slot (DD can't counter it)
- DD strips the NEXT spell slot, not the LoH that was just used
- So LoH is effective: Bazgar gets 25 HP; Sabriel loses one spell slot to DD
- This is the correct dynamic: DD punishes future spell use, not past LoH

**Dominant LA in this scenario: Drain Divinity (confirmed, post-Sabriel R1).** Void Ray and Tentacle not used R1 per hold protocol. This is the 36th consecutive fire confirming DD as the dominant LA strategy.

### Specifically flagged feel issue: FEEL-F36-01

**Average non-crit beholder multiattack (with VF) = 50 HP damage to Bazgar.** Bazgar has 49 HP. The fighter-tank can be one-shot by average damage from the beholder's primary action.

The sequence with max-probability path (beholder wins init 85%, Bazgar fails UG save 85%):
- P(this sequence) = 0.85 × 0.85 = **72%** of combats begin with Bazgar prone before his turn
- On those combats: E[beholder multiattack vs prone Bazgar] = 36.9 → Bazgar at 49−36.9 = 12 HP average, but variance is high (can range from 0 to ~75)
- P(one-shot Bazgar in R1): roughly P(all three attacks hit at VF-boosted rolls, Maw crits) ≈ P(TL1 hits) × P(TL2 hits) × P(Maw hits and crits) = 0.698 × 0.698 × 0.5 = **24%**

In **~1 in 4 combats**, Bazgar is dead (0 HP) before he takes a single action. This feels arbitrary rather than dramatic. The counterplay options are:
- Sabriel pre-heals before beholder acts (impossible R1 if beholder wins init)
- Bazgar uses Battle Master Riposte (reaction, but only available after being hit by a melee attack — would give Bazgar an attack back, not prevent TL damage)
- Marwen counterspells (TL is a weapon attack, not a spell — Counterspell doesn't work)
- No option exists that prevents the R1 one-shot probability

**Suggested note (log only — do not edit source):**
> *Consider capping beholder multiattack to TL+TL only in R1 (Maw held for R2 when grapple is established). This preserves the grapple threat while giving Bazgar one round of agency. Alternatively: reduce Maw to 4d6+3 (avg 17 normal / 31 crit) — still devastating but below Bazgar's max HP threshold. Not a balance issue; a pacing issue.*
