### NEW FINDING — FEEL-F16-01: "R1 = Multiattack" protocol is position-gated

The tactics note `FIX-EV7-32-A` states: *"R1 = Multiattack (grapple); R2 = Void Scream. Do NOT fire VS on R1."*

This slice exposes a prerequisite that the note does not state: **Tentacle Lash reach is 10 ft.** When the beholder wins initiative before any PC has moved, PCs are at their starting positions at the chamber entrance — approximately 40–60 ft from the altar where the beholder hovers. No PC is within 10 ft of the beholder.

**Consequence:** "R1 = Multiattack" is mechanically impossible when the beholder has the highest initiative. The beholder has no valid melee targets. Its viable actions are:

1. **Disintegration Ray** (120-ft range, +7 vs AC 15 Marwen = 65% hit, one-shot on 32 HP)
2. **Compel Thrall** (60-ft range, utility — repositions a thrall, no immediate damage)
3. **Shrine-Drift** (bonus action reposition, no damage)
4. **Hold action** (wait for a PC to enter melee range — wastes the action economy)

The correct R1 action is Disintegration Ray. "R1 = Multiattack" should be updated to: *"R1 = Multiattack if Bazgar (or another melee PC) has already moved within 10 ft of the beholder before the beholder's turn; R1 = Disintegration Ray if beholder has highest initiative and PCs have not yet closed the gap."*

**This is a new, distinct finding from FEEL-F9-01** (which flagged the *effect* of DR killing Marwen R1 without counterplay, but did not identify the *positional mechanism* that makes DR the only available action when beholder wins initiative).

### NEW FINDING — FEEL-F16-02: Beholder action + lair action create a "two-nova before PC response" window

When beholder wins initiative (init 21), the sequence is:

1. **Beholder turn (21):** DR → Marwen disintegrated.
2. **Lair action (20):** UG → Bazgar prone.
3. **PCs act (14, 11, 8):** Marwen is already gone; Sabriel has no melee option; Bazgar is prone and out of range.

Both the beholder's action and the lair action fire before any PC can respond. This creates a structural asymmetry unique to the "beholder wins initiative" scenario: the party faces a two-action nova (DR kill + UG debilitation) before they take a single turn.

The lair action at init 20 is documented as "On initiative count 20 (losing ties)." With beholder at init 21, it always fires AFTER the beholder's own turn and BEFORE the PCs. This is correct 5e RAW but creates a mechanically harsh opening when paired with beholder high-init.

**This is not a bug — it is a correct interaction.** But it is not documented as a scenario the DM should consider. DM note suggestion:

> *"When the beholder wins initiative (common with Dex +3 or +4), the lair action fires AFTER the beholder's turn but BEFORE all PCs (init 21 → lair 20 → PCs 14/11/8). The beholder effectively takes two actions (its own + lair) before the party responds. Against a party that hasn't yet moved, DR on Marwen R1 + UG on Bazgar via lair R1 is the standard opening combo. No PC will have acted by the time Bazgar is prone and Marwen is ash."*

  - Expected R2 damage to Bazgar: ~37 HP (see FEEL-F15-03 math). Bazgar likely downed R2.

---

### Summary: what the slice reveals

1. The "multiattack vs DR" decision on R1 is not a real decision — it's position-gated. DR is the only viable action when the beholder wins initiative.
2. The party has no effective R1 response: the beholder + lair combo eliminates Marwen (permanently) and debilitates Bazgar before any PC acts.
3. Sabriel is the last effective combatant by R2–R3; the beholder faces its primary threat (sanctified weapon) only after it has spent 1 DR charge and 3 LA charges (DD), with full HP.
4. The encounter plays out as designed (CR 13 vs level-5 party = intended loss), but the "no-agency R1" experience for Marwen's player (disintegrated before acting) is the sharpest feel issue in the encounter.
