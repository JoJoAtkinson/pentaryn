### Void Ray vs Disintegration Ray naming check

The stat block uses:
- **Disintegration Ray** — main action, Recharge 5–6, +6 to hit (attack roll), 10d8 force, disintegrate on 0 HP
- **Void Ray** — LA costing 2, DC 16 DEX save (not an attack roll), 4d10 force, no disintegrate

The cheat sheet SAVE DCs table labels them unambiguously and distinguishes attack-roll vs save-based. A DM reading only the cheat sheet is unlikely to confuse them.

**However:** During live play, both abilities fire as "rays from the beholder." If a DM is running from memory or a simplified reference, applying the disintegrate clause from DR to a Void Ray hit is a plausible mistake. The consequences are severe (accidental permanent PC death). The naming risk is real but low if the cheat sheet is in use.

**FEEL-R162-01 (NEW):** Both rays share "Ray" in their names and both deal force damage at 120 ft. Risk of at-table misattribution is low but consequence is high (accidental disintegration). Recommend adding "(main action)" and "(LA, save)" parentheticals to both ability headers in the stat block and cheat sheet.

### Drain Divinity vs Void Ray + Tentacle LA economy

Per the rotation.yml slice, the DM chooses between two LA strategies per round:

**Strategy A (Damage-LA):** Tentacle (1) after Sabriel + Void Ray (2) after Bazgar → ~24.6 expected damage to Bazgar and Sabriel.

**Strategy B (Attrition-LA):** Drain Divinity (3) after any PC turn → 0 immediate damage + P(fail DC 16 CHA) × slot removal + THP for beholder.

Sabriel (divine martial 5) assumed CHA mod: if +2: P(fail DC 16 CHA) = P(d20+2 < 16) = 65%. Sabriel level 5 slots: 2× 3rd, 3× 2nd, 4× 1st.
- If Drain Divinity hits: Sabriel loses her highest remaining slot. On a 3rd-level slot: beholder gains 6 THP. On a 2nd-level slot: 4 THP.
- Drain Divinity from a 3 LA spend: removes healing capacity (Cure Wounds, Divine Smite) for 6 THP in return.

**Round 1-2 comparison:**
- Strategy A R1: 24.6 dmg to Bazgar/Sabriel. Does not remove healing.
- Strategy B R1: Sabriel loses top slot (65% chance). Beholder gains 6 THP. No immediate damage output from LA.

At L5, Sabriel has only 2× 3rd-level slots. If both are drained by rounds 1-2, she cannot upcasted-heal or smite above 2nd level for the remainder of the fight. This is strategically devastating at this party level.

**Verdict:** DD is stronger long-term but Damage-LA is better for rapid kill of Marwen (32 HP). Against a 3-PC party where Marwen is the primary threat eliminator (Fireball, concentration spells), killing her quickly with DR + keeping LA for damage is correct; once she's dead, DD becomes the dominant LA strategy against Sabriel.

This reproduces and confirms the FEEL-R157-01 / FEEL-R161-03 finding: DD is autopilot-dominant once Marwen is eliminated. No new flag needed; confirms existing item.
