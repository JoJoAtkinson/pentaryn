### Mechanical verification: Drain Divinity

**Full text (stat block):** "The beholder targets one creature within 30 feet that has spell slots, divine favor, or clerical powers. That creature must make a DC 16 Charisma saving throw. On a failed save, it loses one spell slot of the highest level it has remaining (or loses one use of a divine ability if it has no spells). The beholder gains temporary hit points equal to twice the spell level lost."

**Key rules interaction confirmed:** THP do not stack in 5e (PHB p.198). "When you have temporary hit points and receive more of them, you decide whether to keep the ones you have or gain the new ones." The rotation.yml description states "beholder gains at most 16 temp HP (6+6+4)" — **this is incorrect.** (See FEEL-R140-01 below.)

**Range:** 30 ft. Beholder's typical hover position is 40-60 ft above altar. Drain Divinity requires descending to within 30 ft of Sabriel. (See FEEL-R140-03 below.)

### Key findings from this simulation

**FEEL-R140-01 (NEW): rotation.yml THP math error in `drain-divinity-vs-sabriel` description**  
The scenario description states "beholder gains at most 16 temp HP (6+6+4)" as if THP accumulate over multiple activations. THP do not stack in 5e; each drain's THP grant either replaces current THP (if higher) or is discarded (if lower). After the first 3rd-level drain (6 THP), subsequent equal drains add nothing, and subsequent lower-level drains are discarded entirely. The correct maximum THP from the drain sequence is **6 THP** (from the first or second 3rd-level drain, whichever fires). The rotation.yml description needs correction.  
*Action required: correction to rotation.yml (not a stat block auto-fix; flag for Joe).*

**FEEL-R140-02 (NEW — confirms FEEL-NEW-05, R139): Drain Divinity is self-balancing via LA opportunity cost**  
T+VR delivers ~83-88 expected damage over 3 rounds; 3 rounds of Drain Divinity delivers 0 direct damage plus ~24 expected healing denial (probabilistic). The trade is only net-positive for the beholder if Sabriel's healing would prevent more than 83 damage from landing — which requires the party to be nearly stable against the beholder while the beholder is below ~60 HP and at risk of losing a trading war. In a "party meant to lose" encounter at level 5, this condition never arises; the party collapses from direct damage pressure before Sabriel's healing budget matters. **Drain Divinity is not a dominant or degenerate choice; it is a suboptimal trap** that the beholder's INT +3 should prevent it from overusing. The prior "degenerate every round vs divine casters" flag from the briefing is overstated — confirmed FEEL-NEW-05 carry-forward.

**FEEL-R140-03 (NEW): Drain Divinity 30 ft range forces beholder out of hover position**  
Per tactics section: "Hovers near the shrine altar to maximize Void-Feeding bonus." Altar hover height is 40-60 ft. Drain Divinity requires target within 30 ft — effectively melee range for a hovering creature. To use Drain Divinity, the beholder must descend to floor level or near-floor, which: (a) enables grapple attempts from Bazgar (Athl +5 vs DC 16; needs 11+ → 55% grapple success on advantage from grapple rules), (b) removes the height advantage that keeps it out of Bazgar's melee reach, (c) puts it within Sabriel's divine weapon range. This cost is significant and undocumented. **The 30 ft range constraint is the real limiter on Drain Divinity, not the LA cost.** The tactic section already says "uses Disintegration Ray freely" while hovering — Drain Divinity requires an entirely different positioning decision. Recommend adding a note in tactics: "Drain Divinity (3 LA): descend to within 30 ft; high tactical cost; reserve for rounds when Sabriel will otherwise use slots for party-saving heal."
