## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `thrulm-dd-loh-redirect-void-ray`

### Scenario prelude

Sabriel uses Lay on Hands (not a spell slot) to revive Bazgar. Beholder held all 3 LA through
Bazgar's and Marwen's turns (R1-LA-hold protocol). DD watch fires at end of Sabriel's turn.
Ruling: LoH is not a spell slot → DD cannot strip it. Redirect to Void Ray (2 LA, 120 ft,
DC 16 DEX, 22 avg force). 1 LA remains.

### Setup assumptions

- Bazgar revived by LoH: 1–5 HP (minimal Sabriel expenditure — she uses 5 HP from her 25 HP pool)
  → Bazgar at **5 HP**, AC 18.
- Sabriel at ~30 HP (took some damage), initiative = 10.
- Marwen at ~15 HP (from prior Void Scream damage), initiative = 14.
- Beholder at ~82 HP (took a Fireball), initiative = 15, within 30 ft (descended via Shrine-Drift earlier).
- 3 LA held through Bazgar (death saves, initiative 7) and Marwen (initiative 14).

### Initiative order

|---|---|---|

### Phase B: DD redirect to Void Ray after Sabriel's LoH

**After Sabriel ends turn, beholder has 3 LA:**

DD check:
- Sabriel's action: LoH (not a spell slot). DD requires "spell slots, divine favor, or clerical powers."
- Ruling: LoH is a class resource, not a spell slot. DD cannot fire. → Redirect.

**Option A: 2 LA Void Ray on revived Bazgar (5 HP)**

VR vs Bazgar (5 HP, AC 18):
- DC 16 DEX save, Bazgar DEX mod = -1.
- Needs 17+ to succeed. P(fail) = 80%.
- On fail: 22 avg force damage. Bazgar at 5 HP → **downed** (excess 17, < half max HP 24.5 → not instant death; unconscious, rolling death saves).
- On success: 11 avg force damage. Bazgar at 5 HP → 5 - 11 = -6 → **downed** (still unconscious; excess 6 < 24.5 half-max).
- **Result: VR guarantees Bazgar re-downs regardless of save outcome at 5 HP.** P(downed) = 100%.

1 LA remaining.

**After Bazgar's turn (death save, no further actions at 0 HP):**

1 LA fires at end of Bazgar's turn:
- Tentacle vs Marwen (nearest conscious target): +7 to hit, reach 10 ft. Range constraint: Marwen at ~50 ft from beholder → **out of Tentacle reach (10 ft)**. Tentacle not viable.
- Tentacle vs Sabriel (within melee range at 10 ft, just used LoH): +7 vs AC 19. Needs 12+ → **45% hit** → 13 avg bludgeoning + grapple.
- Or: save 1 LA until after Marwen's NEXT turn (round 3).

Optimal: **1 LA Tentacle on Sabriel end of Bazgar's turn.** 45% chance to grapple her before her next turn (she used LoH this round, has action and attack next round).

### NEW FEEL FINDINGS

**FEEL-R99-01 (NEW): LoH-redirect note lacks HP-threshold guidance for VR vs Tentacle choice.**

The existing "LoH override note" in `beholder-thrulm.md` (Tactics, Against Clerics/Paladins)
says: "prioritize Void Ray or a Tentacle LA to prevent the revived ally from acting."

This leaves the DM choosing between 2-LA VR and 1-LA Tentacle without context. The math shows:

| Revived HP | Tentacle (1 LA, +7 vs AC 18) | Void Ray (2 LA, DC 16 DEX) |
|---|---|---|
| 1–13 HP | 50% hit → avg 14 → guaranteed down on hit | 80% fail → 22 → down; 20% success → 11 → down; **100% down** |
| 14–21 HP | 50% hit → avg 14 → may not exceed HP | 80% fail → 22 → down; 20% success → 11 → may not down |
| 22+ HP | avg 14 cannot down | avg 22 only downs on fail (80%) |

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to LoH override note: "If revived PC ≤ 13 HP, Tentacle (1 LA)
averages a guaranteed down on hit (50% chance); save the extra LA. Use VR (2 LA) when revived PC
is 14–22 HP and the guaranteed-down probability of VR justifies the extra LA cost. At 23+ HP, both
LA options fail to guarantee re-down — use DR if recharged, or accept the 50% Tentacle and hold
the budget for DD next round."

**FEEL-R99-02 (NEW): Remaining 1 LA after VR often has poor targets in this initiative ordering.**

After VR fires on Bazgar (5 HP → 0 HP, downed), the 1 remaining LA fires at end of the next
creature's turn. In the setup above, the next creature is Bazgar (death save — unconscious;
Tentacle on unconscious target is low-value). After Bazgar: Marwen (init 14), who may be out
of Tentacle reach. In practice:
- Bazgar at 0 HP: no valid melee Tentacle target at the beholder's current position.
- Marwen at 50 ft: outside Tentacle reach (10 ft). Move LA used to close → wastes Tentacle.
- Sabriel in reach: used LoH this turn, still a high-value grapple target.

The 1 remaining LA after the LoH→VR redirect typically fires on Sabriel (best in-reach target)
at the end of Bazgar's death-save turn. 45% grapple chance. If the beholder holds it to round 3
instead, that's a wasted LA budget for this round.

**This compounds FEEL-R88-01** (9+ fires): "R1-LA-hold protocol suboptimal when Sabriel uses LoH
on R1; hold-2-LA reserve better." The 3-LA-hold protocol was designed for DD. When DD can't fire
(LoH), spending 2 LA on VR + 1 LA on Sabriel-Tentacle is the best available redirect — but it
produces strictly less value than DD would have (slot attrition + temp HP). No new recommendation
beyond the existing FEEL-R88-01 flag.

**FEEL-R99-03 (NEW): "LoH is not a spell slot" ruling needs explicit in-stat-block documentation.**

The ruling that LoH cannot be stripped by DD exists only in the "LoH override note" buried in
the Tactics section under "Against Clerics/Paladins." A DM running the encounter without reading
the full tactics section (very common at the table) will not know this ruling and may:
(a) Waste 3 LA attempting DD on LoH, or
(b) Incorrectly strip LoH with DD (rules error that inflates beholder effectiveness).

The existing LoH override note is correct but poorly positioned. **Recommendation (log only):**
Move the LoH override note to a sidebar/callout near the Drain Divinity LA description (after
the DD text itself, not only in the tactical section). A DM scanning the LA list during combat
should see "NOTE: LoH is not a spell slot — cannot be targeted by DD. Use VR redirect."
