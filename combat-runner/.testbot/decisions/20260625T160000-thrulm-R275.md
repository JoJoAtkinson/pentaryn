Expected: 0.35×11 + 0.65×5.5 = 7.43 to Marwen (32 HP → ~25 HP). All others take similar (Bazgar DEX +0: 0.45×11 + 0.55×5.5 = 7.97).

Attack 2: 5.8 expected damage. Total ≈ **11.6 avg damage to Bazgar** (49 HP → ~37 HP).

Comparison without Unstable Form: 0.35 × 10 × 2 = 7.0 expected. Unstable Form adds **+4.6 expected damage, a 66% output increase**.

*Marwen (init 14):* Fire-based attack on shrine-touched (fire vulnerability doubles damage).  
Scorching Ray (×3 beams, +7 to hit vs AC 16, needs 9+, 65% each): each beam avg 7 fire → 6.5×3 = 13.5 expected rays fire. Vulnerable: ×2 = 27 expected fire damage.  
Shrine-touched HP ~26 − 27 = −1 → **dead** (avg outcome).

**Key observation:** At L5, the shrine-touched reliably dies in Round 1 if the party focuses it first. Oath-Breaking Retaliation fires at most ONCE (the first hit that doesn't kill it outright). In the scenario, the two-hit death sequence (-28, -29) is scripted — but at the real table, Sabriel's smite + Marwen fire often resolve it in one or two actions with no second reaction opportunity.

The reaction mechanic is designed to punish focused fire, but the party can eliminate the threat before the mechanic cycles more than once. The self-destruction narrative (shrine-touched burning itself with each retaliation) has **zero mechanical impact** in a one-round kill — the creature dies from party damage before its own self-damage accumulates.

### New findings this fire

**FEEL-R275-01 — Death-blow reaction: runner fires prompt without incapacitation check**

The runner fires the `oath_breaking_retaliation` prompt on the killing blow (−29, reducing shrine-touched to 0 HP). Per PHB, when a creature is reduced to 0 HP it falls unconscious "immediately" and is incapacitated — preventing reactions. However, the runner surfaces the reaction prompt without checking whether the damage drop triggers incapacitation.

This is **by design in the scenario** ("Oath-Breaking Retaliation fires on death blow" is in the scenario title), but the stat block text doesn't include a ruling note. Two possible interpretations:
- **Design intent (recommended):** The shrine's power is the trigger, not the creature's own will. The reaction fires before the creature fully processes its own death — a dying burst. This needs a stat block note: "This reaction fires even if the damage reduces the shrine-touched to 0 HP (the shrine's power persists one final instant)."
- **PHB strict:** Incapacitation prevents the reaction. The scenario title is aspirational — the ruling needs to go the other way.

**This is a new item. Not in prior open items.** Joe must decide. Log as FLAG-R275-01.

**FEEL-R275-02 — Self-destruction curve is narratively compelling but mechanically irrelevant at L5**

The Oath-Breaking Retaliation causes the shrine-touched to take 1d4 psychic self-damage after each retaliation, regardless of whether the attack hits. Over a full 3-round combat where the reaction fires every round:
- Total self-damage: 3 × avg 2.5 = **7.5 HP** (16.7% of the 45-HP pool).
- But at L5, the shrine-touched dies in Round 1–2 from party focus fire. Reaction fires at most once or twice before the creature is dead. Total self-damage from retaliation in a typical fight: avg **2.5–5 HP** (5.6–11% of HP pool).

The self-destruction flavor ("the shrine consumes it from within") reads dramatically but is invisible in practice — the creature is always killed by party damage before the self-immolation accumulates. If the self-damage is meant to create a "double edge" risk for the reaction, consider scaling it up (1d8 self-damage, or cumulative 1d4 per use this turn), or tying it to incoming damage ("takes self-damage equal to half the triggering damage, rounded down") to make it sting when the party lands heavy hits.
