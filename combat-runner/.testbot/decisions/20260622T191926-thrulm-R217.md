### NEW — Triple-Move Analysis (FEEL-R217-02)

**Finding:** A thrall can move up to 90 ft in a single initiative round via three layered movement sources:

1. **Base movement (thrall's turn):** 30 ft (Speed 30 ft)
2. **Compelled Movement (BA, thrall's turn):** +30 ft, directed by beholder
3. **Compel Thrall (Beholder BA, beholder's turn):** Triggers an *immediate* 30 ft move on the thrall (before the thrall's own turn)

The beholder's Compel Thrall fires on the beholder's turn, moving the thrall 30 ft toward a target. Then when the thrall's own turn arrives, it can move another 30 ft (base) and use Compelled Movement BA for another 30 ft.

**Example use-case:** Beholder uses Compel Thrall to push Thrall #2 30 ft into melee with Sabriel (beholder's turn). On Thrall #2's own turn: base move 30 ft to flank Sabriel + Compelled Movement BA 30 ft to position behind cover. Net: thrall travels 90 ft and ends up flanking a PC with cover — a relay maneuver not documented in the tactics section.

**Impact:** Against the Compass Edge (who might try to kite or use spacing), the thrall triple-move makes it effectively impossible to outrun a thrall once the beholder sets it in motion. Bazgar (no mobile speed advantage) cannot outpace a thrall moving 90 ft in one round (vs his base 30 ft + possible dash for 60 ft). This creates an encirclement threat the tactics section doesn't discuss.

**Is this intended?** The Compelled Movement BA and the Compel Thrall mechanic clearly have different fictional framings (the thrall's own body-movement vs the beholder's command) but no rules text prevents both from triggering in the same round. Likely not unintended — the design clearly builds in multiple movement sources for the thrall. But the emergent 90-ft reach is undocumented and should be noted for the DM.

#### FEEL-R217-01 (MINOR — mechanical flavor gap)

**Shrine-Touched `oath_breaking_retaliation` self-damage is halved by the creature's own psychic resistance.**

**Location:** `shrine-touched-derro.md` — Oath-Breaking Retaliation reaction; combined with Damage Resistances (necrotic, psychic).

**Text:** "Regardless of whether the attack hits, the derro takes 2 (1d4) psychic damage as the shrine's power consumes it from within."

**Problem:** The shrine-touched has **resistance to psychic damage** (explicitly listed under Damage Resistances). This means the self-inflicted 1d4 psychic damage from each Oath-Breaking Retaliation use is halved (round down) to 1 effective HP per use. Over the shrine-touched's 45 HP, it would need 45 reaction uses before dying from self-damage — an impossible number in any combat.

The stated flavor ("the shrine's power consumes it from within") implies meaningful self-attrition. Mechanically, 1 HP per use is negligible noise, not attrition. The self-destructive element that makes the shrine-touched feel dangerous-to-itself is entirely suppressed by its own resistances.

**Not auto-fixable** (would require either removing the resistance, changing the damage type, or increasing the self-damage — all design calls). Log only.

**Options for Joe:**
- Change self-damage type to radiant or fire (shrine-touched is VULNERABLE to both; 1d4 fire/radiant self-damage would feel meaningful — up to 4 HP before resistance halving, then doubled = 4 avg, notable).
- Keep as-is and accept the flavor is purely cinematic.
- Remove the self-damage clause entirely (cleaner stat block, no flavor loss since it was invisible mechanically anyway).

#### FEEL-R217-02 (MEDIUM — undocumented triple-move relay)

**Thrall can travel 90 ft in a single round via stacked movement sources; encirclement tactic undocumented.**

**Location:** `beholder-thrulm.md` Compel Thrall (BA) + `thrall-derro.md` base speed + Compelled Movement (BA).

**Sequence:**
1. **Beholder's turn:** Compel Thrall (BA) → thrall moves 30 ft immediately toward beholder's designated target (DC 16 CHA save, thrall CHA -2 → near-certain obedience).
2. **Thrall's own turn:** Base move 30 ft + Compelled Movement (BA) 30 ft → thrall moves another 60 ft in beholder-designated direction.
3. **Net:** Thrall moves up to 90 ft from its starting position in a single round.

**Tactical implication:** At 90 ft/round, a thrall easily catches any party member using Dash (60 ft), making encirclement a genuine threat even when the party tries to spread out. Bazgar can Dash for 60 ft; the thrall can still close from 30 ft away and engage. Against Marwen (no Dash, Speed 30 ft), a thrall starting 60 ft away can close fully and attack in the same round.

**Not documented** in thrall tactics ("Flees if the beholder commands it" and "attacks targets the beholder indicates" — no mention of multi-source movement or relay speed). Consider adding to `_overview.md` terrain notes or thrall tactics section.

**Design-call** (not a bug — the mechanics stack intentionally, the documentation is just thin).
