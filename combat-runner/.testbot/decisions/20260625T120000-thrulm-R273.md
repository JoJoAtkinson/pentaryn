### Compelled Movement — key mechanical observations

**1. Two-tab workflow friction (NEW FINDING — FEEL-THRALL-R273-01):**

Compel Thrall is a BEHOLDER bonus action. The beholder's DB has `compel_thrall` with verbs ["compel", "command thrall", "direct", "send", "push"]. The THRALL's DB has `compelled_movement` with verbs ["move", "advance", "compelled", "walk"].

A DM running this live must:
1. On beholder tab: type "compel" → narrates the command and designates direction.
2. Switch to thrall tab: type "compelled" → executes the movement.

Two tabs, two verbs. The thrall verb list does NOT include "compel" (without the "d"), so a DM who types "compel" on the thrall tab gets no match → LLM fallback. Minor friction but real in live play.

**Recommendation (DB fix, MCP session):** Add "compel" to thrall `compelled_movement.verbs`. Low risk, unambiguous.

**2. Shrine-Drift vs. Compel Thrall — bonus action trade-off (NEW FINDING — CS-OMIT-R273-01):**

The beholder has two bonus actions competing for one slot per turn:
- **Shrine-Drift:** Move 30 ft. Essential for positioning into Drain Divinity range (30 ft).
- **Compel Thrall (1/Turn):** Direct one charmed creature 30 ft. Tactical value scales with number of thralls.

In R1 simulated here, the beholder chose Shrine-Drift over Compel Thrall because repositioning for Multiattack range took priority. In R2, with position secured, Compel Thrall became available. This trade-off recurs every time the beholder needs to reposition.

The round-by-round checklist says nothing about this. DMs who don't track BA usage might use both in the same turn accidentally.

**Recommendation:** Add to checklist: "Beholder BA: only ONE of (Shrine-Drift / Compel Thrall) per turn."

**3. Thrall tactical role confirmed — positional only:**

The Compel Thrall maneuver redirected Thrall 1 to Sabriel and triggered an attack (0.3 expected dmg). In exchange, the beholder did NOT reposition on R2 — it already had good position from R1. Net value: negligible damage, some harassment of Sabriel. The thrall's real contribution is forcing Sabriel to choose between attacking and moving (she can't Disengage unless she uses her action, so attacking the thrall is a waste of a level-5 divine martial's action). Confirmed: thralls are HP sinks and action-economy harassers, not damage dealers.
