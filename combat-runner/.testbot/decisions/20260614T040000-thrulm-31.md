### Dispatch verification

Verb: "attack" on thrall-derro tab.

Verb `"attack"` appears in `thrall-derro / multiattack` verb list: `["attack", "hit", "swing", "stab", "dagger", "melee", "multiattack"]`. Match: `multiattack` (first NPC-specific match). Type `single_attack`. Fires one attack.

Actual `attacks` array from actions.jsonl:
```json
{"name": "Dagger (Desperate)", "to_hit_bonus": 3, "damage": "1d4", "damage_modifier": 2, "damage_type": "piercing"}
```

This fires correctly without crash. **The dispatch works.** However, the dispatched stats are the Dagger, not the Hand Axe — see BUG-A31-08.

Also confirmed: separate `dagger` action (same `attacks` array) is a functional duplicate. Typing "dagger" routes to the standalone `dagger` DB row; typing "attack", "hit", "swing", "stab" routes to `multiattack`. Both produce identical output. The distinction is cosmetically visible in the DB key names only.

### Feel simulation: thrall combat role in a typical Beholder-led fight

**Party:** Bazgar (HP 49, AC 18), Marwen (HP 32, AC 15), Sabriel (HP 44, AC 19).

**Setup:** Beholder directs a thrall (HP 22, AC 14) at Marwen. Beholder bonus action: Compel Thrall (redirect thrall into Marwen's zone, no attack yet). Next round: thrall acts.

**Thrall attack (Dagger Desperate, per DB): +3 to hit vs Marwen AC 15**
- Roll needed on d20: 12. Hit range: 12–20 (excl. nat 1 vs AC 15: need 12+ regardless). P(hit) ≈ 45%.
- E[dmg] on hit: 1d4+2 = avg 4.5. E[dmg per turn] = 0.45 × 4.5 = **2.0 HP/turn** vs Marwen.

If the DB used Hand Axe (Weak, +1 to hit, 1d4) per the .md intent:
- Roll needed: 14. P(hit) ≈ 35%. E[dmg] = 0.35 × 2.5 = **0.875 HP/turn**.

The DB version deals ~2.3× the .md-intended damage. Against a 32 HP wizard, the DB thrall takes 16 turns per kill (vs 37 turns with hand axe). Still negligible pressure, but wrong.

**Thrall as positioning piece only:**

Even with the inflated Dagger stats, 2 HP/turn from a single thrall is inconsequential. The thrall's real function: (1) occupies space (blocking PC movement paths), (2) forces the party to spend actions killing it before advancing, (3) absorbs Opportunity Attacks if the beholder uses Compel Thrall to pull it through PC threat zones.

The Manifest Thralls lair action gives 1 THP + one reaction attack per thrall. Against the Compass Edge, any PC's attack kills a thrall outright (22 HP, no B/P/S resistance). The 1 THP is noise; the reaction attack vs Marwen has expected damage of 1.125 HP (dagger, AC 15, 45%×4.5... wait, OA uses the attack available: same dagger stats, 45%×4.5 = 2.0 expected). Still low.

**Thrall pool per combat:** The beholder can have up to 6 thrall derro active (via Clay-Shaping, excluded from combat per dead-weight note). In a typical fight, 2–3 thralls arrive from the encounter setup. All 3 are cleared by R2–R3 by the Compass Edge (Extra Attack + cantrip). Thrall combat contribution across a 3-round fight: 3 thralls × 2 rounds × 2.0 HP/turn (Marwen-targeted) = ~12 expected damage total — comparable to one Tentacle Lash hit.

**LA economy check (thrall fight context):**

In a single-thrall scenario (scenario prelude), the beholder has no allied NPCs competing for action priority. Its turn structure:
- Action: Multiattack (two Tentacle Lash + Maw, target Marwen or Bazgar per tactics priority)
- Bonus Action: Shrine-Drift (reposition toward altar, maintain Void-Feeding) OR Compel Thrall (redirect the single thrall)
- Legendary Actions: hold all 3 for DD post-Sabriel (R1); Void Ray or Tentacle in R2+

The BA choice between **Shrine-Drift** and **Compel Thrall** is always resolved in favor of Shrine-Drift unless: (a) the thrall is already within 5 ft of Marwen, and (b) the beholder is already in its optimal Void-Feeding position. In practice, FEEL-F31-01: Compel Thrall almost never fires in normal encounter flow.

**Dominant LA:** Drain Divinity (post-Sabriel R1) remains dominant. Void Ray is secondary. Move LA remains least efficient (Shrine-Drift handles repositioning better as a BA). No change from prior fires.
