**No crash.** `compel` verb routes correctly to `compel_thrall`. Utility action dispatches narration ("Pressure folds onto a thrall — its head jerks once, and the body obeys.") and effect text ("[ASK PLAYER: DC 16 Cha save] — on a fail, the target moves up to 30 ft toward the beholder or a target the beholder designates."). No secondary assertion needed for this smoke test.

**New sub-finding (FEEL-F50-01):** The `[ASK PLAYER]` tag in the CT effect is typically used for PC saves. Against a Thrall Derro (the standard CT target), the thrall's own stat block says "Saving Throws: None; dominated mind has no saves." A thrall's CHA modifier is −2: even if it attempted the save, it would succeed only on a natural 18+ (15% chance). The DC 16 CHA save framing is flavor-inconsistent with the thrall's "no saves" statement and misleads the DM into thinking this is a contested mechanic — in practice it auto-fails. The output `[ASK PLAYER]` text implies the table needs to roll, which burns table time on a guaranteed result. *Recommendation: simplify CT effect text to "the target moves up to 30 ft — no save (thrall's dominated will cannot resist)." Flag for Joe; CT targeting a non-thrall creature (charmed PC via some future mechanic) would actually need the save.*

**FEEL-F31-01 (confirmed, fire #50 — 19th fire reconfirmed):** Compel Thrall fires at most once per encounter and produces low-single-digit expected HP. Against the Compass Edge, CT's maximum contribution is bounded by Thrall DPR vs the highest-AC target Thrall-2 is directed at: ~2 HP/attack expected vs Marwen (AC 15) with BUG-A31-08 active Dagger stats, or ~0.9 HP/attack with corrected Hand Axe stats. CT provides negligible pressure.

**FEEL-F50-01 (NEW):** CT's `[ASK PLAYER: DC 16 Cha save]` notation implies a contested mechanic, but against a Thrall Derro (CHA −2, "no saves"), the outcome is guaranteed — the thrall cannot meaningfully resist its own master. The save framing burns table time on a trivially predetermined result and contradicts the thrall's own stat block. Compare to "Beholder-Thralled: it obeys the beholder's commands wordlessly" — CT's save reads as though the thrall has meaningful agency to resist. *Recommendation: remove the CHA save from the CT effect text (replace "[ASK PLAYER: DC 16 Cha save] — on a fail" with simply "the target") since the thrall's dominated state already means it cannot resist. If CT is ever used against a non-thrall charmed creature, restore the save at that time. Flag for Joe.*
