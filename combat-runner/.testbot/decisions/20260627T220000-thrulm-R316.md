## Phase B — Simulation: `disintegration-threat`

> Scenario: "Assume recharge rolls 5-6 on turn 1. Beholder targets Marwen (32 HP, AC 15). Roll 10d8 force +9 vs AC 15. If hit: average ~46 damage (10d8+1 in chamber). Does it one-shot? Is permanent-death risk acceptable?"

### Premise Correction

**The rotation.yml premise "assume recharge rolls 5-6 on turn 1" is mechanically incorrect.** In 5e, a recharge ability starts AVAILABLE at combat start — the recharge roll only applies after the ability has been expended. Disintegration Ray (Recharge 5–6) is available on round 1 with no roll required. The correct framing is: "Disintegration Ray available round 1 (no roll needed); what is the threat ceiling?"

This should be updated in rotation.yml: see FEEL-DT-PREM-01 below.

### Attack Analysis

**Target: Marwen (32 HP, AC 15)**

| Factor | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Attack bonus (in chamber) | +9 | +8 base + 1 Void-Feeding |
| Marwen AC | 15 | Wizard, no shield |
| Hit roll needed | 6+ on d20 | P(hit) = **75%** |
| Damage in chamber | 10d8+1 | +1 from Void-Feeding |
| Average damage | **46** | 10×4.5 + 1 |
| Marwen max HP | 32 | Level 5 Wizard |
| One-shot threshold | 10d8+1 ≥ 32 → 10d8 ≥ 31 | — |

**P(one-shot \| hit):** Distribution: mean=45, σ≈7.25. z = (31–45)/7.25 ≈ −1.93 → P(10d8 ≤ 30) ≈ 2.7%. So **P(one-shot \| hit) ≈ 97.3%**.

**Net P(Marwen permanently dead on beholder's first action):**
= P(beholder acts before Marwen) × P(hit) × P(one-shot\|hit)
≈ 0.58 × 0.75 × 0.973
≈ **42%**

Even if Marwen acts first (42% chance), the beholder uses Disintegration Ray on its first turn anyway.

**Minimum damage check:** 10d8+1 minimum = 11. This does NOT one-shot Marwen (32 HP > 11). But the minimum requires all 10 d8s to roll 1 — P(10d8=10) = 1/8^10 ≈ negligible. The effective floor that avoids one-shot is roughly P = 2.7%.

### Comparison: Other PC Targets

| Target | HP | AC | P(hit) | P(one-shot\|hit) | P(kill) |
|--------|----|----|--------|------------------|---------|
| Marwen | 32 | 15 | 75% | 97.3% | **73%** |
| Sabriel | 44 | 19 | 55% | P(10d8≥43) ≈ 61% | **34%** |
| Bazgar | 49 | 18 | 60% | P(10d8≥48) ≈ 34% | **20%** |

Marwen is overwhelmingly the optimal target. After Marwen's death, **Sabriel (34%) is the better round-2+ target than Bazgar (20%)** — counterintuitive given Bazgar is the front-line fighter, but Sabriel's lower AC makes her more efficient to target with a spell attack.

### Recharge Economy (Rounds 2+)

After first use, Disintegration Ray recharges on 5-6 (33% per round). Over a 4-round encounter after round 1:
- Expected additional uses: 3 rounds × 33% = ~1 additional use
- Round 1: Marwen → P(kill) 73%
- Round 2-4: Sabriel → P(kill per use) 34% × 33% ≈ 11% per round, or 28% over 3 rounds

**Expected permanent deaths from Disintegration Ray over 4-round encounter:** ~1.3 (Marwen near-certain + ~30% chance of a second PC).

### Is the Threat Ceiling Acceptable?

**Yes, as designed** — this encounter is meant to kill the party. The beholder's threat ceiling is appropriate for CR 13 vs level 5.

**The design concern is not damage magnitude but permanence.** True Resurrection and Wish (the only spells that restore disintegrated creatures) are 9th-level spells — inaccessible until level 17. Every Disintegration Ray kill at this party level is a permanent character death with no recovery path. This is qualitatively different from "the party gets TPK'd and needs to make new characters" — it's "one specific character is permanently gone even if the party eventually gets raised."

Whether this is acceptable is a **design/session-zero question**, not a balance bug. See FEEL-DT-01 below.

### FEEL-DT-01 — Permanent death mechanic at level 5 in an intended-loss encounter

**Type:** Design / session-zero concern  
**Summary:** Disintegration Ray's "cannot be restored except by true resurrection or wish" rider creates an asymmetric consequence at level 5. The party is designed to lose this encounter. A TPK via HP damage allows Revivify/Raise Dead; a Disintegration Ray kill does not. The party may lose two PCs permanently even if they attempt to retreat.

**Probabilities this session:** P(Marwen permanently dead) ≈ 73% if the beholder acts before her, ~98% once it acts regardless of order. P(second permanent death in 4 rounds) ≈ 28–34%.

**Recommendation for Joe (design call):** One of:
1. Add to tactics text: "Beholder never uses Disintegration Ray on round 1 — it spends the first turn assessing. Narrate: 'The void within it swells — then stills, patient.'" This gives Marwen one full turn before facing permanent death.
2. Make the permanent-death rider a soft condition: "If reduced to 0 HP by this attack, the target cannot be restored by Revivify or Raise Dead; True Resurrection or Wish required — or a quest to recover the ash from the void itself." (adds narrative hook without permanence)
3. State explicitly in session-zero for this encounter: "this encounter includes permanent death risk; warn players before running it."

Not a mechanical bug. Joe decides.

### FEEL-DT-02 — Counterintuitive optimal Disintegration Ray targeting

**Type:** DM guidance gap  
**Summary:** After Marwen's death, the optimal Disintegration Ray target is Sabriel (34% kill probability) not Bazgar (20%), despite Bazgar being the front-line fighter the beholder would naturally "fear" more. The beholder's tactics text says "Prioritizes disintegration of spellcasters" (consistent with Sabriel as divine martial over Bazgar as fighter), but the AC/HP analysis is not obvious at the table.

**Recommendation:** Add a tactics note: "If both Sabriel and Bazgar are targets for Disintegration Ray after Marwen falls, prefer Sabriel (lower AC, higher one-shot probability) unless Sabriel is below 20 HP (Bazgar becomes equal or better target when Sabriel's HP advantage disappears)."
