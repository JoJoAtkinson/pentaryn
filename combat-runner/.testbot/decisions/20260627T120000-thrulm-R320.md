## Phase B — Feel Issues

### FEEL-R16-01 — Marwen one-round kill before acting

**Flag type:** Unfair-rather-than-lethal  
**Detail:** Marwen (32 HP, AC 15) has a ~56% chance of reaching 0 HP before her own turn (init 15 < beholder's 18). On average hits (75% per attack), she takes 50+ damage. Even at minimum, two hits = 36 damage, exceeding her HP.

The encounter is designed as a loss, but Marwen has **zero agency in round 1**. She contributes nothing before going down. The question isn't whether she survives — she isn't meant to — but whether she gets to meaningfully participate before dying.

**Compare:** Bazgar (HP 49, AC 18) and Sabriel (HP 44, AC 19) both survive round 1 and can act. Only the wizard is consistently one-rounded on the beholder's opening action.

**Options for Joe:**
1. Accept it — Marwen going down round 1 is the intended horror of a CR 13 encounter.
2. Give Marwen a Shield spell trigger reminder in her combat card (reaction +5 AC, bringing her to AC 20 and making the beholder need 11+ to hit = 50%). This cuts the round-1 kill probability significantly.
3. Start the encounter with the party having cover from the shrine columns (forcing the beholder to move before multiattacking). This buys Marwen one round.

### FEEL-R16-02 — Drain Divinity economic collapse

**Confirmed known issue** (flagged in prior fires).  
From live simulation: Spending all 3 LA on Drain Divinity vs Sabriel yields ~3.9 expected THP (DC 16 CHA save, ~65% fail, 2×3rd-slot THP = 6). Spending 3 LA on Tentacle (1) + Void Ray (2) yields ~27.3 expected damage.

The beholder has no rational economic reason to use Drain Divinity unless the table explicitly narrates the beholder as prioritizing slot-stripping over damage. But in a "meant to lose" encounter, spending all 3 LA on ~4 THP while the party is still healthy is tactically suicidal for the beholder. The result: either the DM never picks Drain Divinity (making it dead code) or picks it for narrative reasons and the beholder underperforms.

**Suggested fixes (design call for Joe):**
1. Reduce Drain Divinity LA cost to 2 — makes it compete with Void Ray directly.
2. Add a fear/psychic rider on failed save ("target is also frightened for 1 min") to create counterplay tension beyond slot loss.
3. Make it scale: beholder gains THP equal to 3× the spell level lost, not 2×, so a 3rd-level slot gives 9 THP (total expected ~5.85 at 65% fail).

### FEEL-R16-03 — Tentacle+Void Ray is always dominant LA combo

**Flag type:** One-note strategy  
Over a full round (3 LA budget): Tentacle (1) + Void Ray (2) consistently outperforms any other LA combination in terms of raw expected damage. Move (1) is situationally useful for repositioning, but Drain Divinity (3) never competes on damage. The beholder's LA decision tree is effectively: always Tentacle + Void Ray unless the DM specifically wants to use Drain Divinity for narrative effect.

This reduces tactical variety. The beholder becomes predictable after round 1: Void Ray on Bazgar (DEX -1 likely), Tentacle on whoever isn't grappled.

### FEEL-R16-04 — Disintegration Ray permanent-death risk on a "meant to lose" fight

**Confirms existing FEEL-003.**  
Marwen stabilized at 1-4 HP after round 1 heal. Round 2: beholder recharges Disintegration Ray on 5-6 (33%). If it fires vs Marwen at 4 HP: +9 to hit vs AC 15 = 75% hit, avg 45+1 = 46 force damage. Marwen instant-downed again; 46 > 32 (max HP) = **instant death** (takes damage > max HP while at 0).

Actually: Marwen has 32 max HP. She's at 4 HP when the ray fires. 46 damage brings her to -42 HP. She needs to make a death save for each excess damage die beyond her max HP... actually in 5e: if damage exceeds max HP while at 0, it's instant death only if excess from a SINGLE hit exceeds max HP. 46 damage while at 4 HP = excess from 0 HP is 46-4 = 42. 42 > 32 (max HP). **Instant death.**

This means if Disintegration Ray fires vs Marwen at any HP ≤ 45, she's either dropped to 0 or — if already at 0 — permanently dead. In a "meant to lose" encounter, permanent PC death from a recharge ability on round 2 may be unintentional (disintegration as permanent death was designed for "boss-encounter capstone" situations, not a mandatory first-act loss).

**Joe's decision needed:** Is permanent disintegration of Marwen acceptable in this encounter? If not, options:
- Exclude Marwen from Disintegration Ray targeting (beholder hunts divine magic, not arcane)
- Change disintegration threshold to "only if dropped below -max HP" (softer kill)
- Add flavor text that the beholder WANTS prisoners (thralls), not ash
