**Beholder (init 20, R2 start):** Recharge roll for VS: d6 = **6** → VS recharged!

Decision: fire VS now? All 3 PCs are within 30 ft. Marwen is grappled (can't move) at 1 HP.
Bazgar just escaped grapple but is 15 ft from shrine. Sabriel is in melee with Shrine-Touched.

VS fires: 33 (6d10) psychic, DC 16 Wis save. Creatures within 10 ft of shrine: Marwen (grappled in
place, likely 15–20 ft from shrine; Bazgar uncertain; Sabriel at shrine perimeter).

Assume: Marwen grappled IN the shrine zone (beholder hover above altar → tentacle reach 10 ft → Marwen
dragged to within 10 ft of altar). → Marwen disadvantage on save.
Bazgar escaped grapple, moved 5 ft away — at ~15 ft from shrine. No disadvantage.
Sabriel at 20–25 ft from shrine. No disadvantage.

**Marwen (WIS +1, disadvantage from shrine proximity):**
Roll 1: 9 + 1 = 10 < 16 → FAIL (would fail anyway; disadvantage irrelevant at this roll).
Full damage: 33 psychic. Marwen 1 → **-32 HP → DOWNED** (death save territory, already at 0
once; now at -32 which exceeds max HP 32 → DEAD outright if threshold counts from 0).
Actually: PC dies outright only if damage drops them to 0 AND the remaining damage exceeds their max HP.
Marwen was at 1 HP; VS deals 33. Total damage = 33. Excess over 0 = 32. Max HP = 32. 32 ≥ 32 → **INSTANT DEATH**.

**Bazgar (WIS +1):**
Roll: 5 + 1 = 6 < 16 → FAIL.
Full damage: 33 psychic. Bazgar 15 → **-18 HP → DOWNED** (excess 18 < max HP 49 → death saves, not dead).

**Sabriel (WIS +3):**
Roll: 11 + 3 = 14 < 16 → FAIL.
Full damage: 33 psychic. Sabriel 44 → **11 HP**.

**Frightened rider (DB, not in .md):** Marwen is dead. Bazgar downed. Sabriel frightened 1 min (DC 16
Wis save ends). Sabriel disadvantage on attacks vs beholder in line of sight — which it always is in

### Findings

**FEEL-R107-01 (NEW): VS R2 produces instant-death outcome for Marwen under any realistic R1 
multiattack scenario.** The sequence is: R1 beholder multiattack → Marwen at ≤17 HP (two-tentacle 
hit pattern) → VS on R2 → 33 avg psychic kills Marwen outright (excess damage ≥ max HP 32 when she 
enters the turn at 1 HP from Lay on Hands, or simply dies if not healed). This is *not* a single-roll 
fluke; it is the expected outcome of the R1→R2 multiattack-into-VS combo. Marwen dies before acting 
meaningfully in R2. The party never had a winning line.

**Design tension:** The encounter IS supposed to be unwinnable. But "Marwen dies outright in R2 from
combined multiattack+VS, potentially before acting on R2" crosses from *lethal* into *unfair* because:
1. Marwen can't disengage from the grapple (tentacle grapple + maw R1 → her R1 action was escape 
   attempt, which failed).
2. VS cannot be countered or avoided.
3. No saving throw on the prior Maw attack (attack roll, no save).
4. R2 VS lands before Marwen's turn (beholder init 20 → Marwen init 12).

The party gets one meaningful round for Sabriel and (if they survive) Bazgar before VS resolves the 
fight. Recommend: flag for Joe whether "VS kills grappled low-HP PC before their R2 turn" is intended.

**FEEL-R107-02 (NEW): Shrine proximity disadvantage (10 ft, VS) is untracked and will disappear at 
most tables.** The VS text specifies: "Creatures within 10 feet of the shrine have disadvantage on this 
save." To apply this, the DM must:
1. Know where the shrine/altar is on the map (grid square, not just "the room").
2. Track which PCs are within 10 ft of that specific location each time VS fires.
3. Know the rule exists (not in cheat sheet save table, not bolded or called out in stat block).

In the Marwen-grappled scenario (beholder hovering above altar, Marwen pulled by tentacle), the DM
must adjudicate whether "within 10 ft of the altar" means within 10 ft of the altar's edge or center.
The 10-ft shrine zone is a meaningful tactical layer (changes Marwen's VS save from P(fail)=70% to
P(fail-both-rolls)=49%, a significant reduction in lethalness for grappled PCs) but it will silently
disappear if the DM doesn't flag it. **Recommend adding "(shrine proximity: disadvantage if within 10 ft 
of altar — see map)" to cheat sheet save DC row for VS. Log only.**

**FEEL-R107-03 (CONFIRM): VS Frightened rider (DB, not in .md) critically matters for Sabriel as last 
PC.** Sabriel survives VS at 11 HP. She is Frightened (per DB entry). Frightened means disadvantage on
attack rolls vs beholder while beholder is in line of sight — which it always is hovering in the chamber.
She has 1 action left (Lay on Hands on Bazgar to stop death saves?). With disadvantage on attacks, her
melee effectiveness is halved, and the beholder will spend 3 LA on Drain Divinity post-Sabriel-turn to
strip a slot before R3. This rider is critical to the endgame feel and is invisible to DMs using only the 
stat block. Already documented BUG; confirming it matters most in exactly this VS-survivor scenario.

### LA economy observation — DD dominance pattern confirmed

After VS (beholder main action R2), beholder refreshes 3 LA for R2:
- After Bazgar's turn (downed, no action): no LA spent.
- After Sabriel's turn: **Drain Divinity** (3 LA), DC 16 CHA vs Sabriel. Sabriel CHA assume +2 → 
  needs 14+, P(save)=35%, P(fail)=65%.
  - Roll: 8 + 2 = 10 < 16 → FAIL. Sabriel loses highest slot (L3 smite). Beholder gains 6 THP.
- Move LA (1): never considered. Tentacle LA (1): no target worth spending on. Void Ray (2): overkill
  on 11-HP Sabriel when R3 multiattack handles it.

DD consumed ALL 3 LA. Move and Void Ray never fired. **This is the 4th consecutive run where DD 
consumes the entire LA budget while Sabriel is standing.** The degenerate-every-round pattern flagged
in the briefing is confirmed again. When Sabriel has L1+ slots, DD is strictly dominant — Move and
Void Ray have no competing logic.
