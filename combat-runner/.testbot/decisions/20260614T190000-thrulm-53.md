**DEATH SAVE QUESTION — BUG-A53-01 candidate:**
Does Void Ray damage count as a death save failure for Bazgar at 1 HP? PHB: any damage to a creature at 0 HP = 1 failed death save; damage ≥ creature's max HP = instant death. Bazgar at 1 HP takes 22 force (fail) or 11 force (success) — either way, drops to 0 HP from 1 HP. The damage is dealt *to a creature at 1 HP*, not at 0 HP, so it does NOT count as a death save failure — it simply reduces Bazgar to 0 HP and knocks him unconscious again. His death save count resets. This is correct RAW. *(Not a bug — noting explicitly because the fire #52 sim applied VE damage to a 0-HP PC as death save failures; VR targeting a 1-HP PC is a different case.)*

### New findings

**FEEL-F53-01 (NEW): DD effect text missing LoH-override warning — silent DM trap**

When the `drain_divinity` watch trigger fires post-Sabriel, the runner outputs the DD narration and `[ASK PLAYER: DC 16 Cha save]`. The DB `effect` text says nothing about valid-target qualifications. A DM unfamiliar with the LoH-override rule will attempt to apply DD against a LoH action — which is mechanically invalid (LoH is not a spell slot, Channel Divinity charge, or "clerical power" in the DD sense).

There's a secondary ambiguity: the DD stat block says "or loses one use of a divine ability if it has no spells." Sabriel at Paladin 5 has Channel Divinity (1/rest). If Sabriel used LoH (not a slot), she still has CD remaining. DD could legally strip a CD charge — but the runner fires with "Sabriel used LoH → DD watch" framing, implying the LoH is the valid DD target. It isn't. CD is the valid fallback target.

**Recommendation:** Add to DD `effect` text:
> `⚠️ Valid DD targets: spell slots, Channel Divinity charges, or named divine abilities (Turn Undead, etc.). Lay on Hands is NOT a spell slot and NOT a divine ability for DD purposes — it is a healing pool. If Sabriel's action was LoH only, the DD watch fires but the strip target is incorrect — use Void Ray instead unless Sabriel has a CD charge remaining (DD can strip that).`

Requires `combat_action_upsert`. Log only — design/wording call for Joe.

**FEEL-F53-02 (NEW): Revive-then-VR loop — LoH revive negated at any HP ≤ 22**

The 2-LA Void Ray (DC 16 DEX, 22 on fail / 11 on save) trivially re-downs Bazgar at any revive HP ≤ 11 (both fail and save exceed ≤11 HP). At ≤22 HP revive, a failed save (80% probability for Bazgar DEX −1) re-downs.

| LoH spent | Bazgar HP post-revive | VR on fail (22) | VR on save (11) | Survives VR? |
|-----------|-----------------------|-----------------|-----------------|--------------|
| 1 HP | 1 | 0 HP (down) | 0 HP (down) | Never |
| 10 HP | 10 | 0 HP (down) | 0 HP (down) | Never |
| 11 HP | 11 | 0 HP (down) | 0 HP (down) | Never |
| 12 HP | 12 | 0 HP (down) | 1 HP (**survives on save**) | 20% |
| 22 HP | 22 | 0 HP (down) | 11 HP (**survives on save**) | 20% |
| 23 HP | 23 | 1 HP (**survives on fail**) | 12 HP | **~100%** |

**To guarantee Bazgar survives VR:** Sabriel must spend 23+ HP from a 25-HP LoH pool — effectively exhausting it in one action. Spending 23 HP = LoH pool nearly empty; Bazgar survives VR but Sabriel has only 2 HP remaining in her pool.

**Tactical implication:** Reviving a downed PC in range of the beholder's LA budget is strategically wasteful unless Sabriel spends 23+ HP of LoH. Any cheaper revive is 100% countered by Void Ray. This is mechanically correct for a CR 13 boss encounter the party is **meant to lose**, but it means the party's primary survival action (LoH revival) is rendered near-useless unless:
1. The beholder has already spent 2 LA this round (leaving <2 LA for VR).
2. Sabriel can move Bazgar out of VR range (120 ft — essentially impossible at table scale).
3. The beholder is below 30 HP and retreating (at which point it likely won't waste 2 LA on a 1-HP PC).

**Recommendation:** The tactics section for LoH override could note: "Void Ray on a low-HP revived PC is almost always the correct LA spend. Sabriel will be forced to commit 23+ HP of LoH to make the revive VR-proof." Log only — this is by design.

**BUG-A53-01 (NEW): DD effect text has no guard for exhausted-slot targets + CD ambiguity**

Per Phase B analysis: when Sabriel has no spell slots remaining (e.g., all smite slots spent) but still has Channel Divinity, DD can legally strip a CD charge — but the DB `effect` text says "[ASK PLAYER: DC 16 Cha save]" with no mention of what gets stripped. At the table:
- DM fires DD prompt.
- Player doesn't know if their slot, CD, or LoH pool is the strip target.
- DB text is silent.

This is a DM-clarity gap. The `drain_divinity` effect should specify the strip priority: `"Strip target priority: highest remaining spell slot first; if no slots remaining, strip one Channel Divinity use; if no CD uses remaining, strip one use of a named divine ability (Sacred Weapon, Turn Undead, etc.); if nothing stippable, DD fails with no effect."` This mirrors the stat block intent but makes it unambiguous for at-table resolution.

Requires `combat_action_upsert`. Log only — wording call for Joe. | Low | **Joe + MCP**
