### Initiative probability — beholder vs Marwen

The beholder (DEX +3) vs Marwen (DEX +2) duel:

P(beholder wins initiative including tiebreak by DEX) = sum of P(d20_B+3 > d20_M+2):
= P(d20_B ≥ d20_M) = Σ(k=1..20) P(d20_M=k) × P(d20_B≥k) = (1/400) × Σ(21-k, k=1..20) = 210/400 = **52.5%**

Ties (P = 20/400 = 5%): resolved by higher DEX → beholder wins all ties (DEX 16 vs 14).

Final: **P(beholder acts before Marwen) ≈ 57.5%** (52.5% strict + 5% tiebreak). Marwen goes first in roughly **42.5%** of rounds.

| Initiative outcome | Probability | Marwen's options before DR fires |
|-------------------|-------------|----------------------------------|
| Beholder first | 57.5% | Only Shield (reaction after being targeted) |
| Marwen first | 42.5% | Full action + bonus action before DR |

### Defensive options matrix

**Case A: Beholder goes first (57.5% of rounds)**

Marwen's only proactive option: Shield spell (reaction). Already analyzed in R194.
- Base hit rate: 65% (beholder +7 vs AC 15, needs d20 ≥ 8)
- With Shield: AC 20 (+5 AC), beholder now needs d20 ≥ 13 = 40% hit rate
- P(perm-death per DR cycle | no init advantage): 0.33 × 0.65 × 0.98 ≈ **21%** base, **13%** with Shield

**Case B: Marwen goes first (42.5% of rounds)**

Marwen has full action before DR fires. Relevant defensive options:

| Spell | Level | Effect vs DR | P(perm-death per cycle) | Notes |
|-------|-------|--------------|-------------------------|-------|
| Mirror Image | 2nd | 3 duplicates: 3/4 chance DR hits image (attacker rolls for target); if image hit → no perm-death | 0.33 × 0.65 × 0.25 × 0.98 ≈ **5.3%** | Lasts 1 min; affects ALL subsequent attacks |
| Blur | 2nd (conc) | Disadvantage on attacks vs Marwen | 0.33 × P(hit with disadv vs AC 15) × 0.98 | Disadv: P(hit) = 1-(1-0.65)² = 87.75%... wait, this is ADVANTAGE for attacker. Blur gives disadv to attacker. P(miss twice) = 0.35² = 12.25%. P(hit) = 87.75%. WORSE than base? No — disadv means attacker rolls twice, takes LOWER. P(hit w/disadv) = 0.65² = 42.25%. Perm-death: 0.33 × 0.4225 × 0.98 ≈ **13.7%** | Requires concentration; slightly worse than Mirror Image but protects across multiple rounds |
| False Life | 1st | +1d4+4 THP (~6.5 THP avg) | Perm-death requires reducing to 0 HP; extra buffer doesn't help (10d8 avg 45 >> 32+6 = 38 HP) | Doesn't help vs DR unless Marwen is at reduced HP |
| Shield | reaction | +5 AC when targeted | See Case A — only applies retroactively | Still valid if Marwen went first this round but will face DR on a later turn |

**Mirror Image is the highest-value defensive spell Marwen can cast proactively.** It reduces perm-death from 21% to 5.3% per DR cycle and persists for 1 minute (10 rounds), covering all subsequent DR cycles.

### Case B2: Sabriel goes first (also relevant in ~37% of rounds vs beholder +3)

If Sabriel wins initiative vs the beholder (P ≈ 37%), she can cast Shield of Faith (action, concentration, +2 AC to one target) on Marwen before DR fires:

- Marwen AC with Shield of Faith: 17
- Beholder +7 vs AC 17: needs d20 ≥ 10 = **55% hit rate**
- P(perm-death with SoF, no Shield reaction): 0.33 × 0.55 × 0.98 ≈ **17.8%**
- P(perm-death with SoF + Shield reaction): 0.33 × P(d20 ≥ 10 then ≥ 15 after Shield) × 0.98
  = 0.33 × 0.30 × 0.98 ≈ **9.7%**

Shield of Faith alone is weaker than Mirror Image but doesn't require Marwen to spend her action. SoF + Shield gets perm-death to ~10%.

### FINDING-R198-A: Mirror Image is the encounter-defining defensive spell for Marwen

In this simulation (Marwen wins initiative, ~42.5% probability), Mirror Image:
1. Absorbed a Disintegration Ray that would have permanently killed her
2. Left 2 duplicates for Round 2 (protecting against tentacle grapple chain)
3. Cost: one 2nd-level spell slot at the top of her action

The base perm-death rate without Mirror Image (21%) vs with Mirror Image in a Marwen-wins-init scenario (5.3%) is the largest single defensive improvement in the encounter — better than Shield (13%), SoF (17.8%), or any other option.

**This has never been documented in any prior fire's Phase B.** All prior sims assumed beholder went first or only modeled Shield reaction.

---

### FINDING-R198-B: DR recharge timing creates multi-round mirror image window

Disintegration Ray: Recharge 5-6 → ~33% chance of recharging each turn. Average time between DR fires ≈ 3 turns (geometric distribution).

With 3 Mirror Image duplicates lasting until destroyed, Mirror Image typically outlasts 1-2 DR cycles:
- P(all 3 images destroyed in 1 DR fire): image absorbs on 15/20 = 75%, so DR destroys 1 image 75% of the time (and hits Marwen 25%). Expected images remaining after 1 DR: 3 - 1×0.75 = 2.25 avg.
- After 3 DR fires: expected images remaining = 3 × (1-0.75) = 0.75 images. Mirror Image expires by ~3rd DR use on average.
- Since DR fires ~once per 3 turns, Mirror Image (10 rounds max) provides ~3-4 turns of protection covering 1-2 DR cycles.

**Practical result:** Mirror Image on Round 1 gives 3-4 turns of reduced perm-death risk. This covers the most critical phase (Rounds 1-3) when party resources are full and the encounter decides the outcome.

---

### FINDING-R198-C (mechanical): Mirror Image vs Counterspell — only one works

Counterspell blocks spells. Disintegration Ray is NOT a spell (it's a supernatural beholder ability). **Counterspell does not work against DR.** This is a trap for players expecting to counterspell the beholder's most lethal ability.

Mirror Image, Shield, and Blur DO work (they modify the attack's hit conditions or its target, which are mechanical, not spell-dependent). This distinction is worth noting in the encounter guide: "DR is not a spell — Counterspell fails. Shield, Mirror Image, and Blur are the valid counters."

This is new text for the encounter guide / cheat sheet. Not previously documented.
