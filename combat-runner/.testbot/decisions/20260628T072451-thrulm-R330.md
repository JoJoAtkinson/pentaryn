## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `void-scream-clustering`

**Rotation index 3 of 8** — simulating Void Scream area threat, save timing, and shrine-proximity disadvantage.

---

### Setup

**Note:** fire#7 (counter 4→5) ran counter-based scenario #4 (`orren-action-surge` from scenarios.yml) instead of rotation index 3 from `thrulm-rotation.json`. thrulm-rotation.json was left at next_index=3. This fire picks up the missed rotation slot.

**Scenario premise:** Round 2. Beholder hovers at 50 ft altitude. Marwen was grappled in round 1 by Tentacle Lash (avg 14 damage, 32→18 HP). Beholder's main action on round 1 was multiattack (Tentacle×2 + Maw). Void Scream recharged on initiative (recharge 6: 1/6 per round). Beholder uses Shrine-Drift (BA) to descend 30 ft → now at ~20 ft altitude. All three PCs are within 30 ft horizontal of the altar and within range.

**Party entering round 2:**
- Marwen: 18 HP, grappled (took tentacle lash round 1)
- Bazgar: 49 HP, no conditions
- Sabriel: 44 HP, standing at the altar (within 10 ft of shrine)

---

### Save simulations

**Assumed save modifiers:**
- Bazgar: WIS +1, no WIS save proficiency. Save total: +1. Needs 15+ to succeed vs DC 16. Success = 30%, fail = 70%.
- Sabriel: divine martial, WIS +3, assumed proficient in WIS saves. Save total: +6. Within 10 ft of shrine → **disadvantage**. Success rate normally 55% (needs 10+); at disadvantage: P(both ≥ 10) = 0.55² ≈ 30%. Fail = **70%**.
- Marwen: Wizard class (WIS save proficiency), WIS +1. Save total: +4. Needs 12+ to succeed. Success = 45%, fail = 55%.

**Per-target outcomes:**

| PC | Save total | Save bonus | Shrine disadv? | Fail rate | Avg damage (6d10=33 fail, 16 half) | Expected dmg |
|----|------------|------------|----------------|-----------|-------------------------------------|--------------|
| Bazgar | +1 | none | no | 70% | 0.70×33 + 0.30×16 | **27.9** |
| Sabriel | +6 | proficient | yes (≤10 ft) | 70% | 0.70×33 + 0.30×16 | **27.9** |
| Marwen | +4 | proficient | no | 55% | 0.55×33 + 0.45×16 | **25.4** |

**FIX-FC48-A check:** If Marwen went unconscious in round 1 (tentacle 14 dmg + maw avg 22 dmg = 36 total, 32 HP → 0 HP), she is excluded from VS per the "can hear it" clause. In that case only Bazgar and Sabriel are in range → combined expected damage 55.8.

In the 25% of timelines where Marwen survived round 1 (she dodged one attack or the maw missed), all 3 are hit → combined expected 81.2.

---

### Key findings

**FEEL-R330-01 — VS + Frightened creates total melee action starvation; synergy is mechanically airtight.**

On a VS round where Bazgar and Sabriel both fail their saves (70% × 70% = 49% probability for that specific outcome), both are:
- Frightened: **disadvantage on attack rolls while the beholder is in line of sight; cannot willingly move closer**
- Damaged: Bazgar ~49→21 HP, Sabriel ~44→16 HP

The cannot-move-closer clause is the key. Both melee-focused PCs cannot close with the beholder. Bazgar's only ranged options are thrown weapons or hand crossbow (suboptimal). Sabriel's divine smites require melee. Once frightened, neither can contribute their primary offense.

Compounding factor: the next round, the beholder re-ascends to 40+ ft (Shrine-Drift BA), restoring its altitude advantage. The frightened condition lasts 1 minute (DC 16 WIS save at end of each creature's turn to end). With the same modifiers as above:
- Bazgar: needs 15+ to break it → 30% chance per turn
- Sabriel: with proficiency but no disadvantage (not near shrine mid-combat): +6 save, 55% chance per turn

Expected rounds of frightened for Bazgar: 3.3 rounds (expected). Bazgar spends rounds 2–5 fighting at disadvantage and range-only. Expected rounds for Sabriel: 1.8 rounds. Sabriel recovers faster.

**DM coaching note:** VS is the single most impactful ability in the encounter. It can take two PCs offline for 2–3 rounds. It fires only 16.7% chance per round (recharge 6), so the beholder cannot rely on it. When it does fire, the encounter state collapses quickly toward the party dying.

---

**FEEL-R330-02 — VS functions as an "encounter bomb"; binary feel risk.**

Recharge 6 (1/6 per round = 16.7%) means:
- P(VS fires by round 3): 1 - (5/6)² ≈ 31%
- P(VS fires by round 4): 1 - (5/6)³ ≈ 42%
- P(VS fires by round 5): 1 - (5/6)⁴ ≈ 52%

VS fires in roughly half of 5-round combats. When it does, the encounter swings sharply. When it doesn't, the encounter is a pure single-target attrition fight (DR + LA rotation). These play very differently at the table.

Suggestion (log only, design call): change Void Scream to recharge 5-6 (33.3% per round). P(fires by round 3): 56%. This makes VS a likely encounter feature rather than a rare bomb, and lets DMs plan around it. Trade-off: it hits more often, compounding the action starvation from FEEL-R330-01. Joe's decision.

---

**FEEL-R330-03 — Shrine-proximity disadvantage is correct but easy to miss in play.**

VS text: "Creatures within 10 feet of the shrine have disadvantage on this save." Cheat sheet flags it in the SAVE DCs table. But the ROUND-BY-ROUND CHECKLIST does not prompt the DM to check shrine proximity when VS fires. The checklist item for VS just says "Void Scream (recharge 6), DC 16 Wis" in the Beholder block.

Suggested cheat sheet addition (log only): add a sub-bullet under "Void Scream": "→ Within 10 ft of shrine: disadvantage on save; who's standing on the altar?"

This is especially relevant for Sabriel, who is the PC most likely to be at the shrine (divine martial defending the altar). The shrine-proximity disadvantage mechanically punishes divine-martial proximity to their symbolic object. Nice design feature that the table should not miss.

---

**FEEL-R330-04 — Positive design note: shrine-proximity disadvantage creates a Sabriel tactical choice.**

Sabriel is a divine martial who will naturally want to be near the shrine. The shrine-proximity disadvantage on VS creates an organic choice each round: "stay near the altar and take harder VS saves, or retreat 10 ft and give up whatever you were guarding." This is good encounter design — meaningful positioning with a thematic cost. No change needed; noting as a positive feature to highlight in any encounter writeup or session prep notes.

---

**FEEL-R330-05 — VS descent + Frightened = self-protecting positional move.**

When the beholder descends to use VS (from 40 ft → 20 ft via Shrine-Drift BA), it is temporarily within reach weapons range (10 ft reach = polearm, tentacle, anything with extended reach). Normally, a party would rush the beholder the moment it descends. But VS fires BEFORE the party's next set of turns, and frightened PCs CANNOT WILLINGLY MOVE CLOSER. The beholder's descent + VS auto-defends against the rush it would otherwise invite.

This is airtight mechanical synergy: descend → VS → party frightened → ascend safely (Shrine-Drift BA next turn) → party can't pursue. Observation: this synergy works best when initiative puts the beholder before at least one melee PC (giving VS time to land before they can act). If both melee PCs act before the beholder in initiative, they close before VS can fire. DM should descend + VS only on turns where it can reliably get the action off before Bazgar/Sabriel close.
