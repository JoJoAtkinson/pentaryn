**FEEL-F73-02 (NEW — Log only): Beholder DR vs prone target is self-penalizing — tactics note omits this.**

From FEEL-F72-02: PHB p.292 imposes DISADVANTAGE on **ranged** attack rolls against prone targets. Disintegration Ray is a Ranged Spell Attack. If UG makes Bazgar prone AND the beholder then fires DR at Bazgar, DR is at DISADVANTAGE. The existing DR target priority note (MQ-FC51-B) says "Prefer lower-AC targets — Marwen (AC 15) optimal." This guidance is correct but doesn't address the prone case explicitly.

Concrete scenario: Lair init 20 drops Bazgar prone. Beholder considers switching DR target from Marwen to Bazgar (lower AC + prone advantage from melee). However:
- DR vs prone Bazgar: DISADVANTAGE on the attack roll (ranged attack, prone target per PHB p.292) — hit rate drops from 50% (+7 vs AC 18) to ~25% (P(better of two d20 ≥ 11) = 1 − P(both < 11) = 1 − (10/20)² = 75%... wait, disadvantage means the LOWER of two rolls. P(both ≥ 11) = (10/20)² = 25%). So DR vs prone Bazgar = 25%.
- DR vs standing Marwen: 65% (+7 vs AC 15, no condition). 

DR target priority: **never fire DR at a prone target (disadvantage halves hit probability)**. Continue targeting Marwen even when Bazgar is prone. Current MQ-FC51-B note omits this interaction. The UG + DR combo on the same target is a trap.

**Recommend:** Add to DR target priority note (MQ-FC51-B): *(Prone exception: if a PC is currently PRONE from Unstable Ground, DO NOT target them with Disintegration Ray — ranged attacks vs prone targets suffer DISADVANTAGE per PHB p.292, dropping +7 vs AC 18 from 50% to 25%. Fire DR at a standing PC instead. If all PCs are prone, hold DR until one stands.)*
