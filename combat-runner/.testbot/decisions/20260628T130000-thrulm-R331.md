## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `drain-divinity-vs-sabriel`

**Rotation index 4 of 8** — simulating Drain Divinity LA economy against a divine martial.

---

### Setup

**Scenario:** Beholder targets Sabriel (divine martial 5) with Drain Divinity over 3 rounds.

**Sabriel spell slots at level 5:** 4×1st, 3×2nd, 2×3rd (standard paladin-type progression)
**Sabriel CHA save:** assumed +2 (divine martial with modest CHA)
**Drain Divinity:** DC 16 CHA save, Sabriel needs 14+ → 35% success, **65% fail rate**
**Drain Divinity cost:** 3 legendary actions per use → 1 use per round, 3 LA budget exhausted

---

### Critical Premise Correction — rotation.yml error

**BUG-ROTATION-DD-01 (NEW): rotation.yml `drain-divinity-vs-sabriel` says "Simulate 3 saves"**

The description reads: "Beholder spends all 3 legendary actions on Drain Divinity targeting Sabriel... Simulate 3 saves."

This is WRONG. Drain Divinity costs **3 legendary actions per use**. The beholder has 3 LA total → exactly **1 use per round** → **1 save per round**, not 3. The description appears to have been written when DD cost 1 LA per use (its original design). This is a stale description in the testbot rotation itself.

Correct premise: "Beholder spends all 3 LA on a single Drain Divinity use each round for 3 rounds. Simulate 1 save per round (3 total over 3 rounds)."

This does not change the encounter math, only the framing. The corrected simulation follows.

---

### Altitude constraint: Drain Divinity requires descent

**FEEL-R331-01: DD requires descent from default hover — undocumented tactical cost**

Drain Divinity range: **30 ft**. Beholder default hover: 40-60 ft above altar. At default altitude, the beholder **cannot use Drain Divinity** against any ground-level PC.

To use DD, the beholder must first descend. Options:
1. **Shrine-Drift (bonus action):** Move up to 30 ft. If hovering at 40 ft, Shrine-Drift brings it to 10 ft altitude → within 30 ft of ground targets. Keeps all 3 LA available for DD. ✓
2. **Move (1 LA):** Moves up to 30 ft flying speed. Consumes 1 LA → only 2 LA remain → **cannot afford DD** (costs 3 LA). ✗

**Result:** DD is only viable if the beholder uses its bonus action (Shrine-Drift) to descend, not its legendary Move. A DM who uses Move to descend before DD is spending 1 LA unnecessarily and finds they no longer have budget for DD. This is a DM-trap that isn't flagged anywhere in the tactics section or cheat sheet.

**Recommendation:** Add to beholder tactics section: "To use Drain Divinity from altitude, descend via Shrine-Drift (bonus action), NOT the Move legendary action. Move costs 1 LA, leaving only 2 LA — not enough for DD (3 LA). Use Shrine-Drift to preserve your full legendary budget."

After descent via Shrine-Drift: beholder is at ~10 ft altitude, within 30 ft of ground targets but also within melee reach of all three PCs. This is the **vulnerability trade-off** for DD use.

---

### Simulation: 3 rounds of Drain Divinity

**Void-Feeding active in chamber:** +1 to attack/damage (not relevant to DD saves/THP; DD's THP gain is from spell level, not a damage roll). However, beholder attack bonus becomes +9 if it needs to roll attacks this round.

#### Round 1

Beholder descends via Shrine-Drift (BA) to 10 ft altitude.  
Spends all 3 LA on Drain Divinity targeting Sabriel.  
**Sabriel CHA save:** d20 + 2 vs DC 16 → needs 14+, 35% success.

Representative roll: d20 = 11, total 13 → **FAIL** (65% probability event)

Result: Sabriel loses highest remaining slot (3rd-level slot #1). Beholder gains **6 THP** (2 × 3rd-level = 6).

*Beholder now at 10 ft altitude, within melee range of Bazgar and Sabriel. Antireality reaction is available.*

Party response options:
- Bazgar (Fighter 5): 2 attacks at +7 vs AC 17. Expected hits: 2 × 55% = 1.1 expected. ~9.35 expected damage (1d8+4).
- Sabriel: If divine martial with Sacred Flame (CON save DC ~14, beholder CON +3, no CON prof → ~50% fail) → 4.5 exp damage (2d8 avg 9). OR melee attack if adjacent.

*Antireality covers 1 of these attack rolls. Sabriel acting last if she has lower init → beholder has 0 LA remaining for Void Ray after her turn (spent on DD).*

#### Round 2

Beholder at ~10 ft altitude (already descended). 3 LA refresh.  
Spends all 3 LA on Drain Divinity targeting Sabriel (within 30 ft, in range).  
Sabriel's highest remaining slot: 3rd-level slot #2 (if R1 failed) or still 3rd-level (if R1 succeeded).

Representative roll: d20 = 8, total 10 → **FAIL**

Result (assuming R1 also failed): Sabriel loses 2nd third-level slot. No more 3rd-level slots.  
Beholder gains 6 THP (2 × 3rd-level). **Does not stack** — replaces existing 6 THP with 6 THP (no net change per 5e THP rules).

*After 2 rounds of failed DD saves: Sabriel has no 3rd-level slots. If she's a paladin-type, she can no longer cast Bless (2nd-level kept), cannot use Divine Smite with 3rd-level slots (worst smite now 2d8 using 2nd-level), and cannot upcast healing. Her damage output drops ~50%.*

#### Round 3

Beholder 3 LA refresh. DD targets Sabriel.  
Highest remaining slot: 2nd-level (if R1+R2 both failed) or 3rd-level (various).

Representative roll: d20 = 14, total 16 → **SUCCESS** (35% probability event)

Result: Sabriel resists. No slot lost. Beholder gains no THP.

### THP accumulation — confirms rotation.yml CORRECTION

Over 3 rounds with 2 fails and 1 success (P ≈ 44%):
- R1 fail: 6 THP gained (from 0)
- R2 fail: 6 THP "gained" — does NOT stack. Existing 6 replaced by 6 → **no change**
- R3 success: 0 THP

**Beholder holds at most 6 THP at any point.** If R3 had failed at 2nd-level slot: beholder would gain 4 THP, which is LESS than current 6 → 6 retained (no update). THP never accumulate past 6 in this scenario regardless of how many fails occur, unless a 1st-level slot is the only thing left (2 THP < current 6 → no change).

Confirmed: the rotation.yml CORRECTION note is accurate. Old behavior (adding THP cumulatively) was wrong. Current design: ~4 THP expected per DD use (0.65 × 6 = 3.9), held rather than accumulated.

---

### Economic analysis: DD vs alternative LA spend

This is the core FEEL issue, already flagged as FEEL-R328-01 and FEEL-OPEN-01.

**Drain Divinity (3 LA):**
- Deals 0 damage
- 65% chance: removes 1 spell slot from Sabriel, gains ~4 THP (non-cumulative, replaces)
- Requires descent (bonus action Shrine-Drift) → beholder exposed to melee
- Net: slot drain + positional vulnerability + 4 THP

**Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA):**
- Void Ray: DC 16 DEX, 4d10+1 force (in chamber) → 19.4 expected damage
- Tentacle: +9 vs AC 18/19, 3d6+4 → 8.4 / 8.1 expected damage
- Total expected: ~27.8 damage, no positional vulnerability at altitude
- Condition: Void Ray works at altitude (120 ft range); no descent required

**Net verdict:** Against a divine martial, DD removes smite fuel and healing capacity over multiple rounds — this is genuinely impactful for rounds 2+ of a longer fight. But at level 5, "winning by slot drain" takes 3-4 rounds of successful DD applications while the party deals 20-22 expected damage per round. Beholder HP 110 → dead in ~5 rounds. The slot-drain payoff is back-loaded; the party likely runs out of HP before slots become the limiting factor.

DD's correct use case: round 1-2 setup if the encounter is designed to go 6+ rounds. Against a "meant to lose" fight that ends in PC death/retreat by round 3-4, DD provides negligible value compared to direct damage.

**FEEL-R331-02 (confirms FEEL-R328-01, FEEL-OPEN-01):** Drain Divinity at 3-LA cost is economically irrational in the Compass Edge encounter at any round count ≤5. The slot drain never determines the encounter outcome; the party runs out of HP first. DD's THP gain (max 6 non-cumulative) is also negligible against party DPR (~21 to beholder per round). Joe must decide: reduce DD to 2 LA cost OR add a damage component (e.g., "Sabriel takes 2d6 psychic on a failed save in addition to losing the slot") to make DD a real LA option vs Void Ray.

### Paladin smite interaction — critical context

**FEEL-R331-03: If Sabriel can Divine Smite, slot-drain is disproportionately strong vs her specifically**

Sabriel as divine martial likely has Divine Smite (or similar feature). Each spell slot she expends on Smite adds 2d8 radiant per spell level. Losing 3rd-level slots:
- Loses 4d8 smite potential (3rd-level Smite = 4d8 radiant in 5e)
- After 2 DD fails: max Smite is 3d8 (using 2nd-level), down from 4d8

This is the **intended** DD value — targeting the divine magic source. But the math shows: losing 1d8 avg smite damage (4.5 per attack) is worth much less than the 19.4 expected damage the beholder forgoes by not using Void Ray instead. The slot drain is narratively satisfying but mechanically sub-optimal.

**Exception:** If the encounter goes long (6+ rounds) and Sabriel's smite was going to be the fight-deciding resource, DD prevents the decisive blow. Worth running the "6-round extended fight" slice to test this. Add to rotation after `retreat-threshold`.
