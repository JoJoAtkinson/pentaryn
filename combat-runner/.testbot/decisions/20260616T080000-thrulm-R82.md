**NEW-R82-01 (LOW — LOG ONLY): Shardcaller Shard-Throw damage absent from .md**

`derro-shardcaller.md` is in compact stat-header format and gives no damage die or average for Shard-Throw. Tactics section states "+4 to hit, range 30/60" but no damage. The damage (1d8+2, avg 6 piercing per hit) lives only in `actions.jsonl`. A DM running without the combat-runner GUI would not know what damage to roll. The compact format was intentional (stat block says "Action mechanics live in the DB"), but this creates a gap if the DM wants to run the encounter manually with the .md only.

No auto-fix: this would require adding a damage line to the .md, which is a design/format decision.

**NEW-R82-02 (LOW — LOG ONLY): Shard-Barrage DC 13 vs formula DC 12**

DB entry: `save.dc = 13`, `save.ability = "Dex"`. Shardcaller initiative mod +2 implies DEX +2; WIS save +4 at PB +2 confirms PB +2. Standard 5e formula: 8 + DEX +2 + PB +2 = **12**, not 13. The DC is 1 point high relative to the formula. This may be an intentional design choice (DM rounded up, or used a different base stat). Low impact — DC 12 vs DC 13 shifts Bazgar's fail rate from 60% to 55% (marginal). Flag for Joe; do not auto-fix (not a damage average, not a tag).

Note: this mirrors BUG-A56-01 (Rager Taunt DC 12 vs CHA-formula DC 9 — consistent with STR+2 as the source). The SC Shard-Barrage DC 13 is not easily explained by any single stat. Possible it uses a different modifier or is a flat DM choice.

## Feel Assessment: `shardcaller-team`

### FEEL-R82-01 (NEW — LOG ONLY): SC-C CW charge deployment timing underdocumented for 3-SC opener

**Finding:** In the 3-SC assignment (FI-SCT-34-A), SC-C (lowest init) fires Barrage R1 and holds CW per post-hoc rule. But the tactics section gives no explicit guidance for when SC-C should first deploy CW in R2+. With 3 total CW charges, losing one full round of SC-C's CW is a material efficiency loss (~12% reduction in allied hit probability that round). 

**Root cause:** The 3-SC assignment focuses on the R1 stagger configuration (who fires Barrage, who CWs, who holds). It does not follow through to R2 — leaving SC-C's first CW fire timing ambiguous. By R2, SC-C has acted last (init 9) while allies SC-A and SC-B have already taken their R2 turns — MQ-12 blocks CW on them (already acted). SC-C should CW itself? No — CW targets an *ally*, not self. SC-C's R2 CW must target a surviving ally who hasn't yet acted this round (i.e., an ally with higher initiative). But with SC-A at 9 HP and retreating, and SC-C at init 9, SC-C's CW at the bottom of init is nearly always targeting already-acted allies. **SC-C's 3 CW charges are structurally difficult to spend efficiently from a bottom-init position.**

**Recommendation (LOG ONLY):** Add guidance: "SC-C's CW should fire on its FIRST turn in R2 before taking its main action — verify a higher-init surviving ally exists who hasn't acted this round. If all allies have already acted (common at bottom-init), hold the CW to R3 when the order resets."

### FEEL-R82-02 (NEW — LOG ONLY): Shard-Barrage 15-ft line geometry in large-vault chamber

**Finding:** The Thrulm chamber is a "large vault." The party entering from a single direction can use 10+ ft spacing to break Barrage line coverage. In this simulation, SC-C caught Marwen+Sabriel near the entrance because they were clustered — if the party had 15+ ft spacing between all three PCs, SC-C's Barrage would have hit at most 1 target (near-worthless). Contrast with the "narrow corridor" note in the tactics (fire even on 2 PCs) — the large-vault chamber does NOT trigger this note.

**Impact:** If players know to spread (a standard tactical instinct), SC-C's Barrage never fires on 3 targets and rarely fires on 2 after R1. The remaining SC damage output drops to multiattack-only: 3 SCs × (2 shots at 35% vs AC 18 × 6 dmg) = ~12.6 avg/round total, which is nearly negligible against the party. The formation's threat ceiling hinges almost entirely on catching clustered players in Barrage.

**Recommendation (LOG ONLY):** Add a note to `_overview.md` or shardcaller tactics: "If the party spreads >15 ft apart at the chamber entrance, Shard-Barrage cannot fire on 2+ targets — shardcallers fall back to multiattack only. DM should reward clustering (readied actions, Bless) as a meaningful trade-off vs the Barrage risk." Consider whether the encounter should include a chokepoint (collapsed pillar, shrine debris) that naturally channels the party into line-of-Barrage.

### FEEL-R82-04 (LOG ONLY): Mirror Image invalidates SC focus-fire hard; no SC counter

**Finding:** Marwen at 10 HP (R1 exit) playing Mirror Image (L2 action) reduced SC expected damage to ~1.5 avg per SC turn vs Marwen for 3+ rounds. The SCs have no counter to Mirror Image (no AoE that bypasses it, no Counterspell, no forced-move ability). The only answers are: (a) wait out the 3 images (3 SC attacks × miss-rate variance ≈ 6–9 attacks to exhaust), or (b) switch targets.

SC-A correctly switched to Tactical Retreat (below 12 HP). SC-B correctly switched to Bazgar. But in R3 with only SC-C remaining, Mirror Image provides Marwen near-invulnerability until exhausted.

**Impact (design note):** This is fine for the shardcaller-only slice. Against the full thrulm encounter (beholder + shardcallers), the beholder's Disintegration Ray bypasses Mirror Image (ranged spell attack, not a weapon attack → Mirror Image only blocks "attack rolls" PHB p.260, which includes spell attacks → Mirror Image DOES block DR). So Mirror Image is relevant vs the beholder too. If Marwen gets Mirror Image up before DR fires, the beholder should switch to Void Scream (area effect, saves-based, ignores Mirror Image) or grapple+Maw. LOG for future beholder-slice review.
