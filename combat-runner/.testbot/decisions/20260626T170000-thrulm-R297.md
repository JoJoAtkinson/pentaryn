### Feel observations

**1. BUG-RAGER-BERSERK-TYPE confirmed still present.**
The DB row type "utility" produces narration with no dice. In this 2-round slice, Berserk recharged
and would have hit Sabriel (1d12+2=12) under manual adjudication. Without runner output, the DM must
know to adjudicate 2 attacks vs 2 different PC ACs, track which had advantage from Incoming Damage
Aggro, and add Madness Endurance +1 manually. In live play this consistently stalls the DM for
~30 seconds per Berserk activation. **15+ fires overdue.**

**2. Rager's reactive traits are dormant until Round 2.**
In Round 1, the party focused on the beholder (correct priority). Rager hit nobody and took 0 damage,
so Madness Endurance and Incoming Damage Aggro never fired. The Rager's Round 1 contribution was:
Taunt BA (Bazgar failed CHA save) + 2 missed greataxe attacks. Its threat level was negligible.
By Round 2, after Sabriel's smite hit it, both traits activated and the Berserk landed for 12 damage.
**Design consequence:** The Rager is frontloaded on survivability (52 HP) but backloaded on threat
(traits need to be "primed" by damage). At level 5 with 3 PCs, the party can delay priming
indefinitely by ignoring the Rager for 2+ rounds. This is probably intentional (it's a tank, not an
assassin), but the Berserk bug means even when primed, the signature moment produces no mechanical
output.

**3. Drain Divinity dead in standard positioning.**
For the second consecutive playtest cycle (R296 confirmed the same), Drain Divinity was unavailable
because the beholder hovers 40-60 ft above and Drain Divinity has 30 ft range (FEEL-021). Using
Drain Divinity requires either (a) Shrine-Drift BA to descend, (b) spending a move action to
descend, or (c) burning a LA on Move first. In this slice, the beholder's action was fully consumed
by multiattack; descending first would sacrifice its damage output. Drain Divinity did not fire in
either round. Confirming FEEL-021 and FEEL-LA-DRAINANOMALY-R295-01 as systemic.

**4. Sabriel's Lay on Hands pool is load-bearing.**
Sabriel used 15/25 HP of Lay on Hands in 2 rounds: 1 to stabilize Marwen, 14 to save Bazgar from
likely death. Without it, Bazgar drops at 6 HP (beholder's Void Ray brings him to 6 → next beholder
multiattack finishes him). Sabriel's 25-HP pool is the only buffer keeping the party from cascade
failure after Round 1. This underscores existing FEEL-003 (DR permanent death risk): if the beholder
had opened with Disintegration Ray on Marwen instead of multiattack, Marwen is at 0 HP to dead-dead
with no Lay on Hands recourse, and the pool is available for Bazgar — but the loss of Marwen's
arcane support is permanent.

**5. Rager Taunt effective even at low DC.**
Bazgar failed DC 12 CHA save (+1 CHA modifier). That's a base 55% fail rate for a fighter with
no CHA save proficiency. Disadvantage on non-Rager attacks cost Bazgar effectively one attack in
Round 1 (he made 2 attacks, both could have targeted the beholder; disadvantage on the second
pushed it from ~35% hit to ~15% hit). **Taunt is the Rager's most impactful ability in practice**
— not the greataxe, not Berserk (bugged). Consider whether 3/round Taunt (currently once per BA)
is intentional vs unlimited-until-resisted.

**FEEL-RAGER-AGGRO-DORMANCY-R297-01 (NEW)**
*Severity: feel/design. Log only.*

Rager's reactive traits (Madness Endurance, Incoming Damage Aggro) require incoming damage to
activate. When the party correctly prioritizes the beholder, the Rager goes 1-2 rounds before
taking a hit, leaving it with base +4 unaided attacks (miss rate ~65% vs AC 18, ~80% vs AC 19).
The creature's design pattern — durable tank that gets scarier the more it's hit — functions, but
the opening round is mechanically thin. In the Berserk scenario, the Rager's Round 1 contribution
was two misses and a Taunt. Its meaningful turn (Berserk with advantage and Madness) required
Sabriel to spend a smite on it first.

This is not a bug but may be relevant if the Rager ever runs solo: without the beholder drawing
attention, it would get hit immediately and come online faster. The "priming" dynamic is a design
feature but creates a dead zone in mixed encounters.

---

**FEEL-BEHOLDER-POSITIONING-R297-01 (NEW, extends FEEL-021)**
*Severity: medium/systemic. Log only.*

Drain Divinity failed to fire for the second consecutive Phase B cycle (R296 and R297). Both slices
had Sabriel in the party; both had the beholder at altitude where 30 ft Drain Divinity range could
not reach. The beholder's tactics block ("Against Clerics/Paladins: Focuses on Drain Divinity")
contradicts its positioning ("Hover near the shrine altar... 40-60 ft up"). At 40-60 ft altitude,
the beholder is physically incapable of Draining without first spending movement. The tactics text
reads as if Drain Divinity is a primary tool; its actual usage rate in standard altitude positioning
is near 0.

Combined with FEEL-LA-DRAINANOMALY-R295-01 (Drain Divinity mechanically dominated by Tentacle+VoidRay
in EV terms), this means Drain Divinity's actual usage rate is: 0% when beholder is at standard
altitude (can't reach), and low when it descends (LA budget better spent on Tentacle+VoidRay unless
Sabriel is actively healing). The tactics section should either be updated to reflect this, or Drain
Divinity's range should be extended to 60 ft.
