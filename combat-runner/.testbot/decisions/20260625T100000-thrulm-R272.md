**Round 1:**

**Lair action (init 20):** Unstable Ground on Bazgar. DC 16 DEX save, Bazgar DEX +0 (in plate) → needs 16+, P(fail) ≈ 75%. Likely prone. Bazgar must spend half movement standing on his turn.

**Beholder turn (HP 110):**

Bonus action — Shrine-Drift: floats 20 ft toward Marwen (closest, squishiest target at AC 15/32 HP). Now within tentacle reach.

Main action — Multiattack (Tentacle ×2 + Maw):

| Attack | Target | Roll needed | Hit% | Expected dmg |
|--------|--------|-------------|------|-------------|
| Tentacle 1 | Marwen (AC 15) | +7 → need 8+ | 65% | 9.1 (14 avg) |
| Tentacle 2 | Sabriel (AC 19) | +7 → need 12+ | 45% | 6.3 (14 avg) |
| Maw (Marwen grappled) | Marwen (AC 15) | +7 → need 8+ | 65% | 14.3 (22 avg) |

Using average roll of 10 throughout (deterministic check):
- T1 → 10+7=17 vs AC 15. **Hit.** Marwen grappled (escape DC 16). Damage: 14.
- T2 → 10+7=17 vs AC 19. **Miss.** Sabriel not grappled.
- Maw → 10+7=17 vs AC 15. **Hit.** Damage: 22.

**Marwen HP: 32 − 14 − 22 = −4. DOWN at 0 HP. Round 1, before her turn.**

> Key observation: Marwen never acts in Round 1. She has no opportunity to use Shield (reaction), Misty Step (bonus action for cover), or any first-turn spell. The 13.5-avg combined Tentacle+Maw damage of 36 exceeds Marwen's 32 HP max on average rolls. This is expected given the design intent ("party is meant to lose"), but the zero-agency aspect is the central feel concern already logged (FEEL-DR-R269-01).

**After Beholder turn — LA spend decisions:**

Beholder has 3 LA. With Sabriel going last (init 8), the beholder can:
- **Option A (Drain Divinity):** Save all 3 LA → DD at end of Sabriel's turn. Strips 1 spell slot (3rd-level at P=50%). EV: 7.5 HP denial. Sacrifices ~22 expected dmg from Tentacle×3 LAs.
- **Option B (Tentacle×3):** After each of Marwen's DST, Bazgar's turn, Sabriel's turn → 1 LA Tentacle each. EV: ~22 expected damage.
- **Option C (Tentacle×1 + Void Ray):** After Marwen's DST: Tentacle (1 LA). After Sabriel: Void Ray (2 LA). EV: ~21.9 expected damage.

Optimal choice (from R270 analysis): **Option B (Tentacle×3)** dominates Round 1 vs all alternatives. DD is dead weight until R3+ only if Sabriel heals successfully. Carries forward FEEL-LA-R270-02 conclusion.

**After Marwen's DST turn:** 1 LA → Tentacle vs Bazgar (prone, AC 18). +7 vs AC 18 → 10+7=17. **Miss** (needs 18). No damage. 2 LA remaining.

**Bazgar's turn (HP 49, prone):**
- Spends half movement to stand. Advances to beholder.
- Multiattack (2 attacks, Extra Attack at level 5): STR +4, PB +3 → +7 to hit? Wait — at level 5 Battlemaster, PB is +3. STR 16 (+3) is a reasonable assumption for a Fighter. Attack bonus: +3 (STR) + 3 (PB) = +6. vs AC 17 → needs 11+. Hit% = 50%.
- Attack 1: 10+6=16 vs AC 17. **Miss.**
- Attack 2: same (no maneuver needed this turn). 16 vs AC 17. **Miss.**
- Note: even if Bazgar hits, beholder has resistance to non-sanctified B/P/S weapons. Each hit deals ~3.75 expected damage (halved 7.5 base). Negligible threat.

**After Bazgar's turn:** Beholder has 2 LA remaining. 
- Option: 2 LA → Void Ray on Bazgar (49 HP, full). DC 16 DEX, Bazgar DEX +0 → P(fail) ≈ 75%. Expected: (0.75×22) + (0.25×11) = 16.5+2.75 = 19.25 dmg. Bazgar: 49 → ~30 HP.
- Option: 1 LA → Tentacle on Bazgar. +7 vs AC 18 → 50% hit. Expected: 7 dmg.
- Void Ray is better here (19.25 EV vs 7 EV): spend 2 LA. Bazgar ~30 HP remaining.

**Sabriel's turn (HP 44, AC 19, not grappled):**
- Sees Marwen down. Could spend a healing slot (Lay on Hands or cure_wounds) or attack.
- Lay on Hands (pool 25 HP at level 5): stabilizes Marwen for 1 HP. Marwen stable at 1 HP.
- OR attack beholder: +6 to hit vs AC 17 → 45% hit. Longsword 1d8+3 = 7.5, halved (non-sanctified) = 3.75 per hit. Two attacks: 2 × 0.45 × 3.75 = 3.4 expected dmg. Minimal.
- Sabriel most likely heals Marwen (this is the "protect the caster" divine martial role).

**After Sabriel's turn — beholder has 0 LA (spent 2 on VR after Bazgar). No LA action.**

> Drain Divinity window: Sabriel just used Lay on Hands (not a spell slot). Drain Divinity only strips "spell slots or divine abilities." Lay on Hands is an HP pool, not a spell slot. Does DD strip it? The .md says "loses one spell slot of the highest level it has remaining (or loses one use of a divine ability if it has no spells)." "Divine ability" is ambiguous — does it include Lay on Hands? This is an unresolved ruling gap: **FLAG-R272-01 (NEW).**

**Rager turn (HP 52, init 5):**

**Thrall turns:** Hand Axe +1 vs AC 18 → needs 17+, P(hit) = 20%. Expected: 0.20 × 2 = 0.4 dmg each. Negligible.

**End of Round 1:**

### New findings from Phase B simulation

**FEEL-DRAIN-R272-01 (NEW):** `drain_divinity` DB entry has no `la_cost` field; the runner narrates the ability but provides zero LA-budget feedback. DMs must independently track that Drain Divinity costs 3 LA, Void Ray costs 2, and Tentacle costs 1 — none of the four beholder legendary actions encode their cost in the DB spec. At the table, a DM who types "drain" after spending 1 LA on a Tentacle will have the runner narrate Drain Divinity as if it succeeds, even though the beholder only had 2 LA remaining. The runner cannot catch this mistake. LOW priority (DM rules knowledge), but worth surfacing as a DM-usability gap since the 3-vs-2-vs-1 LA split is the central complexity of this boss's action economy.

**FLAG-R272-01 (NEW — needs Joe ruling):** Lay on Hands (Paladin pool) and Drain Divinity interaction: .md says DD strips "one use of a divine ability if it has no spells." Does Lay on Hands count as a "divine ability" for DD purposes? If yes, DD strips LoH uses when Sabriel has no slots remaining — mechanically coherent but ruthless (strips the only recovery tool when the party is losing). If no, DD becomes a dead action whenever Sabriel's slots are exhausted. Joe must decide: (A) Lay on Hands = divine ability (DD strips HP pool in 5-HP increments? Or strips all remaining uses?), (B) LoH = not divine ability for DD purposes (DD dead when slots exhausted), or (C) DD doesn't fire against Sabriel once she's out of slots (beholder shifts target to any remaining caster). Recommend ruling B for table simplicity.

**Initiative-order observation (carry-forward weight):** In this simulation, Sabriel (init 8) goes last, which means the beholder can always save all 3 LA for Drain Divinity on Sabriel — no trade-off required. But the beholder ALSO did better by spending those 3 LA as Tentacle+VoidRay (EV ~21.9) than DD (~7.5). The "Drain Divinity degenerate" finding (FEEL-R261-01, FEEL-LA-R270-02) holds here and is directly observable in this scenario: even with Sabriel perfectly positioned for DD, it's never the right play in a 3-round fight.
