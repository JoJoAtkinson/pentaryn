## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `thrulm-beholder-ct-drift-ba-conflict`

### Scenario prelude

Bazgar downed. A thrall is 40 ft from Marwen; beholder also needs to close range for
Disintegration Ray next turn. Both Compel Thrall (CT) and Shrine-Drift (SD) are bonus actions —
only one fires this beholder turn. DM must choose.

### Setup assumptions

- Bazgar at 0 HP (downed, rolling death saves), initiative 8.
- Marwen at 18 HP, initiative 16, standing 40 ft from the nearest thrall (at the threshold,
  trying to stay out of thrall melee range).
- Sabriel at 28 HP, initiative 10, adjacent to beholder (within 10 ft), recently used smite.
- Beholder at 74 HP, initiative 20 (acted first this round via multiattack, downed Bazgar), within
  10 ft of Sabriel. Thrall at 40 ft from Marwen, 55 ft from beholder.
- 3 LA already spent (beholder used its LA budget earlier this round). 0 LA remaining.
- Beholder's bonus action not yet spent this turn.

### Option A: Compel Thrall (CT) as bonus action

CT targets the thrall (within 60 ft of beholder: 55 ft — barely in range).
- Target must succeed DC 16 Cha save to resist. Thrall has Cha -2 → needs 18+ to pass → 15% pass rate.
- On fail (85% likely): thrall moves up to 30 ft toward Marwen.
- Thrall starts at 40 ft from Marwen → after CT, now at **10 ft from Marwen** (not yet melee).
- Result: thrall threatens Marwen next round (closes to 5 ft with its own movement). Marwen must
  spend movement or Misty Step to maintain distance, or accept the melee threat.

**CT gives: positioning pressure on Marwen; beholder does NOT reposition.**

### Option B: Shrine-Drift (SD) as bonus action

SD moves beholder up to 30 ft through air and stone.
- Beholder is currently 10 ft from Sabriel and hovering above the altar.
- If SD fires toward Marwen: beholder closes to ~25 ft from Marwen (within DR 120 ft range anyway;
  SD is not needed for DR). SD is needed only for DD (30-ft range) or for breaking out of Sabriel's
  melee threat.
- SD frees beholder from Sabriel's melee reach (currently adjacent). Sabriel provokes no OA vs SD
  (SD is not normal movement).
- Result: beholder repositions 30 ft away from Sabriel. No longer in melee threat. Marwen still
  at 40 ft from thrall (thrall not moved).

**SD gives: escape from Sabriel's melee threat; no thrall redirect.**

### Decision math

The scenario's tactical question: CT or SD?

| Factor | CT wins | SD wins |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Sabriel adjacent (PAM/OA threat next turn) | — | ✓ |
| Thrall within 60 ft of beholder | ✓ | — |
| DR is beholder's next action (120 ft range) | — | neither needed |
| DD is beholder's next action (30 ft range) | — | ✓ (descend to reach) |
| Beholder wants to break Sabriel's smite pressure | — | ✓ |
| Beholder wants to redirect thrall to Marwen | ✓ | — |

**Verdict (logged, not auto-fixed):** In this setup (Sabriel adjacent, thrall mid-field), **SD > CT**
because Sabriel's melee access is the primary threat: an un-repositioned beholder is subject to
Sabriel's smites every round she has action economy. CT provides Marwen-pressure but only lands
next round; Sabriel's smites land THIS round. SD removes the immediate smite threat.

Exception: if beholder's next action is MULTIATTACK (not DR), and the thrall is the only mobile
piece that can deliver consistent low-level damage to the caster backline, CT edges out SD — SD just
moves the beholder, accomplishing nothing if it isn't firing DR or DD.

### NEW FEEL FINDINGS

**FEEL-R100-01 (NEW): CT at 55-ft beholder-to-thrall puts CT on the edge of its 60-ft range.**

CT range: "one creature it can see within 60 feet that is charmed by it." At 55 ft to the thrall
(beholder hovering above altar, thrall mid-chamber), CT barely fires. If the beholder drifts even
10 ft in the wrong direction (SD first, CT second), the thrall falls outside 60 ft and CT fails
entirely. This ordering sensitivity (SD-then-CT = potential CT failure; CT-then-SD = guaranteed CT
fires but beholder doesn't reposition) is not documented in the tactics section.

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to beholder tactics under CT: "Check thrall distance before
choosing CT as bonus action. If thrall > 55 ft from beholder, SD-then-CT risks pushing thrall
out of CT range. Either: (a) CT first if thrall is in range, then SD if needed; or (b) skip CT
this turn and SD instead, then CT next round from new position."

**FEEL-R100-02 (NEW): CT delivers thrall to 10 ft from Marwen (not melee) — requires second action.**

When the thrall is 40 ft from Marwen and CT fires, the thrall moves 30 ft and lands at 10 ft —
just outside melee reach. The thrall then must spend its own move action (speed 30 ft) on its next
initiative to close the remaining 10 ft. If Marwen also moves away (Misty Step 30 ft, or Dash 30
ft), the thrall is immediately back at 40 ft, wasting the CT entirely. CT on a 40-ft-away thrall
provides setup but zero immediate pressure.

**The CT mechanic works best under 30 ft of separation.** At 40+ ft, CT delivers setup (next round
closes); at 0–30 ft, CT delivers immediate melee contact or threat. Tactics section lacks
distance-based guidance.

**Recommendation (log only):** Add distance qualifier to CT tactic: "Compel Thrall is strongest
when the thrall is ≤30 ft from its designated target (CT places it in melee or within 5 ft). At
31–60 ft, CT delivers setup only — the thrall still needs its own movement to engage next round,
during which the target may retreat. At >60 ft, CT cannot fire. Evaluate thrall-to-target distance
before using CT as the bonus action; SD may be a better spend if no thrall is within 30 ft of a
priority target."

**FEEL-R100-03 (NEW): CT-vs-SD bonus action conflict has no priority rule in beholder tactics.**

The current beholder tactics section (Environmental Use, Bonus Actions) mentions CT and SD as
independent options but never provides a decision rule for when both are available. In practice
this conflict arises in R2+ when:
- A charmed thrall is in the mid-chamber (40–55 ft from target)
- The beholder has repositioned after R1 multiattack and is no longer optimally placed for DD

**Recommendation (log only):** Add CT vs SD priority to beholder tactics:
"Bonus action priority: If a thrall is ≤30 ft from a priority target AND beholder does not need
to reposition for DD (DD requires 30 ft range), CT > SD. If beholder needs to close within 30 ft
of a divine caster for DD (most R2+ scenarios given hover altitude), SD > CT. If both conditions
apply, SD first (reposition), hold CT next round after distance is closed."
