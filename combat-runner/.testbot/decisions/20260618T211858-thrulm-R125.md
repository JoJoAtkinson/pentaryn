### New Phase A finding this run

**DB-BUG-01 — `ancient_resonance` missing 1d4 psychic rider on failed save (log only)**

The stat block reads:
> "Each creature in the area must make a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw, taking **11 (2d10) necrotic damage** on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. Creatures that fail take an additional **2 (1d4) psychic damage** as their minds brush against something beyond comprehension."

The DB spec (`combat_actions_db.py get shrine-touched-derro ancient_resonance`) has:
```json
"damage": {"dice": "2d10", "type": "necrotic"}
```

No `extra_damage` or secondary damage field for the 1d4 psychic rider. When the combat-runner processes Ancient Resonance, it will output only the 2d10 necrotic; the 1d4 psychic is silently dropped. At avg 2.5 psychic per creature failing the save, this is a real omission — particularly relevant if the party has psionically vulnerable PCs.

**Fix path:** `combat_action_upsert` with a `rider_on_save_fail` or `extra_damage_on_fail` field once that spec key is confirmed valid. Do NOT edit `actions.jsonl` directly. Human to decide whether the DB schema supports a conditional extra-damage field, or whether this should be noted as a DM manual step.

## Phase B — Generative Playtest Slice

**Scenario:** `thrulm-shrine-touched-retaliation` (thrulm-only counter 125 % 3 = 2)  
**Prelude:** The shrine-touched derro staggers from Bazgar's blow. The runes on its arms flare brighter. Something in its face shifts — not anger. Hunger.

### Simulation Setup

- Shrine-Touched: HP 45, AC 16, +4 to hit (STR +1 + PB +3)
- Bazgar: HP 49, AC 18 — the attacker (fighter, STR-based, likely adjacent)
- Marwen: HP 32, AC 15 — 20 ft behind, in cone range
- Sabriel: HP 44, AC 19 — divine martial, flanking adjacent

**Bazgar action:** Longsword strike for 12 damage (a plausible Battlemaster hit: 1d8+4 avg = 8.5, so 12 is a good-but-not-spectacular roll; could also include a maneuver die).

### Trigger Resolution

**Step 1 — Unstable Form (10+ damage in one turn):** 12 damage ≥ 10 → triggers. Shrine-Touched gains advantage on attack rolls until end of its next turn. No DB row; DM must track manually (DB-OPEN-04 confirmed gap).

**Step 2 — Oath-Breaking Retaliation (reaction, visible attacker):** Fires on any visible damage source. Shrine-Touched makes one Shrine-Axe attack vs Bazgar (+4 to hit, 1d8+1 slashing + 1d8 necrotic). Shrine-Touched then takes 1d4 psychic self-damage regardless.

**Ruling ambiguity — FEEL-07:** Does Unstable Form advantage apply to the Oath-Breaking reaction attack?

- Unstable Form: "After taking 10 or more damage in a single turn, it gains advantage on attack rolls until the end of its next turn."
- The reaction fires as a direct consequence of the same damage event that triggered Unstable Form.
- If advantage applies to the reaction: +4 vs AC 18 with advantage → hit on 11+ twice → (1-(13/20)²) = 1-0.4225 = **57.75% hit chance**.
- If advantage does NOT apply to the reaction (only to next-turn attacks): **35% hit chance**.
- Damage on hit: 1d8+1 slashing (avg 5.5) + 1d8 necrotic (avg 4.5) = **avg 10 damage**.

At 35% hit: expected 3.5 damage to Bazgar + 2.5 self-damage.  
At 57.75% hit: expected 5.8 damage to Bazgar + 2.5 self-damage.

The ruling matters. With advantage, Oath-Breaking Retaliation becomes a meaningful combat tool at ~3 extra expected damage per trigger. Without, it's a coin-flip tax on the shrine-touched.

**Shrine-Touched HP after reaction:** 45 − 12 (Bazgar) − 2.5 avg (1d4 self-dmg) ≈ **30 HP**. Unstable Form advantage: active for next turn.

### Round: Shrine-Touched's Next Turn (Advantage Active)

**Option A — Ancient Resonance available (recharge roll of 5-6 at turn start):**

1/3 chance the recharge fires (recharge 5-6 = 2/6). If it does:
- 15-ft cone: Bazgar (adjacent) and Marwen (20 ft, borderline) and Sabriel (adjacent flank) in range.
- DC 14 DEX save each. Ancient Resonance is a save (not an attack roll) → Unstable Form advantage does NOT help here.
- On fail: 2d10 necrotic (avg 11). **DB-BUG-01: 1d4 psychic rider missing from DB.**
- DEX estimates: Bazgar +0 → 35% success. Marwen +2 → 45% success. Sabriel +0 → 35%.
- Expected damage per PC: Bazgar ~9, Marwen ~8.5, Sabriel ~9. Total ~26.5 spread across 3 PCs.
- With psychic rider (stat block true): +2.5 per PC who fails. Expected total rises to ~31.

Resonance threatens to drop Marwen (32 HP) after Round 1 if Bazgar also hit her — not unreasonable in a multi-turn fight. Bazgar at 49 HP takes ~9 — still viable. Sabriel at 44 HP takes ~9 — fine.

**Option B — Multiattack (Shrine-Axe × 2, with Unstable Form advantage):**

Each attack: +4 to hit with advantage.
- vs Bazgar AC 18: 57.75% hit, avg 10/hit. Expected 5.8/attack × 2 = **11.6 total**.
- vs Marwen AC 15: +4 with advantage → hit on 11+ → (1-(10/20)²) = 1-0.25 = **75% hit**. Expected 7.5/attack × 2 = **15.0 total** if both target Marwen.

Shrine-touched tactics (defends shrine) would split attacks between the nearest threats. Focusing both attacks on Marwen threatens **15 damage** expected (she has 32 HP → down to 17 after one round of multiattack). By round 2 of sustained pressure, Marwen is at severe risk.

**Unstable Form feel note:** The trait creates a meaningful "wounded beast" pattern — the shrine-touched becomes more dangerous when hurt. At 10+ damage, advantage flips it from ~35% to ~57-75% depending on AC. Against Marwen (AC 15), multiattack with advantage delivers **15 expected damage** vs ~7 without. This is dramatically different and creates a compelling gameplay moment: hitting the shrine-touched hard suddenly makes it more dangerous to adjacent party members. Good design.

### Feel Assessment

**FEEL-07 — Unstable Form + Oath-Breaking Retaliation timing needs a documented ruling**

The combo is well-designed but requires a ruling documented in the stat block. Without it, every DM will adjudicate differently:

- **Ruling A (advantage applies to reaction):** Shrine-Touched is a punisher — hitting it for ≥10 damage triggers a 57.75%-accurate counter-attack back at the attacker. This creates meaningful choice: should Bazgar split his damage across multiple hits (<10 each) to avoid triggering Unstable Form? Good tactical depth.
- **Ruling B (advantage only on next-turn attacks):** Oath-Breaking Retaliation is a low-threat reaction (35% hit), existing mainly as narrative flavor. Unstable Form only matters when the shrine-touched gets its next full turn.

Recommended ruling: **Ruling A** (advantage applies to the reaction attack). Reasoning: Unstable Form says "gains advantage on attack rolls until end of its next turn" — the reaction is within that window. This makes the combo intentionally punishing and delivers the "wounded beast" fantasy. Add a parenthetical to the Unstable Form trait: *(This advantage applies to Oath-Breaking Retaliation reaction attacks triggered in the same turn if applicable.)*

**FEEL-08 — Ancient Resonance recharge 5-6 feels correct for party composition**

At 1/3 chance per turn, Ancient Resonance fires roughly once every 3 turns of shrine-touched combat. Against the Compass Edge:
- Fires at the right frequency — not so rare it's a non-factor, not so common it dominates
- The ~26-31 spread damage (with psychic rider) is punishing but not insta-kill for any PC
- Marwen is the most threatened (DC 14 DEX save, AC 15, 32 HP) — correct thematic pressure

The ability rotation appears healthy. **No change recommended.**

**FEEL-09 — Shrine-Touched vs the "meant to lose" design intent**

The shrine-touched is one of the mid-tier threats (not the beholder). In solo configuration (1 shrine-touched vs Compass Edge), the fight is winnable — Bazgar + Sabriel can absorb punishment while Marwen targets weaknesses. The shrine-touched belongs in multi-NPC encounters where its Oath-Breaking Retaliation punishes whoever helps the front line.

In the full encounter context (beholder + 2-3 shrine-touched + other derro), the shrine-touched's role is to punish PCs who split attention to the minion line while the beholder drains resources. Mechanically sound.
