#### FEEL-R176-01 (NEW — HIGH): Permanent PC death before first turn is the most likely round-1 outcome

**Finding:** With beholder initiative advantage (DEX +3 vs Marwen's DEX +2), the beholder acts before Marwen in **52.5%** of encounters (wins all ties). Disintegration Ray hits Marwen on **65%** of attack rolls (+7 vs AC 15, in-chamber). When it hits, damage avg 45 > Marwen max HP 32 — instant kill **~100%** of the time.

**Combined probability: 52.5% × 65% × 100% ≈ 34%** — one in three encounters ends with Marwen permanently dead before she takes a single action.

**Why this crosses "unfair rather than lethal":** The design intent (per briefing) is "the party is meant to lose." A TPK after 3–4 rounds of dramatic combat is appropriate. Permanent death of a PC before her first action in round 1 is a different category: the player had zero agency. In a homebrew encounter, players expect to influence their fate; permadeath on round 1 from an attack roll the target had no defense against feels determined rather than dramatic.

**Design factors contributing to this:**
1. DR is a Ranged Spell Attack (no save) — Marwen cannot Shield this (Shield is a reaction to being targeted, not to an attack roll... actually she CAN Shield against a ranged attack roll). Wait: Shield reaction adds +5 AC until start of her next turn. If she uses it as a reaction on the beholder's attack: +5 AC → AC 20. Beholder needs 13+ to hit instead of 8+ → 40% hit rate down from 65%. This reduces P(Marwen dies before her turn) to **52.5% × 40% ≈ 21%**. Still one in five.
2. If Marwen has Shield prepared: this is her single most impactful round-1 decision. The runner does not surface this (Marwen is a PC tab, not an NPC). DM should remind Marwen's player that Shield applies to the incoming DR.

**Recommendation (log-only):** Add to Tactics section: "Shield reminder — before rolling DR, remind Marwen's player that Shield (if prepared) applies as a reaction +5 AC. At AC 20, DR hits 40% vs 65%, reducing P(perma-death R1 before Marwen's turn) from ~34% to ~21%. Both are high; this does not resolve FEEL-R169-01 (pre-session consent for permadeath clause) but gives the player meaningful agency."

**Interaction with FEEL-R169-01 (pre-session DR permadeath note):** FEEL-R176-01 is a severity escalation. Not just "DR can perma-kill PCs" but specifically "DR will perma-kill Marwen before her first turn in ~1/3 of encounters." Must accompany pre-session consent discussion.

#### FEEL-R176-02 (NEW — MEDIUM): Drain Divinity dead weight round 1 due to altitude — confirms and quantifies FEEL-R172-04

**Finding:** In this encounter configuration, Drain Divinity (range 30 ft) cannot reach Sabriel in round 1. With the beholder at 40–60 ft altitude hovering above the altar, and the party entering from the corridor 30–50 ft horizontally from the altar:

- Diagonal distance to Sabriel at ground level ≈ √(35² + 50²) ≈ 61 ft. Outside 30 ft range.
- For beholder to use Drain Divinity, it must descend to ≤15 ft altitude (putting it within melee reach of Bazgar) OR Sabriel must advance to within 30 ft horizontally.

**Consequence:** Round 1, Drain Divinity is mechanically impossible without the beholder sacrificing its altitude advantage. The beholder cannot use its most thematically resonant ability (Drain Divinity against the divine martial) until round 2 or later, and only by accepting increased melee exposure. The Tactics section says "Prioritizes Drain Divinity against clerics/paladins" without noting this positioning dependency.

**Additional LA economy observation this fire:** After spending 2 LA on Void Ray and 1 on Move, the beholder has 0 LA remaining for the rest of round 1. Sabriel, Rager, Bazgar, and Thralls all act after the LA spend with no further legendary response. The beholder is reactive-action-free for the final 3+ initiative slots of round 1.

**Recommendation (log-only):** Add pre-engagement positioning note to Tactics: "For Drain Divinity to be viable round 1, the beholder needs to open at 25–30 ft altitude (rather than 40–60 ft). This trades altitude safety for Drain Divinity access. If the beholder wants to open with DR and save DD for round 2, it should plan to descend as a Move LA at the end of round 1 to within DD range of Sabriel before round 2 begins."

#### FEEL-R176-03 (NEW — MEDIUM): Shardcaller Call Weakness round-1 amplifier is invisible in the cheat sheet tactics section

**Finding:** The Shardcaller's round-1 BA (Call Weakness → Rager gets advantage) creates a significant round-1 damage spike: Rager's first Greataxe hit rate jumps from 35% to 57.75% on the first attack. Over the full Rager multiattack, this adds approximately **+1.8 expected damage** (modest numerically but meaningful on a PC at ~17 HP after earlier hits).

More importantly, Call Weakness enables the Shardcaller to **control initiative order value.** If the Shardcaller goes before the Rager (which it does on avg: init +2 vs +0), it can pre-buff the Rager before the Rager acts — a reliable synergy that punishes the party in round 1. If the Shardcaller goes after the Rager, the buff is wasted for round 1.

**Cheat sheet gap (CS-DRIFT-22, NEW):** The "When Derro Acts" section in COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md covers the Shardcaller's action choices (Call Weakness → ally, Shard-Throw, Tactical Retreat) but does not mention the round-1 priority: use Call Weakness on the Rager if the Rager hasn't acted yet. The Shardcaller's initiative advantage over the Rager (+2 vs +0) means this is a reliable round-1 setup.

**Recommendation (log-only):** Add to COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md "When Derro Acts" → Shardcaller section: "Round 1: if Rager hasn't acted yet, Call Weakness on the Rager (advantage on first attack). This fires before the party has a chance to Disengage or split the Rager off. The Shardcaller acts before the Rager on average (init +2 vs +0) — this is the default opening."

*This is a cheat sheet improvement, not a stat block bug. CS-DRIFT-22 logged.*
