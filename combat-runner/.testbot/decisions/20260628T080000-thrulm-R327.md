### Setup

**Scenario premise:** Beholder wins initiative. Party has not acted. Beholder at full resources (HP 110, DR available, VS available, 3 LA, 3 LR, Antireality ready, Void-Feeding active in chamber).

**Party starting positions:** Party has just entered the thrulm chamber. Beholder hovers 40–60 ft above the shrine altar (~40 ft above floor level). Marwen (AC 15, 32 HP) is the closest soft target; the beholder can see all three PCs.

**Key decision at the top of round 1:** Multiattack (2 Tentacle Lash + 1 Maw) vs Disintegration Ray — both are available, both can target Marwen.

---

### Probability Breakdown

**All attack rolls in chamber: +8 (base) + 1 (Void-Feeding always active) = +9. Damage: +1 to all.**

**Multiattack vs Marwen (AC 15 — needs 6+ on d20 = 75% hit per attack):**

| Attack | Bonus | vs AC | P(hit) | Avg dmg (in chamber) |
|--------|-------|-------|--------|----------------------|
| Tentacle Lash 1 | +9 | 15 | 75% | 3d6+4 = 14.5 → **14** bludg + Grapple (DC 16) |
| Tentacle Lash 2 | +9 | 15 | 75% | 3d6+4 = 14.5 → **14** bludg + Grapple (DC 16) |
| Maw | +9 | 15 | 75% | 4d8+4 = 22 piercing (Grappled Marwen: escape-check disadvantage only) |

Expected multiattack damage to Marwen: 0.75×14 + 0.75×14 + 0.75×22 = 10.5 + 10.5 + 16.5 = **37.5 expected damage**

P(Marwen incapacitated ≥ 0 HP) from multiattack:
| Hits | Damage | Result | Probability |
|------|--------|--------|-------------|
| TL1+TL2+Maw all hit | 14+14+22=50 | Down (no instant death: 50-32=18 < 32) | (0.75)³ = 42.2% |
| TL1+Maw hit, TL2 miss | 14+22=36 | Down | 0.75×0.25×0.75 = 14.1% |
| TL2+Maw hit, TL1 miss | 14+22=36 | Down | 0.25×0.75×0.75 = 14.1% |
| TL1+TL2 hit, Maw miss | 14+14=28 | Alive at 4 HP, grappled ×2 | 0.75×0.75×0.25 = 14.1% |
| Single TL hit only | 14 | Alive at 18 HP, grappled ×1 | 3×(0.75×0.25×0.25) = 14.1% |
| Maw only | 22 | Alive at 10 HP, ungrappled | 0.25×0.25×0.75 = 4.7% |
| All miss | 0 | Alive at 32 HP | (0.25)³ = 1.6% |

**P(Marwen incapacitated): 42.2 + 14.1 + 14.1 = 70.4%**
**P(Marwen critically wounded + grappled at ≤4 HP): 14.1%**
**P(Marwen alive at ≥10 HP): ~15.5%**

**Disintegration Ray vs Marwen (AC 15 — same calculation: 75% hit):**

| Result | Damage | Outcome | Probability |
|--------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Hit | 10d8 = 45 avg force | Marwen at 0 HP, DISINTEGRATED (permadeath) | 75% |
| Miss | 0 | No damage, DR now on recharge 5–6 | 25% |

**DR expected damage to Marwen: 0.75 × 45 = 33.75**

### Comparing the Options

| Metric | Multiattack | Disintegration Ray |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Expected damage | **37.5** (higher) | 33.75 |
| P(Marwen incapacitated) | 70.4% (not perma-dead) | 75% (perma-dead) |
| Sabriel can stabilize Marwen? | **Yes** (Medicine or Healing Word) | **No** (disintegrated) |
| Grapple control obtained? | 84.3% probability | No |
| Action "wasted" on miss? | No (multiattack always deals some damage on partial hits) | Yes — 25% chance of zero damage |
| Maw advantage on grappled Marwen? | No (Maw gains no attack advantage from grapple) | N/A |

**Key finding:** Multiattack has *higher expected damage output* (37.5 vs 33.75). But DR has a 75% chance to remove Marwen permanently — a resource no party action can undo. The strategic value of permadeath exceeds the arithmetic EV difference.

**An INT 17 beholder reasons:**
1. Marwen's Fireball (8d6, avg 28, DC 13 reflex) threatens the derro thralls and rager — eliminating her immediately protects allied forces.
2. DR miss (25%) wastes the action — but DR recharges on 5-6, roughly 33% per round. Burning it round 1 vs a 75% chance of permadeath is the high-value move.
3. Grapple from multiattack controls Marwen but keeps her alive. Sabriel can stabilize her in one action. Permadeath avoids this.
4. Multiattack requires the beholder to descend to within 10 ft of Marwen (Tentacle Lash reach 10 ft, beholder at 40-60 ft hover). DR fires at 120 ft range — no descent required.

**Conclusion: Optimal beholder round 1 is DR on Marwen from altitude.** Multiattack is better EV but requires descending into melee range and leaves Marwen saveable. DR from altitude eliminates her 75% of the time without exposing the beholder.

### Round 1 Simulation (Beholder fires DR)

**Beholder's turn:**
- Position: hovering 40 ft above altar, 80 ft from Marwen (within 120 ft range)
- Action: Disintegration Ray, +9 vs AC 15
- **Result (75% scenario — hit):** 45 force damage → Marwen at -13 HP → 0 HP. Reduced to 0 by DR → DISINTEGRATED. Marwen is ash. No death saves. Cannot be restored except by True Resurrection or Wish.
- Bonus action: Shrine-Drift — reposition to altar column for cover (not needed immediately; beholder holds position)

**End of beholder's turn → Legendary Actions (3 LA available):**

PCs still to act: Sabriel (init 14, next after beholder), Bazgar (init 10).

After Sabriel's turn → beholder uses **Void Ray (2 LA)** on Sabriel:
- DC 16 DEX save. Sabriel DEX estimated +1 (divine martial, STR-focused): needs 15+, 30% success → 70% fail.
- Expected: 0.70×(4d10+1) + 0.30×(2d10+0.5) = 0.70×23 + 0.30×11.5 = 16.1 + 3.45 = **19.6 expected damage**
- Sabriel (44 HP) → ~24 HP expected

After Bazgar's turn → beholder uses **Tentacle (1 LA)** on Bazgar:
- From 40 ft altitude: reach 10 ft. Bazgar is on the floor. **Tentacle LA cannot reach Bazgar at altitude.**

**NEW FINDING: Tentacle LA unavailable when beholder is at altitude.** Reach 10 ft vs 40 ft separation. The beholder must descend to use Tentacle LA on ground targets. Since it fired DR from altitude and chose NOT to descend for multiattack, the Tentacle LA in its LA budget is dead weight this round.

After Bazgar's turn → beholder's only legal LA option: **Move (1 LA)** (reposition 30 ft).

**Net LA usage if beholder stays at altitude after DR:**
- Void Ray (2 LA) on Sabriel → ~20 expected damage
- Move (1 LA) → reposition
- **Tentacle (1 LA) wasted** because no target within 10 ft

This means the beholder effectively has 3 - 2 (Void Ray) - 1 (Move) = 0 LA remaining, BUT had it chosen differently it could have: Tentacle(1) + VoidRay(2) for full LA budget — but only if it descended. By staying at altitude and firing DR, the beholder loses its Tentacle LA availability for the round.

### If DR Misses (25% scenario)

Beholder has wasted its action. DR is now on recharge 5-6 (~33% per round to recover).

Beholder's only remaining damage options this turn (after using main action):
- None — the action was DR (which missed). Bonus action: Shrine-Drift (movement, no damage).

**Bonus action: Shrine-Drift** → reposition.

LA window: Void Ray (2 LA) and Tentacle (1 LA, if beholder descends) or Move (1 LA).

On round 2, beholder must use Multiattack (main action) or wait for DR recharge. If it waits and DR doesn't recharge (67% chance per round), it's stuck with Multiattack as its only substantive action. **This creates the dead-zone noted as FEEL-OPEN-dead-action.**

### Sabriel's Response (after round 1 DR hit)

Marwen is dead. Sabriel (divine martial 5, 44 HP, AC 19) is hit by Void Ray for ~20 damage → 24 HP.

Sabriel cannot resurrect Marwen (no True Resurrection at level 5). Her options:
- Attack beholder at altitude: melee is impossible (40 ft up). Ranged spell attack or cantrip.
  - Sacred Flame (cantrip, CON save DC ~14, 2d8 radiant): no attack roll, Antireality-immune. 9 avg, 50% success for beholder → **4.5 expected radiant damage**. Safe option.
  - Guiding Bolt (1st-level, attack roll +6): Antireality fires → 25% hit rate. 4d6=14 radiant avg → **3.5 expected damage.** Poor use of slot with Antireality.
- Disengage from beholder's threat range (it's at altitude — no threat zone at ground level).
- Advance toward beholder for eventual melee if it descends.

**Sabriel correctly casts Sacred Flame** — Antireality-immune, uses CON save beholder is not proficient in (beholder has no CON save listed → base CON +3 = 13, needs 1 on d20 to fail DC 14). Wait: DC 14 = Sabriel's spell save DC = 8 + PB(+3) + ability mod. Sabriel as divine martial likely uses CHA or WIS. If WIS +3: DC = 8+3+3=14. Beholder WIS save +7 → needs 7+ to save → 70% success. Expected: 0.30×9 = **2.7 expected damage.** Lower than estimated — beholder WIS is +7, very high.

**FEEL-R327-01 — Beholder WIS save (+7) makes CON-/WIS-targeting cantrips ineffective for Sabriel:**
The divine martial's Sacred Flame uses WIS save, against which the beholder has +7. At DC 14, the beholder saves 70% of the time. Sabriel's best action at altitude is effectively 2.7 expected damage from Sacred Flame. She has no good options until the beholder descends. This is a positioning trap: a divine martial designed to be in melee is stuck doing 2.7 expected damage per round from range.

### Bazgar's Response

Bazgar (Fighter 5 Battlemaster, +7/2 attacks, 49 HP, AC 18) faces a flying beholder at 40 ft altitude with no ranged spell options. Options:
- Throw a handaxe (+7, 1d6+4=7.5 avg): Attack 1 faces Antireality (beholder still has reaction available — Void Ray used 2 LA, not the reaction). 55%→30% with disadvantage. 2nd attack at 55%.
  - Expected round damage: 0.30×7.5 + 0.55×7.5 = 2.25 + 4.125 = **6.4 expected damage**
- Second Wind (bonus action, 1d10+5 = 10.5 HP recovered) if already wounded. Bazgar is at full HP → not yet needed.
- Action Surge (1/encounter): Double attacks (4 total). But Antireality only fires once per round — Action Surge turns 2 attacks into 4, and Antireality only affects 1. At 4 attacks: (0.30 + 0.55 + 0.55 + 0.55) × 7.5 = 1.95 × 7.5 = **14.6 expected damage.** Antireality is proportionally less punishing with Action Surge.

**Bazgar should save Action Surge for when the beholder descends (melee range, no Antireality on a good round).** At altitude, even Action Surge damage (~14.6) is modest vs the beholder's HP pool.

**FEEL-R327-02 — Tentacle LA dead weight when beholder stays at altitude**

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Mechanic | If the beholder fires DR from altitude (40-60 ft hover) and doesn't descend, its Tentacle LA (reach 10 ft) cannot hit ground targets. The Tentacle option in its LA budget is wasted. In the round where DR fires from altitude: only Void Ray (2 LA) and Move (1 LA) are usable. |
| Design implication | The beholder's LA menu has an effective "altitude tax." From altitude: Move(1) + VoidRay(2) = 3 LA, fully spent — no Tentacle. At ground level: Tentacle(1) + VoidRay(2) = 3 LA, fully spent — no Move. The beholder can either attack on the ground (with Tentacle) OR attack from range (with VoidRay) in the same LA round, but not both because 1+2=3. The altitude constraint makes the choice automatic. |
| Tactics section coverage | Not currently documented. A DM might try to use Tentacle LA from altitude and discover it doesn't work (or make a ruling error). |
| Status | Log for Joe. Suggestion: add "Tentacle LA requires descending to within 10 ft" to the beholder's start-of-turn checklist. Minor clarification. |

**FEEL-R327-03 — First-round DR permadeath: 75% chance the encounter ends in 2v1 before Marwen acts**

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Mechanic | With +9 vs Marwen's AC 15, DR has a 75% hit rate. If it hits on initiative round 1 before Marwen has acted, she dies without having had a turn. Combined with the 70.4% multiattack incapacitation probability from prior analysis, there is roughly a 70-75% chance the party is effectively down to 2 members by the end of round 1 (before or after Marwen's first turn depending on initiative). |
| Feel note | This is mechanically correct for a CR 13 vs level-5 party (the party is meant to lose). The issue is whether round 1 permadeath before any PC action feels different from round 2-3 permadeath after PCs have had some agency. R326's FEEL-R326-01 already flagged the Tentacle LA auto-crit kill (PCs downed before their death save); this is the equivalent for initial engagement. |
| Positive framing | The 75% first-round DR scenario can serve as a strong table moment: "The Hunger Below's first act is to erase the wizard completely. The party now fights to survive, not to win." This is consistent with the encounter's narrative premise. |
| Question for Joe | Is the DM expected to use DR on Marwen on round 1, or to use multiattack (slightly lower EV but gives Marwen a turn to act before going down)? The tactics section says "Prioritizes disintegration of spellcasters" which implies DR first. But this creates a 75% chance of instant-permadeath that some tables would find frustrating. A DM fiat "use multiattack round 1, save DR for round 2" gives Marwen one action of agency. |
| Status | Log for Joe (design call on round-1 tactics guidance in .md). |

**FEEL-R327-04 — Shrine-Drift drag on grappled creature: interaction undocumented**

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Mechanic | If beholder multiattacks and grapples Marwen, then uses Shrine-Drift (bonus action, move 30 ft through objects) — grapple rules say the grappler drags the grappled creature when it moves. Shrine-Drift passes through solid objects. Does Marwen pass through the stone column with the beholder? |
| Rules ambiguity | RAW: grapple says creature moves "with" the grappler. Shrine-Drift says the beholder can move through creatures/objects. No ruling on whether a dragged creature also passes through objects, or whether it collides with them (stopping the grapple's drag effect). |
| Impact at table | If Marwen is dragged through the altar pillar, she'd take forced object-collision damage (none stated for Marwen) or the grapple ends when she can't pass through solid matter. A DM needs a ruling here. Most naturally: Marwen can't pass through stone, so Shrine-Drift effectively ends the drag (or the beholder drops the grapple to drift freely). |
| Status | Log for Joe. Recommend adding a footnote to Shrine-Drift: "A grappled creature cannot be dragged through solid objects — Shrine-Drift effectively releases any grappled creature if the beholder moves through solid terrain." |
