### Phase B — Analysis

#### FEEL-F81-01 (NEW): Lair action range gap at chamber entrance

Default beholder position (hovering above shrine, far end of chamber) puts it at or near the 60-ft range limit of Unstable Ground and Manifest Thralls when the party enters at the threshold. In chambers ≥ 60 ft long, lair actions miss entirely in R1 unless:
1. The party advances on the first initiative segment, OR
2. The beholder repositions toward the party during R1 (on its own turn)

The beholder's fly speed is 30 ft. To close to within 60 ft from a 70-ft starting gap, the beholder must burn 10 ft of movement just to bring the party into lair-action range. This competes with the fly-down-to-melee-range movement needed for TL attacks.

**Impact on R1 protocol:** When the chamber is large (60+ ft) and the party hasn't advanced, the lair action on init 20 may effectively be wasted (targets out of range) even when the beholder calls it. DM should announce "the lair action fires but no targets are in range — a round is wasted" vs quietly skipping it.

**Recommendation (log only):** Add a lair range note — "Unstable Ground and Manifest Thralls both require targets within 60 ft of the beholder. If the beholder hasn't advanced toward the party by init 20, check range before picking a lair option. Void Eruption (20 ft of shrine) is not an option until PCs advance into the shrine zone (R3+ typically)."

#### FEEL-F81-02 (NEW): Manifest Thralls is dead weight for the Compass Edge party composition

In 2 rounds of simulation and all prior fires, Manifest Thralls never produced a meaningful result:
- Both thralls (Axe +3 or Dagger +3) vs AC 18 (Bazgar) / AC 19 (Sabriel) = 25% / 20% hit rate per reaction attack
- Marwen at AC 15 = 40% hit rate — marginally viable but a +3-to-hit weapon averaging 1d4+2 = 4.5 damage at 40% = expected 1.8 damage per thrall reaction attack
- 2 reaction attacks: expected ~3.6 damage total from Manifest Thralls vs. Unstable Ground at ~60% fail (DC 16 DEX −1 for Bazgar) = crowd control + multiattack synergy

Unstable Ground is strictly better than Manifest Thralls for every fight state in this encounter, **unless** a non-Compass-Edge party member has AC ≤ 14. The switch condition in FIX-BEL3-38-A says "landing 0 hits on a high-AC party (AC 18+)" — but with Marwen at AC 15, the technical condition is "not 0 hits." However, Marwen AC 15 at 40% thrall hit rate is below the "strongest when thralls can actually hit (AC ≤ 14)" bar.

**The FIX-BEL3-38-A rule has an AC-15 gap:** the switch condition ("AC 18+ → switch to UG") would NOT trigger for Marwen, leaving Manifest Thralls as a "valid" option that is in practice never worth it for this party. The rule should read "AC 16+ → prefer UG" to cover the full Compass Edge AC profile.

**This is a log-only feel issue (design call — Joe may decide AC threshold).**

#### STD-A AR cone range miss (new pattern)

In R1, STD-A advanced 30 ft toward the party but was still 30 ft from the closest PC — outside the 15-ft AR cone. AR was never fired until STD-B reached cone range on the same turn. This creates a **soft stagger that isn't in the stagger rules**: both STDs advance R1 but often only one (the faster/closer) can fire AR that round because cone range requires the STD to be within 15 ft of the target. The second STD, arriving at the same 30-ft position, also misses the cone. Neither fires AR R1 in this configuration.

Consequence: both STDs' AR barrages are delayed to R2. R1 is effectively a positioning turn for STDs.

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to STD tactics — "In R1, if the party is at the vault entrance (60 ft from shrine), STDs must spend their full 30+ ft move to close before AR cone reaches targets. Unless the party has advanced toward the shrine, expect AR to fire R2 at earliest."

#### Maw auto-crit downing Sabriel pre-turn (FIX-FC40-A exception case)

This run confirmed: beholder's R1 multiattack can one-shot Sabriel (from 44 HP) via TL grapple + Maw auto-crit (avg 40 damage), eliminating the DD window before she acts. The FIX-FC40-A exception ("if Sabriel goes down before her R1 turn... shift to reactive LAs immediately") correctly handles this. No new finding here — confirmed behavior from prior fires.
