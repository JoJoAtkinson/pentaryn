**Lair action (init 20):** Unstable Ground on Bazgar (DC 16 DEX). Bazgar DEX save +2 (estimated), needs 14+, P(fail) ≈ 35%. If prone: disadvantage on melee attacks until he spends movement to stand. Minor effect; Bazgar was probably going to attack at range or advance anyway.

**Beholder turn (init 17):**

| Maw (if Marwen grappled) | Marwen (AC 15) | +7 | 65% | 22 | 14.3 |

Expected multiattack damage: **30.4** (spread across two targets; Marwen takes up to ~36 expected, Bazgar ~7).

Bonus action — Shrine-Drift: positions beholder within 30 ft of Sabriel for potential Drain Divinity later. No cost beyond the bonus action.

**Round 1 LA spend options:**

*Option A: Three Tentacle LAs (1+1+1)*

- After Marwen's turn: Tentacle vs Marwen (AC 15), 65% hit, avg 14. Expected: **9.1**
- After Bazgar's turn: Tentacle vs Bazgar (AC 18), 50% hit, avg 14. Expected: **7.0**
- After Sabriel's turn: Tentacle vs Sabriel (AC 19), 45% hit, avg 14. Expected: **6.3**
- Total LA expected damage: **22.4**
- Total R1 expected damage (multiattack + LAs): **52.8** = 42% of 125 HP party pool

*Option B: Void Ray + Tentacle (2+1)*

- Void Ray (2 LA) vs Marwen at end of her turn: DC 16 DEX save. Marwen DEX +2 estimated → needs 14 on d20, P(fail) ≈ 35%. VR expected: (0.35 × 22) + (0.65 × 11) = 7.7 + 7.2 = **14.9**. Saves reduce VR to roughly same EV as a single Tentacle. Not worth 2 LA in R1 before anyone is bloodied.
- Remaining 1 LA → Tentacle vs another PC: **~7**
- Total LA expected: **21.9** — marginally worse than Option A and uses 2 LA on one target.

*Option C: Drain Divinity (3 LA)*

Uses all 3 LA on Sabriel at end of her turn. Sabriel CHA save: Paladin 5 with proficiency = CHA mod + PB. Estimated CHA +2 + PB +3 = +5. DC 16 → needs 11, P(fail) = 50%.
- If fails: Sabriel loses one 3rd-level spell slot (most valuable remaining). Beholder gains 6 THP (negligible vs 110 HP total).
- If succeeds: no effect.
- Zero LA damage this round. Zero Tentacle/Move use.
- Expected damage from DD: 0. Expected slot disruption: 0.5 spell slots (probabilistic).

DD costs the beholder ~22 expected damage this round to strip (on average) 0.5 spell slots from Sabriel. Against a paladin who has 2× 3rd-level slots, stripping one has meaningful narrative weight but low combat impact in round 1 — Sabriel hasn't yet cast anything worth stopping. DD value increases in R2+ when Sabriel has spent her healing resources and DD strips the last available slot.

**Round 1 verdict: Three Tentacles (Option A) dominates Round 1.** DD is dead weight in R1.

**Move LA analysis:**

The beholder has a bonus action Shrine-Drift (fly 30 ft through objects) and a full-speed action (fly 30 ft hover). The Move LA (1 LA, fly up to 30 ft) replicates what Shrine-Drift does as a bonus action — cover distance — but costs a legendary action. 

Optimal positioning: the beholder should use Shrine-Drift to maintain ≤30 ft from Sabriel for Drain Divinity threat while staying above the melee. This is free (bonus action). The Move LA becomes relevant only if:
1. The beholder is out of position AND already used its bonus action on something else (no current ability competes with Shrine-Drift for the bonus action slot — Compel Thrall is also bonus action but rarely fires in a 3-PC fight).
2. The beholder needs to retreat more than 30 ft per turn when below 30 HP.

**Move LA never fires in a standard 3-round fight against Compass Edge.** It's available as a tactical fallback but has no optimal turn where it outperforms Tentacle.

### Phase B findings summary

**FEEL-LA-R270-01 (NEW): Move LA is dead weight in the Compass Edge scenario.**

The Move LA (1 LA, fly 30 ft) is completely eclipsed by Shrine-Drift (bonus action, fly 30 ft through objects). The beholder's bonus action has no competing use in a 3-PC fight with no thralls to Compel — Shrine-Drift handles all repositioning for free. A DM who plays optimally will spend R1, R2, R3 as T+T+T or VR+T and never use Move. The presence of Move on the LA list creates a false choice: it looks like it enables repositioning flexibility but the bonus action covers that niche already. Result: 4 LA options → 3 viable options (Tentacle, Void Ray, Drain Divinity), and the simplest (Tentacle × 3) is optimal for 2 of 3 rounds.

*Joe's options:* (1) Remove Move LA and replace with something that doesn't overlap Shrine-Drift (e.g., a free grapple-without-attack that pins a target it's already adjacent to). (2) Remove Shrine-Drift as a bonus action (makes Move LA the only free repositioning option — costs LAs appropriately). (3) Leave as-is (DMs who don't read the bonus action carefully will use Move LA occasionally by accident, with no mechanical harm).

**FEEL-LA-R270-02 (NEW): LA economy delivers a reliable ~52 damage/round floor regardless of action choices — Drain Divinity is a soft denial tool, not a damage option.**

Against Compass Edge, the beholder deals expected 30–35 damage from Multiattack and 22 damage from three Tentacle LAs = **~52 expected damage per round** with 3 live PCs. Party HP pool: 125. Expected time-to-wipe: 2.4 rounds. Drain Divinity replaces this with a 50% chance of stripping one spell slot — at Sabriel's level (3rd-level max slot), the striped resource is worth perhaps 1-2 turns of healing denied, which recovers ~12–20 HP per cast. DD expected denial value: 0.5 × 15 = 7.5 HP equivalent per round. vs. Tentacle×3 at 22 damage EV. **DD is strictly worse than Tentacle×3 in terms of round-by-round fight outcome.** DD only matters as a "break Sabriel's healing chain" disruption in multi-round encounters (4+) where spell slots matter — this fight ends in 2-3 rounds. 

*Interaction with FEEL-R261-01 (DD strictly dominated in ≤2-round fights):* confirmed at level 5, 3-PC scale. DD is situationally worth it in Round 3+ only if Sabriel has successfully healed a party member back above 15 HP and the beholder can't finish them this turn via damage.

**Beholder 1-target focus-fire is optimal.** The math strongly favors focusing Marwen (AC 15, 32 HP, squishy caster) in Round 1: drop her before she acts to eliminate ~33% of party spell economy. T×2 + Maw on Marwen gives ~36 expected damage = ~100% kill probability on average rolls. The Compass Edge never reaches its full action potential in a beholder-initiative scenario.
