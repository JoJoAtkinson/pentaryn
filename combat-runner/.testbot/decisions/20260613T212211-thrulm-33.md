### Trigger timing analysis

The scenario types "-15" (beholder takes 15 damage) and expects `reaction_prompts_min: 1`. With `trigger.event: "damage"`, this works — the beholder taking damage fires the reaction prompt. **The prompt confirms Antireality is available as a reminder.**

Problem: the damage event fires AFTER Bazgar's attack has already hit. The effect text says "Declare BEFORE the attack roll resolves" — physically impossible if the DM only sees the prompt after the hit. At the table, the correct workflow is:

1. Bazgar's player announces an attack on the beholder.
2. DM remembers to check Antireality (from .md knowledge, NOT from the prompt).
3. DM applies the reaction before Bazgar rolls.
4. Bazgar rolls at disadvantage (intended) or DM mentally treats AC as 19 (current DB).
5. After combat resolves, the runner's "-15" damages the beholder, and the Antireality prompt fires as a retroactive reminder.

The prompt is therefore a **post-hoc reminder, not a real-time reaction gate.** This is a workflow limitation of the combat runner that cannot be fixed without adding a pre-roll intercept event. Current guidance for DMs:

> When Bazgar (or any PC) declares a melee or ranged attack against the beholder, PAUSE before Bazgar rolls. Ask: "Is Antireality available?" (Has the beholder already used it this round?) If yes, declare Antireality immediately; Bazgar rolls with disadvantage. The "-15" damage input and the GUI prompt confirm the round's Antireality state, but should NOT be the trigger for the decision.

### Key findings from this Phase B

**FEEL-F33-01 (High — Antireality timing gap):** The Antireality prompt fires post-hit. DMs who rely on the GUI prompt will apply the reaction too late (or not at all). The only reliable use of Antireality is for DMs who know the .md well enough to pre-empt the GUI. Recommend adding a DM note to the .md explicitly saying "DO NOT wait for the combat runner prompt — declare Antireality when the attack is announced, before the dice roll." (Minor .md edit, safe to apply; see Phase C.)

**FEEL-F33-02 (Medium — +2 AC underwhelms at table):** The "+2 AC" bug produces a reaction that barely registers. Bazgar hits on 10+ vs AC 17 (55%) and on 12+ vs AC 19 (45%): at-table, the DM and players barely notice the 10pp hit-rate drop. Disadvantage would produce a dramatically different feel: even experienced players notice rolling two d20s and taking the lower.

**FEEL-F33-03 (Low — Antireality on Sabriel: diminishing returns):** Against Sabriel (+7 attack, AC 18 beholder → need 10+, same 55%), Antireality with disadvantage brings her to 30.25% — same math as Bazgar. But Sabriel has fewer attacks per turn than Action-Surge Bazgar and may be more likely to retreat due to Drain Divinity pressure. The beholder's one Antireality reaction is best spent on Bazgar (higher sustained attack count) over Sabriel or Marwen.
