### AUTOMATION-GAP-R302-02 — Unstable Form has no DB representation

**Source:** `npcs/shrine-touched-derro.md`, scenario `thrulm-shrine-touched-retaliation`

Unstable Form grants **advantage on all attack rolls** (including reactions) when the shrine-touched takes 10+ damage in a single turn. This triggers frequently at level 5 (a single Fire Bolt at fire-vulnerability can deal 30+ damage).

There is no DB row, no `watch_suggestion`, and no `start_of_turn` prompt for this effect. The DM must:
1. Remember the 10-damage threshold was crossed
2. Apply advantage to the reaction attack (Oath-Breaking Retaliation) that fires **immediately** on the damage event — before the shrine-touched's own turn
3. Apply advantage again for multiattack on the shrine-touched's own turn

Missing this advantage is very likely at the table, especially since the reaction fires instantaneously during the enemy's turn.

**Recommendation:** Add a `start_of_turn` utility action (or a watch_suggestion via the `action_executed` event on the shrine-touched taking damage) that reminds DM: "Shrine-Touched took 10+ damage → ADVANTAGE on all attacks until end of next turn." This is the most common mid-battle trigger that has no automation at all.

**Not auto-fixed** (requires `combat_action_upsert` with interactive MCP session and `validate` run).

### FEEL-BUG-R302-03 — Oath-Breaking Retaliation self-damage is inert

**Source:** `npcs/shrine-touched-derro.md`

The reaction reads: "the derro takes 2 (1d4) psychic damage as the shrine's power consumes it from within."

The shrine-touched has **resistance to psychic damage** (listed under Damage Resistances). Self-damage from Oath-Breaking Retaliation is therefore halved → **average ~1 HP actual self-cost per use**.

The reaction can fire on every incoming hit (once per round). With 1 HP self-cost, it is effectively free — no resource trade-off. The flavor intent ("consumed from within") is mechanically invisible.

**Options:**
1. Change self-damage to necrotic (the creature is also resistant to necrotic — same problem).
2. Change self-damage to psychic damage that **ignores the creature's own resistance** (add a note: "this damage ignores the shrine-touched's psychic resistance").
3. Change self-damage to force or fire (no resistance/immunity → actual cost).
4. Remove the self-damage and add a different cost (e.g., the shrine-touched loses 5 ft speed until end of its next turn).

**Recommendation:** Option 3 (force) is cleanest — no new keywords, no resistance exceptions, and thematically "the shrine's power forces something real." Magnitude 1d4 force avg 2.5 would be a meaningful cost on a 45 HP creature using this reaction multiple times.

**Not auto-fixed** (changes dice type — would need dice expression edit, which is outside auto-fix scope per hard rules. Joe must decide.)

### DC-R302-05 — Shrine-Touched Ancient Resonance DC 14 vs formula DC 13

**Source:** `npcs/shrine-touched-derro.md`

Ancient Resonance save DC is **14**. Standard monster formula: 8 + PB + primary ability modifier.

With PB +3:
- If based on CON (+2): 8 + 3 + 2 = **13** (off by +1)
- If based on DEX (+2): 8 + 3 + 2 = **13** (off by +1)
- If based on WIS (-1): 8 + 3 + (-1) = **10** (implausible)

DC 14 is +1 above any formula derivation. This mirrors BUG-R314-02 (Shardcaller Shard-Barrage DC 13 vs formula 12). Both are off by the same amount (+1).

The cheat sheet correctly lists DC 14 (consistent with the stat block), so there's no cheat-sheet drift here. The question is whether both DCs were intentionally bumped up by 1 (a design choice for lethality at level 5) or whether both represent a systematic formula error.

**Recommendation:** Given both the shardcaller (BUG-R314-02) and shrine-touched are off by exactly +1, this looks like a systematic design choice rather than arithmetic error — the designer may have used DC 8+PB+mod+1 for these creatures. Flag for Joe to confirm: if intentional, add a comment in both stat blocks; if not, correct both to the formula value.

**Not auto-fixed** (DC is not a damage average — not in auto-fix scope).

## Phase B — Scenario Simulation

**Scenario:** `thrulm-shrine-touched-retaliation` (counter 83)
**Party:** Compass Edge level 5 — Bazgar (Fighter/Battlemaster 5, HP 49 AC 18), Marwen (Wizard 5, HP 32 AC 15), Sabriel (divine martial 5, HP 44 AC 19)

### Round 1 play-through (median rolls)

**Initiative:** Marwen 12 / Shrine-Touched 12 (tie → party first) / Sabriel 11 / Beholder 8 / Bazgar 5

**Marwen:** Fire Bolt (+5 ranged spell vs AC 16 → hits ≥11). Avg 3d10=16 fire × 2 (vulnerability) = **32 fire damage**. Shrine-Touched HP: 45→13. Unstable Form triggers (took 32 > 10 damage). **[AUTOMATION GAP: no DB prompt; DM must manually track advantage]**

**Oath-Breaking Retaliation (immediate reaction):** Shrine-Axe +4 vs Marwen AC 15. **[DB-BUG-R156-01: no embedded dice in DB row → narration only, no dice output]** If resolved manually: hits on 11+, avg 11 damage to Marwen (HP 32→21). Self-cost: 1d4 psychic halved = ~1 HP. Reaction is effectively free.

**Shrine-Touched turn (Unstable Form, advantage active):** Multiattack, 2×Shrine-Axe with advantage vs Marwen (AC 15). P(hit) per attack with advantage ≈ 81%. Likely both hit → 22 damage to Marwen (HP 21→down). **Ancient Resonance (if recharged):** would have already finished Marwen. Note: at this point, Marwen is downed with advantage attacks hitting nearly every time in a single burst turn.

**Beholder (LA):** Drain Divinity (3 LA) vs Sabriel — DC 16 CHA save, ~50% fail. On fail: removes Sabriel's 3rd-level slot; beholder gains 6 THP. **All 3 LA used. Void Ray and Move unused — Drain Divinity is always dominant.**

### Feel assessment

1. **Fair-lethal, not unfair:** The shrine-touched bursts hard in the one turn before Sabriel finishes it (fire vulnerability means it dies to a 2nd attack). The burst is "punishing but comprehensible" — Marwen chose to stand in melee range of a melee-reaction creature.

2. **One-round down risk for Marwen is high** if Unstable Form advantage is applied correctly. If DM forgets Unstable Form (the AUTOMATION GAP), Marwen survives longer — the encounter plays EASIER without the correct rule.

3. **Drain Divinity dominance confirmed** for this encounter setup: Sabriel on the field → beholder spends all 3 LA on Drain Divinity every round. Void Ray never fires while Sabriel is standing. This is the "one-note legendary strategy" open issue.

4. **No permanent-death risk in this slice** (Marwen downed but not disintegrated — the beholder didn't use Disintegration Ray here). FEEL-003 (permanent death concern) remains an open design flag for the beholder's Disintegration Ray scenario.

5. **Counterplay exists:** Sabriel can use radiant damage against the shrine-touched (vulnerability) to one-shot it before it acts, removing the burst threat entirely. Bazgar can position to draw the shrine-touched's reaction instead. The encounter rewards tactical positioning — this is good design.
