## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `disintegration-threat`

**Rotation index 1 of 8.** This slice interrogates the beholder's Disintegration Ray deployment: optimal timing, permadeath thresholds against Compass Edge, and the round-2 LA trade-off (Drain Divinity vs Void Ray).

---

### Setup

**Scenario premise:** Round 2 of combat. Round 1 ended with the beholder using Multiattack (did not use DR round 1): Marwen took 2 Tentacle hits (2×14=28 damage) and is grappled ×2 → **Marwen: 4 HP, grappled**. Bazgar took a Tentacle LA hit for 14 → **Bazgar: 35 HP, grappled ×1**. Sabriel was untouched (beholder targeted casters) → **Sabriel: 44 HP**.

Beholder: 110 HP, full resources: DR available (not used round 1), VS available, 3 LA, 3 LR, Antireality available, Void-Feeding active.

**Initiative (assumed from round 1 results):**
- Beholder: 20
- Sabriel: 15 (divine martial)
- Bazgar: 12 (Fighter)
- Marwen: 8 (Wizard — grappled, speed 0)

---

### Question 1: Does the beholder use DR on Marwen (4 HP) at start of round 2?

**Against Marwen at 4 HP:**
- DR: +9 vs AC 15 → 75% hit → 45 force damage → Marwen from 4 HP to 0 HP → **DISINTEGRATED** (DR text, not massive damage rule). Marwen cannot be restored except by True Resurrection or Wish.
- Multiattack (2 TL + Maw): P(at least one TL hits) = 1-(0.25)² = 93.75%. Any TL hit alone drops Marwen to 0 HP. She goes to 0 HP from the first hit, then subsequent hits deal damage to a 0-HP target → death save failures. At 0 HP she is NOT disintegrated.

**Optimal beholder reasoning (INT 17):**
1. Multiattack drops Marwen to 0 HP on 93.75% and inflicts death save failures from follow-on hits, without consuming DR.
2. DR on a 4 HP target is wasteful — it guarantees permadeath on 75%, but multiattack achieves incapacitation at 93.75% while preserving DR for a fresh, full-HP target.
3. Key asymmetry: if Sabriel stabilizes Marwen (Medicine DC 10 or Healing Word), Marwen comes back. Permadeath via DR prevents this. But Sabriel must use her action to stabilize — that is one action NOT spent attacking the beholder.

**Verdict:** The beholder should use Multiattack (not DR) on Marwen at 4 HP. The 75% DR hit rate is worse than the 93.75% multiattack incapacitation rate, and DR is more valuable against a full-HP target.

**BUT:** if the beholder's intelligence tells it that Sabriel will use Healing Word (bonus action) to immediately restore Marwen, the beholder should prioritize DR for permadeath over multiattack. This creates a genuine tactical dilemma: does the DM know whether Sabriel has Healing Word? If she does, DR is correct; if she doesn't (or has spent those slots), multiattack is correct.

---

### Question 2: DR permadeath thresholds for Compass Edge

**Disintegration Ray: +9 vs AC, 10d8 (avg 45) force damage. "If this damage reduces the target to 0 HP, the target is disintegrated."**

| PC | Max HP | AC | P(DR hits) | P(disintegration on hit) | Notes |
|----|--------|-----|------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Marwen | 32 | 15 | 75% | 100% (45 > 32 at full HP) | Full-HP Marwen is always disintegrated on a DR hit |
| Sabriel | 44 | 19 | 55% | 100% (45 > 44, barely) | Full-HP Sabriel is always disintegrated on a DR hit |
| Bazgar | 49 | 18 | 60% | 0% on non-crit (45 < 49) | At full HP, DR hits leave Bazgar at 4 HP — NOT disintegrated |

**Bazgar's safety window:** Bazgar (49 HP) cannot be disintegrated by DR on a non-critical hit while at full HP. The DR leaves him at 4 HP. Only a critical hit (20d8 = avg 90, guaranteed 0 HP) disintegrates him. P(DR crit on Bazgar) = 5%. Bazgar is practically immune to disintegration until he drops below 45 HP.

**NEW FINDING — FEEL-R328-02:** The Compass Edge has an implicit "disintegration risk" ordering that the DM should track:

1. **Marwen (32 HP):** Always disintegrated on any DR hit from full HP. Priority 1 DR target.
2. **Sabriel (44 HP):** Always disintegrated on any DR hit from full HP. Priority 2 DR target.
3. **Bazgar (49 HP):** Not disintegrated from full HP (non-crit). DM should wait until Bazgar is below 45 HP before using DR on him for permadeath effect.

This creates clear DR targeting priority. A DM who doesn't know this might "waste" DR on a full-HP Bazgar and fail to disintegrate him — producing only 45 damage (leaving him at 4 HP) instead of the permadeath the DM expected.

**Recommendation:** Add "DR permadeath threshold" note to tactics section or cheat sheet. "DR average (45) exceeds Marwen and Sabriel's max HP — always disintegrates on hit from full HP. Bazgar (49 HP) survives a non-crit hit — wait until he's below 45 HP for DR permadeath."

### Question 3: Round 2 LA economy — Drain Divinity vs Void Ray

After beholder uses Multiattack (main action) on Marwen in round 2, it has 3 LA available. Two options:

**Option A: Drain Divinity (3 LA, all remaining)**
- Range: 30 ft. If beholder is at altitude 40-60 ft, must descend first (uses movement). Costs all 3 LA this round.
- Effect: DC 16 CHA save. Sabriel (divine martial, estimated CHA +4) needs 12+ to succeed → 45% success → **55% chance she loses her highest slot.**
- Damage to beholder: 0. THP gain: 2 × slot level. At level 5, highest slot is 3rd-level → 6 THP on success. 55% × 6 = **3.3 expected THP.**
- After DD: 0 LA remaining. No Void Ray, no Tentacle LA.

**Option B: Void Ray (2 LA) + Move or Tentacle (1 LA)**
- Void Ray: DC 16 DEX save. Sabriel (divine martial, DEX likely +1) needs 15+ to succeed → 30% success → **70% chance 22 force damage.**
  - Expected damage to Sabriel: 0.70 × 22 + 0.30 × 11 = 15.4 + 3.3 = **18.7 expected damage.**
- Remaining 1 LA: either Move (reposition) or Tentacle (if beholder descends to within 10 ft of Bazgar).
  - If Tentacle on Bazgar: +9 vs AC 18 → 60% hit → 3d6+4=14.5 → **8.7 expected damage + grapple.**
  - Total Option B expected damage: 18.7 + 8.7 = **27.4 expected damage.**

**Comparison:**

| Option | LA cost | Expected Damage | Effect on Sabriel | Beholder Gains |
|--------|---------|-----------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Drain Divinity | 3 (all) | 0 | 55%: loses 1 slot | 3.3 expected THP |
| Void Ray + Tentacle | 3 (split) | 27.4 expected | 70%: 18.7 avg damage | 0 THP |

**Void Ray + Tentacle is 8.3× more efficient in expected damage output.** DD at level 5 is outcompeted in every scenario except:
1. Sabriel has a 4th or 5th-level slot (THP would be 8 or 10 — still far below VR's expected damage)
2. The beholder is at full HP and needs THP as a damage buffer (110 HP — 6 THP makes no meaningful difference)
3. Sabriel's next action is Banishment or Dispel Magic — DD removes that specific threat

**NEW FINDING — FEEL-R328-01:** Drain Divinity (3 LA) is not competitively costed versus Void Ray (2 LA) at any level-5 scenario configuration. The math confirms FEEL-OPEN-01. Options for Joe:
- Reduce DD to 2 LA (making it competitive in a 1+2=3 budget with Tentacle)
- Add a necrotic/psychic damage component to DD (e.g. "the target takes 2d6 psychic damage as the divine power tears free") so it deals damage AND drains
- Keep at 3 LA but add a 2nd use effect (e.g. nearby thralls heal, or the beholder regains a use of Legendary Resistance) to justify the premium cost

### Simulated Round 2 (optimal beholder play)

**Beholder's turn (round 2):**

Main action: Multiattack on Marwen (4 HP, grappled ×2, AC 15)
- TL1: +9 vs 15 → hit on 6+ (75%). Result: **HIT (assume average)**. Damage: 3d6+4=14. Marwen: 4-14 = **0 HP** (incapacitated, death saves start next turn).
- TL2 on 0-HP Marwen: hit (75%) → 1 death save failure.
- Maw on 0-HP Marwen: hit (75%) → 1 death save failure.

Expected: Marwen at 0 HP with 2 death save failures after one multiattack. She needs just 1 more failure (either from natural death save roll or third attack hit) to die.

**P(Marwen accumulates 3 death save failures from round 2 multiattack):**
- Need TL1 to drop her to 0, then TL2 AND Maw both hit: (0.75) × (0.75)² = 42.2%
- Need TL1+TL2 drop to 0, then Maw hits (same calc): also ~42.2%
- With 2 failures already, Marwen's next death save roll: P(rolls 1-9 = fail) = 45%. Combined P(Marwen dies this round via 3 failures): ~42.2% × 1 + ~14.1% × 0.45 + ... ≈ **50-60% P(Marwen dies in round 2 without ever taking a turn this round.**

Bonus action: Shrine-Drift — reposition to cover (optional).

**LA window (round 2, after Sabriel's turn):**
- Void Ray (2 LA) on Sabriel: 70% → 22 damage → Sabriel: ~22 HP.
- Tentacle (1 LA) on Bazgar: 60% → 14 damage + grapple → Bazgar: ~21 HP.

**NEW FINDING — FEEL-R328-03:** Round 2 pacing — by the end of round 2 (beholder's main action + LA), the party state is:
- Marwen: 0 HP, 2 death save failures (dies on next failed save — 45% at start of her turn if she gets one)
- Sabriel: ~22 HP
- Bazgar: ~21 HP (grappled)
- Beholder: 110 HP, DR on recharge (33% per round), VS available, Antireality ready

The party's total resource depletion in 2 rounds: ~50% HP on all PCs, Marwen effectively dead. This is consistent with "meant to lose." At DPR ~12 combined (from altitude, with Antireality), the party will deal ~24 damage to the beholder in 2 rounds of tries — roughly 22% of its HP. The beholder needs ~3-4 more rounds to finish the remaining 2 PCs with its LA economy.

**FEEL-R328-03:** DM tip missing in tactics: "If Marwen is already at ≤14 HP (any single Tentacle Lash kills her), use Multiattack rather than DR. Save DR for fresh targets. Multiattack incapacitates at 93.75% vs DR's 75% hit rate, and costs no recharge."

### Sabriel's counter-strategy analysis

Sabriel (divine martial 5, 44 HP, AC 19) faces the beholder at altitude. Her options:

1. **Sacred Flame (cantrip, CON save DC ~14):** Beholder CON is +3 → +3 base, no CON save proficiency listed → needs 11+ on d20 → 50% success rate. Expected: 0.50 × 9 (2d8) = **4.5 expected damage.** Antireality-immune (no attack roll). This is her best ranged option.
   - Wait: R327 found beholder WIS save is +7. But Sacred Flame targets CON, not WIS. The beholder has CON +3, no CON save proficiency noted → DC 14 → needs 11+ → 50% fail rate → **4.5 expected damage.** Better than the R327 WIS-save calculation.
   - **Correction from R327-FEEL-01:** Sacred Flame targets CON (DC ~14, beholder needs 11+ → 50% save) not WIS. Expected damage is 4.5, not 2.7. FEEL-R327-01 understated Sabriel's cantrip effectiveness.

2. **Guiding Bolt (+6 vs AC 17 → 56% hit; Antireality → 31% hit):** Expected: 0.31 × 14 = **4.3 expected damage** (slot-consuming, Antireality punished). Less efficient than Sacred Flame cantrip.

3. **Lay on Hands / Healing Word on Marwen:** If Marwen survives to death save territory, Sabriel can stabilize with Medicine (bonus action, DC 10) or Healing Word (bonus action, 1d4+3). This denies the beholder's kill — but the beholder can respond by using DR next round to disintegrate a stabilized-but-wounded Marwen.

**Sabriel's correct play:** Sacred Flame (cantrip) every round from range + Healing Word on Marwen if she survives to death saves. Don't burn spell slots into Antireality disadvantage.
