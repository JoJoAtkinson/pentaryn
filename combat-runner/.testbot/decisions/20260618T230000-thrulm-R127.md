### Feel assessment — thrulm-thrall-compelled-move scenario

**Scenario mechanics clean:**  
Both test questions confirmed working: hand_axe_weak correct, compelled_movement correct as bonus-action utility. The two-tab workflow (beholder fires compel_thrall on its tab, DM switches to thrall tab and fires compelled_movement) is mechanically correct but slightly confusing — the "no saving throw" note in the thrall's DB effect clarifies intent. No bugs; usability acceptable.

**FEEL-12 (new) — BM Maneuver dead weight vs beholder immunity suite:**  
Bazgar (Battlemaster 5) has 4 maneuver choices that are effectively useless against the beholder: Menacing Attack (frightened immune), Tripping Attack (prone immune), Pushing Attack (restrained immune... wait, this would just push them — not restrained, just movement, so NOT wasted — Pushing Attack IS viable to reposition beholder away from Sabriel). Disarming Attack (beholder has no held weapon — unclear). Commander's Strike (valid, gives Sabriel an attack). Precision Attack (valid, +1d8 to attack roll).

Effective maneuvers vs beholder: Precision Attack, Commander's Strike, Pushing Attack (repositioning only), Goading Attack (disadvantage on attacks vs non-Bazgar — POTENTIALLY IMMUNE if frightened immunity blocks; Goading imposes disadvantage, not frightened, so likely VALID — needs ruling).

Net: ~50% of BM maneuvers are partially or fully wasted vs beholder's immunity suite. This is a notable encounter feel issue: the Fighter's signature subclass feature is half-dead. Flag for Joe as FEEL-12.

**Carry-forward: Drain Divinity vs Void Ray decision still open.** At L5, Void Ray+Tentacle (22+13 force+bludg avg = 35 in round, two creatures) is better action economy than Drain Divinity (4 THP + one slot stolen). The beholder's optimal play at this party level never uses Drain Divinity until late game. Known-state concern about "degenerate every round" is less urgent at L5 (confirmed by R126 FEEL-10 analysis, confirmed again here).
