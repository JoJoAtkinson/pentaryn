## Phase B — Scenario: `retreat-threshold` (thrulm-rotation index 6)

*Slice definition: Simulate combat from round 1 to the point where the beholder crosses below 30 HP.
Evaluate the retreat mechanic: when does it trigger, how it manifests in the GUI/runner, what happens
to the encounter once the beholder retreats, and whether the "meant to lose" design holds up when
the escape hatch fires.*

---

### Setup

Party (Compass Edge, Level 5):
- Bazgar: Orc Fighter (Battlemaster) 5, HP 49, AC 18, assumed STR +3, PB +3 → melee attack +6
- Marwen: Wizard 5, HP 32, AC 15 (Mage Armor), assumed INT +3, spell save DC 14
- Sabriel: divine martial 5, HP 44, AC 19, assumed STR +3 + PB +3 → attack +6; Lay on Hands 25 HP pool

Beholder: HP 110, AC 17, Void-Feeding active in chamber (+1 all attack/damage rolls)
Derro support present: Rager ×1 (HP 52, AC 16), Deep Watch ×2 (HP 27, AC 15).
Thrall Derro ×2 (HP 22, AC 14) positioned near altar.

### DPR Calculation — Party vs Beholder

#### Bazgar (Battlemaster Fighter 5, 2 attacks/round)

Weapon: longsword (1d8) or battleaxe (1d8/1d10). Beholder has resistance to nonmagic B/P/S weapons.
Assumed non-magical weapon: damage halved after modifiers.

- Per attack: +6 vs AC 17 → P(hit) = 50%. 1d8+3 = avg 7.5. Halved by resistance = 3.75.
- 2 attacks/round: EV = 2 × 0.50 × 3.75 = **3.75 EV per round** (heavily suppressed by resistance)
- With Action Surge (round 1 only): 4 attacks → 7.5 EV that round
- With Battlemaster maneuver die (1d8 extra, counted as damage): maneuvers also halved → +2 per hit
- **Effective DPR: ~5–8 (round 1 with surge and maneuvers), 3–4 after**

*Finding: Bazgar is largely irrelevant vs the beholder unless he carries a magical/sanctified weapon.
Without one, his contribution is ~4 EV/round — equivalent to one Thrall Derro attack (3 avg, not resisted
because Thrall attacks are non-magic too... wait, the Thrall would also be resisted). This is a significant
feel problem: Bazgar is the tank and front-liner but does nearly nothing to the beholder.*

#### Marwen (Wizard 5)

Marwen's primary contributions: Fireball (8d6 fire, DC 14), Magic Missile (force, auto-hit), Scorching Ray.

- **Fireball (L3):** 8d6 = 28 avg fire damage. Beholder DEX save +6 vs DC 14.
  P(fail DC 14) = P(roll 8 or less) = 8/20 = 40%.
  EV = 0.40 × 28 + 0.60 × 14 = 11.2 + 8.4 = **19.6 EV** (fire, no resistance). 2× L3 slots.
- **Magic Missile (L1, auto-hit):** 3 missiles × 1d4+1 = 3 × 3.5 = 10.5 force damage, no resistance.
  At L2: 4 × 3.5 = 14. At L3 (if not used on Fireball): 5 × 3.5 = 17.5.
  **EV per L1 cast: 10.5**
- **Scorching Ray (L2):** 3 rays, each +5 vs AC 17, hit → 2d6 fire.
  P(hit) = 9/20 = 45%. EV per ray = 0.45 × 7 = 3.15. 3 rays = **9.45 EV**.

Marwen's slot budget (L5 wizard): L1×4, L2×3, L3×2.
Expected Marwen contribution over ~4 rounds before she goes down:
- Round 1: Fireball (19.6 EV)
- Round 2: May be at 0 HP from multiattack. If alive: Magic Missile L2 (14 EV)
- Rounds 3–4: If healed back, Scorching Ray or MM.
**Total Marwen contribution to beholder: ~35–50 damage (heavily caveated by death-risk).**

#### Sabriel (Divine Martial 5, assumed Paladin)

Paladin 5: 2 attacks/round (Extra Attack), +6 to hit, 1d8+3 longsword.
Beholder resistance: Sabriel's longsword is nonmagic → halved. UNLESS she uses Divine Smite.

- **Without smite (nonmagic):** +6 vs AC 17, P(hit) = 50%. 1d8+3 = 7.5, halved = 3.75. 2 attacks: **3.75 EV**.
- **With Divine Smite L1 (2d8 radiant):** Radiant damage is NOT halved (not B/P/S). Hit grants +9 radiant.
  Per smite hit: 3.75 slashing (halved) + 4.5 radiant = 8.25. 2 attacks, smite on first:
  1 smite hit EV: 0.50 × 8.25 = 4.125. Second attack no-smite: 0.50 × 3.75 = 1.875.
  **Total: ~6 EV per round with one smite.**
- **With Divine Smite L2 (3d8 radiant = +13.5 radiant):** First hit: 3.75 + 6.75 = 10.5. EV: 5.25.
  + second attack: 1.875. **Total: ~7.1 EV with L2 smite.**
- **Lay on Hands instead of attacking:** Sacrifices 0 EV but heals Marwen.

*Finding: Sabriel's damage to the beholder is also heavily suppressed. Even with smite she's contributing
~6–7 EV per round to the beholder (the radiant from smite helps but smite slots are limited at L5:
4×L1 + 2×L2 = 6 potential smites). Her smite budget runs dry fast.*

|-------|-----------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 1 | 7.5 (surge) | 19.6 (fireball) | 6.0 (smite) | **33.1** |

| 4 | 3.75 | 10.5 (MM L2) | 3.75 | **18** |
| Total | ~19 | ~45 | ~18.5 | **~82 over 4 rounds** |

**Conclusion: The party can theoretically deal ~82 damage to the beholder across 4 rounds — just barely
crossing the 80-HP threshold (110 - 30 = 80 needed) to trigger the retreat.**

However, this assumes:
1. Marwen survives long enough to fire Fireball (very uncertain: multiattack avg 36 vs her 32 HP).
2. Sabriel does not spend her entire action healing Marwen instead of attacking.
3. No Legendary Resistance used (beholder has 3/day — if party uses Banishment or Hold Person,
   some LA budget goes to LR saves rather than offensive LA).
4. Beholder focuses ONLY on the beholder (not clearing derro first to reduce action pressure).

**The retreat threshold is reachable in approximately 4 rounds under near-optimal party play.
Under realistic conditions (Marwen downed R1 and Sabriel healing), it probably takes 5–6+ rounds,
if it triggers at all before a total party failure.**

### Retreat Mechanic — Rules Audit

Per `beholder-thrulm.md`:
> *"If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block
> pursuit. If below 20 HP, it attempts to crush/disintegrate the party while fleeing."*

**Finding FEEL-026 (NEW): Retreat has no DB representation.**

There is no `retreat` action in the DB for `beholder-thrulm`. The combat runner will not prompt
the DM that retreat mode has triggered. The DM must:
1. Manually note when the beholder crosses 30 HP.
2. Manually change the beholder's behavior (move toward the lower shaft, send thralls to block).
3. Interpret whether "using thralls to block pursuit" means using Compel Thrall (bonus action) or
   some other mechanism.

**No automation gap workaround exists.** Unlike the FRIGHTENED rider on Void Scream (AUTOMATION-GAP-R281-01),
the retreat condition doesn't fire on any existing DB event. A `note` command in the GUI is the
only way for the DM to mark this state change. **Log for Joe: consider a `retreat` utility action
with a trigger reminder and narration.**

---

**Finding FEEL-027 (NEW): <30 HP and <20 HP behaviors are contradictory.**

- **<30 HP:** "retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit" → implies
  the beholder is moving AWAY from the party and not using attacks.
- **<20 HP:** "attempts to crush/disintegrate the party while fleeing" → implies offensive use of
  Disintegration Ray AND movement in the same turn.

These two thresholds have opposite intent (escape vs. last stand). The .md does not clarify:
- Does the beholder use its main action for Disintegration Ray while spending movement to flee?
- Does it stay in range to use Drain Divinity (which requires ≤30 ft from target) during retreat?
- Do Legendary Actions (Tentacle, Void Ray) continue during retreat?

A beholder that continues spending all 3 LA on Drain Divinity while retreating is mechanically
identical to a beholder that is NOT retreating, from Sabriel's perspective. **The retreat threshold
doesn't change the beholder's offensive output unless the DM actively decides to stop Drain Divinity.**

**Recommendation (log for Joe): Define exactly two behaviors:**
1. **Flee mode (<30 HP):** beholder uses all movement away from party each turn; main action = either
   Shrine-Drift (to phase through obstacles) or Disintegration Ray at range; no Drain Divinity (requires
   ≤30 ft range — beholder would have to stop fleeing to use it); LA = only Move or Void Ray (2 LA).
2. **Death gambit (<20 HP):** beholder stops retreating; turns to face; uses every remaining resource
   offensively, including any unspent LR; may use Disintegration Ray on whatever target is closest.

---

**Finding FEEL-028 (NEW): Thrall blocking is contingent on thralls surviving to round 4+.**

The Thrall Derro (HP 22 each) are extremely fragile. Against Compass Edge:
- Bazgar 2-attack multiattack: even at halved nonmagic damage, thralls have no resistance to B/P/S.
  Bazgar vs Thrall (AC 14, +6): P(hit) = 65%. 1d8+3 = 7.5 avg. 2 attacks = 2 × 0.65 × 7.5 = **9.75 EV/round**.
  Thrall dies in ~2.3 rounds from Bazgar alone.
- Marwen Scorching Ray vs Thrall: 3 rays × P(hit 65%) × 7 = **13.65 EV in one action.** Thrall dies round 1.
- Sabriel with longsword (nonmagic, no resistance for thrall): same as Bazgar.

Any PC that focuses a Thrall Derro for 1–2 rounds eliminates it. By round 4, both Thrall Derro are
almost certainly dead. **The retreat mechanic assumes living thralls as blockers, but the timeline
for thrall survival doesn't overlap with the beholder's retreat threshold (rounds 4–5+).**

**Exception:** If the beholder creates new thralls via Clay-Shaping (1 minute ritual) during combat,
they would be available. But Clay-Shaping requires the beholder to be stationary and unable to attack
for 1 minute — this never happens in combat. **The retreat blocker is mechanically unavailable
in any realistic combat timeline.**

---

**Finding FEEL-029 (NEW): Post-retreat state is undefined — creates unplanned encounter chain risk.**

Per the .md: "it retreats deeper into the lower shaft." The lower shaft is described elsewhere as
containing "something [that] moves down there—not the beholder, but something worse."

If the party pursues, they enter the lower shaft. That content is undefined in the current encounter
folder. There is no `_overview.md` or encounter file for the lower shaft. The encounter folder at
`world/factions/dulgarum-oathholds/locations/thrulm/` does not have a `lower-shaft/` directory.

If the beholder retreats and the party pursues immediately, the DM has no reference material.
Options:
1. DM invents the lower shaft on the fly (dangerous; ruins the "can be run unattended" design goal).
2. Party cannot follow (lower shaft blocked, beholder seals it somehow — not in .md).
3. Encounter effectively ends when the beholder escapes — but the party has no signal that it's "over."

**Log for Joe: The lower shaft needs a stub `_overview.md` (even just a one-paragraph description)
and the beholder should have a mechanical blocker (e.g., "the shaft seals itself with stone when the
beholder passes through") if the encounter is intended to end at the retreat threshold.**

### Dominant Legendary Action under Retreat

When the beholder is at <30 HP and fleeing:

| LA | Usable During Retreat? | Notes |
|----|------------------------|-------|
| Move | Yes | Costs 1 LA; beholder flies 30 ft further away |
| Tentacle | Yes (range 10 ft) | Requires beholder to be in melee — counterproductive while fleeing |
| Void Ray | Yes (range 120 ft) | 2 LA; can fire while moving away; covers retreat |
| Drain Divinity | **No** — 30 ft range | Beholder must be within 30 ft to drain; retreat takes it out of range |

**Finding: During retreat, the dominant LA is Void Ray (2 LA) + Move (1 LA). This is actually
MORE VARIED than the normal Drain Divinity lock. If retreat is triggered, the LA economy becomes
interesting again — the one-note Drain Divinity problem resolves itself because range separates
the beholder from Sabriel.**
