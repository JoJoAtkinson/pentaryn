## Phase B — Generative Playtest Slice: Disintegration Ray Perma-Death Scenario

**Rotation:** `rotation.md` index 2 (date 2026-06-22, day=22, 22%10=2)

**Scenario premise:** Beholder recharges Disintegration Ray, targets Marwen (32 HP wizard). Simulate hit and no-hit. Note: zero-HP = permanent death (no revival except true resurrection/wish). Evaluate feel at level 5.

---

### Setup

Beholder in-chamber stats:
- DR: Ranged Spell Attack, **+7 to hit** (+6 base + Void-Feeding +1), range 120 ft
- Damage: 10d8+1 (Void-Feeding always active; avg **46** force)

Marwen: HP 32, AC 15.

---

### Hit path simulation

Hit condition: d20+7 ≥ 15 → d20 ≥ 8. **P(hit) = 13/20 = 65%.**

Damage on hit: 10d8+1. Mean = 46.

For Marwen to survive the hit: need 10d8+1 ≤ 32 → 10d8 ≤ 31.
- Mean 10d8 = 45; P(10d8 ≤ 31) ≈ 3% (z ≈ −2.07 from normal approximation, σ ≈ 7.25).
- **P(hit → disintegration) ≈ 65% × 97% ≈ 63%.**

Per-round perm-death risk (recharge 5–6 = 33.3%/round):
**0.333 × 0.63 ≈ 21% per round** — consistent with FEEL-R176-01.

Cumulative over 5 rounds: 1 − (1 − 0.21)^5 ≈ **67%** probability Marwen is permanently dead.

*Note for FEEL-R176-01:* The stated 96% kill-on-hit figure is slightly understated. Corrected with Void-Feeding +1: P(10d8+1 > 32) ≈ 97%. Negligible in practice but correct.

---

### No-hit path simulation

P(miss) = 35%. Beholder rolls ≤ 7 on d20.

On a miss:
- Beholder's **main action is spent** — no Multiattack this turn (DR and Multiattack are both Actions; only one per turn).
- **Only** way the party gains a "free" main-action window is on a DR miss.

Beholder after a DR miss:
- **Bonus Action:** Shrine-Drift (reposition, fly 30 ft through obstacles) or Compel Thrall (redirect thrall to Marwen).
- **Legendary Actions** during the LA window (end of each PC's turn):
  - 1 LA: Move or Tentacle Lash (+7, 65% hit → 14.5 avg bludgeoning + DC 16 STR grapple)
  - 2 LA: Void Ray (DC 16 DEX, avg 23 force in-chamber on fail)

**Simulated no-hit round (reference point: Round 1, beholder went first):**

Round 1, beholder's turn (DR fires, misses):
- Roll: 6 → 6+7=13 vs AC 15 → MISS.
- Beholder shifts via Shrine-Drift to maintain altitude, compels a thrall toward Marwen.
- No Multiattack.

End of Bazgar's turn (init assumed lower): Beholder spends 1 LA → Tentacle Lash vs Marwen.
- d20+7 = 15 → HIT (just barely, representative roll).
- Damage: 3d6+4 (Void-Feeding) = avg 14.5 → **15 bludgeoning** (rounded up on roll).
- Grapple rider: DC 16 STR save. Marwen STR ~8 (−1 mod). d20−1 = 4. **FAIL** (needs 17+; ~80% fail rate).
- Marwen: 32 − 15 = **17 HP. GRAPPLED (speed 0).**

LA budget: 3 − 1 = 2 LA remaining.

End of Sabriel's turn: Beholder spends 2 LA → Void Ray vs Bazgar (beholder pivots threat after Marwen is grappled).
- DC 16 DEX. Bazgar DEX +1. d20+1 = 8 → FAIL (needs 15+).
- Damage: 4d10+1 = avg 23 → **23 force.** Bazgar: 49 → 26 HP.
- LA budget: 0.

**Round 1 (no-hit DR) end state:**
- Marwen: 17 HP, GRAPPLED — Speed 0, cannot move away from beholder.
- Bazgar: 26 HP — took 23 from Void Ray.
- Sabriel: 44 HP — untouched this round.
- Beholder: 110 HP, 0 LA remaining. DR did NOT fire. DR recharges at start of Round 2 (33.3% chance).

**Key observation:** The DR miss did NOT produce a safe round for the party. The Tentacle LA immediately re-established the grapple threat, and Bazgar took 23 force damage from Void Ray. The party net damage this round was 38 combined, with Marwen grappled and set up for the Maw combo on Round 2 if the beholder gets Multiattack.

Round 2 if DR recharges (33.3% chance):
- Beholder fires DR vs grappled Marwen (17 HP, AC 15, still +7 to hit).
- P(hit) = 65%. On hit: 10d8+1 avg 46 >> 17 HP → **100% disintegration if hit** (at 17 HP, even minimum 10d8+1 = 11 would KO; need < 11 to survive which is impossible at +1 modifier minimum 11).

Wait — 10d8+1 minimum = 10×1+1 = **11**, which equals Marwen's remaining 17 HP… no, 11 < 17. Let me recalculate: minimum 10d8+1 = 11. P(10d8+1 > 17) = P(10d8 > 16). Mean 45, that's almost certain. P(10d8 ≤ 16) is approximately P(Z ≤ (16-45)/7.25) = P(Z ≤ -4.0) ≈ 0.003%. **Effectively 100% disintegration if the ray hits a 17 HP Marwen.** P(perma-death round 2 given grappled Marwen at 17 HP) = 33.3% × 65% × 100% ≈ **21.6%** (same as round 1 surprisingly, because the raw kill probability if hit was already ~97% at full HP; being at 17 HP pushes it to 100%).

Round 2 if DR does NOT recharge (66.7%):
- Beholder uses Multiattack: Tentacle Lash 1 + Tentacle Lash 2 + Maw.
- Marwen is grappled → Maw has "disadvantage on the saving throw" rider (per stat block; technically the Maw hit is an attack roll, not a saving throw — **see BUG-R184-01: Maw orphaned clause**).
- Tentacle 1 → Marwen (+7, 65% hit): avg 14.5. Expected damage: 9.4.
- Maw → Marwen (+7, 65% hit; grappled adds disadvantage on a saving throw, not the attack): avg 22 (4d8+4 with Void-Feeding). Expected damage: 14.3.
- Combined expected multiattack damage on Marwen round 2: **9.4 + 14.3 = 23.7** — but Marwen is at 17 HP.
- P(Tentacle hit alone ≥ 17): 3d6+4 avg 14.5 — minimum 7. P(3d6+4 ≥ 17) = P(3d6 ≥ 13) ≈ 9%. 
- P(Maw alone ≥ 17): 4d8+4 avg 22 — minimum 8. P(4d8+4 ≥ 17) = P(4d8 ≥ 13) ≈ 80%+.
- P(Marwen KO'd this turn from Multiattack at 17 HP): high (~85%+) from the Maw alone.

**Round 2 summary:** Regardless of whether DR recharges, Marwen is extremely likely to go down on Round 2 if she's at 17 HP and grappled. The no-hit DR path effectively ends in the same outcome (Marwen KO'd or disintegrated) one round later.

### FINDING-R200-A (NEW — MEDIUM): Shield reaction economy trap

Marwen's best single-turn defense against DR is the **Shield** spell (reaction, +5 AC, requires 1st-level slot):
- With Shield: AC 20. Hit condition: d20+7 ≥ 20 → d20 ≥ 13. **P(hit with Shield) = 8/20 = 40%.**
- P(perm-death with Shield active): 0.40 × 0.97 ≈ **39%** (vs 63% unshielded).
- Shield is meaningful mitigation — reduces per-fire perm-death by ~24 percentage points.

**The reaction sink hole problem:**

After the beholder uses DR (whether Shield deflected it or it missed naturally), the beholder's legendary actions activate at the end of each PC's turn:

- Tentacle LA: +7 to hit, 65% hit chance, grapple rider (DC 16 STR, ~80% fail for Marwen).
- If Marwen used **Shield on DR**, her **reaction is spent** until her next turn.
- The Tentacle LA arrives before Marwen's next turn (it fires at end of Bazgar's turn).
- **Marwen cannot use Shield against the Tentacle LA.**

If Tentacle grapples Marwen (52% combined probability: 65% hit × 80% grapple fail):
- Marwen is grappled (Speed 0) and set up for Maw on the beholder's next Multiattack.
- Maw combo on grappled Marwen: 42% one-turn KO chance from Multiattack alone (FEEL-R199-01).

**The dilemma Marwen's player faces:**

| Choice | DR outcome | Tentacle LA outcome | Net result |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|------------|
| A) Use Shield on DR | 39% perm-death risk | No reaction → 52% grapple | Reduced perm-death, high grapple exposure |
| B) Hold Shield for Tentacle | 63% perm-death risk | Can Shield Tentacle (40% hit) | High perm-death, Tentacle somewhat mitigated |
| C) Do nothing | 63% perm-death risk | 65% Tentacle hit | Worst case |

**Neither A nor B is satisfying.** Option A reduces the campaign-ending risk but locks Marwen into a grapple chain that leads to KO (not perma-death, but equivalent for this encounter). Option B protects against one Tentacle attack but fully accepts the 63% perma-death risk.

The reaction sink hole is a **cross-ability resource conflict** that is not explicitly documented anywhere in the encounter materials. It is qualitatively different from FEEL-R193-02 (grapple chain no counterplay) because it involves a deliberate trade-off the player must make in real time, with imperfect information, under CR 13 pressure.

**Recommendation:** Add a DM note to beholder tactics: "If Marwen uses Shield to deflect DR, immediately follow with Tentacle LA to exploit the spent reaction — this is the beholder's correct tactical sequence." And/or: document this dilemma in FEEL-R176-01 as part of the "no satisfying counterplay" finding.

### FINDING-R200-B (LOW): DR-miss costs beholder's main action — partial party upside

On a DR miss (35% probability), the beholder effectively "wasted" its main action:
- No Multiattack that turn (~28–37 damage foregone).
- Expected Multiattack damage per turn: ~28 (two tentacles + maw on average targets, below R199 LA figure).

From the party's perspective: in 35% of rounds where DR fires, the beholder loses its Multiattack. This is a genuine party upside — the beholder trading an attack-roll gamble for a potential permanent elimination.

Expected value for the beholder per round where DR is available:
- **Use DR:** 0.65 × (perm-death value) + 0.35 × (Multiattack value forgone). The perm-death eliminates a PC entirely, making the "value" enormous even at 65% hit rate.
- **Use Multiattack instead:** Guaranteed ~28 expected damage, no perm-death, no action gamble.

From a tactical evaluation standpoint: the beholder almost always benefits from using DR when available because perm-death of the wizard is worth more than one round of Multiattack. This makes the beholder's decision tree trivially simple (FEEL-R199-01 already noted this), but the DR miss case reveals an asymmetry: **the beholder "pays" 35% of the time when DR is available by forgoing Multiattack, and the party "gains" that free round approximately 0.33 × 0.35 = 11.6% of the time overall.** This is the only opening where the party can act without being simultaneously assaulted by Multiattack.

**Feel implication:** The encounter has a hidden rhythm — approximately 1 in 9 rounds, the beholder fires DR and misses, and the party gets a partial reprieve (beholder has Tentacle LA and Void Ray but no Multiattack). This is not telegraphed or documented. A DM may not realize that a DR miss means no Multiattack, potentially also spending the Tentacle LA on a misplay. Worth a cheat-sheet note under the beholder section.
