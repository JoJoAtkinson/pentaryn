## Phase B — Manual playtest: antireality-timing

*Rotation.yml index 7. Tests: Antireality reaction economy over 3 rounds of Bazgar attacking.*

**NOTE: rotation.yml slice is stale (A11-06, open since fire #11).** The slice describes the OLD mechanic (+2 AC post-roll, "declared after seeing the roll"). The current .md says "impose disadvantage on that attack roll (once per round)" (pre-roll disadvantage). This fire simulates BOTH mechanics for comparison; see findings below.

---

### Setup

- **Bazgar:** HP 49, AC 18, Fighter (Battlemaster) 5. Attack bonus +7 (STR +4, PB +3). Greatsword: 2d6+4 slashing. Nonmagic weapon → beholder resistance applies (halved). 2 attacks/turn (Fighter Extra Attack). Probable Dex: +0 (STR-focused build, confirmed by party roster comment "chain shirt + shield").
- **Beholder:** HP 110, AC 17, DEX +3. Antireality reaction (once per round, disadvantage on attack roll).
- **Sabriel:** HP 44, AC 19, divine martial 5. Attack +7. Sanctified weapon = full damage (not resisted). Divine Smite available.
- **Marwen:** HP 32, AC 15, Wizard 5. No Antireality interaction (spells use saves, not attack rolls).

**Initiative** (using average-ish rolls to expose the key scenario):
- Bazgar: d20+0 → assume roll 17 = **17**
- Sabriel: d20+0 → assume roll 14 = **14**
- Marwen: d20+2 → assume roll 11 = **13**
- Beholder: d20+3 → assume roll 9 = **12**

Order: **Bazgar (17) → Sabriel (14) → Marwen (13) → Beholder (12)**

This is the key test case: beholder acts LAST, so Antireality can fire on one PC per round (first to attack before the beholder's turn). The beholder must choose which attacker to spend it on.

---

### Round 1

**Bazgar (init 17):**
- Attack 1: Beholder uses Antireality (disadvantage). Roll 2d20, take lower. Needs 10+. P(hit with disadvantage) = (11/20)² ≈ 30%.
  - Example: dice 14 and 7 → lower = 7+7 = 14 vs AC 17. **Miss.** Antireality worked.
  - Reaction spent.
- Attack 2 (no reaction): 1d20+7 vs AC 17. Needs 10+. 55% hit.
  - Example: roll 16 → 23. **Hit.** Damage 2d6+4 = avg 11, halved by resistance = **5–6 damage**.
- Beholder takes ~5 damage from Bazgar's turn. Beholder at ~105 HP.

**Sabriel (init 14):**
- Antireality already spent (reaction used on Bazgar). Sabriel attacks freely.
- Attack: 1d20+7 vs AC 17. 55% hit.
  - Example: roll 13 → 20. **Hit.** Sanctified weapon, full damage. 1d6+3 = avg 6.5 + divine smite (2nd level, 3d8 = avg 13.5) = avg **20 damage**.
  - Beholder at ~85 HP.

**Marwen (init 13):**
- Casts Fireball (8d6) centered on beholder position. DC 14 Dex save (+6 for beholder = needs 8+, 65% save).
  - Expected fire damage: 28 × 0.35 (fail) + 14 × 0.65 (save) = 9.8 + 9.1 = **~19 damage**.
  - Not resisted (fire is not nonmagic B/P/S). Full damage.
  - Beholder at ~66 HP.
  - NOTE: If Bazgar is grappled at ground level with the beholder hovering within 20 ft, Bazgar is in the blast radius. Downed unconscious creatures auto-fail DEX saves (PHB 197) → Bazgar would take 28 fire damage = 2 instant death save failures. In this scenario Bazgar survived R1, so not applicable yet.

**Beholder (init 12):**
- Reaction refreshes at start of beholder's turn. (Antireality available again.)
- Multiattack vs Bazgar (front-liner, grapple priority):
  - Tentacle 1 (+7 vs AC 18, needs 11+, 50% hit): **Hit.** 3d6+3+1 = avg ~15. Grapple (escape DC 16).
  - Tentacle 2 (+7 vs AC 18, 50%): **Hit.** ~15. Bazgar at 49 − 6 (R1 hit) − 15 − 15 = 13 HP.
  - Maw vs Bazgar (grappled = auto-crit): +7 vs AC 18, 50% hit: **Hit.** 8d8+4 = avg 40.
  - Bazgar at 13 − 40 = **−27 HP. DOWN.**
- After Beholder's R1 turn: Bazgar unconscious.

**End Round 1:** Beholder at ~66 HP. Bazgar down (0 HP). Sabriel and Marwen standing.

### Round 2

**Bazgar (init 17):** Death save. Roll d20, needs 10+. P(death) = 50%, P(success) = 45%, P(nat20 stable) = 5%.
- Example: roll 14 → **1 success, 0 failures.** Bazgar continues dying.

**Sabriel (init 14):**
- Antireality available (refreshed at beholder's R1 turn start). Beholder uses Antireality on Sabriel's attack.
  - Attack with disadvantage: Roll 2d20 take lower. 55% → 30% hit rate.
  - Example: dice 18 and 9 → lower = 9+7 = 16 vs AC 17. **Miss!** Antireality blocked Sabriel's attack.
- Reaction spent.
- Sabriel's bonus action: Healing Word on Bazgar (1d4+4 = avg 6 HP). Bazgar at 6 HP, conscious.

**Marwen (init 13):**
- Casts some spell (not attack roll, Antireality irrelevant). Say Scorching Ray (3×2d6+0 ranged spell attacks, +5 to hit vs AC 17). Each ray: 55% hit. Avg damage = 3×7×0.55 = ~11.5 fire damage.
  - Beholder at 66 − 11 = ~55 HP.

**Beholder (init 12):**
- Reaction refreshes.
- Multiattack. Bazgar is back up at 6 HP — priority target.
  - Tentacle 1 (+7 vs 18, 50%): **Hit.** ~15. Bazgar at 6−15 = **−9 HP. DOWN AGAIN.**
  - Tentacle 2 (no valid grapple on another target; redirects to Sabriel): +7 vs AC 19, needs 12+, 45% hit.
    - Hit or miss, no grapple. Say miss.
  - Maw vs Bazgar (grappled, auto-crit): +7 vs 18, 50%: **Hit.** 8d8+4 = avg 40. Bazgar at −9−40 = −49 HP. **2 instant death save failures** (exceeds half max HP of 24).

**End Round 2:** Beholder at ~55 HP. Bazgar has 2 death save failures, 1 success → 1 more failure = dead. Sabriel and Marwen standing.

**New Issue — FEEL-F15-01: Antireality target priority is undocumented.**

When the beholder has low initiative (common, since DEX +3 often loses to PC initiatives) and multiple PCs attack before its turn, the beholder must choose which PC to use Antireality on. The correct choice is:
- **Sabriel** (sanctified weapon, full damage + smites, ~20 damage per hit)
- NOT Bazgar (nonmagic weapon, halved, ~5–6 effective damage per hit)

Using Antireality on Bazgar instead of Sabriel wastes ~14 points of expected damage prevention per round. Over 4 rounds: ~56 HP difference.

The tactics text has no guidance on Antireality target prioritization. DMs will fire it on the first attacker (Bazgar) rather than holding for Sabriel.

**Suggested DM note (for Antireality or Tactics/Environmental Use text):**
> *"Antireality target priority: when multiple PCs attack before the beholder's turn, hold the reaction for Sabriel (sanctified weapon, full damage) rather than spending it on Bazgar (resisted weapon, half damage). The expected damage prevention is 3–4× higher against Sabriel. Exception: if Sabriel is not attacking this round (e.g., she's healing), redirect to whichever PC has the highest expected hit damage."*

## NEW FINDING: rotation.yml slice factual error

**FEEL-F15-02.** The `antireality-timing` rotation slice contains a factual error independent of the BUG-F7-02 stale-mechanic issue:

The slice states: *"Bazgar rolls 14 (total 21, hits). Beholder uses Antireality (+2 AC → AC becomes 19). Does 21 still hit? **No.**"*

This is wrong. In 5e, an attack roll of 21 vs AC 19 **is a hit** (attack hits when the total ≥ AC). The slice claims 21 misses AC 19, which is incorrect regardless of which Antireality variant is in use.

For the +2 AC mechanic to flip a hit vs AC 17, the attack total must be exactly 18 or 19 (i.e., rolls of 11 or 12 at +7 bonus). A total of 21 would hit even the buffed AC 19.

This slice needs a full rewrite once BUG-F7-02 is resolved (correct mechanic selected). The example should use a total of 18 or 19 if the +2 AC variant is chosen, or any hit roll if disadvantage variant is chosen.
