## Phase B — Scenario: `disintegration-threat` (thrulm-rotation index 1)

*Slice definition: Disintegration Ray fire probability, lethality per target, permanent-death exposure
rate over a full fight vs Compass Edge (Bazgar Ftr5 HP49 AC18, Marwen Wiz5 HP32 AC15, Sabriel
divine-martial 5 HP44 AC19).*

---

### Setup

- **In-chamber Void-Feeding**: +1 to all beholder attack rolls and damage → DR to-hit is **+7**, damage
  is **10d8+1** (avg 46).
- **DR recharge**: available round 1 (starts charged); after use, recharges on 5–6 each subsequent turn
  (P = 33.3% per round).

---

### Lethality per target — DR hit analysis

| Target | AC | P(hit at +7) | Avg DR dmg (in chamber) | Max HP | P(hit → 0 HP) | P(disintegrated \| hit) | P(round-1 disintegration) |
|--------|----|--------------|--------------------------|---------|--------------|-----------------------|--------------------------|
| Marwen | 15 | 65% | 46 | 32 | ~99% (46 >> 32) | ~99% | **~64%** |
| Sabriel | 19 | 50% | 46 | 44 | ~99% (46 >> 44) | ~99% | **~50%** |
| Bazgar | 18 | 55% | 46 | 49 | ~85% (P(10d8+1 ≥ 49) ≈ 45%; avg 46 barely below) | ~45% | **~25%** |

*P(10d8+1 ≥ 49) computed: 10d8 mean 45, each die avg 4.5; to reach 48+ (= 49 − 1) requires above-mean
rolls. Estimate: P(10d8 ≥ 48) ≈ 40% via symmetry + standard-deviation approximation (σ ≈ 12); used 45%
here as conservative estimate. Marwen and Sabriel are almost always reduced to 0 by avg damage alone.*

**Beholder optimal target: Marwen every time.** 64% disintegration probability per round-1 fire.

---

### Recharge cadence — expected DR uses in 5 rounds

| Round | P(recharged this round) | Cumulative P(used ≥1 time total) |
|-------|------------------------|----------------------------------|
| 1 | 100% (starts charged) | 100% |
| 2 | 33% | 33% |
| 3 | 33% | 56% cumulative recharge |
| 4 | 33% | 70% cumulative recharge |
| 5 | 33% | 80% cumulative recharge |

Expected total DR uses in 5 rounds: 1 (round 1) + 4 × 0.333 = **2.33 uses**.

**P(Marwen disintegrated across 2.33 DR uses targeted at her):**

P(not disintegrated per use) = 1 − 0.64 = 0.36
P(not disintegrated across 2.33 uses) ≈ 0.36^2.33 ≈ 0.096
**P(at least one disintegration) ≈ 90%**

In a full 5-round fight — which is already a brutal "meant to lose" scenario — there is a **~90% chance
Marwen is permanently destroyed** (no restoration except True Resurrection 9th or Wish 9th; Compass
Edge at level 5 has no access to either).

---

### Feel findings

**FEEL-010: Permanent death at 64% probability in round 1 is character-tombstone territory.**

Design intent is "the party is meant to *lose* this encounter" — intended as a dramatic retreat/escape
moment, not a character funeral. The distinction matters:

- "Lose" = retreat, take losses, return stronger → player retains character
- "Lose" + permanent death = the character is gone unless Joe retcons or the campaign escalates
  to 17th-level spell access to undo it

The disintegration permanent-death rider on what is mechanically a ~46-point hit transforms a
"dramatic loss" into a "character termination." At 15% probability (P(beholder wins init and all 3
tentacles hit Marwen)), the party could also lose Marwen before she EVER ACTS, with no way back.
Even if this is "fair" by math, it's anti-climactic and likely not what the encounter is for.

**Recommendation (log only — Joe's call):**
Option A: Add to DR text a "soft kill" exception: "Compass Edge campaign: disintegration reduces
target to 0 HP with 3 failed death saves rather than ash. (Homebrew, table-specific.)"
Option B: Lower DR from 10d8 → 6d8 (avg 27; keeps Marwen at risk but doesn't auto-disintegrate
on a hit). Change dice expression only with Joe's sign-off.
Option C: Give disintegration a size/HP threshold: "Creatures with max HP ≥ 50 can make a DC 18
CON save to avoid disintegration, taking 0 HP instead." Protects Marwen without touching Bazgar.
Option D: Keep as-is — this encounter is intended to be a catastrophic wipe. Communicate at session
zero that this fight can kill characters permanently.

---

**FEEL-011: DR targeting is deterministic — Marwen is always the optimal target, every round.**

Marwen: AC 15, HP 32 → easiest to hit, almost certain disintegration on hit.
Sabriel: AC 19, HP 44 → harder to hit but also near-certain disintegration.
Bazgar: AC 18, HP 49 → harder to hit, ~25% disintegration per round.

The beholder (INT 17) never has a reason to deviate from "target Marwen with DR." This is tactically
correct but makes the encounter feel like Marwen's problem while Bazgar and Sabriel watch. Adds to the
"one-note dominant strategy" concern (see Drain Divinity). No two consecutive rounds produce a
different targeting puzzle.

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to beholder tactics: "Rotate DR targeting after Marwen falls —
Sabriel is next priority (divine martial = highest divine resource threat to Drain Divinity); Bazgar
last (high HP reduces disintegration reliability)."
