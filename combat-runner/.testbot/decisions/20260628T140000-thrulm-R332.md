## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `lair-action-round`

**Rotation index 5 of 8** — simulating the lair action decision tree (initiative count 20).

---

### Setup

**Scenario context (from rotation.yml):**
- 2 thrall derro present (HP 22, AC 14 each), both within 60 ft of beholder
- Bazgar is within 20 ft of the shrine
- Initiative count 20 fires before any PC or NPC turn this round
- Lair action options: Unstable Ground, Manifest Thralls, Void Eruption

**Assumed party state mid-fight (~round 2):** Bazgar 36 HP (took Rager hit round 1), Sabriel 37 HP (took Tentacle LA round 1), Marwen 4 HP (stabilized from round 1 beholder multiattack).

**PC save modifiers (estimated):**
- Bazgar: DEX +0 or +1 (Fighter, STR-primary). DC 16 DEX save: needs 15+ → 30% success / 70% fail.
- Sabriel: DEX +1 (divine martial, heavy armor). DC 16 DEX save: needs 15+ → 30% success / 70% fail.

---

### Option Analysis

#### Option 1: Unstable Ground (DC 16 Dex, Bazgar prone)

Bazgar (DEX ~+0): 70% fail rate → prone 70% of the time.

If prone:
- Bazgar must spend half movement (15 ft) to stand on his turn — cuts effective approach distance
- Melee attacks *against* prone Bazgar have advantage (Rager's multiattack gains advantage on both axe swings)
- Bazgar attacks at disadvantage until he stands (unless the beholder is within 5 ft, which it is not at 40-60 ft altitude)

Rager's advantage bonus with Greataxe vs Bazgar AC 18: base hit = (1-0.55)×2 = 45% without advantage, with advantage = 1-(0.55²)=0.6975 → ~70% hit each. Two attacks: 0.70×2×8.5 = 11.9 expected vs 6.3 expected without prone. Net gain: +5.6 expected damage from Rager.

Bazgar attacks beholder at disadvantage: 0.55²=30% hit rate vs 55% base. Two attacks forgone or at disadvantage for the round. This is a significant action economy swing.

**Expected value:** 70% chance × (5.6 Rager bonus + ~9 forgone Bazgar damage) = 10.2 expected "advantage swing."

#### Option 2: Manifest Thralls (2 thralls gain +1 THP + reaction attack or move)

2 thralls get 1 THP each (trivial). Each can use reaction to make a weapon attack:
- Hand Axe Weak: +1 to hit vs Bazgar AC 18 → needs 17+ → 20% hit, avg 2 damage
- Expected: 2 × 0.20 × 2 = 0.8 damage

Or thralls use reaction to move toward PCs (no damage, just repositioning).

**Expected value:** ~0.8 damage and trivial repositioning.

**Verdict: Manifest Thralls is always dominated.** 1 THP per thrall vs 27 HP threats; sub-1 expected damage from reaction attacks. This option was never competitive and is dead code as a lair action choice.

#### Option 3: Void Eruption (all within 20 ft of shrine, DC 16 Dex, 2d10 force)

Bazgar is within 20 ft of shrine. If Sabriel has also advanced (likely, as divine martial): 2 PCs in range.

Per PC: DC 16 DEX, 70% fail → 0.70×11 + 0.30×5.5 = 7.7+1.65 = **9.35 expected force damage per PC in zone**.

2 PCs (Bazgar + Sabriel): **18.7 expected damage**. Note: thrall derro, if within 20 ft of shrine, also take this damage. Beholder is indifferent to losing thralls.

Void Eruption also covers Marwen IF she has advanced, but at 4 HP the DM would likely exclude her from the beholder's targeting priority (a 4 HP PC dying to Void Eruption is anti-climactic and noted in FEEL-FC48-A / FEEL-R328-02 context).

**Expected value:** ~18.7 expected damage when 2 PCs are in shrine zone. Zero value if only Bazgar is near shrine.

---

### Optimal Decision: Context-Dependent

**Round 1 (party just entered, spread out):**
- Only Bazgar near shrine → Unstable Ground (10.2 swing value)
- Void Eruption = 9.35 (Bazgar only) — worse than Unstable Ground
- **Optimal: Unstable Ground**

**Round 2+ (melee chars advanced, both Bazgar and Sabriel near shrine):**
- Void Eruption = 18.7 expected — dominant
- Unstable Ground targets 1 creature only
- **Optimal: Void Eruption**

**Lair action decision tree (practical):**
- Init 20 fires → check who is within 20 ft of shrine
- 2+ PCs in shrine zone → Void Eruption
- 1 PC in shrine zone (melee char) → Unstable Ground
- No one near shrine → Manifest Thralls (wasted)

---

### Feel Issues Found

**FEEL-R332-01: Lair action tree effectively 2-option; Manifest Thralls confirmed dead code**

Confirmed via simulation. The lair action decision point is: "Unstable Ground vs Void Eruption." Manifest Thralls delivers ~0.8 expected damage vs 10-19 for the other options. A DM who picks Manifest Thralls has been baited by the name — it *sounds* like it should add combat pressure, but 1 THP per thrall + a 20% attack chance does nothing. This confirms FEEL-OPEN-03.

**Recommendation (existing):** Redesign Manifest Thralls as either (a) "Thralls immediately make a full attack action (not reaction attack)" which raises expected damage significantly, or (b) "Thralls gain immunity to being knocked unconscious this round, and advantage on attack rolls" (tactical screen) rather than THP.

**FEEL-R332-02: Void Eruption's shrine-proximity tension is real but asymmetric**

Void Eruption creates a genuine "if you close to melee range, you take damage" deterrent — 9.35 expected force damage per round if within 20 ft of the shrine. But the melee fighters (Bazgar, Sabriel) have no alternative to closing; they cannot reach the beholder at altitude with melee, so they are incentivized to close toward the shrine in hopes it descends. The deterrent is thus partially "free": the beholder punishes PCs for doing the only thing they can do, and they accept it because the alternative (staying back) also loses.

This is fine for a "meant to lose" encounter design. However, it means Void Eruption *feels like* an environmental hazard the party has no agency against, rather than a tactical choice they could avoid. Noting for feel record.

**FEEL-R332-03: Lair action timing (init 20) gives beholder an extra free action before its own turn**

Lair action fires at init 20, beholder acts at ~18. So in the same round, the beholder:
1. Prongs Bazgar or deals Void Eruption (lair, init 20)
2. Takes full multiattack + bonus action Compel Thrall (its own turn, init 18)

This back-to-back sequencing (lair → beholder turn) creates a double-action burst that can feel unfair from the player side. Against a "meant to lose" encounter this may be intentional, but worth flagging: if Bazgar is proned by lair action at init 20, the beholder's multiattack at init 18 benefits from 2 enemies with advantage against prone Bazgar. Could combine for massive damage before any PC acts.

This is by-design for a TPK encounter — logging as design note, not a bug.
