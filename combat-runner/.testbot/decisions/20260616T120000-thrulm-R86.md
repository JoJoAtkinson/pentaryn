### NEW Phase A Findings This Fire

**NEW-R86-01 (LOW — LOG ONLY): Thrall Dagger damage modifier +2 inconsistent with DEX +1.**

`thrall-derro.md` stat block: STR 9 (−1), DEX 13 (+1), PB +2.

Dagger (Desperate): "+3 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. *Hit:* 4 (1d4 + 2) piercing."

- To-hit: +3 = DEX (+1) + PB (+2) ✓
- Damage: 1d4 + **2**. A finesse weapon uses the higher of STR or DEX modifier for damage. DEX +1 > STR −1, so damage should be 1d4 + **1** (avg 3.5→3), not 1d4 + 2 (avg 4.5→4).

The parenthetical average 4 is arithmetically correct FOR the stated expression (1d4+2). However, the expression itself has a +1 over-stated modifier relative to the stat block's DEX. The hard rule says "never change dice expressions" and "only fix the parenthesised average when the expression itself is correct." This is an expression-level design issue (wrong modifier), not just a wrong average — **not auto-fixable**. Options: (a) lower modifier to 1d4+1 (avg 3; parenthetical "3"), or (b) document as intentional (flavor: the desperation strike benefits from technique). Design call for Joe.

Note: BUG-A31-08 (thrall `attack` verb fires Dagger stats instead of Hand Axe Weak) remains the higher-priority Thrall bug; this is a secondary concern about whether the Dagger expression itself is correct once BUG-A31-08 is fixed.

**NEW-R86-02 (LOW — LOG ONLY): Shrine-Touched Ancient Resonance DC 14 implies PB+3; CR3 expects PB+2 → DC 13.**

`shrine-touched-derro.md` concise stat line: "Saves Dex +5, Con +5" with #cr-3 tag.

CR 3 → PB +2. Dex save +5 = DEX +3 + PB +2 → DEX 16 (+3) ✓ for the save.

Ancient Resonance DC: "DC 14 Dex". If DC = 8 + DEX + PB = 8 + 3 + 2 = **13**. Stated DC is 14, which implies either:
- PB+3 (not correct for CR3), or  
- DEX+4 (but save implies DEX+3), or
- An intentional flat DC (design choice to exceed stat-derived floor).

This is the same PB-overcount pattern seen in BUG-A57-01 (Con save +5 inconsistent with HP-implied CON+2). Taken together, the Shrine-Touched appears to have been authored with a non-uniform PB in different fields. The DC 14 is likely a by-feel design choice rather than a derived value. Net effect: 1 additional fail from DC 14 vs DC 13 = 5 percentage-point shift — marginal. **LOG ONLY** — design call for Joe.

### FEEL-R86-01 (NEW — LOG ONLY): Unstable Form "last round" wording creates same-round ambiguity

**Checklist item 4:** "if shrine-touched took 10+ damage **in a single turn last round**, it has advantage on all attacks until end of its next turn."

**Ambiguity:** "last round" is strict. Consider an initiative order where the STD (init +3) acts **before** all PCs in round 1 (rolled high). PCs then damage the STD on their respective turns (later in round 1). At the start of STD's round 2 turn, "last round" = round 1. The damage occurred in round 1 (PC turns). UF triggers correctly. ✓

But: if the STD acts **after** a PC who deals 10+ damage in the **same round** (STD init 13, Sabriel init 10: Sabriel goes first — no, in this scenario STD (13) acts before Sabriel (10), damage from Sabriel comes after STD in round 1, so UF activates at start of STD's turn in round 2). ✓ Standard case is fine.

**Edge case:** STD (init 13) acts. THEN Sabriel (init 10) deals 16 damage later in round 1. THEN round 2 starts, STD's turn again. "Last round" = round 1. Sabriel's damage was in round 1 (after STD's R1 turn). UF triggers → STD has advantage. ✓ This case is clear.

**True ambiguity:** what if a PC deals 10+ damage to the STD on the same round AND before the STD acts (i.e., PC has higher initiative than STD this round)? "Last round" = the previous full round, but the damage occurred earlier in the CURRENT round. Strict reading: "last round" means round n-1, so damage in the current round (round n, before STD's turn) does NOT activate UF for this turn — it would activate UF for round n+1 (when round n becomes "last round").

**Effect in practice:** If the STD has low initiative and a PC with high initiative deals 10+ damage in the same round before the STD acts, UF would NOT apply this turn (strict reading). DMs often apply it immediately ("she just hit me hard, I'm enraged"). The intended behavior is unclear.

**Recommendation:** Change "last round" to "since this creature's last turn" — unambiguous, covers both same-round and prior-round damage regardless of initiative order. Design call for Joe.

### FEEL-R86-02 (NEW — LOG ONLY): Scenario prelude implies OBR; scenario tests main-action multiattack

**Scenario prelude:** "Sabriel's radiant smite burns into shrine-flesh. The derro screams — then its skin ignites with cold grey light and it swings back harder than it should."

"It swings back" reads as OBR (the reaction counter-swing). But the scenario's turns sequence is:
1. `-12` (damage event, unattributed — no OBR trigger because it's not from a melee attacker within 5 ft in GUI terms)
2. `shrine-axe` (main-action multiattack on the STD's own turn)

These are two different attack events:
- **OBR** fires as a **reaction on the attacker's turn** (when the STD takes melee damage from a visible attacker within 5 ft)
- **Shrine-axe** in the scenario is the STD's **own-turn multiattack** (which benefits from UF advantage)

The scenario correctly tests the own-turn multiattack with UF context. The prelude's "swings back" is misleading — it implies the reactive strike (OBR), not the deliberate next-turn swing (multiattack + UF). Cosmetic ambiguity; DM reading the prelude may momentarily confuse OBR with multiattack.

**Impact:** None in practice (scenario tests the right thing). Suggest updating the prelude to "On its next turn, the shrine-touched attacks with cold fury — Unstable Form active." but this is a polish note, not a bug. Design call for Joe.
