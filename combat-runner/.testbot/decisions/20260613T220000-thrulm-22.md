## Phase B — Generative Playtest: retreat-threshold

**Rotation.yml index 6.** Tests: beholder retreat at 28 HP (below 30 HP threshold), Shrine-Drift as escape tool, retreat viability against level-5 party.

### Setup

**State (late R3):** Beholder at 28 HP, AC 17, fly 30 ft (hover). Two thrall-derro alive (HP ~10 each). Bazgar (HP ~20, AC 18, speed 30 ft) and Sabriel (HP ~30, AC 19, speed 30 ft) are both in melee range (~5–10 ft). Marwen (HP ~15, AC 15, speed 30 ft) is at 30 ft range. Lower shaft entrance = ~60 ft from altar (per overview: "60 ft beyond the main chamber"; actual altar-to-shaft distance dependent on unspecified room width — see FEEL-F22-03 below).

---

### Lair action (init 20)

Options: Unstable Ground (DC 16 Dex, prone 1 target) / Manifest Thralls (+1 THP + reaction attack) / Void Eruption (2d10 force in 20 ft of shrine).

**Optimal choice before retreat:** Unstable Ground on Bazgar. Rationale: prone Bazgar imposes disadvantage on his own attack rolls (including OA). P(Bazgar OA hit) normal = ~55% (+7 vs AC 17 needs 10+). P(Bazgar OA hit prone) = ~30% (P(both rolls ≥10) = 0.55²). Expected OA damage drops from ~5.5 to ~3.0. Manifest Thralls (+1 THP / reaction attacks at +1 vs AC 18) = near-zero pressure; skip per BUG-F10-02 and DD-MT.

---

### Retreat turn simulation

**Action economy:**
- Move (fly): 30 ft toward shaft
- Bonus action (Shrine-Drift): 30 ft toward shaft, phases through objects
- **Total per turn: 60 ft**

**Execution order:**
1. Shrine-Drift BA fires first (bonus action, move 30 ft). Triggers OA from any creature the beholder was adjacent to at BA declaration. Bazgar OA: **prone → disadvantage → ~30% hit → expected ~3 dmg**. Sabriel OA (no UG on her): **+7 vs AC 17 → ~55% hit → expected ~9 dmg base**. If Sabriel has a L2 smite slot remaining: OA + smite = +3d8 = avg ~13.5 on hit → total expected ~12.4 dmg.
2. Reactions now spent (Bazgar used OA from Shrine-Drift).
3. Fly 30 ft (no further OAs — reactions expended from step 1).

**Damage during retreat:** Expected ~3 (Bazgar, prone) + ~9–12.4 (Sabriel, w/ or w/o smite) = **~12–15 expected damage**. From 28 HP → ~13–16 HP remaining if both OAs land at expectation.

**Survivability:** 50% chance the beholder survives OAs with >10 HP; ~35% chance it dies during retreat if Sabriel crits or smites. This is acceptable for a "meant to lose" encounter — dramatic retreat that sometimes ends in last-stand rather than clean escape.

---

### Does Shrine-Drift work as an escape tool?

**Partially yes, partially no:**

- ✓ Shrine-Drift lets the beholder navigate through rubble/pillars that would otherwise require costly movement around obstacles. If the shrine chamber has structural debris (collapsed shrine remains per the .md), Shrine-Drift is genuinely useful for pathfinding.
- ✗ Shrine-Drift does NOT grant Disengage or OA immunity. Moving out of a creature's reach (even via a bonus action move) triggers OA per PHB. Shrine-Drift + fly = 2 OA triggers (Bazgar, Sabriel) rather than 1 — the beholder moves further, but takes 2 attacks instead of 1.
- ✗ BA conflict: Shrine-Drift (retreat) and Compel Thrall (thrall blocking) cannot both fire on the same turn. The beholder must choose one. Per tactics ("use thralls to block"), the intended play is Compel Thrall to move a thrall into the party's path — but this requires spending the BA on Compel Thrall, not Shrine-Drift. Without Shrine-Drift, retreat is only 30 ft (fly speed). With Shrine-Drift, thralls cannot be positioned this turn.

**Verdict:** Shrine-Drift is a useful escape tool for terrain navigation but not for OA avoidance. The design implies "Shrine-Drift + fly = escape in one turn" but the actual escape also requires the BA budget, which conflicts with Compel Thrall. Joe must choose the intended retreat sequence.

### Chamber dimensions problem (FEEL-F22-03)

The beholder's total retreat per turn is 60 ft (30 fly + 30 Shrine-Drift). The overview says "The lower shaft: 60 feet beyond the main chamber." Interpretation A: the shaft entrance is 60 ft from the main chamber's far wall. Interpretation B: the shaft is 60 ft deep, with its entrance at the near wall of the main chamber.

If the altar is at the far end of the main chamber and the shaft entrance is at the near end, and the main chamber is ~30–40 ft wide (reasonable for a vault), the beholder needs **~30–40 ft** to cross the chamber. That's achievable in 1 turn (60 ft total). Escape is feasible.

If "60 ft beyond the main chamber" means the shaft entrance is 60 ft past the chamber wall (the shaft is a separate 60-ft corridor), the beholder needs the full chamber width (~30–40 ft) + 60 ft = **~90–100 ft total**. At 60 ft/turn, that's 2 turns. With 28 HP and two OAs per turn, the beholder dies on retreat.

Room dimensions are not specified in any .md file. Retreat viability is **indeterminate without explicit room size.** See FEEL-F22-03.

### Feel assessment

**Retreat reads as anticlimactic in the following cases:**

1. **Marwen can just shoot.** At 30–60 ft, Marwen fires Firebolt (+5 vs AC 17 = ~45% hit, 3d10 avg 16.5) or Magic Missile (guaranteed 10.5+ avg). The beholder gains no protection from ranged attacks by retreating — it only escapes melee. A Wizard with 3 spells/slots remaining wins the chase from range without moving.

2. **The party can Dash to match beholder speed.** Bazgar (30 ft base + Dash action = 60 ft) can match the beholder's full retreat speed. If Bazgar Dashes instead of attacking, the beholder is never out of his melee reach until it enters the shaft. The retreat becomes an eternal adjacent-chase unless the beholder has a speed advantage, which it doesn't (fly 30 = speed 30 = Dash 30).

3. **No Disengage.** The beholder takes OAs on EVERY movement action that moves it out of melee reach. With two melee PCs engaged, it takes two OAs per retreat turn — equivalent to fighting while retreating. At 28 HP, this is a coinflip on survival per turn.

**Retreat reads as dramatic in the following cases:**

1. **Sabriel is out of smite slots** (post-DD attrition from earlier rounds). Sabriel's OA is then ~9 avg, manageable.
2. **Bazgar is prone** (from lair UG). Bazgar OA disadvantage cuts threat to ~3 avg.
3. **Marwen is low on spells** or out of concentration and holding for a dramatic last shot.

**Severity:** Medium — affects every fight that reaches the 30 HP threshold.

**Finding:** The beholder has no Disengage option. Shrine-Drift (BA) does not grant OA immunity — moving out of a creature's reach via any movement triggers OA per 5e RAW. The retreat turn structure (action=?, BA=Shrine-Drift OR Compel Thrall, move=fly 30) is not specified in tactics. "Uses thralls to block" (Compel Thrall) and Shrine-Drift cannot both fire on the same retreat turn (both BA). Choosing Shrine-Drift abandons thrall positioning; choosing Compel Thrall halves retreat speed (fly 30 ft only).

**Suggested fix (log only):** Either add "Shrine-Drift — no OA" to the ability text (explicit design) or add a Dodge action on the retreat turn (action = Dodge, BA = Shrine-Drift, move = fly 30, total movement 30 ft with attacker disadvantage). ### FEEL-F22-02 (new): "Below 20 HP: crush/disintegrate while fleeing" is mechanically unimplemented

**Severity:** Low — edge case at HP 20.

**Finding:** Beholder tactics say "If below 20 HP, it attempts to crush/disintegrate the party while fleeing." There is no action combination that allows simultaneous Disintegration Ray (main action) + full retreat (standard fly 30 ft + Shrine-Drift BA = 60 ft total) in the same turn. If DR fires (action), Shrine-Drift (BA) still gives 30 ft of movement, but the standard fly move is no longer being doubled. DR + Shrine-Drift + fly = 30+30 = 60 ft still (move is independent of action). Actually: move (30 ft fly) + BA (Shrine-Drift 30 ft) + action (DR) is valid in 5e — you can move, use BA, and take an action in any order on your turn. So "crush/disintegrate while fleeing" IS mechanically possible: action = DR, BA = Shrine-Drift, move = fly 30 ft, total 60 ft retreat. This is correctly implemented.

**Clarification needed:** The tactics text says "retreat deeper" AND "disintegrate." The move-action and DR-action are separate, so both happen in the same turn. However, this requires DR to be recharged (5–6 on d6). If it's not recharged, the beholder has no "crush" action for the sub-20 HP band. The tactics should specify the fallback: "if DR not recharged at <20 HP: fire Void Scream (if recharged) or Multiattack (grapple to prevent pursuit)." Currently the tactics are silent on the non-recharged case.
