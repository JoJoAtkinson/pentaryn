### DB Verb Resolution (confirmed this fire)

- `"retreat"` → `tactical_retreat` (utility, 30 ft no-OA move) ✓
- `"throw"` → `multiattack` (two Shard-Throws, +4 to hit, 1d8+2 each, range 30/60 ft) ✓
- `tactical_retreat` type: `utility`; `multiattack` type: `multiattack` — distinct action types in DB, no double-spend possible ✓

### Manual Simulation (3 rounds)

**Setup:**
- SC: HP 33, AC 14. Starts at 40 ft from party; Bazgar (init −1) charges end-of-R1, closing to 10 ft.
- Bazgar: HP 49, AC 18, speed 30 ft, attack +7 (2d6+5 avg 12).
- Marwen: HP 32, AC 15 (primary SC throw target, 50% hit rate).

**R1 (SC init 14, Bazgar init 7):**
- SC acts first (Bazgar not yet in 15-ft range): no TR trigger. SC fires Multiattack at Marwen.
  - Two Shard-Throws, +4 vs AC 15 (50% each): E[damage] = 2 × 0.50 × 6.5 = 6.5 avg.
  - Marwen at ~25 HP after (32 − 6.5 ≈ 25).
- Bazgar moves 30 ft → 10 ft from SC. Attacks SC: +7 vs AC 14 (80%): E[dmg] = 0.80 × 12 = 9.6 avg.
  - SC at ~23 HP (33 − 9.6 ≈ 23).

**R2 (Bazgar now within 15 ft):**
- SC turn: TR fires (bonus action) — moves 30 ft away from Bazgar (no OA). Now 30 ft from Bazgar.
- SC main: Multiattack at Marwen. E[dmg] 6.5 avg. Marwen at ~19 HP (25 − 6.5 ≈ 19).
- Bazgar: moves 30 ft → adjacent (5 ft). Attacks SC: E[dmg] 9.6 avg. SC at ~13 HP (23 − 9.6 ≈ 13).

**R3:**
- SC: HP 13 — below 12 HP threshold, now "retreat behind Rager or pillar" (but none present).
  - No Rager available; tactics say "accept the exposed position and fire."
- SC: TR again (bonus action), moves 30 ft. Main: Multiattack at Marwen. E[dmg] 6.5 avg. Marwen at ~12 HP.
- Bazgar: moves 30 ft → adjacent. Attacks SC: E[dmg] 9.6 avg. SC at ~3 HP.

**R4 (SC effectively dead):**
- SC at 3 HP fires one last TR + throw, then falls to Bazgar. Marwen at ~5–6 HP from SC throws.

### Phase B Findings

**Scenario passes mechanically:** TR (bonus action) and Shard-Throw (main action) are correctly
distinct DB action types. No double-spend is possible. ✓

**Kiting loop characterization:**
The TR + Throw cycle creates a ~3-round chase before the SC falls. During this window, the SC deals
~12–18 avg damage to Marwen (two 50%-hit throws per round). At Marwen's 32 HP, this represents
37%–56% of her health — significant when combined with beholder damage in the full encounter.

**LA economy note (beholder, broader context):**
In R1 the 3-LA Drain Divinity window is dominant post-Sabriel. In R2+ (slots depleted), the beholder
defaults to Tentacle (1 LA) + Void Ray (2 LA) every end-of-turn — a fixed mechanical pattern with
no decision variability. This one-note R2+ LA rotation is the FEEL-R83-02 issue. The SC kiting
loop adds an independent low-pressure damage channel on Marwen during the same window where the
beholder's Void Ray may also target her — the combined fire could down Marwen by end of R2.

**FEEL-R92-01 (LOG ONLY):** In the full thrulm encounter, the SC's TR kiting creates an undocumented
secondary damage channel on Marwen while Bazgar pursues. SC fires ~6.5 avg/round at Marwen during the
~3-round kite window. Combined with beholder Void Ray (22 avg if Sabriel LoH redirects LA) or SC
Shard-Barrage (if 2 PCs are in a line during the chase), Marwen can be downed without being the
primary beholder target. The SC tactics note says "Multiattack the visible spellcaster / healer first"
— this is correct, but there's no explicit note that TR kiting maintains this pressure on Marwen even
while Bazgar is in pursuit. Joe: low-priority wording gap; no mechanical change needed.

**NEW-R92-01 (LOG ONLY):** DB gap confirmed — the `tactical_retreat` effect text says "Move up to 30 ft
directly away from the nearest enemy. This movement does NOT provoke opportunity attacks." The .md
adds a restriction absent from the DB: "must move toward the shrine if not in sight of combat." This
out-of-combat direction clause is not in the DB record. At-table, this only matters during a retreat
chase outside the shrine chamber — an edge case. No mechanical impact in the thrulm chamber
encounter. Document for completeness; no fix needed unless a chase scenario is added.
