### Marwen KO Risk

6d10 ~ N(μ=33, σ≈7.04). P(KO | fail) = P(6d10 ≥ 32) ≈ P(z ≥ −0.21) ≈ **58%**.

- Without shrine proximity: P(KO) = 0.50 × 0.58 = **29%**
- With shrine proximity: P(KO) = 0.75 × 0.58 = **43.5%**

Marwen at 0 HP from psychic damage: **not** a disintegration event (VS has no instant-kill rider), so she drops to death saves and can be revived. This is meaningfully different from DR — VS is a severe but recoverable hit.

### FRIGHTENED Condition — Duration and DPR Impact

On a failed VS save: PC is FRIGHTENED for 1 minute; DC 16 WIS save at end of each turn ends the effect.

**Expected rounds FRIGHTENED (per PC, no shrine proximity):**

| PC | WIS bonus | P(save per turn) | E[rounds FRIGHTENED] |
|----|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Bazgar | +1 | 30% | 3.3 rounds |
| Sabriel | +3 | 40% | 2.5 rounds |
| Marwen | +5 | 50% | 2.0 rounds |

While FRIGHTENED: disadvantage on all attack rolls vs beholder (always in line of sight). Against beholder AC 17:
- Bazgar (est. +7 atk): base hit P = 55%; with disadvantage = 30% (0.55² ≈ 30%)
- Sabriel (est. +6 atk): base hit P = 50%; with disadvantage = 25%
- Marwen (spell attacks, est. +6): base hit P = 50%; with disadvantage = 25%

Party DPR roughly halved for ~2-3 rounds post-VS. In a fight expected to run 4-6 rounds total, this is 1-2 rounds of half-DPR — significant but not infinite.

**P(at least 2 PCs FRIGHTENED) — no shrine proximity:**
= 1 − P(0 fail) − P(exactly 1 fail)
= 1 − (0.30×0.40×0.50) − (0.70×0.40×0.50 + 0.30×0.60×0.50 + 0.30×0.40×0.50)
= 1 − 0.06 − 0.29 = **65%**

**P(at least 2 PCs FRIGHTENED) — shrine proximity (disadvantage):**
= 1 − (0.09×0.16×0.25) − (0.91×0.16×0.25 + 0.09×0.84×0.25 + 0.09×0.16×0.75)
≈ 1 − 0.004 − 0.066 = **93%**

### Is the Shrine Proximity Rule Clear Enough to Run Without Prep?

The rule appears in the save DCs table: "within 10 ft of shrine: **disadvantage** on this save."
It does **not** appear in the Round-by-Round checklist. A DM following the checklist flow during fast play may not look up the save DCs table mid-turn and will miss the penalty.

Additionally, the combat map note positions the beholder at "40-60 ft up" above the altar. When the party closes for melee (which requires being near the altar), they are almost certainly within 10 ft of the shrine — but the cheat sheet gives no direct "if PCs close to beholder → check shrine proximity for VS" prompt.

Recharge 6 = 16.7% per turn. In a 4–6 round fight:

### Key Findings

**FEEL-R311-01: VS+shrine creates a 43–65% chance the encounter pivot happens in one action**

A single Void Scream with shrine proximity (93% chance of FRIGHTENED on 2+ PCs) simultaneously:
1. Deals 72% party HP in expected damage
2. Locks in 2-3 rounds of half party DPR via FRIGHTENED

The combination is the single most impactful action in the encounter — more so than DR (which threatens 1 PC) or multiattack (which targets 1-2 PCs). VS is the beholder's true "win button." This is intentional for CR 13 vs L5, but the FRIGHTENED + damage combo at shrine proximity creates a near-deterministic outcome if the party is clustered near the shrine.

**Feel assessment: "lethal but not unfair"** — the party has a legible counterplay (spread out, stay 10+ ft from shrine) and Marwen's KO from VS does not carry a permanent death rider (unlike DR). VS is recoverable in a way DR is not. This is well-designed.

**FEEL-R311-02: VS shrine proximity penalty absent from Round-by-Round checklist**

The cheat sheet's save DCs table includes the rule ("within 10 ft of shrine: disadvantage on this save"), but the Round-by-Round checklist has no prompt to check PC shrine proximity when the beholder uses Void Scream. During a busy combat round, DMs following the checklist flow are likely to forget this penalty.

Recommend adding to Round-by-Round checklist under "When Beholder Acts":
> **VS shrine-proximity check:** Any PC within 10 ft of the shrine when Void Scream fires? → They roll with **disadvantage** on the WIS save.

**FEEL-R311-03: VS optimal follow-up is DD, but DD requires the beholder to remain descended**

Post-VS, 65-93% chance 2+ PCs are FRIGHTENED. While PCs have FRIGHTENED disadvantage on attacks, the beholder's optimal follow-up is to use DD on Sabriel (to strip divine smite slots) — but DD requires being within 30 ft of Sabriel. Since the beholder likely descended for DD in R1 (per FEEL-R310-01), it may already be positioned for this follow-up. However, remaining at 30 ft elevation means melee-range PCs (Bazgar, Sabriel) can reach it next turn with full attacks — even with FRIGHTENED disadvantage, a crit from a GWM fighter at close range is significant.

The optimal beholder sequence appears to be:
1. R1: Multiattack (stay high), LA = Tentacle + Void Ray
2. R2: If descended for any reason: VS (if recharged) → DD follow-up from the descended position
3. R3: Ascend back to 40+ ft, resume ranged pressure (DR if recharged, Void Ray LA)

This sequence isn't documented in the tactics block and is non-obvious even with the cheat sheet. Log as a tactics documentation gap.

**FEEL-R311-04: Shrine proximity creates a catch-22 for melee PCs**

Staying ≥ 10 ft from shrine avoids VS disadvantage but means PCs cannot reach the hovering beholder in melee (beholder at 40-60 ft). Closing to melee requires approach via the shrine area → shrine proximity → VS disadvantage. Ranged attacks are the only VS-safe approach, but Compass Edge has only one ranged specialist (Marwen). Bazgar and Sabriel are effectively forced into a choice: melee advantage + VS disadvantage, or ranged attacks at reduced effectiveness.

This is a well-designed constraint (forces tactical thinking) but should be noted in the "IF YOUR PC ASKS" section: "Paladin/Fighter closing to melee on the beholder? They're near the shrine — warn them about Void Scream disadvantage."
