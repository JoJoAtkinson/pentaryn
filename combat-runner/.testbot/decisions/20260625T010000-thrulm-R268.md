### Setup

- **Compass Edge:**
  - Bazgar: Fighter 5 (Battlemaster), HP 49, AC 18, attack bonus +7 (est. STR +4, PB +3), init +2. Superiority dice d8 × 4.
  - Marwen: Wizard 5, HP 32, AC 15, init +3 (est. DEX +3). 3rd-level spell slots.
  - Sabriel: Paladin 5 (divine martial), HP 44, AC 19, init +2. CHA save proficiency (Paladin class feature). 3rd-level spell slots.
- **Lair actions:** active (beholder uses on init count 20, losing ties).

### Round 1

**Initiative count 20 — Lair Action:**

Void Eruption (within 20 ft of shrine) is unavailable unless PCs have already closed; they haven't (chamber entry). Manifest Thralls: ~0 EV (1 THP each — FEEL-LA-R265-02, confirmed open). Best option: **Unstable Ground** on Bazgar (the charging fighter).

- Target: Bazgar. DC 16 DEX save. Bazgar DEX +2 (est.). P(fail) = P(d20+2 < 16) = 65%.
- If fail → Bazgar prone. Half movement to stand (15 ft of 30 ft movement lost). Attacks vs prone Bazgar at melee range: advantage (beholder). Bazgar attacks at disadvantage if prone and target is 5+ ft away — but Bazgar will stand before attacking, so the disadvantage-on-attacks part is moot (he uses movement to stand, then attacks normally).
- Net impact of Bazgar prone: he must spend 15 ft movement standing before his turn. If the beholder is 30+ ft away, Bazgar cannot both stand AND reach it — he must choose. This forces either (a) a non-attack turn on Bazgar's initiative or (b) a half-movement turn that limits engagement.

**Beholder's turn (init 18):**

Default hover: 40–60 ft above altar. Shrine-Drift (bonus action, 30 ft movement, passes through solid matter): descends 30 ft → now approximately 10 ft above altar floor.

At 10 ft altitude, Bazgar (ground level, at most 30 ft horizontal from altar) is within reach of Tentacle Lash (reach 10 ft). Distance to Bazgar: sqrt(10² + 20²) ≈ 22 ft — well within 10 ft only if Bazgar closed to 5 ft horizontal. Revise: the beholder at 10 ft altitude can reach any PC standing directly below by "leaning" with 10 ft tentacles. If Bazgar is within 0–5 ft horizontal of the altar, tentacle reach 10 ft connects at the floor.

For the alpha-strike scenario, Bazgar has charged in — assume he's within 10 ft horizontal of the altar position.

**Multiattack (action):** 2× Tentacle Lash + 1× Maw. Target: Bazgar (nearest, most dangerous).

*Tentacle Lash #1:* +6+1(VF) = +7 vs AC 18. Needs 11+. P(hit) = 50%.
- Hit: 13+1 = 14 (3d6+4, with Void-Feeding) bludgeoning damage + grappled (escape DC 16).
- If Bazgar prone (65% from lair action) AND at melee range: attack has advantage (prone + melee). With advantage: P(hit) = 1 – 0.5² = 75%.

*Tentacle Lash #2:* +7 vs AC 18. P(hit) = 50% (75% if prone). If hit: 14 bludgeoning + second grapple.

*Maw:* +7 vs AC 18. P(hit) = 50%. If hit: 22 (4d8+4 with VF) piercing. If Bazgar grappled by either tentacle: he has disadvantage on the contest to escape — but Maw gives disadvantage on ability checks to escape the grapple (per FIX-R220-A). The Maw damage is the same either way.

**Expected damage, multiattack (with 65% prone chance applying advantage to TL rolls):**

P(TL1 hits) = 0.65 × 0.75 + 0.35 × 0.50 = 0.49 + 0.18 = 0.67. Expected: 0.67 × 14 = 9.4  
P(TL2 hits) = same = 0.67. Expected: 0.67 × 14 = 9.4  
P(Maw hits) = 0.50 (no prone bonus, different reach). Expected: 0.50 × 22 = 11  

**Total expected R1 multiattack damage on Bazgar: ~30 HP.** Bazgar enters his turn at ~19 HP (49 – 30). Likely grappled by 1–2 tentacles.

**Legendary Actions (during PC turns):**

*After Bazgar's turn:* 1 LA → Tentacle Lash on Bazgar (+7, P 50–67%, 14 avg). Expected: ~8 additional damage. Bazgar now at ~11 HP.

*After Sabriel's turn:* Drain Divinity (3 LA) on Sabriel.

**KEY FINDING — DD range check:** Beholder at 10 ft altitude, Sabriel at ground level. If Sabriel is within 30 ft horizontal of the altar, distance = sqrt(10² + H²) where H = horizontal distance. For H ≤ 28 ft: distance ≤ 30 ft. In a typical chamber where PCs advance to engage, this is almost always satisfied. **The 30 ft DD range is automatically within strike zone when the beholder has descended to attack — see FEEL-R268-01 below.**

Sabriel CHA save: Paladin 5 has saving throw proficiency in Wisdom and Charisma (PHB class feature, not Aura of Protection). CHA save = PB +3 + CHA mod. Assumed CHA +3 (moderate CHA for a Paladin): save = +6. P(fail DC 16) = P(d20+6 < 16) = P(d20 ≤ 9) = 45%.

45% chance Sabriel loses her 3rd-level spell slot. Beholder gains 2×3 = 6 THP.

*After Marwen's turn:* 0 LA remaining (used: 1 for Tentacle, 3 for DD).

**End of Round 1:**

- More likely: Sabriel attacks a derro (Rager or Shrine-Touched) or uses a ranged divine smite. Or she casts Sacred Flame on the beholder (ranged, DC 16 DEX, 2d8 radiant — 9 avg, 50% success = 4.5 expected).

**Total party damage to beholder R1: ~15.5 (Bazgar) + ~10 (Sabriel) + ~21 (Marwen) = ~46.5 HP.** Beholder enters R2 at ~63 HP.

### Round 2 outlook

Beholder R2 action priorities (HP ~63):
1. Disintegration Ray (+7 vs AC 15, P(hit) = 65%) → 45 avg force on Marwen (32 HP). Expected: 0.65 × 45 = 29.3. **Marwen drops with ~89% probability in one round** (P(45 hits) = 65%, P(Marwen dies) = high since 45 > 32).
2. If DR used, lair action: Unstable Ground on Sabriel or Void Eruption if PCs have clustered.
3. LA economy R2: Tentacle LA after Bazgar (1 LA) → Bazgar now at ~5–7 HP. If Bazgar fails to heal between rounds, LA Tentacle may drop him. DD on Sabriel (3 LA) again if she has remaining slots.

After R2, the encounter has likely reached its conclusion:
- Marwen: down (disintegrated or dropped to 0)
- Bazgar: ~5–7 HP, grappled, one LA hit from dropping
- Sabriel: ~44 HP but minus 1–2 spell slots from DD

**The encounter achieves its design intent (party loses) in 2–3 rounds. Bazgar outlasts Marwen and possibly makes it to round 3; Sabriel is the last standing.**

### Key findings from this simulation

**FEEL-R268-01 (NEW): Drain Divinity range is auto-satisfied in attack rounds — the "must descend" tactical constraint is overstated.**

The beholder must descend (Shrine-Drift BA, 30 ft) from its default 40–60 ft hover to make Tentacle Lash attacks on ground-level PCs (Tentacle reach 10 ft requires ≤10 ft distance to target). After descending to 10 ft altitude, ALL ground-level PCs within 28 ft horizontal are automatically within 30 ft — satisfying Drain Divinity's range with zero additional cost. The "must descend to use DD" constraint in the tactics section implies a meaningful trade-off, but in an attack round, descent is obligatory regardless. DD is NOT meaningfully range-limited in rounds where the beholder attacks physically.

**Consequence:** Every combat round where the beholder multiattacks, Drain Divinity (3 LA) is freely available against any PC within 28 ft horizontal. The prior tactical framing ("the beholder descends — it wants DD badly enough to leave altitude") describes a stylistic tell, not a mechanical constraint. Relevant to FEEL-DD-R264-02 (open): "DD descent requirement is undocumented tactical tell (positive)." That flag's positive framing should be revised — the descent is not specific to DD; it's required for any physical attack. DD fires opportunistically, not as a dedicated descent maneuver.

*Joe should decide: is the "beholder descends for DD" narrative tell worth preserving even if it's mechanically unforced? If yes, update tactics to clarify this is intentional roleplay framing, not a mechanical constraint. If no, remove the implication that DD descent is a special cost.*

**FEEL-R268-02 (NEW): R1 action density leads to Bazgar at ~11 HP before Round 2 — dramatic but may crowd out counterplay.**

Beholder R1 economy: lair action + Shrine-Drift + Multiattack (~30 HP to Bazgar) + LA Tentacle after Bazgar's turn (~8 HP more) = Bazgar at ~11 HP entering R2. Bazgar has acted once (dealing ~15.5 damage to beholder), but has essentially spent his R1 fighting from a disadvantaged position (prone → must stand → restricted movement) and ends the round bloodied and grappled.

The concern is not that the party is losing — they're meant to lose — but that Bazgar's agency on R1 is constrained by the lair action (Unstable Ground → prone) BEFORE he can act. The party has no response to init-count-20 lair actions; it fires before any PC turn regardless of initiative.

Unstable Ground forcing prone (65% chance) denies Bazgar's full movement, enabling the multiattack with advantage to hit harder. This is a technically valid combat opener, but at the table it reads as: "Before you even act, you're prone, then you take 30 damage." The *feel* is "the beholder has already decided the outcome before Round 1 begins."

*This does NOT need to be fixed — the encounter is designed lethal — but Joe should brief the DM: the lair action on init 20 is a strong pre-round taxation that may frustrate players. Consider whether the lair action in R1 should be held (for atmosphere) or if Unstable Ground should default to targeting a mid-initiative PC rather than the highest-init PC.*

**Sabriel CHA save clarification (refinement of FLAG-DD-R264-04, no new decision needed):**

Paladin 5 has CHA saving throw proficiency as a class feature (PHB). Sabriel CHA save = PB +3 + CHA mod (+2 to +3 depending on build). P(fail DD DC 16) = 45–50%, not the ~60% implied in prior reports that assumed no proficiency. FLAG-DD-R264-04 correctly identifies the proficiency, but the "+5" assumed (+2 mod + 3 PB) may understate CHA if Sabriel is CHA +3 → save +6 → P(fail) = 45%. Either way the correction from "no prof (fail ~75%)" to "prof (fail ~45–50%)" is significant. Carries forward as FLAG-DD-R264-04 OPEN; no new entry.
