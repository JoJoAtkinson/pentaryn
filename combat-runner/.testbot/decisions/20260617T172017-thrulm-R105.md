### Scenario

Beholder wins initiative. Recharge check: DR starts combat recharged (5-6 available at combat start). Question: fire DR at Marwen (32 HP, AC 15) immediately, or follow the prescribed MA-first protocol (FIX-EV7-32-A)?

### Disintegration Ray threat ceiling analysis

**Option A: Fire DR at Marwen on beholder's R1 turn (beholder acts at init 17, Marwen already acted at init 14)**

Wait — Marwen acts at init 14 (before the beholder at 17). She has already taken her turn. If Marwen cast a concentration spell or used a slot, DR on a Marwen who has already acted means the beholder eliminates her *after* she contributed to the fight. She gets one action before the risk.

Correct threat calculation: **Marwen acted at init 14 before beholder (init 17) fires DR.** Marwen is at full HP 32 unless she took damage (unlikely in R1).

Attack: +7 vs AC 15, needs 8+ → **P(hit) = 65%**  
Damage: 10d8, avg 45, min 10, max 80  
P(≥ 33 damage | hit) = P(Marwen at 0 HP) ≈ 96.3% (approximation: mean 45, σ ≈ 7.2; Z = (32−45)/7.2 = −1.81; P(X ≤ 32) ≈ 3.5%)  
**P(Marwen disintegrated this turn) = 65% × 96.3% ≈ 62.6%**

This is the same figure as FEEL-R104-02 but the dramatic context is different: **in this initiative order, Marwen has already acted.** She cast a spell, moved, did something. The disintegration is still abrupt (she gets exactly 1 action), but she had agency. This is meaningfully better player experience than the R104 beholder-wins-init scenario where Marwen dies before acting at all.

**Option B: Follow MA protocol (FIX-EV7-32-A) — Multiattack Bazgar**

Bazgar at init 12, Sabriel at init 10, Rager at init 9 (all after beholder's init 17 turn). Beholder attacks Bazgar before Bazgar has acted.

TL1: +7 vs AC 18 = 55% hit → 13 avg bludgeoning + grapple (escape DC 16)  
TL2: +7 vs AC 18 = 55% hit  
P(at least one TL hits) = 1 − 0.45² ≈ **80%**  
If Maw fires on grappled Bazgar: auto-crit → 8d8+4 avg 40 piercing  
P(Bazgar downed R1, MA route) ≈ 80% × 55% ≈ **44%**

Against Marwen at init 14 (already acted): beholder cannot retroactively swap target.

**Comparison:**
- DR at Marwen: 62.6% she's disintegrated, but she had 1 action. Beholder burned its main action on a single target.
- MA at Bazgar: 44% Bazgar downed before he acts. Beholder also sets up Maw-crit economy for R2.
- MA is prescribed (FIX-EV7-32-A) and is strategically superior: grapple pressure persists, Maw R2 is set up, and the beholder's action economy isn't depleted on a coin-flip recharge.

**FEEL-R105-01 (NEW): Initiative order significantly changes the DR R1 dilemma's player-experience impact**

In R104's simulation (beholder init 20 > all PCs), DR fires before Marwen acts — 62.6% she never plays. In this run's simulation (beholder init 17, Marwen init 14), Marwen acts first and DR fires after her turn. The "player agency" problem exists only when the beholder wins initiative over Marwen specifically. P(beholder beats Marwen on initiative) = P(beholder total > Marwen total):
- Beholder init: d20+3. Marwen init: d20+2. 
- P(beholder > Marwen) ≈ 47% (slightly less than half due to +3 vs +2 advantage).
- P(tie, beholder wins, Marwen never acts before DR) = additional ~5%.
- **Total P(Marwen gets no action before DR R1) ≈ 50%.**

This means the worst-case player experience (wizard disintegrated before acting) happens in about half of all DR-R1 scenarios. The stat block prescription (MA R1, not DR) correctly avoids this ~31% compound probability (50% × 62.6%) of "zero-agency disintegration."

**Existing log item FEEL-R104-02 recommends adding a DR timing note to the beholder stat block.** This run confirms that recommendation. The note should mention: "Even if the beholder wins initiative over Marwen, hold DR — firing it before the wizard acts eliminates a player before they participate."

### Round 2 DR analysis (more useful window)

With DR held for R2 (beholder has MA'd R1, now on R2 turn at init 17):

Marwen has acted once (R1) and is likely at full HP (no melee reach, back-line). She may have used her action to cast a concentration spell.

DR at R2: same 65% hit, 62.6% disintegration probability. But now:
- Marwen has contributed 1 full round of spellcasting
- If she dropped concentration (e.g. from VS or melee), DR disrupts nothing further
- If she maintained concentration, DR ends it permanently (no save if disintegrated)

The R2 DR window is strictly better from a player-experience standpoint AND offers a tactical bonus (disrupting maintained concentration if applicable). This confirms FIX-EV7-32-A is correct.

### Move LA value re-evaluation

**FEEL-R105-03 (NEW): Move LA is NOT dead weight — it drags grappled targets via standard fly movement**

Per FIX-FC48-B, Shrine-Drift (bonus action) does NOT drag grappled creatures (it is not standard movement). Move LA uses the beholder's fly speed (standard movement) — and per RAW (PHB p. 195), "if the grappled target is willing or the grappler is dragging it, the grappler can move at half speed." Move LA = 30 ft fly speed → drag a grappled creature 15 ft at half speed.

Use case: Tentacle LA (1 LA) grapples Bazgar → Move LA (1 LA) drags Bazgar 15 ft toward the shrine (into 10 ft radius). On the beholder's next action, Void Scream fires — Bazgar and any nearby PCs within 10 ft of the shrine roll VS with **disadvantage**. Total spend: 2 LA for "grapple + repositioning that adds VS disadvantage."

Comparison: VR (2 LA) = 17.6 expected HP + no positioning. Tentacle+Move (2 LA) = grapple + drag, no immediate damage, but sets up VS disadvantage (VS avg 33 psychic × 80% fail rate at disadvantage vs Bazgar Wis save = ~26.4 expected HP from R2 VS) plus Maw auto-crit on beholder's next main action (~40 piercing).

The Tentacle+Move 2-LA combination may be strictly better than Void Ray in rounds where VS is recharged and the beholder plans to use it next main turn. This interaction has not been documented in the stat block.

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to the legendary actions section: "Move LA and Shrine-Drift are not interchangeable — Move LA uses standard fly movement (can drag a grappled creature at 15 ft) while Shrine-Drift is a discrete bonus action (cannot drag). Consider Tentacle (1 LA) + Move (1 LA) as a 2-LA setup for Void Scream shrine-proximity disadvantage on the following turn."
