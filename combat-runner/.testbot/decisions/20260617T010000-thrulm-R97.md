## Phase A — New Findings This Fire

### BUG-R97-01 (NEW): "tentacle" / "maw" / "bite" appear in both multiattack and individual-attack verb lists — dispatch ambiguity

From the DB:

```
beholder-thrulm.multiattack verbs:
  ["attack", "hit", "swing", "tentacle", "tentacles", "maw", "bite", "melee", "multiattack"]

beholder-thrulm.tentacle_lash verbs:
  ["tentacle", "lash", "tentacle lash", "whip"]

beholder-thrulm.maw verbs:
  ["maw", "bite", "crush", "devour"]
```

Overlapping verbs:
- `"tentacle"` → in both `multiattack` AND `tentacle_lash`
- `"tentacles"` → in `multiattack` only ✓
- `"maw"` → in both `multiattack` AND `maw`
- `"bite"` → in both `multiattack` AND `maw`

Impact: When a DM types `"tentacle"` or `"maw"` to fire the single-attack variants (e.g., for a legendary action — 1 LA Tentacle, or to fire a standalone Maw after explicit tentacle setup), the runner may dispatch `multiattack` (3 attacks) instead of the single-attack row. With `max_llm_fallback: 0` in several thrulm scenarios, the runner cannot use LLM disambiguation to resolve context.

Affected scenarios (all use single-verb dispatch, no-LLM):
- `thrulm-maw-autocrit-grappled-target`: uses `text: "tentacle"` twice, then `text: "maw"` — expects 3 single-attack dispatches but may get 2× multiattack + 1× maw.
- `thrulm-maw-rider-on-hit-text`: uses `text: "maw"` alone — expects single maw, may get multiattack.
- `thrulm-lair-prone-melee-synergy`: uses `text: "tentacle"` and `text: "maw"` — same risk.

Note: scenarios that use `"tentacle lash"` (two-word) are unaffected — that phrase is only in `tentacle_lash` verbs.

**Fix requires:** `combat_action_upsert` to remove `"tentacle"`, `"maw"`, `"bite"` from `multiattack` verbs. Suggest replacing with `"full attack"`, `"all attacks"`, or keeping only `"attack"`, `"multiattack"`, `"hit"`, `"swing"`, `"melee"`. Cannot auto-fix. **Needs Joe + MCP.**

Severity: HIGH — actively breaks no-LLM scenario test assertions for maw/tentacle dispatch.

## Phase B — Scenario: `disintegration-threat` (rotation.yml idx = 97 % 8 = 1)

**Slice definition:**
> Assume recharge rolls 5–6 on turn 1. Beholder targets Marwen (32 HP, AC 15). Roll 10d8
> force +6 vs AC 15. If hit: average ~45 damage. Does it one-shot a 32-HP Wizard? If yes,
> is the TPK risk from a single die roll acceptable for this encounter's intent? Record
> threat ceiling and feel note.

### Setup

- Bazgar: HP 49, AC 18. Fighter (BM) — STR +4, DEX -1.
- Marwen: HP 32, AC 15. Wizard — DEX +2. Assumed WIS save: +1.
- Sabriel: HP 44, AC 19. Divine martial — DEX +0. Assumed WIS +3, CHA +2+PB+3 = +5 save.
- Beholder: HP 110, AC 17. Void-Feeding active (+1 atk/dmg). DR effective: +7 to hit.
- DR begins combat available (5e convention: recharge abilities start recharged).

### Disintegration Ray Threat Ceiling

**Attack math:**
- DR: +7 to hit (base +6 + Void-Feeding +1) vs Marwen AC 15.
- Needs 8+ on d20 → **65% hit rate**.

**Damage on hit:**
- 10d8 force damage. Avg = 45. Min = 10, Max = 80.
- Marwen HP 32. To disintegrate, need ≥ 32 damage.
- P(10d8 ≥ 32): min is 10, so we need ≤ 31 to NOT disintegrate. 10d8 avg 45 with SD ≈ 8.2.
  P(≤ 31) ≈ P(Z ≤ (31−45)/8.2) = P(Z ≤ −1.71) ≈ **~4%**.
- Therefore P(disintegrate | hit) ≈ **96%**.

**Combined:**
- P(disintegrate Marwen on this action) ≈ 0.65 × 0.96 ≈ **~62%**.

**Disintegration outcome:**
- Marwen reduced to 0 HP by DR → turns to ash. No body.
- LoH, Revivify, Healing Word, and Cure Wounds all fail (require a living or recently-dead creature with a body).
- Only True Resurrection or Wish can restore a disintegrated PC.
- This is **permanent** for all practical intents at the table level.

### "Is a 62% one-shot before the player's first turn acceptable?"

The slice asks directly: is this acceptable for the encounter's intent?

**Yes, mechanically.** The encounter is designed as a CR 13 vs level-5 party TPK. One-shots are within scope.

**Conditionally acceptable at the table.** The qualifier is the "before the player acts" element. Two different scenarios:
- **A) Beholder wins initiative, acts before Marwen:** 62% chance Marwen never takes a turn.
- **B) Beholder wins initiative but acts after Marwen (lower initiative number):** Marwen has at least one action; DR is a response to her threat.

In representative initiative rolls (R96: beholder init 17, Marwen 14), the beholder acts before Marwen ~60% of the time (beholder DEX +3 vs Marwen DEX +2; roughly 55% beholder wins). Combined:
- P(Marwen never acts before disintegration) ≈ 0.55 (beholder wins init) × 0.62 (DR hits and kills) ≈ **~34%** per combat.

One-in-three combats, Marwen is disintegrated before taking a single action.

This is the "unfair rather than lethal" edge case the briefing flags. It is:
- Not a bug (mechanically valid)
- Not a balance issue (encounter is designed to be lethal)
- **A table-feel risk** that a DM running this cold should be warned about

### Variance Anatomy

The R96 rotation noted the tactics section prescribed "R1 = Multiattack; R2 = Void Scream" (FIX-EV7-32-A) without discussing DR as an R1 option. FEEL-R96-01 flagged this gap. This cycle confirms the math:

| R1 Path | Hit Rate | Avg HP removed | Outcome (on success) |
|---------|---------|---------------|----------------------|
| Multiattack → Bazgar (prone, VF) | 93.75% ≥1 TL + 75% Maw = ~70% auto-crit | 51 avg | Bazgar downed, recoverable |
| DR → Marwen (VF active) | 65% hit | 29 avg (0.65×45) | Marwen permanently eliminated; 35% wasted action |

Multiattack is still recommended R1 (higher avg HP, preserves DD window). But DR→Marwen R1 is not irrational — permanent elimination of the party's AoE threat trades ~22 HP of average damage for irreversibility. Against this specific party (wizard is their primary area damage), eliminating Marwen R1 denies the Fireball that would otherwise hit 2 Ragers + 1 Shardcaller and halve the derro force.

**Damage-economy case for DR R1:** If Marwen's Fireball would deal 28 avg to 3 derro (84 HP total), the beholder gives up 84 HP of derro damage mitigation by choosing Multiattack → Bazgar instead. A beholder with full information would recognize this. The tactics note should acknowledge both paths.

### Phase B Findings

**FEEL-R97-01 (NEW): DR starts combat recharged — tactics note implicitly assumes it doesn't.**

The tactics section's "R1 = Multiattack (grapple); R2 = Void Scream" protocol (FIX-EV7-32-A)
says "Do NOT fire VS on R1" and prescribes grapple-first. This is correct for VS. But it
doesn't address DR, which unlike VS starts combat available (per 5e convention: recharge
abilities start recharged).

A DM running this cold will look at DR and ask: "Can I fire this turn 1?" The answer is yes.
The tactics section doesn't say so or explain the tradeoff. FEEL-R96-01 flagged the gap for
the DR target note; this adds the recharged-state component:

**Recommended addition to DR entry in tactics:**
> "Disintegration Ray begins combat recharged (5e convention). The prescribed R1 Multiattack
> path is suboptimal by raw damage-output but optimal for LA economy (preserving DD window
> post-Sabriel). DR→Marwen R1 (before she acts, 65% hit, 96% disintegrate on hit) trades ~22
> HP of expected damage for irreversibility — against this party, a smart beholder may prefer
> it. No tactics note prohibits R1 DR; the prescribed Multiattack is a DM recommendation, not
> a mechanical rule. Recommend DR R1 only if the DM is prepared to narrate a player's
> disintegration before their first turn, which ~34% of combats will produce. See FEEL-R96-01."

Log-only. No source file edit without Joe's decision.

**FEEL-R97-02 (NEW): 10d8 force vs 32 HP — variance is entirely on the attack roll, not the damage roll.**

P(10d8 ≥ 32) ≈ 96%. The disintegration threshold (32 HP) is below the damage floor at all
but minimum-roll outcomes. From a DM experience standpoint: "Roll 10d8. If the attack hits,
the result is almost certainly disintegration. The 10-dice roll is theatrical, not meaningful."

This is not a balance problem — it is a **player experience observation**. When a DM picks up
10d8 and the outcome is known 96% of the time before the dice land, the roll loses tension.
The 10-dice cascade is visually dramatic; the outcome certainty works against the drama.

Options (all design-call, log only):
1. **Keep as-is.** The theatrical roll is part of the encounter's horror. Fine if intended.
2. **Reduce to 7d8 (avg 31.5, floor 7, max 56).** At Marwen's 32 HP, P(≥32) ≈ 49% on a hit —
   the damage roll becomes the actual tension. P(disintegrate per action) ≈ 65% × 49% ≈ 32%.
   Feels like a coin flip per attempt rather than a foregone conclusion. Threat is still lethal.
3. **Add a HP-threshold clause** ("if this damage reduces the target to −10 HP or fewer, they
   are disintegrated") to make disintegration a specifically brutal outcome, not automatic-on-0.

All three are design choices for Joe. This note logs the mechanical observation.
