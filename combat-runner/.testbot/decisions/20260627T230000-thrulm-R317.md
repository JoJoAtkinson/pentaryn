## Phase C — Issues Found

### Feel Issues (log-only, no auto-fix)

**FEEL-R317-01 — Drain Divinity collapses LA economy to one decision.**

Every round Sabriel is in range, the "correct" beholder decision is Drain Divinity (3 LA). This is because:
- DD prevents Divine Smite from landing extra d8s on beholder's psychic/nonmagic resistance-defying sanctified weapon attacks
- The THP gain is irrelevant (4 THP/round on a 110 HP boss)
- No other 3-LA combination delivers comparable strategic value

The result: the DM has no real decision to make. Every round Sabriel acts, the beholder will DD her. This creates a tell ("I know it'll DD after my turn") and a pure war of attrition — Sabriel has 6 slots; she runs dry in 6 rounds.

**Recommendation (Joe's call):** Change DD to cost 2 LA. This frees 1 LA for Move or Tentacle, creating real round-by-round decisions. Alternatively: add a 1-per-short-rest cap so DD isn't available every round. The current 3-LA cost feels like a deliberate throttle that produces the opposite problem — rather than limiting use, it locks the budget completely.

**FEEL-R317-02 — Marwen instant-kills before her turn, permanently.**

Round 1, Beholder at init 14 vs Marwen at init 12. On any roll of 6+ (70% hit chance, +9 vs AC 15), Marwen is disintegrated. She never acts in round 1. The "disintegrated" permanent-death clause is applied before the player takes a turn.

The encounter is designed to be lethal (correct per briefing). But this crosses from "lethal" to "unfair" when: the kill is permanent (no Revivify), the PC never acted, and the probability is 70% on round 1.

This could be intentional — the beholder is terrifying. But at a 5th-level party, losing one PC permanently in round 1 with no counterplay creates a specific type of table unhappiness.

**Recommendation (Joe's call):** Not a bug. Flag as design consideration. If the encounter needs a "one act before dying" guarantee: reduce Marwen's position in initiative (she acts before beholder), or have the beholder spend round 1 multiattacking (flavor: it senses the party, grapples them, doesn't immediately recognize Marwen as the most dangerous target). Neither change is appropriate for the bot to make.

**FEEL-R317-03 — Drain Divinity THP payoff is noise.**

When DD succeeds, the beholder gains 4 THP (2nd-level slot × 2). Against a party dealing 20–27 damage/round to the beholder, 4 THP is recovered in a fraction of a turn. The beholder doesn't accumulate THP (each DD call replaces, not stacks, any prior THP under standard rules).

If DD is meant to make the beholder progressively harder, it isn't working — 4 THP is irrelevant at 87 HP. The THP amount should probably be 10× level drained (so a 2nd-level slot = 20 THP) to be meaningful, or the beholder's healing should be regen-style (a design change Joe must approve).

Not re-tested directly this fire but confirmed still open from DB inspection. Would surface if shrine-touched-derro engages Sabriel in a fight where retaliation would trigger. Blocks the `thrulm-oath-breaking-dual-damage` scenario.
