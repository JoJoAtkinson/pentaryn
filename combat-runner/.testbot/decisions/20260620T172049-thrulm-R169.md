### Scenario: `disintegration-threat` — Disintegration Ray threat ceiling

**Slice description:** Assume recharge rolls 5–6 on turn 1. Beholder targets Marwen (32 HP, AC 15). Roll 10d8 force +6 vs AC 15 (note: in-chamber it's +7 — CS-DRIFT-18 above). If hit: average ~45 damage. Does it one-shot a 32-HP Wizard? If yes, is the TPK risk from a single die roll acceptable for this encounter's intent?

---

### Setup

Party: Bazgar HP 49 AC 18, Marwen HP 32 AC 15, Sabriel HP 44 AC 19.

Beholder in Thrulm chamber: Void-Feeding always active. All beholder attack rolls +7, all attack damage +1.

Disintegration Ray:
- "Ranged Spell Attack: +6 to hit, range 120 ft., one creature. Hit: 45 (10d8) force damage."
- In chamber: +7 attack, 10d8+1 force damage (avg 46).
- Recharge 5–6 (available round 1 by default; recharges on 5–6 each subsequent turn start).
- Permadeath clause: "A creature reduced to 0 HP by this attack cannot be restored to life except by true resurrection or wish."

---

### Attack math vs Marwen

**+7 vs AC 15:** needs 8+. Hit on 8–20 = 13 results / 20 = **65% hit rate**.

(With Antireality disadvantage on the beholder's attack? No — Antireality is a reaction to attacks *against* the beholder, not its own attacks.)

---

### Damage math

10d8+1 (in-chamber): mean 46, range 11–81.

**P(DR damage ≥ 32 | hit):**
Using normal approximation: 10d8 mean 45, σ ≈ 7.25.
With in-chamber +1: mean 46.
P(10d8+1 < 32) ≈ P(Z < (32 - 46) / 7.25) = P(Z < -1.93) ≈ 2.7%.
→ **P(damage ≥ 32 | hit) ≈ 97.3%.**

**Combined: P(Marwen reduced to 0 HP round 1):**
0.65 × 0.973 = **63.2%**

**P(Marwen permanently dead round 1):**
If reduced to 0 HP: "cannot be restored to life except by true resurrection or wish."
- True Resurrection: 9th-level spell. Party is level 5. Inaccessible.
- Wish: 9th-level spell. Inaccessible.
- Revivify (3rd-level, accessible to party): explicitly does NOT work on disintegrated targets.
→ P(Marwen permanently dead if DR hits and deals ≥ 32) = 100%.
→ **P(Marwen permanently dead round 1) ≈ 63%.**

---

### Countermeasures available to Marwen

**Shield spell (1st-level reaction):** Declared after seeing the attack roll, before it's resolved.
- Shield adds +5 AC (AC 20 temporarily).
- Rolls that hit AC 15 but miss AC 20: attack total 15–19 (rolls 8–12). That's 5/20 = 25% of all rolls.
- With Shield: hit rate drops from 65% to 40% (rolls 13–20 = 8 results / 20).
- P(permanent death | Shield used on this attack) ≈ 0.40 × 0.973 ≈ **39%**.
- Cost: 1st-level slot. Marwen has 4 at level 5.
- Marwen doesn't know the beholder has DR until the beholder uses it. By the time she knows (the beholder declares Disintegration Ray), the DM asks "does Marwen react?" Marwen's player has ~2 seconds of real-time to decide whether to burn a 1st-level slot or die with ~63% probability. This is non-trivial table pressure.

**Counterspell (3rd-level reaction):** DR is labeled "Ranged Spell Attack." If it counts as a spell (it does — spell attack implies a spell), Counterspell (3rd-level) can target it. DC to counter 3rd-level-or-lower spell = automatic success. If the beholder casts DR at any level, Marwen can burn a 3rd-level slot to nullify it. This completely prevents the ray.
- However: does DR qualify as a "spell"? It's a "Ranged Spell Attack" — this implies it's a spell (spell attack = a spell that uses an attack roll). Under standard 5e RAW, any ability that says "Spell Attack" is a spell and can be Counterspelled.
- If DR is Counterspellable, it changes the DR dynamic significantly. Marwen burning a 3rd-level slot prevents the permadeath. But the beholder may use DR when Marwen is out of 3rd-level slots or has already used Counterspell.
- **Design ambiguity:** The stat block uses "Ranged Spell Attack" which makes DR Counterspellable by RAW. For this CR-13 encounter (intended TPK), this may or may not be the intended interaction. **Log for Joe: confirm whether DR is intended to be Counterspellable.**

### One-shot risk assessment

| Scenario | P(Marwen at 0 HP R1) | Marwen restorable? |
|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| No reaction | 63% | No (permadeath) |
| Shield reaction | 39% | No (permadeath) |
| Counterspell reaction | 0% (DR negated) | N/A (DR failed) |
| Beholder misses (35%) | — | N/A |

Comparison to Void Scream (next-highest damage, area):
- VS: 33 (6d10) psychic, DC 16 Wis. Marwen Wis ~+2, needs 14+ = 35% success.
- P(VS reduces Marwen to 0) ≈ 0.65 (fail save) × P(6d10 ≥ 32 | Marwen already at full HP).
  6d10 mean 33, σ ≈ 7.4. P(6d10 ≥ 32) ≈ 55%. So: ~36% vs Marwen at full HP — but NOT permadeath.
- VS at 0 HP = Revivify-able. DR at 0 HP = Wish/True-Resurrection-only.

The asymmetry: DR and VS both have roughly similar P(drop Marwen), but DR triggers a 30× harder resurrection requirement.

---

### Threat ceiling conclusion

The Disintegration Ray is the encounter's defining threat. Its threat ceiling is:
- **63% probability of permanent character death before Marwen's first turn** (assuming beholder goes first and uses DR round 1).
- This does not change as encounter difficulty scales; it's a fixed property of DR + Marwen's stats.
- For 63% of combats in which the beholder opens with DR vs Marwen: the party loses their wizard **permanently** at the level-5 table before the wizard's player takes a single action.

The encounter *is* designed to be a loss. But permanent death of a specific PC before they act is a qualitatively different outcome from "the party retreats / is driven out / is defeated in a hard fight." The player experience of "I rolled initiative, I rolled lower than the beholder, my character is now permanently dead and cannot return" is distinct from the intended brutal-but-fair encounter design.

For the level-5 Compass Edge, a Disintegration kill = permanent character loss (no accessible resurrection). **P(permanent loss round 1) ≈ 63%.** The stat block should include a DM note in the Tactics section, e.g.: "DM: Pre-session consent — Disintegration Ray causes permanent death for this party level. Options: (a) keep the clause as written; (b) treat disintegration as conventional unconsciousness; (c) allow DR to use 0-HP result and note the 'no resurrection' clause only if the PC is already at 0 HP from a prior effect." Joe must decide default mode; add a one-line note to Tactics.

**FEEL-R169-02 (NEW):** Counterspell interaction with Disintegration Ray ("Ranged Spell Attack") is unresolved. DR uses the "Ranged Spell Attack" tag which makes it Counterspellable under 5e RAW. For a CR-13 encounter designed to be a party loss, DM should decide: (a) DR is a spell attack and can be Counterspelled (Marwen's primary defensive option); (b) DR is a creature ability with an attack roll but not a "spell" in the Counterspell sense (beholder is not a "spellcaster"). If (a): DR is ~Counterspellable until Marwen runs out of 3rd-level slots (1 slot at level 5). If (b): DR is uncounterable and the 63% permadeath probability stands with no full-stop counter. Add a ruling note to the stat block (e.g. "DM: DR is [is/is not] a spell for Counterspell purposes.").
