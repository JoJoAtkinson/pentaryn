## Phase B — Feel Analysis

### FEEL-F77-01 (NEW — LOG ONLY): Pre-turn kill window from UG-prone + beholder init advantage

**Trigger:** Beholder rolled init 17 vs Bazgar init 11. Lair action at 20 fired Unstable Ground → Bazgar prone BEFORE BAZGAR EVER ACTED. Beholder then multiattacked prone Bazgar with advantage (+7 vs AC 18, ~82% hit) + Maw auto-crit on grappled target.

**Expected damage sequence:** TL1 (13) + TL2 (13) + Maw crit (avg 39) = 65 total. Bazgar at 49 HP max → 0 HP before taking a single action.

**The "unfair vs lethal" test:** This is the scenario briefing's key question. The party IS meant to lose, but the fighter going unconscious before rolling initiative-based action is table-feel jarring. At a real table: "I got downed before I swung once."

**Frequency:** Bazgar's DEX −1 means the beholder (DEX +3) beats him on initiative ~60% of the time. VUG-init-lair fires at 20, always before the beholder's turn. Combined probability of this pre-turn-kill window: if beholder beats Bazgar on initiative (60%) AND both TL hits AND Maw hits (roughly 80% × 80% × 82% ≈ 52%). So roughly **31% of Hard combats result in Bazgar going down before taking any action.**

**Recommendation for Joe:** Consider whether the R1 lair action should default to STD-A/STD-B instead of Bazgar (UG on a front-liner lair-to-multiattack combo is very strong). Alternatively: cap the multiattack + Maw chain against a grappled+prone target at the .md level (e.g., "if Maw would auto-crit AND target is also prone from lair action this round, redirect Maw to a different target"). This would still be lethal but not one-turn eliminating.

**Design note:** The UG→TL(grapple)→Maw(crit) synergy is documented and intended. The prone-melee-advantage scenario (FEEL-F40-01) and the thrulm-lair-prone-melee-synergy scenario both test this chain. The NEW finding here is the **probability quantification** (~31%) and the "zero player actions" table-feel impact.

### FEEL-F77-02: DD fires correctly, THP gain confirmed noise-level

DD stripped Sabriel's L3 slot in R1 as designed. Beholder gained 6 THP vs 99 HP pool = <7% increase. The THP gain is statistically invisible in the simulation. The slot attrition IS meaningful — stripped L3 removes: Revivify (emergency revive), a 4d8 Divine Smite, or Aura of Vitality (powerful healing over time). The design intent (drain the paladin's toolkit) works. The THP rider does not. Confirming known open issue: no change recommended without human decision.

### FEEL-F77-03: DD window closes in R2 because VS down Sabriel

The designed cadence (DD in R1 → VS in R2 into a depleted paladin) worked exactly as documented. VS downed Sabriel in R2 (33 psychic, her HP was 13 at that point). After R2, DD could never fire again. The R2 VS effectively executed the coup-de-grâce before DD could strip a second slot. This confirms: **DD is a R1-only mechanic against Sabriel.** If Sabriel is downed before her R1 turn (unlikely here since she had init 8 and beholder had 17), the DD window is moot. In practice, the DD window fires ~once per combat against the Compass Edge party. Not a bug — just a design limit.

### FEEL-F77-04 (NEW — LOG ONLY): DD hover-range descent not documented in R1 sequence

**Issue:** DD has 30-ft range. The beholder's default hover altitude in the chamber is 40-60 ft. For DD to fire post-Sabriel (init 8), the beholder MUST be within 30 ft of Sabriel at the altar at that moment. The beholder can only descend via fly movement on its own turn (init 17) — it can't use Shrine-Drift (BA) mid-LA-spend or use "Move" LA (1 LA) to close distance while holding the other 2 LAs for DD (DD costs 3 LA, uses entire budget).

**The implicit requirement:** The beholder must spend some of its R1 fly movement (30 ft/turn) descending during its multiattack turn. This is never stated in the tactics notes. A DM not thinking about it would hover at 50 ft, use the LA budget on DD, and find the target is out of range — DD wasted.

**Concrete case:** Sabriel advances from the entrance (~60 ft from altar) toward Bazgar (near altar). If Sabriel ends up at 40 ft from the altar stone after her LoH move, and the beholder is at 50 ft altitude directly above the altar, range = √(40² + 50²) ≈ 64 ft. DD out of range.

**Fix recommendation:** Add one sentence to the beholder's tactics notes under "Drain Divinity": *"DD descent requirement: on the beholder's own R1 turn, spend 30 ft of fly speed descending toward the altar floor before multiattacking, placing the beholder at ~20-30 ft altitude. This puts any PC at the altar within DD's 30-ft range. Shrine-Drift (BA) cannot substitute — SD fires on the beholder's own turn, not as a reaction or LA. If the beholder fails to descend in R1, DD is likely out of range post-Sabriel (BUG-A12-06)."*

This is LOG ONLY (not an auto-fixable source edit per briefing — it's a DM-play guidance addition, not an arithmetic fix).
