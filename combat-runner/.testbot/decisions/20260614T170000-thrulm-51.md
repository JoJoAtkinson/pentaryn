**Crash: No.** The `maw` verb dispatches without crash in both the standalone and multiattack routing paths.

**NEW FINDING — BUG-A51-01 (Maw verb shadowing — specific manifestation):**
The `maw` verb appears in BOTH `beholder-thrulm/multiattack` (verbs include "maw") AND `beholder-thrulm/maw` (single_attack). When a DM types "maw" intending only the Maw follow-up attack (the two tentacle lashes have already been resolved), the runner must disambiguate between two matching DB rows. If `multiattack` wins disambiguation, the DM receives:
1. A full 3-attack sequence (TL1 + TL2 + Maw) instead of just the Maw
2. The multiattack Maw sub-entry's rider text: "If the target is grappled by the beholder, this attack is a critical hit instead" — less explicit than the standalone row's "damage dice doubled"

If the standalone `maw` single_attack wins, the DM receives the correct isolated Maw attack with the explicit rider text. Disambiguation logic determines which; this is not exercised headlessly.

**Wording inconsistency (BUG-A51-01 component 2):**
- Standalone `maw` row rider_on_hit: "…it scores a critical hit **(damage dice doubled)**."
- `multiattack` Maw sub-entry rider_on_hit: "…this attack is a **critical hit instead**."

"Damage dice doubled" is explicit about the mechanical effect; "critical hit instead" relies on DM knowing the crit rule. Recommend standardizing to "damage dice doubled" in the multiattack Maw sub-entry for consistency.

### Maw scenario — Phase B analysis

**Scenario target `no_crash`:** ✓ The Maw verb fires without crash in both routing paths.

**Scenario target `rider_on_hit visible`:** ✓ The text is in the DB row. DM would see it in the runner output.

**FEEL-F51-01 (NEW — Maw zero-counterplay R1 confirmation):**
With Void-Feeding active and Unstable Ground from init 20, the beholder's R1 sequence deals an average of ~68 HP to Bazgar (TL+TL+Maw-crit), exceeding his 49 max HP with considerable margin. Bazgar is downed before his turn in the majority of R1 encounters where:
1. He is in melee reach of the beholder when it acts (requires beholder to descend or Bazgar to advance)
2. He fails the Unstable Ground save (80% rate at DEX −1)
3. TL-1 hits (75% at advantage vs prone AC 18)

The combined probability that Bazgar is downed before acting (all three conditions): ~0.80 × 0.75 × P(TL2 or Maw finishes job) ≈ 60%+ of R1s. This is not new (confirmed in fires #40, #45, #50), but this fire's simulation makes explicit that the auto-crit rider_on_hit mechanic works as intended and the nova is math-correct, not a bug.

**FEEL-F51-02 (NEW — Prone + ranged attack conflict in LA spend):**
After Bazgar goes prone (from UG), the LoH override redirects the beholder's VR away from the prone target to Marwen (non-prone). This correctly applies FIX-EV7-34-A (no DR/VR vs prone). However: the VR-vs-Marwen and the remaining 1 LA Tentacle vs Marwen path — after Marwen is downed by VR, the 1 remaining LA is wasted (no valid target except downed PCs and Bazgar at 10 HP who is prone again after TL in R2). The LA economy post-LoH redirect is slightly inefficient when prone logic removes one potential VR target. Not a design bug, but a note for the DM: if both Bazgar (prone) and Marwen (standing) are valid targets post-Sabriel, VR goes to Marwen; the remaining 1 LA Tentacle can validly target Sabriel (standing). Check target availability before committing LA sequence.
