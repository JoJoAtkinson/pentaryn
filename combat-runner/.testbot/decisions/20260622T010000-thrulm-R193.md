### New bugs found this fire

**BUG-R193-01 (NEW — MEDIUM): Targetable tentacle HP untracked — multiattack reduction not implemented**

Beholder stat block says: "The beholder has four tentacles; it can grapple up to four creatures at once. Each tentacle can be targeted separately (AC 15, 15 HP)."

The DB has no tentacle HP tracking mechanism. If a player explicitly targets a tentacle:
- The runner has no sub-target tab or HP pool for individual tentacles
- When a tentacle reaches 0 HP, multiattack should drop from 2x Tentacle + Maw to 1x Tentacle + Maw (or 0x if both severed)
- Currently unreachable: a player typing "attack tentacle" would either trigger the multiattack (wrong) or fail to match any verb

Impact: mechanically valid party tactic is entirely unavailable in GUI. At this party level (Bazgar, Fighter 5, likely 14+ STR + +4 weapon), tentacle-sever in 1-2 hits is plausible (15 HP, AC 15). Missing this option reduces available counterplay vs the grapple threat.

**BUG-R193-02 (NEW — LOW): Grapple escape implemented as saving throw, not ability check**

`apply_condition_on_hit: {condition: grappled, save_ability: str, save_dc: 16}` in both Tentacle Lash DB attacks (multiattack + tentacle_legendary).

5e RAW grapple mechanics:
1. Grapple applies **automatically** on a successful hit (no initial save)
2. Escape requires a **STR (Athletics) or DEX (Acrobatics) ability check** (contested or flat DC)

The DB field `save_ability: str` likely fires as a DC 16 STR **saving throw** rather than a DC 16 STR ability check. These use different modifiers for some characters:
- Characters proficient in Athletics but not STR saves get the same modifier (PB applies to both via Athletics proficiency)
- Characters proficient in STR saves but not Athletics get the same modifier in reverse
- In practice, for this party (Bazgar is a Fighter = proficient Athletics), difference is zero; for Marwen (Wizard, not proficient in either), also zero; for Sabriel (divine martial, likely proficient in STR or CON saves, not necessarily Athletics), could differ

Secondary issue: if the runner applies the save at hit-time (as a save to avoid grapple), it gives targets an initial save they don't have RAW. If it only shows the save at escape-attempt, it may or may not allow subsequent escape attempts per turn.

Impact: low for this specific party composition (modifier parity), but mechanically incorrect. Flag for runner schema review.

### Feel findings

**FEEL-R193-01 (NEW — HIGH): Drain Divinity range vs hover altitude — confirmed conflict**

DD requires the target to be within 30 ft. The beholder hovers 40-60 ft above the altar. For DD to reach a PC, the beholder must descend to ≤30 ft of the target's height. If PCs are on the chamber floor (~5 ft), the beholder must descend to 35 ft altitude or lower. This:
(a) brings it closer to Bazgar's melee range (if Bazgar can grapple or climb/jump to 35 ft — unlikely, but narrows the buffer)
(b) reduces Void Scream's height advantage (VS from 40 ft up catches more of the chamber floor)
(c) conflicts with "hover near shrine altar" tactic (altar likely at floor level, so hovering 40-60 ft above it IS the shrine vicinity)

The beholder can't use DD without a deliberate, tactically costly descent. DM currently has no cheat-sheet guidance on when to descend for DD vs stay high. FEEL-R188-01 (DD positioning conflict) carry-forward; newly quantified: the beholder must surrender ~10-20 ft of altitude safety to use its most thematic 3-LA ability.

**FEEL-R193-02 (NEW — MEDIUM): No counterplay exists for multiattack grapple chain**

In Round 1, all three party members have DEX +1 to +2 and STR -1 to +3. Escape DC 16 STR check succeeds on 13+ for Bazgar (d20+5 = 35% success), on 16+ for Marwen (d20+something = <25% for wizard). Marwen is grappled and goes to 0 HP before her turn arrives. She cannot attempt escape. The grapple-then-Maw sequence (2 Tentacle Lash hits → auto-grapple → Maw at high EV) has no meaningful counterplay for a party at this level. A 5th-level party facing a CR 13 grapple boss with no Freedom of Movement, no Shield spell (Marwen could have Shield but is unconscious before acting), and limited Misty Step charges has minimal options. Noted as expected design (party MEANT to lose), but the specific grapple mechanic feels less "lethal and fair" and more "cannot-act."
