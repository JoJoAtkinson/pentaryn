### Round 2 opening — LA economy and prone combo

**IC 20 — Lair action (Unstable Ground, DC 16 DEX):** Sabriel (DEX +0): roll 9 → fail → **prone**.

**Beholder (init 18) — Multiattack with prone targets:**
Sabriel is prone and within 5 ft of beholder → attackers within 5 ft have **advantage** vs prone targets.
1. Tentacle vs Sabriel (prone, advantage on +7 vs AC 19): P(hit) = 1-(12/20)² = 64%. Rolls 16 & 9 → higher 16+7 = 23 → **hit**. 3d6+4 = 17 dmg → **1 HP**, grappled.
2. Maw vs Sabriel (grappled + prone, advantage): Roll 18 → hit. 4d8+4 = 24 → **0 HP, unconscious**. Grappled.

*Finding: Unstable Ground lair action (prone) + grapple (from round 1 LA tentacle) → advantage on all beholder attacks vs Sabriel in round 2. This 3-element combo (lair → prone → grapple → advantage) is not called out in the cheat sheet but is realistically achievable.*

**After Marwen's turn (round 2) — Drain Divinity (3 LA, full budget):**
DC 16 CHA vs Sabriel (already at 0 HP — **Drain Divinity cannot target unconscious creatures** since they have no spell slots accessible and the DC 16 CHA save is auto-failed). Retarget: only Bazgar remains conscious. Bazgar is a Fighter — no spell slots, no divine ability. **Drain Divinity finds no valid target.** All 3 LA wasted in this attempt; beholder must use Tentacle instead.

*NEW FINDING: Drain Divinity requires a target "that has spell slots, divine favor, or clerical powers." In a late-round scenario where Marwen and Sabriel are down, Bazgar (Fighter) has none of these. Drain Divinity becomes a dead ability — beholder has 3 LA but can only use Tentacle or Move. This is FEEL-R140-02 context: Drain Divinity is only valuable rounds 1-2 when spellcasters are conscious.*
