#### FEEL-R218-01 (MINOR — ability-rotation gap)

**Unstable Form advantage is structurally dead weight when Ancient Resonance is available.**

**Location:** `shrine-touched-derro.md`, Unstable Form trait + Ancient Resonance action.

**Issue:** Unstable Form grants advantage on attack rolls when the shrine-touched takes ≥10 damage in one turn. Ancient Resonance (its most powerful action) uses a saving throw, not an attack roll — Unstable Form advantage doesn't apply. In any round the creature has Ancient Resonance recharged, it will use it (higher total damage vs multiple targets), meaning the advantage bonus from Unstable Form never fires in practice.

**Example from simulation:** Shrine-Touched took 13 damage, triggering Unstable Form (advantage on attacks). It then used Ancient Resonance (save, no attack roll). Advantage went unused.

**Edge cases where advantage fires:**
- Ancient Resonance is on cooldown (recharging on 5–6 ≈ not recharged ~66% of turns). Then shrine-touched uses Multiattack with advantage.
- If shrine-touched has already used Ancient Resonance this round (not possible per action economy).

**Estimate:** Unstable Form advantage fires in roughly 66% of turns where the creature was hit for ≥10 damage (the turns where AR is not available). This is significant but not zero. Whether this is "dead weight" depends on encounter length. In short encounters (2–3 rounds vs Compass Edge), the creature fires AR on the first recharged turn (often round 1 or 2) and Multiattack on others. The advantage only triggers in Multiattack rounds.

**Design call — not a bug.** The interaction is coherent; the question is whether Unstable Form should also apply to saves (making it more broadly useful) or is intended to synergize specifically with the Multiattack follow-up. Flag for Joe: if Unstable Form is meant to feel meaningful every time it triggers, consider "advantage on attack rolls AND +2 to DC of ancient_resonance" or just "advantage on the next Multiattack OR the next Ancient Resonance save DC is increased by 2." Currently it only matters on Multiattack turns.
