### Beholder legendary action decision analysis

The Tentacle+Void Ray sequence is straightforwardly dominant **every round** for all game states in which the beholder is above ~30 HP and not under imminent threat of taking concentrated damage. This is confirmed:

- **Move (1 LA)** is only worthwhile if the beholder is out of reach for a critical follow-up, or needs to force Antireality on a different angle. In practice, the beholder hovers and can Shrine-Drift as a bonus action anyway, reducing Move's value further.
- **Drain Divinity (3 LA)** is dominated by Tentacle+VoidRay for direct damage (28.5 vs effectively 0 direct damage from DD). DD is viable only for attrition if Sabriel is the last PC standing or the party has infinite HP but limited spell slots. At level 5 this is a slow route.

**NEW FEEL-R170-01:** The LA menu creates a degenerate rotation. Every round reduces to the same choice: Tentacle (1 LA) vs Void Ray (2 LA) fill the budget identically. There is no round where spending LA differently (other than forced repositioning) is clearly correct. A second 2-LA option (e.g., "Frighten" one target for a round, DC 16 WIS) or a 1-LA save-based option would force the beholder player (DM) to actually make strategic decisions round-to-round. The current menu makes the LA segment of the beholder's turn completely predictable after round 1.

### Void Ray vs Disintegration Ray naming check

The rotation description asks to "verify Void Ray (save-based) vs Disintegration Ray (attack-roll) naming is unambiguous for the DM."

**Finding:** The cheat sheet's LA table labels them separately:
- "Void Ray (Costs 2 Actions)" → 22/23 (in-chamber) force damage, save-based
- "Disintegration Ray (Recharge 5–6)" → 45 (10d8) force damage, attack-roll

In the stat block, both are titled "\*\*[Name] Ray\*\*" with their distinction in the attack/save text. In live play, a tired DM could announce "I'm using my Ray" ambiguously.

**NEW FEEL-R170-02:** Suggest adding parenthetical distinguishers to the beholder stat block and cheat sheet, e.g.:
- Disintegration Ray → "Disintegration Ray *(Ranged Spell Attack — roll to hit)*"
- Void Ray (LA) → "Void Ray *(Legendary, 2 LA — DEX save, DC 16)*"

This is a presentation suggestion, not an auto-fix. The DM should be able to run without confusion but the verbal shorthand "Ray" covers two different mechanics.
