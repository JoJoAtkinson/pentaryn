## Phase B — Playtest slice: `lair-action-round`

*(thrulm-rotation.json index 5 — lair action timing, option dominance, and damage stacking vs Compass Edge)*

### Setup

- **Beholder:** HP 80 (after Round 1 exchanges), AC 17, 3 LA/round. Void-Feeding active. Hovering 40 ft above altar.
- **Derro present:** 2 Thrall Derro (HP 22, AC 14 each), 1 Shrine-Touched Derro (HP 35, AC 16) — supporting cast; not primary focus this slice.
- **Party HP at round start:** Bazgar 30/49 (AC 18, DEX +1), Marwen 15/32 (AC 15, DEX +2), Sabriel 28/44 (AC 19, DEX +1). Total pool: 73 HP.
- **Positions:** Bazgar and Sabriel within 20 ft of shrine (melee engagement). Marwen at ~35 ft (pillar cover, outside shrine proximity zone).

### Initiative count 20 — Lair action

**Options evaluated:**

| Option | Expected damage | Targets | Conditions |
|--------|-----------------|---------|------------|
| Manifest Thralls | ~0.4 HP per thrall (1 THP granted; +1 hit vs AC 18+) | 2 thralls | No meaningful combat impact |
| Unstable Ground | 0 dmg, 65% prone on one target | 1 creature | Advantage granted to follow-up melee |
| Void Eruption | E[dmg] = 7.2 per PC (65% × 11 avg) × 2 PCs in zone | Bazgar + Sabriel | Pure damage, no concentration |

**Beholder selects Void Eruption** (highest EV with 2 PCs in zone — trivially dominant).

**Results (d20 rolls):**
- Bazgar DEX save: d20+1 = 9 vs DC 16 → FAIL. 2d10 = 7 force. Bazgar: 30 → **23 HP**.
- Sabriel DEX save: d20+1 = 8 vs DC 16 → FAIL. 2d10 = 7 force. Sabriel: 28 → **21 HP**.

### Key observations

**FEEL-LA-R265-01 — Void Eruption "taxation before action" (NEW):**
Void Eruption fires at init count 20, before Sabriel (init ~17) or either PC acts. Result: Bazgar and Sabriel were each down 7 HP before they took a single action this round. Mechanically correct (lair actions are a beholder feature), but the table experience is: PCs sit down at the start of the round already 7 HP behind what they were at end of last round. This is a "taxation before action" feel — unavoidable, unchallengeable, and invisible to anyone not watching the init counter. At the table, players often forget lair actions fire at init 20 and experience the damage as a mystery.
*Joe decides: add explicit DM cue in the .md start-of-turn checklist ("BEFORE anything else: lair action fires at count 20 — announce it as a visible seismic event") so players know what's happening.*

**FEEL-LA-R265-02 — Lair action decision trivially dominated (NEW, confirms prior logs):**
With 2+ PCs within 20 ft of shrine (the default state for any melee engagement), Void Eruption dominates Unstable Ground (multi-target vs single) and is vastly better than Manifest Thralls (~0.4 EV per thrall). Manifest Thralls confirmed at approximately zero effective combat value: Thrall hand axe at +1 to hit vs AC 18–19 = 20% hit rate × 2 dmg = 0.4 HP expected per thrall per reaction attack. CHA +1 THP (1 THP per thrall) was also negligible. DM has effectively one lair choice: Void Eruption. The Manifest Thralls option, as written, is never correct in this encounter.
*Joe decides: redesign Manifest Thralls (reaction-attack trigger already floated in known-state log); consider whether Unstable Ground should be upgraded to multi-target or replaced.*

**FEEL-LA-R265-03 — Combined output 99 HP/round is overwhelming, not dramatically threatening (NEW):**
The beholder's full-resource round (lair + 3 LA + action) dealt 99 HP against a 73 HP party pool. The mechanism (lair fires early, LA fires reactively after each PC, action fires last) creates a cascade where no single ability feels dangerous — instead, all HP drains simultaneously across a long sequence. At the table, this reads as "everyone's already dead by the time the beholder acts" rather than "the beholder made a lethal choice and the party scrambled." Compare to a single Disintegration Ray (45 HP = one player KO'd dramatically) vs the 99-HP cascade spread across all three. The spread is arithmetically more lethal but experientially flatter.
*Joe decides: is 99-HP cascade the right "meant to lose" texture, or does the encounter want at least one discrete scary choice? Potential fix: cap lair+LA damage within a single round to prevent total pre-action-phase wipe.*

**Legendary action economy observation:**
The beholder spent: 1 LA (Tentacle) + 2 LA (Void Ray) = all 3 LA. Neither Move nor a second Tentacle was used. Drain Divinity (3 LA, targets spellcasters within 30 ft) was unavailable — beholder was >30 ft from any PC until its own turn. The LA spend was correct (Tentacle to grapple Sabriel, Void Ray to neuter Bazgar), but note that all 3 LA went to offense rather than repositioning. Post-fight Marwen remains grappled, which is the correct state for the beholder to enter Round 3 with.
