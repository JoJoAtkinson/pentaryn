### Key findings

**FEEL-R143-01 (NEW): LA economy confirmed — Tentacle (1 LA) dominates Void Ray (2 LA) on DPR-per-LA.**  
Against AC 18 (Bazgar) or AC 15 (Marwen), Tentacle Lash (1 LA) yields 9-9.1 avg expected damage plus a meaningful grapple chance, while Void Ray (2 LA) yields 11-15 avg expected damage (DC 16 DEX, casters fail ~35-45% of the time). Per LA spent: Tentacle = 9/LA, Void Ray = 5.5-7.5/LA. The cheat sheet's implicit suggestion that Void Ray is the "damage" option and Tentacle is the "control" option is misleading — Tentacle is also better damage per LA against low-AC targets. DM guidance: save Void Ray for when Marwen is specifically the target at range (higher fail chance) or when Tentacle grapple is tactically undesirable. Not an auto-fix — log for tactical guidance update.

**FEEL-R143-02 (NEW): Beholder threatens party extinction even on a "dead" turn.**  
This "empty-void" round — no Disintegration Ray, no Void Scream — still expected to down Marwen (32 HP) and brings the party close to total wipe. The beholder's floor DPR (multiattack + 3 LAs) is ~50-70 expected across the party per round. A level 5 party has ~125 combined HP. At floor DPR the party is fully down in ~2-2.5 rounds. This means the "interesting" abilities (Disintegration Ray, Void Scream) are gravy — not the threat. The core sustained attack action is already lethal. Consequence for feel: even if the DM avoids Disintegration Ray to let the party last longer, the beholder still wins in round 2-3. The "dramatic" abilities (Drain Divinity, Disintegration Ray) serve narrative flavor more than they change the outcome. This is correct design for "party meant to lose" but the DM should be explicitly briefed that holding Ray/Scream in reserve doesn't buy the party much more time.

**FEEL-R143-03 (NEW): Grapple-then-Maw combo (34 max / ~13 expected per sequence) is real but fragile.**  
If Tentacle Lash hits AND grapple saves fail AND Maw hits (all independent rolls), Sabriel takes 28 damage in one action. But the probability of all three succeeding: 0.45 × 0.40 × 0.45 = 8.1%. The high-damage scenario is a 1-in-12 event. In practice the beholder gets Tentacle + failed grapple most rounds, then the Maw attack is against a non-grappled target. The combo is real but the "grapple advantage on saving throw" text in the Maw description still reads as orphaned (Maw is an attack, not a save — see STAT-02). Grappled targets get disadvantage on their own attacks and speed 0, which is valuable positioning; the Maw damage is a separate bonus if it hits. Feel note: the grapple-plus-Maw combo is not as brutal as the stat block implies because the orphaned save text does nothing mechanically for Maw itself.

**FEEL-R143-04 (CONFIRM): Drain Divinity 30 ft range forces beholder floor-level; this was first flagged FEEL-R140-03 and confirmed again here.**  
In this simulation, the beholder at 20 ft altitude stays at 20 ft for Multiattack reach (10 ft reach on tentacles, so beholder must be within 10 ft of Sabriel's position — altitude included). Drain Divinity would require dropping to within 30 ft of Sabriel. The beholder using Drain Divinity in the same round as grapple/Maw is physically impossible unless it opens with Drain Divinity (before grappling), spending the entire 3 LA budget. This confirms the existing flag: Drain Divinity is a full-LA burn that forfeits all other LA this round AND potentially requires dangerous proximity. The DM should be reminded to use it early (before grapples) or wait for a subsequent round when targets are already grappled and the beholder's altitude is already low.
