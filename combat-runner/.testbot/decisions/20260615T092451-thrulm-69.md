### Key findings from simulation

**FEEL-F69-01 (NEW — Log only): Fighter probabilistic one-shot R1 before his turn — quantified.**

The UG lair action (init 20) + beholder multiattack (init 19) with advantage creates a mathematical near-certain damage delivery:

- P(Bazgar prone from UG) = 75% (d20+0 vs DC 16, need ≥ 16: 25% pass rate)
- P(T-A hit with advantage, need 11+) = 75%
- P(Maw hit with advantage after grapple established, need 11+) = 75%
- P(T-A + Maw both hit | both have advantage) = 75% × 75% = 56.25%
- Damage if T-A+Maw land: 14 + 39 = 53 > 49 HP → Bazgar DOWNED
- P(Bazgar downed R1 if prone) = ~56%
- P(Bazgar downed R1 overall) = P(prone) × P(down given prone) ≈ 0.75 × 0.56 = **~42%**

Without prone (beholder multiattack without advantage, e.g. Bazgar not prone):
- P(T-A hit, no advantage, need 11+) = 55%
- P(Maw hit, no advantage) = 55%
- P(both hit) = 0.55² = 30%
- P(Bazgar downed, no prone) = ~30%

The UG lair action raises the R1 fighter-downed probability from 30% to 42% — a meaningful uplift. Critically: this happens before Bazgar (init 10) has acted. The fighter has no counterplay window. Sabriel can LoH (her action), but this wastes Sabriel's turn AND triggers DD immediately after.

**Category:** *Lethal, not unfair* — the encounter is designed for a TPK. But the init sequence (lair at 20, beholder at 19, Bazgar at 10) guarantees the tank absorbs a heavily-advantaged multiattack before acting. At level 5, this is the "opener that defines the fight's arc." The feel concern: is a level 5 player's fighter being KO'd in the first ~5 seconds before rolling dice an appropriate dramatic beat, or does it collapse the tension prematurely?

**Design note:** The beholder's ability to close 60 ft in one turn (30 move + 30 Shrine-Drift) combined with Unstable Ground prone means R1 is almost always a multiattack-with-advantage scenario on whoever the beholder decides to target. If the intent is to preserve one dramatic PC action before the first KO, consider: UG targeting the back-line caster (disadvantage on ranged attacks from prone, less decisive) or using Manifest Thralls in R1 (lower threat ceiling). As documented: this is a known-deadly encounter. Just noting the probability for Joe's table narrative.

**FEEL-F69-02 (NEW — Log only): OBR loop creates attrition on Sabriel if she melee-attacks STD in R2.**

If Sabriel decides to attack the STD in R2 (melee, after spending R1 on LoH), the OBR loop fires:
- Sabriel attacks STD (melee, within 5 ft) → STD takes damage → OBR fires: +4 vs Sabriel AC 19 (30% hit), on hit 1d8+1+1d8 necrotic = 10 avg, STD self-damage 1d4 psychic (avg 2.5).
- If Sabriel hits twice (two attacks per turn for divine martial 5?), OBR fires once per round (reaction). One OBR counter = 10 avg necrotic/slashing + 2.5 self-damage.
- STD's self-damage from OBR (2.5) pushes toward Unstable Form threshold (10 dmg in a turn). If Sabriel deals 10+ in her attack turn (possible with smite), STD enters Unstable Form next round: advantage on all attacks. Sabriel's attacks trigger an accelerating STD threat.

**Practical upshot:** Killing the STD fast with concentrated fire is better than chipping it down with Sabriel's melee (OBR creates feedback that benefits the STD). Using Fireball/radiant from outside melee range avoids OBR and fire vulnerability is suppressed anyway in Altar Zone. Marwen's safest STD-kill path is the already-demonstrated Fireball (which killed the thralls as a bonus). Sabriel should smite from range if possible, or cast a no-attack action, to avoid triggering OBR.

No current guidance in beholder.md, STD.md, or cheat sheet tells the party (or DM adjudicating NPC tactics) that Sabriel's melee attacks create an OBR-attrition risk. Suggest an NPC-tactics note in shrine-touched-derro.md: "DM note: OBR counter-attacks are triggered by melee hits from PCs. Against Sabriel (divine martial with multiple attacks), OBR fires once per round when she hits in melee. If STD takes 10+ total damage in a round (including OBR self-damage), Unstable Form activates next turn — STD becomes more dangerous the more it's hit in melee. Preferred counter: concentrate ranged/AoE on STD before engaging melee."

**FEEL-F69-03 (NEW — Log only): Thralls die to Fireball R1 before Manifest Thralls can ever fire.**

Both thralls are dead after Marwen's Fireball R1 (init 11). Manifest Thralls grants thralls 1 THP + reaction attack at init 20. If the lair action in R1 is Unstable Ground (correct per priority note), Manifest Thralls never fires in R1. If the beholder saves Manifest Thralls for R2, the thralls are already dead. Result: Manifest Thralls never fires at all in a Fireball-opening scenario.

The lair action choice in R1 is effectively forced: UG is correct (Manifesting dead thralls is useless, VE has no PCs in range yet). The beholder gets exactly one impactful lair action option in R1 (UG). This removes a decision — the DM doesn't need to think, just use UG. Good for table speed; slightly boring as a boss mechanic.

R2+ lair actions: thralls dead → Manifest Thralls unavailable. If PCs close to shrine (within 20 ft), VE becomes an option. UG is still valid (any PC in 60 ft range). The beholder's lair action in R2-R3 is typically UG (keep melee fighter prone) or VE (if party crowds the altar). Lair action variety is structurally low in this formation once thralls die.

**Design note:** This is the "Manifest Thralls useless vs AC 18+ party" problem (flagged since early fires, also BEL3-38-A in prior log). It's compounded here by the thralls being Fireball-killable in R1. If thralls survive R1 (no Fireball, or beholder shields them), Manifest Thralls becomes meaningful in R2. Consider: swap thralls for 1 Shrine-Touched (much harder to kill with AoE) in the shrine-wedge encounter variant, or double thrall HP to 44 (average of 8d8+8 for CR 1/4-scaled variant). Joe's call.
