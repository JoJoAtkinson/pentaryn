**FEEL-TENTACLE-VERB-R298-01 (NEW)**
*Severity: low. Verb collision risk. Log only.*

The beholder's `multiattack` action has `"tentacle"` as a verb, while the legendary
`tentacle_legendary` action has `"lash"` and `"legendary tentacle"` as verbs. If a DM types
`tentacle` at the table intending to invoke the 1-LA legendary action, the runner will match the
full multiattack (3 attacks, no LA cost) instead. The correct verb for the legendary action is
`lash` or `legendary tentacle`.

This is a documentation gap, not a DB bug. The verb lists are internally consistent (no
duplicate cross-entries), but `tentacle` as a natural word for the legendary action will
mismatch. A table note or a disambiguation comment in the start-of-turn checklist would prevent
this: e.g., "**Legendary Tentacle** → type `lash` (not `tentacle` — that fires full multiattack)."

---

**FEEL-PRONE-LOOP-R298-01 (NEW)**
*Severity: medium/feel. Log only.*

Sequence in simulation: Lair Action (Unstable Ground, init 20) knocks Sabriel prone → beholder
acts at init 18 → one Tentacle Lash drops Sabriel from 12 HP to 0 before she can act.

The "can't counterplay" moment arises from: (1) lair action fires before beholder's turn, and
(2) beholder has the highest initiative. Against a party that has their healer (Sabriel) at low
HP going into a round, this combo deterministically prevents any healing action. The party had
no opportunity to break the sequence — Sabriel went prone before her turn, and the beholder got
to act first.

This is by-design for a "meant to lose" fight. The note is: if Joe wants to give the party *a
moment* of meaningful choice before the cascade, consider either (a) granting a reaction to
half-speed movement upon regaining consciousness (optional RPG mercy mechanic), or (b) having
the beholder's standard tactics not Tentacle-Lash already-12-HP targets when healthier PCs are
available (beholder is intelligent — it might pivot to Bazgar instead). Neither is a fix, just
a DM-discretion option.

---

**FEEL-DRAIN-ALTITUDE-R298-01 (NEW, extends R297-01)**
*Severity: medium/systemic. Third confirmation. Log only.*

Drain Divinity (30 ft range) failed to fire in Round 1, Round 2, or within any simulated
legendary-action budget in this run. Sabriel was present as the divine target throughout. The
beholder remained at standard hover altitude (40-60 ft). Three consecutive Phase B simulations
(R296 void-scream-aoe, R297 rager-berserk-gate, R298 this run) have all failed to use Drain
Divinity due to altitude. This pattern now meets the threshold for a **design recommendation**
rather than a feel note:

**Recommendation for Joe:** Either (a) change Drain Divinity range to 60 ft (matches the
beholder's standard hover altitude), or (b) add a specific tactical note that "beholder must
descend to use Drain Divinity (spend Shrine-Drift BA or movement before attacking)" so the
DM knows the trade-off exists and can plan around it. As written, the tactics say "Focuses on
Drain Divinity" vs divine casters but the positioning makes this mechanically impossible without
intentional pre-movement.
