### Step 4: Death blow — "-29" damage

With shrine-touched at 15 HP (after OBR self-damage if manually tracked; 17 HP if ignored):
- Sabriel uses second attack: longsword + 2nd-level Divine Smite.
- Runner is told to deal 29 damage. HP: 17 (or 15) − 29 → **dead**.

**OBR on death blow — NEW BUG-R192-01 (reaction economy conflict):**

The scenario's `expect_watch` for OBR is at step 5 (after the death blow). But strict 5e rules: a reaction resets at the **start of the creature's turn**. The shrine-touched already used its reaction at step 2 (the -28 hit). The shrine-touched never takes another turn before the -29 kills it. Therefore:

- **RAW:** OBR cannot fire at step 4 (reaction already spent at step 2). The `expect_watch` at step 5 would **fail** if the runner tracks reaction economy.
- **Scenario design intent:** The thematic flavor says "the oath-echo fired anyway" — implying OBR fires as a death-throes effect regardless of reaction economy.
- **Gap:** The stat block does not include any text granting OBR special death-throes status (e.g., "even if the shrine-touched has already used its reaction this round"). Without that text, a runner correctly tracking reactions would NOT surface the OBR prompt at step 4.

This scenario cannot pass cleanly until one of the following is resolved:
1. The OBR stat block text is updated to explicitly allow "even if the reaction has already been used this turn" on killing blows.
2. The scenario is restructured: only one damage hit before the killing blow (no earlier OBR trigger to spend the reaction), so OBR can fire at the death blow as intended.
3. The expect_watch is moved to after step 2 (the -28 hit, first OBR trigger), and step 4 is a death-blow survivability test only.

The most mechanically sound fix is **option 3** — the scenario currently proves the wrong thing. The death-blow flavor ("oath-echo fired anyway") requires a stat block update (option 1) that Joe must decide.

### Feel analysis

**Finding 1 — BUG-R192-01 (NEW): OBR reaction economy conflict in scenario design**

The scenario is mechanically invalid as written. If OBR fires at step 2 (the -28 hit), the reaction is spent and cannot fire at step 4 (the death blow). The `expect_watch` at step 5 tests a situation that RAW cannot occur. Until OBR gets explicit "death-throes" language in the stat block, the scenario tests an untested assumption.

Recommended fix (scenarios.yml): remove the -28 step entirely, replace with a single -45+ killing blow (e.g., "-45" on a shrine-touched at 45 HP). OBR fires once on the killing blow with reaction intact. This makes the scenario clean and validates the death-throes thematic intent without the reaction economy problem.

**Finding 2 — FEEL-R192-02 (NEW): Unstable Form × OBR stacking invisible to runner**

When OBR fires at the -28 hit, Unstable Form is already active (28 ≥ 10 triggered it). The OBR attack roll should have advantage. The runner has no mechanism to:
1. Track that Unstable Form triggered on the incoming hit
2. Apply advantage to the OBR attack that fires at the same event
3. Show both the advantage roll AND the dual damage dice

This triple-automation gap (Unstable Form tracking + OBR dice + OBR self-damage) means the shrine-touched's most tactically interesting turn (hurt → retaliates with advantage, then attacks harder) is entirely manual for the DM. The creature's design is most legible on paper; it runs opaquely in the GUI.

**Finding 3 — BUG-R156-01 dual-damage confirmed still CRITICAL blocking issue**

Until OBR is updated via `combat_action_upsert` with a `single_attack` `attacks` array (1d8+1 slashing + 1d8 necrotic as separate damage fields), this entire scenario class (`thrulm-shrine-touched-retaliation`, `thrulm-oath-breaking-dual-damage`) runs silently broken. Both are regression-gate scenarios for the same unresolved root bug.

**Finding 4 — Encounter-wide pattern: Shrine-Touched dies before its arc lands (carry-forward FEEL-R191-01)**
