### Phase B Findings

**FEEL-R109-01 — Thrall attacks negligible vs party, but thralls as POSITIONING tools are the real threat:**  
Expected per-thrall damage vs Marwen (AC 15, lowest in party): +1 to hit = 35% hit chance × avg 2.5 damage
= **0.875 HP/round per thrall.** Three thralls = 2.625 HP/round combined — immaterial against a 32-HP wizard.
The danger isn't thrall damage. In the simulation, Thrall B dropped Marwen from 1 HP (after Bazgar's
Lay on Hands save) to 0 again — but that was a lucky hit. The real thrall role: body-blockers that
prevent the party from reaching the beholder or escaping via Compel Thrall forced movement. Suggest adding
explicit "thralls as movement-controller, not damage-dealer" note to `_overview.md` or cheat sheet
round-by-round checklist.

**FEEL-R109-02 — Manifest Thralls lair action: 1 THP cosmetic + BA attack adds ~0.875 HP damage:**  
Confirmed this run: 1 THP (CHA +1) is erased by any damage. The BA reaction attack from Manifest Thralls
is one more +1/1d4 swing = 0.875 expected HP. The lair action accomplishes nothing mechanically. This is the
4th-run confirmation. Recommendation: redesign as "one thrall within 60 ft may immediately move up to its
speed toward a target the beholder designates AND make one attack" (remove THP, keep the attack-trigger
flavor). This gives the lair action a real mechanical bite at the cost of re-flavor only. Needs Joe to approve.

**FEEL-R109-03 (re-confirm) — DD consumes all 3 LA every round Sabriel is standing:**  
Same pattern as R107, R108. With Sabriel having any slot remaining, DD (3 LA) strictly dominates.
Tentacle LA (1) fired once here only because beholder had a spare after VR (2). Move LA never fired.
This is the **5th consecutive run** with DD-locks-entire-LA-budget outcome.

**Mechanical note — Antireality "+2 AC" vs disadvantage interaction:**  
Under current .md ("+2 AC after seeing roll"), Bazgar's roll of 14+5=19 still hits the retroactively-boosted
AC 19. Under the fire #6 intended design ("disadvantage on triggering attack"), Bazgar would reroll with
disadvantage: (roll 8, 14) = lower of 8 vs 14 = 8, +5 = 13 < 17 → MISS. The +2 AC version is strictly
weaker than pre-roll disadvantage for attack rolls that land in the 17-18 range. Until Joe confirms the
fire #6 change and it lands in .md + DB, DMs are applying the weaker version.
