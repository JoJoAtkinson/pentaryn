### Feel Analysis

**FEEL-F48-01 (NEW) — DWDs are a slot-tax mechanism, not a threat; cheat sheet overrepresents their combat role**

The two DWDs die in R1 to Fireball + basic attacks at this party level. Their actual encounter value is:
- Force Marwen to spend one L3 slot (Fireball) before the beholder fight begins
- Chip ~6 HP off Marwen from R1 crossbow fire (noise vs 32 max HP)
- Buy the beholder an extra 1-2 rounds of setup time (if DWDs screen the party's advance)

What they do NOT do: deal meaningful damage, threaten a KO, survive to R2 in any standard sequence.

The DWD's "pivot to Disengage-Crossbow" at 5 HP is mechanically valid and denies Bazgar an OA, but it converts "instant death from OA" into "death on Sabriel's next attack." Net result for DWD-2: survives ~5 seconds longer, deals one more crossbow shot (3.25 expected HP to Marwen). The Cunning Action is a real mechanical benefit (saved ~7.8 avg OA damage), but the DWD dies either way.

**Cheat sheet note (recommend for Joe):** The cheat sheet One-Liner Tactics row for Deep Watch says "Stand in formation, focus-fire targets / Break formation, flank, separate from group." This implies sustained tactical play. More accurate for DMs: *"Stall-screen: delay party advance for 1 round, force one slot, then die. Don't plan for R2 unless HP allows. The screen's value is the 3rd-level slot spent on Fireball, not the DWDs' damage."* Low priority for cheat sheet edit; DM-facing flavor note only.

**FEEL-F48-02 (NEW) — Move LA has narrow use case that Shrine-Drift does NOT cover; not dead weight, but close**

Beholder legendary actions include **Move** (1 LA: fly speed reposition). Beholder also has **Shrine-Drift** (BA: 30 ft, can pass through terrain). Comparison:

| Property | Move LA | Shrine-Drift (BA) |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| Distance | Up to fly speed (30 ft) | Up to 30 ft |
| Terrain phasing | No (normal flight) | Yes (through objects/creatures) |
| Cost | 1 LA (uses LA budget) | Bonus action (free slot) |
| Timing | End of another creature's turn (reactive) | Beholder's own turn only |
| Provokes OA? | Yes (normal movement) | Not specified — DM ruling needed |

**Move LA's narrow advantage:** it fires at the END of another creature's turn, enabling reactive repositioning that Shrine-Drift cannot provide (Shrine-Drift is only available on the beholder's own turn). Example: a PC moves to flank the beholder, changing its threat range. Move LA lets the beholder close the gap immediately (end of PC's turn) rather than waiting until its next turn.

**Why Move LA is rarely optimal despite this:** In the 3-LA budget vs Sabriel, the beholder holds all 3 for DD (post-Sabriel protocol). This exhausts all LA before Move is reachable. In R2+, the 1-LA budget is contested by Tentacle (deals damage + grapple, 1 LA) and Void Ray (22 avg force, 2 LA). Move LA provides no damage and equivalent distance to Shrine-Drift.

**Recorded instances of Move LA in optimal-play logs:** 0 across fires #1–#47.

**Recommendation for Joe:** Move LA is mechanically unique (reactive repositioning, no terrain-phasing requirement like Shrine-Drift) but is crowded out by damage LAs in every simulated round. Consider either: (a) leaving it as-is (niche but technically correct), or (b) replacing with a 1-LA option with more combat presence (e.g., "**Eye Pulse:** one creature the beholder can see within 30 ft makes DC 16 WIS save or is frightened until end of its next turn" — anti-divine flavor, 1-round micro-CC, pairs with the VS FRIGHTENED theme without costing the full VS recharge).
