## Phase B — Generative Playtest: antireality-timing

*thrulm-rotation.json index 7 of 8.*

### Setup
Bazgar (Fighter/Battlemaster 5) attacks beholder in melee over 3 rounds. Beholder uses Antireality (reaction, once per round) on Bazgar's first attack each round. Bazgar: +7 to hit vs beholder AC 17 — needs 10+ to hit.

DB status: **Antireality is correctly implemented.** BUG-F7-02 was closed R224. The DB trigger is `action_executed` (pre-roll) and effect reads "impose disadvantage on the triggering attack roll." The rotation.yml scenario description's note "[DB entry still shows old text (BUG-F7-02)]" is **stale** — see DRIFT-R251-01 below.

### Math

**Base hit rate:** Needs 10+, probability = 11/20 = **55%**

**With Antireality (disadvantage):** P(lower of 2d20 ≥ 10) = (11/20)² = **30.25% ≈ 30%**

**Flip rate per first attack:** 55% − 30% = **25%** (expected: 0.25 hits converted to misses per round)

### Round-by-round results (3 rounds, 6 attacks total)

| Round | Attack | Rate | Expected Hits |
|-------|--------|------|---------------|
| 1 | First (Antireality active) | 30% | 0.30 |
| 1 | Second (reaction spent) | 55% | 0.55 |
| 2 | First (Antireality active) | 30% | 0.30 |
| 2 | Second (reaction spent) | 55% | 0.55 |
| 3 | First (Antireality active) | 30% | 0.30 |
| 3 | Second (reaction spent) | 55% | 0.55 |
| **Total** | | | **2.55 hits** |

Without Antireality: 6 × 0.55 = **3.30 expected hits**

Expected hits prevented over 3 rounds: **0.75**. Assuming ~10 avg damage/hit (Battlemaster 5 with longsword+STR), Antireality saves **~7.5 HP** over 3 rounds of sustained melee from a single attacker.

### Feel analysis

**Antireality is mechanically sound and consistently valuable.** The 25% flip rate is significant enough to be felt (roughly 1 hit prevented per 4 attacks), but not dominant. The once-per-round cap correctly limits it — the second Bazgar attack is unaffected.

**FEEL-R251-01 (new): Antireality dead weight when Taunt-imposed disadvantage is active.**
If Rager Taunt succeeds on Bazgar (DC 12 Cha save; Fighter with typical CHA 8-10 → +0 or -1 → ~40-45% fail rate) and Bazgar targets the beholder anyway (fighting through the Taunt), Bazgar already rolls with disadvantage. Per 5e PHB, disadvantage doesn't stack — the roll is a straight roll if any source gives advantage. Wait, the rule is: if you have both advantage and disadvantage, they cancel. If you have disadvantage from two sources, it's still just disadvantage (doesn't compound). So Antireality imposing disadvantage on a roll that's already at disadvantage does nothing additional — the reaction is completely wasted.

**DM implication:** In the likely case where Rager is in front-line melee and Bazgar is taunted, Antireality should be held and directed at Sabriel's attacks instead (Sabriel is not taunted, has no pre-existing disadvantage). This creates a **tactical priority choice** for the beholder that isn't documented anywhere in the stat block or cheat sheet. The beholder is intelligent (INT 17) — it should always redirect Antireality to the most "profitable" attacker (the one without pre-existing disadvantage). **Log as DM guidance gap.**

**FEEL-R251-02 (new): Precision Attack (Battlemaster) partially counters Antireality.**
Bazgar can expend a superiority die for Precision Attack: *after rolling*, add a d8 to the attack roll. With Antireality active (disadvantage), Bazgar rolls 2d20 takes lower. If the lower result would miss, Precision Attack can add 1-8, potentially converting the miss back to a hit. At level 5, Bazgar has 4 superiority dice (d8). Expected value of Precision Attack on a disadvantaged attack: the marginal benefit depends on the margin — a miss by 1-8 (needs +1 to +8 to convert) benefits; a miss by >8 doesn't. Across 3 rounds, Precision Attack nearly cancels Antireality's effect if Bazgar dedicates all 4 dice to countering it. This interaction means **at level 5, Antireality is approximately countered by Precision Attack expenditure** — the beholder's reaction buys nothing if Bazgar commits the dice. This is not documented.

**DM guidance gap:** Beholder with INT 17 should recognise this and redirect Antireality to Sabriel (no Precision Attack) after observing Bazgar's die usage.

### Antireality over full encounter estimate

At 4-6 combat rounds (beholder retreats at 30 HP; from 110 HP with ~12 avg damage/round incoming from full party, ~6-7 rounds): Antireality fires ~6-7 times. Expected hits prevented: 6-7 × 0.25 = 1.5-1.75. HP saved: ~15-18. This is modest (16% of beholder's HP) but consistent. Antireality is not overpowered and not dead weight — it's correctly tuned.
