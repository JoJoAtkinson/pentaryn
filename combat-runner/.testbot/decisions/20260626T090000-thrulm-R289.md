## Phase B — Scenario: `lair-action-round` (thrulm-rotation index 5)

*Slice definition: Simulate the beholder's lair action on initiative count 20. Evaluate all three
lair action options, identify the dominant choice per round phase, and check for known bugs.*

---

### Setup

- Round 1, initiative count 20 (beholder not yet acted this round — lair action fires before most creatures).
- Beholder at altitude ~50 ft above shrine altar. HP 110, in-chamber (Void-Feeding active).
- Party entering the chamber: Bazgar 30 ft from altar, Marwen 50 ft, Sabriel 35 ft.
- Thrall Derro ×2 positioned within 20 ft of the shrine altar (near-altar screen).
- Derro Rager at 15 ft from party (front line).

Available lair actions:
1. **Unstable Ground** — DC 16 DEX save within 60 ft; fail → prone.
2. **Manifest Thralls** — up to 3 charmed derro within 60 ft gain CHA THP + reaction move/attack.
3. **Void Eruption** — DC 16 DEX save within 20 ft of shrine; fail → 11 (2d10) force damage.

---

### Lair Action 1: Unstable Ground — Analysis

Target priority: Bazgar (Fighter 5, HP 49, AC 18) — heaviest armor, lowest DEX.

DEX save probability (DC 16):

| PC | Assumed DEX | DEX save mod | P(fail DC 16) |
|----|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| Bazgar (heavy armor fighter) | 10-12, no DEX prof | +0 to +1 | 75–80% |
| Marwen (wizard) | 14, DEX prof assumed | +2+3 = +5 | 55% |
| Sabriel (divine martial, heavy armor) | 10-12, no DEX prof | +0 to +1 | 75–80% |

Using Bazgar with +0 DEX save: P(prone) = 75%.

**Prone rider on next beholder turn (multiattack):**
Prone targets have disadvantage on attack rolls; melee attacks against them have advantage.
Beholder's Tentacle Lash and Maw are melee (reach 10 ft and 5 ft) — both gain advantage vs prone
target if the beholder descends to melee range. Alternatively, beholder stays at altitude and uses
ranged Disintegration Ray or Void Ray (both ranged, which suffer disadvantage vs prone — netting out
to normal probability, so prone is irrelevant for ranged attacks).

Net effect in-chamber:
- If beholder descends to melee range: Tentacle Lash advantage vs AC 18 → P(hit) = 1-(9/20)² = 80%
  vs normal +7 vs AC 18 = 50% hit rate. Expected damage per Tentacle: 80% × 14 = 11.2 vs 50% × 14 = 7.0.
  Gain: **+4.2 per Tentacle, ×2 = +8.4 expected damage per multiattack**.
- If beholder stays at altitude (>5 ft): prone creature still creates difficult positioning.
  If beholder uses ranged Void Ray vs prone: disadvantage on save — wait, Void Ray is a save not an attack,
  so prone has NO effect on save-based attacks. Void Eruption similarly unaffected.
  **Conclusion: Unstable Ground only has full mechanical value if beholder descends to melee.**

Expected EV from Unstable Ground into melee multiattack:
- P(prone) × additional damage = 0.75 × 8.4 = **6.3 expected additional damage from prone setup**
- This is additive to the beholder's main-action multiattack EV.

Known finding: FEEL-007 (R282) noted "round-1 lair action + multiattack can deal ~42 EV to Bazgar
pre-turn." This fire's simulation confirms: Unstable Ground → prone → advantage multiattack creates
a meaningful burst above the baseline, but only when beholder has descended to melee range. The
"42 EV" estimate from R282 included the Void-Feeding bonus and assumed 2×Tentacle + Maw all with
advantage. Re-derivation:
- 2× Tentacle at advantage vs AC 18: 80% hit, 14 avg → 22.4 expected
- Maw at advantage vs AC 18 and prone: also 80%, 21 avg → 16.8 expected
- Total in-melee-range multiattack with prone Bazgar: ~39.2 expected (slight correction from R282's
  42; difference is whether Maw has Void-Feeding applied — yes it does, so 21 is correct: 4d8+3+1=4d8+4).
  Recalculated: Maw = 4d8+4 in-chamber, avg = 22. 80% × 22 = 17.6. Total: 22.4+17.6 = **40 EV ✓**.

This is consistent with FEEL-007. **Bazgar at 49 HP takes 40 expected damage in a single multiattack
following an Unstable Ground prone — survivable (9 HP remaining) but one below-average roll kills him.**

### Lair Action 2: Manifest Thralls — Analysis

**Confirmed broken. No change from R287 findings (FEEL-017, FEEL-018).**

The lair action reads: "Up to three derro that are **charmed** by the beholder and within 60 feet
of it gain temporary hit points equal to the beholder's Charisma modifier (minimum 1). They can
immediately use their reaction to move or make a weapon attack."

Both blockers remain:
1. **No valid targets:** Thrall Derro have condition immunity "charmed (enslaved instead)" — they
   are enslaved, not charmed. Clay-Shaping-created derro (who would be charmed) require 1 minute
   of uninterrupted action — this never happens in combat. No other charmed derro exist in the
   standard encounter setup.
2. **Reaction blocker:** Even if targets existed, Thrall Derro stat block states "Reactions: None."
   The granted reaction would be wasted on any Thrall Derro target.

In the lair-action-round scenario, the beholder **cannot effectively use Manifest Thralls**.
The DM selecting this option wastes the lair action entirely. **This is a dead lair action slot.**

### Lair Action 3: Void Eruption — Analysis

*DC 16 DEX save, 11 (2d10) force damage, each creature within 20 ft of the shrine.*

At this point in the encounter (round 1, party just entered), party members are NOT within 20 ft of
the shrine. The beholder uses Void Eruption on the near-altar zone:

**Targets within 20 ft of shrine at round 1:**
- Thrall Derro ×2 (HP 22, AC 14, DEX +1, no DEX save prof): P(fail DC 16) = 75%.
  Expected damage: 0.75 × 11 + 0.25 × 5.5 = 8.25 + 1.375 = **9.6 per Thrall Derro**.
  Total damage to 2 thralls: 19.2 expected — significantly reduces their HP (22 HP each).
- Party members: not in range. **0 damage to PCs.**

**Friendly-fire finding (FEEL-006, carry-forward):**
The beholder using Void Eruption in round 1 damages its own thralls for ~9.6 each before they act.
A below-average roll on 2d10 (say 6) with a thrall save fail still deals 6 damage to a 22-HP minion.
Above-average (15+): deals 15 damage, reducing a Thrall Derro below half HP in one shot.
There is no exception for allied creatures — the lair action hits all creatures in the zone.

**Effective use case:** Void Eruption is tactically sound when PCs have closed to within 20 ft of
the shrine AND no thrall derro are near the altar. In round 1 vs the standard setup, it provides
zero PC damage and actively harms the beholder's own minions. **A DM running Void Eruption in round 1
is making a mistake.**

### Dominant Lair Action by Combat Phase

| Round | Best Lair Action | Reasoning |
|-------|-----------------|-----------|
| 1 (open) | Unstable Ground | PCs not near shrine; prone setup nets +8 EV on multiattack |
| 2 (party closing) | Unstable Ground | PCs still >20 ft from shrine most likely; prone value remains |
| 3+ (party at shrine) | Void Eruption (if thralls cleared) or Unstable Ground | Depends on thrall positioning |
| Any round | **Not** Manifest Thralls | Broken; no valid targets |

**Finding: Unstable Ground is the mechanically dominant lair action in rounds 1–2 and only loses
dominance if the party avoids melee (removing the prone-advantage combination) or clusters near
the shrine. The beholder has a clear "correct" lair action in the first two rounds — this is
one-note but not degenerate (the prone-into-advantage combo is exciting and visible).**

### New Findings from this Slice

**FEEL-024:** Void Eruption creates a positioning incentive for the beholder to clear its thralls
from the near-altar zone before using Void Eruption. A clever beholder would use Compel Thrall (BA)
to move a thrall OUT of the 20-ft zone before firing Void Eruption. This is actually an interesting
tactical layer — but it requires spending a bonus action AND a lair action in the same round. The
cheat sheet doesn't mention this combo. **Log only — potential cheat sheet addition for Joe.**

**FEEL-025:** Unstable Ground is the only lair action with a visible decision point (target selection
among the three PCs). The other two are either broken (Manifest Thralls) or map-state-dependent
(Void Eruption). Recommend: when revising Manifest Thralls (which must be redesigned per FEEL-017/018),
consider a mechanic that fires in ALL combat states without requiring specific charmed targets — e.g.,
"Up to three derro within 60 ft (regardless of charm status) can immediately make one weapon attack as
a reaction." This preserves the intent (beholder + minion coordination) without the broken charm
dependency. **Log only — design redesign for Joe.**
