**NEW — FLAG-R256-01 (Derro Rager Taunt DC inconsistency):**
Taunt ability states DC 12 Charisma saving throw. Rager has CHA 9 (-1), PB +2.
Standard derivation: 8 + PB(+2) + CHA(-1) = 9. No stat combination on the Rager's sheet
gives DC 12 with PB+2. Closest: using STR (+2) or CON (+2) gives 8+2+2=12, which could
indicate the designer intended an intimidation-style effect derived from STR/CON. If DC 12
is intentional (and it probably is — DC 9 would almost never stick), document the derivation
stat in the ability text. Otherwise adjust to DC 9 or note "DC 12 (design choice)."
**Joe decides.** Not auto-fixed.

## Phase B — Scenario: `thrulm-oath-breaking-dual-damage`

**Prelude:** Sabriel's radiant smite found the shrine-touched mid-swing. 29 damage.
The creature was already dead when the runes flared — but the oath-echo fired anyway,
and the axe still landed.

**Scenario steps:**
1. `-28` damage → shrine-touched HP: 45 − 28 = **17** (assert: expected 17)
2. `-29` damage (death blow) → shrine-touched HP: 17 − 29 = **0** (dead)
3. `expect_watch` for `oath_breaking_retaliation`

### Initiative (approximate)

| Creature | Init mod | Roll | Total |
|----------|----------|------|-------|
| Bazgar (Fighter 5) | +2 est. | 14 | 16 |
| Sabriel (divine martial 5) | +2 est. | 11 | 13 |
| Marwen (Wizard 5) | +2 est. | 9 | 11 |
| Shrine-Touched | +2 (DEX 15) | 8 | 10 |

Party acts before shrine-touched. Shrine-touched will never act if party focuses it.

### Step 1: `-28` damage

Shrine-touched: 45 → 17 HP ✓

**Unstable Form activated:** 28 damage in one turn ≥ 10-damage threshold → shrine-touched
gains advantage on attack rolls until end of its next turn. DB has no trigger/automation for
this; DM must track manually. No action yet (shrine-touched hasn't acted).

### Step 2: `-29` (death blow)

Shrine-touched: 17 → 0 HP (dead/unconscious).

**Oath-Breaking Retaliation fires on the death blow.** The trigger condition is
`"event": "damage", "match": "any damage from visible source"` — no HP threshold or
survival check in the trigger. The reaction fires the moment damage is taken, before the
HP update resolves to 0. This is mechanically correct and creates the intended drama:
a dying creature's last counter-strike.

**DB-BUG-R156-01 CONFIRMED STILL OPEN:**
The `oath_breaking_retaliation` DB row contains:
- `"type": "reaction"` ✓
- `"trigger": {...}` ✓
- `"narration": "Pain becomes prophecy — the shrine screams through its vessel."` ✓
- `"effect"`: describes "+4 to hit, 1d8+1 slashing + 1d8 necrotic" in text

But there is **NO `attacks` array** in the row. The runner fires the narration and effect
text but outputs no dice. DM must resolve the attack manually from the effect text:

| Step | Roll | vs |
|------|------|----|
| Attack roll | d20+4 | Bazgar AC 18: needs 14+, ~35% hit |
| Hit: slashing | 1d8+1 | avg 5.5 → ~2 avg with hit chance |
| Hit: necrotic | 1d8 | avg 4.5 → ~1.5 avg with hit chance |
| Regardless: self-damage | 1d4 psychic | avg 2.5 to shrine-touched (already dead) |

Expected damage to Bazgar per retaliation: ~3.6 avg. Negligible vs 49 HP — the reaction
is atmospheric rather than threatening.

**The self-damage (1d4 psychic) firing on a creature already reduced to 0 HP is a dead
action.** Cosmetically correct flavor (shrine screams through a dying vessel), but mechanically
inert since unconscious/dead creatures skip further damage checks unless they have death saves
active. DM can safely skip the self-damage roll on a death blow.

**Death blow retaliation: should it fire?** RAW: yes — the trigger fires on "damage from
visible source" with no survival condition. The reaction resolves before the HP update.
This is also consistent with how 5e handles reactions: they interrupt the triggering event.
No design change recommended — the death blow retaliation is a feature, not a bug. The DB gap
(no dice) is the only problem.

### NEW — BUG-R256-02 (ancient_resonance missing secondary 1d4 psychic damage in DB)

While investigating the shrine-touched for this scenario, confirmed the following:

The `ancient_resonance` DB row has:
```json
"damage": {"dice": "2d10", "type": "necrotic"},
"save": {"dc": 14, "ability": "Dex", "on_save": "half"}
```

The stat block says: "taking 11 (2d10) necrotic damage on a failed save, or half on a
successful one. **Creatures that fail take an additional 2 (1d4) psychic damage**..."

The cheat sheet also correctly documents the psychic rider: "(11 (2d10) necrotic; on fail:
+2 (1d4) psychic)."

**The DB has no secondary damage field for the 1d4 psychic.** When "resonance" is typed,
the runner rolls 2d10 necrotic only — the psychic rider is silently dropped. The `area`
type schema (unlike the `attacks` array for single_attack) doesn't expose an `extra_damage`
field in the current DB schema. Fix requires either:
1. A schema extension to support secondary damage on area types, OR
2. Adding a `rider_notes` text field in the DB row describing the psychic damage so the
   runner at least narrates it even if it can't roll it.

**Severity: MEDIUM.** The 1d4 psychic is 2.5 avg expected damage — small but thematically
important (the "glimpse of the sealed god" effect). Requires `combat_action_upsert` fix;
not editable by testbot. **Needs Joe.**

### Legendary action economy note (Phase B observation)

Beholder wasn't present in this scenario but the encounter configuration was reviewed:

- **Drain Divinity (3 LA):** Costs entire LA budget. Against Sabriel within 30 ft: removes
  highest paladin slot + generates 2×level THP. Beholder must descend from default 40-60 ft
  hover to ≤30 ft to use. Dominant against Sabriel but creates a vulnerability window
  (Bazgar can close to melee).
- **Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA):** The 3-action split does more expected damage than
  Drain Divinity against a non-spellcasting target, and maintains positioning flexibility.
- **Observation:** Against Compass Edge (Sabriel with divine slots + Marwen with wizard
  slots), the beholder's optimal round is almost always: Drain Divinity vs Sabriel (3 LA).
  This never varies. The FEEL-F253-09 flag remains valid — one-note LA strategy every round.
