### Key findings from this slice

**FEEL-NEW-07 — VS collateral on thralls creates a real positioning tension.**  
Void Scream also hits the thrall (16 psychic on fail, psychic-resistant). The thrall dropped from 22→6 HP from collateral VS damage. The beholder is effectively damaging its own minions each time it uses VS while thralls are clustered nearby. This is mechanically coherent and creates an interesting tactical constraint: position thralls away from the shrine cluster before using VS, or accept losing them.  
*Design note: the collateral is small (one-action VS to take out clustered PCs is worth the 16 thrall damage), but it makes the beholder smarter if it delays VS until thralls are sent away. No fix needed — this is good emergent behavior.*

**FEEL-NEW-08 — Void Scream has a hidden secondary effect on surviving casters: concentration disruption.**  
On a failed save, the target takes 33 psychic → CON save DC max(10, 33/2=16) → DC 16. Most casters fail this (Marwen CON save +2: 75% fail rate). On a SUCCESSFUL save (Marwen takes 16 psychic): DC 10. Marwen CON save +2: ~30% fail. Even when Marwen saves against VS, there's a 30% chance her concentration spell (Hypnotic Pattern, Web, Slow, etc.) ends. This secondary effect is significant and not visible in the stat block text — DMs may overlook it.  
*Log: DMs should be reminded in the cheat sheet that VS half-save can still kill concentration. Low priority — standard 5e rule — but worth adding a DM note.*

**FEEL-NEW-09 — FRIGHTENED + shrine adjacency creates forced retreat into derro.**  
Sabriel (FRIGHTENED, 11 HP) cannot move toward the beholder. The only escape direction in the Thrulm chamber is toward the entrance — where Deep Watch derro are positioned. A frightened divine martial retreating into 2 Deep Watch derro (multiattack, 2×3 dmg = 6/round) while at 11 HP is a death trap. The FRIGHTENED condition synergizes with the dungeon layout to push PCs backward into more danger. This is good design — the chamber geography amplifies the fear condition. No fix; flag as an intentional emergent design property.

**FEEL-NEW-10 — VS "can hear" clause and the unconscious Marwen edge case.**  
In this run, VS activated BEFORE Marwen's turn (beholder at IC 20, Marwen at IC 16). VS drops Marwen to 0 HP. She is now unconscious for the rest of the round. The FIX-FC48-A ruling (fire #48) states: unconscious creatures cannot hear VS and are excluded. But Marwen was CONSCIOUS when VS fired — she simply drops mid-activation. The ruling only applies to PCs who were ALREADY unconscious before the action. No interaction here; ruling applies cleanly.  
*Confirmation: ruling is correct and this scenario doesn't create ambiguity.*

**Feel validation — expected DPR analysis:**  
VS avg expected damage per activation (all 3 targets):  
- Bazgar: P(fail with disadvantage, save +0) ≈ 80% fail → 33×0.80 + 16×0.20 = 29.6 expected  
- Sabriel: P(fail with disadvantage, save +4) ≈ 55% fail → 33×0.55 + 16×0.45 = 25.4 expected  
- Marwen: P(fail, save +5) ≈ 35% fail → 33×0.35 + 16×0.65 = 22.0 expected  
**Total expected per VS use: 77 DPR against 125 total party HP (62% party HP per activation).**  
With Recharge 6 probability (16.7%/round): effective DPR contribution = 12.8/round. Significant but not reliable.

VS feels appropriately catastrophic when it fires in a clustered scenario. Recharge 6 ensures it's not spammable. Design is working as intended for a "party meant to lose" encounter.
