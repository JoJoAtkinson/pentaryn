"Marwen is down. The beholder angles toward Bazgar — the void within it hungry for something

### Simulation: Disintegration Ray vs. Bazgar at 24 HP

**Beholder turn:** Disintegration Ray on Bazgar (AC 18), range 120 ft.
- Attack roll: +7 (base +6, Void-Feeding +1) vs AC 18 → needs 11. Hit rate ~50%.
- Damage on hit: 10d8 force = avg 45.
- Bazgar at 24 HP: 24 − 45 = −21 → 0 HP.

**Disintegration trigger:** The stat block reads: "If this damage reduces the target to 0 hit
points, the target is disintegrated." Since 45 >> 24, the trigger fires on ANY hit.

**5e instant-death check:** Excess damage = 45 − 24 = 21. Bazgar max HP = 49. 21 < 49 → standard
instant-death rule does NOT apply. But the disintegration clause applies independently of the
instant-death rule. Bazgar is reduced to 0 HP by the ray → disintegrated → cannot be restored
by Revivify or Raise Dead. Requires true resurrection (7th-level) or wish (9th-level).

**At level 5, the Compass Edge has no access to true resurrection or wish.** Sabriel (divine
martial) likely has Revivify (3rd-level) — which is explicitly insufficient for a disintegrated
creature. If Bazgar is disintegrated, he is effectively a permanent-death event for the party
unless they seek out a much higher-level NPC caster.

### Feel analysis: FEEL-003 escalation

**FEEL-003 (carry-forward from fire #6, still OPEN):** This simulation confirms the threat
ceiling in specific numbers:
- A 50% single die roll permanently kills a level-5 Fighter (the party's primary tank).
- Mitigation options available to Bazgar at this point in the fight:
  - Dodge action: requires spending his action, leaving him unable to attack. Disadvantage on
    beholder's roll reduces hit rate from 50% to ~25% (both dice need 11+: 0.50² ≈ 25%). Worth
    considering, but Bazgar may need his action to attack.
  - Action Surge: 1/short rest — already used if Bazgar used it Round 1. Probably burned.
  - Shield of Faith (Sabriel): +2 AC → Bazgar AC 20 → beholder needs 13+ → 40% hit rate.
    Reduces but does not eliminate the threat.
  - Cover/hard cover: The encounter room has "half-destroyed shrine remains" (cover). Full cover
    would block the ray entirely, but moving to full cover may cost Bazgar his attack.

**At realistic combat state (Marwen unconscious, Bazgar ~24 HP after 2 rounds), the Disintegration
Ray represents a ~50% permanent-kill flip from a single beholder attack roll.** This is "meant
to lose" design intent, but permanent character death (not death's-door, PERMANENT) at level 5
against a boss encounter is a specific design choice that needs explicit flagging.

### Round continuation after Disintegration Ray

**If Bazgar is disintegrated:**
- Sabriel alone faces the beholder (~76 HP) with limited slots.
- Beholder pivots: Drain Divinity on Sabriel next (if she's within 30 ft), or Tentacle Lash
  to grapple and Maw to finish.
- Sabriel, divine martial, HP 33, likely has 1–2 remaining 2nd-level slots.
- Beholder at 3 LA per round: Drain Divinity (3 LA) burns her last slot. Once slots gone,
  beholder shifts to multiattack + Void Ray (2 LA) for damage.
- Avg damage per beholder round vs Sabriel: Tentacle (14) + Tentacle (14) + Maw (22) = 50 main
  action + Void Ray (19 avg on fail). Sabriel has 33 HP → dies round 3, possibly round 2 if Maw
  connects while grappled.

**Encounter verdict:** Party is TPK'd by end of Round 3 in most initiative orderings. This is
within design intent. The key feel question is whether the path to TPK feels "epic and deadly"
or "mechanically inevitable before the party gets meaningful turns."

### FEEL-R278-01: Disintegration as soft-kill variant — recommendation (LOG ONLY)

**Joe must decide:** For the Compass Edge's campaign context, is permanent character death on a
first-encounter CR-13 boss acceptable? If not, consider:

**Option A — Narrative soft-kill:** The beholder "disintegrates" a PC into a holding state —
they are absorbed into the shrine's void, preserved but inert. They can be retrieved if the
party ever comes back with higher-level allies to purify the shrine. The disintegration becomes
a plot hook rather than a character death certificate. No mechanical change needed; just a DM
ruling clarification.

**Option B — Mechanical save escape:** Add a CON saving throw rider: "If the creature succeeds
on a DC 16 Constitution saving throw before being disintegrated, it is reduced to 0 HP and falls
unconscious instead of being disintegrated." This lets the party still lose the fight without
losing the character. Requires updating the .md and the DB action spec.

**Option C — As-written:** Keep permanent death. The encounter is designed to lose; the party
should flee before the beholder fires Disintegration Ray. The DM should telegraph the recharge
roll prominently so the party knows when it's available.

Recommend Option A or C; Option B changes the ability significantly from the original design.
