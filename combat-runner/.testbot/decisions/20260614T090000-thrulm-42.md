### When does the beholder realistically reach 30 HP?

**Party damage per round (post-R1, standard encounter state):**
- Bazgar (Fighter 5, Battlemaster): 2 attacks at +5 vs AC 17 (45% hit) × avg 8.5 per hit = 7.65 avg/round. Action Surge R1 doubles to 4 attacks ≈ 15.3 avg.
- Marwen (Wizard 5): Fireball (8d6 = avg 28; beholder DEX save +6 vs DC 14 = 65% success → 35% full 28, 65% half 14 → expected ≈ 19 avg). R2+ with Scorching Ray: 3 beams at +6 vs AC 17 (45% each) × avg 7 = 9.45 avg.
- Sabriel (divine martial 5): 2 attacks at +6 vs AC 17 (45% hit) × avg 8.5 = 7.65 avg/round (sans smites).

**Total party output R2+ (full party, no casualties): ≈ 24.75 avg/round.**

To drop the beholder from 110 HP to 30 HP requires 80 damage → ≈ 3.2 rounds at full party output. But the beholder kills or disables PCs by R2–R3 (design intent). With realistic casualties:
- Marwen eliminated R2 (~62% from DR as simulated fire #39): party output drops to ~15 avg/round.
- Reaching 30 HP then requires 5+ more rounds from a 2-PC party.

**Conclusion: the 30 HP retreat threshold is a corner-case escape hatch, not a standard combat arc.** It triggers when the party has an exceptionally lucky R1 (Action Surge, Fireball, smite crits all connecting) before the beholder's first nova. Roughly estimated: <10% of full encounters will reach the 30 HP threshold with any PC still standing.

### Retreat sequence: action economy analysis

When the beholder IS below 30 HP and wants to flee to the lower shaft:

**Option A — Safe retreat (Disengage action + Shrine-Drift BA):**
- Action: Disengage → no OAs from any PC.
- BA: Shrine-Drift → 30 ft movement, phasing through obstacles.
- Normal fly speed: up to 30 ft additional movement (total mobility: 60 ft).
- Cost: **full action slot lost** — no multiattack, no DR, no VS.
- At 27 HP, the beholder sacrifices all offensive output for guaranteed escape.

**Option B — Offensive retreat (multiattack/DR + Shrine-Drift BA, OAs accepted):**
- Action: multiattack or DR fires.
- BA: Shrine-Drift → 30 ft + normal fly movement → 60 ft potential.
- OA cost: up to 2 OAs (Bazgar, Sabriel; Marwen typically not in melee reach).
- Expected OA damage: Bazgar (45% × 8.5 = 3.8) + Sabriel (45% × 8.5 = 3.8) = **7.6 avg combined**.
- At 27 HP: 7.6 avg OAs leaves 19.4 HP expected. Non-trivial risk of death during retreat (~15–20% probability of combined OA total ≥ 27).

**Option C — No Shrine-Drift (fly-speed retreat only):**
- Fly speed: 30 ft/turn. Against party movement of 30 ft (all PCs), beholder cannot escape in 1 turn.
- This is the slowest option and leaves the beholder in combat range.

**DM decision gap:** The tactics section says "retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit." It does NOT specify whether the beholder uses Option A or B. A DM who reads this at the table will likely choose B (offensive) because the stat block leads with aggressive behavior, but the OA risk at 27 HP makes B potentially self-defeating. **The tactics section should explicitly specify: "below 30 HP, use Option A (Disengage + Shrine-Drift) — forgo multiattack to guarantee escape."** This is a sub-addition to LOG-F7-05.

### "Below 20 HP: crush/disintegrate while fleeing" — conflict with safe retreat

The beholder's retreat text states two thresholds:
- **<30 HP:** "retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit."
- **<20 HP:** "attempts to crush/disintegrate the party while fleeing."

The <20 HP behavior ("crush while fleeing") **requires the action slot** (multiattack = action; DR = action). But the safe-retreat protocol (Option A) also uses the action (Disengage). The two behaviors directly conflict: a beholder below 20 HP cannot simultaneously "crush/disintegrate" AND safely Disengage before Shrine-Drifting.

If the DM follows the <20 HP text and uses the action for offensive output (Option B), the beholder accepts OA risk that at 15 HP could easily end the combat via OA death — turning the dramatic last-stand into a whimper killed by Bazgar's reaction attack. Narratively anticlimactic.

**Recommendation (sub-addition to FEEL-F22-02):** The two HP thresholds should have explicit priority guidance:
> "Below 30 HP: Disengage (action), Shrine-Drift (BA), retreat. Do not use offensive actions during retreat.
> Below 20 HP: if within 1 action's reach of ending Sabriel or killing a standing PC with DR, fire offensively this turn only; accept the OA risk. Otherwise, Disengage retreat takes priority."

### NEW — FEEL-F42-01: Shrine-Drift phase-through OA trigger is ambiguous

**Finding:** Shrine-Drift allows the beholder to "move through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain." RAW opportunity attacks trigger "when a hostile creature leaves your reach using its movement" (PHB 195). The normal resolution: OA fires when the beholder leaves a PC's reach zone during its movement.

When the beholder phases **through** a PC (enters from one side and exits the other), the OA trigger fires as the beholder exits the PC's threat zone on the far side. This is mechanically equivalent to a normal move-past. No ambiguity in this case.

**The ambiguity:** If the beholder Shrine-Drifts directly away from the PC (never entering the PC's occupied space), does the OA trigger as normal the moment the beholder crosses the 10-ft reach boundary? Yes — this is standard, same as any movement. No issue here.

**Edge case:** The beholder begins its movement **inside** a PC's reach (within 10 ft, standard combat position). It Shrine-Drifts 30 ft away in one movement. The OA triggers at the moment the beholder "leaves reach" — but with Shrine-Drift's phasing property, does the beholder conceptually "teleport" out of reach (no OA) or "move through" reach (OA fires normally)?

Ruling required: **Shrine-Drift is movement, not teleportation.** The phasing property says "moves through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain" — this explicitly describes movement, not instantaneous repositioning. Therefore, the OA fires normally at the moment the beholder exits the PC's reach zone, identical to a normal move-past.

**Table risk:** DMs may incorrectly adjudicate Shrine-Drift as teleportation (bypassing OA) because "phasing through" reads as magical translocation. The stat block does not include language clarifying the OA interaction, and no existing DM note in the beholder's reaction/bonus-action sections addresses this.

**Recommend adding to Shrine-Drift text (Joe edits):**
> *(Shrine-Drift is movement, not teleportation — it provokes opportunity attacks normally when leaving a creature's reach. The beholder must Disengage [action] before Shrine-Drifting to avoid OAs.)*

This is a cross-reference to LOG-F7-05 but the specific wording is needed in the stat block itself.

### LA economy during retreat

If the beholder retreats below 30 HP, its legendary action economy for the escape turn:

LA refreshed at start of beholder's turn. After the beholder's turn (where it Disengages + Shrine-Drifts), the remaining PCs take their turns. LAs available: 3.

**Standard reactive-LA spend (post-Bazgar turn):** Tentacle LA (1 LA) — but the beholder has Shrine-Drifted away; is Bazgar within 10 ft? If the beholder moved 60 ft away, no. **LAs are wasted this round** because the beholder is now out of Tentacle reach and VS/DR aren't LAs. Void Ray (2 LA, 120 ft range) is still viable even from 60 ft away. 

**Corrected R1 retreat LA spend:**
- Post-Bazgar turn: Void Ray (2 LA, 120 ft range, 22 avg force vs Bazgar). This still functions regardless of retreat distance.
- Post-Sabriel turn: 1 LA remaining → Tentacle (1 LA) if in range; otherwise hold.

This is unchanged from existing LA-economy guidance. The retreat distance does not negate Void Ray (120 ft range), which is the key mid-range LA.
