### Feel analysis

**Damage output:** 3 slashing damage on a 35% hit rate. Expected DPR vs Marwen (AC 15): 0.35 × 2.5 = **0.88 avg per round**. Over 4 rounds (expected encounter before TPK): ~3.5 total thrall damage. Against Marwen's 32 HP, this rounds to zero impact.

**What the thrall actually does:** The thrall is a beholder-directed positioning piece, not a damage source. Its function is to:
1. Occupy Marwen's attention (forcing her to move or fight the thrall rather than the beholder)
2. Trigger beholder-directed flanking geometry (via Compelled Movement)
3. Die efficiently (22 HP, AC 14) to make the party feel wasteful for spending actions on it

This design intent comes through clearly in this scenario. A player watching the thrall hit for 3 damage will ignore it. That's the trap: Compelled Movement next round puts it back in the way.

**Compelled Movement timing clarity:** The stat block says Compelled Movement is the thrall's **bonus action** ("if the beholder can see it"). In this simulation, it fires on the thrall's turn. This is distinct from the beholder's own Compel Thrall (beholder bonus action, 1/turn), which can force movement on the beholder's turn. The two mechanics stack: thrall can move (bonus action) on its own turn AND the beholder can direct it (beholder bonus action) on the beholder's turn. This creates a double-movement opportunity the cheat sheet doesn't call out.

### FINDING 1 — LoS conditional on Compelled Movement is unverifiable in the runner

Thrall's Compelled Movement reads: "The derro moves up to 30 feet in a direction chosen by the beholder (if the beholder can see it)."

The beholder has **truesight 120 ft** and the **Alien Perception** trait ("it cannot be blinded; when it loses sight of a creature, it can still sense them if they are within 60 feet of the shrine"). In practice, the LoS condition is never meaningfully restrictive — the beholder perceives everything near the shrine — but the stat block language creates a conditional the runner cannot verify automatically.

A DM reading the thrall's bonus action text in isolation (without cross-referencing the beholder's Alien Perception) might incorrectly rule that the thrall cannot use Compelled Movement when the beholder is behind cover.

**FEEL-R190-01 (NEW):** Thrall Compelled Movement has a LoS conditional ("if the beholder can see it") that is trivially satisfied given the beholder's truesight + Alien Perception, but not explicitly resolved in the thrall stat block text. A DM reading only the thrall's stat block could apply the conditional incorrectly. Recommend adding a cross-reference: "The beholder's truesight (120 ft) and Alien Perception trait make this condition almost always satisfied within the Thrulm chamber. Within 60 ft of the shrine, the beholder senses any creature even through full cover."

### FINDING 3 — Thrall double-movement opportunity undocumented

The beholder can move the thrall on two separate turns: the thrall's own Compelled Movement (thrall bonus action, init ~6) and the beholder's Compel Thrall (beholder bonus action, init 19). In the typical initiative order where the beholder acts early and the thrall acts late, the sequence within a round is:

1. Init 19 (beholder): beholder uses Compel Thrall (BA) → thrall moves up to 30 ft now
2. Init 6 (thrall): thrall attacks, then uses Compelled Movement (BA) → thrall moves another 30 ft

This means a thrall can relocate up to 60 ft per round (30 from beholder BA + 30 from thrall BA) in addition to its normal 30 ft walk, for a potential total of 90 ft of positioning per round. Neither the stat block, cheat sheet, nor tactics text documents this triple-movement potential.

Against a party spread across the chamber, a thrall can cross from one flank to another in a single round. This is not obviously broken, but it is unintuitive and may surprise players who expect minions to behave predictably.

**FEEL-R190-03 (NEW):** Thrall can move up to 90 ft per round: base 30 ft walk + Compel Thrall (beholder BA, 30 ft) + Compelled Movement (thrall BA, 30 ft). None of the documentation mentions this or frames it as a feature. Recommend adding to cheat sheet: "Thrall (Beholder BA, Compel Thrall): beholder can move a thrall 30 ft on the beholder's turn; thrall also has Compelled Movement (30 ft BA on thrall's own turn). Combined with walk: thrall can cross 90 ft per round."
