### Disintegration Ray Mechanics Breakdown

**To-hit:** +6 to hit vs AC 15 (Marwen). With Void-Feeding (+1 near shrine): +7.

| Bonus | Hit threshold | Hit probability |
|-------|--------------|----------------|
| +6 (base) | 9+ on d20 | 60% |
| +7 (Void-Feeding) | 8+ on d20 | 65% |

**Damage:** 10d8 force. Min: 10, Max: 80, Avg: 45.

**Disintegration rider trigger:** fires when damage reduces target to 0 HP. At Marwen's max HP (32):
- P(10d8 ≥ 32) ≈ 99.99% — any hit at full HP disintegrates Marwen with overwhelming probability.
- Even at 44 HP (Sabriel's pool): P(10d8 ≥ 44) = P(avg 45 > 44) ≈ 54%. At low initiative cost this is still meaningful.
- At 49 HP (Bazgar): P(10d8 ≥ 49) ≈ 27% — DR is no longer a reliable one-shot vs the fighter.

**Implication:** DR is a "designated Marwen kill" ability. Any hit disintegrates her. Retargeting to Sabriel (HP 44) is ~54% one-shot; to Bazgar (HP 49) is only ~27%. The AC-priority note (prefer Marwen for hit rate) and the HP-priority analysis (Marwen is also the most likely to be disintegrated) converge — Marwen is always the correct DR target.

### New Findings

#### FEEL-F24-01 (new): Counterspell vs Disintegration Ray — interaction unspecified, material to party survival

**Severity:** Medium  
**What this slice reveals:**

Disintegration Ray is written as a "Ranged Spell Attack," which in 5e means the creature is casting a spell (using the Spell Attack action). PHB 203: Counterspell targets "a creature that you can see within 60 feet of you" that "is casting a spell." If DR is a spell (not merely a "monster action"), Marwen (Wizard 5, Counterspell known) can use Counterspell as a reaction when she sees the beholder begin casting.

- Counterspell (L3) against a spell of L3 or lower: automatic success (no check).
- Against a spell above L3: DC = 10 + spell level. DR has no listed spell level (it's a monster ability). DM adjudication needed: is DR treated as a spell for Counterspell purposes?

**If DR is Counterspellable:**
- Marwen spends her L3 slot reaction. Check (if required): INT +3 vs DC 10 + spell level.
  - If treated as L4: DC 14. Marwen rolls 11+3=14 → success (50th percentile roll). Saves Marwen.
  - If treated as L5: DC 15. Marwen needs 12+3=15 → success at average roll.
  - If auto-success (no level): Marwen saves without check.
- Consequence: Marwen burns her best damage slot (L3 Fireball → L3 Counterspell) for personal survival, reducing party offense significantly.

**If DR is NOT Counterspellable (DM ruling: monster ability, not a spell):**
- Marwen has NO defensive option against DR. A 65–70% first-round kill is entirely passive from her perspective.

This interaction has materiially different outcomes across the two rulings. Neither ruling is wrong by RAW — monster "Ranged Spell Attack" abilities occupy a rules gray zone. The beholder notes should specify which way Joe rules this encounter.

**Log only.** Suggested DM note addition to beholder-thrulm.md:

> *Counterspell vs DR (FEEL-F24-01):* Disintegration Ray is a "Ranged Spell Attack." Joe's ruling: [Counterspellable at DC X / Not counterspellable — monster ability, not a player-facing spell]. If Counterspellable, a Wizard with Counterspell prepared can expend a reaction + L3 slot to stop it. Inform players of this option before the beholder fires R1 — they may not know DR is potentially counterable.

#### FEEL-F24-02 (new): DR "charged from turn 0" asymmetry makes R1 the dominant DR moment; R2+ DR is a lottery

**Severity:** Low (design awareness)  
**Finding:**

Per 5e RAW, recharge abilities begin combat recharged. DR (Recharge 5–6) is available R1 with certainty. After R1 use, it recharges at P = 2/6 = 33.3% per start-of-turn. Expected turns between subsequent uses: ~3 turns (geometric distribution mean = 1/p = 3).

In a 3–4 round encounter (typical against this party at this health total), expected DR uses:
- R1: certain (1 use).
- R2–R4: expected 1 additional use (P ≈ 1 − (1−0.333)³ ≈ 70%).

This means DR dominates R1 (high-certainty kill of Marwen) and then becomes sporadic. The encounter has a "two-phase DR experience":
1. R1: guaranteed DR. If Marwen is targeted, she is almost certainly disintegrated (99.99% at full HP 32).
2. R2+: DR is present ~70% of encounters but fires unpredictably. DMs who save DR for a "dramatic moment" may never see it recharge.

**Design implication:** If Joe wants DR to feel like a present but conditional threat (rather than a R1 coin-flip execution), he might consider a one-time use (like a breath weapon) rather than Recharge 5–6, or set it to Recharge 6 only (16.7%/turn = expected 6 turns between uses), making R1 the singular guaranteed use and subsequent fires rare enough to feel special.

**This is distinct from FEEL-F9-01** (which flagged the *player-agency* problem) — this filing identifies the *mechanical asymmetry* between R1 (100% availability) and R2+ (probabilistic).
