### SCENARIO-BUG-01 (NEW) — Timing error in scenario definition

The scenario types "drain" *before* "multiattack" on the beholder's own tab at the top of combat, before any PC has taken a turn. **Drain Divinity is a legendary action costing 3 LA**, which can only be used at the **end of another creature's turn** — not during the beholder's own action. The scenario as written asks the runner to dispatch a legendary action at an illegal timing.

Expected runner behavior: uncertain. The `drain_divinity` DB row is typed `utility`, with no LA trigger guard. The runner may dispatch it anyway (treating it as an ordinary action), which would:
- Incorrectly remove a PC's spell slot out of sequence
- Deprive the beholder of its main action (only if runner treats it as an action)
- Or silently no-op if the runner detects no valid LA trigger

**Impact:** This scenario cannot be run as written without violating the legendary action timing rules. **Fix required:** Move "drain" to a turn-end event fired at the end of Sabriel's initiative — model as: beholder multiattack → click_round → (Sabriel's turn) → drain. Since the scenario has no Sabriel-turn step, the drain step should be reordered to after a `click_round` and after Sabriel acts.

**Not auto-fixable** (scenario is in scenarios.yml — design file, not a stat block arithmetic error). Logging; Joe must revise.

### FEEL-13 (NEW) — Marwen R1 kill probability from multiattack triple-focus

The beholder targeting Marwen with all 3 multiattack hits (both tentacles + maw) yields:

| Scenario | Condition | Damage | P(kill) |
|----------|-----------|--------|---------|
| TL1 hits + Maw hits (TL2 doesn't matter) | Both connect | 14.5 + 22 = 36.5 > 32 | P = 0.65 × 0.65 = **42%** |
| TL1 miss + TL2 hits + Maw hits | Two of three | 14.5 + 22 = 36.5 | P = 0.35 × 0.65 × 0.65 = 14.8% |
| TL1 hits + TL2 hits + Maw hits (overkill) | All three | 51 avg | P = 0.65³ = 27.4% |

Combined P(Marwen at 0 HP before her turn, Round 1): approximately **~57% across scenarios where at least one TL + Maw connect** (36.5 avg > 32 HP threshold). The floor is 42% (TL1 + Maw only).

**This exceeds the "unfair rather than lethal" threshold.** The encounter is designed to be lethal, but a ~50% chance Marwen never takes a turn in Round 1 removes player agency at the most critical dramatic moment (first contact with the beholder). The grapple + maw combo has no counterplay once the beholder wins initiative.

**Distinction from prior feel logs:**
- FEEL-06 (general): "No PC decision changes encounter outcome after initiative" — applies at campaign scale.
- FEEL-13 (new, specific): Quantified for Marwen's R1 survivability. ~50% die before her turn from multiattack alone, independent of Disintegration Ray.

**Recommend:** Table note when running beholder — if the beholder wins initiative and triple-focuses Marwen, consider having it split attacks (TL1 on Marwen, TL2 on Sabriel or Bazgar, Maw on grappled target next round). This doesn't change the encounter's lethality but gives Marwen at least one turn before dying. Not a rule change — a DM judgment note.

### LA economy assessment (confirmed)

Three LA strategies compared this round:
1. **Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA):** Expected ~26 total damage (19.25 to Bazgar + 6.5 to Sabriel). Tactically strong — drains Bazgar (biggest DPS threat) and grapples Sabriel.
2. **Drain Divinity (3 LA) vs Sabriel:** 0 HP damage. P(Sabriel fails DC 16 CHA, ~50–55%) → lose highest slot (3rd-level), beholder gains 6 THP. Disrupts Sabriel's slots but no immediate HP threat.
3. **Tentacle + Tentacle + ... (3× 1-LA Tentacles):** Would cost 3 LA for 3 attacks. Illegal — only 1 legendary action per turn end, so 3 Tentacles = one per each of 3 PC turns. Max spread 3 × 6.5 avg = 19.5 total; less efficient than Void Ray + Tentacle.

**Verdict:** Strategy #1 (Void Ray + Tentacle) is dominant for raw damage this round. Strategy #2 (Drain Divinity) is dominant for long-term resource denial. The one-round Drain Divinity trade-off (24 lost expected damage vs ~6 THP + 3rd-level slot denial) favors resource denial IF Sabriel has multiple 3rd-level slots remaining. At level 5, Sabriel has exactly 2 × 3rd-level slots. Drain Divinity removes one per success.

**Carry-forward confirmation:** Drain Divinity all-or-nothing (3 LA) remains dominant vs Sabriel specifically. Against a party with no divine casters, it's useless. This party's composition makes it the beholder's best long-term tool, but the 3-LA cost and DC 16 CHA save (vs Sabriel's likely +3–4 CHA bonus) means roughly 50% success rate per use.
