**FEEL-F72-01 (NEW — Log only): Multiattack verb list contains dead entries that conflict with single_attack verbs.**

The `multiattack` DB action for beholder-thrulm lists verbs: `["attack", "hit", "swing", "tentacle", "tentacles", "maw", "bite", "melee", "multiattack"]`. The verbs `"tentacle"`, `"tentacles"`, `"maw"`, and `"bite"` are also mapped to standalone single_attack actions (`tentacle_lash` and `maw`). In the combat runner's verb resolution, NPC-specific single_attack actions will preempt the multiattack for these overlapping verbs (more specific match wins, and DB sort puts `maw` before `multiattack` alphabetically for beholder-thrulm). Practical consequence: DM typing "tentacle" or "maw" during a beholder turn gets a single_attack result, not the full multiattack sequence. The overlapping verbs in `multiattack.verbs` are non-functional dead entries.

**DM guidance:** always use `"attack"`, `"multiattack"`, `"hit"`, or `"melee"` for the full 2-TL-plus-Maw sequence. `"tentacle"` and `"maw"` are single-attack triggers. This creates a latent DM-error path: narrating "the beholder tentacles Bazgar" and typing "tentacle" delivers only one TL, not the full multiattack. Recommend removing `"tentacle"`, `"tentacles"`, `"maw"`, `"bite"` from the multiattack verb list (requires `combat_action_upsert` via MCP; cannot auto-fix).

**FEEL-F72-02 (NEW — Log only): Prone advantage vs TL reach 10 ft — RAW conflict with existing encounter documentation.**

The `thrulm-lair-prone-melee-synergy` scenario documentation asserts TL (reach 10 ft) fires at **advantage** vs prone Bazgar. RAW PHB p.292: melee attacks against a prone target have **advantage** only if the attacker is **within 5 ft**; from beyond 5 ft the attack is at **disadvantage**. The beholder's standard tentacle-reach hover position is 10 ft from the target — which by strict RAW means TL vs prone = DISADVANTAGE (+7 with disadvantage = ~25% hit rate vs AC 18), not advantage (+7 with advantage = ~75%).

The existing documentation calculates hit rates at advantage, inflating expected R1 damage from the UG+TL combo. The correct beholder tactic to gain advantage on TL vs a prone target is to close to within 5 ft (using fly movement or Shrine-Drift BA before the multiattack), accepting that this closes Maw range naturally. If the beholder stays at 10 ft, TL is at disadvantage vs prone — and UG becomes a self-sabotage (beholder's most-used attack is penalized). This requires a design/ruling decision from Joe:

- Option A: Rule that TL advantage vs prone holds at 10 ft (non-RAW, but simpler table play — document it explicitly).
- Option B: Enforce RAW: TL vs prone at 10 ft = disadvantage. The beholder must close to 5 ft before attacking a prone target (costs movement; creates OA risk from Bazgar standing back up). The lair-prone-melee-synergy scenario documentation would need correction.
