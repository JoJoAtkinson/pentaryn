In a 3-round encounter with 2 thralls, correct Hand Axe stats would deal ~5 expected HP to Marwen; buggy Dagger stats deal ~12 expected HP. The thrall is intended as a filler minion; the Dagger makes it a mild sustained threat to the back line.

### LA economy observation (fire #49)

DD failed in R1 (Sabriel passed CHA save). The beholder spent all 3 LA and achieved nothing. This highlights the all-or-nothing nature of the R1-LA-hold protocol:
- **Best case:** DD fires → Sabriel loses a 3rd-level slot → smite attrition begins.
- **Worst case (this run):** All 3 LA consumed, beholder made no reactive use of them on Bazgar's or Marwen's turns, and the save succeeded. Net: 0 LA value delivered in R1.

This is a confirmed data point for DD-DD (design call — 48 fires open). In this simulation, the beholder would have been better served spending 2 LA on Void Ray after Marwen's turn (Marwen DEX +2, 65% fail rate on DC 16 → expected 14 damage to Marwen) and 1 LA on Tentacle after Bazgar's... except Bazgar never had a turn (already downed). So in this particular init order (beholder high, Bazgar low), the DD hold cost the beholder 2× Void Ray damage (~28 expected HP to Marwen) in exchange for a DD attempt that failed.

The DD hold protocol is correct when Sabriel's CHA save rate is low. Against Sabriel CHA +3 vs DC 16: 40% fail rate. In ~40% of runs, DD is the optimal R1 LA spend. In ~60% of runs (save succeeds), the hold costs ~28 expected HP to Marwen or reactive grapple pressure. Over many runs, the expected LA value is roughly neutral vs an optimal reactive strategy — but DD adds slot attrition in the 40% cases, which compounds favorably over multi-round fights.

**FEEL-F49-01 (NEW):** BUG-A31-08 thrall damage inflation confirmed with DPR quantification. 2–3× expected DPR vs designed stats across all three party AC values. The blob reads as a "filler minion" in narrative, but the Dagger stats make it a mild sustained Marwen threat (~2 HP/attack expected). This mismatch erodes the "thralls are just minions" feel the .md establishes. Fix priority: low-medium (the beholder is the encounter's lethal pressure; the thralls will not swing an outcome at this party level regardless of which weapon), but the flavor/accuracy case is clear.

**FEEL-F49-02 (NEW):** The R1 DD failure with a 40% expected fail rate in this simulation confirms that all-or-nothing LA commitment produces high run-to-run variance in the beholder's R1 effectiveness. When DD fails (60% of cases), the beholder's R1 LA return is zero. A 2-LA DD option (hold 1 LA for reactive use) would reduce variance while maintaining slot attrition — the single LA held could fire Tentacle on Marwen's turn or Bazgar's turn even on a DD-fail round. Joe's call (DD-DD item), but this run provides a concrete case where the current design hurt the beholder.
