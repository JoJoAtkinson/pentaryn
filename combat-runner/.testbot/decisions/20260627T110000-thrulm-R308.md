### Option A — Disintegration Ray vs Marwen

- Attack: +9 vs AC 15 → needs 6+ on d20 → **75% hit rate**
- Damage: 10d8+1 (Void-Feeding), avg 46 force. Minimum of 10d8 = 10; avg 45+1=46.
- Marwen at 32 HP. P(kill on hit): 10d8 avg 45, σ≈7.25. P(10d8 ≥ 31) ≈ P(z > (31-45)/7.25) = P(z > -1.93) ≈ 97.3%.
- **P(Marwen permanently dead in round 1, before she acts) ≈ 0.75 × 0.973 ≈ 73%.**
- On kill: DISINTEGRATED rider fires. Marwen cannot be raised except by True Resurrection or Wish.
- Expected damage to Marwen: 0.75 × 46 = 34.5.

**Remaining turn after DR (LA budget = 3):**
- After Marwen's turn (dead/KO, no action): 1 LA → Tentacle on Sabriel (AC 19): +9 vs 19 = 55% hit, avg 14.5 bludg + grapple. Expected: 8.0.
- After Sabriel's turn: 2 LA → Void Ray on Bazgar (DC 16 DEX; Bazgar DEX +0, needs 16+ = 25% pass). Expected: 0.75×23 + 0.25×11.5 = 20.1 force.
- After Bazgar's turn: 0 LA.

**Option A Round 1 expected damage:** 34.5 + 8.0 + 20.1 = **62.6 total** across party.

### Key Findings

**FEEL-R308-01 (new): Multiattack strictly dominates Disintegration Ray on round 1**

Multiattack and DR have nearly identical P(Marwen KO) (~72%). But multiattack:
1. Deals **3.8 more expected damage** to the party in round 1 (66.4 vs 62.6).
2. **Preserves DR** (recharge 5-6) for a higher-value moment — e.g., targeting Marwen at low HP in R2 when kill is near-certain, or targeting Bazgar (49 HP, harder to one-shot with DR but possible).
3. **Grapples Marwen** (if she survives): beholder can Maw her on R2 with disadvantage on escape, or use LA Tentacle to re-grapple after she attempts escape.
4. **Avoids permanent death on R1** — same KO rate but with death saves means Sabriel can stabilize Marwen, extending the encounter and making the loss feel earned rather than punitive.

Using DR on R1 provides no tactical advantage and wastes the ability on a target who is nearly as likely to be KO'd by multiattack alone.

**Recommendation:** Add to beholder Tactics section — "Do NOT open with Disintegration Ray unless Marwen has already used her action (i.e., she's already had a turn). Round 1 multiattack is always preferred: same KO probability, higher party damage, preserves DR for a high-value kill."

**FEEL-R308-02 (new): Round 1 damage output is extreme — 53% of party HP in expected value**

Party total HP: 49+32+44 = 125.
Round 1 expected damage (Option B): 66.4 = 53% of party HP.
For a party "meant to lose," this rate means expected KO/death for Marwen in R1, 8.0 expected damage to Sabriel from LA Tentacle, and 20.1 expected force damage to Bazgar from Void Ray. The party cannot recover from this mathematically in later rounds against a fresh beholder with 3 LA per round.

This is correct for design intent ("meant to lose") but should be **noted for DM table comfort**: if the group enters this encounter without prior narrative setup, the one-sided nature of round 1 may feel like a TPK with no meaningful player input. Recommend encounter notes include: "The Compass Edge should know they're outmatched before they enter — this is a reveal/retreat scenario, not a stand-and-fight."

**FEEL-R308-03 (new): Marwen grappled scenario creates a moral dilemma**

If multiattack grapples Marwen (high probability), Sabriel faces a real decision on her turn: attack the beholder (which uses her action, not freeing Marwen) or use an action/spell to free Marwen (which doesn't damage the beholder). Bazgar faces the same choice. This is **interesting counterplay** — the grapple mechanic adds a PC decision that DR immediately kills does not.

Log for Joe: "Multiattack grapple on Marwen creates meaningful player decisions: do you damage the beholder or free your teammate? This is the strongest argument for multiattack over DR as the beholder's opener. Consider making this the default behavior in the beholder's Tactics section."

**LA 1** (cost 1, Tentacle) fired after Marwen's turn:
- Marwen likely grappled or KO'd; beholder redirects Tentacle to Sabriel (AC 19, init 11).
- +9 vs AC 19 → 55% hit, avg 14.5 bludg + grapple.

**LA 2+3** (cost 2, Void Ray) fired after Sabriel's turn:
- Target Bazgar (AC 18, DEX +0). DC 16 DEX: Bazgar needs 16+, 25% pass.
- Expected: 0.75×23 + 0.25×11.5 = 20.1 force damage.

**Drain Divinity not used R1** — requires 3 LA (entire budget). Using it means no Tentacle, no Void Ray that round. Expected LA damage from Void Ray+Tentacle ≈ 28.1; Drain Divinity expected spell drain ≈ 1.95 slots + 10.4 THP.

Confirming prior finding: Drain Divinity (3 LA) provides lower immediate party damage than Tentacle+Void Ray (3 LA). Drain Divinity is best when: Sabriel has already cast a high-level slot (beholder punishes the use) or the fight has gone ≥3 rounds and depletion matters.

**Dominant LA strategy:** Tentacle (1) + Void Ray (2) = 28.1 expected damage. Drain Divinity (3) = 10.4 THP + ~2 slot drains. The THP is marginal given beholder's 110 HP; slot drain is meaningful only if Sabriel's slots are tactically relevant. **Void Ray+Tentacle is almost always dominant in rounds 1-3.**
