**Beholder R2 action: Void Scream**

**Altitude question (NEW FINDING — FEEL-F62-01):** The beholder is hovering "40-60 ft" above the chamber floor (per cheat sheet). VS range is 30 ft radius **from self**. At 40 ft altitude with PCs on the ground: 3D distance from beholder to any ground-level PC = at minimum their altitude (40 ft for a PC directly below). All ground targets are ≥ 40 ft from a 40-ft-altitude beholder → **ALL OUT OF VS RANGE**.

The beholder MUST descend before using VS. Descent options:
1. **Fly movement (action not consumed):** Fly 30 ft down during R2 movement. At ~10-ft altitude, all PCs within 30 ft horizontal are in VS range. Descent via fly speed costs movement, not action — VS can fire as the action.
2. **Shrine-Drift (BA):** Descend 30 ft instantly. But per FIX-FC48-B, Shrine-Drift releases grappled creatures if they're now > 10 ft away. Bazgar was grappled; Shrine-Drift descent might break the grapple (beholder moves out of its own Tentacle reach).

**Optimal descent path:** Use **fly movement** (not Shrine-Drift) to descend to ~10 ft altitude. This preserves the grapple (fly movement can drag a grappled creature at half speed — dragging Bazgar 15 ft at half-speed while descending 15-30 ft). At 10-ft altitude: beholder is now 10 ft above the floor, with Bazgar still hanging from tentacles below it. All PCs in the open vault are within 30 ft.

**VS resolves:** DC 16 WIS save. All three PCs within 30 ft (beholder now at low altitude). "Within 10 ft of shrine" for disadvantage: Bazgar (grappled directly below beholder at altar base) IS within 10 ft of the altar stone. Disadvantage applies to Bazgar.

### Feel assessment

**What worked as designed:**
- Grapple-lock + auto-crit Maw R1 is decisive and cinematic. Bazgar at 0 HP R1 is expected (he's absorbing the boss nova).
- LoH redirect catch (DD can't counter LoH per LoH override note) worked correctly.
- Void Eruption correctly applies a second downed-state to Bazgar in R2, accelerating death-save accumulation with no heroic counterplay — correct for a designed-to-lose encounter.

**What felt wrong or unclear:**

**FEEL-F62-01 (NEW — LOG ONLY): VS altitude gap is completely undocumented.**
The beholder's "default hover 40-60 ft" is explicitly in the cheat sheet but VS (30-ft radius from self) cannot reach any ground-level target from that altitude. The beholder must descend before VS fires. This descent:
- Is not mentioned anywhere in the tactics section under VS firing cadence
- Creates an implicit counterplay window: at ≤30 ft altitude, Bazgar (with reach weapon or even normal melee if the beholder descends to 5 ft) and Sabriel can strike the beholder
- Via fly movement vs Shrine-Drift has different grapple-maintenance consequences (fly drags grappled creatures; Shrine-Drift does not)

The tactics note says "R1 = Multiattack; R2 = VS" but does not say "also descend using fly movement first, not Shrine-Drift, to preserve grapple." DMs running this without the stat block annotation will either skip the descent (VS fires but no targets) or instinctively use Shrine-Drift (releasing Bazgar before VS detonates). The descent-via-fly-movement path is the correct play but is entirely implicit.

Recommendation: Add a descent-before-VS note to the VS block in beholder-thrulm.md (alongside the existing R1/R2 sequencing note FIX-EV7-32-A). Flag for Joe — is this a tactics-note auto-add or does the hover altitude itself need revision?

**FEEL-F62-02 (NEW — LOG ONLY): Shrine-proximity disadvantage is unplayable without prep.**
The "within 10 ft of shrine" VS disadvantage rider is a meaningful effect (83% fail rate for a +1 WIS fighter) but requires knowing the exact position of the altar stone and exactly who is within 10 ft of it. In the chaos of R2 combat (beholder descending, prone grappled Bazgar, PCs spreading), the DM will not track this reliably. The effect is either forgotten (never applies) or applied ad hoc (applied inconsistently). As designed, it adds correct tactical depth; as operated at the table, it's invisible.

Consider: simplify to "within 10 ft of the beholder" (tying the disadvantage to the creature already tracking position via grapple range) or make the shrine-proximity area a flat zone visible on the encounter map. Not a bug, but a friction point.

**FEEL-F62-03 (CONFIRMED — existing open issue escalated): VS range and DD range both require descent.**
This fire confirms the same altitude-descent problem previously logged for DD (BUG-A12-06, 44 fires open) also applies to VS. The only abilities that work at 40-60 ft altitude are attack-roll abilities (Tentacle Lash reach 10 ft, Maw reach 5 ft) and Disintegration Ray (120 ft range) — which means at full altitude, the beholder's entire tool set is: tentacles + maw (requires flying down to 10-15 ft above PC, effectively ground-level) + Disintegration Ray (works at any altitude). VS, DD, Void Ray, and all save-based abilities require descent to ≤30 ft altitude. The "hover high and disintegrate" vs "descend and use full kit" trade-off is a genuine tactical axis but is completely unresolved in the current stat block.

**Recommendation for Joe:** Add a combat header note to beholder-thrulm.md: "ALTITUDE: At 40+ ft hover altitude, only Disintegration Ray (120 ft) reaches ground targets. VS, DD, and Void Ray require descent to ≤30 ft altitude via fly movement. Shrine-Drift descent releases grappled creatures (FIX-FC48-B). Default hover ≤20 ft when VS/DD/VR are available and useful." This is a DM reference note, not a rules change.
