### Simulation — 3 rounds

**Setup:**
- Shrine-Touched (ST): HP 45, AC 16; starting position near altar, in melee range of Bazgar.
- Bazgar: HP 49, AC 18, Fighter-BM 5 (multiattack 2×, Extra Attack). In melee.
- Sabriel: HP 44, AC 19, divine martial 5. In melee range (she closed in).
- Marwen: HP 32, AC 15, Wizard 5. Staying back 30 ft — outside 15-ft cone range.

**Round 1 — Bazgar attacks ST (party's turn, ST takes 12 damage):**

Bazgar multiattack vs ST (AC 16): +5 to hit. Hit on 11+. P(hit) ≈ 50%. Two attacks.
Assumed hit for 12 total (per scenario turn: "-12"). ST HP: 45 → 33.

**Unstable Form triggers:** 12 ≥ 10 → ST gains advantage on attack rolls until end of its next turn.

**Oath-Breaking Retaliation (reaction, on damage from visible source):**
- ST makes one Shrine-Axe attack vs Bazgar (AC 18): +4 to hit.
- Under Ruling A (Unstable Form advantage applies to same-turn reactions — FEEL-07):
  - With advantage: P(hit 14+) = 1 − (13/20)² ≈ 57.8%.
  - Hit: 5 (1d8+1) slashing + 4 (1d8) necrotic = avg 9 damage to Bazgar.
  - Bazgar expected damage from reaction: 9 × 0.578 ≈ 5.2.
- **ST takes 2 (1d4) psychic self-damage after attack.** ST HP: 33 → ~30.5 avg.

Under Ruling B (advantage does NOT apply — triggers the same round advantage is "gained"):
- No advantage. P(hit 14+) = 7/20 = 35%.
- Expected: 9 × 0.35 ≈ 3.2 damage. Self-damage same.

**Ruling A gives a meaningful reaction bite; Ruling B makes it marginal.** FEEL-07 recommend Ruling A and this simulation supports it: Ruling B gives ~3 damage average for a reaction that also costs the shrine-touched 2.5 HP — a net loss every time it fires with no advantage.

### Feel Assessment

**FEEL-12 (NEW) — Double-cone threat from multiple Shrine-Touched**

With 2 Shrine-Touched active (beholder can create these — "Variant: Multiple Shrine-Touched"):
- Two simultaneous Ancient Resonance cones (recharge 5–6, each independently):
  - P(both recharge same round) = 0.33² ≈ 11% per round.
  - If both fire at the same target group: Bazgar and Sabriel each face two DC 14 DEX saves.
  - Bazgar (DEX +0): P(fail both) = 0.65² ≈ 42%. On two fails: 2 × 13 = 26 avg damage from cones alone.
  - Against Bazgar's 49 HP: 26 damage in one round = 53% of HP. Survivable but only just.
  - Against Marwen (if she's been pulled into range): 26 / 32 HP = instant down risk.

This is a **feel edge case worth watching** — not a balance issue per se (beholder encounter is meant to be lethal), but against a 3-PC party with one soft caster (Marwen, 32 HP), two simultaneous Resonance cones threaten a one-shot before Marwen can act. At 11% per round, this happens once every ~9 rounds, but with 2 shrine-touched in the room, early rounds carry meaningful permadeath risk for Marwen.

**Recommend:** When running "Hard" or "Deadly" scaling (per stat block), stagger shrine-touched positions so cones can't fully overlap. Call this out at the table if 2+ shrine-touched are present.

**FEEL-07 — Unstable Form + Oath-Breaking Retaliation same-turn advantage (ruling needed)**

Simulation confirms the impact:
- Ruling A (advantage applies): ST reaction deals ~5.2 avg to a melee PC. Feels dangerous and reactive.
- Ruling B (no advantage): ST reaction deals ~3.2 avg. Doesn't outpace the 2.5 self-damage cost. Mechanically pointless.

**Recommend confirming Ruling A.** Without it, Oath-Breaking Retaliation is a net HP loss for the shrine-touched every time it fires without corresponding threat to the party. Ruling A is also more thematically consistent (shrine-power floods through the wound).

**DB-BUG-01 — ancient_resonance missing 1d4 psychic rider (3rd confirmation)**

This run is the third consecutive time the gap has been confirmed in simulation. At the table, the runner will roll 2d10 necrotic (correct) but not prompt for the 1d4 psychic rider (missing). The practical miss: ~1.3 avg damage per target per Resonance. With 2 shrine-touched in a Hard encounter, this could be 2+ missing psychic hits per encounter. Not game-breaking but it's a systematic undercount that should be fixed via MCP upsert in an interactive session.
