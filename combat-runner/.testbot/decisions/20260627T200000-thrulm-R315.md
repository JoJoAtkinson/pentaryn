### ROUND 1

**Init 20 — Lair Action:**
Beholder chooses Void Eruption (DC 16 DEX, 11 avg force damage, all within 20 ft of shrine).
Assuming Bazgar and Sabriel have advanced to melee range near the altar, both are within 20 ft.
- Bazgar (DEX save +1): needs 15+ on d20 → 25% success, 75% fail → E[dmg] = 0.75 × 11 = **8.25**
- Sabriel (DEX save +2): needs 14+ on d20 → 30% success → E[dmg] = 0.70 × 11 = **7.7**
- Marwen (at distance, not in range): unaffected.

State after lair: Bazgar ~41 HP, Sabriel ~36 HP (expected).

**Bazgar's turn (init 17):**
Bazgar has 2 attacks (Extra Attack). Targets beholder (closes to melee if beholder is hovering low enough, or uses a ranged option if not).

Beholder must be within melee reach for Multiattack (tentacles reach 10 ft, maw reach 5 ft) → it starts near the altar, approximately 10-15 ft off the ground to target the party. Bazgar can melee it.

- Attack 1: +7 vs AC 17. Hits on 10+ → 55%. If nonmagic longsword: beholder resistance halves damage. E[dmg] = 0.55 × (7.5/2) = **2.1**
- Attack 2: Same. E[dmg] = **2.1**
- Battlemaster maneuver: **Pushing Attack** (push beholder 15 ft on hit). If successful, beholder moves 15 ft away from Marwen (removing tentacle reach). This is the most tactical option vs a flying creature with reach attacks — it disrupts round-2 grapple attempts. Expected push: P(hit) × P(STR save fail) = 0.55 × P(beholder fails DC 14+1d8 STR save). Beholder STR -1, DC ~15+: P(fail) ≈ 80% → P(push) ≈ 44%.

Bazgar total HP dealt to beholder: ~**4 HP** (after resistance). Very low unless magical weapon.

*Beholder reaction after Bazgar's attack attempt: **Antireality.*** Bazgar announces his attack → beholder uses reaction to impose disadvantage. Bazgar rolls twice, takes lower. Expected hit rate drops from 55% to 30%. E[dmg per attack with resistance and disadvantage] ≈ 0.30 × 3.75 = 1.1. Two attacks: ~**2 HP**. Antireality nearly erases Bazgar's round.

**Beholder's turn (init 13):**

LA budget 3 after Bazgar's turn — already used Antireality. Options:
- Spend 2 LA on Void Ray vs Bazgar: DC 16 DEX, E[dmg] = 0.75 × 22 = **16.5**
- Spend 1 LA on Tentacle vs Bazgar: +9 vs AC 18 → hits on 9+ (60%), E[dmg] = 0.60 × 14 = **8.4**, plus grapple DC 16.

Optimal: Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA) vs Bazgar. Spends all 3 LAs.
- Void Ray E[dmg]: **16.5** → Bazgar ~24 HP remaining (from 41).
- Tentacle E[dmg]: **8.4** → Bazgar ~16 HP. Grapple check: 60% chance Bazgar grappled.

Beholder main action: **Disintegration Ray** (+9 vs AC 15 Marwen). Roll: 14 → hits (need 6+, 75% base + Void-Feeding already at +9).
- Damage: 45 (10d8) force. Marwen has 32 HP → **reduced to 0 HP. Marwen is disintegrated.**
- Marwen cannot be restored to life except by true resurrection or wish. At level 5, neither is accessible.

> **CRITICAL STATE: Marwen is permanently dead before her first turn (init 12 < beholder's 13).**
> The beholder used its main action on Disintegration Ray before Marwen acted.

**Marwen's turn (init 12):** DEAD. No action. Death saving throws do not apply — disintegration reduces to ash instantly; no body for stabilization.

**Thrall-1 (init 11):** Beholder uses Compel Thrall (bonus action) to direct it at Sabriel. Thrall moves 30 ft + bonus compelled movement toward Sabriel, attacks: +1 vs AC 19 → hits on 18+ (15%). E[dmg] = 0.15 × 2 = **0.3**. Negligible.

**Rager (init 10):** Charges Sabriel (nearest PC after Marwen's death). Multiattack 2× Greataxe.
- Taunt (BA) vs Sabriel: DC 12 CHA save. Sabriel as divine martial has CHA likely +3: needs 9+ → 60% success. 40% chance Sabriel has disadvantage vs non-Rager targets.
- Attack 1: +4 vs AC 19 → hits on 15+ (30%). E[dmg] = 0.30 × 8 = **2.4**
- Attack 2: same. **2.4**
- Rager round vs Sabriel: ~5 HP expected.

**Sabriel's turn (init 8):**
- State: ~36 HP (from Void Eruption), Rager Taunt possibly active (40% chance).
- If Taunt active: Sabriel has disadvantage on attacks vs beholder. She must hit rager first to negate it, or accept disadvantage.
- Sabriel likely spends a slot on Divine Smite and targets the beholder:
  - +6 vs AC 17 → 50% base. With disadvantage (if Taunt): 25%.
  - E[dmg] without smite: 0.50 × 8 = 4 (1d8+3). With 2nd-level smite (+3d8 = avg +13.5): 0.50 × 21.5 = **10.75**
  - But Antireality resets at start of beholder's turn — now available again. Beholder uses Antireality on Sabriel's attack → drops to 25% hit rate.
  - With Antireality + Taunt (disadvantage stacks to even lower for two sources — same as just disadvantage once): P(hit) ≈ 25%. E[dmg with smite] = 0.25 × 21.5 = **5.4**

**Thrall-2 (init 4):** Negligible. +3 dagger vs Sabriel (AC 19): hits on 16+ (25%). E[dmg] = 0.25 × 3 = **0.75**.

### ROUND 2

*Init 20 — Lair Action:*
Pick Unstable Ground vs Bazgar (trying to go prone, making beholder's attacks have advantage vs him).
- DC 16 DEX vs Bazgar (+1): 75% prone. Bazgar already grappled → prone while grappled = speed 0, attacks vs prone Bazgar have advantage.

**Beholder (init 13):**
- Antireality resets.
- Recharge check: Disintegration Ray (recharged on 5-6): 33% → probably not recharged.
- Void Scream recharge (on 6): 17% → probably not.
- Main action: **Multiattack** (2× Tentacle + Maw) vs Bazgar (grappled, prone if lair action succeeded).
  - Bazgar prone and grappled: attacks vs Bazgar have **advantage**.
  - Tentacle 1 (+9, advantage vs AC 18): P(hit with advantage) = 1-(0.55)² = 0.70. E[dmg] = 0.70 × 14 = **9.8**. Second grapple (Bazgar can be grappled by multiple tentacles, but rules say speed 0 regardless of number of grapples).
  - Tentacle 2 (+9, advantage vs AC 18): P(hit) = 0.70. E[dmg] = **9.8**.
  - Maw (+9, advantage vs grappled Bazgar — plus "if grappled, disadvantage on escape" already noted): P(hit) = 0.70. E[dmg] = 0.70 × 22 = **15.4**
  - Bazgar expected round-2 damage: 35 HP → from 16 HP, Bazgar is reduced to **-19 HP → 0 HP**.

**Bazgar dies round 2.** If the beholder rolled the Disintegration Ray on round 2 instead of multiattack (33% recharge), Bazgar could also be disintegrated. But multiattack with advantage vs grappled target is near-certain kill (P(all 3 miss) ≈ 0.30³ = 2.7%).

At this point (round 2, after beholder's main action), **2 of 3 PCs are dead or at 0 HP.** Sabriel remains with ~26 HP after round-1 rager attacks and round-2 lair action (Unstable Ground may have targeted her instead).

LA budget round 2: **Drain Divinity (3 LA)** is now optimal — Sabriel is the only active PC with spell slots/divine abilities.
- DC 16 CHA vs Sabriel (CHA +3 for a divine martial): needs 13+ → 40% success → 60% fail.
- On fail: Sabriel loses highest remaining slot (probably 3rd level) → beholder gains 6 THP.

**Round 2 ends.** Sabriel is alone with:
- 1 rager at full HP
- 2 thralls
- Beholder at ~102 HP (barely scratched)
- No healing options (Marwen was the wizard; Sabriel is the divine caster with depleted slots)

### Outcome

**Round 4 maximum survival:** Sabriel realistically lasts 1-2 more rounds before dying to beholder multiattack or Void Ray legendary. The party achieves a statistical zero — Sabriel cannot deal meaningful damage (beholder at ~100 HP, Sabriel deals ~5-11 HP per attack vs resistance). No PC reaches round 4 in a healthy state.

This matches design intent: **the beholder is meant to kill the party.** The encounter is NOT meant to be winnable at level 5.

### Feel Analysis

**FEEL-R315-01 — Marwen disintegrated before her first turn: permanently unresurrectable at level 5**

The beholder opens with Disintegration Ray (available round 1, not recharged) at +9 vs AC 15. P(hit) = 75%. Expected damage 45 — nearly 1.5× Marwen's max HP (32). A hit disintegrates Marwen; she cannot be resurrected by any spell available at level 5 (Revivify, Raise Dead both fail on disintegrated targets; True Resurrection and Wish are 9th-level).

In 50% of combats, the beholder's init (13) places it BEFORE Marwen (12 → needs 11+ on d20+2 to beat 13 → 50% chance). In those combats, Marwen is dead before acting.

This is mechanically valid (CR 13 is supposed to kill them) but the **permanent loss of a PC** from a single round-1 hit — with zero counterplay and no resurrection path — creates a different kind of table consequence than ordinary death. "Lethal" means the party loses this fight and likely retreats or dies. "Unfair" is when a party member dies with 0 chance of recovery, before taking a single action.

**Recommendation (log for Joe):** Consider one of:
1. Limit Disintegration Ray recharge to 5–6 (meaning it does NOT start available) — beholder must first do something before it can use its strongest ability.
2. Change "disintegrated = no resurrection" to "disintegrated = requires Raise Dead DC 20 Arcana check instead" — keeps the dramatic flavor without the permanent consequence.
3. Make Disintegration Ray a round-2+ tactic (beholder "studies" target first) — add to tactics text: "Beholder never uses Disintegration Ray on round 1; it spends round 1 assessing."

This is a **design decision for Joe** — not an auto-fix.

**FEEL-R315-02 — Init-20 lair action + beholder main action = 4 hostile actions before the party responds**

Initiative sequence: Lair (20) → Bazgar → Beholder → Marwen(dead) → Thrall-1 → Rager → Sabriel → Thrall-2.

Even with Bazgar going first (favorable init): the party sequence is Bazgar (1 PC act) → then beholder acts + lair already acted + 3 more enemies act before Sabriel. The effective opening sequence is:

- ENEMIES: Lair action (AoE) → Beholder (kill Marwen) → Thrall-1 → Rager → Thrall-2
- PARTY: Bazgar → Sabriel

Party ratio in round 1: 2 actions vs 5 hostile actions. The lair action amplifies this — the party effectively faces a 5-action volley in round 1 with only 2 responses. The asymmetry isn't from mechanics being broken; it's from the lair action adding a "free" hostile turn at init 20.

This is a well-known DM table feel problem with lair actions in round 1. Some DMs rule "lair actions do not fire on round 1" to give the party one round of setup. Not a bug — document it as a DM opt-in note in the tactics section.

**Recommendation (log for Joe):** Add to beholder tactics: "Optional: skip the lair action on round 1 to give the party one full round of play before the siege begins. The encounter is already overwhelmingly lethal at this party level."

**FEEL-R315-03 — Move LA (1 cost) is dead weight when Shrine-Drift (bonus action) exists**

The beholder has both:
- **Move LA (1 LA cost):** move up to fly speed (30 ft).
- **Shrine-Drift (bonus action, 0 cost):** move up to 30 ft, passing through creatures and objects.

Shrine-Drift is STRICTLY SUPERIOR to Move LA in almost every case:
- Same distance (30 ft).
- Shrine-Drift can pass through walls and PCs (useful for escape and repositioning).
- Move LA costs a legendary action; Shrine-Drift is free.

The only edge case where Move LA beats Shrine-Drift: beholder has already used its bonus action (Compel Thrall or Shrine-Drift used that turn), AND it needs to reposition at end of an enemy's turn (LAs fire between other creatures' turns, bonus actions only on beholder's own turn).

In practice: Move LA is the 4th-priority option every round (Drain Divinity → Void Ray → Tentacle → Move). It almost never fires because the 3 LA budget is better spent on damage options, and Shrine-Drift covers repositioning.

**Recommendation (log for Joe):** Either (a) make Move LA cost 0 (matching some official beholder variants), or (b) give Move LA a secondary effect to justify 1 LA cost: "The beholder moves up to its flying speed and may make one Compel Thrall check as part of this movement." This gives Move LA a unique value proposition that Shrine-Drift doesn't replicate.

**FEEL-R315-04 — Battlemaster maneuvers largely dead weight vs beholder**

Bazgar (Battlemaster 5) has 6 superiority dice (d8). Classic maneuvers vs beholder:
- Menacing Attack: target frightened → **immune** (condition immunity).
- Trip Attack: target prone → **immune** (beholder immune to prone; flies).
- Disarming Attack: target drops held object → beholder holds nothing.
- Commander's Strike: ally makes weapon attack → useful only if ally is in melee reach.
- Rally: give Bazgar or ally THP. Works, but not aggressive.
- Precision Attack: +d8 to Bazgar's attack roll. Works, but negated by Antireality (disadvantage cancels the extra die benefit if the lower roll still misses).
- Pushing Attack: push 15 ft on hit. Works mechanically — pushes beholder away. But the beholder has fly speed and Shrine-Drift; it trivially repositions.
- Parry: -d8 damage from melee hit. Works against tentacle/maw if Bazgar uses reaction.

Of 9 maneuvers, 3 are completely useless (frightened/prone/disarming), 3 are partially degraded (Precision/Push/Commander's), and 3 work normally (Rally, Parry, Riposte).

Not a balance bug — this is the nature of a CR 13 aberration vs a level 5 party. But worth noting in the DM guide: "Bazgar's Battlemaster maneuvers are limited vs the beholder; Parry (reaction to reduce a tentacle hit) and Rally (THP on Sabriel) are the highest-value options." This prevents the player from feeling their class features are meaningless.
