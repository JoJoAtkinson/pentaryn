#### STAT-R156-02 — Thrall Derro "Saving Throws: None" contradicts Fractured Will (NEW)

`thrall-derro.md` stat block header: `"Saving Throws | None; dominated mind has no saves"`

Trait text, Fractured Will: `"When the derro is forced to act against its original oath... it makes a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw."`

These are contradictory. "None" likely means "not proficient in any saving throw" (a flavor explanation for the formatting choice rather than a mechanical rule), but a DM reading the stat block top-to-bottom sees "no saves" before encountering Fractured Will and may skip the Wisdom roll entirely.

There is also a secondary question: without a WIS saving throw proficiency, is the Fractured Will save WIS modifier only (–1)? The stat block is ambiguous.

**Disposition:** LOG ONLY. Suggested fix: remove "None; dominated mind has no saves" flavor text and leave the saving throws row blank (standard 5e formatting), OR add a note in the trait: "The thrall uses its unmodified Wisdom modifier (–1) for this save." Joe decides which reading is intended.

### Key findings

#### DB-BUG-R156-01 — `oath_breaking_retaliation` missing embedded damage dice (NEW, PRIMARY FINDING)

The Oath-Breaking Retaliation reaction DB row (`type="reaction"`, trigger: self, event="damage", match="any damage from visible source") contains only a narration preview and trigger spec. **No damage dice are embedded** (confirmed by DB list output: no `damage`, `dice`, or `attack` fields visible).

When the reaction fires, the runner must either:
1. Look up the `shrine-axe` damage independently (requires cross-action reference, which the runner doesn't do), or  
2. Emit the narration and prompt the DM to roll manually, or  
3. Fall to LLM, which may correctly output "1d8+1 slashing + 1d8 necrotic" or may miss the necrotic component.

This is the **direct parallel to DB-BUG-01** (ancient_resonance's 1d4 psychic rider absent from DB). The shrine-axe has a more complex case: its dual damage type (two separate dice, two damage types) must both appear in the reaction's output. If the LLM outputs only 1d8+1 slashing, DMs lose the necrotic component — small per-hit (avg 4) but multiplied across multiple retaliation fires across a session.

**Verified this run:** The retaliation fired on the killing blow in round 1, dealing 10 damage (7 slashing + 3 necrotic). Without the DB spec, the runner would have narrated the attack without certainty of rolling both components.

**Disposition:** LOG ONLY. Needs `combat_action_upsert` in interactive MCP session to add damage spec to the reaction row (spec: single_attack embedding 1d8+1 slashing + 1d8 necrotic rider).

#### FEEL-R156-01 — Oath-Breaking Retaliation on lethal hit: undocumented edge case (NEW)

The Oath-Breaking Retaliation fires when the shrine-touched takes damage from a visible source. When Sabriel's killing blow dealt 29 damage (dropping the shrine-touched from 17 to −12), the reaction triggered **before the creature died** — per 5e rules, a reaction triggered by the damage event resolves immediately, before the damage is applied to kill the target.

Result: the shrine-touched retaliated once with a Shrine-Axe attack before dying, dealing 10 damage (7 slashing + 3 necrotic) to Sabriel, who had advantage from Unstable Form (28 fire damage earlier that turn).

This is mechanically correct but surprising in play. DMs may:
- Not know that reactions fire on lethal damage in 5e
- Not realize Unstable Form advantage carries into the reaction
- Apply only the slashing damage and forget the necrotic component

The cheat sheet's "Shrine-Touched: Oath-Breaking Retaliation" entry (in the When-Derro-Acts section) notes the reaction but does not mention the lethal-hit case or the Unstable Form interaction.

**Disposition:** LOG ONLY (cheat sheet update). Add: "Oath-Breaking Retaliation fires even on the killing blow (reaction resolves before death is applied). Unstable Form advantage applies to the counter-attack if the shrine-touched took ≥10 damage this turn."

#### FEEL-R156-02 — Drain Divinity vs unconscious targets: CHA save ambiguity (NEW)

When Drain Divinity targeted downed Sabriel in round 2, the ruling turned on whether unconscious creatures auto-fail CHA saving throws. PHB 292 specifies auto-fail only for STR and DEX saves. Drain Divinity calls for a CHA save.

**RAW result:** Sabriel rolls normally even while unconscious. She rolled 5 (total) → failed anyway. But if she had rolled high, the ruling matters.

This is a common table dispute. The encounter's design assumes Drain Divinity hits divine casters — if the DM rules that unconscious PCs auto-fail all saves (common house rule), DD can target any downed PC. If strict RAW, unconscious PCs have a real (if impaired) chance.

**Impact at this party level:** Sabriel (CHA likely +2) needs a 13+ on a d20 to save against DC 16 even unmodified. Effectively fails ~70% of the time even conscious. Low mechanical impact, but a table dispute could arise if a DM applies the "auto-fail" ruling after the PC is down and the player objects.

**Disposition:** LOG ONLY. Add a DM note to the beholder tactics section: "Drain Divinity vs unconscious PCs: PHB auto-fail applies only to STR/DEX; CHA saves are rolled normally. The PC is highly likely to fail regardless (DC 16 vs +1 to +3 CHA). Ruling is table preference — standardize before the encounter."

#### FEEL-R156-03 — Legendary action economy: Void Ray before Drain Divinity is correct (CONFIRM)

Round 1: Beholder used Void Ray (2 LA) to drop Bazgar's HP (32→... wait: Void Ray hit Bazgar who had 49 HP → 32 HP; didn't down him). Round 2: Drain Divinity (3 LA) vs downed Sabriel.

**Pattern this run:** In round 1 the beholder couldn't use Drain Divinity because no divine caster was within 30 ft. It used Void Ray instead. In round 2 it used Drain Divinity once Sabriel was down and grappled.

This confirms the FEEL-R155 finding: DD fires only when a divine caster is within 30 ft, which only happens after the beholder descends to grapple range. The **actual priority order** (Void Ray → then Drain Divinity once in grapple range) is logical, but the cheat sheet implies DD is a first-round option. It rarely fires round 1 unless Sabriel or Bazgar close immediately.

**Void Scream:** Recharged round 2 (roll 5), fired immediately, downed Sabriel (30 psychic). This is FEEL-R153-01 confirmed again — when Void Scream recharges early, it's the single most impactful action in the encounter. The party has no counterplay short of a reaction-counterspell.

**Disposition:** LOG (confirm existing FEEL findings). No new action.
