### Retreat trigger

Beholder at 28 HP, start of its R3 turn. Per tactics: "If reduced below 30 HP, retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit."

**Actions available:**
- Standard action: Multiattack (TL×2 + Maw), Shrine-Drift (bonus action), fly movement (30 ft)
- Remaining abilities: DR recharged (was used R1, has rolled 5+ on one recharge check); VS used R2 (recharge 6, ~17% per round)

---

### Retreat mechanic analysis

**Movement ceiling on retreat turn:**
- Fly speed: 30 ft
- Shrine-Drift (BA): 30 ft, can pass through walls and objects
- Total: 60 ft in one turn (bonus + movement combined)
- The lower shaft entrance (per beholder.md description) is approximately 60 ft from the shrine floor where the beholder typically hovers during combat

**Can it reach the shaft in one turn?** If the beholder has drifted close to the altar during DD/multiattack (descending to 30 ft for DD range): yes, 60 ft combined is sufficient to phase into the shaft. If it has been maintaining high hover (40-60 ft altitude): may need 2 turns to clear.

**OA exposure:**
- At the beholder's altitude during retreat (20-40 ft), Bazgar and Sabriel cannot reach it with melee (5-ft reach). No OA threat on aerial retreat.
- Exception: if beholder descended for DD in R2 and is now at ground level or near-ground, retreat movement past Bazgar (within 5 ft reach) provokes Bazgar's OA.
- Bazgar: +7 to hit vs AC 17 = 55% hit (base); +1d8 from Action Surge or superiority die if available. Average: ~8 (1d8+4) or ~14 with Trip Attack. Against beholder's 28 HP: significant.

**Thrall-blocking mechanics:**
- Thrall 2 alive at 12 HP, within 60 ft.
- If beholder uses Shrine-Drift as BA: can't ALSO use Compel Thrall (BA conflict, documented FEEL-F50-01 / thrulm-beholder-ct-drift-ba-conflict scenario).
- Therefore: on the retreat turn, the beholder cannot BOTH use Shrine-Drift (escape) AND Compel Thrall (reposition thrall to block). Must choose.
- Optimal choice: use Shrine-Drift (escape 30 ft through stone). Thrall 2 can move 30 ft on its own initiative (init 8, later in round) but PCs act before it.

### Round 3 retreat sequence (optimal)

**Init 20 (lair):** VE fires if Bazgar is within 20 ft of shrine. Bazgar DEX −1, 80% fail, 9.9 avg damage. Bazgar HP: ~20 − 9.9 → ~10. Beholder's lair action weakens Bazgar before retreat.

**Beholder turn (init 21):**
- **Action:** Disintegration Ray (recharged) vs Marwen. +7 to hit vs AC 15 = 65% hit. On hit: 45 avg force → Marwen (18 HP remaining) disintegrated. On miss: no action benefit.
  - If DR hits (65%): Marwen eliminated, 2v1 going forward.
  - If DR misses (35%): beholder wasted its action; consider Multiattack as fallback.
- **Bonus Action:** Shrine-Drift — phase 30 ft toward lower shaft.
- **Movement:** Fly 30 ft toward lower shaft.
- **Net:** If DR hits, beholder exits combat 60 ft deeper into the shaft and has likely eliminated Marwen. Dramatic — a last-gasp kill before escape.

**Bazgar (init 17):** Cannot reach beholder (now 60 ft into shaft, effectively beyond pursuit). Action wasted on no target OR pursues but has no fly speed — can't enter the shaft.

**Marwen (init 14):** If alive: Counterspell opportunity on prior turn? DR is a ranged spell attack, not a spell — cannot be counterspelled. She may fire Fireball or Shatter into the shaft entrance, but beholder has moved 60 ft in. Outside most spell ranges or line of sight.

**Sabriel (init 11):** Cannot pursue vertically. Could LoH a downed ally (none) or prepare for the deeper shaft to open.

### Feel assessment

**FEEL-F65-01 (NEW — Log only): DR-on-retreat is the most dramatic beat, but only fires 65% of the time.**

The most satisfying retreat turn is: VE chips Bazgar → DR eliminates Marwen → beholder escapes. This is dramatically satisfying: the boss makes one final devastating strike before fleeing into the dark. However:
- 35% of the time DR misses and the beholder escapes without killing anyone (anticlimactic)
- If DR was already used this turn and hasn't recharged, the retreat is purely procedural (drift + fly + goodbye)

The retreat mechanic has no fallback dramatic beat when DR is unavailable. When the beholder retreats without a "final kill," the fight ends with "it ran away," which is weak for a CR 13 boss. 

**Recommend:** Give the beholder a *guaranteed* dramatic exit option when DR is unavailable — e.g., a final Void Scream as a free action on retreat below 30 HP ("the shrine cracks as the Hunger Below descends"), costing no action. This ensures the escape always has a mechanical impact on the party rather than a silent vanishing.

**FEEL-F65-02 (NEW — Log only): Shrine-Drift self-damage (5 force, 1d10) at 28 HP is a real but non-decisive risk.**

At 28 HP, the self-damage (5 avg) if the beholder ends inside an object is 18% of remaining HP. In practice:
- Retreating through the lower shaft *entrance* (open air passage): self-damage does NOT trigger.
- Phasing through a closed stone wall: self-damage triggers.
- The beholder would choose the open entrance, so self-damage in retreat is almost never relevant.

Self-damage feels thematic (the god-scar inside the shaft resists even the Hunger) but is mechanically a non-factor in practice. This is design fiction with no real bite. Could be interesting if the shaft entrance were explicitly sealed (DM option: stone door gives the beholder a 1d10 self-damage cost to escape, creating tension at low HP). As-written: moot.

**FEEL-F65-03 (NEW — Log only): "Something down there scares even this creature" has zero mechanical expression in the retreat.**

Beholder.md states: "It cannot go deeper into the lower chamber willingly; something down there scares even this creature." But the retreat tactic is *toward the lower shaft*. If the beholder is scared of the lower chamber, why does it retreat *into* it?

Two interpretations:
1. The beholder retreats to the *shaft entrance* (not the lower chamber) — it hides just inside the passage, below the shrine room, but refuses to go deeper.
2. The beholder retreats completely and is gone, and the "something scares it" is a future hook, not a combat constraint.

Either way, this lore detail has no mechanical expression and creates a contradiction with the retreat tactic: "retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block" implies it goes below, but lore says it won't go willingly deeper.

**Recommend:** Add a range cap to the retreat: "retreats to the upper 30 ft of the lower shaft (refuses to descend further); any PC who pursues it into the shaft will fight it cornered and desperate there, not in open space." This makes the retreat dramatically meaningful (it's trapped between the party and the thing it fears) rather than a pure escape.

**FEEL-F65-04 (NEW — Log only): BA conflict on retreat turn forces Shrine-Drift over Compel Thrall.**

At retreat HP, the beholder SHOULD use Compel Thrall to put Thrall 2 in the shaft entrance to block pursuit. But it also SHOULD use Shrine-Drift to escape quickly. Both are bonus actions. The BA conflict (FEEL-F50-01) forces a choice at the worst possible moment.

On the retreat turn, Shrine-Drift is strictly necessary (escape) and Compel Thrall is nice-to-have (blocking). This means Thrall 2 can only block if it moves on its own initiative (init 8), which comes after Bazgar (17) and Marwen (14) have already acted. By the time Thrall 2 can move, the pursuit window has closed.

The "uses thralls to block pursuit" tactic is written as if the beholder can reposition thralls on its escape turn — it cannot due to BA conflict. Thrall blocking is only effective if the beholder prepared one round earlier (used CT on a prior turn to pre-position Thrall 2 near the shaft). If thralls were not pre-positioned, they provide no blocking on the actual retreat turn.

**This is a tactics text bug.** The line "retreats ... using thralls to block" implies the beholder can reposition thralls on its escape turn. It cannot. The text should read: "If thralls have been pre-positioned near the shaft entrance (via Compel Thrall on prior rounds), they can slow PC pursuit. If not pre-positioned, thralls cannot block on the retreat turn (BA conflict with Shrine-Drift)."
