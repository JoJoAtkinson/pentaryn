### NEW FINDING — FEEL-F19-01: VS R2 drops both melee PCs before their R2 turns (beholder init 14)

With initiative order Sabriel (15) > Beholder (14) > Bazgar (11), the beholder fires VS at init 14 — AFTER Sabriel has acted in R2 but BEFORE Bazgar acts. Result:
- Sabriel gets her R2 action (she went at 15); then VS resolves at 14 → drops Sabriel.
- Bazgar never gets his R2 action (he's downed by VS before init 11).

Sabriel's R2 action (likely: attack or Lay on Hands on downed ally from R1) happens. Bazgar's R2 action does not.

P(beholder init > Bazgar init) ≈ 75% (beholder +3 vs Bazgar −1). **In ~75% of rounds, Bazgar is denied his turn by VS before he acts.** This is the same pattern as FEEL-F13-02 but for VS in R2 rather than multiattack in R1.

When combined: R1 = fighter denied turn by multiattack-nova (FEEL-F13-02) + R2 = fighter denied turn by VS (FEEL-F19-01) = Bazgar could go an entire 2-round stretch without acting, down to 0 HP twice.

**This is the sharpest player-experience issue in the encounter.** Joe may want to add a DM note: "If Bazgar has not yet acted in the current round and VS would drop him, consider holding VS until after his initiative count — he gets to swing once before going down, which is more dramatic."

### NEW FINDING — FEEL-F19-02: Shrine-proximity disadvantage is positionally ambiguous at the table

The .md notes that "within 10 ft of the shrine" applies to the physical altar stone. The beholder hovers above the altar; melee fighters attacking the beholder at tentacle-reach (10 ft) are in an ambiguous zone.

If "shrine" = the altar stone on the floor and "beholder" = hovering 5–10 ft above it, a fighter at the beholder's reach (10 ft horizontal) could be anywhere from 5–15 ft from the altar depending on exact positioning. The rule will be inconsistently applied without a table marker.

**Fix suggested:** Add a cheat-sheet row for VS or a DM sidebar: *"Shrine proximity = within 10 ft of the ALTAR STONE (the physical object on the floor), not the beholder. Mark the altar on the battle map or use a token — melee fighters who ran directly to the altar are automatically within 10 ft; melee fighters who engaged the beholder away from the altar may not be."*

### VS vs Void Ray comparison — ability-rotation note

The slice implicitly raises the question: when should the beholder use VS vs VR (legendary)?

| Ability | Cost | Target | Expected damage | Rider |
|---------|------|--------|----|---|
| Void Scream | 1 main action + Recharge 6 | 30-ft AoE | 57.6 vs 2 PCs (Sub-A) | FRIGHTENED |
| Void Ray (LA) | 2 legendary actions | 1 PC | 22 × P(fail) ≈ 17.6 avg | None |

VS vs all 3 PCs (Sub-B): 81.2 expected vs VR's 17.6 per use. VS is strictly better in terms of damage when it connects all 3 PCs. But VS requires recharge 6 (17% chance each round after first use) and consumes the main action. VR is repeatable (3 LAs per round) and doesn't consume main action.

**VS is the burst damage spike; VR is the sustained LA attrition tool.** They operate on different economies and shouldn't be compared directly. The existing tactics note correctly prioritizes: R1 = Multiattack/grapple, R2 = VS (if party is clustered), R3+ = DR + VR LA attrition.

The rotation slice surfaces that VS delivers massive R2 damage when the party has pushed to within 30 ft — confirming the R2 timing is correct. No new issue here beyond the positioning ambiguity.
