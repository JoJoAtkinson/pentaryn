### FINDING 1 — Altitude vs Drain Divinity range conflict

**Drain Divinity:** range 30 ft.

Beholder hover altitude (per tactics/cheat sheet): 40–60 ft above altar floor.

At 40 ft altitude, the closest ground-level target (directly below) is 40 ft away. Even at 40 ft altitude, Drain Divinity CANNOT reach any ground-level PC without the beholder descending.

At 25 ft altitude: a PC at ground level directly below is 25 ft away ✓. A PC 15 ft horizontally from directly-below: √(25²+15²) ≈ 29 ft ✓. But a PC 20 ft horizontally: √(25²+20²) ≈ 32 ft ✗.

**Conclusion:** To use Drain Divinity, the beholder must first descend to ≤30 ft altitude via Shrine-Drift (bonus action: move up to 30 ft) or Move LA (1 LA).

This is a significant constraint not mentioned anywhere in the stat block, cheat sheet, or tactics text. The tactics say "hover above altar (40-60 ft up)" and also say "Focuses on Drain Divinity against clerics/paladins." These two instructions conflict.

**FEEL-R188-01 (NEW):** Drain Divinity requires beholder to descend to ≤30 ft, but tactics say hover 40-60 ft. Resolution requires either: (a) Shrine-Drift BA to descend (competing with evasive repositioning uses), or (b) Move LA (1 LA, reducing round to 2 LA for actual attacks). Neither is mentioned in tactics. Recommend adding: "To use Drain Divinity, use Shrine-Drift (BA) to descend to 25 ft before the LA window. In round 1, descend during the action turn so DD is available at end of Shardcaller's turn (init 14 — first LA window)."

### Drain Divinity EV analysis (damage economy comparison)

Using the same 3 LA budget:

**Option A: Drain Divinity (3 LA)**
- 65% chance Sabriel loses 2nd-level slot → beholder gains 4 THP
- Prevents one potential: Healing Word (~1d4+CHA avg ~6 HP) or Divine Smite add-on (~3d8 avg 13.5 on a hit)
- Expected THP gain: 0.65 × 4 = **2.6 THP**
- Expected damage prevented (conditional on Sabriel using the slot offensively): 0.65 × 13.5 × P(smite this round) ≈ 0.65 × 13.5 × 0.5 ≈ **~4.4 damage prevented**

**Option B: Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA)**
- Void Ray: 4d10 avg 22 force (+1 in-chamber = 23 avg). DC 16 DEX save (Sabriel DEX ~+2, 55% fail). Expected: 0.55×23 = **12.7 avg damage.** + 0.45×11.5 = **5.2 avg on save.** Total: **~12.7+5.2 = ~17.9 avg.**
- Tentacle (LA): +7 vs AC 19 → 45% hit × 13.5+grapple = **~6.1 avg**
- Total: ~**24.0 avg damage** from 3 LA

**Option C: All 3 LAs as Tentacle (3 separate turns)**
- Each: 45% hit × 13.5 = 6.1 avg. Total: **~18.3 avg damage** + potential 3 grapple attempts

DD expected outcome: 2.6 THP + ~4.4 conditional damage prevented = net **~7.0 value**
Void Ray + Tentacle: **~24.0 avg direct damage**

Drain Divinity is mathematically dominated by Void Ray + Tentacle for pure action economy. Its value is non-zero only if:
1. Sabriel's healing would have kept the party alive through additional rounds (survivability chain extends encounter duration significantly), AND
2. Those extra rounds would not have resulted in Sabriel's death via direct damage anyway.

At level 5 with Marwen at 32 max HP, the party cannot extend the encounter meaningfully through healing: Healing Word restores ~6 HP and doesn't alter the outcome enough to change DD's calculus.

**FEEL-R188-03 (NEW — confirms/escalates existing open issue):** Quantified DD EV: expected value ~7.0 (2.6 THP + ~4.4 conditional damage prevented) vs Void Ray + Tentacle at ~24.0 avg damage from same 3 LA budget. DD is ~3.4× less efficient as an action. The "degenerate against divine casters" open issue is confirmed and quantified. At this party level, DD is never the optimal LA spend. Recommend either: (a) reduce DD cost to 2 LA (same budget as Void Ray; comparable EV) and reduce THP gain to match, OR (b) add a mechanic that makes DD uniquely dangerous in this context (e.g., DD on fail also imposes frightened or removes divine damage immunity for 1 round).

### Beholder legendary economy summary

| Action | Rounds Used | LA Spent |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Drain Divinity (3 LA) | Round 1 | 3 of 3 |
| Save success / no DD | Round 2 | DD failed (save) → 3 LA wasted |
| No remaining LA | Round 2 | 0 of 3 |

In both rounds, the beholder committed all 3 LA to DD and got meaningful return only in round 1. Round 2 DD was wasted (save). Round 2 LA budget entirely sacrificed to DD = **no Move, no Tentacle, no Void Ray** in round 2. This confirms: DD as currently designed creates a "round lockout" in rounds where the target succeeds their save.

**FEEL-R188-04 (NEW):** When DD save succeeds, the beholder loses its ENTIRE legendary action budget for that round (3 LA consumed by the failed DD attempt = zero attacks, zero repositioning). No other LA use in 5e works this way — Void Ray (2 LA) at least guarantees half damage on a save. A DD miss is a full dead round. Recommend: make DD cost 3 LA on a miss refundable (1 LA refund on target save success, so beholder retains 1 LA for Move), OR lower cost to 2 LA so an alternative 1-LA action is still available.
