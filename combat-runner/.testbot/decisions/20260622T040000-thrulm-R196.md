### Setup

**Bazgar:** Fighter Battlemaster 5. STR 18 (+4), PB +3. Attack bonus: +7. Two attacks per action (Extra Attack). AC 18, HP 49.

**Beholder:** AC 17, HP 110. Antireality reaction: once per round, when targeted by an attack it can see, impose disadvantage on that attack roll — must be declared **before** the attack roll.

**Initiative order (from R188 baseline):** Beholder 19 → Shardcaller 14 → Marwen 12 → Shrine-Touched 11 → Sabriel 11 → Bazgar 9.

Beholder acts at init 19 (before Bazgar's turn at init 9). Reaction resets at start of beholder's turn. So by the time Bazgar attacks each round, the beholder's reaction is fresh. **Antireality can fire on Bazgar's first attack every round.**

### Finding 1 — BUG-F7-02 makes Antireality non-functional in the runner

This is the core problem for live sessions. The DB row:

```json
{
  "type": "reaction",
  "trigger": {"scope": "self", "event": "damage", "match": "incoming attack"},
  "narration_preview": "The stone ripples — the blow passes through something that was there a moment..."
}
```

- `trigger.event: "damage"` — fires AFTER the attack resolves (on the damage event), not at attack declaration
- The intended behavior requires triggering BEFORE the attack roll

In a live runner session:
1. Bazgar announces attack — **no runner prompt fires**
2. Bazgar rolls, hits → damage resolves
3. Runner fires Antireality prompt with "+2 AC" (the old, wrong mechanic)
4. DM retroactively applies "+2 AC" — this cannot affect a roll that already happened

The mechanic as designed in the stat block does not exist in the runner. The DM must completely override the runner — declare Antireality before Bazgar's roll, ignore the runner's prompt entirely. For 104 fires this has been the required workflow.

**Impact this scenario**: If BUG-F7-02 were fixed (trigger = attack-declared, pre-roll), the runner would surface a "DECLARE ANTIREALITY?" prompt the moment Bazgar switches to the beholder's tab and types an attack command. This would catch the most common timing failure at tables running the GUI.

### Finding 2 — NEW FEEL-R196-01 (MEDIUM): Antireality is the highest DM cognitive burden per round

Manual Antireality workflow (per round):
1. Notice Bazgar is about to attack the beholder
2. Check reaction availability (used this round?)
3. Decide yes/no on Antireality before Bazgar rolls
4. Declare "Antireality — take disadvantage" to the player
5. Player rolls with disadvantage
6. Runner later fires "+2 AC" prompt (from BUG-F7-02) — DM ignores it

This 5-step manual interrupt is required at the start of every round Bazgar attacks. With 3 PCs acting each round, plus lair actions at init 20 and 3 legendary actions spread across PC turns, the DM is already juggling 7+ decisions per round cycle. Antireality adds a constant low-level attention demand that fires before rolls are made — the exact moment the DM may be narrating the previous action.

**Recommendation (no source edit — log only):** Add a red-border note under "When Beholder Acts" in the cheat sheet:

> **⚠️ ANTIREALITY (REACTION):** Declare before Bazgar announces his roll — NOT when the runner prompts. The runner fires this too late (BUG-F7-02). Say "Antireality — disadvantage on that roll" at the moment the player picks up the dice.

This is CS-level guidance, not a stat block fix. Until BUG-F7-02 is resolved, this callout is the only mitigation.

### Finding 3 — NEW FEEL-R196-02 (LOW): Battlemaster Precision Attack directly counters Antireality

Bazgar's Battlemaster subclass has **Precision Attack** (superiority die, 1d8, add after missing to potentially hit). Antireality → disadvantage → near-miss creates exactly the scenario where Precision Attack is most valuable.

Example (Round 1, Attack 1 above): Antireality imposed disadvantage, lower roll 8 → total 15 vs AC 17 — missed by 2. Bazgar spends a Precision Attack die (avg 4.5): P(8 + 4.5 ≥ 10 on Antireality rolls) ≈ 70% that the Precision die converts the disadvantaged roll into a hit.

**Net effect:** Antireality vs a Battlemaster with Precision Attack dice available is closer to ~15% DPR reduction (not 22.7%), because roughly half the Antireality-induced misses get countered by Precision Attack. Bazgar has 4 superiority dice per short rest (Combat Superiority feature, Fighter 3+).

This isn't a bug — it's emergent counterplay. But it means Antireality's effective DPR reduction against the specific Compass Edge party (Bazgar = Battlemaster) is lower than against a generic fighter. Worth a DM note: "Antireality is least effective against Bazgar specifically — he has tools to recover from the imposed disadvantage."

Sabriel started at 44 HP; if she took damage during earlier rounds, she's likely around 26–30 HP heading in. Void Ray: 26–19.5 = 6.5 HP. Sabriel on the edge by Round 3.

- End of Marwen's turn: 1 LA remaining. Tentacle on Marwen (+7 vs AC 15, 65% hit → avg 14×0.65 = 9.1 expected) or Move (if reposition needed).

**Observation:** The LA economy during Antireality rounds is robust. Spending the reaction on Antireality does NOT spend an LA — they are independent pools. Antireality is "free" from the LA perspective. This is correct per 5e rules but worth noting for DMs who conflate reaction and LA costs.
