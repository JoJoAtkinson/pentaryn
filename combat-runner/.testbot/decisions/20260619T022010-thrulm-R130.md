### New Phase A findings this run

**STAT-04 (NEW) — Void-Feeding uses "checks" where "saving throws" is correct**

The beholder's Void-Feeding trait reads:
> "Advantage on checks to resist being turned by divine magic"

In 5e, Turn Undead and equivalent abilities (Turn the Aberrant for Oath of Watchers paladin, which is the relevant ability for Sabriel) require the target to make a **Wisdom saving throw** — not an ability check. "Checks to resist being turned" is incorrect rules terminology. The trait should read "saving throws to resist being turned by divine magic."

This is a log-only issue (terminology, not auto-fixable under Phase C rules). Against Sabriel at level 5 with a divine martial archetype: if she has Turn-like ability targeting Aberrations, the beholder having advantage on the save matters tactically (beholder WIS +2 + PB on an ability-contested roll = at least +5 total with advantage — very hard to Turn at a level-5 paladin's save DC of ~13). With advantage on the WIS save: P(succeed on WIS save vs DC 13) = 1 - (10/20)^2 = 75%. Turn is nearly non-functional against this beholder, which is probably intentional.

**DB-OPEN-06 (NEW) — Beholder lair actions have no DB rows**

Three lair actions exist in the beholder stat block:
- **Unstable Ground** — DC 16 DEX save, fall prone
- **Manifest Thralls** — charmed thralls in 60 ft gain THP + reaction move/attack
- **Void Eruption** — DC 16 DEX save within 20 ft of shrine, 2d10 force damage (avg 11)

None of these have rows in `actions.jsonl`. Typing "unstable ground", "manifest thralls", or "void eruption" into the beholder's tab will go to LLM fallback. At a table running fast, the DM may skip all lair actions because there's no verb entry to trigger them.

Also notable: Void Eruption damage `11 (2d10)` — the average is correct (2×5.5=11 ✓), but this is confirmed only from the stat block; the DB has no row to independently verify.

Fix path: author three rows via `combat_action_upsert`. Recommended types:
- `unstable_ground`: type `utility` (or `area` with a save), trigger on initiative 20
- `manifest_thralls`: type `utility`
- `void_eruption`: type `area`, save DEX DC 16, damage 2d10 force

**CS-DRIFT-06 (NEW) — Antireality reaction absent from cheat sheet**

The beholder's only reaction, Antireality, does not appear anywhere in the COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md. The "When Beholder Acts" section lists offensive decisions but gives no DM reminder that the beholder can react to attacks. Given:
1. The timing is critical (declare *before* the attack roll, not after — per FIX-F33-A note in stat block)
2. The DB fires the trigger on the `damage` event (post-hit), so the runner won't auto-surface it at the right moment
3. The cheat sheet is the primary at-table reference

A DM running from cheat sheet alone will miss Antireality every time the beholder is attacked, cutting its effective survivability significantly.

**Recommended cheat sheet addition** (under "When Beholder Acts"):
> **REACTION — Antireality:** When the beholder is targeted by an *attack it can see*, declare before the attack roll — imposes disadvantage. Once per round. (DB fires post-hit as a workaround; DM must declare early manually.)

**CS-DRIFT-07 (NEW) — Beholder lair actions absent from round-by-round checklist**

A DM running from the cheat sheet will likely forget all three lair actions (Unstable Ground, Manifest Thralls, Void Eruption). **Recommended addition** to "Start of Round" section:

**CS-DRIFT-08 (NEW) — Legendary action costs not listed in cheat sheet**

The cheat sheet mentions "Drain Divinity" and other legendary actions by name but never lists the LA costs. DM running from cheat sheet cannot manage the beholder's 3 LA budget without knowing:
- Move: 1 LA
- Tentacle (one lash): 1 LA
- Void Ray: 2 LA
- Drain Divinity: 3 LA (entire budget, once per round)

At-table consequence: DM may spend all 3 LA on Drain Divinity every round (it's the most thematically interesting option) and never use Move or Tentacle for repositioning/grappling, leaving the beholder unnecessarily static.

**Recommended addition** to the Beholder section or Save DCs table: a "Legendary Actions (3/round)" row with costs.

### Simulation — 2 rounds

- Marwen: HP 32, AC 15 (soft target, 20 ft back)
- Sabriel: HP 44, AC 19 (divine martial, other flank)

**Round 1 (Thrall's turn):**

*Action — Hand Axe (Weak) vs Bazgar (nearest target, AC 18):*
- Roll: d20 + 1. Hit on 17+. P(hit) = 20%. Expected damage: 2.5 × 0.20 = **0.5 DPR**.

*Bonus Action — Compelled Movement (beholder can see thrall, is within 60 ft):*
- Automatic; not a save. Thrall moves up to 30 ft toward Marwen per beholder's direction.
- Thrall is now adjacent to Marwen at round-end.

*Beholder bonus action this same round — Compel Thrall (1/Turn):*
- This is a SEPARATE mechanic: beholder targets the thrall (already charmed), forces DC 16 CHA save.
- Thrall CHA modifier: -2. Need 18+ to resist → 15% success, 85% compelled.
- On fail: Thrall moves 30 ft toward beholder or another target.
- **FEEL-11 ambiguity:** If BOTH fire in the same round — beholder uses Compel Thrall (BA) AND thrall uses Compelled Movement (thrall's BA) — does the thrall get 60 ft of forced movement? Or are these meant to be the same effect from two perspectives? The stat block doesn't clarify which takes precedence or whether they stack.

**Round 2 (Thrall's turn):**

Thrall now adjacent to Marwen (from Compelled Movement).

*Action — Hand Axe (Weak) vs Marwen (AC 15):*
- Roll: d20 + 1. Hit on 14+. P(hit) = 35%. Expected damage: 2.5 × 0.35 = **0.875 DPR**.

*If DM types "dagger" instead (Dagger Desperate):*
- Roll: d20 + 3. Hit on 12+. P(hit) = 45%. Expected damage: 4.5 × 0.45 = **~2.0 DPR**.
- Still low threat but measurably functional.

### Feel assessment

**FEEL-10 (NEW) — Thrall offensive contribution is negligible; this is correct design**

Thrall DPR against any Compass Edge PC:
- vs Bazgar (AC 18): ~0.5 (hand axe) or ~1.35 (dagger)
- vs Marwen (AC 15): ~0.875 (hand axe) or ~2.0 (dagger)  
- vs Sabriel (AC 19): ~0.375 (hand axe) or ~1.13 (dagger)

At 1-3 DPR, the thrall deals ~10–20 damage over a 10-round encounter. Against Marwen's 32 HP, this is survivable. The thrall is NOT a damage dealer.

**However, this is correct for the encounter design:** The thrall's value is:
1. Positioning (Compelled Movement gets it adjacent to a target the beholder specifies)
2. Opportunity attacks (if it flanks a PC, that PC can't disengage without provoking OA from the thrall)
3. Action sink (PCs spend actions killing it instead of hitting the beholder)
4. Beholder body extension (Compel Thrall redirects the thrall as a bonus action, cheap for the beholder)

The encounter math is: thrall takes 1-2 PC actions to kill. If those actions didn't go to the beholder, that's 1-2 fewer rounds of the party damaging the actual threat. At CR 13, the beholder needs all the help it can get from action economy.

**No change recommended.** Thrall is working as designed.

**FEEL-11 (NEW) — Compel Thrall + Compelled Movement double-movement needs a ruling**

Beholder Compel Thrall (BA, 1/turn): Forces DC 16 CHA save on a charmed target → move 30 ft on fail.  
Thrall Compelled Movement (BA): Moves up to 30 ft in beholder-designated direction, automatic.

Can both apply in the same round? If yes, a thrall could be forced to move 60 ft (30 + 30) per round — covering significant ground in the chamber. If no, one overrides the other.

**Recommend ruling:** They are distinct mechanics and CAN stack, but the beholder should only use Compel Thrall if the thrall hasn't already moved via Compelled Movement (otherwise it wastes the beholder's BA for redundant movement). At the table, treat Compelled Movement as the "default" that fires each of the thrall's turns, and Compel Thrall as an emergency redirect if the thrall ended up in the wrong position.

**Add to stat block or cheat sheet:** A parenthetical clarifying that Compel Thrall and Compelled Movement can both apply in the same round but represent distinct control vectors.
