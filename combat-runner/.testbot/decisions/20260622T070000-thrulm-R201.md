### FINDING-R201-01 (NEW — HIGH): Lair action → Multiattack cycle recurs EVERY round, not just Round 1

**Root cause:** Lair action fires at init count 20, independent of the beholder's personal initiative. Bazgar DEX +0 means he rolls init at avg ~10-11 — always after init 20, always after the lair action. Unstable Ground (DC 16 DEX save, Bazgar +1) succeeds 70% of rounds.

**Damage impact:** Without prone (no advantage): expected Multiattack damage = 2×(0.50×14.5) + 0.50×22 = **25.5**. With prone (advantage): **38.3**. Delta: **+12.8 per round from prone-triggered advantage**. This difference compounds every round Bazgar stays in the encounter.

**Structural implication:** FEEL-R197-01 ("Bazgar architecturally bypassed Round 1") understates the issue. The correct framing is: **Bazgar is the designated lair action target every round, and 70% of those rounds he receives a ~38-HP assault before acting.** A level-5 fighter with 49 HP is typically KO'd in ~Round 2-3 from this cycle, before dealing consistent damage.

**Counterplay assessment for Bazgar:**
- No BM maneuver addresses the prone cycle directly.
- Evasive Footwork adds to AC during movement — doesn't help against attacks with advantage.
- Second Wind (BA) restores ~1d10+5 (~10.5 HP) — useful at low HP but competes with Cunning-Action-equivalent repositioning Bazgar doesn't have.
- Action Surge: if Bazgar survives to his turn, he can double-attack — but this is reactive, not preventive.

**Party-level counterplay:**
- Sabriel might be able to smite the beholder to force it down faster, but the beholder at 110 HP requires sustained assault.
- Marwen's best option is Banishment (DC 14 WIS save vs beholder WIS +2 — 60% chance; beholder will use Legendary Resistance).
- Killing the beholder in 2-3 rounds is unrealistic at this party level.

**Recommendation for Joe:** Add cheat sheet note: "Bazgar's DEX is too low to reliably beat init count 20. Consider having him hold action or ready a Shove (to knock beholder down from altitude) rather than advancing into the lair action cycle every round."

### FINDING-R201-02 (NEW — LOW): Shrine-Drift vs Compel Thrall BA conflict — altitude cost of Multiattack undocumented

**Root cause:** Both Shrine-Drift and Compel Thrall are Bonus Actions, and the beholder has only one BA per turn. To use Multiattack with Tentacle reach 10 ft, the beholder must first descend via Shrine-Drift (30 ft movement). This forecloses Compel Thrall on the same turn.

**Dead-action turns:** On rounds where DR and Void Scream are both unreacharged AND the beholder chooses to stay at altitude (not spend BA on Shrine-Drift), Multiattack is physically impossible. The beholder's only remaining main-action options are Disintegration Ray (unreacharged) and Void Scream (unreacharged). **The beholder has no effective main action at altitude on a rechargeless turn.** This creates "wasted" turns that aren't documented.

**Frequency estimate:** P(neither DR nor VS recharged) = P(DR miss) × P(VS miss) = (1 − 1/3) × (1 − 1/6) = 0.667 × 0.833 ≈ **56% of rounds**. On these turns, the beholder must either descend (Shrine-Drift → Multiattack) or have no effective main action. In practice, it will always descend — making Shrine-Drift a near-mandatory BA expenditure, effectively removing Compel Thrall from the beholder's toolkit in most turns.

**Recommendation:** Add to beholder tactics text: "Shrine-Drift (BA) is required to bring tentacle reach to bear; this forecloses Compel Thrall. Compel Thrall is realistically available only on turns where DR or Void Scream is recharged."
