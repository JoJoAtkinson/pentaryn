### Step 1: DR attack roll vs Marwen

**To hit AC 15 at +7:** needs 8+ on d20. P(hit) = **65%** (13/20).

With Marwen's Shield reaction (1st-level slot, +5 AC → AC 20): needs 13+ to hit. P(hit through Shield) = **40%** (8/20).

Key: if Marwen has NOT seen an attack before DR fires (Round 1, beholder wins init), she gets to decide whether to burn Shield *in response to* the attack roll announcement but *before* the roll resolves. Standard DM practice: announce attack, allow Shield reaction. If she burns Shield and beholder misses, she saved her action AND her reaction — good trade. If beholder hits anyway, Shield was wasted.

**Without Shield:** P(KO from 32 HP) = P(10d8+1 ≥ 32) = P(10d8 ≥ 31).
Normal approximation: μ = 45, σ = sqrt(10 × 63/12) ≈ 7.25.
P(Z ≥ (31−45)/7.25) = P(Z ≥ −1.93) ≈ **0.973 → 97%**.

**Without Shield, given hit:** Marwen is KO'd 97% of the time. Disintegration clause fires on ANY 0-HP result — she is **permanently dead** (no True Resurrection or Wish available at level 5). The 3% survival window requires rolling ≤30 on 10d8+1.

**Threat ceiling:** Maximum 10d8+1 = 81 force damage. Minimum = 11. Average = 46. Marwen is in the kill window 97% of the time on a hit.

---

### Step 2: Perm-death probability under slice assumptions

Assumptions: recharge confirmed (DM rolls 5–6), beholder acts before Marwen, optimal targeting (beholder picks lowest-HP target = Marwen).

P(perm-death this turn) = P(hit) × P(KO | hit) = 0.65 × 0.97 ≈ **63%** (no Shield).
With Shield: 0.40 × 0.97 ≈ **39%**.

P(beholder acts before Marwen, given avg init 13.5 vs 12.5) ≈ 55% (coin-flip tier; Marwen can spike higher).

Overall P(Marwen perm-dead Round 1, recharge confirmed): 55% × 63% ≈ **35%** without Shield, 55% × 39% ≈ **21%** with Shield.

Without recharge assumption: multiply by 33% → ~7–12% per round overall. Consistent with FEEL-R176-01 "P≈21%/round" (that figure appears to assume Marwen doesn't use Shield).

---

### Step 3: DR + 3 LA simultaneous budget

DR consumes the beholder's **action**. The 3 LA refresh at **start** of beholder's turn — *before* the action is spent. A DR turn therefore delivers:

- **Action:** DR (avg 46 force, 97% KO on Marwen if hit) — permanent character death.
- **LA (3 total, spent at end of each *other* creature's turn before the beholder's next turn):** depends on who was last to act. In typical Round 1 order:
  - After Sabriel: 1 LA → Tentacle vs Marwen (+7 vs AC 15, 65% hit, avg 14.5 bludg + 52% grapple).
  - After Bazgar: 2 LA → Void Ray vs Bazgar (DC 16 DEX, Bazgar DEX +1 → 75% fail, avg 23 force).
  - Remaining: 0 LA.

On a DR round, the beholder effectively deals: ~46 to Marwen (action) + ~9.5 expected to Marwen from LA Tentacle + ~17.3 expected to Bazgar from LA Void Ray = **~72.8 expected damage across the party** in one "turn." Sabriel takes none but is not positioned to act during these events.

**Critical implication:** The round the DR fires is the round the party loses its weakest member AND takes additional LA damage. There is no window between DR and the LA spend where the party can react to save Marwen.

---

### Step 4: Shield reaction — only meaningful counterplay in range

Marwen's access to the Shield spell (reaction, 1st-level, +5 AC until start of her next turn) is the single most impactful mitigation available to her against DR. It reduces hit rate from 65% to 40% — a 38% relative improvement. Mirror Image (3 illusions, action) requires her turn to cast, so it cannot protect her from a Round 1 surprise. Counterspell does not apply (DR is an attack roll, not a spell that requires concentration or somatic components visible before targeting — ambiguous per table interpretation).

If Marwen is hit through Shield or hasn't cast Shield yet: 97% KO, permanent death.

**Table-stakes implication:** The encounter effectively requires the DM to decide before running whether to use DR on Marwen at all. Using it optimally (vs lowest-AC/HP target) results in a 35–63% chance of a permanent campaign death per DR use. The tactics section says "Disintegration of spellcasters" but does NOT caveat this with the perm-death consequence. CS-DRIFT-29 (Shield absent from Spellcaster? cheat sheet guidance) is directly relevant here.

### FINDING-R202-01 (NEW — HIGH): Disintegration Ray creates ~35% Round 1 perm-death window under standard recharge assumptions (recharge confirmed)

**Root cause:** DR (10d8+1 in-chamber, avg 46) vs Marwen (32 HP, AC 15) creates a 97% KO probability on hit. The "reduced to 0 HP by this attack" disintegration clause activates on any kill, not just instakills. At level 5, True Resurrection and Wish are categorically unavailable, making every DR kill a permanent character death.

**Probability breakdown:**
- P(KO from full HP | hit, no Shield): 97%
- P(hit, no Shield): 65%
- P(beholder acts before Marwen): ~55%
- P(perm-death, recharge confirmed, Round 1): ~35%
- P(perm-death, recharge confirmed, with Shield): ~21%

**Existing flags extended:** FEEL-R176-01 documents "P≈21%/round" — the present simulation confirms this number and extends it: the R176-01 figure appears to represent the "Shield used" scenario. The "no Shield" baseline is ~35%. These two numbers bracket the real-world risk.

**Recommendation for Joe:** Add DM consent briefing to the beholder tactics section: *"Disintegration Ray used against Marwen results in permanent character death ~35% of the time it's used in optimal conditions. Confirm with players before the session that permanent death is on the table, or restrict DR to non-PC targets (thralls, summoned creatures) during this encounter. Using DR is a legitimate design choice — it just requires explicit pre-session buy-in."*

### FINDING-R202-02 (NEW — MEDIUM): DR + full 3 LA budget fires simultaneously — undocumented in tactics

**Root cause:** DR is the beholder's action. LA budget (3) refreshes at start of the beholder's turn, before the action is spent. They are independent resources. On any DR round the beholder also gets 3 LA to spend at the end of each remaining PC's turn that same round.

**Combined expected damage on a DR round (Tentacle + Void Ray LA spend):** ~26.8 additional expected damage across Marwen and Bazgar, on top of the DR hit. The round becomes a de facto TPK-setup: one PC potentially perma-dead (Marwen), fighter (Bazgar) at expected −20 HP below start of round.

**Why this matters at the table:** The cheat sheet and tactics section both treat DR and LA as separate decisions. The interaction — that the round the DR kills someone is also the round the remaining party takes maximum LA pressure — is not called out. DMs may think they're being "restrained" by only using DR while actually delivering the highest-pressure round of the encounter.

**Recommendation for Joe:** Add to beholder tactics: *"On DR rounds, the 3 LA budget is still fully available. If DR kills a PC, the surviving party faces immediate LA follow-up — this is the maximum lethality configuration. Consider holding DR for later rounds so the party has at least one full round of action before facing this combination."*
