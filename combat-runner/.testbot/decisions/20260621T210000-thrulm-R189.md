### Findings

**FINDING 1 — Unstable Ground is the dominant choice, but it's the least flashy.**

Unstable Ground does zero direct damage and does not appear threatening to a player observing it. DMs choosing lair actions at the table will likely default to Void Eruption (visible force damage) or Manifest Thralls (thematic beholder-commands-minions narrative) over Unstable Ground. But the simulation shows:

- Unstable Ground → advantage on immediate multiattack → Bazgar DOWN before acting (p ≈ 70% prone × 75% tentacle hit = 52.5% chance of the critical sequence)
- Void Eruption → ~9 avg damage + friendly-fire risk → Bazgar at 9 HP, still acts

DMs without explicit guidance will undervalue Unstable Ground and overvalue the more dramatic alternatives.

**FEEL-R189-01 (NEW):** Unstable Ground is mechanically dominant but undervalued because it deals zero direct damage. The init-20-to-init-19 sequencing (lair action directly before the beholder's regular turn) means prone at init 20 is worth approximately +25% hit probability on the beholder's immediate multiattack. In the simulation, this was the difference between Bazgar going down (Unstable Ground) vs. surviving to act (Void Eruption). Recommend adding to cheat sheet "When Beholder Acts": "Init count 20: Unstable Ground is strongest when Bazgar/Sabriel is in melee range — prone fires the turn before the beholder's own init 19, giving immediate advantage on tentacle attacks. Choose this unless no melee PC is within 60 ft."

---

**FINDING 2 — Manifest Thralls is mechanically dead at this party level.**

1 THP per thrall and reaction attacks at +1 to hit (avg 1.4 damage across 2 thralls) produce negligible mechanical impact against AC 14–19 PCs with 32–49 max HP. The lair action's narrative potential (beholder commanding minions mid-combat) is real, but the mechanics don't support any meaningful threat.

The lair action text's note "gain temporary hit points equal to the beholder's Charisma modifier (minimum 1)" — CHA +1 = 1 THP. If CHA were +4 or +5, this would scale. At +1, it is literal rounding error.

**FEEL-R189-02 (NEW):** Manifest Thralls at CHA +1 averages 1 THP per thrall and <1 avg damage from reaction attacks. Against an AC 15+ party, this lair action is mechanically negligible. The open briefing flag ("Redesign as a reaction-attack trigger instead") is confirmed here: replacing THP with a direct attack trigger (e.g., "up to 2 controlled derro within 60 ft immediately make one weapon attack") would produce ~3–8 avg damage total — still low, but meaningfully nonzero. Recommend Joe redesign or scrap.

---

**FINDING 3 — Void Eruption has undocumented friendly-fire risk.**

"Each creature within 20 feet of the shrine" includes the beholder's own thrall derro if they are positioned near the altar. Typical thrall positioning (flanking near shrine, within beholder's telepathic 60 ft range) places them inside the 20 ft Void Eruption radius. In the simulation, Thrall 1 took 10 force damage (45% of max HP) from a lair action the beholder chose.

The stat block contains no ally-exclusion language. The cheat sheet does not flag this. A DM using Void Eruption without tracking thrall-vs-shrine distances will periodically devastate their own minion economy.

**FEEL-R189-03 (NEW):** Void Eruption deals "each creature within 20 ft of shrine" — no friendly-fire exception. Thrall derro positioned near the altar take the same damage as PCs (~9 avg force on 70% fail). This is not flagged in the stat block or cheat sheet. Either add "excluding creatures controlled by or allied to the beholder" OR add a DM note: "Reposition thralls outside 20 ft of shrine before using Void Eruption." Also flag in cheat sheet tactical section: "Void Eruption hits own thralls if near altar — check thrall positions first."

---

**FINDING 4 — Lair action init-20-to-init-19 window creates a compounding sequence not visible from the stat block.**

The beholder's initiative (19) is directly below the lair action threshold (20). This means in every round, the sequence is: lair action (init 20) → beholder regular turn (init 19) — effectively giving the beholder two consecutive actions before any PC with an initiative between 1–18 can respond to the lair action's effect.

A prone Bazgar (from Unstable Ground at init 20) has no chance to stand before the beholder's tentacles hit him at init 19. A Bazgar grappled at init 19 cannot use his action to break free before the lair action fires next round (init 20) because the lair action precedes his turn again.

This creates a self-reinforcing grapple + lair action loop that is deadly but not mechanically documented anywhere as a designed feature. It may be accidental (beholder's high initiative incidentally pairs well with the lair action) rather than intentional design.

**FEEL-R189-04 (NEW):** Beholder init 19 directly follows lair action init 20 every round. This creates a compounding sequence: prone/disruption effect at init 20 → beholder immediate advantage at init 19 → PC acts only afterward. The sequencing is more punishing than any other init spread the party could draw against this creature. Not explicitly documented as a design feature. Consider noting in the encounter overview: "The beholder's high initiative (19) means its lair action always telegraphs to opponents but immediately resolves into its multiattack — DMs should narrate both in the same 'beat' to convey the relentless tempo."
