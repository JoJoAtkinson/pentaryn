## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `tank-wall`

### Scenario prelude

The party enters Thrulm chamber. The derro tank-wall (2 Ragers + 2 Deep Watch) holds the threshold, blocking
advance toward the altar/beholder. No beholder this slice — tests the wall formation in isolation.

### Setup

- **Deep Watch A:** HP 27, AC 15, init +2
- **Deep Watch B:** HP 27, AC 15, init +2
- **Rager A:** HP 52, AC 16, init +0
- **Rager B:** HP 52, AC 16, init +0
- **Bazgar** (init −1 Dex): rolls 11; **Marwen** (+2 Dex): rolls 14; **Sabriel** (+0 Dex): rolls 8

### Initiative order (simulated)

1. Deep Watch A (16)
2. Marwen (14)
3. Rager A (13)
4. Bazgar (11)
5. Deep Watch B (9)
6. Sabriel (8)
7. Rager B (5)

### Round 1

**DW-A (init 16):** Multiattack with Tactical Drilling advantage (+2 to hit vs Bazgar AC 18, need 16+). Two axe
swings at advantage (~10% each) → both miss. Effectively 0 damage.

**Marwen (init 14):** Fireball (L3, DC 15 DEX). Centered on the wall cluster.
- Rager A: DEX +0, roll 8 → fail → 28 dmg. HP: 52 − 28 = **24**.
- Rager B: DEX +0, roll 14 → fail → 28 dmg. HP: 52 − 28 = **24**.
- DW-A: DEX save +4, roll 12 → 16 → success → 14 dmg. HP: 27 − 14 = **13**.
- DW-B: DEX save +4, roll 6 → 10 → fail → 28 dmg. HP: 27 − 28 = **0 (down)**.

Marwen expends 1 × L3 slot. One DW dead R1.

**Rager A (init 13):** Madness Endurance active (+1 to attacks). Charges Bazgar. Taunt targets Marwen
(DC 12 CHA, Marwen CHA ~+1, rolls 7 → fail → Marwen has disadv vs non-Rager-A targets next turn).
Multiattack (+5 after ME): roll 17 hit (7 dmg), roll 20 hit (9 dmg). Bazgar: 49 − 16 = **33 HP**.

**Bazgar (init 11):** Attacks Rager A. +8 vs AC 16.
- Attack 1 (roll 14 → 22): hit, 1d12+5 = 10. Rager A: 24 − 10 = **14**.
- Attack 2 (roll 8 → 16): hit, 1d12+5 = 8. Rager A: 14 − 8 = **6**.
- Uses **Menacing Attack** maneuver on Attack 2: +1d8 damage (avg 4) applies. Rager A frightened?
  **IMMUNE.** The rider does nothing (known, flagged below). Superiority die spent for damage only.

**DW-B (init 9):** Downed. Skip.

**Sabriel (init 8):** Rager A at 6 HP. Attacks Rager A.
- Attack 1 (roll 11 → 19): hit. 1d8+5 = 9 + Divine Smite L1 (2d8 = 9 radiant) = **18 damage**.
  Rager A: 6 − 18 = **dead**. Sabriel expends 1 × L1 slot.
- Attack 2 targets Rager B. Roll 9 → 17: hit. 1d8+5 = 9. Rager B: 24 − 9 = **15 HP**.

**Rager B (init 5):** ME active. Charges Bazgar (+5 vs AC 18). Roll 13 → 18: hit, 8 dmg. Roll 7 → 12: miss.
Bazgar: 33 − 8 = **25 HP**. Taunt targets Bazgar (DC 12 CHA, Bazgar CHA ~+0, rolls 5 → fail → Bazgar
has disadv vs non-Rager-B targets R2).

**End of Round 1:**
- DW-B: dead. Rager A: dead.
- Rager B: 15 HP. DW-A: 13 HP.
- Bazgar: 25 HP (24 dmg taken). Marwen: 32 HP (0 dmg, L3 slot spent). Sabriel: 44 HP (L1 slot spent).

### Round 2

**DW-A (init 16):** Tactical Drilling inactive (DW-B dead). Crossbow vs Marwen (AC 15, +4 = 50% hit rate).
Roll 11 → 15: hit. 6 dmg. Marwen: 32 − 6 = **26 HP**.

**Marwen (init 14):** Fire Bolt (2d10) vs Rager B (+5 vs AC 16). Roll 12 → 17: hit. 2d10 = 9 dmg.
Rager B: 15 − 9 = **6 HP**. (Taunt disadv from Rager A now void — Rager A is dead; Taunt ends with taunter.)

**Bazgar (init 11):** Attacks Rager B (AC 16). +8 vs AC 16, roll 7 → 15: miss. Roll 12 → 20: hit.
1d12+5 = 12. Rager B: 6 − 12 = **dead**.

**Sabriel (init 8):** Attacks DW-A (13 HP, AC 15). +8 vs AC 15: auto-hits on most rolls.
- Roll 9 → 17: hit, 1d8+5 = 10. DW-A: 13 − 10 = 3.
- Roll 12 → 20: hit, 1d8+5 = 8. DW-A: 3 − 8 = **dead**.

**End of Round 2:** All tank-wall NPCs dead in 2 rounds.

**Party resources expended:** 1 × L3 slot (Fireball), 1 × L1 slot (Smite). Bazgar at 25 HP (no Second Wind
used). Marwen at 26 HP. Sabriel at 44 HP.

### Feel Analysis

**FEEL-R101-01 (NEW): Fireball nukes the tank wall in one action.**

Both Ragers (52 HP each) fall to 24 HP on failed Fireball saves; one DW dies outright (28 > 27 HP). After a
single L3 slot, the wall is functionally broken: only cleanup remains in R2. The scenario provides no
meaningful resistance to a L5 wizard holding Fireball. For the wall to function as a gate (requiring 3–4
rounds of pressure), Rager HP would need to be ~80–90, or there would need to be a Counterspell source
(e.g., Shardcaller with a Counterspell-equivalent reaction), or the beholder's presence would be required to
split attention.

**Recommendation (log only):** Note in `tank-wall` scenario documentation that this slice is a setup check
for the formation's *shape*, not its durability. For durability testing vs Fireball, use `beholder-escorts-limited`
(index 3) where the beholder can counterpressure the wizard.

**FEEL-R101-02 (NEW): Rager frightened immunity burns Battlemaster superiority dice at the table.**

Bazgar's Menacing Attack (Attack 2 vs Rager A) spends a superiority die. The rider ("target is frightened
for 1 minute") does nothing — Rager is immune. The extra 1d8 damage (avg 4) is real but the primary payoff
is gone. A player spending a superiority die expecting the full maneuver gets a silent non-event.

The beholder .md already has a call-out for this in the Rager's tactics section, but the call-out is buried
under Taunt notes rather than under the Menacing Attack hook. At the table, the DM must proactively warn
Bazgar's player before they commit the die:
*"Rager is immune to frightened — Menacing Attack will deal the extra damage but the frightened rider won't
land. Still worth using for damage?"*

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to Rager's tactics a top-of-file callout: "⚠ BATTLEMASTER NOTE: Menacing
Attack's frightened rider is wasted against this creature. Warn the player before they spend the die." The
current note at line ~69 in derro-rager.md is accurate but not prominent enough for quick DM scanning.

**FEEL-R101-03 (NEW): Taunt-after-death has no documented ruling.**

When Rager A died (R1, Sabriel's attack), Marwen still had Rager A's Taunt active ("disadvantage on attacks
vs targets other than the rager until end of her next turn"). With the taunting rager dead, the "other than
the rager" clause compares against a creature that no longer exists.

Two legal readings: (A) disadvantage persists until the end of Marwen's next turn, applied to all targets
(vacuously: nothing is "the rager"), OR (B) Taunt ends when the taunting rager dies (source gone, condition
void). At this table, ruling (B) was used — Marwen's R2 Fire Bolt had no disadvantage. Ruling (A) would have
imposed disadvantage on Marwen's R2 Fire Bolt (vs non-dead-Rager-A targets), which is arguably reasonable
flavor but mechanically unintuitive.

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to Rager tactics: "Taunt ends when the rager dies — the 'targets other than
the rager' clause cannot be resolved against a dead creature. The DM should announce the Taunt's termination
when the rager drops."

**FEEL-R101-04 (NEW): Deep Watch melee contribution vs AC 18+ is near zero.**

DW-A spent two attacks per turn at ~10% hit rate each (Tactical Drilling advantage, +2 to hit vs AC 18).
Expected melee damage vs Bazgar: 2 × 0.10 × 3.5 = 0.7 expected HP per round. DW-A's crossbow (+4 vs
AC 15 Marwen = 50%) would deal 0.5 × 6.5 = 3.25 expected HP/round — 5× more effective. In formation
protocol ("hold the line, stay in melee"), DW effectively serves only as HP sponge. Against a high-AC party,
DW crossbow should be the default at 30+ ft regardless of formation.

**Recommendation (log only):** Update DW tactics: "If primary melee target has AC ≥ 18 AND a lower-AC target
(AC ≤ 15) is within crossbow range, switch to crossbow. Tactical Drilling advantage on melee doesn't
compensate for the base +2 to-hit penalty vs heavily armored targets."

**Legendary action economy:** Not tested this slice (no beholder). See prior runs for beholder LA analysis.
The key synergy (hold all 3 LA for DD post-Sabriel) is confirmed to require the party believing the wall is
the main fight — if they drop the wall fast and conserve resources, DD hits Sabriel's full slot pool. The
tank wall falling in 2 rounds sets up an ideal DD moment immediately after.
