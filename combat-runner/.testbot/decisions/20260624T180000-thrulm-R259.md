### Landmark observation: Disintegration Ray in a 2-round TPK fight

With the party dying by Round 2, Disintegration Ray fires at most ONCE in a typical run:
- It fires Round 1 (beholder wins initiative)
- If it hits AND kills (50% × 20% = 10%), that PC is permanently dead
- If it hits but doesn't kill (50% × 80% = 40%), the PC is downed; standard death saves apply
- Legendary Tentacle finishes any survivor by end of beholder's first turn (1 LA)

In the observed simulation, Disintegration Ray hit for 46 damage — didn't disintegrate Bazgar, LA Tentacle finished him anyway. **Practical upshot: in a Round-2-TPK scenario, Disintegration Ray's permanent-death rider fires only ~10% of the time. The feel problem is not "frequent disintegration" but "random permanent death is possible and unannounced."**

FEEL-003 priority reassessment: the risk of disintegration is lower than multi-round estimates suggest, because the party dies fast. In a 2-round fight, only 1 Ray fires; P(PC disintegrated) ≈ 10%. However, the consequence when it does fire is campaign-altering. A single word from Joe — "if it disintegrates, treat as 0 HP + Stunned 1 minute, no permanent death" — would eliminate the risk without changing feel.

### Legendary action economy in this scenario

Dominant play Round 1: Disintegration Ray (main) + 1 LA Tentacle (clean-up) = Bazgar down.  
No Drain Divinity needed; Sabriel downed by Round 2 multiattack.  
Void Ray and Drain Divinity both went unspent.

**FEEL-R259-01 (NEW):** Drain Divinity and Void Ray are dead-weight in a fast-kill scenario. The beholder's optimal play is: Disintegration Ray → Tentacle LA (finish lowest-HP PC) → Multiattack → down. The "legendary-action budget" of 3 is used in practice as: 1 (clean-up Tentacle) + 2 held. Drain Divinity (3-cost, 30-ft range, requires descending from 40-60 ft hover) never fires because the fight ends before the beholder needs to neutralize divine abilities. Against this party specifically, Drain Divinity would matter only if the fight lasted 3+ rounds — which it doesn't vs a level-5 party. This makes Drain Divinity dead weight in the short-fight case and degenerate in the long-fight case. Joe should consider: cap fight length at 3 rounds mechanically (e.g., beholder retreats at 50 HP), or redesign Drain Divinity for a wider range of utility.
