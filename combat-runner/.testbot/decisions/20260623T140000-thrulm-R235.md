### Key Observations

**1. (BUG-R156-01 CONFIRMED — primary failure)**

Reaction fires twice (once per damage event), both times narration-only. Two manual DM steps required:
- Resolve attack roll (+4 / advantage from Unstable Form)
- Resolve dual damage type (1d8+1 slashing + 1d8 necrotic, separate rolls)
- Apply self-damage (1d4 psychic)

The fix per the scenario notes: re-author `oath_breaking_retaliation` via `combat_action_upsert` as type `"single_attack"` with an embedded `shrine_axe` attacks spec. Self-damage can go in an `on_use_self_damage` field or narration effect text.

---

**2. (NEW — FEEL-R235-01) Death-blow reaction creates a "killing blow counter" that is mechanically invisible to the player**

When the shrine-touched fires Oath-Breaking Retaliation on its own death blow (the hit that drops it to 0), the player who lands the kill can take damage without any telegraphing. In this simulation: Sabriel's radiant smite should be a satisfying kill — but with BUG-R156-01 fixed, a 44% chance of 9 damage hits back on that same action. No prompt, no warning, no counterplay. The creature is dead by the time the reaction resolves, which can feel arbitrary.

Severity: LOW. The mechanic is valid per RAW and narratively powerful ("the shrine screams through its dying vessel"). But it needs table communication. Recommend:
- Adding "including on the death blow" to the reaction's trigger text in the .md.
- The combat runner should ideally show the Oath-Breaking prompt before the DM confirms the death, not after.

This is distinct from FEEL-R234-01 (which was about Unstable Form advantage on any reaction). This is specifically about the *killing blow* timing and player expectation management.

---

**3. (CONFIRMED — Unstable Form timing across PC turns)**

The Unstable Form advantage window (from Bazgar's hit, turn 1) carries into Sabriel's turn (turn 2) without interruption, because the shrine-touched hasn't had its own turn yet. Both reactions benefit from the advantage. This is valid per 5e timing — "until the end of its next turn" is inclusive of all reactions before that turn arrives.

Per R234 FEEL-R234-01: two manual DM steps (remember Unstable Form, apply advantage). No new flag — confirming R234 finding applies to the death-blow case too.
