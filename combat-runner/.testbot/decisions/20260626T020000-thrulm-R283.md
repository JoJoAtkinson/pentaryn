### Feel Findings

**FEEL-R283-01: "First attack" heuristic is suboptimal on mixed-threat rounds**

The current pattern (Antireality fires on the first incoming attack per round) works well when
Bazgar is the only melee attacker. But in a full 3-PC round, the initiative sequence generates:
- Marwen: 1 attack spell (Ray of Frost, Toll the Dead, etc.) — attack roll or save
- Bazgar: 2 melee attacks (+7 to hit)
- Sabriel: 1-2 divine-martial strikes with smite potential

The beholder only has 1 reaction. If Marwen leads initiative with a Guiding Bolt (+5 vs AC 17,
59% hit, 4d6 radiant avg 14 + advantage to next attacker), the beholder may be better served
burning Antireality on *that* attack (both because of the high-value advantage rider and because
Sabriel's subsequent smite would benefit). If the reaction fires automatically on the *first*
attack (whatever it is), the DM loses this decision.

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to beholder tactics section: "Antireality is a decision
point, not an automatic trigger. Consider saving it for the highest-threat single attack this
round — a Divine Smite, Guiding Bolt with advantage rider, or a concentration spell that would
change the action economy. Default to first attack only if threat ordering is unclear."

---

**FEEL-R283-02: Antireality wasted vs Reckless-style advantage sources**

Per slice notes: "if Bazgar already has disadvantage from another source, Antireality is wasted
(advantage/disadvantage do not stack — straight roll results instead)."

Symmetrically: if Bazgar has *advantage* from another source (Sharpshooter — irrelevant here;
Battlemaster Rally; prone-to-melee; an ally using the Help action; Reckless Attack if subclassed
incorrectly), Antireality only *cancels* the advantage to a straight roll rather than imposing net
disadvantage. The beholder spends its reaction to achieve exactly the same result as doing nothing
would if Bazgar had no advantage — not a gain.

At level 5, the Battlemaster's Goading Attack maneuver can give Bazgar advantage on his own turn
if used the prior round. If Bazgar Goads on round 1 and Antireality fires on round 2 attack 1:
Antireality neutralizes Goading advantage → straight 55% roll. Effective spend = 0.

**Recommendation (log only):** The runner should surface a "Antireality vs advantage" warning
when the beholder holds reaction: "Check: does this attacker currently have advantage? If yes,
Antireality cancels to straight roll rather than imposing disadvantage."

---

**FEEL-R283-03: Antireality economy versus a 3-PC party is tight**

Over one complete 3-PC turn cycle (6-9 attacks/spells in various forms), the beholder uses
Antireality once. Raw damage suppression per cycle:

- Without Antireality on all 6 attacks: ~3.3 hits × ~12 EV each = ~39.6 EV/cycle
- With Antireality on 1 attack: ~(0.9 + 1.65 = 2.55) hits × ~12 = ~30.6 EV/cycle
- Net suppression: ~9 EV per reaction use

At 110 HP beholder and ~30-40 EV expected damage per full round, Antireality extends survivability
by roughly 9/35 ≈ 0.25 rounds. Over a 5-round fight, that's about 1 extra round of survivability.

**Feel verdict:** Antireality is a meaningful but not dominant reaction. It's efficient against
high-modifier single attackers (Bazgar +7) and nearly irrelevant against saves. The once-per-round
cap feels correct — it doesn't make the beholder unkillable, just moderately tankier on one attack.
No changes recommended to the mechanic.
