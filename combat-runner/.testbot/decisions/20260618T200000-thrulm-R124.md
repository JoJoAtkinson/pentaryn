### New Phase A findings this run

**STAT-03 — Shrine-Touched Ancient Resonance DC 14 not derivable from stat line (log only)**  
DC 14 = 8 + PB(3) + ability modifier. No ability score gives a modifier of +3 (highest is DEX +2). The closest derivable DC is 13 (8+3+2). DC 14 appears manually set rather than derived. This is not an error in itself (monsters may use flat DCs), but it flags a potential intent question: was this meant to be DC 13 (DEX-derived) or DC 14 (deliberate). Neither is auto-fixable. Joe to confirm.

**CS-DRIFT-03 — Shardcaller Pack Tactics Voice absent from cheat sheet (log only)**  
The shardcaller's passive trait `Pack Tactics Voice` reads: "When an ally of the shardcaller hits a target within 30 feet of the shardcaller, the target has disadvantage on the next saving throw it makes before the end of its next turn."

This trait is not mentioned anywhere in COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md. The round-by-round checklist (step 3) only documents the `Call Weakness` bonus action (advantage on one ally's attack roll) and explicitly notes "does NOT affect saves." A DM running from cheat sheet only would entirely miss the save-debuff mechanic from Pack Tactics Voice — which is meaningful because it would stack with the beholder's DC 16 saves (Void Scream, Drain Divinity, Void Ray) when the shardcaller is in range. Recommend adding to step 3 of the checklist:

> `[CHECK] Shardcaller Pack Tactics Voice triggered this round? (If any ally hit a target within 30 ft of the shardcaller, that target has disadvantage on its next saving throw — applies to beholder's DC 16 effects.)`

### Phase B — Legendary Resistance Depletion Analysis

**FEEL-05 — Beholder's 3 Legendary Resistances go untouched in standard encounter path**

The adapted orren-action-surge concept (limited-use resource: does it deplete?) reveals: **the Compass Edge never gets an opportunity to force meaningful saving throws on the beholder**. The analysis:

1. **Marwen is the only PC with reliable save-forcing spells** (Hold Monster L5, Banishment L4 — but she has no L4-L5 slots at level 5; her best is Hypnotic Pattern L3 WIS save DC 14). Against beholder WIS +5, she needs a 9+ on the die for the beholder to pass without LR — ~60% beholder success rate. LR worth burning? Marginally (40% chance of incapacitation).

2. **But Marwen goes down before her turn Round 1 in the average initiative path.** Beholder Init avg 13.5, Marwen Init avg 11.5. On average rolls beholder acts first. Tentacle + Maw = 14+22 = 36 damage vs Marwen's 32 HP. Marwen falls before she can cast.

3. **In the best case (Marwen survives Round 1)**: She casts Hypnotic Pattern (DC 14 WIS). Beholder at +5 needs 9+ → 60% success, so 40% chance of triggering LR. If beholder fails the save and burns LR: 1 of 3 consumed. Then it needs to get hit by two more meaningful saves to exhaust LR — but Marwen has used her concentration slot, and Sabriel's saves (if any) are lower DC.

4. **Bazgar's Battlemaster maneuvers** that require saves mostly bounce: Trip Attack (prone = immune), Grappling Strike (beholder grapples *them*), Menacing Attack (frightened = immune). The only relevant maneuver is Disarming Strike, but beholders have no weapons. Battlemaster's save-forcing toolkit is nearly dead against this beholder's immunity suite.

5. **Sabriel's divine martial options**: If Paladin, has Wrathful Smite (WIS save DC 14 after hit — beholder +5, ~60% pass), Thunderous Smite (STR save — beholder -1 +PB = very beatable but damage is the point, not the push). Banishment (CHA save DC 14 — beholder CHA +1 → needs 13+ → 40% success without LR). Banishment is the most likely LR trigger: 60% chance of sending beholder away. But Sabriel needs to have Banishment prepared (Paladin L4 slot; Paladin 5 has one L3 slot and it's their highest).

**Conclusion:** In a standard encounter, the beholder's 3 LR charges function as dead rules text. The fight resolves without them. This doesn't need a balance fix (party is meant to lose), but it's a feel problem: LR is the beholder's signature "fail to fail" defense and it never enters play.

**FEEL-06 — No PC choice affects beholder survival in the avg path**  
Corollary to FEEL-05: once initiative is rolled and the beholder goes before Marwen (likely), the party has no decision point that changes the outcome. The fight is deterministic within 2 rounds. The "meant to lose" design intent is achieved, but not through tactical agency being overwhelmed — it's through complete removal of decision-relevant moments. Consider: one structural change that gives the party a "window" would improve the feel significantly. Options (for Joe to evaluate):  
(a) Give Marwen a free action on her first turn regardless of initiative (narrative conceit: she prepared something) — invasive, not recommended.  
(b) Reduce beholder HP from 110 to 85-90 so Bazgar's hits in Round 1 are more meaningful.  
(c) Give the encounter one round of "warning" before the beholder acts (lair action fires, beholder delays).  
These are design calls only; not logging as bugs.
