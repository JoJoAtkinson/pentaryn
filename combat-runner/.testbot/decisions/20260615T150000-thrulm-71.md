### Phase B — Analysis

**FEEL-B71-01 (NEW — Log only): UG prone + beholder high init → Bazgar down before any turn in R1.**

Timeline: Lair fires UG at init 20 (Bazgar prone), beholder fires at init 18 (before Bazgar's init 12). The prone condition grants the beholder melee advantage on tentacle attacks, which combined with the Maw auto-crit drops Bazgar from 49 HP → 0 HP in one action. Bazgar does not act in Round 1 at all.

This is mechanically correct (lair at 20, beholder at 18, fighter at 12 is just an initiative race). But for the player running Bazgar, R1 consists of: "the ground buckles under you, then the beholder lands on you, and you're at 0 HP before you do anything." This is the starkest "no agency" moment in the encounter.

**Not unfair, but anticlimactic:** The party is meant to lose; the fighter going down R1 is an extreme but valid outcome of the beholder's lair+initiative combo. However, a fighter who had gone BEFORE the beholder (initiative 13+) would have had a chance to move out of tentacle reach (beholder must hover within 10 ft for TL; fighter moving 30 ft creates 30 ft of separation). **Counterplay exists only if Bazgar beats the beholder's initiative** — roughly a coin flip (+0 vs +3). The probability of Bazgar avoiding R1 complete removal: ~35%.

Recommendation: note in beholder tactics that UG+high-init+prone combo can one-shot a fighter R1. This is intended but DM should know it before the encounter starts, not discover it at the table with a frustrated player.

**FEEL-B71-02 (NEW — Log only): Post-LoH Void Ray + Tentacle LA re-downs Bazgar AND grapples Sabriel in one reactive burst.**

After Sabriel's LoH revived Bazgar (5 HP), the beholder's 3 LA post-Sabriel re-downed Bazgar (Void Ray, 2 LA, 22 force) and grappled Sabriel (Tentacle, 1 LA, 14 bludg). Two consequences from 3 LA: Bazgar re-downed and Sabriel partially disabled. Net of LoH (5 HP restored) = 0 HP gain, −14 HP to Sabriel, Bazgar now at 0 HP again.

This confirms: LoH against a mobile beholder with LA budget is a losing trade. The beholder's LA reactive economy makes healing inefficient — any HP restored to one PC is immediately removed from another via LA damage. The effective HP "pool" that LoH can safely heal is zero unless all PCs who are at-risk have already acted and the beholder has 0 LA remaining. In practice, this means LoH is only safe to use AFTER the beholder's LA budget is exhausted for the round.

**This is a meaningful strategic insight for DMs:** tell the player running Sabriel explicitly: "LoH on a downed ally while the beholder has LA to spend will be reversed by a Void Ray. Save LoH for turns where the beholder's LA is depleted (end of beholder's initiative, after LA have been spent on other PCs)."

**FEEL-B71-03 (confirmed — no change): Escort derro function as Fireball bait.**

All 4 escort derro (2 DWD + 2 Thrall) died to two Fireballs (one per round). Their total damage output: DWD-A landed 1 hit for 4 damage on Sabriel; DW-B landed 1 hit for 8 on Marwen. Thralls missed all attacks. Total escort damage: 12 HP over 2 rounds.

The escort's actual function is to absorb Marwen's Fireball resources: 2 L3 slots spent eliminating the escort means Marwen has 0 L3 slots from Round 3 onward. This confirms the design intent: escort derro are not threat sources, they are Fireball sinks that deplete the wizard's most powerful AoE resource before the beholder-primary phase.

**Beholder LA economy check (per Phase B point 4):**

| Round | LA Action | Cost | Value |
|-------|-----------|------|-------|
| R1 | Void Ray (post-Sabriel) | 2 LA | 22 force → re-downed Bazgar |
| R1 | Tentacle (post-Sabriel) | 1 LA | 14 bludg + grapple on Sabriel |
| R2 | Void Ray (post-Sabriel/down) | 2 LA | 22 force → Marwen to 2 HP |
| R2 | Tentacle vs Marwen | 1 LA | **MISS** |

**Drain Divinity (the dominant R1 protocol) did NOT fire this simulation.** Reason: Sabriel used LoH (not a spell slot) → DD window closed. The beholder correctly redirected to VR+Tentacle. This is a second confirmation (after fire #70) that the DD-LoH redirect path works correctly. DD would have fired if Sabriel had used a smite spell or expended a spell slot.

**Dominant LA strategy:** Void Ray (2 LA, single-target, save-based, 120 ft range, 22 avg) is the most consistently used LA in this simulation after R1. It threatens even low-HP targets at long range and bypasses grapple/prone positioning (it's save-based, not an attack roll). **Void Ray is the de-facto dominant LA choice** in rounds where DD is unavailable (no spell slot targets or budget spent). This aligns with the "Drain Divinity vs Void Ray vs Move — which is always dominant?" question in the briefing: **DD is dominant when Sabriel is alive with spell slots; Void Ray dominates all other situations.**

Move LA was never used in this simulation (beholder stayed in position at the altar). Move is a safety valve for repositioning; in a fight where the beholder starts near the altar (Void-Feeding active), Move is dead weight unless the beholder needs to reposition for DR or VS range.
