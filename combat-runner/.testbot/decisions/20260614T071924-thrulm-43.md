### NEW FINDING — FEEL-F43-01: DD watch fires on LoH; DD cannot execute

**Problem:** The scenario simulates Sabriel using `loh` (Lay on Hands), then asserts `expect_watch drain_divinity`. The watch fires — but **Lay on Hands is not a spell slot**. DD's targeting clause: *"one creature within 30 feet that has spell slots, divine favor, or clerical powers."* LoH draws from a separate HP pool (5 × paladin level = 25 HP at level 5), not a spell slot. DD cannot consume it.

**Consequence:** When the DM sees the `drain_divinity` watch prompt after Sabriel types "loh," the mechanically correct response is:
- **Do NOT fire DD** — LoH use is not a valid DD target.
- **Redirect to Void Ray (2 LA, DC 16 DEX, 22 force, 120 ft range)** targeting the revived PC (per existing tactics: "If Sabriel uses Lay on Hands... the beholder cannot counter with DD. Prioritize Void Ray or a Tentacle LA.").
- The remaining 1 LA can hold for a Tentacle after the next PC's turn.

The current scenario `thrulm-dd-la-hold-protocol` does not document this redirect in its `prelude` or `targets`. A DM who runs this scenario without reading the beholder's tactics notes will incorrectly fire DD against a LoH target.

**What the scenario correctly tests:** The watch fires (runner integration ✓). The timing of the prompt (post-Sabriel turn, with 3 LA banked) is correct ✓.

**What the scenario incorrectly implies:** That `drain_divinity` executes after the watch fires. When Sabriel uses LoH, the correct output is Void Ray dispatch, not DD.

**Cross-reference:** FEEL-F20-02 (DD-immune LoH pool). New scenario `thrulm-dd-loh-redirect-void-ray` added to cover the corrected flow — see scenarios.yml addition this fire.

### LA-hold protocol mechanics: when it works and when it fails

**Correct execution (Sabriel uses spell slot R1):**

Typical initiative (beholder wins most ties, DEX +3 vs Sabriel DEX ~+0):
- Init 20: Lair → Unstable Ground on Bazgar (DC 16 DEX, -1 mod → ~57% fail → prone)
- Init 15 (beholder, median roll): Multiattack. If Bazgar prone: TL at advantage (+7) vs AC 18 → ~75% hit. 2×TL + Maw.
  - TL hit (avg 13 each): if both hit, grappled. Maw vs grappled → auto-crit (8d8+3 avg ~39). Total R1 damage to Bazgar: ~65 avg with both TLs hitting. Bazgar (HP 49) is down.
  - Beholder holds all 3 LA (R1-LA-hold protocol).
- Init 12 (Marwen): Fireball vs beholder. DEX save +6 vs DC 14 → 65% succeed → 35%×28 + 65%×14 ≈ 19 avg. Beholder holds 3 LA.
- Init 10 (Sabriel): If she casts Bless or Shield of Faith (L1 slot) or uses a smite (she hasn't attacked yet since Bazgar absorbed R1): DD watch fires. Beholder spends 3 LA on DD.
  - Sabriel DC 16 CHA save: Paladin 5 CHA modifier ~+3, proficiency in CHA saves → +5 total → fails on 1–10 (50% fail).
  - On fail: Sabriel loses highest slot (L3 if full, 2×3=6 THP for beholder). DD fires.
  - On success: no slot lost, 0 THP gained. Beholder burned all 3 LA for nothing.

**DD protocol achieves maximum value when Sabriel:**
1. Has full L3 slots (hasn't smited yet R1) — stripping L3 before she can smite is the whole point.
2. Uses a spell-slot-consuming action (Bless, Divine Favor, Shield of Faith, or attacks with smite intention).
3. Fails the DC 16 CHA save (50% at CHA +5 vs DC 16).

**Protocol fails (DD never executes) when Sabriel:**
1. Uses Lay on Hands (LoH pool ≠ spell slot) → redirect to Void Ray.
2. Uses Channel Divinity (Turn Undead / other CD) → beholder is an aberration, not undead; Turn Undead doesn't affect it. CD is not a spell slot; DD cannot consume it.
3. Has initiative higher than the beholder (uncommon — beholder avg init 13.5 vs Sabriel avg 10; ~20% probability) and acts before the beholder fires Multiattack. In this case: the beholder still fires DD post-Sabriel, just earlier in the round — actually this is fine, the protocol works correctly per FIX-FC47-A.

**CHA save failure rate at level 5:** Sabriel CHA +3, proficiency in CHA saves (Paladin class feature) → +5 total. DC 16 → fails on ≤10 → 50% chance. DD fires, Sabriel fails save, loses slot: 50% of attempts. Over 3 rounds where DD fires: expected ~1.5 successful strips. At L3 slots (Paladin 5 has 2× L3), DD typically depletes the smite pool in ~1–2 successful fires. This is significant attrition.

**DD range constraint (BUG-A12-06 still open):** DD requires beholder within 30 ft of Sabriel. Beholder's standard hover: 40–60 ft above altar. Sabriel enters combat ~60+ ft from altar. Distance: ~60–100 ft. DD is out of range unless beholder descends. The R1-DD protocol implicitly requires the beholder to descend to within 30 ft of Sabriel's position — this maneuver is undocumented and uses the beholder's movement (not Shrine-Drift, which is a bonus action). This remains the most functionally critical gap in the protocol (32 fires open).

### Phase B conclusion

The `thrulm-dd-la-hold-protocol` scenario correctly tests the **watch-fires timing** (hold LA → watch fires post-Sabriel). The integration logic is sound. However, the scenario uses `loh` as Sabriel's trigger action, which is the DD-immune case — the watch fires correctly but the expected mechanical outcome (DD executes) is wrong. The scenario passes its integration test while testing the *wrong semantic case*.

Two changes recommended:
1. Add a note to the scenario prelude: "Note: LoH is not a spell slot — if Sabriel uses LoH, the DM must redirect to Void Ray (2 LA) instead of firing DD."
2. Add `thrulm-dd-loh-redirect-void-ray` (new scenario this fire) to cover the corrected Void Ray dispatch post-LoH.
