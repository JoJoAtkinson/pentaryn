## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `lair-action-round`

### Setup

- **Round:** 2. Party engaged, frontliners Bazgar and Sabriel within 20 ft of altar to threaten the hovering beholder when it descends.
- **HP entering round 2:** Bazgar 42/49, Marwen 26/32, Sabriel 38/44 (absorbed R1 damage from Void Eruption + multiattack).
- **Beholder:** 110/110 (untouched); 3 legendary actions fresh; hovering 40 ft above altar.
- **Thrall Derro (3):** 22 HP each, adjacent to frontliners.

### Initiative Count 20 — Beholder Lair Action

Three choices: Unstable Ground (single-target prone), Manifest Thralls (1 THP + reaction attack to charmed derro), or Void Eruption (DC 16 DEX save, 2d10 force to all within 20 ft of shrine).

With two frontliners already in the shrine zone, **Void Eruption** is the dominant pick.

**Void Eruption vs Bazgar and Sabriel (both within 20 ft of shrine):**
- DC 16 DEX save. Fighter and divine martial at level 5 typically have DEX +0 to +2 and no DEX proficiency.
- Estimated failure rate: ~70–75%.
- Expected damage each: 0.72 × 11 = **~7.9 avg force**
- Marwen (staying back, caster positioning): not affected.

**Running total damage from lair action alone:** Bazgar ~7.9, Sabriel ~7.9, Marwen 0.

### Beholder Main Turn

Main action: Multiattack (2× Tentacle Lash + 1 Maw). Targeting Marwen (weakest; 26 HP).

**Tentacle 1 vs Marwen (AC 15):**
- +7 to hit (in-chamber Void-Feeding). Needs 8+. Hit rate: 65%.
- Expected damage: 0.65 × 13 = **8.45 avg** + grapple on hit.
- Tentacle 2 vs Marwen: same calculation. If T1 hit (grappled), T2 still targets Marwen.
- Expected T2: 0.65 × 13 = **8.45 avg**. If Marwen grappled, she can't move; no disadvantage on the attack itself.

**Maw vs grappled Marwen (AC 15; grappled = disadvantage on escape checks, NOT on the attack):**
- Maw +7 vs AC 15: 65% hit rate (same, grapple doesn't grant extra attack advantage per stat block).
- Expected damage: 0.65 × 21 = **13.65 avg** piercing.

**Expected total vs Marwen from multiattack:** 8.45 + 8.45 + 13.65 = **~30.6 avg damage**.
Marwen at 26 HP → expected to reach 0 HP (expected -4.6 HP). High probability of going down.

P(Marwen survives multiattack) — requires all three to miss or roll below average:
- Both tentacles miss (~35% each) AND/OR Maw misses (~35%): P(all miss) = 0.35³ ≈ 4%; P(enough miss to keep her up) tiny.
- Realistic assessment: Marwen reaches 0 HP on main action in ~75–80% of simulated rounds.

### Beholder Legendary Actions (3 available)

Marwen likely at 0 HP. Beholder redirects to Sabriel (divine martial, Drain Divinity target).

- Beholder already used main action; now at end of each PC's turn spends LAs.
- Most likely sequence: Void Ray (2 LA) vs Sabriel → 0.65 × 22 + 0.35 × 11 = **18.15 avg** force. Sabriel at 38 - 7.9 (Void Eruption) = 30.1 → 30.1 - 18.15 = 11.95 HP. Survives.
- Remaining 1 LA: Tentacle vs Sabriel (+7 vs AC 19, 45% hit) → 0.45 × 13 = 5.85 avg → Sabriel ~6.1 HP.

**End of beholder's turn / lair-action round:**
- Marwen: ~0 HP (down in 75-80% of outcomes)
- Sabriel: ~6 HP (threatened on any next hit)
- Bazgar: ~34 HP (absorbed Void Eruption; hasn't taken direct beholder attack yet)

### Spatial Trap Analysis (key finding)

**The problem this simulation surfaces:**

The beholder hovers 40–60 ft above the altar. For Sabriel or Bazgar to be in melee range, they must be within 40–60 ft of the beholder AND within 20 ft of the altar (which is roughly below the beholder). This means:

- Any melee combatant threatening the beholder is automatically in the Void Eruption kill zone.
- The beholder gets a free 2d10 force attack via lair action against every melee combatant every round they stay in position.
- Sabriel is a divine martial (likely Paladin) — her main tool (Drain Divinity counterplay, sanctified weapon attacks) requires MELEE range against the beholder, which means she must remain in the Void Eruption zone.
- There is no positioning that allows melee engagement without Void Eruption exposure.

**Counterplay vacuum:** The party has no action that prevents the lair action. Dispel Magic doesn't work on lair actions. Counterspell doesn't apply (it's not a spell). The only escape is moving outside 20 ft of the shrine, which removes the ability to threaten the beholder with sanctified weapons.

**This stacks with FEEL-DRAIN-R248-01** (Drain Divinity forces 30-ft descent, exposing beholder to Bazgar counter — but that's a problem for the beholder; the Void Eruption spatial trap is a problem for the PARTY). These two issues form a locked design space: the beholder is punished for descending (Bazgar counter), the party is punished for being near the shrine (Void Eruption), and the shrine is the only place where the party can exercise their anti-beholder tools.

### Feel Assessment

The lair action round confirms the encounter's "lethal by design" intent — but the MECHANISM is feel-bad rather than feel-lethal. There's a difference between "you die in an epic fight where your choices mattered" and "you die because the spatial geometry of the shrine means you cannot threaten the boss without standing in the automatic damage zone." The Void Eruption, Manifest Thralls, and Unstable Ground lair actions all have different tactical flavour but the one the beholder will always pick (Void Eruption) eliminates that variety in practice.
