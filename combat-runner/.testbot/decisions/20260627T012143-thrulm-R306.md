## Phase B — Simulation: `retreat-threshold`

> Beholder drops to 28 HP (below threshold). Per tactics: retreats toward lower shaft, uses thralls to block. Does it have a meaningful path given hover 30 ft speed and the encounter room dimensions? Does the retreat feel dramatic or anticlimactic? Check if any existing action supports the retreat mechanically (Shrine-Drift is the bonus action move; verify it works as an escape tool).

### Setup

Context: End of round 3. Beholder at 28 HP. Party state (assumed after 3 hard rounds):
- Marwen: previously KO'd round 1 (R305 analysis), now stabilized with a death save success or Sabriel's healing. ~0-8 HP.
- Bazgar: ~12 HP (26 avg LA damage/round × 2 rounds, minus AC 18 mitigation).
- Sabriel: ~28 HP. Has used 1-2 spell slots healing.

2 Thrall Derro alive (HP 22 each). 0 Derro Rager (died round 2 absorbing PC attacks).

### Retreat Mechanics

**Beholder's movement tools at 28 HP:**

| Tool | Type | Range | Notes |
|------|------|--------|-------|
| Fly speed | Regular move | 30 ft | Hover; provokes OA if leaving PC reach |
| Shrine-Drift | Bonus Action | 30 ft | Can pass through walls/objects; no OA immunity; 5 (1d10) force self-dmg if ends inside object |
| Move LA (1 cost) | Legendary Action | 30 ft | At end of PC turns only; beholder has 3 LA/round |

**Total per turn: 30 ft (move) + 30 ft (Shrine-Drift) = 60 ft.** Lower shaft entrance is ~60 ft from altar center (per overview). The beholder reaches the shaft in a single retreat turn.

### Retreat Turn Simulation

**Round 4, Beholder's turn (init 15, Void-Feeding still active):**

1. Beholder is at 28 HP — tactics trigger "retreat to lower shaft, use thralls to block."
2. **Action:** Beholder uses `Compel Thrall` (BA would be Shrine-Drift, so action must be something else — wait, no: Shrine-Drift IS the BA). Rethink:
   - BA = Shrine-Drift (30 ft through wall, directly toward shaft entrance)
   - Action = Compel Thrall OR an attack (per "crush/disintegrate while fleeing" at <20 HP — but beholder is 28 HP, not sub-20, so tactics say "retreat only")
   - Regular move = 30 ft toward shaft (or was consumed by Shrine-Drift direction?)

**Timing note:** Shrine-Drift and regular movement are both "movement" in different senses:
- Regular fly speed: the beholder can fly 30 ft as part of its regular movement action
- Shrine-Drift (BA): explicitly "Move up to 30 ft" — this is separate from the beholder's regular movement

Result: The beholder can use BOTH in one turn (regular move 30 ft toward shaft + BA Shrine-Drift 30 ft through the wall separating the chamber from the descending shaft) = **60 ft total, reaches the shaft entrance in 1 turn.**

**DB status of Shrine-Drift:** `shrine_drift` is a `utility` action with an `effect` text. The runner surfaces a reminder prompt but does not auto-move the beholder token. DM handles movement manually. ✓ Adequate.

**OA risk on retreat:** Beholder is hovering 40-60 ft up at the altar. If it descends to fly toward the shaft at floor/mid-level, it may pass within 5 ft of PCs. Regular fly movement DOES provoke OA when leaving PC reach. Shrine-Drift doesn't have OA immunity in its text (only the "treat creatures as difficult terrain" clause). Bazgar at 12 HP using OA: +8 to hit vs AC 17, 65% hit, avg 10 damage (1d8+4 longsword or 1d10+4 greatweapon) — would drop beholder from 28 HP to ~18 HP if it hits. This forces the beholder to use Shrine-Drift at high altitude to avoid PC opportunity attacks.

**Recommended retreat path:** Beholder flies 30 ft upward/across at max height (above PC reach), then uses Shrine-Drift to phase through the chamber ceiling/wall at an angle toward the shaft. PCs cannot reach it for OA at that height. Beholder exits. **Dramatic but frustrating for party.**

---

### Feel Assessment

**Is the retreat mechanically sound?** Yes. Shrine-Drift through walls makes the beholder effectively unreachable for its retreat turn. The lower shaft is reachable in exactly 1 turn with combined movement.

**Does it feel dramatic or anticlimactic?**

**Dramatic elements:**
- Sphere of void-stone phases through solid rock — visible and eerie
- Thralls stand confused as domination wavers (they still attack but lose beholder direction)
- The lower shaft makes ominous noise as the beholder descends — "something down there scares even this creature"

**Anticlimactic elements:**
- At 28 HP with Legendary Resistance still available (if unused), the beholder could survive 2-4 more rounds. The party may feel robbed of a kill they were close to.
- The trigger is hard-coded at 30 HP, unconditional. No check for whether party is winning or losing overall.
- Party at this point: Marwen near 0, Bazgar at ~12 HP, Sabriel at ~28 HP. The party is actually nearly TPK'd. A beholder that fights to ~15 HP here would MORE likely complete the TPK, which is more thematically consistent ("the party is meant to lose this encounter").

### FEEL-R306-01: Retreat Threshold — Unconditional at 30 HP Creates Anti-Climax

**Finding:** The tactics section triggers retreat unconditionally at <30 HP. At this HP the beholder has ~27% health remaining and the party is simultaneously in poor shape (R305 model shows party avg 30-35% health remaining round 3). The beholder could win by staying. A hard 30-HP retreat lets the party feel "we almost had it!" when the encounter was actually never close.

**Recommendation:** Change tactics text to: "If reduced below 30 HP AND Legendary Resistance is exhausted OR any Legendary Resistance remains but the party appears capable of dealing 30+ damage in one round: retreat. Otherwise fight to 20 HP if the party is more than half down." This creates a conditional retreat that feels like an intelligent predator choosing to live over pride.

**Not a stat block change** — tactics text is flavor/guidance. Log only.

### FEEL-R306-02: Thrall Blocking Is a ~1-Round Speedbump

**Finding:** Thrall Derro (22 HP, AC 14) as pursuit blockers against a level-5 Battlemaster:
- Bazgar 2 attacks + Action Surge = 4 attacks, each +8 vs AC 14 (70% hit), avg 10 damage each. Expected: 4 × 70% × 10 = **28 expected damage** — clears BOTH thralls with Action Surge in a single action.
- Even without Action Surge: 2 attacks = ~14 expected = kills 1 thrall, bloodies the other.

At level 5, 2 thralls are not a meaningful pursuit block. The beholder's retreat will succeed regardless of whether thralls are present. The tactical instruction "uses thralls to block pursuit" is effectively flavor.

**Recommendation for future fires:** If Joe wants pursuit blocking to be meaningful, the Shrine-Touched (45 HP, AC 16, reaction attack) is a much better blocker than thralls. Or a Derro Rager (52 HP, AC 16) using Taunt while the beholder retreats. Log for Joe.

### Shrine-Drift as Escape Tool — Verdict

✓ **Works exactly as designed.** 30 ft through walls, available as BA on the retreat turn. Combined with regular 30 ft fly move, equals precisely the 60 ft needed. One minor caveat: the "5 (1d10) force if ends inside object" clause creates a 50/50 risk if the beholder Shrine-Drifts through a wall of unknown thickness. In practice the DM should declare a clean exit. The DB entry surfaces the self-damage reminder as a utility prompt — correct behavior.

### Legendary Actions During Retreat

On retreat turn, the beholder has 3 LA remaining (refreshed at start of its turn). It can spend them at end of PC turns:
- After Sabriel's turn: Move LA (1 cost) — advance 30 ft down the shaft, now 90 ft from party.
- After Bazgar's turn: Void Ray LA (2 cost) — one final hit on whoever is closest (DC 16 DEX, avg 23 force). Profitable exit shot.

**Result:** The retreat is economically efficient. The beholder deals ~23 avg parting damage via Void Ray while already repositioned to safety. At <20 HP it escalates per tactics: "attempts to crush/disintegrate the party while fleeing" — Disintegration Ray (if recharged 5+) on Bazgar (bloodied ~12 HP) as a parting shot.
