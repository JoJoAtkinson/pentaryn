### Phase B — Manual Simulation: Shrine-Drift Retreat Under Fire

**Encounter composition:** Beholder + 1 Rager + 1 Thrall (Medium variant, late-combat state)

**Late-encounter state (Round 3 start):**

| Init | Actor | HP | Notes |
|------|-------|----|-------|
| 19 | Beholder | 28 | Below 30 HP retreat threshold. 2 LA remaining (1 spent R2). DR recharged (33% chance/round → assume yes). |
| 15 | Marwen | 8 | Grazed by Rager R2. Low HP, at range (40 ft back). |
| 12 | Sabriel | 30 | In melee with Rager, 5 ft from beholder position. |
| 9 | Bazgar | 22 | Grappled by beholder R2 (TL hit). In melee, 5 ft from beholder. |
| 8 | Rager | 14 | Engaged with Sabriel. |
| 6 | Thrall | 18 | Near beholder, charmed. |

**Round 3 — Beholder turn (init 19):**

Retreat triggered (HP 28 < 30). Options:

**Option A: Disengage (action) + Shrine-Drift BA**
- No attack. Safe 30-ft move toward lower shaft. Bazgar and Sabriel cannot OA.
- Beholder exits at 28 HP, 30 ft from melee.
- LA 2 remaining → Void Ray on Marwen (8 HP, DC 16 DEX, 22 avg force). Marwen DEX +2 → needs 14+ → 35% save → ~65% fail → avg 22. Expected: Marwen dies. 1 LA remaining.
- Trade-off: Rager + Thrall block pursuit for 1 round (Thrall: 18 HP, AC 14, dies to Bazgar in 1 hit; Rager: 14 HP, AC 16, survives 1 round vs Sabriel). Effective delay: ≤1 round.

**Option B: Multiattack (action) + Shrine-Drift BA (no Disengage)**
- TL1 +7 vs Bazgar AC 18 → needs 11+ = 55% → avg 13 dmg. Bazgar: 22 → 9 HP. Grappled.
- TL2 +7 vs Bazgar AC 18 → 55% → avg 13. Bazgar: 9 → 0 HP. DOWN. **Grapple on a downed target: grapple persists but Bazgar cannot take reactions (unconscious).**
- Maw +6 vs grappled Bazgar → auto-crit. Bazgar is at 0 HP (unconscious). Per `beholder-thrulm.md` DM note FIX-EV7-35-A: *"redirect Maw to the next available standing target in reach"* if grappled target is already at 0 HP. Sabriel is within 5 ft. **Maw fires at Sabriel instead (no auto-crit).** +6 vs AC 19 → needs 13+ = 40% → avg 21 pierce. Sabriel: 30 → 9 HP (if hit).
- Shrine-Drift BA: beholder moves 30 ft toward lower shaft. Bazgar at 0 HP (unconscious) → **cannot take OA**. Sabriel is conscious → **OA fires**. +6 vs AC 17 → 55% hit → 1d8+3 avg 7.5. Beholder: 28 → 20 HP.
- **Net:** Bazgar down, Sabriel at 9 HP, beholder at 20 HP, 30 ft away. Marwen still at range (8 HP). LA 1 remaining → Tentacle on Sabriel (9 HP, within 120 ft → Tentacle LA requires within 10 ft reach... actually Tentacle LA uses Tentacle Lash: reach 10 ft. At 30 ft distance, the beholder cannot reach Sabriel with Tentacle LA. Corrected: Void Ray (2 LA, 120 ft range, DC 16 DEX 22 avg) is the only LA with reach at 30+ ft. But only 1 LA remains (Tentacle costs 1, Void Ray costs 2). With 1 LA left: Tentacle is 10 ft reach (too far). **1 LA is effectively dead budget at 30 ft range.**

**Key finding from comparison:**

Option B nova-bursts Bazgar (removes OA threat), but the Maw redirect to Sabriel is suboptimal: a non-crit Maw against AC 19 at only 40% hit rate. Option A (Disengage) gives up the attack but guarantees safe retreat at 28 HP and uses the 2 LA on Void Ray to kill Marwen cleanly.

**Verdict for the table:** **Option A is mechanically superior** when 2 LA remain and Marwen is at low HP. Option B is tempting because it "does damage while retreating" but the Maw redirect loses the auto-crit (the main value proposition), the OA from Sabriel eats 7-8 HP, and the 1 leftover LA after Shrine-Drift is stranded at 30 ft. The tactics section should state this priority explicitly.

### Phase B — Simulation Findings

**FEEL-R87-01 (NEW — LOG ONLY): Retreat tactics section missing action-economy priority guidance.**

The `beholder-thrulm.md` Retreat section says: *"the beholder retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit."*

This is incomplete. It does not document:

1. **Disengage vs Shrine-Drift trade-off.** At <30 HP with 2 LA, the beholder should **Disengage (action) + Shrine-Drift (BA) + Void Ray (2 LA)** rather than Multiattack + Shrine-Drift. Multiattack loses auto-crit (Maw must redirect off the downed grappled target) and exposes the beholder to a Sabriel OA. Disengage + Void Ray kills Marwen and removes a resource threat cleanly.

2. **Thrall blocking is largely illusory at L5.** A Thrall (22 HP, +1 to hit, AC 14) delays a L5 Battlemaster (Extra Attack, ~20+ damage per round) for less than 1 full round. The Rager (14 HP at this point) fares slightly better vs Sabriel but still dies R4 without the beholder's support. The tactics note should say: "thralls buy at most 1 round; primary delay comes from the chase distance created by Shrine-Drift."

3. **OA exposure is documented in the scenario prelude** (fire #7 addition) but NOT in the tactics section of `beholder-thrulm.md`. The DM needs to know: if Shrine-Drift without Disengage, conscious adjacent PCs get OAs. At <30 HP, a 7-8 HP OA from Sabriel can be the kill shot.

**Recommendation:** Add a short DM note after the Retreat bullet: *"Retreat sequence: Disengage (action) + Shrine-Drift BA + Void Ray LA on lowest-HP standing PC. Do NOT Shrine-Drift through Disengage's AoO window unless Bazgar is already down — at <30 HP, a single Sabriel OA with smite can end the fight before the retreat lands."*

**FEEL-R87-02 (NEW — LOG ONLY): Shrine-Drift "pass through walls" via self-damage clause creates undocumented invulnerability window.**

Shrine-Drift: *"It can move through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain; it takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside a creature or object."*

The damage triggers on **end of turn** (the full combat round slot), not end of the bonus action. This means:
- **Within the beholder's own turn:** Shrine-Drift (BA) into a stone wall costs 2:1 movement (difficult terrain). The beholder then takes any remaining normal actions (it can still use its fly speed). As long as it exits the wall before its turn ends, it takes **zero damage**.
- **Practical consequence:** The beholder can Shrine-Drift through a 10-ft-thick wall (costs 20 of 30 SD movement ft), emerge on the other side, and have 10 ft of Shrine-Drift remaining + fly 30 ft of normal movement — all without damage. The 1d10 penalty only fires if it ENDS THE TURN inside the wall.

At <30 HP with the lower shaft 60 ft away through stone walls, this is the primary escape vector. The tactics section says nothing about wall-phasing as an escape route.

**Impact:** Without documenting this, a DM will Shrine-Drift only in open space — a suboptimal retreat that keeps the beholder on the dungeon floor and reachable. The correct retreat is: Shrine-Drift through the shrine's north wall (5–10 ft thick → 10–20 ft of SD movement), fly 30 ft down the adjacent corridor toward the lower shaft. The party cannot follow without Misty Step or Gaseous Form.

**Recommendation:** Add tactics note: *"Shrine-Drift passes through stone walls at 2:1 movement cost. The beholder can escape through the north chamber wall if Shrine-Drift has ≥20 ft remaining after crossing (10-ft-thick wall). No damage as long as it exits the wall on its own turn. Against a L5 party with no Misty Step, wall-phasing retreat can end the encounter without giving PCs a final round."*

`beholder-thrulm.md` bonus actions: Shrine-Drift (BA), Compel Thrall (1/Turn, BA).

Clay-Shaping is a 1-minute concentration ritual (not a BA). ✓

Compel Thrall and Shrine-Drift both are BAs — only one fires per turn (5e RAW). The FEEL-F50-01 scenario (`thrulm-beholder-ct-drift-ba-conflict`, fire #50) documents this in the scenario YAML but not in the `beholder-thrulm.md` bonus actions section or in the cheat sheet. The cheat sheet "Round-by-Round Checklist" lists Compel Thrall and Shrine-Drift independently without noting the BA conflict.

This is primarily a table-clarity issue: a DM reading the cheat sheet could try to fire both on the same turn. The scenario runner resolves them independently (no gate), so the check is DM-enforced.

**Risk:** Low. Compel Thrall rarely fires (superseded by Shrine-Drift in most contexts per FEEL-F31-01). But if a thrall is adjacent to Marwen and the beholder wants to reposition AND force a move, the DM might accidentally use both.

**Recommendation:** Add BA-conflict footnote to `beholder-thrulm.md` Bonus Actions header: *"(Both Shrine-Drift and Compel Thrall are bonus actions — only one fires per turn.)"*
