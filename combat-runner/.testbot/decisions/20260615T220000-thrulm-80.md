### Phase B — Analysis

#### FEEL-F80-01 (NEW): Thrall initiative vulnerability — BUG-A31-08 impact window is ~55%

With DEX +1 (init +1), thrall-derro acts near the end of initiative order. In this simulation:
- Thralls init 7 and 6 — AFTER Marwen (12), Sabriel (12), Bazgar (8)
- Marwen had her turn at init 12 and eliminated both thralls with Fireball before either acted

The thrall's turn slot (typically init 5–9) means Marwen (init 12+) will almost always act first. BUG-A31-08 (wrong weapon stats on `attack` verb) only surfaces during an actual thrall attack. The probability that a thrall beats Marwen's initiative is:
- Thrall must roll 11+ on d20 (total > 12 with +1 mod) = probability ~50%
- In corridor/close-quarters where Marwen can't use Fireball safely, thralls survive to act
- In this open-chamber scenario: thralls reliably die before acting (~70% of runs with Fireball available)

**Practical impact:** BUG-A31-08 affects roughly half of Thrulm encounters. In encounters where Marwen leads with Fireball, the bug is invisible. In encounters where Marwen opens with single-target or concentration spells (conserving AOE), thralls survive to act and the bug surfaces.

The bug also surfaces explicitly if Fireball is already spent from a prior encounter or if thralls appear in Encounters 4–5 after Marwen's L3 slot is depleted.

**Recommendation:** BUG-A31-08 is not merely cosmetic. Priority remains Medium. Fix via MCP (`hand_axe_weak` row added to thrall-derro; `multiattack` row updated to point to hand-axe stats).

#### FEEL-F80-02 (NEW): FEEL-F72-02 tactical impact confirmed via simulation

R2 simulation: beholder fired multiattack at disadvantage against prone Sabriel (10 ft range) and **missed all 3 attacks** (TL×2 + Maw all missed). This is the most dramatic demonstration yet of FEEL-F72-02's table impact.

If beholder had used 5 ft of fly movement to close to 5 ft before R2 multiattack:
- TL-1 (advantage, +7) vs AC 19: 1−(11/20)²= ~70% hit rate
- TL-2 (advantage, +7) vs AC 19: same ~70%
- Maw (normal, +7) vs AC 19: ~55% hit rate

At 10 ft disadvantage: each attack ~25% hit rate. The positioning difference (5 ft of fly movement) changes expected damage vs Sabriel from ~4 avg to ~27 avg per turn — a 6× swing.

**Optimal beholder play vs a prone PC at 10 ft:** Use 5 ft of fly speed to close to 5 ft (cost: 10 ft of fly movement with grapple drag at half speed, or 5 ft if ungrappled). This converts TL/Maw to advantage without using Shrine-Drift (which would release grapple per FIX-FC48-B). The tactics section has no mention of this closing-move requirement.

**Requesting Joe decision (same as FEEL-F72-02):**
- Option A (enforce RAW): Add tactics note — "Before multiattacking a prone PC, fly 5 ft to close to within 5 ft; this converts TL/Maw to advantage (PHB p.292). Use fly movement, not Shrine-Drift, to preserve any active grapple."
- Option B (house-rule): TL advantage applies at 10 ft range regardless. Remove the PHB p.292 caveat from documentation. Update lair-prone-melee-synergy scenario to note house-rule applies.

#### Thrall `multiattack` naming issue (log-only)

The `thrall-derro.multiattack` DB action has `type: single_attack` (one attack, not two). Naming an action `multiattack` when it produces a single attack is confusing for the DM — seeing the action name in the runner output may suggest two attacks are expected. Recommend renaming the DB action to `single_attack` or `attack` (requires MCP; must update verb list simultaneously). Log only — not in auto-fix scope.
