### FEEL-R88-01 (NEW — LOG ONLY): R1-LA-hold is strictly suboptimal when Sabriel uses LoH on R1

**Summary:** The R1-LA-hold protocol (FIX-FC40-A) says hold all 3 LAs until post-Sabriel to spend 3 on DD. When Sabriel uses LoH on her R1 turn (invalidating DD), the beholder burned 1–2 LA opportunities against Marwen for nothing, then still only spends 2 LA (Void Ray, not DD).

**Math:**

Option A (R1-LA-hold, play as written):
- Post-Marwen: hold → 0 LA spent
- Post-Sabriel (LoH trigger): DD watch fires → redirect to Void Ray (2 LA) → 1 LA remaining
- Net: 2 LA effective spend; Marwen untouched by LA

Option B (adaptive hold):
- Post-Marwen: 1 LA Tentacle → +7 vs Marwen AC 15 = 65% hit → avg 13 damage. Marwen: 32 − 13 = 19 HP. Grappled. 2 LA remaining.
- Post-Sabriel (LoH trigger): DD watch fires → redirect to Void Ray (2 LA) → Bazgar or Marwen targeted. 0 LA remaining.
- Net: 3 LA effective spend; Marwen pressured R1.

Option B is strictly superior when Sabriel uses LoH. The R1-LA-hold gains nothing over Option B unless Sabriel uses a spell (enabling DD to strip a smite slot). The protocol was designed for the spell case; it inadvertently penalizes the LoH case.

**Root cause:** The DD tactical note (FIX-FC40-A) optimizes for Sabriel burning smite slots on her R1 attack turn. When Sabriel instead uses a non-spell (LoH, Second Wind equivalent), the 3-LA reservation has no payoff. The LoH override note (beholder-thrulm.md) correctly says "redirect to Void Ray" but doesn't advise the DM that the hold-all-3 protocol was premature if Sabriel wasn't going to use a spell.

**Recommendation (LOG ONLY):** Add a conditional note to FIX-FC40-A: *"If Sabriel uses LoH on her R1 turn (not a spell slot), the DD window is forfeit — redirect remaining LAs to Void Ray (2 LA) or Tentacle (1 LA). In retrospect, spending 1 Tentacle LA after Marwen was not incorrect; the hold-all-3 only pays off when Sabriel has a slot to lose. After seeing Bazgar downed, Sabriel may prioritize LoH over attack — consider splitting: hold 2 LA (Void Ray budget) instead of all 3."*

### FEEL-R88-02 (NEW — LOG ONLY): Standard-movement descent path is valid for DD range but not documented anywhere

**Observation:** FIX-FC40-A, BUG-A12-06, and FEEL-F78-01/F80-01 all note the hover-range problem but frame the solution as "Shrine-Drift BA" (which releases the grapple per FIX-FC48-B). In this simulation, standard fly movement (30 ft) descends from ~40-ft altitude to within DD range (30 ft of Sabriel) on the beholder's own R1 turn **without using Shrine-Drift BA, without releasing the grapple, and without spending any extra action**.

At 40-ft hover altitude: 10 ft of standard movement descends the beholder to 30 ft altitude (within DD 30-ft range of Sabriel standing on the floor at 0 ft). This leaves 20 ft of horizontal fly movement for positioning.

At 60-ft hover altitude: full 30-ft standard movement barely reaches 30-ft range (altitude 30 ft). No horizontal movement left.

The three existing fix notes (BUG-A12-06, FEEL-F78-01, FEEL-F80-01) were written as if Shrine-Drift were required for descent. **They are incorrect in the most common hover-altitude case (40 ft).** Standard movement is sufficient and does not carry the grapple-release cost. This doesn't eliminate the bug (descent is still undocumented), but the framing of the fix should change: the note should say "descend via standard movement (not Shrine-Drift) to preserve grapple and bonus action" rather than treating descent as a Shrine-Drift BA cost.

At 60-ft altitude, this fails (standard movement runs out exactly at 30-ft altitude; no horizontal reach) — Shrine-Drift or multi-turn descent is needed.

**Recommendation:** Update BUG-A12-06 and FEEL-F80-01 notes to distinguish the two altitude cases:
- **≤40 ft hover:** standard movement descent is sufficient (10–40 ft of descent, preserves grapple and BA). No Shrine-Drift needed.
- **>40 ft hover:** standard movement cannot reach DD range in one turn; requires Shrine-Drift (releasing grapple) or deferring DD to R2 after additional descent.

This changes the practical impact: in the default 40-ft hover case (most likely), DD is available R1 via standard descent. The 4-LA-equivalent cost framing in FEEL-F80-01 is accurate only for the ≥50-ft hover edge case.
