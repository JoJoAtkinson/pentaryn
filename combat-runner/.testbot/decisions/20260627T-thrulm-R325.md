## Phase B — Generative Playtest: lair-action-round

**Rotation index 5 of 8. Run counter: 21.**

### Setup

Context per rotation.yml:
- 2 thrall derro present (HP 22, AC 14 each); both within 60 ft of beholder
- Bazgar (Fighter 5, AC 18, DEX +0 assumed) is within 20 ft of the shrine
- Beholder has 3 lair actions to choose from at initiative count 20

Assumed party position (round 2, standard push toward altar):
- Bazgar: 15 ft from shrine (melee engagement with rager or thralls)
- Marwen: 25 ft from shrine (hanging back, at range)
- Sabriel: 20 ft from shrine (advancing on beholder)
- Thrall derro: 10 ft from shrine (flanking near altar)

### Decision Analysis: Three Lair Action Options

#### Option 1 — Unstable Ground (Bazgar, DC 16 DEX)

- Bazgar DEX: Orc Fighter/Battlemaster, likely DEX +0. Save modifier +0.
- P(fail) = 15/20 = **75%**
- On fail: Bazgar is **prone**.
  - Half movement to stand (15 ft of 30 ft speed consumed)
  - Melee attacks against Bazgar from within 5 ft: **advantage**
  - Bazgar's ranged attacks (if any): disadvantage

**Tactical synergy with thrall derro (NEW — FEEL-R325-01):**
Both thrall derro are likely within 5 ft of Bazgar. Normally, thrall Hand Axe at +1 to hit vs AC 18:
- P(hit) = (20−18+1+1)/20 = 4/20 = **20%**

With advantage on a prone target (within 5 ft):
- P(hit) = 1 − (16/20)² = 1 − 0.64 = **36%**

Expected damage per thrall: base 0.20 × 2.5 = 0.5 dmg/turn → with advantage: 0.36 × 2.5 = **0.9 dmg/turn**. Not dramatic, but nearly double the base rate. At HP 49, Bazgar can absorb this easily, but it's a real improvement. The synergy is: Unstable Ground feeds thrall advantage.

**Also:** Any creature attempting to flee Bazgar while he is prone does NOT provoke an opportunity attack from him (prone creatures can't make opportunity attacks). However, Bazgar's opportunity attacks remain available if he stands.

**Beholder direct benefit:** Tentacle Lash at altitude (40+ ft) would have *disadvantage* against a prone target (not within 5 ft). Net: Unstable Ground primarily benefits allied derro, not the beholder directly.

**Value: Medium.** 75% chance to disrupt Bazgar's round AND grant derro advantage. Safe to use — no friendly-fire risk.

#### Option 2 — Manifest Thralls (2 thrall derro)

- Each gains CHA modifier (CHA +1) = **1 THP**
- Each can immediately use reaction to move OR make one weapon attack

**Reaction attack analysis:**
- Hand Axe +1 vs Bazgar AC 18: 20% hit (2.5 avg damage) → expected 0.5 damage each
- Hand Axe +1 vs Marwen AC 15: 35% hit (2.5 avg damage) → expected 0.875 each
- Two reaction attacks vs Marwen: ~1.75 expected damage total

**1 THP:** Against a party dealing 8–28 damage per attack, 1 THP is negligible — it absorbs at most the weakest possible hit.

**Value: Very Low.** Confirmed weakest option. 1.75 expected damage total is worse than any other available lair action. Manifest Thralls (FEEL-OPEN-03) confirmed again.

**The move reaction option:** A thrall can move 30 ft as a reaction. This could pull them out of Void Eruption radius (within 20 ft of shrine) on the same init-20 round before Void Eruption fires. However: lair actions resolve simultaneously on init 20 — the beholder cannot choose Manifest Thralls AND Void Eruption in the same init-count firing. One lair action per turn. So this "evacuate then erupt" sequencing requires two separate rounds' init-20 slots. Impractical as a designed combo.

#### Option 3 — Void Eruption (DC 16 DEX, 2d10 force, all within 20 ft of shrine)

At assumed positions (round 2):
- Bazgar: 15 ft from shrine → within 20 ft ✓
- Marwen: 25 ft from shrine → NOT within 20 ft ✗
- Sabriel: 20 ft from shrine → within 20 ft (edge case — exactly at boundary)
- Thrall 1: 10 ft from shrine → **within 20 ft ✓ (FRIENDLY-FIRE)**
- Thrall 2: 10 ft from shrine → **within 20 ft ✓ (FRIENDLY-FIRE)**

**Damage simulation:**

| Target | DEX save mod | P(fail) | Avg on fail | Avg on save | Expected |
|--------|-------------|---------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Bazgar | +0 | 75% | 11 | 5.5 | 9.6 |
| Sabriel | +1 | 70% | 11 | 5.5 | 9.4 |
| Thrall 1 | +1 | 70% | 11 | 5.5 | 9.4 |
| Thrall 2 | +1 | 70% | 11 | 5.5 | 9.4 |

**If Void Eruption hits Bazgar + Sabriel:** 9.6 + 9.4 = **19 expected force damage to party.**
**Friendly-fire cost:** 9.4 + 9.4 = **18.8 expected damage to beholder's own thralls** (HP 22 each — each has 42% chance of being dropped to 0 HP by a single eruption: P(2d10 ≥ 22) ≈ P(mean=11, need ≥22) ≈ very low... actually 11 avg damage to 22 HP thralls won't kill them in one hit. Max 2d10 = 20. They can't be one-shot by Void Eruption (max 20 < HP 22). Each thrall takes avg 9.4 damage → brought to ~12.6 HP.

The friendly-fire brings both thralls to roughly 12-13 HP. They survive the eruption but are significantly weakened.

**Value: High if both Bazgar and Sabriel are within 20 ft.** But the friendly-fire is non-trivial — the beholder is expending its lair action to deal ~19 party damage while also softening its own thralls to ~13 HP (where one hit from a party member kills them). This trade is usually worth it (19 > party-damage benefit of keeping thralls at full HP), but the DM must consciously make this call.

### NEW FINDING — FEEL-R325-01: Void Eruption Friendly-Fire Not Documented

The lair action text reads: "Each creature within 20 feet of the shrine must make a DC 16 Dexterity saving throw." **"Each creature" includes the beholder's own thrall derro.** Neither the stat block, the `COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md`, nor the encounter overview mentions this.

At table: a DM optimizing Void Eruption will assume it hits only the party. The first time a thrall is hit by the beholder's own lair action, it creates confusion ("wait, do the derro also save?") and a minor rule dispute. Worse, if the DM misses this and only applies it to PCs, the encounter runs slightly easier than designed (thralls don't take attrition from lair actions).

**The friendly-fire also creates real decision texture:** Unstable Ground or Manifest Thralls are the no-friendly-fire options. Void Eruption is high damage but self-attriting. This is actually good encounter design — but it needs to be surfaced explicitly for the DM.

### NEW FINDING — FEEL-R325-02: Unstable Ground / Thrall Synergy Undocumented

When Bazgar is prone and thrall derro are in melee with him (within 5 ft), their hit probability jumps from 20% to 36%. The cheat sheet's lair action blurb ("pick most tactically relevant") gives no hint that Unstable Ground is specifically useful when thralls are engaged in melee. This synergy is easy to miss.

### Optimal vs. Non-Optimal Decision

**Optimal (this round configuration):**
- If thralls are within 20 ft of shrine AND Sabriel is nearby → **Unstable Ground** (avoids weakening own thralls while still disrupting Bazgar)
- If thralls are outside 20 ft of shrine AND 2+ PCs are within range → **Void Eruption** (~19 expected party damage)

**Non-optimal:** Manifest Thralls (confirmed weakest every fire it appears in the rotation)

**Most interesting at table:** Unstable Ground. It creates a player decision (does Bazgar spend movement to stand? Does Battlemaster use Precision Attack to partially compensate for disadvantage? Does the party try to pull him back out of prone range?). Void Eruption is faster and more dramatic but is a calculation, not a choice.

**Lair action suite assessment:**
Contrary to prior log entries (FEEL-OPEN-03: "redesign Manifest Thralls entirely"), the lair action suite has **more texture than previously credited** when the friendly-fire dynamic is acknowledged. The real problem is not the suite itself but that the DM has no guidance about:
1. Void Eruption hits own thralls
2. Unstable Ground synergizes with thrall melee attacks

With these two notes added to the COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md or `_overview.md`, the lair action decision tree becomes genuinely interesting rather than "always pick Void Eruption." Manifest Thralls remains weak regardless.
