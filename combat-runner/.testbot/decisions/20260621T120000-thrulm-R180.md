### Finding 1: Altitude-DD Incompatibility

**The beholder's tentacle multiattack requires reach 10 ft. From any altitude above 10 ft, tentacle attacks cannot reach ground-level PCs.** The beholder must descend to ≤10 ft altitude to use its melee action at all.

Drain Divinity requires being within 30 ft of target. At ground-level party, this means beholder altitude ≤30 ft (e.g., 25 ft for safety).

These constraints create three incompatible positions:
- **50+ ft altitude:** Safe from melee, Void-Feeding active, but NO offensive action available (tentacle out of range, DR/VS not recharged). Only Void Ray LA (2 of 3 LA) is useful. Drain Divinity out of range. 1 "dead" action per turn.
- **25–30 ft altitude:** DD in range. Still out of tentacle reach. Still no multiattack. Full action is wasted. Safe from melee. Burns 3 LA for DD.
- **≤10 ft altitude:** Multiattack works (tentacle+maw). DD in range (within 30 ft). Fully exposed to melee from Bazgar and Sabriel.

**The beholder has no position where it can both multiattack AND use DD in the same round without exposing itself to melee.** 

Rounds where DD fires = rounds where multiattack is wasted (action lost, beholder hovering at 25 ft doing nothing with its action). The tactical note in the cheat sheet says "Hover above altar (40–60 ft up)" but DD requires descent to 30 ft or below. These are contradictory unless the DM makes a deliberate tactical decision to "dive" for a DD round.

### Key findings

#### FEEL-R180-01 (NEW, HIGH): Drain Divinity is structurally incompatible with altitude positioning

The beholder has no position where it can simultaneously:
1. Use its multiattack (requires ≤10 ft from target), AND
2. Be in Drain Divinity range (requires ≤30 ft from target).

At 25 ft altitude (the optimal DD range): the beholder's **action is completely wasted** (no tentacle reach, no recharged special ability). It burns 3 LA (all legendary actions) on DD, then has nothing to do with its actual action turn. This makes DD extremely expensive in terms of action economy: the round where DD fires, the beholder contributes ONLY the legendary action. Its entire action + move effectively goes toward hovering.

**DM implication:** Intentional use of DD requires committing to a "DD round" where the beholder sacrifices its action. This is a design call — is that intended? The stat block doesn't flag it.

**Recommended log:** Add to beholder Tactics section: "*Drain Divinity round: beholder must be at ≤30 ft altitude (2 ft below tentacle-attack altitude). This entire round produces no multiattack output; DD is the sole offensive action economy for that turn. DD is best used early (before Sabriel casts high-value spells) or after VS has scattered the party.*"

---

#### FEEL-R180-02 (NEW, MEDIUM): Drain Divinity cannot be Counterspelled — cheat sheet gap

Drain Divinity has no spell tag, no casting time, no components. It is a legendary ability, not a spell. **Counterspell cannot interrupt it.** 

Marwen (the wizard) has Counterspell as her primary "stop the scary thing" reaction. She can Counterspell Disintegration Ray (labeled "Ranged Spell Attack" → likely qualifies as a spell). She **cannot** Counterspell Drain Divinity.

This distinction is tactically significant: players protecting Sabriel's spell slots may incorrectly assume Counterspell stops DD, wasting Marwen's reaction slot.

**Cheat sheet gap:** The "Spellcaster? → Hide or get cover" advice doesn't mention this. Recommended addition to WHAT DO I DO section under Spellcaster: "*Counterspell stops Disintegration Ray (Ranged Spell Attack) but NOT Drain Divinity or Void Scream — those are legendary abilities with no spell tag. Save Counterspell for DR specifically.*"

#### Confirmed FEEL-R172-01 (carry-forward): DD 4 THP gain is near-worthless vs LA alternatives

LA cost comparison for Round 1 after Shardcaller (init 14):

| Option | LA Cost | Expected Outcome |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Void Ray on Marwen (AC15, DEX+2) | 2 LA | ~15 expected force damage |
| Tentacle on Marwen (AC15) | 1 LA | ~10.4 expected dmg + grapple setup |
| DD on Sabriel | 3 LA | 4 THP to beholder + 45% chance Sabriel loses 1×2nd-level slot |

At 45% chance, the expected "value" of DD = 0.45 × 1 spell slot (worth ~4-5 expected THP per slot via future Lay on Hands denial). The direct damage output of foregoing Tentacle+VoidRay = 25.4 expected HP.

**DD is suboptimal in virtually every round of this fight for a 3-PC party.** The THP gain is negligible (beholder at 110 HP, party DPR ~28/round → 4 THP buys <1/7 of a round). DD is only worth considering if:
- Sabriel is about to cast a high-value concentration spell (Bless, Spirit Guardians) AND
- The beholder has already eliminated Marwen (reducing other LA targets) AND
- The beholder somehow has 4+ LA (not possible in base design).

**Recommendation (log only):** Consider giving DD a useful secondary effect beyond THP (e.g., heal beholder for the spell level × 5, OR impose disadvantage on Sabriel's spell saves for 1 minute). As written, the 4 THP is flavor text with statistical noise.
