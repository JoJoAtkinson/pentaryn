## CHEAT-R112-01 — Shrine-Touched vulnerability suppression: cheat sheet only

**Severity:** Medium (produces different combat outcomes depending on which document DM reads)

**Observed:** Cheat sheet (damage table and "WHAT KILLS WHAT FASTEST" table) states that
shrine-touched fire/radiant vulnerability is "suppressed within 60 ft of altar — pull them
away first." This note appears in `COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md` twice (lines 114 and 145) but has
no source in the stat block.

**Stat block says:** `Damage Vulnerabilities: fire, radiant` — unconditional, no range note.

**Shrine-Bound trait says:** creatures take 2d6 psychic damage if >300 ft from shrine. It does
NOT mention vulnerability suppression at any distance.

**Impact:** DMs reading only the stat block apply fire/radiant vulnerability everywhere (correct
per stat block). DMs using only the cheat sheet withhold vulnerability within 60 ft of the
altar (a significant tactical difference — the shrine-touched spends most of the fight in that
zone). These produce materially different outcomes.

**Recommendation:** Joe to confirm which is canon, then either:
- (a) Add to shrine-touched-derro.md Shrine-Bound trait: "While within 60 ft of the Thrulm
  altar, the shrine-touched has immunity to fire and radiant damage instead of vulnerability."
- (b) Remove the suppression note from the cheat sheet entirely.

NOT auto-fixable — adding a mechanic to a stat block requires design confirmation.

## Phase B — Generative Playtest Slice

**Scenario:** `round-1-opener` (rotation.yml, counter 112 % 8 = 0)  
**Core question:** Beholder wins initiative. Is Disintegration Ray or Multiattack the dominant
opener against Marwen (32 HP, AC 15)?

### Setup

Beholder: HP 110, AC 17. In-chamber: Void-Feeding active → +1 attack rolls + damage rolls.  
Effective attack bonus: +6 base + 1 = **+7**.  
Initiative: Beholder 17 > party. Order: Lair (20) → Beholder (17) → Marwen → Bazgar → Sabriel.

### Lair action (init 20, Round 1)

With 2 thrall derro present and party just entering (no PC within 20 ft of shrine yet):
- Void Eruption: no PCs in 20-ft shrine zone → no valid targets
- Unstable Ground: needs a target within 60 ft; party at room entrance ~50 ft away → possible vs Bazgar (DEX −1, P(save) = 20%). Worth firing if Bazgar is in range.
- Manifest Thralls: confirmed cosmetic (1 THP + one BA attack per thrall, ≈ 0.875 HP expected each)

**Best lair action:** Unstable Ground on Bazgar (20% save → 80% prone). Prone: melee attacks vs Bazgar from within 5 ft gain advantage; Bazgar's speed halved. Sets up grapple-while-prone on beholder's turn.

---

### Option A: Disintegration Ray on Marwen

+7 vs AC 15 → hits on 8+, P(hit) = **65%**.  
On hit: 10d8 force. Marwen HP = 32.  
P(10d8 ≥ 32): mean = 45, σ ≈ 7.25. Z = (32 − 45) / 7.25 ≈ −1.79. P(Z ≤ −1.79) ≈ 3.7%.  
→ P(10d8 ≥ 32) ≈ **96.3%**.

**P(Marwen killed by DR) = 0.65 × 0.963 ≈ 62.6%** — with permanent-death rider.  
On miss: 35% → wasted main action, no splash damage.  
Side effect: beholder cannot multiattack this turn; no grapple applied to other PCs.

---

### Option B: Multiattack on Marwen (Tentacle × 2 + Maw)

Targeting Marwen with all three attacks:

| Attack | Hit% | Damage dice | Damage mod | Avg per attack | EV |
|--------|------|-------------|-----------|---------------|-----|
| Tentacle Lash 1 | 65% | 3d6 | +4 (base+VF) | 14.5 | 9.4 |
| Tentacle Lash 2 | 65% | 3d6 | +4 | 14.5 | 9.4 |
| Maw | 65% | 4d8 | +4 | 22.5 | 14.6 |
| **Total EV** | | | | | **33.4** |

EV 33.4 > 32 HP → average multiattack kills Marwen.

**P(Marwen killed by multiattack) breakdown:**
- P(all 3 hit, total ≥ 32) ≈ 0.65³ × 0.95 ≈ 26.1%
- P(Maw + TL1 hit, TL2 miss, total ≥ 32) ≈ 0.65² × 0.35 × 0.85 ≈ 12.6%
- P(Maw + TL2 hit, TL1 miss, total ≥ 32) ≈ 12.6%
- P(TL1 + TL2 hit, Maw miss, total ≥ 32) ≈ 0.65² × 0.35 × 0.30 ≈ 4.4%
- **Total ≈ 55.7%**

Grapple bonus: on each TL hit, escape DC 16 vs Marwen STR (≈ −1 to +0 mod; P(escape) ≈ 25%).
A grappled Marwen has speed 0 and can't move away. Beholder can split TL2 to Sabriel or Bazgar
to apply grapple pressure to multiple PCs simultaneously.

### Decision Tree

| Option | P(kill Marwen R1) | Perma-death | Other-PC pressure |
|--------|-------------------|-------------|------------------|
| Disintegration Ray | **62.6%** | Yes | None (action spent) |
| Multiattack | 55.7% | No | Grapple on 1–2 PCs, ongoing table pressure |

**Result:** DR is marginally more kill-efficient (+7%) with the perma-death rider, but
multiattack produces persistent battlefield pressure across multiple PCs. In a fight designed
to escalate over 4–6 rounds, multiattack generates better ongoing threat even if slightly
less lethal on the opener.

**Core finding: both options kill Marwen before her turn in 56–63% of R1 encounters.**

## FEEL-R112-01 — Both DR and Multiattack cross "unfair, not lethal" threshold vs Marwen

**Finding:** With either option, Marwen (32 HP, AC 15, Wizard 5) dies before her first turn
in 56–63% of beholder-wins-initiative scenarios. She gets no turn, no counterplay, no spell.

This **extends FEEL-R111-01** (which identified only the multiattack path) by establishing:
1. The Disintegration Ray path (62.6% kill) is marginally dominant and adds permanent death
2. Both paths are above 50% first-turn kill probability — the wizard reliably does not act
3. The beholder doesn't need both to be degenerate; either main action choice reaches this threshold

**Aggravating factor:** Disintegration Ray's perma-death rider means no Revivify (level 3) can
restore Marwen. The party's recovery window disappears entirely if this fires.

**Mitigation (DM table-prep, NOT a stat block change):** Add a note to the encounter overview
(`_overview.md` or encounter tactics) that:
- The beholder holds Disintegration Ray until round 2 (let Marwen cast once first)
- Multiattack splits targets in R1: one tentacle to Bazgar, one to Sabriel, Maw to Bazgar
  (applies grapple pressure to two PCs without insta-killing either)
- This preserves the "party slowly overwhelmed" feel rather than "wizard died round 1"

**Not a mechanics change — does not require stat block edits.**
