### Key scenario findings

**1. Drain Divinity deferred to Round 2:** The scenario assumes the beholder "opens with Drain
Divinity" as its first action. In practice this is impossible — Drain Divinity is a 3-LA
legendary action used at end of another creature's turn. With Sabriel acting at init 10 (after
beholder at 13), and beholder consuming all 3 LA on Tentacle + Void Ray for damage before
Sabriel's turn, Drain Divinity cannot fire until Round 2. The scenario prelude misleads — the
beholder opens with DAMAGE, not Drain Divinity. Drain Divinity is a Round 2 finisher, not the
opening salvo.

**FEEL-B-R279-02 (NEW — LOG ONLY):** `thrulm-beholder-alpha-strike` scenario description says
"opens with Drain Divinity." This is mechanically incorrect given the initiative order. The
beholder should open with multiattack (action) + Tentacle/Void Ray legendaries (damage LA). Drain
Divinity fires Round 2 at the earliest. Consider updating scenario prelude or notes in
scenarios.yml to reflect correct action order.

**2. Legendary action economy — Move is always dominated:**
In 3 simulated rounds, Move LA was never the optimal choice:
- Round 1: Tentacle (damage) + Void Ray (damage) → 3 LA consumed
- Round 2+: Drain Divinity (3 LA) when Sabriel is in range
- Move LA would only matter if the party splits / forces the beholder out of Void-Feeding range.
  With Drain Divinity range = 30 ft (beholder must descend), if beholder is 40+ ft up it must use
  Move to descend before Drain Divinity fires. This is the only case where Move is non-trivially
  useful. But the beholder will typically just stay at 40 ft and fire ranged attacks, forgoing
  Drain Divinity for Void Ray (2 LA) instead.

**FEEL-B-R279-03 (NEW — LOG ONLY, carry-forward of R278 concern):** Drain Divinity's 30 ft
range creates a tactical tension: the beholder must choose between staying safe at altitude (where
Tentacle and Void Ray dominate) or descending to Drain Divinity range. This is actually an
interesting design choice, but the beholder's intelligence stat (INT 17) means it should recognize
when Sabriel is low on slots and whether descending is worth the risk of melee. The cheat sheet
should note that the beholder can opt for Void Ray loop instead of Drain Divinity when altitude
provides better protection. This is a DM-note addition, not a bug.

**3. DB queries: drain_divinity (legendary utility)**
From DB: `{"action": "drain_divinity", "type": "utility", "verbs": ["drain", ...]}`.
Type "utility" with 3 LA cost. The runner should surface this as a legendary action prompt
(not an attack roll). No embedded dice. The narration preview ("Sacred power tears itself free...")
fires on legendary declaration. The actual save result and slot loss must be resolved by the DM.
This is working as expected — Drain Divinity is a "declare and DM resolves" utility, not an
attack roll with automated output.

Whether the runner shows all 3 sub-attacks and grapple riders requires table verification, but the
DB row exists and the verbs "tentacle" and "maw" are included in the multiattack entry. Note:
"tentacle" in the multiattack verb list also matches `tentacle_legendary` — this is the known
