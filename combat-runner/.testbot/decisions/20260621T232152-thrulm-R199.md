### FINDING-R199-A (HIGH — NEW): Beholder multiattack is Marwen's primary KO threat, not Disintegration Ray

Prior playtest fires focused on Disintegration Ray as the main threat to Marwen (21% per-round perm-death risk; Mirror Image analysis in R198). This fire's simulation reveals a different primary threat:

**Beholder main action — Tentacle → Maw combo on grappled wizard:**

- Tentacle Lash 1 → Marwen (+7 vs AC 15, 65% hit). On hit: avg 14.5 damage + grapple (Marwen STR save 80% fail rate).
- After grapple: Tentacle Lash 2 → other target (Sabriel/Bazgar).
- Maw → grappled Marwen (+7 vs AC 15, 65% hit). Avg damage: 4d8+4 = 22 (Void-Feeding +1 in chamber).

Expected damage on Marwen per multiattack (Tentacle + Maw sequence, both targeting her):
- P(Tentacle hits Marwen) = 65%; avg 14.5 on hit → 9.4 expected
- Assuming Tentacle hits and grapple applied (80% → effective 52% per turn that grapple lands): Maw on grappled Marwen → 65% hit → 22 avg → 14.3 expected
- Combined expected: 9.4 + (0.52 × 14.3) = 9.4 + 7.4 = **16.8 expected per main action on Marwen**

But the REAL threat is the single-turn burst if both Tentacle AND Maw connect:
- Tentacle hit + Maw hit: 14.5 + 22 = **36.5 damage**
- P(both hit) = 0.65 × 0.65 = 42%
- 36.5 > 32 HP → Marwen KO in one main action with **42% probability** per round

Disintegration Ray (33% recharge per turn) averages 45 force damage on hit but only fires every ~3 turns. Multiattack fires EVERY turn.

**Comparison:**
| Threat | P(KO in 1 turn) | Frequency | Permanent-death risk |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Multiattack (Tentacle+Maw) | 42% | Every turn | No (0 HP, not instant kill) |
| Disintegration Ray | 65% (if recharge) | ~33%/turn | Yes (if below 0 and not resurrected) |
| Combined (DR fires AND MA) | — | — | Multiple threats simultaneously |

**Key insight:** Multiattack alone has a **42% chance to KO Marwen in one turn** without using any recharge ability. This threat is *higher per turn* than the per-turn DR risk (21% = 33% recharge × 65% hit). Multiattack has never been analyzed as an independent KO threat in prior fires — all analysis focused on DR as the dominant threat.

**Implication:** Mirror Image (analyzed in R198) is even MORE valuable than R198 concluded, because it also protects against the Maw attack (Mirror Image affects all attacks targeting Marwen, not just DR). A Tentacle Lash that hits an image can't establish a grapple, breaking the Tentacle→Maw combo entirely.

**Counterplay for Marwen:** Mirror Image on Round 1 protects against both DR AND the Tentacle/Maw combo. If 3 images present, P(Tentacle hits image) = 75%, preventing grapple and breaking the Maw setup. **Mirror Image's true value is even higher than R198's 5.3% perm-death calc stated** — it also dramatically reduces the multiattack KO chance.

### FINDING-R199-C: LA sequence flexibility — dominant order analysis

The rotation prescribes Tentacle (1 LA) after one creature's turn and Void Ray (2 LA) after another's. But order matters:

**Option A: Tentacle first (1 LA), then Void Ray (2 LA):**
- Tentacle grapples a PC early → constrains their movement for the rest of the LA window
- Void Ray can then target the newly constrained PC or another
- Allows Maw to follow up on the grappled target in the beholder's next main turn

**Option B: Void Ray first (2 LA), then Tentacle (1 LA):**
- Void Ray deals damage immediately before the PC takes their turn
- Tentacle could grapple the same PC post-Void-Ray — but if PC is near-KO from VR, Tentacle is wasteful
- Better when PCs are spread out and one needs to be sniped at range

**Recommendation:** Tentacle-first (Option A) is dominant when Marwen is accessible — the grapple sets up the Maw combo on the beholder's next turn for an average 22 additional damage. Void Ray-first makes sense only when Marwen is protected (Mirror Image up) or out of range.

**LA economy note — Drain Divinity geometry confirmed:** DD requires a target within 30 ft. With the beholder hovering 40-60 ft above the altar, all PCs at floor level are 40-60 ft away. The beholder must descend to within 30 ft to use DD at all. Descending to floor-level:
1. Sacrifices the aerial cover advantage
2. Brings it within melee range of Bazgar (who will immediately attack at +7 with advantage of flanking opportunity)
3. The beholder's Shrine-Drift bonus action can reposition 30 ft (through obstacles), but if it moves down for DD then back up, it uses both its main-turn action sequence AND the bonus action on repositioning

Confirmed: **DD is geometrically inaccessible from the beholder's tactical hover height.** The beholder must choose between safe altitude OR DD. Using DD = giving Bazgar a free round of +7 attacks at melee range. This adds another dimension to why DD is mechanically unattractive beyond just the EV comparison.
