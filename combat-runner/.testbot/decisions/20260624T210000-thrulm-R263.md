## Phase B — Playtest slice: `void-scream-clustering`

*(thrulm-rotation.json index 3 — AoE psychic clustering with shrine proximity disadvantage)*

### Setup

- **Beholder:** HP 110, AC 17, init +3, 3 LA/round. Void-Feeding active. Hovering 40 ft above altar.
- **Void Scream:** Recharge 6 — assumed recharged this turn. DC 16 Wis save, 6d10 psychic, half on success. On FAILED save: also FRIGHTENED 1 minute (DC 16 Wis end of each turn ends effect). Creatures within 10 ft of shrine have disadvantage on this save (note: this is the shrine/altar, not the beholder's position).
- **Compass Edge (Round 2, mid-combat HP):** Bazgar (Fighter 5, HP 49, AC 18, WIS +1), Marwen (Wizard 5, HP 32, AC 15, WIS +2), Sabriel (Divine Martial 5, HP 44, AC 19, WIS +3).
- **Assumed positions:** Beholder hovering above altar. Bazgar within 10 ft of shrine (fighter, charged in to engage). Sabriel within 10 ft of shrine (divine martial, engaging beholder's tentacles). Marwen at ~25 ft (wizard, behind pillar, within 30 ft but outside 10 ft).

### Saving throw simulation (one representative roll)

**Bazgar — WIS +1, within 10 ft of shrine → disadvantage:**
- Roll 1: 9, Roll 2: 15 → lower is 9. Total: 9+1=10 vs DC 16 → FAIL.
- 6d10 damage: 4+7+3+8+9+6 = 37 psychic.
- Bazgar: 49 HP → **12 HP. FRIGHTENED** (DC 16 Wis save end of each turn to remove; +1 → 30% success per turn).

**Sabriel — WIS +3, within 10 ft of shrine → disadvantage:**
- Roll 1: 7, Roll 2: 11 → lower is 7. Total: 7+3=10 vs DC 16 → FAIL.
- Same 6d10 = 37 psychic (same roll, area effect).
- Sabriel: 44 HP → **7 HP. FRIGHTENED**.

**Marwen — WIS +2, at 25 ft from shrine → straight roll:**
- Roll: 14. Total: 14+2=16 vs DC 16 → SUCCESS (exactly meets DC = success in 5e).
- Half damage: 37/2 = 18 (rounded down).
- Marwen: 32 HP → **14 HP. NOT frightened.**

### Statistical analysis

| Target | Shrine prox | P(fail) | P(FRIGHTENED) | E[dmg] | HP after (from full) |
|--------|-------------|---------|---------------|--------|----------------------|
| Bazgar (WIS +1) | within 10 ft, disadvantage | 0.91 (91%) | 91% | 31.5 | 49 → ~17 |
| Sabriel (WIS +3) | within 10 ft, disadvantage | 0.84 (84%) | 84% | 30.4 | 44 → ~14 |
| Marwen (WIS +2) | outside 10 ft, straight | 0.65 (65%) | 65% | 27.2 | 32 → ~5 |

*(Disadvantage = P(fail) = 1 − P(success)². At-disadvantage: Bazgar needs 15+ → 30% success → 91% fail. Sabriel needs 13+ → 40% success → 84% fail. Marwen straight roll: needs 14+ → 35% success → 65% fail.)*

**Party HP pool before VS:** 125 HP (49+44+32). **Expected total damage:** ~89 HP.  
VS removes ~71% of the party's HP pool in one action. This is the single highest-EV action in the beholder's kit — higher than multiattack (~50 dmg over 3 attacks) and much higher than Drain Divinity (≤6 THP cost to Sabriel's slots).

**P(Marwen at 0 HP from VS at full HP):**  
P(fail) × P(6d10 ≥ 32) = 0.65 × P(X ≥ 32) where X ~ 6d10 (mean 33, σ ≈ 7.0).  
P(X ≥ 32) ≈ P(Z ≥ −0.14) ≈ 0.556. So: 0.65 × 0.556 ≈ **36% chance of instant KO at full HP.**  
If Marwen already took damage (any prior hit): much higher. From R262 typical Round 1 (tentacle hit to Marwen, 14 dmg → 18 HP): P(6d10 ≥ 18) ≈ 98% → P(KO) ≈ 0.65 × 0.98 ≈ **64% instant KO**.

**P(both Bazgar AND Sabriel FRIGHTENED):** 0.91 × 0.84 ≈ **76%.**

### Key observations

**FEEL-R263-01 (NEW — proximity trap mechanic):**  
The shrine altar is the combat's geometric center: the beholder hovers above it; PCs must approach to engage (no fly speed, limited ranged options). Approaching the altar to fight the beholder means: (1) entering VS 30-ft range, (2) entering shrine proximity disadvantage zone (within 10 ft). The design punishes PCs for doing what the encounter requires. In a "meant to lose" framing this is intentional, but at the table it creates a cognitive mismatch — the natural PC move (advance on the boss) actively worsens their position. The cheat sheet hints at this but doesn't frame it as an explicit zone hazard.  
*Joe decides: add an explicit "danger zone" annotation to the positioning map, or change shrine proximity disadvantage to apply only to divine-flavored saves (thematic, less punishing to Bazgar).*

**FEEL-R263-02 (NEW — FRIGHTENED doubly locks melee PCs):**  
After a failed VS save, Bazgar and Sabriel are FRIGHTENED (91% and 84% chance respectively). FRIGHTENED means: "cannot willingly move closer to the source of fear." The beholder hovers above the altar. Frightened Bazgar/Sabriel cannot close the gap — they're frozen in place at melee range of the altar but 40+ ft below the beholder. Their attacks are limited to javelins (Bazgar, no class javelin emphasis) or ranged cantrips (Sabriel has none by default). Marwen — the only unfrightened PC — is at 5–14 HP with ≤2 spells remaining. The encounter effectively ends here. The frightened condition from VS stacks with the hover geometry to create a locked-out state that has no counterplay at level 5 (no Freedom of Movement, no fly spells from a beaten-up wizard).

This confirms and deepens FEEL-R260-01 (Frightened + no ranged weapon = Bazgar removed). FEEL-R263-02 is specifically about the VS-induced double lockout: frightened stops approach, hover negates melee, and 76% of encounters have BOTH melee PCs in this state simultaneously.

**CS-OMIT-R263-01 (NEW — shrine proximity check missing from Round checklist):**  
The SAVE DCs section of the cheat sheet correctly notes "within 10 ft of shrine: **disadvantage** on this save" for Void Scream. However, the Round-by-Round Checklist does not include a reminder to check shrine proximity for positioning. When Void Scream fires, the DM needs to: (1) identify which PCs are within 30 ft, (2) identify which PCs are within 10 ft, (3) apply disadvantage to the 10-ft subset, (4) resolve frightened on fails. Step 2 is easy to forget under table pressure.  
*Auto-fix candidate: add a shrine proximity reminder to the round checklist. Low effort, reduces DM cognitive load. Logging only — cheat sheet changes are low-risk but this is the DM's call for house formatting.*

**VS vs LA economy:**  
Void Scream costs the beholder's full action (not a LA). After VS, beholder has 3 LA unspent.  
Optimal post-VS LA sequence (assuming Bazgar and Sabriel both frightened):  
- After Marwen's turn (AC 15, ~5–14 HP, unfrightened): 1 LA → Tentacle Lash, +7 vs AC 15 = 65% hit, avg 14 dmg. Marwen at 5 HP: **KO on hit** (65% chance).  
- After Sabriel's turn (AC 19, ~7 HP, frightened but may still throw javelin): 2 LA → Void Ray, DC 16 DEX, Sabriel DEX +1, ~65% fail, avg 22 dmg. Sabriel at 7 HP: **KO on fail** (65% chance).  
- After Bazgar (AC 18, ~12 HP, frightened): 0 LA remaining — nothing. Bazgar alone at 12 HP vs beholder at full HP.  

Expected sequence: VS + 3 LA sequence → 3-PC wipe within 1 round. TPK probability this round: ~0.65 × 0.65 × 0.50 ≈ **21% one-round TPK** after VS; 2-round TPK much higher.
