## Phase B — Generative Playtest Slice

**Scenario:** `retreat-threshold`  
**Rotation index:** 142 % 8 = 6 → `retreat-threshold`  
**Setup:** Beholder drops to 28 HP during combat. Per tactics text: "If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit." Evaluate whether the retreat is mechanically supported and whether it creates a dramatic moment or an anticlimactic exit.

---

### State assumptions entering this slice

- Beholder: 28 HP, 3 LA available, all recharges pending (Disintegration Ray may recharge this turn)
- Compass Edge survivors (worst-case typical state after 2-3 rounds): Bazgar 20 HP, Sabriel 11 HP, Marwen unconscious (1 death save rolled)
- Active thralls: 2 (HP ~16, AC 14 each, within 60 ft of beholder)
- Beholder is hovering ~40 ft above altar, 50 ft from lower shaft entrance
- Room: main chamber (approx 80 × 60 ft), lower shaft entrance is a 10-ft opening in the northwest floor

---

### Movement analysis: can the beholder reach the lower shaft in one turn?

**Normal movement:** 30 ft fly speed.  
**Shrine-Drift (BA):** Move up to 30 ft additional, phase through creatures/objects; 1d10 self-damage if ending inside an object/creature (avg 5.5, max 10).

From hover position (40 ft above altar) to lower shaft entrance (floor level, 50 ft horizontal):  
- Straight-line distance from hover to shaft entrance ≈ √(40² + 50²) ≈ 64 ft.  
- Moving diagonally (half-distance rule optional at table): ~64 ft needed.  
- Movement available: 30 ft (main) + 30 ft (Shrine-Drift BA) = 60 ft.  

**Result: beholder cannot reach the shaft entrance in one turn from hover position at 40+ ft altitude.** It falls short by ~4 ft if moving diagonally at full speed. DM must decide whether the beholder starts descending (closing altitude first) or whether the distances are slightly adjusted. This is a real table problem — the opening turn of retreat often fails to actually exit.

**Workaround:** Beholder at 28 HP drops altitude to 15 ft (spending 25 ft of main movement to descend), then Shrine-Drifts 30 ft horizontally toward shaft = 55 ft of travel, reaching within 5 ft of the shaft entrance. Next turn: 5 ft normal movement enters the shaft. Over 2 turns, the retreat succeeds but the beholder takes 1 more full round of attacks at an exposed position.

**Alternative:** If the DM rules the beholder is already at near-floor level (10 ft), the distance is ~55 ft. Main move 30 ft + Shrine-Drift 30 ft = 60 ft. Retreat succeeds in one turn (just).

---

### Legendary action "Move" as pursuit evasion

With 3 PCs, the beholder gets up to 3 × 1 LA = Move 30 ft × 3 = 90 ft of LA movement per round.  
Plus 60 ft of its own turn movement (main + Shrine-Drift).  
**Total per-round pursuit evasion: 150 ft theoretical maximum.**

In practice:
- Beholder burns all 3 LA on Move (foregoing Tentacle / Void Ray attacks while retreating)
- Each PC gets 1 attack action before the beholder gets its next Move LA
- PCs at melee range must Dash (Action: 30 ft) to keep up; they give up attacks

**Chase simulation (1 round of retreat):**

| Event | Beholder HP | Party state |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Beholder turn: main 30 ft + Shrine-Drift 30 ft | 28 | Bazgar: 20 HP, Sabriel: 11 HP, Marwen: KO |
| After Bazgar turn (ranged attack, Battlemaster): +7 vs AC 17 → 8 required, 60% hit; avg 12 damage; Beholder: 16 HP | 16 | Beholder uses 1 LA Move: +30 ft |
| After Sabriel turn (melee, 5 ft behind): Bazgar used Dash, Sabriel too far → ranged smite (if no bow: Sabriel can't attack); Sabriel Dashes | 16 | Beholder uses 1 LA Move: +30 ft |
| After Marwen: KO, no action | 16 | Beholder uses 1 LA Move: +30 ft |
| **Beholder is in the lower shaft.** | 16 | Party 5-15 ft behind shaft entrance |

In this trace: one PC (Bazgar, assumed to have a ranged option from Battlemaster) deals ~7.2 expected damage during the chase round. Sabriel has no ranged option and loses her action Dashing. Marwen is KO. The beholder loses ~7-12 HP while escaping, drops from 28 to ~16-21 HP, and exits combat.

### Key findings from this slice

**FEEL-R142-01 (NEW): Shrine-Drift / Compel Thrall bonus-action conflict during retreat.**  
When retreating, the beholder ideally wants to:  
  1. Use Shrine-Drift (BA) for +30 ft movement to escape, AND  
  2. Use Compel Thrall (BA, 1/turn) to redirect a thrall to block the shaft entrance.  
Both are bonus actions. The beholder can only do one. Consequence: the beholder cannot simultaneously escape via Shrine-Drift AND place a blocking thrall in the same turn. The DM must choose a turn sequence:  
  - Turn 1: Compel Thrall to move a thrall to the shaft entrance (no Shrine-Drift — beholder only moves 30 ft, doesn't exit). Beholder remains in range of party attacks.  
  - Turn 2: Main + Shrine-Drift to finally exit (if not killed turn 1).  
OR:  
  - Turn 1: Shrine-Drift to exit immediately (beholder escapes). No thrall blocker placed. Party can walk straight into shaft.  
**Recommendation:** Add to tactics section: "During retreat, use Compel Thrall BA on the prior turn (while still healthy) to pre-position a thrall at the shaft entrance. When the retreat trigger fires, the blocker is already in place, freeing the retreat turn for full movement (main + Shrine-Drift)." The beholder's INT +3 should plan ahead; this is the smart play that isn't currently documented.

**FEEL-R142-02 (NEW): Legendary "Move" effectively guarantees the retreat at the cost of forfeiting LA offense.**  
The beholder spending all 3 LA on Move during the retreat round generates 90 ft of additional travel (3 × 30 ft) on top of 60 ft of own-turn movement. This is overwhelming pursuit evasion at level 5 party speeds. The retreat *will succeed* if the beholder commits to it, because the party simply cannot keep up. This is correct for the encounter's design intent (party loses, beholder escapes to fight another day) but makes the end of the fight feel mechanical rather than dramatic. There's no "final stand" tension — the beholder just flies away faster than anyone can chase. Consider whether one of the following would improve the exit:  
  (a) **Lair "Seal" action (new):** At the instant the beholder crosses 30 HP threshold, trigger a lair action that collapses debris across the shaft entrance (Dex DC 16, 2d10 bludgeoning, passable but at half speed). This creates 1-2 more rounds of engagement before the beholder disappears.  
  (b) **Narrative callout:** The beholder emits Void Scream (if recharged) as a parting shot before retreating — spending its action on the blast rather than on attacking, then Shrine-Drifting out. More dramatic, but forfeits further damage.  
*This is a design suggestion for Joe, not an auto-fix.*

**FEEL-R142-03 (NEW): Shrine-Drift self-damage is mechanically irrelevant during retreat.**  
At 28 HP, the 1d10 self-damage from ending inside a creature/object (avg 5.5, max 10) during Shrine-Drift represents 20-36% of remaining HP. It could in theory kill the beholder (if Shrine-Drift self-damage + a lucky party hit = 0 HP). However, the beholder's normal use of Shrine-Drift is to phase through open air toward the shaft, not through solid walls — there's nothing to end inside if the path is clear. The self-damage deters reckless tactical phasing (e.g., passing through a PC cluster at the exit). At retreat HP, the beholder must choose a clear path. Self-damage functions correctly as a deterrent to "just phase through the party" — confirmed design intent. No fix needed.

**FEEL-R142-04 (NEW): No dramatic terminal moment for the party — retreat happens too cleanly.**  
In this encounter's narrative context, the beholder is meant to be an overwhelming threat. When it retreats at 28 HP after a brutal 3-round fight, the surviving PCs (Bazgar at ~20 HP, Sabriel at ~11 HP) watch a CR 13 creature fly away while their wizard lies on the floor dying. The retreat should feel like a narrow escape for the beholder, not a casual withdrawal. Currently the mechanics (LA Move × 3 + Shrine-Drift + normal movement = 150 ft/round) make the escape mathematically certain. **The party has no ability to stop it.** This absence of agency at the encounter's conclusion is a significant feel problem for a "meant to lose" scenario — the party doesn't lose dramatically; they're ignored while the beholder exits.  
*Flag for Joe: consider whether a "last gasp" mechanic exists — something the party can DO in the retreat round that has a chance of affecting the outcome, even if it's a 10-15% chance of a slightly more dramatic ending. E.g., "if the beholder is below 20 HP when it exits, roll a d6 — on a 1-2, it is weakened and the shaft collapses behind it (cannot return)." This is a narrative mechanic, not a stats change.*
