### Retreat mechanics: Shrine-Drift as escape tool

**Movement budget:**
- Fly speed: 30 ft (hover). Cannot walk (speed 0 ft).
- Shrine-Drift (BA): up to 30 ft additional movement, phasing through creatures and objects.
- Total per turn: 30 ft (action-free fly) + 30 ft (Shrine-Drift BA) = **60 ft**.

**Shrine-Drift phasing rule:** Can move through objects as difficult terrain (no half-speed penalty text, but difficult terrain costs 2 ft per ft → 15 ft of actual wall if the BA is used entirely for wall-phasing). However, re-reading the action: "It can move through other creatures and objects **as if they were difficult terrain**." Difficult terrain halves speed. So a 5-ft-thick stone wall costs 10 ft of movement to pass through, and the beholder may NOT end its turn inside an object.

**Effective retreat distance (open corridor):**
- Move 30 ft (fly), Shrine-Drift 30 ft = 60 ft total if no obstacles.
- If passing through 5 ft of stone wall (corridor wall, not solid rock): costs 10 ft → 20 ft of effective Shrine-Drift travel. Total movement through obstacle: 30 + 20 = 50 ft in the exit direction.

**Can the beholder reach the lower shaft in one turn?**
Depends on vault geometry (not specified precisely in `_overview.md`). Assumptions:
- Vault width: ~60 ft.
- Beholder hovering near altar (center-rear): ~30 ft from the lower shaft entrance.
- 30 ft fly + 30 ft Shrine-Drift = 60 ft → **yes, reaches the lower shaft entry in one turn**, even accounting for difficult-terrain phasing through a wall edge.

**Conclusion: Shrine-Drift is a mechanically viable single-turn escape tool.** The beholder can exit the main vault in 1 turn (60 ft total) even against a melee-adjacent party. The phasing aspect (passing through stone) is visually dramatic — it "fades into the rock." The party cannot follow through solid stone without special abilities.

### FEEL-R174-01 (NEW — MEDIUM): BA conflict makes "retreat + redirect thralls" impossible in one turn

The Tactics section states:
> "If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower shaft, **using thralls to block pursuit**."

Mechanically, this requires on the same turn:
- **Shrine-Drift** (BA, 30 ft movement + phasing) to escape.
- **Compel Thrall** (BA, 1/turn) to redirect the thrall derro to block the corridor.

**Both are bonus actions.** The beholder can only use one BA per turn (PHB). It cannot escape via Shrine-Drift AND redirect its thrall simultaneously.

**Consequences:**
- If the beholder uses **Shrine-Drift** to retreat: the thrall derro acts on its own initiative, which is likely slow (DEX +1 → usually last). The party gets 1–2 turns in the corridor before the thrall positions itself. No meaningful block.
- If the beholder uses **Compel Thrall** to reposition the thrall: the beholder moves only its fly 30 ft per turn. At 30 ft distance, the party has a chance to pursue and land attacks before the beholder fully exits. Bazgar (30 ft walk speed) can likely stay adjacent.

**Required pre-condition for tactics text to work:** The beholder must have redirected the thrall to a blocking position 1–2 turns **before** it hits the retreat threshold. The Tactics section implies contemporaneous execution, but the BA conflict prevents that.

**FEEL-R174-01 recommendation:** Add a DM prep note to the Tactics section: "Pre-position thralls to block the lower shaft exit when the beholder drops below ~50 HP. By the time the retreat threshold (30 HP) is reached, the thrall should already be blocking — the beholder cannot use Shrine-Drift (BA escape) and Compel Thrall (BA redirect) on the same turn."

### FEEL-R174-02 (NEW — HIGH): STAT-R166-01 resolution proposal (retreat contradiction)

**The contradiction** (STAT-R166-01, open 8 fires):
- **Tactics section:** "If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower shaft."
- **Weaknesses section:** "**The deeper shaft:** It cannot go deeper into the lower chamber willingly; something down there scares even this creature."

Three resolution options analysed:

**Option A (tactics text is wrong):** Change "retreats to lower shaft" → "retreats to the altar's highest point, hovering at maximum altitude." The beholder never enters the lower shaft willingly. Retreat = circling at 60 ft altitude, phasing through ceiling if it exists.
- *Problem:* The altar chamber is finite; hovering at altitude doesn't remove the beholder from combat. It still takes attacks. Not a true retreat.

**Option B (weakness text is wrong):** Remove "cannot go deeper willingly." The beholder does retreat into the lower shaft; the weakness was flavorful but not mechanically intended.
- *Problem:* Loses the "something worse down there" narrative hook. Weakens the encounter's atmosphere.

**Option C (split threshold — recommended):** Update both sections to be consistent:
- **30 HP:** Beholder retreats *within the chamber* — rises to maximum altitude, phases through pillars/ceiling (Shrine-Drift), uses range attacks only. It is still dangerous but has lost the will to grapple.
- **Below 20 HP:** Imminent death overrides the deeper fear. The beholder phases through the floor into the lower shaft. This is narratively significant — *whatever is down there frightens even this creature, and the beholder still chose it over death.* Creates a dramatic hook: the party now knows there is something worse below, and the beholder fled to it.

**Option C mechanics:**
- 30 HP → altitude retreat within chamber (no stat block changes needed; just updated tactics text).
- 20 HP → Shrine-Drift through the floor. Beholder is effectively removed from combat unless the party follows into the lower shaft.
- "Crush/disintegrate while fleeing" (current 20 HP text) becomes: during the turn the beholder descends through the floor, it may take its action normally before phasing. This keeps the 20 HP turn aggressive and dramatic.

**FEEL-R174-02 recommendation:** Joe decides between Options A/B/C; Option C is most narratively interesting and resolves the contradiction without losing the "something below" hook. Requires updating Tactics text only (2 lines); no stat block mechanical changes.
