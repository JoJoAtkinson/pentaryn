## Phase B — Scenario: `antireality-timing` (rotation.yml idx = 95 % 8 = 7)

**Slice definition:**
> Bazgar attacks beholder: +7 to hit vs AC 17 — needs 10+ to hit (55% base). Beholder uses
> Antireality (reaction, once per round) to impose DISADVANTAGE on the triggering attack roll.
> With disadvantage, hit rate drops to ~30% (P(both rolls ≥10) = 0.55²). Simulate Bazgar's
> Action over 3 rounds (2 attacks per round): Antireality fires on the FIRST attack each round
> only (one reaction per round). Record how many of Bazgar's 6 total rolls are flipped from hit
> to miss by disadvantage. Note: if Bazgar already has disadvantage from another source,
> Antireality is wasted (advantage/disadvantage do not stack — straight roll results instead).

### Setup

- Bazgar: HP 49, AC 18. Greatsword, STR +4, PB +3 → +7 to hit vs AC 17 (confirmed from
  roster YAML; fighter melee attack inline). Avg damage per hit: 2d6+4 = 11 HP.
- Beholder: HP 110, AC 17. Antireality = impose disadvantage on one attack per round (reaction,
  pre-roll declaration per stat block). DB `trigger.event="damage"` fires this post-roll —
  BUG-F7-02 confirmed active; simulation uses correct pre-roll intent.
- Baseline hit rate: needs 10+ on d20 → P(hit) = 11/20 = 55.0%
- Disadvantaged hit rate: P(both rolls ≥ 10) = 0.55² = 30.25%

### 3-Round Dice Simulation (representative)

**Round 1:**
- Attack 1 — Beholder declares Antireality (BA used). Bazgar rolls disadvantage.
  - d20 rolls: 14, 7 → lower = 7 → MISS (needs 10+). Antireality works.
- Attack 2 — Antireality spent; normal roll. d20 = 13 → HIT. Damage: 2d6+4 = 9 HP.
  - Beholder: 110 − 9 = 101 HP.

**Round 2:** Antireality refreshes at start of beholder's turn.
- Attack 1 — Antireality declared. Rolls: 18, 5 → lower = 5 → MISS.
- Attack 2 — Normal. d20 = 16 → HIT. Damage: 2d6+4 = 14 HP.
  - Beholder: 101 − 14 = 87 HP.

**Round 3:**
- Attack 1 — Antireality declared. Rolls: 11, 3 → lower = 3 → MISS.
- Attack 2 — Normal. d20 = 9 → MISS.
  - Beholder: 87 HP (unchanged this round).

**3-round totals:**
- Antireality attacks: 3 rolled with disadvantage → 0 hits (all 3 misses in this sim).
- Normal attacks: 3 rolled → 2 hits; 3rd missed on the round-3 natural roll.
- Total hits: 2 out of 6. Total damage: 23 HP over 3 rounds (~8 HP/round from Bazgar).

### Expected-Value Analysis

| Scenario | Expected Hits (6 atks) | Expected Damage |
|----------|----------------------|-----------------|
| No Antireality | 6 × 0.55 = 3.30 hits | 36.3 HP |
| With Antireality (3 AT + 3 normal) | 3 × 0.30 + 3 × 0.55 = 2.55 hits | 28.1 HP |
| Savings from Antireality | 0.75 hits avoided | 8.2 HP saved (7.5% of 110) |

**Action Surge impact:** If Bazgar uses Action Surge (R1), he makes 4 attacks total. Antireality
covers only the first (30% hit) vs 3 normal attacks (55% each):
- Expected hits: 0.30 + 3 × 0.55 = 1.95 vs 4 × 0.55 = 2.20 without AR.
- AR saves 0.25 expected hits = 2.75 HP on an Action Surge turn. Substantially less
  efficient: burning the one reaction to shave 2.75 HP on the turn most likely to deal 24+
  damage.

### Phase B Findings

**FEEL-R95-01 (NEW): Beholder missing "Menacing Attack rider wasted" note.**

The rager stat block documents: "Frightened immunity — Menacing Attack rider wasted: the Rager
is immune to the Frightened condition. If a PC uses Menacing Attack... the rider does nothing."
The **beholder** is also frightened-immune (Condition Immunities list) but has no equivalent
note. Bazgar (Battlemaster) has Menacing Attack as a common maneuver — the 1d8 bonus damage
lands but DC 15 WIS save vs frightened is wasted. At level 5 with 4 superiority dice (d8),
Bazgar will likely use Menacing Attack multiple times against the beholder. No DM warning exists
in the beholder stat block.

**Recommendation:** Add to the beholder's Resistance/Immunity reminder section:
"Menacing Attack: Battlemaster PC Menacing Attack bonus damage (1d8) applies; the Frightened
rider (DC 15 WIS save) does nothing — beholder is frightened-immune. Inform the player when
they spend the superiority die so they can choose a different maneuver if preferred (Trip Attack,
Disarming Strike, etc. may be more effective)."

**FEEL-R95-02 (NEW): Antireality self-waste when Bazgar is prone.**

When Unstable Ground (lair, init 20) primes Bazgar prone and the beholder acts immediately
after at its own initiative, Bazgar's melee attacks already have disadvantage (RAW PHB p.292:
prone creature has disadvantage on attack rolls). If the beholder then uses Antireality on
Bazgar's first attack, advantage/disadvantage do not stack — the result is still straight
disadvantage. **Antireality's reaction is wasted**.

Probability of this self-conflict:
- Unstable Ground targets Bazgar (optimal per tactics): DC 16 DEX, Bazgar DEX −1 → rolls on
  1-16 (needs 17+) → **~80% fail rate → Bazgar prone ~80% of the time UG fires**.
- When Bazgar is prone: his attacks already have disadvantage → Antireality on his first attack
  is a complete waste (zero additional defensive benefit).
- **Net: in ~80% of rounds where UG hits Bazgar, Antireality is wasted if the beholder tries
  to use it on Bazgar.**

**Practical consequence:** The beholder's two primary passive defenses (Unstable Ground lair
action + Antireality reaction) actively conflict when both target the same attacker in the same
round. If both fire on Bazgar, Antireality is consumed for zero gain ~80% of the time.

**Recommendation for tactics section:** "If Bazgar is prone from Unstable Ground, do NOT use
Antireality on his melee attacks — he already has disadvantage and the reaction is wasted. Hold
Antireality for Sabriel's first melee attack or Marwen's ranged spell attack instead (neither
is typically prone at this stage)."

**Antireality effectiveness summary (quantified for Joe):**

| Attacker State | AR Benefit |
|----------------|-----------|
| Bazgar standing (normal) | 55% → 30% hit; saves ~2.75 HP/attack, ~8 HP/3 rounds |
| Bazgar Action Surge | saves ~2.75 HP total on 4-attack turn |
| Bazgar prone | 0 HP saved — wasted reaction |
| Bazgar Taunted by Rager (disadvantage) | 0 HP saved — wasted reaction |
| Sabriel standing | 55%→30% (same math as Bazgar, she's also +7 vs AC 17 approx) |

The reaction has real but modest value when applied to a standing attacker. Its main practical
issue is the self-conflict with UG, which currently wastes it ~80% of the time on Bazgar-focused
rounds. Antireality is more efficiently held for Sabriel's attacks (she typically isn't prone
from UG since the lair-action priority is Bazgar first per FIX-FC39-B).

**BUG-F7-02 confirmed once more:** The DB `trigger.event="damage"` means the runner surfaces
Antireality prompt post-hit on the damage event — too late for the pre-roll mechanic. All
Antireality analysis above assumes the **stat-block-correct** mechanic (declare before die roll).
At the table, DMs must self-prompt Antireality before the attack roll; the runner cannot enforce
this.
