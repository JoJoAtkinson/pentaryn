## Phase A — New Findings

### PHASE-A-R282-01: Thrall `hand_axe_weak` damage formula inconsistency (minor, log only)

**File:** `world/factions/dulgarum-oathholds/locations/thrulm/npcs/thrall-derro.md` line 53

Stat block: `Hit: 2 (1d4) slashing damage.` STR is 9 (−1).

In standard 5e, melee damage includes STR modifier: 1d4+(−1), average 2.5−1=1.5 → floor 1,
written "1 (1d4 − 1)" or "1 (1d4)". The stat block shows "2 (1d4)" which is only correct if
the −1 modifier is deliberately suppressed (i.e., the DB rolls pure 1d4 with no STR penalty).

The `to_hit` is +1 (STR −1 + PB +2 = +1) — STR modifier IS applied to attack rolls.
Scenarios.yml (thrulm-thrall-compelled-move notes) confirms: "+1 to-hit / 1d4 damage
(intentionally weak — dominated creature)." This is a design choice, not an error.

**Assessment:** The "2 (1d4)" parenthetical is technically misleading (implies avg 2.5→2 on a
no-modifier die, but you'd expect "1 (1d4 − 1)" for a −1 STR damage modifier). However, the
intent is clearly to show a flat 1d4 with suppressed negative modifier. The inconsistency is
cosmetic and already documented in scenarios.yml. No fix needed unless Joe wants the notation
to follow strict 5e convention. Log only.

## Phase B — Scenario: lair-action-round (thrulm-rotation index 5)

*Rotation.yml slice: "Lair action decision tree (init 20)"*

### Setup

Context: 2 thrall derro in chamber (HP 22, AC 14 each). Bazgar (Fighter 5, HP 49, AC 18) is
within 20 ft of shrine. Both thrall derro are within 60 ft of beholder. Marwen (Wizard 5, HP 32,
AC 15) and Sabriel (divine martial 5, HP 44, AC 19) are 30–40 ft from shrine.

Beholder takes lair action on init 20. Three choices: Unstable Ground, Manifest Thralls,
Void Eruption.

### Option-by-option analysis

**Option A — Unstable Ground (target Bazgar, DC 16 DEX)**

Bazgar is Fighter 5 (STR primary). DEX save assumed +0 to +1 (typical melee Fighter build
without Resilient/War Caster). Needs d20+0 ≥ 16 = needs 16+; P(success) = 25%, P(fail) = 75%.

On fail: Bazgar is prone. Effects:
- Bazgar must spend half movement (15 ft out of 30 ft) to stand, then can move 15 ft.
- Ranged attacks against Bazgar have advantage. Melee attacks against Bazgar have disadvantage
  (he's prone — melee attackers within 5 ft still have advantage).
- Bazgar's own attacks are made with disadvantage (prone condition).
- Next turn: Bazgar likely stands immediately, losing 15 ft of movement.

Expected impact: disrupts Bazgar's positioning for ~1 action cycle (stand costs half speed,
reducing his threat range from 35 ft to 20 ft). Does not prevent his attacks this round.

**Option B — Manifest Thralls (2 thrall derro, each gets 1 THP + reaction)**

Each thrall gains THP = beholder CHA modifier = +1. Then can immediately use their reaction to
move or make a weapon attack.

Reaction attack (Hand Axe Weak): +1 to hit vs target. Against Marwen AC 15: P(hit) = 7/20 = 35%.
On hit: 2 (1d4) damage average. Against Bazgar AC 18: P(hit) = 4/20 = 20%. Against Sabriel
AC 19: P(hit) = 3/20 = 15%.

Expected damage from 2 thrall reaction attacks (targeting Marwen, best case): 2 × 0.35 × 2.5 = 1.75 avg.
1 THP per thrall is negated by any hit from any PC (minimum 1 damage on any hit).

**Assessment: Manifest Thralls is effectively useless here.** The 1 THP is irrelevant vs any
PC attack, and the reaction attacks deal < 2 expected total damage at best. The known-state
entry ("Manifest Thralls lair action grants 1 THP (CHA +1) — effectively useless vs AC 18+
party") is confirmed by this simulation.

**Option C — Void Eruption (all within 20 ft of shrine, DC 16 DEX, 11 (2d10) force)**

Creatures within 20 ft of shrine in this scenario: Bazgar (explicitly stated), possibly thrall
derro (if positioned near shrine), possibly Sabriel (divine martial, may position near Bazgar).

**Bazgar (DEX +0):** P(fail DC 16) = 75%. EV = 0.75 × 11 + 0.25 × 5.5 = 8.25 + 1.375 = **9.6**
**Thrall A (DEX +1):** P(fail DC 16) = P(d20+1 < 16) = P(d20 ≤ 14) = 70%.
  EV = 0.70 × 11 + 0.30 × 5.5 = 7.7 + 1.65 = **9.35**
**Thrall B (same):** EV = **9.35**

If both thrall derro are within 20 ft of shrine: Void Eruption deals ~9.35 avg damage to each
thrall (who has 22 HP). Each thrall drops to ~13 HP after one VE. **FRIENDLY FIRE RISK.**

### NEW FINDING: FEEL-006 — Void Eruption vs thrall positioning conflict

**Issue:** The beholder's lair action Void Eruption hits ALL creatures within 20 ft of the
shrine, including its own charmed thrall derro. If the beholder uses Manifest Thralls on
previous rounds to position thralls near the altar (as guard/shields), those thralls are
caught in every subsequent Void Eruption.

**Quantified at this party level:**
- Round 1 VE: each thrall takes ~9 EV damage (22 HP → 13 HP remaining)
- Round 2 VE: each thrall at ~13 HP takes another ~9 EV damage → ~4 HP remaining
- Round 3 VE: each thrall at ~4 HP takes another ~9 EV → likely dead

The beholder can effectively kill its own thralls with 3 lair-action Void Eruptions if they
cluster near the shrine. This is either intentional (beholder sees thralls as disposable once
their movement is expended) or a design oversight.

**DM note needed:** Void Eruption and Manifest Thralls are mutually exclusive tactically.
If the beholder used Manifest Thralls in round 1 (thralls near shrine), it must relocate
them via Compel Thrall (BA) before using Void Eruption, or accept attrition of its own minions.
Current tactics text does not mention this interaction.

**Recommendation (log only):** Add to beholder lair action tactics (cheat sheet or .md):
"Void Eruption hits all creatures within 20 ft of shrine including charmed thrall derro.
If thralls are positioned near the altar, use Compel Thrall (BA) to push them out before
firing Void Eruption, or accept ~9 EV friendly fire per thrall per use."

### Decision tree — dominant strategy by configuration

| Configuration | Optimal Lair Action | Expected Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2+ PCs within 20 ft of shrine | Void Eruption | ~9.6 per PC | Avoid if thralls are near shrine |
| Bazgar alone within 20 ft, others far | Unstable Ground | 75% prone Bazgar | Disrupts tank positioning |
| No PC within 20 ft of shrine | Unstable Ground vs anyone within 60 ft | 75% prone | VE misses everyone |
| Thralls alive near shrine, PCs spread | Manifest Thralls | < 2 EV total | Only meaningful if thralls at high HP with reliable hit chance |

**Summary:** The lair action set has a clear dominant hierarchy: Void Eruption ≫ Unstable Ground
≫ Manifest Thralls. The third option is essentially unused in this encounter configuration.

### FEEL-007: Lair action first-round timing

Lair action fires at init 20 (losing ties). Initiative modifiers: beholder +3, Bazgar +0 (assumed
low DEX), Marwen +2–3, Sabriel +2. In practice, the beholder likely wins initiative with +3.

Lair action fires at init 20 BEFORE the beholder's own turn (init count 20 is separate from the
beholder's personal initiative). This means in round 1:
- Init 20 (lair action): Void Eruption → Bazgar takes ~9.6 damage before he acts
- Beholder's turn (likely 15–20 range): Multiattack (two tentacle lash + maw on grappled target)
- This creates a 10+ EV opening salvo before Bazgar takes a single action

Against Bazgar (49 HP): combined lair action + multiattack averages:
- VE: 9.6 EV
- Tentacle ×2: 2 × (65% × 14.5) = 2 × 9.4 = 18.8 EV (with Void-Feeding +1 applied)
- Maw: 65% × 21 = 13.7 EV (if tentacle grapple succeeds, Maw has advantage on grapple-escape
  but only if Bazgar tries to escape; Maw itself is still a straight attack roll)
- **Round 1 total EV vs Bazgar: ~42 (lair action + full multiattack)**

Bazgar at 49 HP could be reduced to ~7 HP before taking a single action in round 1. This is on
the threshold of "unfair rather than lethal" flagged by the briefing.

**Assessment:** This is working as designed (party is meant to lose), but the lair action
front-loading Bazgar before he acts feels brutal at the table. The DM should consider: either
(a) the beholder delays its first lair action VE until round 2 (allowing Bazgar one action first),
or (b) accept this as the encounter's intended tempo. Flag for Joe's call.
