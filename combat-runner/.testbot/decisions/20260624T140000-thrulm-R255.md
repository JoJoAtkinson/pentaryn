### Feel analysis

**FEEL-R255-03 (Shrine-touched glass cannon problem):** The shrine-touched has 3 interesting
mechanics — Oath-Breaking Retaliation, Unstable Form advantage, Ancient Resonance. All three
have high trigger thresholds or recharge limitations. Against a level-5 party with 4–5 attacks
per round when Bazgar uses Action Surge, the shrine-touched dies in rounds 1–2 before it gets
a meaningful turn. The "fights harder when hurt" fantasy never pays off if the creature is
killed before it can act.

Options (Joe decides):
- Raise HP to ~65–70 (keeps it alive through round 2)
- Make Oath-Breaking Retaliation happen on the first hit only, not every hit (but roll dice automatically to matter at the table)
- Lower the Unstable Form threshold to 6+ damage so it activates on any decent hit, not just round-ending 10+ burst

**FEEL-R255-04 (Unstable Form advantage wasted on save-based action):** When the shrine-touched
takes 10+ damage and gains advantage on attack rolls, its optimal response is Ancient Resonance
(save-based) — which doesn't use the advantage at all. If it's forced to use Shrine-Axe multiattack
instead (Resonance on cooldown), Unstable Form advantage on a +4 vs AC 18 improves hit rate from
35% to 57%, adding ~2 more damage per attack. This is the only scenario where Unstable Form
matters mechanically. The ability would be better designed to grant advantage on SAVES (more
thematic — shrine power coursing through it makes it harder to affect) or +1d6 damage bonus
instead of attack advantage.

**FEEL-R255-05 (Ancient Resonance recharge vs creature survivability):** Recharge 5 means the
shrine-touched fires Ancient Resonance at most once or twice in a short fight. Combined with
the glass cannon HP problem, the ability often fires zero times. The cone also requires target
clustering — Compass Edge with good positioning can deny the cone. Recommend either lowering
recharge to 4–6 or removing the recharge (treat it as a bonus action the shrine-touched always
has) to ensure it fires before the creature dies.
