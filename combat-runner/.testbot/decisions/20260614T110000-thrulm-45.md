## Phase B — Playtest Simulation

**Scenario:** `thrulm-maw-autocrit-grappled-target`  
**Prelude:** Two tentacles have found Bazgar. The maw opens.

### Setup

- Beholder enters combat from above the altar. Void-Feeding active (+1 attack/damage, adv vs Turn). Attack bonus: +7 on all attacks (base +6 + VF +1).
- Lair action (init 20): Unstable Ground on Bazgar. DC 16 Dex, Bazgar Dex +0 → saves on 16+ (25%). Expected result: prone 75% of the time.
- If prone: melee attacks vs Bazgar gain advantage. +7 with advantage vs AC 18 = 75% hit rate (vs 50% standing).

### Multiattack Sequence (Beholder turn, Bazgar prone)

**TL1 vs Bazgar (advantage, +7 vs AC 18):** P(hit) ≈ 75%. Damage: avg 13 bludgeoning. On hit: grappled (escape DC 16).

**TL2 vs Bazgar (advantage, +7 vs AC 18):** P(hit) ≈ 75%. Second tentacle; grapple state already established. Damage: avg 13 more bludgeoning.

**Maw vs grappled Bazgar:** Auto-crit rider fires if Bazgar is grappled. Still needs to hit (+7 advantage vs AC 18, 75%). On crit: 8d8+3 = avg 39 piercing (doubled dice).

**Combined damage if all three land:** 13 + 13 + 39 = **65 damage** → downs Bazgar (HP 49) in the first beholder multiattack with **~42% probability** (0.75³).

**If TL1 hits but TL2 misses:** Bazgar is grappled (10 ft speed → 0, but can still attack). Maw auto-crit fires if it hits: 39 avg. Total with TL1: 13 + 39 = 52 → still downs Bazgar with ~56% probability (P(TL1 and Maw both land)).

### Key Mechanic Validated

The scenario confirms: Maw MUST target the grappled PC (Bazgar), not pivot to a "third PC." The auto-crit (8d8+3 ≈ 39) vs the normal roll (4d8+3 ≈ 21) is ~18 damage swing — exactly the margin that determines whether Bazgar goes down or survives the round.

### Feel Issues Identified

**FEEL-F45-01 — Maw auto-crit is all-or-nothing: grapple discipline is binary**

If both TLs land and Maw fires into the grappled target, avg 65 damage. If TLs miss or DM incorrectly pivots Maw to a third PC, avg output drops to 13 (one hit) or 0. The encounter's damage ceiling is massively higher than its floor. The auto-crit design is correct and intentional, but the binary nature means a bad TL roll (both miss) = anticlimactic beholder turn with 0 damage. Consider whether the encounter description should note a "floor" strategy for when tentacles miss (e.g., Void Scream repositioning).

**FEEL-F45-02 — No counterplay window between TL and Maw in the same multiattack**

Bazgar cannot interrupt between TL (grapple) and Maw (crit) because multiattack is a single action with no pause. His only pre-Maw counterplay is Sabriel using the Protection fighting style reaction (if she has it) to impose disadvantage on Maw. No other PC reaction interrupts the Maw crit once grapple is established. This is appropriate for a CR 13 boss (it should be scary), but worth noting at table so the DM announces TL hits loudly ("Bazgar is grappled — does anyone react?") before rolling Maw.

**FEEL-F45-03 — Grapple escape decision is low-value for Bazgar**

Bazgar's escape probability: Athletics +7 (STR 18 +4, PB +3) vs DC 16 = 55% chance per attempt (needs 9+). Escaping costs Bazgar's full action on his turn. If he escapes, the beholder re-grapples next round. If he doesn't escape, he suffers Maw auto-crit next round. Expected value: spending an action to escape (and re-provoking re-grapple) is lower value than using Extra Attack to deal damage to the beholder. The grapple trap is well-designed — it forces a lose/lose decision — but the DM should be aware that Bazgar's "optimal" choice is usually to stay grappled and swing rather than escape and be immediately re-grappled.

### Legendary Action Economy (R1)

If Bazgar goes down in the multiattack (TL+TL+Maw), beholder holds 3 LAs through Marwen's turn (Fireball, likely) and Sabriel's turn (LoH or smite). DD fires after Sabriel's R1 action. Beholder gains temp HP (2× spell level stripped, likely 4–6 THP) — negligible vs 110 HP, but the smite pool disruption is the real value.
