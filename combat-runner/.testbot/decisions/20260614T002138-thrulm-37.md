### Feel simulation: LR under Turn Undead pressure

**Party:** Bazgar (Fighter 5, HP 49, AC 18), Marwen (Wizard 5, HP 32, AC 15), Sabriel (divine martial 5, HP 44, AC 19).

**Sabriel's Channel Divinity — Turn Undead (TU):**
- Cost: Channel Divinity (1 use/rest); NOT a spell slot.
- Effect: Each undead within 30 ft that can see/hear Sabriel must make a WIS save. On a fail, the undead is turned for 1 minute (must flee on its turn, can't take reactions or bonus actions other than Dash to flee, can't willingly move closer).
- DC = 8 + PB(3) + CHA modifier. Sabriel CHA assume +3 (CHA 16): DC 14.
- Beholder WIS save: +5. Against DC 14: needs 9+ on d20 → 60% success rate. **40% failure rate.**

Verdict on LR spend: If the beholder fails Turn Undead, it must flee for 1 minute — effectively ending a 3-4 round fight as the party stabilizes and then pursues at full HP. The beholder's EV from fleeing is essentially 0 (cannot deal damage while turned). LR is absolutely correct to spend here. The spend is correct and unremarkable.

---

### Critical finding — DD does NOT counter Turn Undead

**Drain Divinity text:** "loses one spell slot of the highest level it has remaining (**or loses one use of a divine ability if it has no spells**)"

The "divine ability" clause activates ONLY when the target has ZERO spell slots remaining. Against Sabriel at full load (4×L1, 3×L2, 2×L3 at paladin 5), DD strips spell slots first. Even after 2× DD fires in R1-R2, Sabriel retains all L1 slots and her Channel Divinity use is untouched.

**Implication for R1-R2 encounter flow:**
1. R1 DD (post-Sabriel): strips Sabriel's L3 slot. Turn Undead still intact.
2. R2 Sabriel action: Turn Undead (CD). Beholder likely fails (40%), spends LR (3→2).
3. R3 onwards: beholder has 2 LR remaining for other save threats (Fireball DEX, Hold Monster WIS, etc.).

**DD and LR are independent defensive resources.** DD strips smite slots (offensive attrition); LR handles save-based shutdown abilities (Turn Undead, Hold Monster). The tactics text implies DD as the primary anti-divine counter, but the single most disabling ability Sabriel possesses (Turn Undead) bypasses DD entirely while she has any spell slot remaining.

This is a design documentation gap, not a design flaw. The beholder correctly handles Turn Undead with LR, but the tactics text should acknowledge that LR is the actual counter to Turn Undead and DD only addresses slot attrition.
