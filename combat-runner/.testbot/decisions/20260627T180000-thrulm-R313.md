### Key Findings

**FEEL-R313-01 (new): Compel Thrall DC 16 CHA save is vestigial for dominated thralls**

The beholder's Compel Thrall (BA) reads: "the target must succeed on a DC 16 Charisma saving throw or move." The thrall stat block says "Saving Throws: None; dominated mind has no saves." The thrall's CHA is -2; even treating "no saves" as "no proficiency" (not auto-fail), the thrall needs 18+ = 15% success — effectively irrelevant at the table. The flavor text implies the thrall always obeys. However:

- Compel Thrall is *also* usable on non-thrall charmed creatures (e.g., Clay-Shaping derro) who may have better CHA saves. The save is meaningful for those targets.
- For actual dominated thralls, rolling the save adds resolution time with a near-certain outcome.
- The DB entry for `compelled_movement` correctly notes "No saving throw" — but that's the *thrall's own BA*, not Compel Thrall (beholder's BA). The two mechanics are distinct.

**Recommend:** Add parenthetical to beholder's Compel Thrall text: "*(Dominated thralls auto-fail this save — treat as unconditional.)*" Removes ambiguity at the table without changing ability design. DM doesn't need to ask Sabriel's player to roll for the thrall.

**FEEL-R313-02 (new): Compel Thrall + Compelled Movement stacking — thrall moves 90 ft/round, undocumented**

A thrall can move in three separate segments in one round:
1. Beholder's Compel Thrall BA (beholder's turn): 30 ft — happens *before* the thrall's own turn
2. Thrall's standard movement (thrall's turn): 30 ft
3. Thrall's Compelled Movement BA (thrall's turn): 30 ft additional

**Total: 90 ft in a single round** for a creature with 30 ft base speed. This is not noted in the cheat sheet, the tactics block, or the beholder's start-of-turn checklist.

Tactical relevance: A thrall starting the round 80 ft from a PC can close to melee by end of round (Compel Thrall 30 ft, then 60 ft on its own turn). Against a retreating Marwen (wizard attempting to create range), this positioning pressure is substantial — more than the thrall's attack output would suggest.

The interaction is legal per 5e (different sources of movement from different game events on different turns), but DMs running this encounter for the first time will miss it. The compound movement rate creates a better "pursuit blocker" role than the thrall's +0.5 DPR attack implies.

**Recommend:** Add note to cheat sheet "TACTICAL QUICK-SCAN" item 5 (Thralls section): "Thralls can close 90 ft/round if beholder uses Compel Thrall (BA) on its turn AND the thrall uses Compelled Movement (BA) on its own turn — useful against retreating casters."

No DB bug associated — this is a cross-creature interaction requiring human-readable documentation.
