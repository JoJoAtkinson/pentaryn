### NEW FINDING — BUG-18-01: void_ray / disintegration_ray verb collision

`void_ray` verbs: `["void ray", "void", "force ray", "ray (legendary)"]`  
`disintegration_ray` verbs: `["disintegrate", "disintegration", "ray", "force ray", "beam"]`

Verb collision: **"force ray"** appears in both. **"ray"** appears in DR as a standalone; VR only has "ray (legendary)".

**At-table risk:** DM intending to trigger the Void Ray legendary action types "force ray" or "ray" → fires Disintegration Ray instead (incorrect action, consumes DR recharge). "void ray" (exact, two words) is the only unambiguous safe verb.

**Fix (Joe + MCP):** Remove "force ray" from one of the two actions (suggest: remove from `void_ray` since "force ray" is more naturally associated with DR; add an unambiguous unique verb to VR, e.g., "la ray" or "ray la"). Remove "ray" standalone from `disintegration_ray` (or make it "ray (full)" to force two-token match).

### Phase B feel note: LA economy functioning

The beholder's LA budget (3/round) creates genuine decision pressure:
- Tentacle(1)+VR(2) = max off-DD damage (~29 avg) ← what this slice tests
- Hold all 3 for DD ← correct vs Sabriel with slots

The two strategies are incompatible in a single round (both cost exactly 3 LA). DM must commit before Sabriel acts — there is no hedging. This is a well-designed constraint that generates real decisions. **No feel issue here.**

The collision risk is that "force ray" / "ray" aliases send the beholder's LA spend to the wrong action (DR), wasting the recharge and misfiring the LA. A misfired DR counts as the beholder's main action for the round (consuming it), while the intended VR was a legendary action (separate economy). The confusion collapses two different action economies — a serious mechanical error at the table.
