## Phase B — Playtest Slice: retreat-threshold

**Setup:** Beholder at 28 HP (below the 30 HP threshold). ~Round 4–5. Assume 2 thrall derro alive (22 HP each), Bazgar in melee, Sabriel near altar, Marwen low HP or down.

### Mechanical simulation

**Beholder's retreat turn:**
- **Move (action):** Hover fly 30 ft toward lower shaft entrance.
- **Shrine-Drift (BA):** Pass through any obstructing creature or object, move up to 30 ft more. In-chamber damage if it clips an object at turn end: 5 (1d10) force. At 28 HP this is a ~18% HP cost — survivable once but not sustainable.
- **Legendary Move (1 LA) during next PC's turn:** Another 30 ft repositioning.

Total displacement: up to 90 ft over its own turn + one LA burn, enough to reach the lower shaft entrance from anywhere in the main chamber.

**Thrall blocking during retreat:**
- Beholder cannot use Shrine-Drift (BA) AND Compel Thrall (BA) on the same turn. Both are bonus actions.
- **BUG-RETREAT-BA-01 (new):** Tactics text (beholder-thrulm.md) says "retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit." This mechanic requires Compel Thrall (BA) to redirect thralls. But retreat also requires Shrine-Drift (BA) for obstacle-phasing. These share the bonus action slot — the DM must choose one. The tactics text implies both are available simultaneously; they are not.
- **Workaround:** Pre-position thralls before retreat via lair action. On init 20, Manifest Thralls gives charmed thralls within 60 ft a free reaction attack or move. This is the *only* way to set up blocking without sacrificing Shrine-Drift.

### Feel analysis

**FEEL-RETREAT-01 (new):** Retreat is mechanically decisive but dramatically flat. Shrine-Drift lets the beholder pass through the chamber ceiling or walls — the party has zero melee interception. At level 5, none of the Compass Edge PCs have fly speed:
- Bazgar: grounded (no fly).
- Marwen: can Misty Step (BA, 30 ft) but is likely low HP; Fireball could punish the beholder if it stays in line-of-sight through a shaft opening.
- Sabriel: no fly speed (divine martial without Wings or similar).

The encounter effectively ends when the beholder decides to leave. There is no dramatic chase. The retreating beholder can still fire Disintegration Ray (if recharged) while moving — this is interesting but the action is consumed on the ray, not on an attempt to *create tension* around escape.

**Recommendation (log only — design call):** Describe the lower shaft entrance as a physical traversable opening (e.g., a carved stairway or a 10-ft-diameter shaft in the floor), not a wall the beholder phases through. This transforms the retreat into a race: the beholder must fly across 60 ft of open chamber to reach the entrance, giving the party one round of opportunity attacks (Bazgar if it passes within 5 ft) and concentrated ranged fire. Shrine-Drift stays useful as a "pass through the altar rubble" tool for the last 10–15 ft of the approach, not as a wall-escape.

**FEEL-RETREAT-02 (new):** The below-20-HP behavior (stat block: "attempts to crush/disintegrate the party while fleeing") conflicts with speed-prioritized retreat. A beholder using its action on Disintegration Ray while retreating moves only 30 ft that turn (Shrine-Drift BA only, no action movement). The party likely can't close 30 ft in one round against a flier, so this feels correct — the beholder buys time for one final threat. But Disintegration Ray (recharge 5-6) may already be spent. If it isn't recharged, the <20-HP turn becomes "Beholder moves 30 ft and does... nothing notable." Flag: lore-dramatic moment (below 20 HP = desperation mode) has no guaranteed mechanical expression. Consider adding a 1/rest panic multiattack or guaranteed void eruption at this threshold.
