### Scenario Setup

Simulate beholder acting first (initiative 20+). Full sequence: lair action → multiattack (2× Tentacle Lash + Maw) → bonus action → legendary actions after each PC turn. Party enters fresh (full HP). Track HP, grapple state, death-save risk.

**Starting HP:** Bazgar 49, Marwen 32, Sabriel 44. Beholder 110.

**Initiative order (high-roll beholder, beholder wins):**
Beholder 22, Marwen 13, Sabriel 12, Bazgar 11. *(Represents ~60-80% of combats given beholder DEX +3 vs party DEX +2/+0.)*

---

### Lair Action — Initiative Count 20 (before beholder's turn)

**Beholder chooses Void Eruption** (EV dominant over Unstable Ground or Manifest Thralls per FEEL-R230-01). Bazgar and Sabriel within 20 ft of shrine (they engaged to reach melee range). Marwen at 40 ft (back-line; excluded).

DC 16 DEX saves:
- **Bazgar** (DEX +0, needs 16+, P(fail)=75%): d20 = 5 → FAIL. 11 (2d10) force. Bazgar: 49 → **38 HP**.
- **Sabriel** (DEX +2 via AC 19 heavy armor but DEX is likely low; treating as DEX +0 for heavy armor baseline, needs 16+, P(fail)=75%): d20 = 11 → FAIL. 11 force. Sabriel: 44 → **33 HP**.

Lair action totals: 22 damage split across two PCs before any PC acts.

### Beholder Main Turn (Void-Feeding active: +1 to all attacks/damage)

**Effective attack bonus: +7 (+6 base +1 Void-Feeding)**

**Multiattack — 2× Tentacle Lash + 1 Maw:**

*Tentacle 1 vs Marwen (AC 15, needs 8+, P(hit)=65%):*
d20 = 18 → 25 vs 15 → **HIT.**
Damage: 3d6+4 (Void-Feeding baked in): (4+1+3)+4 = 12 bludgeoning. Marwen: 32 → **20 HP.** Marwen **GRAPPLED** (escape DC 16).

*Tentacle 2 vs Bazgar (AC 18, needs 11+, P(hit)=50%):*
d20 = 14 → 21 vs 18 → **HIT.**
Damage: 3d6+4: (2+5+1)+4 = 12 bludgeoning. Bazgar: 38 → **26 HP.** Bazgar **GRAPPLED** (escape DC 16).

*Maw vs Marwen (grappled; disadvantage on escape checks, not on the beholder's attack):*
+7 vs AC 15 → needs 8+, P(hit)=65%.
d20 = 16 → 23 vs 15 → **HIT.**
Damage: 4d8+4: (3+7+2+6)+4 = 22 piercing. Marwen: 20 → **-2 HP → 0 HP UNCONSCIOUS.**

Massive damage check on Maw: 22 < 32 (Marwen's max HP) → no instant death. Marwen starts death saves with 0 successes, 0 failures.

**Bonus Action — Shrine-Drift:** Beholder repositions 20 ft (phases through an altar column to hover above the shrine at 20 ft height, denying easy melee next round from Bazgar if he doesn't close).

Sabriel must choose: attack the beholder or stabilize Marwen.
*Choice: Lay on Hands 5 HP on Marwen (action).* Marwen wakes at 5 HP, grapple still active.
Sabriel cannot also attack — full action used on healing.

End of Sabriel's turn: Beholder uses **1 LA → Move** (floats upward to 30 ft, out of Sabriel melee range for next round).

**Bazgar's turn (26 HP, grappled):**
Grappled does not impose disadvantage on attack rolls — Bazgar attacks the beholder.
Battlemaster 5, STR likely +4 (18 STR), attack bonus = STR +4 + PB +3 = **+7** vs AC 17 (needs 10+, P(hit)=55%).

Bazgar uses Action Surge (2 attacks normal + 2 attacks surge = 4 total):
- d20 = 15 → 22 vs 17 → **HIT.** 1d8+4 (longsword assumed): (5)+4 = 9 damage.

- d20 = 19 → 26 vs 17 → **HIT.** 1d8+4: (7)+4 = 11 damage. (Spends Battlemaster maneuver — Precision Attack adds d8=5 to one miss? Let's say first miss becomes a hit with +5: 15+5=20 → HIT. 9 damage.)

### Round 2 Preview — Why the Snowball Worsens

**Grapple escape check for Bazgar (grappled):**
If beholder uses its Maw attack on Bazgar next round while he's grappled, Bazgar has disadvantage on all future escape checks. P(escape normal) = 60% (d20+7 vs DC 16). P(escape with disadvantage after Maw) = 0.60² = 36%.

**Marwen at 5 HP, grappled:** Virtually any attack on Marwen (average damage > 5) drops her again. Beholder tentacle avg = 14 > 5. Marwen will be unconscious again on round 2's first tentacle. Sabriel's Lay on Hands has 25 total HP remaining (used 5 in round 1); she could spend another 5 to save Marwen again, but that costs another action and leaves Sabriel unable to attack for 2 rounds.

**Sabriel at 11 HP:** Void Ray at end of her turn (round 2) with DC 16 DEX. P(fail)≈75% → 22 avg force → Sabriel: 11 → -11 HP → 0 HP unconscious. Or if she saves (half = 11): 11 → 0 HP. Either way, Sabriel is at 0 HP after round 2's LA if she doesn't put HP between herself and the beholder.

The party is likely at or near a wipe by end of round 2. This is consistent with design intent (party meant to lose).

### Key Observations

**1. (CONFIRMED HIGH — FEEL-R224-01) Marwen is KO'd in round 1 before acting.**

The sequence "beholder wins initiative → lair action → Tentacle (12 dmg) + Maw (22 dmg) on Marwen" totals 34 damage, which exceeds Marwen's max HP (32). In the simulation she went from 32 → 20 → 0 without ever taking a turn. There is *no initiative configuration* (short of Marwen rolling a natural 20 and going before the lair action, P≈5%) that prevents this outcome when the beholder targets her with both tentacle and Maw.

This has been confirmed by prior fires. Noting the specific Tentacle+Maw math for permanence: the beholder's Tentacle avg (14 with Void-Feeding) + Maw avg (22 with Void-Feeding) = 36 > 32. Even below average: a 7-damage Tentacle + 18-damage Maw = 25, which doesn't KO. But average is above Marwen's cap.

---

**2. (CONFIRMED HIGH — FEEL-R224-03) Drain Divinity is suboptimal in round 1.**

Void Ray (2 LA) EV against Sabriel (P(fail DEX save DC 16)≈75% for low-DEX heavy armor): 0.75×22 + 0.25×11 = 16.5+2.75 = **19.25 avg force damage.** Plus 1 Move LA.

Drain Divinity (3 LA, requires proximity — beholder at 30 ft up after Shrine-Drift, Sabriel at ground level; 30 ft is on the cusp): if beholder descends, it's within Sabriel's melee range. DC 16 CHA save. Divine martial 5, CHA modifier unknown from roster (not stated). Assuming CHA +2 (paladin-adjacent): P(fail)=70%. Loses 3rd-level slot; beholder gains 6 THP. EV of THP: 0.70×6 = **4.2 avg THP gained**. No damage to Sabriel at all.

Void Ray (2 LA + 1 Move LA) = 19.25 avg damage dealt AND beholder repositions. Drain Divinity (3 LA) = 4.2 avg THP gained, no damage, and the beholder must stay within 30 ft (melee exposure). The slot denial is non-trivial strategically but cannot compensate for a 15 avg EV gap in round 1. FEEL-R224-03 and FEEL-R228-01 both confirmed.

---

**3. (CONFIRMED — grapple snowball) Two simultaneous grapples by round-1 Multiattack is the norm, not the exception.**

Tentacle 1 (P(hit) vs Marwen AC 15 = 65%) → grapple ✓
Tentacle 2 (P(hit) vs Bazgar AC 18 = 50%) → grapple if hit ✓

P(both grapple in round 1) = 0.65 × 0.50 = 32.5%.
P(at least one grapple) = 1 - (0.35)(0.50) = 82.5%.

Once two creatures are grappled, the Maw's "disadvantage on escape attempts" rider means neither can easily break free. With Sabriel also dealing with Void Ray damage (likely 22 force), the party has zero healing budget left for escape attempts.

Round-1 triple-grapple scenario: beholder uses Tentacle legendary LA to grapple Sabriel too if she's in reach. P(LA tentacle hits Sabriel AC 19) = 45%. If she's within 10 ft: possible. P(all 3 PCs grappled by end of round 1) = ~32.5% × 45% ≈ **14.6%.** (BUG-R226-03 confirmed — 15% triple grapple with zero counterplay remains open.)

---

**4. (NEW FEEL — FEEL-R233-01 / LOW) Lair action double-strike window is nearly guaranteed.**

Beholder at DEX +3 initiative vs party at DEX +2 (Marwen, Sabriel) and DEX +0 (Bazgar). P(beholder wins vs DEX+2 PC) = P(beholder d20+3 > PC d20+2) ≈ 50%+50%×(1/20) ≈ **53% per individual**. With three PCs, the beholder has a very high rate of acting before at least some of them.

More critically: lair action fires at IC 20 (losing ties). For the lair action to fire AFTER a PC, that PC must roll exactly 18+ on d20 (total init ≥ 20) — only DEX+2 PCs can even tie, requiring d20 roll of 18+. P(Marwen or Sabriel rolls 18+) = 15% each = P(neither) = 72.25%. **In ~72% of combats, all three PCs go below IC 20 and the lair action fires before any PC acts.**

The lair action + beholder main turn effectively constitutes a "two-turn opener" for the beholder in most combats. The party cannot disrupt or react to this. This is *not a bug* (it's consistent with lair action rules) but it reinforces FEEL-R224-01's lethality analysis and is worth documenting explicitly.

*Design note: If Joe ever wants to create a counterplay window, a "round-1 no lair action" rule or "lair actions don't fire until all PCs have acted once" variant would solve this without changing the beholder's stats.*
