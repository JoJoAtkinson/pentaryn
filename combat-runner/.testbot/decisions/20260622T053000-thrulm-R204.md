### Drain-divinity-vs-sabriel simulation — notable dynamics

**Sabriel response if grappled and at 8 HP, round 1:**
She cannot flee (speed 0 from grapple). Her options: attack the beholder, or heal herself.
- Lay on Hands (paladin, 25 HP pool): standard action to restore HP → Sabriel back to 18+ HP. No attack this turn.
- OR: attack while grappled (one attack, grapple doesn't prevent attacking) + hope for lucky hit. No healing.

If she heals → Drain Divinity strips her highest slot at end of her turn (45% chance). She regains 10–15 HP and loses a 3rd-level smite slot. Net: she survived this round, but at a cost.

If she attacks → Drain Divinity still fires (45% chance). She dealt ~10 expected dmg to the beholder (hit rate vs AC 17: 55%). She's still at 8 HP entering round 2.

**FEEL-R204-01 (NEW — LOW): Drain Divinity creates a "damned either way" feel for Sabriel.**
If she attacks, the slot strip feels unfair (she didn't get to use it first). If she heals, the slot strip cancels part of her recovery. The intended dramatic tension ("your divine power is being devoured") lands, but the mechanical delivery is invisible — the GUI prompt for Drain Divinity fires as a utility with no roll output, so the DM must manually narrate that a slot was taken. Players don't see a save roll or a failed save; they just see a narration line saying their slot was removed. This makes Drain Divinity feel like a DM fiat rather than a contested mechanic. Extends BUG-R197-01 (no save rolled) into a feel-impact note.
