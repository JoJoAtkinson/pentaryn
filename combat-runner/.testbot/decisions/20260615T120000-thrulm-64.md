## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `lair-action-round`

**Slice:** `lair-action-round` (rotation.yml index 5)
**Title:** "Lair action decision tree (init 20)"

### Setup

| Creature | HP | AC | DEX | Init |
|----------|----|----|-----|------|
| Bazgar (Fighter/BM 5) | 49 | 18 | −1 | −1 |
| Marwen (Wizard 5) | 32 | 15 | +2 | +2 |
| Sabriel (Divine Martial 5) | 44 | 19 | +0 | +0 |
| Thrall 1 | 22 | 14 | +1 | +1 |
| Thrall 2 | 22 | 14 | +1 | +1 |
| Beholder | 110 | 17 | +3 | +3 |

**Scenario state (per rotation.yml brief):**
- 2 thrall derro alive, full HP, within 60 ft of beholder.
- Bazgar within 20 ft of shrine.
- Sabriel and Marwen positions unspecified — assumed 30+ ft from shrine (back-line/mid-field).
- Initiative count 20: lair action fires.

**Thrall position question (not specified in brief):**
Two sub-cases are meaningful. The scenario spec says "both derro within 60 ft of beholder" but not whether they're within 20 ft of the altar stone. Sub-case A = thralls in altar zone (≤20 ft from shrine); Sub-case B = thralls outside altar zone (>20 ft from shrine).

---

### Option 1: Unstable Ground (target Bazgar)

**Mechanics:**
- DC 16 DEX save. Bazgar DEX −1 → needs 17+ to save. P(fail) = 80%.
- On fail: Bazgar falls **prone**.
  - Prone effects: ½ movement to stand (15 ft from 30 ft speed = 15 ft remaining); disadvantage on attack rolls against targets not within 5 ft; ranged attacks against him also at disadvantage.
  - At beholder's typical hover altitude (40-60 ft), beholder is NOT within 5 ft of a ground-level prone Bazgar — **beholder does not gain melee advantage vs the prone Bazgar from UG at high altitude**. See FEEL-F64-03 below.
- No friendly fire risk to thralls.
- Reusable every round.

**Expected value:** 80% chance of significant tactical disruption (mobility cost + attack disadvantage on non-adjacent targets). Zero damage. Ongoing effect that stacks with grapple if beholder descends later.

---

### Option 2: Manifest Thralls (2 thralls alive, both in range)

**Mechanics:**
- Each thrall gains beholder CHA modifier (CHA 13 → +1) in temporary HP = **1 THP each**.
- Each thrall may immediately use its **reaction** to move OR make a weapon attack.

**Reaction ATTACK analysis (vs AC 18/19):**
- Thrall Hand Axe (Weak): +1 to hit. Vs Bazgar AC 18: needs 17+. P(hit) = 20%. Avg dmg 2 (1d4).
- 2 thralls vs Bazgar: 2 × 20% × 2 = **0.8 avg damage**. Negligible.
- Vs Marwen (AC 15): needs 14+. P(hit) = 35%. 2 × 35% × 2 = **1.4 avg damage**. Still negligible.

**Reaction MOVE analysis (underexplored — see FEEL-F64-01 below):**
- Thralls can move up to 30 ft via reaction (Compelled Movement bonus action also available on their own turn, but reaction move happens NOW at init 20).
- A thrall can reposition to: (a) flank Bazgar (creating OA threat if he ignores thrall), (b) move OUT of the 20-ft VE zone to enable VE on a future round without friendly fire, (c) close to Marwen as a soft-target threat.
- The reaction move is tactically more interesting than the reaction attack against AC 18+ targets.

**Expected value (reaction attack path):** 1 THP + 0.8–1.4 avg damage. Weakest option. Per existing tactics notes, MT is explicitly flagged as wasteful against AC 18+ (FIX-BEL3-38-A).

---

### Option 3: Void Eruption

**Mechanics:**
- All creatures within 20 ft of shrine: DC 16 DEX save, 11 (2d10) force on fail, half on success.
- Hits ALL creatures in zone — no friendly fire immunity.

**Sub-case A (thralls within 20 ft of shrine):**

| Target | DEX mod | P(fail) | Avg dmg (fail 11, success 5.5) | Expected |
|--------|---------|---------|-------------------------------|---------|
| Bazgar | −1 | 80% | 0.80×11 + 0.20×5.5 | **9.9 avg** |
| Thrall 1 | +1 | 70% | 0.70×11 + 0.30×5.5 | **9.35 avg** |
| Thrall 2 | +1 | 70% | 0.70×11 + 0.30×5.5 | **9.35 avg** |

Total damage dealt: Bazgar 9.9 + Thrall 1 9.35 + Thrall 2 9.35.
**Friendly fire: 18.7 avg damage to own forces.** Thralls go from 22 HP to ~12.7 HP each. One more hit from a PC and they're dead; the beholder is actively weakening its own meatshields.

**Sub-case B (thralls outside 20-ft zone):**

| Target | DEX mod | P(fail) | Expected |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Bazgar | −1 | 80% | **9.9 avg** |

Only Bazgar is affected. No friendly fire. **9.9 avg damage** with no downside.

### Decision Comparison

| Option | Bazgar damage | Friendly fire | Ongoing effect | Table interest |
|--------|--------------|--------------|----------------|---------------|
| UG | 0 | None | Yes (prone, mobility cost) | High — player faces stand/swing-prone choice |
| MT | 0.8 avg | None | No | Low — attack is near-miss; reaction move underappreciated |
| VE (sub-B, no thralls) | 9.9 avg | None | No | Medium — reliable chip, no decision for player |
| VE (sub-A, thralls present) | 9.9 avg | 18.7 avg to own forces | No | Low — damages own forces; suboptimal |

**Optimal choice:** UG or VE depending on thrall position.
- If thralls are outside the 20-ft zone: **VE** is marginally better raw-damage-per-lair-action than UG prone (9.9 avg vs 0 damage). However, UG creates richer player-facing decisions.
- If thralls are in the zone: **UG** is unambiguously better — VE damages own forces for an equivalent expected PC HP result.

**Non-optimal choice:** Manifest Thralls (reaction attack path) is clearly dominated by both alternatives at this party's AC levels. Per existing tactics notes (FIX-BEL3-38-A), this is already documented. The reaction MOVE path is not documented.

### Feel Assessment

**What worked as designed:**
- The three-way decision is genuinely interesting for a DM to navigate — each option has a clear use case.
- UG's 80% failure rate on Bazgar is consistent with design intent (low-DEX fighter is the preferred target).
- VE's 9.9 avg damage is meaningful in the context of a fight designed to go 3-5 rounds; chip damage accumulates.

**FEEL-F64-01 (NEW — Log only): Manifest Thralls reaction MOVE is undocumented and potentially stronger than reaction ATTACK at this party AC.**

The MT tactics notes only evaluate the option via reaction ATTACK (20-35% hit rate, ~1-1.4 avg damage). Reaction MOVE is never evaluated. Against AC 18+ targets, reaction MOVE is the stronger use:
1. **Forced disengage threat:** A thrall moving adjacent to Marwen (AC 15, back-line) forces her to account for a melee threat. She either provokes an OA trying to back away or spends movement on tactical reposition.
2. **VE setup:** Thralls can use reaction move to exit the 20-ft shrine zone, enabling the beholder to safely use VE on a future round (see FEEL-F64-02).
3. **Compel Thrall (BA) synergy:** On the beholder's own turn, Compel Thrall (BA) can move a thrall up to 30 ft — in the same round as MT, both abilities can fire, repositioning two thralls simultaneously (one via MT reaction move, one via CT on beholder's subsequent turn). This double-reposition is tactically significant and not documented.

Recommend adding a sentence to MT tactics: *"Against AC 18+ targets where reaction attack hit rate falls below 25%, use reaction MOVE instead — reposition thralls to threaten back-line PCs or exit the VE zone for next round."*

**FEEL-F64-02 (NEW — Log only): VE vs friendly fire dependency is completely undocumented.**

The tactics note for VE says: *"Rotate to Void Eruption once melee fighters cluster within 20 ft of the shrine (R3+)."* This advice implicitly assumes the thralls have been killed or moved out of the altar zone by R3. If 2 thralls are still alive and positioned near the altar at R3, VE at init 20 deals 18.7 avg damage to the beholder's own forces — more friendly fire than PC damage.

The note should be updated with: *"Only fire VE if no living friendly NPCs (thralls) are within 20 ft of the shrine. If thralls are present in the zone, use UG or MT (reaction move to exit) instead."*

Without this caveat, a DM following the "rotate to VE at R3+" note will self-damage their own meatshields repeatedly in fights where thralls survive to R3.

**FEEL-F64-03 (NEW — Log only): UG melee-advantage synergy requires beholder altitude ≤5 ft and is never achievable at the default 40-60 ft hover.**

PHB p.292: melee attacker within **5 ft** of a prone creature has advantage. The beholder hovers 40-60 ft above the shrine floor. Even at minimum hover (40 ft), the beholder is 40 ft from Bazgar standing on the ground — 35 ft beyond the 5-ft threshold.

This means UG prone provides ZERO melee-attack advantage to the beholder at its default altitude. The only mechanical benefits of UG prone at high altitude are:
1. Bazgar spends 15 ft to stand (costs him movement, potentially preventing a full charge).
2. Bazgar's attacks have disadvantage vs creatures he is not adjacent to (e.g., ranged attacks or reach-weapon attacks against elevated beholder).

The tactics note (FIX-FC39-B) describes prone as "sustained melee disruption" implying beholder melee advantage — but this is only achievable if the beholder descends to within 5 ft of Bazgar. At the default hover altitude, UG is a **mobility tax**, not a **melee-setup**.

Compare: VE at high altitude works fine (force damage doesn't require proximity). UG at high altitude is weaker than it appears in the tactics text. The note should clarify that UG's "melee advantage" synergy requires the beholder to have already descended.

This is the same class as FEEL-F62-01 (VS range requires descent) and BUG-A12-06 (DD range requires descent). All three implicate the same root problem: the beholder's 40-60 ft hover altitude creates a dead zone where save-based abilities work but melee-synergy effects do not.
