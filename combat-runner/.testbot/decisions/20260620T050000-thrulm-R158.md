### New Phase A flags this run

#### TECH-R158-01 — `"hand axe"` dispatches via prefix, not verb list (HARDENING NOTE)

The `hand_axe_weak` action has verbs: `["attack", "axe", "hit", "swing", "strike"]`. The input `"hand axe"` is NOT in this verb list. The dispatch succeeds via **strategy 3 (prefix match)**: the normalized action name `"hand axe weak"` starts with `"hand axe"`, yielding a unique prefix match.

This dispatch path works correctly today but is fragile:
- If the action is ever renamed (e.g., `weak_hand_axe`), "hand axe" would fail to match and fall through to difflib or LLM.
- The scenario `thrulm-thrall-compelled-move` relies on this path silently.

**Disposition:** LOG. Recommend adding `"hand axe"` to the verb list explicitly for robustness. Requires `combat_action_upsert` in an interactive MCP session.

#### FEEL-R158-02 — Beholder BA conflict: Compel Thrall vs Shrine-Drift undocumented

The beholder has two bonus actions: **Shrine-Drift** (phase movement, always useful for repositioning + avoiding melee) and **Compel Thrall (1/turn)** (redirect a charmed thrall). These compete for the single BA slot.

In this simulation:
- Round 1: Used Compel Thrall. Could NOT use Shrine-Drift.
- Round 2: Used Shrine-Drift (Compel Thrall skipped). Thralls moved on their own Compelled Movement BA instead.

The beholder used Shrine-Drift in 1 of 2 rounds, despite Compel Thrall being available. **Shrine-Drift is almost always higher value** because:
- Beholder repositioning optimizes attack angles (ranged attacks, range to casters)
- Shrine proximity = Void-Feeding (+1 atk/dmg)
- Compel Thrall grants 30 ft forced movement on a charmed creature — the same creature can use Compelled Movement (its own BA) for another 30 ft without the beholder spending anything

**Net assessment:** The beholder should default to Shrine-Drift. Compel Thrall is only better when the beholder is already well-positioned AND needs a thrall to immediately reach a target before the thrall's own initiative. The tactics section doesn't document this prioritization.

**Disposition:** LOG. Add to beholder tactics: "Compel Thrall (BA) and Shrine-Drift (BA) compete for the same slot. Default: Shrine-Drift. Use Compel Thrall only when: (1) beholder is already optimally positioned AND (2) a thrall needs immediate repositioning before its own turn, AND (3) the thrall's own Compelled Movement BA isn't sufficient."

#### TECH-R158-02 — Double-move interaction (Compel Thrall BA + Compelled Movement BA) undocumented

- Beholder's Compel Thrall (BA): forces a thrall to move 30 ft (on the beholder's turn)
- Thrall's Compelled Movement (BA): thrall moves 30 ft per beholder's direction (on the thrall's own turn)

Together + the thrall's standard 30 ft movement: **up to 90 ft total displacement per round.** This creates a surprisingly mobile minion.

Practical use: Drop a thrall 30 ft onto an escaping PC using Compel Thrall (round N), then thrall's turn: moves 30 ft more into blocking position + attacks.

This combo is never mentioned in cheat sheet or tactics but could produce table-surprise if the DM (or a sharp player) realizes the full 90 ft movement potential.

**Disposition:** LOG. Add DM note: "Maximum thrall displacement per round: 90 ft (30 Compel Thrall + 30 Compelled Movement + 30 standard move). Costs beholder's BA slot. Use to intercept fleeing PCs."
