#### FRIGHTENED cascade analysis (NEW finding)

FRIGHTENED (1 minute): disadvantage on attack rolls while beholder in LoS; cannot willingly move closer to beholder.

Bazgar ongoing WIS save vs DC 16 at +0: 70% fail per turn → **average 2.9 rounds** before breaking free.

During those rounds:
- Bazgar attacks at **disadvantage** while beholder in LoS. Against AC 17 (beholder), his +6–+8 attack at disadvantage drops hit rate from ~50% to ~30% — losing ~40% of his melee damage contribution.
- He **cannot move closer** if the beholder retreats or Shrine-Drifts. Once the beholder positions above the altar with Void-Feeding, Bazgar may be 40 ft away and cannot close without ending FRIGHTENED first.
- Combined with FEEL-F13-02 (prone R1 from lair UG, already disadvantaged) and FEEL-F16-02 (beholder may nova before Bazgar acts), the fighter tank's effective first 3–4 combat rounds are: **R1 prone/grappled, R2+ frightened/disadvantaged**. Bazgar contributes almost nothing offensively until R4+ if he fails 3 consecutive WIS saves.

Sabriel has higher WIS but similar outcome at 50% fail. If both are frightened and the beholder retreats behind the shrine column (30 ft Shrine-Drift BA):
- Bazgar: can't close (FRIGHTENED + grapple check eats his action).
- Sabriel: could end FRIGHTENED by making a save (50% per turn), then close at normal speed next turn. But that's 2–3 turns before Sabriel can land a sanctified weapon hit — by which point the fight may be over.

**Implication:** VS + retreat is a soft "reset to range" that effectively ends all melee threat from the party for 2+ rounds. The beholder's DR, VR, and Void-Feeding bonuses all favor ranged targeting. Once the melee PCs are frightened and the caster (Marwen) is downed, the fight is Sabriel vs a ranged beholder at 40+ ft — which Sabriel cannot win alone.

#### Single-HP overshoot analysis (NEW finding)

At full HP, VS fails Marwen by exactly 1 point (32 HP − 33 damage = −1). This means:
- Any prior damage to Marwen (even 1 HP) makes VS lethal on a failed save regardless.
- If R1 Shardcaller hits Marwen for 5 (crossbow) or R1 Rager hits for 7, Marwen enters VS R2 at ≤27 HP.
- Against 27 HP Marwen: failed save = dead; saved = 11 HP remaining.
- Against 16 HP Marwen (two prior hits): failed save = dead; saved = 0 HP downed anyway (half = 16 = 16 HP, Marwen at 0 HP = downed).

A Marwen who took **any damage** in R1 is almost certainly downed by VS R2 (success or fail). The threshold is so tight that Marwen is effectively a one-round combatant in this encounter unless she receives healing before R2. Sabriel has no bonus-action heal (LoH is an action). The only way to heal Marwen between R1 and R2 is if Sabriel uses her R1 action on LoH instead of attacking — which sacrifices all offensive pressure that round.

This creates a binary triage choice for Sabriel R1: **attack or protect Marwen**. Either way, one resource (offensive or HP buffer) is lost.
