## Phase B — Manual playtest: lair-action-round

*Rotation.yml slice 5. Tests: lair action decision tree at init 20, choice between Unstable Ground / Manifest Thralls / Void Eruption, DM decision depth, and tactical value of each option.*

---

### Encounter setup

- Beholder HP 110, AC 17, Void-Feeding active (+1 atk/dmg; +7 total attack bonus inside chamber)
- 2 Thrall Derro alive (HP 22, AC 14 each), within 60 ft of beholder
- Round 2 or 3: Bazgar has advanced to within 20 ft of the shrine (prone or standing)
- Sabriel at ~25 ft from altar (advancing); Marwen at ~35–40 ft (staying back)

---

### Initiative 20 lair action fires — three choices

**Option A: Unstable Ground on Bazgar (DC 16 Dex, prone on fail)**

Bazgar (Orc Fighter 5, DEX −1): d20−1 vs DC 16. Needs 17+. P(success) = 20%, P(prone) = 80%.

*If prone:*
- Bazgar attacks at **disadvantage** until he stands (half movement = 15 ft of his 30-ft speed to stand).
- Beholder's ranged attacks vs prone Bazgar = **disadvantage** (PHB, FIX-EV7-34-A). DR and Void Ray should NOT target prone Bazgar — negates the benefit.
- Tentacle Lash (reach 10 ft, if beholder descends): attack **with advantage** vs prone target. Synergy with multiattack.
- Thrall melee attacks vs prone Bazgar: advantage (+1 hit with advantage ≈ 35–40% vs AC 18 instead of 20%). Not decisive but a real improvement.

**Expected value:** ~80% procs disadvantage on a level 5 fighter's full attack action (2 attacks at disadvantage = significant DPR reduction). Best outcome is pairing with beholder multiattack descending to tentacle range for the advantage-on-Tentacle-Lash crit fishing.

**Option B: Manifest Thralls (2 thralls get +1 THP, bonus reaction attack)**

Beholder CHA +1 → 1 THP per thrall. Thrall Hand Axe (Weak) +1 to hit vs:
- Bazgar AC 18: +1 hit → needs 17+ → 20% hit rate. Expected 2.5 × 0.20 = **0.5 dmg per thrall**.
- Both thralls: **1.0 expected damage total**.

This is the worst option by a wide margin. The reaction attacks have no tactical impact against AC 18+ targets. The 1 THP is noise.

*Confirmed useless (carries DD-MT, first flagged fire #1).*

**Option C: Void Eruption (DC 16 Dex, 2d10 force, all within 20 ft of shrine)**

Bazgar (DEX −1, P(fail) = 80%): E(dmg) = 0.80×11 + 0.20×5.5 = **9.9 damage**.
Sabriel (~25 ft from shrine, likely outside 20-ft radius): **not affected** unless she closes.
Thralls if within 20 ft of shrine: DEX +1, P(fail) = 75%. E(dmg) = 0.75×11 + 0.25×5.5 = **9.6 dmg per thrall**. Friendly fire risk if thralls are in the shrine zone.

If only Bazgar is in the 20-ft zone: **9.9 expected damage** from VE. This is higher raw damage than UG but lower tactical disruption (prone from UG removes attacks; VE just chips HP).

---

### Decision matrix

| Option | Expected direct damage | Tactical disruption | DM complexity | Risk |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|---------------|------|
| Unstable Ground | ~0 (no damage) | HIGH — Bazgar attacks at disadvantage, beholder gets tentacle advantage | Low | Beholder can't use DR/VR vs prone Bazgar next turn |
| Void Eruption | ~10 dmg to Bazgar | Moderate — HP pressure, forces saves | Low — but must track who is within 20 ft | Friendly fire on thralls |
| Manifest Thralls | ~1 dmg total | None vs AC 18+ | Medium | Wasted action |

**Optimal play:** Unstable Ground. Bazgar's full-attack action at disadvantage (2 attacks) removes roughly 40–50% of his turn's expected DPR. Pair UG with the beholder's own multiattack (after init 20) for tentacle advantage + Maw auto-crit setup. The total pressure is more valuable than 10 flat damage.

**Most interesting at the table:** Unstable Ground — "The stone buckles!" creates a dramatic moment and forces a player decision (stand up and lose half movement, or stay prone and attack at disadvantage). Void Eruption is also dramatic but is a pure dice roll with no player agency.

### NEW FINDING: Void Eruption dead zone — level 5 party rarely enters the 20-ft radius

**FEEL-F13-01.** The Void Eruption radius is 20 ft from the altar stone. A rational level 5 party fighting a CR 13 beholder will not advance to within 20 ft of the altar:
- The beholder hovers 40–60 ft above the altar, putting it 40–60 ft directly above the 20-ft zone.
- Closing to within 20 ft of the altar means being directly below the beholder with no cover.
- Marwen (AC 15, 32 HP) will not advance within DR range without cover; Sabriel (divine martial) might advance to engage the beholder but stays at the beholder's hover level, not at the altar floor.

In practice, only Bazgar (melee, high HP) would press into the altar zone — and only if he climbs or the beholder descends. From fire #11, Bazgar is unconscious by end of R1 in ~91% of simulated runs. If VE fires at init 20 of R2 and Bazgar is already down, VE targets no standing PCs and the lair action is completely wasted.

**Result:** Void Eruption is likely to fire into an empty zone in R2+ in the majority of runs. Its trigger condition (within 20 ft of shrine) is rarely met against a level 5 party that is either (a) dead or (b) staying at range.

**Suggested fix (design call for Joe):** Widen the VE radius to 30 ft from the altar, or change the trigger to "within 30 ft of the beholder" to make it fire reliably from the beholder's hover position. Alternatively, reframe VE as the lair action that fires when any PC approaches the altar (flavor: the sealed god's void reacts to proximity), keeping the 20-ft trigger but adding a DM note: *"Use VE only when a PC has actually closed to altar range. Otherwise default to Unstable Ground."*

### NEW FINDING: Init-20 UG + beholder multiattack nova — no counterplay window for Bazgar

**FEEL-F13-02.** The lair action fires at initiative count 20. If the beholder acts before Bazgar (likely — beholder DEX +3, Bazgar DEX −1 → beholder wins init in ~80% of contested rolls), Bazgar has no actions between the UG lair and the beholder's multiattack.

Sequence: **Init 20 UG primes Bazgar (prone, disadvantage, 80%)** → **Beholder turn (say init 17) multiattack vs prone Bazgar with tentacle advantage**:

| Attack | Hit rate (advantage vs prone AC 18) | Damage on hit |
|--------|--------------------------------------|---------------|
| Tentacle 1 (grapple) | ~70% | 14 bludgeon |
| Tentacle 2 | ~70% | 14 bludgeon |
| Maw (vs grappled, auto-crit) | ~90% | 8d8+3 avg 39 pierce |

Expected R1 damage on Bazgar (both tentacles hit): **14+14+39 = 67 damage** → Bazgar at −18 HP (0 HP + instant death check from 67 damage vs 49 HP max = −18 → not instant death; goes unconscious, needs 3 death saves). With one tentacle hit: **14+39 = 53 damage** → same result. P(at least one tentacle hits with advantage) ≈ 1−0.30² = **91%**.

Bazgar has not had a single action in this sequence. He entered combat, the floor buckled (init 20, before his turn), and the beholder disassembled him before he could act.

This meets the "unfair rather than lethal" criterion from the briefing: **one-round kill before the player got a turn**. It is consistent with the encounter's "you're meant to lose" design intent, but the specific mechanism (lair action + beholder action sequence, both before Bazgar's turn) removes the player's turn from round 1 entirely if they go 3rd or later in init. Against a level 5 fighter whose core fantasy is "I hit things," this feels bad.

**Note:** Not a bug — UG is working exactly as intended. This is a **feel issue** logged for Joe to decide. Options: (a) accept it as part of "you're meant to lose"; (b) cap UG to "cannot target the same creature the beholder targets on its following turn" (adds counterplay but reduces beholder power); (c) lower UG DC to 14 (less reliable prone = more counterplay windows).

---

### Lair action decision depth — effectively a 2-way choice

**FEEL-F13-03.** Manifest Thralls is the correct choice only when thralls are alive AND facing targets with AC ≤ 14 (thrall +1 to hit = ~45–50% hit rate). Against this party (AC 18/19/15), MT is a non-choice. The DM at the table must still read three options and mentally discard MT every round. Consider adding a DM note:

> *"Skip Manifest Thralls if no thrall is within 60 ft or the party's lowest AC is ≥ 15. It contributes nothing against this party."*

This reduces cognitive load without changing the mechanic.

## Feel issues this fire

### FEEL-F13-01: Void Eruption (20-ft shrine radius) fires into empty space in most runs

VE requires a PC within 20 ft of the altar. Against a level 5 party fighting a CR 13 hovering beholder:
- Bazgar is unconscious by R2 in ~91% of runs (fire #11 confirm).
- Marwen and Sabriel won't advance to within 20 ft of the altar under a live beholder.
- In R1, the lair fires before Bazgar is downed — but Bazgar starts ~30–40 ft from the altar at encounter start. He may not have advanced to within 20 ft yet on the first lair action.

**Result:** VE may never fire in normal play. Suggested trigger expansion: 30 ft, or "within 30 ft of the beholder."

### FEEL-F13-02: UG + beholder multiattack nova — Bazgar downed before acting in R1 (no counterplay)

Init-20 UG (80% prone) followed by beholder multiattack with tentacle-advantage (fires before Bazgar on ~80% of initiative rolls) creates a reliable R1 one-shot: 67 avg damage vs 49 HP max. Bazgar's entire R1 is skipped. Design call for Joe: accept / cap / soften UG DC.

### FEEL-F13-03: Manifest Thralls is a non-choice in this encounter

Against AC 18+ party, MT contributes ~1 expected damage from both thralls' reaction attacks. Three-option decision tree collapses to two. Suggest DM note: skip MT when party lowest AC ≥ 15.
