### Lair action (count 20, round 1)

With no derro to buff via Manifest Thralls and party not yet clustered within 20 ft of shrine, the optimal lair choice is **Unstable Ground** on Bazgar (the melee threat). DC 16 DEX save; Bazgar DEX +3 → needs 13+ → 65% save rate. Expected: Bazgar is prone 35% of the time before his own turn.

### Beholder's main turn — the key decision

**Option A: Disintegration Ray at Marwen (+7 in chamber, 10d8+1 force)**

- Hit probability: +7 vs AC 15 → needs 8+ on d20 → **65% hit rate**
- Damage on hit: 10d8 + 1 (Void-Feeding) = avg 45.5 force
- Marwen HP: 32. Minimum roll on 10d8 = 10; P(10d8 ≤ 31) ≈ 4–5%
- **On hit: ~95% chance of instant death (0 HP → disintegrated)**
- Cannot be resurrected except by true resurrection or wish
- **Overall P(permanent death, round 1):** 0.65 × 0.95 ≈ **62%**

**Option B: Multiattack (two Tentacle Lash + Maw)**

- Requires beholder to be within 10 ft of targets (tentacle reach)
- Beholder's default position is hover 40–60 ft above altar
- Round 1 opener: party likely hasn't closed to melee range
- To use multiattack, beholder must either (a) use Shrine-Drift bonus action to pass through the room and descend, or (b) forgo round 1 multiattack
- If beholder descends pre-action via Shrine-Drift (bonus action), it can then multiattack:
  - Tentacle Lash 1: +7 vs AC (e.g. Sabriel AC 19) → needs 12+ → 45% hit → avg 14+1=15 bludgeoning + grapple (escape DC 16)
  - Tentacle Lash 2: same → 45% hit → avg 15 + grapple
  - Maw: +7 vs AC 19 → 45% hit → avg 22+1=23 piercing
  - Expected total (vs Sabriel): 0.45×(15+15+23) = **24 avg damage** spread over 3 rolls

**Decision analysis:**
There is no meaningful decision here. Disintegration Ray at Marwen (AC 15) is strictly dominant:
- Higher expected damage (45.5 × 0.65 = ~29.6 expected force vs ~24 expected melee)
- 62% chance of permanently removing the party's wizard before she acts
- Requires no repositioning
- Recharge 5–6 means it's not available every turn — spending it round 1 on the softest target is correct

The multiattack option requires a bonus action (Shrine-Drift) and melee range, deals similar or less damage, and produces no permanent consequences. A CR 13 intelligent creature with INT 17 and telepathy 120 ft would never choose multiattack over Disintegration Ray as the round 1 opener.

### Feel assessment

**Is this "unfair rather than lethal"?**

The encounter is designed to be lethal — the party is expected to lose. However, the round-1 Disintegration Ray presents a specific type of lethalness that warrants a note:

1. **No counterplay on the first round.** Marwen cannot cast Mirror Image, Blur, or Shield before she acts because she goes third in initiative. The wizard's defensive options are unavailable when she most needs them. If the DM rolls well on initiative, Marwen is disintegrated before she has touched her spell slots.

2. **Permanent death, no engagement.** A 62% chance of permanently losing a PC before that PC takes a single action is narratively sharp. The player never gets to participate in their own death — there's no "last desperate spell." At a table where permanent death requires consent, this opener will require an OoS check before combat begins.

3. **Degenerate optimal play.** Targeting Marwen (AC 15, 32 HP) is the objectively correct play for the beholder. A DM who knows the statblock will always do this; a DM who improvises may make a less optimal choice. This produces wildly different encounters depending on DM prep, which is a feel issue even if both outcomes are "party loses."

4. **Void-Feeding not in runner DB.** The DM must manually add +1 to every beholder attack roll and damage roll. There is no in-runner reminder. The runner will show `hit: +6 vs AC 15 → 55% hit rate` when the correct in-chamber value is `65%`. Easy to forget at a busy table.

**Legendary action economy check:**
The LA sequence (1: Tentacle, 2: Void Ray = 3 total) is correct and functional. The rotation's concern about "Drain Divinity vs Void Ray vs Move — which is always dominant?" is answerable: **Void Ray is almost always correct** (2 LA, 20+ avg damage) unless Sabriel has spell slots remaining and is within 30 ft (which requires the beholder to descend from default hover). Move LA is rarely worth spending (gives up Tentacle + half Void Ray budget). Drain Divinity (3 LA) is correct only when Sabriel is within 30 ft and has 3rd-level slots — otherwise Void Ray is better damage.

The degenerate Drain Divinity loop (flagged in prior fires) would require the beholder to hover at 30 ft or less to maintain range on Sabriel — positioning itself within melee range of Bazgar. The tactical cost is non-trivial, which makes the ability less degenerate in practice than the math suggests.
