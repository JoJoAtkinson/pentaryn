### NEW STAT-06 — Grapple escape modeled as STR save (not Athletics contest)

The beholder multiattack DB row uses `"apply_condition_on_hit": {"condition": "grappled", "save_dc": 16, "save_ability": "str"}`. Per 5e RAW, grapple escape is a contested check (escape creature's Athletics OR Acrobatics vs grappler's Athletics), not a saving throw. The DB models it as a DC 16 STR save — mechanically simpler for the runner but incorrect per RAW. Impact: STR saves are rarer than Athletics checks for melee-fighter PCs (Bazgar has STR +3, Athletics likely +5 with proficiency, but STR save is also +3 — could go either way). The Athletics-contest model slightly favors the escape attempt; the STR-save model is equivalent for Bazgar but worse for other PC types. Log only — runner simplification, likely intentional.

### Scenario question 1: does drain_divinity surface cleanly as a legendary-cost utility?

**Answer: Partially.** The DB `effect` field correctly states "LEGENDARY (costs 3 actions)" — this text displays when the DM types "drain." The cost is visible. However:
- The runner treats `drain_divinity` as a plain utility action — no LA budget is tracked. The runner won't prevent the DM from firing it twice in a round.
- Typing "drain" on the beholder's own turn violates RAW (LA can only be used at the end of ANOTHER creature's turn). The runner does not enforce this timing restriction.
- **In the scenario script, "drain" appears as step 2 on the beholder's own turn.** This is mechanically wrong (can't use LA on own turn). The scenario is testing runner output, not rules enforcement. DM must know to fire Drain Divinity between PC turns, not during the beholder's action step.

**Recommend:** Add "(must fire BETWEEN other creatures' turns — not on beholder's own turn)" to the DB `effect` text for drain_divinity, tentacle_legendary, and void_ray_legendary. This is text-only and wouldn't require `validate` re-run (no structural change), but fix path is still MCP `combat_action_upsert` session.

### Feel assessment — thrulm-beholder-alpha-strike

**FEEL-11 confirmed (new angle):** The alpha-strike scenario demonstrates the lair action + multiattack combo is the decisive kill mechanism, not a sustained combat. Marwen never gets a turn. Players with low initiative have no decisions before losing a party member. The "unfair rather than lethal" threshold: this is lethal AND unfair (no PC agency). The lair action Unstable Ground is the trigger — it primes the one-round KO by giving the beholder advantage on all three multiattack rolls vs AC 15.

**Mitigations available (suggest for Joe):**
- Option A: Beholder uses first lair action to Manifest Thralls (if any present) rather than Unstable Ground — avoids the prone-guarantee on Marwen.
- Option B: Add a one-round "warning" rule: beholder telegraphs its attention in the surprise round (uses Compel Thrall or Shrine-Drift BA only), delaying the full alpha-strike to Round 2, giving PCs one turn to act.
- Option C: Cap lair action targeting — Unstable Ground can only target creatures that have acted this combat (prevents proning Marwen before she acts). Simple DM rule, no file change needed.

**Option C is the least intrusive:** it keeps everything as-is mechanically but adds a table rule that lair actions can only prone/target a creature after it has taken its first turn. This preserves the beholder's lethality while giving each PC at least one action.

**FEEL-12 carry-forward:** Bazgar's Battlemaster maneuvers confirmed dead vs beholder. In the 2-round sim, Bazgar never gets a useful maneuver in — he either can't reach the beholder (Round 1) or has to stabilize Marwen (Round 1 action used). Goading Attack ruling still needed.
