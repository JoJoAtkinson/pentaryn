## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `antireality-timing`

### Scenario

Bazgar (Fighter-BM 5, +7 to hit, nonmagic greatsword) attacks the beholder (AC 17, reaction = Antireality).

Base hit rate: Bazgar needs 10+ on d20 (+7 vs AC 17) = **55%**.

Antireality (once per round, pre-roll): imposes disadvantage on the triggering attack roll.

Hit rate with disadvantage: P(both dice < 10) = 1 − 0.55² = ~**30%**.

**Expected math:**
- Without Antireality: 6 attacks × 55% = **3.3 expected hits**
- With Antireality on 3 first attacks: (3 × 30%) + (3 × 55%) = 0.9 + 1.65 = **2.55 expected hits**
- Antireality prevents ~0.75 expected hits over 3 rounds = **23% hit-rate reduction on first attack per round**

**Damage prevented (nonmagic weapon):**
- Each hit = avg 2d6+4 = 11 slashing, halved by resistance = ~5.5 HP
- 0.75 prevented hits × 5.5 = **~4 HP total** prevented over 3 rounds vs 110 HP pool

**Damage prevented (sanctified weapon):**
- Resistance bypassed. Each hit = avg 2d6+4+1d8 = ~15.5 HP full damage
- 0.75 prevented hits × 15.5 = **~12 HP total** — meaningful (~11% of beholder HP)

### Feel Analysis

**FEEL-R103-01 (NEW): Antireality wasted when Bazgar already has Taunt disadvantage — no DM prompt.**

In the standard thrulm encounter configuration (Rager + Beholder), a Rager will Taunt Bazgar as a bonus action. If Bazgar attacks the beholder (non-rager target), he already has Taunt disadvantage on that roll. Applying Antireality adds nothing: 5e RAW — multiple disadvantage sources don't stack; you either have disadvantage or you don't.

The DM must check Bazgar's condition flags BEFORE declaring the Antireality reaction. This is a split-second read of the table state under pressure. The beholder.md tactics section and the Antireality stat block note do NOT document this condition check. The rotation slice description (rotation.yml) mentions it, but the DM does not see that file during a live fight.

**Risk:** The beholder wastes its only reaction per round on a condition that provides zero mechanical benefit. Against a Rager-fronted encounter, this could happen 2 of every 3 Bazgar turns (Taunt is a bonus action, Rager is likely within 30 ft, Bazgar is the primary melee target for both beholder and Rager).

**Recommendation:** Add to beholder.md Antireality note: "⚠ Pre-check: if the attacker already has disadvantage from any source (Taunt, prone, etc.), Antireality provides no additional benefit — save the reaction."

**FEEL-R103-02 (NEW): Antireality covers only 1 of 4 attacks on Action Surge turns.**

At level 5, Bazgar has Action Surge (1/short rest). On a surge turn: 4 total attack rolls. Antireality fires on the first only. The other 3 proceed at full 55% hit rate. Expected hits on a surge turn without Antireality: 2.20. With Antireality on first: (1 × 30%) + (3 × 55%) = 0.30 + 1.65 = 1.95. Hit-rate reduction: **11%** — less than half the normal-round 23% reduction.

The surge turn is the highest-threat moment. Antireality is at its least effective precisely when the beholder is under the most pressure. No design fix is needed (reactions are once per round by RAW), but the DM should understand the reaction's ceiling and not count on it as a reliable defense on surge rounds.

**FEEL-R103-03 (NEW): "Bait the reaction" counterplay is unavailable with this party composition.**

A front-liner attacks first to bait the Antireality reaction → then a ranged PC makes an attack roll the beholder can't deflect. This counterplay requires two attack-roll users. Sabriel has melee attacks (could theoretically bait Antireality away from Bazgar). Marwen (Wizard) uses save-DC spells, not attack rolls — she cannot bait or exploit the reaction opening.

With the Compass Edge composition (1 melee fighter, 1 divine melee, 1 save-DC caster), the only realistic bait scenario is: Sabriel attacks first (absorbs Antireality), then Bazgar attacks the same turn at full hit rate. This costs Sabriel initiative order — it works only if Sabriel acts before Bazgar. In the simulated initiative above, Sabriel (15) acts before Bazgar (12), so the bait is available. However, coordinating it requires deliberate tactical communication not prompted by the encounter design.

**FEEL-R103-04 (NEW): Antireality is mechanically significant only vs sanctified weapons; marginal otherwise.**

Against Bazgar's nonmagic greatsword (resistance halved), Antireality prevents ~4 HP over a 3-round fight. Against a sanctified weapon (resistance bypassed, blessed by dwarf-priests per beholder Weaknesses), prevention rises to ~12 HP. The reaction is essentially designed for the sanctified-weapon scenario. Against a default-equipped level 5 Fighter, it is cosmetic defense.

This reinforces the design intent: the beholder is beatable with sanctified weapons, and Antireality is the specific counter to that approach. Without sanctified weapons, the party is forced into save-DC approaches (Marwen's spells) or healing-out (Sabriel's LoH) rather than direct melee damage. The encounter economy works, but the reaction's value is invisible to the DM in the common nonmagic-weapon scenario.

**FEEL-R103-05 (CONFIRM BUG-F7-02): DB trigger fires post-roll on `damage` event — causes two simultaneous table errors.**

The rotation slice explicitly flags BUG-F7-02: "DB entry still shows old text (+2 AC)." This simulation confirms the compounded error:
1. **Wrong timing:** The runner prompts Antireality AFTER the attack resolves (on the `damage` event). At this point, imposing disadvantage is retroactive and impossible — the dice have already been rolled.
2. **Wrong effect:** The runner displays "+2 AC" not "disadvantage on this roll." Even if a DM sees the prompt and tries to apply it, they apply the wrong mechanic.

A DM who relies on the runner's Antireality prompt will: (a) apply it after the roll (retroactive, invalid) AND (b) apply "+2 AC" (wrong effect). The reaction will never function correctly through the runner UI until BUG-F7-02 is fixed. DMs must declare Antireality manually before any dice are rolled — the runner cannot assist.

This bug is not a display quirk; it actively misleads. The stat block note (FIX-F33-A) documents the workaround, but the runner's prompt will override the DM's attention.

### Legendary Action Economy (observed)

- Tentacle LA (1 LA): +7 vs AC 18 (Void-Feeding in chamber) = 50% hit → avg 6.5 expected HP + grapple rider. Grapple enables Maw auto-crit (8d8+3 avg 39 pierce) on next main turn.
- Void Ray LA (2 LA): DC 16 DEX vs Bazgar (DEX −1) → 80% fail → 22 avg force × 0.8 = **17.6 expected HP**. Best single expected-HP LA spend in the kit.
- Drain Divinity LA (3 LA): DC 16 CHA vs Sabriel (+5) → 50% fail → strip 1 slot (avg L2, 4 temp HP) + 0.5 slot stripped. Expected: 2 temp HP + 0.5 slots. At this cost, Tentacle+VoidRay (1+2 LA = same budget, ~24 expected HP) is strictly better for raw damage when Sabriel's slot pool is already depleted or she is out of range.

DD remains dominant only in R1 before Sabriel has spent smite slots. The "hold all 3 LA for DD post-Sabriel" protocol (FIX-FC40-A) is correct when: Sabriel's smite pool is intact AND she is within 30 ft of the beholder. Per FEEL-R102-03 (prior fire), Sabriel is frequently out of range in R1 if the party enters at threshold. The protocol's applicability is narrower than stated.
