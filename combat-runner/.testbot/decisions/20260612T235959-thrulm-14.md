## Phase B — Manual playtest: retreat-threshold

*Rotation.yml index 6. Tests: retreat mechanics at 28 HP (below the 30-HP threshold), Shrine-Drift as escape tool, thrall-blocking tactic viability, dramatic vs. anticlimactic feel.*

---

### Encounter state at retreat trigger

Beholder drops to 28 HP (typically somewhere in R3–R4 given the encounter dynamics established in earlier fires). Likely party state:
- **Bazgar (HP 49, AC 18):** Unconscious in ~91% of runs (fire #11 stat); either dead or on death saves by R2.
- **Marwen (HP 32, AC 15):** High DR-priority target (fire #9 MQ-FC51-B); likely downed R1–R2 in runs where DR recharged.
- **Sabriel (HP 44, AC 19):** Most likely survivor. Has used some divine smite slots (consumed by DD in prior rounds per R1 protocol). Likely at 20–35 HP.

Working assumption: Sabriel is the sole standing PC when the beholder drops below 30 HP. She is adjacent to or within 5 ft of the beholder (she was in melee, forced to engage to apply sanctified-weapon damage).

---

### Mechanical path of the retreat

**Step 1 — Initiative 20 lair action:**
- Unstable Ground on Sabriel (DC 16 Dex). Sabriel is a divine martial; Dex likely +0 to +1 (heavy armor implies STR-based build). P(prone) ≈ 75–80%.
- If prone: Sabriel's OA is made at disadvantage (RAW: Restrained/prone imposes disadvantage on attack rolls from the prone creature vs a non-adjacent target... wait: a prone creature has disadvantage on attack rolls regardless of target distance per PHB 291). However: OAs are not "attacks from a prone creature vs a creature moving away" — they ARE attack rolls, and the prone condition applies. Sabriel's OA is at disadvantage while prone. **This dramatically reduces her OA threat** (see Step 3).

**Step 2 — Beholder turn: movement and Shrine-Drift**

Available movement options:
| Option | Movement | Total |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Fly speed + Shrine-Drift (bonus action) | 30 ft + 30 ft | **60 ft** |
| Fly speed + Shrine-Drift + Dash (action) | 30 ft + 30 ft + 30 ft | **90 ft** |

Room geometry (from encounter .md): the lower shaft entrance is "60 feet beyond the main chamber." From the altar center (beholder's hover position), the shaft is at most 60 ft away. With 30 ft fly + 30 ft Shrine-Drift, the beholder covers 60 ft in **one turn without spending its action** — reaching the shaft entrance with its action still available.

**Conclusion: the beholder can reach the lower shaft in a single turn and still take a full action (e.g., Disintegration Ray on Sabriel as a parting shot).**

**Step 3 — Opportunity attacks on retreat**

The beholder's 60-ft movement passes through Sabriel's reach (5 ft melee range). Shrine-Drift has no "no OA" clause (LOG-F7-05, open since fire #7). OA triggers as the beholder leaves her reach.

| Sabriel state | OA hit rate | Expected damage | Beholder result |
|---------------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Standing (+7 vs AC 17) | ~55% | 1d6+3+1d8 ≈ 12 avg (sanctified weapon) | Beholder at **16 HP** |
| Prone (UG applied, disadvantage) | ~32% | 12 avg | Beholder at **16 HP** if hit |
| Standing, L3 smite crit (5% chance) | — | ~38 avg | Beholder at **−10 HP → down** |
| Prone + L3 smite crit (5% × 32% = 1.6%) | — | ~38 avg | Beholder at **−10 HP → down** |

The OA is almost never lethal on its own. The crit-smite kill probability is very low (1.6–5%). The beholder almost always escapes.

**Antireality interaction during retreat (emergent finding — see FEEL-F14-02):**
If the beholder used Antireality earlier this round (e.g., to contest Sabriel's melee attack before the retreat turn), its reaction is **spent** — it cannot use Antireality to impose disadvantage on the OA. If it held Antireality for the retreat, it can force disadvantage on the OA (from ~55% hit to ~32% hit). This is a meaningful, undocumented DM decision.

**Step 4 — Parting shot (action still available)**

Assuming Shrine-Drift + fly = 60 ft and action not spent on Dash: the beholder still has its action at the start of its turn. Options:
- **Disintegration Ray on Sabriel** (+6 vs AC 19, 35% hit; 45 avg force damage on hit → Sabriel at ~−5 to +20 HP depending on prior damage; 35% × 100% disintegration on kill). High variance, high reward.
- **Void Scream (if recharged)**: 30-ft radius from beholder's current position — fires BEFORE moving. If beholder is still adjacent to Sabriel at turn start, VS at DC 16 Wis would hit her. But VS is "recharge 6" — may have fired in R2 already. Low probability of recharge.
- **Tentacle Lash** (melee, +6 vs AC 19, ~35% hit; 14 bludgeon + grapple): grappling Sabriel while retreating is counterproductive — it prevents Shrine-Drift from functioning as a clean escape (see FIX-FC48-B ruling: Shrine-Drift doesn't drag grapples).

**Optimal retreat turn:** Fire Disintegration Ray on Sabriel if recharged (high-stakes parting shot), then Shrine-Drift (bonus action) + fly 30 ft. Use Antireality on Sabriel's OA if reaction is available. At 28 HP entering this turn, the beholder reaches ~16 HP after a typical OA hit, or escapes untouched if the OA misses.

### NEW FINDING: Single-turn escape creates no dramatic final moment

**FEEL-F14-01.** The retreat completes in a single turn. The party has no round where they know the beholder is "almost there" — it exits the chamber before anyone can respond. At a table, the sequence is:

1. DM: "The beholder drops to 28 HP. It fires one last shot at Sabriel, then uses Shrine-Drift to phase toward the lower shaft."
2. Players: "Can I chase it?" — No. Sabriel can't fly and the shaft entrance is 60 ft away.
3. DM: "It's gone. The chamber is silent except for the sound of Bazgar's labored breathing."

This is an acceptable ending for the "you're meant to lose" design intent. But it requires no player agency in the final moment. Sabriel doesn't even get to try to stop it — she was probed and downed before this point in most runs.

**Design options (for Joe):**
- (a) **Accept as intended.** The beholder escaping is the correct dramatic beat; it leaves a hook for the lower shaft.
- (b) **Give Sabriel a chase option.** Add a DM note: the shaft entrance is a narrow crawl (Dex DC 12 to squeeze through for a humanoid); the beholder phases through instantly but Sabriel gets a contested Athletics/Dex roll as a reaction to grab/block the entrance.
- (c) **Slow the retreat.** The shaft is 90 ft from the altar; the beholder must use Dash to reach it in one turn, sacrificing the Disintegration Ray parting shot. Creates a "chase for one turn" moment.

---

### NEW FINDING: Antireality reaction hoarding creates an undocumented DM choice

**FEEL-F14-02.** During the retreat turn, the beholder faces a hidden tradeoff:
- Earlier in the round (resolving Sabriel's attack), use Antireality to negate her hit → spend the reaction.
- Hold Antireality for the OA during Shrine-Drift → protect the retreat with a 55%→32% hit-rate reduction on the OA.

This is a meaningful decision but it is **not mentioned anywhere in the tactics text or the Antireality/Shrine-Drift write-ups**. A DM running the beholder for the first time will miss it. The antireality reaction has maximum value as a retreat screen, not as an in-combat hit-negation.

**Suggested DM note (for Antireality or Tactics text):**
> *"On a retreat turn, consider holding Antireality for the OA that fires as the beholder leaves Sabriel's reach via Shrine-Drift, rather than spending it on an incoming attack earlier in the round. The OA is the party's last chance to stop the escape — Antireality on that OA is worth more than on any prior attack this turn."*

---

### NEW FINDING: Thrall-blocking tactic confirmed inoperative at retreat threshold

**FEEL-F14-03.** The tactics text says: *"If reduced below 30 HP, the beholder retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit."*

At the point the beholder reaches 28 HP, the encounter has typically been running 3–4 rounds. Thrall-derro (HP 22, AC 14) die in 1–2 hits from Sabriel or Bazgar (if alive). They are almost certainly dead by R2 at the latest. In the statistically likely scenario (Bazgar down, Marwen down, Sabriel sole survivor), there are no thralls alive to block.

**Consequence:** the retreat corridor is unimpeded. The only check on the beholder's escape is Sabriel's OA (which has ~55% to hit, rarely lethal). The tactic "thralls block pursuit" is functionally dead text in the normal-run-state.

This confirms LOG-F7-06 (open 8 fires). Suggested fix:
> *Replace "using thralls to block pursuit" with "using Compel Thrall (if any thralls are alive and within 60 ft) to redirect surviving minions toward Sabriel, or simply accepting the unimpeded retreat if thralls are dead."*

## Feel issues this fire

### FEEL-F14-01: Single-turn escape — no final-moment player agency

The beholder exits the chamber in one turn (Shrine-Drift 30 ft + fly 30 ft = 60 ft; shaft entrance at ~60 ft). No player can pursue. The last action the party has is Sabriel's OA (~55% hit, rarely lethal). The retreat is mechanically clean but dramatically zero-agency for the surviving player.

**Design call for Joe:** Accept the instant escape (correct for "meant to lose"); add a chase mechanic; or widen the room so retreat takes 2 turns.

### FEEL-F14-02: Antireality reaction-hoarding creates undocumented DM decision

Antireality held for the Shrine-Drift OA reduces OA hit rate from 55% to 32% and is the single best use of the reaction during the retreat turn. This tradeoff is not documented. DMs without prep will spend Antireality earlier in the round and have nothing for the OA.

**Suggest DM note** in Antireality or Tactics/Retreat text (see findings above).

### FEEL-F14-03: "Use thralls to block pursuit" confirmed inoperative

Thralls are dead by R2–R3 in virtually all runs. The retreat tactic that relies on them is dead letter. LOG-F7-06 carry confirmed with this slice.
