### Movement analysis: can the beholder escape?

**Available movement per turn:**
- Standard move: 30 ft (fly)
- Shrine-Drift (bonus action): 30 ft, can pass through creatures/objects

Total per turn: **60 ft**

**Distance to lower shaft (estimated from encounter layout):**
Per encounter description, the lower shaft entrance is ~60 ft beyond the main chamber. Beholder is hovering 40-45 ft above the altar. Path:
- Descent to floor level: ~40 ft
- Horizontal travel to shaft entrance: ~60 ft
- Total: ~100 ft

At 60 ft/turn, escape takes **2 turns minimum**.

**During those 2 turns, the beholder still has its action.** In D&D 5e, movement is not part of the action. Even while retreating at full 60 ft, the beholder can still:
- Use its **action** for multiattack, Void Scream, or Disintegration Ray (if recharged)
- Use its **legendary actions** (3 available at end of each PC's turn)

This is a critical detail missing from the tactics text.

### BA conflict: Shrine-Drift vs Compel Thrall

If the beholder wants to block pursuit using thralls (via Compel Thrall BA), it must give up Shrine-Drift that turn. This cuts its movement from 60 ft to 30 ft, adding a third retreat turn during which it takes pursuit damage. The trade-off is real but undocumented in the tactics text.

### Feel findings this fire

**FEEL-R195-01 (NEW — MEDIUM): Retreat ≠ stop attacking. Tactics text is incomplete.**

The beholder's stat block framing makes "retreat" sound like a defensive maneuver, but mechanically the beholder still has full action economy during retreat turns: action (Void Scream, Disintegration Ray) + 3 legendary actions per round cycle. A party chasing the retreating beholder is still under full threat pressure. The tactics section says "retreats... using thralls to block" but doesn't mention that the beholder can fire Void Scream while retreating. Add to tactics: "Retreat does not mean ceasing to threaten — continue Void Scream or Disintegration Ray on retreating turns while using movement + Shrine-Drift to create distance."

**FEEL-R195-02 (NEW — LOW): Shrine-Drift/Compel-Thrall BA conflict undocumented.**

On retreat turns, the beholder must choose between Shrine-Drift (fast escape, 60 ft/turn total) and Compel Thrall (slow escape 30 ft/turn, but positions blockers). This is a genuine tactical decision but there is no guidance on it. Add a note in tactics: "On retreat, choose: Shrine-Drift for speed OR Compel Thrall to position blockers — not both."
