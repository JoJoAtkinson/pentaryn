## Phase B — Generative Playtest: `legendary-action-loop`

**Rotation index 2 of 8** — simulating beholder LA pacing across one full PC-initiative round.

---

### Setup

**Scenario premise:** Round 2. Beholder has just taken its main-action turn (used Multiattack round 1; Disintegration Ray on recharge). Party is in initiative order, PC turns remaining for the round. Beholder has 3 LA available (fresh — refreshed at start of its turn).

**Party HP entering round 2:**
- Marwen: 18 HP (hit by 1 Tentacle Lash round 1: 32-14=18 HP, grappled)
- Bazgar: 49 HP (not targeted round 1)
- Sabriel: 44 HP (not targeted round 1)

**Initiative order (representative — higher DEX / initiative):**
- Marwen: 11 (low initiative; grappled, speed 0)
- Bazgar: 14 (Fighter, Battlemaster)
- Sabriel: 17 (divine martial, higher init)

Prescribed LA sequence from rotation: Tentacle (1 LA) after Marwen's turn → Void Ray (2 LA) after Bazgar's turn → 0 LA remaining after Sabriel's turn.

**In-chamber attack/damage bonus:** +1 from Void-Feeding on all attacks and damage rolls.

---

### Antireality reaction coverage across 3 PCs

The beholder's Antireality reaction resets at the START of its own turn (once per round). With 3 PCs acting this round:

- Antireality fires on the **first attack roll targeting the beholder** this round.
- If Marwen attacks first with Scorching Ray: Antireality fires on beam 1 → EXPENDED.
- Bazgar (second to act): Antireality **unavailable**. Both attacks at full hit rate.
- Sabriel (third to act): Antireality **unavailable**.

**Result:** With 3 PCs, the beholder gets Antireality protection against exactly 1 of 3 PCs' attacks per round. Two PCs bypass Antireality every round.

Bazgar attacking without Antireality: +7 vs AC 17 = 55% hit per attack × 2 = expected 2 × 0.55 × 8.5 = **9.35 expected damage** (longsword 1d8+4).

If Sabriel acts after Bazgar (third, Antireality still gone): Sacred Flame (CON save DC ~14 vs beholder CON +3 base, no CON proficiency → 50% fail) = **4.5 expected damage** (2d8 avg 9).

Combined PC expected damage to beholder this round (at altitude with no descend): 
- Marwen (Scorching Ray, 3 beams, first with Antireality): ~7.9 exp (see R327)
- Bazgar (2 attacks, no Antireality): ~9.35 exp
- Sabriel (Sacred Flame cantrip, CON save): ~4.5 exp
- **Total party expected damage:** ~21.75 expected damage per round at altitude.

Beholder at 110 HP needs ~5 rounds to die from this DPR if it never descends. But the party won't last 5 rounds — consistent with "meant to lose" design.

---

### LA simulation — Tentacle + Void Ray sequence

**After Marwen's turn → Tentacle (1 LA) on Marwen (to deepen grapple control):**

Marwen is at 18 HP, already grappled. Using Tentacle LA on her:
- +9 vs AC 15 = 75% hit
- 3d6+4 = 14 avg damage (Void-Feeding included)
- Expected: 0.75 × 14 = **10.5 expected damage**
- If it hits: Marwen at 4 HP, grappled ×2

**BUT:** This is suboptimal if the DM intends to use Multiattack next turn — Tentacle LA "piles on" a near-dead target when Marwen could be brought to 0 HP by Multiattack alone with 93.75% probability (R328). See FEEL-R329-02.

**After Bazgar's turn → Void Ray (2 LA) on Bazgar:**

Bazgar (Fighter Battlemaster): DEX estimated at +1 (STR-focused build).
- DC 16 DEX save: Bazgar needs 15+ = 30% success → **70% fail rate**
- 4d10 force on fail (base) + 1 (Void-Feeding) = 4d10+1 = avg 23 on fail
- Half on success: 23/2 = 11.5 → 11 (round down)
- Expected: 0.70 × 23 + 0.30 × 11 = 16.1 + 3.3 = **19.4 expected damage**
- Bazgar after Void Ray: ~30 HP expected

**Total LA expected damage this sequence:** 10.5 + 19.4 = **29.9 expected damage**

(Note: 10.5 uses Marwen as the Tentacle LA target rather than Bazgar. If Tentacle targets Bazgar instead: +9 vs AC 18 = 60% hit → 0.60 × 14 = 8.4 exp. Then total = 8.4 + 19.4 = 27.8 expected. Marwen is a softer Tentacle target; rotation's ~28.5 estimate is approximately right either way.)

---

### LA timing and initiative-order analysis

**FINDING — FEEL-R329-01: Sabriel acting last in initiative gets a completely LA-free turn.**

In the prescribed sequence (Tentacle after Marwen, Void Ray after Bazgar), the beholder spends all 3 LA before Sabriel takes her turn. Sabriel acts with 0 LA remaining against her:

- No Tentacle to grapple her
- No Void Ray to force a DEX save
- No Move to reposition
- Antireality still available (not yet used if Marwen cast from speed-0, non-attack-roll spell)

Sabriel at AC 19 and 44 HP is the most durable PC. Letting her act with zero LA response means she gets her full action economy — smite, spell, or Sacred Flame — with no punishment from the beholder this round.

**DM tip needed in tactics section:** "If Sabriel acts last in initiative, hold 2 LA (Void Ray) to fire after her turn, not Bazgar's. Spend only 1 LA (Tentacle or Move) in the first two PC-turns. Save the Void Ray for the most dangerous action."

---

**FINDING — FEEL-R329-02: Tentacle LA on near-dead targets is wasteful.**

When Marwen is at 18 HP (1 Tentacle Lash away from incapacitation), using the Tentacle LA on her after her turn:
- Deals 10.5 expected damage → probably drops her to 0 HP now
- But next turn's Multiattack would drop her to 0 HP at 93.75% anyway (R328)
- The 1 LA could instead be used on a fresh target or saved for a better sequence

Better sequence when Marwen is at ≤14 HP (within Tentacle kill range):
- Skip Tentacle LA on Marwen (she'll go down from Multiattack anyway)
- Use the full 3 LA on: Void Ray (2 LA) on Bazgar + Move (1 LA) for repositioning
- OR: Tentacle (1 LA) on Bazgar + Void Ray (2 LA) on Sabriel

**DM tip needed in tactics section:** "Tentacle LA: use on full-HP melee targets, not on wounded casters who'll go down from Multiattack next turn."

### Void-Feeding correction to R328

**FEEL-R329-03 — R328 Void Ray expected damage understated by 0.7:**

R328's Phase B used 18.7 as Void Ray expected damage (0.70×22 + 0.30×11 = 15.4+3.3), omitting Void-Feeding +1 to damage rolls. Void-Feeding trait explicitly states "+1 to attack rolls and damage rolls" while inside the chamber — it applies to Void Ray's damage dice.

Corrected in-chamber Void Ray expected damage: **19.4** (0.70×23 + 0.30×11 = 16.1+3.3).

Difference is small (0.7 per ray) and doesn't materially affect R328's conclusions, but the running calculation should use 19.4 for consistency.

---

### Void Ray vs Disintegration Ray naming

**FEEL-R329-04 — Naming confusion risk; low priority but worth a footnote:**

Both are called "rays" and both target a single creature. DMs who have run other beholder stat blocks may default to "all rays are attack rolls." The cheat sheet already distinguishes them clearly in the SAVE DCs table. The beholder .md does not explicitly flag this in the Legendary Actions section.

Suggested .md addition (optional, low priority):
> *(Note: Void Ray uses a DC 16 DEX saving throw — it is NOT an attack roll and does not trigger Antireality. Compare: Disintegration Ray IS an attack roll.)*

This prevents a common DM error of applying Antireality disadvantage to Void Ray or missing the fact that Void Ray cannot be avoided by Antireality.
