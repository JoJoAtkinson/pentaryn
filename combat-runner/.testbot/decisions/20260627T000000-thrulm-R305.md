### Round 1 Lair Action (init count 20):

The beholder chooses from: Unstable Ground / Manifest Thralls / Void Eruption.

**Option A: Unstable Ground**
- Target one creature, DC 16 DEX save or fall prone.
- Marwen (DEX +2, no prof): needs 14+, 35% pass → **65% prone**.
- Effect on prone target: ranged attacks (beholder, shardcaller) have **disadvantage** vs prone target; melee attacks (rager, thrall) have **advantage** vs prone target.
- **Anti-synergy detected:** The beholder's primary attacks (Tentacle Lash, Maw, Disintegration Ray, Void Ray) are all ranged. Knocking a PC prone HURTS the beholder's own attack rolls if it stays airborne. The Rager and thralls benefit from the prone condition. Unstable Ground is a net loss for the beholder's own offense unless it plans to descend and use Maw (within 5 ft = melee, gets advantage on prone target).
- **FEEL-R305-01:** Unstable Ground anti-synergizes with beholder ranged offense. DM should only choose it if (a) a derro is already in melee with the target and will attack this round, or (b) the beholder is descending to use Maw. The cheat sheet Round-by-Round checklist doesn't capture this caveat. Log for Joe.

**Option B: Manifest Thralls**
- Up to 3 charmed thralls within 60 ft gain 1 THP and can use reaction to move or attack.
- 2 thralls present: each gains 1 THP (CHA+1 = 1). Trivially negligible.
- Reaction attack (Hand Axe Weak): +1 vs AC 15 (Marwen), needs 14+, 35% hit. Expected: 2 × 35% × 2.5 = **1.75 total damage**.
- **This is worse than useless as a damage option.** The only value is the reaction move — redirecting a thrall to block a PC or reach a target. This is more of a movement option than a combat option.

**Option C: Void Eruption** (strongest if party is near shrine)
- DC 16 DEX save, 11 (2d10) force damage, all within 20 ft of shrine.
- If all 3 PCs are within 20 ft of shrine: each makes DEX save (~+1–2, no prof): needs 14–15+, 30–35% pass → **65–70% take full 11 damage**.
- Expected per PC: 11 × 0.68 + 5.5 × 0.32 = 7.5+1.8 = **~9.3 damage**. Three PCs: **~28 total**.
- This is the most efficient lair action by a large margin when applicable.

**FEEL-R305-02: Lair action power variance is extreme.**
- Void Eruption (when applicable): ~28 total expected damage, no attack roll, force type.
- Unstable Ground (situational): prone one target, benefits derro not beholder.
- Manifest Thralls: ~1.75 total damage plus minor movement utility.

The gap between Void Eruption and the other two options is so large that the cheat sheet should guide the DM to use Void Eruption almost always, and only fall back to the others when PCs are out of range. Currently the cheat sheet lists all three as equal options without priority guidance. **Recommend: add a note to cheat sheet or beholder tactics section: "Default lair action = Void Eruption unless PCs are outside 20 ft of shrine; then Unstable Ground only if a derro is in melee with the target."**

### Round 1, Beholder's turn:

Beholder has acted via lair action already at count 20. Now it acts on its own initiative (15). Targets Marwen (23 HP, AC 15) with multiattack.

- Tentacle Lash 1: +9 vs AC 15 → 70% hit → 13 damage + grapple. Marwen → 10 HP if hit.
- Tentacle Lash 2: if Marwen already took 13: 10 HP remaining. Second Lash (70%): another 13 → down to -3 HP → **KO**.
- Maw: not needed; Marwen is already at 0 HP after 2 tentacle lashes on average.

**Round 1 KO probability for Marwen:** The lair action dealt ~9 damage first, leaving her at ~23 HP. The beholder then needs ≥23 damage from 2-3 attacks to KO her. Two Tentacle Lashes average 26 damage at 70% each: P(both hit) = 0.7² = 49%; P(one hits with Maw reaching 21 = 34 total) = ~49% as well. Overall KO probability round 1: **~70% or better.**

Marwen is reliably down by end of round 1 whether she goes before or after the beholder.

**Legendary Actions (end of Bazgar's turn, 1+2 LA split):**

- Void Ray (2 LA) → Bazgar: DC 16 DEX, Bazgar +1 → needs 15+, 30% pass. Expected 18.7 damage.
- Bazgar takes avg 25.9 expected LA damage → 49 − 26 = **23 HP** after round 1.
