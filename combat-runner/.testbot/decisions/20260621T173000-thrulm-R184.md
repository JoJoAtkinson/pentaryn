### NEW BUG-R184-01: Maw attack has orphaned "saving throw" clause — no save exists on this attack

**File:** `world/factions/dulgarum-oathholds/locations/thrulm/npcs/beholder-thrulm.md`

**Text (line 64):**
> "**Maw.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 21 (4d8 + 3)
> piercing damage. If the target is a creature grappled by the beholder, **the target has
> disadvantage on the saving throw.**"

**Problem:** Maw has no saving throw. The clause "disadvantage on the saving throw" references
a saving throw that does not exist in this attack. The text is either:
1. A vestigial remnant of a deleted mechanic (e.g., a STR save to escape the bite that was
   removed during editing), or
2. A misworded attack bonus ("the beholder has advantage on the attack roll against grappled
   targets" would be the mechanically coherent intent), or
3. Intended to grant disadvantage on the *next* saving throw the target makes (e.g., from Void Scream).

**Table impact:** DMs running from the stat block will either ignore the clause (if they
notice it's unresolvable) or try to find a save to apply it to and stumble mid-combat.

**Status: LOG ONLY — design call needed.** Three options:
- Remove the clause entirely (simplest; grapple sets up bite adjacency with no extra modifier)
- Change to "the beholder has advantage on this attack roll" (logical grapple-drag-into-maw mechanic)
- Change to "the target has disadvantage on the next saving throw it makes before the end of
  the beholder's next turn" (synergy with Void Scream / lair actions)

**Preferred recommendation:** Option B (advantage on attack roll). It rewards the grapple
mechanic with a meaningful payoff and is unambiguous. The grappled condition in 5e already
limits movement — adding attack advantage on the maw-bite against a held creature is intuitive.

### Decision A: Open with Disintegration Ray on Marwen?

Disintegration Ray (available turn 1 if pre-charged — reasonable for boss setup):
- Attack: +7 vs Marwen AC 15 → needs 8+ → **65% hit**
- Damage: 10d8 force (avg 45) + Void-Feeding +1 = avg 46
- Marwen HP: 32 → any hit = 32 damage → reduced to 0 instantly (46 >> 32)
- Disintegration trigger: reduced to 0 HP → turned to ash, no return except True Resurrection or Wish
- At level 5, party cannot cast True Resurrection or Wish

**Expected first-turn outcome:** 65% chance Marwen is permanently killed before acting.

**Is this unfair vs lethal?**

The party is MEANT to lose this encounter. However, "lose" means different things:
- GOOD lethal: party retreats in round 3 at 30–40% HP, having seen what the beholder can do
- BAD lethal: one PC is ashed before she rolls a die, and can never be raised at current level

The difference matters enormously at the table. If Marwen is disintegrated on the first attack
of the encounter, her player has had zero agency — they sat down, described their character,
and lost them in a dice roll before acting. This is the encounter design's highest feel risk.

**Probability of this outcome:** 65% × ~99.9% (any 10d8 total ≥ 32) ≈ **65% per encounter start.**

**FEEL-R184-01: Disintegration Ray round-1 death risk is the encounter's biggest table problem.**

This affects every encounter start. Not a balance issue (the beholder is supposed to win) but a
*player experience* issue: permanent first-turn death is qualitatively different from losing a
fight over 3–4 rounds. The stat block tactics say "Prioritizes disintegration of spellcasters."
Without a DM note, a by-the-book run has a 65% chance of a permanently dead Wizard in round 1.

**Recommendation (LOG ONLY — design call):** Add a tactical note to the beholder's tactics section:
> *"Against a level-5 party, the beholder typically delays Disintegration Ray until round 2+,
> after targets have acted at least once. First-turn disintegration of a PC before they act
> is mechanically valid but creates an anticlimactic encounter. If early Disintegration Ray
> fires, prefer targeting Bazgar (HP 49, can survive a hit) over Marwen (HP 32, instant death
> on any hit)."*

Alternatively, introduce a Legendary Resistance check or minimum HP threshold clause as a
soft design guardrail.

### Phase B Findings

**FEEL-R184-01 (already logged above):** Disintegration Ray round-1 or early-round: 65% first-turn hit chance against Marwen, permanent death. DM needs a tactical note recommending Bazgar or delayed targeting as alternative.

**FEEL-R184-02: Disintegration Ray always fires by round 2–3. Party cannot survive Marwen's loss.**

With Disintegration Ray recharging on 5-6 (33% per turn), expected turns to fire:
- Turn 1 (if pre-charged): fire immediately
- If not pre-charged: 3 turns average

Marwen has 13 HP by end of round 1 in this simulation. Even with emergency healing, she's
perpetually below the one-hit threshold for any attack the beholder makes (+7 vs AC 15 = 65%
hit, 14 avg minimum damage on Maw). The disintegration risk is effectively a ticking clock:
Marwen will be targeted and disintegrated within 3–4 rounds with ~90%+ probability.

**The party cannot keep Marwen alive at their current level against this beholder.** This is
by design (meant to lose), but the disintegration mechanic means the loss includes permanent
character death — not just a tactical retreat. This is a significant table expectation to set
in advance.

**FEEL-R184-03: Beholder multiattack + LA first round is already threatening without Disintegration.**

Multiattack alone (26.1 avg) + full LA spend (29 avg) = ~55 avg round 1 damage. This pushes
Marwen to 0 HP without the disintegration mechanic. The fight is brutal even if the DM avoids
using Disintegration Ray early. The encounter's "meant to lose" design goal is met by the
action economy alone.

**Conclusion:** The encounter is brutal in the correct way. The Disintegration mechanic is a
separate severity tier (permanent death) that sits uncomfortably atop an already overwhelming
encounter. A DM guidance note is needed to distinguish when to use it.
