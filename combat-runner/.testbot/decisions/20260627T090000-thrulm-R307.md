## Phase B — Simulation: `antireality-timing`

> Bazgar attacks beholder: +7 to hit vs AC 17 — needs 10+ to hit (55% base). Beholder uses Antireality (reaction, once per round) to impose DISADVANTAGE on the triggering attack roll. Simulate Bazgar's Action over 3 rounds (2 attacks per round): Antireality fires on the FIRST attack each round only. Record how many of Bazgar's 6 total rolls are flipped from hit to miss by disadvantage. Note: if Bazgar already has disadvantage from another source, Antireality is wasted.

### Setup

Context: Round 1 start. All combatants at full HP. Initiative order assumed:
- Beholder: init 18 (DEX +3, rolled 15)
- Sabriel: init 17 (assuming +4 Dex mod or similar)
- Shardcaller: init 15
- Bazgar: init 12 (DEX +0 + roll 12)
- Marwen: init 10
- Thralls: init 9

Antireality fires on the first attack targeting the beholder each turn — which means it fires on Sabriel's first attack (she acts before Bazgar), then is exhausted for Bazgar's entire turn. Bazgar gets zero disadvantage from Antireality if Sabriel goes first.

This is the key finding: **initiative order determines which PC absorbs Antireality, not the beholder's strategic choice.**

---

### Antireality Math

P(hit, normal): +7 vs AC 17 → needs 10+ on d20 (55% = 11/20 values)

P(hit, disadvantage): both dice ≥ 10 = 0.55 × 0.55 = 0.3025 ≈ 30%

**Damage per hit (Bazgar Battlemaster 5):**  
Assuming longsword (1d8+4 = 8.5 avg) or greatsword (2d6+4 = 11 avg).  
Using greatsword throughout: avg 11/hit.

Maw: 60% × 22 avg = 13.2. (Maw clause: if grappled, disadvantage on escape — Bazgar STR +2 vs beholder at +8, roughly DC 16.)  
LA budget = 3.

**Sabriel's turn (init 17):**  
Sabriel attacks beholder. Beholder uses Antireality (reaction, 1/round) on Sabriel's first attack.

- Sabriel attack 2 (no Antireality): 50% hit, avg 11 → 5.5 expected damage.  
Antireality reaction now **exhausted**. Resets at start of beholder's next turn.

**Shardcaller turn:** Shard-Throw twice, hangs back. Applies Pack Tactics Voice on a hit.

**Bazgar's turn (init 12) — Antireality ALREADY SPENT:**  
- Attack 1 (no Antireality): 55% hit, avg 11 → 6.05 expected damage.

### Antireality Effectiveness Against Bazgar: ZERO

**Finding:** In standard initiative order where Sabriel acts before Bazgar, Antireality fires on Sabriel's attacks every round. Bazgar's 6 total rolls over 3 rounds (or 8 with Action Surge) receive **no disadvantage from Antireality**. The reaction provides value to the beholder, but not specifically against Bazgar — it's fully absorbed by the first attacker each turn.

**Counterpoint:** If Bazgar wins a high initiative (say init 16, acting before Sabriel at init 12), Antireality lands on Bazgar's first attack each round. Over 3 rounds with Action Surge in round 3:
- 3 disadvantaged attacks: 3 × 30% × 11 = 9.9 expected
- 5 normal attacks: 5 × 55% × 11 = 30.25 expected
- Total: 40.15 expected damage

Without Antireality (8 attacks): 8 × 55% × 11 = 48.4 expected  
Antireality saves 8.25 avg damage (17% reduction from a zero-cost reaction).

### Key Feel Findings

**FEEL-R307-01: Initiative order determines Antireality recipient, not beholder intent**

The beholder has no agency over which PC absorbs the Antireality reaction — it fires on whoever swings first after the beholder's turn. If three melee PCs surround the beholder, Antireality fires on the highest-initiative attacker, who may not be the biggest threat. A Battlemaster with Action Surge (4 attacks) is the highest-priority target for the reaction, but if a weaker melee PC goes first, Antireality is "wasted" on a lower-threat attacker.

**Suggested fix:** Add a line to Tactics or the cheat sheet: "Declare Antireality on the PC most likely to roll a critical hit this round (i.e., the one with Elven Accuracy, Advantage, or the most attacks). If unsure, hold the reaction until the biggest melee threat swings — but remember the reaction resets each beholder turn, not each PC turn." This gives DMs guidance on the most common failure mode.

**FEEL-R307-02: Antireality is wasted while Void Scream FRIGHTENED is active**

If Void Scream hits in round 1 and frightens Bazgar (disadvantage on attacks vs beholder), Antireality is redundant for up to 1 minute (or until he saves). Stacking disadvantage has no effect in 5e (two sources of disadvantage = one source of disadvantage; also advantage from any source fully cancels). The beholder wastes its reaction for every round Bazgar remains FRIGHTENED.

Beholder's optimal sequencing: delay Antireality until Bazgar saves against FRIGHTENED, then resume. The cheat sheet doesn't describe this. Worth adding to the cheat sheet's "When Beholder Acts" section.

**FEEL-R307-03: Antireality vs Sabriel with GWM or Polearm Master**

Divine martial (Sabriel, AC 19) likely uses Great Weapon Fighting or a similar feat at L5. Against a Polearm Master, Sabriel gets 3 attacks/round — Antireality fires on attack 1, leaving attacks 2 and 3 unimpeded. Against GWM (-5/+10), Antireality flipping attack 1 to disadvantage is especially valuable (GWM hit rates drop sharply under disadvantage). This is a scenario where Antireality targeting the first attacker IS the optimal play regardless of initiative order.

Verdict: Antireality has the most value against feat-heavy melee PCs (GWM, Polearm Master). The cheat sheet should note "prefer Antireality targets with GWM or Polearm Master" as a targeting hint.

**Antireality "always dominant" verdict:**

Antireality is the beholder's only reaction; there's no alternative to compare against. The question is whether it's *always worth using* (versus saving the reaction for... nothing). Answer: yes, always use it — there is no competing reaction. The reaction economy is simple: fire on the first attack you see, reset on your next turn. The cheat sheet correctly prompts this behavior. No rotation deadlock.

### Battlemaster Precision Attack Interaction

If Bazgar uses Precision Attack (Superiority Die, 1d8 to the attack roll) after rolling disadvantage, the d8 is added to the lower of the two d20 results. This can convert a disadvantage miss to a hit.

Example: roll 7 and 14 (take 7 with disadvantage). Add Precision Attack d8 (avg 4.5): 7 + 4.5 = 11.5 → hit vs AC 17. Precision Attack partially negates Antireality.

**Implication:** Antireality drains Battlemaster Superiority Dice — Bazgar must choose between saving dice for maneuvers or spending them countering the beholder's reaction. This creates real resource tension and is **interesting play**.

**Log note for Joe:** "Antireality directly competes with Bazgar's Superiority Dice economy. This is a good design interaction — the beholder's reaction forces a meaningful PC decision. Consider reinforcing in encounter notes."
