## Phase B — Playtest slice: `retreat-threshold`

*(thrulm-rotation.json index 6 — retreat at 30 HP: tactical viability, Shrine-Drift as escape tool, thrall blocker availability)*

### Setup

- **Beholder:** HP 28/110 (below 30 HP retreat threshold), AC 17. 3 LA available (restored at top of its turn). Void-Feeding active.
- **Party state (assumed round 3–4):** Bazgar HP ~25/49 (melee range of beholder, DC 17 on death blow is close); Sabriel HP ~10/44 (probably grappled from prior rounds); Marwen HP ~5/32 or down.
- **Thralls:** 2 Thrall Derro dead (assumed — HP 22 each; rarely survive beyond round 2 vs party dealing 15–20 DPR per fighter). Shrine-Touched also likely dead (HP 45, fire/radiant vulnerability). Rager possibly alive (HP 52, AC 16).
- **Position:** Beholder hovers at 40 ft above shrine altar. Lower shaft entrance: ~60 ft horizontally from altar, through the far wall of the chamber.

### Retreat mechanics analysis

**Shrine-Drift (bonus action):** "Moves up to 30 ft; can move through other creatures and objects as if difficult terrain; takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside a creature or object."

At 28 HP, ending inside a wall deals avg 5.5 (floor 5) force = ~18% of remaining HP. The beholder must time its Shrine-Drift to exit the far side before ending its turn. Through 30 ft of stone is feasible if the chamber walls are thinner than 30 ft (probable — typical dungeon wall is 5–15 ft).

**Total retreat distance in one turn cycle:**

| Phase | Movement | Source |
|-------|----------|--------|
| After PC 1's turn | 30 ft fly | LA: Move (1 LA) |
| After PC 2's turn | 30 ft fly | LA: Move (1 LA) |
| After PC 3's turn | 30 ft fly | LA: Move (1 LA) |
| Beholder's own turn — Shrine-Drift (BA) | 30 ft through walls | Bonus action |
| Beholder's own turn — movement | 30 ft fly | Normal movement |
| **Total** | **150 ft** | 3×LA + BA + action |

Three legendary Move actions during enemies' turns add 90 ft of repositioning before the beholder even takes its own turn. Combined with 30 ft movement + 30 ft Shrine-Drift, the beholder can cover ~150 ft in one turn cycle — far exceeding any plausible encounter room dimension.

**Practical constraint:** The beholder must reach the lower shaft opening before using Shrine-Drift. If LA Moves carry it to the shaft wall, one Shrine-Drift passes through. Clean exit.

### "Use thralls to block" — availability check

Per tactics section: "retreats deeper into the lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit."

**Problem:** By round 3–4, all thralls are likely dead. Even if one thrall (HP 22, AC 14) survives, it has no meaningful blocking capacity vs Bazgar (multiattack, avg ~20 DPR). A live thrall blocking the shaft entrance:
- AC 14 vs Bazgar attack bonus +6: hits on 8+, 65% hit rate.
- Two attacks: P(both miss) = 0.35² = 12%. Expected HP loss per round: 2 × 0.65 × 6.5 (avg dagger/axe dmg Bazgar) ≈ 8.5 HP, though Bazgar likely deals much more with Action Surge or Fighting Style.
- Thrall at HP 22 dies in ~2-3 hits from any PC. Delays pursuit by 1 turn at most.

**FEEL-RETREAT-R266-01 (NEW):** "Use thralls to block" is the stated retreat tactic, but thralls are almost certainly dead by the retreat threshold (~round 3+). The tactic is documented but mechanically unavailable in typical fight progression. Beholder can use Rager if alive (HP 52, more durable blocker), but the .md tactics don't mention this contingency.

### Retreat feel analysis

**At 30 HP (clean retreat):**
- Party has dealt 80 damage to a CR 13 creature. This represents significant resource expenditure.
- Beholder uses LA×3 during enemies' turns to open distance, then Shrine-Drifts through the floor.
- The encounter "ends" without a decisive kill — beholder escapes into the lower shaft.
- **Narrative problem:** For a "meant to lose" encounter, the beholder retreating (even while the party is battered) reads as the party winning by survival, not losing. They didn't die; the boss fled. This contradicts the encounter's design intent.

**At 20 HP (berserk flee):**
- Beholder uses Disintegration Ray or Void Scream while repositioning. More dramatic.
- Players experience a final "panic attack" from the beholder before it escapes.
- This mode better serves the "meant to lose" texture even if the party technically survives.

**FEEL-RETREAT-R266-02 (NEW):** The 30-HP clean retreat threshold is set too high for "dramatic loss" feel. At 28 HP remaining (the simulated threshold), the beholder has 74.5% HP gone. In a "meant to lose" encounter, the party that reduces a CR 13 boss to 28 HP has done extraordinarily well — the clean retreat feels like a reward they didn't expect. *Recommendation: collapse the two thresholds (lower the clean-retreat threshold to 15–20 HP, or eliminate it entirely and have the beholder fight until death/true 30 HP threshold, using a single mode: "fight while fleeing at 30 HP").* Joe decides.

**FEEL-RETREAT-R266-03 (NEW):** Shrine-Drift as retreat tool is mechanically elegant (phase through walls, no OA, clean separation). But at 28 HP with self-damage risk (avg 5.5 if mismanaged), the beholder should be noted as "takes self-damage on the retreat Shrine-Drift only if it misjudges wall thickness." DM may forget the self-damage clause and let it phase freely. *Add reminder to start-of-turn checklist: "Shrine-Drift self-damage applies if beholder ends turn inside a creature or object — time the exit."*

### Legendary action economy during retreat

With all 3 LA spent on Move (1 LA each × 3 PC turns), the beholder sacrifices Void Ray (2 LA) and Drain Divinity (3 LA) entirely on the retreat round. This is the correct call — the beholder needs distance, not damage. However, this is not documented in the tactics section. Currently the tactics section describes the beholder's aggressive LA use (Tentacle + Void Ray, or Drain Divinity) but gives no guidance on defensive LA use during retreat.

**FEEL-RETREAT-R266-04 (NEW, minor):** Tactics section should note: "On retreat round, spend all 3 LA on Move to maximize separation before PCs can act. Do not use Void Ray or Drain Divinity on the retreat turn unless a PC is adjacent and within Drain Divinity range."
