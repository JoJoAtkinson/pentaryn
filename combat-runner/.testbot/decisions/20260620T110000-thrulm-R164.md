### Scenario: `drain-divinity-vs-sabriel` — legendary action economy test

**Premise (from rotation.yml):** Beholder spends all 3 legendary actions on Drain Divinity targeting Sabriel (divine martial level 5; slots: two 3rd-level, three 2nd-level, four 1st-level). DC 16 Cha save each time (assume +2 Cha for Sabriel). Simulate 3 consecutive rounds. Is this mechanically sound? Does spending all 3 LAs on one effect feel correct?

---

### Save math — 3 rounds of Drain Divinity vs. Sabriel

Sabriel CHA save: d20+2 vs DC 16 → need 14+. P(fail) = 65%, P(success) = 35%.

| Round | LA spend | Target slot | P(fail) | Expected slot drain | THP gain (raw rule) |
|-------|----------|-------------|---------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 1 | 3 LA (Drain Divinity) | Highest remaining: 3rd-level | 65% | 0.65 slots | 6 THP |
| 2 | 3 LA (Drain Divinity) | Highest remaining: 3rd-level (if round 1 failed), else stays 3rd | 65% | 0.65 slots | 6 THP |
| 3 | 3 LA (Drain Divinity) | Highest remaining: depends on rounds 1–2 | 65% | 0.65 slots | 4 or 6 THP |

Expected slots drained across 3 rounds: 3 × 0.65 = **1.95 ≈ 2 slots**

Outcome distribution over 3 rounds:
- P(0 drains) = 0.35³ = **4.3%** — Sabriel keeps everything
- P(1 drain) = C(3,1) × 0.65 × 0.35² = **23.9%** — loses 1 third-level slot
- P(2 drains) = C(3,2) × 0.65² × 0.35 = **44.3%** — loses both third-level slots
- P(3 drains) = 0.65³ = **27.4%** — loses both third-level + one second-level

**In 71.7% of combats, Sabriel loses both 3rd-level slots before the encounter ends (3-round estimate).**

---

### Critical finding: THP gain is non-cumulative (5e RAW)

PHB p. 198: "When you gain temporary hit points and already have some remaining, you decide whether to keep the ones you have or gain the new ones. You can't combine them."

Applied to Drain Divinity over 3 rounds:

| Round | Beholder THP before | DD fires (65%) | THP gained | THP after |
|-------|--------------------|----|------------|-----------|
| 1 | 0 THP | 3rd-level | 6 THP | **6 THP** |
| 2 | 6 THP (assuming no dmg) | 3rd-level | 6 THP | **6 THP** (equal, keep current) |
| 3 | 6 THP | 2nd-level | 4 THP | **6 THP** (keep current; 4 < 6) |

**Maximum THP the beholder retains from 3 rounds of Drain Divinity: 6 THP.**

The `rotation.yml` scenario description states "beholder gains at most 16 temp HP (6+6+4)" — this is **incorrect per 5e RAW**. THP pools replace, not stack. The true ceiling is **6 THP** from the first 3rd-level drain. (See FEEL-R164-01.)

---

### Drain Divinity value analysis beyond THP

Even though THP gain is illusory past round 1, Drain Divinity remains tactically dominant against Sabriel because:

1. **Smite disruption is massive.** A 3rd-level Divine Smite deals 4d8 ≈ 18 extra radiant damage on one hit. Draining both 3rd-level slots removes ~36 potential burst damage from Sabriel over the fight. (She still has 2nd- and 1st-level slots for weaker smites.)

2. **Paladin resource asymmetry.** At level 5, Sabriel has only 2 third-level slots. Losing them in round 1–2 means the rest of the fight she can only smite for 2d8 (1st) or 3d8 (2nd). Her sustained damage drops ~25%.

3. **Beholder LA opportunity cost.** 3 LA on Drain Divinity vs. distributing 3 LA as Tentacle (1 LA) + Void Ray (2 LA):
   - Tentacle: ~8.5 avg bludgeoning (65% hit vs AC 19 = +7 vs 19 → need 12+, 45% hit; 0.45 × 13 = 5.9 avg) + grapple chance
   - Void Ray: 0.70 × 22 + 0.30 × 11 = **18.7 avg force** vs Bazgar (DEX +1 → P(fail DC 16) = 70%)
   - Total: ~24.6 avg damage per LA round via Tentacle+VoidRay

   vs. Drain Divinity: 0 direct damage, but removes ~18+ potential smite damage. Break-even if Sabriel would have smited within the combat; Drain Divinity is tactically better if the beholder survives long enough to benefit from reduced incoming burst.

4. **Linear optimal strategy confirmed.** Against this party, beholder should:
   - Round 1: Disintegration Ray at Marwen (likely kill; 45 avg force vs 32 HP, AC 15)
   - LA rounds 1–3: Drain Divinity on Sabriel every round

   This strategy has no variation point — the beholder never needs to choose a different LA. This is the "degenerate every round vs. divine casters" concern from fire #6 **re-confirmed**.

---

### Drain Divinity vs. non-slot divine abilities — THP gap

If Sabriel runs out of spell slots, Drain Divinity shifts to "loses one use of a divine ability." The ability text continues: "The beholder gains temporary hit points equal to twice the spell level lost."

When a divine ability (not a spell slot) is drained, there is **no spell level** to double. The THP formula has no defined output for this case. (See FEEL-R164-02.)

---

### Legend action budget summary (one full round: 3 PC turns + beholder turn)

Beholder LA budget = 3, refreshes at the start of its own turn.

Correct sequence (using all 3 LA against 3 PCs):
- End of Marwen's turn: Drain Divinity (3 LA) → 0 LA remaining
- End of Bazgar's turn: 0 LA (budget spent)
- End of Sabriel's turn: 0 LA
- Start of beholder's turn: 3 LA refresh

**Finding:** When Drain Divinity is chosen, the beholder has ZERO LA available for Tentacle or Void Ray for the entire round. This is mechanically consistent ("Costs 3 Actions") but means the beholder's LA economy is completely consumed by one ability. Contrast Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA) = same budget, 2 effects, 2 targets.
