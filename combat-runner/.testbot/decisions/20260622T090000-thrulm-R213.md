### Step 1: First hit — 28 damage

Shrine-Touched HP: 45 − 28 = **17 HP**.

Unstable Form check: 28 ≥ 10. **Unstable Form activates.** Shrine-touched has advantage on attack rolls until end of its next turn.

OBR trigger: damage from visible source (Sabriel). Shrine-touched declares reaction → Shrine-Axe vs Sabriel.

**DB response for `oath_breaking_retaliation`:**
The DB row is type "reaction" with `narration_preview` only and no attacks array. Runner outputs:
> "Pain becomes prophecy — the shrine screams through its vessel."
…and stops. **No attack roll. No damage roll. BUG-R156-01 fires as expected.**

What *should* happen (per stat block):
- Attack: d20+4 with **advantage** (Unstable Form active). AC 19 target — needs 15+. P(hit at least once with advantage) = 1 − (14/20)² = 1 − 0.49 = **51%**.
- On hit: 1d8+1 slashing (avg 5) + 1d8 necrotic (avg 4) = avg **9 total**.
- Self-damage: 1d4 psychic (avg 2), reducing shrine-touched from 17 HP further.

Expected HP after step 1 if OBR functioned: Shrine-touched = 17 − ~2 (self-damage) = **~15 HP**. Sabriel = 44 − ~9 (51% hit chance → ~4.6 expected) = **~39 HP**.

### Step 2: Kill shot — 29 damage

Shrine-Touched HP: 17 − 29 = **−12 → 0 HP. Dead.**

OBR trigger: fires on the damage event (before death resolves per design intent). Does it fire?

**RAW ambiguity (NEW — FEEL-R203-01):** The Shrine-Axe attack is made as a reaction to taking damage. The trigger fires the moment damage is applied. Per 5e PHB, a creature reduced to 0 HP is **incapacitated** and cannot make attacks. The stat block says "it can use its reaction to make one Shrine-Axe attack" but does not say "even if this damage reduces the derro to 0 hit points." Per strict RAW, the OBR attack cannot be made on a lethal hit because the creature is incapacitated at the time the reaction resolves.

However, the scenario notes explicitly describe this as the intended design: "The creature was already dead when the runes flared — but the oath-echo fired anyway, and the axe still landed." This house ruling is compelling narrative design ("death-swing" mechanic) but requires explicit stat block text to be table-safe.

**If DM applies house ruling (OBR fires on death blow):**
- Unstable Form check: 28+29=57 total damage taken this turn. 57 ≥ 10. **Advantage applies** (Unstable Form is still active, granted by the previous 28-damage hit this turn).
- Attack: d20+4 with advantage vs Sabriel AC 19. P(hit) = **51%**.
- On hit: 1d8+1 slashing + 1d8 necrotic = avg **9 total** damage to Sabriel.
- Sabriel (if took ~4.6 from first OBR): **~34 HP** after the full sequence.

DB response: Same bug fires again — NARRATION ONLY. Runner outputs the narration_preview with no dice. **BUG-R156-01 confirmed on lethal trigger.**

### FINDING-R213-02 (NEW — MEDIUM): OBR death-blow house ruling not in stat block text

**Root cause:** The scenario design explicitly expects OBR to fire on a lethal blow ("the oath-echo fired anyway, and the axe still landed"). This is intentional design — the shrine's power convulses through the vessel even at the moment of death, creating a memorable death-swing moment. But the stat block text does not support this under strict 5e RAW (incapacitated creatures cannot make attacks).

**Table consequence:** DMs unfamiliar with the design intent will rule OBR cannot fire on a killing blow. The death-swing moment — arguably the most narratively distinctive thing about this creature — will silently fail roughly half the time (whenever the killing hit is the first hit in a turn, the creature hasn't used its OBR reaction yet and the DM faces the ambiguity).

**Recommendation for Joe:** Add one sentence to the Oath-Breaking Retaliation text: *"This reaction can fire even if the damage reduces the derro to 0 hit points; the shrine's power convulses through the vessel in its final moment."* This resolves the RAW conflict and preserves the intended feel at no cost to balance.

This is a **log-only** item (text change, no dice/CR impact, Joe should review). Do NOT auto-fix.

### FINDING-R213-03 (NEW — LOW): Unstable Form + OBR synergy creates a 51% death-swing vs Sabriel (AC 19)

**Observation:** When Sabriel deals ≥10 damage in a single hit to a shrine-touched (highly likely at level 5 — her avg hit on a divine martial is ~15–20), Unstable Form grants the OBR attack with advantage. Against AC 19, this pushes hit probability from 30% (straight roll) to **51% with advantage**. The shrine-touched hits harder on the turn it's hardest hit.

**At this party level:** Sabriel has enough damage to one-shot shrine-touched (45 HP) if critting with a smite. More likely she deals 15–25/hit (kills in 2 hits). In both of those hits, Unstable Form will have activated, meaning every OBR fires with advantage.

**Feel implication:** The death-swing 51% hit with 9 expected damage against a 44-HP divine martial is not threatening (Sabriel ends at ~35 HP if both OBRs fire). But the mechanic is compelling — the shrine fights harder as it dies, punishes the player who committed to it, and creates natural drama. The gap is that it's entirely invisible without DB fixes and the cheat sheet "When Derro Acts" checklist doesn't mention Unstable Form (CS-DRIFT-15 carry-forward). DMs will miss this unless they happen to remember the trait text.
