### Scenario Setup

Simulate a round in which all three PCs attack the beholder and the beholder must decide when to spend Antireality. Starting conditions:

- **Beholder:** HP 110 (full), Void-Feeding active, Antireality AVAILABLE. It has just ended its turn. Three PCs are in attack range (beholder hovering 10 ft up, accessible by melee).
- **Initiative order:** Bazgar (13), Marwen (11), Sabriel (9). Beholder LA windows fire at end of each PC's turn.

### The Antireality Decision Window

Antireality (current mechanic): **disadvantage on one triggering attack roll, once per round, declared when the attacker announces the attack (pre-roll).**

The beholder must decide on Bazgar's FIRST attack whether to spend the reaction, before knowing what Sabriel will do later this same round.

**Optimization problem:** Expected damage saved by Antireality depends on who the beholder targets.

| Target | Attack bonus vs AC 17 | P(hit normal) | P(hit w/ disadvantage) | Expected damage/hit | EV saved by Antireality |
|--------|----------------------|----------------|------------------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| Bazgar attack | +7 | 55% | 30.25% | ~10.5 (1d12+4) | (0.55−0.3025)×10.5 = **2.62 avg** |
| Sabriel attack (no smite) | +6 | 50% | 25% | ~7.5 (1d8+3) | (0.50−0.25)×7.5 = **1.88 avg** |
| Sabriel attack (2nd-slot smite) | +6 | 50% | 25% | ~16.5 (weapon+2d8) | (0.50−0.25)×16.5 = **4.13 avg** |
| Sabriel attack (3rd-slot smite) | +6 | 50% | 25% | ~21 (weapon+3d8) | (0.50−0.25)×21 = **5.25 avg** |

**Dominant Antireality target: Sabriel's first attack IF she will smite.** A 3rd-level smite saves 5.25 avg damage vs. 2.62 for a normal Bazgar attack. The catch: the beholder doesn't know in advance whether Sabriel will smite, and Bazgar attacks first in initiative.

### Simulated Round

**Bazgar's turn (initiative 13):**

Beholder decision: Bazgar is up first. The beholder doesn't know Sabriel's intent. Spend Antireality now or save it?

*Assume the beholder saves it for Sabriel (suboptimal but plausible if Sabriel is telegraphed as a paladin).*

- Bazgar Attack 1: d20 = 17 → 17+7 = 24 vs AC 17 → **HIT.** 1d12+4 = (roll 8)+4 = 12 damage. Beholder: **98 HP.**
- Bazgar Attack 2: d20 = 6 → 13 vs AC 17 → MISS.

End of Bazgar's turn: Beholder spends **Void Ray (2 LA)** on Marwen. DC 16 DEX save. Marwen DEX +2 → needs 14+ on d20 → 35% save. P(fail) = 65%. Roll 9 → fail → 22 (4d10) force. Marwen: **32 − 22 = 10 HP.** (1 LA remaining.)

**Marwen's turn (initiative 11):**

Marwen with 10 HP casts Fireball (3rd level) — DC 14 DEX save. Beholder DEX save +6 → needs 8+ → 65% save rate. Roll d20 = 12 → 18 ≥ 14 → **SAVE.** Half of 28 (8d6 avg) = 14 damage. Beholder: **84 HP.**

End of Marwen's turn: Beholder spends remaining **1 LA → Move** (reposition upward to 30 ft to deny next melee).

**Sabriel's turn (initiative 9):**

Beholder has moved to 30 ft altitude. Sabriel can no longer melee it (moved out of reach). She has no ranged option listed. She uses her action to heal Marwen (Lay on Hands 25 HP → Marwen back to 32 HP?). Or she casts a ranged spell... divine martial at level 5 likely has Divine Smite and Sacred Flame at minimum.

*Assume Sacred Flame (ranged cantrip, DC 14 DEX save):* Beholder DEX +6, needs 8+ → 65% save. On fail: 2d8 radiant (avg 9). P(fail) = 35%. EV = 0.35 × 9 = **3.15 avg.** Roll 14 → 20 → SAVE. 0 damage.

End of Sabriel's turn: Beholder Antireality remains **UNSPENT** (beholder moved up high; no attacks came against it after Bazgar).

### Counterfactual: Beholder Spends Antireality on Bazgar's First Attack

- Bazgar Attack 1: disadvantage. Two rolls: d20=17, d20=8 → take 8 → 15 vs AC 17 → **MISS.** (Saved 12 damage.)
- Everything else identical: Beholder takes 0+14 = 14 damage total (vs 26 without Antireality).

Antireality would have saved **12 damage** in this specific simulation. Over an expected distribution: saves 2.62 avg per round when used on Bazgar's first attack.

### Key Findings

**1. (FEEL-ANTIREALITY-01 / MEDIUM — NEW) Antireality optimal timing is informationally gated, creating DM frustration.**

The beholder must decide on the FIRST incoming attack whether to spend Antireality, without knowing whether a smite or high-damage attack follows. With Bazgar going before Sabriel, the beholder faces a genuine decision under uncertainty:
- Spend on Bazgar: save ~2.6 avg damage, guaranteed.
- Save for Sabriel: potential 5.25 avg if she smites, but zero if she can't reach the beholder or uses a save-based ability.

In the simulation, saving Antireality for Sabriel backfired (beholder moved up, Sabriel couldn't melee, reaction wasted). This produces a "feel-bad" moment for the beholder's DM: the reaction feels wasted. **Design note:** The "once per round, announce pre-roll" rule creates authentic tactical tension but may be punishing for DMs who don't model the party's full intention before declaring. Consider whether a FAQ note belongs in the cheat sheet.

---

**2. (FEEL-ANTIREALITY-02 / LOW — NEW) Antireality scales poorly as party size grows; current 3-PC setup is near its floor.**

With Compass Edge (3 PCs, ~5 attacks/round including spells), Antireality blocks 1 in 5 attacks. Expected damage saved per round: ~2-5 avg depending on target choice. This is a ~7-15% reduction in party DPR.

At 5 PCs with Extra Attack + Action Surge: ~10+ attack rolls per round. Antireality blocks 1 in 10 → ~3-5% DPR reduction → essentially trivial.

The reaction is meaningful at the current party size but not by much. Not a bug — but worth noting if the party expands.

---

**3. (CONFIRMED) Antireality post-R224 timing is correct: fires pre-roll.**

The `action_executed` event trigger means the runner surfaces the Antireality prompt before any dice are rolled. This is the intended behavior. DM declares after attack is announced, before rolling. The cheat sheet documents this correctly (updated R231). No regression found.

---

**4. (CONFIRMED) Void Ray (2 LA) remains the dominant LA choice in mid-combat.**

In the simulation, Void Ray on Marwen (32 HP) dealt 22 force damage (65% chance full, 35% half) — EV = 0.65×22+0.35×11 = 18.15 avg. This is substantially more valuable than:
- Tentacle LA (1 LA): EV ~0.55×14 = 7.7 avg against Bazgar
- Drain Divinity (3 LA, requires proximity): EV is slot-level-dependent but extremely costly in LA

Void Ray's DEX DC 16 vs. Marwen (DEX +2 → 65% fail rate) makes it effective. Against Bazgar (likely DEX +0 → 75% fail, even better). Drain Divinity at 3 LA = entire budget, confirmed degenerate if range is met. This confirms FEEL-R228-01 (Drain Divinity dominated by Tentacle+Void Ray even vs Sabriel).
