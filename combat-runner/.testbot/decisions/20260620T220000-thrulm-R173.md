### Lair action menu — Round 2 analysis

Three options on initiative count 20. Tactical assessment for this exact party state:

**Option 1 — Unstable Ground (DC 16 DEX → prone)**

Best targets: Bazgar (grappled → speed 0, can't move away; DEX +1 → needs 15+ = 30% pass → 70% prone) or Sabriel (DEX +0–+1 → similar pass rate, free to move but prone hinders her approach).

- Bazgar is already grappled and speed 0. Making him prone = attacks from within 5 ft have advantage. The beholder's tentacles reach 10 ft — close enough. If the beholder descends to 10 ft on its turn (Shrine-Drift or movement), it would be within 5 ft → auto-advantage. But the beholder hovers and doesn't normally descend. At 10–30 ft altitude with 10 ft reach: technically adjacent, so attacks may qualify as "within 5 ft" only if the beholder descends.
- Against a STANDING Bazgar who was NOT grappled: prone-before-multiattack would grant advantage on all three attacks this round. Since Bazgar IS already grappled (and tentacle attacks already resolve normally), Unstable Ground vs Bazgar adds the prone condition but the attack-advantage benefit is contested by altitude geometry.
- **Verdict:** Unstable Ground is most useful vs a FREE standing target (Sabriel or Marwen), not a grappled target (the grapple already provides beholder control of Bazgar's positioning). Best use this round: Unstable Ground on **Sabriel**, knocking her prone before she acts on initiative 7.

**Option 2 — Manifest Thralls (up to 3 charmed derro gain 1 THP + reaction move/attack)**

Available targets: 1 thrall derro, 1 shrine-touched derro — both within 60 ft and charmed by beholder.
- Each gains 1 THP (CHA modifier +1). This THP is absorbed by the very next hit.
- Each may use their reaction to move or make a weapon attack.
- Thrall reaction attack (hand_axe_weak, +1 to-hit): vs Bazgar AC 18 → needs 17+ → 20% hit → avg 1d4 = 2.5 → expected 0.5 dmg. Effectively zero.
- Shrine-touched reaction attack (shrine-axe, +4 to-hit): vs Bazgar AC 18 → needs 14+ → 35% hit → avg 9 dmg (5 slashing + 4 necrotic) → expected 3.15 dmg. Nontrivial but not impactful.
- **Verdict: Dead weight.** The shrine-touched reaction is the only meaningful output (3.15 expected damage), but Bazgar is already at 15 HP — the beholder's main action threatens a kill; the shrine-touched reaction attack is noise. Manifest Thralls never rises above negligible for this party.

**Option 3 — Void Eruption (DC 16 DEX → 11 (2d10) force, all within 20 ft of shrine)**

Who is within 20 ft of the shrine? This depends on positioning:
- Bazgar: grappled by beholder at ~10 ft altitude; his position is beholder-relative. If beholder hovers within 20 ft of shrine horizontally, Bazgar (dangling from tentacle) is also within range. **If beholder hovers near the altar, Bazgar is in range.**
- Sabriel: approaching from 30 ft away; if she closed in R1, she could be ~15 ft from shrine. **Possibly in range.**
- Marwen: 30–40 ft back (safe positioning for a Wizard). **Not in range.**
- Shrine-touched: intentionally stays near altar (Shrine-Bound trait); **in range.**
- Thrall: near beholder's designated position; **possibly in range.**

If Bazgar and Sabriel are both within 20 ft: Void Eruption expected damage per target:
- Bazgar DEX +1: 70% fail → 0.70 × 11 + 0.30 × 5.5 = 7.7 + 1.65 = **9.4 expected force dmg**. Bazgar HP: 15 − ~9 = **~6 HP** (edge of unconscious).
- Sabriel DEX +0: 75% fail → 0.75 × 11 + 0.25 × 5.5 = 8.25 + 1.375 = **9.6 expected force dmg**. Sabriel HP: 44 − ~10 = **~34 HP** (meaningful chip).
- Shrine-touched takes forced damage from its own beholder's lair action: 9.4 expected dmg from 45 HP = negligible self-damage, but this is **friendly fire on the shrine-touched.** Beholder takes no self-damage (lair action states "each creature" — the beholder is NOT a creature affected by its own lair action in RAW 5e).

**Verdict: Void Eruption vs clustered party is the dominant lair action if Bazgar + Sabriel are both within 20 ft of the shrine.** Expected ~19 damage to two PCs from one initiative-20 free action. The catch: it also damages the shrine-touched (friendly fire), which may discourage use when shrine-touched is still alive at low HP.

### Lair action rotation findings

**Finding 1 (FEEL-R173-01 — MEDIUM): Lair action selection is effectively binary (Unstable Ground vs Void Eruption). Manifest Thralls never makes sense.**

In three rounds of a typical fight:
- Round 1: Unstable Ground on Bazgar (prone before R1 multiattack) — best opener
- Round 2: Void Eruption if PCs clustered; Unstable Ground again if spread
- Round 3: Beholder retreating (if ≤30 HP) or still operating — lair action doesn't change the calculus

The DM never meaningfully deliberates between all three options. This is a flat lair action menu — two good options and one dead weight. As noted in FEEL-R165-02 (8+ fires), Manifest Thralls redesign is needed.

**Finding 2 (FEEL-R173-02 — MEDIUM): Void Eruption triggers friendly fire on shrine-touched derro; this creates a hidden DM hesitation.**

If a shrine-touched derro is near the altar (its designed position per Shrine-Bound trait), Void Eruption damages it. A DM who has invested in the shrine-touched as a meaningful combatant (46% of its HP pool from the party's fire/radiant attacks) will hesitate to Void-Erupt if the shrine-touched is at low HP. This creates an unwritten trade-off: **lair action vs protecting the derro.** The interaction is not called out anywhere.

No current text in the stat block or cheat sheet mentions this friendly-fire interaction. A note under Void Eruption ("note: damages all creatures near the shrine, including allied derro") would remove ambiguity and make it a conscious tactical choice rather than a surprised-DM moment.

**Finding 3 (FEEL-R173-03 — LOW): "Within 20 ft of the shrine" requires 3D geometry and is ambiguous when the beholder grapples PCs at altitude.**

Bazgar is grappled by the beholder hovering at 40 ft altitude. The shrine is on the floor. Bazgar's 3D position is "40 ft above the shrine." Distance from shrine = 40 ft (vertical only) if beholder is directly overhead. 40 ft > 20 ft → Bazgar is OUT of Void Eruption range while grappled at altitude.

However: if the beholder descends to 20 ft altitude while holding Bazgar, Bazgar is within 20 ft of the shrine and IN range. The beholder WANTS to use Void Eruption on clustered PCs but must first DESCEND (losing its altitude advantage) to bring Bazgar into range.

**This is an unintended geometry trap.** The lair action that should punish shrine-clustering is ineffective against the PC the beholder is actively grappling (because grappled = altitude). A DM or player who works this out can intentionally let the fighter be grappled to PROTECT him from Void Eruption — a counterintuitive defensive strategy.

Recommend adding a DM note: "Void Eruption checks distance from the shrine altar. A grappled PC at altitude may be outside 20 ft of the shrine; the beholder must descend to bring grappled targets into range."
