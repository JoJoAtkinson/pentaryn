### New findings from Phase B simulation

**FEEL-R276-01 — Berserk expected damage is sub-Multiattack vs this party at any realistic encounter count**

Even if BUG-RAGER-BERSERK-TYPE were fixed, Berserk (1d12+2 per creature, +4 to hit) underperforms vs Multiattack (two 1d12+2, +4) at party ACs 18–19. Break-even requires 3+ targets at AC ≤14 simultaneously — a condition the 3-PC Compass Edge party never creates. The ability has narrative value ("whirlwind") but zero tactical incentive to use vs this party once fixed.

**Recommended adjustment (log only):** Raise Berserk's to-hit to +6 (using fury-fueled precision), or add a secondary rider ("targets must DC 13 CON save or be knocked back 5 ft") to make it tactically distinct from Multiattack rather than just a worse version of it.

**FEEL-R276-02 — Rager's late initiative position removes its signature moment**

The Rager goes last (Init +0). Against this party, PCs have landed ~18–20 damage before the Rager acts in Round 1. Combined with Rager's 52 HP pool and ~19 damage/round from two PCs, the Rager is dead in ~2 rounds from the end of Round 1. This means:
- Round 1: Rager acts once (possibly Berserk).
- Round 2 (33% recharge): Rager may not get to act if Bazgar or Sabriel crits.
- Round 3: Rager at ~14–18 HP, likely killed this round before its turn.

The Rager's identity ("melee tank that punishes focus-fire") requires it to **survive multiple rounds of punishment**. At L5 with 3 PCs, it doesn't. Taunt (Bonus Action, DC 12 CHA save) fires but the Rager is dead before the disadvantage matters beyond 1 turn.

**Suggested design direction (log only):** Give the Rager one defensive reactive — e.g. Madness Shell: when first reduced below half HP, gain THP = CON modifier × 2 (8 THP). This extends its presence by ~half a round and lets it use its Taunt+Berserk combination before dying. No stat changes needed beyond a trait update.
