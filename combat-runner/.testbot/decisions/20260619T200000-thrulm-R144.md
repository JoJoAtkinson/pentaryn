### Turn simulation: Beholder opens

**Decision: Multiattack vs Disintegration Ray on turn 1?**

**Option A — Multiattack (2× Tentacle Lash + Maw), targeting Sabriel + Marwen:**

Void-Feeding active; all attacks at +7.

1. Tentacle Lash 1 vs Sabriel (AC 19): needs 12+ = 45% hit. Expected damage = 0.45 × 14 = **6.3 avg**. On hit: grapple DC 16 STR. Sabriel (divine martial, assume STR +3): needs 13+ = 40% fail → 60% grappled.
2. Tentacle Lash 2 vs Marwen (AC 15, priority — lowest HP + lowest STR): needs 8+ = 65% hit. Expected = 0.65 × 14 = **9.1 avg**. On hit: grapple DC 16 STR. Marwen (Wizard, assume STR −1): needs 17+ to succeed = 20% success → **80% grappled**.
3. Maw vs Marwen (AC 15, whether grappled or not): +7 vs 15 = 65% hit. Expected = 0.65 × 22 = **14.3 avg**.

**Total expected Multiattack: 29.7 dmg across Sabriel + Marwen. Plus: ~52% chance Marwen is grappled (speed 0, cannot move away), ~27% chance Sabriel is also grappled.**

**Option B — Disintegration Ray vs Marwen (AC 15, 32 HP):**

+7 vs AC 15: 65% hit. On hit: 45 avg force (10d8). Expected damage = 0.65 × 45 = **29.25 avg**.

- On hit: Marwen takes 45 avg → 45 − 32 = 13 excess. Instant death requires excess ≥ max HP (32). Average roll → no instant death; Marwen KO'd at 0 HP.
- On near-maximum roll (10d8 = 70+): 70 − 32 = 38 > 32 → instant death. P(10d8 ≥ 64) ≈ 12%. So ~12% × 65% hit = **~8% chance of instant death on this action**.
- **On miss (35%)**: beholder's entire action wasted. No damage round 1.

**EV comparison:**
- Multiattack: 29.7 avg + meaningful grapple control + guaranteed partial damage even on partial hits
- Disintegration Ray: 29.25 avg + 8% instant-death upside + 35% total-miss risk

**Conclusion:** EV is nearly identical, but Multiattack eliminates the wasted-turn risk. Against a party "meant to lose," a round-1 miss on Disintegration Ray is anticlimactic — the beholder's opening move fails and the party gets a morale boost from surviving it unscathed. **Cheat sheet should explicitly advise Multiattack round 1; save Ray for round 2+ when Marwen has been softened below the instant-death threshold or is grappled (helpless target).**

### Lair action timing (NEW analysis)

In this initiative order (beholder init 17), the lair action at IC 20 fires **before the beholder's own main turn**. Round 1 sequence:

1. **IC 20 — Lair action fires** (beholder has not yet acted this round). Options:
   - Unstable Ground (prone Bazgar, DC 16 DEX): if Bazgar is within 60 ft, 50% fail rate. Prone = disadvantage on attacks, half speed to stand.
   - Void Eruption (DC 16 DEX, 11 avg force to all within 20 ft of shrine): if party is already at the altar — unlikely round 1 if entering from the doorway.
   - Manifest Thralls (1 THP to derro + bonus-action reaction): useful if derro are engaging party, otherwise low value.

2. **IC 17 — Beholder main turn** (Multiattack or Disintegration Ray as above).

3. **IC 14 — Marwen acts.** Beholder can spend 1 LA at the *end* of Marwen's turn.

4. **IC 12 — Bazgar acts.** Beholder can spend up to 2 LA at end of Bazgar's turn.

5. **IC 12 — Sabriel acts.** Beholder spends remaining LA at end of Sabriel's turn.

**Effective output in round 1:** Lair action (Unstable Ground) + Multiattack (29.7 avg) + 3 LAs. That's four discrete offensive beats before the round refreshes. A DM unfamiliar with lair action ordering may run the lair action at the *end* of the beholder's own turn (common error), which would shift it from a "pre-turn bonus" to a "post-turn bonus" and change the feel significantly. Explicit DM note needed.

### Slow spell counterplay (NEW analysis)

With Marwen acting at IC 14 (before Bazgar/Sabriel and before beholder's LAs on Marwen's end-of-turn), her round-1 spell choice matters:

**Slow (3rd-level, DC 14 spell save) vs beholder WIS +2:**
- Beholder needs 12+ on d20 (+2 vs DC 14) = 45% success → **55% chance Slow lands**.
- If Slow lands: beholder loses 2 of 3 Multiattack attacks (reduced to 1 attack per action), loses Antireality reaction, speed halved, −2 AC/DEX saves. LAs remain functional.
- Beholder saves at end of each of its turns (WIS +2 vs DC 14, 45% success per round). Expected rounds affected: **~2.2 rounds** (geometric distribution, p=0.45).

**Impact:** A successful Slow round 1 reduces beholder's expected Multiattack damage from ~29.7 avg to ~11 avg per round (1/3 of attacks) for ~2 rounds, and removes Antireality. Marwen burns a 3rd-level slot but extends party survival by approximately 1 full round.

**Feel note (FEEL-R144-03):** This is the single highest-EV spell Marwen can cast round 1 if her initiative fires before the beholder acts (beholder init < Marwen init = ~30% of sessions). Even when the beholder acts first, Slow on Marwen's turn (IC 14) applies immediately — the beholder's LAs and any remaining actions would be affected if Slow landed on Marwen's turn before the beholder's LA window on her end-of-turn.

Beholder immunity check for Slow: The beholder is not immune to the restrained condition... wait, it IS restrained immune. But Slow doesn't impose restrained — it imposes speed halved, −2 AC, −2 DEX saves, 1 attack max, no reactions. None of these are condition immunities the beholder has. **Slow works fully against this beholder.** This is a meaningful, rules-legal counterplay window.

This is NOT a design flaw (the beholder should have some counterplay at level 5 — it's meant to be threatening, not a foregone conclusion in round 1). But DMs should be aware: if Marwen Slows the beholder and it lands, the encounter becomes a 3-4 round fight rather than 2-2.5 rounds. The end result is the same (party loses), but the experience of the fight is meaningfully different.
