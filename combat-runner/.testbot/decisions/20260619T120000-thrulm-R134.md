### New Phase A finding: DB-BUG-03 — Berserk missing recharge and wrong type

Inspecting `derro-rager.berserk` in the DB (JSONL-read this run):

```json
{"npc": "derro-rager", "action": "berserk", "type": "utility", "verbs": ["berserk", "frenzy", "spin", "whirl"], "narration_preview": "The rager stops moving and becomes a whirlwind of steel — the greataxe finds ..."}
```

Two problems:

1. **Missing `"recharge": 5`**: Stat block says "Berserk (Recharge 5–6)." The runner will never prompt for a recharge roll on this ability. DM must manually track whether Berserk is available each turn.

2. **Type is `utility` instead of `area`**: Stat block says "The rager makes one Greataxe attack against each creature it can reach." This is a melee AoE sweep — correctly `area` (or possibly a custom multiattack type) since it targets every creature in reach. As `utility`, the runner will not roll any attack dice or prompt for damage when it fires. The DM gets a narration and nothing else — no attack prompts, no damage values.

**Impact:** Combined with the missing recharge, Berserk is currently dead weight in the runner: it fires as flavor text, never recharges, and generates no attack rolls. In a front-line encounter with the Rager, the DM would need to remember to recharge manually AND resolve the mechanic manually every time they type "berserk."

**Fix required:** MCP `combat_action_upsert` with `"type": "area"` and `"recharge": 5`. Not safe to author unattended (area type schema unclear for melee sweeps — might need `single_attack` with targeting notes). Logged as **DB-BUG-03**. Needs interactive MCP session.

### Feel assessment

**FEEL-15 (NEW) — Oath-Breaking Retaliation is a non-threat vs front-liners**

Over 2 simulated rounds vs Compass Edge:
- Oath-Breaking Retaliation fired 3 times (each time the Shrine-Touched took damage from a visible source on its reaction window).
- Against Bazgar (AC 18): 35% hit rate, 4.6 expected damage.
- Against Sabriel (AC 19): 30% hit rate, 3.9 expected damage.
- Against Marwen (AC 15): 50% hit rate, 6.5 expected damage — but Marwen is a ranged caster not adjacent.

In practice, the reaction MISSES the front-liners ~65-70% of the time. Each miss costs the Shrine-Touched 1.25 HP of self-damage (after psychic resistance). Over 2-3 rounds: 3-4 triggers × 1.25 = 3.75-5 HP self-inflicted for ~0-4.6 actual damage output. The reaction's cost (a reaction slot + self-damage) frequently exceeds its return.

Against the front-liners this encounter was designed around, the reaction is an ability-rotation gap. It would fire correctly vs Marwen (50% hit), but Marwen rarely closes to melee range. The reaction appears designed for melee swarms where enemies close in — not for a 3-PC party where 2 of 3 PCs have AC 18/19.

**Recommend logging, no fix.** The reaction could be improved by changing to "reaction: when hit (not just when taking damage)" with a +4 bonus scaled to AC 16 rather than 15. Or add a grapple rider so a miss still does something (echoes the "at least they're now threatened" feel).

**DB-OPEN-04 confirmed active: Unstable Form automation gap**

The scenario notes already flag this; confirming it fired in simulation. Bazgar's ~13 damage in one turn triggered Unstable Form. The runner produced no prompt. The Shrine-Touched's multiattack on its next turn should have had advantage, but only a DM reading the trait carefully would apply it.

In practice, at-table speed, Unstable Form will be missed on most turns it triggers. The trait is effectively dead if the DM isn't tracking single-turn damage totals manually. This reduces the Shrine-Touched's effective output by ~20% (losing advantage on 2 attacks that would have been +advantage).

**Ancient Resonance timing and feel**

The recharge-5-6 ability has a 33% fire chance per round. The Shrine-Touched lives approximately 1.5-2 rounds vs Compass Edge. Expected resonance triggers over its lifetime: 0.5 (33% × 1.5 rounds). The party faces Ancient Resonance approximately half the time it faces a Shrine-Touched. The other half of the time the creature dies without firing its signature ability.

This isn't necessarily a bug — high-variance creatures can feel exciting or anticlimactic depending on the recharge roll. But it does mean that if Joe runs this encounter twice, one playthrough will feel much scarier than the other. If the design intent is "every Shrine-Touched reliably fires Ancient Resonance," consider Recharge 6 (1/6 per round, reduce variance by lowering frequency expectations) or allow the Shrine-Touched to start with one Ancient Resonance charged (i.e., it fires on Round 1 as a guaranteed opener).

**DB-BUG-01 confirmed relevant in scenario**

If Ancient Resonance fires, the 1d4 psychic rider (on failed saves) is missing from the DB. DM relying on the runner would see only the 2d10 necrotic output and miss the psychic add-on. Against a party with psychic resistance (none in Compass Edge), this is a DPR gap: ~2-2.5 psychic per failed save, across 2-3 PCs = 5-7.5 extra damage per activation that the runner silently drops.
