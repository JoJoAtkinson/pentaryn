### Shrine proximity disadvantage — positioning ambiguity

The stat block says: "Creatures within 10 feet of the shrine have disadvantage on this save."

The beholder hovers "near the shrine altar (40–60 ft up)" per the tactics note. Void Scream is "each creature within 30 feet [of the beholder]." These two radii (30 ft from hovering beholder, 10 ft from ground-level shrine) are difficult to reconcile on a standard grid map:

- If beholder is 40 ft above the shrine altar: creatures at ground-level are 40 ft below the beholder → outside VS range. VS cannot hit anyone on the ground floor while the beholder is at hover height.
- If beholder descends to 20–30 ft altitude to use VS: PCs on the ground are now within 30 ft (VS range). Those directly below/beside the altar (within 10 ft horizontally) also trigger shrine proximity disadvantage.

**The rule is not self-adjudicating.** At the table, the DM needs a map with marked positions or a ruling on whether "within 10 ft of the shrine" uses 3D or 2D distance. Against a hovering creature, this matters.

**Further issue:** The Void Scream note says "Creatures within 10 feet of the shrine have disadvantage." Shrine-Touched Derro are thematically drawn to the shrine and may be within 10 ft — but they are Frightened-immune, so the secondary effect is irrelevant for them. However, their presence near the shrine could "pull" PCs into the 10-ft zone if PCs engage them in melee.
