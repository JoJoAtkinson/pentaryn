Prone imposes disadvantage on melee attack rolls vs standing creatures, and grants advantage to attackers within 5 ft (and disadvantage from 5+ ft). Since the beholder is flying (not on the ground), it qualifies for the ≥5 ft rule only if hovering high. But standard ruling: melee attacks against prone targets have advantage if the attacker is within 5 ft; ranged (beholder's tentacles reach 10 ft) have disadvantage. - Attacker within 5 ft → advantage on melee attacks vs prone.

### Feel assessment

**FEEL-13 (NEW) — Legendary action timing not enforced by runner**

The scenario types "drain" on the beholder's tab during (or before) the beholder's own turn to test Drain Divinity. The runner fires the effect immediately regardless of game timing. Drain Divinity is a legendary action and may only be used *at the end of another creature's turn*, not on the beholder's own turn. The runner cannot enforce this. DM must manually declare Drain Divinity only when the runner has finished processing a PC's turn. If the DM forgets and types "drain" during the beholder's action phase, the encounter gains an illegal action — the beholder effectively acts twice.

**Recommend:** Add a comment to the cheat sheet's "When Beholder Acts" block noting that legendary actions must be declared in the "between turns" window, not during the beholder's own turn block. Not a stat-block change — cheat-sheet annotation only. Logging here; this would be CS-DRIFT-09 if Joe wants to apply it.

**Drain Divinity degenerate confirmed (R128-R132 repeat)**

Against Sabriel (likely paladin 5, 9 spell slots): beholder can fire Drain Divinity every round it has 3 LA budget. Within 5 rounds it can drain all slots. THP gain is minor (avg 4-6 per use). The real damage is Sabriel loses Divine Smite, Cure Wounds, and paladin buffs. With no slots and no LA pressure, the beholder switches to Void Ray — but the tactical incentive to drain-first-then-switch is obvious and dominant. There is no countermove for Sabriel (she can't "hide" her slot count).

Design note from R131 remains open: consider a once-per-short-rest cap on Drain Divinity, or increase LA cost to 4 (making it unusable given 3 LA/round budget), or add a "no effect vs same target twice in succession" rider.

**Marwen threat window confirmed ✓**

Disintegration Ray (33% per round) one-shots Marwen from full HP (32 < 46 expected damage). Void Ray legendary (2 LA) depletes Marwen in 2 applications (18.2 expected × 2 = 36.4 > 32). These findings are as designed — the encounter is lethal. No fix recommended.

**Lair action → multiattack combo: lethal, NOT unfair**

The Round 1 Unstable Ground → multiattack (advantage) sequence is punishing but telegraphed. The beholder hovering above the shrine is visible before initiative; players can preemptively spread out to avoid AoE lair actions. The combination is lethal by design. No balance flag.
