# Sabriel — The Naelith Encounter

A scene built around one line. Sabriel runs into [Naelith](../../world/factions/elderholt/elders/naelith-the-unmoored-seer.md) somewhere odd — a tunnel beneath Elderstead, the edge of the witch-tree's roots, a doorway no one uses.

> **Naelith:** "Oh — did you find the shrine to Khôr-Bhog-Khôr?"
>
> *(Sabriel asks for context.)*
>
> **Naelith:** "The deepest shrine in the Dulgarum. Oh — it hasn't happened yet."

**She's been working on the name.** Across her drifts, Naelith has been chasing the right pronunciation of Khôr-Bhog-Khôr for a long time — practicing it from the chest, fighting the nose. The line above is the version where she finally has it, and she's coming up to Sabriel to *test it on her.* That's why she's excited.

If she has slipped earlier in her own work, the opener changes:

- **Mid-work (still butchering it):** *"Oh — did you find the shrine to Khor-Mog-Khor? …closer. I keep losing it."*
- **Before she had it (hasn't met you yet, from her side):** *"Oh — your god. (she searches your face) …have I asked you yet? What's his name? I'll need it later."*

The recursion is the point: in one of these timelines, she learns the name *from Sabriel.* Then carries it forward through her own drift until she finally gets to greet her with it.

Played well, Sabriel can extract: the god, the location, the guardian, the cost, and the consequence. Played impatiently, Naelith slips and the scene is gone.

---

## Naelith's Four Anchors

She does not announce which she is in. **Roll 1d4 at the start.** She drifts when pressed too hard or held too long.

1. **Past** — the shrine's last living worshipper. Who built it, who the god was, why it was sealed. Elegiac. Past tense for things Sabriel hasn't lived yet.
2. **Near-future** — Sabriel kneeling in the deep. What guards it, what she'll choose. Hesitant, surprised. *"Oh — it hasn't happened yet."*
3. **Far-future** — after the rite. The cost, who survived, what Elaerith felt. Quiet. Sometimes mournful.
4. **Sideways** — a Sabriel who refused. What the world looks like if she walks away. Confused. *"But you said you would — oh."*

---

## The Slip Mechanic

Each beat of conversation = one **anchor check**. Sabriel picks her approach:

- **Persuasion** — keep her present
- **Insight** — read which anchor she's in and meet her there

DC scales with depth:

- **DC 10** — register (mood, color, weather)
- **DC 13** — facts (the god's name, the guardian, the cost)
- **DC 16** — paths (how to bypass the guardian, how to leave alive)
- **DC 19** — *why* (what Elaerith will feel when the rite ends)

Outcomes:

- **Beat DC by 5+** — two fragments from current anchor
- **Beat DC** — one fragment from current anchor
- **Miss by 1–4** — Naelith slips. Roll 1d4; she answers from the new anchor instead, usually sideways to the question
- **Miss by 5+** — scene ends. *"I should not be here yet."* / *"You already asked me this. The answer doesn't help."*

**Grounding bonus.** A small "where/when are you?" question from Sabriel gives +2 to the next check if Naelith can answer it. If she can't, she drifts immediately and the bonus is lost.

## Player Handout

*(Copy-paste this for Nicole — no campaign spoilers, just the mechanic.)*

**About Naelith — what you've picked up.**

She's not addled. She's just not entirely *here.* Naelith lives across four versions of when she is, and she doesn't always know which one she's standing in.

**Her four anchors:**

- **Past.** Something that already happened — sometimes long ago, sometimes a moment from yesterday. She'll talk in past tense about things that haven't reached you yet.
- **Near-future.** Something just ahead — minutes, days, a season. She'll sound surprised when you tell her it hasn't happened.
- **Far-future.** Years ahead. After the thing, whatever the thing is. Quieter when she's here. Sometimes mournful.
- **Sideways.** A version of you that made a different choice. She'll be confused that you said something different — or that you haven't said it yet.

She does not announce which one she's in. Listen.

**How to talk to her without losing her:**

You can either *anchor her in place* (**Persuasion**) or *meet her where she actually is* (**Insight**). Pick before you speak.

The harder you push, the harder it gets:

- **DC 10** — small things: mood, color, weather.
- **DC 13** — names, facts, plain answers.
- **DC 16** — paths, options, how-to.
- **DC 19** — the *why* of something large.

What happens when you roll:

- **Beat by 5+** — she gives you two pieces of what she knows from her current anchor.
- **Beat the DC** — one piece.
- **Miss by 1–4** — she slips. DM rolls a d4; she answers from a different anchor, usually sideways to what you asked.
- **Miss by 5+** — she's gone. *"I should not be here yet."* Don't chase.

One trick: a small grounding question — *"what month is it for you?"*, *"is it raining where you are?"* — gives you +2 to your next roll, **only if she can answer it.** If she can't, she drifts immediately and you lose the bonus.

She wants to help. She just can't always reach where you're standing.

## Fragment Bank

Pick as needed. Not ordered. Not exhaustive — write more for your table.

### Past

- "He had no name. Names came later. They called him with their hands on the stone."
- "The first ones who knelt there were not dwarves. The dwarves came after and built doors over what was left."
- "It was sealed because something was *answered*, not because something was asked."
- "There were three doors. Two are remembered. The third you will find by smell."
- "The shrine was older than the deeps. The Dulgarum forgot they were standing on a roof."

### Near-future

- "There is something in the dark there. It has not eaten in a very long time."
- "You will set the book down at the threshold. You won't choose to. You'll simply… set it down."
- "The guardian does not stop you. It witnesses."
- "Take the long stair. The short stair is a lie the dwarves tell themselves."
- "Marwen will be with you. Or she won't. I keep losing that page."

### Far-future

- "Your hair will not change again."
- "She will not be angry. She will be *curious*. That is worse."
- "One of you will not come back up. I have not yet decided who. *(pause)* You have not yet decided who."
- "The book will read itself, in the end. You will only have held it."

### Sideways

- "But you said you would not go. *(beat)* Oh. You haven't said it yet."
- "In the version where you turn back, I never speak to you. So this is not that version."
- "There is a Sabriel who never came to Elderholt. She is smaller. She is happier."

## The Shrine (sketched, not finished)

- **Location.** Beneath one of the [Dulgarum Oathholds](../../world/factions/dulgarum-oathholds/_overview.md) — the *darkest* hold, deeper than the Sealed Depths. The dwarves built their breachgates over a foundation that was already there. Naelith calls it *"the roof the Dulgarum forgot they were standing on."*
- **God.** **Khôr-Bhog-Khôr.** Ancient. Pre-mortal. Pre-dwarven. Spoken from the chest, never the nose — sandwich-chant, three syllables, middle one fattest. The name is what worshippers gave him after the fact; he had none of his own.
- **Guardian.** One creature. Pick or roll:
  - **Oath-revenant** — a dwarf who swore to guard the shrine before the Binding of Oaths and never released his vow.
  - **Hollow Shepherd** — the god's last priest, made into something else by waiting.
  - **Oathstone construct** — Dulgaric work, mistaken about what it was sealing.
  - **Faith-eater** — harmless until prayed at; drawn there by the residue of worship.
- **The rite.** The book is the key. Naelith hints the cost is paid in something Sabriel doesn't realize she values — until she sets it down.
