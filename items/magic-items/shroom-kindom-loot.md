## Golem Token
At first glance, it is only a coin: dull metal, no ruler’s face, no denomination, no writen words. 
At second glance, the markings seem to shift when you are not looking directly at them, as if the lines remember a different shape than the one they currently hold.

The magic inside it is self-contained and stubborn. It does not project power so much as *declare identity*:
this token is authentic, this token belongs to a system that still believes itself alive.

No one agrees on what it was for.  
Currency? A work-pass for ancient masons? A command marker for construct labor? A debt tally? A key?  
Every theory fits for a moment, then fails on one detail.

What is clear is this: the token was made to be recognized by something that is no longer here.
Or worse, by something that still is.

# Golem Spine Rivet

> A thumb-length rivet of black-silver metal, heavy for its size, etched with machine-runes worn almost smooth. When set against a surface, it vibrates like a held breath.

An unused spine rivet from an ancient construct line. It was built to lock massive assemblies together under load, not for battlefield use. Most smiths mistake it for a strange coin until they feel the weight and see the bore-runes.

## Activation

Press the rivet flush against the contact point and speak the command word: **"KAR-THUL."**

Once activated, the rivet fires itself in a perfectly straight line and cannot be stopped by nonmagical means.

## Unstoppable Bore (Core Rule)

The rivet tunnels through material in a 1-inch channel and keeps going until one of these happens:

- It exits into open air.
- It has traveled through **10 feet of contiguous dense material** (stone, iron, steel, packed earth, bone, masonry, or similar).

In practice, this means it will pass through flesh, armor, wood, and common stone as if they were layers in one job. It does not ricochet, curve, or seek targets. Can only be activate when pressed firmly on the target, and will stop once it finds a large air or water gap on the other side. 

## Uses

### 1) Spine-Fasten (Utility)

If two objects are touching, you can bind them permanently.

- As an action, place the rivet across the seam and speak the command word.
- The rivet bores through both and locks them as one joined piece.
- The bond is permanent until broken by one of the following:
- `disintegrate`
- 10 minutes of work with adamantine tools and a successful DC 22 Strength (Athletics) check
- comparable high-tier magic, I.E., Wish

This can weld doors shut, pin beams in place, anchor chains, or patch emergency structure failures.

### 2) Spine-Drive (Combat)

If you press it flush against a creature within 5 feet and activate it, the rivet is fired through them.

- This is a melee **touch attack**.
- Make a melee attack roll using Strength or Dexterity (your choice) + your proficiency bonus.
- For this attack, the target gains no AC benefit from nonmagical armor or nonmagical shields.
- On a hit, the target takes **10d8 magical piercing damage**.
- On a miss, the rivit drops unused onto the floor (only activates if firmly pressed onto the target)
- If a solid surface is directly behind the target, a few inches of either water or air gap, the target is now bolted (pinned) until it uses an action to make a DC 20 Strength (Athletics) check to pull free, and take an addional 10d4 damage to rip the bolt through their flesh. 

The damage ignores benefits from nonmagical armor and shields because the rivet is boring through, not striking around defenses.
