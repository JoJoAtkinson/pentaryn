# Velaravos (veh-lah-RAH-vohs) Compression Band

> A loop of black-silver metal rests in your palm, smooth as poured glass and colder than it should be. It looks like a ring only because it has chosen, for now, to be small.

Modern scavengers call it a ring, but that is almost certainly a misunderstanding. The inner face bears repeating glyph-clusters from the lost ancient empire, and the metal answers to measured hand-gestures rather than to hinges, seams, or clasps. When awakened, the band widens or contracts with unnerving precision, as if pressure itself were its intended language.

## Description

The band is made from a dense ancient alloy that resembles silver gone dark with age, though it never tarnishes and never quite warms to the touch. At rest it can be worn as a ring, bracelet, collar, belt-loop, or anklet, depending on the wearer and the current size. There is no visible join, no maker's mark, and no obvious mechanism.

When its magic is active, the wielder must keep one hand free and shape the motion directly: a tightening fist commands contraction, an opening hand commands expansion. The movement is slow enough to watch, but far too strong to ignore.

## Properties & Mechanics

### Attunement Profile

When you attune to the band, choose Strength or Dexterity. The band uses that choice until the attunement ends.

- **Band Attack:** your proficiency bonus + the chosen modifier
- **Band Save DC:** 8 + your proficiency bonus + the chosen modifier

### Controlled Expansion

As an action, you awaken the band for up to 1 minute. This requires concentration, as if concentrating on a spell, and one free hand. The band remains active only while all of the following are true:

- You are conscious.
- You maintain concentration.
- You have one free hand to control it.
- The band is within 30 feet of you and you can see it.

While active, the band is a rigid circle. It cannot become a chain, rope, blade, corkscrew, or saw.

- While active, it can be targeted as a Small magical object with AC 20.
- When activated, choose any diameter from 2 inches to 20 feet.
- At the start of each of your turns, you can increase or decrease the diameter by up to 5 feet.
- If the band cannot shrink farther because a creature or object blocks it, the remaining motion becomes pressure instead.
- If concentration ends, the band immediately goes inert, stops exerting pressure, and can be removed with an object interaction if it is not physically wedged in place.

### Placement Rules

The band must be able to physically pass around the intended target. It cannot appear inside a creature, slip through solid armor, pass through walls, or phase through matter.

#### Willing or Helpless Target

You can place the band around a willing creature, an unattended object, or a creature that is incapacitated, unconscious, restrained, or otherwise unable to resist as part of the action used to awaken it.

#### Unaware Target

You can try to slip the band over an unaware creature within 5 feet. Make a Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) check contested by the target's passive Perception.

- On a success, you place the band without alerting the target until it tightens or the creature otherwise notices it.
- On a failure, the creature notices before the band is properly set.

#### Hostile Target in Combat

As an action, make a Band Attack against a creature within 5 feet of the band.

- On a hit, you place the band around one exposed limb, the torso, or a similar enclosed body section.
- To place it around a neck or other vital choke point, the target must be grappled, restrained, incapacitated, surprised, or unaware.
- A closed helm, rigid gorget, sealed collar, or anatomy that lacks a meaningful choke point can make neck placement impossible, at the DM's discretion.

### Pressure Effects

Once the band is in place, you decide what it is constricting as it tightens.

#### Limb Lock

If the band is tightened around an arm, leg, tail, or similar limb, the target is grappled while the band remains tight.

- Each round you continue tightening against resistance, the target takes 2d6 bludgeoning damage.
- If the trapped limb is holding an item, the creature must succeed on a Constitution saving throw against your Band Save DC or drop that item.

#### Torso Lock

If the band is tightened around a creature's torso, the target is restrained while the band remains tight.

- Each round you continue tightening against resistance, the target takes 2d8 bludgeoning damage.

#### Neck Lock

If the band is tightened around a creature's neck or equivalent breathing structure, the target is restrained while the band remains tight.

- The creature cannot speak above a strained rasp and cannot provide verbal spell components.
- Each round you continue tightening against resistance, the target takes 2d6 bludgeoning damage.
- If the band remains tight through the end of your next turn, the creature begins choking. From that point, use the normal suffocating rules.
- A creature that does not need to breathe ignores the choking portion of this effect, though it still suffers the restraint and damage.

### Escaping the Band

A creature trapped by the band can use its action to make a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check against your Band Save DC, ending the effect on a success.

- This check is made with disadvantage if the band is around the creature's neck.
- Another creature within reach can use its action to attempt the same escape check on the target's behalf.
- Breaking your concentration is often the fastest counter. If your concentration ends, the band immediately relaxes.

### Tourniquet and Pressure Brace

The band can be used carefully instead of violently.

- When wrapped around a willing creature's limb and kept only snug, it grants advantage on Wisdom (Medicine) checks made to stabilize that creature or control severe bleeding.
- If your table uses ongoing bleeding rules, the band can suspend one bleeding effect affecting the enclosed limb while it remains in place.
- The band is not gentle. Leaving it tightened as a tourniquet for more than 10 minutes risks further tissue damage at the DM's discretion.

### Ancient Compression

The band was built for pressure, not cutting.

- It can bend, crush, or deform unattended nonmagical metal objects such as bars, chains, hinges, locks, braces, and thin portcullis members.
- It can also crack stone that is already fractured or poorly supported, but it is not a siege engine and cannot simply erase castle walls.
- Treat the band as exerting or resisting up to 12,000 pounds of force safely.
- Adamantine, heavily anchored structures, and magically reinforced metal count as much greater resistance; minor enchantment usually doubles the effective load, while powerful magic may exceed the band's limits entirely.

If a player proposes a use beyond those limits, the default answer is not "yes." The default answer is "the band holds, fails to move the target, or shatters."

### Threat Display

While a creature can see you controlling the band and it is visibly around that creature's neck, throat, or a similarly vulnerable body part, you have advantage on Charisma (Intimidation) checks made against that creature.

This advantage reflects immediate mortal threat. It does not charm the target, override fanaticism, or force compliance from mindless creatures.

### Shatter Threshold

The band is durable, but not indestructible.

- If a single effect forces the active band to bear more than 16,000 pounds of pressure, it shatters.
- The band cannot be shattered on command by simply willing it to fail.
- It is easiest to break when active, braced, and already under heavy strain.

When the band shatters:

- Every creature within 40 feet of it takes 4d6 thunder damage.
- The fragments immediately scream back toward the attuned user.
- Each creature in a 5-foot-wide line between the band and the attuned user must make a Dexterity saving throw against your Band Save DC, taking 6d6 slashing damage on a failed save, or half as much on a success.

### Breaking It Deliberately

Deliberately breaking the band requires extraordinary force or superior magic.

- A direct hit from a +2 or stronger magic weapon against the active band shatters it.
- A creature wielding a maul, sledgehammer, or similarly heavy striking tool can try to shatter the active band only if it is braced against a solid surface or already under meaningful tension.
- Doing so requires an action and a DC 27 Strength (Athletics) check.
- Ordinary improvised blows, casual hammering, or attempts made while the band hangs loose do not shatter it.

## History & Lore

No surviving text has yet explained what the band was truly for. Ruin scholars do not even agree that "Velaravos" is its original name; it may only be the closest modern rendering of the rune-sequence repeated along the inner face.

The strongest theory is that it was an ancient load-control tool from agcents a variable-tension band used by engineers, surgeons, or construct-tenders who needed precise pressure without wedges, ropes, or screws. If that is true, later scavengers discovered its crueler applications long before they rediscovered its intended ones.

## Attunement Details

- **Requirements:** None
- **Attunement Process:** Wear or hold the band for 1 hour while practicing the opening and closing hand-gestures that command it.
- **Known Limitation:** The band answers only to one attuned controller at a time.

## DM Notes

### Balance Notes

- This item is dangerous because it rewards setup, not because it should auto-win combat.
- Neck placement is intentionally gated behind stealth, surprise, restraint, or a helpless target.
- The cleanest counterplay is to attack the user and break concentration.
- The band does not fly around the battlefield like a whip; it expands and contracts where it has been placed.

### Adjudication Limits

- It cannot cut by expanding through a creature or object.
- It cannot teleport into sealed armor, through walls, or around anatomy it cannot physically reach.
- If a target changes shape, becomes incorporeal, or otherwise no longer fits the band, the creature escapes.
- If a creative use would require more than the listed pressure budget, the safest ruling is that the band stalls or shatters.

### Tone

This is an ancient tool with horrifying side effects, not a cartoon execution gimmick. When it succeeds, it should feel deliberate, earned, and frightening.
