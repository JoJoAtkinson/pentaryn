# Boots of the Last Watch

> A worn pair of dark leather boots, slightly too cold to the touch. No inscription. No maker's mark. They fit perfectly, regardless of the foot.

## Description

The Boots of the Last Watch look like nothing. Dark leather, worn at the heel, unremarkable. They could belong to any traveler. There is no identifying mark, no sigil on the sole, no magical glow. They are, by every visible measure, boots someone left by the fire.

But once attuned, they remember you. Specifically, they remember when you are supposed to be asleep, and ensure you are not — not for the last hour of it.

They don't jolt you awake. There is no alarm, no magic flash. You simply open your eyes one hour before your companions expect to rouse you, with the quiet, certain knowledge that your shift has started. Whether you were assigned a watch or not.

Your companions will eventually stop arguing about whose turn it is.

## Properties & Mechanics

### Curse — The Unasked Watch

The boots are cursed. Once attuned, you automatically wake during the final hour before dawn (or the final hour of any agreed rest period), regardless of assigned watch schedule. You cannot sleep through this window. *Remove Curse* ends the attunement but the feeling of the watch lingers for 1d4 days — you wake anyway, out of habit, glaring at nothing.

### Benefit — Cannot Be Surprised

- You **cannot be surprised**. If an enemy initiates combat while your party is resting, you are automatically conscious and alert. You act on the first round of combat without restriction.
- You have **advantage on all Perception checks made at night** or in low-light conditions while on watch.
- Once per long rest, you may **warn a sleeping ally** as a free action at the start of combat — that ally wakes immediately and is not surprised, though they may still act last in initiative depending on their roll.

## Curse Removal

*Remove Curse* cast at 3rd level or higher ends attunement. The boots can then be safely removed. They will immediately attune to the next person who puts them on and falls asleep.

## History & Lore

No one agrees on where the first Boots of the Last Watch came from. The most common story is a militia commander who couldn't trust her sentries — so she made the watch the boots' problem instead of theirs. An enchanter, tired of being blamed for ambushes, agreed to help. The agreement was meant to be temporary.

The boots did their job so well she never took them off.

When she died, reportedly in bed at a reasonable hour for the first time in years, the boots were found set neatly beside the door. No one is certain how they got there. No one wanted to put them on to find out.

There are believed to be several pairs in circulation. They look identical. There is no way to tell them apart from ordinary travel boots until someone sleeps wearing them.

## Notes & DM Hooks

- A merchant who hasn't slept a full night in three years is quietly desperate to sell this ring. He hasn't told anyone what it does.
- The party finds a guard post abandoned — the guards are technically still present, just all awake at the wrong time, exhausted, and deeply confused about whose fault this is.
- An Araethilion scout was found sleeping at her post. She's been wearing this ring for four months and didn't know. The elves do not find this as funny as the party might.
