# Tile of the False Wall

> A flat ceramic tile, roughly hand-sized, glazed in dirty grey. One side is blank. The other is painted with a brick pattern so precisely rendered it looks more like a wall than most walls do.

## Description

The tile is unremarkable to look at — a ceramic square, about the size of a palm, with a painted brick face on one side. The glaze is old and slightly cracked at one corner. It looks like something pried off a building by someone who couldn't explain why they kept it.

When pressed flat against any solid surface and released, the tile clings in place and projects a seamless illusion of a continuous wall section extending outward from it — 10 feet wide, 10 feet tall, matching the texture and colour of the surface it's mounted on. A doorway becomes a wall. A corridor becomes a dead end. A cart becomes part of a building.

The illusion is visual only. It makes no sound and has no substance. A creature that touches it or succeeds on a DC 13 Investigation check (as an action) sees through it.

## Properties & Mechanics

### False Wall (1/Day)
Press the tile to any solid vertical surface as an action. Until the tile is removed or 1 hour passes (whichever comes first), a 10-foot-wide, 10-foot-tall illusion of a solid wall appears, centred on the tile. The illusion:
- Matches the visual texture of the surface it's mounted on
- Does not produce sound, smell, or physical resistance
- Can be seen through by a creature that succeeds on a DC 13 Wisdom (Perception) or Intelligence (Investigation) check (action required)
- Ends immediately if the tile is pulled free

The tile resets at dawn.

### Recovery
The tile can be retrieved after use. It does not break when removed.

## History & Lore

The origin is mundane: a tile-maker's apprentice who also dabbled in minor enchantment, primarily to hide the part of the workshop where he kept his private wine. The design was later refined by a thieves' guild in Merrowgate, who found it considerably more useful than the apprentice had intended.

Several copies exist. They are traded, stolen, and occasionally left behind in places where someone needed a wall very badly and forgot to take it with them.

## DM Notes & Hooks

- Found propped against the *inside* of a bricked-up doorway, suspiciously clean
- A merchant swears the alley exit was a wall when she checked it an hour ago
- Someone used this tile to hide a door; they hid it so well they can no longer find the tile
