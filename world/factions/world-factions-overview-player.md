## üßë‚Äçüåæ Humans

### Ardenhaven (AR-den-HAY-vun) ‚Äî The Haven Realm

- **People:** Mostly humans, with notable halfling, gnome, and other minorities.
- **Reputation:** Hopeful, argumentative, rebuilding from war; a place where common voices matter more than in most realms.
- **Government (Public View):** A charter-bound monarchy checked by a powerful council of lords, guilds, and temples.
- **What People Say:**
  - "Messy, but at least you can speak your mind."
  - "They're always debating something, but they mean well."
- **Relations:** Locked in a wary peace with the Calderon Imperium; friendly trade and defense pacts with orc confederations and some dwarven leagues.
- **Capital:** Ardenford (kingdom seat)

### Calderon Imperium (CAL-der-on im-PEER-ee-um) ‚Äî The Ashen Empire

- **People:** Primarily humans, ruling over many subject peoples.
- **Reputation:** Powerful, disciplined, heavily organized; admired for its roads and laws, feared for its armies.
- **Government (Public View):** A central Emperor with a vast bureaucracy, legions, and a network of officials and magistrates.
- **What People Say:**
  - "If you like safety and order, you'll find it there."
  - "Don't cross their laws; their justice is efficient and unforgiving."
- **Relations:** Cold peace and tense trade with Ardenhaven; constant attention on its borders with orcish lands and other rivals.
- **Capital:** Ironstead (imperial seat)

### Merrowgate (MER-oh-gate) ‚Äî The City of Bargains

- **People:** Truly mixed‚Äîhumans, dwarves, elves, orcs, and more, with no single group dominant.
- **Reputation:** The crossroads of the world, where coin speaks louder than blood or banners.
- **Government (Public View):** A council of merchant princes, guildmasters, and influential families.
- **What People Say:**
  - "If it can be bought, you'll find it in Merrowgate."
  - "Everyone smiles; nobody shows their whole hand."
- **Relations:** Officially neutral; trades with almost everyone, often playing factions off each other with contracts and clever diplomacy.
- **Capital/City-State:** Merrowgate

### Elderholt (EL-der-HOLT) ‚Äî The Coven Realm

- **People:** Humans, with witches standing as a distinct and respected order.
- **Reputation:** Safest countryside in the region against monsters and curses, but known for its uncanny witchcraft and strict traditions.
- **Government (Public View):** Local covens hold power alongside village leaders, all sworn to a High Coven or Witch-Queen.
- **What People Say:**
  - "You may not like their ways, but their wards hold."
  - "If you're desperate, you go to Elderholt and make a bargain."
- **Relations:** Mostly inward-focused, trading protection rituals, charms, and herbal lore; some realms rely on Elderholt wards during bad years.
- **Capital:** Elderstead (coven seat)

### Dulgarum (DOOL-gah-room) ‚Äî The Oathbound Holds

- **People:** Dwarves.
- **Reputation:** Traditional, unyielding, fiercely honorable; known for ancient fortresses and unmatched heavy infantry.
- **Government (Public View):** A High King or Queen chosen from the clans, ruling from the mountain throne with a council of elders.
- **What People Say:**
  - "If they swear an oath, they keep it."
  - "They delve deeper than most would dare‚Äîand guard what they find."
- **Relations:** Seen as reliable but conservative partners; trade metals, crafted arms, and stonework, and are cautious about long-term alliances.
- **Capital:** Khargun-dur (KHAR-gun-DOOR) (mountain throne)

### Garhammar Trade League (GAR-ham-mar) ‚Äî The Free Guild-League

- **People:** Primarily dwarves, with many non-dwarf workers and partners.
- **Reputation:** Innovative, practical, and profit-driven; considered modernists compared to the more traditional holds.
- **Government (Public View):** Guild charters and trade councils instead of kings‚Äîled by the wealthiest and most influential guilds.
- **What People Say:**
  - "They'll sell you anything‚Äîif the coin is right."
  - "Don't expect sentiment; expect contracts."
- **Relations:** Maintains trade with nearly everyone, including human empires and orc confederations; famed for weapons, machines, and engineering.
- **Capital:** Garhammar (guild seat)

## ü™ì Orcs

### Garrok Confederation (GAR-rok) ‚Äî Western Orc Confederation

- **People:** Orcs, with some mixed-heritage and allied warriors.
- **Reputation:** Stern but honorable; slow to trust, slow to forgive, but steadfast once bonds are forged.
- **Government (Public View):** Independent clans bound by shared customs and councils; in times of great danger, they choose a High War-Leader.
- **What People Say:**
  - "If a Garrokan gives their word, take it seriously."
  - "They remember old raids‚Äîand old favors."
- **Relations:** Historically raiders and enemies to many, now bound to some neighbors by hard-won treaties and mutual respect.
- **Capital:** Garrok (confederation seat)

### Rakthok Horde (RAK-thok) ‚Äî Eastern Orc Horde

- **People:** Orcs.
- **Reputation:** Hard-bitten survivors from harsh, blighted lands; feared for sudden, decisive campaigns.
- **Government (Public View):** Spiritual shamans guide scattered warbands; in crises, visions and omens raise a Chosen Warlord to lead.
- **What People Say:**
  - "They've been burned too many times to trust easily."
  - "When their banners move, someone's border is about to bleed."
- **Relations:** Long-standing conflict with the Calderon Imperium; most neighbors view them with wary respect or open fear.
- **Capital:** Rakthok (spiritual seat)

## Big Picture

**Four Living Powers:** Humans, Dwarves, Elves, and Orcs shape the world's politics and control its largest territories. These four races dominate trade, diplomacy, and warfare.

**The Fifth Power is Dead‚ÄîBut Not Gone:** A long-fallen ancient empire once ruled vast lands. Though its people are gone and its cities lie in ruins, their magic lingers. Artifacts, wards, and ancient workings still pulse with power in forgotten places. If someone were to master even a fraction of what remains, they could reshape‚Äîor destroy‚Äîthe world. The ruins are both treasure and threat, and everyone knows it.

**But the World is Diverse:** Halflings, gnomes, tieflings, dragonborn, and countless other peoples live throughout the lands. Some thrive in the great cities as merchants, artisans, and citizens. Others maintain their own towns, villages, and communities‚Äîsome hidden, some remote, some openly trading with the major powers. You'll find all kinds of folk on the road, in taverns, and standing beside you when trouble comes calling.

**No Matter Your Heritage:** Whether you were raised in a human kingdom, a dwarven hold, an elven sanctuary, an orc confederation‚Äîor in a halfling river village, a gnomish workshop district, a tiefling quarter, or a dragonborn merchant clan‚Äîthere's a place for you in this world. Your story matters as much as any lord's or emperor's.
