# Thrulm — The Sealed Hollow

**Dwarven:** *Thrulm* — sealed hollow; the absence where stone should be; a wound in the mountain.

---

## Overview

Thrulm is not a place dwarves speak of. It is not on maps. The High King's records refer to it in euphemisms: "the place beyond the third gate," "the chamber we do not enter," "the sealed thing."

It is real. It sits beneath the lowest hold of Dulgarum, in a shaft so deep that the air grows thin and cold. The dwarves who discovered it centuries ago sealed it with **breachgates and runes**, and decreed that no dwarf should go there.

No dwarf has, for two hundred years.

Until now, something below has begun to disturb the seals. The stone trembles. The runes flicker. And the Deep Watch has lost contact with the watch-posts stationed at the third gate.

---

## What Thrulm Is

A natural cavity in the mountain—or something that *was* natural. The walls are unworked stone, ancient beyond measure, and in places they are smooth in ways stone should never be smooth. The floor is hard-packed earth mixed with something that glitters in torch-light but leaves no trace when touched.

The air tastes of copper and deep earth. In the deepest part of the chamber, where the runes are thickest, there is a silence so profound it seems to press against the ears.

**The Beholder's Domain:** Something has made Thrulm its home. The dwarves call it many things—none of them names. The Deep Watch calls it "the hunger in the deep." It is not bound to the chamber, but it *prefers* it. The stone seems to know it. The air seems to obey it.

---

## Geography

### The Approach
- **Third Sealed Gate:** Heavy iron, warded with runes of binding and closure. The seal is cracking. Dwarves should not go further.
- **The Shaft:** A natural descent, carved slightly by dwarf-hands centuries ago. Handholds are worn smooth by age. It slopes down for nearly 300 feet.
- **The Threshold:** Where the shaft opens into the main chamber. The floor here is marked with old blood and newer scratches—something has been moving in and out.

### The Chamber Itself
- **The Main Vault:** Vast. Natural columns of stone rise from floor to ceiling. Shadows pool in corners that torchlight can't quite reach.
- **The Altar (Central Point):** A formation of stone shaped like a table or a throne. This is where the thing *rests*. When it's here, the stone around it seems to *listen*.
- **The Sealed Shrine:** A collapsed structure in one corner, older than the chamber itself. Whoever built it did not build like dwarves. Some of the stones have writing that hurts to look at.
- **Clay Deposits:** Large veins of pale, malleable clay run through the chamber walls and floor. The beholder has gathered these into shallow pits and work-areas. Fresh clay is still damp; older deposits are dried and cracked. This is where the beholder *shapes* its derro.
- **The Deeper Shaft:** Beyond the main chamber, a second descent continues downward. It has never been fully explored. Something moves in that darkness.

## Inhabitants

### The Beholder (Name TBD)
A creature drawn to this place by the *absence* of divinity—the shrine is sealed, its god is gone, and the magic meant to contain both has created a void the beholder feeds on.

**It is not a standard beholder.** It does not see with eyes. It perceives through the warping of space around the sealed shrine, the way pressure builds in places where magic tries and fails to hold.

It is territorial but not mindless. It gathers thralls—derro who wander into its domain and are changed by proximity to the shrine and the beholder's presence.

### The Derro (Multiple Variants)
Derro from the upper holds sometimes descend this far, drawn by the hum of forbidden magic or sent by the beholder itself. They are changed by what they touch down here.

**Variants include:**
- Standard Deep Watch derro (intact, still loyal to Dulgarum)
- Beholder-thralls (partially dominated, caught between obedience to the oath and slavery to the hunger)
- Shrine-touched derro (infused with the power of the sealed god, dangerous and unpredictable)

---

## Encounter Progression

The party does not face the beholder immediately. Thrulm reveals itself in stages:

1. **First Contact:** Ruins of a Deep Watch camp; evidence of panic and retreat
2. **Lower Descent:** Encounters with lesser derro and the beholder's influence growing stronger
3. **The Threshold:** A major derro encounter; the party realizes something larger is orchestrating this
4. **Approach to the Chamber:** The beholder's presence becomes undeniable; the stone itself seems hostile
5. **The Confrontation:** The beholder in its domain, fully aware of the party, and the question of what it *is* and why it's really here

---

## The Sealed Shrine

Before Thrulm was sealed, it contained a shrine to something dwarves no longer speak of. The shrine was desecrated or destroyed—records are unclear. What remains is:

- Fragments of stone carved with non-dwarven runes
- A sensation of *wrongness* when approaching—the body resists, the mind recoils
- The beholder's fascination with it; the creature circles the ruins as if reading something invisible

**Mystery:** Did the dwarves seal the shrine, or did something seal itself *within* the shrine, and the dwarves sealed the chamber to contain it?

---

## The Air of Thrulm

Everything here feels ancient and angry. The silence is not restful—it is the silence of something holding its breath.

Dwarves who enter Thrulm experience:
- **Unease:** A sense of being watched by the stone itself
- **Disorientation:** Distances seem wrong; the chamber is larger inside than its entrance suggests
- **Hunger:** Not for food, but for *answers*—an almost maddening need to understand what happened here
- **Pressure:** A weight on the chest, as if the mountain is leaning down to listen

## DM Notes

### Why the Beholder is Here
The beholder was not *sent* to Thrulm. It *found* Thrulm, the way a wound draws infection. The sealed shrine, the broken oaths, the hollow space where a god once was—these create a void the creature feeds on.

The beholder is not evil in the way a demon is evil. It is an *absence* made manifest. It unmakes things. It feeds on the space between what should be and what is.

### What the Party Can Learn
- The shrine belonged to Khôr-Bhog-Khôr (the ancient dwarven god)
- The beholder arrived *after* the sealing, drawn by the void
- If the beholder is driven out or killed, the seal will fail—and something else might escape from deeper down
- The High King knows about Thrulm, but not its true nature. A terrible bargain has been made to keep it sealed

### Potential Endings
- **Seal it again:** Difficult ritual, requires understanding of the original sealing
- **Drive the beholder deeper:** It retreats to the lower shaft; the problem is delayed, not solved
- **Understand the beholder:** It may not be an enemy at all—just a thing that exists in the absence
- **Restore the shrine:** Dangerous; might attract the *real* god, or something worse
