### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC13-A** — FEEL: VS 45 psychic (dispatch), all-fail saves, instant wipe of healthy-ish party.
Breakdown: Bazgar 30 HP → -15 (overkill 15), Marwen 20 HP → -25 (overkill 25), Sabriel 38 HP → -7 (just over). Average 6d10 = 33; this roll was 45 (86th percentile). At average damage: Bazgar drops, Sabriel barely survives (38-16=22 half-dmg pass case). The fight outcome is extremely roll-dependent on both VS damage AND all-three-fail saves. DC 16 Wis vs party Wis mods +2/+1/+0 yields individual fail rates of ~70%/75%/80%. All-three-fail probability ≈ 42%. When all three fail, VS wins regardless of damage. This is the mechanism — not a bug — but DM should call out "this is likely going to end the fight" before rolling, per FI-93 prior note.

**FI-FC13-B** — FEEL (13th-cycle confirm): Beholder took **0 HP damage** in 13th consecutive final-confrontation cycle. Party output: 0 hp to beholder. Bazgar's 2 attacks on STD-B (15 hp) and Marwen's Fireball on NPCs (not beholder) are the entire offensive output. DR missed (R1), and VS ended combat before any meaningful counterplay could target the beholder directly. The killing blow cycle: STD-A AR → VS → over. Party never got Bazgar into melee range of beholder.

**FI-FC13-C** — FEEL (pattern): DR missed AGAIN (to-hit 8, d20=2). DR to-hit +6 vs Marwen AC 15 requires d20≥9 (60% hit rate). Second consecutive FC run with DR miss R1. Previous run (12th cycle) was a crash. 10th cycle also had DR miss. This is normal variance but contributes to R1 survivability being heavily RNG-gated on a ~40% miss-or-survive outcome.

**FI-FC13-D** — FEEL (confirm): DC 16 Wis save with party Wis mods +2/+1/+0 yields 70-80% per-PC fail rates. All-three-fail probability ≈ 42% per VS fire. When VS fires, coin-flip whether all three go down vs 1-2 survive. The pattern: 1-2 survivors is actually the minority outcome. The encounter consistently plays out as either "party never sees VS" (DR kills someone first and VS is never worth firing) or "VS wipes everyone." No middle ground.

**FI-FC13-E** — FEEL: Healing spell hazard (healing spells deal necrotic instead of healing) never came up — Sabriel went unconscious before attempting any spell-based heals. LoH was the intended backup but it too was rendered moot. Over 13 FC cycles, this chamber hazard has come up in narration zero times in the actual combat flow. Consider a callout box in the .md to ensure the DM announces it before the first heal attempt (currently documented in the Interaction Notes but not prominently flagged in the checklist).

**FI-FC13-F (DESIGN DECISION)** — VS timing in a full encounter: the beholder held VS in R1 (chose DR instead) and fired it in R2 from altitude ~20 ft after shrine_drift descend. This was tactically correct per the .md guidance. But the result is a 2-round fight with 0 PC counterplay against the beholder. Party counterplay = kill STDs (partial success: T2 eliminated, STD-B wounded) and survive long enough to close. They didn't. Is "correct beholder play → 2-round wipe" the intended table experience? Previous run (12th-cycle crash) was also extrapolated R2-3. 1st-cycle (2026-05-28) was R2 TPK. 9th-cycle (2026-05-27) was R3 TPK. 10th-cycle (2026-05-27) was R1-2 sim artifact. Pattern: full encounter resolves R2-R3 TPK regardless of approach.

**FI-FC13-G** — FEEL (first-time data): STD-B fired AR in R2 (STD-A failed recharge), but BOTH Bazgar and Sabriel saved (d20=19, d20=20 vs DC14). First time both targets saved an STD-B AR in any FC run. The high-roll saves were irrelevant because VS wiped everything 3 init-steps later. ### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC13-A** — STD-A Strike 1 was a nat 20 (to-hit 24 = 20+4). The dispatch does not model crit-dice-doubling in its output (it shows dmg 12, which is normal damage). At table, crit would double dice: 2d6+2+2×d4 necrotic. The dispatch's single-hit damage (12 slashing incl necrotic) may under-represent a crit. This is the MQ-77 class (dispatch vs sim roll divergence) applied to crit detection. **Recommendation**: the runner should note "dispatch nat-20 = double dice at table" and apply crit dice separately when to-hit equals nat-20+bonus. No spec change needed — this is DM execution guidance.

**MQ-FC13-B** — Lair R2 manifest_thralls: 3 thralls gain 1 temp HP and each MAY use a reaction to move or make one weapon attack. This reaction was not modeled in the sim. In a live run, T1 (in range of Sabriel) and T3 (in range of Bazgar) would each have had a reaction attack immediately at init 20, before beholder acted at init 19. These 2 extra attacks could have softened the party further before VS. **This is a sim gap, not a spec bug.** At table: manifest_thralls reaction attacks are real and should be declared immediately by DM.

**MQ-FC13-C** — DD vs unconscious Sabriel (end of Marwen's R2 turn, Sabriel already at -7 HP): Sabriel is unconscious but was downed this same round. She still has spell slots (L2×1). Per FI-NEW-EV6-C (applied fix): DD on unconscious target with no standing melee threat → DD proceeds (no downgrade needed since all 3 PCs are down). Applied correctly. But edge case: DD Cha save on an unconscious creature — RAW, unconscious creatures auto-fail Str and Dex saves but NOT Cha saves (d20+mod per RAW). The sim applied Sabriel's Cha mod (+3) and she rolled 6→total 9, fail. Correct RAW. Logged for confirmation.
