### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SRR15-A — Berserk 3-line cap vs alive PCs (design question, not auto-fixed)**
Berserk spec has exactly 3 attack entries hard-coded (for the 3-PC Compass Edge party). Once Marwen died (R2) and Sabriel died (R4), Berserk continued printing 3 attack lines with the third line never applied. The DM (or harness) must skip attack lines beyond alive-PC count. This is workable at-table but the spec silently assumes party-size-3. If the party ever shrinks to 2 or grows to 4, the spec should be re-authored. For now: DM coaching note acceptable; no spec change needed.

**FI-SRR15-B — Berserk fired 4 consecutive turns R3–R6 for R3 (seed 494635 variance)**
After R1 was slain, R3 recharged Berserk in rounds 3, 4, 5, and 6 consecutively (recharge values: 6, 6, 6, 5). Probability: (2/6)^4 ≈ 1.2%. This is extreme variance, not a balance issue. The feels-consequence was that R3 effectively never used Multiattack in rounds 3–6. With Berserk locking movement, R3 spent 4 consecutive turns rooted in place swinging at everyone in reach. The "plants feet and spins" narration fired 4 times back-to-back; a human DM might want to vary the tactic. No spec change warranted; worth flagging as a dice-variance artifact.

**FI-SRR15-C — Taunt DC 12 mostly resisted (5/6 saves in rounds 1–3)**
Same structural finding as prior cycles (14th, 13th): Taunt DC 12 is resisted ~70% of the time by this party. In the two rounds where it succeeded (R2: R2 taunted Sabriel, R3 taunted Bazgar), the disadvantage applied correctly but had marginal mechanical impact (PCs mostly targeted the lowest-HP rager anyway, which happened to be the taunting rager). The one meaningful case: Bazgar had disadvantage on attacks vs R2 in R3 (taunt from R3), but he was already attacking R2 as focus target — so disadvantage activated on the "wrong" target pairing (taunted by R3, attacking R2 = disadvantage should fire). This is correct per spec but invisible in narration. 15th cycle confirm of FI-SRR13-B/14-B: DC 12 CHA is structurally too easy for a level-5 party. Recommend DC 13 or switch to WIS save.

**FI-SRR15-D — Madness Endurance +1 invisible and never decisive (15th cycle confirm)**
Madness Endurance (+1 attack rolls after taking damage until end of next turn) applied every round to every rager that was hit, but the +1 never converted a miss to a hit in the simulation outcomes. At +4 base to-hit vs AC 15/18/19, the +1 gives 5% delta. Across 15 cycles, it has not been cited as a deciding factor. The trait is mechanically correct but creates invisible bookkeeping overhead with near-zero table impact. Consider: (a) fold into base to-hit (+5 permanent), (b) change to advantage on next attack (more visible), or (c) keep as-is and accept it's flavor.

**FI-SRR15-E — Pyrrhic victory feel (POSITIVE)**
Marwen died R2 from back-to-back Multiattack hits (AC 15 = ~50% hit rate for each swing; two hits in sequence = 25% per rager). Sabriel died R4 from Berserk at 2HP (unavoidable). Bazgar won solo R5–R7. The encounter felt "right" for a pure melee rush: caster went first, divine martial survived longer, armored fighter outlasted everyone. The death progression was meaningful — each death was traceable to a mechanic (Marwen: low AC vs high-frequency attacks; Sabriel: attrition from Berserk arcs; Bazgar: AC 18 wall carried him). No feel complaints for the encounter design at this slice.

**FI-SRR15-F — Berserk "cannot move" constraint unenforced in simulation**
The berserk spec includes prerequisite text: "Rager cannot move on this turn." In R5–R6, with R3 being the last rager, the "cannot move" constraint was moot (R3 was already in melee). But in a real fight with repositioning, the movement-lock is a meaningful constraint that prevents Berserk from being used to charge in AND arc-sweep. This is unenforceable in harness simulation and requires DM coaching. Existing guidance in derro-rager.md Tactics section doesn't mention the movement trade-off explicitly. Consider adding a bullet in the Tactics section: "Berserk is a stand-still option — use only when already in arc-range of multiple targets."

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SRR15-A — Taunt cross-round persistence (ongoing from 14th cycle)**
When a rager with initiative 7 (R3) taunts Bazgar with initiative 18, the taunt lands after Bazgar's turn. Bazgar then acts first in the next round while the condition is active — so he gets the full effect. But when a rager acts after Bazgar in the SAME round that Bazgar already moved to clean up, the taunt is effectively wasted for that round. This is standard 5e timing and working as intended, but coaching note for DM: "Taunt is most effective when the rager acts earlier in initiative than its target." No spec change.

**MQ-SRR15-B — Berserk-3-line spec vs party-size-flexibility (ongoing from 14th cycle)**
The hard-coded 3-attack Berserk spec is documented as a known limitation. If the party ever changes composition or size, the spec needs manual updating. Low priority given encounter is Compass-Edge-specific, but noting for future maintainability.
