### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SCT-6-A: CW double-buffer on barrage-firing ally — DM error magnet**
In R1, both SC-A and SC-B independently called weakness on SC-C (the barrage firer). The tactics text does correctly say "Do not Call Weakness an ally who plans to use Shard-Barrage this turn" but this rule is buried mid-paragraph after the init-priority and range-check paragraphs. In a 3-SC formation where SC-C has the highest initiative (acts first, fires Barrage), the other two SCs' "highest-init living ally" heuristic points directly at SC-C — right into the forbidden case. Simulation reproduced the mistake on the first cycle.

Recommendation: Add a one-line priority checklist at the TOP of the Call Weakness bullet:
> *(1) Never CW an ally that will Barrage this turn. (2) Among remaining allies, pick highest-initiative. (3) One CW per ally per round — stagger targets.)*

Not a spec bug; existing text is correct. This is a DM-coaching clarity issue.

**FI-SCT-6-B: Fireball as SC-formation killswitch**
Marwen's R2 Fireball (8d6 = 30 fire) reduced all 3 SCs to single-digit HP simultaneously. The SC-only formation has no melee body to block AOE access or impose difficult terrain between the caster and the back-line. The fight lasted exactly one round past the Fireball. This is structurally expected — SCs are a squishy ranged formation — but the "killswitch" quality means the slice is nearly binary: does Marwen land Fireball before dropping? If yes, VICTORY. If no (e.g. Marwen drops R1 from the triple-SC focus + PTV Barrage), potentially a win for the SCs.

This is design information, not a bug. The slice correctly tests the "feels distinct" goal — SCs are glass-cannon kite pressure, not durable. Log for human awareness.

**FI-SCT-6-C: Pack Tactics Voice disadv applied correctly and decisively**
Marwen had PTV disadv on her R2 Barrage save (SC-A hit her R1). She rolled (15, 7) → 7 vs DC13 — FAIL, 9 damage vs likely-HALF (15 would have passed). This is exactly PTV working as designed: a previous hit converting a later-round save to a fail. Confirmed positive mechanical function.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SCT-6-A: CW priority ordering in SC-only formation**
The tactics text has two rules that interact in an underspecified way:
1. "Pick the highest-initiative alive ally for CW"
2. "Do not CW an ally who will Barrage this turn"

When the highest-initiative ally is the barrage firer (as here: SC-C init 21), rule (1) and rule (2) point in opposite directions. The text implies rule (2) takes precedence — but a DM reading the checklist items in order will hit rule (1) first and commit the CW before reaching the Barrage warning.

**Recommendation:** State the precedence explicitly in checklist item 2: "Check if your CW target plans to Barrage BEFORE applying initiative priority. Barrage-check takes precedence."

Not a fundamental balance issue — total CW budget this run was 9 charges (3 per SC) and only 3 were spent (all wasted). The SC formation still lost, with or without CW functioning. No design-decision required, but a clarity edit to derro-shardcaller.md would help.

**MQ-SCT-6-B (MQ-77 class — known):** Shard-Barrage dispatcher rolled 3d6=4 internally; simulation re-rolled for HP impact. Output shows both numbers (dispatcher "Damage: 4 piercing" then sim "Rolled 3d6 barrage: 13"). Same class as previous threshold-patrol and solo-rager cycles. Not a new issue.
