### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-BEL3-3-A** Void Scream is the reliable TPK vector in the "limited" slice (no DR).
VS fired R3 (recharged from start — never spent), dealt 29 psychic to both standing PCs, who had been softened to 21 HP each by lair + multiattack. Even a half-save (14-15 psychic) would have dropped Bazgar from 21 HP. VS is the actual encounter-ender whether or not DR exists. In the "limited" slice, DR's absence barely matters — VS does the same work. Design implication: if playtesters ask "why didn't the beholder just use DR?", the answer is that VS is equally lethal and no less scary. Positive feel observation: the party got 2 full rounds of fighting before VS landed, and it felt earned.

**FI-BEL3-3-B** Drain Divinity failed to connect (Sabriel saved on 19, +3 = 22% chance to save given Cha +3 needing 13+). The beholder spent 3 LA on DD in R2 and got 0 return. However, the fight was over in R3 anyway, so the LA opportunity cost was negligible. Per FI-EV7-21-B (consecutive-fail escape hatch): this was only 1 failure so the escape hatch doesn't trigger. Coaching note: DD is correct play even with ~40% save rate — its resource denial value exceeds Void Ray output in longer fights.

**FI-BEL3-3-C** Thrall contribution marginal but distinct. Total direct damage from thralls this fight: 4 HP (Thrall-C's R3 axe swing on Marwen at 1 HP). However, their indirect contribution was significant: (a) the R3 manifest_thralls reposition put Thrall-C in position to deliver the re-down on Marwen, (b) the compel_thrall bonus action redirected correctly to Bazgar after Marwen went down (FI-FC27-A compliance). Thralls function as position-pressure and LoH-bleed rather than damage units — consistent with prior observations.

**FI-BEL3-3-D** LoH sustainability compressed by lair actions. Marwen went down twice: R2 from void_eruption (lair), R3 from Thrall-C's axe after manifest_thralls reposition. Sabriel's LoH revive in R2 was immediately negated by the lair+thrall chain in R3. With only 25 HP in the LoH pool and each revival costing 1 HP minimum, the beholder's lair rotation effectively drains LoH faster than Sabriel can spend it. This is elegant design — no rule change needed.

**FI-BEL3-3-E** Beholder took minimal damage: only 17 HP across the entire fight (10 fire from Fireball half-save, 7 slashing from Bazgar). Marwen's Fireball was the high point; the party dealt zero meaningful burst. The "intended to lose" design goal is well-achieved even without DR.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-BEL3-3-A** `unstable_ground` DC discrepancy — DB spec says DC 16 Dex save (effect: prone). Standard 5e beholder lair uses DC 13 Dex for the prone knockdown. Our version at DC 16 is consistent with the beholder's other lair DCs (void_eruption also DC 16 Dex). Confirm: is DC 16 intentional for unstable_ground? If yes, no change needed. If this was meant to be DC 13 (SRD default), the DB spec needs updating. Note: at DC 16 vs Bazgar's +2 Dex mod, he needs 14+ (35% save rate), making prone very likely each round it fires.

**SIM-BEL3-3-A** Simulation used DC 13 Str for unstable_ground (simulation error) vs spec's DC 16 Dex. Bazgar's Str save 13 vs DC 13 = barely saved; if correctly applied (DC 16 Dex, +2 mod), he would have needed 14+ and likely failed. This is a harness accuracy issue (class pattern, same as prior SIM-* notes) — no spec change.
