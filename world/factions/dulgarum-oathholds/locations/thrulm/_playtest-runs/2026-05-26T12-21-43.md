### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-130 (NEW — sim harness / MQ-61):** Stagger rule violated in simulation. STD-B had higher init (25) than STD-A (9). The sim checks stagger at STD-A's block (mid-round), but STD-B had already fired AR at init 25. Both STDs fired AR in R1 — exactly the pattern the stagger rule was designed to prevent. At table, the DM checks both STDs at START of round before any combatant acts, so this is a sim-only failure. Result: Bazgar took 18 necr+psych R1 and Marwen took 29 combined necr+psych (both ARs + T2 dagger) before acting — more pressure than correct stagger would produce. Correct play: STD-B fires AR (higher init), STD-A holds AR for R2. See MQ-61.

- **FI-131 (NEW — feel):** Disintegration recharged in 1 turn (d6=6, 16.7% per roll) and fired back-to-back R1+R2. Sabriel spent her entire R2 action on LoH to revive Marwen (10 HP), then the beholder immediately Disintegrated Marwen on its next action. The "revive-and-die" loop is mechanically correct and narratively brutal — the beholder uses its legendary economy (DD) to drain Sabriel's resources, then eliminates Marwen with the main action. **DM NOTE (recommend adding to beholder.md):** "If Sabriel revives Marwen at low HP (<15 HP), the beholder should prioritize Marwen for Disintegration on its next turn — this creates the 'you cannot save her' despair moment. Don't soften this. The correct cruel play is to make the revive feel wasted."

- **FI-132 (NEW — positive, kill-feel):** Lair action Void Eruption delivered the killing blow on Sabriel for the FIRST TIME across all 8 FC cycles. Sabriel at 5 HP (stripped by Drain Divinity + STD-B AR) was one-shot by VE 13 force (fail save 7 vs DC16). Previously, Disintegration and VS killed PCs; the lair action as Sabriel's killer creates a distinct narrative: the chamber itself kills her while the beholder is resource-managing from range. Felt earned — she'd been weakened over two rounds by multiple pressure sources.

- **FI-133 (NEW — positive, feel):** Beholder took 38 total damage (most across all 8 FC cycles; prior high was ~60 in specific cycles, some cycles took 0). Marwen's Scorching Ray 3-hit sequence (all hits, 27 fire R2) before Disintegration was a meaningful counterplay moment. The party-had-a-real-exchange feel was present even in a TPK — progress toward a 2-round beholder kill is visible. Design is working: party deals real damage but is eliminated by pressure accumulation.

- **FI-134 (NEW — feel/DM note):** The "STD-A multiattack vs high-AC Bazgar" zero-damage pattern continues (0/4 hits in R2). STDs are ineffective against Bazgar unless AR fires. Lair action VE (Dex save) was what softened Bazgar enough for STD-B's finishing blow. **Consider adding tactic note to shrine-touched-derro.md:** "When Bazgar (AC18) is the only remaining target and AR is on cooldown, multiattack will miss ~60% of swings (+5 vs AC18 = 45% hit chance). At this point, the DM should signal VE lair action as the intended finisher — call the altar 'pulsing' as flavor cue." This is a low-risk authoring addition.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-61 (NEW — sim harness):** Pre-round stagger check must fire BEFORE any combatant acts to correctly enforce the rule when STD-B has higher initiative than STD-A. Current sim evaluates stagger at STD-A's block only. Correct implementation: at the START of each round (before the first combatant in the order acts), check if both STDs are alive and both have AR available — if so, mark the LOWER-INITIATIVE STD as holding AR for this round. At table this is naturally handled by the DM's round-start mental checklist; the sim needs explicit pre-round logic.

- **MQ-62 (NEW — dispatch/sim dice gap):** Void Eruption (lair action) DB dispatch showed "Damage: 10 force (5+5=10)" but sim rolled 2d10 = 4 for Bazgar. For Sabriel, sim rolled 2d10 = 13 while dispatch DB showed 10. These are independent dice systems (dispatch uses pre-seeded cache from seed=42; sim uses hourly seed=494388). Outcome was unchanged (Sabriel died regardless of whether the value was 10 or 13 from 5 HP). Pattern continues from MQ-34/MQ-49/MQ-59. No fix needed; harness limitation noted.

- **DD-33 (8th FC confirm):** STD-B and STD-A consistently win high initiative positions (25 and 9 this cycle). High-init STDs consistently fire AR before PCs can act R1, adding 13–18 combined pressure before party's first action. The party has never "won" the AR race in FC. Design note: the encounter intends this (overwhelming pressure), but DM should be aware that STD-B init 25 effectively means the fight is decided in the first two actions.
