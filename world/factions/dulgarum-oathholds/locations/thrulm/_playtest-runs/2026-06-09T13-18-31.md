### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SCT6-47-A — CW 9/9 unspent 47th cycle (structural)**
  This is the 3rd consecutive cycle where CW fires zero times in the SCT formation. Root cause is compounding:
  1. SC-C (highest init, first to act) fires Barrage → cannot CW (own Barrage turn per tactics).
  2. SC-A and SC-B (init 4) never reach their turns — Fireball and Sabriel kill them first.
  3. SC-C in R2 has no alive allies to CW.
  CW as a "team mechanic" requires 2+ SCs alive for 2+ rounds. Against a Wizard with Fireball this never happens. The mechanic exists in a dead zone for this formation. **DESIGN DECISION needed:** consider making CW usable as a bonus action on SC's own attacks (self-advantage variant), or flag it as expected-dead-with-Fireball and accept the structural gap.

- **FI-SCT6-47-B — Fireball trivially decisive 47th consecutive SCT cycle**
  32 fire to all three SCs with no saves ≥ DC 15 (SCs: Dex +2, avg roll ~12, avg total ~14 — 1 below DC). A single Fireball eliminates the formation's threat in one action. Even when one SC saves, they're at ≤1 HP. Against a level-5 Wizard the SCT formation has essentially zero staying power. **DESIGN DECISION needed (carry from FI-SCT6-46-B):** consider adding a 3rd SC thrall buffer, raising SC HP to 40–45, or adding psychic resistance (thematic) to buy an extra round.

- **FI-SCT6-47-C — Fight resolved in 1.5 rounds, shortest SCT ever**
  SC-A and SC-B never acted. PTV had exactly one trigger (no impact). Stagger rule enforced correctly but moot. The encounter provides zero tension — no PC was ever in danger (Marwen's 20/32 HP and Sabriel's 28/44 HP are moderate at worst). For a formation that's *meant* to test ranged kiting feel, the fight ends before the kiting can develop.

- **FI-SCT6-47-D — Spread ≥25 ft rule not enforced in sim (carry MQ-SCT6-47-B)**
  The .md tactics explicitly say to spread ≥25 ft to prevent single-Fireball wipe. This sim places all SCs within Fireball radius. If spread were enforced, Fireball would hit at most 1-2 SCs per cast, dramatically extending the fight. **This is a sim gap, not a tactics bug.** DM note: at-table Fireball should only clip 1-2 SCs if spread is enforced. DM needs to announce spread at encounter setup.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-SCT6-47-A — Barrage-carrier assignment vs initiative exposure (carry MQ-SCT6-46-A)**
  FI-SCT-34-A assigns lowest-init SC as Barrage carrier. This means the highest-value AoE action is held by the SC most likely to die before acting (lowest init = acts after PCs). Recommend clarifying: if SC-C (highest init) can set up a decisive Barrage line R1, override FI-SCT-34-A and have SC-C fire. The current rule wastes Barrage when the lowest-init SC dies before acting.

- **MQ-SCT6-47-B — Spread enforcement and Fireball clipping (carry)**
  Sim never enforces ≥25 ft spread. The SCT formation's primary anti-Fireball defense is position, but position is invisible to the sim. Recommend: add a "Fireball clips N SCs" roll to Phase B sims (1d3: 1=one SC, 2=two SCs, 3=all three — with "all three" meaning formation broke spread) so the sim captures tactical variance. Otherwise SCT always dies to Fireball in R1.

- **MQ-SCT6-47-C — CW dispatch gap (carry from MQ-SCT6-45-B, 46-B)**
  `roll_combat_action(npc="derro-shardcaller", action="call_weakness")` returns narration only — it doesn't track or apply the advantage state. CW advantage is entirely simulated by the harness (manually granting advantage flags). If a DM dispatches CW via the runner, the advantage isn't automatically applied to the target's next roll. This is a runner UX gap (not a spec bug) but worth flagging.
