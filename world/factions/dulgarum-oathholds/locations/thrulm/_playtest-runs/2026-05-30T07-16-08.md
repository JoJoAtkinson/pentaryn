### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SRR-B (15th consecutive SRR cycle confirm) — Berserk fizzle vs AC 18-19:**
This cycle: 8 Berserk attack rolls across 4 Berserk uses → 3 hits, 5 misses. The two fully-fizzled Berserk uses (R1 Rager-B with aggro+ME, R5 Rager-C recharged) both whiffed against AC 18-19 targets. The "big dramatic sweep" lands less than 30% of the time against Sabriel (AC 19). This is the 15th consecutive solo-rager-rush cycle confirming this pattern. Prior recommendation (recharge 4-6 or miss-consolation) stands; situation not resolved.

**FI-SRR-15-A (NEW) — Healing Word revive into immediate re-down:**
Sabriel used Healing Word (BA) to revive Marwen at 6 HP in R4. Rager-C immediately targeted Marwen and re-downed her in the same round (both multiattack hits, 3+14 = 17 total, easily exceeding 6 HP). The revive was a heal-sink. The problem: Rager-C has a consistent habit of targeting the squishiest creature; Marwen at 6 HP post-revive is a trivial kill. **This feels bad at the table — Sabriel burned her only heal and it lasted one enemy turn.** Mitigations to consider: (a) Sabriel delays Healing Word until R-C is occupied / dead, (b) add a DM coaching note that Healing Word revives should wait until the PC can disengage or the rager is taunted away, (c) rager targeting behavior after a revive (does the rager remember the downed PC, or re-target fresh threat?). DESIGN DECISION for human review.

**FI-SRR-15-B (NEW) — Thunderwave vs Rager-C Con +4 unreliable:**
Marwen's Thunderwave (DC 14, 2nd-level, 3d8 push) against Rager-C: DC 14 vs +4 Con save = ~50% success rate. R-C rolled 17 (total 21) — succeeded. Result: half damage, no push. Marwen's primary emergency gap-creator and zone-control spell failed to create space. The interaction makes Thunderwave feel weak as a rager counter. Contrast with Beholder slice where Thunderwave would be irrelevant; here it was Marwen's key counter but the math is near-even. Not a bug, but worth noting that the rager's Con +4 makes DC 14 Thunderwave a coin flip rather than reliable escape. DESIGN DECISION flag: should rager Con be +3 instead of +4 to make wizard utility spells more reliably effective, or is the coin-flip intentional difficulty?

**FI-SRR-15-C — Madness Endurance DM tracking burden (cycle confirm):**
The +1 to-hit bonus on "took damage last turn" was incorrectly tracked at least once in this simulation (R2 Rager-A, where prev_took_damage was True but simulation read the wrong dict). At the live table, DMs would need to mentally note for each rager: "did it take damage on its last turn?" across 3 simultaneous NPCs while also tracking Berserk recharge and Taunt targets. Cognitive load is high. Consider printing the expected bonus in the Multiattack/Berserk output header, or adding a start-of-turn prompt line to the .md checklist that's harder to skip.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SRR-15-A — Rager-C targeting after Healing Word revive:**
When Marwen was revived at 6 HP and Rager-C multiattacked in the same round, the simulation had Rager-C target Marwen (lowest HP = squishiest target). The .md says "charge the strongest-looking enemy" R1, then "always Taunt the squishiest." What is the targeting priority for actions (not Taunt) mid-fight? Does Rager-C switch to lowest-HP targets after R1, or maintain original target? Clarification recommended in Tactics section.

**MQ-SRR-15-B — Taunt "until end of next turn" vs round-end reset:**
The simulation cleared Taunt status at end of each full round. The .md says "until the end of its [taunted creature's] next turn." If a PC is taunted AFTER their initiative slot, the disadvantage should persist through their next turn (i.e., next round), then expire. If taunted BEFORE their slot, it expires end of current round. This timing distinction matters for Taunts applied by ragers in initiative positions below the PC. Recommend the .md explicitly call out "the Taunt lasts until the END OF THE AFFECTED PC's next turn — if the PC has already acted this round, the effect persists until they act in the following round."
