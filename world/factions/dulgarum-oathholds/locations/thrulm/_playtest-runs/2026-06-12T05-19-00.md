### Simulation artifacts (not DB bugs)
- **SIM-SCT6-54-A (PTV solo-SC disadv):** In R4, SC-C is the sole surviving SC. The simulation incorrectly applied PTV disadvantage to Sabriel's Barrage save because PTV tracking was set by SC-C's own R3 multiattack hit. PTV fires "when an **ally** hits" — a solo SC has no allies, so PTV is disabled from R4 onward. This is the same error as SIM-TW2-45-B / MQ-TW2-45-A (14th consecutive SCT6 carry). Without PTV, Sabriel's R4 save would be a straight d20+0 — likely a save (R4 Barrage damage would be 4 half instead of 9, leaving Sabriel at 24 HP). This does not change the outcome but inflates R4 Barrage pressure.
- **SIM-SCT6-54-B (Fireball suppressed):** Simulation switched Marwen R1 from Fireball to Magic Missile because SCs were ≥25 ft apart. But a Fireball (20-ft radius) centered between two SCs would catch both even with 25-ft spread. Marwen should have Fireballed 2 SCs R1 (SC-A + SC-B or SC-B + SC-C). The MM substitution is overcautious — Fireball would have been available. (13th consec SCT6 Fireball-related sim decision issue.)

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SCT6-54-A (CW near-zero — 14th SCT6 consecutive)**
Only 1 of 9 CW charges fired (SC-A on SC-B, R1). SC-B held all 3 (MQ-12: SC-A already acted). SC-C held all 3 (no eligible allies remaining by the time it acts in most rounds). The 14th consecutive SCT6 playtest with effectively dead CW in a shardcaller-only formation. Corroborates FI-SCT6-53-B retire/redesign recommendation. The core problem: MQ-12 + the stagger assignment leave SC-mid and SC-low as permanent hold-charge-forever SCs. Consider: (a) allow SC-mid to CW SC-high if SC-high has not yet fired a bonus action (a "look-forward" exception); or (b) relax MQ-12 for 3-SC shardcaller-only formations to allow SC-mid to CW SC-low (same-round benefit IF SC-low is lowest init, not yet acted). Either would surface CW as a real gameplay element.

**FI-SCT6-54-B (Solo-SC endgame CW/PTV both dead)**
From R4 onward (SC-C alone): CW has no valid ally target (wasted resource); PTV has no triggering ally hits (passive dead). Solo SC is pure 2-shard-throw DPS + TR repositioning — mechanically fine but distinctively flat vs the multi-SC tactical texture. This is a structural feature (solo SC should feel weaker), but it currently feels *very* flat. A minor solo-SC rule could help: "if the last surviving shardcaller, CW may target itself (advantage on its own next attack roll, reflecting the voice calling its own intent)." Would cost 0 balance and add at least one interesting choice. For DESIGN DECISIONS.

**FI-SCT6-54-C (Sabriel AC19 almost immune to shard-throw)**
After Marwen fell (R2), both remaining SCs shifted to Sabriel (AC19). SC shard-throw +4 vs AC19 hits only on natural 15+ (~30%). SC-C fired 10 shard-throws at Sabriel in R3–R6 and scored only 3 hits. The fight extended purely because of hit-rate floor. Suggest: for SCT6 specifically, in the absence of the low-AC wizard target, SCs should prioritize Bazgar (AC18, ~35% hit rate) over Sabriel (AC19, ~30%). AC18 is modestly better, and Bazgar has more HP but less defense. Minor targeting change, mostly for feel.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SCT6-54-A (PTV ally definition — 14th carry)**
"When an ally within 30 ft hits": does the hitter set PTV on the target (ambient to all adjacent SCs), or does each OTHER SC set PTV independently when observing the hit? In a solo-SC endgame, PTV is either entirely disabled (hitter = self = not an ally of itself) or completely ambiguous. The MQ persists from TW2-45 and prior SCT6 cycles. A definitive ruling in the .md would resolve simulation tracking and DM ambiguity. Recommendation: "when ANY allied shardcaller within 30 ft of this SC hits a creature, this SC's voice marks the weakness. The HIT CREATURE has disadvantage on its next saving throw before end of its next turn." This explicitly makes the triggering SC responsible for others' hits, making the effect fully disabled when solo.

**MQ-SCT6-54-B (Barrage stagger reassignment — 14th carry from MQ-SCT6-52-A)**
If the designated low-init Barrage SC dies before its turn (common: Marwen Magic Missile or Fireball priority-targets the lowest-HP SC, often the designated Barrage SC), the Barrage is wasted for the round. Should the stagger assignment be adjusted to put Barrage on the HIGH-init SC (SC-A, init 22 this run)? High-init SC fires first and is less likely to be dead before acting. Counter: high-init Barrage means CW from SC-A is wasted (can't CW right before Barrage SC — CW the Barrage SC is useless). But this cycle showed CW is near-zero anyway. Recommend testing with SC-A designated Barrage R1 in next SCT6 run.
