### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-EV7-24-A: Lair action dead card (Manifest Thralls in solo encounter)**
In the empty-void slice, Manifest Thralls is permanently useless — there are no charmed creatures to buff. The DM has only 2 real lair action choices. This creates a narrower decision space than intended. Recommendation: add a line to `beholder-thrulm.md` lair actions section: *"In encounters where no thralls are present, treat Manifest Thralls as unavailable; use Unstable Ground or Void Eruption instead."*

**FI-EV7-24-B: Void Eruption is always optimal when party is clustered (structural)**
When all 3 PCs are alive and within 20 ft of the altar, Void Eruption (2d10 AOE, DC 16 DEX, damages all PCs) is strictly better than Unstable Ground (0 damage, 1 creature prone). This run: VE both R1 and R2 with no decision weight. Means lair action is effectively "roll 2d10 damage" every round, not a DM choice. If PCs spread out, Unstable Ground becomes viable — but the party has no incentive to spread away from the paladin's Aura of Protection. Consider: make VE 1/combat or require a "cooldown round" after use; or add a lair action that incentivizes spreading.

**FI-EV7-24-C: Beholder last-initiative dilutes dramatic opener (d20 = 1 this run)**
Initiative: Bazgar 20, Marwen 18, Sabriel 13, Beholder 4. Result: lair action → full party turn → legendary actions → THEN beholder's main action. Void Scream fired last, after the party had already acted twice. All three saves passed (half damage) — the dramatic opener landed with 45 total psychic damage but felt muted because the party had already chunked the beholder for 40 HP before VS fired. When Void Scream is the "opener," it needs to fire early. Consider: initiative advantage (roll 2d20 keep higher), static +5 to initiative, or "Void Scream is a legendary action (2 costs)" option that can be deployed reactively on a PC's turn.

**FI-EV7-24-D: Pyrrhic victory feel — POSITIVE (strong narrative)**
4th-cycle empty-void produced the most compelling outcome across all 24 empty-void cycles: Marwen permanently disintegrated, Bazgar downed by Void Ray, Sabriel alone at 8 HP delivering the killing blow with a divine smite. This is the exact intended loop — the party CAN win empty-void but it costs permanent resources (Marwen's life here). The disintegration mechanic functioning correctly creates the "earned victory" feeling. No spec change needed; flag as confirmation that the solo encounter design goal is being met.

**FI-EV7-24-E: Drain Divinity temp HP irrelevant at fight end (DD-34 re-confirm)**
Drain Divinity fired R3 (beholder at 11 HP) and R2 (beholder at 28 HP). Both attempts:
- R3: stripped Sabriel's 1st-level slot → beholder gained 2 temp HP. At 11 HP, 2 temp HP is pure noise.
- R2: stripped Sabriel's last 2nd-level slot → beholder gained 4 temp HP. At 28 HP, 4 temp HP bought one extra hit worth ~4 HP absorption.
The ability design assumes Drain Divinity fires in round 1-2 when the beholder is at full HP and temp HP accumulates to create a meaningful buffer. But in 24 empty-void cycles, Drain Divinity has only fired in R1 twice — the beholder is usually spending its legendary actions on Void Ray instead. This is a structural timing conflict: DD is a 3-action cost that competes with Void Ray (2 actions per damage). Recommendation (for human review): give DD a tactical trigger clause — e.g., "use only if Sabriel's HP is ≥ 30 and beholder HP is ≥ 60."

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-EV7-24-A: Lair action rotation when Manifest Thralls is unavailable**
The `.md` lists three lair actions with no priority ordering among them. In a solo encounter the DM naturally gravitates to VE every round, which the sim confirms (both rounds all-PCs-alive → VE). Should the `.md` prescribe a rotation (e.g., R1: VE, R2: UG, R3: VE, ...) to ensure Unstable Ground gets used and creates the prone→Maw grapple chain? Currently "Unstable Ground → prone → grapple → Maw critical" is a listed tactic but the lair action selection never reaches UG unless the DM consciously rotates.

**MQ-EV7-24-B: Maw "critical if grappled" trigger — confirm unconditional text**
The Maw output this run reads: *"if HIT: If the target is grappled by the beholder, this attack is a critical hit instead."* The beholder's multiattack description in `.md` says TL1+TL2+Maw together. In this run TL1 hit but grapple save passed. The Maw then tried to follow up. This interaction reads as: Maw always fires in multiattack regardless of grapple state, but only crits if target is already grappled. This matches 5e intent. Confirm in tactics note that Maw should always fire as part of multiattack (not conditional on grapple), and that the grapple-crit is a rider, not a prereq.

**MQ-EV7-24-C: Legendary action budget reset timing**
This run: beholder init = 4. Legendary budget starts at 3 at top of round. After Bazgar acts: used 2 for Void Ray + 1 for Move = 0 remaining. After Marwen: 0 remaining. After Sabriel: beholder should have gained its next round's legendary budget at start of ITS OWN TURN — which is init 4. Per 5e rules, legendary actions reset "at the start of [the beholder's] turn." In this sim, we tracked it as "resets at start of round" which effectively gave the budget a round earlier. For the 5-player-turn window between beholder turns, the budget should only reset at the beholder's init position. This sim gap doesn't change the 4-round outcome but matters for the exact timing of when legendaries become available again in rounds 2+.
