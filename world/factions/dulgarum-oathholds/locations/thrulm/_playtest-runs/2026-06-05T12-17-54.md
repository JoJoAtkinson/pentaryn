### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-TP26-A — Fireball trivializes DWD formation (expected, but worth noting)**: Both DWDs have HP 27, AC 15, Dex save +4. A DC 14 Fireball deals avg 28 damage; even on a save (14) they're near death. In a narrow corridor where both DWDs cluster, a first-initiative Fireball ends the patrol encounter before it begins. This is expected at level 5 vs CR 1/8 enemies — the slice is designed as a warning encounter, not a lethal one. However, if the design intent is that DWDs "feel like a genuine threat to the party's resources," the current setup may not deliver. DESIGN DECISION: intentional? Or should DWDs in threshold-patrol have a "patrol spread" starting position that makes double-Fireball less automatic?

- **FI-TP26-B — SC Call Weakness rarely pays off in threshold-patrol**: With initiative variance, SC frequently loses to Marwen (Dex +2 vs +2 — near-equal). When Marwen acts first and Fireballs the DWDs dead, SC's CW charge is wasted per MQ-66 (no valid target if DWD2 also falls). CW's value in this slice is essentially tied to whether DWDs survive round 1. Across 8 prior cycles (cycles 18-25 in the log reference), CW has been wasted 6/8 times in threshold-patrol. Consider: is CW's design intent primarily to shine in other slices (tank-wall, final-confrontation), where the Rager it buffs survives longer?

- **FI-TP26-C — Tactical Retreat trigger timing correct**: TR did not fire in round 2 because Bazgar hadn't closed to 15ft yet at SC's turn start. This is mechanically correct per MQ-73. However, it means TR *never* fires if SC dies before Bazgar closes — SC's signature ability never activates in this slice. Low-priority feel note.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-TP26-A — Simulation damage re-roll vs dispatched output**: The playtest script re-rolls damage independently for HP tracking rather than parsing the dispatched output's numbers. This creates minor divergence (Shard Barrage: dispatched output was 8 piercing, re-roll was 11). The correct approach for simulation accuracy is to parse the dispatched output and use those exact numbers. Future cron iterations should parse `output` and extract damage values rather than re-rolling. No spec change needed.

- **MQ-TP26-B — CW pre-check (MQ-66) redirect branch not in simulation**: The simulation doesn't enforce the MQ-66 redirect logic. In correct play, SC should redirect CW to DWD2 when DWD1 falls before SC's turn. The spec text covers this correctly. Simulation methodology gap only.
