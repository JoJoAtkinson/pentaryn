### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SCT-22-A: Corridor geometry breaks every "open-vault" confirmation (HIGH, POSITIVE)**
- All 21 previous shardcaller-team cycles used open-vault geometry (FI-SCT-6-A: PTV 0 activations; FI-SCT-6-B: Barrage 0 fires; FI-SCT-6-D: TR blocks CW). This cycle, corridor geometry changed all three: Barrage fired 3 times, PTV activated and flipped a save, and CW chains worked (3 charges used vs average ~1). The shardcaller formation is *terrain-dependent* in a way the test suite hasn't been exercising. Recommend: (a) add corridor-geometry to the rotation's terrain note so at least some cycles test it, (b) add "corridor / chokepoint" coaching section to tactics doc (already partially there but not highlighted as a formation-defining scenario).
- **Human review: does the corridor variant need separate slice rotation, or just a terrain-flag field in thrulm-rotation.json?**

**FI-SCT-22-B: PTV FIRST CONFIRMED ACTIVATION in shardcaller-team slice (HIGH, POSITIVE)**
- After 21 consecutive 0-activation cycles in open vault, PTV finally fired: SC-A hit Marwen R1, SC-B hit Marwen R1 → Marwen had PTV disadv on next save. SC-B's R2 Barrage: Marwen rolled SAVE (16), but PTV disadvantage second roll was 6 → FAIL. Marwen went DOWN. PTV changed the fight outcome by one down/not-down state. Mechanic works. The corridor geometry enabled it by keeping SCs in attack-cohesion range of the same target.
- **No spec change needed. Log as positive confirmation.**

**FI-SCT-22-C: CW advance-grant (SC-B → SC-C at end of R1) worked correctly but is fragile**
- SC-B granted CW to SC-C at init 5 R1 for use in R2. SC-C had already acted (init 17). Per MQ-12, CW was valid because SC-C's HP > 20 at time of grant. In R2, SC-C's Barrage did NOT recharge, so it Multiattacked with CW advantage (attack-roll turn → CW useful). CW used correctly. However: IF the Barrage had recharged in R2, SC-C would have fired Barrage (priority ability per tactics), wasting the CW advantage entirely. The granter (SC-B) had no way to predict the recharge outcome. This is the "CW-before-Barrage" recharge-uncertainty scenario: the spec guides *don't* grant CW to a Barrage-user, but the granter can't know whether the Barrage will recharge on the next turn.
- **Recommendation (coaching, not spec change):** Add to tactics: "When granting CW to an ally with an unrecharged Barrage, note that if their recharge succeeds before your CW expires, the advantage is wasted. This is acceptable: grant CW to the highest-initiative alive ally regardless of their recharge state — the alternative (holding all CW every round a recharge is possible) is worse."

**FI-SCT-22-D: Scorching Ray all-miss (0/3 beams, 9% event) left SC-B alive to fire Barrage**
- Marwen's R2 Scorching Ray missed all 3 beams. This is a 9% probability event. Had even 1 beam hit SC-B (10 HP, beam avg 7 dmg), SC-B would have been at 3 HP and Sabriel could kill it before SC-B fires the Barrage that downed Marwen. The all-miss was the pivotal bad-luck moment. Not a spec issue; variance is fine. Note for DM coaching: against a low-HP target (≤12 HP), Fire Bolt (1 attack, higher expected dmg per spell slot) is sometimes a better option than Scorching Ray (3 attacks at lower damage each, higher miss risk as a set).
- **No spec change. Log as high-variance outcome.**

**FI-SCT-22-E: SC-C "last SC alone" AC19 wall confirmed (22nd cycle)**
- In R3, SC-C was the sole surviving SC vs Bazgar (AC 18) and Sabriel (AC 19). Both throws in R3 missed (need 15+ on d20 with +4 = hit on ≥ 15, 30% hit chance). AC 19 is nearly a dead zone for +4 to-hit. This is a pattern from prior cycles (FI-SRR11-B "AC19-wall"). When one SC survives alone, its contribution drops to ~30% hit rate against divine martials. Not a bug — the kiting feel is intentional and the SC is not supposed to win 3v1 — but DM should narrate the SC as frantic, not threatening, at this stage.
- **No spec change. Log as confirmed pattern.**

**FI-SCT-22-F: 3× Barrages fired across the fight (vs 0 in all prior cycles) — corridor changes pacing**
- Barrages fired: SC-C R1 (9 dmg, corridor entry), SC-B R2 (10 dmg, PTV-flip key moment), SC-C R4 (11 dmg, recharged). Compare to 21 prior cycles: 0 barrages. The 3-SC stagger protocol worked correctly in all 3 fires. This fight felt distinctly different from prior cycles — more area pressure, more save checks, Marwen forced DOWN. Corridor is the key variable.
- **Positive pacing note. The shardcaller formation was designed to use Barrage as a pressure tool, and it finally executed that design.**

**MQ-SCT-22-B: Is SC-B (≤12 HP, stagger open) correct to fire Barrage R2?**
- Tactics say "3+ enemies in a line → Shard-Barrage." In R2, PCs have partially spread (Sabriel closed to melee R1, Marwen is mid-corridor). Can SC-B draw a 15-ft line through 2+ PCs?
