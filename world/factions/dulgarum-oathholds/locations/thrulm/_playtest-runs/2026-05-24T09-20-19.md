### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**DD-47 (new, authoring fixes applied above):** Three sub-issues identified in 3rd-cycle shardcaller-team:

1. **Tactical Retreat 0-fire across all 3 shardcaller-team cycles** — structurally never triggered. SC-A died R1 before Bazgar closed; SC-B was at 19 HP when Bazgar melee'd it (it chose to barrage instead of retreat — correct tactically, but Tactical Retreat is a bonus action and should have ALSO fired alongside the barrage). SC-C was at 5 HP end of Bazgar's R3 turn, didn't get to act. Pattern: Bazgar closes, SC is either too busy dealing damage or already dead. At table, DMs will likely also miss the bonus-action TR window.

2. **CW endgame ambiguity (2-SC scenario):** When only SC-B and SC-C remain (R2), both had Barrage available. Sim's barrage_sc logic assigned SC-B as barrage_sc; SC-B CW'd SC-C. But SC-C then also fired Barrage (sim violation of stagger rule), wasting SC-B's CW. Correct play: SC-B (first in init) multiattacks + CWs SC-C; SC-C fires Barrage. Tactics text lacked this 2-SC endgame guidance. Fixed above.

3. **"Below 12 HP retreat behind a Rager" — inapplicable in shardcaller-only formations.** SC-B at 19 HP had no Rager to retreat behind. Without a melee bodyguard, low-HP guidance is incomplete. Fixed above.

**FI-12 (new):** Shardcaller formation resolves in 3 rounds for 3rd consecutive time (1st cycle: 3 rounds, 2nd cycle: 2 rounds via Fireball, 3rd cycle: 3 rounds without Fireball). Without Fireball, the party still wins decisively via Scorching Ray + Sabriel smite focus on SC-A. The formation has no way to prevent Bazgar from closing by R2 (Tactical Retreat is 30 ft, Bazgar movement is 30 ft, and Bazgar can Dash). **Design question:** Should the shardcaller formation be playable in the encounter, or is it always a speed-bump? If it's a speed-bump encounter (2-3 rounds), current HP/AC is calibrated correctly. If it's meant to tax the party across 5+ rounds, shardcallers need terrain barriers, elevated positions, or a melee ally to buy time for ranged kiting to develop.

**FI-13 (new):** PTV disadv triggered on Marwen's Barrage save in R2 (SC-B multiattacked twice in R1 → PTV active). Marwen saved exactly (13 vs DC 13 with disadvantage). This was the most exciting moment of the fight — without PTV disadv, Marwen saves 90%+ of the time on a DC 13 Dex save. PTV is the mechanical interaction that makes shardcaller multiattacks feel consequential beyond raw damage. **Confirmed: PTV working as intended.** No fix needed. Note for human: PTV's most impactful moment is the barrage-follow-up on a previously-hit PC; consider highlighting this in the encounter notes.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-8 (new):** At table, can Bazgar Dash and still make Extra Attacks in the same turn (Extra Attack feature vs Dash action)? In the sim, Bazgar throws handaxes R1 and somehow closes to melee range by R2 — this assumes Dash as a bonus action (Second Wind doesn't grant Dash; no Rogue/Monk levels). As a Battlemaster with no Cunning Action, Bazgar must use his main action to Dash if he needs to close 50 ft in one turn. He cannot both Dash and attack R1 unless he uses Action Surge. The sim modeled Bazgar as throwing handaxes + Action Surge R1, which is mechanically valid. However, the implication is Bazgar is still 20 ft away after R1. At 30 ft speed, R2 move + attack is fine (20 ft → close, attack with 10 ft to spare). **Not a NPC spec bug — tracking for DM awareness.** No fix needed.

- **MQ-9 (new):** Simultaneous double-barrage in R2 (sim error). If the stagger rule IS violated at table (DM forgets), Marwen faces two DC 13 Dex saves back to back in R2. With PTV disadv from R1 hits, Marwen's effective save probability drops to ~25%. She likely fails one. The "accidental double-barrage" is actually a stronger play than the intended stagger if the party clusters. Should the stagger rule be softened to: "stagger unless the party is still clustered AND both Barrages have recharged"? Left for human review.
