### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SW1-40-A — UF-advantage-wasted-below-20-HP-40th-SW1:**
Unstable Form triggered on STD-B (exactly 10 fire dmg from Fireball in R1). However, STD-B dropped below 20 HP during R2 (Sabriel smite 16 + OBR self 4 = 20 → 15 HP) before its turn. The below-20-HP priority forced AR (reckless) over Multiattack, meaning UF advantage on attack rolls was wasted both in R2 and R3. The narrow window where UF advantage actually fires — STD above 20 HP, exactly 2 targets in cone — is rarely hit (39 prior SW cycles, expressed once in 39th). The two rules compete:
- UF says "prefer Multiattack if 2 targets in cone" (to express advantage)
- Below-20 HP says "fire AR recklessly if 2+ targets in cone"
The below-20 rule wins, wasting UF. **Recommendation (for human review):** Either (a) modify below-20 to "fire AR if 3+ targets in cone; Multiattack if 2 targets and UF active," allowing the low-HP STD to express UF at least once; or (b) give UF a save-based rider ("on UF, reduce AR save DC by 1") so it contributes even when AR is forced.

**FI-SW1-40-B — STD-A stagger-hold-wasted-40th (2nd-consec):**
STD-A held AR R1 (correct — lower init, both AR available). STD-A was killed in R1 before acting (Fireball). The stagger rule correctly prevented a double AR alpha-strike, but STD-A's held AR was never used. This is consistent with 38th-SW1 (same outcome). The stagger hold is doing its job (preventing worst-case front-loading), but the cost is that the lower-init STD's AR is permanently lost if it dies before acting. The rule achieves its design purpose. No change recommended; logging as pattern.

**FI-SW1-40-C — Altar-zone-suppression-decisive-40th-confirm:**
STD-B survived Fireball (21 raw fire, save success → 10 dmg after suppression). Without the altar zone, STD-B would have taken 10 fire + vulnerability doubled = 20 fire from the halved roll, or 42 from a failed save. With suppression, STD-B survives with 35 HP, adding a full additional round of meaningful combat. Marwen's near-death (2 HP) from R2 AR is a direct consequence of STD-B surviving to act. The altar zone suppression is load-bearing. ✓

**FI-SW1-40-D — AR-from-below-20-HP-STD-nearly-kills-Marwen:**
STD-B at 15 HP fired AR from Driven Escape position (recklessly). Marwen rolled a Dex save failure against DC 14, took 23 dmg (20 necro + 3 psychic), dropping from 25 HP to 2 HP. This is a dramatic and appropriate near-kill from a cornered, desperate shrine-touched. No change recommended — the feel is good.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SW1-40-A — UF+below-20-HP interaction creates near-permanent UF waste at low HP:**
When a shrine-touched drops below 20 HP with UF active, the below-20 priority fires AR recklessly and UF advantage on attacks is never expressed. This happens because UF grants advantage on *attack rolls* (relevant to Multiattack) but AR is *save-based* (DC 14 Dex). There is no mechanic that bridges UF and AR. Recommendation: clarify in tactics whether this is intentional ("UF helps with Multiattack finishers; below-20 HP you're just flailing with AR") or whether a hybrid tactic should exist. The current rules produce consistent UF waste at low HP.
