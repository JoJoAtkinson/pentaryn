### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC4-A: STD-B Ancient Resonance is the actual TPK mechanic — beholder signature abilities never fire.**
In the final-confrontation slice, both Void Scream and Disintegration Ray went unused. The party was TPK'd entirely by STD-B's AR in R2. The slice goal is "see WHICH ability lands the killing blow and whether the death feels earned" — this playtest cycle gives the answer: it's the STDs, not the beholder. This is a design concern for player experience: the beholder is the intended boss threat, but in practice a lucky AR roll (21 dmg vs all-failed DC14 saves) ends the fight before the beholder's cinematic abilities get to fire. Recommendation for human: consider whether the final-confrontation should front-load beholder action (e.g., higher beholder initiative or lair actions that specifically suppress the STD initiative window). Log as DESIGN DECISION.

**FI-FC4-B: Fire vulnerability suppression is functionally decisive.**
Fireball vs STD-A: 29 fire dealt as normal damage (not doubled) → STD-A reduced to 4 HP but survives to R2. Without altar-zone suppression, STD-A would have taken 58 fire damage and been eliminated R1, removing two of its R2 attacks on Bazgar (4 and 10 = 14 dmg). Marwen's Fireball catching STD-A at full doubled damage R1 would significantly reduce derro pressure. The altar zone mechanic is working as designed and is materially important.

**FI-FC4-C: 2-round TPK is dramatically too short for "final confrontation."**
The briefing expects up to 10 rounds. R2 TPK means no dramatic tension arc — the party entered, took one AR, got ground down, died to a second AR. The beholder never used its most dramatic abilities. At the table this will feel anticlimactic. This is the second consecutive "early TPK" pattern (previous slice #3 was R3 TPK, also without DR firing). Recommendation: consider whether STD AC 16 + 45 HP is correct calibration for the final slice — if the party takes less R1 damage (e.g., STD-A rolled lower), they might survive to R3+ where beholder mechanics become relevant.

**FI-FC4-D: R1 LA protocol (hold 3 LA for DD) creates zero LA reactive pressure on early turns.**
The beholder held all 3 LA for DD post-Sabriel in R1. DD attempted, Sabriel saved (21 vs DC16 — high CHA). Result: 3 LA spent on a failed DD, no reactive tentacles or VR on Bazgar/Marwen in R1. Combined with the beholder's low initiative (init 7, second-to-last), the beholder effectively had minimal R1 impact despite being CR13. The derro did the work. Design note: previous playtests (FI-BEL3-D confirmed) DD has ~35% hit rate vs CHA+3 Sabriel. If repeated misses compound, DD is economically wasteful vs the reactive pressure alternative.

**FI-FC4-E: STD-A Driven Escape (below 20 HP bonus action) correctly fires.**
At 4 HP, STD-A used Driven Escape (bonus action, 30 ft toward altar) as mandated by tactics. Main action was Multiattack (AR unavailable). Driven Escape worked as intended. Note: STD-A survived R2 at 4 HP because the TPK resolved before anyone targeted it. If combat continued, STD-A would be extremely vulnerable to any ranged AoE.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC4-A: STD OBR (Oath-Breaking Retaliation) not modeled in sim.**
When Bazgar hit STD-A for 12 dmg (R1, melee), STD-A's OBR reaction should have fired — a Shrine-Axe counter vs Bazgar (AC18). At that point Bazgar had 17 HP. If OBR connected (to-hit +5: needs 13+, ~40% chance), estimated dmg = 1d8+3+1d4 ≈ 10 avg. This could have pushed Bazgar below 0 HP in R1 (17–10 = 7 HP, or 17–15 = 2 HP). Sim did not model OBR. In live play, OBR fires automatically — DM must track this and roll immediately when shrine-touched takes melee damage. No spec fix needed; DM process note.

**MQ-FC4-B: Beholder 10-ft reach vs prone target — advantage or disadvantage?**
Beholder's Tentacle Lash has reach 10 ft. Bazgar was prone (via Lair Unstable Ground). Per PHB, melee attackers get advantage if within 5 ft of prone target, disadvantage if beyond 5 ft. With 10-ft reach, the beholder is typically beyond 5 ft from its target — does this mean it attacks prone targets at *disadvantage*? This would be counterintuitive (beholder's lair action Unstable Ground makes its own attacks harder). Recommend table ruling: interpret "beyond 5 ft" as the distance the attacker occupies, not the theoretical max reach — if the beholder floats to within 5 ft, it gets advantage; if it stays at range, disadvantage. Worth clarifying in the beholder tactics block.

**MQ-FC4-C: Maw auto-crit when TL2 (not TL1) grapples — rules reading.**
In R1, TL1 missed Bazgar, TL2 hit Sabriel (GRAPPLED). Per FIX-FC39-A: "both Tentacle Lash attacks fire first. If **either** hits, that PC is grappled. The Maw's third attack **MUST** then target the grappled PC — this triggers the auto-crit rider." This clearly means TL2-only-hit should also trigger Maw auto-crit on Sabriel. Sim code only modeled TL1-hit path. In live play: TL1 miss → TL2 hits Sabriel → Maw fires at Sabriel for auto-crit (8d8+4 avg ~40 piercing). Sabriel at 13 HP post-TL2: Maw auto-crit would very likely down her R1 instead of surviving to heal. No spec fix needed — sim limitation; DM reminder to apply Maw against whichever tentacle lands first.

**MQ-FC4-D: Beholder's VS and DR never firing in full-confrontation slice.**
This has now been noted across multiple slices (#3 beholder-escorts, #4 final-confrontation). In both, the beholder's signature abilities (VS recharge 6, DR recharge 5–6) did not fire because either: (a) fight ended before beholder's turn, or (b) all PCs down before R2. Recommend reviewing whether the full-confrontation slice should include explicit guidance for the DM on sequencing the beholder's turn earlier (higher initiative) to ensure VS/DR have at least one opportunity to fire before the derro swarm finishes the fight.
