### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-TP-0-A: SC CW violated ≤20HP guard (sim fidelity note, not a DB bug).** After Bazgar's R1 attacks, DW-A dropped to 12HP. The shardcaller checklist states "Never CW an ally at ≤20HP (DD-8/DD-25 guard still applies)." My simulation dispatched CW on DW-A at 12HP — this is incorrect; SC should have held the CW charge. In a real combat run this means: in this formation, SC almost never has a valid CW target after R1 melee trades (DWDs are low-HP enough to fall below the 20HP threshold fast). *Logged for human review: does CW need a lower HP threshold guard, or should the DWDs have more HP to survive long enough to benefit from CW advantage?*

- **FI-TP-0-B: CW 3/day feels wasted in the 2-DW+1-SC patrol formation.** The only valid R1 CW window is before Bazgar's attacks land (if SC wins initiative over Bazgar). Here Bazgar init=14 > SC init=12, so Bazgar struck before SC even acted. By SC's turn DW-A was already at 12HP (≤20HP guard fires). Result: 0 of 3 CW charges usefully spent. In the patrol formation, CW is dead weight unless SC acts before all PCs — which requires winning a contested initiative. Consider either (a) moving CW to "free action once per turn" so it doesn't compete with Barrage's bonus slot logic, or (b) lowering the guard threshold to 10HP for this formation only. **DESIGN DECISION** — log for human.

- **FI-TP-0-C: Patrol slice resolves too cleanly (party victory R3, 50-60% HP remaining).** This is correct — threshold-patrol is an early escalation encounter, not a TPK slice. The SC being spiked down by Marwen's Scorching Ray R1 (27 damage out of 33HP) is the decisive moment. Feel is good: the DWDs provided meaningful pressure, and Sabriel's smite preserved HP while Marwen nuked the backline. No balance concern. *Note for human: initiative matters a lot here — if SC wins over Marwen, SC gets Barrage off before Scorching Ray and the party takes ~12-15 more damage total.*

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-TP-0-A: Shard Barrage dispatcher output shows total damage pool ("5 piercing") while individual-target resolution requires per-target saves.** The ASKING PLAYER prompt is clear (DC13 Dex save, full on fail / half on save), but the "5 piercing" line could be misread as "each target takes 5." Suggest the output line be more explicit: "3d6 piercing: rolled 5 — fail: 5, save: 2." Low severity, cosmetic clarification only.

- **MQ-TP-0-B: SC/DW-B initiative tie (both init=12) determines whether CW lands.** When SC acts BEFORE DW-B (SC wins tie), SC can CW DW-B (27HP valid) before DW-B's first attack, maximizing CW value. When DW-B acts first (my sim assumption), SC cannot CW DW-B (already acted). This is a meaningful tactical swing — recommend the encounter stat block specify that the Shardcaller defers to other derro in initiative ties (i.e., SC acts LAST among tied derro) to preserve the CW→ally pattern the checklist describes. *Or* specify that the SC always beats its DW allies in ties (gets CW advantage before they act). Flagging as a design/authoring ambiguity.
