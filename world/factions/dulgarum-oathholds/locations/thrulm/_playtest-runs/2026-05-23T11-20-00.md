### Bugs auto-fixed

1. **`derro-rager.md` — Taunt target tiebreaker added:** Previous text said "squishiest visible non-engaged target (caster or rogue)." In both tank-wall runs, Marwen was Taunted while already targeting the Rager — mechanically inert. Added tiebreaker: "prefer a target who is currently attacking the Shardcaller or ignoring the rager entirely; if the squishiest target is already focused on the rager, redirect Taunt to whoever is threatening the back line."

2. **`derro-shardcaller.md` — Call Weakness + Berserk first-swing note added:** Added to Call Weakness tactics bullet: "When calling on the Rager before a Berserk turn, advantage applies to the Rager's *first* Greataxe swing only — the benefit is spent after that roll; remaining Berserk swings are flat." Prevents DMs from applying advantage to all Berserk swings.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**DD-24: Taunt mechanically inert when target already focusing Rager**
- In both 2nd-cycle tank-wall runs, the Rager Taunted Marwen — but Marwen's optimal play was already to focus the Rager (nearest, most dangerous threat). The DC 12 Cha save produced a fail R1 (Marwen Cha 3) but the effect was irrelevant because Marwen then cast Scorching Ray *on the Rager* (disadvantage does not apply — she was already targeting the Rager). The Taunt only triggers disadvantage when attacking someone *other than* the Rager; if the target was going to attack the Rager anyway, Taunt is zero mechanical value.
- Auto-fix #1 above adds a tiebreaker heuristic. But the *fundamental* issue: in a 2-NPC fight, the party almost always has reason to focus the front-liner first. Taunt would need to either (a) force movement toward the Rager (pull mechanic) rather than just disadvantage-on-other-targets, or (b) have a higher DC (15+) to create meaningful fail/save tension.
- **Recommendation:** Does the current Taunt design match intent? If Taunt is meant to protect the Shardcaller from being targeted, it should trigger on attacks against the Shardcaller specifically (reaction), not as a preemptive bonus action. Log for human review.

**DD-25: Call Weakness wasted on Rager below DD-8's HP floor in same-initiative-window**
- Round 2: Shardcaller called weakness on Rager at 16 HP (above 15 HP guard from DD-8). Sabriel then reduced Rager to 10 HP *on the same initiative tick*, before the Shardcaller acted. In initiative order, Sabriel went before the Shardcaller — so when Shardcaller decided to spend Call Weakness, it couldn't have known Rager was about to drop to 10 HP. The Rager then fired Berserk (missed both targets) before being killed by Marwen.
- Result: Call Weakness was spent on a dying Rager that missed all Berserk swings anyway.
- **Recommendation:** DD-8 HP guard (≤15 HP) is insufficient when the Rager can drop below threshold between the Shardcaller's assessment and the Rager's actual action, particularly when Rager acts after Shardcaller in initiative. Consider raising guard to ≤20 HP (one Sabriel smite away from floor). Authoring-only change.

**DD-26: 2nd-cycle tank-wall still party victory R3 — pairing never demonstrated**
- First tank-wall run (03:17 UTC): party victory in 5 rounds. Second run (this run): party victory in 3 rounds. In neither run did the Berserk + Call Weakness synergy produce a meaningful moment — the Rager died before Berserk could land (R2 Berserk missed both AC 18/19 targets). The slice's design goal is to show the Taunt → aggro-draw → Call Weakness → Berserk kill-shot pattern. That loop requires the Rager to survive at least to R3 with Berserk recharged and Call Weakness active and a clustered target.
- Echoes DD-14 (solo-rager HP too low) and DD-5 (Unstable Form snowball) — pattern: every time a mechanic requires 3+ rounds to develop, the fights end in 2-3 rounds under concentrated level-5 fire.
- **Recommendation:** Raise Rager HP from 52 to ~70 (10d8+30) to absorb one full round of focused fire and reach R3 functional. This is a balance-adjacent change — log for human review. Do NOT auto-fix.
