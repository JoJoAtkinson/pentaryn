### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-67 (NEW): Drain Divinity under-fires at altitude — altitude gate vs 30 ft DD range**

DD (3 legendary, highest-value legendary) requires a slot-holder within 30 ft. When the beholder stays at 30–40 ft altitude, the vertical component alone puts any ground-level PC outside 30 ft range. In this run, DD only fired once (R2, after the beholder descended using fly + shrine_drift). The beholder's "below 60 HP: prefer ranged / hold 60+ ft altitude" tactic directly conflicts with DD's 30 ft range requirement.

The tactics section currently reads: *"Legendary priority order: Drain Divinity (3 actions) on the FIRST PC's end-of-turn if a slot-holder with L2+ is within 30 ft."* The within-30-ft condition is never met when beholder is at altitude, so DD degrades to Void Ray + Tentacle spending — which is 3 legendary total but less efficient than one DD. Without thralls to absorb legendary budget, this gap is more visible.

**Recommendation (do NOT auto-fix):** Add to tactics: *"To fire DD at a slot-holder, descend to ≤20 ft altitude via shrine_drift bonus before the main turn ends, so the PC is within 30 ft at the end of their turn when the legendary window opens. Balance this vs the melee exposure created by descending."*

**FI-68 (NEW): Solo beholder wastes 1 legendary per round when party is spread / at range**

In R1 (budget 3, no thralls, Bazgar at ground level 40 ft below): after spending 2 legendary on Void Ray (Sabriel's end-of-turn), 1 legendary remained. Tentacle reach is 10 ft — no melee target at altitude. Budget wasted. Again R3 end-of-Bazgar: 1 legendary remaining, beholder repositioned 30 ft back via shrine_drift — no 1-action ability usable. The solo beholder consistently "leaks" 1 legendary per round in these positioning situations.

**Observation:** without thralls (who could absorb compel_thrall or serve as DD redirects), the beholder's 3-action legendary budget is hard to spend cleanly. Consider: does the beholder fight in a way that minimizes 1-action waste? Tentacle (1 action) is only useful within 10 ft — the beholder is rarely at that range at altitude. If the beholder always stays at elevation, Tentacle is dead weight as a legendary option.

**FI-69 (NEW / confirms FI-56): AC 19 negates beholder multiattack reliably; Maw auto-crit never triggered**

In R3, beholder moved to melee (10 ft from Sabriel, 5 ft altitude). Tentacle Lash 1: 4+6=10 (MISS). Tentacle Lash 2: 12+6=18 (MISS vs AC 19). Maw: 4+6=10 (MISS). 0 damage from the main action against Sabriel's AC 19. The beholder's multiattack is tuned for AC 14–17 targets; against AC 19, the expected hit rate per tentacle is (6+6)/20 = 60% → actually (20 - 19 + 1 + 6) / 20 = 8/20 = 40%. Three independent attacks at 40% → P(all miss) = 0.6³ = 21.6%. So a full-miss multiattack happens ~22% of the time against Sabriel, making the primary melee action feel unreliable.

**FI-56 (second confirm):** When Maw DOES land on a grappled target, it auto-crits. As previously noted (beholder-escorts-limited run), this is the encounter's kill button. In this run Maw never connected due to missed tentacles. The pattern holds: Maw/grapple combo is devastating when it lands, invisible when the tentacles miss.

**FI-70 (NEW): Healing hazard forces LoH dependency — DM callout needed**

The chamber hazard (healing spells → necrotic) makes Lay on Hands Sabriel's only in-combat healing option. In this run, Marwen was disintegrated before she could be healed anyway. But in fights where a PC is merely downed (not annihilated), Sabriel's player may instinctively reach for Healing Word — the DM must stop this immediately. The existing `_overview.md` note is buried in a long hazard section. **Recommend:** add a DM callout box (bold/table) at the top of the encounter's tactical section: *"HEALING HAZARD: Healing Word, Cure Wounds, any slot-consuming healing → necrotic instead. Lay on Hands = OK. Potions = OK. SAY THIS BEFORE THE FIRST PC DROPS."* No DB change needed.

**FI-71 (NEW): Negotiation outcome feels predetermined — suggest middle option**

The beholder's price (leave lower shaft unexplored) is directly incompatible with the party's mission. The Persuasion check failed anyway (13 vs whatever threshold). But even a success would require the party to choose between mission and peace — a choice the mission structure answers for them. The negotiation functions as lore delivery (beholder communicates why it's here, what it feeds on) rather than a real decision point.

**Recommendation:** add a middle-ground option to Interaction Notes: *"The beholder will consider a temporary truce IF: (1) the party offers to seal one divine item into the shrine's void as payment — e.g., a sacred text or a consecrated weapon — satisfying its hunger for absent divinity; OR (2) Sabriel voluntarily oath-breaks (automatic if agreed) as a living sacrifice of divine potential. These are offered telepathically as 'sensations of transaction,' not words. The DM should sense-check whether the party realizes what's being offered."* Gives a real mechanical cost that creates genuine choice.

**FI-72 (NEW): Lair action locked on `unstable_ground` — party never near altar**

`void_eruption` (20-ft radius from shrine) never fired. `manifest_thralls` invalid (no thralls). The beholder is stuck cycling `unstable_ground` every lair turn. In a 4-round fight, that's 4× the same lair action. The lair action suite has three options but effectively one is available in the "party never approaches the altar" positioning typical of a cautious fight.

**Observation (not a fix):** this is correct design if the beholder punishes altar approach with eruption. But it means the lair action feels monotonous in fights where the party plays the range game from the threshold. Consider whether the beholder should prioritize moving toward the party (lair actions take no tactical actions to use, but choosing eruption vs ground is a DM-facing decision that gets trivial when only one fires).

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-39 (NEW): Disintegration rider — does it fire if target was already reduced to 0 by a different source?**

In this simulation, Marwen was dropped to -7 HP by the Disint Ray hit (39 force vs 32 HP), so yes — this disintegration is clean. Bazgar was at 21 HP and took 45 force → -24 HP, again past 0. Both disintegrations are valid per the spec: *"If this damage reduces the target to 0 HP, target is disintegrated."*

However: the Void Ray (legendary) on Sabriel dropped her from 9 to 0 HP. She is NOT disintegrated (Void Ray has no disintegrate rider). Sabriel's player can make death saves. At table, the DM must track: Disint = permanent death, Void Ray/Void Scream/tentacles = normal downed. **Ensure the table has a clear "disintegrated = gone" signal.**

**MQ-40 (NEW, DESIGN DECISION): Two PCs permanently lost in 4 rounds from a single ability**

Marwen was disintegrated R1. Bazgar was disintegrated R4. That is 2 of 3 PCs permanently removed from the campaign by Disintegration Ray alone, in one fight. The party has no access to Wish or True Resurrection at L5.

This is a CR 13 beholder against a L5 party — the power gap is intentional and correct. BUT: permanent character death (no resurrection possible) is a table-culture decision, not a mechanics decision. If the user runs this as-written, two players lose their characters forever from a single encounter.

**Options for the human to consider:**
1. **Accept as-is:** Thrulm is a "you are not meant to win this" encounter. Players who enter Thrulm's chamber are playing with fire. Make this explicit in session 0 or encounter framing.
2. **Disintegration rider = narrative only:** the PC's body is disintegrated, but True Resurrection is available at L5 from an NPC (e.g., High King's mages) as a campaign cost, not player resources. Keeps the mechanical drama without permanent character loss.
3. **Add a one-time legendary resistance bypass:** before Disint fires, the beholder tests Legendary Resistance? No — Disint is a to-hit attack, not a save. Not applicable.
4. **House rule:** first time Disint would kill a PC at 0 HP, treat as "reduced to 0 HP, no death saves needed for 1 round" instead. Temporary stabilization. Allows drama without permanence.

**Do NOT auto-fix.** Human decision required.
