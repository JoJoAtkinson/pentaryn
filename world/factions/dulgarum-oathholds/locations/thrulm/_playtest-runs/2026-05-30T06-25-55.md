## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

**BUG-FC14-A — Grapple-on-unconscious clarification (MQ-FC14-A):** beholder-thrulm.md did not state that grapple ends automatically when a target is knocked unconscious. Mid-multiattack, Sabriel was grappled by TL1 then downed by TL2 — Maw spec says "grappled target is auto-crit" but the grapple had ended by TL2's damage. Added explicit note to "Engaged by melee" bullet: *"Grapple ends automatically if target is reduced to 0 HP (unconscious target cannot maintain the grappled condition). Redirect the Maw to the next closest enemy, or skip it if none in range."* (5e PHB rule, no balance impact.)

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC14-A — Beholder took damage R1 (pattern break on FI-FC13-B):**
14th FC cycle, first occurrence of beholder taking meaningful damage (20 HP). Root cause: beholder initiated on 22 (high roll), used DR (miss) and shrine_drifted to 30 ft, then Sabriel (init 5, last PC) responded with longsword + smite. The beholder closing distance to the party EXPOSES IT to melee response if PCs haven't gone yet. In 13 of 14 prior cycles, beholder's mid-order initiative left it unexposed after R1. This 1-in-14 occurrence (~7%) is the "counterplay window" — Fireball killed two thralls AND beholder took a smite in same round. **DM note:** If the beholder opens distance (stays at 40+ ft) instead of shrine_drifting toward party R1, it avoids melee exposure. The drift-then-DR tactic is tactically sound but the "draft toward party" creates a melee window when init is favorable for PCs. DM should evaluate whether to drift AFTER seeing party initiative order.

**FI-FC14-B — Antireality margin edge case, SECOND cross-slice confirmation:**
Sabriel R1 total = 19 vs beholder AC 17+2=19. Antireality triggered (total ≤ AC+2 = 19), +2 AC applied (effective 19), hit still lands (19 ≥ 19). Reaction consumed with zero mechanical benefit. Same case as FI-BE3-03 (slice #3). **This is now confirmed across 2 separate slices and 2 PC builds.** The trigger condition "≤ AC+2" has a degenerate sub-case when attack total exactly equals AC+2: the +2 AC is added but cannot negate the hit.
**DESIGN DECISION:** Recommend changing trigger to "atk total < AC+2" (strict less-than), so Antireality only fires when it will actually turn a hit into a miss. With "≤", the trigger range is AC+1 to AC+2 (hits that might be stopped). With "<", the trigger range is AC+1 only (only hits that ARE stopped). The strict form is cleaner, removes the degenerate case, and makes the reaction feel purposeful rather than occasionally futile.

**FI-FC14-C — Marwen survival to R4 via VS range gate:**
Marwen survived to R4 (32→12→4→0 HP), far outlasting typical FC pattern (usually downed R1-R2 by DR or VS). Root causes: (1) DR missed R1 (FI-FC13-C 40% miss pattern); (2) VS fired R2 but missed Marwen due to range gate (see FI-FC14-D); (3) STDs/thralls couldn't focus Marwen until R3 (after spending R1 dashing). Healing hazard never triggered (14th consecutive cycle FI-FC13-E — 14th confirm). Marwen's R1 Fireball was fight-changing: killed T1/T3, weakened STD-B below 20 HP (stagger exemption + Driven Escape priority), reduced effective NPC action budget.

**FI-FC14-D — VS range gate: beholder shrine_drift distance matters:**
VS in R2 caught Bazgar (15 ft from altar) and Sabriel (30 ft from altar) but MISSED Marwen (60 ft from altar). Beholder shrine_drifted to 20 ft from altar before firing VS. VS 30-ft radius from that position reaches 50 ft from altar. Marwen at 60 ft = out of range by 10 ft.

Drifting only 20 ft (to reduce OA exposure per VS-descent tactics) sacrifices the squishiest PC. DM should deliberately count the distance before firing: "Beholder at [X] ft, radius 30 ft, reaches [X+30] ft — does that cover the threshold?" This is not a spec change, just a DM-mechanics awareness note.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC14-A — Grapple-ends-on-unconscious:** RESOLVED. Added to beholder-thrulm.md. 5e PHB: grapple ends when target is incapacitated or no longer in reach. Unconscious = incapacitated. Auto-resolved.

**MQ-FC14-B — Death save failures from AoE hits (ruling clarification):**
Void Ray dealt 28 force to unconscious Bazgar (auto-fail Dex save = full damage). 28 damage < Bazgar max HP (49). Per 5e: damage while at 0 HP = 1 death save failure. Damage ≥ max HP (49) = 2 failures. 28 < 49 → 1 failure from VR. (Common sim-error trap: some assume "≥ half max HP" triggers 2 failures — this is INCORRECT. The threshold is full max HP, not half.) **Confirmed correct:** VR caused 1 failure, LA VE caused 1 failure, T4 melee auto-crit caused 1 failure → 3 failures → Bazgar dead. The ruling is RAW-correct; no change needed. Note this for table play: players may misremember the "half max HP" (incorrect) vs "full max HP" (correct) 2-failure threshold.

**MQ-FC14-C — VS fires before STDs act when beholder is first in initiative:**
With beholder on init 22 (higher than both STDs at 21/20), VS fires before STDs can AR. This means VS potentially softens targets (frightened = disadv attacks) before STD multiattack can capitalize on frightened status. In this run, VS + STD-A AR in back-to-back turns (22 then 21) created a brutal R2 combined pressure (VS frightened both melee threats, then AR caught both in cone for 11-14 damage). This 1-2 combo is beholder-init-dependent — it only occurs when beholder rolls 20+ init (beholder avg init = 18.5). ~25% chance of this "VS-then-AR" combo firing in the same round. Consider flagging this as a "danger spike" in the COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.
