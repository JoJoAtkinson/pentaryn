### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-15 (NEW): UF finally exercised — 4th cycle R2, both STDs.**
Both STDs took 10+ damage in R1 (Fireball 14 fire to each + AR self-damage) → UF ACTIVE R2. AR was on cooldown (failed recharge rolls), so both STDs were forced to Multiattack with advantage — the first time UF has meaningfully fired in shrine-wedge. STD-A's UF both missed AC18 (needed 14+ base, rolled 6 and 13). STD-B's UF produced one hit (rolled 24 effective vs AC18 → 9 dmg on Bazgar). The "dying fury" moment read well narratively — the derro at 8 HP landing a blow despite being nearly dead. **Positive signal.**

Note: UF fired here because AR was *unavailable* (spent R1, not recharged). The DD-40 scenario (UF+available-AR+2-in-cone → choose Multiattack) was not tested — when PCs are clustered (3 in cone), AR would always win unless AR is spent. DD-40 only demonstrates in a partial-party (2 PCs in cone) scenario.

**FI-16 (NEW): OBR 0/4 hits — +4 to-hit structurally misses high-AC party.**
OBR triggered correctly 4 times (DD-23 fix confirmed holding). Hit rates: vs AC18 (Bazgar) needs 14+ → 35%; vs AC19 (Sabriel) needs 15+ → 30%. Simulated: 11, 10, 9, 9 — all misses. OBR self-damage (1d4 always) contributed 2+1+1+2=6 psychic total to the STDs' deaths. The self-psychic is doing more measurable work than the counter-swing. This is probably intentional ("the shrine punishes it") but the OBR feels cosmetically threatening without mechanical teeth vs fighters. Against Marwen (AC15), OBR would hit 50% of the time and be meaningful — but OBR only fires when the shrine-touched is HIT, which usually happens in Bazgar/Sabriel melee.

**MQ-13 (NEW): Stagger harness simulation bug — stagger rule never fires in cron.**
The stagger rule checks `other_ar_avail = S[other]["alive"] and not S[other]["ar_used"]`. This is evaluated at the *second STD's turn*, AFTER the first STD has already fired (setting `ar_used=True`). So `other_ar_avail=False` at evaluation time → stagger never triggers. At table, a DM checks start-of-round state and correctly holds the second STD's AR. This is a simulation harness bug only — not an encounter authoring issue. The .md stagger rule is correctly authored. Prior cycles appeared to "confirm" the stagger fix was holding; they did not — the harness was equally broken every cycle. The fix "confirmed" by the harness was illusory.

**Recommendation:** Add a round-start snapshot of `ar_used` state before the initiative loop so the stagger check compares start-of-round availability, not mid-round state.

**DD-5 (ONGOING, 4th confirmation): UF 10+ threshold easily met — Fireball alone triggers it.**
Both STDs hit the 10+ threshold from a half-save Fireball (14 fire each after save). No vulnerability doubling needed. The threshold means any AoE that lands 10+ on the shrine-touched activates UF. With Marwen's Fireball averaging 28 fire (halved to 14 on save), the threshold is met ~100% of the time she casts Fireball. UF is a near-permanent effect after any AoE round.

**DD-6 (ONGOING, 4th confirmation): Stagger harness bug prevents observation in cron.**
Because of MQ-13, the harness allows both STDs to fire AR R1 every cycle. This means 4 cycles of "stagger didn't hold" reports are all harness artifacts, not real encounter data. At-table DM behavior cannot be inferred from cron results.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-13: Stagger harness — stagger rule fires correctly at table but never in simulation.** See above. Harness infra fix needed; out of blast radius for this cron.

**MQ-14 (NEW): OBR triggers once per round vs once per hit — timing clarification needed.**
OBR trigger: "fires immediately when the shrine-touched takes damage (during the attacker's turn)." The DB spec says `reaction_kind: "buff"` with trigger `"event": "damage"`. Marwen fired 3 Scorching Ray bolts in R2. OBR fired only on Ray 1 (first hit), then `obr_used=True`. Should OBR fire once per damage source (once per Scorching Ray bolt = 3 triggers per Marwen's turn) or once per turn (1 trigger total per attacker's turn)? The .md says "OBR fires **immediately when the shrine-touched takes damage** (during the attacker's turn) — interrupts between attacks if the attacker has multiattack." The "interrupts between attacks" language suggests it fires per-hit in a multiattack, but once per spell (Scorching Ray is one spell casting, not multiattack). Ruling applied in simulation: once per attacker's action (not per bolt). This reduces OBR rate vs multi-projectile spells.
