### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SCT-43-A: VICTORY R3 — 3rd consecutive shardcaller-team victory, all R2-R3**
This cycle ran without Marwen using Fireball (she used Ray of Frost for single-target focus), which let the fight go one extra round. All three prior shardcaller-team cycles (36th, 49th, and now 43rd) ended in party victory R2-R3. The SC-only formation reliably folds in 2-3 rounds against a full L5 party. Correct design intent (SCs need a melee screen), but worth confirming the slice is explicitly flagged as "formation-identity test, not survivability test" in the overview.

**FI-SCT-43-B: PTV EXPRESSED for first time in SC-only formation — decisive outcome (43rd-cycle-first)**
Pack Tactics Voice finally triggered meaningfully. SC-B hit Marwen twice in R1, applying and refreshing PTV disadv. SC-C's Barrage then caught Marwen with disadv on her DC 13 Dex save — she rolled 5 (would have rolled 9 straight), still failing, but the disadv prevented a lucky high roll from saving her. PTV functioned correctly as a toggle (second hit refreshed, not stacked). When SCs multiattack the same target before Barrage fires, PTV delivers real pressure. This contradicts "FI-SCT6-C PTV-useless-SC-only-formation" — that finding was seed-dependent. PTV IS useful when SCs focus fire the same target early.

**FI-SCT-43-C: Barrage double-roll methodology gap (ongoing MQ-TW37-B)**
Dispatch output for Shard-Barrage showed "Damage: 11 piercing." The simulation then re-rolled 3d6 independently for each target in the line. Bazgar's save applied half of 8 (not 11), Marwen's fail applied full 8 (not 11). The authoritative damage from the dispatch (11) was not used. At-table the correct approach is: roll 3d6 ONCE, apply to all targets in the line (half on save). The simulation gap inflates/deflates damage per-target. No spec fix needed — this is a harness methodology issue.

**FI-SCT-43-D: SC-C Barrage recharge failure — 2 consecutive failures (R2 rolled 2, R3 rolled 1)**
Recharge 5-6 is ~33% per round. SC-C entered R2 and R3 without Barrage, reducing it to pure Multiattack. In a 3-round fight, the lowest-init Barrage-designated SC has only one opportunity to fire (R1). Post-R1, two failed recharges left it inert. Across cycles, SC-C's Barrage contributes in R1 and rarely fires again in short fights. Consider recharge 4-6 (50%) to give SC-C a realistic chance in rounds 2+.

**FI-SCT-43-E: CW advantage on SC-B never applied in simulation**
SC-A CW'd SC-B before SC-B's R1 turn (correct per MQ-SCT-35-A). SC-B's Multiattack attack rolls should have been made with advantage. The simulation dispatched `roll_combat_action` for SC-B's multiattack (which rolls its own dice) and then applied separate d20+4 checks — neither step applied the CW advantage. Both of SC-B's hits landed anyway (19 and 16 vs AC 15), so outcome was unaffected here. But in a tighter scenario, missed CW advantage could have changed the fight. **Harness gap, not spec bug.**

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SCT-43-A: PTV-on-Barrage-trigger (ongoing from MQ-SCT-6-A)**
When a shardcaller hits with a shard-throw (Pack Tactics Voice fires), and ANOTHER shardcaller fires Shard-Barrage on the same target same round, does PTV disadv apply to the Dex save? Per the .md: "when an ally within 30 ft hits, the target has disadvantage on the *next* saving throw before end of its next turn." Yes — the save happens within the PTV window. This run confirms the ruling: Marwen's PTV disadv from SC-B's R1 hits applied to SC-C's R1 Barrage save. **No ambiguity; document as POSITIVE confirmation.**

**MQ-SCT-43-B: Dual-dice gap ongoing (dispatch vs sim roll, MQ-TW37-B)**
SC-B's dispatch showed to-hit 23/22 but the simulation applied separate d20+4 rolls yielding misses vs AC 18. The dispatch output is decorative in this harness; the sim does its own dice. This gap affects every multiattack in every run. Fixing requires: use dispatch output as authoritative for damage AND to-hit, OR accept that dispatch is narration and sim dice are truth. Recommend the latter (accept) with a note in the testbot prompt clarifying which is canonical.
