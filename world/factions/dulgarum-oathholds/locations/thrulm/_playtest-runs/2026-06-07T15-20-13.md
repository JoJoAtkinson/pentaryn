### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-EV7-30-A Void Scream R2 decisive — 30th-cycle confirm** (carry from FI-EVD-7-A, FI-FC39-A, FI-BEL3-38-A)
Void Scream high roll (46 psychic) downed Marwen and Sabriel in a single action in R2. After VS, the fight was a one-on-one attrition Bazgar vs. beholder across 5 more rounds. VS fires R2 because it recharged after being "available from the start" (it was charged, then fired, then recharged 6/6 at R2 end). The bimodal fight-ender pattern continues: VS early + high roll = TPK within 2 rounds of its trigger. VS later or low roll = contested fight. Recommend: recharge 6-per-encounter cap (1 use only, regardless of recharge dice), OR reduce to 5d10. Existing recommendation: recharge 5–6 (from FI-FC39-A). Carry.

**FI-EV7-30-B Legendary Resistance unused — 30th-cycle pattern**
Marwen's Fireball DC is 14 (INT 18, proficiency +3). Beholder's Dex save is +6 → succeeds on 8+. With only ~35% failure rate and max 21 fire damage at L3, the beholder never needed LR. LR is a 13 CR core feature; if it never triggers at L5, it's a phantom CR contribution. Note: Sabriel's divine smites (which bypass resistance) and DD (drain divinity) are the actual pressure vectors, not Marwen's saves. LR is only relevant if Marwen upcast (L5+ slot, no longer available at L5 PC) or if Sabriel uses a save-based divine ability. Accept: LR is dormant vs L5 party. Log for human awareness (not a fix needed, but tells Joe to expect LR to start mattering at L7+ when spell DCs reach 15+).

**FI-EV7-30-C Drain Divinity never fired — 30th-cycle confirm**
DD requires 3 LA and targets Sabriel's spell slots. But VS in R2 put Sabriel at 0 HP before DD could be used as a post-Sabriel LA. In R1, beholder used 2 LA for Void Ray + 1 for Tentacle (per rotation); DD not prioritized. Root cause: the beholder's natural R1 priority (multiattack + LA spend on damage) leaves DD underused whenever VS fires before or during the round Sabriel first acts. Suggest: add explicit tactic branch: "If Sabriel has L2+ smite slots AND vs has NOT fired this combat, lead R1 with DD (LA cost 3) as post-Sabriel LA instead of Void Ray; DD early denies smites before VS fires." The 29th cycle (DD twice) shows DD works when VA is absent — the two abilities are competing for the same timeline.

**FI-EV7-30-D Bazgar solo-DPR resistance ceiling** (carry from FI-EV7-28-F)
Bazgar dealt ~35 damage over rounds 2–7 (7 hits, resistance-halved from an average of ~9 → ~4.5 per hit). The beholder's miss rate during this phase (6 TL misses in rounds 4–6) extended the fight beyond what balance intends. If the beholder's dice had been median, Bazgar would have fallen at R5 instead of R7. The solo attrition phase felt long and repetitive — Bazgar attacking for 2-6 resisted damage while lair chips for 0-11 force. Consider: at-table DM should narrate "the beholder withdraws toward the shaft" at beholder HP <50 rather than grinding out solo-Bazgar rounds (Tactics section says retreat below 30 HP, but nothing about retreat behavior in no-thrall encounters).

**FI-EV7-30-E Frightened condition not applied in combat** (simulation gap — not a DB bug)
Bazgar failed his Wis save vs VS in R2 and should have been FRIGHTENED for 1 minute (per the spec). The sim did not track this condition. Frightened would have given Bazgar disadvantage on attack rolls while the beholder was in his line of sight — materially reducing his R2 action surge output and all subsequent attacks. The spec (`void_scream.save.notes`) correctly encodes it. At-table DM should track this. This is a playtest harness gap, not a spec bug.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-EV7-30-A Disintegration Ray on already-downed target** (carry from MQ-EVD-7-A)
In R3, DR fired on Marwen who was already at 0 HP (unconscious, making death saves). The spec says "If this damage reduces the target to 0 HP, target is disintegrated — cannot be restored except by true resurrection or wish." Since Marwen was already at 0, she was not "reduced to 0" by the DR — she was already there. RAW (PHB p.197): damage to a creature at 0 HP causes death save failures. The disintegration clause requires "reduces to 0 HP" which implies starting above 0. Recommend: add tactic note "DR targets conscious creatures only; if all PCs are downed, hold DR and use Multiattack or Void Ray instead." This also improves action economy (DR on a downed target doesn't trigger its most distinctive effect).

**MQ-EV7-30-B Antireality window too narrow** (carry from FI-EVD-7-D)
Beholder AC 17 + Antireality +2 = AC 19 window. With Bazgar's +5 attack and Sabriel's +6 attack, Antireality only blocks on rolls of exactly 12 (Bazgar) or 11-12 (Sabriel). That's ~10% of attacks. The reaction has excellent flavor but near-zero mechanical impact at L5. FI-EVD-7-D recommends +4 AC or advantage-on-save. Carry for human decision.

**MQ-EV7-30-C Beholder tactical AI: prioritize DR on Marwen over Bazgar in R3**
In R3, the beholder fired DR on the already-downed Marwen instead of repositioning to attack Bazgar (the only conscious PC). The tactic logic ("softest target") applied to downed Marwen because HP=0 was the minimum — an AI priority error. The `disintegration_ray` action's rider text ("reduces target to 0 HP = disintegrated") makes it tactically redundant against downed targets. Recommend: add to beholder tactics "DR targets only conscious creatures; against a solo surviving PC, prefer TL+Maw for grapple-crit or DR if they have >60 HP."
