Previous text only mentioned "divine power" in context. Diff: `cleric/paladin present → burn legendary on Drain Divinity` section now includes explicit note that wizard slots are valid targets. Also added priority note: below 30 HP prefer Void Ray over Drain Divinity.

2. **beholder-thrulm.md — Solo retreat path missing:** Below-30-HP tactics previously said "retreats using thralls and lair actions as cover" — nonfunctional in solo (no-thrall) configuration. Added explicit solo-config retreat clause: shrine_drift each round toward deeper shaft entrance + unstable_ground on chasing PC. Beholder hovers at shaft lip.

3. **beholder-thrulm.md — Antireality trigger guidance:** Sharpened reaction text from vague "save for the biggest expected hit" to actionable threshold: trigger on any stated Divine Smite, stated Power Attack, or visible burst ≥10 estimated damage. "When in doubt, trigger it."

4. **beholder-thrulm.md — Chamber Hazards callout sharpened:** Lay on Hands / spell distinction was present but easy to miss. Rewritten with bold callout, added potions exemption, added explicit DM timing instruction ("make the ruling before the first heal is attempted"). Also added sanctified-weapon rider DM note as a separate bullet.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**[FI-1] Fireball wasted on solo beholder** — Marwen opened with Fireball (AOE L3 spell) on a solo target. This is a player-choice issue, but in single-target fights the party's primary AoE caster is underpowered relative to single-target builds. Consider adding encounter notes to the solo-beholder configuration: "In solo-beholder fights, AOE spells are wasted — the fight will pressure the party to use single-target or save-or-suck options." No game-balance change; inform the DM.

**[FI-2] Lay on Hands / Chamber Hazard triage loop** — The healing-backfire hazard forces Sabriel into a LoH-only triage role (used all 25 LoH HP keeping Marwen in the fight). This is interesting and creates real tension (when does Sabriel stop spending LoH on a wizard and start healing herself?). Recommend making this decision explicit in encounter notes for the DM: "Sabriel's choice to spend LoH on Marwen is a high-stakes tactical decision — point it out when the first LoH fires."

**[FI-6] Drain Divinity temp HP feels cosmetic at the creature's HP scale** — Gaining 4 temp HP on a 110 HP creature is less than 4% of max HP. The strategic value of Drain Divinity is entirely the slot drain (stripping smites + heals), not the temp HP rider. This is probably intentional design, but the temp HP text in the DB output reads as a significant benefit when it isn't. Consider adding a parenthetical to drain_divinity narration: "(temp HP is incidental — the slot drain is the point)." Or raise to 3× slot level.

**[FI-3] Beholder double-targeting Marwen (Void Ray legendary + Disintegration main action)** — Tactically sound but creates a punishing pile-on feel. Marwen went down 3 times in 7 rounds. Consider adding a tactics note: "Once the caster is below 10 HP, redirect Disintegration to the next highest threat rather than finishing the downed target — let the party feel the threat distributed."

### Mechanical questions

**[MQ-1 / DD-19] Drain Divinity: wizard slots vs divine-only** — The ability says "spell slots or divine power." This could be read as divine-only. Auto-fix clarifies it targets all spell slots. If the intent was divine-only, revert the auto-fix and update the DB spec to add `target_filter: "divine_slots"`. **Recommend: all spell slots (as fixed)** — the Drain Divinity is more tactically interesting when it can drain Marwen.

**[MQ-2 / DD-20] Solo beholder retreat tactic** — The below-30-HP retreat assumes thralls exist. In the empty-void slice (no thralls), the fight ran 7 rounds instead of potentially ending in round 3-4 when the retreat should have triggered. Is the intended play for the empty-void slice that the beholder FIGHTS TO DEATH (desperate creature cornered in its lair), or that it RETREATS (and the party must pursue)? This changes the encounter feel significantly.
- **Retreat** → party wins the chamber but faces the beholder again lower in the shaft; builds tension.
- **Fight to death** → the party gets a pyrrhic "victory" that costs them severely; beholder's last words matter.
- **Recommend:** add to solo-config retreat clause a DM decision flag: "If the beholder has been driven to this configuration by choice (e.g., negotiation slice), it is more likely to fight to death — remove the negotiation condition from the Interaction Notes and have it invoke its retreat tactic only if PCs move toward the deeper shaft."

**[DD-21] Antireality never triggered** — Zero uses of Antireality in 7 rounds. The sharpened guidance (auto-fixed) should help at table. But: was the mechanic actually available this whole fight? Every round, the beholder had legendary actions AND the reaction refreshed at start of its turn. Antireality firing once on Sabriel's R5 attack (8 damage hit that survived) would have saved the beholder ~4 HP and potentially changed the outcome. The reaction is genuinely valuable but the "after seeing the roll" window means it requires active DM vigilance. Track whether this fires at all in future runs.
