### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-35 (new) — PTV structurally blocked by initiative math:**
PTV triggered (SC-B double-hit Marwen R2) and the disadv marker survived through all of R3, but was never consumed. Root cause: shardcallers cannot Barrage and multiattack in the same turn — so the SC that triggers PTV via multiattack cannot immediately follow with a Barrage save. The Barrage-firing SC (highest init) always acts BEFORE the multiattacking SCs, meaning by the time an SC hits and triggers PTV, the round's Barrage has already fired. Cross-round PTV consumption is blocked by Marwen's initiative (15) falling BEFORE SC-C's Barrage opportunity (14) — Marwen acts first, consuming or refreshing the PTV window before SC-C fires. Result: across 5 SC-team cycles, PTV has triggered multiple times but never paid off against a Barrage save. **DESIGN DECISION: Consider reordering PTV to benefit a second Barrage-firing SC, OR change PTV to confer disadv on the next attack roll (not save) so it interacts with the melee PCs' ACs instead.**

**FI-36 (new, positive) — TR creates genuine kiting tension when PCs close:**
This is the first SC-team cycle where TR fired. The dynamic (Bazgar Dashes to force TR, SC-A retreats, Bazgar re-pursues, SC-A is nearly cornered) felt tense and thematic. The vault's stone columns are critical to making TR feel good — without cover to break line-of-sight, the retreat is a straight-line chase that PCs can win by speed math. **Recommendation: DM notes should explicitly note 2–3 column positions that SCs can duck behind post-TR, extending the kiting arc.**

**FI-37 (new, potential design question) — TR retreat distance vs PC movement is razor-thin:**
SC-A retreated 30 ft (TR distance), and Bazgar with 30 ft speed matched it exactly — spending his move in R2 to close back to 5 ft and attack. The only reason Bazgar caught SC-A was the exact movement match. If Sabriel (also 30 ft speed) had been the chaser, same result. Faster PCs (or a PC with Longstrider/fly) or one bad positioning roll makes the retreat effectively void. **Design question: Should TR grant 40 ft of retreat movement to guarantee a speed-30 PC cannot close the gap in one move? Or is the razor-thin tension intentional — the "catch me if you can" risk is part of the kiting feel?** Log for human: is TR intended to guarantee escape or create a chase?

**DD-18 nuanced update (from new data):** In cycle 5, Marwen deployed Fireball at R3 (not R1) and it was a decisive tactical high point, not a formation-trivialization. The "trivializes" framing in DD-18 applies specifically to R1 grouped-formation deployment (cycles 1–2). When Marwen is forced to delay Fireball (spread formation, friendly-fire risk, or tactical withholding), the spell's impact feels earned. **Recommendation: add a DM note to the encounter header — "If Marwen drops Fireball R1 on a tight grouping, all SCs may fall in one action. Brief the DM on the kiting formation start to naturally prevent this."**

**DD-47 RESOLVED (TR structural absence):** First confirmed TR activations — twice in a single round. The root cause of 0-activation in prior cycles was that PCs never closed to melee range. Bazgar's explicit Dash in this cycle was the trigger. No authoring change needed; the tactics text is correct. Flagging as resolved.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-23 (carry-forward):** SC-B's CW advantage in R2 was not blocked by long-range disadvantage because TR repositioned SC-B to short range before the attack. The cancellation scenario (CW advantage + long-range disadv = straight roll) still needs a formal ruling for cases where TR does NOT fire and SC-B attacks from long range with CW advantage. Existing .md guidance is correct; no change needed until a cycle demonstrates the scenario.

**Harness sim bug — stagger R2:** SC-C was incorrectly blocked from firing Barrage in R2. The code used `barrage_available["SC-A"] == False` as a proxy for "SC-A fired this round," but SC-A's Barrage had been spent from R1 without firing in R2. Correct check: track whether any Barrage was dispatched *this round*, not whether any SC's tracker is False. This harness error likely made R2 slightly less damaging than it should have been (SC-C multiattacked with misses instead of Barrage). Not an authoring spec bug — no action on NPC files.

**Marwen init order (sim error):** In R3, SC-C was processed before Marwen in the sim despite Marwen having init 15 and SC-C init 14. In correct play, Marwen acts before SC-C. This means SC-C's R3 CW (on SC-B) should fire before Marwen's Fireball, but Marwen's Fireball kills SC-B before SC-B can ever use the CW advantage. Same outcome (SC-B dies); the ordering error had no mechanical impact.
