### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-1 / DD-14 CONFIRMED (2nd cycle): Solo-rager-rush still resolves in R2 — signature mechanics untested**

Exact mirror of 1st-cycle run. Two Fireballs (L3) solved the encounter before Berserk, Taunt, or Madness mechanics could compound into anything meaningful. Breakdown:
- Berserk fired **twice** (Rager#3 R1, Rager#1 R2) — 2-of-6 attacks landed (1 hit on Bazgar, 1 hit on Sabriel). More Berserk activity than cycle 1, but ragers still dead before any second Berserk cycle could execute.
- Taunt fired **5 times** — 4 FAILs — zero mechanical effect (see DD-36 below).
- Madness Endurance: active on R2 Rager#1 (+1 to-hit) — produced 2 additional misses (would have missed anyway vs AC 18/19). Not meaningfully observable at AC 15 targets.

Rager HP (52) is structurally too low for the slice's stated goal. Two 8d6 Fireballs avg ~28 each; combined with smites, the party can kill all three before R3 with no Berserk recharge tension. DD-14 requires human sign-off to fix.

---

**DD-36 (NEW): Taunt immunity — save-based spellcasters bypass Taunt entirely**

Taunt imposes disadvantage on **attack rolls** only (DB `effect` field: "disadvantage on attack rolls against any creature other than the rager"). Marwen's entire combat kit is save-based: Fireball, Scorching Ray, and Fire Bolt are all either saves or ranged spell attack rolls (which are attack rolls — but Fire Bolt is the only one). Key distinction:
- **Fireball (save):** Taunt has ZERO effect. Marwen cast Fireball twice while Taunted; no disadvantage applied.
- **Scorching Ray (ranged attack roll):** Taunt disadvantage WOULD apply if she cast this while Taunted by a rager she's not targeting.
- **Fire Bolt (ranged attack roll):** Same as Scorching Ray — Taunt applies.

Structural gap: if the party's primary damage source is a save-based AoE caster, Taunt is irrelevant in the solo-rager slice. Marwen spent both L3 Fireballs while Taunted (once by Rager#3 in R1, once by Rager#1 in R2) with zero Taunt interaction.

**Recommendation (logged for human):** Add a caveat to Taunt tactics: "Taunt does not affect save-based spells (Fireball, etc.) — prioritize Taunting attack-roll casters and melee fighters over pure-save casters." The wizard Taunt is functionally a wasted bonus action unless Marwen switches to attack-roll spells. Auto-fix applied: clarification added to `derro-rager.md` tactics.

---

**MQ-5 (NEW): Double-Taunt conflict — same PC taunted by two ragers simultaneously**

R1: Sabriel was Taunted by both Rager#1 (fail) and Rager#2 (fail) in the same round. This creates an ambiguous condition: Sabriel has "disadvantage on attacks vs non-Rager#1" AND "disadvantage on attacks vs non-Rager#2." The two effects are logically contradictory if she attacks Rager#3 (neither taunter). No existing ruling covers this.

**Proposed ruling (applied as auto-fix in .md):** If two ragers Taunt the same target simultaneously, the target has disadvantage only on attacks against creatures that are NOT either taunting rager — they may attack either taunting rager freely. This is the least-punishing interpretation and makes tactical sense (the creature can focus on either rager without penalty).

This situation arose naturally in R1 because both high-init ragers targeted Sabriel (highest Cha in party — ~16) on the Taunt. In a 3-rager solo slice this will recur whenever the squishiest non-melee PC fails both saves.

**FI-3 (CONFIRMED): Taunt targeting is redundant when ragers naturally converge on the same PC**

All three ragers targeted Marwen for Multiattack (squishiest, lowest HP). Both Rager#1 and Rager#2 then Taunted Sabriel (squishiest non-engaged). Since both were attacking Marwen, Taunting the fighter-healer Sabriel was technically correct per tactics — but Sabriel simply smited through the disadvantage (rolled 20 both times). Taunt advantage is highest when it re-routes attention; lowest when the squishy target is already taking all the hits and the taunted target is a high-HP melee PC who can absorb disadvantage.
