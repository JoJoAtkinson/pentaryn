### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-BEL3-11-A — Maw auto-crit: 11th consecutive BEL3 cycle, 0 fires (carry from FIX-FC39-A)**
In 4 rounds, the Maw auto-crit never fired. R3: TL hit Bazgar and Marwen, but Maw missed Bazgar (to-hit 14 vs AC18). R4: TL1 killed Bazgar outright (downed by TL1 damage), leaving Maw no living grappled target. Pattern: the Maw auto-crit either (a) misses after the grapple lands, or (b) the grappled PC dies before Maw executes. Recommendation carry: if 11 cycles produce 0 auto-crit fires, consider whether the Maw should auto-crit on ANY grappled creature (not requiring a hit roll), or whether the `rider_on_hit` note should clarify that a TPK by TL doesn't give Maw a free shot.

**FI-BEL3-11-B — Sabriel LoH-treadmill confirmed R3 (carry from FI-BEL3-10-C)**
Sabriel used Lay on Hands (10 HP) to revive Marwen in R3. Beholder's TL2 downed Marwen again the same round (13 bludgeoning). Marwen was downed before she could take any action. The revive was completely negated. At the table this would feel deeply frustrating. Design note: the beholder tactically should consider NOT immediately re-targeting a freshly-revived PC (1 round grace) to create more dramatic tension, rather than the instant undo. However, mechanically there's no rule obligating this — log for human.

**FI-BEL3-11-C — Void Ray output format AoE-confusion (carry, unsolved)**
Void Ray LA roller output reads: "each creature in 120-ft range rolls DC 16 Dex save" — formatted as an area effect. The action is single-target. The DM note in the output acknowledges this, but the format remains confusing. Human review: consider whether the Void Ray roller template should be updated to suppress the area-format line, or whether the DM note is sufficient.

**FI-BEL3-11-D — Manifest Thralls reaction-pressure vs AC15 targets**
With 3 thralls alive, Manifest Thralls (lair action) grants 3 reaction attacks per round. In R2: 2/3 hit Marwen for 11 damage (5+6 pierce). In R3: 2/3 hit Sabriel for 9 damage. These reaction attacks contributed ~20 damage across the fight — comparable to 1-2 rounds of beholder TL damage. Against AC15 (Marwen) this lair action is extremely efficient. Note the FIX-BEL3-38-A threshold: once thralls can't hit (AC18+), switch to UG/VE. In this run, the beholder correctly targeted Marwen (AC15) with reactions until she was downed, then shifted to Sabriel.

**FI-BEL3-11-E — Fireball DC vs Beholder: structural auto-save**
Marwen's Fireball (DC13, L3 slot) gave the beholder a +6 Dex save target: needs ≤6 to fail = 30% failure rate. Beholder rolled d20(7)+6=13 = exactly the DC (save). In practice, beholder auto-halves L5 Fireball unless the wizard has at least INT 16 (DC14) and even then 35% fail rate. Marwen's L3 Fireball consumed her best nova slot for 12 damage (halved) to the beholder. Design note: the beholder's Dex +6 save suppresses Fireball-class spells below DC15. This is intentional difficulty. Log for human: if Marwen's party role is "AoE controller," she has no viable AoE against this boss (beholder +6 Dex, thralls roll well on L3 saves). Her best contribution is force/fire targeted spells (Scorching Ray, Magic Missile) — not AoE.

**FI-BEL3-11-F — Beholder HP at TPK: 43/110 (39%), 67 total party DPR over 4 rounds**
Party dealt 67 damage to the beholder before TPK (R1: 5+25=30, R2: 25+6=31, R3: 6 beholder THP absorbed, net 0 direct after absorption, R4: 6 from Bazgar). Sabriel's radiant smites (25+25=50 damage across 2 rounds) were the dominant damage source. Bazgar's resisted physical attacks contributed ~6 damage total (heavy cost for 2 hits across 4 rounds). Without smite slots, the party would have dealt ~17 damage total — the beholder would be nearly untouched at TPK.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-BEL3-11-A — Void Feeding DB gap (carry from MQ-BEL3-10-A)**
The void-feeding trait (+1 atk/dmg while near shrine) appears in the .md but is NOT in the DB action specs. The simulation applied +1 manually to all roll outputs. In live play, DM must remember to add +1 to all beholder dice results. Recommend: either add a `void_feeding_active: true` boolean to the beholder's actions as a reminder, or add the +1 directly to the `to_hit_bonus` and `damage_modifier` fields of each action spec (noting the condition). Currently the DB specs don't encode situational modifiers.

**MQ-BEL3-11-B — LA-reserve protocol: low-initiative beholder trivially satisfies R1 hold**
Beholder rolled init 8 (last) in this run. The R1-LA-reserve protocol (hold all 3 LAs for DD post-Sabriel) was trivially satisfied because the beholder acts after all PCs anyway — no reactive LA was available before Sabriel's turn. The protocol note is most critical when the beholder rolls high initiative (acts before Sabriel) — see FIX-FC47-A. This run doesn't test that case. Consider adding a note to the protocol: "If beholder acts after Sabriel in initiative (init < Sabriel's init), R1 DD is automatic — spend reactive LAs freely from start of R2."

**MQ-BEL3-11-C — VE vs unconscious PCs: consistent with MQ-FC45-B but sim applied DM option**
VE has no consciousness qualifier (RAW: hits all creatures within 20 ft). The sim excluded Marwen from VE per the DM waiver option in MQ-FC45-B. If VE had hit Marwen in R4 (11 force → death save failure), the outcome remains TPK-R4. The ruling (exclude unconscious) is a DM choice, not a mechanical requirement. Consider whether to codify one canonical ruling rather than leaving it optional.
