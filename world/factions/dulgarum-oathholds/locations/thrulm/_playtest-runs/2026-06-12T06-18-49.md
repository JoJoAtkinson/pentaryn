**Sim architecture note (not a DB bug):** The simulation calls `roll_combat_action()` for narration/flavor text, then independently re-rolls d20s for its own hit determination. The roller's printed to-hit values (e.g., "Tentacle Lash 1 to-hit 16") are NOT used by the sim. This means Maw auto-crit logic depends on *sim* dice, not roller output — the two systems are disconnected. This is an existing architecture choice. Documented here for morning review; no fix needed unless the testbot is extended to parse roller output for actual hit values.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**DD-EV7-A — Negotiation has no mechanic (DESIGN DECISION)**
The empty-void slice is framed as "party tries to negotiate," but the .md negotiation conditions are purely narrative (no DC, no faction check, no timing). In each run, the DM decides arbitrarily when negotiations break down. For this slice specifically, the interesting question is *whether* negotiation can succeed. Recommend adding a resolution note to `_overview.md`: e.g., "Negotiation requires a DC 18 Persuasion check; advantage if the party offers a divine artifact; automatic failure if a weapon is drawn before the offer is made." Without this, the empty-void slice always resolves to combat with no meaningful negotiation variance between runs.

**DD-EV7-B — Void Scream (recharge 6) never fired in 5 rounds (~60% chance it fires at least once)**
In solo mode, VS is the only party-wide threat (no thralls for mob pressure). This run it never recharged. Five rounds with no VS means no frightened conditions, no psychic blast — the fight was exclusively tentacle/maw/LA spam. Not wrong, but feels thin. Consider whether for solo-beholder slices, VS recharge 5–6 (from the R1 note: "fire R2") is better than recharge 6, since R1 is reserved for Multiattack anyway. Alternatively, accept that VS not firing in solo mode is the "quiet" variant of the beholder — a valid dramatic beat.

**DD-EV7-C — Marwen treadmill feel**
Beholder Void Ray (2 LA) → Marwen down (8 HP) → Sabriel LoH revive → repeat. This cycled three times before LoH ran dry. Mechanically sound (correct target priority, correct LA spend), but the repetitive pattern made R1–R3 feel formulaic. The treadmill broke naturally in R4 when LoH exhausted, and R4–R5 escalated into real crisis. The pacing arc (treadmill → crisis) felt somewhat earned, but the treadmill phase is longer than necessary. No fix needed — this is inherent to Sabriel's LoH + Marwen's low AC.

**MQ-EV7-A — Surprise and negotiation-to-combat initiative transition**
The .md doesn't address whether the beholder can be surprised during negotiation. Truesight + telepathy strongly implies it cannot. But does the party lose their first action? In this run, Bazgar had initiative 19 (higher than the beholder's 9), so the party got to act before the beholder's main action (only lair at init 20 went first). This meant Bazgar attacked before the beholder — which feels slightly off for "we just started a fight with a CR 13 aberration." Consider whether the beholder should get an automatic first action (surprise clause) in the negotiation-break scenario, since it anticipated hostility via telepathy. Recommend: leave as-is (standard initiative roll), but note in `_overview.md` that the beholder's high-initiative threat comes from the lair action (init 20) not from individual initiative — the beholder intentionally has low Dex (16 +3) and rarely beats melee fighters.

**MQ-EV7-B — DR miss rate vs Sabriel confirms existing note**
DR (to-hit +6 vs AC 19) = 35% hit rate. DR recharged R5 but missed on a nat-3 roll. This confirms the MQ-FC51-B note: always prefer Marwen as DR target (AC 15 = 65% hit). In this run, Marwen was already downed when DR recharged, forcing the beholder to fire at Sabriel. The miss was not poor play — it was the correct application of the targeting priority note.
