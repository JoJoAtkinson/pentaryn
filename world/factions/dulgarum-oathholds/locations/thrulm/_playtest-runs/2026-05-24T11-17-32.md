### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-14 (NEW): Sabriel untouched — 4th consecutive threshold-patrol cycle.** Sabriel takes 0 damage across all 4 threshold-patrol runs. DWs target Bazgar (closest melee); SC targets Marwen (caster priority). Sabriel's high AC (19) and late initiative mean she engages after the DWs are committed. Divine Smite bursts make her the most efficient PC but the safest. At table this may feel odd (the tankiest PC never gets hit). Possible fix: note in SC tactics that the divine martial is the party's sustain engine and should be targeted by Barrage if the line of fire allows. Low-risk authoring — but requires human sign-off (changes targeting priority).

- **DD-22 CONFIRMED 4th cycle — Tactical Drilling harness overcounting.** In Round 2, DW-B gained TD advantage from SC-X being alive (50 ft away, no melee contact). Correct RAW: TD requires an ally within 5 ft of the target. SC-X never qualified. DW-B's R2 attack roll (19+2=21 HIT) may have been advantage-assisted incorrectly. Effect: DW-A/DW-B threat is overstated by ~24pp per attack when the only surviving ally is the ranged SC. Still unresolved; requires harness change (out of blast radius).

- **DD-39 fix confirmed correct behavior, but CW 0/3 fires 4th consecutive cycle.** Root cause this cycle: DW-A (DD-39 designated target — acts after SC-X at init 3) was killed by Sabriel's Divine Smite before SC-X's turn (init 12). DW-B had already acted (init 22). No CW target remained with actions pending. The fix correctly skips CW when primary target is dead. But the structural outcome is unchanged: CW is a dead card in any fast-kill round where Sabriel front-loads a smite burst. Pattern across 4 cycles: CW fires 0 uses in threshold patrol. Consider whether CW should have a "fallthrough to any ally who hasn't yet attacked this round" clause — see MQ-12.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-12 (NEW): CW fallthrough when primary DD-39 target dies.** When the designated CW target (ally acting next after SC-X per DD-39) dies before SC-X's turn, should CW (a) skip entirely (current behavior), (b) fall through to any other living ally regardless of initiative position, or (c) hold the charge and fire next round? Option (b) risks wasting CW on an ally who already attacked this round (e.g. DW-B at init 22 in this run). Option (c) is always available — fire CW at round start next round. Option (a) is safe and correct given wasted-CW-on-already-attacked-ally risk. Recommendation: option (a) with a note — "if primary CW target is dead, hold the charge for next round (never fire on an ally who has already attacked this round)." Low-risk authoring addition.
