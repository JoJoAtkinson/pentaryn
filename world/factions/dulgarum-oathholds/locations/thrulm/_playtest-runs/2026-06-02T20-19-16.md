### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SRR10-A (10th-cycle confirm): Marwen focused-death pattern** — Both R1 and R3 independently chose Marwen as "squishiest target" in R1. R3's two hits (9+5=14 dmg) brought her from full to 6 HP; R2's follow-up in R2 finished her (8+12=20 dmg). Kill comes from mundane multiattack focus, not from Berserk or any signature ability. Same as 9th-cycle. The rager "earns" the kill through correct targeting, but the moment is flat — no special verb fires.

- **FI-SRR10-B (10th-cycle new): Berserk missed all targets in R1** — R2 Berserked in R1 with all attacks hitting at effective +6 (ME active after Fireball). Hit chance vs ACs 18/15/19 is approximately 45%/60%/40% per attack. All 3 missed (rolls 7, 7, 9). This is valid variance but a 3-miss Berserk has occurred in 4+ of 10 cycles. Berserk's signature moment is structurally unreliable. Consider whether the tactical condition (2+ in reach) is too often met while hit rates remain low.

- **FI-SRR10-C (new): Bazgar 1v1 vs R3 endgame anticlimactic** — Rounds 4–6 with Bazgar solo vs R3 at 27 HP played as a moderate grind. R3 dealt 13, 11 damage per round; Bazgar dealt 9, 8, 12. Neither was near death quickly. The fight resolved cleanly but without drama. The rager has no "cornered animal" escalation ability. At low HP (10/52), Madness Endurance just restores the +1 attack bonus — it doesn't spike output. A rager going from 3 allies to solo 1v1 should feel more desperate. **Recommendation:** add tactics coaching note that below 15 HP with all allies dead, rager attempts a final Berserk if available (treats any single target as "multiple" — actually Berserk requires 2+ in reach, so it can't fire in 1v1). Alternatively, note that the 1v1 endgame is by design: it's the rager's last mad stand.

- **FI-SRR10-D (new): Taunt entirely blocked in this run** — All 3 Taunt saves that mattered: Bazgar failed (R1 vs R1's Taunt) but Bazgar was already targeting R1 directly, so the disadvantage was irrelevant. Marwen passed hers (R2 vs R2's Taunt). Sabriel failed (R2 vs R3's Taunt) but then went down the next round before taking a taunted attack. Taunt generated zero mechanical effect in this run. 10th-cycle pattern: Taunt's DC 12 is low vs party Cha modifiers; when it does land, the target often goes down before the disadvantage is exercised. Consider raising DC to 13 or flagging for human review.

### Mechanical questions

- **SIM-SRR10-A: Taunt re-targeting after primary target drops** — When Marwen went down in R2, R2's assigned Taunt target (Marwen, per FI-31 split) was no longer valid. MQ-NEW-2 correctly skips unconscious/dead targets, but in R2 both Bazgar and Sabriel were alive — R2 should have redirected Taunt to one of them. My simulation incorrectly skipped the Taunt entirely in R2 and R3 because the hard-assigned mapping ("R2 taunts Marwen") wasn't updated on drop. Same issue in R3 (R3's Sabriel assignment expired; Bazgar was alive). The .md's FI-31 corollary specifies "split Taunts" but doesn't address reassignment when a split-target drops. **Recommendation:** add coaching note: "If your assigned split-target drops, redirect Taunt to the nearest standing PC not already taunted by another rager this turn. If all living PCs are already taunted, skip per MQ-NEW-2."

- **MQ-SRR10-B: Berserk availability on R1 vs harness gating** — This simulation gated Berserk on a `d6 >= 5` check before deciding to use it. This is wrong: Berserk starts as available (no initial roll needed), and the 5-6 recharge only triggers at the START of subsequent turns after Berserk has been used. The harness error means R2's Berserk in R1 was correctly used but for the wrong reason. The DB spec and .md are correct; the harness had a spurious gate. No action needed in the DB.
