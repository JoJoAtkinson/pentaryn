### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC32-A (FEEL — POSITIVE): Void Scream R1 as the beholder's "opener" is devastating and correct.**
The beholder went last in initiative (init 5). By the time it acted, Fireball had already reduced it to 45/110 and the escort screen was half-gone. Despite being hurt, it still cleared 2 of 3 PCs in a single action. The near-shrine disadvantage (within 10 ft of altar) made Wis saves near-impossible for Bazgar (mod +1) and Marwen (mod +1). This feels RIGHT for a CR 13 beholder — the party got meaningful hits in, but VS is a TPK threat regardless. Communicating to players that "the beholder will definitely use this" is important pacing info.

**FI-FC32-B (CONFIRMED — POSITIVE): Void Scream starting recharged now works correctly.**
Previous cycle SIM-BEL3-5-A flagged VS as never firing because `vs_recharge=False` at start. This cycle had VS starting available and firing R1 — correct per 5e RAW ("Recharge X" abilities start charged). The simulation fix is working. **No spec change needed.**

**FI-FC32-C (DESIGN DECISION): Drain Divinity burns 3 LA for 0 result across 2 attempts.**
Beholder spent all 3 legendary actions on Drain Divinity both times Sabriel took a turn (R1 and R2). Both failed — Sabriel made Cha saves of 20 and 22 (with +3 mod). The tactical spec says "Drain Divinity is priority vs divine martial" but this creates a scenario where the beholder's entire legendary budget is burned on a single-target save that Sabriel has ~25% chance of resisting. RECOMMENDATION: If DD is attempted once and resisted in R1, the beholder should fall back to 2-LA Void Ray + 1-LA Tentacle in subsequent rounds rather than spending another full 3-LA on DD. This isn't a spec bug but a tactics depth issue Joe should decide: should the .md specify a fallback after 1 failed DD?

**FI-FC32-D (CONFIRMED): Fireball kills the escort screen but altar-zone suppresses STD fire vulnerability.**
Thrall-1 and Thrall-3 died to Fireball (22 HP each, took 25 fire = dead). STD-2 took 25 fire but was NOT doubled because it was within 60 ft of the altar. This distinction is working perfectly — the STDs survived to deal meaningful damage in R2. The altar zone creates a meaningful tactical decision: draw STDs out or push into the zone and lose the fire advantage.

**FI-FC32-E (FEEL): Killing blow was Void Ray, not a signature "beholder" ability.**
The final kill (Sabriel at 1 HP → Void Ray) was mundane. Disintegration Ray never fired. The Maw-grapple crit combo never fired. This is the 32nd cycle of this slice; across the cycles, the killing patterns seem to be: VS+escort-pile rather than the cinematic beholder moves. RECOMMENDATION: Not a balance issue, but for table feel, consider coaching notes in the .md Tactics section reminding the DM to narrate when DR and Maw-grapple CAN'T fire (i.e., name the "bullet dodged") to build tension even when VS is the actual kill.

**FI-FC32-F (DESIGN DECISION): Manifest Thralls lair action is consistently wasted against Bazgar (AC 18).**
0/3 reaction attacks connected in R1 (rolls: 9, 6, 18 vs AC 18 — the 18 was an exact hit that just barely connected in actual simulation but missed here). The thrall's +1 to-hit means they hit AC 18 only on natural 17+, a 20% base rate. Confirm: should beholder be directing these thrall attacks at Marwen (AC 15)? The lair description says "beholder designates" — the tactical spec should probably say: "on Manifest Thralls, direct reaction attacks toward the lowest-AC target." Note: this was flagged as FI-BEL3-5-D in cycle 31 (≥2 thralls above 50% HP threshold) but the direction-of-attack question is distinct.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC32-A: Drain Divinity fallback — when should beholder stop spending 3 LA on DD?**
Current .md says beholder focuses on DD against clerics/paladins. There's no guidance on when to stop. RECOMMENDATION: Add a tactical note: "If DD is attempted once and resisted, fall back to Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA) for that round. Only re-attempt DD if the target has already lost 2+ spell slots."

**MQ-FC32-B: Frightened condition on PCs downed by Void Scream.**
Bazgar and Marwen were marked frightened when VS knocked them to 0 HP. When Sabriel revived Bazgar via LoH, he woke up at 15 HP — still frightened (failed the save on his turn). Per 5e RAW, the frightened condition persists through unconsciousness. Bazgar couldn't move toward the beholder for his turn — in this run, this prevented any meaningful action from the revived PC. Is this the intended player experience? Probably yes (beholder's scream is horrifying), but it's worth verifying Sabriel's LoH revive spec accounts for the frightened state being active on the newly conscious PC.
