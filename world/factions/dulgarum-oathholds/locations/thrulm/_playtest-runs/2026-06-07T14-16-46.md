### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SCT6-A: ≥25 ft spread insufficient vs Fireball** (human decision required)
The shardcaller spread guidance (≥25 ft apart to avoid single-AoE wipe) does not protect against Fireball. Fireball has a 20-ft *radius* (40 ft diameter). With SCs 25 ft apart, the Fireball center placed at the midpoint is only 12.5 ft from each SC — well within the 20-ft radius. Both SC-A and SC-B took 31 full fire damage (both failed saves) from one L3 Fireball, leaving them at 2 HP each and effectively removing them from the fight. The spread needs to exceed 40 ft to guarantee no two SCs are caught in one 20-ft-radius blast. But 40+ ft separation across the chamber may conflict with Barrage line coverage and CW range (30 ft). Human decision: raise spread guidance to ≥45 ft OR add explicit "if facing a Wizard: spread until two SCs are >40 ft apart (sacrifice line coverage to survive first-round Fireball)."

**FI-SCT6-B: Stagger rule creates asymmetric R1 damage** (design note, no fix required)
Following FI-SCT-A (one Barrage per round), SC-C fired Barrage last (init 3) for only 6 total damage (3d6=6, both PCs saved for half). The two non-barrage SCs Multiattacking dealt 17 total damage to Marwen. Marwen's R1 Fireball response dealt 62 damage across two SCs (31 each). The stagger rule is correct per FI-SCT-A and is the primary balance lever for the formation — but this cycle shows that in open terrain with a caster, a low-value Barrage roll + two failed SC Dex saves creates a catastrophic R1 asymmetry. The stagger rule effectively capped SC damage at ~17 (two Multiattacks vs one PC) while Marwen was free to AoE two SCs. This is inherent to the design; no fix recommended, but it confirms that the shardcaller formation's damage ceiling is low in L5-Wizard matchups.

**FI-SCT6-C: PTV near-useless in shardcaller-only formation** (feel issue, low priority)
Pack Tactics Voice (target hit by SC has disadvantage on next save before end of its next turn) never meaningfully activated. Marwen had PTV active from SC-B and SC-A hits, but her next action was casting Fireball — she was not making a save. PTV is excellent in mixed-NPC formations (beholder rays, ancient resonance, compel_thrall) where save-based attacks follow after SC hits. In SC-only slices, PCs who get hit typically spend their turn attacking outward, not making saves. The SC-C Barrage (the only save-based SC ability) fires last in initiative, usually after affected PCs have already acted. Recommend adding to the shardcaller's `.md` tactics: "PTV's save-disadvantage is most valuable in formations that include save-imposing NPCs (beholder rays, shrine-touched resonance). In SC-only formations, PTV rarely activates — prioritize Barrage line-ups and CW over relying on PTV as a damage multiplier."

**FI-SCT6-D: No last-resort Barrage tactic when SC is below 5 HP with charged Barrage** (spec gap, minor)
SC-A was at 2 HP in R2 with its Barrage still fully charged. Current tactics: below 12 HP → retreat behind pillar + Multiattack (per 2-SC endgame). The 2-SC rule says SC-A should Multiattack (not Barrage), saving Barrage for SC-C. But SC-A was about to die regardless — the Multiattack produced two misses (rolls 5, 3), and Magic Missile killed it anyway. A SC at 2 HP with a charged Barrage and a certain-death turn should arguably fire the Barrage as a last resort rather than saving it for a formation partner who may not need it. Current tactics do not cover this case. Recommend adding to start-of-turn checklist: "If Barrage is charged AND SC has ≤5 HP AND the formation partner is at ≥15 HP, fire Barrage now — do not hold for stagger." This preserves stagger discipline when both SCs are viable but allows a dying SC to use its most powerful tool.
