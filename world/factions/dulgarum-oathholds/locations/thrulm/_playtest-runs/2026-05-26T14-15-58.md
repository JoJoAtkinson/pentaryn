### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-144 (NEW — structural/CW): 7th consecutive SC-team cycle with 0 effective CW charge applications.**
- This cycle: SC-B used CW on SC-C twice (R1: SC-C fires Barrage → wasted; R2: SC-C multiattacks → advantage used, but both Throw 1 dice were low (5,8) → didn't matter). SC-A dead before acting. SC-C had 3 charges but no ally alive to use them.
- Root cause: the stagger rule (one Barrage per round) always puts the higher-init SC in a multiattack turn and the lower-init SC in a Barrage turn. The higher-init SC then CW's the lower-init SC before they act — wasting the CW on a save-based area attack. Tactics spec says "do not CW an ally planning Barrage" but the sequencing means the CW fires before the CW-giver knows whether their ally will Barrage (the decision is made by the recipient after the CW is applied). The DM running this at table would know to check before spending the bonus action; the sim does not. Need a tactic re-order: "As the higher-init SC, DECIDE whether your next-lower-init ally will Barrage BEFORE spending the CW bonus action. Only CW if they will multiattack."
- **Not a balance fix — this is a doc/tactic clarity issue.** Logging as MQ-64 for doc patch.

**FI-145 (NEW POSITIVE — first low-FB cycle): Fireball rolls 21 (below average 28). All 3 SCs survive R1.**
- This is the first SC-team cycle where all 3 SCs are alive after Marwen's Fireball R1. Result: a genuine 4-round fight with SC action economy exercised across multiple rounds. Encounter feels richer when SCs survive the opener.
- **Design implication (DD-18 7th confirm):** The encounter outcome is heavily seed-dependent. At average FB (28), all SCs fail and die or nearly die in R1-R2 (2-round fight). At low FB (21), both save and fight drags to R4 with genuine tension. Bazgar at 6 HP entering R4 is the closest any SC-team cycle has come to a TPK. The variance (21 vs 28 fire damage) determines whether the encounter is a 2-round stomp or a 4-round nail-biter.
- **DESIGN DECISION (FI-145): Should shardcallers be given more resilience to survive an average Fireball (28 fire)?** Options: (a) raise HP to 45 (6d8+12), (b) give Dex saving throw proficiency (+4), (c) add Evasion as a passive (half damage on successful save, zero on failed), (d) scatter starting positions 30+ ft apart (explicitly noted in _overview.md) so Fireball can't hit all 3. Option (d) is the lowest-impact game change and most realistic tactically. **Human decision. Do not auto-fix.**

**FI-146 (NEW — PTV never influenced outcome): Pack Tactics Voice technically triggered but never mattered.**
- SC-B hit Marwen twice in R1, applying PTV disadvantage-on-saves to Marwen. SC-C's Barrage then targeted Marwen (+Bazgar). Marwen's PTV disadvantage was on her Barrage save (DC13 Dex +2 mod) — she would have needed 11+ flat, with disadvantage ~44% fail vs ~36% fail. She failed both (result 5). PTV didn't change the outcome.
- This is the 4th-or-5th cycle PTV has fired but had no meaningful impact. The ability is mechanically sound but situationally irrelevant in this slice: SCs rarely both survive long enough for PTV to stack into a save that matters. The note in the .md ("call this out in the reply so the DM remembers it") is correct; at table a DM would roll disadvantage and it might matter. In the sim, Marwen is already low-saving and PTV doesn't push the needle.
- **Not a bug. Log as feel/DM-attention note: PTV is invisible in SC-team solo slice but valuable in multi-NPC encounters where SCs support melee allies.** No fix needed.

**FI-147 (NEW — Barrage recharge R4 unused): SC-C recharged Barrage (d6=6) at 5 HP in R4 but didn't fire it.**
- Per tactics: "below 12 HP, retreat behind a Rager or pillar; only fire if no melee can reach." SC-C used Tactical Retreat (bonus) + multiattack (main). The recharged Barrage was available but holding it didn't help — SC-C died before getting another turn.
- The interaction is: if SC-C fires Barrage instead (main action) and skips Tactical Retreat (or uses TR before Barrage), SC-C would take the OA from Sabriel's reaction (Sabriel has OA available). But Tactical Retreat explicitly grants no-OA movement. So Tactical Retreat then Barrage from the new position is the correct sequence. SC-C used main action for multiattack, not Barrage. At 5 HP with Barrage recharged and both PCs alive and threatening, the optimal play might have been TR + Barrage (18 avg fire damage to 2 PCs at half on save) rather than TR + multiattack (low hit-rate vs AC18+). With Bazgar at 23 HP, a 9-18 piercing Barrage could have dropped Bazgar. Instead multiattack hit twice (10+7 = 17) doing nearly the same work. In this specific case the play was equivalent — but the tactic note doesn't address this decision point.
- **Log as tactic gap: add note "When below 12 HP with Barrage recharged and both the retreat and the Barrage are available, prefer TR + Barrage over TR + multiattack if 2+ PCs are in the line — the Barrage has better expected damage vs high-AC fighters." Low-priority doc note.**

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-64 (NEW — tactic sequencing gap): CW pre-declaration order vs stagger-Barrage decision.**
- In the stagger rule, the higher-init SC decides "I multiattack this round and CW the lower-init SC." The CW bonus is spent, then the lower-init SC acts and fires Barrage (per stagger rule). The CW advantage is wasted on the Barrage save.
- The tactic spec already says "Do not Call Weakness an ally who plans to use Shard-Barrage this turn." This rule requires the CW-giver to know whether their ally will Barrage *before* spending the bonus action. At table, the DM can check; in the sim, the CW fires first. The fix is a tactic re-order in derro-shardcaller.md:
  > "**CW pre-check:** before spending the bonus action on CW, confirm your target's planned action. If the target will fire Shard-Barrage this turn, HOLD the CW charge — use Tactical Retreat (bonus) instead or save the charge for a later round. Only CW allies who are multiattacking."
- **Auto-fix candidate:** add this pre-check note to the shardcaller CW tactic section. Low-risk doc addition, no balance change.
