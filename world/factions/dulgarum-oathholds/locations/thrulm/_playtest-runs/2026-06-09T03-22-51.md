### Sim notes (harness issues, not spec bugs)
- **SIM-FC43-A Stagger check inverted:** STD-B has init 14, STD-A has init 6. Correct stagger: LOWER-init STD (STD-A) should hold AR when both are available. Sim checked `std_b.init < std_a.init` (false → didn't flag STD-B as holding), so both fired. Outcome: Marwen took double-AR R1 (16+5 extra necrotic/psychic = ~21 bonus damage). Spec stagger rule is correct; sim implementation wrong. At the table, DM should identify the lower-initiative STD at round start and mark it "holding AR." No .md change needed — the rule is already in the tactics text.
- **SIM-FC43-B VS-not-fired-despite-recharge:** Script only fires VS in R2 (hardcoded). VS recharged at R3 start but the sim pivoted to MA. At table, DM would fire VS in R3 instead of MA (or as bonus action replacement). The non-use is a sim flow issue.
- **SIM-FC43-C Beholder MA missing resist notation:** Tentacle Lash is bludgeoning from a creature weapon — Bazgar's nonmagic resistance reduces this. Sim did not halve Tentacle damage to Bazgar (applied full 11 bludgeoning R1, 16 R3). At table: Bazgar's nonmagic B/P/S resistance applies to both Tentacle Lash hits. HP math slightly off in sim — Bazgar would have survived slightly longer under correct resistance application, but outcome same (Maw auto-crit is not affected by resistance, being the primary killer).

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-FC43-A VS-never-fired-R3-3rd-consec-FC-confirm:** VS recharged at round start R3, but fight was already decided. The beholder used MA to finish Bazgar. VS would have been devastating (6d10 psychic, 2 PCs in range), but the TPK resolved via MA+STD multiattack before VS could swing. This is the 3rd consecutive slice-4 cycle where VS fails to fire before the fight resolves. Carry FI-FC42-B. DESIGN QUESTION: should VS be Recharge 5-6 and fire in R2 as the primary area effect (post-DD smite drain), rather than Recharge 6 only? The current recharge 6 (17%/round) means a 3-round fight has only 43% VS-fires probability.

- **FI-FC43-B Beholder-zero-damage-43rd-FC:** Beholder took 0 damage across 3 rounds. Breakdown: Bazgar spent most turns downed or prone; Marwen fired Fireball at thralls (correct tactic) and died before beholder; Sabriel missed her only smite attempt (roll 15 vs AC 17). This is expected for CR13 vs level-5 party. However, zero damage means no abilities except Maw and lair expressed from the beholder side (DR never recharged, VS fired but not used, legendary resistance never needed, antireality never triggered). Carry FI-BEL3-38-D (Bazgar-zero-agency): the combination of beholder-first-init, grapple, and Maw auto-crit means the frontline fighter has 0 offensive impact.

- **FI-FC43-C STD-A-killing-blow-R3:** The final kill (Sabriel DOWN) was dealt by STD-A shrine-axe (roll 21 vs AC 19, 17 damage). This is the second FC cycle where a non-beholder NPC delivers the killing blow. In slice-4 encounters the STDs are doing meaningful kill-work alongside the beholder: STD double-AR in R1 downed Bazgar a second time and reduced Marwen to 1 HP. The beholder is the action-economy engine (grapple+Maw), but the STDs are the sustained DPS closer. Feel note for DM: announce STD as "the shrine's chorus strikes the killing blow" to give it narrative weight, not just beholder-filler.

- **FI-FC43-D LoH-yoyo-10th-FC-confirm:** Sabriel spent all 25 LoH reviving Bazgar twice (R1 +15, R2 +10). Both revivals were immediately neutralized (STD AR downed Bazgar again R1; lair UG prone + Void Ray + STD AR chipped to 3 HP R2; Tentacle downed R3). The beholder's DD correctly stripped L3 slot R1 (Sabriel failed Cha save 4 vs DC16). However, the DD target was the slot pool — Sabriel used LoH (not slots) to revive, so DD's anti-divine effect was partly wasted. Carry FI-FC41-A: DESIGN QUESTION: should Drain Divinity also consume uses of LoH? As written, DD strips slots but LoH is a separate HP pool that DD doesn't touch.

- **FI-FC43-E DR-never-recharged-43rd-FC:** Disintegration Ray did not recharge in any of the 3 rounds. P(no recharge in 3 rounds at recharge 5-6) = (4/6)^3 ≈ 30%. This run landed in that 30%. DR is the dramatic capstone ability (disintegrate = no body); when it doesn't fire, the fight ends without its strongest narrative moment. Carry MQ-FC41-C.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-FC43-A Stagger-by-initiative-needs-table-tracking:** The stagger rule says "the STD acting LATER in initiative holds AR." At the table, DM needs to track which of the two STDs has lower initiative at round start, then mark that one "holding." If both have the same init (tied), a disambiguation rule is needed. Recommend adding a sentence to the STD-A/B tactics: "At round start, circle the lower-initiative STD on your tracking sheet as 'AR-hold.' "

- **MQ-FC43-B Bazgar-nonmagic-resist-and-Tentacle-damage:** Tentacle Lash is bludgeoning from the creature's own body (nonmagic). Bazgar's damage resistances include nonmagic B/P/S. This means Tentacle hits deal half damage to Bazgar. Sim did not apply this. At table: Tentacle Lash 1 hit Bazgar for 11 → actual 5-6 bludgeoning. The Maw auto-crit (piercing) is also nonmagic B/P/S → halved to ~19 from the 38 rolled. Under correct resistance, Bazgar might survive R1 MA (5+19=24 damage, 49-24=25 HP remaining). This significantly changes the R1 kill order. DESIGN QUESTION: is Bazgar intended to survive R1 MA against the beholder, or is the instant-down part of the slice's intended design (overwhelming CR13 demonstration)?
