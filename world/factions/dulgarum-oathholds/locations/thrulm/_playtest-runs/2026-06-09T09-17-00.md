### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TW39-A: Taunt 3× zero effect vs Wizard — 39th-cycle carry** (NEW emphasis)

All 3 Taunt uses fired on Marwen. Effect achieved: zero. Breakdown:
- R1: Taunt succeeded (save 1 — worst possible roll). Marwen cast Fireball (save-based, not attack roll). Taunt's "disadvantage on attack rolls" does not apply to Dex save targets. Complete bypass.
- R2: Taunt succeeded (save 8). Marwen used Fire Bolt targeting the Rager (= taunter = valid target). No disadvantage vs valid target. Complete bypass.
- R3: Marwen saved (rolled 14). No effect.
- Net: 0/3 Taunt uses produced any constraint on Marwen.

**Root issue**: Taunt disadvantage only applies to attack rolls against non-Rager targets. Any save-based spell (Fireball, Shatter, Thunderwave, Web) bypasses it entirely. Against a Wizard with Fireball, Taunt is structurally near-zero.

**Carry from FI-TW38-C (DC 12 too low) + new insight: even DC 14 wouldn't matter when Marwen chooses save-based spells.**

DM recommendation: consider a second rider on Taunt — e.g., "additionally, the target is compelled to move toward the rager on its next turn (Cha save to resist)" — that can't be bypassed by spell choice. Human decision.

**FI-TW39-B: SC solo endgame anticlimactic — 39th-cycle carry**

SC survived to R4 at 23 HP but whiffed both attacks (rolled 4 and 1). Both missed. Marwen cleaned up with Fire Bolt. Two anti-climactic rounds of the SC shooting at Marwen after Rager fell.

- Shard-Barrage recharge never checked in this run (SC only had one enemy in sight worth barraging, no line-up). The endgame drag is identical to FI-TW38-D.
- Carry recommendation: Barrage recharge 4-6 (was 5-6). Increases interestingness of solo-SC endgame.

**FI-TW39-C: Bazgar crit 2 HP — near-death spike POSITIVE**

Rager's R3 crit (Axe 2: nat 20) dealt 26 slashing, dropping Bazgar from 28→2 HP. This is a **good spike moment** — the Rager felt dangerous even at 6 HP. At the table, Sabriel should immediately use Lay on Hands or Healing Word before Bazgar acts (not needed to survive R4, but correct play).

The near-death was earned: Rager took 3 rounds of focus fire and still managed a threatening moment before dying.

### Simulation errors (not design bugs)

**SIM-TW39-A: CW advantage cleared at round start (harness gap)**

The simulation script reset `marwen_cw_adv = False` at the start of each round, before any combatant acted. This is wrong. CW granted by SC at end of R1 should persist until end of SC's R2 turn (i.e., through Rager's R2 action).

Correct R2 behavior: Rager's Axe 1 rolls 2d20-take-higher. The actual roll was d20=8 → MISS. With advantage, a second die would need ≥14 to flip to HIT vs AC 18 — 35% chance. Not guaranteed but non-trivial.

Similarly R3: CW granted R2 should apply to Rager's R3 Axe 1. R3 Axe 1 rolled d20=9 → MISS; with adv, 30% flip chance vs AC 18.

**Net impact**: Rager was effectively denied 2 chances at advantage-boosted attacks. Real-play Rager would have been slightly more dangerous in R2 and R3. Outcome (Rager dying R3) likely unchanged given the wide HP gap, but Bazgar might have taken more damage.

This is a recurring harness methodology gap (CW timing tracked incorrectly). Recommend: track CW expiry as `cw_expires_after_combatant = sc` and check at each CW-beneficiary's turn instead of resetting at round start.

**SIM-TW39-B: Sabriel never used Lay on Hands despite Bazgar at 2 HP**

At the start of R4, Bazgar had 2 HP. Sabriel had 25/25 LoH pool unused. A real Sabriel would have used a bonus action (or LoH as part of her action economy if she chose Healing Word equivalent) to bring Bazgar to safe HP before Bazgar acted. The simulation doesn't model Sabriel's LoH decision-making.

This is a simulation gap: Sabriel's reactive healing is not modeled. At the table, this would be a natural play — LoH costs a bonus action on Sabriel's turn.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TW39-A: Taunt vs spell attack rolls — explicit text confirmation**

The Rager's .md explicitly states: "Includes ranged spell attack rolls (e.g., Fire Bolt, Eldritch Blast) — Taunt disadvantage applies to any attack roll targeting a non-rager, magical or mundane. *(MQ-TW36-C)*" 

This is correctly spec'd. But Fireball and other save-based spells are NOT affected. The distinction between "attack rolls" and "saving throws" should be surfaced at the table — DMs may confuse them. A brief DM note in the Taunt DB effect text ("save-based spells bypass this disadvantage") would prevent table confusion.

**MQ-TW39-B: CW timing note for cross-initiative-order benefit**

When SC acts after Rager in initiative (as happened here: Rager init 20 > SC init 11), CW granted on SC's turn only benefits Rager's **next-round** attack. The .md tactics note says "fire CW on Rager in R1 before it charges (when SC acts first in initiative); R2+ CW on a melee Rager is impossible without SC repositioning." 

But SC should still grant CW on R1 even when SC goes second — the benefit is merely delayed to R2 (not impossible). The tactics note implies CW is useless when SC goes after Rager, which is wrong. Recommend updating: "when SC goes after Rager, CW R1 still benefits Rager's R2 attack — worth spending; hold CW only if Rager is at ≤10 HP and unlikely to survive to R2."
