### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC49-A (carry): LoH-then-immediate-VR-LA counter (same action sequence)**
Sabriel used Lay on Hands on Bazgar (full action, revived to 10 HP), then beholder fired Void Ray LA immediately after her turn, re-downing Bazgar for 22 force (back to 0 HP). The LoH consumed 10 of 25 LoH charges and produced literally zero rounds of benefit — Bazgar was down before his next death save. This is explicitly documented in tactics ("LoH override note: Prioritize Void Ray or Tentacle LA") and is mechanically correct. For human review: does this feel earned or frustrating? The beholder *should* counter LoH aggressively — that's the anti-divine design — but 10 LoH charges for 0 seconds of ally presence is a stark table moment. Consider whether a minor wording note in the .md ("announce this counter explicitly so players understand the beholder *read* their divine action") would help the table understand it as intelligent play vs gotcha.

**FI-FC49-B (new): Beholder 0 damage at TPK (49th-FC-cycle)**
The beholder took zero damage across 3 rounds. Bazgar missed all strikes vs Thrall-A (required 7+, rolled 4+6), never action-surged. Sabriel missed her smite (rolled 6+8=14 vs AC17). Marwen spent L3 on Fireball (correct) and L2 on Scorching Ray vs the dying Thrall-A. This run the party correctly prioritized clearing minions, but Fireball+Scorching Ray both went to minion cleanup — zero resources applied to beholder. Zero-beholder-damage is a recurrence across multiple FC cycles. Structural question: should the party have ANY path to dealing meaningful beholder damage at level 5? Or is "survive 4+ rounds against beholder" the only achievable goal? (47th cycle had 81 HP beholder damage — Sabriel L2+L3 smites can land.)

**FI-FC49-C (confirm): VS recharge variance — recharged R3 (roll=6), not R2 (roll=5)**
VS requires 6+ to recharge (1/6 = 16.7% per round). It recharged R3 this cycle. R1-R2 pattern (actions=Multiattack+Multiattack) means the killing VS blow lands R3+ when it doesn't recharge R2. The prior 48th-FC cycle had VS fire R2 (decisive). This run: Multiattack R2 grinded down PCs instead, VS finished Marwen R3. Both outcomes result in TPK but the feel differs — R2 VS is a thunderclap; R3 VS after 2 rounds of multiattack feels like an inevitable grind. The grind read may actually be more thematically appropriate for this encounter (not a lightning-strike boss fight but an agonizing void-consumption).

**FI-FC49-D (confirm): STD stagger rule executed correctly**
STD-A (init 23) fired AR R1; STD-B (init 22) was correctly held by stagger rule. STD-B fired MA R1 (0 hits vs AC18) and then AR R3 for the killing blow on Sabriel. The stagger rule is functioning and adding meaningful variation in who delivers the decisive hit.

**FI-FC49-E (new): STD-B sole killing blow on a PC (Sabriel)**
In this run, a shrine-touched-derro dealt the kill shot on Sabriel (AR R3, 16 dmg vs 6 HP). This is the first FC cycle where an STD delivered the final blow on a PC rather than the beholder. It felt narratively earned: Sabriel had been fighting the STD-B in melee range, and the radiant-vulnerable-suppressed-in-altar-zone mechanic prevented her smites from exploiting the STD's usual weakness. Good feel indicator — the STDs have distinct threat presence even when the beholder is the main focus.

**FI-FC49-F (new): Thrall 0/12 attack hits across 3 rounds**
Thralls made 12 total attacks (2 per round × 4 thralls over rounds 1-2, with 2 dead by round end, fewer in R3). 0 hits. All against AC18 (Bazgar) and AC19 (Sabriel) with +3 to-hit. Need ≥15 (Bazgar) or ≥16 (Sabriel) to hit. Hit rate ~30% each. Probability of 0 hits in 8 attempts at 30% = ~5.7%. This confirms the existing mechanic note but is worth flagging: in this run the thralls contributed ZERO offensive pressure. Their role was entirely as action-economy distractors (forcing Fireball/Scorching Ray expenditure). That may be intentional (designed as beholder fodder), but they also consumed 2 full PC spell slots.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC49-A (carry): VoidFeeding +1 attack/damage DB gap**
The beholder's VoidFeeding trait (+1 attack rolls, +1 damage rolls while in shrine chamber) is in the .md but not encoded in the DB spec. The sim applied it manually (+1 added to all beholder to-hit and damage totals). Dispatch output does NOT include this bonus — the dispatcher calculates from spec fields only. This creates a persistent gap where the dispatcher's to-hit/damage numbers are systematically 1 lower than actual. Ongoing carry from EV7-39-C, BEL3 series. Recommendation: encode as a flat `to_hit_bonus` +1 and `damage_modifier` +1 in each attacking action spec, or document that VoidFeeding is a post-dispatch manual add.

**MQ-FC49-B: Maw-vs-downed-grappled redirect (FIX-EV7-35-A applied)**
R2 Maw redirected from downed-grappled Bazgar to standing Sabriel per FIX-EV7-35-A ("redirect Maw to the next available standing target in reach"). Sabriel was in reach (10 ft); Maw missed (17 vs AC19). The redirect rule worked correctly and the miss is valid. No issue, confirming the rule executes correctly.

**MQ-FC49-C: Beholder initiative vs lair action conflict (carry MQ-BEL3-3-A)**
Beholder rolled init 20 this run, same as lair action firing count. Beholder acts first (higher is better; lair fires "at count 20, losing ties"). So beholder acted before its own lair action would fire — beholder used Multiattack on its action, then lair UG fired "at count 20" effectively after the beholder (since beholder won the tie). In this case UG and Multiattack were on different turns with no conflict, but the rule interaction (beholder > lair when beholder rolls exactly 20) is documented. Per MQ-BEL3-3-A carry, this ordering means beholder can do its full action AND benefit from lair UG in the same "effective round" when init=20.
