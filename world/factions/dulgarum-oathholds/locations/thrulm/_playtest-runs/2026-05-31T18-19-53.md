## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- none (all 29 Phase A actions clean)

### Simulation script bugs (testbot, not encounter spec)

- **SIM-BUG-SCT6-A: `parse_hit_and_dmg` false-positive matching**: The inline regex `r'HIT'` matches on "to-hit" and "_to-hits:_" lines in the multiattack output, causing every multiattack to report 3 "hits" instead of the correct 1–2. This inflated PC damage by ~50% per SC per round, leading the raw sim to report TPK R5 where the corrected outcome is likely VICTORY R3. The roll_combat_action output format gives raw to-hit totals; comparison to target AC must be done by the DM (or a corrected simulation). **Fix needed in testbot harness:** parse `to-hit XX` integer, compare to target AC, rather than matching the word "HIT" in the output line.

- **SIM-BUG-SCT6-B: Barrage assignment not pre-set before first SC acts**: The `barrage_sc_this_round` variable was only set AFTER a barrage fired, so when SC-A (first in initiative) was choosing a CW target, it did not correctly exclude SC-C (the designated barrage SC). SC-A CW'd SC-C (whose advantage was wasted on a save-based Barrage). Corrected behavior: designate barrage SC at start of round, before any SC acts, so CW logic can exclude the barrage SC. The tactics document correctly states this rule (MQ-41 / CW note); the simulation harness failed to pre-compute it.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SCT6-A (FEEL): Marwen R1 automatic kill even with stagger.** With 3 SCs staggered (1 Barrage + 2 multiattacks), Marwen (32 HP, AC 15) takes enough damage in R1 to die before her initiative. SC-A ST2 (6 dmg) + SC-C Barrage (10 dmg, PTV disadv) + SC-B ST2 (9 dmg) = **25 dmg** in R1 alone, leaving her at **7 HP** if she survives to act. She then draws the 2nd barrage or multiattack attention next round. The stagger helps slightly (she's not dead R1 in the corrected sim), but she falls early R2 reliably. Whether this is acceptable depends on design intent: if the Shardcaller Team is meant to punish squishy casters, this is correct. If there should be a "save the wizard" moment, the party needs a way to interpose — Bazgar's R1 Dash gap is the core problem. **RECOMMENDATION: No change needed if this encounter tier is intended as a punishing late-encounter. If used as standalone encounter, consider starting distance at 70+ ft to give Bazgar R1 attack options.**

- **FI-SCT6-B (FEEL): PTV + Barrage disadv combo is meaningful.** The Pack Tactics Voice disadvantage on saves is thematically crisp and mechanically visible. When SC-A hit Marwen before SC-C's Barrage, Marwen rolled DC 13 Dex with disadvantage. Even though she failed either way (rolled 5), the combo creates a narrative identity: the shardcallers coordinate precisely, exploiting each other's hits. No balance change needed — this is working as designed.

- **FI-SCT6-C (FEEL): Solo SC endgame anticlimactic — but moot with corrected sim.** The raw simulation's TPK R5 involved a single SC-C kiting a wounded Bazgar for 2 rounds. This felt like a slow bleed rather than a tense finale. With the corrected simulation (SCs dead R3, Bazgar + Sabriel standing), this scenario becomes a party victory rather than a TPK. The anticlimactic endgame was an artifact of the simulation bug. No design change needed.

- **FI-SCT6-D (FEEL): Shard-Barrage recharge felt slow.** SC-C failed to recover Barrage for 4 consecutive rounds (rolled 4, 1, 3, 4 on recharge). This meant the 2nd-round area pressure came entirely from SC-B (who had Barrage). In a real encounter, if both remaining SCs fail recharge in the same round, the formation loses its area-control identity and becomes a pure multiattack line. Recharge 5–6 (33% per turn) is standard but may feel unreliable across a 3-round fight. **LOG ONLY — no change recommended without more cycles.** Prior runs (slice #3, #4, #5) should be cross-checked for Barrage recharge frequency.

### Mechanical questions

Current interpretation: 2nd-highest-initiative SC fires Barrage in a 3-SC formation (1st and 3rd multiattack). **RECOMMENDATION: Add to derro-shardcaller.md checklist under item 5:** *"3-SC formation barrage assignment: SC in 2nd initiative position fires Barrage R1; rotate so each SC fires one Barrage before any SC fires a second. Designate at start of round before first SC acts, so CW logic can exclude the barrage SC."*

- **MQ-SCT6-B: CW + already-acted ally for full-HP SC.** The MQ-12 guard says "hold CW if already-acted ally has ≤20 HP." SC-C CW'd SC-A (already acted, full HP) in R1 — this was technically within the rules and SC-A collected the advantage in R2. But the advantage was slightly stale (SC-A had already moved for the round). **RECOMMENDATION: Consider adding guidance that CW on an already-acted ally is acceptable only if that ally's HP is above half maximum, not just >20. At full HP (33), SC-A was a fine target; at 21 HP with full max 33 (64%), borderline. The current ≤20 guard is probably fine.**
