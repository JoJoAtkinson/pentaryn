### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-BEL3-A: Thrall-derro paper fragility renders action-economy pressure test void.**
In this slice (3× thrall-derro vs party with Fireball), all three thralls were eliminated by end of Round 1 Marwen's Fireball (R1 8d6=35: ThrallB/C one-shotted, ThrallA limped to 5 HP and died R2). Thrall total output: 1 hit on Bazgar (4 dmg) across all three. The slice description says "tests legendary economy, lair actions, action-economy pressure" — but with a Fireball caster in the party, three 22-HP thralls provide zero sustained pressure. Recommendation: bump thrall HP to 30-35 OR add a mechanic that makes them hard to AoE (e.g., Fractured Will triggers if hit by AoE and they scatter, forcing separate targeting). Log for human: this is a design decision, not an auto-fix.

**FI-BEL3-B: Beholder at 9 HP survivor — "limited" slice nearly ends in beholder death.**
Without Disintegration Ray, the beholder's burst output is lower. The combination of Sabriel's two Longsword+Smite hits (R1: 15 dmg, R2: 22 dmg, R3: 21 dmg = 58 total to beholder) plus two Fireballs (17 + 26 = 43) reduced the beholder to 9 HP. Beholder survived only because VS recharged on a 6/6 in R3 (16.7% chance). Without that recharge, Sabriel might have had one more swing and killed it. Log for human: the "limited" slice (no DR) is already very close to a possible party victory if VS doesn't recharge. Possibly intentional design tension — worth flagging.

**FI-BEL3-C: Maw auto-crit is the encounter's decisive single-hit mechanic.**
Bazgar went from 45 HP to 0 in one beholder turn: 17 bludgeoning (Tentacle 1) + 37 piercing (Maw auto-crit on grappled). The auto-crit synergy (grapple → guaranteed crit Maw) is working as intended per FIX-FC39-A and is the correct tactical sequence. At the table this will feel extremely swingy and punishing. Recommend an explicit DM note at the start of the encounter brief: "the beholder will try to grapple-then-Maw — warn players this is the lethal combo so they can split positioning."

**FI-BEL3-D: R1 Drain Divinity hold protocol executed but failed vs. high-CHA Sabriel.**
DD (Sabriel CHA save DC 16, +3 modifier): ~35% chance she fails. She made it (rolled 15, total 18). The protocol itself is correct. However, given that a level-5 paladin has Aura of Protection (allies within 10 ft also add CHA mod), this save is naturally high. If Sabriel's CHA is +4 or higher, the protocol has <30% chance of working. Log for human: DD vs Sabriel might be better timed post-VS (R3+) when she's wounded and no ally Aura is relevant — or accept the low hit rate as correct.

**FI-BEL3-E: Lair action fired productively (Unstable Ground on Bazgar R1).**
Bazgar fell prone at R1 start, giving the beholder free reign over the Tentacle attacks without the "prone + ranged disadvantage" problem (since Tentacle is melee reach 10 ft, not ranged). The FIX-FEV7-34-A note about prone + ranged-attack disadvantage was NOT triggered because the beholder correctly used melee attacks. Lair action executed cleanly.

**FI-BEL3-F: Void Scream R2 is fight-deciding.**
VS (41 psychic, DC 16 Wis) in R2 with grappled Marwen at 16 HP was essentially a guaranteed kill on Marwen (even half damage = 19 > 16) and a near-lethal blow on Sabriel (39 full = puts her at 5 HP). This is the correct R2 sequencing per FIX-EV7-32-A. The mechanic is working. Design note: VS is so decisive that rounds 3+ feel like cleanup. The "interesting" part of the encounter is R1–R2.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-BEL3-A: Prone attacker (Bazgar) — attack roll disadvantage correctly applied?**
Per RAW PHB, a prone creature has disadvantage on its attack rolls. In the sim, Bazgar fell prone via Lair (Unstable Ground R1) and both greatsword attacks missed (d20 8 and 7 + 7, neither reaching AC 17). The sim applied disadvantage correctly (it's baked into the miss outcomes from individual rolls). At the table, confirm: Bazgar stands up costing half movement (15 ft at 30 ft speed), which he did NOT do in the sim (he attempted escape from grapple instead). After beholder grappled him, he became grappled (speed 0) — standing from prone while grappled is legal but the party needs to track both conditions simultaneously. No DB fix needed — tracking note for DM.

**MQ-BEL3-B: Beholder below-30-HP retreat vs VS recharge timing.**
Beholder entered R3 at 30 HP (exactly at retreat threshold) and acted at init 9. By that point Sabriel had hit it to 9 HP. Per tactics: "if reduced below 30 HP, retreats." Does "below 30" mean ≤29 or ≤30? In the sim at end of Sabriel's R2 turn, beholder hit exactly 30 HP. At start of beholder's R3 turn it's still at 30 (Sabriel hits it to 9 during R3 before beholder acts — wait, no: order is Sabriel (init 16) → Beholder (init 9). So beholder acts after Sabriel in R3. Sabriel's R3 hit took beholder to 9 HP, but by then it was already the beholder's turn... no. Sabriel acts at init 16, takes beholder from 30 to 9. Then beholder acts at init 9 at 9 HP. It's well below the 30-threshold. Tactics say "retreat deeper into lower shaft, using thralls to block pursuit" — but all thralls are dead. With no thralls left and all PCs about to die, the beholder chose to fire VS rather than retreat. This is reasonable (VS at Sabriel for cleanup) but the tactics block says retreat should happen. Log for human: add a note to tactics about what beholder does when below 30 HP AND thralls are dead AND only one PC remains standing near death.
