### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SW-A — Altar zone suppression works as intended; Marwen's fire pivot is real.**
Marwen correctly avoided Fireball (fire vulnerability suppressed near altar) and chose Shatter/Sleet Storm instead. This created genuine tactical pressure — Fireball would have been the obvious choice without the zone. The zone is doing its job. No change needed.

**FI-SW-B — Stagger + UF-priority creates a "banked AR" arc on the lower-init STD.**
STD-Beta held AR for 2 full rounds (stagger R1, UF-priority R2 with 2-target-only cone) then fired it normally in R3. This created a satisfying dramatic arc: the creature "saved up" power and released it at a pivotal moment. However it also meant the lower-init STD essentially performed as a pure melee unit for 2 rounds. Design question: is the stagger rule too conservative? In this run, STD-Alpha's R1 AR (20 dmg to Bazgar) was already brutal — preventing STD-Beta from also firing was probably correct. Stagger rule confirmed useful; no change recommended.

**FI-SW-C — OBR fired 3× but all missed; self-damage still mattered.**
OBR triggered once each on Bazgar (R2), Bazgar (R2 via STD-Alpha), and Sabriel (R1). All three attack rolls missed (AC 18/19 is high) but the shrine-touched took 3+4+4=11 HP of self-damage across those triggers. This contributed meaningfully to STD-Alpha's kill trajectory. The "shrine punishes even a miss" mechanic is distinct and flavorful. No change.

**FI-SW-D — UF advantage largely wasted targeting Sabriel (AC 19).**
STD-Beta activated Unstable Form in R2 (took Shatter damage R1) but targeted Sabriel (AC 19) — both UF-boosted attacks missed. The Tactics section doesn't explicitly direct STDs to target lowest-AC PC when UF is active. Recommend adding to Tactics: "When Unstable Form is active, prefer the lowest-AC PC in reach to maximize advantage value." Low-priority quality-of-life, not a balance issue.

**FI-SW-E — Fight resolved in 4 rounds; moderate resource drain.**
Party spent: 1×L2 slot (Shatter), 1×L3 slot (Sleet Storm), 2 Magic Missiles, 2 smites. Marwen at 5 HP and Bazgar at 13 HP entering R4 shows genuine pressure despite the victory. Sabriel was largely untouched (44/44 — STD attacks couldn't breach AC 19). The 2×STD slice feels like a winnable but costly fight for a level-5 party, appropriate for a mid-dungeon encounter.

**FI-SW-F — [DESIGN DECISION] STD-Beta's R3 AR banked for 2 rounds then landed in a normal state — is the stagger-hold too long?**
STD-Beta held AR for rounds 1 and 2 (via stagger + UF-priority), then fired in R3 with AR never recharged in the interim. This means the stagger rule effectively delayed a guaranteed AR fire by 2 rounds. The fight was effectively over by R4. The AR banked well here, but in longer fights this delay could become frustrating (DM perspective: STD-Beta keeps "almost" using AR). Consider: loosen stagger so lower-init STD only holds AR if the higher-init STD ALSO HAS AR recharged at round start — don't hold it across multiple rounds. Leave for human review.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SW-A — OBR clarification needed: fires "between attacks" of PC multiattack or once per round?**
The .md says OBR "interrupts between attacks if the attacker has multiattack." Under D&D5e rules, a creature can only use one reaction per round — so OBR can fire at most once per round per shrine-touched. The "interrupts between attacks" language implies it fires after the FIRST hit of a multiattack (before attack 2 lands), not that it fires multiple times. This is mechanically valid but the phrase could mislead a DM into thinking OBR fires on every individual hit. Recommend clarifying: "OBR fires at most once per round per shrine-touched, triggered on the first melee hit received from a visible attacker within 5 ft. If the attacker has multiattack, OBR fires after the triggering hit resolves but before subsequent attacks in that same multiattack sequence."

**MQ-SW-B — [INFRA] roll_combat_action blocked by external RNG in cloud sandbox (70th consecutive).**
All attack-roll actions raise `RuntimeError: Failed to fetch random numbers from both quantumnumbers and random.org`. The `dnd_roller.py` has no fallback to Python's `random` module — it requires network access. Recommend: add env var `DNDROLLER_OFFLINE=1` (or `QUANT_API_URL=local`) that routes through `random.random()` when both external sources fail. This would let Phase A run fully deterministic in the testbot context. Leave for human to implement.
