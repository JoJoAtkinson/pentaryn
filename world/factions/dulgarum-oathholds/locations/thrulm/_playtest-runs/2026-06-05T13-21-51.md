### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SW29-A (NEW): AR stagger + cone requirement → 0-AR R1, double-AR R2 nova pattern.**
- In R1, STD1 had only Sabriel in its 15-ft cone (Marwen was 20+ ft away). AR requires 2+. STD1 could not fire AR. STD2 was stagger-held. Net result: zero ARs fired R1, both STDs entered R2 with AR ready.
- R2: both STDs fired AR in sequence — STD1 AR (Sabriel/Bazgar both failed, 17 each), then STD2 AR (both saved, 2 each). The spike is extreme on a bad run: if both R2 saves had failed, the combined damage would have been 17+17 = 34 to Sabriel and 17+17 = 34 to Bazgar in a single round.
- Prior SW cycles (25th–28th): at least one STD typically died R1 or fired AR R1, preventing the double-AR R2 surge. This cycle's quiet R1 + full nova R2 is a distinct dangerous pattern.
- **Recommendation:** Clarify in tactics whether the stagger-hold should release when the higher-init STD *couldn't* fire AR (cone < 2) vs *chose not to* fire AR (held for tactical reasons). If hold releases when STD1 doesn't fire, STD2 could fire AR R1 at Marwen (1-target cone — but AR still needs 2+, so same problem). The root issue is the "2+ targets in cone" threshold creating rare windows when both STDs are blocked from firing R1. Consider whether AR should allow single-target firing at reduced damage as a fallback. **Human design decision.**

**FI-SW29-B (29th-cycle confirm): Altar zone fire/radiant suppression.**
- Sabriel's L1 smite dealt 9 radiant (normal) vs ~18 expected outside the altar zone. Marwen's Scorching Ray dealt normal fire in all rays.
- DM must track this manually — the DB output for these actions contains no mention of the zone effect.
- The vulnerability suppression meaningfully shaped spell choice (Marwen used SR rather than Fireball, correctly) but this requires table announcement at combat start.
- **29th consecutive cycle confirm.** Recommend adding a combat-start reminder to COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md.

**FI-SW29-C (NEW): Stagger-hold persists when higher-init STD cannot fire AR.**
- The stagger rule says "check BOTH at start of round; if both have AR, mark lower-initiative as HOLD." In R1, STD1 had AR but could not fire (cone < 2 targets). STD2 remained held.
- The .md says "Stagger exemption (low HP): a shrine-touched below 20 HP... fires AR recklessly." There is no exemption for "higher-init couldn't fire due to positioning."
- This is likely intentional (stagger tracks availability, not execution), but it creates the double-AR nova pattern when party spread prevents R1 AR. The .md should state explicitly: "Stagger hold persists regardless of whether the higher-initiative STD actually fires AR — the hold is based on availability at start of round, not on actual execution." Currently implied, not stated.
- **Recommendation:** Add one sentence to stagger rule clarifying persistence behavior. Minor text fix, not a balance change.

**FI-SW29-D (confirm): UF (Unstable Form) never expressed as advantage.**
- STD2 had 10+ damage from single-turn in R3 (5 slashing + 3 OBR self = 8 — below threshold; Marwen's 6 fire in Marwen's own turn doesn't count for STD2's UF calculation since UF tracks "single turn" of incoming, not cumulative per round). UF did not trigger.
- Even if it had triggered for R4, STD2 would have used AR (2+ targets, correct per tactics), where advantage is wasted.
- UF has now been active in STD action and then AR-chosen in multiple cycles without triggering. The .md correctly explains the tradeoff. No spec change.

**FI-SW29-E (29th-cycle confirm): OBR dispatch is text-only (reaction_kind:buff).**
