## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **BUG-SW17-A** (OBR fires while incapacitated): Simulation fired Oath-Breaking Retaliation for STD-B in R2 and R3 while STD-B was incapacitated by Hypnotic Pattern. An incapacitated creature cannot take reactions. Shrine-touched-derro.md OBR section updated to explicitly state: "OBR does NOT fire while the shrine-touched is incapacitated, stunned, paralyzed, or otherwise unable to take reactions." Applied to `world/factions/dulgarum-oathholds/locations/thrulm/npcs/shrine-touched-derro.md`.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SW-17-A** (Dispatch harness bug, not a spec bug): Simulation dispatched actions using instance names "STD-A"/"STD-B" rather than the slug "shrine-touched-derro". DB returned error; AR dealt 0 necrotic damage. Only the 1d4 psychic riders fired (via hand-rolled simulation fallback). True AR would have dealt 2d10 necrotic per failed save on top of the riders — expected 9–15 necrotic per PC who fails. This misrepresents the encounter. With correct AR output: Marwen (30 HP) likely takes ~14 damage from two AR hits and approaches 16 HP after R1 — not the low-pressure 30 HP shown. **Outcome confidence: LOW for this run — true outcome may be closer to 5th/6th cycle (VICTORY R2-3 at 50-70% PC HP) rather than near-full HP VICTORY.**

- **FI-SW-17-B (FI-159 17th-cycle confirm)**: PCs won initiative (Marwen 22 > STD-A 16). Marwen acted before STD-A, casting Hold Person (which STD-A saved). If STD-A had won initiative and AR'd first, the fight trajectory changes substantially. Init-dependence pattern confirmed for the 17th time in shrine-wedge.

- **FI-SW-17-C**: Hypnotic Pattern (Wis save 2 vs DC 14) incapacitated STD-B for the entire fight. Combined with STD-A's R1 Action Surge death, the result is a whitewash. Pattern: whenever a hard CC lands (Hold Person OR Hypnotic Pattern), the STD effectively has 0 offensive turns. With Wis save ~+0, a level-5 wizard has a ~70% chance to incapacitate an STD with a 2nd-level spell. *(Previously logged as FI-NEW-SW7-A, 8th-cycle; this confirms the structural concern at 17th-cycle.)*

- **FI-SW-17-D**: Unstable Form never activated — both STDs died before their next NPC turn. Pattern continues (FI-188 17th-cycle UF-unreachable confirm). The UF threshold of 10 damage is easily met, but the STDs consistently die before they get an additional turn to exploit it.

- **FI-SW-17-E**: Stagger worked correctly (STD-B held AR R1 with correct initiative check at round start). AR was released mid-R1 when STD-A fired, and STD-B correctly chose to fire its own AR. STD-A dropped end-of-R1 before stagger created much drama.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-SW17-A**: Harness instance-name dispatch. Simulation called `roll_combat_action(npc="STD-A", ...)` — DB expects slug `"shrine-touched-derro"`. The testbot should always dispatch by NPC slug, not by encounter-instance label. Phase A confirmed the slug works correctly. This is a harness coding gap, not a spec bug. No DB or .md change needed.

- **MQ-SW17-B**: Stagger hold re-evaluation mid-round. When STD-A fired AR and released STD-B's hold, STD-B then also fired AR on the same round. Is that intended? The tactics text says the stagger hold is "released" after the first fires — so yes, STD-B can fire AR on its turn even within the same round if released. This creates the scenario where both fire AR in R1 if their initiative is far apart (STD-A acts R1 early, stagger released, STD-B acts R1 late with AR freed). Worth confirming this is intended vs. "the hold lasts until the next round."

- **MQ-SW17-C (DESIGN DECISION)**: Should the Stagger hold be "until next round" rather than "until after the first fires"? If the hold expires only at start-of-next-round, a mid-round release would never cause double-AR in R1. The current text says the second fires "next round" — but mid-round release lets it fire same round if its turn hasn't come yet. Recommend: add clarification that if the first fires and releases the hold, the second cannot fire AR **until the following round** (consistent with "holds Resonance for the next round" wording). *(Low-priority, only matters in narrow initiative window.)*
