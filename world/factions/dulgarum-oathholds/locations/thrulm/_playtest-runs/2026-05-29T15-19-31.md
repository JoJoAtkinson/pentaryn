### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-TP-1 (feel): SC collapses too fast once focused** — SC entered R3 at 2 HP (from Sabriel's R2 attacks) and died before acting. The SC is CR 2 and threshold-patrol is the opener, so some fragility is expected. However, the SC never got to demonstrate its full tactical kit (CW stagger, TR bonus action, R2 Barrage recharge play). In live play the DM should position SC behind columns at 60 ft and use terrain to discourage focus-fire. This isn't a stat bug — it's a live-DM positioning note. No auto-fix.

- **FI-TP-2 (feel): Call Weakness never fired — initiative-order structural problem** — In this initiative order (DW2=19, DW1=10, SC=10), both DW allies act BEFORE the SC in every round. The SC's `already-acted ally guard` (MQ-12: "hold CW if already-acted ally has ≤20 HP") means CW is almost always held. By R2, DW1 had 7 HP (well below the 20-HP guard) and was dead before SC could CW. In R1, CW was correctly suppressed (Barrage turn). Result: CW 0/3 uses in the entire encounter. This is NOT a spec bug — the rules are correctly authored and MQ-12 is correct. But it highlights that **CW utility is heavily dependent on the SC winning the initiative contest against its DW allies.** In threshold-patrol specifically, the two-DW + SC formation almost guarantees DW initiatives beat SC initiative (DW and SC have the same +2 Dex bonus, 50/50 per roll). DESIGN DECISION: consider whether threshold-patrol should position SC to act first (e.g., SC is on high alert and gets surprise-adjacent positioning bonus in init), or whether CW's structural underperformance in this formation is acceptable.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-TP-1 (simulation protocol):** The Phase B simulation runs `roll_combat_action` for NPC narration text, then re-rolls independently via local `random.Random` for hit resolution. This causes the dispatch output and the simulation outcome to diverge (e.g., R3 DW2 crossbow: dispatch showed to-hit 7 = miss, local dice showed a hit). This is a testbot architecture issue, not a spec bug. For accuracy, the simulation should parse the to-hit number from the dispatch output rather than re-rolling. Worth fixing in `run_one_scenario.py` — low priority since the mismatch only affects automated playtest fidelity, not live-table use. No auto-fix this cycle.
