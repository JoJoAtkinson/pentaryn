### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP-1 (feel): SC Call Weakness never fires in threshold-patrol — structural initiative lock**
SC's tactical identity is built around Call Weakness (3/Day, bonus action, advantage on ally's next attack roll). In this encounter, DWD1 (init 12) and DWD2 (init 11) both beat SC (init 9) on a typical roll — both DWDs act before SC every round. MQ-12 correctly holds CW because spending it on an already-acted ally is wasted. But the DWDs die by R1-R2 (Sabriel + Bazgar clean them up), so there is no surviving DWD alive by the time SC could usefully pre-buffer CW for a future round.
Result: CW spent 0/3 uses across the entire fight. SC's signature tactical ability went unused. The encounter completed in 3 rounds; SC did 1× Barrage and 2× missed throws, then died.
**Log for human:** Is this intended for the threshold-patrol slice, or should SC be given Dex +3 (or +4) to more reliably act before the DWDs and pre-buffer CW? Alternatively, increase SC count or drop DWD count so a DWD is alive when SC acts.

**FI-TP-2 (feel): SC Shard-Throw hit-rate is too low against this party**
SC's Shard-Throw is +4 to hit. Against the Compass Edge party (AC 14/15/18/19), hit probability per throw:
- vs Marwen AC15: 30% hit
- vs Bazgar AC18: 15% hit
- vs Sabriel AC19: 10% hit
In R2, SC threw twice at Marwen (AC15) and missed both. Expected: ~51% miss-miss probability at AC15 (+4 to hit). SC deals ~0 sustained damage outside the Barrage. For a CR 2 back-line skirmisher, this may be intentional (Barrage is the payload), but it means SC feels irrelevant after Barrage fires.
**Log for human:** Consider whether SC should have +5 or +6 to hit on Shard-Throw to feel threatening at range, or leave as-is (Barrage is the threat; throw is a nuisance).

**FI-TP-3 (feel): encounter resolves too cleanly — no round-3 feeling of danger**
Party won 36/49/44 HP remaining. Marwen spent 1 L2 slot + was at 11 HP (meaningful stress). Bazgar took 13 from Barrage (meaningful). Sabriel was untouched. DWD2 landed a crit on Marwen (9 damage from a nat 20 — felt good). The Tactical Drilling advantage mechanic made DWD1's attack feel legitimately dangerous (rolled 19, hit). Overall: encounter DOES feel like a credible warning shot. If Shatter had rolled lower and the Barrage had also hit Sabriel, Marwen could be down.
**Assessment:** slice 0 feel is CORRECT — it's a warning encounter, not a killer. Marwen under real stress, others lightly bruised.

**FI-TP-4 (mechanical): Barrage double-dice discrepancy (tracking note)**
DB output printed "15 piercing" (DB used its own cache draw) while simulation rolled 13 (independent `random` draw). This is expected dual-track dice; no bug. However, for consistency in logged output it could cause confusion if the DM uses both DB text and simulation numbers. Recommend: when the DB output shows a damage total, use that number in the simulation rather than re-rolling.

### Mechanical questions
- **MQ-TP-1:** DWD has Tactical Drilling: "advantage on melee attack rolls against the target" when an ally is within 5 ft and the target is in the ally's reach. At 10 ft from the party in a narrow corridor, DWD1 and DWD2 are side by side → both should have TD adv on both party members in the front of the line. Simulation gave TD adv to DWD1 (vs Marwen) but not DWD2 (because DWD1 was downed before DWD2 acted, so TD was already gone). This is correct. But if both DWDs act before DWD1 falls, both should get TD adv simultaneously. Confirm: TD adv persists for all attacks in DWD1's turn even if the "ally" is the other DWD who hasn't acted yet. (Standard 5e: allies are checked at the point of the attack, not at the start of the turn.)
