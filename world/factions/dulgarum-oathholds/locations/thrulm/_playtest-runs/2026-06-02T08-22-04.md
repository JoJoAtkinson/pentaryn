**FI-SW23-B — Fight duration and resource drain**

Broadly the fight deals meaningful pressure on the party at full resources (1 slot + 2 smites + 2 heals for a 2× CR3 fight).

**FI-SW23-C — Altar zone de-fangs Fireball AND smite simultaneously**
Both Fireball (fire) and Divine Smite (radiant) lose their bonus damage within 60 ft of the altar. Sabriel's two smites were ~10 damage each instead of ~14 (no doubling). Marwen's Fireball hit for 27/29 instead of estimated 54/58. Combined resource drain on suppressed tools: meaningful. Worth surfacing as a table note — if the party knows the zone suppresses these damage types, they may choose differently (pull STDs away, use non-fire/radiant attacks). Currently no visible cue is described in the encounter (see DD-SW23-A below).

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SW23-A — OBR death edge case (new)**
When a melee hit kills a shrine-touched (drops to 0 HP), does Oath-Breaking Retaliation fire? The trigger is "took melee damage from a visible attacker within 5 ft." In 5e, reactions are declared in response to triggers — but if the triggering damage incapacitates the creature (reduces to 0 HP), the creature cannot use reactions. In both R1 (Sabriel kills STD-A) and R2 (Sabriel kills STD-B), the killing blow was a single smite hit, and OBR did not fire. Recommend: add explicit note to OBR `.md` text — "OBR does not fire if the triggering damage drops the shrine-touched to 0 HP (killing blow = no reaction)." The current wording does not address this case.

**MQ-SW23-B — OBR reaction_kind: "buff" schema gap (ongoing from MQ-SW22-A context)**
OBR is a to-hit counter-attack but uses `reaction_kind: "buff"` to satisfy the validator (since `"damage"` kind requires `attacker_save`, which doesn't apply here). This means the combat runner does NOT auto-roll the OBR attack — the DM sees effect text and must roll manually. The reaction schema has no `to_hit` kind. Consider adding `reaction_kind: "to_hit"` to support counter-attacks that use attack rolls rather than saving throws. Until then, OBR at the table requires the DM to notice the effect text and roll independently.

### Design decisions for human review

**DD-SW23-A — Altar zone invisibility to party**
The altar zone's fire/radiant suppression is a powerful tactical mechanic that punishes common party tools (Fireball, Divine Smite) without a visible in-fiction cue. A Fireball hitting the altar cluster and doing "normal" damage rather than doubled may confuse players or waste a slot. Recommend: add a visible tell (a line in the chamber description about the shrine's ambient suppression of holy/fire energy, or a Perception/Arcana check to detect the dampening aura before committing spells). Low-priority if the party is expected to discover through play.

**DD-SW23-B — OBR schema gap (MQ-SW23-B above)**
Adding `reaction_kind: "to_hit"` to `combat_actions_db.py` would let OBR auto-roll its counter-attack, improving at-table speed. Required fields for a `to_hit` reaction: `to_hit_bonus`, `damage` (dice + type), `self_damage` (optional). This is a code change to `scripts/combat_actions_db.py` (validator + renderer) and then a spec update for OBR. Not a balance change — just surfacing the attack roll automatically.
