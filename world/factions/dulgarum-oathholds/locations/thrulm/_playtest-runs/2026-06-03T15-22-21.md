### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SCT-6-A: Pack Tactics Voice (PTV) — 0 activations across 3 rounds**
PTV (passive: ally hit → target disadv on next save) never triggered. In open-vault formation: (1) R1 SCs all missed or used CW, no hit+save chain; (2) R2 SCs missed or were nearly dead before Barrage could be staggered to exploit a fresh hit; (3) R3 SC-C solo, no allies. PTV's trigger requires a hit *and* a subsequent save-based ability on the *same* target before its next turn — a tight coordination window the formation repeatedly failed to set up. This is likely endemic to the open-vault geometry: PTV is designed for the corridor/chokepoint where SCs pack together and can chain hit→barrage. In the main vault's open spread, PTV functions as dead-weight passive. **DESIGN DECISION:** Consider an explicit tactics note for open-vault formation: "PTV pays off only when SC-X hits in turn N and SC-Y barrages the same target in the SAME round (before target's next turn). In open vault, where hit rates at long range are ~25% at disadvantage, PTV activation is statistically ~6% per round per target. Accept that PTV may not trigger in open-vault encounters; its threat value is psychological (forces PCs to consider save-based positioning)."

**FI-SCT-6-B: Shard-Barrage recharged twice, fired 0 times (open vault geometry)**
SC-B recharged Barrage in R2 but: HP at 6 (critical), party spread, no valid 2-PC line, TR priority. SC-A recharged Barrage in R5 slot (pre-computed, not reached). In 3 rounds of combat, Barrage was available twice and used zero times. The 15-ft line geometry combined with the stagger rule (1/round max) and the TR-vs-CW bonus-action conflict means Barrage is near-unfiable in the open vault against a smart party. **DESIGN DECISION:** This may be *intentional* — Barrage is a corridor ability whose threat shapes table tactics ("stay spread, or suffer"). In the open vault, its non-activation is correct. However, DMs should be briefed: "If the fight happens in the main vault, Barrage will rarely fire. Its presence in the kit is a positioning deterrent, not a rotation centerpiece." Add a sentence to SC tactics accordingly.

**FI-SCT-6-C: CW depleted to ~2/9 total charges due to HP guard**
3 SCs × 3 CW/day = 9 total charges. Only 2 fired (SC-C → SC-B, SC-B → SC-A, both R1). From R2 onward, all allies were ≤20 HP (guard DD-8/DD-25 blocks CW). In practice, CW is a 1-round ability per formation fight. The 3/day limit implies 3 fights between long rests — but per-fight usage is 1 charge. **DESIGN DECISION:** The HP guard is the right call mechanically (no point CWing an ally about to die). The 3/day designation is historically accurate but creates cognitive overhead at the table ("I have 3 charges! ...and can only use 1 of them this fight"). Consider simplifying to 1/encounter or keeping 3/day with a clearer tactics note that "in practice you will expend 1 CW charge per encounter before the HP guard kicks in."

**FI-SCT-6-D: TR consistently blocked CW after R1 (bonus-action monopoly)**
From R2 onward, every SC with a nearby PC used TR (bonus action), preempting CW. Per documented FI-181/FI-182, TR and CW are mutually exclusive per turn. The sim confirms: in a 3-SC formation against a party of 3 melee-capable PCs, TR fires almost every round from R2 onward, leaving CW entirely to R1. The bonus-action conflict is functioning as intended, but worth confirming at table play: DMs may expect CW to be usable mid-fight, when in practice it's a R1-only ability once PCs commit to closing distance.

**FI-SCT-6-E: Sabriel took ZERO damage; Bazgar took ZERO damage — Marwen absorbed all damage (22 of 22 HP dealt)**
3 SCs dealt 22 total damage, 100% to Marwen. This is mechanically correct (SCs prioritize lowest-AC target = Marwen AC 15 per tactics). However, Sabriel and Bazgar ended fully healthy. The encounter feels lopsided: frontline PCs are irrelevant to SC targeting until they close distance, which takes 1-2 rounds. **DESIGN NOTE:** This is the intended design (SCs kite and target casters). Not a bug. But note for the table: Bazgar/Sabriel players may feel "left out" during R1 while Marwen is targeted. The slow-close dynamic is exactly the kiting-feel this slice tests. Result: **kiting feel confirmed — it works, and it's frustrating for melee players in a realistic way.**

**FI-SCT-6-F: 2-SC endgame stagger rule never activated**
SC-A died in R2 before SC-C and SC-B both acted in the same round from a 2-SC state. The rule (first-acting SC multiattacks + CW, second fires Barrage) was never tested. This is a coverage gap. A future run that starts the fight with 2 SCs would fully exercise this path.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SCT-6-A: PTV + Barrage timing on same round**
If SC-B multiattacks and hits Bazgar (Str-heavy, likely fails Dex saves), does SC-A firing Barrage in the SAME round against Bazgar benefit from PTV disadvantage? Per spec: "target has disadvantage on the *next* saving throw before end of its next turn" — YES, PTV applies to the Barrage DC 13 Dex save if triggered before Bazgar's next turn. This should be called out more explicitly in the checklist: "PTV stacks with Barrage only if the hit and the barrage target the SAME creature in the SAME round before that creature's next turn." Recommend adding a callout line to checklist item 4.

**MQ-SCT-6-B: Barrage direction when PC is pursuer within 15 ft**
When a PC charges and closes to within 15 ft of an SC, TR fires (SC retreats). Question: if the SC is trying to hit the pursuing PC who is now BEHIND it (relative to the PC's original approach direction), can Barrage fire backward (toward the SC's original position, catching the pursuer)? Nothing in the spec addresses line orientation. This matters if a SC wants to fire Barrage as it retreats past a pursuer. Recommend adding: "Barrage line can be oriented in any direction from the SC's position; it does NOT have a 'forward-only' constraint."
