### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-BEL3-33-B: VS R1 one-shots both casters (35 psychic, 33rd-cycle confirm)**
  Marwen (fail, 7 vs 16): 35 from 32 HP → down. Sabriel (fail, 3 vs 16): 35 from 44 HP → 9 HP (survives but barely). VS is the dominant party-shaping ability in every cycle where the beholder acts before all PCs: it wipes or critically wounds the casters before they cast, killing the slot-economy fight before it begins. Recommendation: strong candidate for recharge-6-only-per-encounter or damage reduction (5d10 / 2d10). Carry-forward from FI-EV7-27-A.

- **FI-BEL3-33-C: Beholder 110/110 at party collapse — zero damage taken (33rd-cycle)**
  Bazgar used Action Surge on thrall clearance (correct priority, wrong outcome for beholder HP). After thralls fell, the party landed 0 hits on the beholder across R2–R4 (8 attack rolls total: 9,13,12,6,13,6,9,6 — all below AC 17). Cold dice, not spec bug. But structurally, the beholder is deeply asymmetric — party is dead at full beholder HP. Human note: confirm this asymmetry is intentional for the "CR 13 vs level 5" design intent. Prior victories only occurred with VS/DR cold streaks (31st FC cycle).

- **FI-BEL3-33-D: thralls-R1-Fireball/Action-Surge one-shot (33rd-cycle, 6th confirm of DD-BEL3-32-D)**
  Bazgar Action Surge wiped all 3 thralls in R1 without Fireball (Marwen had Fireball but was PRONE and then dead before her turn). 3× thrall-derro at 22 HP cannot survive even half a Fighter's Action Surge. Escort pressure never materializes. The slice's intent is to test "action-economy pressure" from escorts, but thralls fold too fast. Recommend HP 28–30 or starting-stagger (thralls not adjacent at combat start) to survive R1. Carry-forward from DD-BEL3-32-D.

- **FI-BEL3-33-E: LoH revival loop futile (33rd-cycle confirm)**
  Sabriel spent 20 LoH points reviving Marwen in R2 and R3. Both revives lasted <1 round — TL2 re-killed Marwen both times (Marwen STR -1, fails DC16 STR save on rolls of 1 and 3, always grappled, then crushed). LoH is not a spell slot (DD can't counter), but the beholder's TL2+Maw loop negates it anyway. This is structurally correct for an overwhelming encounter — feels earned as a "futile heroism" beat. No fix recommended.

- **FI-BEL3-33-F: manifest_thralls lair wasted R3 (33rd-cycle, auto-fix applied)**
  See FIX-BEL3-33-A above. Coaching note added.

- **DD-BEL3-32-C (33rd-cycle VS Frightened confirm): Frightened rider fires but is academic**
  Both Marwen and Sabriel were Frightened by VS R1 (both failed Wis saves). Marwen was already down the same round; Sabriel recovered only partially. The Frightened condition (DC 16 Wis to end at turn end) never influenced any decision or action — Marwen died before her turn, Sabriel spent R2 on LoH/attacks. The rider is flavor-only in practice. DB spec includes it; .md does not mention it (DD-BEL3-32-C ongoing). Recommend: either add the Frightened rider to the .md or remove it from the DB spec. Human decision.

- **DD-BEL3-32-B (33rd-cycle TL grapple STR save confirm): Marwen always grappled; Bazgar resisted**
  Marwen fails STR saves (rolls 1, 3 vs DC 16; STR -1 → max save ~15 on nat-20). Functionally auto-grappled by any TL hit. Bazgar resisted both times (rolls 17, 22; STR +4 → 55% pass rate at DC 16). DB spec (STR save) creates meaningful grapple-resistance for high-STR targets. The .md says "auto-grapple on hit" — the two interpretations diverge for Bazgar but converge for Marwen. Ongoing human decision (DD-BEL3-32-B).

- **DD-BEL3-32-A (33rd-cycle Maw auto-crit): not expressed this cycle**
  Bazgar resisted grapple (STR save 17, 22). Maw attacked non-grappled targets and missed R2. R4 Maw killed Sabriel via standard 4d8+4=24 (not a crit). The auto-crit mechanic only fires when TL hits AND target fails STR save — in 2 of 4 beholder turns, the Maw target had already resisted grapple. Ongoing.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-BEL3-33-A: Drain Divinity never fired — LA priority guidance needed**
  Sim consumed Move LA at end of Sabriel's turn R1, leaving 2/3 LA — not enough for DD (costs 3). In practice the beholder should use DD at the earliest opportunity after a high-smite target acts, before spending LAs on Move. Recommend adding explicit LA priority order to Tactics: "DD > VR > Move; only use Move LA when all targets already out of range."

- **MQ-BEL3-33-B: Thrall TL2 re-kill loop — intended?**
  Marwen was revived and re-killed via TL2 twice in the same fight. The re-kill happens because TL2 targets Marwen (lowest AC), she fails STR save (always), then is immediately grappled+hit again. This feels correct for the beholder's intelligence and efficiency — but is the "LoH revive → TL2 re-kill same round" loop intended as a designed beat, or an emergent pathology? Note for human.
