### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-EV12-A (NEW) — DR nat-1 miss on surprise round: feel vs. MQ-EV10-A unresolved**

The beholder's surprise-round Disintegration Ray rolled a natural 1 (1+6=7 vs Marwen AC 15 = MISS). This is the 12th cycle of empty-void. Distribution across cycles:
- DR hits in surprise round, disintegrates or near-disintegrates Marwen: ~7/12 cycles
- DR misses or is deflected: ~5/12 cycles

When DR hits: fight is effectively over in R1-2 (Marwen permanently dead, party collapses without slot-based pressure). When DR misses: fight extends to R3-5, beholder wins via VS + frightened + LA economy.

Both outcomes feel degenerate in opposite directions — "instant loss" vs "death by thousand cuts." The surprise round itself is the core feel issue.

**MQ-EV10-A persists (12th cycle unresolved):** The hard-trigger → surprise DR is too binary. A natural 1 reduces the opening to anticlimax; a mid-roll hit (14+6=20 on the .md-prescribed Marwen) is a permanent PC death before combat formally begins.

*Recommendation (same as MQ-EV10-A from 10th cycle):* Replace the DR surprise-round action with a **Void Ray warning shot** (2 LA, DC 16 Dex, 4d10 force, half on save). This:
- Deals meaningful damage without permanent-death risk on surprise
- Signals lethality without removing a PC before they act
- Reserves DR for the first initiative round when the party can react
- If the hard-trigger target fails (VR full damage = ~22 avg), still creates urgency

Alternatively: remove the surprise round entirely for the first hard-threat exchange, and move to standard initiative. The .md already notes "second entry after dismissal = hostile on sight" — suggest the FIRST hard-threat triggers a charged approach (loud telepathic warning + initiative), not immediate DR.

---

**FI-EV12-B (confirm) — VS fear rider decisive even on low damage roll**

6d10 rolled 12 total (1,1,2,2,3,3 — extremely low, ~4% probability). All 3 PCs still failed Wis save. The frightened condition reduced Bazgar's javelin output to 0/4 hits in Rounds 2-3. The fear rider — not the psychic damage — was the decisive effect, confirming FI-149 in the beholder.md. This is positive design confirmation.

However, the VS rolled only 12 on 6d10 here (avg 33). In a real table session, a 12-damage area burst would feel underwhelming before the DM explains the fear condition. *Suggestion:* Add a DM-facing note to the beholder.md checklist item for VS: "Even on low damage rolls, VS is a fight-winner via frightened — announce 'a wave of wrongness washes over you' BEFORE damage, so players feel dread when they fail." This keeps fear as the emotional anchor regardless of damage dice.

---

**FI-EV12-C (pattern data) — VS recharge-6 fight-outcome binary**

VS recharged on d6=6 in R2. 12-cycle data:
- VS fired: 5 cycles (≥1 recharge in rounds 1-3) → all 5 were TPK R2-3
- VS did not fire: 7 cycles → fights extended to R4-6, outcomes varied

P(VS fires ≥ once in 3 rounds) = 1-(5/6)^3 = 42%. The fight outcome is essentially gated on this dice roll. When VS doesn't fire for 4+ rounds, the beholder struggles without its area-threat and LoH/smite cycles the party back.

*Recommend considering recharge 5-6 for VS (SRD standard) rather than 6-only. This raises P(fire in 3 rounds) to 70%. It makes the fight more consistently threatening — the beholder solo without VS is beatable at L5; with VS it's a near-certain TPK. The wide swing is a table problem.*

---

**FI-EV12-D (NEW) — Drain Divinity consecutive save streak**

Beholder spent 6 LA total on Drain Divinity against Marwen in R1 and R2 (3 LA each). Both times Marwen saved exactly at threshold (17 vs DC 16, CHA +1). Probability: (~30% save rate)^2 = ~9%. The 3-LA cost for each attempt means the beholder lost its full LA budget twice with no return.

The beholder.md has extensive DD targeting guidance but no **"if DD fails, adapt"** clause. Suggested addition to the DD priority section:

> "If DD fails (caster saved), do NOT retry DD on the same target next LA budget. Switch to Void Ray (2 LA) once, then retry DD on the following budget. Pressing the same save twice hands the caster free repeated save attempts; the VR creates HP pressure that makes the save more costly to fail next round."

---

**FI-EV12-E (confirm) — LoH pool unused in solo beholder config**

25 HP LoH pool. Sabriel used only 5 pts (revive Marwen R3). Beholder was downed R3 before the LoH pressure mechanic (FI-125 LA Tentacle) became relevant. 12-cycle data: LoH is consequential only in cycles where VS doesn't fire (gives Sabriel multiple turns to act). When VS fires, Sabriel's concentration/HP is compromised before she can establish a revive loop. This is not a design issue — it's the encounter working as intended. The LoH pressure notes (FI-125) are defensive guidance for the non-VS scenarios. Positive confirm.

**MQ-EV12-C (new) — DR to-hit miss rate vs design intent**

DR `to_hit_bonus: 6` → vs Marwen AC 15 = 55% hit, vs Bazgar AC 18 = 40% hit, vs Sabriel AC 19 = 35% hit. DR missed TWICE this run (surprise round nat-1, R2 roll 8). When DR misses on the surprise round, the feel is: "the most dangerous ability in the encounter whiffed." Is this the intended tension — a high-stakes gamble? Or should DR have a higher to-hit (e.g., +8 or +9) to feel reliably threatening?

*This is a design question, not a bug. The +6 might be intentional to prevent the surprise round from being a guaranteed opening kill. But MQ-EV10-A and FI-EV12-A together suggest the miss feels worse than the hit (anticlimax vs permanent death). A middle ground: +6 on DR but replace surprise-round DR with VR (per FI-EV12-A recommendation).*
