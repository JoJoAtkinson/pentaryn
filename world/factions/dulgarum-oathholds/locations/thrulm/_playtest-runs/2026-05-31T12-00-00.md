### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**MQ-SW20-A (NEW) — Downed PC in cone for UF priority check:**
In R2, STD-B had UF active and 2 standing PCs (Marwen, Sabriel) in cone — Bazgar was already downed at 10 HP entering STD-B's turn. Per .md: "if UF active, PREFER Multiattack over AR when only 2 enemies in cone." The sim incorrectly counted unconscious Bazgar as a 3rd enemy. Ruling recommendation: downed/unconscious PCs do NOT count as "enemies in cone" for UF priority calculation. Multiattack with UF advantage should have fired instead.
- Impact: if corrected, STD-B uses MA with UF advantage in R2, hits Sabriel reliably (need 3+ on adv vs AC19 ≈ 92%), deals ~12-13 avg. AR happened to down Bazgar who was at 10 HP anyway — almost identical tactical outcome, but uses UF correctly.
- Recommend: add one line to shrine-touched-derro.md under UF priority: "Unconscious/downed creatures do not count as enemies in cone for UF priority."

**FI-SW20-B (POSITIVE FEEL — OBR decisive):**
OBR hit Bazgar for 10 dmg during his R2 attack sequence, immediately after Bazgar killed STD-A. Created a "dying while killing" moment. Bazgar went 20→10 HP during his own turn via OBR counter. STD-B's AR then downed him next. The OBR → HP deficit → AR chain is a satisfying kill pattern that requires DM attention but feels earned.

**FI-SW20-C (20th-cycle CONFIRM — UF wasted on AR with 3+ in cone):**
STD-A fired AR while UF was active (3 PCs in cone → AR wins per .md). UF advantage on attack rolls was entirely wasted since AR is save-based. This is correct per the current rules, but at the table DMs may feel confused why UF was "active" for a turn that used no attack rolls. Recommendation: add a DM coaching note to .md: "When UF is active and you fire AR anyway (3+ in cone), UF's advantage is wasted — narrate the STD's instability as erratic misfires, not precision, to avoid player confusion."

**FI-SW20-D (POSITIVE FEEL — Altar zone fire suppression felt):**
Marwen's Scorching Ray (L2) hit for only 12 fire total. Sabriel's Divine Smite did only 8 radiant. Both attackers noticed. The altar zone suppression shaped Marwen's slot decision and reduced Sabriel's burst potential. This is the encounter's signature mechanic working correctly — moving STDs away from the altar to re-expose vulnerability is a real tactical choice.

**FI-SW20-E (POSITIVE FEEL — UF advantage decisive on MA):**
In R3, STD-B used Multiattack with UF advantage correctly (AR was spent). UF pushed a roll from d20=12+4=16 (miss vs AC19) to d20=18+4=22 (hit). Without UF, both attacks would have missed Sabriel. UF-on-Multiattack is working exactly as designed.

**FI-SW20-F (20th-cycle CONFIRM — Sabriel Dex save sheltered from AR):**
Sabriel's Dex +5 against DC14 means she passes AR on d20 ≥ 9 (55% raw, 85% effective). She took only 4 half-dmg per AR cone. This pattern continues from FI-SW19-D — Sabriel is functionally safe from AR, which means the shrine-touched's primary AoE threatens Bazgar/Marwen but not the divine martial. Recommend adding a DM coaching note that targeting the cone toward Marwen+Sabriel (not Bazgar+Marwen) may improve AR impact — though Bazgar's Dex +0 makes him the ideal cone anchor.
