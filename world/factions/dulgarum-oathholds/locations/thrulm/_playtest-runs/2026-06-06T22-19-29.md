### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS
- **FI-SCT-35-A (POSITIVE)**: Stagger rule FI-SCT-34-A confirmed working — lowest-init SC fires Barrage R1, other two multiattack+CW. SC-A's CW on SC-B fires and collects same round (SC-B lands 2 hits with advantage). This is the clearest shardcaller-team R1 performance in 35 cycles: focus-fire downs Marwen by end of R1 via 37 combined damage, and stagger preserved Barrage for R2.

- **FI-SCT-35-B**: Fireball save razor-thin (both SCs rolled exactly 14 vs DC15). One-point swing changes outcome significantly: a save of 15+ would leave each SC at ~11HP instead of 0/3HP. The post-Fireball burst-down pattern is init-sensitive and luck-sensitive. At median Fireball damage (28), both SCs survive with 6HP each on a save; Bazgar's Action Surge would still finish them. Structural pattern stable.

- **FI-SCT-35-C (MQ-12 paradox confirmed)**: SC-B CW'd SC-A (already-acted) — advantage goes to R2, SC-A dies in R1 before collecting. CW charge wasted per MQ-12 prediction. In 3-SC teams, the "CW each other" stagger intent creates a structural paradox: the lower-init non-barrage SC cannot CW the higher-init SC in a same-round useful way. The only valid CW spend for the lower-init non-barrage SC is next round. Fixed in .md (FIX-SCT-35-A).

- **FI-SCT-35-D**: SC-C's 3 CW charges entirely unused at death (3/3). With lowest initiative: SC-C fires Barrage R1 (CW inapplicable), TR monopolizes bonus R2 (melee threat), dead R3 before acting. In 35 cycles of shardcaller-team, the lowest-init SC's CW charges are structurally inaccessible. This is a formation weakness, not a bug — DM coaching note may help (SC-C should consider pre-combat positioning to fire CW on a high-init ally before initiative is rolled, or accept that its CW budget will be spent in R2+ only if it survives).
