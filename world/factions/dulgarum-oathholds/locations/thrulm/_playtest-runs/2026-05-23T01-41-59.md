## Findings

- **`shrine-touched-derro / ancient_resonance` — missing +1d4 psychic on fail**: The `.md` tactics (line "2+ enemies in 15-ft cone: Ancient Resonance... +1d4 psychic on fail") describe a secondary psychic damage rider that was absent from the DB spec. Updated the narration to include a bracketed DM reminder `[DM: each creature that FAILS the save also takes 1d4 psychic from shrine feedback — roll separately per failer.]` and added a `notes` field to the save dict for future roller improvements. DB validated OK (0 errors).

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**1. Shard-Barrage fired twice in 3 rounds (rounds 1 and 2)**
- Recharge 5+ means ~33% chance per round, but here it rerolled as 6 in round 2, so it fired consecutively. Two barrages across 3 rounds (6d6 ≈ 21 average piercing, AOE on all three PCs) was the dominant damage source of the encounter — the two Deep Watch melee derro dealt far less. At level 5 with clustered PCs, the barrage at recharge 5 may trigger too often for a threshold-patrol slice. The Shardcaller effectively outperformed both DW derro combined.
- **Recommendation:** consider recharge 6 for Shard-Barrage in the context of this slice (where the party IS clustered), OR flag as intentional — the Shardcaller IS meant to be the tactical threat here.

**2. Call Weakness wasted on a nearly-dead ally (round 2)**
- The shardcaller spent its second Call Weakness charge on DW #2 (4/27 HP, already on a disengage path), which provided no combat value — DW #2 missed both attacks. The `.md` tactics say "save the 3 uses for the fight's most dangerous round" but don't specify NOT to spend on an ally below ~5 HP or in retreat mode.
- **Recommendation:** add to tactics: "Do not use Call Weakness on an ally below 5 HP or who has already disengaged."

**3. Deep Watch below-half retreat ambiguity (crossbow vs continue melee)**
- DW #2 reached ≤13 HP in round 2, triggered Cunning Action disengage, but continued making melee attacks in rounds 2 and 3. The tactics say "Disengage, fall back" but don't clarify that a retreated derro should switch to its crossbow. At the table, a DM might let DW #2 keep slapping at melee range even after "falling back," which contradicts the tactical intent. 
- **Recommendation:** add to Deep Watch tactics: "Once below half HP and disengaged, switch to **Light Crossbow** from cover — don't re-enter melee."

**4. Pack Tactics Voice passive rarely fires meaningfully in this slice**
- The passive triggers when the shardcaller hits (enemy has disadvantage on its NEXT save). In the threshold patrol slice, there are no follow-up saves called by other NPCs in the same round, so the passive's effect consistently goes unrealized. The passive is well-designed for encounters with beholder lair actions or shrine-touched derro (who call saves frequently), but in a pure DW + Shardcaller lineup, it does nothing.
- **Log for human**: this is expected — the passive is designed for multi-NPC combos. Not a bug; just context for slice design.

**5. Marwen as the sole focus target of all NPCs**
- All three NPCs targeted Marwen (caster priority doctrine). She reached 8/32 HP at her lowest. If either DW had rolled better in round 1 (they both missed), she could have gone down before the party had a chance to heal her. The encounter tests "kill the wizard" strategy competently. No fix needed — this is the intended feel.

### Mechanical questions

**1. Tactical Drilling (passive) not visible in runner output**
- The Deep Watch `.md` grants advantage on melee attacks when an ally is within 5 ft of the target. This passive is entirely absent from the DB (correct, since it's passive) and doesn't appear in the roller's multiattack output. A DM running at-table must remember to apply it from the `.md`. In rounds 1–2 of this run, Tactical Drilling gave DW advantage — if the DM forgets it, DW #1's round-1 attack (raw 13 vs AC 15) would miss instead of... wait, even with advantage it missed in R1. But it DID hit in R2 (advantage roll of 22). So Tactical Drilling meaningfully changed outcomes here.
- **Recommendation:** the runner could call this out if both DW NPCs are alive (a check the harness can't currently do, but a note in the `.md` Start-of-Turn Checklist already covers it). Current state is acceptable — just remind DMs to read the checklist.

**2. Sandbox random API block — infrastructure finding**
- In Anthropic's cloud sandbox, both the quantum numbers API and random.org are blocked (HTTP 403). The roller's disk cache was pre-seeded with local `random.Random(42)` numbers to work around this. The 4096 pre-seeded numbers were consumed across Phase A (27 actions × ~4 numbers each) and Phase B playtest. For longer fires, the cache may exhaust.
- **Recommendation:** add a local-random fallback path to `dnd_roller.py` (e.g., env var `DND_ROLLER_OFFLINE=1` → use `random.Random` seeded by timestamp). Outside blast radius (scripts/), log for human.
