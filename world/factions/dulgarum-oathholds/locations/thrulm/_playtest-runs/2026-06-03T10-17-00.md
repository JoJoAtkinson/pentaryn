### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SW25-A — Very low-attrition R2 victory (25th shrine-wedge cycle)**
Bazgar 34/49, Marwen 21/32, Sabriel 44/44 untouched. This is the lowest-attrition SW run recorded (compare SW24: Marwen DOWN; SW22: Bazgar+Sabriel DOWN; SW21: near-TPK). Root cause: Bazgar got Action Surge immediately, deleting STD-1 before STD-2 could use its stagger-held AR. Marwen then Firebolted STD-2 to 1 HP, and Sabriel finished. The key variable is initiative order — when Bazgar acts before Marwen/Sabriel (and STDs act first), there's a window where Action Surge + Fireball clears both STDs in R1. When Bazgar acts *after* both STDs, AR chains R1+R2 and the fight is genuinely punishing. The 2-STD slice range spans "easy 2R win" to "near-TPK 3R fight" purely based on initiative. This is high variance, but both extremes feel narratively coherent. No spec change recommended; log for DM awareness.

**FI-SW25-B — Altar zone suppression 25th-cycle confirm (DD-SW23-A)**
Fireball 30 fire without vulnerability still decisive. The altar zone suppression had zero impact on outcome. The mechanic is correct per design, but in 25 cycles only a small number have shown the suppression making a meaningful difference (when both STDs fail saves AND the vulnerability would have doubled damage AND the damage would have changed the outcome). Running pattern: Fireball one-shots or near-one-shots regardless of vulnerability state. DESIGN DECISION: the altar zone pull-them-away puzzle is invisible to players at this damage threshold. Recommend adding a DM coaching line to encounters.md noting when to proactively signal the zone (e.g., when the smite's radiant light dims on impact).

**FI-SW25-C — Unstable Form 25th-cycle confirm: never activates in R2-win outcomes**
Both STDs died before completing a second turn. UF requires surviving a round of 10+ damage, then getting a next turn. In R2-win cycles (increasingly common with Fireball + Action Surge), UF has zero opportunity to fire. It activates meaningfully only in R3+ fights (SW21, SW22, SW24). The ability is well-designed but contingent on fight duration. No action needed — it fires when conditions are right and the earlier cycles confirm it works correctly.

**FI-SW25-D — OBR self-finishing-blow (flavor note)**
STD-2 died to its own OBR reaction (4 psychic self-damage, 1 HP remaining after Fireball). This is striking at the table — the shrine destroyed its own servant in the act of retaliation. Both OBR attacks missed this cycle (Bazgar AC18, Sabriel AC19 vs +4 attack). At +4, OBR hits Bazgar on 14+ (35% chance), Sabriel on 15+ (30% chance) — making it mostly a cosmetic/flavor trigger at these ACs. The self-damage (1d4 avg 2.5) is the more mechanically reliable component and frequently contributes to STD death when they're at low HP.

**FI-SW25-E — Stagger rule correct, zero downstream impact this cycle**
Stagger fired correctly (STD-2 held AR in R1). STD-2 died in R1 before ever getting to use the held AR. This is the expected failure mode of the stagger rule in fast-win cycles — the hold is correct but the STD dies before getting to use the saved charge. The stagger rule shines in R3+ fights (SW21, SW24 show this clearly). No change needed.
