### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP-0-A — DW2 restrained but sim allowed melee charge (harness gap, not spec bug):**
The simulation let DW2 "charge" to melee after being restrained by Web (speed 0). In actual play DW2 should have been pinned at 20 ft and forced to fire its Light Crossbow at disadvantage (ranged attack, no movement required) rather than closing to hand-axe range. The NPC spec and DB are correct (no fix needed). The harness does not enforce movement restrictions from conditions — this is a known sim limitation. **DW2's actual best action while restrained:** crossbow at disadvantage, single shot. Impact: one fewer axe hit on Marwen in R1; Marwen survives at ~24 HP instead of 29 HP — outcome unchanged. Log for human: harness should track speed=0 for restrained.

**FI-TP-0-B — Threshold Patrol resolves in 2 rounds vs. level-5 party with concentration control:**
Web (DC 12 Dex) caught 2/3 NPCs in R1, completely nullifying SC movement and granting Sabriel advantage for the R2 sweep. Party spent modest resources (1 spell slot, 2 smites) and lost only ~25% HP aggregate. As a "warm-up" encounter (pre-beholder staging), this difficulty level is likely intended — but the encounter is near-trivial if the party has AoE control. The formation cannot survive concentration crowd control at level 5. This is a **design flag, not a bug**: if the intent is to drain party resources before the beholder, the Threshold Patrol may need either a higher-initiative DW (to threaten Marwen's concentration) or a 3rd DW to diversify target spread. Recommend: DM run this encounter without Web available (or with PCs entering surprised) for higher pressure.

**FI-TP-0-C — DW1 never attacked (died R1 before its initiative):**
DW1 (init 8) acted last among DWs, after Sabriel (init 9) double-hit + smited it to death. The Deep Watch's tactical identity (formation fighting, Tactical Drilling advantage) never emerged because DW1 was one-shotted. Over many cycles, DW1's Tactical Drilling will never fire if Sabriel or Bazgar act first. Consider bumping DW1 initiative via a static bonus (+4 instead of +2) or giving DWs a "defensive readiness" trait that grants +2 initiative at combat start. **Design decision for human.**

**FI-TP-0-D — CW on already-acted DW2 (R1): charge spent correctly per spec but lost:**
SC spent 1/3 CW charges on DW2 after DW2 had already acted in R1 (HP 27 >20 per MQ-12 exception). DW2 was eliminated before using the advantage in R2. This is spec-correct per the MQ-12 guard (spend if >20 HP), but the outcome shows the >20 HP threshold doesn't protect against "alive but killed before your turn in R2." Over multiple cycles this pattern (lone surviving DW with CW advantage dying before it acts) may account for CW charges feeling wasted in this formation. **Feel flag for human:** consider tightening MQ-12 to "alive ally with >20 HP whose turn comes BEFORE an anticipated party heavy-hitter," not just >20 HP threshold.

**FI-TP-0-E — SC Barrage while restrained is rules-correct (feel fine):**
SC fired Shard Barrage from restrained position. Shard Barrage is a DC-save area (no attack roll), so restrained condition doesn't impose disadvantage on the attack. Rules-correct. The narrative ("unleashes a spitting fan of obsidian") reads fine for a stationary trapped creature. No fix needed.

### Mechanical questions
- **Restrained + Barrage:** Confirmed rules-correct: restrained imposes disadvantage only on attack rolls (not saving-throw triggers). SC Barrage (DC 13 Dex save) unaffected. No ambiguity.
- **Web concentration vs. SC at 50 ft:** Web has 30 ft radius (centered in corridor). At 50 ft, SC is at the edge — DM should confirm the center placement. In this sim assumed SC within range; if DM centers Web closer to DWs, SC may be outside the area. Note for live play.
