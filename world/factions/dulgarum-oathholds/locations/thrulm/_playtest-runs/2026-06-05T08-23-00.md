### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-FC31-A POSITIVE — FIRST PARTY VICTORY FOR SLICE #4** in 31 cycles. Cause: DR missed all 3 attempts (P ≈ 4.3%) + VS never recharged (0/4 attempts). When the beholder's two "insta-win" abilities both underperform simultaneously, the party can win in a 4-round attrition fight. This is the correct dramatic shape — the fight SHOULD be possible to win if both burst abilities misfire, but only barely.

- **FI-FC31-B VS recharge structural tension** — 0/4 VS recharge attempts across 4 rounds (recharge 6-only = 16.7%/round). VS absence completely changes fight identity. VS absent: 4-round grind fight where the party wins narrowly. VS present R1-R2: expected TPK R2-R3 (confirmed by prior cycles). The gap between "VS fires" and "VS doesn't fire" is the biggest single variance in this encounter. *No spec change recommended* — the extreme variance is a feature, not a bug: the party needs luck AND good play to survive.

- **FI-FC31-C DR 0/3 miss (3 consecutive)** — DR miss-rate vs AC 15 at +6 is 45% per shot. Three consecutive misses is 4.3% probability. Combined with VS 0/4, this was the luckiest possible outcome for the party short of beholder rolling 1s. This confirms FI-FC30-A (DR-R1-eliminates-caster-before-orient is the *normal* case) — the 31st cycle shows the anomaly clearly.

- **FI-FC31-D Marwen killed by multiattack not DR** — Unusual kill vector. Two Tentacle Lashes at +7 vs AC 15 (65% each) landed simultaneously in R3 and killed Marwen outright. The multiattack is nearly as lethal as DR against the wizard — it just takes two turns instead of one. DM coaching: target the wizard with multiattack in the same round DR is available even if DR is on cooldown.

- **FI-FC31-E LoH revival arc positive** — Bazgar died R2, Sabriel used LoH bonus to revive R3, beholder responded with Tentacle LA to pressure Sabriel. Classic push-pull. LoH 25 exhausted as a result, removing all healing from the party. Then Bazgar died again? No — Bazgar survived R3 and R4. LoH was the decisive resource that kept the fight alive.

- **FI-FC31-F Scorching Ray 0/6 total across R2+R3** — Marwen missed all six SR rays at +7 vs AC 17 (55% hit chance per ray). P(0/6 hits) = 0.45^6 ≈ 0.8%. The coldest dice of the fight on Marwen's key damage turns. *No spec change* — this is variance; Fireball (area save) is more reliable than SR (6 attack rolls).

- **SIM-FC31-A Unstable Form not tracked** — STD-A took 35 fire from Fireball (≥10 damage threshold for UF). Should have had advantage on attacks until end of its next turn. This was not applied in the sim. Given STD-A went 0/2 and 0/4 hits anyway, no outcome impact — but note for future sim harness.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-FC31-A Beholder DR + full LA budget same round**: The beholder fired DR as its main action in R1/R2 and had 3 full LA remaining that same round. Is this correct? Yes — DR replaces the main action, LAs are separate. So a DR + DD + VR + Tentacle round is mechanically possible. In R1, the beholder used LA (DD on Sabriel after THR-4's turn), and later couldn't fire LA again because the round continued. Confirm at table: LA reset at the start of the beholder's next turn, not on hit/kill.

- **MQ-FC31-B Grapple-ends-on-Marwen-death**: When the second Tentacle Lash killed Marwen (reducing her to 0), should the Maw still fire? Per MQ-FC14-A, unconscious ends grappled. Marwen died on TL2 — Maw was already part of the multiattack sequence and technically fired simultaneously. The sim skipped it (dead target, maw didn't fire). This is correct per MQ-FC14-A — grapple ends on 0 HP, Maw loses advantage and has no valid primary target at that moment. Log this as correct behavior, confirmed.

- **MQ-FC31-C Revivify viability post-fight**: Marwen is dead but NOT disintegrated. Her body is intact. Revivify (L3 spell) could bring her back if cast within 1 minute. Sabriel has no L3 slots (drained by DD and used on smite). Party has no L3 access. Unless they have a scroll, Marwen is dead until a long rest + higher-level raise. This is the correct "costly victory" outcome — beholder dies but leaves permanent damage.
