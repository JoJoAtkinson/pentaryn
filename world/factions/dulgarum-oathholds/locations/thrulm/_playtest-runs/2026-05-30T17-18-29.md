## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed
- none

### Simulation error (not a DB bug)
- **SIM-EV7-A:** DR applied as DC16 Dex save throughout. The DB spec is `single_attack` with
  `to_hit_bonus: 6`. At the table, DR is an attack roll vs AC. Marwen (AC15) takes a hit on
  to-hit ≥15; the R0 roll of 23 would guarantee a hit and 50-force disintegration. Sim outcome
  approximately correct (Marwen dies early either way) but mechanics were wrong. No DB fix needed.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-EV7-A: DR in the surprise round at level 5 = permanent PC loss.** Even with correct
  attack-roll mechanics (23 vs AC15), Marwen's death in the surprise round is permanent (no True
  Resurrection at level 5). The beholder.md has a "DM discretion to soften" note (FI-NEW-FC4-LOH)
  but no explicit guidance on *whether* DR should be held in solo-negotiation-gone-wrong scenarios.
  Recommendation: add a tactics note — "HOLD DR when transitioning from active negotiation to
  combat (solo configuration). Fire Void Ray (legendary, 2 LA) as the opening shot instead;
  reserve DR for R1 of sustained combat. A negotiation-trigger surprise-round disintegration
  creates permanent PC loss from a roleplay scene, which may undermine table investment."

- **FI-EV7-B: Beholder Dex +8 neutralises Fireball.** With Dex +8, the beholder passes DC14–16
  saves on a roll of 6–8+. Marwen spent her only L3 slot for an expected ~15 avg fire (half on
  save). Even at DC17 (Int 18), the beholder passes on d20+8 ≥17, i.e. rolls ≥9 (60%). Fireball's
  identity as "Wizard's big nova" is particularly hollow here. Not a balance fix (CR13 is supposed
  to be overwhelming) but worth noting for session-prep: DM should coach the player pre-session
  that force/psychic damage (Disintegrate, Mind Spike) outperforms fire/cold against this creature.

- **FI-EV7-C: Antireality negates every declared smite.** Sabriel 0/2 smites landed (both
  blocked). Antireality triggers on any attack within AC+2 (≤19) with estimated damage ≥10. A
  declared L2 smite always clears the damage threshold. The beholder only has ONE Antireality per
  round — if Sabriel uses Action Surge or multi-attacks in a round, the second pass is unblocked.
  Tactics note in .md does not mention this. Recommend adding: "Antireality can block only ONE
  attack per turn — if the attacker has Action Surge or Extra Attack declared as a second wave,
  the follow-up smite lands unreacted."

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-EV7-A: Hard trigger after successful persuasion — should negotiation success state carry
  weight?** The beholder was open to dealing (persuasion pass R2) when Marwen triggered the hard
  trigger (R3). FI-NEW-EV5-B says hard trigger = immediate DR surprise round. But after a PASS,
  the beholder's psychology would be more "betrayed + aggressive" than "threat + pre-emptive."
  Recommendation: when hard trigger follows a persuasion PASS, replace the DR surprise with a
  "Void Ray (2 LA) warning shot + initiative roll immediately." Only trigger DR disintegration if
  the party acts after the warning. This preserves the hard-trigger consequence while giving the
  table one beat to react to the betrayal.

- **MQ-EV7-B: DR spec is `single_attack` (vs-AC) — confirm this is intentional, not SRD-save.**
  The DB stores DR as `single_attack`, `to_hit_bonus: 6`. The SRD 2024 beholder uses DC 16 Dex
  save. The MM beholder uses a +7 ranged spell attack. The .md does not specify which mechanic
  is authoritative. The to_hit_bonus of 6 is lower than the MM (+7–+9 range for CR13). Recommend:
  (a) confirm attack vs save is intentional; (b) if attack, verify to_hit_bonus is correct for
  this homebrew beholder's proficiency/ability-mod breakdown; (c) add a one-line note to the .md
  tactics section clarifying the mechanic so DMs don't have to check the DB during play.

- **MQ-EV7-C: VS targeting vs unconscious cluster.** In R3, Void Scream centered on the beholder
  hit both Sabriel (standing) and Bazgar (already at 0 HP). The tactics note says to skip
  `unstable_ground` on unconscious targets; there is no equivalent note for VS. Since VS is
  self-centered (30-ft radius), the beholder can't easily exclude a downed PC from the blast. But
  the beholder SHOULD consider whether descending within VS range also exposes it to melee
  retaliation (MQ-64/shrine_drift note covers this). The R3 VS was mechanically correct (VS
  centered on beholder, Bazgar happened to be nearby) but wasted half its psychic damage on an
  already-down target. No rule change needed; flag as a "be aware" for DMs running this scene.
