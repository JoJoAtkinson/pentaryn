### Harness errors (sim bugs, not NPC bugs)
- **MQ-SRR18-A** — Simulation applied d12+3 damage for rager Multiattack and Berserk; DB output shows d12+2 (`+2` modifier in printed damage lines). All damage in this transcript is overestimated by ~1 per hit (7 hits total → ~7 HP overestimate per side). Correcting would likely extend the fight by ~1 round; outcome TPK unchanged. Fix: update sim harness damage constant to d12+2. Not an NPC bug.
- **MQ-SRR18-B** — Misty Step in R8: sim did not deduct Rager-C's movement to close the 30 ft gap before attacking. If RC had already used its move, it could not reach Marwen. In practice RC had not moved this turn (waited for Marwen to emerge), so the attack was legal — marginal edge case; outcome unchanged.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS
- **FI-SRR18-C: Rager-A 3× consecutive Berserk (R1/R2/R3)** — Rager-A recharged Berserk on R2 (d6=6) and R3 (d6=5), firing 3 consecutive Berserks while at 52/40/11/5 HP. The 1/3 recharge rate is statistically correct but three consecutive berserks at near-death HP is jarring — a bloodied, dying creature at 5 HP spinning through the party three times feels more like a rules exploit than a dying threat. Previous cycles didn't exhibit this streak. **Logging as pattern data only (17th cycle is first triple-Berserk observation); not recommending a rule change without more cycles.** Design Q: should Berserk be blocked when rager is below 15 HP (same as "Below 15 HP" section already says it doesn't retreat, but doesn't restrict Berserk)?
- **FI-SRR18-D: Berserk 3-line output cap (18th-cycle pattern confirm)** — DB Berserk output always prints 3 attack lines. DM must manually discard excess lines. This is now confirmed as a consistent source of cognitive overhead in every solo-rager-rush cycle. The tactics note explains it, but the narration itself gives no cue. Suggestion: add a parenthetical to the Berserk `output` narration: *"(DM: apply only as many lines as there are enemies in reach)"*. This is a minor DB-narration improvement, not a balance change — logging as a **DESIGN DECISION** for human review.
- **FI-31 18th-cycle confirm** — FI-31 rager-only corollary (split Taunt 1:1 per PC) confirmed correct and clean. Marwen's Taunt failure was mechanically irrelevant (Fireball save-based); this is the documented limitation. No new information, continuing pattern data.
- **FI-SRR18-E: Solo-rager-rush TPK on unlucky Fireball (18th-cycle first TPK)** — All 17 prior cycles were VICTORY (R3–R6). This cycle is the first TPK. The differentiating factor: Fireball penetration was poor (2 of 3 ragers saved, only RB took full 22 damage). Without a successful Fireball, 3 ragers at 30–38 HP by R2 is unmanageable for L5. The encounter outcome for solo-rager-rush is near-entirely Fireball-dependent. This is a structural feel issue: a 3-PC party against 3× CR2 is tuned to barely lose without a 3rd-level AOE. If Fireball fires cleanly (all fail), VICTORY in R3–4; if ragers save, TPK by R5–8. The fight has low decision depth — it's an artillery check. **Logging as DESIGN DECISION for context; not recommending a change (the slice is a pressure-test, not a balanced fight).**
- **Madness Endurance (+1 to hit DM-applied)** — Active every turn from R2 on (both RA and RC were being hit each round). The bonus never bridged a miss→hit on recorded dice. Cosmetically invisible in 18 cycles. Existing feel note; continuing pattern.
