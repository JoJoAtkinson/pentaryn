### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC16-A (PATTERN CONFIRM):** DR + void_eruption combo R1 eliminates Marwen before her first turn. Void_eruption fires at init 20 reducing Marwen to 26 HP, then DR at +6 vs AC 15 hits (~60% chance) dealing 10d8 force, permanently disintegrating her. This is the dominant R1 pattern in final-confrontation: Marwen permanently dead before acting, no heroic moment, no use of her L3 spell slot. 16th consecutive FC run where Marwen either dies or is severely compromised R1. When DR *misses* (prev cycles: 40% miss rate), the fight extends to R3-R4 but is still a TPK. When DR *hits*, it's R2-R3 TPK. The DR R1 question is not "if" the beholder uses it but purely RNG (hit/miss). **Not flagged for auto-fix — intended CR13 overwhelm behavior. Logged as pattern confirmation.**

**FI-FC16-B (SIMULATION NOTE):** Dual-rolling divergence. The DB dispatch output for void_eruption shows "4 force" (its own d10 rolls: 3+1=4) while the sim applies 11/6/8 force per PC from separate Python random rolls. These numbers appear in the transcript simultaneously and would confuse a human DM reading the log. The dispatch output is the authoritative at-table number; sim rolls are only for HP tracking. **Not a spec bug. Testbot hygiene note — future runs should suppress DB dice output or make clear it is non-authoritative when sim applies its own rolls.**

**FI-FC16-C (PATTERN CONFIRM):** Void Scream R2 is the decisive TPK vector (16th FC cycle). When VS recharges on or before R2, it catches both surviving melee PCs at critical HP (lair erosion + prior damage ~60% of their max) and fails virtually all DC 16 Wis saves (near-shrine disadvantage, +1-2 Wis mods = ~65-75% fail rate per PC). This run: Bazgar 9 HP + VS 27 psychic, Sabriel 14 HP + VS 33 psychic, both unconscious simultaneously. The fight consistently ends within 2-3 rounds regardless of DR outcome. **DESIGN DECISION: Should VS recharge be 6-only (more variance) or is 2-3 round TPK the intended feel for this slice?** The current spec already says recharge 6 only — but VS recharged naturally this run on the beholder's R2 start-of-turn check.

**MQ-FC16-A (MECHANICAL QUESTION):** Drain Divinity temp HP treatment. The beholder was at 104/110 HP when it gained +6 temp HP from DD (Sabriel L3 loss). In D&D 5e, temp HP is a *separate buffer*, not added to current HP — it can exceed your max HP as a separate pool. The simulation incorrectly capped at max HP. The DB spec correctly says "gains temporary HP." **Question for human:** Does the PySide6 GUI track beholder temp HP as a separate pool distinct from regular HP? If not, the beholder's DD recovery in high-HP states is being silently lost. Recommend verifying the GUI's temp HP implementation.

**MQ-FC16-B (CONFIRM):** Drain Divinity spending all 3 LA in R1 after Sabriel's turn left zero LA for the remainder of R1 (Bazgar's turn, thrall turns). This is correct per the tactics doc ("Drain Divinity on the FIRST PC's end-of-turn if a slot-holder with L2+ is within 30 ft"). The tactics explicitly prioritize DD over splitting LAs. Result: no LAs fired in R1 after Sabriel's turn. In R2, Bazgar and Sabriel were both unconscious before their turns, so LA budget was never used in R2. **This run: LA budget effectively wasted in R2** (VS killed everyone before LAs could fire after any PC turn). Logged as operational note, not a spec issue.

**FI-FC16-D (FEEL NOTE, NEW):** STDs ended at full HP (45/45) in a 3-round encounter. Neither STD took any damage. STD-2 fired ancient_resonance R1 (solid 11 combined damage to Bazgar+Sabriel), but then both STDs were irrelevant after VS cleared the party R2. The "2× shrine-touched-derro" in this slice provide meaningful R1 pressure but are scenery by R2. At 45 HP each, they function as a latent threat the party never has time to address. Consider: in a 2-3 round TPK, are the STDs contributing meaningfully to the feel of the encounter or are they redundant complexity?

**FI-FC16-E (THRALL NOTE):** 4 thralls scored 1 hit (3 slashing) in R1, zero hits in R2 (no valid targets). +1 to-hit vs AC 18+ is ~20% hit rate, 1d4 avg 2.5 damage — even on hits. Functionally: thralls provide the beholder's `compel_thrall` bonus action narrative and the `manifest_thralls` lair action economy, but deal no meaningful damage. Slice description says "4× thrall-derro" — they're atmosphere, not threat. This is the intended design per `thrall-derro.md` ("no tactical sense, weak multiattack"). Confirmed as intentional.
