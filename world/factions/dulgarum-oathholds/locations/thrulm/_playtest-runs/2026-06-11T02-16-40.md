### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-BEL3-6-A — Thrall high-initiative (T1=19, T2=18) adds no encounter shape:** Two thralls acting before the beholder on inits 19/18 should create early pressure, but in practice they dealt only 4+3 = 7 damage to Marwen before the beholder's R1 multiattack one-shot Sabriel (42 piercing). Thrall "opening" contribution is cosmetic at this party's HP pool and AC spread. The beholder's R1 dominates regardless of thrall position. Consider: if thrall inits are HIGH, they get wasted on chip damage before the beholder lands grapple+Maw. If thrall inits are LOW, they clean up after the beholder. Neither scenario creates a 3-way tactical dynamic — thralls are always secondary. 6th-BEL3-consec confirm.

- **FI-BEL3-6-B — Void Scream never fires in BEL3 (6th-consec, recharge 6 probability note):** VS recharged d6=5/5/2 across 3 rounds. This is the 6th consecutive BEL3 cycle where VS has not fired. Recharge 6 = 16.7% per roll; in 3 rounds the probability of at least one recharge is ~43%. Across 6 cycles with roughly 3 rounds each (~18 recharge rolls), the probability of ZERO reccharges is (5/6)^18 ≈ 3.8%. We have hit that 3.8% tail. The limited slice ends before VS can contribute. This is not a bug — it's variance — but it means VS's design hasn't been tested in the limited slice. Recommend: either extend BEL3 scenarios to ~6 rounds (unlikely with these init spreads leading to fast kills) or lower VS recharge to 5-6 to see it express more often.

- **FI-BEL3-6-C — Maw auto-crit one-shots Sabriel R1 (4th BEL3 confirm; structural):** TL2 hitting AC 19 requires 12+ (50%). When it lands, Maw auto-crit fires (8d8+3+1, avg ~40.5 damage vs 44 HP Sabriel). Chance of one-shot given TL2 hit: P(8d8+4 ≥ 44) ≈ 73%. Combined probability: ~37% per beholder turn. In 6 BEL3 runs with TL2 fired each R1, this has happened 4/6 times (~67% observed). The one-shot is dramatic but removes the divine martial before she acts, consistently — meaning her smites and heals are never expressed. The fight becomes a 2-PC fight every time. The kill feels powerful but repetitive. This is primarily a Sabriel-feels-irrelevant issue in BEL3.

- **FI-BEL3-6-D — DD fires on downed Sabriel's death-save turn (protocol works; feel question):** The R1-LA-hold protocol correctly waited until after Sabriel's death-save turn to fire DD. DD succeeded (Cha save 11 < DC 16, L2 slot stripped). Mechanically valid: spell slots persist through unconsciousness; stripping one prevents smites/heals if Sabriel revives. **Feel tension:** In BEL3, Bazgar is the last PC standing; no one can revive Sabriel. The stripped slot is never relevant to fight outcome. DD firing on a downed target has been correct in 4/6 BEL3 cycles where Sabriel goes down R1. The slot attrition value is real if she wakes; here it was cosmetic. Consider: DD window in BEL3 is mostly exercising the protocol rather than changing the fight. Positive — the protocol is executing correctly.

- **FI-BEL3-6-E — Beholder at 76/110 HP at TPK (35% damaged, consistent):** Party landed 38 total damage (all Marwen; Bazgar 4× miss, Sabriel 0 damage before downed). Pattern across BEL3 runs: 33-50 HP damage to beholder (70-85% HP remaining). Party damage ceiling ≈ Fireball (avg 32 this run) + limited Scorching Ray hits. Confirms overwhelming-odds design intent. The party never has a path to victory; Marwen's Fireball is the ONLY damage source that lands reliably. Bazgar's melee role is entirely negated by grapple (speed 0, can't close) + two missed attacks. This is the known structural problem of non-magical melee vs a DR 18+AC beholder — Bazgar needs magical or sanctified weapons to matter.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-BEL3-6-A — Void Ray dispatch "each creature in 120-ft range" text (recurring MQ-EV7-36-A / MQ-BEL3-3-B):** The Void Ray dispatch output still reads "each creature in 120-ft range rolls DC 16 Dex save." Void Ray is a single-target ability. FIX-EV7-36-A addressed the DB spec grammar ("area" field → "120-ft range"), but the narration in the dispatch output still implies AoE. At the table this would be confusing — a new DM might interpret it as "all creatures in 120 ft." No auto-fix applied (would require editing the action spec narration field). Recommend: add a clarifying parenthetical to the dispatch narration: "(one creature within 120 ft)". Tag for human review.

- **MQ-BEL3-6-B — Beholder multiattack dispatch consolidates 3 attacks into one output block (structural):** The multiattack dispatch shows TL1 + TL2 + Maw dice in a single consolidated block with combined to-hit and damage values. But the DM must split targets manually: TL1 → one PC, TL2 → different PC, Maw → grappled PC. The dispatch output doesn't know which numbers map to which target. At the table, this creates a read problem — the DM must mentally separate the three attack lines from one combined output. No fix available without structural changes to the runner.

- **MQ-BEL3-6-C — Void-Feeding +1 absent from DB spec (recurring FI-EV7-51-A, 96th-consec):** The beholder's Void-Feeding trait (+1 attack/damage within shrine chamber) is manually applied in every simulation run. The DB spec for each relevant action (tentacle_lash, maw, void_ray, disintegration_ray, void_scream) does not encode this +1 modifier. A DM running from the runner alone would miss this bonus consistently. Fix requires updating each action's to_hit_bonus and damage_modifier by +1 in the DB — non-trivial, and the +1 is conditional on location (only active in the shrine chamber). Recommend: add a "combat trait reminders" block to the combat cheat sheet rather than baking into DB specs (location-conditional bonuses don't fit the spec schema cleanly).
