### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP21-A — CW 0/3 used: 21st-cycle structural confirm (severity: DESIGN)**
SC-1 rolled initiative 3 (dead last). Both DWs acted at init 22 and 21 before SC's turn at init 3 — the "already-acted-ally guard" (MQ-12) fires, blocking CW. Independently, SC-1 is 50 ft from the DWs, exceeding CW's 30 ft range. This is the 21st threshold-patrol cycle with CW unused. The double block (range + already-acted) makes CW structurally inaccessible in this formation regardless of die rolls. The .md already documents both guards. No spec bug. **Design recommendation (human review):** either (a) position SC-1 closer to DW pair in threshold-patrol narration (20–25 ft behind, not 50 ft) to bring CW into range, or (b) accept CW as "off in patrol, on in shrine/rager combos." Option (a) also exposes SC to Fireball splash — may be intentional tension.

**FI-TP21-B — 2× Fireball decisive: 21st-cycle confirm (severity: DESIGN)**
Marwen burns both L3 slots to resolve a 3-NPC patrol (CR 1/8 + 1/8 + 2 = ~CR 2.5 encounter weight). First Fireball does the setup (all NPCs below 30 HP), second finishes. Fight resolves in 2 rounds. This is expected given the CR delta, but Marwen's resource expenditure is disproportionately high. **Design note (20+ cycle confirm):** this patrol appears designed as a resource-drain encounter; current outcome (2× L3 slots burned) is consistent with that intent. No change needed unless the campaign context shows Marwen needing those slots within the same session.

**FI-TP21-C (NEW) — DW front-load pressure window (severity: FEEL / POSITIVE)**
DW-B and DW-A both rolled high initiative (22, 21) and acted before Marwen (19). Bazgar absorbed 22 HP before Marwen cast anything (27/49 HP remaining). This is the scenario's best pressure moment: if the DWs had rolled similarly on a previous round, or if Bazgar had been below half-health entering this patrol, the DW front-load could produce a real threat. Current run is the highest pre-Marwen DW damage across recent TP cycles. The DW formation-drill pressure is working as intended; it just resolves when Fireball clears the board.

**FI-TP21-D — SC Barrage disparity (severity: MINOR)**

Not a spec issue — dispatch uses its internal random cache, sim uses fresh rolls from the same seed. Bazgar FAIL took the full 12/12 (sim), Marwen PASS took 6. ### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TP21-A — SC initiative structural gap**
SC Dex+2 rolled 3 (total init 3). In 21 threshold-patrol cycles, SC's average initiative is low enough that it consistently acts last or near-last. This structurally ensures CW fires after both DWs have already acted. Is this intentional (the SC is cautious, reactive) or a feel gap (the SC never lands its signature move in the patrol formation)? **Recommendation:** no stat change needed, but the tactics note could advise: "if you act last in initiative, spend the CW bonus action on yourself (if you have Pack Tactics Voice) rather than hold — you get a Multiattack ADV instead of wasting the charge."

**MQ-TP21-B (NEW) — SC 50 ft back: intentional or terrain artifact?**
The patrol narration places SC "50 ft behind" the DWs in the alcove. 50 ft > 30 ft CW range. Every patrol cycle, CW is range-blocked. If SC is supposed to support the DWs with CW, the patrol positioning needs to tighten to ≤30 ft (25 ft is safer). If SC is intended as a rear artillery piece that joins after the DWs engage, then CW is correctly never used in R1 patrol. The .md does not specify a precise "patrol gap" — the DM sets this. **Recommendation:** add a note to the threshold-patrol setup in `_overview.md` or `encounters.md`: "SC starts 25 ft behind DW pair (within CW range)."
