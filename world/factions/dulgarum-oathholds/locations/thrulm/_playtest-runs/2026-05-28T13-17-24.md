### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FEEL-1 (log only): Shard Barrage recharged twice in 4 rounds (R2 and R4)**
Both times rolled 5 on a d6 (33% per round). This is within variance but it means the SC fired Barrage three times total (R1, R2, R4) — it was the dominant damage source for the encounter. In a longer fight this wouldn't matter, but in a short 4-round patrol it means Barrage was never really "depleted." Not a balance bug — just noted for pattern-checking across cycles. If multiple cycles show Barrage 3×/4-round, consider whether the 5-6 threshold is too low for a patrol-strength encounter.

**FEEL-2 (log only): SC solo endgame — kiting not modeled**
When SC was the last NPC standing (R3–R4), the simulation didn't model movement. Per tactics the SC should Tactical Retreat when melee closes to 15 ft. With Bazgar + Sabriel both closing from ~30 ft, the SC could kite down the corridor, forcing PCs to spend movement each round. The sim treated this as static fire. Result: fight resolved faster than it would at the table. DM note: at the table, the SC-alone phase should last 1-2 extra rounds as it retreats toward the vault door — that's more interesting than just standing and trading shots.

**FEEL-3 (log only): Marwen death-trap from focus fire**
DW1, DW2, and SC all correctly focused Marwen (caster). She went from 32 HP to -8 HP over two rounds despite making her Dex saves against Barrage. The derro did *exactly* what the tactics say. This is correct and earned — but worth noting that a level-5 wizard with 32 HP survives less than 2 rounds of derro focus fire in a choke corridor. Mechanically fine; it means wizard players will feel the danger strongly in this encounter.

**FEEL-4 (log only): CW advantage on DW1 R1 — impact not visible in narration**
SC used Call Weakness on DW1 before DW1's turn. DW1 should have had advantage on all its R1 attacks. The simulation applied rerolled dice for the higher value but the printed output from `roll_combat_action` showed flat rolls (it can't know CW was active). DMs running this live may forget to apply CW advantage. The .md checklist mentions this but there's no output marker in the action text to remind the DM. Low-priority but worth a future UX note.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-NEW-TP1: Tactical Drilling and Marwen's position**
Tactical Drilling (advantage on melee if an ally is within 5 ft of target) requires both a DW and an ally to both be adjacent to Marwen. In R1, both DWs charged from the vault door — they can enter adjacent formation easily. Tactical Drilling correctly fires. But if the PCs spread out (Bazgar up front, Marwen 30 ft back), only one DW can reach Marwen in R1 (no ally adjacent to Marwen). The tactics sheet doesn't address this explicitly: should one DW chase Marwen and one engage Bazgar, or both stay in formation near Bazgar? **Recommendation:** add a tactic note — "If the party spreads (caster 20+ ft from melee PCs), DW2 peels to the caster while DW1 holds the front; Tactical Drilling won't fire on the peeler's first turn but resumes if DW1 also closes next round."

**MQ-NEW-TP2: SC fires Barrage R4 when Bazgar+Sabriel are in melee range**
In R4, the SC was at 21 HP and both PCs had closed to within melee. The .md says "if melee threat closes within 15 ft: Tactical Retreat (bonus), then fire." The SC should have TR'd as bonus action AND fired Barrage (or multiattack) as action. But both Bazgar and Sabriel are within melee range, so TR just delays the inevitable. At 21 HP with Sabriel still at 44 HP (and smite slots), the SC was going to fall this round regardless of retreat. The TR rule is sound; the R4 situation is just endgame. No fix needed — just noting that TR at 1 HP ≤ 12 (flee) vs TR at 21 HP (tactical) is a meaningful distinction the DM needs to track.
