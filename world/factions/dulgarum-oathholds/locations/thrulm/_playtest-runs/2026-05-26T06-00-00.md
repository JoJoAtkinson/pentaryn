### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

1. **FI-88 (2nd beholder-escorts-limited cycle confirm, escalates FI-56): Grapple+Maw auto-crit instant-kills the Fighter in consecutive cycles.** Pattern: beholder R1/R2 multiattack hits Bazgar with first tentacle, Bazgar fails DC 16 Str save (40% success rate, +4 mod), Maw auto-crits the grappled target for ~48 piercing. Bazgar goes from any HP below ~65 to instant death in one action. This cycle: Bazgar at 18 HP, took 15 (TL1) + 48 (Maw crit) = 63 in one multiattack. Cycle 1: similar pattern. Two consecutive confirming cycles. The same-turn grapple+autocrit chain removes all Fighter counterplay — the decision point is a DC 16 save with 40% success, then if you fail, you die. **DD-11 (grapple-crit timing: same-turn vs prior-turn) URGENTLY needs human ruling.** If ruling = prior-turn only, Maw R2 does not autocrit and Bazgar survives with ~−5 HP (still down but stabilizable). This single ruling changes the encounter's lethality profile significantly.

2. **FI-89 (2nd cycle confirm FI-57): Drain Divinity permanently gated when Marwen wins initiative.** When Marwen (init 21) acts before beholder (init 20), the beholder's legendary budget gets spent on Void Ray (2 actions) at Marwen's end-of-turn, then 1 remaining action goes to Tentacle at Sabriel's end-of-turn, leaving 0. Beholder's own turn refreshes to 3, but then Marwen's turn fires VS/multiattack before any PC ends their turn with budget = 3. The only clean Drain Divinity window is: budget = 3 AND a slot-holder within 30 ft is ending their turn. In a 3-round escort fight, with Marwen frequently winning initiative (she has +3 Dex mod, beholder +6, but Marwen needs lower roll — not structural), this window doesn't open. 2/2 escort cycles: zero Drain Divinity outcomes. **Log for human: DD's attrition design requires multi-round slot erosion; the escort encounter resolves too fast. Consider whether DD should cost 2 actions (not 3) to give it a practical window when Void Ray + Tentacle (3 total) already consumes the budget.**

3. **FI-90 (confirm FI-58 / recharge pattern): VS recharge-6 never fires post-R1 in 3-round fight.** Rolled 3, 4 in R2/R3. Cumulative probability of VS firing in R2 or R3 = 1 − (5/6)² ≈ 31%. This run fell in the 69% failure bracket. Across escort-limited cycles 1+2, VS fires R1 (always available), then has ~31% chance to fire again by R3. **Not a bug — recharge-6 is designed to be rare. Design note: in encounters lasting ≤3 rounds, VS is essentially a one-shot weapon.**

4. **FI-91 (NEW): High variance in party damage output based on beholder's Dex save vs Fireball.** This cycle: beholder rolled 13 on Dex save vs DC 15 Fireball → failed → 34 fire damage dealt. Cycle 1: beholder saved → ~0 Fireball damage to beholder. 55% chance beholder fails DC 15 save (beholder +6 Dex vs DC 15, fails on ≤8 roll → 40% fail rate. Wait: DC 15, beholder +6 → needs ≥9 to succeed → succeeds on 9-20 = 60% chance → fails on 1-8 = 40% chance). This cycle beholder failed, dealing 34 HP. Without Fireball connecting, party output is essentially zero (cantrips + physical attacks bounced off AC 17+). **Feel note: Marwen's slot economy is the entire party's offensive budget. She spent her last L3 slot on Fireball R1, then died R1. The party had 2 remaining L3 slots but never used them. Slot scarcity + fragility bottleneck: Marwen's death immediately ends meaningful party output.**
