### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP4-A (CW structurally locked again — 4th threshold-patrol confirm):**  
SC Call Weakness was unused across both rounds:
- R1: Both DWs acted before SC (DW-B init 21, DW-A init 15, SC init 10). At SC's turn, DW-A is dead; DW-B has 14 HP (≤20 → MQ-12 guard fires → hold). CW correctly held.
- R2: Marwen Shatters DW-B before SC acts. SC has no allied targets at all. CW moot.

This is the 4th threshold-patrol cycle. CW has been collected once in prior cycles (3rd cycle: DW-B at 27 HP, already-acted, CW deferred to R2 but DW-B died before SC's R2 turn). Pattern: **when both DWs roll higher initiative than the SC, CW is structurally dead weight in this slice.** The 3/day limit is never the constraint — the initiative window is.

Recommendation (for human): In the threshold-patrol staging note, add: *"SC initiative matters more than DW initiative. If SC loses initiative to both DWs, CW has no valid window in patrol-length fights (2-3 rounds). No balance change needed — CW is an encounter-scale ability, not guaranteed to fire in every slice."* This is a log/coaching clarification, not a spec change.

**FI-TP4-B (DW Tactical Drilling not applied in R1 — simulation accuracy gap):**  
My simulation did NOT grant DW-B advantage on its R1 attack against Sabriel. Per the .md: *"Round 1, in formation: Multiattack (two hand axes) on the closest melee threat that has an ally in reach — Tactical Drilling kicks in."* DW-A was 30 ft away attacking Bazgar, not adjacent to Sabriel. So DW-B correctly had no Drilling (ally not within 5 ft of DW-B's target). No spec fix needed, but a coaching note clarification would help: *"Both DWs must target the SAME creature (or be adjacent to each other's target) for Drilling to fire. Splitting targets in R1 loses the formation bonus."* Fourth-cycle confirm of this ambiguity.

**FI-TP4-C (DW-B missed Sabriel AC 19 on all 4 attacks, 2 rounds):**  
DW-B rolled effectively 5 and 6 to-hit in R1 (both miss vs AC 19), then 5 and 14 in R2 (both miss vs AC 19). At +2 to-hit vs AC 19, hit chance is 20% per attack. Missing 4/4 is (0.8)^4 ≈ 41% — not that unusual. But the pattern is notable: targeting Sabriel (AC 19) with +2 axes is a poor matchup. If DW-B had Tactical Drilling (advantage), per-attack hit chance rises to ~36% — still bad. **Design note for human:** DW should generally target lower-AC PCs (Bazgar AC 18 or Marwen AC 15), not the heaviest armor (Sabriel AC 19). The .md targets "closest melee threat" but doesn't rank by AC. Sabriel as a divine martial in heavy armor is a poor DW primary target; she should absorb blows but shouldn't be the primary DW focus. Consider adding: *"If multiple PCs in reach, prefer lowest-AC target — DW axes are +2, not enough to threaten AC 19 reliably."* Fourth-cycle Sabriel-untouched pattern; Sabriel's AC is genuinely a DW-wall.

**FI-TP4-D (2-round resolution, patrol exits at light attrition):**  
Party exits at Bazgar 44/49, Marwen 26/32, Sabriel 35/44. Two L2 spell slots and 1 smite used. This is well within the intended "patrol is easy; beholder encounter wrecks you" design. ### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TP4-A (DW Tactical Drilling — targeting clarity):**  
The .md text "on the closest melee threat that has an ally in reach" is ambiguous about whether *both* DWs must attack the same target for both to receive advantage, or whether each DW independently gets advantage if any ally is within 5 ft of *their* target. Per 5e RAW, Pack Tactics requires "an ally within 5 ft of the creature it is attacking" — so DW-B attacking Sabriel gets advantage only if DW-A is within 5 ft of Sabriel (not within 5 ft of DW-B). The .md tactic of "in formation" implicitly means same target, but it's never stated. **Recommend adding:** *"Tactical Drilling fires when your ally is within 5 ft of YOUR target, not within 5 ft of you — both DWs must converge on the same creature in R1 to guarantee the bonus for both."*

**MQ-TP4-B (CW ≤20 HP guard vs ≥20 HP already-acted ally):**  
The existing rule: *"hold the CW charge if the already-acted ally has ≤20 HP at the time of CW."* What about an already-acted ally with >20 HP? Example: if DW-B had 21 HP at SC's turn (just above guard), should CW be spent? The advantage would apply next round only if DW-B survives. In a 2-round fight, DW-B at 21 HP after Shatter would still likely die before R2 SC turn. The ≤20 guard is a reasonable proxy but isn't really about survivability — it's about expected value of the deferred advantage. Worth noting: this is academic in threshold-patrol since the fast resolution makes deferred CW almost always wasted. No spec change needed; log for human context.
