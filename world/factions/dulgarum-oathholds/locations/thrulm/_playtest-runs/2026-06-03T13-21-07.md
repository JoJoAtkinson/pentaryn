- **FI-FC28-A (DESIGN DECISION — CONFIRMED PATTERN):** Killing blow again from STD-2's Ancient Resonance (R2), not the beholder. This is now confirmed in two consecutive final-confrontation cycles (FC27 and FC28). The beholder's DR dealt the iconic moment (Marwen disintegrated), but the party's physical collapse was caused by mob pressure. Is STD mob-kills intended, or should the beholder have more decisive late-round action? The beholder's DR/VS are the memorable moments; the mob is doing the tallying. Recommend: no change (the multi-stage kill pattern is thematically correct — the beholder unmakes one PC dramatically, then the mob applies pressure). But flag for human review.

- **FI-FC28-B:** Marwen disintegration (R1, DR HIT 54 force vs 9 HP) was dramatically powerful and mechanically correct. Disintegration was inevitable regardless of AR outcome — even at full 32 HP, 54 force > 32. Notably: the DR announcement rules from the .md (FI-NEW-FC4-LOH) were not tested because the party had no opportunity to attempt LoH (Marwen died this turn, and Sabriel acts after beholder in init). The "announce disintegration before Sabriel's action" clause is critically important for table play — DM must front-announce.
