### Simulation harness bugs (not encounter spec bugs — log for reference only)

- **MQ-19 (NEW)**: Lair action fires at top of round in sim, not interleaved at init count 20. Lair should fire after STB (init 25) but before STA (init 20). In this run, lair fired before STB. Same total round damage; TPK outcome unchanged. Future sim revisions should insert lair at init 20 in the turn order.
- **MQ-20 (NEW)**: Bazgar used Second Wind (bonus action) after being knocked to 0 HP by OBR. In D&D 5e, falling to 0 HP = immediately unconscious; bonus actions cannot be taken. Second Wind should be checked BEFORE attacks to confirm HP > 0 throughout. Low impact this cycle (VS finished him regardless), but stat-tracking is incorrect.
- **MQ-21 (AOE per-PC roll — NEW)**: Void Eruption and Void Scream damage was rolled separately per PC rather than once. In D&D 5e, AOE damage is rolled once and applied to all targets. Each PC got a different damage value (Bazgar 33, Marwen 35, Sabriel 43 from VS). Should roll once, apply uniformly.
- **MQ-14 (CONFIRM — existing)**: OBR fires as a reaction to each damage event. Current sim fires OBR after each individual attack that hits. Only safe here because the first attack missed — if both attacks hit, OBR would incorrectly fire twice. MQ-14 (OBR per-action vs per-hit ambiguity) is still live. Recommendation: OBR fires once per round, triggers on ANY damage taken that round, not per-hit. This should be clarified in the spec.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-23 (NEW) — R1 TPK with zero party counterplay window**: The party took devastating AOE damage (Lair VE + STB AR + STA AR) before any PC could act in Round 1. Final damage tally BEFORE any PC acted: Bazgar -39 HP (79.6%), Marwen -28 HP (87.5%), Sabriel -19 HP (43.2%). Then Sabriel, Marwen, and Bazgar used their actions but couldn't prevent the Beholder's VS from finishing everyone. The party was functionally dead before they could respond. The encounter design is intentionally overwhelming, but this cycle produced *zero mechanical counterplay* — no interesting decisions, no feeling of fighting back. Even a TPK should feel like "we almost made it" not "we never had a chance." This is the strongest signal yet that DD-33 requires resolution before this slice can be considered playtest-useful.

- **FI-24 (NEW — POSITIVE) — Simultaneous OBR death is dramatically satisfying**: When OBR (Oath-Breaking Retaliation) killed Bazgar while simultaneously STA's self-psychic finished STA, the result was a mutual destruction moment. Bazgar's last act was to slay the shrine-touched at the cost of his own life, while the shrine's power consumed the derro that harmed him. This is *exactly* the kind of death that feels earned and resonates thematically. Confirm that simultaneous mutual death is intended — if yes, note in STA/STB tactics as a signature moment.

- **DD-33 CONFIRMED 5th cycle — Shrine-touched initiative dominance**: STB init 25, STA init 20. Both ahead of the entire party (again). Combined with the lair action at init 20, this produces three AOE hits before ANY PC acts in R1. Across 5 cycles of final-confrontation:
  - Cycle 1: shrine-touched init 23 both, killed 2 PCs before beholder acted
  - Cycle 2: similar
  - Cycle 3: shrine-touched went first, beholder followed
  - Cycle 4: shrine-touched went first (DD-33 confirmed 3rd cycle)
  - Cycle 5 (this run): shrine-touched 25 and 20, total pre-PC damage 79%/88%/43% of max HP
  **RECOMMENDATION (do NOT auto-fix — human review)**: Either (a) lower shrine-touched Dex from +5 to +3, reducing their average init from 15.5 to 13.5; or (b) give them a "shrine-bound hesitation" trait: they cannot act in Round 1 until after init count 15 (they are in a trance state at combat start and emerge slowly). Option (b) is thematically richer.

- **DD-34 CONFIRMED 5th cycle — Drain Divinity zero tactical impact in final confrontation**: 5 cycles of final confrontation, DD has fired correctly (given beholder's legendary budget) in cycles 2-5 but has never changed the outcome. This cycle: Sabriel rolled exactly 16 (ties the DC = save), no slots lost, budget of 3 legendary actions spent with nothing to show. The design intent (siphon healing resources) is sound, but the encounter ends before DD can compound. **RECOMMENDATION**: DD is strategically interesting in longer-form encounters (empty-void, beholder-escorts-limited) but is a dead card in final-confrontation because the fight resolves in R1-R2. No spec change needed; this is a composition question — if final confrontation is meant to show beholder's full toolkit, the party composition needs a chance to survive to R3+.

- **DD-49 (NEW) — VS disadvantage blanket application**: In the sim, ALL PCs were assumed within 10 ft of the shrine (taking disadvantage on the VS Wis save). The spec says "creatures within 10 ft of the shrine have DISADVANTAGE." In the final confrontation, PCs may be spread across the 60-ft chamber. If they're not clustered near the altar, some or all PCs might not get disadvantage. At table this is a positioning question. **RECOMMENDATION**: Clarify that DM should explicitly track PC positions before VS fires; if PCs have not approached the altar stone (within 10 ft), they save normally (no disadvantage). Add this to the beholder .md tactics note under VS.

- **MQ-20** (see sim harness section above): Second Wind (and other bonus actions) must not fire when PC is at 0 HP.
- **MQ-21** (see sim harness section above): AOE damage must be rolled once and applied uniformly.
