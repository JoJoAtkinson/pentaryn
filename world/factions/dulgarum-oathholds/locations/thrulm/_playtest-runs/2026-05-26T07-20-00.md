### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-103 (NEW): First EV cycle where Marwen deals damage before R1 Disintegration — Marwen init 13 > Beholder 11**

In prior EV cycles, the beholder won initiative over Marwen and Disint'd her before she acted. This cycle, Marwen rolled 13 vs beholder's 11. She landed 2/3 Scorching Ray rays (15 fire) before Disint fired. The beholder still Disint'd her R1 (after its turn), but by then VR had already softened her to 18 HP. The meaningful finding: **when Marwen wins initiative over the beholder, the fight opens with PC offense before the Disint threat resolves.** This is not a bug — it's correct initiative variance — but it changes the felt tempo significantly. In cycles where Marwen goes last, the party absorbs a disintegration before taking a turn; in this cycle, they spent a slot AND took the loss.

Across 3 EV cycles: cycle 1 = beholder first (Disint R1 before Marwen), cycle 2 = beholder first, cycle 3 = Marwen first. The beholder's Dex initiative is the same as Marwen's (+2), so coin-flip distribution is expected. **No action needed — expected variance.**

**FI-104 (NEW): Middle-option negotiation succeeds on dice but fails on party resources — relic not planted**

Both Arcana (24) and Persuasion (18) succeeded. Persuasion 18 hit the middle-option threshold: the beholder offered to accept a consecrated relic sealed into the void. The party had none. Combat started anyway. The negotiation structure has a dead end: even perfect rolls lead to the same outcome if the party hasn't been seeded with a relic earlier in the campaign. **This is a session prep issue, not a mechanics issue.**

**Recommendation (DESIGN DECISION — for human):** In the session preceding Thrulm, plant one of the following within the party's reach: (a) a consecrated Dulgarum artifact from the faction lore (Oathhold relic, sealed-god fragment), or (b) give Sabriel's holy symbol a "consecrated" property that the beholder would accept — at cost of losing her focus item. Option (b) creates real stakes: Sabriel can end the fight peacefully by sacrificing her spellcasting focus. This is thematically resonant and gives the player a meaningful choice. **Do NOT auto-fix.** Human decision on which option fits the campaign.

**FI-105 (NEW): Beholder dead main-action turn R3 — Disint and VS both on cooldown, out of melee**

R3 recharge failed for both Disint (d6=2) and VS (d6=1). The beholder was at 40+ ft altitude, out of tentacle range. It had 3 legendary actions available — spent on Drain Divinity after Bazgar's turn (Sabriel saved) — but its MAIN ACTION was completely wasted. Zero damage from the main action in R3.

**Pattern:** In a solo beholder fight, the main-action dead turn is qualitatively worse than in a full encounter. With thralls present, NPC turns create constant pressure even when the beholder recharges. Without thralls, the dead turn gives the party a "free" round — a relief beat that feels arbitrary rather than earned (the party didn't DO anything to cause it; the RNG just happened). In a real fight, this would be the round where the DM might narrate the beholder retreating to reposition — which is correct tactics-wise (below-60-HP → prefer ranged) but feels like a pause button.

**This is the first time a dead main-action turn has been observed in EV.** In context of 3 EV cycles, it has a ~33% occurrence rate (1/3 cycles). Given that Disint has a 2/6 recharge chance each round and VS has a 1/6 chance, the expected frequency of double-fail recharge in a given round is approximately 4/6 × 5/6 ≈ 55% — so dead action turns are structurally expected. **For human: consider whether the beholder should have a tertiary main action option (e.g., a weaker ranged attack like Eye Ray variants) to fill dead-action turns without relying on melee reach.**

**FI-106 (NEW): Void Eruption first appearance in empty-void slice — lair kills both PCs R4**

In prior EV cycles, the fight resolved in R3–R4 with VS and Disint as the primary kill mechanisms. This is the first EV cycle reaching R4 with 2 PCs still standing (albeit very low HP), allowing VE to fire from the lair. VE (2d10=11 force, DC16 Dex, half on save) hit both PCs for 11 each on failed saves. Both PCs were at exactly the HP threshold where VE produced downs: Bazgar 11→0, Sabriel 6→−5.

**Feel note:** The kill blow came from the lair action (VE) rather than the beholder's main-action Disint or legendary abilities. The beholder's subsequent Disint on Bazgar (0 HP) was redundant. At the table, the DM should narrate this as: *"The altar pulses as you approach — the shrine itself doesn't want you here"* before VE fires, so the kill-from-environment feels intentional rather than incidental. **Recommend adding this DM callout to `_overview.md` or the lair action description in `beholder-thrulm.md`.**

**FI-107 (NEW): VR "set-up kill" pattern — Void Ray R1 legendary softens Marwen, Disint kills her; not in tactics**

After Marwen's R1 turn: beholder spent 2 LA on Void Ray on Marwen. She saved (half = 14 force → 18 HP). Beholder then Disint'd her for 39 force → dead. Without the VR softening (if beholder had gone DD instead), Marwen at 32 HP + 39 Disint = dead anyway (39 > 32). So the VR was actually unnecessary — Disint would have killed her from full HP regardless. **However**, this revealed a tactics gap: **when should the beholder use VR vs DD vs hold legendary after the caster's turn?**

Current tactics: "burn legendary on Drain Divinity rather than Void Ray when cleric/paladin present." In this run, after Marwen's turn, the beholder should have used DD on Marwen (saving her slots) rather than VR — but DD requires a slot-holder within 30 ft, and Marwen was the caster at range. Also, DD targets "a living target with spell slots" — Marwen qualifies. The beholder's "correct" play after Marwen's R1 turn was DD on Marwen (strips a slot, beholder gains +6 temp HP) rather than VR (deals damage that was moot given Disint would kill her anyway).

**Recommendation (DESIGN DECISION — low-risk .md edit):** Add to tactics: *"After a caster's turn, if Disint is available for the beholder's upcoming main action and the caster is the primary Disint target: spend ALL 3 legendary on Drain Divinity (not Void Ray) — strip their slot before Disint fires. If Disint kills them, the slot-stripping is wasted; but if Disint misses (AC 15, to-hit +6 = ~55% hit rate), the slot loss remains, and the 6 temp HP is real value."* **Do NOT auto-fix without human sign-off on the tactical change.**

**FI-108 (NEW): VS R2 both PCs fail Wis save — Bazgar d20+2=9, Sabriel d20+3=6. Both fail; 38 psychic each**

Bazgar 49→11 HP (-38), Sabriel 44→6 HP (-38). This is the defining moment of the fight — without VS, both PCs survive comfortably to R4+. With VS, they enter R3 critically wounded. The Wis save DC 16 vs Bazgar's +2 and Sabriel's +3 means:
- Bazgar hits DC16 on d20+2 ≥ 14 → 35% chance of saving (fails 65% of time)
- Sabriel hits DC16 on d20+3 ≥ 13 → 40% chance of saving (fails 60% of time)

Expected: ~60% chance both fail in a given VS cast. VS is THE fight-decider in the EV slice. When it fires and both fail, the party is in critical condition by R3. When one saves, they might last another round. **Structurally correct — no change needed. Log for DM awareness.**
