## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **FIX-SW30-A: OBR verbs[] → populated.**
  `oath_breaking_retaliation` had an empty verbs list, making it unfindable via the GUI's fuzzy-action-by-name grammar (e.g. "3 retaliation" would not dispatch). Added verbs: `["retaliation", "obr", "counter", "oath retaliation", "counter-swing"]`. No mechanical change — purely a discoverability fix.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SW30-A: Slice #1 VICTORY R2 — 30th cycle confirms slice is too lightweight.**
  2× STDs (45 HP each) die within 2 rounds against a level-5 party with Fireball. Slice is designed to test resonance recharge + Unstable Form rider, but STD-A recharged AR in R2 and died before firing; STD-B had UF active and AR still available, also died before acting. Neither showcase mechanic expressed in ~8 of 10 cycles. Recommendation: add 1× thrall-derro to the slice as a speed-bump, or boost STD HP to 55–60. **DESIGN DECISION — do not auto-fix.**

- **FI-SW30-B: Altar zone suppresses both fire (Fireball) and radiant (Divine Smite) — 30th cycle confirm.**
  Fireball dealt 12 (save)/24 (fail) vs expected 24/48 outside zone. Sabriel's smite dealt 9 radiant vs expected up to 18 with vulnerability. Both PC damage-type advantages are neutralized by altar zone. This is working as designed and creates a real tactical decision (pull STDs off the altar to re-expose vulnerability). However, in practice the fight ends before the party can maneuver. No spec change. DM should announce altar zone at combat start. Candidate for a COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET callout.

- **FI-SW30-C: Unstable Form triggered on both STDs (≥10 dmg) but neither survived to use it — 30th cycle confirm.**
  UF is a meaningful mechanic (advantage on all attack rolls) but is consistently invisible because the party kills STDs between the damage event and the STD's next turn. This is a pacing issue tied to FI-SW30-A: if the slice had a speed-bump NPC, STDs might survive to R3+ where UF would express. **DESIGN DECISION — not auto-fixable.**

- **FI-SW30-D: OBR fired twice (both misses), self-damage accumulated 5 total psychic.**
  OBR miss rate vs Bazgar AC 18 is structural (+4 to-hit, needs 14+ = ~35% hit chance). OBR self-damage (1d4) is the reliable piece — 1+4=5 psychic contributed meaningfully to STD-A's kill condition. The "punishes itself for being hit" flavor is landing. No change needed, but at-table the DM should call out OBR self-damage explicitly to make it feel like a cost/flavor moment.

- **FI-SW30-E: Stagger rule evaluated correctly; STD-B held AR R1, fired multiattack (both miss).**
  Stagger working as documented. Confirms prior cycles' behavior.
