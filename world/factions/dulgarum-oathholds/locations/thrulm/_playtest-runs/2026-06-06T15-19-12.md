### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-EV7-27-A VS-R1+R3-double-fire-27th-cycle** (feel: NEGATIVE)
Void Scream fired R1 and again R3 (recharge d6=6 R2, ~17% chance). Both fires were decisive kill shots. VS is the dominant win condition for the beholder — not DR (the thematic "disintegration" finish) and not multiattack. Two VS fires in 3 rounds is statistically unlikely but confirms VS is consistently more dangerous than DR. The "hunger made manifest" feel doesn't land when the kill is a 30ft psychic burst rather than a dissolving touch. *Recommend: add a 1/encounter restriction on VS or reduce dice to 5d10 fail / 2d10 pass, keeping it brutal but not fight-ender.*

**FI-EV7-27-B DR-miss-marquee-whiff-27th-cycle** (feel: NEGATIVE, persistent)
Disintegration Ray missed again (rolled 5 on d20 → total 11 vs AC15). DR has now missed or underperformed in the majority of cycles reviewed. The marquee "disintegrate on 0HP" mechanic that makes this beholder distinctive has never delivered a disintegration in the logged runs. *Recommend: increase DR to-hit bonus to +8 (vs current +6), or give the beholder advantage on the attack roll if target is GRAPPLED.*

**FI-EV7-27-C LoH-revival-loop-futile-27th-cycle** (feel: NEGATIVE)
Sabriel revived Marwen R1 (LoH 10HP) — Marwen was immediately grappled and dropped to 0 by TL-LA the same round. Sabriel revived Bazgar R2 (LoH 1HP) — Bazgar failed VR-LA the same round and dropped again. Both revival actions were negated within the same round by beholder LA pressure. The LoH arc provides a story beat ("Sabriel buying time") but the outcome is: 2 LoH charges spent for 0 net benefit. *This is structural, not a bug — the beholder's LA economy overwhelms LoH at this tier. Positive: it creates a dramatic "futile revival" narrative beat that feels earned for an overwhelming encounter.*

**FI-EV7-27-D Sabriel-smites-never-fired-27th-cycle** (feel: neutral, structural)
Sabriel used both action turns reviving downed allies (LoH R1+R2). 3 divine smites went entirely unspent. In a more spread-out fight, Sabriel lands 2d8 radiant (not resisted) which is the party's best non-Fireball damage path. The "always reviving, never attacking" arc is mechanically correct (keep ally count up) but leaves Sabriel's offensive toolkit invisible. *Note for DM coaching: in overwhelming encounters, discuss whether to use LoH early (keeps 3 PCs fighting) vs letting one PC fall and attacking with Sabriel. Running recommendation: LoH is the right call R1; by R2 Sabriel should evaluate whether reviving buys a full turn or just a death-round.*

**FI-EV7-27-E resistance-wall-confirmed-27th-cycle** (feel: NEGATIVE for Bazgar)
Bazgar's 4-hit Action Surge delivered only 14 damage (after halving non-magic slashing). His contribution was ~3.5 DPR vs 110HP pool. Without magical or sanctified weapons, Bazgar is tactically reduced to a meatshield. This is design intent (beholder .md calls it out) but the COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET does not have a prominent "non-magic weapons deal half damage — clarify PC equipment at session start" callout. *Recommend: add to cheat-sheet.*

**FI-EV7-27-F VS-save-half-kills-low-HP-PC** (feel: NOTABLE)
Bazgar saved against VS R3 (WIS 22 vs DC16 PASS) but still died to 3d10 = 23 psychic at 10HP. VS average half-damage is 16.5 (3d10) — which exceeds any PC's HP if they've taken two rounds of punishment. "Saving throw" becomes meaningless when half-damage still kills. *This is mathematically correct and appropriate for a CR13 encounter designed as a TPK — flag it as a note, not a fix.*

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-EV7-27-A VS-half-save-exceeds-remaining-HP-structural** — In late-round combat, 3d10 (avg 16.5) exceeds any party member's remaining HP. Is "save for half" meaningful on VS against a depleted level-5 party? Recommendation: intended — VS is the terror ability. No spec change.

**MQ-EV7-27-B beholder-HP-89/110-at-collapse-27th-cycle** — Consistent across all empty-void cycles reviewed (26th: 68/110, 25th: ~10HP corrected). 27th-cycle the beholder barely interacted with the party's damage (resistance wall + DR miss + Fireball pass save). Is the "no magic weapons" assumption correct for the Compass Edge? Human review needed: does any PC have a +1 weapon, Shillelagh, or other magical damage source?

**MQ-EV7-27-C beholder-high-init-vs-lair-timing** — Beholder rolled 23 init, acts BEFORE lair (20). In high-init scenarios, the lair fires immediately AFTER the beholder's action (same round), creating a beholder→lair double-punch in R1. UG R1 → lair init 20 came AFTER beholder VS, which had already resolved. This ordering gave the lair less impact R1 (UG was moot since VS already dealt damage). At lower initiative, lair would fire before beholder, potentially setting up VS disadv with prone — a more punishing order. *Recommend: beholder .md tactics note clarifying lair-then-beholder vs beholder-then-lair split depending on init roll.*
