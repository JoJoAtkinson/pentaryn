### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-EV7-25-A: Surprise DR miss swings entire fight.** When DR misses in surprise round (36% chance — 7+ on d20 needed for AC 15, so 70% hit rate normally; d20=2 → 7 total, miss by 7), Marwen enters R1 at full HP and contributes 2× Fireball across R1–R2. Party output skyrockets. When DR hits, Marwen is gone before acting. The variance delta is enormous: one die roll in the surprise round functionally determines whether party wins or gets TPK'd. *(This is FI-EV7-23-A from 1st-cycle, confirmed again at 25th.)*

- **FI-EV7-25-B: VS + LoH recovery arc POSITIVE.** VS downed Marwen R1, Sabriel LoH'd her back for 16 HP, Marwen cast Fireball (17 fire) same round. The revival beat felt earned and dramatic. LoH pool dropped to 9 remaining — resource drain without permanent loss. Fear condition on Sabriel R2 further shaped the narrative (disadvantage, missed R2 attacks, then broke free end-of-R2). *(POSITIVE feel — no change.)*

- **FI-EV7-25-C: No-thrall lair rotation feels mechanical.** With no thralls available, the only lair choices are VE and UG. The rotation collapses to VE/VE/UG/VE/VE/UG. Over 3 rounds: VE×2, UG×1. No tactical variation. DM has no decision to make — the rotation is deterministic. Consider adding a 3rd lair-capable option for solo beholder fights (e.g., a "seal-pulse" option that recharges one of the beholder's USED eye rays). Logged for human review.

- **FI-EV7-25-D: Drain Divinity temp HP from L1 slot (end-state) is noise.** R3 DD on Sabriel when she had no L3 remaining — L2 drained, +4 tmp HP. Beholder at ~28 HP gained 4 tmp at that point. Numerically irrelevant when beholder is one hit from death. FI-EV7-24-E confirmed again: DD temp HP only matters in mid-fight (R1–R2) when beholder is above 40 HP.

- **FI-EV7-25-E: Altitude retreat creates a distinct second act.** The beholder's below-30-HP retreat (shrine_drift to shaft lip, unstable_ground on pursuers) is the most tactically interesting piece of the fight, but it's never simulated because the sim beholder stays in place. At table, the retreat would force ranged-only PCs (Marwen fire bolt, Sabriel javelin) to be the closers — a complete reversal of the R1–R2 dynamic where Bazgar was the main damage dealer.

- **FI-EV7-25-F: DR 0-for-2 (surprise + R3) anomaly.** Both DR attempts missed (d20=2 → 8 total; d20=1 → 7 total). These are genuine low-probability events (~30% miss on AC 15 each), but two in a row is ~9%. The fight outcome was heavily shaped by this. Prior cycles with DR hits (24th, 1st) saw at least one disintegration. This run demonstrates the variance band for DR in the empty-void slice.

- **FI-EV7-25-G: Antireality never fired.** Bazgar's attack totals were 23, 23 (R1), crit-25, 23 (R2), 10(miss), 21 (R3). For Antireality to trigger: total must be ≤ AC+2 = 19, i.e., the attack barely hits. Only Sabriel's R2 attack total of 21 was close, but 21 > 19. AR trigger rate for +5-to-hit attackers vs AC 17 is ~15% of hits (d20=12–14 → total 17–19). In 5 hits across the fight, statistically 0–1 AR triggers expected. This run confirms: AR rarely triggers in normal play. 25th-cycle structural confirm.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-EV7-25-A: DD vs LoH-TL priority clarification needed.** When Sabriel uses LoH (< 30 HP condition met) AND the beholder has full 3 LA budget, the spec now has FIX-EV7-25-B added: TL fires before DD consideration. But the "FIRST PC's end-of-turn" DD priority note says DD fires "on the FIRST PC's end-of-turn." If Sabriel acts first, TL (1 LA) fires, leaving 2 LA — not enough for DD. The beholder then uses VR (2 LA) on the next PC's turn. This is a meaningful LA allocation change from "3 LA DD on Sabriel" to "1 LA TL on Sabriel + 2 LA VR on Bazgar." Net impact: less temp HP for beholder, more consistent chip damage. Human review recommended to confirm this priority is intentional.

- **MQ-EV7-25-B: DR recharge 5–6 vs off-by-one in DM tracking.** The DR spec says "recovers on 5–6." A d6=6 should recover. But DMs commonly misread "recovers on 5–6" as ">5" (i.e., only on 6). This was the sim bug in SIM-EV7-25-D. Recommend adding to the checklist: "DR: rolls 5 or 6 on d6 = recovered (both 5 and 6 recover, not just 6)." This parallels the VS clarification already in the checklist.

- **MQ-EV7-25-C: Solo configuration lair options — consider a 3rd option.** Empty-void removes manifest_thralls, leaving only 2 lair choices. The beholder .md now notes this with FIX-EV7-25-A (VE/VE/UG rotation), but the underlying design gap (no lair variety in solo fights) is a human design decision. Possible resolution: add a "void lure" lair option that only fires in no-thrall configurations (pulls one PC toward the altar — force reposition).
