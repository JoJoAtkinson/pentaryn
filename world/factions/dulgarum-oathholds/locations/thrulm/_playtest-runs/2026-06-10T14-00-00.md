## Negotiation phase (pre-combat)

The party descends the final 40 ft of the shaft and emerges at the threshold. Torchlight bends away. The air tastes of copper. The beholder drifts near the altar — a hollow sphere of coagulated shadow, four bone-white tentacles trailing beneath it. No eyes. Yet the feeling of being completely seen is immediate.

**SABRIEL** steps forward and speaks: "We are not here to restore the shrine. We want to go deeper — the lower shaft. We will not disturb what you have built here."

The beholder's response arrives as sensation: a cold taste of ancient copper, the pressure of a lie being tested, and a question felt rather than heard: *what divinity do you carry?*

Sabriel is a divine martial. The beholder senses her spell slots immediately. Its telepathy sharpens: *You carry the mark of something that should not exist down here. Leave the mark. Or leave entirely.*

**MARWEN** offers a middle path: "She could renounce one oath here, before the shrine. Would that satisfy it?"

**SABRIEL**: "I'm not breaking my oath for this thing."

**BAZGAR**: "Then we fight."

The beholder pulses — stone cracks beneath it — and combat begins.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**EV7-7-A — Shrine-Drift movement stacking (DESIGN DECISION)**
The beholder has fly speed 30 ft and Shrine-Drift as a bonus action (move up to 30 ft). It is ambiguous whether Shrine-Drift is *additive* (total 60 ft/turn) or *replaces* the fly movement. If additive: the beholder can close from altar (60 ft) to threshold (0 ft) in a single turn, catching the whole party in VS range immediately. If they are the same pool: beholder reaches 30 ft and VS threatens only advanced party members. This matters significantly for positioning and VS coverage. **Recommendation:** Clarify in the .md whether Shrine-Drift adds to or replaces the base fly speed. If additive, note the total mobility range in the tactics section.

**EV7-7-B — Maw auto-crit not reflected in dispatcher output (feel/usability)**
The `multiattack` dispatcher output lists Maw damage as a normal (non-crit) value. The auto-crit rule ("if target is grappled, this attack is a critical hit instead") is noted in the output text, but the DM must manually apply 8d8+3 vs the shown 4d8+3 value. In this run, Maw hit grappled Sabriel but the dispatcher printed 20 (not ~39 avg crit damage). DM caught it and applied crit manually, but this is easy to miss at the table. **Recommendation:** Add a crit-damage field or inline note to the Maw multiattack output row: e.g., `→ CRIT if grappled: avg 39 (8d8+3)`. Low effort, high clarity.

**EV7-7-C — Hold Person burning LR at L5 (balance observation)**
Marwen used Hold Person (DC 14) twice, burning 2/3 LR in 2 rounds. The beholder failed both saves naturally (rolls: 4, 7) — the LR depletion was not guaranteed but realistic. If the party survives long enough for a 3rd Hold Person, they could exhaust LR and potentially paralyze the beholder. This is thematically interesting (the party can "crack" the beholder's invincibility via sustained save-pressure) but creates a potential "winnable angle" that contradicts the intended TPK dynamic. Not flagging as a bug — this is valid per D&D 5e rules — but worth a conscious table decision: is LR-exhaustion → paralysis → party win the intended possibility, or should LR be 4 or 5 for this encounter scale?

**EV7-7-D — LA timing: refresh at beholder's own turn, not round start**
Beholder LA refreshes at the start of its own turn (standard 5e). In this run, the beholder spent all 3 LA on Drain Divinity at the end of R1 (post-Sabriel). In R2, it had 0 LA available between the lair action and its own turn — meaning no reactive Void Ray or Tentacle after Sabriel and Marwen's turns before the beholder acted. This is RAW-correct but felt awkward: the beholder was "defenseless" reactively for the first half of R2. The R1 LA-reserve protocol (FIX-FC40-A) recommends holding all 3 LA for DD — this trade-off is confirmed: DD fires, but the beholder has no reactive LA for R2 pre-own-turn. For a solo encounter where burst threat matters, this means the party gets "free" Sabriel + Marwen actions in R2 before the beholder can respond. **Not a bug — design trade-off confirmed.** DD is worth the reactive silence in R2 when smite slots are fat.

**EV7-7-E — Negotiation scene feel**
The negotiation-to-combat transition felt earned. The beholder's demand to "leave the mark" targeting Sabriel's divine power is thematically strong — it forces a player choice (break oath / pay a slot as toll / fight). The .md mentions "sacrifice of divinity (a cleric's oath-breaking, a paladin's fall from grace)" as a de-escalation hook. At the table, there's an unexplored middle option: the beholder accepts one spell slot as a toll for passage (Drain Divinity as voluntary tribute, not combat). This could be a genuinely interesting non-combat path. **DESIGN DECISION: flag for DM.** The empty-void slice is the right place to test this; the current .md lists it as an option but doesn't give the DM concrete mechanics for "toll" negotiation.
