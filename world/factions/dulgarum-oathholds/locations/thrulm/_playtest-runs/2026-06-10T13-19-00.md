### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SCT6-49-A — Fireball decisive against shardcaller-only formation (49th consecutive SCT confirm)**
Two Fireballs cleared all 3 SCs in 2 rounds. The ≥25-ft spread prevents all-3-in-one-Fireball but cannot stop 2-per-Fireball: the 20-ft Fireball radius (40-ft diameter) clips 2 SCs even with 25-ft inter-SC spacing. This is the 9th consecutive SCT cycle ending in R1-R2 Fireball victory. The formation has no meaningful answer to repeated Fireball unless:
(a) SCs gain psychic resistance or fire resistance,
(b) vault terrain prevents line-of-sight (pillars in the way),
(c) the slice is replaced with a mixed-formation that gives the wizard a different threat to manage, or
(d) SC HP is raised to ~40-45 so they survive the first Fireball and continue applying pressure.
**Do not auto-fix — fundamental balance scope.**

**FI-SCT6-49-B — SC-C Barrage never fired (4th consecutive SCT cycle)**
SC-C had Barrage available all fight: in R1, the stagger rule assigned SC-B as the Barrage carrier (lowest init); in R2, SC-C retreated (TR + Multiattack) and then died to Sabriel's smite before getting another action. This is the 4th consecutive SCT cycle where SC-C is the highest-init SC and the stagger rule assigns Barrage to the lowest-init SC, meaning SC-C's Barrage is chronically unused. The stagger rule (FI-SCT-34-A) assigns Barrage to the lowest-init SC for good tactical reasons (spreading pressure across rounds), but the practical result is that the highest-init SC's Barrage slot is a dead resource when the party bursts the formation in R1-R2. **Consider whether the highest-init SC should fire Barrage R1 as pressure opener before the party's caster acts, accepting the stagger trade-off.**

**FI-SCT6-49-C — Pack Tactics Voice (PTV) triggered but had zero impact (2nd consecutive SCT confirm of PTV near-zero)**
SC-C hit Marwen in R2 (Throw 2), applying PTV disadv on Marwen's next save. Marwen then cast Fireball (a spell she makes, not a save she rolls), so the PTV disadv was consumed without ever triggering a save-disadvantage check. PTV requires the target to make a saving throw *after* being hit — but Marwen's follow-up is typically a spell, not a reaction that requires saving. PTV is structurally invisible against casters who act immediately after being hit. The passive would need the target to be affected by a save-based ability *before* their next action — which only happens in the same round if another NPC hits them before their initiative and then a second NPC uses a save-based ability also before their initiative. That's a narrow window. **Log as feel issue — PTV almost never matters against this party's action patterns.**

**FI-SCT6-49-D — CW advantage wasted on SC-C R1 (miss, both throws, 9th consecutive SCT wasted-CW count)**
SC-A granted CW advantage to SC-C in R1 (initiative 21 — the CW fires on-turn, so SC-C benefits immediately this round, correct per spec). SC-C rolled 5 and 6 on d20 — even with advantage (best of two), SC-C only hit a 6+4=10 vs AC 15. The advantage technically did its job (applied correctly per spec), but both raw rolls were low. This is not a spec bug. However, CW remains chronically low-yield against this party: the party's AC floor is 15 (Marwen), and a +4 to-hit on a shardcaller needs an 11+ on d20 to hit AC 15, 15+ to hit AC 18, 16+ to hit AC 19. CW adds one reroll opportunity but +4 to-hit vs AC 15-19 is already borderline.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SCT6-49-A — Per-target vs single-roll AoE damage in simulation**
The simulation rolled separate 3d6 for each PC hit by SC-B's Barrage (resulting in 13/13/13 — by coincidence the same as the API total; prior runs differed). The `shard_barrage` API output correctly returns a single damage total for all targets. For accuracy, the test harness should apply the API's single damage total to all targets and ask the DM to roll per-target only for saves. Not a spec bug. (This issue recurs across SCT cycles; tracked as SIM-SCT6-49-A.)

**MQ-SCT6-49-B — SC-B Barrage fires after SC-A death, which is tactically wrong**
SC-B (init 4) fires Barrage after SC-A has already been killed by Sabriel. Per the checklist, SC-B's CW charges become post-hoc (all targets acted before SC-B). The Barrage is correct — SC-B has Barrage available and fires it as the designated R1 Barrage carrier. But the tactical note is: SC-B fires into a party that has already dealt and absorbed R1 damage without the Barrage providing any pre-emptive pressure. The stagger rule assigns Barrage to the lowest-init SC, but when that SC has init 4 (going after the entire party), the Barrage becomes a pure cleanup shot. This is structurally the same finding as FI-SCT6-49-B above — the stagger rule creates a systematic "Barrage comes after the wizard" pattern.

**MQ-SCT6-49-C — SC-C Tactical Retreat R2: bonus action vs Barrage still available**

SC-C still fired Multiattack (main action). However, SC-C never got to use its Barrage (recharged but never assigned as stagger carrier).
