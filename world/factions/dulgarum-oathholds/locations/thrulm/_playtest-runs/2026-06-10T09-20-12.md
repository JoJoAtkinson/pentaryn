### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-TW2-41-A: Taunt+CW core combo structurally blocked when Rager rolls high initiative (41st TW2 confirm).**
  Rager init 16 goes first → charges to melee → SC acts at init 7 (last). By the time SC acts, Rager is ~50ft away (> 30ft CW range). CW 3/3 charges spent: 0. The "tank-wall" design intent is to test the Taunt + CW pairing — but that pairing requires SC to act BEFORE the Rager (SC init > Rager init). Rager Dex +0 vs SC Dex +2 means this is near-50/50. When Rager wins initiative (the more threatening scenario, which is when this combo matters most), the whole mechanic collapses. **Recommendation:** Add a note to SC tactics that CW in tank-wall is initiative-gated; OR give the Rager a small initiative penalty (Slow-starter trait: "The Rager is always last on R1") to guarantee the CW window fires at least once before it charges.

- **FI-TW2-41-B: Taunt disadv clause is decision-irrelevant vs front-line Rager threat (41st TW2 confirm).**
  Marwen failed the Taunt save (9 vs DC 12), but immediately chose to Scorching Ray the Rager (the taunting creature) — which bypasses the disadv entirely. Taunt's mechanical hook ("disadv vs non-Rager targets") only bites if Marwen would rather target the SC. But with a bloodied Rager in melee destroying Bazgar, Marwen naturally shoots the Rager. The condition never triggers. 39th + 40th + 41st cycles all confirm: Taunt saves are failed, but the disadv clause goes untriggered because the Rager IS the preferred target. Taunt's effectiveness in this slice depends entirely on the Rager surviving past R1 — which it cannot against focused L5 alpha strike.

- **FI-TW2-41-C: Rager R1 death from alpha-strike burst (41st TW2 confirm).**
  Rager 52 HP survived 0 rounds after acting. R1 damage received: 27 fire (Scorching Ray) + 15 slash (Trip Attack w/ sup) + 29 radiant (L2 smite) = 71 total. Even if the Trip Attack had missed (worst case 27+29=56), Rager still dies R1. The Rager cannot absorb a 3-PC focused alpha-strike at level 5. Without surviving past R1, Taunt and Berserk both fire once and then the Rager dies before the fight develops any aggro texture. **DESIGN DECISION:** Should the Rager have HP 70–75 in the tank-wall slice, or should the slice description note "Rager is expected to die R1; SC is the sustained threat"?

- **FI-TW2-41-D: CW 0/3 spent — all charges dead weight (41st TW2 confirm).**
  Previous run (40th) spent 1/3 CW but the Rager died before collecting the advantage. This run: 0/3 spent. Across 2 consecutive TW2 cycles, CW has produced 0 advantage activations. The range constraint (30ft, Rager in melee at 50ft from SC) and initiative timing (SC acts after Rager charges) combine to make CW non-functional in the standard setup. **Structural carry from 40th cycle (FI-TW2-A + FI-TW2-B).**

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-TW2-41-A: Prone + melee attack should grant advantage to attackers.** When Bazgar's Trip Attack knocked the Rager prone (Str save 6 failed), Sabriel's subsequent melee attacks should have been at advantage (PHB: prone target imposes adv on melee attacks from within 5ft). The sim didn't apply this — Sabriel still hit (17 HIT on flat roll), but at advantage the kill probability would have been higher. DM note: when Trip Attack/prone lands on the Rager, all melee attackers after the prone trigger get advantage. Not a spec bug — a sim tracking gap.

- **MQ-TW2-41-B: SC Barrage 3d6 = 6 (minimum territory).** The Barrage rolled 2+3+1=6 total. With 3 PCs and corridor geometry, even a low Barrage (3 damage on saves) registers on every PC — the "miss" case never exists in a corridor. At average (10.5), Barrage would have hit harder. Consider whether a minimum Barrage damage floor (7 minimum, or 2d6+2 roll-and-keep) is warranted to avoid anticlimactic post-Rager plays.
