## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **FIX-BEL3-32-A — thrall-derro/dagger damage_modifier 1→2:** The `.md` stat block says "4 (1d4 + 2) piercing" for the Dagger (Desperate). The DB spec had `damage_modifier: 1` (1d4+1 avg ~3.5). Corrected to `damage_modifier: 2` (1d4+2 avg ~4.5) via `combat_action_upsert`. Minor (1 avg damage per hit) but clear .md mismatch.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

#### DD-BEL3-32-A: Maw "critical hit when grappled" rider vs. .md "save" text

- **Context:** Slice #3, cycle 32. Seed 494651.
- **Finding:** The `.md` says: *"If the target is a creature grappled by the beholder, the target has disadvantage on the saving throw."* There is no saving throw in the Maw attack (it's a melee weapon attack). The DB spec interpreted this as `rider_on_hit: "If the target is grappled by the beholder, this attack is a critical hit instead."` — a critical hit doubles all damage dice (4d8 → 8d8, +modifier). In this run the Maw crit killed Bazgar (30 piercing, from 11 HP with excess) and would have killed Marwen from 2 HP anyway (55 piercing). Average grappled Maw = ~40 damage; average normal Maw = ~22 damage. The crit rider is the decisive kill mechanic in this slice.
- **Three options for human review:**
  1. **Keep crit (current):** Most lethal interpretation; grapple+maw combo is a near-instant-kill for most level 5 PCs. The spec author chose this.
  2. **Remove rider:** Maw is just 4d8+3 regardless of grapple. The .md text was erroneous copypaste; excise it.
  3. **Change rider to "advantage on beholder's next save-based ability vs target":** More thematic (being grappled makes you vulnerable to void effects), less swingy, and pairs well with void_scream's frightened condition.
- **Human decision — out of blast radius.**

#### DD-BEL3-32-B: Tentacle Lash grapple — "DC 16 STR save" vs. automatic on hit

- **Context:** Slice #3, cycle 32. Seed 494651.
- **Finding:** The `.md` says: *"the target is grappled (escape DC 16)"* — meaning grapple is automatic on a hit, escape requires a DC 16 Athletics or Acrobatics check. The DB spec has `apply_condition_on_hit: {condition: "grappled", save_dc: 16, save_ability: "str"}`, which the roller outputs as "[ASK PLAYER: on a HIT, target rolls a DC 16 STR save — on fail, apply grappled]". This means the grapple is only applied if the target FAILS a STR saving throw — approximately 50% of the time for a typical level 5 character. In the simulation I applied grapple automatically (following the .md), but at the table the printed output would instruct DMs to call for a save. This creates a table discrepancy.
- **Impact:** Making grapple automatic would make the beholder slightly more reliable at entering the grapple→Maw-crit combo. Making it a save (current spec) reduces the combo's reliability but adds player agency.
- **Recommendation:** Clarify which is intended. If automatic: remove `save_dc` and `save_ability` from the spec, change to `apply_condition_on_hit: {condition: "grappled"}`. If save-based: update the `.md` to say "the target must succeed on a DC 16 Strength saving throw or be grappled." **Human design decision — out of blast radius.**

#### DD-BEL3-32-C: Void Scream adds Frightened condition not present in .md

- **Context:** Slice #3, cycle 32. Void Scream never recharged so this was not triggered in play.
- **Finding:** The `.md` describes Void Scream as: *"Each creature within 30 feet that can hear it must make a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw, taking 33 (6d10) psychic damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. Creatures within 10 feet of the shrine have disadvantage on this save."* — damage only, no Frightened. The DB spec adds: *"On a FAILED save, target is also FRIGHTENED of the beholder for 1 minute (DC 16 Wis save ends at end of each of the target's turns)."* Frightened imposes disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks, and prevents moving closer to the beholder. This significantly increases Void Scream's power level (it becomes a soft crowd-control + damage ability, not just damage).
- **Recommendation:** If the Frightened condition is intentional, update the `.md` to include it. If it was an erroneous addition, remove it from the DB spec. Either way, the two sources contradict. **Human design decision — out of blast radius.**

#### DD-BEL3-32-D: Thralls die to single Fireball R1; action economy pressure never materializes

- **Context:** Slice #3, cycle 32. Confirmed across 5 of 5 slice #3 runs (cycles 4, 31, 32 in this log; plus 2 earlier confirmed cycles).
- **Finding:** Three 22-HP thralls die to a single L3 Fireball on a failed save (22 fire = exactly max HP). Even a partial hit pattern (2 fail, 1 save = 22, 22, 11) leaves survivors at ~half HP before they act again. The slice is intended to "test legendary economy, lair actions, action-economy pressure" — but by the time any thrall acts twice, they're all dead. Total thrall contribution in this run: 4 piercing + 6 piercing + 0 = 10 damage across two hits in R1, then gone.
- **Recommendation A:** Increase thrall HP to 28–30. At 28 HP, Fireball (22 avg on fail) leaves a thrall at 6 HP — alive, actionable, but wounded. Two thralls survive the Fireball and continue applying pressure for 2–3 more rounds.
- **Recommendation B:** Add +2 Con modifier (+Dex save) to thrall stat block to give Fireball a 30–40% chance of leaving them at half HP rather than dead outright.
- **Recommendation C:** Stagger thrall starting positions so at most 2 are in the 20-ft Fireball radius; the third is held back 50+ ft and approaches during the beholder's Compel Thrall sequence.
- **Human design decision — out of blast radius.**

#### DD-BEL3-32-E: Void Scream never fires in limited slice (Recharge 6 ≈ 17%/round)

- **Finding:** Over 3 rounds with one recharge attempt per round, probability of never recharging = (5/6)^3 ≈ 57.9%. In a 3-round TPK fight, Void Scream fires on average 0.43 times after first use. This makes it feel absent in short encounters. In the full confrontation (slice #4, 5+ rounds), it fires more reliably. This may be intentional (Void Scream as rare terror in extended fights) but DMs should be coached that it won't appear in sub-4-round encounters.
- **Impact:** Feel issue, not a bug. Void Scream contributes 0 damage in this entire run.
