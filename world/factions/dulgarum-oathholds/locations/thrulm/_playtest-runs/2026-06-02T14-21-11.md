### Bugs auto-fixed

- **[FIX-EV7-A] beholder-thrulm.md start-of-turn checklist item 1:** Added clarification that DR is a **ranged attack roll (+6 to hit), NOT a Dex saving throw.** The original text said only "recovers on 5–6" with no mechanic stated. ### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-NEW-EV7-A: DR as attack roll (no save) — Marwen disintegrated before acting:** With DR as a to-hit attack (+6 vs AC 15 = 60% hit rate), Marwen is disintegrated in the surprise round before rolling initiative in a majority of runs at this seed pattern. Full 10d8 (avg ~44.5) force far exceeds her 32 HP — any hit is lethal. A player watching their character die permanently before taking a single action is a significant table-impact moment. beholder-thrulm.md already notes the LoH-after-DR ruling, but the "permanent death before initiative" path is more common than the existing note implies. **Recommendation:** Add to tactics: "In the surprise round, DR can permanently disintegrate Marwen before initiative is rolled. Communicate this outcome to the player immediately and let them choose a replacement character narrative before the fight continues. Do not silently proceed past this moment."

- **FI-NEW-EV7-B: Drain Divinity monopolizes LA budget in solo fights:** With no thralls, the beholder uses Drain Divinity (3 LA) every available round (after the first PC's turn with a slot). 3 of 3 LA budgets went to DD. DD "passed" twice (Sabriel CHA +3 vs DC 16 — roughly 35% pass rate), leaving the beholder's legendary contribution at zero for 2 of 3 rounds it was used. The monotone pattern (DD or nothing per LA budget refresh) makes legendary actions feel like a coin flip rather than a tactical choice in solo fights. **Recommendation:** Add a fallback clause to the DD priority rule: "If DD has failed (target saved) twice in a row this encounter, skip it — switch to Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle Lash (1 LA). Resume DD next budget if a new target or a fresh round is available. The beholder reads the odds."

- **FI-NEW-EV7-C: Legendary Resistance unused:** All 3 LR were untouched at encounter end. Against a level-5 party with no save-forcing spells (Hold Monster, Banishment, Command all require spell slots beyond L5), LR is a dead stat in this encounter. This is fine — LR is a safety net. No action needed.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-EV7-A: DR to-hit vs standard 5e Dex save — mechanic is intentional but undocumented:** The DB defines DR as `single_attack` with `to_hit_bonus: 6` (attack roll, no save). Standard 5e beholder DR is a DC 16 Dex save. The design choice here (attack roll) makes DR more predictable (against low-AC targets) and slightly less lethal against high-AC targets vs. a save. With this run the distinction matters: hit rate vs Marwen AC 15 = 60%, vs Dex save DC 16+2 mod = 70% fail rate. At-table, DMs familiar with 5e may default to the save version — the beholder-thrulm.md clarification added this fire will help prevent this. No further DB change needed; the auto-fix above is sufficient.

- **MQ-EV7-B: Simulation methodology — sim used own dice for PC outcomes rather than reading DB to-hit output:** The playtest sim dispatched DB calls for narrative but ran parallel Python `random` rolls for HP tracking. This means sim HP values diverge from what the DB's internal rolls would produce. In particular: the DR miss in R1 (DB to-hit 13 vs Marwen AC 15) was a genuine miss per the DB, but the sim applied its own damage roll anyway. **This does not invalidate the qualitative findings** — the encounter is still a TPK — but the per-round HP numbers are approximations. Future sim iterations should parse the DB's to-hit value from the output string and compare to target AC before applying damage. (Low priority — the qualitative findings are sound regardless.)
