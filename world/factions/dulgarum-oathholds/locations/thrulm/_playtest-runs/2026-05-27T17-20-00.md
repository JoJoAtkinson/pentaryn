## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed
- **beholder-thrulm.md — Interaction Notes negotiation pacing (FI-NEW-EV5-B):** Added explicit DM guidance: beholder runs at least 2 exchanges before combat triggers; soft failures get a dismissal ("Come back with proof"), not an attack; only hard threats to the seal trigger the DR surprise round. Affects only DM roleplay guidance, not stats/spec. Prior text left the trigger condition ambiguous, creating cycles (1/4) where the sim fired DR after 0 exchanges — not representative of intended behavior.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-NEW-EV5-A: Maw grapple autocrit — DD-11 5th EV confirm (URGENT)**
- Sabriel: 31 HP entering R2 Multiattack. TL2 grapple → Maw autocrit = 43 piercing. Total 50 damage in one multiattack.
- Pattern confirmed across 5 EV cycles + multiple BEL cycles. Grapple+Maw autocrit is a structural one-shot mechanism on any PC with <44 HP when caught in melee range.
- The .md spec is mechanically faithful to 5e (grapple → subsequent attack is a crit is a common beholder ability). Question is whether the *feeling* of insta-kill in R2 is intentional. Beholder is CR 13 fighting L5 party — this may be correct design.
- **Human decision needed (DD-11 URGENT):** Is the Maw autocrit on grapple intentional as a one-shot PC kill for this party? Options: (a) keep as-is (appropriate for CR 13 vs L5); (b) cap autocrit dmg at 30 (still dangerous but survivable for Sabriel); (c) remove autocrit, keep grapple + normal Maw hit.

**FI-NEW-EV5-B: Near-success negotiation → surprise is a better narrative than zero-exchange → surprise (DD-53 nuance)**
- This cycle ran 2/3 exchanges before the truce collapsed. The surprise attack felt more earned than prior cycles (cycle 4: DR mid-parley after 1 exchange; cycles 1-3: no parley at all).
- Recommend: Interaction Notes should specify that the beholder grants at least 2 exchanges before combat triggers, regardless of fail rolls. Even on 0/2, it should give a final warning ("*Come back when you can prove what you claim.*") and allow the party to leave or draw weapons — making the surprise round a PC choice to push past, not a passive ambush.
- This would also address FI-104 (dead-end branch when party has no artifact) by giving them a graceful out.

**FI-NEW-EV5-C: Sabriel smite stranding — 5th consecutive EV confirm (FI-184)**
- Sabriel's full slot budget (L1×4, L2×3, L3×2) was intact at death for the 5th consecutive empty-void cycle.
- Root cause: (a) vs surprise-round she has no action; (b) in R1 she acts last (init 6) by which time both DR and VS have fired; (c) in R2 she's grappled and killed before her turn.
- No smite will ever land unless Sabriel rolls very high initiative OR the beholder skips DR in the surprise round.
- **Design question (DESIGN DECISION):** Should the EV slice DM be advised to delay the beholder's surprise-round attack by one exchange (give the party 1 free round to establish positions before the truce breaks)? This would allow Sabriel to use her first turn meaningfully.

**FI-NEW-EV5-D: Both PCs saved VS R1 (rare event)**
- Bazgar Wis 22 vs DC 16 (saved), Sabriel Wis 17 vs DC 16 (saved). No frightened condition in R1.
- Despite no fear, Bazgar and Sabriel missed all 4 attack rolls in R1-R2. +5 to-hit vs AC 17 needs 12+: ~50% hit rate. Four misses in a row = ~6% event (0.5^4). 
- This is the most favorable VS roll the party has had in any EV cycle, yet party output was zero. The beholder's 110 HP wall is the primary obstacle, not fear.
- Positive: confirmed that fear condition would compound damage deficit further (FI-149), but fear is not the *only* reason the party can't land hits.
