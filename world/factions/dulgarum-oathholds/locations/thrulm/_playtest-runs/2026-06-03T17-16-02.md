## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **FIX-DW-TP22-A: Tactical Drilling simultaneous-charge clarification** (auto-fix to `deep-watch-derro.md`):  
  The tactics note "Round 1, in formation: Tactical Drilling kicks in" was ambiguous when both DWs charge simultaneously from range. In this run (DW-A init 21, DW-B init 14), DW-A acts first — DW-B is not yet adjacent — so DW-A attacks **flat** (no Drilling). DW-B acts after DW-A is already in melee, so DW-B correctly gets Drilling advantage. The sim applied advantage to both (incorrect); the .md has been updated to state: "Drilling applies only when an ally is ALREADY adjacent to the target — not when both DWs are simultaneously closing. First DW to act in a mutual charge attacks flat; second DW gets Drilling." Outcome unchanged for this run (DW-A's natural roll of 20 and 14 would have hit AC18 flat anyway; DW-B missed AC18 regardless).

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-TP22-A: SC CW 0/3 used — 22nd-cycle double-block confirm (SEVERITY: HIGH)**  
  SC init 7 (last in initiative) → all DWs have acted before SC each round. In R2, the only living DW (DW-B, 17 HP) is below the ≤20 HP guard AND already-acted, double-blocking CW. Result: CW never fires in 22 consecutive threshold-patrol cycles. The mechanic's designed function (boosting the highest-initiative DW for a decisive early attack) is structurally prevented by SC being lowest-initiative in the formation. **Recommendation:** Either (a) move patrol positions so SC acts earlier (DM guidance in encounters.md), or (b) cap the HP guard at ≤15 for the 2-NPC escort configuration where no higher-HP target will appear. This is a balance-adjacent change requiring human review — **do NOT auto-fix.**

- **FI-TP22-B: DW Wis save structurally weak vs caster crowd control**  
  DW Wis -1, no Wis save proficiency → ~30% pass rate vs DC14 Hideous Laughter each turn. DW-A failed R1, barely saved R2 (15 on d20), then died anyway. In 4+ rounds, a DW will almost certainly be laughing for 1–2 rounds. A level-5 wizard has THL, Hold Person, Web, Hypnotic Pattern — all Wis-save dependent. The patrol loses most of its threat if the wizard lands a Wis-save spell on DW-A in R1. **Recommendation:** Add a note to Deep Watch tactics that they close aggressively to deny the wizard's movement (force range-disadvantage CC or force verbal-only spells). Or raise DW Wis to 10 (even +0 saves helps). Design decision — **do NOT auto-fix.**

- **FI-TP22-C: R3 solo SC Barrage from 20 HP feels like cleanup, not pressure**  
  SC at 20 HP with both DWs dead, firing a second Barrage against 3 focused PCs, felt anticlimactic. The area threat is real (Marwen ended at 9 HP) but emotionally reads as a last-stand chip rather than a dangerous formation ability. The *patrol's* distinctive threat (the SC coordinating from range while DWs hold melee) collapses the moment both DWs die. **Recommendation:** Add a retreat trigger for SC when all melee allies are dead and SC is < full HP — retreat via Tactical Retreat each round rather than standing and firing. This would make the SC feel more tactically distinct as a commander type. Design decision — **do NOT auto-fix.**

- **FI-TP22-D: Encounter outcome VICTORY R4, consistent with recent cycles**  
  22nd-cycle win. Bazgar took most damage (49→18 HP), Marwen was genuinely threatened (32→9 HP). Sabriel untouched at 44 HP. No slots above L2 burned. The patrol is appropriately manageable for level-5, confirming its role as encounter stage 1. No balance concern here — POSITIVE signal.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-TP22-A: DW Tactical Drilling — simultaneous charge resolution (FIXED above as FIX-DW-TP22-A)**  
  Rule: When two DWs charge from equal range on the same turn order, the higher-initiative DW attacks flat (no ally yet adjacent); the lower-initiative DW gets Drilling advantage (ally already in melee). The .md now states this explicitly.

- **MQ-TP22-B: SC tactics "do not CW ally who plans Barrage" vs bonus-action conflict rule**  
  The checklist says "Do not CW an ally who plans Barrage." But Barrage is a MAIN action and CW is a BONUS action — they're not in conflict for the SC itself. The restriction is: don't CW an ALLY who will fire Barrage, because Barrage is save-based (CW advantage on the Barrage-firing ally's save-based attack is wasted). This is correctly stated but worth confirming: a SC firing Barrage itself CAN spend a bonus action on something else (TR or CW). In this run SC correctly skipped CW (targeting issue, not Barrage conflict). No spec change needed, but the coaching note should explicitly say "the SC itself can CW a DIFFERENT ally while firing Barrage; the restriction is only that you don't CW the Barrage-firer's attack rolls."
