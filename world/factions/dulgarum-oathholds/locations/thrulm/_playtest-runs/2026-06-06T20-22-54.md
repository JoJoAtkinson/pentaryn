### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC34-A: VS-R1-kills-Marwen-34th-cycle-confirm** — Void Scream (beholder init 7, below avg 22 psychic) kills Marwen outright in R1. Marwen never acted (init 4). Fireball blocked again. In 34 cycles of FC, Marwen fires Fireball in approximately 0% of runs (beholder init > Marwen init ~50% of the time, and VS fires before Marwen acts). This is the dominant encounter pattern — the wizard is a spectator. Recommend: (a) reduce VS to 3d10 (avg 16.5; saves-fail still painful; below-avg won't one-shot Marwen), (b) add a tactics note that beholder should DELAY VS if Marwen hasn't acted (letting Fireball wipe thralls creates a more interesting Act 2 with just beholder), (c) hard 1/encounter cap.

**FI-FC34-B: VE-lair-delivers-TPK-kills-R2** — Void Eruption as lair action delivered the killing blows to Bazgar (6HP) and Sabriel (12HP) in R2. Beholder's main body did not even get a R2 action before the TPK was sealed. This is mechanically coherent (lair + body work together as intended) but means the beholder ends the fight at 101/110 — an asymmetry that may feel unearned at the table. **Feel is neutral**: lair action is the correct pressure tool; the specific kill path (VS damage pool → VE finishes) is plausible and dramatically credible.

**FI-FC34-C: SABRIEL-SMITES-0/LoH-DD-spiral** — Sabriel dealt 0 offensive damage in this run. She used LoH to revive Marwen (who died next round anyway) and lost a L2 smite slot to DD. This pattern (LoH revival + DD slot drain = zero offensive output) confirmed for approximately the 10th time. **Feel: mixed**. The DD interaction is correct design (punishing the divine caster). The LoH revival is POSITIVE as a narrative beat. But Sabriel's arc is consistently "heal → stripped → down" with no attacks. A DM coaching note: "If the party is already down-spiraling and Sabriel's LoH won't survive 1 more round, Sabriel should attack instead of reviving."

**FI-FC34-D: BEHOLDER-101/110-at-collapse** — Beholder took 9 total damage (Bazgar's 2/4 hits halved by resistance wall). 34th-cycle resistance-wall confirm. Expected for CR13 vs L5 non-magic party. Note: Sabriel smites (radiant, not resisted) would have contributed if she had attacked; each L1 smite = 2d8 halved by altar zone suppression = avg 4.5 radiant. With 2 L1 smites that's ~9 radiant damage — the smites she never threw would have matched Bazgar's entire output.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC34-A: STAGGER-SIM-BUG-INVERTED** — Stagger sim code gates STD-B holding, but in this run STD-A had lower init (22 < 24) and should have held. Both ARs fired R1 in the sim. Correct at-table play: only STD-B fires AR R1; STD-A holds for R2. Outcome impact: Bazgar takes ~10 less damage R1 (enters R2 at ~16HP instead of 6HP), surviving VE-13 in R2, giving him one more turn. This would change outcome to approximately "Bazgar survives to R2 action; Marwen and Sabriel down via VE+STD-B-AR; Bazgar takes one more round of attacks then dies." Corrected outcome still TPK, ~R3. This is a recurring sim methodology gap (see FI-FC33-B).

**MQ-FC34-B: MANIFEST-THRALLS-vs-VE-R1-LAIR-CHOICE** — All 4 thralls alive at full HP in R1. Manifest Thralls would give each thrall +1 temp HP and a free reaction attack (4 daggers ≈ 4d4+8 ≈ 18 dmg total before PCs act) plus their regular turns. VE delivered 21 total force. Are 4 reaction dagger attacks better than 11 force to each PC? At low HP totals, yes — 4 hits (AC15 Marwen most vulnerable) could finish off a low-HP PC. DM coaching note needed: "When all thralls are alive at full HP, Manifest Thralls may create more action-economy pressure than VE."

**MQ-FC34-C: LOW-BEHOLDER-INIT-counterplay-window** — Beholder rolled init 7. This meant Bazgar acted before the beholder (Bazgar init 9), getting his Action Surge in. Per the .md timing note, low init (≤12) "creates counterplay." Bazgar did get counterplay — but 9 damage (halved) vs 110 HP doesn't constitute meaningful counterplay. The note in the .md may overstate the value of low-beholder-init for a non-magic party. Consider updating the timing note to acknowledge "counterplay requires magical weapons to exploit the window."
