### Simulation bugs (not spec bugs — no DB changes required)

**SIM-FC51-A (minor) — Stagger eval timing.**
Sim re-evaluated stagger condition after STD-B consumed its AR flag, allowing STD-A to fire AR on R1 in violation of the stagger hold rule. Correct DM behavior: pre-evaluate at round START, lock in the hold for the full round. Effect: ~16 extra party damage in R1; TPK outcome unchanged. The `.md` stagger documentation is correct and explicitly warns against this error.

**SIM-FC51-B (MATERIAL) — Aura of Protection applied to level-5 paladin.**
Code applied +Cha mod (+3) + Aura (+3) = +6 to Sabriel's DD save. Aura of Protection is a level-6 paladin feature; Sabriel is level 5 and does NOT have it. Correct roll: d20 + Cha mod = 12 + 3 = 15 < DC 16 → **DD should have SUCCEEDED.** Sabriel would have lost her L2 slot; beholder gains +4 THP. Crucially, Sabriel used LoH both turns (never attacked), so the stripped slot would not have changed her actions — TPK R3 outcome unchanged. However, this bug has likely inflated DD success-rate expectations in prior sims that may have also incorrectly applied Aura. See MQ-FC51-A.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC51-A — Marwen pre-empted before first action (structural, 51st FC cycle).**
Beholder rolled init 16 (d20+3=13+3=16), Marwen rolled init 13 (d20+2=11+2=13). Beholder acts before Marwen on R1, grapples and Maw-crits her before she casts Fireball. Marwen never cast a single spell this entire fight. For a 3-round TPK, the party's primary AoE damage (Fireball) never deployed. This pattern fires whenever the beholder rolls ≥ 11 on init (55% probability of pre-empting Marwen at init 13). Over 51 FC cycles, this is the dominant caster-assassination pathway. **DESIGN DECISION (human):** Is this intended? Options: (a) accept it — beholder hunting casters first is thematically correct; (b) lower beholder's Dex to +1 (from +3) to make caster pre-emption 30% instead of 55%; (c) add a Marwen home-rule that she can cast Fireball as a reaction when she's grappled and about to take Maw damage (trigger: first Maw damage to her). Do not auto-fix.

**FI-FC51-B — LoH healing loop: zero net value.**
Sabriel used LoH both turns to revive Marwen. Both times, Marwen was re-downed within the same round before acting. Net outcome: Sabriel expended 20 LoH HP, Marwen contributed 0 attacks, 0 spells. The "correct" play for Sabriel in this encounter is to NOT heal and instead smite the beholder — but this requires accepting a party member's death and committing to the fight. This is probably intended tension. **DM NOTE (not a bug):** At the table, signal to Sabriel's player that healing is a trap vs the beholder — the minions will re-down revived allies before they act. Let the paladin choose their own doom.

**FI-FC51-C — Thrall-derro structural zero-pressure (51st-FC confirm).**
4 TDs delivered ~3 hits and ~9 total damage across 3 rounds (vs party max HP = 125). Against AC 15–19, TDs at +3 to hit (35–15% hit rate per attack) have ~0.5 hits per turn each. They are narrative mass and target saturation, not damage dealers. This has been logged 51 times without change — flagging as STRONG RETIRE CANDIDATE for the 4-TD configuration. Recommendation: reduce to 2 TDs and replace the other 2 with a mechanic that uses their Compelled Movement ability (beholder redirects TDs as living shields or projectiles). Do not auto-fix.

**FI-FC51-D — Beholder at 63/110 HP after TPK (structural, 51st-FC pattern).**
Bazgar did all 47 HP of damage via 4 longsword hits over 2 rounds. Sabriel contributed 0 offense (LoH both turns). Marwen contributed 0 offense (pre-empted before acting). The beholder is effectively at ≥57% HP at every FC TPK in recent cycles. This is expected for CR 13 vs level 5, but combined with FI-FC51-C (TDs useless) and FI-FC51-A (Marwen pre-empted), the fight has a mechanical rhythm problem: the beholder barely exerts itself while the party implodes from minion spam + grapple autocrit. The "final confrontation" reads as a slaughter rather than a desperate last stand. **DESIGN DECISION (human):** Consider a "final confrontation" setup that gives the party better position (e.g., they arrive after clearing the derro encounters, Sabriel at full slots, Marwen already knowing the beholder's weakness). This is a narrative pacing question, not a mechanical bug.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC51-A (MATERIAL) — DD vs level-5 paladin: no Aura of Protection.**
Aura of Protection (PHB Paladin, Level 6) grants +Cha modifier to all saves for self and allies within 10 ft. Sabriel at level 5 does NOT have this feature. Correct DD save: d20 + Cha mod. If Sabriel has Cha 14 (+2): needs 14+ = 40% save = **60% DD success rate.** If Cha 16 (+3): needs 13+ = 40% save = **60% DD success rate.** Either way, DD is considerably more reliable vs a level-5 paladin than the current sim has been calculating (prior sims applied +6 to saves incorrectly). Recommend: confirm Sabriel's Cha score in `world/party/the-compass-edge/combat-roster.yml` and update sim notes. All prior DD-vs-paladin stats should be treated as inflated-save-rate estimates.

**MQ-FC51-B — DR hit rate vs Sabriel AC 19.**
Disintegration Ray has +6 to hit. vs AC 19 needs 13+ = 35% hit rate. The ray missed this cycle (roll 9). The DR is intended as a "kill shot" finisher — but against Sabriel at AC 19, it fires successfully only ~35% of the time. Against Bazgar at AC 18: 40%. Against Marwen at AC 15: 65%. Implication: the beholder should strongly prioritize DR against Marwen (if she's standing), not Sabriel. The DR targeting note (FIX-EV7-30-A) says "conscious standing targets" but doesn't rank by hit rate. Recommend: add a line noting "prefer lower-AC targets for DR; Marwen (AC 15) is optimal — 65% hit vs 35% for Sabriel."
