### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SW22-A (22nd cycle shrine-wedge — caster-escape pattern NEW SEVERITY):**  
Marwen survived at 32/32 HP (full, untouched). Both front-liners (Bazgar, Sabriel) downed. The AR cone geometry means PCs in melee get swept; the rear caster naturally stays out of cone. STDs targeted Sabriel for multiattack (correct per tactics), never Marwen. This is the *most severe caster-escape run so far* — in 19 and 21 Sabriel survived; here even Sabriel falls but Marwen is pristine. Pattern: the encounter creates intense front-liner pressure but the wizard essentially free-rolls. This is functionally a TPK if Marwen isn't there, and an easy ride when she is. **22nd-cycle confirm of the tactics gap — recommend reviewing whether STDs should chase the caster when both front-liners are bloodied.** *(Log for human — potential balance question.)*

**FI-SW22-B (22nd cycle — stagger payoff failure):**  
For the 4th consecutive shrine-wedge run, the stagger payoff (STD-B holds AR R1 → delivers tactical second-wave) failed to materialize. This run: STD-B held AR in R1 (correct), then took 29 dmg in R2 (Sabriel smite + OBR self), triggering the low-HP (<20) panic-fire rule which overrides the tactical hold. The stagger rule thus created a *one-round delay* on STD-B's AR at best. The payoff requires STD-B to stay above 20 HP long enough to use its held AR tactically — which Sabriel's smite prevents almost every run. *(Confirm of ongoing pattern — log for human.)*

**FI-SW22-C (OBR hit cluster — feel note):**  
All three OBR triggers in this run HIT (OBR vs Bazgar R1 = 12 dmg, OBR vs Bazgar R2 = 11 dmg, OBR vs Sabriel R2 = 16 dmg). Combined OBR output: 39 damage in two rounds. OBR self-damage total: 2+4+1 = 7. Ratio is approximately 5.5:1 damage-dealt-to-self-consumed. The self-damage functions as flavor not real constraint. When OBR connects at this rate the STDs deal more damage from their reaction than from their main action in the same round. *(Feel observation — may be statistical cluster; logging for pattern tracking.)*

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SW22-A (SIM-SW22-A — paralysis not enforced, 1st shrine-wedge occurrence):**  
STD-A was paralyzed by Hold Person from R1 through mid-R2, but the simulation executed Multiattack (with UF advantage) on STD-A's turns in R2 and R3. Under correct 5e paralysis rules: no actions/reactions, auto-fail Str/Dex saves, attack rolls by attackers have advantage, hits within 5 ft are critical hits. If enforced correctly: STD-A would skip both turns; Bazgar and Sabriel would land crits on it in R2, likely killing it before it can act; Bazgar likely survives (the invalid STD-A R2 Multiattack dealt ~17 dmg that dropped Bazgar to 1 HP before STD-B's AR finished him). Corrected trajectory: likely VICTORY R2-3 with Bazgar and Sabriel both alive at ~30+ HP. Fight is more PC-favorable than this sim shows. **Recommend adding paralysis-state check to harness turn-skip logic.** *(Harness bug — not NPC spec issue.)*

**MQ-SW22-B (UF threshold: OBR self-damage contributes, but when paralyzed, OBR also can't fire):**  
The .md says OBR self-damage counts toward UF threshold (10+ dmg → UF active next turn). But if the shrine-touched is paralyzed, OBR cannot fire (requires reaction). STD-A was paralyzed in R2 and would have taken 0 self-damage from OBR — the OBR that did fire was a harness artifact. UF tracking under paralysis is an untested edge case, likely irrelevant in practice since paralyzed STDs should just die under advantage attacks. *(Minor mechanical note — no change needed.)*
