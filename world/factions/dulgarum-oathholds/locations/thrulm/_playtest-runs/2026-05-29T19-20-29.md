### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-BE3-01 — Thrall Fireball fragility (table coaching note):**
Three thralls wiped by a single L3 Fireball in R1 (8d6 = 34 avg; thralls at 22 HP with no fire resistance). Two thralls clustered near the beholder were in the blast radius. Escorts evaporate in one action. This dramatically reduces escort-as-pressure-tank function from R2 onward. The beholder fought solo from R2 with only 1 thrall surviving. Scene setup should note that thralls should be **scattered** — no more than 1 per 20-ft zone. DM coaching note, no stat change needed. The fragility is appropriate for CR 1/4; the distribution is the fix.

**FI-BE3-02 — Drain Divinity LA budget exhaustion pattern:**
DD costs 3 LAs (the entire per-round budget). Beholder used DD after Bazgar's turn every round (R1 Sabriel saved; R2 Sabriel failed; R3 Sabriel failed). Result: 0/3 LA for every subsequent turn (Thrall-C, Marwen — when alive) in each round. The beholder's legendary action economy feels "spent" for 3 of 4 turns per round. Per tactics, DD over VR+Tentacle is correct when slot-holders are in range, but the feel consequence is that Void Ray and Tentacle as legendary actions **never fired in 3 rounds**. DESIGN DECISION: Is DD-first-every-round the right priority when the slot-holder is at 1 slot remaining (Sabriel R3)? The tactics note (FI-128) says to switch to VR when target is at L1-slots only. In R3, Sabriel was at L2 slot (pre-R3 drain), so DD was still valid. But if the fight continued, R4+ would have Sabriel at L1, triggering VR switch.

**FI-BE3-03 — Antireality margin-trigger with no benefit (edge case):**
Bazgar's R1 attack roll of 19 exactly equals beholder AC + 2 (17+2=19). Per DD-43, Antireality triggers on atk ≤ AC+2. +2 AC raises threshold to 19; atk = 19 → hit still lands. Reaction was consumed with zero effect. At table this reads as the beholder "flinching for nothing." Recommendation: tighten condition from `atk ≤ AC+2` to `atk < AC+2` (i.e., trigger only if raising AC by 2 would actually negate). With strict less-than, trigger range becomes 17–18 (hits that become misses), removing the degenerate "trigger but no benefit" case. **DESIGN DECISION for human review.**

**FI-BE3-04 — Void Scream R2 was fight-decisive:**
VS fired R2, dealt ~38 psychic (half on save) to all in 30-ft radius. Bazgar (24 HP, prone) failed → instantly downed. Sabriel (44 HP) failed → 8 HP remaining + frightened. The single VS effectively ended the fight: Bazgar was revived only through LoH (now depleted) and re-downed R3 by multiattack. VS's fear rider on Sabriel prevented her from regaining offensive footing (disadvantage on attacks while frightened). **Confirming:** VS is the fight's defining ability in this slice configuration. Even in the limited (no DR) slice, VS + legendary DD drain is sufficient for TPK against L5 party. Fight arc is correct: VS kills momentum, DD strips resources, lair actions finish.

**FI-BE3-05 — Thrall multiattack verb vs single_attack type (naming clarity):**
The `thrall-derro` DB action named `multiattack` is typed `single_attack` with one Hand Axe (Weak) swing (+1, 1d4+0). The .md explicitly says "no multiattack." A DM new to the encounter who issues `2 multiattack` expecting two attacks will get one. The verb `multiattack` is the action-router trigger for the melee attack, which is fine, but the action name is misleading. **Recommendation (low priority):** rename DB action to `hand_axe` or `attack`, add `multiattack` as a verb alias only. This is a naming/clarity issue, not a rules error.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-BE3-01 — Void Scream damage scale with no DR in play:**
In the limited slice (no DR), VS is the beholder's only spike damage. The 38-psychic output (dispatch) maps to 6d10 avg=33. Without DR as the primary kill threat, VS becomes the de facto finisher at range. At party L5 (Bazgar 49 HP, Sabriel 44 HP), a solo VS fail deals potentially 60 psychic (6×10) — a 1-shot even at full HP for the squishier targets. Is this intentional for the limited slice? The slice description says "tests legendary economy, lair actions, action-economy pressure" — but VS might be too decisive for a "limited" test. Consider: run slice #3 again with VS disabled (or with a house-rule cap of 4d10 for the limited config) to isolate legendary + lair action feel.

**MQ-BE3-02 — Healing spell backfire with downed PCs (no safe revive edge):**
When LoH is depleted and Sabriel has only spell slots, any healing spell (Healing Word, Cure Wounds) deals necrotic damage instead. At R4 with LoH at 0, Sabriel had no revive option beyond potions. The void hazard closes off the primary divine-martial recovery tool. This is by design but needs explicit DM communication before the first heal attempt. **Recommendation:** Add a line to the COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md flagging LoH depletion as the "point of no return" for revives in the void chamber.
