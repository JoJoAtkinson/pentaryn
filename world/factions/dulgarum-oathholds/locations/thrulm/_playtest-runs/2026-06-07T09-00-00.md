### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SW33-A** (carry from FI-SW32-A/FI-SW31-A): **2×STD-lightweight — 6/6 cycles VICTORY.** This cycle was the closest yet (Marwen + Sabriel both DOWN), but Bazgar at 31/49 was never truly threatened. The fight resolved before UF+AR rotation could sustain pressure past R3. Design recommendation unchanged: +1 thrall-derro buffer OR raise STD HP to 55-60 to allow more AR cycles and UF expression.

- **FI-SW33-B POSITIVE: Low-HP exemption fired correctly for first time.** STD-B at 15 HP in R3 used Driven Escape (BA) + AR recklessly. Even though Sabriel rolled the SAVE (15 vs DC14), half of 13 = 6 necrotic at 4 HP = DOWN. The death-thrash feel is correct: wounded STD fires one last desperate AR that kills the party's healer. **No fix needed; recommend DM narrate explicitly.**

- **FI-SW33-C POSITIVE: Unstable Form expressed meaningfully — first meaningful UF cycle.** STD-A took 23 dmg R1 (Bazgar 12 + Shatter 7 + OBR-self 4) → UF active R2 → advantage on both swings. Both swings rolled 23+ total, one HIT Sabriel for 15 (would have been ~45% chance without advantage vs AC19). Drove Sabriel to 4 HP entering R3. Without UF, the STD-A swing likely misses and Sabriel survives the fight. **UF is doing real work when the fight runs long enough — reinforces FI-SW33-A: a longer fight means more UF payoff.**

- **FI-SW33-D: OBR attack threat is weak vs high-AC targets.** 3 OBRs fired, 0/3 attack rolls hit (15 vs AC18, 14 vs AC18, 17 vs AC18). +4 to hit gives 30% hit rate vs Bazgar (AC18), 25% vs Sabriel (AC19). OBR self-damage (cumulative 4+2+4+1=11 across 3 OBRs) contributed meaningfully — the R3 OBR self-4 dropped STD-B from 5 to 1 HP, enabling Bazgar's second swing to finish it. **Feel split: OBR as chip damage is correct; OBR attack rarely landing feels weak. Consider +5 or +6 to-hit (log for human — balance change).**

- **FI-SW33-E: All-3-PCs-fail AR R1 is the high-threat scenario.** All 3 PCs failed the DC14 Dex save in R1 (saves: 9/5/6). 16 avg necrotic + 2.5 avg psychic = ~18.5 per fail. This knocked Bazgar to 32, Marwen to 15, Sabriel to 27 before anyone acted. The subsequent STD-A multiattack finished Marwen at 15 HP. This is the "correct" design intent: AR threatens the party significantly on a bad init day.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-SW33-A (SIM-SW33-A): Dual-dice-gap — AR damage should be rolled once for the area.** The simulation re-rolled 2d10 per-target rather than using the dispatch's rolled value (16) for all targets. Per 5e RAW, area damage is rolled once and applies to all who fail (or half to those who save). The dispatch is correct; the simulation methodology should use the dispatch-rolled value for all targets. No spec change needed; sim note only.

- **MQ-SW33-B: OBR to-hit bonus vs high-AC party (open from FI-SW33-D).** +4 to hit vs AC18/19 = 25-30% hit rate. If OBR is intended as a meaningful deterrent, +5 or +6 would bring it to 40-50% vs this party. Human decision: is OBR primarily a damage deterrent (higher to-hit) or a chip-damage + self-punishment mechanic (current design, where the self-psychic matters more than the counter-attack)?
