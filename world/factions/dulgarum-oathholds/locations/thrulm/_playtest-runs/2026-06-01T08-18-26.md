### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TW23-A (NEW — positive data):** CW collected successfully this cycle.
- Shardcaller (init 19) CW'd Rager at the start of R1 before any PCs acted.
- Rager (init 11) survived R1 long enough to act (27 HP remaining after Bazgar 5 dmg + Sabriel 20 dmg).
- CW advantage fired on first greataxe swing: [5,16] best=16 → total 21 vs AC 18 → HIT for 9 dmg on Bazgar.
- **First confirmed CW collection in tank-wall series.** Contrast with FI-TW22-B (22nd-cycle Rager died before its turn, CW wasted) and FI-TW20-A (CW lockout due to range).
- **Mechanism that makes it work:** Shardcaller high init (19) + Rager low init (11) creates a clean CW window BEFORE Marwen's Fireball (init 8) ends the round. As long as the Rager survives Bazgar (init 17) and Sabriel (init 14) hits before its own turn, CW pays off.
- **However:** Even with CW paying off (9 dmg on Bazgar), the fight ends in 2 rounds. Bazgar took 9 dmg and is at 40/49 HP — negligible pressure. The Rager's damage output when CW works is one hit for ~9 dmg before dying. The pairing's feel problem is not CW firing; it's that the Rager falls too fast even when CW is functional.

**FI-TW23-B (23rd confirm of Rager R1 death pattern / DD-TW-18-1):**
- Rager dies in R1 again (this cycle: 5 + 20 + 29 fire = 54 total, from 52 HP).
- Marwen's Fireball (8d6 = avg 28) is the decisive blow, but even without Fireball the Rager would reach ~27 HP by end of Rager's own turn.
- Pattern is structurally robust: level-5 party (Sabriel with L1 smite = 2d8+4 = ~13 dmg first attack; Bazgar 1d12+3 = ~9 avg; Marwen Fireball ~28) consistently applies ~50 dmg R1 to a 52-HP Rager. The "tank" role cannot survive a focused R1 burst.
- **No auto-fix possible.** This is a balance/design question: is the Rager meant to be a speed-bump that falls in R1 and buys one CW-assisted swing, or a true wall that holds for 2-3 rounds? 23 cycles of data say it cannot hold.

**FI-TW23-C (Taunt DC 12 structural mismatch, 23rd confirm):**
- Marwen rolled 15 vs DC 12 — success. At level 5, even a Wizard with -1 Cha mod has a 55% chance to pass DC 12. Taunt almost never lands on the intended caster target. Confirmed across every tank-wall cycle.
- Per existing log, this is a design decision not a bug. Logging for cumulative record.

**FI-TW23-D (PTV timing edge case — new observation):**
- Pack Tactics Voice (passive on Shardcaller): triggers when "an ally within 30 ft hits." Rager was the only ally. Rager's turn (init 11) comes AFTER Shardcaller's multiattack (init 19). So PTV can only fire on Shardcaller's attacks in R2+ if the Rager hit someone in a PRIOR round. In R1: Rager never hit before SC's turn. In R2: Rager is dead.
- **PTV is structurally inactive in this formation when SC init > Rager init.** The ally never hits before SC's own attack window. This is a new framing of FI-TW20-B ("PTV dead in standard formation") — the root cause is initiative ordering, not range. If SC's initiative were lower than Rager's, PTV would fire every turn the Rager hits.
- **Design recommendation (log for human):** The checklist note "call this out in the reply so the DM remembers it" implies PTV should be surfacing regularly. In the tank-wall slice (and wherever SC init > Rager init), PTV will never surface R1. Consider adding a note to Shardcaller .md: "PTV is inert in R1 when SC acts before all melee allies."
