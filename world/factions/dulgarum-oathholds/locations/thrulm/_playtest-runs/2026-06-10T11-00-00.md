## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **FIX-FC46-A:** `beholder-thrulm.md` Void Scream description missing FRIGHTENED condition.
  The DB `void_scream` action (`actions.jsonl`) has carried `save.notes = "...On a FAILED save, target is also FRIGHTENED of the beholder for 1 minute..."` since at least the 33rd cycle. The `.md` action description omitted this entirely, so DMs running from the `.md` (the authoritative human-readable doc) would never apply FRIGHTENED. Auto-fix: added FRIGHTENED condition description to VS paragraph in `beholder-thrulm.md`, including the Wis save to end the effect. Also added contextual DM note (FIX-FC46-A) about FRIGHTENED's interaction with grappled/prone PCs and opportunity attacks.
  Impact: FRIGHTENED (disadv on attacks when beholder visible) would have applied to Bazgar and Marwen after the R2 VS. This makes Bazgar's R3 death saves slightly faster (can't attack to break the condition while downed) and would impose disadv on any R3 attacks they might have attempted. Net effect: small mechanical correction, dramatic feel improvement (beholder's VS now has a lasting psychological hook beyond damage).

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC46-A [DESIGN DECISION — CARRY] Drain Divinity never fires (46th FC cycle)**
- Pattern: Sabriel's initiative (8) consistently falls below the beholder's initiative (15) in nearly all cycles. The beholder's R1 Maw auto-crit (grapple + auto-crit, average 41 piercing) downs Sabriel before she acts. Even with FIX-FC40-A's correct R1-LA-reserve protocol (hold all 3 LA for DD post-Sabriel), the beholder's own multiattack eliminates the DD window before Sabriel's turn.
- Root cause: Sabriel's Dex modifier is +1 (initiative bonus = +1). Beholder's Dex +3. With d20 variance, the beholder acts before Sabriel in ~70% of R1 scenarios. When it does, Maw auto-crit (nearly certain on grapple + Maw combo) downs her with average ~41 damage vs her 44 HP.
- Three distinct mechanics designed around Sabriel's presence (DD, Drain Divinity reminder in tactics, FI-FC40-A protocol) are all blocked by this single init/HP interaction.
- **Recommendation for human review:** (a) Increase Sabriel's Dex modifier to +3 (AC 19 + Dex +3 = common paladin profile), giving her average initiative ~12 and higher probability of acting before the beholder. (b) Alternatively, add explicit "DD-on-downed-Sabriel" clarification: a downed creature still has spell slots; DD targets "one creature... with spell slots" — no consciousness requirement. If DD can target downed Sabriel R2, the beholder can drain her remaining L3 slot even while she's dying. This would be unusual but worth a DM note.

**FI-FC46-B [DESIGN DECISION — CARRY] Sabriel's toolkit (smites, LoH) never expressed in 46th FC cycle**
- Sabriel downed by Maw auto-crit before she ever acts. L3 smite (never fired), L2×2 smites (never fired), LoH 25 pts (never used), +1 longsword bypass (never expressed). She contributed 0 damage.
- The design tension: Sabriel is meant to be the anti-divine target (Drain Divinity counters her smite pool). But if the beholder acts first and eliminates her before DD fires, neither side of this interaction resolves. Sabriel "absorbs a kill that would have taken Bazgar out" (Maw redirected from Bazgar to Sabriel via grapple) — that's a valid role, but it leaves her as a pure HP-buffer rather than a combatant.
- Note: This is consistent across most FC cycles. Sabriel fires at least one smite in perhaps 1/5 cycles (when she has high initiative or when the beholder misses tentacles).

**FI-FC46-C [CONFIRMED STRUCTURAL] Thralls are cosmetic**
- 3/4 thralls wiped by a single Fireball (L3 slot, 37 fire). T1 survived at 4 HP and landed zero hits over 3 rounds. Total thrall damage contribution: 0.
- Thralls exist to: create initiative slots (useful for lair-action pacing), provide Fireball targets (forces Marwen to burn a L3 slot early), and feed Manifest Thralls lair action (if alive). In this cycle: Manifest Thralls never used (all thralls dead R1). Fireball drain is the main function.
- Consider: 2 thralls at HP 30–35 would be less trivially wiped while still being consumed by Fireball. 4 thralls at 22 HP is reliably a 3/4-wipe from a single 8d6 Fireball (avg 28 fire vs 14 half-save for a DC 14 failure rate of ~45%).

**FI-FC46-D [POSITIVE] Void Scream R2 killing blow — dramatic and earned**
- VS rolled 35 psychic (6d10): simultaneously downed Marwen (15 HP remaining after AR) and critically wounded Bazgar (37→2 HP). Both failed the DC 16 Wis save.
- The arc: ST1's Ancient Resonance R1 brought Marwen from 32→15 HP; VS R2 finished her. The two abilities chain-kill in a satisfying way — AR softens the caster, VS finishes.
- This felt distinct from the Maw auto-crit (melee horror) vs VS (reality-unhitching psychic scream). Both signature abilities fired in the same encounter without competing.
- FRIGHTENED condition (now documented post FIX-FC46-A) adds an additional dread layer to VS hits.

**FI-FC46-E [BALANCE NOTE] Beholder HP damage rate: ~12 HP / 3 rounds (10.9%)**
- Party can deal only ~5 HP effective per round to the beholder (Bazgar with nonmagic greataxe = half damage). Sabriel's +1 longsword bypasses resistance but never fired. Marwen's Fireball targeted thralls (not beholder). Ray of Frost (2d8 cold) not resisted but never needed — fight was over before Marwen could pivot to targeting the beholder.
- The nonmagic resistance wall means only Sabriel (magical +1 sword) + Marwen (spells) can deal full damage. With Sabriel downed R1 and Marwen targeting thralls R1 and downed R2, the beholder's HP pool is nearly invulnerable in this cycle configuration.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC46-A [MECHANICAL QUESTION] Can Drain Divinity target an unconscious/downed creature?**
- The beholder .md says: "The beholder targets one creature within 30 feet that has spell slots, divine favor, or clerical powers."
- RAW 5e: nothing in the DD description requires the target to be conscious. Spell slots persist through unconsciousness. A downed Sabriel at 0 HP still has L3×1, L2×2, L1×2 remaining.
- If DD can target downed Sabriel, the beholder could drain her slots R2 even after she's been downed by the Maw. This would strip her smite slots before any possible revivify, making resurrection less impactful.
- **Recommendation:** Add DM note to DD: "A downed creature still has spell slots. DD targets within 30 ft with spell slots — no consciousness requirement. If Sabriel is downed and within range, DD is still valid. This is unusual but resolves the structural R1 DD window problem."

**MQ-FC46-B [MECHANICAL QUESTION] FRIGHTENED tracking overhead after FIX-FC46-A**
- With VS FRIGHTENED now documented (FIX-FC46-A), DMs must track: which PCs are frightened, who is in line of sight of the beholder, and when DC 16 Wis saves end the condition.
- At this encounter difficulty, all PCs failing the VS save is common (~70% rate for DC 16 with typical Wis modifiers). FRIGHTENED significantly affects the last PC standing (Bazgar in this cycle would have had disadvantage on all R3 attacks if conscious).
- DM cheat sheet should note: "VS FRIGHTENED: Wis save DC 16 at end of each frightened creature's turn. While frightened: disadv on attack rolls vs beholder (line of sight), cannot willingly move closer. Effects compound with grapple (can't run) and prone (already at disadv)."
