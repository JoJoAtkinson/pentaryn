## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **FIX-BEL3-2-A** — `beholder-thrulm.md` Compel Thrall retargeting note (FI-FC27-A): extended "earlier this same turn" to "OR is currently at 0 HP from any prior round". The original text only covered same-turn kills; this run showed Marwen down R1, revived R3 by LoH, CT kept targeting her original position in R2. Multi-round LoH revival cycles require the retargeting check every turn, not just on the turn of death.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-BEL3-2-B (2nd-cycle VS R1 nova confirm):** Void Scream fired R1 with all three PCs failing saves (rolls 8, 13, 11 vs DC 16). This dropped Bazgar (38→0 HP) and Marwen (25→0 HP) in a single action at the cost of a recharge-6 ability. The fight was essentially decided in R1 despite the party surviving on Sabriel's LoH for 3 more rounds. This is the second consecutive slice-3 run with VS as the decisive R1 nova. The beholder's R1 VS option is mechanically correct (per tactics), but at table this may feel like the fight was over before it began. **DM coaching note**: if VS is available R1, consider spending the bonus action `shrine_drift` to descend first (making the approach visible to players) before using VS — this signals intent and gives the table a dramatic "oh no" beat rather than an instant wipe. Alternatively, delay VS to R2 and use Multiattack R1 to establish the melee pressure threat first.

- **FI-BEL3-2-C (DD 2-consecutive-fail escape hatch check):** Drain Divinity was resisted R1 (Sabriel save 22) and R2 (save 22). Per the escape hatch rule (FI-EV7-21-B), after two consecutive rounds of zero LA output from DD, the beholder should switch to VR+TL. In R3, DD was still attempted and finally landed (save 4). By rule, R3 should have been VR (2 LA) + TL (1 LA) instead. The escape hatch is correct design; the simulation applied it improperly. **No spec change needed** — the rule is clear. **DM coaching**: re-emphasize the two-consecutive-failure threshold; DD failing Sabriel twice in a row (Cha +3, ~40% success rate per save) is within normal range, and the escape hatch preserves LA value.

- **FI-BEL3-2-D (Sabriel LoH as primary counter-mechanism confirmed):** LoH revived Bazgar R2, Marwen R3, each time extending the fight by ~1 round. LoH pool went 25→15→5. The LoH mechanic is working exactly as designed — it's the party's primary attrition buffer, and the beholder's best counter is HP pressure (Tentacle Lash LA at end of Sabriel's turns per FI-125). Sabriel was not targeted by the LA pressure tactic in this run; if TL had been used on Sabriel in R1-R2 to deplete her HP and force self-heals, the fight would have ended sooner.

- **FI-BEL3-2-E (Beholder multiattack all-miss R2 feel note):** The R2 multiattack produced three consecutive misses on Bazgar (AC 18) — roughly 17% probability. This is within normal variance, but the subjective experience at table is "the beholder failed to do anything meaningful." With DR unavailable (slice constraint), the beholder's only main-action fallback is multiattack, and three misses in a row can feel like the boss is toothless for a round. **No spec change** — this is dice variance, not a design flaw.

- **FI-BEL3-2-F (Lair action killing Marwen before her turn R4):** The void eruption lair (init 20) dropped Marwen (10 HP) before she could act on her init 19. Marwen had just been revived by LoH and was killed again without a single action. This creates a "death-feel" moment that may frustrate the table. **DESIGN DECISION for human**: is the init-20 lair action killing a just-revived PC acceptable pacing? The beholder is designed to kill the party, so this is mechanically correct, but it may feel punishing if overused. The lair rotation rule (FI-129) says don't repeat VE more than 2 consecutive rounds — this run hit VE in R3 and R4 consecutively (exactly the maximum), then VS would have been the next tool. Within spec.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-BEL3-2-A (Compel Thrall DC 16 Cha save confusion):** The `compel_thrall` DB spec says `"[ASK PLAYER: DC 16 Cha save] — on a fail, the target moves up to 30 ft..."` The "target" here is the charmed thrall — but thrall-derro has "No saves (dominated mind)" per its `.md`. At table, this "[ASK PLAYER]" prompt creates ambiguity: which player rolls the save, and for which creature? If the intent is that the thrall resists CT (a save against being directed), it conflicts with the no-save ruling. If the intent is for *a PC* to make a save (e.g., an arcane countermeasure), that's nonstandard. **Recommendation**: Remove "[ASK PLAYER: DC 16 Cha save]" from compel_thrall's effect text and replace with a plain description: "Bonus action (1/turn). One creature charmed by the beholder within 60 ft immediately moves up to 30 ft toward the beholder or a target the beholder designates. No save — the thrall is already dominated." The Cha save may be a vestige of an earlier design where CT could target non-dominated creatures.

- **MQ-BEL3-2-B (Void Scream lair-action R4 timing):** VS recharged on R4's start (recharge 6 rolled). By the time the beholder's turn would have arrived in R4, all PCs were already at 0 HP from the lair action (init 20). VS recharged but had no targets. This is a non-issue mechanically, but confirms that the recharge-6 mechanic means VS typically fires once every 3-5 rounds when most of the damage is already done. **Not a bug** — VS's fear rider is the long-term value (if the party survives), but in 4-round fights it only fires once.
