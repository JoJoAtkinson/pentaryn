### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TW-1 (DESIGN DECISION): CW on Rager before Berserk — advantage is partially wasted**

The shardcaller correctly called CW on the rager before it acted (SC init 4 > Rager init 2). The Rager then chose Berserk (the tactically correct play — 3 PCs in reach). CW advantage applied only to the *first* of three Berserk swings; the other two were flat rolls. In this run the advantage was wasted anyway (lifted swing from 5+4=9 → 10+4=14, still a miss vs Bazgar AC 18). 

The shardcaller Tactics section says "Do not Call Weakness an ally who plans to use Shard-Barrage this turn — Barrage is save-based." But there is no equivalent guard for Berserk, where advantage only helps the first of N swings and is still wasted if the target has high AC. 

**Recommendation:** Add a CW tactics note: "When the Rager has 2+ PCs in reach and Berserk is not on cooldown, hold CW for the R2 multiattack instead — Berserk burns the advantage on one of three swings and the Rager's +4 to-hit already pressures AC 16 sufficiently." This is a coaching note for the DM, not a spec change.

**FI-TW-2 (feel): Taunt nearly vestigial in 2-NPC slice**

Marwen failed Taunt's Cha save (5 vs DC 12) but targeted the Rager anyway on her next turn — the disadvantage vs non-rager attacks never triggered. In a 2-NPC encounter, the back-line caster naturally focuses the front-line threat first; Taunt's disadvantage only matters after the rager falls (when Marwen would switch to the shardcaller). By that point Taunt is long expired. 

This is a slice-composition issue, not a Taunt spec bug. In larger encounters with multiple NPCs, Taunt is meaningfully disruptive (wizard can't freely Fireball the shardcallers without penalty). Calling this out for awareness — Taunt shines in the larger formations, not in 2-NPC isolation.

**FI-TW-3 (feel): Shard Barrage low-roll event — 4 total on 3d6 (min-ish: 3)**

Both PCs saved and took 2 hp each. No visible impact on the fight outcome. The variance range (3–18) is wide and expected, but a 4-total roll that hits every PC for 2 damage isn't distinguishable from "nothing happened." Consider whether a minimum of 6 (2 per die) is worth houseruling for feel — or just accept the variance. Logging for awareness; no spec change.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TW-1: CW + Berserk interaction — spec is silent**

The `call_weakness` spec says "advantage on its next attack roll." Berserk fires multiple attack rolls. The spec doesn't clarify whether the advantage persists across all Berserk swings or is consumed by the first. Per 5e rules, "next attack roll" means the first roll; the advantage is spent there. The DB output doesn't state this. **Recommendation:** add a `note` field to the CW spec: "advantage applies to the first attack roll only — consumed after that roll regardless of hit/miss." This prevents DMs from accidentally granting multi-swing advantage.

### Simulation methodology note

The playtest bot uses two independent random number pools:
1. NPC actions: drawn from dnd_roller's pre-seeded disk cache (uint16 cache, tagged random_org)
2. PC actions and secondary resolution: drawn from Python `random` module (seed = hourly bucket + 1)

This causes the to-hit values printed in DB narrative output to differ from the values used in simulation resolution. DB showed Berserk target-1 at 22 (hit vs any reasonable AC), but sim resolved via separate pool as 14 (miss vs Bazgar AC 18). The combat outcome is still meaningful — but DMs reading the transcript should note that the DB narration and the sim HP tracking are not perfectly synchronized. This is a known class artifact (SIM-class-dual-pool) and does not reflect a spec bug.
