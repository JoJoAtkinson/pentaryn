### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-38 (NEW): Unstable Form (UF) never triggered — mechanic invisible in this run**

The UF mechanic requires an STD to take 10+ damage in a single turn. Bazgar's R2 crit dealt 8 slashing (below threshold). Sabriel's R2 hit dealt 8 slashing (below threshold). No STD ever triggered UF across all 3 rounds. For UF to activate at all, a PC needs to land hits totaling ≥10 in a single turn against the same STD. This is achievable — Bazgar Action Surge (2 attacks + 2 attacks) or Sabriel smite on a hit — but in this run the party was too suppressed by R1 AR damage to press an offensive.

**Feel question:** UF is a tactically interesting mechanic (grants advantage on multiattack, discourages pile-on), but if it never fires in 3 rounds vs a level-5 party, it may be effectively phantom at this encounter timing. Consider: (a) reduce threshold to 8+, (b) add an alternate trigger "3+ hits in the same turn from different attackers," or (c) accept that UF is a catch-mechanic for when a PC Action Surges — which is *correct* but requires the party to survive long enough to surge.

**Recommendation:** Log only. The threshold may be appropriate — a CR-3 creature having UF is a reward for sustained focus-fire, not a common buff. Wait for more cycles before adjusting.

---

**FI-39 (NEW): Driven Escape (DE) not triggered — mechanic phantom in 3-round fights**

DE triggers below 20 HP. STD-A ended R3 at 28 HP (well above threshold). In a fight resolved in 3 rounds, DE will almost never trigger because the party's damage output is too low to push a STD below 20 HP before the fight ends via TPK. DE is effectively a "what happens if a STD survives focused fire" mechanic — but the fights end too quickly.

**Feel question:** Is DE intended for longer fights (5+ rounds) where the party chips away at STDs? If so, it's probably fine for CR-3 context. If DE is supposed to create dynamic movement around the altar (retreat toward shrine while draining PCs), the mechanic needs a trigger that fires earlier — e.g., "below 30 HP" or "when Ancient Resonance is spent and both PCs are within melee."

**Recommendation:** Log only. Do NOT auto-fix. DE is a narrative beat for scenarios where the party wins — a fleeing STD is a different encounter moment. If the party always loses before DE can trigger, the mechanic exists for edge cases and one-off dynamics.

**FI-40 (NEW POSITIVE): OBR mechanic works correctly and narrates well**

OBR fired once (STD-A vs Sabriel R2 hit). The counter-attack missed (roll 7 vs AC 19). The 1-psychic self-damage is cosmetic but narratively distinct. The OBR mechanic is transparent: it fired at the right moment, the "shrine punishes it" narration is evocative, and the self-damage rider is a nice texture.

**Observation:** Against Sabriel (AC 19) and Bazgar (AC 18), OBR at +4 to hit is ~25-30% hit rate — often a miss. Against Marwen (AC 15) OBR is ~50%, more impactful. With Marwen down in R1, OBR only ever fired against high-AC targets this cycle. The mechanic is correct; it's just less scary against a party with high-AC frontliners. No action needed.

**FI-42 (NEW POSITIVE): Both-AR-R1 outcome (via MQ-28 harness bug) is more realistic than stagger rule suggests**

The stagger rule in the `.md` is clear, but in practice at a real table, a DM might forget to enforce it when both STDs are in initiative before any PC acts (initiative 22 and 9 vs PC range 13-18). If BOTH STDs fire AR R1 (ignoring stagger), the party is at ~50% HP before their second turn. This is narratively brutal and mechanically correct for a "designed to overwhelm" encounter. The stagger rule is good design — it prevents a degenerate double-AR R1 opening that trivializes the fight — but it requires active DM attention.

**Recommendation:** Add an explicit callout box to the `.md` tactics section: *"⚠️ Stagger rule is evaluated at START of round, not at each STD's individual turn. Track at top of round whether both STDs have AR recharged — if yes, mark the lower-initiative STD as 'holding AR' before any actions resolve."* This is an authoring fix.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-28 (NEW — harness): Stagger rule evaluated mid-round in sim**

The Phase B simulation tracked `ar_used` mid-round. By the time STD-B's turn resolved, STD-A's `ar_used = True` — but the stagger rule says "if both have AR recharged at start of round, only the FIRST in initiative fires." The harness should check AR availability at START of round (before any actions resolve) and pre-mark the lower-initiative STD as "holding AR" for that round. This is out of blast radius (harness code). The stated `.md` rule is correct.

**Impact:** Both STDs fired AR in R1 instead of just STD-A. Double AR R1 dealt ~43 HP to party across all PCs (vs ~25-28 HP for single AR + multiattack). This compressed the TPK from probable R4-5 to actual R3. The strategic conclusion (party loses this encounter) remains unchanged.
