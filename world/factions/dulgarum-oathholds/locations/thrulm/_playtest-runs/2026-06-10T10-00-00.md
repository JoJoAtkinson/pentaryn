### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SW1-38-A — Reload spike: double AR in same round (R2)**
The stagger rule correctly prevented both STDs firing AR in R1. However in R2, STD-A recharged at the start of its own turn (rolled 6) and fired AR; STD-B still had AR loaded from R1 stagger and also fired. Both blasted in the same round — Marwen absorbed two AR hits in R2 (total 9 psychic/necrotic combined), dropping her to 13/32. The stagger rule only catches "both STDs start the round with AR loaded." It doesn't catch the recharge-and-fire-same-round scenario. Whether this is intentional is a design question (see Mechanical Questions below).

**FI-SW1-38-B — Altar zone suppression invisible at table; Fireball and smites quietly halved**
Marwen burned her only 3rd-level slot (Fireball). Without altar zone, STD-A takes 44 fire (dead R1) and STD-B takes 22. With suppression, STD-A takes 22 and STD-B takes 11 — fight continues two more rounds. Sabriel's smites also ran at half (14 radiant instead of 28 in R2 crit). Neither PC received any cue that their fire/radiant burst was suppressed. Recommend: add a brief narration cue in the altar zone section (e.g., "the runes absorb the heat — flames die at their skin") so the DM can telegraph it and players can pivot. Currently the suppression is invisible until they notice STDs aren't dying as fast as expected.

**FI-SW1-38-C — Bazgar 0/6 hits R1+R2 (runs OK; Sabriel carries fight)**
With +7 to hit vs AC 16, Bazgar should hit on 9+ (60% per attack). Rolling 2,8,4,3,7,5 base is unlucky but not impossible. In this seed, Sabriel carried all the NPC damage (crit+smite kills both STDs). No design issue — just a bad-dice feel flag: the Fighter contributed 1 hit (6 slashing R3) in a 3-round fight. If this recurs in future seeds it would indicate an AC mismatch.

**FI-SW1-38-D — Unstable Form preference (multiattack over AR when UF+2targets) never triggered**
The UF preference in the tactics section says to prefer Multiattack when UF is active and only 2 enemies are in cone. In this run 3 PCs were always in cone so AR won regardless. The UF-preference logic dead-lettered this whole slice. Recommend running a future slice that forces ≤2 PCs in range (e.g., one PC retreating) to exercise the preference path.

**FI-SW1-38-E — Shatter 0/2 meaningful hits; Con save is too easy for STDs**
Both Shatter casts did ~half damage (or less) because STD Con save (+5) against DC 14 is a 45% fail rate. Marwen used two 2nd-level slots for minimal output. This isn't a bug — it's correct balance (STDs are tough). But the pattern (Fireball underwhelming due to altar zone, Shatter underwhelming due to Con save) means Marwen's kit is nearly entirely blunted this encounter. Only ray-of-frost type attacks or non-save spells would perform well here.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SW1-38-A — Reload spike: is double-AR-in-same-round intentional?**
When STD-A (used AR R1, recharges at start of its R2 turn) fires AR in R2, AND STD-B (held R1) also fires AR in R2, both blast the party in the same round. The stagger rule says "evaluated at START of round" based on current state. If STD-A's AR is unavailable at round start, stagger doesn't fire, and STD-A recharges and fires freely later in the same round STD-B fires. Recommendation: if both STDs firing AR in the same round is unintended, add a "mid-round stagger" check — when an STD recharges AR at start of its own turn, check if any other STD already has AR; if yes, hold until next round. This would require a note in the start-of-turn checklist.

**MQ-SW1-38-B — OBR output is instruction, not auto-resolved roll**
The OBR dispatch returns "Make one Shrine-Axe melee attack against the attacker — +4 to hit, on hit 1d8+1 slashing + 1d8 necrotic" — the DM must carry out the roll manually. All other actions auto-resolve dice inline. Is this intentional? If OBR should auto-roll to-hit against the attacker (as other attacks do), it needs to specify the attacker's AC as a parameter. Since reaction targets are dynamic, this may require DM roll regardless. No fix needed unless auto-resolution is desired.
