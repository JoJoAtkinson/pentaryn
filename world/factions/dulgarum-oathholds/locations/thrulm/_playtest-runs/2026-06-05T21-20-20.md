### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-EV7-26-A: Lair UG → VS R1 timing synergy**
Lair fires at init 20 (UG on Marwen → prone). Beholder fires at init 22 (acts AFTER lair, immediately). Marwen takes VS disadvantage from shrine proximity (existing condition), not from prone (prone doesn't affect saves). Sequential timing (2-tick gap between lair and beholder) feels intentional but may read as automatic at table. The beholder "always" benefits from the lair action it just set up because it acts almost immediately after. Consider adding a tactics note: if beholder has high init (>18), lair often fires immediately before it, creating a natural flow; if beholder has low init, the party can disrupt between lair and beholder.

**FI-EV7-26-B: Bazgar resistance wall — 26th-cycle confirm**
Without magical or sanctified weapons, Bazgar's greatsword deals ~5 effective DPR (9 total from Action Surge / 2 attacks = 4.5 per hit, halved). Against 110 HP beholder: 22+ rounds to kill solo. Fighter is functionally a diversion, not a damage contributor. Prior cycles have noted this. **Recommend human decision**: does the Compass Edge party have magical weapons in canon? If not, the solo-beholder fight is unwinnable via melee for Bazgar, which may be intentional (Sabriel's radiant smites are the intended melee damage vector).

**FI-EV7-26-C: VS average (33 psychic fail) auto-kills Marwen (32 HP)**
On average, VS on a fail deals 33 psychic vs Marwen's 32 HP — one over threshold. Any roll ≥4 on each of 6d10 kills her outright. Below-average rolls (≤32) leave her alive with 1+ HP. This is a ~50% swing on "does Marwen die this round?" The variance feels high for a party resource question. Dispatch rolled 20 this cycle (below average), which would have left Marwen alive at 12 HP — the fight continues and gets interesting. But on-average vs. above-average VS, the encounter route is entirely different.

**FI-EV7-26-D: DD temp HP noise — 26th-cycle confirm**
6 temp HP after 3 LA burned + Sabriel L3 drained. At 87 HP beholder, temp HP is irrelevant. The strategic value of DD is *slot attrition* (denying smites), not the HP gain. The .md tactics section correctly emphasizes this, but the temp HP printout may mislead a DM into thinking DD had mechanical punch. Consider removing or de-emphasizing the temp HP in the DB spec narration (or noting explicitly in tactics: "temp HP is incidental; the real value is the slot drain").

**FI-EV7-26-E: Antireality narrow-band — 26th-cycle confirm**
Bazgar rolled exactly 17 (vs AC 17), AR fired, converted to miss. The activation window is ~10% of Bazgar's hit-window rolls (rolls of 10-11 totaling 17-18). Working as designed. No change needed.

**FI-EV7-26-F: Beholder at ~87/110 HP after full R1 PC actions**
After VS (20 psychic to PCs), Fireball (14 fire back, save), and Bazgar's Action Surge (9 after resistance), beholder is at 87 HP. Bazgar, Marwen, and Sabriel collectively dealt 23 HP in R1 vs beholder's 110. The beholder entered R2 at 79% HP with all major abilities still available. This confirms the "impossible for level-5 party" framing — but it also means the fight is uninteresting mechanically (no tension about whether the beholder might die). The value of this slice is the *narrative arc* (negotiation → disintegration) not combat uncertainty.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-EV7-26-A: Dual-dice gap — class pattern, 26th-cycle**
VS dispatch output = 20 psychic. Simulation independently rolled `roll(6,10) = 41` via `random`. Sim used 41, producing false TPK-R2. Corrected analysis with dispatch dice yields corrected TPK R3 (Marwen disintegrated R2 by DR, Sabriel downed R3 by VS recharged). The class dual-dice gap is the single largest source of sim inaccuracy. Recommendation: sim should capture the rolled values FROM the dispatch output string and use those for HP tracking, rather than rolling independently. This requires parsing the narrative output (fragile) or the dispatch returning a structured `damage` field. Medium-priority harness improvement.

**MQ-EV7-26-B: Are all PCs within 10 ft of shrine at combat start?**
Sim assumed yes (all three took VS save disadvantage). The vault is described as "vast." If PCs descend the shaft and the shrine is in a *corner*, PCs at the threshold (~60 ft from shrine) would not have disadvantage. Only PCs who advance to the shrine would qualify. The .md and _overview.md do not specify starting positions. Recommend: add a "combat starting position" callout to beholder.md or _overview.md — e.g., "PCs at the threshold are 40 ft from the shrine; only PCs who advance to the altar during combat take shrine-proximity disadvantage on VS."

**MQ-EV7-26-C: Shrine-Drift self-damage never triggered**
The beholder used Shrine-Drift (bonus action) to reposition each round. Per the .md: "takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside a creature or object." In the sim, the beholder always ended outside objects (passing through, not stopping inside). This self-damage clause seems situational (only punishes a beholder that stops inside a creature/pillar deliberately). Confirm this is intended as a balance lever, not an oversight — it could matter in tight spaces.
