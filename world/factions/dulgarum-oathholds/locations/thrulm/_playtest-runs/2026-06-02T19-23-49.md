### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-FC27-B (altar zone fire suppression, 27th-cycle confirm):** Marwen's Fireball dealt normal (not doubled) damage against both shrine-touched derro in the altar zone. STD-A took 24 fire (half the 48 she'd take outside), STD-B took 12 (half of 24). This consistently under-delivers on the party's biggest AoE. The fight had Marwen spending a L3 slot for ~60% of expected output vs the STDs. DESIGN DECISION: Should the DM foreshadow altar-zone suppression before combat (DC 12 Arcana check, or a visible shimmer when approaching)? Without prior knowledge, the party will burn resources into sub-optimal targets and not understand why. Ref: FI-SW24-D, FI-SW18-A, FI-SW19-C — ongoing pattern.

- **FI-FC27-C (thrall contribution negligible):** 4 thrall-derro fired 6 attacks (1 hit on Marwen for 1 slashing, 1 hit on Marwen for 4 slashing = 5 total damage) across 2 rounds before Marwen died. +1 to-hit vs AC 15 is ~35% base, vs AC 18+ is near-useless. Thralls functioned as set dressing and compel_thrall targets, not damage dealers. This is likely by design (CR 1/4 fodder in a CR 13 encounter), but worth noting: in the final confrontation slice, thralls are irrelevant mechanically. The encounter's lethality comes entirely from beholder + STDs. DESIGN DECISION for human: is this the intended feel? Thralls as ambient pressure vs as meaningful contributors?

- **FI-FC27-D (killing blow from STD-B, not beholder):** Sabriel died to a Shrine-Axe, not to the beholder. The beholder was at 110/110 HP. While this is the second consecutive cycle where an STD lands the kill (slice #1 also has STDs as killing-blow source), for the final confrontation it may feel anticlimactic. DESIGN consideration: should the beholder always get to fire the killing blow in the "final confrontation" slice? The TPK arc (lair prone → STD AR → DR disintegrate → VS double-down → STD cleanup) flows narratively, but the player-facing death scene is "a derro hit me with an axe," not "the void-creature unmaking me." The beholder never directly touched a PC in melee.

- **FI-FC27-E (Bazgar double-kill arc):** Bazgar fell to lair void_eruption (R2), was revived by Sabriel's LoH (1 HP), then immediately killed by Void Scream (33 psychic) before his turn at init 4. This is a satisfying narrative moment — the LoH revival was immediately punished by VS, illustrating perfect beholder timing. Positive data point: the VS descent via shrine_drift (OA-safe) was the correct play, and it landed decisively.

- **FI-FC27-F (VS fires in 3-round fight — resolves FI-FC4-C):** Previous 26th-cycle ended in 2 rounds with VS never firing. This cycle went 3 rounds and VS fired in R2. Confirms that VS fires reliably when the fight extends past R2. The 2-round TPK (FI-FC4-C) was a variance artifact of beholder going first (init 12 vs lower PC inits in that cycle). In this run, with beholder at init 12, VS was still delivered on R2 — the descent via bonus shrine_drift made it OA-safe and decisive.

- **FI-FC27-G (LoH pool barely used):** Sabriel used 1 LoH charge (reviving Bazgar). The pool of 25 remained at 24 at TPK. This confirms FI-FC4-B: party resources are largely untapped at TPK. Marwen had L3/L2/L1 slots mostly intact when she died. The fight ends before resources are meaningful. This is expected at CR 13 vs level 5, but worth noting for table feel.

### Mechanical questions

- **SIM-FC27-A (stagger assignment reversed):** In R1, STD-B (init 19) acted first and was told to hold AR while STD-A (init 17) fired AR. The stagger rule says "the one acting FIRST in initiative fires." STD-B acts first, so STD-B should fire AR and STD-A should hold. My sim code had this inverted (checking `if who == "STD-B" and not state["STD-A"]["ar"]"`). The game outcome was the same (one AR per round, correct total output), but the stagger assignment was wrong. Harness-only issue; DB spec is correct. Add to sim known issues.

- **MQ-FC27-A (compel_thrall direction after disintegration):** After Marwen was disintegrated (Thrulm's turn R1), the beholder used compel_thrall as a bonus action. The valid thrall targets still existed (THR-1–4 are charmed). The direction command ("go toward Marwen") is now pointing at empty space. The mechanics of compel_thrall say "move toward the beholder or a target the beholder designates" — designating a dead PC's position is ambiguous. FIX-FC27-A added a re-target clause to the .md, but this is also a mild mechanical ambiguity: does "target" in compel_thrall have to be a living creature, or can it be a location? Per DC rules, it's a location — so directing toward Marwen's last position is technically valid even though Marwen is dust. DM coaching note added via FIX-FC27-A.

- **MQ-FC27-B (init timing of Bazgar getting up from prone and VS arrival):** Bazgar was prone (lair R2). Sabriel revived him (Sabriel init 18). THR-1/3/4 missed him. Thrulm fired VS at init 12, killing Bazgar before his turn at init 4. Bazgar never got to stand up in R2 (he was dead before his turn). Technically, Bazgar was still prone when VS hit him — but VS is a radius effect (not a targeted attack), so prone gives no benefit or penalty. However, the lair void_eruption that knocked him to 0 HP was a Dex save (prone imposes no penalty on saves). The prone condition was effectively irrelevant once he was dead and revived at 1 HP with no conditions. No ruling needed; noting for completeness.
