- **SIM-TP24-A**: The playtest harness misread the DWD multiattack compact-table output. Lines like `Hand Axe 1 to-hit 17 / dmg 4 slashing` have the final to-hit number as the third token — the harness parsed "Hit" keyword and incorrectly applied damage from format-mismatched lines. Correct reading: both DWDs' attacks missed Bazgar (AC 18) on all four swings (to-hits: 4, 8, 17, 4 — all below 18). True Bazgar HP at fight end: 45/49 (not 25/49 as harness reported). ### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-TP24-A** — **CW structural gap: Barrage-first + Fireball-wipe** (24th-cycle confirm): DSC had highest initiative (16), which per the 23rd-cycle positive finding should unlock CW. But: (1) DSC correctly held CW on R1 (Barrage-first rule — correct); (2) Fireball + Bazgar attacks cleared both DWDs by end of R1; (3) In R2, DSC was last NPC alive — no eligible CW target. CW cannot fire when: DSC uses Barrage R1 AND party AoE clears the DWD escort in the same round. This requires either (a) DWDs surviving R1 (not possible with Fireball), or (b) staggered initiative where some DWDs act *after* DSC but DSC delays CW to R2. The 23rd-cycle CW fire was only possible because Fireball didn't wipe both DWDs AND DWDs acted after DSC in the same round — a narrow window. **Structural**: CW in threshold-patrol is near-impossible unless (1) SC high init AND (2) DWDs survive R1 AND (3) no Fireball nukes the bodyguards before SC's R2 turn. Recommend human review of formation HP or whether CW is even intended to fire in this formation.

- **FI-TP24-B** — **DWD +2 to-hit irrelevant vs AC 18/19** (24th-cycle confirm): Four axe attacks (two DWDs × two each) dealt zero damage to Bazgar across all four swings (to-hits: 4, 8, 17, 4 — all miss AC 18). With Tactical Drilling advantage, expected hit rate rises to ~43.75% vs Bazgar, ~36% vs Sabriel — still low. The DWDs' primary threat is only against Marwen (AC 15). The shardcaller's Barrage opens the fight with more total party damage (14 HP across Bazgar+Marwen) than either DWD's full round. The DWDs are functioning as meatshields for the DSC, not damage dealers. **Question for human**: is this intentional? If DWDs are pure meatshields, their tactics note ("Focus-fire on caster") is well-chosen, but their melee output is near-zero vs front-liners. May want +4 to hit to make Drilling activations feel rewarding.

- **FI-TP24-C** — **Fireball-decisive 24th-cycle confirm**: Marwen's single Fireball (31 fire, DC 14) killed DWD-B outright, crippled DSC to 18/33, and reduced DWD-A to 2/27. Combined with Bazgar and Sabriel cleanup, the entire formation effectively ended R1. This is the 24th consecutive cycle in which Fireball is the decisive action. At some point the question becomes: is this formation intended to test the DSC toolkit, or just to be cleared by Fireball? If the answer is "Fireball ends it," the DSC's rich tactics (CW, TR stagger, Barrage timing) are largely invisible.

- **FI-TP24-D** — **DSC Tactical Retreat fired correctly R2**: With Bazgar closing after DWDs fell, DSC TR'd 30ft before throwing. This is the first confirmed cycle where TR resolved correctly in a solo-DSC endgame (prior cycles DSC died before TR could fire). Positive feel note — the DSC ran while throwing shards, which matches the tactical identity. The TR didn't change the outcome (both throws missed AC 18 anyway) but the narration beat was correct.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-TP24-A** — **DWD to-hit 17 vs Bazgar AC 18 (miss)**: The roller output showed `to-hit 17 / dmg 4 slashing` — this is the final attack roll (15 raw +2 bonus = 17). Vs AC 18, this is a miss. Confirms the compact-table format: the `to-hit` column is the **total final roll**, not the raw die. DMs must compare this number against AC themselves. The format is correct but could confuse if misread as "roll result before bonus." No spec change; document in harness parse logic.

- **MQ-TP24-B** — **CW self-target edge case**: In R2, DSC was last NPC alive and had 3 CW charges available. Can DSC Call Weakness on itself to gain advantage on its own shard-throw attacks? Per 5e, an ability that grants "an ally" attack advantage typically cannot self-target. The .md tactics say "target the ally hitting hardest" — implies targeting other creatures. No edge case in practice (DSC never reached this state before; it died to a CRIT). Recommend confirming in the .md: "Call Weakness targets an ally (not self)" to close the ambiguity.
