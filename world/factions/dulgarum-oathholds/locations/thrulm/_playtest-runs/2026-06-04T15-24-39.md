### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-FC30-A: DR-in-R1-eliminates-caster-before-party-orients (30th-cycle confirm)**
  Same pattern as FI-FC29-A and FI-EV7-23-A. DR fires R1 at Marwen before the party has a round to establish positioning. When DR hits (which it will ~70% of the time vs AC 15), Marwen is one-shot from full HP (44 force average vs 32 HP). This removes the party's only area-damage source before they can act. The encounter has Bazgar in init 16 and Marwen in 13 — both acted before the beholder (init 6). The "party gets a turn" design is working, but Marwen acts R1 with thralls still up, burning her L3 slot on Fireball, then dies before R2. Net result: party has no L3 slots and no caster by R2 start.
  **Coaching note (not a spec change):** DM should telegraph DR's likely target so Sabriel can position to screen. No stat change warranted — this is the intended final-confrontation dynamic.

- **FI-FC30-B: VS-recharge-5-first-possible-opportunity-30th-cycle**
  DR recharged on d6=5 in R2 (≥5 = recharged). The beholder then chose VS over the recharged DR because VS had just come off. This is working per the recharge check. However, VS recharging on 5 after just one round feels fast — the spec says "recovers on 5–6" (for DR) and "recovers on 6 only" (for VS). Wait — VS recharge is 6-only. But the sim used VS because it was available (not recharged from R1, it was first used in R2). Checking: VS was marked "not used" at round start (first time). So VS fired first use in R2. That's correct — it had never been used before. No spec bug. The fight simply went 2 rounds.

- **FI-FC30-C: VS-30-psychic-one-shot-depleted-party (30th-cycle confirm)**
  VS dealing 30 psychic on a fail (6d10 average ~33) versus a party that entered R2 at 26/32/27 HP is effectively a guaranteed TPK on failed saves. Wis saves are Bazgar +2, Sabriel +4, Marwen +1 — DC 16 means 30%/40%/25% pass rates. At ≤30 HP, any fail is lethal. **The VS fear rider never matters in this slice because the psychic damage kills before fear can express** — same note as FI-FC29-C and FI-BEL3-B. Human review: if the goal is to make the fight *feel* more deliberate rather than a one-shot, consider whether 6d10 or 5d10 changes anything. At 5d10 average 27.5, Bazgar (26 HP) still dies on a fail. The fix, if any, is earlier LoH use or party HP, not the dice.

- **FI-FC30-D: Compel-Thrall-empty-by-R2 (30th-cycle pattern)**
  Fireball in R1 consistently kills 3-4 thralls (22 HP, no dex save bonus — they fail on anything below a 15). By R2, compel_thrall has at most 1 thrall to direct. This is the 30th cycle of this slice running and CT has had effective thralls to direct for fewer than half of them. The beholder's bonus action degrades to near-zero value after the party's first Fireball. **Design question:** Is this intended? The thralls serve as ablative HP sponges in R1 and force the party to use their best spell slot early. If CT's value is "bonus in R1 when 4 thralls are up," that's fine — it's a one-round burst tool. But if the intention is for CT to be a sustained pressure tool, the thrall HP (22) and Dex saves (+0) need to be higher.

- **FI-FC30-E: DD-first-resist-then-hit cadence working (positive feel)**
  Sabriel resisted DD in R1 (save 20 — a natural 17, well above DC 16), then failed in R2 (save 8, total 11). This back-and-forth felt earned: the save created drama, then the drain landed. Beholder gained +6 temp HP from a L3 drain, which absorbed the lair's 3 force on Bazgar. The mechanical flow was clean.
