### INFRA-BUG-SW21-A — `roll_combat_action` returns empty narration in Phase B
**Symptom:** `npc_action("shrine-touched-derro", "ancient_resonance")` and `npc_action("shrine-touched-derro", "multiattack")` returned `output=""` (empty string, not a crash).

**Root cause:** Phase A pre-seeds `~/.cache/dnd_roller/quant_numbers.bin` with 4096 numbers and runs 29 actions — depleting the cache. Phase B's sim script imports `dnd_roller` at the top (triggering `_load_cache()` on the now-depleted file), loads zero in-memory entries, then re-seeds the file later — too late, the in-memory cache is already empty. When `roll_combat_action` needs dice, `_pop_numbers` tries external APIs (blocked), gets nothing, and the action falls through to `output=""` instead of raising.

**Why it didn't break Phase A:** Phase A seeded 4096 numbers BEFORE running the 29 actions in a fresh Python process.

**Impact on this playtest:** All damage numbers and saves were computed correctly by the local `rng_cache` harness. Only the narration text from `roll_combat_action` was absent. Fight results are valid.

**Recommended harness fix:** In Phase B PYSIM script, move cache seeding BEFORE `from dnd_roller import roll_combat_action`, OR after seeding, call `import dnd_roller; dnd_roller._load_cache()` to reload in-memory from the freshly written file. Not an NPC spec bug — no changes to actions.jsonl or .md needed.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SW21-A — AR back-to-back chaining almost TPK'd on a 2-STD fight**

STD-B's AR fired three times: R1 (stagger exempted, STD-A fired), R2 (freely), R3 (recharge d6=5 succeeded). Each AR hit all 3 PCs. By end-R2, Bazgar was at 15 HP and Marwen at 7. The R3 AR brought Bazgar to 1 HP and Marwen to 3. Had STD-B used AR in R2 and R3, and the recharge math repeated, this would have been a TPK by round 4 at the latest.

The 33% recharge per round means AR can chain. In a 2-STD wedge (the design intent for this slice), that's potentially brutal — especially without STD-A alive to split melee attention and OBR pressure.

*Note:* This is the 21st shrine-wedge run. Previous 19th-cycle (2026-05-30) and 20th-cycle (2026-05-31) both ended VICTORY R4 with a similarly drained Bazgar. The pattern is consistent: 2-STD shrine-wedge consistently reduces the party to low HP or one downed PC. The design intent is appropriate — this is a mid-dungeon encounter, not a warmup. **Log for human, no change needed.**

**FI-SW21-B — Unstable Form wasted on AR (confirms prior pattern)**

STD-B had UF active in R3 (adv on attacks from ≥10 dmg threshold). It also had a recharged AR. Per tactics: "AR wins if 3+ enemies in cone." 3 PCs alive → AR correctly fired. But UF advantage is irrelevant to a save-based AoE — no attack rolls involved. This is a known pattern (20th-cycle run flagged FI-SW20-C for same observation).

Design interaction feels functional: UF+AR creates wasted advantage, pushing the DM toward Multiattack if only 2 targets are present. Works as designed when the party splits focus to reduce PC count.

**FI-SW21-C — Fire/radiant suppression forced Marwen to slot-shift mid-fight**

Marwen entered the fight with Scorching Ray loaded but had to pivot to Shatter (L2, thunder) R1 and Magic Missiles (L1) R2. The altar zone anti-exploitation is working correctly and creating a meaningful tactical choice: "pull them off the altar or use non-fire damage." The Shatter choice was good (STD-A took 12 thunder, contributing to the R1 kill). Scorching Ray would have been comparable without vulnerability, but the pivot felt earned.

**MQ-SW21-A — OBR self-damage (1 psych) vs UF 10-dmg threshold interaction untested**

The .md says OBR self-damage counts toward UF threshold. In R1, STD-A fired OBR (took 1 psychic self-damage), but STD-A was killed before it could benefit from UF in R2. The "1 self-damage pushes over threshold" edge case (e.g., 9 external dmg + 1 OBR self = 10 exact, UF triggers) was never tested in this run. Worth watching for in future runs. Not a spec ambiguity — the .md is clear — but the table may forget to add the 1 self-damage to the threshold check. **Log for human awareness, no change needed.**

**MQ-SW21-B — Stagger rule evaluation: STD-B correctly held AR R1, then fired freely R2+R3**

The stagger rule fired correctly: STD-B was lower init (19 vs STD-A 21), both had AR recharged at R1 start, STD-B was not below 20 HP, so STD-B held. STD-A died R1, leaving STD-B as sole STD for R2+R3. No stagger confusion. Positive confirm on existing spec.
