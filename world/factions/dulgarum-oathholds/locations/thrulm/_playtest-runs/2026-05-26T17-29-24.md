### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-158 NEW (CRITICAL): First shrine-wedge TPK in 4 cycles. Root cause: STD-A init 23 > Marwen 21.**

Prior 3 cycles: VICTORY. Marwen acted first → Slow slows STD-A before AR fires → STDs attack at -2 AC + no reactions. This cycle: STD-A acts first → AR craters entire party (15 dmg each) → stagger-MA kills Marwen before she takes a turn. Encounter trajectory is a binary branch on one initiative roll (STD +5 vs Marwen +3). The 2-point edge is too thin to be meaningful design; it reads as pure RNG determining a TPK/VICTORY split.

**FI-159 NEW DESIGN DECISION: Initiative-dependence of shrine-wedge is too high.**

Across 4 cycles: 3× VICTORY (Marwen init ≥ STD-A) / 1× TPK (STD-A init > Marwen). The entire encounter pivots on whether Slow lands before AR fires. Options for human review:
- (a) **Give party a surprise round** if they approach stealthily (scouting the chamber entrance). This removes the coin-flip entirely.
- (b) **Reduce STD initiative bonus** (+5 Dex mod → +3). Narrows the gap but doesn't eliminate it.
- (c) **Script a delayed AR** — STDs don't fire AR in R1 (they're still "resonating" with the shrine). R1 is their setup round; AR available from R2 onward. This most strongly rewards aggressive PC action.
- (d) **Accept the variance** — some tables like binary high-variance combat. The 75% VICTORY / 25% TPK split might be intended as "this fight sometimes just goes wrong."

**FI-160 NEW FEEL: Marwen downed R1 without taking a single action (first time in shrine-wedge).**

Sequence: STD-A AR (17 HP remaining) → STD-B stagger-MA (0 HP). Marwen was 21 init but STD-A was 23. The player's highest-initiative PC was KO'd without casting a spell. This is the most frustrating possible outcome in a tabletop fight — no agency.

**FI-161 NEW POSITIVE: Stagger rule functioned correctly for 4th consecutive cycle.**

STD-B correctly held AR both rounds (R1 because both recharged, R2 for same reason). The stagger rule prevents the double-AR alpha strike that would have made this encounter nearly impossible in any configuration.

**FI-162 NEW FEEL: OBR is a Bazgar trap — his "hit the low-HP target" aggression triggers OBR every time he lands a hit.**

R1: Bazgar hits STD-A → OBR fires → 12 damage to Bazgar → Bazgar enters R2 at 22 HP (down from 49). Then UF-advantage MA finishes him in 2 hits. OBR + UF is a punishing feedback loop: OBR self-damage procs UF for the STD, making the retaliation-MA more lethal next turn. This is good design — it creates an interesting tactical question for Bazgar (attack the same target again, or spread damage to interrupt UF?). Worth flagging as a positive design discovery.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-NEW (minor): AR extra psychic damage is a DM-roll instruction, not included in dispatcher output number.**

The `ancient_resonance` dispatcher outputs the base 2d10 necrotic damage and tells the DM to "roll separately" for 1d4 psychic per failer. This is correct per the design. Sim inflated the damage slightly by combining both. No game bug — just a sim note for future playtest writers.
