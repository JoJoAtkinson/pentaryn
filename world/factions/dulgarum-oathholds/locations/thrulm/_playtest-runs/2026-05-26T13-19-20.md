### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-135 (POSITIVE, new):** Slow opener dramatically changed R1 feel — all 3 ragers failed Wis saves (2, 3, 3 vs DC15). Rager R1 output dropped from typical 3-4 hits to 1 hit (rager_C's 12 to Marwen). AC −2 on all ragers made Bazgar's Action Surge 3-for-4 hits more likely. The asymmetry (wizard opens with Slow instead of Fireball) created a visibly different fight texture. Mechanically, Slow is concentration — the wizard trades Fireball slot for sustained-debuff-plus-later-Fireball.

- **FI-136 (POSITIVE MILESTONE, new):** First time in 9 solo-rager-rush cycles that Berserk fired on all three ragers (R2). This is the slice's primary test objective. The extension from R2 → R3 (Slow-induced) was the enabling factor. DD-14/DD-26 are partially mitigated by Slow opener: Slow + 2× Fireball took 3 rounds vs Fireball + Fireball taking 2. If Marwen had Fireball on R1, fight would have resolved R2 again. Conclusion: **rager HP remains the root constraint**, but the Slow opener confirms the fight CAN extend to 3+ rounds with different wizard choices.

- **FI-137 (new — Taunt-with-Slow interaction):** Bazgar taunted by rager_B in R1 (d20=11, failed DC12 Cha). In R2 Bazgar faced choice: attack rager_A at disadvantage OR attack taunter rager_B with no disadvantage. He attacked rager_A (lowest HP) at disadvantage, rolling [8,1] and [1,5] — 0 hits. This is a DM call decision (optimal play would attack rager_B to avoid disadv), but the mechanic correctly forced a routing decision. **Taunt has real bite in rounds 2+ when Berserk doesn't wipe targets first.** The Tactics note could be more explicit for the PC side: "A PC taunted by a rager can avoid disadvantage by attacking the taunting rager instead of another target."

- **FI-138 (9th-cycle confirm — FI-97):** Aggro marks were set (Bazgar hit rager_A 3× in R1; Marwen's Fireball R2 set marks on surviving ragers), but Berserk R2 bypassed all marks — Berserk hits ALL PCs in reach regardless of mark. Aggro-mark only affects targeting in standard Multiattack turns. In this 3-round fight, only R1 Multiattack turn used marks (rager_C targeted Marwen per lowest-HP logic, not aggro-mark specifically). FI-97 remains confirmed: mark expression is minimal in Berserk-dominant rounds.

- **FI-140 (POSITIVE, new):** First 3-round fight in 9 cycles. Previous cycles: 1st=9R, 2nd=5R, 3rd=2R, 4th=2R, 5th=2R, 6th=2R, 7th=2R, 8th=2R. The Slow opener is the only tested mechanism that pushes past 2 rounds. This validates that the slice CAN achieve its test objectives if the DM deliberately avoids Fireball R1.

- **FI-141 (POSITIVE, new — MILESTONE): First Berserk recharge ever in solo-rager-rush.** rager_B recharged on d6=6 in R3 (exact 5-6 threshold). The recharged Berserk swept all 3 PCs and missed 0/3 (rolls 1, 2, 5 — all natural misses). The recharge mechanism worked correctly; the outcome was anticlimactic (zero damage). **Feel note for DM:** at table, narrate the recharged Berserk as "one last frantic whirl" even if all misses — the sound/visual of the axe arc communicates the mechanic's presence. Zero-damage recharge is rare (all-miss on 3 attacks against AC 15-19 with +5 happens ~(8/20)^3 ≈ 5% per attack combo) but possible. Not a balance issue.

- **FI-143 (DESIGN DECISION — new):** Slow is more reliable vs ragers than Fireball in narrow corridors when the DM has concentration available. All-fail on 3 Wis saves (2, 3, 3) was unlucky for ragers but only a 34% event. Even with 1-2 ragers resisting, Slow on 1-2 ragers + Fireball R2 still extends the fight. **Question: Should derro-ragers have Wis save proficiency?** Current stats: no Wis proficiency assumed (+0 modifier). Thematically, ragers have Madness Endurance (they endure pain, not psychic clarity), so Wis −3 is flavor-appropriate. Adding +4 Wis save proficiency would raise success threshold to ~50%, making all-fail 12.5% — much rarer. Low-risk change, but changes the Slow vs. Fireball tradeoff for wizard players at table. **Human decision needed — do not auto-fix.**
