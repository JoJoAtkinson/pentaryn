## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed
- none (all 29 Phase A actions executed cleanly; OBR not auto-rolling is a design decision, not a spec crash)

### Simulation methodology gaps (not NPC spec bugs)
- **SIM-SW31-A UF timing (current-round vs last-round)**: Sim activated Unstable Form for STD-A in Round 1 using damage accumulated in that same round (18 from Sabriel), rather than checking previous-round damage. Correct behavior: STD-A should NOT have UF R1 (no prior round). This caused STD-A to skip AR and use Multiattack instead. Corrected: if STD-A had fired AR R1, Bazgar and Sabriel would have faced ~11 necrotic on fail (avg 2d10=11) plus 1d4 psychic — potentially 14–15 damage before UF is even relevant.
- **SIM-SW31-B Bazgar/Sabriel target selection**: Script continued attacking downed STD-A in Rounds 3–4 instead of redirecting to STD-B. In real play, the fight resolves R3 with focused fire on STD-B (MM + two melee attacks would have ended it). Five rounds is a simulation artifact; real fight is likely R3–4.
- **SIM-SW31-C AR half-damage display**: Sim showed "8 necrotic" as Sabriel's saved half of 2 necrotic. Correct: 2//2 = 1 necrotic. Minor math error in display.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SW31-A: 2×STD too lightweight — 31st-cycle VICTORY**
Three shrine-wedge cycles: R4 victory (29th), R2 victory (30th), R5 victory (31st). The 2×STD configuration is consistently beatable in ≤5 rounds for Compass Edge at level 5. UF was never expressed in any cycle (STDs die before their UF turn). Ancient Resonance fired only once this cycle (and rolled minimum 2 on 2d10). The shrine-wedge slice doesn't showcase either of the STD's defining mechanics. Recommend: add 1× thrall-derro buffer OR increase STD HP to 55–60 to give the fight more tempo.

**FI-SW31-B: Ancient Resonance extreme low-roll (2d10=2)**
STD-B fired AR in R3 for 2 total necrotic. Both PCs saved → 1 necrotic each. After spending a recharge action slot, AR contributed zero meaningful threat. The 2d10 range (2–20, avg 11) creates enormous variance. At the table, a 2-necrotic AR roll after waiting 3 rounds for the slot to recharge will feel demoralizing to the DM. This is a recurring feel issue (previous cycles noted same concern). No spec change recommended yet — variance is intentional — but the DM should be coached that AR's recharge tension is the interesting part, not individual roll outcomes.

**FI-SW31-C: OBR counter-attack not auto-rolled — 31st-cycle confirm**
`roll_combat_action("shrine-touched-derro", "oath_breaking_retaliation")` returns narration text and `rolls: {}`. The +4 to-hit and 1d8+1+1d8 counter-swing must be hand-rolled by the DM. This is the ongoing OBR design decision from cycles 29–30. At a busy table, OBR timing is already easy to forget (fires mid-other-turn); adding a hand-roll increases the cognitive load. Recommend: change OBR DB spec from `reaction/buff` (effect-text only) to a structured attack field so the combat runner auto-rolls the counter-swing.

**FI-SW31-D: Altar zone fire+radiant suppression — 31st-cycle confirm**
Fireball 30 damage → both STDs saved → 15 fire each (no vulnerability). Outside altar zone, failing targets would take 30×2=60. The suppression is mechanically sound and well-documented in the .md. The tactical counterplay (pull STDs away to expose vulnerability) has never been realized across all 3 shrine-wedge cycles — party goes to melee, STDs stay near altar, vulnerability never triggers. DM needs a proactive signal. Recommend: add a COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET callout: "Altar Zone active: fire+radiant deals NORMAL damage to STDs within 60 ft. Call it out when a fire/radiant attack lands and announce the suppression."

**FI-SW31-E: UF payoff never expressed — 31st-cycle confirm**
In all three shrine-wedge cycles, Unstable Form activated (both STDs took 10+ damage) but neither STD survived to act under UF with correct timing. The UF mechanic is interesting on paper (advantage on attacks as a reward for tanking a big hit) but the party's burst damage pattern kills STDs in the same round or the following round before UF can be expressed. This is tied to FI-SW31-A (too lightweight). With higher HP, STDs would regularly act under UF.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SW31-A: OBR auto-roll recommendation (ongoing)**
Should the `oath_breaking_retaliation` action spec be restructured to include a `single_attack` field so the combat runner rolls the +4 to-hit and 1d8+1+1d8 damage automatically? Currently classified as `reaction/buff` with attack in effect text. Three options:
1. Keep as-is (DM hand-rolls; minimal spec change)
2. Add `attack` sub-field to the reaction spec and update the roller to execute it
3. Split into narration dispatch + separate `shrine_axe_single` action the DM calls manually

Recommend option 2 if the roller supports it; option 1 is acceptable at-table with strong DM coaching.

**MQ-SW31-B: UF same-round vs last-round ambiguity**
The .md says "if shrine-touched took 10+ damage in a single turn last round." In Round 1, Fireball hits STD-B before STD-B's turn in the SAME round. Does this count as "last round" for STD-B's R1 start-of-turn check? If initiative order is treated as the boundary, it is "previous initiative slot" damage — argument can be made it qualifies. Current sim activated UF; real table would likely do the same. The .md wording could be clarified: "in the same or previous round of initiative" or "before the start of this turn."

**MQ-SW31-C: Stagger rule release when higher-init STD can't fire AR**
In R1, STD-A (higher init) skipped AR due to (incorrect) UF preference. STD-B's stagger hold applied regardless. If STD-A legitimately cannot or does not fire AR (UF correctly suppresses it), does the stagger hold on STD-B release? Per current .md: stagger is evaluated at start of round — once the hold is applied, STD-B is bound to Multiattack for that round regardless of what STD-A does. This seems correct (prevents retroactive release) but creates a scenario where neither STD fires AR R1 even when both have it available. Worth a one-liner clarification in tactics: "stagger hold is evaluated and locked at round-start regardless of what STD-A ultimately does."
