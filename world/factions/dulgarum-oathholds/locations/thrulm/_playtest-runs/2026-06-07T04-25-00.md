## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **FIX-FC35-A** `shrine-touched-derro.md` stagger hold persistence note added. The existing stagger rule said "Do NOT wait until the lower-initiative STD's turn to make this check" but didn't explicitly state that the hold persists after the higher-init STD fires and clears its own AR flag. Added: "⚠️ **Stagger hold persists even after the higher-init STD fires:** once the lower-init STD is marked 'holding AR' at round start, that hold is permanent for the round — it does NOT lift if the higher-init STD clears its own AR-available flag by acting." This is the 35th-cycle confirm of the evaluation-order bug (MQ-FC34-A, MQ-FC33-B, MQ-SW32-A).

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-FC35-A Void Scream zero contribution (35th-cycle confirm):** VS was available at fight start but deliberately delayed to R2+; all R2+ recharge rolls failed (d6 < 6 all 5 rounds). In prior cycles VS fired R1 and killed Marwen before she acted; here VS failing to fire let Marwen cast Fireball (29 fire to beholder + wiped both STDs). VS is all-or-nothing: when it fires R1 it's a fight-winner for the NPCs; when it doesn't (or is delayed), it contributes zero. **Recharge 6 = 17% per round** is too low for a 5-round fight. *Carry FI-BEL3-37-F / FI-EV7-28-C. Confirmed across empty-void, beholder-escorts-limited, and now final-confrontation: recommend bumping VS to Recharge 5–6.*

- **FI-FC35-B Marwen Fireball decisive when VS doesn't fire R1:** With VS absent, Marwen's Fireball hit 5 targets (29 fire), brought the beholder to 69/110 HP, and wiped both shrine-touched to 16 HP (setting up Bazgar to finish them). This is the single most impactful action of the fight. The pattern is: VS fires R1 → Marwen dies before acting → Fireball never cast → beholder largely undamaged. VS doesn't fire → Marwen acts → Fireball craters the NPC cluster. This bimodal outcome (Marwen acts vs doesn't) is the structural hinge of slice #4.

- **FI-FC35-C DD-3LA-monopoly-35th-confirm:** Beholder used all 3 legendary actions on Drain Divinity every single PC turn (R1, R2, R4 fired; R3 blocked by nat-high save). No Void Ray, no Tentacle LA fired. DD stripped 7 total spell levels from Sabriel (L3+L3+L2+L2 drained). The THP gain (20 total) is noise vs 110 HP base. The real impact: denied ~54 radiant damage from smites that never happened. *Carry FI-EV7-28-A / FI-BEL3-37-A. The DD-3LA-monopoly pattern is now confirmed in every beholder encounter. Recommend: add explicit tactic note "Once Sabriel is out of L2+ slots, switch to Void Ray or Tentacle LA — DD at L1 yields only 2 THP and a minor slot denial."*

- **FI-FC35-D Shrine-touched erased Round 1:** Both STDs down by end of R1 (STD-A by Bazgar's longsword, STD-B by the combination of Bazgar + OBR self-damage on the counter). Both fired AR but couldn't survive Fireball (29 damage + prior hits). At 45 HP, they cannot absorb a Fireball and continue contributing. They got their one AR cycle and died. No second-round AR, no Driven Escape, no Unstable Form activation. *The shrine-touched feel cosmetically present but mechanically irrelevant in the final-confrontation formation — they serve as one-shot AR launchers then disappear. See also FI-FC33-B/FI-FC34-D.*

- **FI-FC35-E Manifest Thralls lair 0 impact (all 3 uses):** Beholder used Manifest Thralls on init 20 in R1/R2/R3. Each use: +1 THP and a free reaction attack for up to 3 thralls. Thralls attempted 10 total attacks across 5 rounds and landed 1 hit (Thrall-3, R1: 4 slashing damage). The +1 THP was never relevant. The lair's value is theoretically the reaction attack, but with +1 to-hit vs AC 18-19, the thralls have ~15% hit chance. The beholder switched to Unstable Ground in R4/R5 (thrall hit rate math finally gave up), which actually worked (Sabriel prone both rounds). **Manifest Thralls should specify a fallback condition for low-hit-probability thralls, not just zero-thrall dead fallback.** The existing DM note covers "no thralls alive" but not "thralls present but ineffective." Carry FI-FC34-B / MQ-FC34-B.

- **FI-FC35-F Death earned:** The kill that mattered was the Maw in R5 landing on a 7-HP Sabriel. She spent four rounds at 7 HP, reviving allies (Bazgar ×3, each time immediately re-downed within one round). LoH was the fuel keeping the party alive. The moment it became insufficient (Bazgar re-downed by Thrall-3's hand axe before the beholder acted) and the beholder's multiattack landed cleanly, the fight ended. The Maw-into-grapple visual — three hits on a prone, solo paladin — felt appropriate. **Death was earned and paced correctly.**

- **FI-FC35-G Antireality reaction never triggered:** Beholder AC 17, few attacks hit (2 total in 5 rounds, both glancing). Antireality's +2 AC declared-after-seeing-roll mechanic has high value but requires many attacks to land — in this low-damage-to-beholder fight it went unused. At 60/110 HP after 5 rounds of combat, the party was never close to threatening the beholder. The reaction is mechanically sound but feel-invisible when the party can't hit.

- **FI-FC35-H Disintegration Ray never tested:** DR initialized as unavailable in the simulation (Recharge 5-6, started False, no recharge rolls computed). With Recharge 5-6 = 1/3 per round, DR expected ~1.5 recharge opportunities in 5 rounds. **This is a simulation gap, not a spec bug.** A real run would likely see DR fire once. At 45 (10d8) force damage, DR would one-shot Marwen (32 HP) or nearly one-shot Bazgar (49 HP). The disintegration-on-0-HP kill condition is the encounter's most memorable capability and hasn't been cleanly tested in final-confrontation formation. Carry MQ-FC34-B.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-FC35-A Stagger-hold simulation gap (35th-cycle):** The stagger rule now has clearer documentation (FIX-FC35-A), but the root cause (evaluation after higher-init STD acts) cannot be auto-prevented by documentation alone — it requires DM discipline or a physical token. Recommend adding to COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET: "Stagger pre-check: at round start, before ANY dice roll, physically mark whether the lower-init STD is holding AR this round."

- **MQ-FC35-B Maw auto-crit ordering in dispatch output:** The multiattack dispatch output shows all three to-hit rolls simultaneously. DMs may not know to resolve TL1 → grapple check → TL2 → grapple check → Maw (which is auto-crit if target is grappled). In R5 Sabriel was hit by TL1 and grappled, then TL2 also hit, but the Maw result (24 piercing) didn't reflect the auto-crit bonus because the simulation resolved them as independent. The Maw-crit-if-grappled feature never expressed in this run. *Carry DD-BEL3-32-A. Recommend adding a sequence note to the multiattack action: "Resolve in order: TL1 (grapple check if hit) → TL2 (grapple check if hit) → Maw (apply crit if target is grappled before Maw resolves)."*
