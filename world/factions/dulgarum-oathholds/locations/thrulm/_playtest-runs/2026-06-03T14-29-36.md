### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SRR11-A — Berserk pre-Fireball burst pattern (11th cycle confirm)**
All 3 ragers Berserk R1, but initiative sequencing determines output: R3 (init 15) and R2 (init 13) fire before Marwen (init 6). R3: 1/2 hits (14 dmg Bazgar). R2: 0/2 hits. R1 (init 5, post-Fireball at 25 HP): 2/2 hits (6 Bazgar + 12 Marwen). Total pre-Fireball berserk output: 14 dmg. Post-Fireball R1 berserk: 18 dmg. The "opening shock" of 3× Berserk depends almost entirely on whether ragers act before Marwen — highly variable by initiative. Not a spec bug; DM coaching: "Rager Berserk before the wizard acts is the correct play even if initiative is low."

**FI-SRR11-B — Sabriel AC19 wall (11th cycle confirm, SEVERITY HIGH)**
R3 targeted Sabriel every round and landed 0/6 attacks across R1-R4. R3 needs 15+ to hit (d20 ≥ 11 with +4 bonus). 0/6 = (0.65)^6 ≈ 7.5% — rare but happened again (cf. FI-SR9-A, FI-SRR10-B). More importantly, the STRUCTURAL problem: R3 is positioned adjacent to Sabriel (the highest-AC target) and will always underperform. Recommendation: add tactic note — "if rager is 0/2 attacks on AC19 target, redirect to AC15 Marwen for next turn." Human review: should encounter coaching specify a rager-to-Marwen redirect trigger?

**FI-SRR11-C — Berserk recharge drought post-R1 (pattern confirm)**
6 consecutive Berserk recharge fails across R2-R3 (all three ragers). Berserk fires once at R1 full strength, then disappears for the fight. The memorable flagship ability is a one-time event in most 4-6 round fights. DESIGN DECISION: consider recharge 4-5-6 instead of 5-6 to give ~50% recharge chance per turn.

**FI-SRR11-D — Taunt on Sabriel mechanically vestigial when taunter = target (11th cycle confirm)**
R3 Taunted Sabriel in R1, R2, R4 (all FAIL sv). But Sabriel's primary attack target is always the lowest-HP rager — which is R3 in most rounds. The Taunt disadvantage applies ONLY when Sabriel attacks someone OTHER than R3. Since Sabriel consistently focuses R3, the Taunt is inert. (Cf. FI-SRR10-D DC12 structural limit; this is an additional mechanical dead-end.) Recommendation: R3 should Taunt MARWEN (re-assign TAUNT_MAP) to force the wizard off optimal targeting, rather than wasting Taunt on Sabriel.

**FI-SRR11-E — LoH as the decisive margin (POSITIVE)**
Sabriel used her full 25 HP LoH pool over R2-R3 keeping Bazgar up. Without LoH, Bazgar would have been downed R2 (at 11 HP after R2 MA). LoH was a genuine game-changer. This is a positive design confirm — the divine martial's healing output is proportionate to the threat level of the rager focus-fire.

**FI-SRR11-F — Scorching Ray 0/3 hits in R2 (L2 slot waste)**
Marwen spent her second L2 slot on Scorching Ray (3 rays vs R1 at AC16, spell atk +5). Hit rate per ray ≈ 50%; all-miss probability (0.5)^3 = 12.5%. This is the second cycle where the L2 slot produced 0 output. DESIGN note (not spec): DM coaching should suggest Fire Bolt or Shatter when rager has low HP remaining — concentrating damage on finishing vs spreading.

**SIM-SRR11-A — Taunt disadvantage not applied to Bazgar attacking R3 (R4)**

His to-hits were 23 and 21; the PRONE condition on R3 (from trip) gives Bazgar advantage on adjacent melee — these cancel to a straight roll. Net effect: (adv from prone) + (disadv from taunt) = straight roll. Outcome: identical to sim (23 and 21 are both hits). No material impact, but coaching note needed in rager.md: "Taunt disadvantage and Prone advantage cancel to straight roll when both apply."
