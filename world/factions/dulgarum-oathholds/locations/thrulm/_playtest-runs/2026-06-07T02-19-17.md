### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TW-1 — CW never fires in tank-wall; positioning breaks core mechanic.**
The SC's primary support ability (Call Weakness) requires an ally within 30 ft. The standard tank-wall positioning places the SC 55 ft from the party. The Rager charges 25 ft in R1 — leaving a ~30 ft SC-to-Rager gap at minimum (if SC acts first) and a ~55 ft gap (if Rager acts first, as in this run). In this seed, SC acted last (init 4); Rager had already charged to 0 ft before SC's first turn, making CW impossible for the entire encounter. CW charges used: 0/3. The mechanic being tested by this slice never activated.

*Recommendation:* Specify in the tank-wall scenario that SC starts 20–25 ft from party front (not 55 ft), keeping SC within 30 ft of the Rager at all times. Alternatively, extend CW range to 60 ft.

**FI-TW-2 — Rager dies before second turn; tanks nothing.**
52 HP was erased by 3 PCs in one round of focused fire (11+5+19+14 = 49 damage, dead mid-R2). The Rager never gets a Berserk follow-up, never Taunts meaningfully, and the "tank wall" feel collapses. The encounter is decided before the SC's first offensive action matters.

*Recommendation (log for human):* This is a known CR 2 fragility. A tank-wall slice probably needs either 2 Ragers or a Rager with legendary-ish HP for the slice to function as a "wall." This is a balance design decision, not an auto-fix.

**FI-TW-3 — Taunt target priority not specified; Bazgar taunted when Marwen should be.**
The Rager taunted Bazgar (highest HP, already focusing Rager) — mechanically zero effect. The correct Taunt target in this setup is Marwen (caster, not in melee), where "disadvantage on attacks vs non-Rager" would force her to either redirect Scorching Rays at the Rager or fire at disadvantage against the SC. The .md says "forces a target to either hit it or suffer disadvantage" but doesn't specify targeting priority (caster first? soft-target first?).

*Recommendation:* Add a Taunt priority note to derro-rager.md: "Prefer the PC NOT already engaging you in melee — taunting the back-line caster or healer is highest value."

**FI-TW-4 — Berserk output always shows 3 attack lines regardless of enemies in reach.**
When dispatching "berserk" with 2 PCs in reach, the output table shows 3 rows (target 1, 2, 3). The .md has a note about this ("DB output shows 3 attack lines — cap at actual in reach"), but the DM must manually read and apply the caveat. A DM unfamiliar with this note will roll damage for a phantom third target. Low-severity, well-documented, but could confuse new DMs.

*Recommendation (log for human):* Consider whether the spec can dynamically cap attack entries, or whether the existing doc note is sufficient.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TW-A — Simulation correctness: dispatch output vs re-roll.**
My fight simulation dispatched `berserk` (consuming cached d20 rolls) and then independently re-rolled for the attack resolution. This means the simulation's HP tracking is slightly inaccurate (Berserk's actual dispatched rolls were all misses vs AC 18/19, but the simulation recorded a hit on Sabriel for 9 damage). This is a simulation bug, not a spec bug. Noting it so the outcome HP numbers are interpreted with appropriate skepticism.

**MQ-TW-B — Recharge tracking start-of-encounter vs start-of-turn.**
Per 5e rules, recharge abilities are available at the start of combat (no first-turn roll needed). My simulation rolled recharge at the top of each round rather than at the start of each NPC's own turn. In R1, Berserk "recharged" before any use (which is correct — it starts available — but for the wrong reason). Subsequent rounds: recharge should fire at start of the creature's OWN turn. Not a spec bug; a simulation methodology note for future runs.
