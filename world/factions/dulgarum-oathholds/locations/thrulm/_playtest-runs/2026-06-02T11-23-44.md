### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC4-A: Marwen's killing blow comes from a shrine-touched, not the beholder**
The slice-4 design goal is "see WHICH ability lands the killing blow and whether the death feels earned." Marwen was dropped by ST-1's Ancient Resonance (combined with DR half-damage softening her first). This is mechanically correct — a 32 HP wizard who takes 26 half-damage from DR and then enters range of a 15-hp area-necrotic is dead. But it feels anticlimactic: the party wizard dies to a side-mob, not the boss. At table this might land fine (the encounter as a whole feels overwhelming), but the beholder's marquee kill was "wasted" on a SAVE result vs its flagship DR. Consider: DM option to reorder initiative so beholder acts before shrine-touched in R1, ensuring DR + main action is the primary kill driver.

**FI-FC4-B: 2-round TPK leaves most PC resources untouched**
Sabriel's LoH pool (25 HP), 2 divine smite slots, and Marwen's 7 remaining slots all survived. The encounter resolves so fast that the PCs never get to mount a last stand or make meaningful resource decisions. This is probably unavoidable with slice-4's NPCs (beholder full + 4 thralls + 2 STs), but worth noting: a table that wants a "feel the weight" experience should run slices 3 or earlier before dropping slice-4, so players have seen the beholder's toolkit before dying to it. Recommend adding a DM note to slice-4's entry in the briefing: "run this after the party has seen at least one previous beholder slice."

**FI-FC4-C: Void Scream never fires in 2-round TPK**
VS has Recharge 6 (16.7%/round). Across 2 rounds it never recharged (rolls: 1, 3). In slice-4 as configured, VS is effectively absent. Yet the tactics section (FI-149) calls VS the beholder's "most durable effect" via the fear rider. This ability is invisible in the most extreme encounter slice. Design question: should VS start available in slice-4 to ensure the fear rider is demonstrated at least once? The current setup means testing VS requires either a lucky recharge or a multi-round fight, neither of which slice-4 reliably produces.

**FI-FC4-D: Bazgar's Battlemaster Trip Attack is wasted every round**
MQ-82 (beholder hover-immune to prone) is correct, but it means Bazgar's primary Battlemaster maneuver does only bonus dice damage without the tactical effect. In a 2-round fight where Bazgar misses R1 Atk1 and spends R2 prone + taking VR damage, he functionally contributes 8 damage to the beholder before going down. At table this will feel frustrating for the Fighter player. The tactics section notes this, but consider adding a player-facing advisory in the encounter setup: "If a player brings a Trip Attack Battlemaster, warn them before initiative that the beholder hovers."

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC4-A: AR stagger rule requires pre-round marking — sim code violated it**
In the sim, the stagger check was re-evaluated at ST-2's turn (after ST-1 had already consumed AR), causing `both_ar_alive` to read False and bypassing the hold. Both STs fired AR in R1 — double-cone necrotic into a 3-PC group is catastrophic (contributed to R1 Marwen kill). The .md says "evaluated at the START of the round." At real table, this needs a physical marker on the lower-init ST's card placed at top-of-round before any turns. The sim implementation was incorrect; the spec is correct. No DB fix needed. DM advisory: pre-round stagger marking is mandatory, not optional. *(If the stagger had been correctly enforced in this sim, ST-2 would have used Multiattack in R1 — roughly 8-11 fewer damage to the PCs. Outcome likely still TPK by R3, not R2.)*

**MQ-FC4-B: Sabriel at 0 HP from DR save — narrative TPK edge case**
When a PC drops to exactly 0 HP from a DR save (vs full 10d10 disintegration), they're unconscious and making death saves — NOT disintegrated, NOT LoH-blocked. Sabriel ended R2 at 0 HP (death save state). With Bazgar down and Marwen down, there's no one to stabilize her, and the beholder + 2 STs are fully active. Recommend: in slice-4's DM notes, add a line: "once all 3 PCs are at 0 HP or DOWN (regardless of disintegration state), declare TPK — do not run death saves when there is no healer left and the full encounter force is active."

**MQ-FC4-C: Drain Divinity used on a PC who dies the same round — 3 LA "wasted"**
The beholder spent 3 LA on Drain Divinity against Marwen (end of her turn in R1), draining her L3 slot. Marwen was then killed by ST-1's AR later in R1, meaning the stolen slot generated +6 beholder HP that was immediately lost. The 3 LA could have been Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle Lash (1 LA) against Bazgar or Sabriel instead, potentially applying more pressure. The tactics section covers DD priority (FI-128) and slot-holder threshold, but doesn't address "target is about to be killed by an ally this round." Not auto-fixable (requires initiative prediction), but worth flagging as a table-feel issue: if the DM knows ST-1 is about to AR-cone Marwen to 0 HP, maybe hold the LA spend.
