### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SR8-A: Berserk never fires until R3** (8th-cycle confirm, also seen 7th-cycle). In the corridor approach, ragers are bursting from different alcoves and often can't reach Berserk positions (≥2 enemies in reach, no movement) until the fight consolidates in R3. Berserk's "no movement" prerequisite causes systemic late-firing. Recharge mechanism (5-6) never exercised since Berserk wasn't used in R1/R2. *Feel issue, not bug: Berserk is lethal when it fires (all 3 do in R3) but it always defers. Recommend: add a coaching note to .md that in burst-ambush formations, ragers should try to line up their opening charge so Berserk IS available R1 if both PCs are adjacent on approach.*

- **FI-SR8-B: Scorching Ray total miss on +5 vs AC 16** (SIM-SR8-B). Three consecutive misses at 12.5% probability wiped an entire L2 slot. Not a bug (variance), but this is the *second* L2 slot Marwen wasted this fight (Shatter did only 6 damage per rager against high Con saves). Marwen's best play against ragers is unambiguously Fireball/Shatter (save-based, Taunt-immune) — attack-roll spells are strongly punished when disadvantaged. The slot economy here (2×L2 for ~15 total damage vs 1×L3 for 27 each-fail) heavily favors upcasting.

- **FI-SR8-C: Berserk sweep low-damage pattern** (2nd cycle confirm, see also 7th-cycle). In this run, three simultaneous Berserks produced only ~26 total damage to the party (4+5+4+3+5 hit damage across 9 attempts, 7 of which hit). Max is 1d12+2 avg 8.5 but actual average came in low (~4-5). The greataxe die (1d12) has very high variance. At +4 to-hit vs ACs 15-19, hit rate is 25-50%. Even when hitting, rolls clustering in the 3-6 range made the sweep feel weak. *Design note: Berserk is spectacular on paper but underwhelming in play due to damage variance compounding with miss rate. Consider whether +4/1d12+2 correctly reflects intended threat level — MQ-SR8-A below.*

- **MQ-SR8-A: Taunt single-turn duration vs simulation persistence** — Taunt's DB effect text says "until the end of its next turn," meaning it expires after the taunted creature's very next action. In this simulation, the taunted_by list was carried across rounds for tracking purposes, but in practice each Taunt lands, applies for one turn, then evaporates. Ragers correctly re-Taunt via bonus action each turn. The "persistent Taunt" modeling in the harness inflates the perceived burden — in real play, the caster gets Taunt disadvantage only on their single next turn's attack rolls, not multiple. This is the same class of issue as SIM-SR7-A. *Recommendation: note in derro-rager.md that Taunt is specifically a one-turn-duration disadvantage (bonus action re-application required each round).*

- **MQ-SR8-B: Madness Endurance not exercised** — The start-of-turn checklist says "+1 to attack if took damage last turn." In this run, the party hit R1 in R1 (Bazgar 2 hits) and R2 (Bazgar 1 hit) but R1's Madness Endurance was not applied (+1 to each printed roll — DM applies manually). The manual-application note ("roller output does NOT include this bonus; add +1 to each printed to-hit result") creates a tracking burden. No DB fix needed, but a coaching note in the checklist warning that this is easy to skip at speed would help.
