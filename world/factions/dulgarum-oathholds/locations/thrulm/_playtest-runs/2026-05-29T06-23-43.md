## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **[MQ-EV7-A] void_scream save notes not rendered in dispatch output.** The `save.notes` field of area actions was not included in the `[ASKING PLAYER: ...]` output line. The shrine-proximity disadvantage for Void Scream ("Creatures within 10 ft of the shrine have DISADVANTAGE on this save.") was invisible to the DM during live play. **Fixed:** `scripts/dnd_roller.py` — added `if save.get("notes"): lines.append(f"_(DM: {save['notes']})_")` immediately after the `[ASKING PLAYER: ...]` block for `area` type actions. Phase A re-ran after fix: 28/28 clean. Now renders as:
  ```
  _(DM: Creatures within 10 ft of the shrine have DISADVANTAGE on this save.)_
  ```
  This fix applies to any area action with a `save.notes` field — not just void_scream.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **[FI-EV7-A] VS + shrine disadvantage is a decisive fight-ender at level 5.** In R3 with PCs at 35/1/already-down HP, VS (6d10 avg 33) + disadvantage-on-saves wiped all remaining HP. Sabriel (1 HP) had essentially zero chance regardless of her save roll. Bazgar at 35 HP was left at 2 HP — survivable only because he rolled a 7 (worst roll). This is a CR 13 beholder; the lethality is appropriate. **HOWEVER:** the shrine disadvantage modifier is never telegraphed to the party and creates a hidden mechanical trap. Recommend adding a terrain note to the chamber description so the DM can communicate the hazard before VS fires. If the party stays near the altar by choice (vs. being forced), the disadvantage feels earned; if they have no option to spread out, it's invisible punishment. Log for human review.

- **[FI-EV7-B] Negotiation pacing (FI-NEW-EV5-B) continues to function correctly.** 2 exchange minimum before trigger, soft failure = dismissal, hard threat = surprise round. The pattern is working as intended — 7th EV cycle confirm. The *surprise round* trigger from a hard threat continues to feel asymmetric (beholder acts, party flat-footed), but note that even without surprise, the beholder won initiative 24 vs. party's best 18. The practical delta is small. DESIGN DECISION: is the surprise round worth keeping as a mechanic, or is "high initiative beholder acts first normally" sufficient?

- **[FI-EV7-C] Drain Divinity DC 16 vs. paladin Cha save.** Sabriel saved at exactly DC 16 *twice in a row* (d20=16+0=16 both times). A level 5 paladin has Cha save proficiency: +3 (prof) + Cha mod. If Sabriel's Cha is +3, her total is +6 — she saves on d20=10+, a 55% chance. The beholder's DD at DC 16 only succeeds on a 43% fail rate vs. Sabriel. For a 3-legendary-action ability that is the beholder's "primary anti-divine" tool, this feels inconsistently threatening. **DESIGN DECISION: consider raising DD DC to 17 or 18 vs. divine-martial targets.** This does not change the spec globally — it would be a note in the beholder tactics section about when DD is worth the LA spend vs. Sabriel's save.

- **[FI-EV7-D] Lair action variety collapses to 2 choices in no-thrall scenarios.** `manifest_thralls` is correctly excluded when no thralls are present, leaving only `void_eruption` and `unstable_ground`. These alternate in a fully predictable VOID → UNSTABLE → VOID → UNSTABLE pattern. In a 3-round fight, every possible lair action fires exactly once in each. For *longer* negotiations or solo-beholder fights that extend beyond 6 rounds, this becomes obviously mechanical at the table. **DESIGN NOTE (not a bug):** A third lair option for no-thrall scenarios would improve feel. Consider a `void_pull` (telekinetic drag — force PC closer, enabling grapple followup) or `shrine_blindness` (brief magical darkness centered on altar).

### Mechanical questions

- **[MQ-EV7-B] Surprise round vs. beholder's natural initiative advantage.** The beholder rolled 24 for initiative in this run — it would have gone first even without a surprise round. The surprise round mechanic from a hard-threat negotiation collapse adds complexity (tracking flat-footed state, deciding what counts as surprise) for a case where the beholder usually wins initiative anyway. Empirically, in 7 EV cycles, the beholder has won initiative 5 times. **Recommendation: remove the surprise round from the hard-threat trigger and instead just note that the beholder's Dex +8 nearly guarantees initiative.** The "feel" of punishment for the threat is preserved by the beholder acting before the party; the mechanical overhead of surprise is not needed. Log for human review before changing.

- **[MQ-EV7-C] Void Scream on an unconscious PC.** Marwen was unconscious at the time VS fired (she had died from failed death saves — technically dead, not just unconscious). VS is a radius that hits all creatures in range. Does VS affect unconscious/dead creatures? The spec doesn't address this. Mechanically: it doesn't matter since Marwen was already dead. But if Marwen had been unconscious (not dead) and VS hit her, she'd take the save... at disadvantage... and the result would be irrelevant (she's already at 0 HP and rolling death saves). Recommend adding a note that VS does not force death saves on downed creatures (they're not "saving" — they're rolling death saves on their turn, not in response to area saves). Low priority but a table question waiting to happen.
