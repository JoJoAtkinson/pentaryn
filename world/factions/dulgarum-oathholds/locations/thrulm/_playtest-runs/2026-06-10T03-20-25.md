### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC45-A: Ancient Resonance never fires in final-confrontation slice (structural)**
- Both STDs used only Multiattack all 4 rounds. AR recharged R3 (STD-B) and R4 (STD-A), but all PCs were down before the STDs could act with AR. This is the structural consequence of a 4-round TPK: AR needs 2+ targets in a 15-ft cone, but by R3 all PCs are in the death-save pile. In slice #4, the beholder's AoE (Fireball R1 from Marwen, VS R2) terminates the fight before AR has a meaningful window.
- **For human:** This is expected given the slice composition — not a stat block problem. AR shines in slices 1 and 6 (shrine-wedge, shardcaller-team) where STDs get more turns. Log as feel note only; no fix needed for slice 4.

**FI-FC45-B: Void Eruption lair action as de facto final-kill on last standing PC (anticlimactic)**
- Sabriel's final zero-HP in R3 came from the Void Eruption lair action (2d10 force, rolled 17, save succeeded but halved still dealt 7 force to 6 HP). The lair action is a background mechanical element; it's not a dramatic named ability. The subsequent Disintegration Ray kill on Marwen IS dramatic, but the "last conscious PC" was knocked out by what reads as ambient area damage.
- **DM note to add:** "If the last remaining conscious PC has ≤ 10 HP and the beholder has a main-action recharge available (VS, DR, or Maw-grapple setup), consider skipping the lair action to preserve initiative for the cinematic kill on the beholder's main turn." This is a DM-judgment note only — do not change the lair action's raw mechanics.
- **For human:** Log as feel issue; consider adding a DM note to beholder-thrulm.md Lair Actions section.

**FI-FC45-C: DD fired (FIX-FC40-A validated 45th-FC-cycle), Sabriel still dealt 22 radiant R2**
- DD successfully stripped 1×L2 smite slot post-Sabriel R1 (Sabriel failed Cha 14 vs DC16). Sabriel still had 2×L2 and used one for 20 radiant damage R2 — her highest-damage turn. At L5 with 3×L2, a single DD firing (max 1 per round due to 3-LA cost) leaves 2 remaining L2 smites. The attrition is real but slow.
- **Observation:** This cycle confirms DD working as designed. The tempo of DD (1×per round max, only post-Sabriel) means it takes 2+ rounds to exhaust smite slots, and the party rarely survives to round 3 with all smite windows intact. The mechanic is correct; log as positive confirmation.

**FI-FC45-D: Marwen nat-20 death save comeback → immediately disintegrated (dramatic positive)**
- Marwen stabilized at 1 HP via nat-20 death save in R4. The beholder, seeing a conscious target (1 HP), correctly targeted her with Disintegration Ray. Hit confirmed, 47 force → DISINTEGRATED. This is the best-feeling death in any FC cycle: the miracle that wasn't. The sequence (downed → stabilizes → unmade) communicates that even recovery doesn't save you from this boss.
- **For human:** No fix needed. Flag as a positive design validation — the DR + nat-20 interaction creates unique narrative drama.

**FI-FC45-E: Thrall-3 dagger (3 damage) lands the first 0-HP of the fight on Marwen**
- Thrall-3 survived Fireball via save, then dealt 3 piercing damage to Marwen's 5 HP — dropping her to 0. A 5 HP target dying to a 3-pierce dagger is tactically correct but mildly anticlimactic: the world's most dangerous beholder's minion kill is a light weapon attack from a barely-alive thrall. The thrall's existence as a cleanup vector for low-HP targets is confirmed, though.
- This is the same "thrall as last-hit janitor" pattern noted in FI-BEL3-38-C (thralls negligible DPS until they're not). No fix needed; it's a table moment — DM narrate the dwarf-inside-the-thrall landing the final punch on Marwen as a moment of irony.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC45-A: DR targeting a stabilized (nat-20 death save, 1 HP) PC — confirm valid, add to tactics note**
- The beholder's DR note (FIX-EV7-30-A) says "target conscious creatures only." A PC who stabilized via nat-20 death save is at 1 HP and IS conscious. The sim correctly fired DR at Marwen. However, the tactics note doesn't explicitly distinguish "downed at 0 HP" from "stabilized at 1 HP via nat-20" — the phrasing could confuse a DM.
- **Recommend:** Add to beholder-thrulm.md Tactics section under "Against Casters": "A PC stabilized at 1 HP by a nat-20 death save IS a valid DR target — they have a body, they're conscious, and disintegration is both mechanically valid and narratively decisive. Do NOT skip DR because they were recently downed; the nat-20 reset them to 1 HP (conscious). Fire DR."
- **Tagging as DM note** (not a spec change — no DB update needed).

**MQ-FC45-B: Void Eruption vs downed PCs — RAW hits all creatures in zone (including dying bodies)**
- The sim excluded downed (0 HP) PCs from Void Eruption targeting (only hit conscious Sabriel in R3-R4). But Void Eruption's text says "each creature within 20 feet of the shrine" — no consciousness qualifier. RAW, a downed PC within 20 ft of the altar takes VE damage, which counts as a failed death save per 5e (PHB: each instance of damage to a 0-HP creature = 1 death save failure; damage ≥ half max HP = instant death).
- If VE hits Bazgar and Marwen in R3-R4 (both downed within altar zone), they'd accumulate death-save failures from VE. At 2d10 average 11, that's 1 failure per VE instance per downed PC. In R3 VE (17 dmg): Bazgar → 1 failure. Marwen → 1 failure. In R4 VE (5 dmg): Bazgar → 1 failure. Marwen → 1 failure. That could kill both downed PCs before Sabriel's nat-20 matters.
- **This is a significant mechanical gap.** If VE correctly targets downed PCs in the zone, the TPK would have ended in R3 (Bazgar/Marwen accumulating fatal failures from the lair action). The sim's exclusion of downed PCs from VE may have lengthened the fight by 1 round and allowed Marwen's nat-20 to occur.
- **Recommend:** Add DM note to beholder-thrulm.md Void Eruption lair action: "VE targets ALL creatures within 20 ft of the shrine, including dying (0 HP) PCs. Each VE hit = 1 failed death save for each dying PC in the zone (RAW). DM may waive this if the downed PCs are clearly out of the fight and the drama is better served by a focused kill — but the legal read is that VE accelerates all dying bodies simultaneously. If the party has retreated downed PCs to the threshold (outside 20 ft), VE does not reach them."
- **Tagging as DESIGN DECISION** — this changes how fast the lair action kills downed bodies. Significant balance implication (lair action becomes death sentence for anyone down in the shrine zone). Flag for human review before implementing.
