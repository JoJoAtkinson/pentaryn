### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-BEL3-3-A: Maw auto-crit = instant one-shot any melee PC, R1** (log for human)
Bazgar had 49 HP. Tentacle hit (18 bludgeoning) + Maw auto-crit (8d8+4 = 41 piercing) = 59 damage in a single multiattack. Even without the auto-crit, the prior Tentacle hit meant Maw needed only 39+ to down him from full — the auto-crit just makes it reliably lethal. Sabriel (HP 44) went down R2 to the same combo (15 + 46 = 61 damage). This creates an extremely sharp damage spike: get grappled = effectively dead that same turn. The feel is decisive but very binary — once a tentacle hits, the outcome is nearly certain. *Consider whether escape DC 16 (Athletics vs DC 16 as a bonus action — wait, grapple escape uses full action in 5e) means PCs effectively have zero counterplay once grappled. A grapple-aware party might prioritize "never let a tentacle land" as the only viable strategy.*

**FI-BEL3-3-B: Void Scream wasted when beholder already won** (log for human)
VS fired R2 per the R2-protocol (FIX-EV7-32-A). But by the time it fired, Bazgar and Sabriel were both down (end of beholder's R2 multiattack). VS hit only Marwen, who saved for half. VS's design value — hitting multiple clustered PCs — was fully negated because the beholder's own multiattack had already cleared the melee fighters. This is the 1st BEL3 cycle, so no prior pattern to confirm, but it mirrors the EV7 tension: multiattack is so lethal that the "save VS for R2" protocol leaves VS firing into a near-empty battlefield. *Design question: should VS be R1 in beholder-escorts compositions where grapple pressure doesn't need to land first? Or is VS-R2 the right call and this is just a sign of how deadly the R1 multiattack is?*

**FI-BEL3-3-C: Thralls never threaten armored PCs (carry from FIX-BEL3-38-A)** (log for human)
In R1, all 3 thralls targeted Sabriel (AC 19) and missed. In R2, they targeted Marwen (AC 15) and landed 2 hits for 8 total damage. Expected damage per thrall vs AC 15: ~1.8 HP/turn. Three thralls = ~5 HP/turn vs Marwen only. The thralls are irrelevant action-economy against a party with 2 AC 18+ melee fighters. Their value in this slice is forcing Fireball expenditure (Marwen had to cast Fireball R2 to clear them), which is meaningful — Fireball consumed Marwen's L3 slot and 1 move action. *The thralls' real value is Fireball-bait, not direct damage. Worth noting in the beholder tactics: "Three thralls clustered at the altar guarantee a Fireball; if Marwen has Fireball, the beholder should scatter the thralls (Compel Thrall bonus action) on R1 to deny the AoE window."*

**FI-BEL3-3-D: Drain Divinity minimal value in fast-ending fight** (log for human)
DD correctly stripped Sabriel's L3 slot. But Sabriel was downed R2. She had exactly 1 turn of action post-DD, using only L2 slots. The L3 slot she lost would have added ~6 radiant damage (one extra smite die) — effectively zero impact on the outcome. DD value is slot-attrition over multiple rounds; in a sub-3-round fight it barely matters. *This is a feature of the encounter pace, not a bug. In longer fights (solo beholder vs party at full HP), DD early matters a lot. In this "escorted" slice, the beholder kills 2 PCs in 2 rounds and the slot pool never becomes a real resource.*

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-BEL3-3-A: Beholder init > 20 means beholder acts BEFORE its own lair** (flag for human)
Beholder rolled init 21. Lair fires at init 20 (losing ties). So turn order R1 was: beholder (21) → lair (20) → sabriel (19) → … The beholder's multiattack downed Bazgar BEFORE the lair's Unstable Ground could make him prone. Had UG landed first, the prone condition would have given the beholder advantage on the Tentacle hit (already had advantage on a prone target). The tactics note says "If the beholder has high initiative (>18), it acts almost immediately after the lair action" — this language implies the beholder fires after the lair, but at init 21 the beholder fires before it. The note should say "If the beholder has initiative ≤19, it acts after the lair action; if the beholder has initiative ≥21, it acts before the lair." At init 20+, lair and beholder fire in the same slot (lair loses ties, so beholder at exactly 20 would go first; lair at 20 goes second). Recommending a documentation fix: add explicit note "If beholder rolls >20, it acts before the lair on turn 1 — this breaks the grapple+prone synergy that UG is meant to set up."

**MQ-BEL3-3-B: Sim/dispatch VR damage gap (carry from prior cycles)** (recurring)
Void Ray dispatch output said 13 force (4d10 from cache). Sim hand-rolled 4d10 = 26. Marwen (7 HP) would go down from either value, so the outcome was identical — but the transcript number is wrong (13, not 26). Root cause: sim rolls dice with `random` module in parallel to dispatching actions; the two RNG sources diverge. Marwen's final HP tally in this run should show -6 HP (7 - 13 = -6), not -19 HP. This is the same gap flagged as MQ-TW2-42-A in prior cycles. Not a spec bug; a sim-harness gap. No auto-fix.

**MQ-BEL3-3-C: Thrall target selection sim error R1** (minor)
Sim targeted Sabriel (AC 19) with all 3 thralls in R1 because she appeared first in the sorted PC list. Per thrall-derro tactics ("attacks targets the beholder indicates"), the beholder would direct them toward the softest target (Marwen, AC 15). In R1, the beholder was acting before the thralls (init 21 vs thrall1 init 16), so it could have issued telepathic commands between its turn and thrall turns. Corrected mental model: R1 thralls should target Marwen. Impact on outcome: none (all 3 missed Sabriel anyway; even against Marwen at 25% hit rate they'd likely do ≤6 damage R1). Flag for sim quality.
