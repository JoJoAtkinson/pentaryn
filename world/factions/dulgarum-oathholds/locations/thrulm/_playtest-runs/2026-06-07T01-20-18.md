### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SW32-A: Low-HP stagger exemption evaluation order**
The stagger rule states "⚠️ Stagger is evaluated at the START of the round." The low-HP exemption states the STD "is NOT subject to the stagger hold." These two bullets don't specify ordering. At the table: should the DM check HP before assigning stagger, or assign stagger first then immediately lift the hold for low-HP STDs? Recommend: explicit note that HP check is first. "Before assigning the stagger hold, check if either STD is below 20 HP — exempt them from the hold."

**MQ-SW32-B: OBR self-kill sequencing**
When OBR fires and the self-damage (1d4 psychic) would reduce the shrine-touched to 0 HP (because it was already near-death from the triggering hit), what is the sequence? This run: STD-B at 2 HP was hit for 19 dmg (already at 0 from hit), OBR triggered. Ruling taken: OBR counter-attack resolves first (hit for 12 dmg), then self-damage resolves (3 psychic kills STD-B if not already dead). This feels correct — the "last gasp" counter-attack lands before the NPC drops. Recommend adding a ruling note to the OBR description: "If the triggering damage reduces the shrine-touched to 0 HP, OBR still fires (counter-attack and self-damage both resolve as the shrine-touched falls — the self-damage is incidental at that point)."

**MQ-SW32-C: STD-A R2 low-HP action priority (carries MQ-SW31-B)**
STD-A at 8 HP in R2 had AR available (never fired R1 due to stagger hold). Correct play per stagger exemption: fire AR recklessly (not held). But Driven Escape is a bonus action, so: bonus = Driven Escape toward altar; action = AR (if 2+ in cone) OR multiattack. Recommend .md clarification: "Below 20 HP: Driven Escape (bonus action, move toward altar); action = AR recklessly if available + 2+ targets in cone, else Multiattack."
