## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **Call Weakness / Barrage incompatibility documented in tactics** — `derro-shardcaller.md` Tactics section lacked any note that Call Weakness advantage (attack roll) is wasted when the benefiting ally fires Shard-Barrage (which requires no attack roll). DM at table would not know this is a dead interaction. Added explicit guard: "Do not Call Weakness an ally who plans to use Shard-Barrage this turn." Also added stagger note for multi-shardcaller formations.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-1 / DD-16: Call Weakness is almost entirely wasted in a shardcaller-only formation.**
In this slice (3× shardcaller, no melee allies), Call Weakness has no valid targets in many situations:
- When the ally plans to Barrage: incompatible (save-based, not attack roll).
- When the ally is at ≤15 HP: correctly withheld (DD-8 guard).
- With only shardcallers, every R1 action is Barrage → every R1 Call Weakness is wasted.
In Round 2, two shardcallers spent Call Weakness, but the advantaged ally then missed anyway.
Net result: 5/9 charges spent, 0 produced any measurable effect.
**Recommendation:** In shardcaller-only formations, consider giving one shardcaller a "commander" role with enhanced Call Weakness (targets: self for next Multiattack, or the highest-initiative shardcaller for a specific Multiattack turn). Alternatively: document in tactics "if no melee allies are present, hold Call Weakness until a round when Barrage is not available."

**FI-2 / DD-17: Triple front-loaded Barrage — then nothing.**
All 3 shardcallers fired Barrage in Round 1 (540% of expected per-round fire rate). Barrage recharge never fired in this 3-round fight (recharge 5–6, ~33%/round — expected recharges in 2 more rounds is ~0.5 each). The signature area weapon of the shardcaller was a one-shot R1 burst, then pure multiattack.
This is the same structural problem as DD-6 (both shrine-touched burning Resonance R1). The fight's most interesting ability fires once, never to return.
**Recommendation:** Stagger tactic (added to .md auto-fix above). With staggering, one fires R1, one holds for R2, one fires if/when a line is available. This creates sustained pressure and more interesting resource decisions. Also consider recharge 4–6 to increase the probability of barrage looping in longer fights.

**FI-3 / DD-18: Fireball trivializes the shardcaller formation (analogous to DD-14 for ragers).**
Marwen's L3 Fireball at Round 2 dealt 22 fire to all 3 shardcallers (33 max HP each). Two failed saves: instant 22 fire each drops them from 33 to 11. Bazgar then cleaned up. Shardcallers have no fire/spell resistance and relatively low HP for a 3-NPC cluster. The party felt powerful, not threatened, from Round 2 onward. The shardcallers never kited (Tactical Retreat triggered but was irrelevant — Fireball is 150-ft range).
**Recommendation:** Add terrain constraint: columns and barricades limit Fireball AOE overlap to 2 shardcallers max at any one time. Or raise shardcaller HP to ~42 (same as DD-14 recommendation for ragers, +25% HP buffer). Or give shardcallers Evasion (half damage on successful Dex save, zero on failed) — but that's a significant mechanical upgrade.

**FI-4: Ranged kiting ("the slice's main feel goal") never materialized.**
The briefing says this slice tests "ranged kiting feel." Tactical Retreat fired 3× in R2–R3, but the party never attempted melee — Marwen and Sabriel engaged at range (Fireball, attacks), and the shardcallers were effectively kiting nobody. The kiting mechanic only has feel when a melee PC is closing in on a shardcaller and the retreat prevents an OA. In a Fireball + ranged-DPS fight, Tactical Retreat is flavor text.
**Recommendation:** This is an encounter design note, not a bug. The kiting feel requires at least one melee PC to be pressuring the shardcallers. Consider adding terrain that forces melee engagement (narrow bottleneck that forces parties into melee range), or adding a "3× shardcaller + 1× derro rager" variant to the slice so the rager absorbs melee while shardcallers kite.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-1: Pack Tactics Voice — 3 shardcallers stacking — DM tracking burden.**
With 3 shardcallers, each hit from any shardcaller triggers the passive for the target PC. In R1, Bazgar and Marwen each had the disadvantage-on-next-save passive applied twice (once per barrage). In practice, the mechanic is:
- "Hit by shardcaller → target has disadv on next save until end of target's next turn."
But with 3 shardcallers firing in R1, Bazgar was hit (Barrage R1 SC-B), received Pack Tactics Voice, then hit again (Barrage R1 SC-C) — the passive just refreshes. There's no stacking beyond "has disadv." This is reasonable but could be clarified: does the passive stack to "disadv on TWO saves" or just "has disadv (period, refresh on each new hit)"? Current read: it's a toggle, not a stack.
**Recommendation:** Add to `derro-shardcaller.md` Start-of-turn checklist item 4: "This is a toggle — a target either has disadv on its next save or it doesn't. Multiple hits don't stack to 'disadv on two saves'; they simply refresh the duration."

**MQ-2: Call Weakness says "attack roll" — does it apply to Taunt (opposed Cha check)?**
In a mixed formation (shardcaller + rager), could the rager use Call Weakness advantage on its Taunt action? Taunt is described in `derro-rager.md` as DC 12 Cha save (target fails → disadvantage on attacks not targeting the rager). If a shardcaller Call Weaknesses the rager, the rager technically has advantage on its "next attack roll" — but Taunt is a saving throw, not an attack roll. Same incompatibility as Barrage.
**Recommendation:** Note in shardcaller tactics: "Call Weakness grants advantage on attack rolls only — not saving throws, contested checks, or ability checks. Valid uses: Shard-Throw, greataxe swing. Invalid: Shard-Barrage save-area, Rager Taunt save."
