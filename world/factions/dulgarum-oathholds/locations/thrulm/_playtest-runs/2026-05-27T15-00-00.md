### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-NEW-A (NEW — FEEL): Slow broke R1 on first concentration check (DC10, rolled 6).**
Marwen cast Slow R1 and RG1 failed the save — but RG2 (unslowed, init 16) immediately crossed to Marwen and landed a crit hit for 4 damage, making the concentration DC only 10. Marwen's +2 CON save rolled a 4 — failure. Both attacks hit and both concentration checks failed. Slow lasted exactly 0 rounds of useful effect after RG1's turn.

The structural fragility: in mixed-initiative fights where a non-Slowed rager goes before the melee PCs (Bazgar/Sabriel), that rager can immediately punish the concentrating caster before the fighters can intercept. This is correct and expected 5e behavior. The DM should note: **Slow works best when cast from cover or when the fighters go first in R1**. When ragers reach Marwen before Bazgar/Sabriel can form a screen, concentration is likely to break.

This is the **first cycle where Slow broke intra-R1** (before any rager had a second turn). All prior cycles broke concentration in R2+ or didn't break at all.

**FI-177 BROKEN (MILESTONE — 11th SRR cycle): Berserk landed 13 slashing damage (Bazgar, R4).**
After 10 consecutive SRR cycles with 0 Berserk damage (FI-177), Berserk finally connected this cycle. Conditions that enabled it: (a) RG3 survived to R4 (party was focused on RG1/RG2 first), (b) both Bazgar and Sabriel were adjacent to RG3, (c) party had spent action economy on RG2 (Hold Person + auto-crits). The Berserk hit for 13 slashing vs Bazgar (AC 18), reducing him from 42 to 29 HP.

This data point suggests: **Berserk is achievable when the rager is NOT the primary focus target and survives to a late round with multiple PCs adjacent.** In fights where ragers are eliminated in order of priority (weakest/slowed first), the last rager standing eventually has Berserk conditions met.

FI-177 conclusion: Berserk's 0-damage history was partly a function of which rager is targeted first. RG3 as the "ignored" rager reached Berserk-eligibility naturally.

**FI-NEW-B (NEW — FEEL): Longest SRR fight (5 rounds). Mixed-initiative is the more interesting scenario.**
This cycle's mixed initiative (RG2 before Bazgar/Sabriel) created a 5-round fight vs. the 2–4 rounds of all-PC-first cycles. The fight had narrative arcs: Slow gambit → Slow breaks → Fireball R2 → Hold Person R3 swing → Berserk R4 → final cleanup. This is far more engaging than cycles where Fireball front-loads all damage in R1 before ragers act.

**Design recommendation (supports FI-176 option b):** The "staggered entry mechanic" (one rager pre-positioned adjacent to the party at combat start) would consistently produce mixed-initiative dynamics regardless of dice. This cycle naturally produced that feel from RNG. A mechanical guarantee would make every SRR cycle feel like this one.

**Hold Person = rager death (design note):**
RG2's Wis nat 1 save paralyzed it → 3 auto-crit attacks delivered 18+18+32 = 68 damage over 2 attacks from Bazgar + Sabriel (RG2 had 22 HP). Hold Person effectively tripled the damage output of both melee PCs in 1 round. At +0 Wis save (no proficiency), derro-ragers are extremely vulnerable to Hold Person. This connects to FI-143 (Slow vs ragers Wis proficiency question — still pending) and should be considered jointly: if ragers get Wis save proficiency, both Slow and Hold Person become less reliable.

**Aggro-mark mid-combat shift (positive feel note):**
RG3 correctly redirected from Bazgar (initial charge target) to Marwen (Fireball attacker) after R2, then to the next target after hitting Marwen. The aggro-mark mechanic creates natural target-switching behavior that reads well narratively: the rager "remembers" whoever just hurt it. This worked cleanly across 3 mark states in one fight. No bug; flagging as a positive design note for the DM cheat sheet.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-NEW (SIM BUG — no spec change): Berserk dispatch format "Greataxe (target N)" differs from Multiattack "Greataxe N". Phase B sim parser regex missed Berserk damage.**
The Berserk spec uses attack names `"Greataxe (target 1)"` / `"Greataxe (target 2)"` while Multiattack uses `"Greataxe 1"` / `"Greataxe 2"`. The Phase B simulation's regex `Greataxe\s+\d+\s+to-hit` matches `\d+` not `(target 1)`. Result: Berserk attacks were dispatched and displayed but damage was not applied to HP tracking.

Corrected R4 damage: Berserk Atk 1 (to-hit 22 vs AC18) HIT → 13 slashing → Bazgar 42→29 HP (not 42 as tracked). R5 outcome unchanged (party still wins). Corrected final HP: Bazgar 29/49, Marwen 0→1 (stabilized), Sabriel 44/44.

**No spec change needed.** The Berserk action spec is correct; the attack names are fine for human readability. The sim parser needs a broader regex to handle `(target N)` format. Harness fix only; outside cron blast radius.

**Taunt on unconscious target (MQ-NEW-2):**
RG3 in R4 attempted to Taunt Marwen (unconscious at 0 HP). Unconscious creatures auto-fail Charisma saves per RAW, but Taunt applies "disadvantage on attack rolls" — unconscious creatures can't make attack rolls anyway. The Taunt output is null. Redirect rule (FI-31 corollary) should specify: if Taunt's intended target is unconscious or dead, redirect to the highest-threat conscious target. The derro-rager.md tactics say "redirect Taunt to whoever is threatening the back line" but don't explicitly exclude unconscious targets from initial selection. **Minor clarification needed — recommend adding: "Skip unconscious/dead creatures as Taunt targets; redirect to nearest conscious threat."** Low priority.
