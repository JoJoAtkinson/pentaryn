**FI-188 (FEEL CONFIRM — 5th cycle):** Unstable Form (UF) remains structurally unreachable in the shrine-wedge slice. Both STDs had UF queued entering R2 (STD-A dmg_last_round = 42; STD-B = 23 — both ≥10 threshold). Neither survived to R2 NPC turns. **5th consecutive SW cycle with UF active-but-unused.** Root cause: 2× CR-3 vs L5 party with initiative = fight resolves in 1-2 rounds before any STD R2 turn arrives. If UF is designed to be a meaningful mechanic in this slice, the STDs need more HP (+8–10) or the party needs a delayed entry (no L3 slot R1). Otherwise UF is dead weight in shrine-wedge configuration. Prior FI-80/115/162 all confirm the same pattern; 5th cycle eliminates possibility this is just RNG. Human decision pending from FI-119 (encounter undertuning).

**FI-189 (FEEL CONFIRM — 5th cycle):** Altar zone fire/radiant vulnerability suppression has not changed any outcome in 5 shrine-wedge cycles. In this cycle: both STDs saved Marwen's R1 Fireball (Dex +5 → saved on 9+, 65% success rate; rolls 25 and 20). The suppression mechanic correctly prevented doubled fire damage — but suppression only matters when a STD fails the save AND the party would otherwise double-dip. With 65% save rate, both STDs saving R1 Fireball is ~42% probability each cycle. Suppression is correctly authored but mechanically invisible in practice. At the 5th cycle, this is a structural finding: the mechanic needs either (a) lower Dex saves on STDs, OR (b) explicit DM prompt to pull STDs away from the altar to expose vulnerability (rewarding tactical play). No auto-fix. Prior FI-81/116 confirm.

**FI-190 (FEEL):** OBR fired once (STD-A vs Sabriel's smite). Missed (6 vs AC 19). Self-damage 4 psychic contributed to STD-A's erosion. OBR is mechanically correct; the self-damage rider feels punishing to the NPC in a useful way — each missed OBR costs the STD 1d4 HP. The mechanic creates a perverse incentive where STDs that get hit a lot slowly kill themselves. This is an interesting tension point (OBR+UF feedback loop per FI-162) but never resolves in 2-round fights. Positive at-table note: players who notice the self-damage will feel clever for "baiting" OBR. No fix needed.
