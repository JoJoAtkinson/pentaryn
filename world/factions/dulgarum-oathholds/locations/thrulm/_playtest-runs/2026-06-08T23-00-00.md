### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP34-A: SC one-shot-path R1 confirmed (34th consecutive cycle carry of FI-TP33-C)**
Sabriel deals 22 total damage (10 slash + 12 smite) on atk1 alone, reducing SC from 33→11; atk2 brings SC to 4 HP. SC survives only because Sabriel's second smite would be wasteful at 4 HP remaining. SC dies R2 to a single 8-slash hit. SC at 33 HP has no rotation window against a L5 paladin with smites. Every TP cycle: SC dies R1 or R2 before Barrage can recharge. Recommendation carry: HP ~45-50 for even a 2-round window.

**FI-TP34-B: CW 0/3 34th consecutive cycle (structural init lockout — carry)**
SC init 7 (lowest in the encounter). Init order: DW-A 19, Sabriel 18, Marwen 17, DW-B 11, SC 7, Bazgar 5. By the time SC acts, BOTH DW allies have already taken their turns. MQ-12 correctly fires: no future-acting ally with HP>20 exists at SC's bonus-action window. All 3 CW charges unspent, again. This is not a spec bug — MQ-12 is working as designed — but the CW ability is structurally unspendable in this formation. In 34 TP cycles, CW has never been spent productively on a same-round ally. The SC needs either higher initiative (Dex +3/+4 to reliably beat at least one DW) or an explicit "if all allies have acted, CW pre-buffs next round's first actor" protocol.

**FI-TP34-C: Marwen at 8 HP — best threat-vector observed in recent cycles**
DW-A hit Marwen R1 for 3 (adv via TD, one hit), DW-B hit Marwen R1 for 3+6=9 (adv via TD, two hits), and SC Barrage hit Marwen R1 for 8 (all-save). Total R1 damage on Marwen: 20, leaving her at 12. DW-B then hit for 1 more R2, leaving Marwen at 8/32 (25%). This is the closest any TP cycle has come to downing Marwen. The threat vector works — Marwen's AC 15 is the real target. If DW-A's second swing had hit (MISS on 12 vs AC 15) Marwen would be at 9 HP entering SC's barrage; with a failed save she'd be at 1 HP. One good DW round and Marwen goes down.

**FI-TP34-D: SC fires Barrage from 4 HP — desperation feel gap**
Mechanically correct (Barrage is save-based, not an attack roll; SC can fire while dying). But the narration gap is jarring at the table — a creature at 4 HP (12% health) executing a disciplined 15-ft line suppression feels wrong. Consider a tactics note: "Below 6 HP, Barrage is a death-thrash — narrate as desperate spray rather than tactical execution." No spec change needed, just a DM note.

**FI-TP34-E: Cunning Action zero fires in 34 consecutive TP cycles**
The Cunning Action bonus action (Dash / Disengage / Hide) has never been used by a DW in 34 cycles. Per the recent FIX-TP33-B note, Dash is appropriate when formation breaks; Disengage when below 10 HP and retreating. In this cycle DW-A was at 11 HP R2 but fought to the death instead of disengaging. At table this is fine (oath-bound loyalty), but the ability is dead weight in practice. Consider whether the Cunning Action flavor is important enough to keep or whether it should be replaced with something that fires in combat (e.g., a "+2 AC reaction" or a bonus-action crossbow swap).

**MQ-TP34-A: DW-A prone not enforced in sim R2**
Bazgar's Trip Attack in R1 knocked DW-A prone (STR save 9 FAIL). In R2, DW-A should spend half movement to stand before attacking. The sim did not track or enforce this stand-up cost. Already flagged in prior cycles (FIX-TP33-A region). No spec fix needed — DM must track.

**MQ-TP34-B: Barrage damage discrepancy (carry MQ-TW37-B)**
Dispatch output shows "12 piercing (3d6)" but local sim rolled 17 piercing. The dispatch value (12) is the authoritative narration per the harness methodology; the sim re-rolled independently. This dual-dice gap is a known ongoing harness issue. At table the DM should use the dispatch value (12 piercing, 6 half) — the sim's 17 was a re-roll artifact.

**MQ-TP34-C: TD advantage misfires on BOTH swings even after one misses**
Tactical Drilling grants advantage on a melee attack roll when "an ally is within 5 feet of the target and the target is within the ally's reach." The sim applies adv to both swings unconditionally while both DWs are alive. RAW this is correct per the trait text — it's a per-attack check, not per-multiattack. No spec bug, but worth confirming: does TD advantage persist if the allied DW is incapacitated mid-multiattack? At table: yes, per RAW, each swing checks the condition independently.
