### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-53 (NEW): Berserk dead-weight confirmed in 1-PC-melee scenarios**
- Berserk never recharged (rolled 1,1,4,3 across R2–R5 — statistically unlucky but below-average in 4 rolls is ~20% likely). More critically, even if recharged, only Bazgar was in melee range throughout. Berserk requires 2+ creatures in reach — impossible in this slice's formation. Tank-wall structurally guarantees Berserk is dead weight (Marwen at 40+ ft, Sabriel hangs back for spells/heals). Log for human: Berserk is only meaningful in slices with 2+ melee-range PCs (solo-rager-rush, final-confrontation). Not a bug — a design observation. Berserk's presence in the stat block may mislead table DMs into expecting it to fire in 2-NPC encounters.

**FI-54 (NEW POSITIVE): Tank-wall fight felt appropriately dangerous at 9 rounds**
- First tank-wall cycle with corrected CW range harness. Party victory but at cost: Marwen down×2, both Healing Words spent, both Divine Smites spent. Bazgar barely scratched (45/49). Sabriel at 14/44 HP. The SC proved the more dangerous NPC — Barrage R1 front-loaded pressure, then multiattack chipped Sabriel for 10 damage in R5. Rager was durable but all attacks missed Bazgar (AC 18 with poor dice; Rager +4 vs AC 18 = 35% hit rate, all missed across 5 rounds). The fight length (9 rounds) suggests this 2-NPC slice is at the low end of challenging for the party — probably correct for Encounter 3 placement.

**FI-55 (NEW): Taunt fired successfully once in 5 rounds (R3: Sabriel Cha 6 vs DC 12)**
- Taunt's R3 success triggered the "disadvantage on attacks vs non-Rager" condition on Sabriel. Practical effect: zero (Sabriel was attacking the Rager regardless — Taunt forced an action she was already taking). Confirms the Taunt's impact is contingent on the taunted PC's intended target. In this formation, Sabriel's rational target is always the Rager (highest threat), making Taunt redundant on Sabriel. The Rager should Taunt Marwen (the wizard whose Fire Bolts target the SC) — but the sim had Marwen as the Rager's attack target, preventing that logic. See MQ-29.

**DD-41 (2nd tank-wall confirm): CW stranded R2-R9 — range gap persists**
- Confirmed again: R1 CW fired correctly (Rager within 30 ft pre-charge). R2-R9: Rager at melee (~5 ft from Bazgar, ~45-50 ft from SC) → gap exceeds 30 ft. All 2 remaining CW charges held through 9 rounds. DD-41 behavior fully reproduced. 2nd tank-wall cycle confirms this is structural, not seed-dependent. Pattern now: 2/2 tank-wall cycles with CW 1/3 usable (R1 only).

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-29 (NEW — sim harness): Rager attack-target selection includes out-of-range PCs**
- Rager chose Marwen (lowest HP = 17) as attack target in R1-R2. Marwen was 40+ ft from the Rager's melee position. The tactics say "charge the strongest-looking enemy IN REACH." Bazgar was in reach (5 ft). Marwen was not reachable. The sim's target logic picked by lowest global HP regardless of range. This caused: (a) CW advantage was "available" for the Rager but applied to a wrong-range swing; (b) Taunt targeted Sabriel instead of Marwen, since the code excluded the (incorrectly-chosen) melee target Marwen. At table, the DM would obviously attack Bazgar (in reach) and Taunt Marwen (the caster threatening the SC). Harness-only issue, out of blast radius. No .md change.

No cover available post-Rager-death; fallback from DD-47 ("accept exposed position in shardcaller-only formation") applies.
