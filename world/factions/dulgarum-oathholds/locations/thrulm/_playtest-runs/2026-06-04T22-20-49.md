### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-BEL3-4-A: FIRST PARTY VICTORY for this slice (4th cycle) — VS absence = decisive factor**

Prior 3 cycles all resulted in TPK (R3/R4/R6). In all 3, Void Scream fired and was the TPK vector. In this run, VS never recharged (needed a 6; 0 successes in 3 end-of-turn recharge rolls). This is ~42% probability per 3-round fight. When VS doesn't fire, the beholder's remaining offense (multiattack to-hit +6 vs AC 18–19 = ~40% per swing) is insufficient to stop Fireball+Smite focus fire. **VS is the sole fight-deciding variable for this slice.** Recommend: consider raising VS recharge to 5–6 to increase fire frequency (from ~42% never-fires to ~26% never-fires in 3 rounds), making the encounter more consistently threatening. Alternatively, accept that the "limited" slice has a ~40% clean-win path by design.

**FI-BEL3-4-B: DD all-in LA creates binary zero-output turns — escape hatch triggers too late**

Sabriel saved both DD attempts (Cha saves 16 and 20, both exactly at or above DC 16). 6 LA burned for 0 effect. The escape hatch (FI-EV7-21-B) would fire after these two consecutive fails and shift to VR+TL, but by that point the beholder was at 28 HP entering R3. **Recommend:** Trigger the escape hatch after ONE consecutive full-round DD fail (not two) when beholder HP is below 50. The current rule (two consecutive fails) means the beholder can waste 6 LA before adapting. At 40% Sabriel save rate, two consecutive saves happens ~16% of the time — but when it does, the beholder has no other LA output for 2 rounds against the most resilient PC.

**FI-BEL3-4-C: Lair rotation violated in simulation (SIM-BEL3-4-A)**

Lair used `unstable_ground` all 3 rounds. FI-129 requires rotation after 2 consecutive uses. In R3, with beholder below 30 HP and retreating, the correct lair was `void_eruption` (2+ PCs near altar) or skip entirely. Simulation scripting gap — no spec change needed. DM coaching: enforce rotation rule explicitly at R3.

**SIM-BEL3-4-B: Maw attack not applied in playtest simulation (scripting gap)**

The dispatched `multiattack` output includes 3 attacks: Tentacle 1, Tentacle 2, and Maw. The simulation only processed 2 attacks. In R2, Maw rolled 25 to-hit and landed on Sabriel for 21 piercing (non-critting, since Sabriel was not grappled that swing). In R3, Maw rolled 14 and missed. Had the R2 Maw been applied, Sabriel would have entered R3 at ~11 HP instead of ~38 HP. Party outcome unchanged (Bazgar kills beholder before Sabriel's R3 action), but HP tracking error — Sabriel was much more endangered than simulated. Fix in harness for future runs.

**MQ-BEL3-4-A: `shrine_drift` not used for retreat when HP < 30 (simulation gap)**

Per beholder tactics: "Below 30 HP, thralls alive: retreats into lower shaft using thralls and lair actions as cover." `shrine_drift` is the designated bonus action for this (OA-safe movement). The simulation continued using `compel_thrall` in R3. Since thralls T1/T2 were dead (only T3 alive), the correct bonus action per FI-20/MQ-BEL25-A and below-30-HP tactics was `shrine_drift`. This is a simulation error, not a spec conflict — the .md is clear on the retreat trigger.
