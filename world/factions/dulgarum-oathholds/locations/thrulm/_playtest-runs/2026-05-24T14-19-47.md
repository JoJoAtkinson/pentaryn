**FI-17 — SC Call Weakness range simulation was wrong (sim bug, not authoring bug):**
The playtest simulation blocked CW R1 with a hardcoded `round_num >= 2` check, citing "Rager ~50ft away." This was incorrect: at fight-start, neither NPC has moved — SC-to-Rager gap is actually ~25 ft (party entry 0 ft, Rager 30 ft from entry, SC 55 ft from entry → SC-to-Rager = 25 ft), which is WITHIN the 30 ft CW range. In a real table run, R1 CW on the Rager would be legal and the correct play per the SC tactics. This is a sim harness error, not an authoring error.
- **Impact on findings:** R1 CW + Rager Multiattack advantage was never exercised. The taunt+CW synergy was not tested.
- **Recommendation (sim only):** Fix the R1 CW range check. The tank-wall slice needs a re-run with CW firing R1 to test the actual synergy.
- **Authoring status:** No change needed to `.md` files.

**FI-18 — Fireball trivially decides the tank-wall encounter before synergy can fire:**
Marwen's R1 Fireball (34 fire, both NPCs fail saves) one-shotted the SC and reduced the Rager to 8 HP. The fight resolved in 2 rounds with no NPC landing a meaningful hit. The tank-wall slice's stated purpose is "tests taunt + call_weakness pairing" — that pairing never engaged because AOE removed both NPCs before initiative looped back to an NPC turn.
- **Feel:** Anti-climactic. The two-NPC encounter has no resistance to area damage. If Marwen ever has Fireball, the fight is over R1.
- **Design question (do NOT auto-fix):** Is the tank-wall intended to survive a Fireball? If yes, both NPCs need higher HP or Evasion. The Rager at CR 2 with 52 HP is already reasonable; the SC at 33 HP is fragile for a back-line support NPC facing a level-5 party with full spell slots. Consider raising SC HP to 45–50 or adding Evasion. Alternatively: have the tank-wall include an additional Rager to absorb Fireball and let the SC survive into R2.
- **Priority:** DESIGN DECISION — human review needed.

**FI-19 — Rager last in initiative: Taunt fires after party acts R1:**
Rager rolled 12 for initiative (same as Bazgar, Bazgar won tie). By the time Rager acts R1, Marwen already cast Fireball (reducing both NPCs to near-zero), Bazgar landed a hit, and the fight is functionally over. Taunt and Multiattack were both wasted.
- **Pattern:** Rager acts last in initiative = Rager effectively can't establish front-line presence in R1 at all. With low Dex (+1 modifier), this will happen ~50% of the time on average.
- **Design question:** Should the Rager start combat already in melee range (within 5 ft of Bazgar at encounter open)? That would guarantee presence before the party's first action and give Taunt R1 a chance to disrupt PC turn order.
- **Priority:** DESIGN DECISION — human review.

**FI-20 — Rager Madness Endurance +1 and Aggro Mark both fired but both whiffed:**

Over a multi-round fight this compounds.
- **Feel note:** When both bonuses fire and still miss, the Rager feels impotent despite its threat text. Not a bug; high-AC Fighter is its natural counter.
- **No fix needed;** logging for human awareness.
