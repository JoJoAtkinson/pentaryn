### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP23-A — CW fired successfully for first time in 23 threshold-patrol cycles (POSITIVE)**
SC rolled initiative 15 (d20=13), putting it ahead of both DWs (DW1=12, DW2=5). This allowed SC to call CW on DW1 before DW1's turn in the same round — no "already-acted" guard concern, DW1 at full 27 HP. DW1 then collected the advantage and hit Bazgar (18+4=22). This is exactly the intended mechanic working. The prior 22 consecutive 0-CW-use cycles were driven by low SC initiative (init 3 in cycle 21, init 11 in cycle 22). High-initiative SC unlocks CW entirely. *This supports the existing DESIGN DECISION FI-TP22-A: the "double-block" is initiative-dependent, not structural. Consider a minimum patrol gap note so SC's CW range is never exceeded at spawn.*

**FI-TP23-B — Fireball decisive, 23rd consecutive cycle (FEEL)**
Marwen's Fireball removes DW2 outright (21 dmg vs 27 HP) and brings SC to 23/33. Combined with Bazgar dropping DW1 in R2, the patrol ends in R2. This is the 23rd consecutive threshold-patrol cycle where Fireball is the decisive action. The patrol is well-calibrated for parties without Fireball — the Barrage pressure (27 combined dmg on both front-liners in R1) is meaningful and the SC's kiting is competent. But against a party that leads with AoE, the patrol is a 2-round formality. *No spec change; logging as repeated confirmation.*

**FI-TP23-C — Pack Tactics Voice never cashes in small formations (FEEL)**
DW1's hit on Bazgar in R1 (via PTV trigger) should have imposed disadvantage on Bazgar's next saving throw. No saving throw was required from Bazgar in R2, so PTV went unclaimed. This is the same pattern observed in FI-SCT-6-A (PTV 0 activations in open-vault shardcaller-team). In a 2-DW + 1-SC patrol, PTV's save-disadvantage rarely aligns with the SC's save-based ability (Barrage) because: Barrage fires before the DWs attack (if SC has high initiative) OR the fight ends before PTV + a save coincide. *This is structurally weak in small formations — confirm whether PTV's value is intended to emerge only in full-party (slice #4) encounters where VS/DD follow hit-chains.*

**FI-TP23-D — Tactical Retreat fires correctly as bonus action kite (POSITIVE)**
SC had both DWs down and Bazgar in melee range at start of R2. TR fired cleanly — SC moved 30 ft (no OA) then still multiattacked Marwen for 7 dmg. The "main action proceeds in the new position" behavior is working exactly as intended. This is consistent with 22nd-cycle confirm (FI-TP22-C: "solo-SC-R3-Barrage feels like cleanup not pressure"). The TR is functionally a delay mechanism, not an escape — SC died two turns after the retreat.

**SIM-TP23-B — CW advantage not applied to DW1 in sim (HARNESS BUG)**
Sim tracked Drilling advantage (attack-pair) but not CW advantage (per-attack). DW1's attacks ran flat when they should have had advantage from CW. No outcome change (DW1 hit on natural 18 regardless), but the harness should track a `cw_advantage` flag per combatant and pass it into the attack function. *Harness bug, not spec issue. Logging for harness improvement.*

### Mechanical questions
- **MQ-TP23-A: Pack Tactics Voice — does SC's own hit trigger PTV on its targets?** The passive says "when an ally within 30 ft hits, the target has disadvantage on the next saving throw." Is the SC an ally of itself in 5e terms? The standard 5e ruling is that a creature is not its own ally. If correct, SC's Shard-Throw hits (7 dmg to Marwen R2) do NOT trigger PTV on Marwen. Only DW/derro hits count. Worth confirming in the .md since PTV is the SC's standout passive and its activation conditions should be explicit.
