### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TW2-A: Call Weakness range freeze (CW never lands on Rager when Rager loses initiative)**
The CW mechanic is the Shardcaller's most interesting ability and the core of the tank-wall pairing. But if the Rager acts BEFORE the SC in initiative (or if PCs act before SC and the Rager has already rushed in), the Rager is immediately out of CW's 30 ft range and CW is effectively disabled for the entire fight. In this run (SC init 11, Rager init 11 — tie broken by Dex, SC went first), CW fired correctly in R1. But the margin is thin: if the Rager had rolled 1 pip higher, the CW would have been unusable from turn 1.
- **Recommendation:** Extend CW range to 45 ft, OR allow CW to fire as a triggered response on the Rager's FIRST charge movement (reaction trigger: "when an ally within 45 ft declares a Dash action"). This makes the mechanic reliable regardless of initiative.
- **Balance note:** CW-on-Rager provides advantage on one attack roll. At +4 to-hit vs AC 18, advantage ≈ +4 expected to-hit. Worth addressing but not game-breaking.

**FI-TW2-B: Rager Berserk never fires (HP + starting distance guarantees R1 Dash → R2 death)**
The Rager at 40 ft starting distance MUST Dash in R1 to reach melee (30 ft speed < 40 ft gap). This means no attack in R1. By R2, a focused L5 party with Action Surge can eliminate a 52 HP creature (Bazgar 4× attacks = ~36 avg + Sabriel smite in R1 = 20 → Rager is at ~22 HP entering R2, dead from Action Surge). Berserk — the Rager's flagship ability — never fires.
- **Recommendation A:** Reduce starting distance to 20-25 ft. Rager can reach melee in one normal move and multiattack R1. Full HP entering R2 (or close to it) means Berserk fires R2.
- **Recommendation B:** Increase Rager HP to 65 (from 52) to absorb the R1 burst before going down. Keeps CR 2 feel but survives the burst.
- **Do not auto-fix (balance change).**

**FI-TW2-C: Shard-Throw long-range disadvantage absent from DB spec**
At 60 ft (>30 ft short range), Shard-Throw should impose disadvantage per 5e rules. The DB output shows flat to-hit rolls with no disadvantage notation. In this run, the throws missed (to-hits 11, 12 vs AC 15), so no false hits — but on higher rolls this would produce incorrect outcomes.
- **Options:** (1) Add a `long_range_disadvantage: true` spec field to the roller (new feature), or (2) add a manual DM reminder in the SC checklist ("At ranges >30 ft, roll Shard-Throw with disadvantage — take the lower of two d20+4 rolls").
- **Recommendation:** Option 2 is immediate; option 1 is the correct fix. Log as a tracker item.
- **Do not auto-fix (requires roller feature or spec-level change).**

**FI-TW2-D: Shard Barrage never fires while Rager is alive — positioning axis problem**
Barrage is a 15-ft line from the SC's position. When the Rager is in front-line melee, the SC cannot fire the Barrage through the party (60 ft away — line doesn't reach) or along the party-Rager axis (would hit the Rager). The 15-ft line needs the party in a row directly in front of the SC at short-to-medium range. In practice, once the Rager charges, Barrage is unusable until the Rager falls. Result: Barrage fired R3 on a crippled SC with 4 HP, when it had minimal impact.
- **Recommendation:** Set up tank-wall terrain so SC starts directly behind Rager in a corridor (same axis as party approach). This lets SC fire Barrage R1 down the corridor BEFORE the Rager reaches melee — party PCs advancing in the corridor are in-line. The .md should add: "Barrage is most effective R1 before Rager closes — fire it down the corridor axis at approaching PCs, then Rager charges into a weakened party."
- **This is a DM guidance/positioning note, safe to add to the SC tactics.** But it's borderline balance (affects when the most powerful action fires), so flagging for human review.

### Mechanical questions

1. **Does Madness Endurance (+1 attack) apply to ALL Rager attacks (including Berserk swings) or just the first attack after taking damage?** The .md says "+1 to attack rolls until the end of its next turn" — reads as all attacks in that turn. DB spec should reflect this but the multiattack output doesn't show the +1. DM must apply manually.
2. **CW advantage + Madness Endurance +1 interaction:** Both stack (advantage is a roll mechanic, +1 is a flat bonus). This is standard 5e but the CW output says "ADVANTAGE on its next attack roll" — the DM must also remember to add +1 from ME. Consider adding a note to the Rager tactics: "If both CW (advantage) and ME (+1) are active, roll twice take higher and add +1 to the winner."
