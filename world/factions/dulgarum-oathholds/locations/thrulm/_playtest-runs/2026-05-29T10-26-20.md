### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-NEW-BEL10-A (POSITIVE / RARE): VS all-save R1 enables genuine party agency.**
All 3 PCs saved vs Void Scream DC 16 Wis in R1 (Bazgar +2=12 save, Marwen +3=9 save, Sabriel +5=10 save — all at half damage, no frightened). The probability of all 3 saving at half is roughly 9% (Bazgar ~39%, Marwen ~44%, Sabriel ~52%). This is the first BEL3 cycle where the party was not frightened entering R2, which was the enabling factor for the eventual victory. Combined with Action Surge Fireball (eliminating 2/3 thralls R1), the party built a credible resource advantage. This confirms FI-165 from the reverse direction: when the beholder wins the "VS saves" lottery rather than the party, the encounter plays as an unstoppable 1-sided pressure machine. The 9% all-save window is the narrow path to a non-TPK outcome without disintegration ray.

**FI-NEW-BEL10-B (DESIGN DECISION): Drain Divinity monopolizes legendary action economy.**
DD (3 LA = entire budget) was used every round from R1 through R5 (5 consecutive uses). In no round did Void Ray (2 LA) or Tentacle Lash (1 LA) fire as legendary actions, because DD consumed all 3 LA each round. The budget refreshes once per round, so exactly one DD fires per round and VR/TL are permanently crowded out while slot-holders are alive. Across 5 DD uses: Marwen failed R1 (lost L3), Sabriel saved R2 and R3 (Cha +4 → ~50% save rate vs DC16), Sabriel failed R4 and R5 (lost L2 smite ×2). The mechanic is effective as a resource drain, but the pattern "DD after the first PC's turn, then nothing for the rest of the round" is monotonous — no Tentacle harass, no force chip from VR. **Recommendation:** Either (a) reduce DD to 2 LA (forcing a choice: DD vs VR, or DD + 1 LA for TL after a second PC's turn), or (b) add a tactical note that DD fires after the first PC's end-of-turn but VR fires after the second PC's end-of-turn if LA budget allows. Do NOT auto-fix — this is a spec change.

**FI-NEW-BEL10-C (FEEL, 10th-cycle confirm of FI-127): VS recharge (6 only) never fires post-R1.**
VS failed to recharge in 5 consecutive recharge attempts (rolls: 4, 1, 1, 1, 4). At 17%/round, expected recharge time is 6 rounds. In a typical 5-7 round fight, VS effectively fires once. Without DR in this slice, the beholder's main action was multiattack for rounds 2-6 — identical every round, no tactical variation. The fight would have felt substantially different if VS had recharged even once. **Recommendation:** consider recharge 5-6 (~33%/round) to allow VS to fire 1-2 times per typical fight, making it a recurring threat rather than a fixed R1 opener. This is a spec change — do NOT auto-fix.

**FI-NEW-BEL10-D (FEEL, 10th-cycle confirm of DD-21): Antireality never triggered.**
Bazgar's hits were 17, 24, 25, 22, 21, 22, 24 total to-hit. The Antireality trigger condition is hit total ≤ AC+2 = ≤19. Only one hit (17, R1) was within the margin — and Antireality was held in reserve, meaning it was never used. The reaction is mechanically correct (trigger after seeing roll, before damage, DD-43 margin check) but structurally invisible at this party level. A level-5 Fighter with +5 to-hit against AC17 either misses cleanly (<17) or hits by more than 2 (~50% of hits). Antireality appears most relevant against crit-fishing or precision strikers, not standard Battlemaster greatsword attacks. Not a bug — just a feel observation consistent with DD-21 (10th confirm).

**FI-NEW-BEL10-E (FEEL/QUESTION): Thralls as action-economy pressure remain near-zero.**
3 thralls across 6 rounds dealt 4 total damage (1 hit by Thrall-A in R1 for 1 dmg; 1 hit by Thrall-B in R4 for 3 dmg). Thrall-B's Hand Axe (Weak) dispatches are correct per spec (+1 to-hit, 1d4+0) but rarely connect vs. the party's AC 18-19. The beholder's compel_thrall bonus action was used every round but the thralls it directed almost never landed. 2/3 thralls died R1 to Fireball, leaving the escort role structurally unfulfilled for R2-6. This confirms FI-NEW-BEL6-C (compel_thrall dead weight with 1 surviving thrall). **Note:** The thralls' intentional weakness (dominated, incomplete; "no multiattack") is correct design — their role is to force the party to spend a Fireball and divide attention, not to deal damage. But with all 3 clustered near the altar (Fireball radius), the "divide attention" function fails entirely. A placement note in the encounter file or _overview.md (thralls start dispersed, not clustered) would make them more mechanically meaningful as distractors without changing their stats.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-NEW-BEL10-A: Beholder "prefer ranged below 60 HP" — undefined behavior without DR or VS.**
At R2 (53 HP), the beholder dropped below 60 HP and per tactics should "prefer ranged." But without DR (disabled this slice) and VS expended (never recharged), the beholder has no ranged main action. It continued melee multiattack. The tactics section says "prefer ranged" but does not specify the fallback when both ranged options are unavailable. Should the beholder hover at 60+ ft altitude and wait for VS recharge (skipping main action), or multiattack from closer range? Recommend: add an explicit note — "If both DR and VS are unavailable, multiattack from altitude 40–60 ft (tentacle reach is 10 ft; close enough to attack, far enough to avoid readied melee strikes)."

**MQ-NEW-BEL10-B: Compel Thrall targeting when all Thralls share one side of the vault.**
In this run, after Fireball wiped A and C, only Thrall-B remained. Compel Thrall continued redirecting Thrall-B every round — toward Marwen in R1-2, toward Bazgar in R2-5. With one thrall, the "direct at lowest AC" logic is straightforward, but the value is near zero (B rarely hit). Consider: should compel_thrall be skipped (save the bonus for something) when only one thrall is alive and it's below 50% HP? The beholder has no other bonus action to use (shrine_drift could reposition instead). Not a spec bug — a tactics decision for the DM at-table.
