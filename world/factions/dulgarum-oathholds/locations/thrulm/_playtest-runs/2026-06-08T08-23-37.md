### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC41-A: Drain Divinity fired — but feel was inert (slot stripped, never used)**
First clean DD expression (41st cycle). Sabriel lost 1 L2 slot via failed Cha save (13 < DC 16). She had L2×2 remaining at death. Neither L2 slot was ever used — Sabriel died from accumulated STD AR damage before she could smite. DD stripped a slot that was never going to fire anyway.

The anti-smite design intent (FIX-FC40-B note: "DD early strips smite pool; VS fires R2 into depleted paladin") requires Sabriel to survive long enough to attempt smites, then be denied them by DD. In this run, she died without smiting. The DD effect was technically correct but strategically invisible.

*DESIGN QUESTION: Is the DD's real value the Lay on Hands pool, not the smite slots? LoH is not a spell slot — DD can't drain it. But LoH is Sabriel's primary healing resource. Consider whether the beholder should have a lair action or feature that targets non-slot divine abilities (LoH, Channel Divinity) rather than only spell slots. At L5, Sabriel's LoH (25 HP) was more impactful than any single smite.*

**FI-FC41-B: LoH yo-yo pattern (carry from FI-FC40-D)**
Sabriel spent 25/25 LoH on Bazgar across R1 and R2 (10+15). Bazgar contributed ~0 effective damage due to beholder's nonmagic weapon resistance (greatsword hits halved to ~5 DPR). Both Void Ray LA instances went to Bazgar. Pattern: Sabriel pours resources into a fighter whose output is capped by resistance. The LoH investment had no combat leverage.

*FEEL NOTE for DM: announce the beholder's resistance to nonmagic weapons explicitly at the table when Bazgar first swings. "Your greatsword bites in — but the stone-shadow absorbs half the force." If players know this, they can make informed decisions about LoH vs pressing offense with Sabriel.*

**FI-FC41-C: Ancient Resonance 2d10 low-roll confirmed again (carry FI-SW35-C)**
STD-A R2 fired AR for 2 necrotic (1+1). Half = 1. A resistance check on Marwen would have been 1 necrotic. Near-irrelevant damage. This is the 4th+ confirmed low-variance event for AR. The 2d10 range (2–20) is very wide for a recharge ability; low rolls feel wasted. Prior recommendation: 2d8+2 (range 4–18, avg ~11) tightens the floor without reducing ceiling much.

**FI-FC41-D: Disintegration Ray as narrative capstone — POSITIVE**
Marwen's disintegration was the most impactful moment of the fight. She was the party's only real threat (two Fireballs dealt 21 of the beholder's 21 total HP lost). DR removed her permanently, with the explicit "no body, True Resurrection only" note. At the table this would hit hard. The timing was earned: DR recharged on a natural 6 in R3 after Marwen had already made her plays. The dramatic arc — wizard throws fireballs at a god-eating void-sphere, then gets unmade by it — feels correct for Thrulm's tone.

**FI-FC41-E: Thralls continued irrelevance (carry FI-FC35-E, 8th+ FC cycle)**
4 thralls, 0 hits on AC 18/19 PCs across 3 rounds. Thrall-4 hit Marwen (AC 15) once for 6 damage. That was all. +3 to-hit vs AC 18 = 25% hit rate; +3 vs AC 19 = 20%. In this fight configuration (Bazgar + Sabriel have high AC), thralls are scenery with daggers. STD-A and STD-B landed every meaningful NPC hit on the party. This has been flagged across 8+ cycles. The question of whether thralls should have Pack Tactics or +5 to-hit remains open.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC41-A: Does DD timing create meaningful counterplay or just delay the inevitable?**
FIX-FC40-A says "hold all 3 LA for DD after Sabriel's turn R1." This worked — DD fired. But the stripped slot was unused. The protocol creates a strict turn-order dependency: beholder must have exactly 3 LA free when Sabriel ends her R1 turn. If ANY reactive LA is spent before Sabriel acts (Tentacle on Bazgar, Void Ray on Marwen), DD cannot fire. The fix works, but it requires perfect LA discipline from the DM. Is there a simpler way to express this? Consider: "skip ALL reactive LAs in R1" as a blanket rule, not "skip LAs on Bazgar's and Marwen's turns."

**MQ-FC41-B: Lair Unstable Ground on downed target**
When the lair action fires at init 20 and the beholder's multiattack (init 21) has already downed the intended UG target, the lair is wasted. The tactics note (FIX-FC39-B) says "prefer melee fighters over casters." It doesn't specify "switch to Sabriel if Bazgar is already down." Recommend adding: "if primary target is already down when lair fires, use Unstable Ground on next-highest-priority standing melee PC (or use Void Eruption if party clusters)."

**MQ-FC41-C: DR recharge 5–6 vs recharge 6 balance**
Previous run (2026-06-07 20:26) had DR fire R1 (vs higher-init beholder). This run had DR fire R3. DR is currently recharge 5–6 per the .md (though the DB may differ — verify). In this slice configuration (full encounter), DR firing R1 vs R3 has very different outcomes: R1 DR on Marwen ends Fireball's contribution entirely; R3 DR on a weakened Marwen is the cleanup kill. Recharge probability (2/6 = 33%) means DR appears roughly every 3 rounds, which feels appropriate for a CR 13 signature ability.
