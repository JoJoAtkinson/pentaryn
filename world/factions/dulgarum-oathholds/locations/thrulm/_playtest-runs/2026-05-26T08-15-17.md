### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-109 (NEW, POSITIVE — MQ-41 VALIDATED): Shard-Barrage R1 in corridor geometry definitively changes encounter feel.**
- First Barrage in 7 threshold-patrol cycles. Bazgar −9, Sabriel −9 (both fail saves).
- Sabriel FINALLY takes damage — FI-75 (6-cycle untouched streak) BROKEN.
- Barrage R1 is clearly the correct DM choice in a narrow corridor approach: it distributes damage to PCs normally protected by the DW focus-fire pattern.
- MQ-41 recommended "human manual test"; this cycle is that test. Result: Barrage-first is warranted and should be the default DM choice when corridor geometry exists. Auto-fix applied: added corridor trigger note to shardcaller.md. No balance change — tactics clarification only.

**FI-110 (NEW — exceptional RNG event): Triple-Barrage (3/3 rounds) via back-to-back recharge rolls d6=6, d6=5.**
- First time SC-X fires Barrage in consecutive rounds in any TP cycle.
- Joint probability: ~2.8% (11/36 × 11/36 ≈ 0.028). Rare but demonstrated.
- Total Barrage output: 25 piercing across 3 fires. Transformed the feel of the encounter: Bazgar genuinely threatened (27/49), Marwen borderline (15/32 by R3).
- Without the recharge luck, standard Barrage+Multiattack pattern would produce fewer total hits and Marwen would likely end near-full HP as in prior cycles.
- **Design note: Barrage Recharge 5-6 (33% per turn) is calibrated correctly. Triple-fire is a rare but meaningful event. Recommend no spec change — it's high-variance by design.**

**FI-111 (NEW — DD-39 NUANCE): DW-A acted for first time in 7 cycles (init 16); 0 dmg output even with CW advantage.**
- DD-39 pattern (DW-A always pre-turn casualty) technically broken by initiative roll — but DW-A still contributed nothing.
- DW-A attack sequence: Axe 1 (CW adv) max(5+2,15+2)=17 vs AC 18 → miss. Axe 2: 12 → miss.
- Root cause confirmed: the problem is NOT solely initiative order. Even when DW-A gets a high initiative and acts in R1, +2 to-hit vs AC 18 means DW-A needs 16+ on each die for a hit (20% base, ~36% with adv). In this cycle, both attacks missed.
- **DD-39 refinement: the structural floor is DW-A's melee accuracy vs level-5 PC ACs (18-19), not initiative alone. Raising DW-A's to-hit to +4 (matching crossbow spec) would raise hit chance to 35% base / 58% adv — materially different. Or: give DW-A a secondary option (readied crossbow shot) to make it threatening even at low initiative. Log for DESIGN DECISIONS.**

**FI-112 (NEW): CW advantage on DW-A converted 0/2 attacks (0 cumulative hits in 2 cycles where CW was correctly targeted to DW-A before its turn).**
- Cycle 6 (FI-73 POSITIVE): CW converted 1 miss→hit (baseline roll 4, adv gave 19). That one hit was 4 dmg to Bazgar.
- This cycle: CW on DW-A before its R1 turn. Both attacks miss even with advantage (rolls 15 and 12 vs AC 18).
- Root cause: DW-A melee +2 vs AC 18 means advantage barely crosses 50% hit threshold (~36% with adv). CW has marginally positive expected value but high variance. In 7 cycles total: CW on DWs converted 1 hit in 1/7 cycles.
- **Structural observation: CW is most valuable on targets with ≥+4 to-hit attacking ≤AC 15. In threshold-patrol, the shardcaller's own multiattack (+4 vs Marwen AC 15 = 50% base, 75% adv) would benefit more from CW than any DW melee attack. SC-X can't self-buff (CW targets an ally) but this highlights that CW is wasted when the only melee attackers have low to-hit vs high-AC targets. No spec fix — tactics/feel observation.**

**FI-113 (NEW — sim limitation): PC-NPC initiative tie Marwen 16 = DW-A 16 — unresolved tiebreaker.**
- First TP cycle with tied PC/NPC initiative at the same value.
- Sim placed DW-A before Marwen (dict sort order — not authoritative).
- Outcome analysis: if Marwen had gone first, DW-A would have been at 15 HP before DW-A acts. DW-A still fires 2 attacks on Bazgar (both miss this cycle). Zero mechanical difference this cycle — but in cycles where DW-A has higher HP, Marwen-first could prevent DW-A acting entirely.
- **Harness note: add d20 tiebreak or coin-flip for tied initiative. Low priority; zero impact this cycle.**

**MQ-52 (NEW — sim harness): R2+ Barrage targeting switches from Bazgar+Sabriel to Bazgar+Marwen.**
- R1: Bazgar+Sabriel (advancing in line)
- R2-R3: Bazgar+Marwen (as Sabriel closes to melee, Marwen steps in)

- The R2 Barrage on Marwen (25/32 HP, AC 15) was meaningfully threatening — landed 7 dmg on a failed save. This is better targeting than R1's Sabriel (AC 19, high HP).
- **No spec or sim fix needed — Barrage targeting by HP vulnerability is good emergent behavior.**
