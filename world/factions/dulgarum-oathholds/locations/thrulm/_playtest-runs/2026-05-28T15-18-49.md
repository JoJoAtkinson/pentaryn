### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-NEW-TW12-A — CW structural lockout in tank-wall formation (12th TW cycle confirm):**
The Shardcaller's 3 Call Weakness charges went **entirely unused** for the 12th time in tank-wall cycles. Root cause: in a 2-NPC (Rager + SC) formation, CW is available only in R1 when SC acts *before* Rager in initiative (approximately 50% probability). When SC ties or loses initiative to the Rager, the Rager charges to melee before SC acts, immediately exceeding the 30-ft CW range. From R2 onward, the Rager is ~50 ft away in melee — permanently out of range unless SC repositions into danger.

In this cycle, SC and Rager tied at initiative 4; Rager acted first in the tie, charged, and CW was structurally unavailable for the entire fight. CW fires if and only if SC wins initiative in R1 AND the bonus action isn't claimed by Barrage (which is also optimal R1). Given that both Barrage (main action) and CW (bonus action) are valid R1 actions together, CW activation is limited to: P(SC init > Rager) × P(Rager in range before SC turn) ≈ 50% in R1 only.

Prior TW cycles: FI-163 (11th confirm), FI-NEW-TW3 (3rd confirm, DD-41 range rule), FI-TW2 (2nd confirm).

**Recommendation for human review:** Options: (a) increase CW range from 30 ft to 60 ft — in a formation where the Rager charges at turn 0, this doubles the usable window; (b) give SC a "self-target CW variant" when no ally is in range (advantage on own next attack roll); (c) add a terrain note to tank-wall encounters suggesting the SC starts closer (20–30 ft behind Rager instead of 50 ft) so CW stays in range through R2. Option (a) is cleanest.

**FI-NEW-TW12-B — Berserk recharge rate felt fast (3 consecutive recharges R3-R5):**
Berserk (recharge 5-6, ~33% per round) recharged on all 3 attempts in R3 (d6=6), R4 (d6=5), R5 (d6=5). This meant Berserk swept all PCs in reach every round from R2 onward. Though within normal variance, the *feel* at the table was that the Rager was constantly Berserking. The Berserk recharge is the Rager's primary mechanical identity — this was a high-drama run, but 3-in-a-row creates a "this creature never stops Berserking" impression. Within expected variance; log for pattern detection across further TW cycles.

**FI-NEW-TW12-C — Taunt vs save-based vs attack-roll magic: 3-round suppression effective:**
Marwen's Taunt (failed R1 Cha save, then re-Taunted R2) gave her disadvantage on Firebolt attack rolls vs the Shardcaller for 3 consecutive rounds. Both Firebolt attempts missed (dice 1 and 1 with disadvantage — partly luck). However, Marwen's Shatter R1 (save-based, unaffected by Taunt) hit the Rager. This asymmetry is correct and deliberate per spec: "Taunt's disadvantage only applies to attack rolls — it does NOT affect saving throw-based spells." The encounter feel was: Taunt successfully redirected Marwen's save-spells toward the Rager and suppressed her attack-roll options against SC. This worked as designed. No change needed; confirm for the third time (FI-TW2-2 2nd cycle confirm).

**FI-NEW-TW12-D — Berserk "3 attack lines" display when only 2 PCs in reach (R4-R5):**
From R4 onward, Marwen was unconscious. DB output still showed 3 Berserk attack lines but only 2 were valid (per "DM caps at actual in-reach creature count"). The DM must manually ignore the third line. This is a recurring friction point across TW cycles. Low severity (tactics doc covers it) but DMs consistently forget mid-fight. Consider adding a narrator note to the Berserk DB entry: "*(DB always prints 3 lines; DM uses only as many as there are alive, hostile, in-reach creatures this turn)*". This is a cosmetic authoring fix, not a spec change.

**FI-NEW-TW12-E — CW never dispatched, so Pack Tactics Voice had no NPC-hit triggers through most of the fight:**
Pack Tactics Voice (passive) requires an ally hit to generate the "next PC save has disadvantage" effect. In R1, Rager hit Bazgar AND SC hit with Barrage — PTV should have applied to Bazgar's next save in R2. But the sim did not track PTV state turn-by-turn, so R2 Bazgar advantage checks are possibly slightly off. No outcome impact (Bazgar had no saves forced in R2), but the harness consistently undermodels PTV. **Log as MQ-NEW-TW12-A** (ongoing harness gap, same class as prior MQ-77/PTV notes).

**MQ-NEW-TW12-A — Dispatch vs sim roll divergence (MQ-77 class, 12th TW confirm):**

No outcome change (fight ended R6 regardless), but this is the 12th consecutive TW cycle with this discrepancy pattern. *No spec change needed — this is a sim harness limitation.*

**MQ-NEW-TW12-C — Initiative tie (Rager 4 = SC 4 = Sabriel 4): ordering ambiguity:**

Correct order would be SC > Sabriel > Rager — which would have placed SC *before* Rager, potentially enabling R1 CW. The existing sim uses initiative-roll order without Dex tiebreaks. **No spec change — DMs should use Dex tiebreaks per 5e RAW, which would have changed the CW availability ruling in this specific cycle.**
