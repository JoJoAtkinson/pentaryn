## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **Tactics note added to `derro-rager.md`:** Same-type-formation Taunt coverage gap. When ALL enemies are ragers (no non-rager targets), the Taunt disadvantage clause is structurally irrelevant — added explicit note `(FI-SR5-46-A)` to the Taunt tactics bullet. See diff.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SR5-46-A (CARRY — 6th consecutive SR5 cycle):** Taunt is 100% inert in the solo-rager-rush slice. All 4 Taunt fires produced zero effect because there are no non-rager targets. The bonus action is completely wasted mechanical real estate. Over 6 SR5 cycles, Taunt has never mattered in this slice. Design decision needed: (a) accept it as "flavor with no in-slice effect," (b) give Taunt a secondary effect (e.g., "on fail, target also has disadvantage on its next saving throw" to synergize with Berserk), or (c) replace the bonus action in same-type formations with something else (a rage-shout that grants the rager advantage on its next attack). Recommend: document that Taunt is intended to pressure back-line PCs in mixed formations and is NOT expected to matter in same-type formations.

- **FI-SR5-46-B (CARRY — 6th consecutive SR5 cycle):** Berserk NEVER fires. In the solo-rager-rush configuration, each rager charges a different PC → only 1 PC adjacent per rager → Berserk condition (2+ enemies in reach) never met before ragers die. Berserk is the rager's flagship recharge ability and it never expresses in this slice. The slice is poorly structured to showcase Berserk. To fix the slice, not the stat block: add guidance that 2 ragers target the same PC while the third taunts a different PC — this creates a 2-adjacent-PC cluster for Berserk on the duo ragers.

- **FI-SR5-46-C (CARRY):** Double Fireball is deterministically decisive. Marwen's 2× L3 Fireball does 27+28 = 55 fire spread across 3 ragers (Dex +0; 50% fail rate). Two failed saves = full 27 dmg on a 52-HP rager = 52% one-shot from Fireball alone. The slice has no answer: ragers have no cover, no spread-out positions, no movement deterrent (Taunt is moot). A level-5 wizard clears this encounter before melee can even develop. Consider: (a) stagger rager entry (1 per round, closing at speed 30), (b) have one rager make a Stealth check to flank from behind (splitting Fireball target pool), or (c) note in the slice that this is intended as a "warm-up" encounter that Marwen dominates.

- **FI-SR5-46-D:** Madness Endurance (+1 ATK on damage) confirms as flavor-only in R1-Fireball openers. All 3 ragers take Fireball damage on Marwen's turn (init 22 — highest init), then apply ME +1 on their first attack. However since ragers die in R2 from the second Fireball, they only get 1-2 attack turns total. ME +1 never changed a hit/miss outcome in this run. The passive is correct mechanically but requires the rager to survive more than 1 round for ME to matter. Works as intended in longer fights.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-SR5-46-A (CARRY from 45th cycle):** "Incoming Damage Aggro — next attack has advantage." Does this mean advantage on the FIRST attack roll of multiattack only, or all attack rolls in that multiattack? The .md says "the next attack the rager makes against that enemy." Strict reading: advantage on the FIRST attack roll only, then advantage consumed. Recommend clarifying: "advantage on the first attack roll it makes against that enemy on its next multiattack turn; the remaining attack rolls in the multiattack are flat." This is RAW-compatible and prevents a confusing "does adv-on-next-attack apply to all 2 Greataxe swings" table debate.

- **MQ-SR5-46-B:** Environment note: random.org and quantumnumbers.org are unreachable in this sandbox environment (network policy). Phase A required pre-seeding the cache with local PRNG values before importing `dnd_roller`. The module loads the cache at import time; without pre-seeding, all attack-roll-dependent actions fail. If this cron continues running in a network-restricted environment, consider adding a `USE_LOCAL_PRNG=1` env flag to `dnd_roller.py` that bypasses the remote fetch entirely and falls back to `random` (with a caller-supplied seed). Not a spec bug — environment limitation.
