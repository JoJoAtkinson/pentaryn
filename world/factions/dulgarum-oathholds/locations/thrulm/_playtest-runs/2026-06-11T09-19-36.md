**FI-TW2-44-B — Taunt is ineffective vs save-based casters (NEW)**
The Rager Taunted Marwen every round of this fight (R1–R4 Marwen alive). The Taunt wording explicitly includes "ranged spell attack rolls (e.g., Fire Bolt, Eldritch Blast)". But Marwen's spell sequence was: Fireball R1 (save → ignores Taunt), Magic Missile R2 (auto-hit, no roll → ignores Taunt), Fire Bolt R3+ (attack roll → WOULD be affected). The Taunt only suppresses the cantrip Marwen falls back on when slots are spent. Against a Wizard who leads with AoE saves, Taunt has near-zero mechanical bite. The Rager spends its bonus action every round buying almost nothing. *Design question: is this intentional? Options: (a) accept it — Taunt's value is vs melee PCs and non-casters; the Rager shouldn't be Taunting the wizard; (b) extend Taunt to also apply disadvantage on saving throws made to resist the taunted creature's spells; (c) note in Rager tactics that Taunt is wasted on save-based casters and should target Bazgar/Sabriel instead.*
