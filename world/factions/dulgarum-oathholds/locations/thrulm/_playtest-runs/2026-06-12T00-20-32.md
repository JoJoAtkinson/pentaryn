### Simulation issues (not spec bugs)

- **SIM-SW1-43-A:** OBR counter-attack to-hit rolls not applied to PCs. Only STD self-damage tracked. Affected rounds: R1 (OBR-A vs Bazgar, OBR-B vs Sabriel), R2 (OBR-B vs Sabriel), R3 (OBR-B vs Sabriel). True PC HP at end is likely lower; estimate ~9 additional damage split between Bazgar and Sabriel if half of 4 OBR counter-attacks hit. Outcome unchanged (VICTORY), party HP would be ~5-8 HP lower.
- **SIM-SW1-43-B:** Sabriel's R2 smite damage (13 HP) not applied to STD-B due to `target` vs `tgt` variable scope bug in harness. STD-B HP tracked at 16 going into R3 instead of ≤3. Corrected narrative: STD-B dies end of R2. R3 presented as simulated but is an artifact.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SW1-43-A (carry from 41st, 42nd):** Unstable Form NEVER expressed — 43rd consecutive SW1 run. Structural cause: by R2, both STDs are ≤20 HP (LOW HP path triggers), overriding normal UF×multiattack priority. In this run, STD-A (UF active) fired reckless AR instead of multiattack (wasting UF). STD-B (UF active) used multiattack with advantage but missed both attacks regardless. UF window requires: STD ≥20 HP AND 10+ damage taken prior round AND AR unavailable/held. In practice this window is never reached vs. this 3-PC party. DESIGN DECISION: consider lowering LOW HP threshold to ≤10 HP, or reducing UF trigger threshold to 15+ damage, to create a round where UF advantage can be leveraged on multiattack before STD goes sub-20.

- **FI-SW1-43-B (positive, 43rd confirm):** Altar zone suppression is mechanically decisive. Marwen chose Shatter (thunder) R1 over Fireball (fire), Magic Missile (force) R2 over fire spells. Without suppression, Fireball would have doubled on both STDs (insta-kill). The zone creates a real strategic constraint — this is working as designed.

- **FI-SW1-43-C (new — OBR self-damage accumulation):** OBR fired 4 times in 3 rounds (1 from STD-A, 3 from STD-B). Total psychic self-damage: 1+4+2+3+2=12 psy (some tracked). This represents ~26% of an STD's max HP (45). OBR is significantly accelerating derro deaths. At the table, the visual of the creature punishing itself is distinct and memorable. FEEL: POSITIVE for flavor, but may make shrine-touched fragile faster than intended in melee-heavy fights.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-SW1-43-A (new):** OBR fires "between attacks if the attacker has multiattack" per the .md. Confirmed in this run: OBR fires after Bazgar's Strike 1, then STD-A still receives Strike 2. The question is whether the OBR reaction interrupts the SEQUENCE — i.e., does OBR fire, THEN the second strike happens? Or does OBR fire after the full multiattack resolves? The .md says "interrupts between attacks" which implies OBR fires mid-sequence. This is rules-coherent but should be called out explicitly so the table doesn't miss it. Current wording is adequate; no change needed.

- **MQ-SW1-43-B (carry from prior SW1):** Driven Escape bonus action within altar zone. If both STDs are already within 60 ft of the altar, Driven Escape (move toward shrine, no OA) has no net tactical effect. The ability only becomes relevant if a STD is pulled away or in the LOW HP + not-in-sight-of-combat case. Worth noting for slice design: for Driven Escape to matter in SW1, the fight arena should be large enough that STDs might move OUT of the altar zone during combat.
