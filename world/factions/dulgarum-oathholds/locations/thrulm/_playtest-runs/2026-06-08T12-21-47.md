### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP33-A: Barrage all-saved (seed anomaly — but exposes low-floor damage):** All 3 PCs saved on Shard Barrage R1 (saves: 19/22/13 vs DC13). Half-damage = 6 per PC. Total Barrage damage: 18HP across party. Compare to 32nd cycle (Bazgar FAIL → 10 half? No — 16 full? Check prior log). The Barrage still fired correctly at the right moment, but its floor in the all-save case is weak pressure: 6 damage each, less than a single axe hit. **No fix recommended** — the save distribution is RNG; the mechanic is correct. Worth noting for DM: Barrage feels like a coin flip in the threshold corridor. Two consecutive cycles where Barrage didn't score a FAIL on Bazgar.

**FI-TP33-B: CW → DW-B → both attacks missed (33rd-cycle confirm carry FI-TP32-B):** SC fired CW correctly (pre-DW-B turn, HP>20), but DW-B's +2 to-hit is so low that even advantage doesn't rescue the math. R1: with CW advantage, best of two d20+2 rolls was 9 — still MISS vs AC18. R2: DW-B both MISS without advantage. In 33 cycles of slice #0, CW on a DW has never produced a decisive hit vs Bazgar/Sabriel AC18/19. It works against Marwen (AC15) but the DW tactics prioritize Bazgar (frontline melee). **Structural issue: CW advantage + +2 to-hit vs AC18 = ~40% per swing — feels strong on paper but consistently underdelivers.** DESIGN DECISION: raise DW Hand Axe to-hit to +4 (add proficiency to weapon; currently appears to be STR+0 + no prof = +2), OR change DW tactics to prioritize Marwen for both attacks when she's exposed.

**FI-TP33-C: SC one-shot by smite R1 (33rd-cycle structural confirm):** Sabriel's single L2-smite attack = 28 damage vs 33HP SC. SC goes negative immediately. In every prior cycle, SC either dies R1 or R2 before firing any ability twice. The SC's 33HP places it within one-hit range of a L2 smite + weapon hit. The Shardcaller's design intent is a multi-round tactical actor (Barrage → recharge → CW rotation), but it never survives to execute that rotation. **DESIGN DECISION: SC HP needs to increase to ~45-50HP to reliably survive to R2 (surviving R1 smite and having HP to act R2). Alternatively, SC's tactics should prioritize escaping to cover before Sabriel can target it — currently SC fires Barrage R1 then stands in the open.**

**FI-TP33-D: Tactical Drilling expressed but underwhelming (33rd-cycle confirm carry FI-TP31-C):** DW-A R1: TD reroll 16→20 HIT for 3 damage. DW-B R1: TD reroll 8→9 still MISS. The identity trait of the DW formation ("work together") does nothing meaningful because the base to-hit (+2) and damage (1d6) are too low. The trait is correct flavor but has no table impact. Carry FI-TP-1.

**FI-TP33-E: VICTORY R3 2nd-consecutive-cycle confirm (33rd cycle):** Prior 32nd-cycle was VICTORY R3. Both at ~85%+ aggregate HP. No PC below 60% HP in either run. The threshold patrol is functioning as a warm-up encounter — the party spends ~2-3 slots and sweeps in 3 rounds. This is probably *intentional* design (patrol shouldn't be a major threat). The question is whether the patrol has enough mechanical identity to be interesting at the table. Currently: DWs miss everything, SC one-shots on Sabriel smite, fight ends in 3 clean rounds. There's no moment where the party feels threatened. Compare to slice #1 (shrine-wedge) which at least has AOE-resistant derro.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TP33-A: Cunning Action never triggered in 33+ cycles:** DW Cunning Action (Dash/Disengage/Hide) has appeared in 0 simulated cycles. The threshold patrol topology places DWs in immediate melee with the party from initiative order — there's no gap to close, and no tactical reason to Disengage/Hide. Should the tactics note explicitly state when this fires? Added FIX-TP33-B guidance, but DM should also watch for: party retreats up the shaft (DWs Dash to pursue), or DW below 10HP (Disengage to gate).

**MQ-TP33-B: Restrained-attacker disadvantage confirmed sim gap (ongoing from MQ-TP31-A):** Sim does not apply disadvantage to restrained NPC attack rolls. Both DW-A and DW-B were restrained by Web R1, but the sim continued to roll multiattack without disadvantage. DW-B still missed; DW-A hit with TD reroll. With proper restrained disadvantage, DW-A's R1 TD interaction becomes: advantage (TD) + disadvantage (restrained) = straight roll — the TD reroll was actually cancelled. DW-A would have rolled straight d20+2, likely also missing. The DM note (FIX-TP33-A) addresses this going forward.

**MQ-TP33-C: DW to-hit bonus appears to be STR+0 (no proficiency?):** Hand Axe listed as "+2 to hit" in stat block. STR is 10 (+0), Proficiency Bonus is +2 → expected +2 to hit. So +2 includes proficiency — it's correct. But the actual stat line says "+2 to hit" which means the DW is applying proficiency correctly. The issue is STR+0 means the attack is _only_ proficiency with no ability modifier contribution. For comparison, a goblin (CR 1/4) has +4 to hit with a scimitar (DEX+2 +prof+2). The DW's axe hits at +2 — strictly worse than goblin-tier offense, against AC18+. This is a stat design question, not a bug. Carry FI-TP33-B.
