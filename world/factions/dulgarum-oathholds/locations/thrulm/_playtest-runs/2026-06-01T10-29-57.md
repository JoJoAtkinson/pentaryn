### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC24-A (PATTERN CONFIRM): VS R2 TPK vector — 18th-cycle confirm.**
Void Scream recharged on d6=6 in Round 2 (consecutive recharge: d6=6 in R1 for DR, d6=6 again in R2 for VS recharge). VS one-shot Bazgar (psychic + frightened). This is a persistent pattern: in almost every full-beholder run, when VS recharges in R2, it ends the fight within that round. The beholder's recharge-6 VS fires once in ~3 rounds on average, and when it fires, it's usually lethal given party HP levels at that point.

**FI-FC24-B: Front-loaded wizard fragility.**
Marwen reached 1/32 HP by end of R1 initiative block (lair 9 force + STD AR 13 necrotic + thrall dagger 6 pierce = 28 dmg to a 32 HP wizard) before the beholder acted. The DR kill on Marwen was technically the "killing blow" but against a PC who was already functionally out of the fight. The dramatic high point was Marwen's own Fireball (wiped 3 thralls, chunked beholder 47 HP) — not the disintegration. Consider: should the STD AR + thrall swarm do less cumulative R1 damage so the DR disintegration feels like the pivot moment rather than cleanup?

**FI-FC24-C: Drain Divinity LA waste on Sabriel R1 save.**
Sabriel rolled Cha 20 vs DC 16 in R1, saving against DD and causing the beholder to burn 3 LA for zero effect. The beholder's optimal R1 play (DD on the divine martial) is correct per tactics, but the 35% save rate means it fails roughly 1/3 of turns. When DD fails on the very first use, the beholder's legendary action budget feels "wasted" — no damage, no slot drain. This is an acceptable variance level for a boss, but worth noting.

**FI-FC24-D: Sabriel LoH revival loop — felt earned.**
Sabriel reviving Bazgar with LoH R3 (risking her own low HP, LoH-as-feature bypasses void healing backfire) only to watch him go down again to ST2 was high-drama and mechanically correct. The void chamber's "healing spells → necrotic" rule came up implicitly (DM should pre-state the LoH exception before Sabriel's first revival attempt, per beholder.md notes).

**FI-FC24-E: Shrine-touched effectively bystanders after R1 burst.**
Both STDs survived at 34/45 HP at TPK. Their contribution was concentrated: AR burst R1 (wounded party badly before beholder acted), then mixed multiattack results. In the 3-round fight, neither STD had a decisive role in rounds 2-3 beyond chip damage. This is structural to the slice design (beholder dominates) but makes the shrine-touched feel like expensive front-row spectators after the opening salvo.
