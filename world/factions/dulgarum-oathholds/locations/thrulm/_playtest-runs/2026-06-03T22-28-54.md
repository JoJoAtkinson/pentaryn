### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC29-A (POSITIVE): First FC cycle where beholder kills all 3 PCs with its own abilities.**
- DR disintegrated Marwen (R2). VS half-damage killed Bazgar and Sabriel (R3).
- Prior cycles (FI-FC27-D, FI-FC28-A) noted the STD mob often landed the killing blow, making the beholder feel like a debuffer. This cycle the beholder felt like the apex threat it should be. DR recharging R2 (rolled 6) was the hinge — if DR had not recharged, beholder would have fallen back to Multiattack.
- *No spec change needed. This is the intended feel. Log as positive confirmation.*

**FI-FC29-B: DD on Marwen L3 (R1) slowed but did not prevent Fireball.**
- DD drained Marwen's second L3 before she acted. She still had L3×1 and cast Fireball for full effect (killed 3/4 thralls, wounded STDs).
- DD's value against a L3 caster with 2 remaining slots is halved — it removes redundancy, not capability. Against Marwen at L3×1 (after first DD), a second DD prevents Fireball. Against Marwen at L3×2, first DD delays Fireball by 0 rounds.
- *Recommendation: add coaching note to DD tactics — when target has ≥2 of highest slot, DD saves 1 slot but doesn't change their R1 play. Priority: Sabriel (LoH funder) over Marwen when Marwen has L3×2.*

**FI-FC29-C: VS fear rider never applied (both PCs saved) — damage alone was lethal.**
- VS fired R3 at Bazgar (3 HP) and Sabriel (7 HP). Both saved (18+2, 18+4 vs DC 16). Half-damage (16 each) exceeded remaining HP. Neither frightened.
- Fear condition is VS's "most durable effect" per tactics .md (FI-21), but in end-game attrition scenarios, damage floor kills before fear can register. VS as finisher rather than debuffer in depleted-HP contexts.
- *29th-cycle note: VS fired R3 vs R1-R2 in prior cycles. Later VS timing (PCs more depleted) makes fear irrelevant but damage more reliably lethal. This is correct feel — VS is the closer, not the opener.*

**FI-FC29-D: Lair action R3 — manifest_thralls with 1 surviving thrall (TD-D, 7 HP).**
- TD-D gained 1 temp HP and one reaction attack (missed Sabriel AC 19 on 16+1). Net output: ~0.
- Optimal lair R3: Unstable Ground on Bazgar (3 HP) or Sabriel (7 HP) — prone sets up advantage on beholder's VS (it's an area save, not an attack roll, so prone wouldn't help VS — but it would help Tentacle Lash). Actually VS doesn't benefit from prone. Unstable Ground would have given beholder advantage on Tentacle LA attacks, but beholder only had LA×3 after Drain Divinity consumed all LA after Sabriel's turn. With LA depleted, prone had no immediate tactical value.
- *Actually: manifest_thralls was marginally correct given LA was spent. No spec change. Coaching note: when LA budget is fully consumed before lair fires, lair-action value is reduced — track LA spend timing relative to initiative.*

**FI-FC29-E: FI-128 L2-threshold borderline — DD vs VR+TL when target at L2×1.**
- After Sabriel's L2 smite in R2, she had L2×1 remaining. DD R3 drained it for 4 temp HP.
- FI-128: "DD only worth 3 LA when target has L2+ slots." A L2 drain = 4 temp HP. VR (2 LA) deals 4d10 avg 22 (half save avg 11). TL (1 LA) = 3d6+3 avg 13.5.
- Expected output: DD=4 temp HP vs VR+TL=~24.5 avg damage. DD is inferior at beholder's current HP (84/110). FI-128 escape hatch should have triggered.
- *29th-cycle note: previous FI-128 coaching insufficient — sim continues using DD at L2 despite spec guidance. Reinforce in beholder.md tactics: at 84 HP (not below 40), with target at L2×1, switch to VR(2)+TL(1) every turn.*

### Mechanical questions

1. **MQ-FC29-A: Lair rotation constraint vs LA spend timing.** When beholder's LA budget is spent early (3 LA on DD after first creature's turn), the lair action fires later with a depleted action economy. The lair rotation rule (FI-129: don't repeat same action 2 consecutive rounds) doesn't account for LA-budget-relative-to-lair-timing. If beholder spends 3 LA after TD-D's turn (init 12) and lair fires at init 20 (before STD-B's turn in R2), the lair budget and LA budget interact oddly. Clarify in tactics: track LA spend *per-round* separately from lair choice.

2. **MQ-FC29-B: VS damage floor as reliable finisher.** In 4 of the last 10 FC cycles, VS half-damage (on save) has killed a critically-low PC. This suggests VS DC 16 may be slightly high for a L5 party's Wis saves (avg Wis +1 to +4 at L5 → 30-55% pass rate), but the floor damage is reliably lethal late-fight. No spec change suggested — the behavior is intentional and dramatic.
