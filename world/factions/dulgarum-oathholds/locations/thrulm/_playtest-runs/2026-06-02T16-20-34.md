### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SW24-A (24th-cycle caster-pressure confirm):** Marwen downed again by double-AR in R2. With 32 HP total, this is nearly guaranteed if both land. This is the 24th successive confirm of the "wizard in the cone" vulnerability arc. Pattern appears structural, not random.

**FI-SW24-B (altar zone fire suppression — low-impact cycle):** Both STDs saved against Fireball (21 and 22 vs DC 14). Altar zone suppression was mechanically irrelevant this cycle — saves removed the vulnerability factor regardless. The zone still correctly suppressed, and would have mattered with fails. At-table DM should announce altar zone as active when Marwen prepares fire-based spells, so she can evaluate pivot (but this cycle she had no pivot since both saves were high regardless).

**FI-SW24-C (OBR→UF pipeline — POSITIVE confirm):** OBR triggered on Sabriel's R1 attack, hit Sabriel for 14, and STD-A self-damaged 3 psychic. The self-damage correctly contributed to the UF threshold tracking (25 total = 12 Fireball + 10 Sabriel + 3 OBR self). UF activated correctly in R2. The OBR-self-damage→UF pathway is working as designed.

**FI-SW24-D (UF wasted on AR — 24th confirm):** STD-A had UF active (advantage on attacks) in R2, but fired AR (save-based — advantage doesn't apply). UF advantage was wasted again. Per tactics, with 3 PCs in cone AR correctly wins; UF advantage only fires when STD-A uses Multiattack, which only happens when ≤2 targets in cone. In a 3-PC party, this almost never occurs at full complement. UF payoff comes late-fight when one PC is down — at that point advantage on Multiattack is meaningful. No change recommended (mechanic is correct), but DM should expect UF rarely showing until a PC falls.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SW24-A (Driven Escape cone validity — ongoing):** STD-B used Driven Escape (bonus action) to move 30 ft toward the altar, then fired reckless AR as action. After moving away, the 15-ft cone may not encompass both Bazgar and Sabriel depending on positioning. The sim assumes both remain in cone, which may be generous. At the table: if STD-B moves away from Bazgar, Bazgar would need to be within 15 ft of the new STD-B position to be caught by the cone. DM coaching note recommended: "After Driven Escape, verify that cone still covers ≥2 targets before committing to reckless AR; if only 1 target remains in cone, Multiattack instead." This does not require a DB change.

**MQ-SW24-B (OBR on killing blow — confirm MQ-SW23-A still open):** OBR fires when the shrine-touched takes melee damage from a visible attacker in reach. On R3, Bazgar killed STD-A in two hits (attacks 1 and 2 of Extra Attack). The OBR should potentially fire after hit-1 (STD-A still alive at that point), then STD-A dies from hit-2. The timing: OBR is a reaction that fires during the attacker's turn. In the sim, STD-A's OBR fired in R1 (Sabriel's attack) but not in R3 (killing round). The rule is ambiguous: if hit-1 of Bazgar's multiattack doesn't kill and triggers OBR, OBR fires, then hit-2 kills. STD-A was at 20 HP entering R3; hit-1 dealt 13 → STD-A at 7 HP → OBR trigger; then hit-2 deals 9 → dead. OBR should have fired between the two hits. **This is a simulation oversight — OBR was not triggered in R3 between Bazgar's attacks.** Does not affect this cycle's outcome (STD-A was going to die anyway), but the DM should know OBR fires between multiattack hits if the shrine-touched survives the first hit.

**MQ-SW24-C (Marwen downed before Sabriel acted in R2):** In R2, Marwen went down from STD-A AR before Sabriel's turn. Sabriel then pivoted to finish STD-B and could not LoH Marwen (Sabriel used full action + bonus on attacks). This is correct tactical behavior — Sabriel correctly prioritized killing STD-B to prevent further AR. However, the LoH pool (25 HP) went unused.
