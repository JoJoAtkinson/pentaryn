### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-BEL3-10-A: PARTY VICTORY R3 — FIRST BEL3 CLEAN WIN (POSITIVE, log for human)**
The party defeated the beholder in 3 rounds. Contributing factors in this seed:
1. R1 Fireball hit full (beholder Dex save nat 2 = 8 vs DC14 → full 26 fire, ~24% of max HP).
2. Sabriel's double smite both connected R1 (atk rolls 22, 22 vs AC17) — 40 damage combined (~36% max HP).
3. Combined R1 burst: 40 (smites) + 23 (Bazgar crit) + 26 (Fireball) − 4 THP (DD) = 85 net damage → beholder at 25/110 end R1.
4. R3 beholder went cold on all 3 attacks (raw d20 rolls: 7, 2, 1 → all miss AC17/18/19).
5. Bazgar landed two hits in R3 from 25 HP (crit 21 + hit 12 = 33 damage).
This is a legitimate outcome — not a balance problem — but establishes the "variance floor" for this slice: when Fireball hits full + smites both land + beholder rolls cold R3, the party CAN win. Design intent of this slice is to test legendary economy and lair action pressure; even in a victory, DD fired correctly (10th-BEL3-2nd-confirm) and VS never recharged. Recommend noting this as ~12-15% probability outcome based on independent roll combinations.

**FI-BEL3-10-B: DD fires post-Sabriel but smites already spent (structural DD-timing observation)**
DD stripped Sabriel's final smite slot AFTER she spent 2 smites on R1 attacks. Temporal ordering: Sabriel attacks (using slots) → then DD fires (stripping remaining slot). DD cannot claw back slots already spent in the same turn. This means DD is most effective when Sabriel has not yet attacked (i.e., beholder has lower initiative than Sabriel, or Sabriel is forced to hold her action). In this run: beholder init 23 > Sabriel init 17 → beholder acts, then Sabriel attacks spending 2 smites, then DD fires stripping the 3rd. The DD DM note "strips smite pool" is accurate but overstated — it strips the *remaining* pool. Recommend adding note: "DD post-high-initiative-Sabriel strips only unconsumed slots; still valuable to prevent future smites (R2+), but not as strong as DD fired pre-Sabriel-turn." No spec change needed; this is a tactics annotation.

**FI-BEL3-10-C: Manifest Thralls lair with single surviving TD vs AC18+ targets — switch to UG recommended**
In R2 and R3, the lair used Manifest Thralls with only TD-C surviving (9 HP, +3 to hit). Both reaction attacks missed Bazgar (AC18): rolls 9+3=12 and 13+3=16. The .md note says "switch to UG if thralls land 0 hits on AC18+ targets." But the sim used Manifest Thralls because TD-C was alive. A clearer heuristic: **"prefer Unstable Ground over Manifest Thralls when ≤1 thrall alive AND primary melee targets are AC18+."** TD-C's +3 to hit has ~30% hit rate vs AC15 (Marwen) but only ~5-10% vs AC18/19 (Bazgar/Sabriel). Wasting 2 lair actions on Manifest Thralls when UG would have forced a DEX save is suboptimal. Recommend adding this threshold to the Manifest Thralls DM note in beholder-thrulm.md.

**VS never recharged — 10th BEL3 3rd consecutive (all-time: 0 recharges in 10 BEL3 runs)**
VS recharge rolls: R1=5, R2=3, R3=1. Needs a 6 (recharge 6). Consistent with the pattern from prior BEL3 runs. This is expected variance for a recharge-6 ability (~16.7%/round) — in 10 BEL3 cycles across 3 rounds each = 30 checks, expected ~5 recharges. That 0 have occurred suggests either unlucky seeds OR a systematic cache pre-seeding bias toward lower values. MQ carry from prior cycles. No fix needed — just low variance in short encounters.

### Simulation tracking notes (SIM bugs)

**SIM-BEL3-10-A: Maw autocrit applied without to-hit check (regression from 9th BEL3)**
In R1 and R2, when a grappled target existed, the simulation applied Maw AUTOCRIT (8d8+3+VF = ~32 pierce average) without rolling to-hit against the target's AC. Per beholder.md Actions: "Maw: *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit… If the target is grappled by the beholder, this attack is a critical hit instead." The critical hit is conditional on the attack HITTING (normal to-hit roll required). The 9th BEL3 run correctly showed "Maw-MISS-R3-Sabriel-16vs19-no-autocrit" — confirming the to-hit roll is needed. This run should have: R1 Maw vs Bazgar (AC18), roll the to-hit normally, and if it meets AC18, apply autocrit. Dispatch showed Maw to-hit 17 (11+6=17, misses AC18 by 1). If the sim had respected this, R1 Maw likely misses, Bazgar ends R1 at 35 HP instead of 3 HP, Second Wind doesn't trigger, R2 narrative changes. Fight outcome (VICTORY R3) may still hold due to Fireball+Smite R1 burst (85 net damage) reducing beholder to 25 HP, but HP trajectories are not authoritative. **Flag as SIM regression to fix in next cycle.**
