### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-NEW-BEL3-A — VS R1 kills Marwen before she gets a second turn (FEEL)**
Void Scream with average 6d10=33 psychic damage is near-guaranteed to drop Marwen (32 HP) in round 1 if it fires after she has taken any prior damage. In this run, ThrallA dealt 5 damage to Marwen before the beholder's turn, and VS (27 rolled) brought her from 27 → 0. Even without the Thrall hit, an avg VS roll one-shots Marwen ~60% of the time. For the party this means Marwen has at most one meaningful action before being removed from the fight. The fight is not meant to be fair (CR 13 vs level 5), but whether Marwen having a single-action combat arc is an acceptable experience is worth confirming. No fix recommended — log for human review.

**FI-NEW-BEL3-B — Thralls are cosmetic damage in this slice (FEEL)**
3 thrall-derro across the full fight contributed ~21 damage total (all absorbed by Bazgar). The beholder's VS + legendary actions did all decisive work. Thralls functioned as initiative-slot filler and as compel_thrall targets for positioning. In a live-table run with only 3 compel_thrall eligible targets, the beholder's bonus action feels like bookkeeping rather than a decision. This is probably correct for the "dominated minion" archetype, but worth noting: if the DM wants thralls to contribute meaningfully, directing them toward Marwen (AC 15, +4 to-hit = 45% hit rate) more aggressively each turn would matter. The current tactics note mentions this already; no fix needed.

**FI-NEW-BEL3-C — Healing backfire must be called pre-emptively at the table (USABILITY)**
The chamber hazard (healing spells → necrotic) is documented in beholder-thrulm.md but not in _overview.md or any at-table reminder. In this slice, Sabriel's first instinct would be Cure Wounds on Marwen, which would kill her instead of reviving her. The DM must pre-empt this before the first knock-down. Recommend adding a one-line DM note to _overview.md under Chamber Hazards: "REMINDER: all healing spells deal necrotic to the target; only LoH, potions, and stabilize work." This is a usability patch, not a balance change.

**FI-NEW-BEL3-D — Void Eruption proximity tracking is implicit (USABILITY)**
The lair action Void Eruption (20-ft radius from the shrine) requires the DM to know whether PCs are within 20 ft of the altar. The fight description doesn't establish the altar-to-entry distance. In a typical 60×60 chamber with the altar at center, the party would be ~30 ft from the altar on entry and close to 20 ft once they engage thralls near center. The DM should call this distance explicitly at setup. Not a spec bug; usability note for when this runs live.

**FI-NEW-BEL3-E — Simulation double-roll bug (METHODOLOGY)**
The Phase B simulation script rolls damage twice: once via `roll_combat_action` dispatcher output, and once via independent `random.randint` calls. The dispatcher uses the pre-seeded hardware RNG cache; Python's `random` uses a separate seeded stream. This means damage applied in the sim doesn't match the dispatcher's printed values. In this run the Void Ray was printed as "9 force" by the dispatcher but applied as "18 force" by the sim (lucky double-roll of 9+9 = 18). The corrected analysis (9 force) means Sabriel survived R1 at 8 HP rather than dropping unconscious — but the R2 outcome was TPK either way. Future cron fires should either (a) parse damage from dispatcher output and skip re-rolling, or (b) skip dispatcher output entirely and use Python random for all dice. This does not affect the encounter design; it is a simulation methodology issue. No DB or .md fix needed.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-NEW-BEL3-A — FI-128 threshold in this slice (confirm rule applied correctly)**
In R2, Sabriel had 1 slot remaining (L1). Per FI-128, Drain Divinity switches to Void Ray (2 LA) when the highest remaining slot is L1. The simulation applied Void Ray instead of DD. This is the correct rule application. Noting for confirmation: if Sabriel's only slot was L2, the beholder would have spent 3 LA on DD (gain 4 temp HP) instead of 2 LA on Void Ray (avg 22 damage). In the endgame the trade-off was moot because 27 Void Ray dropped Sabriel at 8 HP. The switch threshold logic is correct as documented.

**MQ-NEW-BEL3-B — Legendary Action firing after vs. before beholder's turn R1**
In R1 the beholder was at init 17. Drain Divinity fired at LA budget 3 (full) after Marwen's turn (init 21), before the beholder had taken its own turn. This is correct — LA budget starts at 3 from the previous round's refresh (or full for round 1). The printed "LA budget=0 at beholder turn → refreshed to 3" in the sim output is a cosmetic tracking artifact. Actual budget was: 3 at start of R1, spent 3 on DD after Marwen's turn, refreshed to 3 at start of beholder's own turn. No rule issue.
