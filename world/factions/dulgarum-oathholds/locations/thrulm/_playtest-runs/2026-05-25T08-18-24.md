### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-56 (NEW) — Grapple + Maw auto-crit one-shots L5 Fighter in Round 1**  
Bazgar (49 HP) went down on the beholder's first multiattack: TL1 hit (15), failed Str save (DC 16 vs +4 = 40% success rate), Maw auto-crit (avg 39, rolled 54). Total: 15+54 = 69 damage in one action. Zero counterplay after the Str save fails — the Fighter is just dead. Narratively this is "the beholder eats the tank," which may be intentional given CR disparity, but removes the front-line player's agency in round 1. **Design question:** Should grapple→crit require the grappled condition to persist from a PRIOR turn, rather than granting auto-crit on the same-turn Maw swing? Or scope to "grappled AND prone" as a 2-turn setup? This is a fundamental design choice — **do NOT auto-fix.**

**FI-57 (NEW) — Drain Divinity stranded in 3-round beholder-escorts fight**  
Drain Divinity (3LA, the beholder's marquee legendary) fired once in round 2; Sabriel saved (Cha 19 vs DC 16). Fight ended R3. In this slice, the beholder's main attrition engine has zero net effect. This is expected in a 3-round TPK — but confirms Drain Divinity's design payoff is only visible in longer fights (slice #4 final-confrontation). No fix needed; flagging as context for slice #4 playtest to watch Drain Divinity more deliberately.

**FI-58 (NEW) — Void Scream (recharge 6) did not fire across 3 rounds**  
Three independent recharge rolls at 1/6 probability: ~42% cumulative chance of at least one recharge. Did not recharge this run. Consistent with FI-3/FI-35 pattern across other slices. In this slice the fight was too short to matter (Void Scream's 30-ft radius still would have been the most powerful ability if recharged). Structural observation: recharge-6 abilities are unreliable in encounters resolved in ≤5 rounds.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-31 (NEW) — Beholder initiative tied with lair-action trigger at init 20**  
Beholder rolled 14+6=20, tying with the init-20 lair-action trigger. Per D&D 5e MM, lair actions fire "on initiative count 20, losing all initiative ties" — so the beholder acts FIRST, THEN the lair action fires. Simulation ran lair action first (more punishing for PCs — Void Eruption + full multiattack in R1 without the beholder losing its lair action to the initiative tie). Recommend adding a note to `_overview.md` or the beholder's start-of-turn checklist: *"Lair actions fire at init 20 LOSING TIES — if beholder is also at init 20, beholder turn resolves first, then lair action."*

**MQ-32 (NEW) — `thrall-derro/multiattack` DB action is type `single_attack`**  
The DB action named "multiattack" contains one attack (Hand Axe Weak). The `.md` correctly states "single swing — no multiattack." However, a DM looking at the DB action named "multiattack" may expect two attacks. The action verb list includes "multiattack" for input matching. Low priority; consider renaming DB action key to `hand_axe` with "multiattack" as a verb alias.

**MQ-33 (NEW — sim harness) — Incapacitated condition not enforced in sim loop**  
All 3 thralls were incapacitated by Marwen's Hypnotic Pattern (L3) in R1. Simulation loop did not check the `incapacitated` condition and thralls continued attacking in R2 and R3. Without incapacitated enforcement: Marwen took ~10 additional damage (thrall hits she shouldn't have taken). If correctly enforced: Marwen would have survived to at least R3 with ~10 HP remaining. The TPK outcome remains likely (Sabriel alone vs beholder + grapple-crit pattern) but the fight would be 1-2 rounds longer. **Harness-only fix — out of blast radius.** Not a spec bug.
