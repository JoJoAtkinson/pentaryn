### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-95 (feel, new):** Berserk multiattack output shows a summed to-hit total across all attacks (e.g. "= 39", "= 48") that is meaningless — the sum of d20 rolls + bonuses across three separate to-hit checks has no mechanical interpretation. DM must ignore the total and read each line individually. Same renderer issue as DD-7 (multiattack combined-type labeling), which is in dnd_roller.py outside cron blast radius. Log for human fix.

- **FI-96 (feel, 7th-cycle confirm):** Solo-rager-rush slice resolved in 2 rounds for the 3rd time via double-Fireball. The slice's stated test objectives — Berserk recharge, aggro-mark rider, Taunt-induced movement — were not exercised: Berserk fired exactly once per rager (first use, no recharge needed), aggro-mark was superseded by Berserk (which hits everyone regardless of mark), and Taunt was bypassed by save-based AoE. Confirms DD-14 (HP too low) and DD-26 (AoE trivializes). 7th cycle without a meaningful recharge-test window. DESIGN DECISION needed: add a forced-Berserk-R1 rule to the harness setup for this slice, or raise rager HP to 70 per DD-26 recommendation, or both.

- **FI-97 (feel, new):** Aggro-mark rider is structurally superseded when Berserk is available. In rounds where Berserk is accessible, the rager attacks ALL PCs in reach regardless of mark. The mark only has tactical effect in rounds where Berserk is unavailable (R1 approach + recharge-fail turns). In a 2-round fight, this is at most 1 multiattack turn. The mechanic reads well on paper but has negligible combat expression. Consider: aggro-mark could redirect one Berserk attack line to the marked target (priority on first attack line) rather than pure DM discretion on ordering.

- **FI-31 fix CONFIRMED POSITIVE (4th cycle):** Three-rager split Taunt (each targeting a different PC) executed correctly. Each PC was funneled to their designated taunter without stacking disadvantage on one target. The fix is holding. Taunt split creates near-total attack-routing paralysis for melee PCs (each has one "safe" target), but is still bypassed by Fireball/Shatter.

- **DD-36 confirmed (7th cycle):** Save-based AoE (Fireball) bypasses all Taunt disadvantage. Both Fireball casts were resolved via saving throws — Taunt's "disadvantage on attack rolls vs non-taunter" had zero effect on either. A party with a functioning AoE caster will bypass the entire Taunt mechanic in every rager-only encounter.

- **DD-14 confirmed (7th cycle):** Fight resolves in 2 rounds whenever Fireball is used without restraint. Recharge, aggro-mark, and Taunt synergies are test-goals the slice cannot reach at current rager HP (52). At 52 HP, two-Fireball bursts do 92-115 total damage, eliminating all three ragers outright. Recommendation from DD-26 (raise to 70 HP) would require both Fireballs to reduce ragers to ~10 HP rather than finishing them, extending the fight 1-2 rounds and enabling recharge rolls.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-47 (new):** Triple-Berserk on R2 — three ragers each Berserking in the same round, stationary in the same corridor. If a PC uses a reaction-movement (Uncanny Dodge, shield-shove, Misty Step used as movement analog) between NPC turns during R2, does a rager that has not yet acted lose Berserk eligibility? Per MQ-35 ruling, *involuntary* movement (Thunderwave push) voids Berserk. But if a rager voluntarily holds its position awaiting its turn (hasn't moved), and nothing pushes it, all three Berserks are independently valid. Clarification: MQ-35 applies per-rager to its own movement, not to siblings' positions. Recommend adding a one-liner to the Berserk prereq note: "Each rager's Berserk eligibility is assessed independently at the start of its own turn."
