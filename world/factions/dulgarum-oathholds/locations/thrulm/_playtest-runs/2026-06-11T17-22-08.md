**SIM-TW2-45-A — Paralysis not enforced before Rager's R3 initiative slot**
Marwen (init 12) cast Hold Person in R3 → Rager paralyzed. Rager (init 11) acts AFTER Marwen, so paralysis was in effect at Rager's turn. Combat outcome unaffected (Sabriel and Bazgar killed Rager regardless). Harness-only issue.

**SIM-TW2-45-B — Pack Tactics Voice triggered on SC's own hit**
PTV fires "when an ally within 30 ft hits." In 5e convention you are not your own ally (PH p. 196 context). SC hitting Marwen should NOT trigger PTV. Sim triggered it anyway. Additionally, in tank-wall geometry the Rager (the only potential ally near the SC) charges 30 ft toward the party — ending 40 ft from the SC (SC at 50 ft, Rager at 10 ft from PCs) — outside PTV's 30 ft range. PTV is geometrically inaccessible in standard tank-wall configurations. Harness-only issue (MQ-TW2-45-A below).

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TW2-45-A — CW fires (init 12>11) but misses anyway**
For only the second confirmed time in 45 tank-wall cycles, the SC acted before the Rager (both tied at 12/11 this cycle). SC correctly CW'd the Rager before Berserk. CW advantage applied to the first Berserk attack vs Bazgar (AC 18): rolled **15** (with advantage) — still a miss. The Rager's problem is hitting AC 18–19 front-liners; even with advantage the to-hit (+4) against AC 18 hits only on a 14+, meaning a 35% base miss rate on each die. Advantage reduces miss rate to ~12% on each die but the rolls still punish. CW successfully fires when init order cooperates, but the mechanical payoff vs high-AC front-liners is modest. This corroborates FI-TW2-43-A (43rd cycle: CW fired, "advantage-R2-Multiattack-both-hit-anyway"). **Pattern: CW fires 2/45 TW2 cycles and has not yet been the decisive factor in a kill or HP-differential shift.** Carry to 46th cycle.

**FI-TW2-45-B — Shardcaller 1-shot R1 by Fireball (33 fire = exactly max HP); pairing never matures**
SC (HP 33) was eliminated by Marwen's R1 Fireball (33 fire, Dex save 4 — FAIL). The SC's HP maximum equals the minimum damage a Fireball at DC 14 deals on a failed save with average roll (8d6 avg = 28; this was a high roll at 33). Even on average damage (28 fire), a failed SC Dex save deals 28 (SC dies at HP 33 – 28 = 5 HP, close but alive). The SC is fragile enough that any Dex save failure against Fireball within range means death. **The Taunt+CW pairing never matures because the SC dies before it can CW a second time or before the Rager can complete Berserk-sequence-plus-Taunt in a meaningful way.** When the SC goes first in initiative and immediately dies to a wizard's first action, the "tank wall" design intent (Rager tanks hits for the SC) only works if the Rager is physically between the SC and the wizard, blocking LoS. In open terrain, the SC has no protection. Carry to 46th cycle.

**FI-TW2-45-C [carry from FI-TW2-44-B, 3rd confirmation] — Taunt bypassed by save-based spells**
R1: Marwen's Fireball fired BEFORE Taunt (Rager acts after Marwen in initiative) — Taunt inapplicable. R2: Taunt landed on Marwen (Cha save 3/12 — FAIL). R3: Marwen used Hold Person (Wis save, DC 14) — Taunt's "disadvantage on attack rolls" clause has no effect on a Wis saving throw. Taunt had **zero mechanical effect on Marwen's spell output** across all 3 rounds (Fireball before Taunt; Hold Person immune to Taunt; Firebolt never cast). Marwen effectively plays as if Taunt doesn't exist as long as she uses save-based options. 3rd consecutive TW2 cycle confirming this pattern. **This is the most significant structural weakness documented across the TW2 playtest series.** A level-5 caster's kit is >70% save-based (Fireball, Hold Person, Web, Hypnotic Pattern); Taunt only penalizes the ~30% attack-roll cantrip fraction.

**FI-TW2-45-D — Second consecutive Berserk recharged (d6=6); double-Berserk R1+R2 pressure meaningful but not decisive**
Berserk recharged on a 6 (fastest possible; 1-in-6 chance) in R2. This gave the Rager 2 consecutive Berserks targeting 2–3 PCs each. Combined damage: Sabriel 6 (R1) + 12 (R2) = 18; Bazgar 14 (R2). All three PCs took damage in R2. Despite this, all three survived comfortably (Bazgar 35/49, Marwen 19/32, Sabriel 26/44). The double-Berserk is a high-drama moment but the party's armor class and HP absorbs it cleanly at level 5. Positive feel finding — the Rager creates real pressure even when outclassed.

**DD-TW2-45-A [DESIGN DECISION — carry from FI-TW2-44-B, 3rd confirmation] — Taunt DC 12 offers no meaningful constraint on save-based casters**
Across 45 TW2 cycles, Taunt has successfully forced attack-roll disadvantage on Marwen several times, but Marwen's response has been to use save-based spells (Fireball, Hold Person) that are completely unaffected. The 44th cycle documented this for Fireball + Magic Missile. This cycle adds Hold Person (Wis save) as a third confirmed bypass route. **Recommendation options (human review required):**
1. Raise Taunt DC from 12 to 14 (matching Shard Barrage DC) — makes it harder for Marwen to resist, but doesn't fix the save-based bypass problem.
2. Extend Taunt to read "disadvantage on attack rolls AND spell save DCs targeting creatures other than the rager" — this is a significant power increase and may overpenalize save-based classes.
3. Accept the limitation: Taunt is designed for targets who rely on attack rolls (other fighters, rangers, rogues). Against Marwen, Taunt is weak by design. Document this explicitly in the NPC tactics note so the DM knows Taunt is most valuable against Bazgar-type targets.
Option 3 may be most correct — Taunt is a soft lock on melee fighters, not casters. The NPC tactics note should explicitly say "Taunt is wasted on a pure caster; if Marwen is the only back-liner and she's a caster, use Taunt on Bazgar instead (forces Bazgar to take disadvantage vs Shardcaller)."

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TW2-45-A [NEW] — Pack Tactics Voice "ally" definition and tank-wall geometry**
PTV fires "when an ally within 30 ft hits." Two sub-questions:
1. Does SC count as its own ally? In 5e, you are not your own ally per standard convention (PH p. 196). If SC's own hits don't trigger PTV, then PTV only fires when the Rager (or another NPC ally) hits within 30 ft.
2. Is the Rager within 30 ft of the SC in tank-wall geometry? SC starts at 50 ft from PCs; Rager charges 30 ft toward PCs → Rager ends 20 ft from PCs, ~30 ft from SC (50-20=30). Borderline — if SC stays put, Rager is exactly at PTV range. If SC retreats (Tactical Retreat), the Rager moves outside PTV range. In practice: PTV never fires in TW2 simulation because (a) SC hits don't trigger it under strict reading, and (b) the Rager is at or beyond 30 ft from SC throughout the fight.
**Recommend:** Clarify in the .md whether PTV fires on the SC's OWN attack hits ("when any creature hits, including myself") or only on non-SC ally hits. Also note the geometric limitation — PTV is structurally dead in tank-wall configurations unless the SC maintains ≤30 ft spacing from the Rager, which conflicts with the "40–60 ft behind the front line" positioning directive.
