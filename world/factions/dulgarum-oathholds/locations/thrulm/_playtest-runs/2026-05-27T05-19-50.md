### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-165 (NEW — DESIGN DECISION): Void Scream fires guaranteed on R1 when beholder wins initiative.**
Beholder rolled init 20 (Dex +4 peak), VS started available (start-of-encounter). This is the 4th beholder-escorts-limited cycle; VS fired R1 in 3 of 4 cycles (missed once due to beholder losing init in cycle 3). R1 VS in this cycle: put Sabriel at 10/44 HP and Bazgar at 24/49 HP before either acted; both frightened. This shaped the entire fight — Sabriel spent R1–R2 missing at disadvantage, Bazgar spent R1–R3 missing at disadvantage. Beholder winning init is ~50% probability; VS starting available is always. Result: ~50% of beholder-escorts fights begin with full-party VS before PCs act. **DESIGN DECISION: Is a guaranteed-available R1 VS intended? Options: (a) VS starts SPENT at combat start (the beholder hasn't screamed this encounter) — more DM control over when the fight "turns"; (b) VS recharge 5-6 rather than 6-only (~2× more frequent fire overall); (c) accept current behavior — VS-on-R1 is a valid terror-opener.** Do not auto-fix.

**FI-166 (NEW — FEEL): VS + Drain Divinity R1 double-punch eliminates Sabriel's agency before she acts.**
In this cycle: R1 VS put Sabriel at 10/44 HP + frightened, then DD (on Sabriel's end-of-turn, beholder 3 LA) stripped her L3 slot. Sabriel entered R2 with 10 HP, no L3 slot, frightened. She never used a spell slot in combat. The DD+VS combo is mechanically correct per tactics ("burn LA on Drain Divinity when cleric/paladin present"), but the combined feel is "Sabriel was dismantled in R1 without an action." This was also the pattern in cycles 2 and 3. **Design question: should Drain Divinity have a round-1 restriction ("wait until R2 to model that the beholder identifies her slot structure") so the first PC actions feel meaningful before the slot drain begins?** Log for human review. Do not auto-fix.

**FI-167 (NEW — FEEL): Thralls' mechanical damage contribution is structurally negligible.**
Over 5 rounds, all 3 thralls combined dealt exactly **2 slashing damage** (Thrall-C R1 to Marwen). Hand Axe +1 vs. AC 15–19 = 15–35% hit rate; 1d4+0 avg 2.5 damage even on a hit. Their functional role: (1) **Fireball sinks** — Marwen correctly spent L3 Fireball on them R1, trading the slot for killing 2 thralls and dealing 15 fire chip to the beholder. Good resource-drain play. (2) **compel_thrall vectors** — beholder could redirect them via BA. In this run, the beholder did not use compel_thrall (VS+DD were higher priority). Thrall-A survived all 5 rounds (7 HP) and never hit. **Design note: thralls are working as pressure valves / Fireball bait, not as damage dealers. This is likely intentional. If thralls should threaten PCs independently, +1 to-hit is too low — raise to +3 or add a gang-attack bonus (+2 if adjacent ally). Do not auto-fix; log for human review.**

**FI-168 (NEW — FEEL): Bazgar dealt 0 damage across 3 rounds (R1–R3) before going down.**
VS fear (R1) + Wis save disadvantage = ~23% hit rate vs AC 17 beholder. He rolled 6 attacks at disadvantage and missed all 6. Wis save escape: +2 mod, DC 16 = 30% pass rate per turn. Failed 3 consecutive turns (7, 14, 13). At table, this is likely the most frustrating player experience: a fighter who does nothing, then gets tentacled down. **No mechanical fix needed** — this is intended oppression. However, the beholder tactics note should call out: "When Bazgar is frightened, narrate his near-misses as the void displacing his strikes, not as simple 'miss.' The fear condition is the beholder's control mechanic; honor it with narration." Low-risk DM-note addition. Not auto-fixing this cycle (borderline scope).

In R5: Tentacle Lash 2 grappled Sabriel, Maw rolled 23 vs AC 19 — HIT for 17 piercing (not an autocrit). The question persists: does "grappled by the beholder" in the Maw rider include a grapple established in the same multiattack? In 4 beholder-escorts cycles, this question has been raised each time (FI-56 cycles 1, 2, 3 + this one). Prior findings flag it as DD-11 URGENT. This is a clarification, not a balance change; human can approve and auto-fix next cycle.** Not auto-fixing without sign-off.
