### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-BEL22-A (confirm, FI-BE3-01 class):** All 3 thrall escorts evaporated R1 from Fireball (Thralls A+C dead, Thrall-B at 5/22). Fireball kills thrall escort formation in a single action every cycle — 22nd consecutive confirmation. The thrall CR 1/4 HP pool (22) and Dex+1 save cannot absorb an L3 Fireball. Their mechanical purpose in this slice is (a) to pull compel_thrall bonus from the beholder and (b) to induce Marwen to spend Fireball rather than holding it for the beholder. Purpose (b) was fulfilled: Marwen spent L3 Fireball on thralls in R1. Purpose (a) was the correct beholder play (shrine_drift won this round). Design intent confirmed — thralls are Fireball bait that costs Marwen her best slot.

- **FI-BEL22-B (feel):** VS R1 one-shots Marwen (38 psychic vs 32 HP max). Marwen is routinely dropped by VS in Round 1, then kept in the fight via LoH revival cycles. Over 22 BEL cycles, the Marwen→LoH→Marwen→LoH loop is the defining structural feature. The fight is mechanically a "how many times can Sabriel LoH revive Marwen before the LoH pool runs dry." The interesting decision — Sabriel choosing between offense and revival — is correct design. But VS R1 makes this loop begin immediately every cycle, which may feel repetitive at table. No auto-fix; design question only.

- **FI-BEL22-C (positive confirm):** Maw grapple-crit R7 (44 piercing, one-shot Sabriel from 8 HP) is the signature melee-closer and felt earned. 7 rounds of attrition led to the beholder cornering the last standing PC with the grapple+crit combo. The sequence — prone from lair action, grapple from TL1, auto-crit Maw — executed flawlessly and matches the tactics guidance. This is the intended "beholder gets dangerous up close" moment.

- **FI-BEL22-D (new DESIGN DECISION — DD L1-threshold pivot never triggers):** Per FI-128, once Sabriel's highest available slot is L1, the beholder should pivot from DD (3 LA, +2 THP) to Void Ray (2 LA). In this run, Sabriel's last L2 was drained in R6, dropping her to L1-only slots by R7. The beholder should have fired Void Ray (2 LA) + Tentacle (1 LA) in R7 instead of DD — but the sim had DR recharged and used the main-action slot rather than LA budget. More precisely: after Sabriel's turn in R6, LA budget = 3, Sabriel has L2×1 remaining (still ≥ L2 threshold), so DD fires correctly. After DD drains that L2, Sabriel is at L1 only. In R7, the beholder should pivot. But the fight ended R7 via multiattack before any LA spend, making this moot. **Logged for the pattern:** in longer fights where Sabriel is depleted to L1-only slots, the FI-128 pivot must fire — the tactics doc is clear, but the sim never reached this branch.

- **FI-BEL22-E (feel):** DR recharged 6/6 in R1 (first check, luckiest possible roll) then 5/6 in R3 (second check). Two DR charges in 3 rounds on a recharge-5 ability (33% per round) is a high-luck cluster. This dramatically shaped the run: R1 DR recharge → R3 Marwen disintegration. Without the R1 6/6 recharge, the fight would have been VS → multiattack → multiattack with no DR, a very different pressure profile. The run is not representative of the average BEL cycle's DR economy.

- **FI-BEL22-F (confirm, FI-BE3-02 class):** DD monopoly — all 3 LA consumed by DD after first PC's end-of-turn every round. LA budget fully spent on DD, leaving 0 LA for Void Ray or Tentacle after remaining PCs act. This is the 22nd consecutive BEL cycle confirmation of the pattern flagged as FI-BE3-02 and DESIGN DECISION DD-BE3-02. The LoH-loop vs DD-monopoly conflict (FI-BEL20-B) was not resolved this cycle: LoH pool ran dry at R3, so the conflict didn't arise. No new action required; pattern is documented.
