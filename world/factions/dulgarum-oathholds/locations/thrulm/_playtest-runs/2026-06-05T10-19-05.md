### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SCT-24-A: CW-stagger-deadlock structural confirm (cycle 24)**
When all 3 SCs have Barrage available in R1 and stagger applies, there is NO eligible CW target: every ally "plans to Barrage" (stagger rule is first-come-first-served by init, so each SC nominally holds Barrage until the previous one fires). The CW tactics note says "two non-barrage SCs should CW each other" but only ONE SC fires Barrage per round — meaning TWO SCs are multiattacking and should receive CW. However, the stagger decision (which SC fires Barrage vs multiattacks) must happen BEFORE CW targeting, and the current .md doesn't specify who fires first vs who holds. Result: CW logic stalls when all 3 enter with Barrage ready. This is cycle 24 confirm of 0/9 CW usage (0% firing rate across 24 shardcaller-team cycles). **Log for human; recommend explicit priority assignment: "SC with lowest init fires Barrage R1; the other two multiattack and should CW each other."**

**FI-SCT-24-B: Shard-Barrage range ambiguity (MQ-SCT-24-A carry-forward)**
DB spec `"area": "15-ft line"` does not specify whether the line starts from the SC (caster-origin) or is a placeable ranged area. In this playtest, R2 Barrage fired at Bazgar (20 ft) and Sabriel (25 ft) — both OUTSIDE 15 ft if the line starts at the caster. Yet the tactics note says "PCs advancing in formation are almost always in a 15-ft line" in a narrow corridor, implying it's a FROM-caster line. If it IS caster-origin, Barrage and TR are in direct mechanical conflict: TR fires when threats are within 15 ft (at start of SC's turn), moving the SC 30 ft away — which then puts PCs OUTSIDE the 15-ft line. This means Barrage can almost never fire after TR pushes the SC back. If Barrage is a RANGED placed line (like a ranged area attack), the "15-ft line" is its SIZE not its origin. **This needs a one-line spec clarification: add `"range": "30/60 ft"` to shard_barrage if it's placeable, or add `"origin": "caster"` note if it starts from the SC. Logging for human review — do not auto-fix spec without confirmation.**

**FI-SCT-24-C: PTV (Pack Tactics Voice) zero-effect 24th cycle (open-vault)**
PTV primed once (SC-C R2 hit Marwen → disadv on next save). That save would have been SC-C's Barrage in R3 — but Barrage targeted Bazgar and Sabriel in melee, not Marwen. Disadv consumed on a non-target. PTV requires the SAME PC to be (a) hit by an SC and (b) subject to a save from an SC in the following turns. In open-vault layouts where PCs are spread and SCs switch targets, this alignment rarely occurs. PTV has 1 confirmed activation in 24 cycles (corridor-22nd), 0 effect in all open-vault cycles. **Log for human; recommend clarifying whether PTV applies "vs any save before end of its next turn" — the current wording ("the next saving throw") suggests it's the single-next-save regardless of source, which would mean Barrage on the primed PC should use the disadvantage even if the SC switches lines. This might make PTV more reliably effective.**

**FI-SCT-24-D: Fireball-decisive 24th-cycle confirm**
Marwen's Fireball (8d6=29, both SCs failed) dropped SC-A and SC-B from full HP to 4 HP in one action — effectively deciding the fight in R1. Both SCs fell to cleanup attacks before acting again meaningfully. The shardcaller formation has no Fireball defense (no concentration break, no magical resistance, no spread rule beyond 25 ft which still allows 2-SC coverage). This is the 24th cycle confirm. **The spread rule (FI-SCT-20-B, ≥25 ft at setup) partially mitigates — can only catch 2 SCs — but cannot prevent a lucky Fireball from one-shotting the whole tactical value of the encounter.** Log for human.
