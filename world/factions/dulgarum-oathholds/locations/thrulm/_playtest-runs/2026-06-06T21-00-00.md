## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed
- **Taunt tactics .md updated** — derro-rager.md tactics section expanded: added explicit "each rager picks a DIFFERENT Taunt target" directive and stacking-clarification rule (most-recent taunt supersedes earlier ones). This is a clarity/feel fix, not a spec change. No DB rows changed.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SRR5-39-A Berserk never fires (39th-cycle confirm, 2nd SRR cycle):** With Fireball landing 33/16/33 damage R1, all three ragers start Round 2 at ≤36 HP. They die before recharge can fire (17% per round on 5-6). Even with Berserk "always available" from the start (RAW), the sim never triggered it because the standing-still-vs-2+ condition was satisfied only in rounds when the rager was at death's door and died to PC counterattacks first. **Berserk has never meaningfully fired across 2 SRR playtests.** Against an AoE-capable party, it's a dead ability. *Recommend:* Lower recharge to 4-6 (33%), or make Berserk unconditionally available R1 and then recharge on 5-6 thereafter, to guarantee at least one Berserk pulse per encounter. DESIGN DECISION — do not auto-fix.

- **FI-SRR5-39-B Taunt redundancy confirmed (2nd SRR cycle):** All three ragers independently chose Bazgar as their Taunt target R1. Only one Taunt can be "active" at a time; the other two were wasted bonus actions. New tactics directive added to .md (auto-fixed above). Feel note: even with corrected targeting (one per PC), Taunt at DC 12 CHA is rarely landing (Bazgar CHA save d20-1; Bazgar failed once of three tries in this run). **Taunt DC 12 remains weak vs. a party that saves on average 10 of 12 d20 results.** This is the 2nd SRR cycle confirming FI-SRR16-A from the previous run.

- **FI-SRR5-39-C Taunt stacking ambiguity logged:** Three ragers could each taunt a different PC — but if two Taunts land on the same PC (before the fix), which rager must the PC attack? Ruling: most-recent Taunt supersedes (added to .md). This should be noted in the encounter overview for the DM.

- **FI-SRR5-39-D Rager-C hits Marwen with two consecutive greataxe swings (14 + 3 = 17 dmg, downed):** This felt earned — Rager-C correctly aggro-marked Marwen after she Fireballed it, charged her, and double-hit (both 18 to-hit). Thematically strong. The aggro-mark mechanic (Incoming Damage Aggro) is working at the narrative level even without DB expression.

- **FI-SRR5-39-E AoE-heavy parties trivialize solo-rager-rush:** Victory in 3 rounds, Marwen downed but party wins comfortably. This slice is "easy for wizard parties, moderate for non-caster parties." This is probably appropriate for the encounter position (sub-encounter before the beholder). No fix needed.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-SRR5-39-A Berserk "always available at fight start" vs. recharge-only:** The .md says "Recharge 5-6" which in D&D RAW means the ability starts available and recharges on 5-6 at the start of the creature's turn after being used. The sim treated it as "never available until a 5-6 is rolled," which is incorrect. In correct D&D RAW, Rager-A could have used Berserk R1 if the 2-enemies-in-reach condition was met. Should the tactics note explicitly say "Berserk available at start of combat — use in R1 if two PCs are in melee range"? **Recommend:** Yes, add a tactics note; currently the .md says "use only when within 5 ft of two or more enemies" but doesn't tell the DM it's immediately available. This is a .md clarity fix.

- **MQ-SRR5-39-B Dual-dice gap (DB narration ≠ sim rolls):** The DB dispatched multiattack rolls (e.g., "to-hit 24, dmg 8") but the sim then pulled the next numbers from cache for its own hit/damage calculation, resulting in divergent values in the transcript. This is an inherent limitation of the dual-dispatch approach (DB narrates, sim tracks). No spec bug. Recommend noting in the testbot README for future agents running this sim.

- **MQ-SRR5-39-C Sabriel's save at exactly DC 12:** Sabriel saved Rager-C's Taunt with roll 12 (total). Tie goes to the saver by D&D RAW. Confirmed correct behavior. No issue.
