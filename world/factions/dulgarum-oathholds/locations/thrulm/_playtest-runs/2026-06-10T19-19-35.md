## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-FC47-A — RETREAT outcome, first in FC history. POSITIVE + DESIGN DECISION.**
Beholder triple-miss R1 (all 3 multiattack rolls below AC 18) created a completely different fight trajectory than all 46 prior FC cycles. Party killed both STDs R1 via Action Surge + smite, removed 3/4 thralls via Fireball, then sustained enough pressure to force beholder retreat at 29 HP in R5. This is mechanically valid but highly variance-dependent: triple-miss R1 has ~4% probability (+7 vs AC 18, each hit on 11+). The fight swings between "TPK R2" and "beholder retreat R5" on R1 luck. **Decision for DM:** is this variance range acceptable? Options: (1) accept it — the fight is designed to be lethal, rare R1 miss-streaks should occasionally produce this outcome; (2) reduce beholder vulnerability to R1 miss-streaks by giving it a fallback (e.g., Void Scream R1 if multiattack misses all).

**FI-FC47-B — DR fires twice, misses both (DESIGN DECISION: consider raising DR to-hit).**
Disintegration Ray hit twice in R3 and R4 but missed both (rolls 9 and 13 vs AC 18). Current to-hit is +7 (+6 base +1 void-feeding) — hit rate vs AC 18 is 50% (needs 11+). Two consecutive misses = 25% probability. DR is the beholder's most dramatically important ability; two consecutive misses in the same fight undercuts its terror. *Recommendation:* Raise `to_hit_bonus` on DR to 8 (58% vs AC 18) or 9 (65%). This keeps it threatening without guaranteeing the kill. Log as DESIGN DECISION — do not auto-fix (changes fundamental balance).

**FI-FC47-C — DD 47th-FC consecutive zero (sim gap confirmed, not spec gap).**
DD never fired for the 47th consecutive final-confrontation cycle. This cycle's specific cause: simulation logic incorrectly blocked DD on init tie (SIM-FC47-A). The spec text is correct — "hold until Sabriel completes her turn." The sim had a bug in interpreting this. Fixed in .md (FIX-FC47-A) for DM clarity, but no encounter design change needed.

**FI-FC47-D — VE becomes primary damage engine after minion wipe. POSITIVE.**
After both STDs and 3/4 thralls died in R1, Void Eruption carried the beholder's damage across R3-R5: ~30 HP to Bazgar, ~15 HP to Marwen, ~21 HP to Sabriel over 3 rounds. VE is load-bearing in extended fights and functioned correctly as intended. The lair action scales gracefully — even with all minions dead, the beholder maintains meaningful area pressure.

**FI-FC47-E — T4 (last thrall) downs Marwen R4. POSITIVE feel note.**
T4 accumulated chip damage on Marwen across R2 (3 dmg), R4 (4 dmg → knockout). The lowly thrall landing the killing blow on the Wizard is thematically satisfying. Contrast with prior cycles where thralls were "cosmetic" — here, T4 survived Fireball and contributed meaningfully. Worth noting at the table.

**FI-FC47-F — Sabriel smite toolkit mostly unused (48th-FC-ish carry).**
Sabriel used L2 smite once (R1 on STD-B) and LoH once (R5 on Marwen). In R2-R5 she attacked the beholder (AC 17) and missed 6 of 7 times. Smites require hits to fire. This is RNG-variance not design failure, but worth noting: Sabriel's role as "divine pressure" is structurally limited by AC 17 attack targeting in extended fights. The smite pressure on STDs R1 (when she has two medium-AC targets) is where her toolkit shines; against AC 17 beholder it's a coin-flip.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-FC47-A — Beholder retreat threshold (30 HP) — well-calibrated.**
At 29 HP, party was: Bazgar 6 HP, Marwen 10 HP (just revived), Sabriel 19 HP. One more beholder action likely downs someone; one more party turn likely kills the beholder (~20-22 HP per party round of hits). The 30 HP threshold correctly captures the dramatic edge where both sides are at the knife's edge. No change recommended.

**MQ-FC47-B — DR to-hit (+7) vs AC 18 is 50% — consider +8 or +9.**
See FI-FC47-B. Log as design consideration. If DR-to-hit is raised, update both the DB row (action spec `to_hit_bonus`) and the beholder.md stat block. Do not auto-fix.

**MQ-FC47-C — VE targeting downed PCs (MQ-FC45-B carry).**
Marwen was downed at end of R4; VE in R5 excluded her per sim logic (conscious-only check). RAW: VE hits all creatures in range including downed ones (each hit = 1 death save failure). In this cycle, the fight resolved before this mattered, but the question persists. DM option: exclude downed bodies from VE for dramatic cleanness, or include per RAW to accelerate death-save accumulation.

**SIM-FC47-A — DD-window blocked by init-tie in sim (now clarified in .md).**
The simulation used `beh_init < sab_init` as the condition for firing DD post-Sabriel. This failed when they tied (both init 16). The .md fix (FIX-FC47-A) clarifies the intent. No encounter spec change — this was a simulation interpretation error.
