### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

#### FI-TW2-A: Call Weakness structurally out of range in tank-wall formation (DESIGN DECISION)
- **Context:** 2026-06-06 18:20 UTC, slice #2 cycle 1 (first tank-wall run). Seed 494658.
- **Finding:** The shardcaller's Call Weakness (30ft range) cannot reach the derro-rager once it charges into melee. Rager starts 15ft from party; SC starts 50ft behind Rager (65ft from party). After Rager charges (round 1), SC-to-Rager distance is ~65ft — more than double CW's 30ft range. The SC held all 3 CW charges unspent the entire fight. The slice's stated goal is "test taunt + call_weakness pairing" — CW never fired.
- **Root cause:** SC back-line position rule (40-60ft from enemies) + CW range (30ft) + Rager charging behavior creates a structural dead zone. The two rules are in direct conflict in any formation where the Rager is the melee actor.
- **Options:** (a) Increase CW range to 60ft to match Shard-Throw range; (b) Add a rule to SC tactics: "If the Rager is the only melee ally, advance to 25ft behind the Rager before R1 (still 40ft from PCs) to keep CW in range until the charge"; (c) Script tank-wall as a shorter starting range (Rager 5ft, SC 30ft) so CW lands R1 before combat opens up.
- **Recommendation:** Option (a) is cleanest — 60ft CW matches Shard-Throw range and the SC's operational envelope. **Human decision.**

#### FI-TW2-B: Shard-Barrage blocked by Rager until Rager dies (feel issue)
- **Context:** Same run.
- **Finding:** Barrage was available both rounds, but the Rager was in the line with HP > 10 (R1: 38HP, R2: 20HP). Per .md rules ("do not fire if a living ally is in the line unless that ally is below 10 HP"), SC could not fire. Barrage only fired in R2 AFTER the Rager was killed by Sabriel — and immediately recharged (d6=6). SC's premiere offensive action effectively never helps in a 2-NPC formation because the Rager's front-line position always blocks the line.
- **Note:** This is consistent with the design intent (don't friendly-fire the tank), but the result is that the SC is reduced to multiattacking at disadvantage through the whole Rager's life. The Barrage becomes an "after the tank falls" cleanup ability, not a synergy tool.
- **Design question for Joe:** Is this intended? SC as "mop up after the Rager absorbs focus" is a valid design. But the .md describes SC as a "tactical support" who "scatters lines with barrages" — the current formation never realizes this.

### Mechanical questions

#### MQ-TW2-A: Incoming Damage Aggro (IDA) + Madness Endurance (ME) overlap — which applies when?
- **Context:** R2, Rager's turn. Rager took hits from Bazgar (crit) and Sabriel in R1. Both IDA and ME could apply.
- **Question:** IDA grants advantage on the next attack vs whoever hit it. ME grants +1 to attack rolls after taking damage. Do they stack? If a PC hits the Rager, does the Rager get both advantage (IDA) AND +1 to-hit (ME) on its next swing vs that PC?
- **Ruling in sim:** Applied both (IDA adv + ME +1) on Rager's R2 Swing 1. This is the most aggressive reading (both are separate traits). If they're intended to stack, Rager's R2 IDA+ME swing was +5 bonus with advantage — notably powerful.
- **Recommendation:** Clarify in Rager .md: "IDA and ME are independent traits. IDA grants advantage, ME grants a flat +1. Both may apply simultaneously on the same attack — the combined effect is advantage + 1 to-hit." This is already how the sim ran it; just needs explicit statement in .md.

#### MQ-TW2-B: Does Taunt's "end of target's next turn" mean the taunt is completely gone after one turn?
- **Context:** Taunt failed both rounds, so this didn't come up in play, but the wording should be clear.
- **Question:** The .md says "has disadvantage on attack rolls against targets other than the rager until the end of its next turn." If Bazgar fails the Taunt save on the Rager's turn in R1, the effect lasts "until end of Bazgar's next turn" = until end of Bazgar's R1 turn (since Bazgar acts after the Rager in this initiative ordering). That's a very short window — potentially the Rager taunts Bazgar and then Bazgar's turn resolves within the same round, clearing the taunt before R2. Is this intended?
- **Ruling in sim:** Applied taunt_on["bazgar"] = rnd + 1, meaning "expires at start of Bazgar's R2 turn" — giving one round of coverage. This feels correct.
- **Recommendation:** No spec change, but worth noting that the "end of target's next turn" effectively gives 0–1 turns of uptime depending on Bazgar's place in initiative. With Bazgar at init 1 (last in order), Taunt can cover his turn and R2 start of turn if he acts before the end clears.
