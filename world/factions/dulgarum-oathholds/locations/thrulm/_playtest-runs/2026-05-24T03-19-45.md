### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-1: Call Weakness initiative blindness (3rd-cycle confirmation)**
- Shardcaller used CW on DW-Derro-1 in R1 (DW-1 goes at init 3, AFTER Bazgar at init 9). Bazgar killed DW-1 before it acted — CW advantage was never used. In R2, CW was applied to DW-2; DW-2 acted BEFORE the Shardcaller (init 17 vs 14), so the window for CW benefit was DW-2's R3 turn — but DW-2 was killed by Sabriel in R3 before attacking.
- Pattern: Both CW uses were wasted because the target was killed before using the benefit. Root cause: Shardcaller tactics don't specify "prefer allies who act BEFORE high-damage PCs in initiative." Correct CW target in this init order would be DW-Derro-2 in R1 (acts at init 17, before Bazgar at 9), giving it advantage on its immediate next attack. CW on DW-1 (init 3, after Bazgar) is almost always going to be wasted in a high-lethality patrol encounter.
- Recommendation: Add to `derro-shardcaller.md` tactics: "Call Weakness on the ally who acts NEXT in initiative — prefer allies whose turn comes before the party's highest-damage dealer. Avoid CW on allies who act after the main damage dealer; they are likely to be dead before benefiting." This is an authoring-only, low-risk fix. This is a new recommendation not covered by DD-8 (which addressed CW on low-HP allies, not initiative-order mismatch).

**FI-2: Tactical Drilling advantage incorrectly modeled in R2**
- DW-Derro-1 was dead in R2; DW-Derro-2's only potential "adjacent ally" would be the Shardcaller, who is 50 ft away. The sim still rolled with advantage in R2 (carryover from R1 logic). In reality DW-2 should have rolled flat d20+4 in R2. This understates the ~56% miss rate vs AC 18 at flat roll. DD-22 (from 2nd-cycle run) identified this harness flaw; it persists.

**FI-3: Sabriel structurally untouched in threshold-patrol (3rd consecutive run)**
- 3rd-cycle: Sabriel 44/44 HP. 2nd-cycle (2026-05-23 09:18): "Sabriel untouched." 1st-cycle (2026-05-23 01:37): implied (damage report focused on Marwen). Pattern: Sabriel's high initiative (tied or first) + AC 19 + NPCs targeting Bazgar (melee) and Marwen (caster) means Sabriel is never prioritized. In a 3-round fight, NPCs never get the turns needed to rotate to Sabriel. The threshold-patrol slice is therefore a lopsided Bazgar damage-sponge fight, not a distributed-threat fight.
- This is informational (not a bug), but suggests that if the encounter design goal is "patrol threatens everyone," either (a) give the Shardcaller explicit caster-targeting for its multiattack toward Sabriel OR (b) have the Shardcaller's Barrage line cover Sabriel's approach corridor. Low priority for human review — the patrol is meant to be beatable, just threatening.

**DD-1 (re-confirmed, 3rd cycle): Shard-Barrage double-fire**
- Barrage fired in R1 (available from start) and recharged R2 (rolled 6/6). 2/3 threshold-patrol runs now show double-barrage in a 3-round fight. The single-barrage-per-fight scenario (only in the 2nd-cycle run where barrage never recharged) may be the rare case.
- At recharge 5-6 (33%/round), expected fires in 3 rounds after first use: 1.0 fires. Two fires in 3 rounds = 2 out of 3 remaining rounds recharged = 67% — above expected but not extreme. The issue is more structural: Barrage is always available R1 (no warm-up), so a minimum of 1 fire is guaranteed, and with 33% recharge there's a real chance of 2. If DD-1's recommendation (recharge 6, 17%) were implemented, expected additional fires in 3 rounds drops from ~1.0 to ~0.5, making double-barrage a coin flip rather than a near-certainty.

### Mechanical questions
- **MQ (new): CW duration window vs initiative** — The "advantage until end of Shardcaller's next turn" window spans roughly one full round of everyone's turns. In this init order, CW applied at init 14 (Shardcaller) benefits allies acting at any time until the next init-14 mark. This includes DW-2 (init 17, next round) — which is actually a broader window than most people expect. Confirm whether this broad window is intended, or if duration should be "until end of that ally's next turn" (narrower, can't be pre-applied before the ally has acted this round). Current wording allows the Shardcaller to "bank" CW advantage on an ally who hasn't yet acted this round, giving immediate use on the ally's upcoming turn in the same round.
