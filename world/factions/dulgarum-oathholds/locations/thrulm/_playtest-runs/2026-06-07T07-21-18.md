### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-EVD-7-A — Unstable Ground lair-spam (log for human)**
The solo beholder defaulted to Unstable Ground every round for all 5 rounds. Bazgar failed 3/4 Dex saves (rolled 12, 4, 12, 6 — all failed; only R5 rolled 20). Each failure cost Bazgar half his movement standing up, heavily curtailing melee contribution — he never reached effective attack positioning without burning Precision Attack maneuvers. Without thralls alive, the beholder has no Manifest Thralls use; it logically falls back to Unstable Ground. Tactics section should note: **when solo and party is clustering, prefer Void Eruption as lair action (20 ft radius, 2d10 force, no save loop)**. Unstable Ground is best as single-target disruption; rotating to Void Eruption R2+ is stronger when party clumps near the altar.

**FI-EVD-7-B — Drain Divinity LA timing and pre-commitment (DESIGN DECISION)**
The beholder spent its full legendary action budget (3 of 3) on Drain Divinity in rounds 1–2. R1: Sabriel resisted (wasted). R2: Sabriel failed (L2 smite stripped). After each DD, zero LA remained for end-of-other-turns reactive use (Tentacle, Void Ray). This is the correct usage per the tactics note ("use DD early R1–R2"), but the consequence is zero reactive LA all round. This is a design tradeoff: all-in on slot attrition vs. distributed reactive pressure. At L5, Sabriel only has 2 L2 slots — after 2 successful DDs, she's reduced to L1 smites (+2d8 vs +4d8). Recommend tactics note add: *if Sabriel resists R1, do not retry DD R2 — switch to Void Ray (2 LA) and Tentacle (1 LA) for sustained pressure instead of burning another full budget on a coin-flip.*

**FI-EVD-7-C — Negotiation path confirmed (log for human)**
Solo beholder, party attempts negotiation. Confirmed: party goal (reach deeper shaft) is antithetical to the beholder's only negotiable concession (leave shafts unexplored). The negotiation hooks in the `.md` are correctly scoped to parties who CAN abandon their quest objective. For the Compass Edge pursuing the main quest, this fight is non-negotiable. This is intentional. **No change needed.**

**FI-EVD-7-D — Antireality reaction effectiveness (DESIGN DECISION)**
Antireality triggered 4+ times across the fight (every Bazgar hit). The +2 AC effect only matters when an attack roll lands at exactly AC 17–18 (i.e., the raw d20 + hit bonus equals 17 or 18). Bazgar's hits: 22, 27, 23, 21, 23 — all comfortably above AC 19. Zero actual blocks in this run. At L5 (+7 attack), the beholder's effective AC against that fighter is 17; the reaction only blocks rolls of 10–11, approximately an 11% chance per triggering hit. The reaction is functionally near-useless against a well-built melee PC and is only occasionally relevant against rogues or lower-bonus attackers. **Recommendation: consider changing Antireality to grant advantage on one saving throw (vs. the triggering attacker) instead of +2 AC, OR increase to +4 AC (still weaker than Shield but more often relevant). Flagged for human review.**

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-EVD-7-A — Maw auto-crit-on-grapple never fired (log note)**
In R4-R5, Bazgar was grappled when the Maw attack was delivered — but my sim targeted Marwen with the Maw instead of the grappled Bazgar, so the auto-crit rider never triggered. At a real table, the DM would prioritize the Maw on a grappled target for the auto-crit. This combo should be explicitly called out in the tactics section: **"When a target is grappled, prioritize that target for Maw attacks — auto-crit from grapple + 4d8+3 is 4d8+3 doubled, averaging ~47 damage, a potentially one-shot on Marwen or a near-kill on Bazgar."**

**MQ-EVD-7-B — Drain Divinity LA timing in simulation**
In this run, DD was modeled as "happening on the beholder's own turn" consuming LA pre-turn. RAW: legendary actions are spent at the end of other creatures' turns, not on the beholder's own turn. At a real table, DD should be used at the end of Sabriel's turn, leaving the beholder's own action for Multiattack or VS. The playtest sim got the LA spend right but the *timing* wrong. No mechanical outcome difference here, but the tactics note should clarify: "use DD as the end-of-Sabriel's-turn LA spend — this preserves the beholder's own-turn action for Multiattack or VS."

**MQ-EVD-7-C — Void Scream half-damage script artefact (non-bug)**
The Void Scream section showed Marwen saving successfully (roll 21 vs DC 16) but taking 0 HP damage — the script applied the full roll from its own `d(10)×6` loop rather than the halved value. Marwen should have taken ~12 psychic on a save; the script zeroed her out due to a conditional branch error in my simulation code. This is a *simulation script bug*, not a DB or `.md` bug. The DB output for `void_scream` is correct. The outcome (Marwen down R3) is therefore slightly off — with correct half-damage, Marwen would have been at ~11 HP after R3, not 0. This would have changed R4 significantly (Sabriel could have attacked instead of using LoH). **Corrected assessment: encounter would have lasted 5-6+ rounds with a more contested outcome. The beholder may not have triggered the retreat threshold.**
