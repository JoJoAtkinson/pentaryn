## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

**BUG-BEL3-S3-1 (FIXED): `apply_condition_on_hit` for tentacle lash incorrectly requiring a STR save to apply grapple.**

Both the standalone `tentacle_lash` action and the tentacle lash attacks within `multiattack` had `apply_condition_on_hit: {condition: "grappled", save_dc: 16, save_ability: "str"}`. This caused dispatch to output `[ASK PLAYER: on a HIT, Tentacle Lash target rolls a DC 16 STR save — on fail, apply grappled]`.

Per the beholder .md: "Hit: ... and the target is **grappled** (escape DC 16)." Grapple is **automatic on hit** — no STR save to avoid it. The DC 16 is the *escape* DC (Athletics/Acrobatics to break free, not to avoid the initial grapple). The old spec would cause a table DM to let a player avoid grapple with a STR 17 roll, which is incorrect.

**Fix:** Changed both records to `rider_on_hit: "target is automatically grappled (no save; escape DC 16 — Athletics or Acrobatics check)"`. DB validated clean (0 invalid records). Phase A re-run: 29/29.

Diff summary: `tentacle_lash` and `multiattack` multiattack attacks — removed `apply_condition_on_hit` block, replaced with `rider_on_hit` string. Impact: dispatch now shows the correct automatic grapple note, not a false STR save prompt.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-BEL3-S3-A (new): Maw auto-crit one-shots Sabriel R2 (41 piercing, 44→-12 from grapple→crit chain). DESIGN DECISION.**
TL2 grappled Sabriel (AC19, barely hit on 25), then Maw auto-critted for 8d8+4 avg ~40. Sabriel went from 44 HP to -12 in one turn (R2). The grapple→Maw crit combo is working as designed, but at a L5 party the result is instant elimination of the highest-HP PC in one turn. Against a L5 divine martial (44 HP), the maw crit is a coinflip kill (needs 44+ on 8d8+4; avg is ~40, so ~45% chance to one-shot). Question for human: is this the intended feel? It's very swingy — the fight hinges on whether the TL hits. Recommend: add a note to beholder tactics explicitly calling out that the maw crit is the flagship lethal spike, and DMs should narrate it as a "this is the moment" beat, not a silent table roll.

**FI-BEL3-S3-B (new): Drain Divinity fired successfully R1 (reserve protocol confirmed working) but stripped slot went unused — Sabriel died before casting any smites.**
DD fired post-Sabriel R1 (Cha save 5 < DC16; lost L3 smite). But Sabriel missed all attacks R1 and died R2 without using any smites. DD's slot strip was wasted — Sabriel never smited. This is the same structural problem flagged in FIX-EV7-30-B: DD is optimal when Sabriel has L2+ slots AND will survive to use them. If Sabriel is grappled+maw-critted R2 before smiting, DD timing doesn't matter. Not a bug — confirms that the R1 protocol works mechanically (DD fires, slot stripped) but the encounter resolves too fast for the anti-smite counter to matter.

**FI-BEL3-S3-C (new): 3 thralls survived all 3 rounds while contributing near-zero meaningful pressure on AC18/19 party.**
All thralls alive end R3. Thralls landed: R1 lair 2/3 hits on Marwen (7 dmg), R1 Thrall-B hit Marwen (3 dmg), R2 lair 1/3 hit Marwen (5 dmg), R2 Thrall-B hit Bazgar (5 dmg), R3 lair 2/3 hit Bazgar (9 dmg). Total thrall damage: 29 over 3 rounds, 9 attack rolls hit of ~27 total (33%). The hits came primarily against Marwen (AC15) through lair reactions. Once Marwen was down in R2, thralls could barely touch Bazgar (AC18; +3 dagger hits on 15+ = 30%). Thralls function as a tax on Marwen's HP — meaningful because Marwen is AC15 and lair reaction doubles their attack rate — but they don't threaten Bazgar or Sabriel. Consider: are thralls intended as "soft target pressure" (Marwen attrition) or as "melee backup" for the beholder? If former, working as intended. If latter, their to-hit is inadequate for the escort role.

**FI-BEL3-S3-D (new): Beholder turn R2 — TL-grapple + maw-crit + LA void-ray eliminated 2/3 PCs in one round. Action-economy snapshot.**
Sequence: TL2 grappled Sabriel → Maw auto-crit 41 piercing (Sabriel DOWN) → LA Void Ray 26 force (Marwen DOWN). Two legendary actions (2 LA cost) allowed a second PC kill in the same round. Total LA budget this run: 3 LA spent on DD R1; 2 LA spent on VR R2; 1 LA remaining R2 (not spent). The beholder had enough LA economy to burn all 3 on DD then refill and spend 2 on VR the next round. Whether this is "too efficient" is a design question: the encounter is *meant* to be overwhelming, and the maw crit + VR sequence is narratively distinct (grapple kill + ranged finish). Logging for human review.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-BEL3-S3-B (new): Grapple-then-Maw-on-different-target ambiguity.**
The beholder's multiattack fires TL1, TL2, then Maw. If TL1 grapples Target A and TL2 grapples Target B, which target gets the maw auto-crit? The maw spec says "if the target is grappled by the beholder" — it's not target-specific. In this run, TL2 grappled Sabriel, so Sabriel was the maw target (most dangerous available grappled target). The mechanics work, but the tactics section doesn't specify maw-target priority when multiple creatures are grappled. Recommend: add "if multiple creatures are grappled, Maw targets the lowest-HP grappled PC" to the beholder tactics section, to standardize DM decisions.

**MQ-BEL3-S3-C (new): Does L5 paladin have Aura of Protection? (Affects DD efficiency.)**
DD save: Sabriel Cha save 5 < DC16. Sabriel's Cha save bonus was modeled as +3 (Cha modifier only). Aura of Protection (L6 paladin feature) adds Cha modifier to all saves within 10ft — including the paladin's own. If Sabriel is L5, no aura (aura triggers at L6). The sim correctly modeled L5 (no aura). At L6, Sabriel's Cha save would be +3+3=+6, making DD DC16 much harder to overcome (~50% success at +6 vs 75% success at +3). Worth confirming party level — if Compass Edge advances to L6 mid-campaign, DD's value jumps significantly vs Sabriel.
