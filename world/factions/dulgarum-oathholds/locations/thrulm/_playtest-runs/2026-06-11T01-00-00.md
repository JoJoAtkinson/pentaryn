### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TW2-43-A CW-FIRES-FIRST-TIME-43rd-TW2 (structural confirm, not a win)**
CW fired once (Rager init 16, SC init 14 — 2-point gap, first time SC acted R1 before enough PC damage to kill Rager). CW advantage collected R2 on Rager's Multiattack — but Rager hit anyway (to-hit 23 vs AC 18) and died mid-R2. The advantage was functionally wasted: with +4 to hit vs AC 18, the Rager misses only on a roll of 13 or less (35% miss rate). CW turns that to ~12% double-miss (with advantage). Marginal value. The one run where CW actually fires shows it still doesn't meaningfully change the fight. **Structural conclusion: CW is a beholder-escort mechanic that provides negligible benefit in the 2-NPC tank-wall slice. Consider either (a) accept it doesn't shine here, (b) raise Rager HP to 65+ so it reliably survives to R2+ and CW advantage compounds over multiple rounds.**

**FI-TW2-43-B Berserk-movement-restriction ambiguity (new — unlogged in prior 42 cycles)**
Berserk action text: "It cannot move on this turn." Tactics text: "Use R1 if the rager can reach 2+ enemies after its charge." These are contradictory. If "cannot move" means zero movement for the entire turn, the Rager starting 30 ft from the party cannot use Berserk productively on R1 (no targets in reach). If "cannot move" means cannot move AFTER the action (standing firm post-swing), then charging first is legal. This ambiguity has been silently ignored in 43 cycles (simulations assumed charge-then-Berserk). **Recommend: clarify in .md whether movement is permitted before Berserk activates. If charge-then-Berserk is intended design, change the restriction to "cannot move after this action" or "movement is reduced to 0 for the remainder of this turn after Berserk resolves."**

**FI-TW2-43-C SC-Barrage-friendly-fire-line-geometry (new)**
The SC's Barrage friendly-fire check in tactics says "Do not fire if a living ally is in the line." The simulation only tested `rager_in_melee` (rager_pos ≤ 0 ft) as the trigger. When the Rager is at 30 ft and SC is at 45 ft, the Rager is BETWEEN the SC and the party — squarely in the 15-ft Barrage line. The sim incorrectly fired Barrage R1 and hit all three PCs for 12 piercing without also tagging the Rager. **Recommend: add to .md tactics: "Also withhold Barrage if the Rager is between you and the target line (i.e., 5–30 ft from party while you are 40+ ft away and have not yet moved). In tight rooms the Rager's charge position is often in your line of fire."**

**FI-TW2-43-D Taunt zero effect 43rd consec TW2**
Taunt fired R1, Marwen failed save, but she attacked the Rager anyway (only rational R1 target). Disadvantage only matters if the taunted PC wants to attack a NON-rager target — in a 2-NPC formation where the Rager charges first, the closest and highest-threat target IS the Rager. 43rd consecutive zero effect in this slice. Same structural conclusion: Taunt shines only when the SC is the primary target preference of the party (e.g., beholder escort where SC is more dangerous to kill first). In tank-wall, the Rager's charge makes itself the obvious focus. Taunt should redirect back-liners toward the SC — but if the SC isn't threatening enough to be the preferred target, the disadv penalty doesn't redirect anyone.

**FI-TW2-43-E Marwen near-death (3 HP) without Fireball**
First time Marwen drops below 5 HP in TW2 without a TPK. Shard-Throw spam over 3 rounds dealt 12+7+10=29 damage. With no Fireball opener to end the fight fast, the SC had two full rounds of throwing. This confirms the no-Fireball variant is a meaningful stress test. Marwen at 3/32 HP is a legitimate second-wind-or-die scenario. **POSITIVE: shows the SC is not trivially ignorable when the Rager holds attention long enough.**

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TW2-43-A Shard-Throw damage 10 — FALSE ALARM (SIM error, spec correct)**
Dispatch R3 showed Shard-Throw "dmg 10 piercing." Investigated: DB spec is `1d8+2` (not `1d4+2` as assumed in the simulation). d8+2 range is 3–10; 10 is a valid roll. The simulation used the wrong damage die (d4 instead of d8) — SIM error, not a spec bug. Dispatch was authoritative and correct. **No action needed on the spec.** The sim's lower damage estimates for Marwen in R1-R2 were understated; actual shard throw output could reach 10 per throw.

**MQ-TW2-43-B CW advance-grant vs. same-turn benefit**
Per MQ-12: CW on already-acted ally at >20 HP is correct. But the advantage collects NEXT turn. In a 2-round fight, CW is spent R1 and collected R2 — but by R2 the Rager is typically at ≤20 HP (guard fires) OR dead. This fire confirmed: Rager at 52 HP R1, SC CWs correctly, Rager at 7 HP R2 so guard HOLDS the next CW charge correctly. The one use of CW gave advantage on a Rager at 7 HP attacking with +4. Advantage on a nearly-dead Rager is not valuable. **Structural: CW requires Rager to be healthy AND unthreatened for multiple turns to provide compounding value. The 2-NPC tank-wall slice makes this nearly impossible without raising Rager HP significantly.**
