### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SCT6-48-A: CW 1/9 spent — 8th consecutive SCT cycle with structural CW waste**
SC-A died holding 3/3 charges (killed R1 before it could spend any). SC-C died holding 3/3 charges (last surviving SC, no allies to CW). SC-B spent 1 charge productively (→SC-C, enabled 2 hits on Marwen). The root cause: the stagger protocol assigns the barrage-carrier role to the lowest-init SC, which also tends to be the most exposed to being killed before acting. When any SC dies, it vacates CW targets for its surviving companions. In a 3-SC formation, losing one SC (especially the middle) leaves the remaining SCs unable to chain CW — there's no valid future-actor ally to target. Carry from FI-SCT6-47-A, 46-A, 45-A, 44-A. **Recommend for DM review:** consider allowing CW self-buff variant (SC grants advantage to themselves when no allies available), or accept that CW economy in the pure-SC formation is a deliberate design weakness that only fires when the formation survives to R2+.

**FI-SCT6-48-B: SC-C Barrage never fires — 3rd consecutive SCT cycle**
SC-C had Barrage ready every round it was alive (R1–R2) but never fired it: R1 — not the barrage carrier; R2 — SC-B had already fired (stagger rule). R3 — died before acting. This is a direct consequence of the stagger protocol + SC-C's mid-priority targeting. The Barrage is the SC's signature area threat, and having one of three SCs contribute zero Barrages feels like dead weight. Carry from FI-SCT6-46-A and 47-C. **Recommend for DM review:** explicitly allow the mid-init SC to fire Barrage in R1 if the barrage-carrier is down to ≤5 HP (pre-emptive Barrage before the carrier is killed). This would let SC-C fire Barrage R1 if SC-A had already been killed — but in this cycle SC-A was still alive at R1 so it's a moot case. More broadly: the stagger rule was designed to prevent triple-Barrage R1, but may be over-restricting output in a 3-SC-only formation vs a 3-PC party.

**FI-SCT6-48-C: Fireball decisive — 8th+ consecutive SCT cycle confirm**
Marwen at init 3 (last) cast Fireball for 34 fire. Both SCs saved half (17 each), dropping to 16 HP. This makes them trivially killable R2 (Bazgar alone can finish with 2 hits). The formation has no Fireball counter: no HP buffer, no psychic resistance, no cover mechanic. Carry from FI-SCT6-47-B, 46-B, etc. DM must announce spread ≥25ft at encounter start — Fireball may still clip only 1-2 SCs at a real table. **Note: in this cycle, Marwen was at 5 HP when she cast Fireball (R1 damage drove her down). The SCs hit Marwen heavily enough to nearly stop the Fireball; they just barely failed to kill her first.**

**FI-SCT6-48-E: SC-B last-stand Barrage at 1 HP — POSITIVE feel note**

Narrate this with desperation flavor (shardcaller screaming, emptying all remaining shards in one spitting burst). **Recommend DM note: when an SC reaches ≤5 HP with Barrage ready, fire Barrage on its action regardless of stagger (it won't survive to R2 anyway).**

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SCT6-48-A: CW self-buff — does the tactics section permit CW on self?**
The DB spec says "Choose one ally the shardcaller can see within 30 ft." Self is not an ally in 5e RAW. However, if a solo SC has no remaining allies, CW charges are always wasted. At 3/3 wasted on SC-C and 3/3 on SC-A, this represents 6 unused charges per cycle. The tactics section does not address the solo-SC case. **Recommendation: add a note to derro-shardcaller.md tactics — when the last surviving SC has CW charges remaining and no allies, use the bonus action for Tactical Retreat instead (bonus action priority already correct; just clarify the CW-hold is intentional, not a miss).**

**MQ-SCT6-48-B: Barrage stagger — mid-round death exception**
FIX-SCT6-45-A added a note: if an SC fires Barrage as a defensive action mid-round, that counts as the round's one Barrage. But if the barrage carrier dies mid-round (before firing), should another SC with a ready Barrage be allowed to fire it? Currently the answer is "no" (stagger prevents a second Barrage even if the carrier never fired). **Recommendation: clarify in .md — if the R1 barrage carrier dies before acting, the next-highest-init SC with Barrage ready MAY fire it that round (the stagger only prevents two SCs firing in the same round, not replacing a dead SC's unfired shot).**
