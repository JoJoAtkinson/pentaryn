## Negotiation Phase

Party enters vault. Beholder hovers motionless near altar. Sabriel steps forward, attempts telepathic communication via holy symbol.

Beholder floods minds with sensation: *cold pressure, absence, copper taste*. In dwarven fragments: *"...passage...you bring the-taste-of-gods... the paladin's light is unwelcome here... give us the oath. Give us the holy."*

Beholder demands Sabriel break her oath (a genuine de-escalation hook per .md interaction notes). Party can't comply. Bazgar attempts Persuasion DC18 → rolls 9 — beholder detects deception. **Combat begins.**

**Negotiation feel note:** The beholder's demand (oath-breaking) is the most interesting hook in the .md but is essentially impossible for the party's composition. The negotiation fails not because it's bad roleplay but because a paladin's divine presence is structurally incompatible with the beholder's "feeding" mechanics. Worth logging: a party without a divine character would have much stronger negotiation chances. Is this intended? It creates a "paladin-tax" on the whole slice.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-EV7-31-A — Maw-auto-crit-R1-Bazgar-51-combined-damage-instant-down-31st-confirm**
Bazgar (49 HP) took 13 bludgeoning (TL1) + 38 piercing (Maw auto-crit 8d8+3) = 51 damage → instant down on the beholder's FIRST ACTION. No PC agency, no counterplay. The auto-crit mechanic is intended (FIX-FC39-A) and the Maw damage range is documented, but 38 piercing from 8d8+3 is the high end of avg ~39 — this particular seed delivered close-to-max. The question is whether Bazgar going down before Sabriel's R1 turn mattered: it did — the DD LA target became moot since the only PC in shrine proximity was Sabriel (who then saved). The beholder's entire R1 was spent on multiattack + correct LA reservation for DD. The feel is "beholder one-rounds the tank before the party can react." For a "meant to lose" encounter this is acceptable, but **the table needs a melee warning**: Bazgar will go down R1 on a multiattack hit if he closes to range. Is this the intended opening? Versus a R1 where the beholder uses Void Scream instead — that would spread damage and give the table more turns.

**FI-EV7-31-B — DD-R1-LA-reserve-correct-Sabriel-saves-31st-cycle**
The R1 LA-reserve protocol (FIX-FC40-A, FIX-DD-TIMING) executed correctly: beholder held all 3 LA until post-Sabriel R1, then spent all 3 on Drain Divinity. Sabriel saved (d20+1=19 vs DC16 — 85th percentile roll). This is the correct play; the outcome (save) is variance. 31st-cycle **DD never fires** pattern extends: Sabriel keeps saving or is downed before DD window. The +1 Cha modifier in the sim may be wrong — paladin-class creatures have proficiency in Cha saves (+4 prof +1 mod = +5 total). A +5 Cha save vs DC16 means 55% success rate. DESIGN QUESTION: is Sabriel's Cha save bonus +1 (just modifier) or +5 (proficient paladin)? This changes DD's expected hit rate significantly. **Recommend: clarify Cha save mod for Sabriel in testbot docs.**

**FI-EV7-31-C — dual-dice-gap-VS-dispatch-16-vs-sim-38-ongoing**
Void Scream dispatch output showed "16 psychic" (6d10 = 1+3+3+1+6+2 = 16 from the cached dice). The simulation then re-rolled 6d10 independently per target and got 38. Marwen "saved" (rolled 23 on Wis) but then took 38/2=19 damage → went down anyway. The dual-dice gap creates a confusing narrative: the "official" roll is 16 (dispatch), but the sim applies a different number. This is a persistent harness issue (MQ-TW37-B, MQ-BEL3-38-B carry). At-table: use the dispatch output as authoritative. **Recommend: sim should parse damage from dispatch output rather than re-rolling.**

**FI-EV7-31-D — VS-R2-wipes-two-PCs-2nd-consecutive-EV7-cycle**
Void Scream fired R2 (first opportunity, R1 saved for Maw/grapple). Both Marwen and Sabriel went down. This matches the 30th-cycle pattern (VS R2 decisive). The beholder's action sequence: multiattack R1 → VS R2 → fight over in 2 rounds. No lair action contributed meaningfully (UG missed both rounds). Disintegration Ray, Void Eruption, and all legendary actions except 1× DD + 1× Void Ray were unused. The beholder has **significant ability abundance for a 2-round fight** — the solo-beholder encounter against 3 PCs resolves too quickly for most abilities to express. This is the 3rd EV7 cycle ending in R2-R3 TPK (27th: R3, 30th: R3, 31st: R2). **DESIGN QUESTION: Is the solo-beholder slice intended to demonstrate overwhelming CR13 power, or to test ability rotation? If the latter, the party needs some means of extending the fight (reinforcements from lower shaft, or slice uses reduced-ability beholder as stated in slice definition — "NO disintegration ray yet" for slice 3; slice 7 implies FULL beholder).**

**FI-EV7-31-E — Antireality-never-triggers-31st-cycle-structural**
Antireality (reaction: +2 AC after seeing hit roll against beholder) never triggered in this cycle or in prior EV7 cycles (noted in 29th-cycle MQ-EV7-28-C, 30th as MQ-EV7-30-B). The beholder acts BEFORE most PCs due to initiative (13, above Sabriel at 6). PCs who act before the beholder use their turns approaching or casting — they attack on turn 2 onward. Beholder has already used its reaction slot for Antireality on the PCs' attacks, but in R1: Bazgar Dashed (no attack), Marwen cast Slow (no attack). Antireality is structurally narrow: triggers only on melee/ranged attacks, not spells, and only when PCs act before the beholder. **Pattern: Antireality will almost never fire when beholder has mid-range initiative and PCs spend R1 on approach/control spells.**

**FI-EV7-31-F — negotiation-paladin-tax-structural**
The beholder's de-escalation hooks require the party to (a) not threaten the shrine, (b) offer something divine, (c) agree to leave the lower shaft. Sabriel's divine aura actively undermines (a) and (b) — the beholder "tastes" her as food. A party WITH a paladin has harder negotiation than a party without. This creates a structural tension: the class most associated with negotiation-through-authority (paladin) is a liability in this specific negotiation. Is this intentional flavor? It's thematically rich (the divine is incompatible with the void) but it should be called out in the DM notes so the human DM knows it's a design choice, not a gap.

**FI-EV7-31-G — beholder-100/110-at-TPK-R2 (extreme-lopsided)**
After a 2-round fight, the beholder is at 100/110 HP. The only damage dealt was 10 fire from Fireball (saved). No smites landed (Sabriel didn't get to attack). Bazgar's nonmagic longsword never swung. The encounter is faster than any previous EV7 cycle. This isn't necessarily wrong (the party is meant to lose) but the encounter ends before any meaningful PC ability expresses — no Action Surge, no smite, no Fireball (L3 spent on Slow). The party's tactical choices (Slow → wasted LR, Fireball → halved) didn't compound into learning. DESIGN QUESTION: should slice #7 include a "negotiation attempt bonus round" before combat starts (e.g., initiative counts from 0, party gets 1 free action each) so the fight is guaranteed to last 3+ rounds regardless of initiative order?

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-EV7-31-A — Sabriel-Cha-save-proficiency-assumption**
The sim used Cha save +1 for Sabriel (ability modifier only). A paladin with proficiency in Cha saves would be +5 at level 5 (prof +4, Cha mod +1). This changes DD's expected success rate from ~40% (DC16, +1) to ~25% (DC16, +5). Over 31 EV7 cycles, DD has landed only once (MQ-EV7-28-B cycle 28, R2 CHA-13-FAIL). If Sabriel is actually +5 Cha save, DD is nearly useless (lands ~25% vs DC16). **Recommend: explicitly define Sabriel's saving throw bonuses in the party roster or testbot config.**

**MQ-EV7-31-B — Unstable-Ground-lair-never-produces-prone-2/2-R1-R2**
Both UG uses in this cycle missed (Bazgar saved 17, Sabriel saved 20). UG has a 33% fail rate for Dex+2 targets and 25% for Dex+3. In a 2-round fight, it's likely to miss more often than not. **DESIGN NOTE: UG contributes meaningful pressure only over 4+ rounds. In short EV7 cycles (R2-R3 TPK), UG is wasted lair action real estate.** The .md should note that if a solo fight ends before R4, the lair contributed nothing.

**MQ-EV7-31-C — VS-timing-vs-multiattack-beholder-action-choice**
The beholder chose multiattack R1 (correct, VS not yet prioritized). But the tactics note says VS fires "R2+" when available. What determines whether VS fires R1 vs R2? In this cycle VS fired R2 (available R1 but beholder chose multiattack). If VS had fired R1 instead of multiattack:
- VS R1 (16 psychic avg): Marwen fails → 16 psychic → 15/32 HP remaining (same as after TL2!). Sabriel fails → 16 psychic → 28/44 HP.
- Multiattack would have been R2, and VS recharge would start R2.
This is a meaningful choice: R1 VS spreads damage but doesn't kill; R1 multiattack + grapple sets up R2 auto-crit Maw kill. The .md tactics don't specify which the beholder prefers. **Recommend: add explicit note on VS-vs-Multiattack R1 priority.**
