## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

1. **Madness Endurance invisible in DB output (derro-rager/multiattack and derro-rager/berserk)** — The Madness Endurance trait (+1 to attack rolls after taking damage) is defined in the .md but produces no reminder in the DB action output. DMs running live will forget to apply the bonus. Fixed by adding `prerequisite` note to both `multiattack` and `berserk` specs via `upsert`. The to-hit bonus is still statically 4 in the DB (cannot be conditional without roller changes), but the prerequisite text now surfaces the reminder at the table.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **DESIGN: FI-SR5-A — R1 Berserk triple-fire creates extreme initiative dependency.** All 3 ragers rolled high initiative (20/17/16) and all had Berserk available R1. Before any PC acted, Bazgar had taken 34 damage (49→15 HP). The outcome was strongly correlated with initiative rather than tactical decisions. In a narrow corridor with no PC-side mitigation (no Shield spell, no readied action), three simultaneous Berserks before the party acts can be nearly decisive in round 1. Recommendation: consider staggering rager encounter entry (e.g., one rager enters from a side passage in R2) to prevent all-Berserks-before-PCs-act, or add a flanking rule that prevents Berserk when no ally is within 10 ft of the rager. **Human decision needed: is the initiative-driven swingy outcome acceptable for this formation?**

- **FEEL: FI-SR5-B — Taunt bonus-action saturates by R2 in 3v3.** By end of R1, Marwen was taunted (Rager-1 hit save 4). By start of R2, Sabriel was also taunted. Three ragers have three bonus-action Taunts; they saturate all PC slots after 1-2 successful saves, leaving every subsequent rager bonus-action dead for the rest of the fight. The .md rule ("If all living PCs are already taunted by allies, skip Taunt this turn") fires correctly but kills the bonus action for 3+ rounds. Consider adding a tactic note: "When all PCs are taunted, use the bonus action to reposition toward a second target cluster — don't waste it." In practice with only 3 PCs and 3 ragers this is purely cosmetic (taunts expire EoNT so they don't stack), but the DM loses any sense of purposeful bonus-action use.

- **FEEL: FI-SR5-C — Berserk recharge rate feels very high in rager-only formation.** Berserk recharged on Rager-1 R2 (rolled 5), Rager-2 R3 (rolled 6), and Rager-3 R3 (rolled 5). Across 5 rounds, 6 Berserks fired in a 3-rager-only encounter. The result was relentless AoE pressure every other round with no breathing room. The "Berserk available at combat start" rule (correct per 5e RAW) means the first round is always maximum pressure. This may be intentional for a pure rager rush, but worth noting that the formation feels more like a damage spike machine than a tactical encounter.

- **MECHANICAL QUESTION: FI-SR5-D — thrall-derro/multiattack DB type is "single_attack" despite name "multiattack".** Not exercised this slice, but noted on DB review. The thrall .md says "makes one melee attack; the domination is incomplete." The type=single_attack is correct, but the action being named "multiattack" in the DB may confuse the runner's verb-matching logic — the DM typing "attack" correctly routes to it, but a DM typing "multiattack" may expect two attacks. Not a crash risk (Phase A passes), but semantically misleading. Recommendation: rename to `attack` or add a narration note: "(One attack only — thrall's domination is incomplete)."
