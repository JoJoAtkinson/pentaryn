### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**MQ-SW7-C (SIM HARNESS BUG — known class):** Hypnotic Pattern incapacitation not enforced past R1. The harness clears `_incapacitated = False` at `sot_checklist()` unconditionally, allowing both incapacitated STDs to act freely in R2+. With proper HP enforcement (both STDs fail Wis saves 5 and 3 vs DC 15), R2 would have been 3 PCs attacking helpless STDs with advantage — almost certainly killing STD1 and heavily wounding STD2 in R2 alone. This run's TPK result is a **simulation artifact**. True encounter result with HP properly enforced: likely VICTORY ~R3. This is MQ-77/79 class — no spec change, harness tracking gap.

**FI-159 7th-cycle confirm:** STDs won initiative (20, 19 vs 9, 5, 5). STD2 AR fired R1 before any PC action, dealing 17 total to Bazgar (fail) and 7 to Marwen (half). FI-159 init-dependence axis confirmed again. Note: this run also had Marwen HP locking both STDs end-of-R1 — so the init advantage was meaningful for ONE round of AR, then HP would have locked things. The real balance axis is: who fires first, STD2's AR or Marwen's Hypnotic Pattern? With STD2 init 20 > any PC, HP cannot fire first.

**FI-NEW-SW7-A DESIGN DECISION — STD Wis +0 too low vs HP DC 15 (75% incapacitation):** Across 7 shrine-wedge cycles, Hypnotic Pattern (DC 15) vs STD Wis save +0 yields ~75% per-STD incapacitation. In this run both failed (5, 3). In prior VICTORY cycles, PCs won init and HP fired first → similar lock-down. The encounter outcome (VICTORY or TPK) is almost fully determined by whether STD2 gets AR off before Marwen casts HP — a pure initiative coin-flip. Consider: (a) give STDs Wis proficiency (Wis save +2, 65% HP rate), or (b) give Shrine-Touched advantage on Wis saves near the shrine, or (c) accept init-dependence as the design (DESIGN DECISION).

**FI-NEW-SW7-B OBR first hit in 7 SW cycles (3 dmg):** Round 6, STD2 OBR vs Sabriel (AC 19): d20=16 +4=20 = HIT. Previously FI-190 showed 0 OBR hits across cycles (miss rate vs AC18-19 is ~70%). Hit confirmed, damage 3 (2s+1n) — trivial. OBR remains a very low-threat reactive. The self-damage (4 psychic R6) exceeded the hit-damage — STD damaged itself more than the PC. Consider whether OBR's primary function is UF-feeding (self-damage) rather than dealing damage, since attack probability vs AC19 is ~25%.

**FI-NEW-SW7-C AR recharge cold patch confirmed:** STD2 failed AR recharge R2, R3, R4 (rolls: 1, 3, 1) — 3 consecutive misses on a 5-6 (expected 1/3 per try). Recharged R5 but only 1 PC remained (Sabriel), preventing the 2-target cone requirement. STD2 therefore fired AR exactly once (R1) and had it recharged at a point where it was useless. With the stagger rule, R1 was the only window where AR could fire at multiple targets. This is variance, but it means the signature ability may fire only once per fight. Consider: recharge 4-6 (~50%/round) would make AR more reliably available for R2+ followup.

**FI-190 7th-cycle confirm (OBR miss vs AC18-19):** OBR fired 4× total this run; hit once (vs Sabriel AC 19 on R6 roll of 16). +4 vs AC18 = 35% hit rate, +4 vs AC19 = 30% hit rate. OBR is a self-punishing reaction that fires reliably but almost always misses the party's front-line ACs. The self-damage (1d4) is more consistent than the counter-attack damage.
