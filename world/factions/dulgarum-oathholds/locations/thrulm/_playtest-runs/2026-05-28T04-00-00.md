### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-NEW-SW6-A — FI-159 6th-cycle confirm (init-dependence → TPK)**
- Root cause unchanged from 4th cycle (2026-05-26 TPK): STD-A init 21 > Marwen 18 → AR fires BEFORE Slow or Hold Person can land → all 3 PCs fail DC 14 Dex save → party enters R2 critically wounded → Sabriel's low HP from AR + R1 STD-B multiattack puts her in UF kill range.
- Cycle record: SW TPKs on cycles 1 (MQ-28 bug), 4 (FI-158), 6 (this run). SW victories on cycles 2, 3, 5.
- Pattern is stark: PCs-win-init → VICTORY; NPCs-win-init → TPK. The encounter is a coin flip decided by who gets init 18+.
- **DESIGN DECISION for human**: Is this binary init-dependence intentional? If not, options: (A) raise AR recharge to 5-6 so R1 AR is softer; (B) give STD-B a fixed lower initiative modifier to break the two-STD init block; (C) add a "partial cover" mechanic when one STD acts before the party reaches position.

**FI-NEW-SW6-B — FI-162 2nd confirm (OBR+UF Sabriel trap)**
- Sabriel smites STD-A R1 (20 dmg — good). STD-A retaliates with OBR (miss). BUT STD-A has UF next round (≥10 dmg threshold met: 22 total R1). R2 UF multiattack with advantage targets Sabriel (13 HP) → killing blow.
- The sequence: Sabriel smites → UF activates → UF kills Sabriel before she can act again. Sabriel effectively "paid" for her smite by enabling UF, which killed her.
- This is punishing but thematically coherent — the shrine-touched REWARDS being hit. Not necessarily a bug; but worth calling out at table that smiting STD-A when Sabriel is wounded is a trap.
- **DESIGN DECISION for human**: Add "UF trigger warning" note to beholder encounter DM guidance — "if Sabriel smites STD-A, expect UF advantage multiattack next round."

**FI-NEW-SW6-C — STD-B untouched after 3 rounds (pattern, 3rd cycle)**
- Cycles 4, 5, and 6 all show STD-B at full or near-full HP after R3. The stagger rule concentrates R1 party attention on STD-A (which holds the initiative advantage). STD-B acts last, holds AR, and deals multiattack damage while drawing no fire.
- After STD-A dies, STD-B enters the fight at full HP with AR recharging — effectively a second wave.
- **DESIGN DECISION**: Is this the intended feel? STD-B fresh for round 4+ is a meaningful second-wind threat. Could be intentional. If not, consider giving STD-B a higher starting initiative modifier (or having STD-B positioned more aggressively so PCs have a reason to target it early).

**FI-NEW-SW6-D — AR DC 14 Dex: all-fail pattern**
- 6/6 PCs failed both AR saves in R1 and R2 (including Sabriel with +3 Dex). The failure rate per PC is 65-75%. With two STDs each firing once in the first two rounds, the party reliably takes 12-18 necrotic+psychic PER PC from AR alone before R3.
- At level 5 with 32-49 HP, this AR burst (cumulative ~100+ dmg across party in R1-R2) essentially decides the fight.
- **DESIGN DECISION**: DC 14 Dex may be slightly too reliable. Consider DC 15 → adds ~5% marginal save rate. Alternatively, the intent is that AR is a punishing "anchor" ability and the party should be playing around it (disrupting with Slow/Hold before STDs act). If that's the design, the DC is correct.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-NEW-SW6-A — AR dispatch vs sim re-roll divergence (MQ-77 class)**
- AR dispatch output showed "14 necrotic" for both fires, but sim re-rolled independently and got 11 and 12. Per MQ-77 convention, dispatch values should be authoritative. If dispatch values had been used: Marwen would have taken 18 (14+4 psychic rider) instead of 15, and 18 (14+4) instead of 13 in R2. Marwen would be at -4 HP after R1 (down R1) — potentially a worse outcome.
- This is a simulation fidelity issue, not a game bug. Flagged for awareness.

**MQ-NEW-SW6-B — Multiattack header damage type label**
- Output: `Shrine-Axe 1 to-hit  19 / dmg  11 slashing  (incl +3 necrotic extra_damage)`
- "11 slashing" in the header includes 3 necrotic points. A DM tracking slashing-resistance would misapply resistance (reducing by 5.5 instead of 4). The per-roll breakdown below is correct, but the header label is wrong.
- Location: `dnd_roller.py` formatter — outside blast radius.
- Impact: low (DM can see the breakdown), but could cause erroneous resistance application in fast play.
- **Recommend**: human fix in dnd_roller.py — either split the header ("8 slashing + 3 necrotic") or remove the type label from the aggregated line.

**MQ-NEW-SW6-C — OBR reaction "buff" type: no dice rolled**
- OBR is typed as `reaction_kind: "buff"` — the roller prints the effect description and does NOT roll the to-hit or damage dice. DM must roll the Shrine-Axe counter-attack manually while reading the trigger description.
- OBR fired 3 times; 0 hits (DM manually rolled 18, 6, 18 vs AC 18-19). The self-damage (d4) IS tracked in DB and fires.
- No `reaction_kind` value supports a "counter-attack" (to-hit + damage) automatically. `"damage"` type requires an `attacker_save` — different schema.
- **DESIGN DECISION**: If DM wants OBR to auto-roll, it would need either (A) a new `reaction_kind: "counter_attack"` in dnd_roller.py, or (B) retyping OBR as `single_attack` and handling it separately from the reaction framework. Both require dnd_roller.py changes outside blast radius. Current "buff" typing is functional — it's a reminder to roll manually — but it's easy to miss at a busy table.
