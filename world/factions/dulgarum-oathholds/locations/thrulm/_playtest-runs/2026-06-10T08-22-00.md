### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-EV7-36-A: Negotiation parley granted — no mechanical path to full success (36th EV7, FIRST parley success)**
Marwen rolled d20(18)+3=21 vs DC16 — the negotiation succeeded. The beholder granted one round of parley and did not attack R1. However, the slice design has no mechanic for *full* negotiation success (avoiding combat entirely). The encounter notes say negotiation is possible if: (a) party is not here to restore the shrine, (b) party agrees to leave deeper shafts unexplored, (c) party offers something connected to the god. None of those conditions are checkable in the sim slice. **Recommend DM note in beholder-thrulm.md:** "If negotiation DC reaches 20+, the beholder may offer genuine terms (see Interaction Notes). At DC 16–19, it grants parley only — combat resumes after 1 round unless the party produces an artifact or makes a binding offer." This is a design note, not a spec fix.

**FI-EV7-36-B: DR two-attempt dramatic arc — POSITIVE (NEW this cycle)**
DR missed in R2 (to-hit 14 vs AC15), recharged in R3 (d6=5), and hit in R3 (to-hit 21 vs AC15, 60 force → disintegration). The R2 miss created a moment of relief ("it missed!") followed immediately by recharge and a decisive R3 kill. This is exactly the dramatic arc the ability should produce. No change needed. Narrate the R2 miss with tension: the reality-fold hisses past Marwen's ear, close enough to leave ozone burns. The R3 hit should land as the inevitable follow-through.

**FI-EV7-36-C: Antireality zero — 36th EV7 confirm (carry)**
The beholder hovers at the altar throughout the fight. Bazgar's attacks reach via grapple range, but the beholder never prompted an Antireality reaction in any of the 36 EV7 cycles. The reaction fires "when the beholder is hit by an attack it can see" — so it should be triggering on Bazgar's hits. Root cause: the sim doesn't model Antireality (it's a reaction that fires after seeing the roll, changing a hit to a miss). At the real table this reaction fires constantly and is effectively a +2 AC bonus on hits, significantly reducing Bazgar's melee throughput. **DM reminder: Antireality fires on EVERY melee hit from a visible attacker. This matters — Bazgar hit beholder 4 times across R1-R4; if Antireality had changed even 2 of those to misses, beholder would have survived comfortably past 14 HP.** Carry from all prior EV7 cycles.

**FI-EV7-36-D: Legendary Resistance phantom — 36th confirm (carry)**
LR:3/3 unused. Marwen cast Fireball twice (DC14) and the beholder saved both easily (Dex+6 = average 16.5 vs DC14). LR was never needed. At level 5 this is expected: the party cannot reliably force a failed save on a creature with Dex+6. LR might consume once if a Sabriel Radiant smite rider triggers a beholder save, but that path didn't develop this cycle. Carry from prior cycles.

**FI-EV7-36-E: Manifest Thralls lair-vacuum — R1 lair wasted (carry)**
R1 lair action selection rolled choice 3 → Manifest Thralls. No charmed allies are present in this slice. The ability fires (dispatch narrates), then nothing happens. Previous cycles (BEL3, EV7) have logged this repeatedly. The lair action selection logic in the DM briefing/tactics should include a guard: "Use Manifest Thralls only if at least one charmed thrall is within 60 ft. Otherwise: Unstable Ground or Void Eruption." Note: in this cycle the negotiation parley suppressed the R1 lair action entirely (parley was granted, so combat didn't start R1 — but the sim still fired the lair action, which is another sim error). Carry.

**FI-EV7-36-F: DD timing — legendary vs regular action (SIM design flaw)**
DD (Drain Divinity) is a legendary action (Costs 3). In this sim it fires on the beholder's regular turn as its main action, then legendary actions Void Ray + Tentacle fire after PCs. At a real table: the beholder's main turn is Multiattack (2× Tentacle Lash + Maw), and DD fires at the END of Sabriel's turn (3 legendary actions). This changes R1 substantially: the party would take tentacle damage R1 before DD fires, and Sabriel would have already used her smite before losing the slot. The sim outcome (TPK R4) is plausible under either interpretation, but the R1 damage profile is wrong. The DB tactics text says "Legendary (3 actions)" in the drain_divinity effect — this is correct guidance for a DM. No spec fix needed. **DM reminder: Drain Divinity fires as a legendary action AFTER a creature's turn, not on the beholder's own turn.**

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-EV7-36-A: void_ray area damage — one roll or per-target?**
Void Ray (4d10 force, Dex save half) targets "one creature." The area spec rolls damage once (correct: 4d10 appears as a single value). The sim re-rolls per-target because it re-rolls all dice independently. Since it's a single target, the question doesn't arise in practice — one roll always. No action needed. Dual-dice gap carry (SIM).

**MQ-EV7-36-B: void_scream area damage — sim re-rolls per PC**
VS is a 30-ft radius and should roll 6d10 ONCE, applying to all targets (with each making their own save for full or half). The sim re-rolled 6d10 per PC, producing values of 28, 26, 43 instead of the dispatch's 33. The 43 for Sabriel is physically impossible (6d10 max is 60, so possible — but it shouldn't be re-rolled). Carry SIM-EV7-36-A. **Recommendation: the playtest harness should extract the rolled damage from dispatch output and apply it uniformly to all targets. This requires parsing the "Damage: **X type**" line from the dispatch output.**

**MQ-EV7-36-C: Disintegration Ray targeting when Marwen is already down**
DR recharged in R4 (d6=6) but wasn't fired because `marwen_hp > 0` was False (Marwen was disintegrated). The beholder defaulted to VS. At a real table the beholder would redirect DR to Bazgar or Sabriel (still alive). The sim's `elif dr_avail and marwen_hp > 0:` guard is too narrow — it should check for ANY living PC, not just Marwen. This is a sim issue, not a spec issue. No spec fix. **However**, it produced a tactically correct result: VS area > single-target DR when multiple PCs are in range and all are below 30 HP. The decision would have been the same at a real table.
