### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-BEL3-5-A: Maw-grapple crit R1 kills Bazgar in one turn (17 + 48 = 65 damage from 37 HP).** The grapple+crit combo is mechanically legal and thematically brutal, but the 65-damage swing from a single-turn sequence (TL hit→grapple→Maw crit) may be opaque at the table. The grapple rule in the .md is correct (DC 16 Str escape). No change recommended — this is the intended kill pattern for the boss. POSITIVE feel if communicated well: "the beholder seized him and the void-gap opened." Note: Lair VE R1 (12 dmg to Bazgar before he acted) set up the kill. The VE damage front-loaded the R1 sequence and made the grapple-crit one-shot possible at 37 HP instead of 49 HP. This synergy (lair preloads damage → multiattack finishes) is working as intended.

- **FI-BEL3-5-B: Fireball eliminates thrall escort R1 (confirmed 5th cycle).** 28 fire (failed save) one-shots T2 and T3 (22 HP each). T1 survived at 8 HP on a pass but contributed only 7 total damage across 5 rounds (3 hits, 3+4 piercing to Marwen). The three thralls collectively dealt 7 damage in 5 rounds vs the beholder dealing ~130+ to the party. The escort is purely a slot-tax: if Marwen has Fireball, the escort dies R1 for one L3; if she doesn't, the thralls chip 2–4 damage/round each. Neither changes the fight's outcome against a L5 party. 4th cycle also noted "VS absent=party-win (FI-BEL3-4-A)" and now with VS still absent the fight lasted 5 rounds with a TPK at 37 HP beholder. Recommend: no mechanical change — this is intentional encounter pressure. DM coaching note: the thralls' purpose is to force Marwen to spend L3 on Fireball R1 (not Fireball the beholder), not to be durable threats.

- **FI-BEL3-5-C: Drain Divinity slot-drain spiral confirmed 5th cycle.** DD fired 4 times: R1 Marwen L3 (fail: drain), R2 Sabriel L3 (pass: resisted), R3 Sabriel L3 (fail: drain), R4 Sabriel L3 (fail: drain). ### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-BEL3-5-A: Does VS start available at combat onset or does it require a recharge roll?** Under 5e RAW, "Recharge X–Y" abilities are available at combat start; the recharge roll fires at START of each of the monster's turns after use. The beholder.md checklist says "If Void Scream is USED, roll 1d6" — this only fires the recharge check after use, implying VS starts available. But previous cycle 4th run notes read "VS never recharged (0/3 recharge attempts)" suggesting it started unavailable in that sim too. **Recommendation:** Add explicit one-liner to beholder.md checklist item 2: "VS starts available at combat onset (standard 5e recharge rule); roll 1d6 ONLY after it has been used at least once." This resolves the ambiguity for both human DM and the sim harness.

- **MQ-BEL3-5-B: Maw non-crit target selection when primary is grappled but downed.** In R4, Bazgar was down (0 HP, lair VE reduced him) before the beholder's turn. The Maw redirected to Sabriel (not grappled). Under MQ-FC14-A: grapple ends on 0 HP. Correct: non-crit Maw on Sabriel (26 piercing) is valid. The redirect logic in the .md ("Redirect the Maw to the next closest enemy, or skip it if no enemy is in range") is unambiguous — good ruling.

- **MQ-BEL3-5-C: LA budget stall on R5 with all PCs down except Marwen at 1 HP.** After Maw downs Sabriel in R4, R5 opens with 2/3 PCs down and Marwen at 1 HP. Beholder had full LA budget (3) after reset. DD target: Marwen has L3×1 remaining — but Marwen is the most valuable remaining slot-holder. However, at 1 HP, Marwen is not threatening anything — she's likely to go down from any damage. The correct play per LA priority: DD (3 LA) on Marwen to drain the L3 before she spends it on escape, but beholder is at 41 HP which isn't below 40 threshold for exception. The sim chose Void Ray (2 LA) → Marwen, which killed her outright for 23 force vs her 1 HP. Void Ray at 1 HP is correct (saves the 3 LA overhead) — the .md's "DD on unconscious target (FI-NEW-EV6-C)" note covers this: "if any standing threat is in melee range, downgrade to VR+TL." With only Marwen standing at 1 HP and no melee threat, both DD and VR would achieve the same outcome (kill Marwen). Sim choice is defensible.
