### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-EV7-28-A: DD-monopolizes-entire-LA-budget-blocks-LoH-counter (28th-cycle confirm)**
Drain Divinity costs 3 LA — the entire per-round budget. The beholder fired DD on Marwen's turn in rounds 1, 2, 3, and 4, consuming all LA every round. The tactics note correctly says "use Void Ray or Tentacle LA to prevent revived ally from acting after LoH," but the beholder **cannot** — it has 0 LA after DD. In 4 rounds Sabriel revived 3 times with LoH and the beholder had zero mechanical response. The DD-cost-3 design creates a forced choice: drain slots OR counter LoH, never both. Consider: (a) reduce DD to 2 LA (allows one Tentacle reaction), (b) add a triggered reaction specifically for LoH use, or (c) document explicitly in tactics that DD=all-in-one-round and accept the LoH blind spot.

**FI-EV7-28-B: LoH-revival-loop-3x-before-pool-exhaustion (28th-cycle)**
Sabriel revived R1 (Marwen), R2 (Bazgar), R3 (Marwen) — each revival kept the party at 2+ combatants and extended the fight. Once LoH hit 0, the party collapsed in one round. This is structurally correct for the "overwhelming" difficulty intent — the LoH exhaust moment is dramatic — but in the solo beholder scenario the pool lasted longer than intended because the beholder could only kill one PC per multiattack. In multi-enemy encounters (slices 3-4) the revival loop collapses faster (FI-BEL3-33-E confirm). No spec change needed; DM coaching: treat LoH exhaustion as the inflection point in narration.

**FI-EV7-28-C: Void-Scream-and-Disintegration-Ray-never-recharged-4-rounds (28th-cycle)**
DR rolls: 1,1,4,2 — never recharged (need 5-6). VS rolls: 1,5,2,4 — never recharged (need 6). The solo beholder's distinctive abilities (disintegrate a caster, AoE psychic wipe) were completely absent. Fight was multiattack + lair + DD only. Prior 27th-cycle had VS fire twice (including recharge R2 d6=6). Solo beholder feel is heavily RNG-dependent on whether signature abilities recharge. 2/2 EV7 cycles with no DR; 1/2 with VS. Structurally: empty-void tests lair/legendary economy but the flagship abilities that distinguish a beholder from a tentacle-monster only appear ~50% of runs.

**FI-EV7-28-D: Beholder-retreat-no-thrall-block-anticlimactic (28th-cycle confirm)**
.md says "uses thralls to block pursuit" at <30 HP. Empty-void has no thralls. Beholder retreated cleanly into the deeper shaft with nothing in the way. Felt like an off switch rather than a dramatic escape. Recommend: add a parting Void Eruption lair action (init 20 fires even during retreat round if beholder is still in chamber) or a flavor note for the no-thrall case ("The creature simply contracts into the dark — no last-ditch fight, because it has learned something about this party's persistence").

**FI-EV7-28-E: Negotiation-path-no-mechanical-scaffolding (28th-cycle confirm, carry from 27th)**
Sim improvised DC 15 Persuasion for "offer something connected to the god." The .md Interaction Notes describe conditions (offer artifact/prayer/true name, not threatening shrine, agree to leave shafts) but provide no DCs, no skill check types, no outcome table. The slice is specifically designed to test negotiation + legendary/lair flow, but without scaffolding the negotiation is just narrative. Recommend: add a sidebar with DC 14 Insight (understand what it wants) → DC 16 Persuasion (negotiate a deal) → DC 18 Persuasion (convince it without a sacrifice). Different success thresholds = different lair action profiles during the negotiation period.

**FI-EV7-28-F: Resistance-wall-Bazgar-crippled-28th-cycle-confirm**
Bazgar dealt 14 (R1 w/ Action Surge) + 2 (R2) + 10 (R3) = **26 total across 4 rounds**, all halved from raw ~52. Without a magical or sanctified weapon, Bazgar is mechanically a speed bump. This is intended by the beholder's design but the cheat sheet (COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md) should call this out explicitly: "Bazgar needs a magic/sanctified weapon or he deals ~5 effective DPR." DM coaching gap, not spec bug.

**DD-EV7-28-A: Temp HP from DD = 20 across 4 rounds (6+6+4+4)**
Meaningful buffer: absorbed what would have been 20 more damage from party. However the beholder ended at 26 HP — temp HP had no decisive influence on retreat timing (10 HP lower without temp HP, still above 0 when retreat triggered). Confirms prior finding: DD's real value is slot attrition, not temp HP. The 26 HP at retreat is actually encouraging — the party pushed it to the retreat threshold despite the resistance wall, suggesting the encounter scaling is working.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-EV7-28-A: DD target criteria — Bazgar excluded always?**
DD targets "creature with spell slots, divine favor, or clerical powers." Bazgar (Fighter BM) has none of these. Beholder always skips Bazgar for DD. Is this intended? If yes, the tactics note should explicitly say "DD never targets Bazgar." If Fighter gets any maneuver superiority dice, are those "divine favor"? Recommend clarify.

**MQ-EV7-28-B: DD cost 3 LA — is this intentional given the LoH blind spot?**
The 3-LA cost was presumably set to make DD expensive and not spammable. But because it equals the entire budget, it creates a binary: either DD (and ignore LoH) or don't DD at all. With a 2-cost DD, the beholder could DD + Tentacle, enabling the LoH counter that the tactics note describes. Human decision needed.

**MQ-EV7-28-C: Chase mechanics undefined**
Sabriel at 31 HP with beholder at 26 HP in the deeper shaft — no mechanics for pursuit. The .md notes the beholder retreats to the lower shaft but doesn't define the chase (movement, terrain, hazards). If the party pursues, is this a new encounter? Does the deeper shaft have its own lair actions? DM gap.

**MQ-EV7-28-D: Persuasion roll scripting artifact (simulation only)**
The sim had a dual-print bug where the persuasion roll was displayed once in the P() line (d20=11, result=13) and evaluated again with a separate d20 call. The narrative initially said SUCCESS before being corrected to FAIL. This is a testbot sim bug, not a game mechanic issue. No action needed on spec.
