## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **FIX-SRR13-A:** `derro-rager.md` start-of-turn checklist: added Item 5 documenting prone-Berserk tradeoff — (a) stand up (spend half move, cannot Berserk this turn — Multiattack instead), or (b) stay prone + Berserk (disadvantage on all attack rolls, DM applies manually). Rager had no guidance when R-A was knocked prone by Bazgar Trip Attack and immediately Berserked — disadvantage was not applied in this simulation run.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SRR13-A: Berserk-while-prone no DM guidance (FIXED inline).** R-A (tripped by Bazgar in R1) immediately Berserked while still prone with no guidance note. At the table, a DM might apply no penalty. The fix adds the stand-vs-stay coaching note. Note: staying prone and Berserking is tactically viable when 2+ PCs are in reach even with disadvantage, but the .md was silent on this tradeoff.

- **FI-SRR13-B: Taunt vs save-based casters — 13th cycle confirm.** Both R-A (→ Bazgar) and R-B (→ Marwen) landed Taunts R1. Marwen's Taunt was mechanically null both rounds she cast Fireball (save-based). The .md documents this limitation correctly, but in a solo-rager encounter with 3 PCs, one rager's Taunt slot is statistically wasted if assigned to the wizard. FI-31 tells ragers to split taunts — consider coaching note: "In solo-rager formations, if one PC is a save-caster (no attack rolls), skip Taunt on that target and double-apply to a melee-reliant PC if FI-31 allows." (Not auto-fixed — this is a balance-adjacent coaching change.)

- **FI-SRR13-C: Berserk all-miss on cold dice — 13th-cycle pattern.** R-B Berserked R1 (3 PCs in reach) and missed all 3 (sim to-hits 9/8/6 vs ACs 18/15/19). Combined with the no-movement prereq, R-B contributed zero damage R1 and died to Fireball before landing a single hit. This confirms the ongoing Berserk feel issue (FI-SRR10-B, FI-SRR11-A): the action is high-variance and the no-movement prereq amplifies the cost of cold dice. Not a spec change candidate — this is intentional high-risk/high-reward — but worth tracking cycle count.

### Mechanical questions

- **SIM note — FI-31 split tracks attempt, not success:** This simulation tracked which PC was successfully taunted (failed save) rather than which PC was targeted by a Taunt attempt. This caused R-C (R2) to re-target Bazgar when R-B had already attempted (but Bazgar resisted) that Taunt. At the table, DMs should split Taunt targets based on which PC each rager is TARGETING, not which ones failed — a resisted Taunt still "uses up" that rager's assignment for FI-31 split purposes. The .md FI-31 note does not explicitly state this. Consider adding: "Split targets are assigned by targeting intent, not by whether the save succeeds."
