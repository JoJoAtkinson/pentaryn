## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed
- **FI-181 (TR vs CW bonus-action conflict):** Added note to `derro-shardcaller.md` start-of-turn checklist item 3: TR and CW are both bonus actions; when TR fires, CW is forfeited. Prioritize TR. Also added to tactics "If a melee threat closes within 15 ft" bullet.
- **FI-182 (Slow + TR forced choice):** Added note to checklist item 3 and tactics: if SLOWED (action XOR bonus action), use Multiattack (action) and abandon TR.
- **MQ-72 (CW + disadvantage cancel to straight roll):** Added note to CW tactics: CW advantage cancels with disadvantage from Slow/Restrained/etc. to a straight roll per 5e — still worth spending (removes the net disadvantage).
- **MQ-73 (TR timing clarification):** Added to checklist item 3: TR is a turn-START conditional, not a reaction. If SC acted before the threat closed this round, TR is available next round.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-180 (POSITIVE):** TR fired for the **first time** in 5 shardcaller-team cycles (R3 SC-C, Bazgar at 5 ft). DD-47's authoring fix ("bonus action, not main action" framing) is working. The key condition: SC-C survived long enough (7 HP) to see Bazgar in melee at the START of its own turn. In previous cycles SCs died before this alignment occurred. R3 TR activation is a meaningful pattern: TR fires only when (a) SC survives past R1-R2 focus fire AND (b) SC's initiative fires AFTER the PC closes (i.e., Bazgar init 17 > SC's init would not work — but here SC-C init 21 > Bazgar init 17, so SC-C acted first each round; Bazgar closed BETWEEN SC-C's turns). Wait — SC-C init 21 > Bazgar 17: SC-C always acts BEFORE Bazgar. So Bazgar closed on his turn AFTER SC-C acted. On SC-C's next turn (R3), Bazgar was ALREADY in melee. This is the correct condition. Conclusion: TR fires reliably when PC has higher initiative than SC and SC survives to see the melee gap at its own turn start.

- **FI-181 (DESIGN DECISION — NEW, auto-fixed in .md):** TR vs CW bonus-action conflict is live. The fight demonstrated: SC-C had a live CW charge when TR fired. The DM must choose TR or CW. TR won. This costs 1 CW charge per TR event. In a 3-SC formation with 3 charges each (9 total), losing 1 CW per TR event is acceptable. But in the 2-SC endgame, losing a CW to TR leaves the surviving SC completely without buff ability. Worth monitoring in future shardcaller-team cycles.

- **FI-182 (DESIGN DECISION — NEW, auto-fixed in .md):** Slow spell creates a hard SC dilemma: SLOWED SC cannot pair TR + Multiattack. This cycle SC-B was forced to choose and picked Multiattack (correct per tactics). But this means a well-timed Slow freezes the kiting mechanic. Slow DC 15 vs Wis +4 means ~55% save rate (7/13 fail). With 3 SCs: expected 1.35 failed saves per cast. One SC immobilized per Slow is typical. **This is a meaningful interaction that the encounter design should accept** — Slow should "punish" the shardcaller's mobility without breaking the encounter. Currently working as intended.

- **MQ-72 (RESOLVED — documented):** CW advantage + Slow disadvantage cancel to straight roll. SC-B had both simultaneously. Net result: SC-B rolled 6 and 14, missing AC 19 Sabriel both times. The "cancel" was invisible in the outcome (misses either way) but was correctly modeled. Rule interaction is now in the tactics notes.

- **MQ-73 (RESOLVED — documented):** TR timing is turn-start conditional, not reactive. SC-C with init 21 always acts before Bazgar (init 17). Bazgar closes on his turn. On SC-C's NEXT turn, Bazgar is already in melee → TR fires. This is correct behavior. The fix is in the .md now. For future cycles: if SC has LOWER initiative than the closing PC, SC will never see the "already in melee at turn start" condition because the PC can close AND attack, but the SC acts before the PC's closing turn — no gap exists.

- **ONGOING DD-47:** TR fires 1/5 shardcaller-team cycles (this cycle only). Still very rare. The structural barrier is: SCs die before Bazgar closes (R1-R2 focus fire) in most cycles. This cycle, Marwen using Slow extended SC survival and SC-C survived to R3. Recommendation: TR will remain rare until SCs have higher HP or the party is more spread. Not a bug — structural consequence of CR2 SCs vs level-5 focused fire.
