### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**DESIGN-TW2-A: Berserk opener whiffs vs high-AC party (carry MQ-SR5-A)**
- Rager Berserk R1: to-hit 14 vs Bazgar AC 18 (MISS), to-hit 7 vs Sabriel AC 19 (MISS). Zero damage on the flagship opener.
- At +4 to-hit: hit rate vs AC 18 = 35%, vs AC 19 = 30%. Expected Berserk damage (2 targets) ≈ 4.5 dmg.
- The Rager's most dramatic ability did nothing R1 against the Compass Edge party. Felt anticlimactic.
- Recommendation: consider +5 or +6 to hit (matches the pattern from MQ-SR5-A; human decision — logged here for aggregation).

**DESIGN-TW2-B: CW range breaks immediately after Rager charges**
- CW (30ft) is only usable on the Rager in R1 when SC acts before Rager (narrow initiative window).
- By R2 the Rager is ~50ft from SC back-line position — out of range. .md correctly documents this (Range note DD-41).
- Result: SC's attack-synergy action (CW) has exactly one opportunity window on its primary ally. R2+ CW is wasted on a dead-action-slot unless SC repositions (into danger).
- Recommendation: expand CW range to 60ft to enable 1-2 more synergy rounds, or add a fallback CW target (thrall) when Rager is out of range. Human decision.

**DESIGN-TW2-C: Tank-wall too easy for level-5 party**
- Outcome: party wins R5, Bazgar 31/49, Marwen 12/32, Sabriel 37/44. No deaths.
- Total PC HP loss: 18 (Bazgar) + 20 (Marwen) + 7 (Sabriel) = 45 HP across 5 rounds vs 2 CR2 enemies.
- This is appropriate for a warm-up encounter, but if tank-wall is intended as a meaningful threat before a boss, it needs more NPCs or a CR3 anchor (e.g., a derro-shardcaller CR3 variant or a third NPC).

**FEEL-TW2-D: Taunt hitting Marwen 3 consecutive rounds felt impactful and thematically good**
- Marwen failed Cha saves R1 (3), R2 (0), R3 (6) — Cha is clearly a dump stat.
- Each failure forced a real decision: attack Rager (waste Wizard turns on a melee tank) or attack SC at disadvantage.
- The Taunt + CW pairing *intention* works well when the SC can actually CW; the range limitation (DESIGN-TW2-B) is the main thing limiting the synergy from fully expressing.

**FEEL-TW2-E: Rager R2 Multiattack burst 27 slashing in one turn (Bazgar 49→8)**
- Madness Endurance fires after Bazgar and Sabriel both hit the Rager. Two Greataxe hits (20+21 vs AC 18).
- This spike felt earned and dramatic. Bazgar nearly went down. The Rager dying the very next round meant the spike came just before death — good dramatic arc.
- The Rager is more dangerous the more it's hit, which plays well against the party's "focus the melee threat first" instinct.

**FEEL-TW2-F: Barrage R1 did 14 piercing (high end of 3d6) — meaningful opening pressure**
- Bazgar failed DEX save (rolled 4), took 14 piercing. 29% of max HP in one shot pre-Rager turn.
- Sabriel passed (rolled 14), took 7. Reasonable spread.
- The SC opening with Barrage before the Rager charges (because SC acts first) created interesting pressure: party already hurt before melee closes.
