### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**DD-11: Maw grapple rider — "critical hit" (DB) vs "disadvantage on saving throw" (original stat sheet)**
- The original `beholder-thrulm.md` stat block stated: *"If the target is a creature grappled by the beholder, the target has disadvantage on the saving throw."* (referring to any saves triggered by the Maw hit)
- The current DB spec (authored by the prior cron during the `feat(thrulm)` conversion) says: *"If the target is grappled by the beholder, this attack is a critical hit instead."* — an automatic critical hit (double dice) rather than a condition modifier.
- These are mechanically different. "Disadvantage on saves" is a conditional rider (only relevant if Maw has an attached save). "Critical hit instead" is a massive damage upgrade (avg Maw damage doubles from 21 to ~39).
- **Recommendation:** Decide which rule is canonical. The "crit on grapple" version is more dramatic and creates stronger incentive for the 2-step grapple→bite combo. If this is intentional, document it clearly in the .md tactics. If the original "disadvantage on saves" was the intent, update the DB `rider_on_hit` on both `multiattack.Maw` and standalone `maw`.
- Both the `multiattack` Maw and the new standalone `maw` action use the "crit on grapple" version for consistency. If Joe updates one, update both.

### Mechanical questions

**Q: Does Misty Step (no concentration, teleport 30 ft) automatically break a beholder tentacle grapple?**
- Teleportation breaks a grapple (the grappling creature can no longer "hold") per RAW. Simulated as "yes" in this run.
- The beholder's Tentacle Lash has no "teleportation escape immunity." Confirm this is intended — a Marwen with Misty Step can always escape grapple with a bonus action, reducing the grapple tactic's effectiveness against a wizard.
- **Recommendation:** Add to beholder tactics notes: "Misty Step / any teleportation breaks tentacle grapple — if Marwen has Misty Step, grapple her last or use bonus action Compel Thrall to redirect a thrall to Marwen's landing zone."

**Q: Antireality (+2 AC after seeing roll) — when Maw "crits on grapple," does Antireality intercept the crit?**
- The crit-on-grapple rule triggers after the attack hits (it's a rider_on_hit). Antireality fires before damage (after seeing the roll). If Maw hits vs AC 17, Antireality turns it to AC 19 — the hit misses. This is fine. But if the attack is already a crit (natural 20 or grapple-crit), does Antireality still intercept? Answer: yes (Antireality adds +2 to AC, affecting whether the roll hits, not the damage). This is consistent.
