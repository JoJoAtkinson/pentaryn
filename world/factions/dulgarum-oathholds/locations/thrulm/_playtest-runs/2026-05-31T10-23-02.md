### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-EV11-A** — DR recharged in same round as surprise use (d6=6 in R1 recharge check), then recharged again in R3 (d6=6 again). Two DRs fired in rounds 0–3 (surprise miss, R1 kill, R3 kill). Back-to-back recharge-5+ rolls (d6≥5) are not rare (~1/3 per check) but consecutive in a 3-round fight feels brutal. Statistically expected; not a bug.

**FI-EV11-B** — Marwen (AC15, 32 HP) is a near-automatic DR kill once DR lands. She was at 24 HP after the lair eruption — any DR roll of 25+ (avg 10d8 = 45, ~95%+ of rolls) disintegrates her. At AC15 with +6 to-hit she's hit on a 9+ (~60%). Expected: whenever beholder fires DR at Marwen = ~57% disintegration. Her survivability in the solo-beholder configuration depends almost entirely on the beholder NOT having DR recharged. This is a feel observation, not a bug.

**FI-EV11-C** — VS never recharged in 3 rounds (d6: 2, 2, 4). In solo-beholder fights that end in R3–R4, VS fires 0 times with ~57% probability (5/6 miss per round, 3 rounds). The .md says "hold VS until 2+ PCs are clustered" — but the fight rarely reaches a round where VS fires AND multiple PCs are clustered near the beholder. The .md guidance is tactically correct but VS is often a dead ability in shorter fights. **DESIGN DECISION: consider whether VS recharge-6 is too rare for its tactical value, or whether this is intentional — it only fires when the party makes a tactical mistake (staying clustered near the beholder).**

**FI-EV11-D** — Sabriel's LoH pool (25 HP) was never used. Bazgar went to 0 on the beholder's turn in R2 — Sabriel had no opportunity to spend an action reviving him before she was killed in R3. The chamber healing-spell hazard was also never relevant (no healing spells cast). In the empty-void slice, the hazard only matters if the fight lasts 4+ rounds and Sabriel has both a target to heal AND is not under immediate attack pressure. This is a feel note, not a fix — the hazard is meaningful in longer/multi-NPC encounters.

**FI-EV11-E** — Grapple + Maw auto-crit delivered 46 piercing damage in a single action (Bazgar was at 26/49 HP). This overkilled by 33 HP. The grapple→Maw combo is the beholder's highest single-turn damage output against a melee fighter. At table this would feel devastating — the Fighter gets grabbed and immediately killed before any escape attempt. The .md correctly notes that grapple ends on unconscious (MQ-FC14-A), so the Maw targeting a grappled target is RAW. Feel note: consider adding a DM reminder that the grappled target may spend its REACTION to attempt escape before the Maw fires (rules allow this — a reaction-escape attempt would happen before the beholder's next attack, but reactions typically fire BETWEEN attacks in a multiattack). This is a table-coaching note, not a DB fix.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-EV11-A (NEW)** — DR recharge timing: the .md says "Start-of-turn checklist step 1: if DR USED, roll d6." The checklist is labeled as the beholder's start-of-turn. However, in the combat sequence: lair actions fire at init 20 (BEFORE the beholder's turn if beholder didn't roll 20). If recharge is truly at beholder's start-of-turn, it fires AFTER the lair action. The sim ran recharge at start of round (before lair action) which may be incorrect — the lair fires first, then beholder's turn starts, then recharge check. **Recommendation: clarify in .md whether recharge check is at start of ROUND or start of BEHOLDER'S TURN. If the latter, the beholder's lair actions at init 20 always happen before DR can recharge.**

**MQ-EV10-B RESOLVED** — DR spec confirmed as single_attack (to_hit +6, 10d8 force). Not a save. The 10th-cycle flag is resolved. The DR to-hit of 25 vs Marwen AC15 was a clear hit; the 9 vs Bazgar AC18 was a clear miss. Spec is mechanically correct.

**MQ-EV10-A (2nd-cycle confirm, UNRESOLVED)** — The hard-trigger surprise DR has now fired in 11 consecutive empty-void cycles without any pre-combat warning. This run: DR missed surprise (target Bazgar AC18) but recharged same round and killed Marwen in R1. The pattern is: beholder misses surprise DR against the high-AC fighter (55% hit rate), then immediately recharged and fires at the low-AC caster (75% hit). This "miss on fighter, kill on caster" is a distinct two-phase sequence that will repeat frequently at table. **Recommendation (carried from MQ-EV10-A): add to .md Interaction Notes — if combat triggers AFTER a persuasion-pass dismissal, start with Void Ray (warning shot) instead of DR. DR should only fire as surprise if the party explicitly states attack intent OR re-enters after dismissal. This gives the party a flight window and makes the scene more narratively grounded.**
