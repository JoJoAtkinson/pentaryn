### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TW-1 (DESIGN DECISION): Call Weakness is structurally dead in tank-wall formation.**
The SC started 50 ft from the party, with the Rager engaging at ~10 ft from party → SC-to-Rager gap ~40 ft, beyond the 30-ft CW range. All 3 CW charges went unused. The slice is explicitly labelled "tests taunt + call_weakness pairing" but CW never fired.

This is not dice variance — it is structural. The standard "Rager blocks, SC hangs back" positioning (per both NPC .md files' tactics) permanently prevents CW coordination at range. For CW to fire on the Rager, the SC would need to reposition within 30 ft of the Rager's expected engagement point, which puts the SC within melee threat range.

**Recommendation:** Either (a) set the slice's SC starting position to 20–25 ft from party (SC-to-Rager gap ≤30 ft, enabling CW from behind the Rager), or (b) acknowledge that CW is intended only for same-engagement-zone support and update the slice description to test something else. The DD-41 range issue is documented for multiple prior runs; this is the first slice where it is the *primary* test objective.

**FI-TW-2 (feel): Rager Berserk fizzle at low HP.**
At 12/52 HP, Rager used Berserk against all 3 PCs in reach and missed all three (rolls: 11+4=15 vs AC 18, 1+4=5 vs AC 15, 2+4=6 vs AC 19). The Rager's climactic ability did nothing. Berserk is already limited (recharge 5–6, no-movement prereq); fizzling at 12 HP on a "last stand" turn makes the Rager's final moment anticlimactic.

This is dice variance (expected), not a spec bug. But it raises a design question: should the Rager have a Madness Endurance–style bonus that applies to Berserk when bloodied (≤25% max HP)? Would make the death more dramatic. Log for human review — do not auto-fix.

**FI-TW-3 (feel/QOL): Berserk 3-line output not self-labelling creature count.**
The Berserk output always prints 3 attack lines. Per the .md, "DM caps at actual in-reach creature count." At the table, a DM under pressure may forget to discard excess lines. Suggest adding e.g. `_(cap to N targets actually in reach — skip lines beyond that)_` inline in the Berserk spec narration or as an MCP output note. Low priority (already documented in .md) but recurs every berserk activation.

**FI-TW-4 (positive confirm): Taunt correctly suppressed Scorching Ray.**
Taunt landed twice on Marwen (R2, R3). Both times, the Scorching Ray attack rolls were correctly made at disadvantage (MQ-36: Scorching Ray is attack-roll-based, not save-based → affected by Taunt). Fireball in R1 was correctly NOT affected (save-based). The distinction is working cleanly and the Taunt mechanic created meaningful table decisions (Marwen had to choose between effective save-based spells or disadvantaged attack-roll spells).

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TW-1: Pack Tactics Voice (SC passive) was not tested.**
SC was 50 ft from Rager at all times. PTV requires an ally within 30 ft to hit. The range condition was never met, so PTV never triggered. In a formation where both SC and Rager are in the same engagement zone (≤30 ft apart), PTV would force saves with disadvantage — potentially a strong combo with the Rager's damage. Not tested in this slice; slate for a formation test where SC starts within 30 ft of the Rager.

**MQ-TW-2: Rager target in R3 fell through to "Sabriel" via last_attacker logic.**
The print line showed "multiattack on sabriel" — mildly confusing since Sabriel had not attacked the Rager more than Bazgar had. The `.md` says "remembered until the rager hits them — focus that target." Since the Rager hadn't hit anyone (0 hits all fight), last_attacker was set from the Fireball (Marwen) then overwritten to "rager" (from Bazgar hitting). Simulation script ambiguity rather than NPC spec bug. No fix needed.
