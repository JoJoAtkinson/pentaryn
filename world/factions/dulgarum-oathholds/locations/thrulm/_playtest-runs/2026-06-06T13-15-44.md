### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SRR16-A — Taunt DC12 inert 16th-cycle confirm** (carry-forward from cycles 12-15)
Marwen failed 3 of 4 Taunt saves and was "taunted" for most of the fight. None of the taunt conditions had mechanical bite: Fireball (R1) is save-based and hits all ragers anyway, Scorching Ray (R2-R4) targeted the taunting rager correctly. The taunter-compliance requirement has never cost the party a single spell or action across 16 cycles. Recommend: raise to DC 13 or change to Wisdom save. A Wis save would hit all PCs meaningfully (Bazgar is Str/Con focused, Wis save ≈ +0/+1).

**FI-SRR16-B — Madness Endurance +1 invisible 16th-cycle confirm** (carry-forward)
The DB dispatches at `to_hit_bonus: +4` even after the rager takes damage. The +1 from Madness Endurance never appears in the roller output. At-table DMs must manually add +1, but the DB gives no visual prompt. Recommend: add narration note in dispatch output referencing the trait, or accept as DM-only coaching. This is not a spec bug — it's a presentation gap.

**FI-SRR16-C — Incoming Damage Aggro decisive: POSITIVE (first clean cycle)** ⭐
Rager-C at 4HP used Incoming Damage Aggro advantage (Bazgar had hit it in R2) to land two consecutive hits vs AC 18 and drop Bazgar in R3. This is the first cycle where the aggro-mark is unambiguously the kill driver. The "4HP rager drops the Fighter" moment is strong feel — the rager burning itself out. The advantage mechanic is doing exactly what the trait description promises.

**FI-SRR16-D — Berserk death-thrash feel: POSITIVE**
Rager-A recharged Berserk in R1 (d6=5) at 18HP and swept the party. It was downed in R2 having already contributed meaningfully. The dying-spin-at-low-HP feel is correct. Note: Rager-B and Rager-C never recharged Berserk (B: d6=3/1/4/3; C: d6=2/4/2). Berserk's 33% recharge rate means only 1 of 3 ragers typically fires it per fight.

**FI-SRR16-E — Action Surge at 13HP wasted (PC coaching gap)**
Bazgar used Action Surge in R2 with 13/49 HP and both missed (rolls 8 and 11). With Rager-C threatening lethal next turn, this was a last-gasp move that failed. The Action Surge should ideally be used earlier (R1 or when above half HP). No spec change — this is a PC tactical note. Could add a coaching line to the encounter overview: "Bazgar should action surge R1 when Rager-A is already below half HP from Fireball."

**FI-SRR16-F — Marwen L2 slots exhausted by R4 (resource drain correct)**
3× Scorching Ray (L2) targeting successive ragers is the right play after Fireball, but drains Marwen's L2 pool completely. By R5 she's on cantrips. This is intentional resource attrition — a sub-encounter that costs the party a spell tier heading into deeper Thrulm. Feel is correct.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SRR16-A — Sim labels Incoming Damage Aggro as "Madness Endurance" (methodology bug)**
The simulation output printed "(adv on Madness Endurance → roll1, roll2 → max)" but this is wrong. Madness Endurance grants +1 to attack rolls (not advantage). Incoming Damage Aggro grants advantage on the next attack vs whoever hit the rager. These are different traits. The sim correctly modeled Incoming Damage Aggro (advantage) but labeled it incorrectly. Corrected in findings. Outcome unchanged: Rager-C's decisive R3 attacks were Incoming Damage Aggro advantage, not Madness Endurance.

**MQ-SRR16-B — Taunt cross-round persistence** (carry-forward from cycle 12)
The taunt condition says "until end of its next turn" — where "its" refers to the taunted PC. Sim clears taunt when the PC acts, which is correct. But if a rager taunts last in the round and the PC has already acted, the taunt window is effectively skipped. No spec change needed — this is inherent to the initiative-order interaction.

**MQ-SRR16-C — Berserk output prints 3 attack lines even for dead/missing targets**
The DB dispatch for Berserk always prints 3 attack lines (for 3 potential targets). If Bazgar is already down when a rager fires Berserk in later rounds, the DM must manually skip the dead-target line. The tactics bullet ("cap at actual creatures in reach") was added in cycle 15 and is visible in the .md. No further spec change — but worth noting that in multi-round fights where PCs go down, DMs will frequently need to skip lines.
