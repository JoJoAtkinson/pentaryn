### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP-2-A: CW structural lockout — 2nd-cycle confirm (new mechanism: DW-A low-HP after Bazgar burst)**
SC's 3 Call Weakness charges went entirely unused. In this run, SC acted last (init 8). By SC's turn in R1, DW-A was already at 11/27 HP (Bazgar hit for 16+trip-dmg). The MQ-12 guard correctly blocked CW (≤20 HP, already-acted ally). In R2, DW-A was already down before SC could act. DW-B was at 19 HP (borderline) but already acted. SC fired Barrage R1 and R2 Slow ended its usefulness.

Pattern: 2nd consecutive threshold-patrol cycle with 0/3 CW charges fired usefully (1st-cycle: FI-TP-0-B). The SC's low initiative in this formation creates structural CW dead weight. Mechanism this cycle: Bazgar's R1 burst drops DW-A below the MQ-12 guard threshold before SC even gets its turn.

**DESIGN DECISION for human:** CW in threshold-patrol is likely never worth the 3/day budget when SC has low initiative. Options: (a) raise MQ-12 guard to ≤15 HP (tighter), letting SC use CW on a 16-20 HP ally that might survive R2; (b) add SC guidance "if both allies have already-acted this round and CW is wasted, SC may spend BA on Tactical Retreat pre-emptively"; (c) accept that CW in 2-DW+SC patrol formation is reserved for high-initiative SC draws only. The 18+ TP cycles logged show CW has effective utility in roughly 0-2% of TP fights.

**FI-TP-2-B: Sabriel untouched — DW-B never pivots from caster (Marwen)**
DW-B peeled to Marwen correctly per MQ-NEW-TP10-1. Marwen took 5 dmg total. But Sabriel (44/44 HP) was completely untouched — the split-peel leaves the divine martial uncontested for 3 full rounds. In this run it was irrelevant (fight resolved R3), but in longer fights Sabriel would have full HP + 2 unused smites going into round 4+. Consider: should DW-B's split-peel tactics have a fallback to pivot to "closest non-peeled martial" if Marwen drops or if DW-A dies early?

**FI-TP-2-C: Slow R1 hit only 1/3 NPCs (DW-A WIS 3)**
Marwen's lvl3 Slow slot caught only DW-A. DW-B WIS save 17 and SC WIS save 23 (Wis +4) both resisted. Expected: SC resists Slow ~55% of the time with Wis +4 vs DC15. This variance is within normal range. DW-A's lock (slowed to 1 action/turn) reduced its output but Tactical Drilling advantage compensated partially — DW-A still landed 2 hits (5+5 slashing) over 2 rounds. Note: if DW-A made the save too, Marwen's slot was spent on 0 effect. Consider whether DC15 Slow is worth a lvl3 slot vs. 3× Scorching Ray in this formation.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TP-2-A: Slow + Multiattack — is the first swing the "one action" or is the whole Multiattack block one action?**
When a DW is Slowed, it can use one action OR one bonus action per turn. The Multiattack action fires 2 attacks. Ruling in this sim: Slowed DW-A uses Multiattack as its 1 action but only resolves the first attack. Alternative ruling: Slowed DW-A can't use Multiattack at all (it's an action that delivers 2 attacks, and Slow restricts to "can't take reactions, only one action per turn" — but 5e Slow doesn't remove any action, it just restricts to 1; Multiattack IS 1 action). Correct ruling: **DW-A can use Multiattack (one action) and resolves BOTH attacks** — Slow doesn't limit attacks-per-action, only actions-per-turn. The simulation applied this incorrectly (only 1 attack hit). Recommend: add a DM coaching note to the DW .md for Slow interaction.

**MQ-TP-2-B: Tactical Drilling timing with DW-B's split-peel — at what point does DW-B leave the 5-ft range?**
Drilling requires "an ally within 5 ft of DW-A's target." DW-B starts adjacent to DW-A formation. On DW-B's turn, it peels toward Marwen (likely 20+ ft from Bazgar). Does DW-A still benefit from Drilling on its turn in R1? In R1, DW-A acts before DW-B in initiative (init 14 vs 10) — so on DW-A's R1 turn, DW-B has NOT moved yet and is still within 5 ft of Bazgar. Drilling should apply R1. R2+: DW-B has moved; Drilling only if DW-B happens to be adjacent again. Simulation assumed R1 Drilling = YES (correct per initiative order). No .md clarification needed — this follows naturally from initiative. However: worth adding a note "Drilling lasts until DW-B moves away (typically after DW-B's first turn)" to make it explicit for table DMs.
