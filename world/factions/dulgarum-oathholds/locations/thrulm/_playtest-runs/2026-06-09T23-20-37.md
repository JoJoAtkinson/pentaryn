## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

**FIX-TP36-A — CW + Tactical Drilling redundancy note added (derro-shardcaller.md)**

Added tactics note to the "Call Weakness" bullet clarifying that CW on a DWD is wasted when Tactical Drilling is already active (both DWDs alive and adjacent to same target) because advantage doesn't stack in 5e. The note explains the productive exception: if the companion DWD will die before the target DWD acts this round (e.g., killed by AoE on a higher-initiative turn), CW can be productively pre-placed on the surviving DWD. This is precisely what happened this cycle — SC placed CW on DWD-B (init 10) at init 19; Fireball killed DWD-A at init 13 before DWD-B acted, leaving DWD-B without TD. CW advantage was the sole advantage source and turned a miss (5+2=7) into a hit (17+2=19). Rule is correct; tactics note was missing.

No DB spec changes.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP36-A — Menacing Attack frighten rider wasted (36th-cycle pattern)**

Bazgar's Menacing Attack frightened DWD-A (Wis save 4 vs DC 15 = FAIL), but DWD-A was immediately killed by Fireball on the same round before it could act under the frightened condition. This is a structural pattern: the maneuver die (d8=7) contributed meaningful damage, but the frighten rider (disadvantage on DWD-A's attacks + blocked movement toward Bazgar) never expressed. In 36 TP cycles, DWDs have never survived long enough for frightened to matter. The extra d8 would be equally valuable on a Trip Attack (advantage for follow-up attackers) or Goading Attack (the DWD ignores Marwen), and those riders pay off immediately on the attack roll, not the target's next turn. **DM note recommended in COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET: against DWDs, prefer Trip Attack over Menacing Attack — Trip advantage is used the same round.**

**FI-TP36-B — VICTORY R2, fastest threshold-patrol (36th cycle)**

Previous cycles averaged R3-R4; R2 is the fastest resolution recorded. Enabled by: SC Barrage (11 pierce) before Bazgar acts → Bazgar wounds DWD-A (17 dmg) → Marwen Fireball (22 fire) wipes DWD-A and near-wipes DWD-B and SC on the same action. Three of four NPC turns never fired. This reconfirms FI-TP34-A/35-A/etc.: the patrol has no answer to Fireball when Marwen achieves initiative above the NPCs. The CW+Barrage R1 from SC is the only NPC contribution before collapse. Carry for human.

**FI-TP36-C — CW productive this cycle (1 of 36), but narrow conditions required**

DWD-B's CW advantage turned a miss into a hit (5+2=7 miss → 17+2=19 hit), dealing 3 slashing to Marwen — the first cycle in 36 where CW visibly changed an outcome on DWD-B. However, this only worked because Fireball killed DWD-A before DWD-B's turn, removing TD. In cycles where DWD-A survives to DWD-B's turn, TD provides the same advantage and CW is wasted. The mechanic is correct and can work, but it requires an unlikely alignment of events. Overall CW productivity in TP across 36 cycles: ~1-2 productive fires out of ~36 total charges spent. The "raise SC Dex or CW range" recommendation from FI-TP34-B remains the better fix.

**FI-TP36-D — Cunning Action: zero fires (36th consecutive cycle)**

Both DWDs died before they could use Cunning Action. DWD-A died at init 8 before ever acting; DWD-B acted once at init 10 and died same round. CA requires surviving past the opening salvo, which has not happened in 36 cycles. CA is effectively a dead ability at the L5 encounter balance — fights resolve before the trigger condition (surviving to use it) applies.

**FI-TP36-E — Sabriel smite overkill on DWD-B (5HP target, 2nd-level smite 3d8=16)**

Sabriel used a 2nd-level smite (threshold: target HP ≤ 20) on DWD-B at 5 HP. The smite dealt 23 total dmg on a 5 HP target — overkill by 18 HP. A 1st-level smite (2d8=16 avg) would have been sufficient. The sim's smite threshold logic (≤20 HP → L2 smite) is aggressive relative to target HP. At-table, Sabriel should prefer 1st-level smite on targets below ~10 HP. Not a bug — a feel note for human: add smite threshold guidance to the COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.
