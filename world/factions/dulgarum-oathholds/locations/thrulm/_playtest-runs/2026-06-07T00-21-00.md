### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP0-29-A — CW structural init-order lockout (5th threshold-patrol cycle confirm)**
DW-A init 16, DW-B init 14, SC init 12 — both DWs act before SC every round. MQ-12 guard correctly holds all 3 CW charges (highest-init alive ally has already acted). CW is structurally unavailable: R1 both DWs acted before SC; R2 DW-B still before SC, DW-A dead; R3 DW-B barely alive (5HP) before SC fires Barrage and kills it; R4 SC alone. Result: 0/5 threshold-patrol cycles have had CW collect. The SC's signature tactical identity (formation command) is invisible in its own primary formation. Carry from FI-TP0-28-A. DESIGN DECISION for human: either lower the DWs' initiative bonus, give the SC a higher Dex, or restructure the formation so the SC acts first.

**FI-TP0-29-B — Shard-Barrage fires 3/4 rounds (consecutive R3+R4 recharge)**
Barrage recharged R3 (d6=6) and R4 (d6=5). P(two consecutive recharges on 5-6 = 1/3 each) ≈ 11%. With Bazgar+Sabriel consistently adjacent, the sustained area pressure is meaningful — Sabriel ended at 18/44 mostly from Barrage attrition, which is good pressure going into deeper encounter stages. The hot recharge dice made the SC feel threatening despite losing its melee bodyguards by R2. This is variance-driven, not a balance bug, but DM should be aware the SC can be a prolonged area-denial threat when recharged.

**FI-TP0-29-C — Tactical Drilling not expressed in actions DB (new flag)**
The DW `.md` lists Tactical Drilling (advantage on melee attack rolls when an ally is within 5ft and the target is also in the ally's reach). The `multiattack` DB spec contains no advantage flag, no rider, no callout. In this run, DW-A and DW-B both targeted Marwen in R1 — they were adjacent, within 5ft of each other, both within melee reach of Marwen. Per RAW both DWs should have had advantage on all melee swings. The dispatcher issued flat rolls. DM must remember manually. This undermines the DW's core tactical identity (formation fighters who reward clustering). Recommend: add a `pre_save` or `note` field to the multiattack spec, or add an explicit Tactics bullet in `.md` calling it out as a "DM must apply: advantage when ally within 5ft of same target."

**FI-TP0-29-D — DW Crossbow action never fires in threshold-patrol**
The `crossbow` action (1d8+2 piercing, +4 to hit, range 80/320 ft) is in the DB and `.md` but never triggered in any threshold-patrol cycle. Both DWs default to melee charge per their Tactics section ("Hold the line," "Target troublemakers"). In this encounter setup, the party arrives at short range (~20 ft) — DWs can engage immediately with axes. If one DW held range to target Marwen with the crossbow while the other blocked, the formation would feel more tactically varied and use the full stat sheet. Current tactic push both into melee. Recommend: add a split-tactics note ("if party has a spellcaster: one DW holds range with crossbow while the other blocks; close only if caster dodges cover").

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TP0-29-A — Should SC tactic explicitly prefer CW on later-initiative allies?**
Current checklist says CW the "highest-initiative alive ally." In threshold-patrol, the DWs consistently outroll SC on initiative (DW Dex +2, same as SC, but the DWs tend to roll higher — 5/5 cycles both DWs above SC). The result is CW almost always targets an already-acted ally. MQ-12 handles this by holding the charge, but the holding means CW never fires when the formation attritions quickly (DWs die R1-R2 before their next turn). Consider adding a note: "In R1 when both allies have already acted, hold CW rather than spending it; spend in R2 on the first ally to act who hasn't acted yet this round." This is implied by MQ-12 but not stated as the primary tactic for this formation.

**SIM-TP0-29-A — Barrage damage rolled per-target rather than once (sim methodology gap)**
The playtest script rolls 3d6 independently for each target in the Barrage line, instead of rolling once and applying the same total (half on save). Result: Bazgar and Sabriel took different full-damage values in R3 (3 and 7) and R4 (4 and 14). This is a harness methodology gap, not a spec error. For future playtest cycles, the barrage loop should roll `full_dmg = d(6)+d(6)+d(6)` outside the per-target loop. Outcome impact: minor (the separate rolls still produced plausible pressure; the feel conclusion holds).
