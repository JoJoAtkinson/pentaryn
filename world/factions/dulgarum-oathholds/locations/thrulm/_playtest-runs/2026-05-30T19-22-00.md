### Mechanical notes

**MQ-SW19-A: UF priority vs AR when below 20 HP (stagger exemption conflict)**
- STD-B at 10 HP has both UF active (advantage on attacks) AND AR recharged. Below-20-HP stagger exemption says "fires AR recklessly." UF priority says "only 2 PCs in cone → prefer Multiattack." These conflict. Resolution used: stagger exemption wins (below-20-HP = AR recklessly regardless of UF state). Confirm with DM.
- Impact: AR dealt only 3+2=5 necrotic+psychic (terrible roll, ~7th percentile for 2d10+1d4). If UF Multiattack had been used instead, STD-B would have attacked with advantage vs Bazgar (1 HP) — likely killing Bazgar and surviving one more round.
- **This is a mechanical ruling question:** when below-20-HP stagger exemption meets UF priority, does the exemption override the preference?
- Recommend: "below-20-HP exemption fires AR if 2+ targets in cone, but if UF is also active and only 2 targets are in range, DM may choose Multiattack for the guaranteed kills." Add to tactics note as DM option.

**MQ-SW19-B: OBR self-damage counts toward UF threshold — confirmed**
- STD-B took 10 dmg from Bazgar's ATK2 R2, then 4 self-damage from OBR. Total 14 dmg. UF threshold 10+ → UF triggers R3. Correct per the rule: "OBR self-damage counts toward UF threshold."
- No fix needed — just confirming the edge case fires correctly.

**MQ-SW19-C: Altar Zone radiant suppression vs Sabriel smite**
- Sabriel used Divine Smite (2d8=14 radiant) R1. Altar Zone suppresses fire and radiant vulnerability — damage is normal, not doubled.
- At level 5, Sabriel smiting deals +2d8 = avg 9 radiant. Without altar zone, against a fire/radiant-vulnerable STD: +18 avg radiant. The suppression halves Sabriel's smite effectiveness.
- Feel note: Sabriel players familiar with vulnerability exploiting will feel blunted here. This is intentional — altar zone creates a tactical pressure to pull STDs away from the altar. But the pull mechanic isn't self-evident at first contact.
- Recommend: add to COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md: "Shrine-touched at altar: fire/radiant = NORMAL. Pull them 60+ ft from altar to expose vulnerability."

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SW19-A: STD-A dies R1 before OBR can fire against Marwen or Sabriel**
- STD-A took: 18 (Sabriel smite) + 11 (Sabriel ATK2) + 11 (Bazgar ATK1) + 5 (Bazgar ATK2) = 45 total damage in one round (exactly at max HP). Sabriel's smite frontloaded the damage; STD-A's OBR fired after ATK1 vs Sabriel (missed), then never fired again (died ATK2 Bazgar before OBR could trigger on Bazgar's hit).
- **OBR timing rule confirmed:** OBR fires during the attacker's multiattack sequence — interrupts between attacks. STD-A's OBR fired after Sabriel ATK1; STD-A was still alive. But after Bazgar ATK2, STD-A was at -1 HP — dead before OBR could trigger on Bazgar's attack.
- **Feel concern:** OBR is a signature mechanic for shrine-touched but it didn't land R1. STD-A died so fast (45 HP all used by 4 attacks in initiative order) that OBR felt irrelevant. Solo-target focus from Sabriel + Bazgar combined made the first STD a "punching bag" rather than a dangerous melee fighter.
- Recommend: tactics note "don't let Sabriel and Bazgar both focus the same STD R1 — STD should force split attention by engaging different PCs."

**FI-SW19-B: STD-B solo pattern — AR loop with OBR feels punishing for Bazgar**
