### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SR5-A: Berserk fires at right timing but whiffs everything — pure lottery feel.**  
R2's Berserk finally recharged R4 (3 rounds of waiting), then missed all 3 PCs (12 vs 18, 9 vs 15, 7 vs 19). With +4 to-hit the probability of hitting at least one of AC 18/15/19 is ~73%, so a clean 0-for-3 is possible but jarring. The ability is a major set-piece (big narration, costs movement lockout) and left zero trace. Recommendation: give Berserk advantage on its attacks (representing committed all-or-nothing momentum) or add a static +2 on the recharge turn to make it reliably land 1-2 hits when it fires.

**FI-SR5-B: Madness Endurance (+1 to-hit after taking damage) is never enforced in practice.**  
The prereq prints on every roll but relies on DM to manually track whether the rager took damage last turn. In a 3-rager fight, the DM is already juggling HP for all three; this bonus is forgotten or waived ~90% of the time. Ragers took damage every round — if enforced, every multiattack in R2+ should be +5 to-hit, not +4. That's a meaningful difference (~5% hit rate shift per attack). Recommendation: remove Madness Endurance from the prereq and fold a +1 into the base `to_hit_bonus` (use +5), making the trait a flavor note rather than a DM-tracked modifier. Alternatively, author a dedicated `on_hit_received` reaction DB row so the bonus gets explicit state.

**FI-SR5-C: Taunt fires 10+ times but never influences outcomes.**  
All three ragers targeted Bazgar (their natural front-liner preference). Taunt's effect (disadvantage vs non-taunting-rager) only matters if Bazgar tries to switch targets — which a Battlemaster focused on focus-firing one rager down never does. Across 6 rounds, Bazgar triggered the Taunt disadv condition 4 times and 0 of those mattered because he stayed on the same target anyway. Recommendation: add a secondary effect to Taunt — e.g., on a failed save the rager also gains advantage on its next attack against the taunted target (reward for successful intimidation), making Taunt feel impactful regardless of what the PC decides to do.

**FI-SR5-D: Party wins comfortably with zero drops — Fireball is the solo encounter answer.**  
R1 Fireball hit all 3 ragers for 23 each (all failed DC 14 Dex saves), reducing them from 52 to 29 HP before any rager acted. This effectively halved the encounter's threat before it started. The ragers have no counter to AoE setup — no spread formation, no fast-close gap ability before Marwen acts. If solo-rager-rush is intended to stress-test the party, it needs either (a) a 4th rager, (b) fire resistance (thematic: madness-born endurance against heat from underground furnaces), or (c) a note that this encounter should be run as an ambush (ragers already adjacent to Marwen at start) rather than a front-loaded approach fight.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-SR5-A: Madness Endurance applies to Berserk attacks but was not applied in this run.**  
R2 was hit every round; on its Berserk turn it should have been +5 vs AC 18/15/19. With +5: hits at 13/10/14 — still missed all three (rolls: 12, 9, 7). Outcome unchanged here, but the question is: is Madness Endurance intended to stack with Berserk? The DB spec lists it on both `multiattack` and `berserk` as the same prereq, which suggests yes. Confirmation needed.

**MQ-SR5-B: Taunt persists "until end of its next turn" — does it reset per rager?**  
All three ragers taunted simultaneously, so Bazgar's Cha save was called 3 times per round. Each failure imposed disadvantage vs creatures other than *that specific rager*. This is rules-legal but creates an odd situation where Bazgar could be simultaneously disadvantaged against R1 (from R2's Taunt) AND disadvantaged against R2 (from R1's Taunt) — effectively disadvantaged on all attacks. Is this the intended interaction, or should Taunt have "only one active Taunt can affect a creature at once"?
