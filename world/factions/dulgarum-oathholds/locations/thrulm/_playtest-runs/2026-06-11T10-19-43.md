### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-BEL3-7-A — Maw auto-crit structural kill (8th BEL3 consec)**
Both melee PCs were one-shotted by Maw auto-crit (Sabriel R1: 39p; Bazgar R2: 50p). Every BEL3 cycle ends this way. Average Maw auto-crit = 8×4.5+4 = 40 piercing; expected kill threshold hit for all 3 PCs (HP 32-49). The fight is effectively: TL grapples → Maw kills → repeat next PC. The kill *feels* earned (telegraphed by the grapple setup), but it's completely deterministic — no PC survives Maw auto-crit from max HP. Design question: is the intent that 1 Maw crit = 1 dead PC on every hit? If yes, the encounter is working correctly. If feel diversity is wanted, consider (a) Maw only auto-crits on grappled *standing* targets (not all grappled), which the .md already says but doesn't change the math, or (b) reviewing whether 8d8+4 avg=40 against L5 PCs with avg ~39 HP is the desired kill rate. **Recommendation: carry — this is the 8th confirmation; document for human review.**

**FI-BEL3-7-B — DD never fires in BEL3 (8th consec)**
Beholder init 17 > all PCs. Sabriel (init 6) was downed by Maw on the beholder's R1 turn, before Sabriel ever acted. DD requires beholder to hold all 3 LA until after Sabriel's R1 turn — which is structurally blocked when (a) beholder rolls high init AND (b) Sabriel takes a Maw auto-crit on that same turn. Both conditions reliably co-occur because the beholder targets Sabriel first (divine martial = highest DD value). The only way DD fires: either Sabriel wins initiative AND beholder's multiattack misses/doesn't grapple Sabriel. That's roughly (40% init) × (30% no-grapple) = ~12% per cycle. **Recommendation: If DD is a core mechanic to showcase in BEL3, add a "Sabriel acts first" scenario variant or raise Sabriel's DEX modifier artificially for the test.**

**FI-BEL3-7-C — Thralls killed Marwen (novel positive)**
Marwen was killed by T3's final crit dagger, not by the beholder. Three thralls concentrated fire on Marwen (AC15) dealt 51 total damage over 2 rounds. This is the first BEL3 cycle where thralls contributed a killing blow rather than acting as pure Fireball bait. The swarm pressure on the low-AC caster is meaningful. *This is what thralls should feel like.* The prior cycles had thrall fire split or directed at AC18+ PCs (zero hits). **Positive finding — thrall-target-Marwen is the correct thrall tactic against this party.**

**FI-BEL3-7-D — Void Scream never fires (7th BEL3 consec)**
VS recharge d6 rolled 4 and 2. Fight ended at R2. In a 2-round fight, VS (recharge 6) has only a 30.6% cumulative chance of firing once. Structural: VS is never going to express in a BEL3 fight that ends R2-R3. **Design question: Is VS intentionally a late-fight mechanic (R3+) for BEL3? If so, accept. If VS should be an active threat in BEL3, consider recharge 5-6 for this slice only, or place VS as a designated R2 action (removing the recharge gate for limited testing).**

**MQ-BEL3-7-A — Void Feeding +1 not in DB spec (8th BEL3 consec carry)**
The beholder's `tentacle_lash` and `maw` DB rows do not include the +1 to-hit / +1 damage Void Feeding bonus. This run required manual application — and the +1 to-hit was DECISIVE: TL1 hit Sabriel at exactly AC19 (roll=19; without VF bonus would have been 18 → MISS). Without VF, Sabriel is not grappled R1, Maw hits normally (no auto-crit), and the fight outcome changes significantly. This is not a cosmetic gap. **Recommendation: Add Void Feeding to the `tentacle_lash` and `maw` DB specs as an environment condition note, or document explicitly that DM must add +1 at the table.**

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-BEL3-7-B — Second TL target fallback when primary melee PC is down (sim gap)**
In R2, only Bazgar was alive as a melee PC. The sim fired only 1 TL (Bazgar) instead of 2 (Bazgar + Marwen). Real play: beholder would use the second tentacle on Marwen (the only other conscious target). The second TL miss/hit doesn't change the R2 outcome (Marwen dies to thralls regardless), but it's a sim fidelity gap. In R1 both TL targets were standing melee PCs, so the sim was correct. **Fix: sim TL-target fallback logic should cascade from melee PCs → remaining alive PCs if fewer than 2 melee targets available.**

**MQ-BEL3-7-C — Antireality never triggers**
Both of Bazgar's attacks hit clearly above AC17 (24, 17). Antireality's value is when the attack roll is near-miss territory (18-19 vs AC17); here the rolls were decisive. In 8 BEL3 cycles, Antireality fires only when Sabriel or Marwen land spell attacks (but those are saves, not attack rolls). Bazgar's +7 to-hit often produces rolls in the 17-24 range — most don't trigger the narrow Antireality window. Carrying from FI-EV7-39-A (Antireality narrow window at +7 party). No spec change needed; DM awareness note.
