## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **FIX-SW1-A: AR .md text missing "save halves" qualifier.** The Tactics section said "DC 14 Dex, 2d10 necrotic, +1d4 psychic on fail" without specifying that the save halves the necrotic. The DB spec correctly has `"on_save": "half"` — the roller outputs "savers take [half]" correctly. The .md text now reads "DC 14 Dex, 2d10 necrotic (save halves), +1d4 psychic on fail only." Text-only fix; no DB change.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SW1-A: Altar zone fire/radiant suppression never meaningfully tested — pull-away mechanic dormant.** The fight lasted 2 rounds with both STDs staying in altar zone throughout. Marwen's Fireball at 8d6 normal was still decisive. The zone's design intent ("pull them away to re-expose vulnerability") requires an incentive for STDs to leave the altar or a reason for the party to avoid area fire. In a 2-STD fight with no board pressure, neither party nor NPCs moved. Consider: (a) start STDs between the altar and the party entrance so they naturally close toward PCs = moving away from altar, or (b) add a 3rd STD to force party spread. This is an ENCOUNTER SETUP suggestion, not an NPC design bug.

- **FI-SW1-B: UF + AR interaction — advantage wasted at 3+ enemy count.** When UF is active (advantage on attack rolls) and 3+ enemies are in cone, tactics mandate AR (save-based). UF's advantage is completely wasted on AR. In this run, STD-B entered R2 with UF and AR both available — it would have dealt more expected damage with UF Multiattack than AR (which the party might pass). The "3+ = AR regardless" override feels suboptimal given UF. DESIGN DECISION: consider making the 3+ enemy override conditional — "unless UF is also active, in which case UF Multiattack wins if all 3 targets are within melee reach."

- **FI-SW1-C: Stagger rule adds DM overhead with limited payoff in a 2-STD fight.** The stagger rule is correct and thematic but requires: (a) comparing both STDs' initiatives at round start, (b) pre-marking the lower-init STD before its turn, (c) locking the hold even if the higher-init STD resolves. This is 3 cognitive steps before the first action of round 1. In a fight where one STD often dies R1 anyway, the stagger rule's R2+ benefit may rarely matter. DESIGN DECISION: keep the rule (it prevents degenerate double-AR turns) but consider a simpler formulation: "If both STDs have AR at the start of Round 1, only the higher-initiative one fires. From Round 2 onwards, no stagger — fire freely." This simplification eliminates the per-round stagger check for ongoing fights.

- **FI-SW1-D: OBR self-damage counts toward UF — marginal but tracking-heavy.** Per .md checklist item 4, OBR's 1d4 self-damage counts toward UF's 10+ threshold. In this run, STD-A's OBR self-damage (2 psychic) added to Bazgar's 12-dmg hit = 14 total, triggering UF before Fireball. This is correct and thematic (the shrine punishing the derro contributes to its Unstable Form). However, tracking self-damage adds a second number to the "total damage this round" accumulator. DESIGN DECISION: acceptable as-is; document the self-damage threshold contribution more visibly in the cheat sheet.

### Mechanical questions

This means STD-B's hold in R1 survived even after STD-A fired. Is the intent that STD-B fires AR at the EARLIEST OPPORTUNITY in R2 (turn start, no stagger check needed since it's a new round)? Current reading: yes. But if STD-A recharged AR in R2 and STD-B also had AR recharged by start-of-STD-B's-turn-R2, would R2 stagger apply? The .md says "Stagger is evaluated at the START of the round" — if evaluated and both have AR, stagger fires again. This is correct but creates edge case: what if STD-A fires AR at its R2 init-17 turn, then STD-B's R2 stagger-hold lifts implicitly? Per .md: the hold is set at round start — it does NOT lift mid-round. Confirmed clear, no fix needed.
