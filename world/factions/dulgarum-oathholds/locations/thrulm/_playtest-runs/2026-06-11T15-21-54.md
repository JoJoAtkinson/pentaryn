### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP41-A — CW structural lockout — 41st TP0 cycle (carry)**
Call Weakness fired 0× for the 41st consecutive threshold-patrol run. Root cause: SC rolls initiative 7 (Dex +2 on a low base), both DWs roll 10+, so DWs always act before SC. By the time SC acts in R1, all DW allies have already taken their turns. Per MQ-12, CW on an already-acted ally is only valid if they have >20 HP (advantage carries to their next turn). DW1 had 22 HP when SC acted in R1 — just above the 20 HP threshold — so CW-DW1 R1 is technically valid. However, both simulation and likely most DMs skip this subtlety because the DW "already went." The CW ability is mechanically sound but almost always dead weight at this encounter's initiative distribution. **Recommendation (carry):** Add explicit DM note to SC checklist: "If CW wasn't used R1 (all DWs already acted), check if the highest-HP alive DW has >20 HP — if so, CW them anyway; the advantage carries to their R2 attack." Consider also allowing CW on Bazgar (enemy target is not required to be a friendly) — re-reading the spec, CW grants advantage to an ally on their next attack roll. Bazgar isn't an ally. So CW is ally-only and SC's only melee ally is the DW. The structural lockout stands.

**FI-TP41-B — Barrage R1 fires correctly in narrow corridor — 41st TP0 confirm (positive)**
SC correctly fired Shard-Barrage R1 (all 3 PCs in line, narrow threshold corridor, per the updated SC tactics note about corridor approach). Total 25 damage spread: Bazgar 10, Marwen 10, Sabriel 5. This is the SC's highest-impact action and it fires reliably when the party advances in formation. Marwen was the most-threatened PC (31% HP loss from barrage alone). Positive: barrage is doing its job as a corridor-control tool.

**FI-TP41-C — DW melee threat floor: 10 dmg/fight vs AC 18-19 party — targeting gap (new)**
Over 2 full rounds of melee (8 axe attacks, TD advantage applied), the DWs dealt 10 total damage: 1 hit on Bazgar (6 dmg, R1-DW1) and 1 hit on Sabriel (4 dmg, R1-DW2). Hit rate vs AC 18-19 with +2 to hit is ~19-25% even with TD advantage (~44% for AC 18; ~36% for AC 19). The DW tactics section says "focus fire on anyone who looks like they can cast or heal" — this points to Marwen (AC 15) where hit rate with TD would be ~83%. Yet the sim (and the written priority order) kept DWs targeting the front-liners. At the table, DWs should immediately redirect one axe throw (they have a Light Crossbow at +4 to hit, not just melee) to Marwen if she's within 80 ft. The Light Crossbow (+4) vs AC 15 = hit on 11+ = 50% without any TD. **Recommendation:** Add a DM note to DW tactics: "If the party's caster or healer is visible and within 80 ft, redirect one DW to Light Crossbow attacks on them (+4 to hit, 80/320 ft range). Don't waste Axe attacks on AC 18-19 melee fighters when a softer target is available."

**FI-TP41-D — SC Barrage recharge failure × 2 (d6=2, d6=3) — expected variance**
After the R1 barrage, the SC failed to recharge Barrage in both R2 (d6=2) and R3 (d6=3). Expected recharge over 2 rolls = 1 - (2/3)² = 56%, so failing both is ~44% probability — well within normal variance. Fight ended before a third recharge attempt. No design issue; noting for the record that in this run the barrage fired once.
