### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

1. **FI-NEW-C (NEW — DESIGN DECISION): CW lockout via TR conflict is now fully mechanized.** This is the 6th SCT cycle and the 1st cycle where the CW lockout mechanism is explicitly traceable: SC-C CW'd SC-A who had Barrage slotted (advice: check stagger before CW — rule exists but DM/sim missed it). SC-B CW'd SC-C who collected advantage in R2 (the ONE effective CW charge in 9 across the whole encounter). The structural issue: TR and CW compete for the bonus action. In any round where melee threats exist (R2+), all SCs use TR, leaving 0 CW fires. In R1 (no melee threat), CW fires but targets who are Barraging (wasted). The only window for effective CW is when: (a) no melee threat, AND (b) target is multiattacking, not Barraging. In a 3-SC formation facing a balanced party, that window is narrow. **This is not a bug — it's an emergent tension in the design.** But the .md tactics read as if CW is a primary tool. It is structurally rare-to-never effective. **Design question for human: Should CW be converted to a passive/triggered effect (e.g., "after you hit with Shard-Throw, one ally within 30 ft gets advantage on its next attack") to guarantee it fires without competing for bonus action? Or is this accepted as-is (SCs are more a barrage/kite platform than a CW-synergy platform)?**

2. **MQ-NEW-5 (NEW — SIM BUG, harness only): Stagger rule violated by mid-round SC death.** SC-A fires Barrage at init 16; SC-A dies at init 13 (Sabriel). SC-B at init 9 then becomes "first alive SC" and also fires Barrage. Double-Barrage in R2 — violates the stagger rule. Correct behavior: track "Barrage-fired-this-round" as a round-level flag (not derived from alive-status). No spec or .md change needed. Sim harness fix only.

3. **MQ-NEW-3 (SIM BUG): TR incorrectly fired for SC-A in R1.** Sim set melee-threat flag for SC-A unconditionally in R1. PCs were at 50 ft and had not closed; TR should not fire. No spec change. Harness fix only.

4. **MQ-NEW-4 (SIM BUG): SC multiattack damage parse failed.** Dispatch output format is `Shard-Throw N to-hit X / dmg Y piercing` not `Hit! ... Y piercing`. Sim regex didn't match this format. SC-C R1 multiattack (hit Marwen twice: 6+5 = 11 piercing) and SC-B R1 multiattack (hit Marwen once: 9 piercing) were not applied. Marwen should have been at 32−11−9−11 (Barrage) = 1 HP after R1, not 21 HP. This was corrected in the narrative above (Marwen at 1/32 end R1 per manual calculation). Harness fix only — parse `to-hit X / dmg Y piercing` or use `Hit` in dispatch template.

5. **DD-18 6th SCT confirm: Fireball low-roll (24) still reduces all SCs to 21/33 HP in R1.** Even with all 3 saving, party wins in 4 rounds. The fight lasted longer than typical (R4 vs prior R2-R3 norm) because Marwen went down and couldn't chain-cast. The SCs were more effective at sustained pressure this cycle than any prior SCT cycle — 3 Barrage fires in R2-R3 plus multiattack pressure on Sabriel got her to 7 HP. Still VICTORY. Design Decision open (scatter SCs to prevent Fireball hitting all 3).

6. **PTV confirmed functional for first time in SCT (positive):** PTV disadv on Marwen's Barrage save rolled the lower die (11 vs 18) and resulted in a fail vs DC 13. Without PTV, Marwen saves for half (8 piercing). PTV cost Marwen 3 extra HP and contributed to her downing by SC-C's multiattack in R2. **This is the first cycle where PTV demonstrably influenced the fight outcome.** Prior cycles: PTV never fired in time or was untracked.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-NEW-3b (CW before Barrage-stagger check):** Tactics say "do not CW an ally who plans to use Shard-Barrage this turn." But SC-C acts first (highest init) and must decide CW target BEFORE knowing SC-A will fire Barrage. SC-C can only know SC-A has Barrage available, not whether it will choose Barrage. **Recommendation:** add to tactics "If you act before another SC and that SC has Barrage available + is the stagger-slot holder, hold your CW charge (don't CW them)." This makes CW+stagger interaction explicit. Minor doc fix — defer to next SCT cycle unless other cycles confirm pattern.
