### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-SCT6-51-A** (CW wasted, 11th consecutive SCT6 cycle): SC-A CW'd SC-C in R1 (correct by tactics — mid-init, HP 33 > 20, acts after SC-A). But Sabriel (init 10) killed SC-C (init 7) in the same round before SC-C could act. Advantage never consumed. The structural problem: CW assignment logic uses initiative order, but initiative order also determines blast-zone survival. The high-init SC is out of Fireball blast; it CWs the mid-init SC, which IS in the blast zone and dies before acting. No tactically sound fix exists within current rules; this is a design observation. **Recommendation**: accept that CW is optimized for the escort formation (where there are non-SC allies as recipients), not the shardcaller-only formation. Consider noting in the .md that CW is lower priority in SC-only formations.

- **FI-SCT6-51-B** (Barrage zero — 5th consecutive SCT6 non-fire from designated carrier): SC-B (lowest init, Barrage-designated) died R1 to Fireball before acting. Only the SC that survives the Fireball R1 blast (the spread-protected high-init SC) remains; that SC was not the designated Barrage carrier. Barrage went unfired. Pattern across 11 cycles: Barrage fires only when the barrage-designated SC acts BEFORE Marwen's Fireball, or when Fireball misses/underkills. Structural: the stagger protocol assigns Barrage to lowest-init SC, who also sits in Fireball's blast zone.

- **FI-SCT6-51-C** (Fireball decisive, 11th consecutive SCT6): 8d6=28 fire in R1 kills SC-B outright (33 HP) and halves SC-C (to 19 HP). Smite finishes SC-C same round. Fight becomes 1v3 by end of R1. Marwen Fireball in R1 is near-deterministic for this slice configuration; spread (FI-SCT-20-B) saves SC-A but only delays the inevitable.

- **FI-SCT6-51-D** (PTV never expressed): PTV requires ≥2 SCs alive (ally in 30 ft). With SC-B+SC-C dying R1, SC-A is alone from R2 onward. PTV activates only in R1 (SC-A hits Marwen for 4, PTV triggers for Marwen's next save) — but the Fireball save rolls before SC-C acts, so PTV wasn't active during the save. PTV has expressed in only 2 of 11 SCT6 cycles and was decisive in 1.

- **SIM-SCT6-51-A** (dispatch/sim dice stream divergence, 11th-consec carry): Dispatch consumes from the pre-seeded quantum cache; sim uses Python `random` module seeded at same seed. Number consumption order differs, producing divergent d20 values. Observed: R1 dispatch to-hit 16 (HIT Marwen), sim rolled 11 (MISS). Outcome unchanged but HP trail differs by ~4 HP. Dispatch output is authoritative. **Recommendation**: parse dispatch output for roll values and feed into sim HP tracking rather than rolling independently. This would eliminate the divergence.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-SCT6-51-A**: In the 51st SCT6 cycle, SC-A won initiative over Marwen for the first time (15 vs 13). Impact: SC-A hit Marwen for 4 piercing before Fireball. This demonstrates that SC-A acting first has meaningful early-game impact (Marwen starts at 28 HP not 32). Worth testing what happens if Marwen starts at 28 HP and ALSO rolls below-average on Fireball — is there a scenario where Marwen can't one-shot SC-B? At 8d6 (avg 28), SC-B has 33 HP. Need avg ≥33 fire for guaranteed kill vs full HP SC-B (Dex fail). Probability: sum(8d6) ≥ 33 = ~47%. In ~53% of cases, SC-B survives a Fireball fail. However SC-B is typically also at full HP; the spread rule means SC-B is often at 60 ft from the main cluster, so Bazgar/Sabriel can't reach it before it fires Barrage. **This may be the Fireball-independent path for Barrage to fire: Fireball does not one-shot SC-B.**

- **MQ-SCT6-51-B**: CW granted to SC-C (alive, HP 33) but SC-C dies before acting. Per MQ-SCT6-50-A rule (added 50th cycle): "if the granting SC dies before its next turn, advantage persists to recipient's next turn." But here the RECIPIENT died, not the granter. MQ-SCT6-50-A doesn't cover this case — it only covers granter death. The advantage simply lapses with recipient death. No doc update needed; situation is covered by standard 5e rules (buff ends when recipient dies). Confirmation: MQ-SCT6-50-A scope is narrower than initially read; RECIPIENT death needs no special rule.
