## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed
- None. All 27 actions executed cleanly in Phase A.
- Minor script bug (not encounter bug): Shard-Barrage half-damage in R3 printed "3" instead of "5" — rounding artefact in the playtest harness script, not in `dnd_roller.py`. The actual roller output shows correct half values.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-1: Call Weakness wasted on dying Rager (Round 3 — last charge)**
- The Shardcaller's tactics say "target the ally hitting hardest" for Call Weakness. In R3, the Rager had 10 HP left and was killed that same round by Bazgar. The final Call Weakness charge gave a now-irrelevant advantage to a target that died before it could attack again.
- **Recommendation:** Add a guard to the tactics: "Do not spend Call Weakness on an ally at ≤15 HP or below half HP — save for a fresh engagement or wait for Berserk." Low-risk authoring fix, no DB change needed.

**FI-2: Pack Tactics Voice passive — ambiguous timing, never enforced in practice**
- The passive (Shardcaller .md) reads: "when an ally within 30 ft hits, the target has disadvantage on the *next* saving throw before end of its next turn." This was noted in the log 3 times but never actually applied to any save. In this run, the relevant saves (Bazgar's Dex saves vs Shard-Barrage) occurred on the Shardcaller's own turn — after the Rager had already attacked. The timing question: does "ally hits" on the Shardcaller's previous turn apply to saves on the Shardcaller's current turn?
- **Ruling needed:** The passive should clarify "triggered when ANY ally (including the Shardcaller itself) hits — applies to the first save the hit target makes before end of that target's NEXT turn." As written, a DM running at speed will miss this every time.
- **Recommendation:** Add an explicit example to the Shardcaller .md: "e.g., if the Rager hits Bazgar on the Rager's turn, Bazgar has disadvantage on Dex saves until end of Bazgar's next turn." Not a critical balance issue, but currently zero enforcement probability at the table.

**FI-3: Taunt (DC 12 Cha) missed all three applications**
- Marwen (Cha -1) rolled 17, 16, 15 against DC 12. Three consecutive successes is within normal range (p ≈ 0.40^3 ≈ 6%), so this is a low-probability outcome rather than a systemic issue. DC 12 is the correct level for a CR 2 NPC. **No change recommended** — evaluate after 3+ additional tank-wall runs.
- **Note:** The Taunt does nothing when Marwen is the target and she succeeds. Because she's the only ranged threat (Firebolt) and Bazgar+Sabriel are already in melee, a successful Taunt on Marwen would force her to choose between attacking the Rager directly (melee risk) or cantripping at disadvantage. If Taunt had landed even once, it would meaningfully change Marwen's positioning. Mechanic is solid; this run just had poor luck for the Rager.

**FI-4: Berserk spec hardcodes 3 attack entries, DM must know to apply only N-in-reach**
- When Berserk fired with 2 PCs in reach, the roller pre-rolled "Greataxe (target 1)", "Greataxe (target 2)", "Greataxe (target 3)" — all three lines printed. DM applied only the first two and discarded the third. This is by design (Berserk pre-rolls max possible, DM uses what's needed), but is mildly confusing at a live table.
- **Recommendation:** Add a parenthetical to the Berserk narration in `derro-rager.md` tactics: "The DB pre-rolls 3 attacks — apply only as many as targets in reach." No DB spec change needed; this is purely a DM-note authoring clarification.

**FI-5: Shardcaller is the primary damage source in this pairing, not the Rager**
- Shard-Barrage fired 3 times in 5 rounds (initial + two consecutive recharges on rolls 5, 6). Total barrage damage: ~22 HP to Bazgar, ~9 HP to Sabriel. Rager dealt ~18 HP to Bazgar via direct hits. The Shardcaller was actually more threatening than the Rager despite being 20 ft further back and 20% frailer.
- This is statistically unusual (two consecutive recharges → ~11% probability) but within design parameters. The feel observation: "tank wall" implies the Rager is the threat and the Shardcaller is support, but at recharge 5+ the Shardcaller can easily be the primary damage source. DM should brief their mental model: "the Rager is the *attention sink*; the Shardcaller is the *damage engine*."
- **No mechanic change recommended** — this is the intended synergy. Log as a DM-framing note.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-1: dnd_roller.py external RNG blocked in Anthropic sandbox**
- Both `quantumnumbers.anu.edu.au` and `random.org` are blocked by the outbound network policy in the cron sandbox. Phase A required a monkey-patch to pre-populate `dnd_roller._number_cache` with `random.Random` output.
- **Recommendation for human:** Add a local pseudo-random fallback directly inside `dnd_roller._ensure_numbers` (after both external fetches fail), seeded from `os.urandom`. This would make the cron agent self-sufficient without monkey-patching. Effort: ~10 lines in `scripts/dnd_roller.py`. Out of cron blast radius — human to implement.
- **Impact on this run:** Zero — all rolls were valid; the local RNG is cryptographically sufficient for D&D dice.

**MQ-2: Pack Tactics Voice passive timing (see FI-2 above)**
- Needs a ruling: does "ally hits → target disadvantage on next save" apply within the same round (e.g., Shardcaller's own barrage on the Shardcaller's own turn after Rager hit on Rager's turn)? Recommend ruling: YES, applies across any save until end of the hit target's next turn, regardless of which actor's turn the save is triggered on.
