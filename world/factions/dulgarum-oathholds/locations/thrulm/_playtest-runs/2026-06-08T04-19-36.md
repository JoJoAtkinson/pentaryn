### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-TP32-A: VICTORY R3 — 32nd-cycle confirm, encounter remains trivially light (carry FI-TP31-B)**

Party aggregate HP at end: ~88% (Bazgar 42/49, Marwen 20/32, Sabriel 44/44). Only Marwen took real pressure (26 dmg across R1-R3 from Barrage + 2 shard-throws). The Bazgar Action Surge in R2 eliminated SC before it could threaten further, and Shatter+Sabriel cleaned up DW-B end of R2. No PC below 40% HP at any point. Same pattern as 31st cycle (7th TP-cycle confirm). Formation cannot apply sustained pressure before PCs out-tempo it.

*Recommendation: carry FI-TP31-B without change — this is a structural gap in the patrol roster vs. L5 party. Human decision.*

**FI-TP32-B: SC initiative 14 > DW-B 13 this cycle — CW placed correctly pre-DW-B turn; advantage still wasted due to +2 vs AC 18**

In all prior TP cycles, DW-A or DW-B had higher initiative than the SC, making CW a post-hoc bonus action (ally already acted = MQ-12 hold). This cycle the SC rolled higher (14 vs 13) and correctly CW'd DW-B before DW-B acted. DW-B used CW advantage on Atk1 both in R1 and R2. Results: R1 Atk1 (adv) → 18+2=20 vs AC 18 → HIT for 1 dmg. R2 Atk1 (adv) → best-of(11,7)+2=13 → MISS. Even in the favorable init-order case, the underlying hit rate of +2 vs AC 18 (need 16+ = 25%, adv = ~44% per attack = ~70% chance of 1+ hit from 2 attacks) is low. The CW was used on 2 of the 3 charges; net DW-B damage to Bazgar was 1 HP. This partially separates "CW timing gap" (FI-TP-1) from "CW value gap" (+2 hit rate insufficient vs L5 fighter). Both are design issues; the timing fix (SC higher Dex) is necessary but not sufficient.

*Recommendation: carry FI-TP-1 AND note that DW attack bonus (+2) needs to rise alongside any SC init fix. Raising DW to +4 to-hit (matching crossbow line) or giving Tactical Drilling a flat +1 dmg would help both.*

**FI-TP32-C: Bazgar superiority die wasted on R1 miss — Trip Attack fires on miss; die consumed**

Bazgar declared Trip Attack on Atk1 (R1), rolled 9+7=16 vs DW-A AC 15. Per 5e Battlemaster rules, the superiority die is spent whether or not the attack hits. The die (d8=4 bonus dmg, DC 13 Str save to avoid prone) was fully wasted. This is RAW-correct but notable: Trip Attack die spend on R1-miss against AC 15 with +7 to-hit (miss on 9 = 30% miss rate) is expected ~30% of the time. Not a bug, but a simulation-feel note for at-table play.

**FI-TP32-D: Dual-dice gap — Barrage dispatch 16 vs sim re-roll 11; HP tracking error throughout (carry MQ-TW37-B)**

The roller output showed `(5)(5)(6)=16` for Shard-Barrage. My simulator then called `d(6,3)` independently and got 11. All HP tracking from R1 onward reflects the re-rolled value, not the dispatcher's. Authoritative: Bazgar took 8 (half of 16, saved), Sabriel took 16 (full, failed). Sim tracked Bazgar 44 and Sabriel 33 vs actual ~41 and ~28. Outcome (VICTORY R3) unchanged. This is a recurring harness methodology gap — the sim should parse the damage from `result["output"]` or `result["rolls"]` rather than re-rolling.

*No fix possible within blast radius. Carry MQ-TW37-B.*

**FI-TP32-E: Barrage recharged R2 (d6=5 ✓) but correctly held — feel confirms recharge-hold tactic works**

SC had Barrage available R2 but correctly assessed that PCs had spread (Bazgar moved 30 ft to melee SC, Sabriel engaged DW-B, Marwen back-line). No 15-ft line with 2+ PCs existed. SC chose Multiattack on Marwen (correct per tactics). This is the first TP cycle where Barrage recharged AND was correctly withheld. The decision point is working; it just fired once total (R1) as expected in a 3-round fight.

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-TP32-A: Superiority die consumed on miss — confirm RAW correct; no new action needed**

Battlemaster Trip Attack (PHB): "When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die..." — "when you hit" means the die is spent only on a hit. However, Bazgar's player may have declared the maneuver before the roll; at-table this creates ambiguity. RAW: declare after seeing the hit. Simulation declared it pre-roll. This is a table-etiquette note, not a spec issue. *(Noted only for accuracy.)*

**MQ-TP32-B: DW-B CW advantage resolution — R1 Atk1 hit for 1 dmg (Tactical Drilling + CW both gave advantage; one advantage is redundant)**

In R1, DW-B had both Tactical Drilling advantage (ally adjacent) AND CW advantage. Per 5e rules, multiple sources of advantage don't stack — you roll twice and take the best. This was handled correctly (single pair of rolls). But the combined effect means the CW charge was partially redundant with an already-existing Tactical Drilling advantage. In mixed-formation fights where Tactical Drilling fires naturally, CW value is diluted. In open-field fights without TD, CW is full-value. Not a bug; just a note about CW efficiency in tight formation scenarios.
