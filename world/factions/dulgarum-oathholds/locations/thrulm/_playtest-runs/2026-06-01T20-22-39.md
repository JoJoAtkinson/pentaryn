### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SCT7-A (POSITIVE): STAGGER is the critical balance lever — 18th-cycle confirm.**
Only SC-C barrages R1 (and R2). Triple-simultaneous-barrage would have applied ~33 total piercing with PTv disadv, almost certainly downing Marwen R1. Stagger brought the fight to a manageable 4-round arc. The rule is working exactly as intended.

**FI-SCT7-B: PTv + Barrage disadv combo is impactful but tracking-heavy.**
In R2, both Marwen and Sabriel had PTv disadv active when SC-C's barrage hit. Both failed their DC 13 Dex saves (rolls: 4 and 10 with disadv). Without PTv disadv, Marwen and Sabriel would have rolled normally — roughly 50% pass rate. The PTv disadv mechanic meaningfully amplified the R2 barrage. However, tracking three simultaneous PTv sources (which PC has disadv on the next save, set by which SC ally hit) is cognitively burdensome for the DM in a 3-SC formation. **DESIGN RECOMMENDATION:** Add a one-line PTv tracking note to the COMBAT-CHEAT-SHEET.md (e.g., "PTv: place a poker chip on each PC who has been hit by an SC ally this round — remove after their next save").

**FI-SCT7-C: CW depletion non-binding — 18th-cycle confirm.**
CW charges used: SC-C 2/3, SC-A 1/3 (then SC-A died before using more), SC-B 1/3. Total 4/9 charges fired across 4 rounds. The 3/day limit was never a real constraint. In a 4-round fight, the CW resource gate is purely cosmetic. This pattern has appeared across multiple cycles. **DESIGN QUESTION (DD-SCT7-A):** Is the 3/day limit intentional as a "signal of scarcity" even if it never binds? Consider reducing to 2/day or converting to 1/encounter to make the resource feel more deliberate.

**FI-SCT7-D: Marwen's Shatter was the turning point — back-line clustering punished.**
SC-B and SC-C both failed Con saves (5 and 13 vs DC 14), taking full 10 thunder damage each. Both SCs group tightly in the back line (40-60 ft from enemies, ~5-10 ft apart), making them naturally vulnerable to area spells. Marwen's Shatter immediately set up R3 cleanup. **Feel observation:** a wizard with any area spell is a hard counter to the SC-only formation. The DM should consider having SCs spread out (15-20 ft spacing) when a spellcaster is detected. **DESIGN RECOMMENDATION:** Add a note to the SC tactics section: "vs. identified spellcaster: spread to 20 ft separation to avoid simultaneous area spell coverage."

**FI-SCT7-E: TR fired correctly — 18th-cycle confirm.**
- SC-B R1: Sabriel closed to 15 ft → TR bonus action fires → SC-B retreats and still multiattacks (FI-181 confirmed: TR is bonus action, not main action).
- SC-A R2: Bazgar in melee (threat) → TR fires → SC-A retreats but still misses on multiattack → Bazgar catches up and finishes SC-A next turn.
- SC-C R4: below 12 HP, Bazgar in melee → TR fires → retreats toward pillar → still downed by Sabriel+Bazgar follow-up.
TR mechanically correct in all three activations. Tactically, TR at 2 HP (SC-A) bought exactly 0 value — Bazgar's 30 ft speed matched the retreat. SC-C's TR at 9 HP similarly failed to escape. **Design note:** TR is more useful with terrain blocking pursuit, or against slower PCs. On an open vault floor, TR just delays the kill by 1 action. Not a bug — correct mechanic under the terrain assumption.

**MQ-SCT7-A: CW advantage not reflected in dispatch output — 18th-cycle confirm.**
When SC-A attacks with CW advantage, the dispatch output shows the base to-hit rolls without noting advantage application. The DM must manually track that CW advantage is in effect and apply it. SC-A's dispatch output showed "to-hit 24 / 5" — the DM needs to know the 24 was with advantage (naturally high roll) and the 5 (nat-1) was also with advantage (both rolls low). No format change needed (the manual tracking is documented in checklist), but worth confirming the DM coaching note is clear.

**MQ-SCT7-B: Terrain assumption for "pillar cover" not specified in _overview.md.**
The SC tactics say "below 12 HP: retreat behind a Rager or pillar" and "if no cover exists, accept the exposed position." The _overview.md describes "natural columns of stone rise from floor to ceiling" — this supports the pillar assumption. No change needed, but confirming the terrain supports the mechanic.

**SIM-SCT7-A: Dual-random-stream desync (structural, persistent).**
NPC action dispatch uses the seeded cache random stream; PC saves and hit resolution use Python `random.randint`. The two streams diverge (e.g., barrage output showed "11 piercing" but sim computed "14" for Bazgar's damage). In this run, Marwen's barrage damage was taken as 5 (half of dispatch's 5) vs the sim's separately-rolled 7. Outcome did not change (Marwen was downed in R4 either way), but the HP tallies have small discrepancies vs a unified-stream simulation. **This is a structural characteristic of the harness, not a bug to fix.** Recommend continuing to treat dispatch output as authoritative for NPC damage and sim-random as authoritative for PC saves/attacks.
