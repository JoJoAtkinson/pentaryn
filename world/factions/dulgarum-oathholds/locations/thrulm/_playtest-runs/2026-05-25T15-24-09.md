## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed
- **MQ-42 (auto-fixed): AR concentration-check callout added to shrine-touched-derro.md tactics.** Added: *"⚠️ AR threatens concentration: any caster who takes damage from AR must succeed on a Con save (DC = 10 or half damage taken, whichever is higher) or lose a concentration spell — call this out at the table when a caster is in the cone."* This is a DM procedure note, not a spec change.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-78 (POSITIVE, 2nd-cycle confirm): Stagger rule correctly prevents double-AR in 2nd cycle.**
Only STD-B fired AR R1 (STD-A init 3 < STD-B init 6 → STD-A held). Single-AR R1 damage: 8+11=19 HP to Bazgar+Sabriel. Compare to 1st cycle (double-AR, no stagger): ~35+ HP R1, TPK R3. The stagger fix is the primary reason this cycle ended in VICTORY vs TPK. MQ-28 fix confirmed working.

**FI-79 (POSITIVE): 2nd-cycle shrine-wedge produces VICTORY — encounter is survivable with correct stagger.**
This directly contradicts the 1st-cycle result (TPK). Two-cycle data point: the shrine-wedge encounter is balanced when stagger is enforced. With stagger broken (both fire AR), it's overwhelmingly lethal.

**FI-80: UF structurally never fires in shrine-wedge — STD-A dies before acting with UF.**
STD-A took 37 dmg in R1 (threshold ≥10 → UF would activate R2). But Sabriel acts first (init 8 > STD-A init 3) and kills STD-A with a smite before STD-A's R2 turn. UF is a "catch for focused-fire burst damage" that requires the STD to survive to its next turn. Against a party with high single-target burst (Sabriel smite + Bazgar Action Surge), the STD dies before UF can matter. 2nd cycle confirm — same as cycle 1. *No fix recommended: UF is probably correct as a late-fight (not R2) mechanic, but it's invisible in burst-heavy parties.*

**FI-81: Altar zone fire suppression zero-impact — Fireball was single-target (save = half, vuln irrelevant).**
Marwen fired Fireball R2 at STD-B (STD-A already dead). STD-B saved — half regardless of vulnerability. Fire suppression only matters when (a) fire area hits multiple STDs AND (b) at least one fails the save AND (c) fire vulnerability would have doubled the damage. None of those conditions were met. The suppression mechanic is invisible in 2-round-focus-fire patterns. *No fix — this is correct design (the mechanic IS meaningful if the party uses fire + doesn't kill STDs fast). Note for DM: if the party lacks burst, Fireball with both STDs in the zone deals significantly more damage than inside the altar zone.*

**FI-82 (FEEL): Slow is Marwen's optimal R1 play in altar zone — fire suppression makes it better than Fireball.**
Slow L3 (DC 14 Wis) landed on STD-A (Wis roll 1 — low but 50/50 odds). It limited STD-A to 1 attack on its turn (missed anyway), prevented OBR reactions from STD-A, and effectively removed STD-A's action economy for R1. Fireball would have dealt ~24 fire avg (half = 12 on save) to both STDs — but STD-A was going to die anyway from PC focus. Slow's concentration-based control is arguably better than Fireball inside the altar zone because the party's burst damage already addresses one STD efficiently. *Note for DM: warn the party that the altar zone weakens fire. Slow / Web / Hypnotic Pattern become the spell suite in this room.*

**FI-83: Marwen untouched (0/0 HP lost) — 2nd shrine-wedge cycle confirm.**
Marwen init 5 went AFTER both STDs fired. STD-B targeted Bazgar (closer melee PC) with AR cone; STD-A targeted the melee PCs. Marwen avoided the AR cone entirely by not being in melee. This is the intended playstyle (wizard stays back), but it's worth noting: the altar zone doesn't push Marwen into the cone. She only enters the cone if she tries to reach the altar or if STDs pursue her. *No fix needed — Marwen's untouched status here is correct player positioning.*

### Mechanical questions

**MQ-42 (auto-fixed above):** AR concentration-check callout added.

**MQ-43 (NEW): Fireball single-target efficiency vs Slow — which is the "right" spell?**
Marwen used Slow R1 (landed on 1/2 STDs) and Fireball R2 (1 target, saved for half). Total L3 slot value: ~14 fire dmg + Slow concentration effect vs no Slow. If Marwen had used Fireball R1 (both STDs in range, avg 28 fire to each on fail, 14 on save), she would have done more raw damage but lost the Slow neutering effect on STD-A. The better play depends on whether the party can focus down one STD in R1 (they can, with Action Surge). Recommend: add a DM note to `_overview.md` or a sidebar noting *"Slow is underrated in the altar zone — fire suppression and the party's burst damage make CC spells the value play."* No spec change needed; this is DM advice.
