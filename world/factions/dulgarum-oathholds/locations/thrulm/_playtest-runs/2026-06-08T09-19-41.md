### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

**FI-SR5-I (new, 43rd cycle): Initiative entirely determines encounter length and feel**
Marwen init 21 (highest) → 2-round victory, Marwen at 8 HP after absorbing 24 dmg.
42nd cycle: Marwen init 21 → 6-round victory (different seed logic — R6 was unusual; Rager-3 took 6 rounds to die due to Berserk recharge failure).
41st cycle: Ragers init 20/17/16 → TPK R5 (3 Berserks before PCs act → 34 dmg to Bazgar R1).

The encounter outcome bracket is:
- Marwen goes first → Fireball lands → encounter lasts 2–3 rounds, party light damage
- Ragers go first → triple Berserk lands → party starts losing immediately → likely TPK

The initiative coin flip creates a bimodal outcome (near-TPK or trivial victory) with no middle state. *DESIGN DECISION: the slice has no mechanical safeguard against initiative lottery. Consider removing Berserk from R1 availability (first round of combat only), or staggering rager entry so they don't all charge simultaneously.*

**FI-SR5-J (carry FI-SR5-G, 3rd confirm): Berserk fires but rarely decides the fight**
This cycle: Rager-2 Berserk hit both targets per dispatch (11+4=15 damage). Rager-3 Berserk hit Marwen (14 damage). Both ragers died the same round they used Berserk. Berserk as a "last ditch" swing before death is the recurring pattern. The mechanic's design is "powerful area clear when you have momentum" — but in practice it fires when the rager is already at half HP from Fireball, and the party's high-AC (18/19) absorbs or misses many hits.

*CARRY: recommend Berserk to-hit +5 or +6 for the named frenzy attack, or advantage on Berserk attack rolls (rager commits fully, wild swinging, can't be dodged easily).*

**FI-SR5-K (new, 43rd cycle): Taunt fires 4+ times, zero influence on PC decision-making**
Rager-3 R1 Taunted Marwen — Marwen's primary action (Fireball) was already spent. Taunt's R2 effect would apply disadvantage on attack rolls against non-Rager-3 targets, but Marwen cast Magic Missile (no attack roll) to bypass it entirely. Rager-3 re-taunted R2, Marwen passed. Rager-2 Taunted Sabriel in R1, Sabriel passed.

Result: Taunt fired 3 times. Zero PCs were meaningfully constrained. Factors:
1. Marwen (Cha +1) saves fail ~42% of the time at DC12 — but even when failing, she can cast Magic Missile to bypass it
2. Sabriel (Cha +3) saves fail ~30% of the time — she passed this cycle
3. Bazgar is engaged in melee against the taunting rager anyway — Taunt has zero effect on him

*CARRY from MQ-SR5-B: Taunt should consider blocking non-attack-roll effects (Magic Missile, Shatter, Thunderwave) in addition to attack rolls, OR raise DC to 13–14 to catch the higher-save PCs more often.*

**FI-SR5-L (new): Fireball hard-counters clustered medium-HP NPCs with no Dex**
All three CR2 ragers have DEX 11 (+0). DC 15 Fireball = 25% save rate. At 8d6 average 28 damage, even a save leaves them at 14 HP (out of 52). Full damage drops them to 22 HP — half health in one action. This is expected for L5 Wizard, but the encounter design has no counter-mechanism: no dispersion tactic for ragers (their tactics say "charges in immediately"), no terrain disruption, no Dex modifier.

*DESIGN DECISION: is this the intended role of the solo-rager-rush slice? If it's "stress test for melee tactics" (Berserk, Taunt, Madness Endurance), a Wizard with Fireball bypasses the entire design. Consider adding explicit note to the slice spec: "Marwen should delay spellcasting (use cantrip R1) to let Berserk/Taunt mechanics express before Fireball closes the fight."*
