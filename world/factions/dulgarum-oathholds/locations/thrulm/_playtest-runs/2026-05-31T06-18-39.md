## Findings

### Bugs auto-fixed

- **BUG-BEL21-A**: `manifest_thralls` narration hardcoded "Three thralls jerk upright" — fires even when 1 or 0 thralls are alive. Fixed via `combat_action_upsert`: changed "Three thralls jerk upright" → "Dominated thralls jerk upright". DB validated 0 invalid records post-fix.

### Feel issues / DESIGN DECISIONS

- **FI-BE3-01 21st-cycle confirm**: Fireball in R1 wipes 2/3 thrall escorts again (Thrall-A and Thrall-B both fail DC 14 save vs 32 fire; Thrall-C survives on 17 Dex save with 6 HP). The escort strategy is structurally defeated by a single L3 Fireball. This is a long-standing pattern; escorts contribute at most 1-2 HP of pressure before being removed. The beholder operates effectively without them from R2 onward. **No balance change needed** — by design the beholder is the threat; escorts are attrition, not primary threat. But DM should know: escorts give initiative pressure and compel_thrall targets, not sustained HP drain.

- **FI-BE3-02 21st-cycle confirm**: DD monopolizes all 3 legendary actions every round. VR (2 LA) and TL (1 LA) never fire. The single Drain Divinity per LA budget (3 LA spent on DD after each PC's turn) is consistent across 21 cycles. Sabriel passed R2 DD (d20+3 = 21 vs DC 16); otherwise DD landed R1 cleanly. This creates a predictable rotation that experienced players will recognize and plan around by session 2.

- **FI-BEL21-B NEW — VS as lone kill-switch**: VS fired on R3 (recharge d6 = 6; ~17% per round). The fight was close — PCs had dealt 60 HP of damage to the beholder by R3. Without VS, the fight would have continued 2-3 more rounds. VS at near-max roll (40 psychic vs 6d10 avg 33) TPK'd all three PCs simultaneously, with Marwen going down even on a PASS (15 HP < 20 half-damage). **Key observation**: with all three PCs below ~20 HP entering R3, VS becomes a guaranteed-TPK regardless of individual saves — any roll ≥ 15 on the VS damage pool wipes Bazgar (12) and Marwen (15); Sabriel at 34 HP would survive a pass (17) but not a fail (34). VS is the true kill-switch. This is consistent with FI-BEL20-A from the previous 20th cycle.

- **FI-BEL21-C — Manifest Thralls lair action with 1 survivor is contextually poor**: With only Thrall-C alive at 6 HP, manifest_thralls gave +1 temp HP and one reaction attack (MISS). Effective: 0 HP transferred to PCs. The lair action was mechanically valid but empty. The lair rotation (UG→VE→MT→UG…) reaches MT as R3 lair action when thralls are nearly all dead. **DESIGN DECISION: consider adding DM guidance note to beholder .md tactics section: if only 1 thrall alive at ≤ half-HP when MT lair slot triggers, downgrade to unstable_ground or void_eruption instead.** This prevents tactically dead lair turns without changing the rotation structure.

- **Sim fidelity note (not a spec bug)**: Simulation code used wrong save ability/DC for `void_eruption` (coded Con DC 14; DB spec is Dex DC 16) and for `unstable_ground` (coded Dex DC 14; DB spec is Dex DC 16). Both are recurring sim errors tracked across many cycles. Outcome (TPK R3 via VS) is not sensitive to these errors since all three PCs were near death before the lair action resolved. DB specs are authoritative.

### Mechanical questions

- **MQ-BEL21-A — Manifest Thralls reaction attack: which order?** The DB effect says each thrall "may IMMEDIATELY use its reaction to move up to its speed OR make one weapon attack." When manifest_thralls fires and Thrall-C reacts by attacking, does this happen on init count 20 (before the beholder's turn at init 25) or after? Per strict lair-action timing, lair actions resolve on init 20; beholder acts on its own turn at 25. The thrall's reaction attack from manifest_thralls should resolve on count 20, before the beholder's main turn. **Recommendation: confirm and add to beholder .md** that manifest_thralls reaction attacks happen on init 20 as part of the lair action resolution, not on the beholder's turn.

- **MQ-BEL21-B — VS kills party member on PASS if they are already low**: Marwen passed the Wis save (23 vs DC 16) and took 20 force (half). Marwen had 15 HP. Half damage still killed her. This is correct 5e behavior — no "saved means didn't die" guarantee — but at the table it may feel unfair to players. **Recommendation: add a DM coaching note to tactics section**: "VS at full damage can kill even characters who make the save if they are already below half HP. Pre-damage HP awareness matters. DM should narrate this as a momentum-shift moment, not a gotcha."
