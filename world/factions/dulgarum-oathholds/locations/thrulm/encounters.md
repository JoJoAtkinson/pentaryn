# Thrulm: Encounter Progression

A five-stage descent into the sealed hollow. Each encounter builds on the last, revealing more about what has happened below and why the beholder has made its home here.

---

## Encounter 1: The Abandoned Watch-Post

**Location:** Third Sealed Gate / Upper Shaft  
**Level:** Party levels −1  
**Difficulty:** Easy to Medium

### Setup
The party has breached or bypassed the warded gate. The shaft descends 300 feet into darkness. Halfway down, evidence of recent violence: scattered gear, dried blood, claw marks that don't match any known creature.

At the platform-watch station (150 feet down), they find the remains of a Deep Watch encampment:
- 3 dead dwarves (2 weeks old)
- Overturned furniture; signs of panic
- A logbook with the last entry: *"Movement below. Orders came from High King himself. Guard the seal. Do not go deeper. Do not. We can hear it now."*

### Encounter: Carrion Scavengers + Atmosphere

**Monsters:** 4 **Giant Rats** (or other deep vermin) feasting on the dead

**Tactics:**
- Rats are territorial; they swarm any attempt to loot bodies
- One rat carries the Deep Watch captain's seal (valuable for later identification)
- Killing them is straightforward but *wrong*—these are just animals feeding on the dead

**Outcome:** 
- The party learns the watch-post was abandoned ~2 weeks ago
- Logbook hints that the High King *knew* about the beholder or whatever is below
- Descending further means passing the open seal

### Loot & Clues
- Captain's seal (Deep Watch insignia)
- 50 gp in scattered coin
- **Logbook excerpt:** Reveals Deep Watch rotation schedules and that "the thing" has been moving more aggressively

## Encounter 2: The Threshold

**Location:** Main Chamber Entrance / The Sealed Shrine's Outer Ring  
**Level:** Party levels  
**Difficulty:** Medium

### Setup
The shaft opens into a vast natural chamber. The air tastes wrong. The stone beneath their feet is smooth in unnatural ways. Across the chamber (200 feet away), a formation of stone rises like a throne or altar.

Between the party and the altar: scattered derro corpses in various states of decomposition. Fresh tracks circle the throne.

### Encounter: The First Derro Patrol

**Monsters:**
- 2 **Deep Watch Derro** (CR 1/8 each, use stat blocks)
- 1 **Derro Guard** variant (CR 1/2, slightly stronger; a sergeant)

These derro are:
- Confused and frightened
- Not immediately hostile (they think the party might be reinforcements)
- Willing to talk for 1-2 rounds before combat becomes inevitable

**What They Know (and Will Say):**
- "*Something is wrong down here. The stones... they moved.*"
- "*The shrine. We can't get close to it anymore. It's like... like it's calling things.*"
- "*More of us are changing. We don't know where they go.*"
- If pressed: "*The High King told us not to ask. Just guard. Just watch.*"

**The Fight:**
- Derro attack if the party mentions the beholder, the shrine, or Khôr-Bhog-Khôr by name
- They fight defensively, trying to protect the passage back up
- One derro attempts to flee if reduced below half HP

**Terrain:**
- Difficult terrain: rubble from collapsed shrine structures
- Natural pillars provide cover
- A 30-foot chasm to the east (not immediately dangerous but forces positioning)

### Outcome
- Party learns the shrine is the problem
- They learn derro are being "changed" by something
- They see the altar/throne and understand the chamber's scale
- If they keep a derro alive for negotiation: major intel about thralls and shrine-touched

### Loot
- Standard Deep Watch gear (axes, crossbows)
- 20 gp total
- One derro wears a recent order from the High King (cryptic, telling them to "observe but not interfere")

## Encounter 3: The Shrine-Touched

**Location:** Mid-Chamber / Altar Approach  
**Level:** Party levels +1  
**Difficulty:** Hard

### Setup
The party advances toward the altar. As they pass through the chamber's center, the temperature drops. The air hums—a sound just below hearing.

From behind three tall pillars, movement. A derro stumbles out, but *wrong*. Its skin glows faintly. Runes are carved into its flesh. Its eyes are empty.

### Encounter: 2–3 Shrine-Touched Derro

Use the **Shrine-Touched Derro** stat blocks.

**Tactics:**
- Shrine-touched are territorial around the altar
- They attack immediately but *speak* while fighting—fragments of prophecy, ancient names
- One mentions the god's name (Khôr-Bhog-Khôr) if the players haven't learned it yet
- They are drawn to the altar and will not leave its vicinity (300-foot radius)

**Environmental Danger:**
- The altar itself is a hazard: creatures that touch it must make a DC 14 Dexterity save or take 1d6 necrotic damage (the seal is *active* but destabilizing)
- The shrine's presence causes the ground to crack; difficult terrain appears mid-combat

**The Fight:**
- Shrine-touched fight *harder* when hurt (Unstable Form trait)
- They are *faster* near the altar (+10 ft. movement)
- They cannot be turned, reasoned with, or dominated—the shrine's will overrides everything else

### Environmental Discovery: The Clay Works
The party notices clay deposits and work-areas throughout the chamber:
- Shallow pits filled with pale, malleable clay
- Clay in various states: fresh and damp, half-formed shapes, hardened sculptures
- Some half-formed clay shapes vaguely resemble *derro*—unfinished, crude, but clearly intentional
- Fresh gouges and marks suggest something *regularly shapes* the clay into deliberate forms
- A few of the shrine-touched derro emerge from clay pits during combat (freshly-formed, still learning to move)

**Outcome:**
- The party sees the altar and understands something *sacred* was sealed here
- They realize the shrine's power is still active and dangerous
- **Unsettling realization:** Derro are not born—they are *made*. From clay. By something down here. (Not confirmed, but the evidence is undeniable)
- If a spell is cast within 30 feet of the altar, it triggers a DC 14 Arcana check—on a failure, the caster is disoriented for 1 round (the seal rejects divine/arcane magic)

### Loot
- Shrine-touched derro carry fragments of the old god's shrine (carved stones, bone charms, ancient coins)
- One wears a half-completed rune-stitched cloak (beautiful and terrible; could be sold or studied for clues)

## Encounter 4: The Thrall Guard

**Location:** Altar's Shadow / Beholder Territory  
**Level:** Party levels +2  
**Difficulty:** Very Hard

### Setup
As the party approaches within 100 feet of the altar, the air *breaks*. Reality warps around several points in the chamber.

The beholder manifests, hovering above the altar. With it: 3–4 **Thrall Derro**, moving in eerie synchronization.

This is not a random encounter. The beholder has been *aware* of the party since they entered Thrulm. This is its defense—a warning and a test.

### Encounter: The Beholder + Thrall Derro

**Monsters:**
- 1 **The Hunger Below** (Beholder) — does not use full Disintegration Ray or Void Scream yet
- 3 **Thrall Derro** (use stat blocks)

**The Beholder's Behavior:**
- Tries to **Drain Divinity** from any clerics/paladins (removing their power is personal)
- Uses **Disintegration Ray** on spellcasters (targets the highest-level caster)
- Keeps distance, using ranged attacks while thralls block melee approach
- If reduced below 60 HP, it uses **Void Scream** and retreats 60 feet toward the altar
- If the fight extends beyond 3 rounds and it hasn't acted on its turn, the beholder begins **Clay-Shaping** ritual—it prioritizes finishing this over direct combat, using thralls as a shield

**Tactical Complexity:**
- Thralls are expendable; the beholder doesn't care if they die
- The beholder coordinates thralls telepathically; they move on its turn
- The party faces ranged pressure (beholder rays) + melee pressure (thralls) + environmental hazard (shrine proximity)

**The Turning Point:**
If the party seems to be winning (beholder at half HP, thralls eliminated), the beholder attempts a final **Void Scream** and retreats down the **deeper shaft** (the descending passage beyond the altar).

It calls out telepathically: *"You cannot seal this. The void remains. The god does not return."*

Then it is gone, leaving silence.

### Outcome
- Party realizes the beholder is intelligent and *negotiates*
- They understand the beholder is trying to *prevent* restoration of the shrine
- They see the deeper shaft and understand Thrulm goes **further down**
- If they examine the altar after the beholder flees, they find:
  - Wards that are breaking
  - Evidence the seal is **2-3 weeks from complete failure**
  - A choice: repair the seal now (dangerous ritual, requires understanding the original binding) or go deeper to confront what the beholder fears

### Loot
- Thrall derro carry nothing of value (dominated creatures don't carry personal effects)
- The beholder itself is not corpse-lootable; it vanishes if "killed" (it is not truly dead, just dispersed)

## Encounter 5: The Final Confrontation

**Location:** The Altar / The Sealed Shrine Heart  
**Level:** Party levels +3  
**Difficulty:** Deadly

### Setup
The beholder has had time to prepare. It has called more thralls. It has *shaped* the shrine-touched derro into hybrid forms. The chamber is no longer a neutral space—it is the beholder's *lair*.

The party has multiple options:
1. **Pursue immediately** — The beholder is waiting, ready
2. **Prepare the seal's ritual** — Takes time; beholder prevents this by attacking at the last moment
3. **Go deeper** — Descend into the lower shaft to understand what the beholder fears
4. **Negotiate** — Attempt to bargain with the beholder directly (risky; it respects strength)

### Encounter: The Hunger Below (Full Power)

**Monsters:**
- 1 **The Hunger Below** (Beholder) at full power, using all legendary actions and lair actions
- 2–3 **Shrine-Touched Derro**
- 4–6 **Thrall Derro**
- Environmental: The altar is *actively destabilizing*; creatures within 20 feet take ongoing damage

**The Beholder's Full Arsenal:**
- Uses **Disintegration Ray** liberally (and creatively—targets ranged characters in cover)
- Uses **Void Scream** if multiple party members are grouped
- Uses **Lair Actions** on the initiative order; the chamber itself becomes a weapon
- Uses **Drain Divinity** specifically against any healer/support character
- Uses **Tentacle Lash** to grapple and pull characters toward the altar (into hazard range)

**What Changes from Encounter 4:**
- The beholder does *not* retreat; it fights to the end
- If defeated, it *dies*—something happens when it perishes (the seal flares; the lower shaft *rumbles*)
- Thrall derro fight with increased desperation (they are tethered to the beholder; losing it breaks the connection)

### The Ritual Alternative
If the party manages to complete a sealing ritual during combat (difficult but possible):
- The shrine responds; the seal strengthens
- The beholder *screams* (telepathic sound that damages all creatures in the chamber)
- The altar stops destabilizing
- The beholder is weakened but not defeated; it retreats deeper, cut off from the void's power

### The Deeper Descent
If the party defeats the beholder and chooses to go deeper:
- The lower shaft descends another 500 feet
- At the bottom: something else. A void. A presence. A memory of what the god *was*
- This is intentionally left open for you to decide: what's down there? What was Khôr-Bhog-Khôr *really*? What is the beholder protecting or preventing?

### Victory Outcomes
- **Beholder slain, seal restored:** Thrulm becomes inert. The shrine no longer calls creatures. The Deep Watch returns to the post. But the lower shaft remains, and something down there goes quiet—not gone.
- **Beholder slain, seal NOT restored:** The party can study the shrine and attempt restoration. This requires time and resources, but succeeds. However, during the ritual, something in the lower shaft tries to *break through* again.
- **Beholder flees:** It retreats to the lower shaft. The party must decide whether to pursue or let it go. An escaped beholder means future complications.
- **Negotiation succeeds:** The beholder agrees to guard the seal from the *deeper* threats rather than prey on derro. Thrulm becomes a strange ally—dangerous but not hostile. The real threat descends further.

## Summary: Challenge Progression

| Encounter | Monsters | XP Award | Key Lesson |
|-----------|----------|----------|-----------|
| 1. Watch-Post | 4 Giant Rats | 25 XP | The seal was *active* until recently; something below caused it to fail |
| 2. Threshold | 3 Derro (various) | 300 XP | The beholder is here; the shrine is the attraction |
| 3. Shrine-Touched | 2–3 Shrine-Touched Derro | 900 XP | The god's power is *still here*, still dangerous |
| 4. Thrall Guard | Beholder (limited) + 3 Thralls | 2,500 XP | The beholder is intelligent; it's not here by accident |
| 5. Final Confrontation | Beholder (full) + 6 Thralls + Shrine-Touched | 5,000–10,000 XP | The choice: restore the seal, go deeper, or negotiate with the void |

## DM Notes

### Scaling Difficulty
- **Too easy?** Add more shrine-touched derro; activate lair actions earlier; have the beholder use Disintegration Ray freely
- **Too hard?** Reduce thrall derro numbers; give the party a brief respite between encounters 4 and 5; reveal the seal's ritual earlier so they have time to prepare

### Non-Combat Alternatives
- **Negotiation paths** exist at every stage
- **Ritual solutions** exist for the seal (requires study and time)
- **Evasion routes** exist if the party wants to bypass encounters (but the consequences escalate)

### What Happens After
- The deeper shaft descends into questions you haven't answered yet
- The High King is implicated in the seal's failure (was it intentional? A test?)
- Other factions may be interested in Thrulm's power (or its threat)
- The beholder, defeated or fled, is not the *real* problem—it's a symptom
