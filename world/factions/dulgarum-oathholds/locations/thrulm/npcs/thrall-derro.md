# Thrall Derro (Beholder-Dominated)

Eyes unfocused. Movements jerky, as if pulled by invisible strings. A derro caught between two commands: the oath that bound them, and the hunger that now *owns* them.

## Combat Traits

**Sunlight Sensitivity.** While in sunlight, the derro has disadvantage on attack rolls and Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

**Beholder-Thralled.** The derro is under the beholder's control. It obeys the beholder's commands wordlessly and cannot be turned against it. However, its own body is dying from the strain—it moves slowly and inefficiently.

**Fractured Will.** When the derro is forced to act against its original oath (e.g., told to attack other derro), it makes a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw. On a success, it resists for one round and moves at half speed that turn. On a failure, it must obey and suffers 2 (1d4) psychic damage afterward.

**Degrading.** Each time the derro is healed or given aid by a non-thrall, it takes 1 psychic damage and loses any temporary hit points gained.

---

## Actions

**Multiattack.** The derro makes one melee attack. It does not make additional attacks; the domination is incomplete.

**Hand Axe (Weak).** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +1 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 2 (1d4) slashing damage. This attack has disadvantage if the target is the beholder or another thrall.

**Dagger (Desperate).** *Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:* +3 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. *Hit:* 4 (1d4 + 2) piercing damage.

---

## Bonus Actions

**Compelled Movement.** The derro moves up to 30 feet in a direction chosen by the beholder (if the beholder can see it). This does not use the derro's own will.

---

## Reactions

None. The derro cannot react independently.

---

## Tactics

The thrall derro has no tactics of its own. It:
- **Attacks targets the beholder indicates.** Priority is mechanical, not strategic.
- **Flees if the beholder commands it.** Retreat comes instantly, without hesitation.
- **Dies if ordered to.** If the beholder demands final obedience, the thrall will walk into fire, off a cliff, or into enemy blades without question.
- **Cannot be reasoned with.** Appeals to the dwarf inside the thrall find no purchase; that dwarf is buried under layers of psychic compulsion.

---

## Interaction Notes

**Motivations:** None. The derro wants nothing. It is a tool.

**Voice & Personality:** Slurred, distant, monotone. Speaks only when commanded. Eyes track the beholder when it enters the room. Otherwise, vacant.

**De-escalation Hooks:**
- If the beholder is killed, the domination immediately unravels—the thrall falls prone and gasps for breath, then weeps or rages, depending on its nature
- Cannot be saved through combat or negotiation; only the beholder's death frees them
- Killing the thrall is mercy; keeping it alive under the beholder is torture

**If Captured:** The thrall cannot disobey the beholder even when separated. It will attempt to alert the beholder to the party's location or escape at the first opportunity.

---

## Description

A derro who was. The armor hangs loose; the body inside has withered. Scars radiate from the temples and the back of the skull—thin lines as if something is stitching the mind shut. The eyes are glassy, and when you look at them, you see *something else* looking back.

---

## What Happened

This derro ventured too close to the beholder's domain and was caught. Rather than kill them, the beholder *took* them. The domination is a slow decay; the longer the thrall lives under the beholder's control, the more it erodes.

Most thralls last 6-8 months. Some last years. None have ever been freed and returned to sanity.

---

## Transformation

If a thrall is freed (beholder dies, domination breaks), it remembers everything. It remembers being helpless inside its own body. It remembers the command to murder its own kin. It remembers obeying.

Some commit suicide. Some become the beholder's most bitter enemies. Some go hollow and never speak again.
