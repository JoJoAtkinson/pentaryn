# Thrulm — The Sealed Hollow

**Status:** Campaign Ready  
**Difficulty Range:** Levels 8–13  
**Key Theme:** A sacred space violated; a god sealed away; something drawn to the void

---

## What Is Thrulm?

Thrulm (Dwarven: "sealed hollow") is a location deep beneath the Dulgarum Oathholds where an ancient dwarven god—**Khôr-Bhog-Khôr**—was imprisoned centuries ago by oath-binding magic.

The seal was holding until recently. Now something has breached it: a beholder-like creature drawn to the *absence* of divinity. It is remaking the derro who touch the shrine's power. It is preparing something. And the seal is failing.

## How to Run Thrulm

### Quick Start
1. Party enters Thrulm (via Sealed Gate or another breach)
2. Run **Encounter 1** (Watch-Post investigation)
3. Escalate through Encounters 2–4 as party descends
4. Finale: Encounter 5 offers multiple victory conditions

### Scaling Encounters
- **Fewer players or lower level?** Reduce thrall derro count; skip some shrine-touched variants
- **More players or higher level?** Add shrine-touched derro to mid-tier encounters; have beholder use full abilities earlier

### Non-Combat Paths
- **Negotiation:** The beholder is intelligent; DC 16+ Insight/Persuasion checks can open dialogue
- **Ritual:** Study the altar/shrine; completing a DC 18 Arcana check grants advantage on seal-restoration ritual
- **Evasion:** Parties can sneak past encounters if they succeed on Stealth checks (consequences escalate)

### Why Thrulm Matters
- **To the party:** A sealed god, a failing seal, and something ancient below

## Important Notes

### The Beholder Is Not Standard
This is not the beholder from the Monster Manual. It is something drawn to the *void* left by Khôr-Bhog-Khôr's sealing. It is intelligent, negotiable, and has motivations beyond "kill invaders."

### The Shrine Is Always Dangerous
Being near the altar (within 20 feet) causes active magical backlash. Divine/arcane casters feel it most keenly. The party cannot simply "fix" the problem with a spell—the seal itself rejects easy solutions.

### Multiple Endings Work
- Combat victory (beholder defeated)
- Negotiated peace (beholder becomes ally/neutral)
- Ritual success (seal restored, beholder weakened)
- Deeper descent (answering what's below)
- Escape (leaving Thrulm for now; consequences later)

All are valid. Thrulm's story doesn't end when the beholder falls—it's the beginning of larger questions.
