# The Still Page

> **"Sit down. It steeps when it's ready."**

- **Location:** Upper Tier, narrow storefront on the lane between the Concordance Library and the scribe quarter
- **Proprietor:** Pell Orvaine (PELL or-VAYN)
- **Type:** Tea house

---

## Overview

The Still Page is four tables, a bench by the window, and a short counter. It smells like dried herbs, old paper, and whatever is currently steeping. Pell Orvaine opened it after thirty years as a copying clerk at the Concordance Library — his eyesight gave out before his attachment to the building did, so he took the nearest available storefront and started serving tea to the people still going in.

## The Proprietor

**Pell Orvaine** is somewhere in his late sixties, quiet, and moves slowly without being frail. He keeps a stool behind the counter and reads when the shop is empty, holding the page close. He knows most of the Concordance staff by name and knows their orders without asking. Strangers he treats politely and without fuss.

He is not forthcoming about himself, but he listened to scholars talk for three decades and retained more than most people realize. If a conversation in the shop turns to old records, obscure history, or which archivist to approach for what kind of request, Pell tends to have a useful thought.

## What's Served

- A small rotating selection of loose-leaf teas — nothing exotic, reliably good
- Hot water and a plain cup if you bring your own
- Occasionally a hard biscuit or a slice of whatever a neighboring vendor dropped off; Pell does not bake

## Atmosphere

The window bench faces the lane toward the library entrance. People come here to think, to wait, to read before the library opens in the morning. Conversation is kept low by unspoken agreement. Pell does not enforce this; he doesn't have to.
