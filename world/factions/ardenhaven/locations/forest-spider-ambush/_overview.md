# Forest Spider Ambush — Road to the Ruins

> The shortcut runs through old wood. In the last quarter mile the birdsong stopped, and the branches overhead are wrong — too grey, too thick, too still.

**Where:** a wooded hollow on the track between Ardenford and the [Western Scrubland Ruins](../ruins-western-scrubland.md) — a roadside fight on the way to the goblin job.
**Tuned for:** 4–6 level-1 PCs. Default 1 giant spider + 2 giant wolf spiders (~300 XP) — moderate, a notch lighter than the goblins. A speed-bump, not the destination.

## Terrain

- **Webbed canopy:** a grey sheet-web 15–20 ft up. Webbing squares are difficult terrain; creatures can be webbed in place (see the giant spider's Web).
- **Forest floor:** undergrowth and roots — light difficult terrain. All three spiders climb trunks and branches at full speed.
- **Light:** dappled shade, effectively dim under the canopy.

## How it opens

The wolf spiders ambush from the leaf litter — fast, low, flanking. The big spider stays up in the web, opens with Web to pin the most dangerous PC, then drops to bite the restrained target. No leader, no parley, no retreat.

## The contrast

Where the goblins kite and scheme, the spiders control and commit — web-restraint, climbing the fight out of reach, poison that grinds a character down. No ranged trades; they want you tangled and close.

## NPCs

- **giant-spider** ×1 — the canopy weaver (CR 1)
- **giant-wolf-spider** ×2 — ground outriders (CR 1/4 each)
