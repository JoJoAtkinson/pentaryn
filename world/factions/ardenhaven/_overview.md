**Motto/Slogan:** "Through unity, hope restored."

## Mission & Goals

### Public Goals
Peace, prosperity, and fairness for all citizens; rebuild from war; maintain independence from the Calderon Imperium; create a society where voices matter.

### True Goals
Survive and stabilize despite limited resources; prevent Imperium expansion; prove that shared governance can work; balance idealism with pragmatism.

### Methods
Council debates, guild partnerships, temple mediation, volunteer militias, diplomatic treaties, incremental reforms.

## History

### Foundation
Born from the Treaty of Havens, when border cities and rebel provinces united against Calderon Imperium expansion, choosing shared rule over imperial autocracy.

### Major Events
- **The Founding Council (Year TBD):** First assembly of lords, guilds, and temples to draft the Haven Charter.
- **The Ironstead Stalemate (Year TBD):** Final battle ending in ceasefire; borders recognized by treaty.
- **The Grain Crisis (Year TBD):** Famine and corruption scandal that nearly collapsed the realm; reforms followed.

### Current Situation
Recovering but fragile. War scars remain: underfunded border defenses, corrupt officials in remote districts, refugee camps. The Council argues constantly, but most citizens believe in the vision. It's the kind of place where heroes can genuinely make things better.

## Leadership Structure

### Hierarchy
A monarch (limited by the Charter) shares power with the Council of Havens (lords, guildmasters, temple representatives). Provincial governors implement policy locally.

```
Monarch (Charter-Bound)
    ↓
Council of Havens (Lords • Guilds • Temples)
    ↓
Provincial Governors
    ↓
Local Sheriffs & Mayors
```

## Membership

### Requirements
Residency and oath to the Haven Charter; citizenship granted after one year; voting rights after property or service.

### Ranks & Titles
1. **Resident:** Registered inhabitant
2. **Citizen:** Full rights under the Charter
3. **Council Delegate:** Representative of a guild, temple, or lordship
4. **Provincial Governor:** Regional administrator
5. **Councillor:** Voting member of the Council of Havens

### Benefits
Fair courts, freedom of speech, guild protections, temple services, open petitions to the Council.

### Obligations
Taxes, militia service (voluntary but incentivized), jury duty, respect for the Charter.

## Resources & Assets

### Financial
- **Treasury:** Modest; recovering from war debts
- **Income Sources:** Trade tariffs, farm taxes, guild fees, temple tithes

### Military
- **Forces:** Volunteer militias, small professional guard, border patrols
- **Equipment:** Mixed quality; some modern, much inherited from the wars

### Magical
- **Spellcasters:** Temple clerics, independent mages, adventurer guilds
- **Artifacts:** None significant; relics from the founding kept in Ardenford

### Political
- **Influence:** Strong moral authority; weak military projection
- **Alliances:** Trade with Merrowgate, Garhammar; mutual defense treaty with Garrok Confederation

## Locations

### [Headquarters — Ardenford](locations/ardenford.md)

### Border Fortresses

Fertile river valleys, mixed forests, and recovering farmland; contested borders with Calderon Imperium.

## Reputation

### Among Common Folk
Hopeful, messy, genuine. People feel heard, even if progress is slow.

### Among Nobility
Seen as naive idealists by autocrats; respected by reformers.

### Among Other Factions
Predictable partner but militarily weak; morally trustworthy; often underestimated.

## Activities & Operations

### Public Activities
Council debates, judicial circuits, infrastructure repairs, refugee resettlement programs.

### Secret Operations
Counter-espionage against Imperium agents; anti-corruption investigations; covert aid to Calderonite defectors.

### Current Projects
Rebuilding border defenses; rooting out grain smuggling rings; negotiating Garrok trade expansion.

## Secrets & Mysteries
A faction within the Council secretly funds anti-Imperium insurgents across the border; if discovered, it could reignite the war. Some governors are compromised by Imperium bribes or Merrowgate leverage.

## Plot Hooks

1. A Council vote deadlocks on border defense funding; the party must secure swing votes.
2. A corrupt sheriff exploits war refugees; exposing them risks destabilizing a fragile district.
3. Garrok emissaries request aid against Imperium encroachment; accepting could breach the treaty.

## DM Notes

### Using This Faction
Ardenhaven is the "good guys trying their best." Make NPCs flawed but earnest. Show the tension between idealism and survival. Reward players for improving systems, not just killing villains.

### Future Plans
A major corruption scandal threatens Council unity; an Imperium incursion tests the Garrok alliance; a refugee crisis forces hard choices about borders and resources.
