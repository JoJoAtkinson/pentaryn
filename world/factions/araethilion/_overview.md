## Lands & Settlements

### Territory

Araethilion holds the high wooded elevations above most settled lands: not the knife-edged peaks, but the broad shoulders of the mountains where forest and stone interlock. Its canopy is dominated by immense hardwoods—trees of fantastic scale—treated as living temples and boundary-markers.

### Cities & Works

Araethilene settlements are shaped with the mountains rather than carved from them. Stone, soil, and slope are woven into terraces, halls, and towers; the mountains themselves are not hollowed out. This is not dwarven delving—the land remains whole, bent gently to elven will.

### Capital — Aeralithil

From afar, [Aeralithil](locations/aeralithil.md) is announced by a vast tower rising where forest meets stone. Its lower reaches are partially carved into the mountainside, anchoring it to the land, but its form rejects traditional elven design: the tower is Elaerith’s, and its architecture is treated as godly rather than elven—authority made manifest.

### Borders & Contrast

Araethilion’s heights are distinct from the dwarven deeps: not the same mountain that holds the mines, and not mistaken for one. The elves claim the upper slopes and living forests; the dwarves claim the depths.

## Mission & Goals

### Public Goals

Preserve elven culture, arts, and forests; sustain harmony between the divine ruler and the land; ensure prosperity and agelessness for all Araethilene.

### True Goals
Maintain Elaerith’s absolute rule through the Nine Rings of Proximity and the Silver Thread; keep the faithful dependent on her presence and favor; suppress dissidents who threaten to fracture devotion or expose the human cost of Severance and exile.

### Methods
The Lifebloom Ritual (binding the Silver Thread); doctrine and songwoven civic ritual; control of access to the courts (proximity as power); exile, Severance, or “voluntary pilgrimage” for dissenters; quiet surveillance via court networks and favored devotees.

## History

### Foundation

Founded when the First Araethil united scattered elven clans under a single crown, claiming divine mandate from the forest itself.

### Major Events

- **The Veiling Ceremony (Year TBD):** Elaerith adopted the Veiled Crown and formalized court life around controlled access, proximity, and appointed service.
- **The Whisper Schism (Year TBD):** Dissident seers fled to form exile enclaves; officially recorded as "voluntary pilgrimages."
- **The Lifelume Accord (Year TBD):** A pact with neighboring powers to maintain borders in exchange for elven artistry and healing.

> **Note:** Araethilion’s core structure is proximity to Elaerith, not hereditary nobility. Favor can run in families, but authority is granted by closeness and appointment.

### Current Situation

Thriving on the surface: abundant harvests, renowned arts, and ageless citizens. Beneath: mounting casualties among seers who glimpse the truth, and growing unrest in exile enclaves trading forbidden prophecies.

## Leadership Structure

### Hierarchy
Elaerith rules directly; court life and civic authority organize around the Nine Rings of Proximity (those closer to her hold more privilege and influence). Temple-wardens and field clergy carry doctrine outward, but power always points back inward, toward the courts.

```
Veiled Crown (Living God)
    ↓
Nine Rings of Proximity (Court & Civic Order)
    ↓
Temple-Wardens & Choir-Sentinels
    ↓
Ritual-Keepers & Choir-Sentinels
```

## Membership

### Requirements

Birth as an elf of Araethilion; participation in seasonal blessings; oaths of service to the Veiled Crown during coming-of-age rites.

### Ranks & Titles

1. **Blessed Child:** Youth under the Crown's protection
2. **Songkeeper:** Adult citizen participating in festivals
3. **Ritual-Keeper:** Clergy or temple servant
4. **Temple-Warden:** Regional administrator
5. **High Priest/Priestess:** Member of the inner court

### Benefits

Agelessness, abundant harvests, access to elven arts and healing, protection from external threats.

### Obligations
Attend seasonal rites and civic ceremonies, serve when summoned to temple or court duty, report major events to the court’s channels, and accept that closeness to Elaerith (and her attention) is the source of privilege.

## Resources & Assets

### Financial

- **Treasury:** Moderate; sustained by artisan exports and tribute from vassal settlements
- **Income Sources:** Enchanted goods, healing services, forest products, songwoven tapestries

### Military

- **Forces:** Choir-Sentinels (ritual warriors), Vine-Wardens (forest guardians), elite Veiled Guard
- **Equipment:** Silverwood bows, enchanted blades, living armor grown from sacred trees

### Magical

- **Spellcasters:** High density of clerics, druids, and bards; all bound to the Veiled Crown's divine mandate
- **Artifacts:** The Veiled Crown itself; Lifelume Arbors (ritual foci); Whisper Stones (communication relics)

### Political

- **Influence:** Strong among elven diaspora; respected for healing and artistry
- **Alliances:** Neutral trade agreements with Merrowgate; uneasy peace with human realms

## Secrets & Mysteries

Elaerith’s divinity is real, but her rule is maintained through dependence: the Silver Thread, proximity, and the terror of Severance. The closer you stand, the more you are sustained—and the more it would destroy you to be cast out.

Severance is not merely punishment; it is a public theology lesson. Those who survive exile are never spoken of again, as if removing them from memory is part of the rite.

Some fear the Veiled Crown also binds Elaerith to an ancient forest entity, and that the Nine Rings are as much a cage for her as they are for her people.

## Plot Hooks

1. A seer seeks proof that the Silver Thread can be safely unmade, and needs protection to test it.
2. A regional rite fails and the faithful panic—was it sabotage, absence, or a warning from Elaerith?
3. An exile enclave requests aid smuggling forbidden prophecies into Aeralithil.

## DM Notes

### Using This Faction

Araethilion should feel beautiful and uncanny—players are drawn in by artistry and healing, then unsettled by the cost. NPCs can be genuine believers, conflicted clergy, or desperate exiles. The moral dilemma is key: is prosperity worth hidden suffering?

### Future Plans

A seer’s prophecy reaches critical mass; an inner-ring faction debates loosening control of travel and proximity; Elaerith’s attention (or health) mysteriously falters.
