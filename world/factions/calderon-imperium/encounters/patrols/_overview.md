# Gar Valley Pass — Traveler Reports & Rumors

## The Official Story

Travelers heading toward Gar Valley report that the Calderon Imperium has recently established a "trade protection initiative" at the mountain pass. The official word, as told by merchants and caravans, is straightforward: the Imperium is safeguarding the route from bandits and ensuring safe passage for all legitimate commerce.

"They've got perhaps thirty soldiers there," one westbound merchant mentioned over ale. "All human, disciplined enough. They say it's to stop the brigands that were plaguing caravans last season."

## The Complication

But the story gets murkier the more travelers you talk to.

"Ardenhaven's got their own force camped not two miles away," another trader said, lowering his voice. "On the other side of the pass, mind you. Far enough that you can't see one from the other. But everyone knows they're *watching* each other."

When pressed, the merchant shrugged. "On paper, Ardenhaven 'donated troops' to the cause. Makes it sound all unified. But out there?" He tapped his temple. "It's two armies keeping each other honest. Barely talking. The trade protection is real enough, but it's almost... secondary."

## Growing Worries

Some travelers have started taking the long way around—choosing trails high in the east side mountains to avoid any confrontation. Their reasoning is consistent: the pass bureaucracy is getting tighter.

Another voice chimed in: "Word is they're polite about it—not harassing folk. But if you look different? If you're not human? There's a bottleneck. And with Ardenhaven watching close by, nobody wants to risk making a scene."

## The Rumor Mill

- Some say the Imperium is looking to get a trade advantage by securing unusual routes.
- Others claim bandits are still a real problem and the dual presence actually keeps them in check.
- A few merchants swear the Imperium soldiers are treating it like boring duty—just logging who passes and where they're headed.
- One caravan master muttered that "they're using the trade protection as cover for a census," though no one's sure what they'd do with that information.

## The Route

The Gar Valley Pass is the main route to Garhammer dwarves' territory. It's the shortest way through the mountains. The alternative—high mountain pass—adds days to the journey and not traversable to trade wagons.
