## Mission & Goals

### Public Goals

From the perspective of outsiders, the Rakthok Horde appears to be a violent, expansionist force. Their raids are seen as attempts to dominate trade routes, seize resources, and gain glory through bloodshed. This perception is fueled by Calderon propaganda, which paints the Horde as savage aggressors. However, this view is a deliberate oversimplification that ignores the Horde's deeper motivations and spiritual mission.

The Horde's public actions, such as raids and territorial skirmishes, are often misinterpreted as acts of aggression. In reality, these actions are driven by their need to protect sacred sites, maintain spiritual balance, and secure resources vital for their survival. The Horde's leaders have made limited attempts to communicate their true intentions to outsiders, as they believe outsiders are too barbaric to understand the spiritual damage inflicted by the Calderon Imperium. From the Horde's perspective, the Imperium's exploitation of spirits and desecration of sacred sites is an affront that outsiders cannot grasp due to their materialistic worldview.

### True Goals

The Horde's true mission is deeply intertwined with the spiritual and cultural values of its tribes. The Horde seeks to protect their tribes and the spirits of the land from desecration and exploitation. Their raids are not acts of conquest but desperate measures to ensure survival and to prevent the Calderon Imperium from corrupting the spiritual balance.

## History

### Foundation

Unified during the Exodus when multiple orc tribes fled blighted lands that became spiritually corrupted and physically poisonous.

### Major Events

- **The Blight (Year TBD):** Homeland corrupted; spirits fell silent or turned hostile.
- **The Exodus (Year TBD):** Mass migration eastward; warbands united under shamanic guidance.
- **The First Betrayal (Year TBD):** Attempted alliance with Calderon Imperium ended in massacre; shaped current paranoia.
- **The Vision Wars (Year TBD):** Shamanic visions drove pre-emptive strikes across borders; created reputation as unpredictable aggressors.
- **Era of Trade Blindspot (Year TBD):** Outside powers reframe generations of war as mere tariff disputes, deepening the Horde’s fury and isolation.

### Current Situation

Desperate and dangerous. The Horde occupies marginal lands near Calderon Imperium borders, launching raids driven by shamanic prophecy and survival necessity. Spiritually adrift; ancestors demand blood and proof of strength. Internally fractured—warbands unified by the Chosen Warlord's visions but plagued by doubt and debate among shamans.

The raids never stopped. The Imperium keeps the border bleeding to harvest its own **Heart-Marked** dead—elite soldiers whose souls can be rebound after death. The Horde’s brutality is, in part, a countermeasure: denial of intact remains and ritual practices meant to keep necromancers from recovering what they came for.

## Leadership Structure

### Hierarchy

A theocratic democracy built from tribal Fire Councils. Each tribe elects a Fire Council of hearth-family heads led by a Chief Shaman—the tribe’s spiritual authority. When omens warn of threats too great for a single tribe, the Chief Shamans leave their circles and assemble as the Vision Council, which selects and oversees the Chosen Warlord who serves as the Horde’s War Voice.

The Warlord commands from the front yet remains accountable to the Vision Council; a Warlord who squanders lives or defies spirit law can be recalled. Warband captains therefore answer to both battlefield orders and the civic mandates delivered through their Fire Councils.

Outsiders simplify the structure into a brute hierarchy, but the Horde understands it as a web of councils:

```
Chief Shamans (Tribal Circles)  ←→  Fire Councils  [mutual counsel; neither commands the other]
                  ↑
          Vision Council (Chief Shamans in Assembly)
                  ↓
          Chosen Warlord / War Voice of the Horde
                  ↓
          Warband Captains
                  ↓
          Raiders / Survivors
```

#### Vision Council (Chief Shamans)

- **Title:** Keepers of Flame and Voice
- **Role:** Assembly of each tribe’s Chief Shaman; interprets shared visions, elects the Chosen Warlord, and can strip a warleader of mandate
- **Description:** Representatives from every tribe, including Dakmar'nak (DAHK-mar-nak) and others, who mediate between tribal needs and the Horde’s survival

#### Fire Council Elders (Tribal Seats)

- **Title:** Hearth-Fire Speakers
- **Role:** Local civic leaders elected within each tribe to manage resources, debate raids, and hold War Voices accountable between Vision Council convocations
- **Description:** Veteran hunters, artisans, and shamans who balance practical logistics with spiritual duty; five elders typically serve per tribe

## Membership

Average fighting strength per major tribe numbers around 1,400 warriors supported by roughly 2,800 civilians—totaling 4,200 souls bound by shared oaths.

### Requirements

Survival of the Exodus or acceptance by a warband; proof of strength; obedience to visions.

### Ranks & Titles

1. **Survivor:** Basic member; has endured the Blight or proven themselves
2. **Raider:** Combat-tested warrior
3. **Warband Elite:** Veteran of multiple successful raids
4. **Warband Captain:** Leader of a warband
5. **Vision-Blessed:** Those marked by shamanic prophecy

### Benefits

Warband protection, share of raid spoils, spiritual guidance, communal survival resources.

### Obligations

Follow the Chosen Warlord's commands, participate in raids, honor ancestors through blood and victory, report visions to shamans.

## Culture & Practices

- **Heart-Sever Rite:** Warriors rip hearts from fallen enemies to deny Calderon soulfire resurrection, believing the act frees the victim’s spirit from Imperial bondage. 
- **Spirit Empathy:** The Horde regards every person and place as spirit-bearing; even enemies are mourned as manipulated victims once their hearts are returned to the land. Material wealth like gold carries little weight compared to safeguarding spirits, shaping the Horde’s disdain for greed and exploitation.
- **Democratic Pride:** Fire Council elections are public, heated affairs—Horde demagogues delight in noting they practice truer representation than their "civilized" critics.
- **Collective Survival:** No single tribe stands alone for long; alliances under a Warlord are essential doctrine to keep the Horde from being isolated and crushed.
- **Civic Education:** Youth receive instruction in spirit law, geography, and enemy customs, producing literate warriors who can challenge outside narratives about the East.

## Tribes (Player Canon)

The Horde is an alliance of tribes with distinct virtues and rivalries. Notable examples:

- **Dakmar'nak (DAHK-mar-nak):** engineers and spiritual preservationists; blackened iron craft and civic schooling.
- **Kuldrak'vor (KULL-drak-vor):** a powerful tribe growing withdrawn, claiming to “interpret the Spirits” more strictly than before.

## Resources & Assets

### Financial

- **Treasury:** Minimal; wealth measured in plunder and portable goods
- **Income Sources:** Raiding, scavenging, extortion of border settlements

### Military

- **Forces:** Mobile warbands (fast-strike raiders), berserkers, shamanic war-casters
- **Equipment:** Blackened iron arms forged in the Black Marsh, supplemented with looted gear adapted for marsh warfare

### Magical

- **Spellcasters:** Shamans (spiritual leaders and diviners), blood-witches (dark magic users)
- **Artifacts:** Ancestor totems, vision bones, cursed relics from the Blight

### Political

- **Influence:** Feared but isolated; no stable allies
- **Alliances:** Opportunistic and temporary; history of betrayal

## Reputation

### Among Common Folk (Outside)

Monsters; unpredictable raiders who strike without warning; stories used to frighten children.

### Among Nobility

Existential threat; justify militarization and extreme measures—yet official ledgers reduce the conflict to tariff disputes and "border incidents."

### Among Other Factions

Untrustworthy; desperate and dangerous. While some factions sympathize with the Horde's plight, they fear their methods and spiritual motivations. Trade-era diplomats frequently insist the Horde "learn from" westernized orcs, deepening the cultural rift. This perception fails to acknowledge the Horde's spiritual anguish and their efforts to protect the spirits of the land, which are often misunderstood as barbaric superstition.

### Current Projects

Major raid planned based on recent visions; ritual to summon ancestors for guidance; investigating rumors of magical solution to the Blight.

## DM Notes

### Using This Faction

Rakthok should feel tragic and terrifying. They are not evil for its own sake—they are desperate survivors driven by paranoia, prophecy, and survival instinct. Show their brutality but also their spiritual anguish. 

Lean into the irony that a Horde branded monstrous may be the most effective democracy on the continent—Fire Council debates are loud, public, and deeply compassionate toward the souls of friend and foe alike.
