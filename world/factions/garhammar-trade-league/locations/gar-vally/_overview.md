# Gar-Vally

> A hidden valley where gnolls live not as Yeenoghu's spawn, but as a lunar, matriarchal, ancestor-eating culture with no pantheon—only spirits and the hunt.

## Geography

Gar-Vally is a natural amphitheater carved into the mountains east of Garhammar, accessible only by a narrow pass along the Dulgarum trade road. The valley floor is ringed by ancient stones and dominated by a central fire-site where bones are arranged in spiral ancestor-maps. Moonlight reaches the valley floor even on cloudy nights; sunlight is scarce and the gnolls avoid it.

The valley is home to the Gar-Vally clan of gnolls, led by a matron who carries within her the eaten mothers of generations past.

## Culture & Beliefs

The gnolls of Gar-Vally do not worship demons. They hold instead a culture older and stranger:

**Their first article:** Death is not an ending but a meal. To eat the slain is to honour them, and to inherit what they were—strength, cunning, the memory of a hunting-ground. To bury a corpse is the gravest sacrilege known to them, equal in offence to leaving meat upon a kill.

**Their second article:** The matriarch of a clan is sacred not by station but by contents. She carries within her the matriarchs before her—eaten, in the proper rite, at their dying. The clan's history is not sung nor written; it is digested. To speak with the matron is to speak with a line of mothers reaching back further than memory.

**Their faith:** They reckon the moon their Mother and the sun a hostile thing—a watcher, a burner, an outsider. Their rites fall at night, and they will not begin a hunt of consequence in daylight if the moon can be waited for. The world is populous with small spirits—of the prey-beast, of the kill-place, of the cracked bone—and these are not worshipped but negotiated with. The hunt is the prayer. The feast is the answer.

**Their naming:** Gnolls do not name themselves. They take the names of their first humanoid victims. Unnamed gnolls are young, unproven. Named gnolls carry the identity of the dead—a stolen name, worn forever.

## The A.V. Account

*(Excerpt from "A Stranger's Record," attributed to A.V., scholar of foreign cultures)*

I went into the valley believing the gnoll-kind worshipped the Demon of the Hungering Maw. Every tract I had read agreed. I returned knowing better, and the truer account is the stranger one.

They keep no temples. They name no high god. What they hold instead is older.

Their first article: death is not an ending but a meal. To eat the slain is to honour them, and to inherit what they were — strength, cunning, the memory of a hunting-ground. To bury a corpse is the gravest sacrilege known to them, equal in offence to leaving meat upon a kill. I watched a young male struck across the muzzle by his matron for walking past a fallen hare without taking it up. The wasting of flesh, she told me afterward through my interpreter, is theft from the dead.

From this follows the second article, and the one I find most remarkable. The matriarch of a clan is sacred not by station but by contents. She carries within her the matriarchs before her — eaten, in the proper rite, at their dying. The clan's history is not sung nor written; it is digested. To speak with the matron is to speak with a line of mothers reaching back further than my grandfather's grandfather. I do not know whether to credit this literally. They believe it absolutely.

They reckon the moon their Mother and the sun a hostile thing — a watcher, a burner, an outsider. Their rites fall at night, and they will not begin a hunt of consequence in daylight if the moon can be waited for.
No pantheon, as I would understand the word. The world is instead populous with small spirits — of the prey-beast, of the kill-place, of the cracked bone — and these are not worshipped but negotiated with. The hunt is the prayer. The feast is the answer.

I record this because the truth of it serves better than the convenient lie. They are not demon-bound. They are something for which my tongue holds no word.

— A.V.

## Adventure Hooks

- **The Teaching (Raven's Quest):** Elder Raven seeks the party to teach the gnolls a religion so she can later "harvest their bodies." The matron will not convert, but may incorporate a new faith into her existing beliefs. This complicates the quest and tests the party's allegiance.

- **The Safe-Pass:** A gnoll name-bone (a carved token bearing a matron's name) grants safe passage through the valley and gnoll territory. The party may earn one by negotiating with the matron.

- **The Lie:** The demon-tracts (Yeenoghu worship) are false. The party can discover, through the matron or A.V.'s journal, that the gnolls are a distinct culture, not Yeenoghu's spawn. This contradicts wider church doctrine and Calderon propaganda.

- **The Threat:** Calderon scouts are trying to exterminate the gnolls or enslave them. The party can warn the matron, earn her trust, or exploit the chaos. The gnolls are not invulnerable—they fear the sun and organized warfare.
