## Combat Traits

**Pack Tactics.** The matron has advantage on melee attack rolls against a creature if at least one other gnoll ally is within 5 feet of the target and the ally is not incapacitated.

**Sun-Burdened.** The matron has disadvantage on attack rolls while in direct sunlight.

**Lunar Hunt.** When a moon is visible (night, outdoors), the matron gains advantage on Stealth checks and adds 1d4 to the first attack roll each round.

**Vessel of Mothers (Passive).** The matron carries within her the spirits of three eaten matrons (Marta, Gordin, Seresh). When the matron speaks, her voice carries echoes and overlays—the voices of the dead.

**Echoing Voice.** When the matron uses its action to speak (negotiation, revelation), all gnolls within 60 feet that can hear her gain advantage on their next attack roll before the end of their next turn.

---

## Actions

**Multiattack.** The matron makes two attacks: one with its bite and one with its claws, or casts a spell.

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit (or +7 in moonlight), reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 10 (2d6 + 3) piercing damage.

**Claws.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit (or +7 in moonlight), reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 8 (2d4 + 3) slashing damage.

## Reactions

**Eat the Fallen (Reaction, Once Per Turn).** When another gnoll within 5 feet of the matron drops to 0 HP, the matron can use its reaction to perform the eating-rite. The matron gains 5 temporary HP and advantage on its next attack roll before the end of its next turn.

**Vessel of Mothers (Reaction, Once Per Combat).** When the matron is reduced to 0 HP, an unnamed Hunt-Lieutenant within 5 feet can use her reaction to perform the **Rite of Succession**:
- The matron dies, but the lieutenant **immediately gains the matron's spell list and all special traits** for 1 minute.
- The lieutenant becomes the new matron for combat purposes.
- If this rite is completed, **Ancestor-Stir does NOT manifest**.

If no lieutenant is within reach or the rite is prevented, **Ancestor-Stir manifests** in the matron's place next round.

---

## Tactics

**Priority Targets:** None—the matron does NOT fight to kill. She fights to *test* and force retreat.

**Movement:** Defensive. Uses Pack Tactics. Stays near her Hunt-Lieutenant.

**Desperation:** Below half HP, the matron does NOT retreat. Instead, she moves closer to her lieutenant, signaling the rite is coming.

**Environmental:** Prefers night and moonlight. In daylight, is significantly weakened.

---

## Interaction Notes

**Motivations:** To protect the clan and preserve gnoll culture. Eventually to be eaten by her successor.

**Voice & Personality:** Speaks slowly, with layered echoes. Contemplative, patient, genuinely curious about outsiders.

**Weaknesses (If Known):** She values ritual and honor. If the party shows respect for her culture, she will listen. She can be negotiated with—violence is not her goal.

**De-escalation Hooks:** Showing reverence for the dead, acknowledging her as a vessel, offering to learn about gnoll culture, asking directly about A.V.'s account.

---

## Description & Flavor

A massive gnoll, ancient and scarred. Her hide is marked with symbols—human letters, dwarf runes, elf script—all carved or tattooed in ritual patterns. She sits cross-legged by a fire, sharpening a blade with ritual slowness. When she looks up, you hear it: not one voice, but many layered together, speaking as one. Her eyes are old, ancient, and weary. Around her neck, she wears bone-carved amulets—each one bearing a name.

---

## Lore & Background

The matron is a living genealogy. She has eaten three matrons before her:
- **Seresh** (the eldest memory, now faint)
- **Gordin** (her own mother, whose wisdom guides her most strongly)
- **Marta** (the matron immediately before her, whose memory is still fresh)

When the matron speaks, she speaks as a chorus of the dead. She is tired of carrying so many dead, tired of decisions already made by those who came before.

When she dies, she will be eaten by her chosen successor (the Hunt-Lieutenant), and that successor will carry her memory forward.

## Treasure & Rewards

**Immediate Loot:** Bone-carved armor (200 gp), ritual blade (200 gp), bone amulets (priceless to gnolls).

**Corpse Treasure:** If the Hunt-Lieutenant performs the Rite of Succession, the matron's body is sacred and must not be looted. If the rite is prevented, the clan will seek vengeance. The matron's death is THE most important moment—handle it carefully.

**Intangibles:** If the party negotiates and survives:
- A *name-bone* (safe passage through gnoll territory forever)
- Confirmation that demon-tracts are lies
- Revelation about A.V.'s account
- Possible alliance or truce with the gnoll clan
