# Mountin Pass — Encounter Overview

A narrow trade-route pass through the Garhammar peaks above the treeline. Caravans cross at the mercy of weather, raiders, and the apex predators of the icy peaks.

## Terrain

- **Snowy and icy throughout.** Knee-deep drifts along both road edges; the road itself is packed snow over scree, slick in patches. Snow-dependent abilities (e.g. Snow Vanish) are always available here.
- **Pass width:** ~30-40 ft road, narrower at the cliff cuts (~15 ft).
- **Upslope:** rises sharply, climbable with climb speed or DC 12 Athletics.
- **Downslope:** sheer drop, 10d6 bludgeoning if forced over.
- **Snowdrifts** at the edges give light cover and ambush concealment.

## Light & weather

- **Light:** dim during day (overcast / snow glare); full darkness at night unless lit. Most apex predators here have darkvision.
- **Cold:** ~0°F. Unprotected creatures take 1d6 cold per hour exposed.
- **Visibility:** clear when calm; reduced to 30 ft in active snowfall.

## Hazards

- **Difficult terrain** off the cleared road (drifts, ice patches) — half movement.
- **Avalanche** can be triggered by loud spells/explosions on the upslope: DC 15 Wis save for the caster to control; failure means 4d6 bludgeoning to anyone in the path + buried (Athletics DC 15 to dig out).

## Caravan tactical layout (typical)

When a party is travelling the pass, they're usually strung along the road:

- **Front line** (fighters, paladins): ~5-10 ft spacing, leading the line.
- **Middle** (clerics, rogues): ~15-25 ft behind front.
- **Back line** (wizards, healers, archers): often **25-40 ft behind melee**, where they have line of sight but are out of immediate danger.

NPCs targeting the back line should weigh ranged options first — closing all the way to melee burns a turn and exposes the predator to the front line on the way through.

## Predator hunt patterns here

- **Vanish-and-pounce** is the dominant strategy when the snow is deep enough — open with stealth, close in one explosive turn.
- **Ranged poison/breath** is the alternative when the target is too far for a single-turn approach, or when the predator wants to soften the line before committing.
- **Retreat upslope** with climb speed when bloodied — pursuers without climb are cut off.
