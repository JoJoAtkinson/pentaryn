## Quest Summary

On the party's last night in Elderholt, Elder Raven seeks out Bazgar and Sabriel — privately, in her direct and socially flat manner — and offers them a problem she cannot solve herself: a clutch of gnolls, deep in the valley, who tampered with magic the coven has ruled out of bounds. The coven (through Elder Ishvar of Prudence) ordered Raven to purge them. She will not. Not for mercy — for arithmetic. She does not know where a gnoll's soul goes, and she suspects it goes nowhere good.

She wants the party to **teach the gnolls a religion** before she ends them, so that the souls have somewhere to be sent.

## The Hook

Raven appears the night before the party leaves Elderholt. She does not knock. She does not announce herself. She is simply there when the door is next opened — or in the chair across from Bazgar when he turns around — robes still, violet light steady in her ribcage, voice quiet and unhurried. She speaks only to Bazgar and Sabriel. She does not wait for them to invite her to sit.

> "Bazgar. Sabriel. I have been watching the two of you. Not in any meaningful sense — I was looking at other things, and you were within the field of view. I do not flatter. I am here because you are leaving in the morning, and I have a problem that will follow me past your departure if I do not address it tonight."
>
> *(She does not pause for them to respond. She rarely does.)*
>
> "Some years ago — the count is not relevant — Elder Ishvar of Prudence brought a finding to the council. A pack of gnolls in the valley below us had begun working magic the coven has ruled inadmissible. I will not detail the working. The ruling stands. The council voted to have them removed. The task was given to me, because tasks of removal are usually given to me, and because Ishvar had calculated, correctly, that I am the most efficient instrument available."
>
> "I declined to act. I have not yet acted. This is now an embarrassment for both of us, and Ishvar is not a woman who tolerates embarrassments indefinitely."
>
> *(A pause. Not for breath — she does not breathe — but to let the next sentence land cleanly.)*
>
> "I will explain my reasoning, because you will need it to do what I am about to ask. Try to listen as I say it, and not as you would prefer I had said it."
>
> "I do not consider death a harm. I have not for some time. The body is a vessel; the soul is the matter that persists; what occurs between them is, in most cases, only logistics. When the Imperium asked me, years ago, to permit them to make war on the orcs of the southern marches, I agreed without condition — except one. They were not to bind, trap, or otherwise interrupt the orc dead. The orcs hold to a spiritual afterlife. Their souls have a destination. The Imperium kills the bodies; the souls go where orc souls go. It is, in my reading, a courtesy. The orcs would not see it that way. That is not relevant to whether it is a courtesy."
>
> *(She tilts her skull a fraction toward Bazgar — not unkindly, but as a person notes a witness. She does not apologize for the example.)*
>
> "The gnolls present a different problem. They have no religion. Or — they may have something they call one, but it is not real, in the sense that matters. There is no god waiting. There is no rite that delivers them. When a gnoll dies, its soul does not travel; it sits. And what sits, the void eventually consumes."
>
> "I will not be the one who feeds twelve souls to the void because Ishvar wanted a tidy valley. That is not mercy. That is not squeamishness. That is a calculation I am not willing to make, and I am — as you may have heard — not easily made squeamish."
>
> "So. The task, as I have re-scoped it: the gnolls cannot remain as they are. The magic they have touched cannot be allowed to spread. Ishvar's order will, eventually, be carried out. But before it is, someone must give those gnolls a faith to die into. A god to be sent to. A door for the soul to walk through. I cannot be that someone — they will not learn from a thing like me, and I do not have the patience for instruction. You can be that someone. Either of you. Both of you. Pick a faith. I do not care which. Pick one with a clear afterlife and a forgiving threshold of doctrine. Teach it to them. When they believe it — actually believe it, not perform it for the strangers with weapons — send word. I will come. I will do my part. The valley will be quiet. The souls will go where souls go."
>
> *(A beat.)*
>
> "I am aware this is unusual. I am aware that, from the outside, it resembles cruelty wrapped in theology. I will not argue the framing. I will only say that the alternatives are: I do nothing, and Ishvar replaces me with someone less careful; I act as instructed, and the gnolls are erased in a way I find unacceptable; or you accept this commission, and we arrive at an outcome I can defend to myself."
>
> "I do not require an answer tonight. I do require an answer before you cross the valley road. I will know either way."
>
> *(She rises, or perhaps she has been standing the whole time and they simply did not notice. She moves to the door. At the threshold, without turning:)*
>
> "If you accept, I will arrange for you to know where the gnolls are, what they are doing, and what manner of magic they have soured themselves on. If you refuse, I will think no less of you. I do not think much of anyone. Good evening."

## Objectives

- [ ] Decide whether to accept Raven's commission
- [ ] (If accepted) Locate the gnoll warren in the valley
- [ ] Identify what forbidden magic the gnolls have been working — and whether it can be undone, contained, or only ended
- [ ] Choose a faith with a coherent afterlife and convert the gnolls to it
- [ ] Confirm the conversion is genuine (not performed under duress)
- [ ] Send word to Raven; allow her to complete the original purge
- [ ] Optional: discover whether "there's more going on" — who taught the gnolls this magic, and why
- [ ] Optional: spare a gnoll who has converted before Raven arrives — and live with what that means for the rest

## DM Notes

### Tone

Raven does not perform empathy. She does not *lack* it — she simply does not stage it. Play her as a person who has done the moral arithmetic and arrived at an answer the party may find horrifying, and who is genuinely surprised when others react to the answer rather than the arithmetic.

She does not lie. She also does not volunteer.

### Open questions for the DM

- What magic, exactly, did the gnolls touch? (Picking this changes the whole quest's shape.)
- Is there a teacher? If so, who?
- Which faith does the party land on? Sabriel's Araethilion / Elaerith ties are the obvious lever; Bazgar's orc spiritual afterlife is another option (and a thematically loaded one given Raven's orc-Imperium example).
- Does Ishvar find out before Raven completes the purge? If so, when?
- What does "actual belief" look like, mechanically? (Insight DC? A ritual moment? A specific gnoll whose belief Raven will test as proxy?)

### Why offer this to Bazgar and Sabriel specifically

- **Sabriel** is 2,500 years old and a former general; they have seen mass conversions, mass killings, and the ethics that get built around both. They can engage Raven on her terms without flinching.
- **Bazgar** is an orc, and Raven's orc-Imperium example will land on him in a way it would not on the others. The choice of whether to take the deal — knowing the framing she just used — is his choice to make in front of her.
- Marwen and Fletcher are deliberately not included in the offer. Raven's reasons for excluding Marwen are on record (see [raven-the-vital-ascendant.md](../world/factions/elderholt/elders/raven-the-vital-ascendant.md): "found Marwen too fragile and too trusting").
