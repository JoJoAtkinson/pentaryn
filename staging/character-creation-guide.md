# Building a Level 1 Character — Quick Guide

## Step 1 — Pick a Class
Your class defines what you do in combat and how you grow.

**The 12 core classes (2024 PHB):**
- **Barbarian** — rage tank, big melee damage
- **Bard** — support caster + skills
- **Cleric** — healing + combat
- **Druid** — nature caster + shape-shifting
- **Fighter** — weapons master, most flexible martial
- **Monk** — unarmed, mobile
- **Paladin** — holy warrior, smite damage
- **Ranger** — wilderness, ranged + light spells
- **Rogue** — stealth, skills, sneak attack
- **Sorcerer** — innate caster
- **Warlock** — pact caster, short-rest recharges
- **Wizard** — biggest spell list

> **Source flexibility:** You're not limited to the core 12. Any class, subclass, species, or background from a book compatible with the **5.5e (2024) ruleset** is allowed — third-party included if it's clearly built for 2024 rules. If you're unsure whether something is compatible, ask before locking it in.

[D&D Beyond — Classes](https://www.dndbeyond.com/classes) · [Free 2024 Rules](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/free-rules)

## Step 2 — Pick a Species
Determines size, speed, and a few baked-in traits.

Common core options: Human, Elf, Dwarf, Halfling, Orc, Tiefling, Dragonborn, Gnome, Goliath, Aasimar.

[D&D Beyond — Species](https://www.dndbeyond.com/species)

## Step 3 — Pick a Background
Gives you skill proficiencies, a tool/language, starting gear, ability score bonuses (+2/+1), and roleplay prompts (Ideals, Bonds, Flaws).

Examples: Acolyte, Criminal, Folk Hero, Sage, Soldier, Guide, Sailor, Artisan.

[D&D Beyond — Backgrounds](https://www.dndbeyond.com/backgrounds)

## Step 4 — Ability Scores (Point Buy)
Six abilities: **STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA**

Use **point buy**: 27 points to spend.

[Point Buy Calculator](https://chicken-dinner.com/5e/5e-point-buy.html)

Apply your background's +2/+1 ability bonuses *after* spending points.

**Modifier formula:** `(score − 10) ÷ 2`, rounded down.

## Step 5 — Fill Out the Sheet
- **HP**: Class hit die max + CON modifier (Fighter = 10 + CON; Wizard = 6 + CON)
- **AC**: 10 + DEX mod, modified by armor
- **Proficiency bonus**: +2 at level 1
- **Saving throws**: Two from your class
- **Skills**: From class + background
- **Attack bonus**: Proficiency + STR (melee) or DEX (finesse/ranged)
- **Spells** (if caster): Pick cantrips + level-1 spells from your class list

## Step 6 — Equipment
Take your class's **pre-built starting kit**. Don't worry about shopping or rolling for gold — anything beyond the kit can be picked up in-character during play.

## Step 7 — Name, Alignment, Personality
Name, alignment (LG, NG, CG, LN, TN, CN, LE, NE, CE), and 1–2 personality traits. Background gives you the prompts.

## Tracking Your Character

You just need to track your character somehow — pick whatever works for you:

| Option | Best for |
|---|---|
| [D&D Beyond Character Builder](https://www.dndbeyond.com/characters) | Easiest — does the math for you, free account |
| [Official Fillable PDF](https://media.wizards.com/2014/dnd/downloads/5E_CharacterSheet_Fillable.pdf) | Type into the PDF, save and share |
| [Printable PDF Sheet](https://dnd.wizards.com/resources/character-sheets) | Print and fill by hand |
| Excel / Google Sheets | Roll your own — search "5e character sheet excel" for community templates |
| Word doc or plain notebook | Totally fine — just track stats, HP, abilities, gear, spells |

**The bare minimum to track:**
- Name, class, species, background, level
- Ability scores + modifiers
- HP, AC, speed, proficiency bonus
- Saving throws + skills (and which you're proficient in)
- Attacks (weapon, attack bonus, damage)
- Spells (if any) — known/prepared, slots
- Equipment
- Personality / Ideals / Bonds / Flaws
